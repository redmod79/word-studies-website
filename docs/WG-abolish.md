# WG: Nullify / Abolish / Destroy

## Overview
Greek NT vocabulary for rendering something void, powerless, or non-existent. These words share the semantic territory of English "abolish," "nullify," "destroy," and "invalidate" but each occupies a distinct register: legal, physical, functional, or visual. The cluster is dominated by katargéō (G2673) in theological contexts and apóllymi (G622) in narrative contexts.

## Roles in the Concept

- **Strip of function:** katargéō (G2673) — render entirely idle; the law's condemning power, death's dominion, partial gifts
- **Tear down / dissolve:** katalýō (G2647) — dismantle the temple, break up a meeting, dissolve a meal-companion arrangement
- **Void legally:** akyróō (G208) — formally invalidate a prior legal instrument (covenants, statutes)
- **Remove from sight:** aphanízō (G853) — make invisible through corruption (moths/rust) or deliberate concealment
- **Destroy / perish:** apóllymi (G622) — the broadest term; total destruction or being lost

## Key Passages

- **Gal 3:17** — G2673 + G208 together: "the law...cannot *katargeō* [the covenant], that it should *akyróō* the promise" — two synonyms for nullification in one verse, distinguishing covenantal authority from legal promise
- **1 Cor 15:24–26** — G2673 alone: "he shall have put down all rule...the last enemy shall be destroyed [is] death" — progressive abolition of hostile powers
- **Eph 2:15** — G2673 alone: "having abolished in his flesh the enmity, [even] the law of commandments" — covenantal/legal register
- **2 Tim 1:10** — G2673 alone: "who hath abolished death" — functional register: death still exists but its dominion is stripped
- **Matt 26:61 / Mark 14:58** — G2647: Jesus accused of threatening to "destroy this temple" — physical dismantling register
