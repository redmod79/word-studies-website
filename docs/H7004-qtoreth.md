## Lexicon Definition

From H6999 קָטַר (to fumigate/make smoke); **a fumigation** — (sweet) incense, perfume.

The word denotes the act-product of smoking/fumigating: the aromatic compound burned on the altar or used as perfume. KJV renders it almost exclusively as "incense" (with modifiers "sweet incense" when paired with H5561 סַם), and rarely as "perfume" in non-cultic contexts.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Sanctuary Design and Ingredients
Specifications for the incense altar and the compound formula.
- **Exo 30:1** — "thou shalt make an altar to burn incense upon: of shittim wood shalt thou make it"
- **Exo 30:35** — "thou shalt make it a perfume, a confection after the art of the apothecary, tempered together, pure"
- **Exo 37:25** — "he made the incense altar of shittim wood: the length of it was a cubit"
- **Exo 37:29** — "he made the holy anointing oil, and the pure incense of sweet spices"

### 2. Daily Priestly Service Before the LORD
Burning incense morning and evening as part of the tamid (continual) service.
- **Exo 30:7–8** — "Aaron shall burn thereon sweet incense every morning... a perpetual incense before the LORD"
- **2Chr 13:11** — "they burn unto the LORD every morning and every evening burnt sacrifices and sweet incense"
- **Lev 16:12–13** — Aaron brings incense into the most holy place on Day of Atonement to cover the mercy seat

### 3. Tabernacle/Temple Inventory
Incense listed among the sacred furnishings and supply items.
- **Exo 25:6** — "spices for anointing oil, and for sweet incense"
- **Exo 35:15** — "the incense altar, and his staves, and the anointing oil, and the sweet incense"
- **Exo 39:38** — "the golden altar, and the anointing oil, and the sweet incense"
- **1Chr 28:18** — "for the altar of incense refined gold by weight"

### 4. Numbers Census Offerings
Each tribal leader presents a golden spoon of incense as part of the tabernacle dedication offering.
- **Num 7:14** — "One spoon of ten shekels of gold, full of incense"
- **Num 7:86** — "The golden spoons were twelve, full of incense, weighing ten shekels apiece"

### 5. Unauthorized Incense / Judgment
Incense used outside divinely sanctioned bounds brings divine judgment.
- **Num 16:7** — Korah's company: "put fire therein, and put incense in them before the LORD"
- **Num 16:35** — "fire from the LORD consumed the two hundred and fifty men that offered incense"
- **2Chr 26:19** — Uzziah "had a censer in his hand to burn incense... while he was wroth with the priests"
- **Lev 10:1** — Nadab and Abihu offer "strange fire" with incense, not commanded

### 6. Prophetic Condemnation
Incense offered to idols or as empty external ritual divorced from obedience.
- **Isa 1:13** — "incense is an abomination unto me; the new moons and sabbaths, the calling of assemblies"
- **Ezek 8:11** — "seventy men of the ancients of Israel... every man his censer in his hand; a thick cloud of incense went up"
- **Ezek 16:18** — "thou hast set mine oil and mine incense before them"
- **Ezek 23:41** — "thou hast set mine incense and mine oil" on the idol table

### 7. Perfume (Non-Cultic)
One occurrence uses qeṭoreth in a domestic, non-liturgical sense.
- **Prov 27:9** — "Ointment and perfume rejoice the heart: so doth the sweetness of a man's friend by hearty counsel"
