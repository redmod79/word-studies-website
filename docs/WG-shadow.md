# WG: Shadow / Type / Image — NT Typological Language

## Overview
Five Greek words form the NT vocabulary for describing how OT institutions, ceremonies, and persons prefigure Christ and the new covenant realities. Centered in Hebrews and Colossians, this cluster contrasts the preliminary (shadow, type) with the substantial (image, body, antitype).

### The Shadow–Image Axis
The core theological tension of this word group: σκιά (preliminary outline) → εἰκών (full reality). Heb 10:1 places these in explicit contrast: "the law having a shadow...and not the very image." The shadow is real but incomplete; the image is the substance.

## Roles in the Concept

- **Preliminary outline:** σκιά (G4639) — what the OT provides: a shadow that points forward
- **Pattern/stamp:** τύπος (G5179) — the OT person or event as a positive model to be fulfilled or followed
- **Full reality:** εἰκών (G1504) — the NT substance that the shadow pointed to; what the shadow is NOT
- **Fulfillment:** ἀντίτυπος (G499) — the corresponding type; OT ceremony = type, NT reality = antitype
- **Action verb:** ἐπισκιάζω (G1982) — to overshadow; used of divine glory covering (cloud at Sinai, Transfiguration, Spirit on Mary)

## Key Passages Where the Concept Is Developed

- **Col 2:17** — σκιά: "Which are a shadow of things to come; but the body is of Christ" — shadow/body contrast; Mosaic regulations (food, Sabbath, new moons) as σκιά
- **Heb 8:5** — τύπος + σκιά: "Who serve unto the example [typos] and shadow [skia] of heavenly things" — Levitical priests serve in a copy of the heavenly tabernacle
- **Heb 10:1** — σκιά + εἰκών: "the law having a shadow of good things to come, and not the very image" — the annual sacrifices can never perfect because they are shadow, not image
- **Heb 9:24** — ἀντίτυπος: the earthly holy places are "figures [antitypa] of the true" heavenly sanctuary
- **1 Pet 3:21** — ἀντίτυπος: baptism is "the like figure" (antitype) of Noah's flood
