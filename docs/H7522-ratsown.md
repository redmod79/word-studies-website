## Semantic Categories

### 1. Acceptable / Voluntary Offering (Cultic)
Used in Levitical law for offerings that are "acceptable" before the LORD, or for freewill/voluntary offerings offered at one's own discretion.

- **Lev 1:3** — "he shall offer it at your own will (râtsôwn) before the LORD"
- **Lev 22:20** — "whatsoever hath a blemish, that shall ye not offer: for it shall not be acceptable (râtsôwn) for you"
- **Lev 23:11** — "he shall wave the sheaf before the LORD, to be accepted (râtsôwn) for you"
- **Exod 28:38** — "Aaron may bear the iniquity of the holy things... that they may be accepted (râtsôwn) before the LORD"

### 2. God's Favour / Grace Toward His People
The LORD's gracious, favourable disposition shown to individuals or Israel — often contrasted with wrath or hiding of face.

- **Psalm 5:12** — "with favour (râtsôwn) wilt thou compass him as with a shield"
- **Psalm 30:5** — "in his favour (râtsôwn) is life: weeping may endure for a night, but joy cometh in the morning"
- **Psalm 89:17** — "in thy favour (râtsôwn) our horn shall be exalted"
- **Isa 60:10** — "in my wrath I smote thee, but in my favour (râtsôwn) have I had mercy on thee"

### 3. Delight of the Righteous / What Is Pleasing (Wisdom)
Proverbs uses râtsôwn for what is deeply pleasing or delightful — to God or to a king — often in antithesis to abomination (תּוֹעֵבַה).

- **Prov 11:1** — "a just weight is his delight (râtsôwn)" [contrasted with false balance as abomination]
- **Prov 12:22** — "they that deal truly are his delight (râtsôwn)" [contrasted with lying lips as abomination]
- **Prov 16:13** — "righteous lips are the delight (râtsôwn) of kings"
- **Prov 15:8** — "the prayer of the upright is his delight (râtsôwn)"

### 4. Acceptable Time / Year of Divine Favour
An appointed season in which God acts graciously; the phrase עֵת רָצוֹן ("time of favour") is a recurring idiom.

- **Isa 49:8** — "In an acceptable time (râtsôwn) have I heard thee, and in a day of salvation have I helped thee"
- **Isa 61:2** — "To proclaim the acceptable year (râtsôwn) of the LORD, and the day of vengeance of our God"
- **Psalm 69:13** — "my prayer is unto thee, O LORD, in an acceptable time (râtsôwn)"
- **Psalm 51:18** — "Do good in thy good pleasure (râtsôwn) unto Zion"

### 5. A Sovereign's Will / Decree
The will or pleasure of a king — what he decides and enacts as royal authority; prominent in Daniel and Esther.

- **Dan 11:3** — "a mighty king shall rule with great dominion, and do according to his will (râtsôwn)"
- **Dan 11:36** — "the king shall do according to his will; he shall exalt himself above every god"
- **Esth 1:8** — "the drinking was according to the law; the king had appointed to all officers of his house that they should do according to every man's pleasure (râtsôwn)"
- **Dan 8:4** — the ram "did according to his will (râtsôwn), and became great"

### 6. Doing God's Will (Devotional Obedience)
The worshipper's personal desire and delight to do God's will — a willing, voluntary orientation of the heart.

- **Psalm 40:8** — "I delight to do thy will (râtsôwn), O my God: yea, thy law is within my heart"
- **Psalm 103:21** — "ministers of his, that do his pleasure (râtsôwn)"
- **Psalm 143:10** — "Teach me to do thy will (râtsôwn); for thou art my God"
- **Psalm 145:19** — "He will fulfil the desire (râtsôwn) of them that fear him"
