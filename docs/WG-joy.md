# WG: Joy / Gladness / Rejoicing

## Overview

The biblical vocabulary for joy spans several cognate clusters in Hebrew and Greek. These words cover the affective state (joy/gladness), its verbal expression (rejoice, exult), and its festal/communal embodiment (feast, celebrate). In the LXX, OT joy-vocabulary consolidates onto a smaller Greek set, producing a strong Hebrew-to-Greek bridge — especially simchâh → euphrosynē/chara and sâmach → euphrainō. In the NT, χαρά (chará) becomes the signature noun for Kingdom/Spirit-grounded joy (Rom 14:17; Gal 5:22; 1 Pet 1:8), with χαίρω (chaírō) as its active verbal counterpart.

## Relationships

### Shared Roots

- **χαρ- cluster:** χαρά (G5479), χαίρω (G5463), χάρις (grace, G5485), χαρίζομαι (G5483) — joy and grace share the same Indo-European root; gift and gladness are etymologically bound.
- **ἀγαλλ- cluster:** ἀγαλλίασις (G20), ἀγαλλιάω (G21) — noun + verb pair for exultant joy.
- **εὐφρ- cluster:** εὐφραίνω (G2165), εὐφροσύνη (G2167) — from εὖ (well) + φρήν (mind) — "good-mindedness."
- **śmḥ cluster (Hebrew):** שָׂמַח / שִׂמְחָה (H8055/H8057) — verb and cognate noun; the dominant OT joy root.
- **śwś cluster (Hebrew):** שׂוּשׂ / שָׂשׂוֹן (H7797/H8342) — verb and cognate noun; paired frequently with śmḥ in poetic parallelism.

The LXX tends to route the **śmḥ** family toward εὐφρ- (festal joy) and route the more exuberant Hebrew verbs (**gîyl, rânan, sûws**) toward ἀγαλλ- (exultation). χαρά primarily picks up simchâh/sâsôwn when rendering OT joy-substantives. This confirms chara ≠ exuberant exultation (that is agalliasis) and ≠ festal merriment (that is euphrosyne); chara is the settled inner gladness.

## Roles in the Concept

- **Inner state (noun):** chará (G5479), simchâh (H8057), sâsôwn (H8342) — "joy" as the condition.
- **Expression (verb):** chaírō (G5463), sâmach (H8055), gîyl (H1523), rânan (H7442), sûws (H7797) — "rejoice" as the act.
- **Intensified exultation:** agallíasis (G20), agalliáō (G21), rânan (H7442), gîyl (H1523) — loud, exuberant, demonstrative.
- **Festal / communal merriment:** euphraínō (G2165), euphrosýnē (G2167), and contexts where simchâh attaches to mishteh (feast, H4960) — joy embodied in shared celebration.
- **Source/grounding:**
  - Hebrew joy is often rooted in YHWH's salvation (Isa 35:10; 51:11; Ps 16), festival worship (Lev 23; Deut 16), and national deliverance (Est 8:16).
  - NT chara is grounded in the Holy Spirit (Rom 14:17; Gal 5:22; Acts 13:52), in Christ's own joy shared with disciples (Jhn 15:11; 17:13), and persists through/because of suffering (Jas 1:2; Heb 12:2; 1 Pet 1:8).
