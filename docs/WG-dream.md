# WG: Dream / Nocturnal Revelation

## Overview
The biblical dream-concept is not a single word but a small vocabulary cluster: the act of dreaming, the dream itself (Hebrew and Aramaic), its interpretation, and the surrounding institutional roles (prophet, visionary) with which dreams are paired or contrasted. The cluster centers on the Joseph narrative (Gen 37–42), the Daniel court-dreams (Dan 2, 4, 7), the false-prophet polemic (Deut 13; Jer 23, 29), and the eschatological outpouring of Joel 2:28 / Acts 2:17.

## Roles in the Concept

- **Act:** חָלַם (chalam, H2492) — the verb: to dream
- **Object (Hebrew):** חֲלוֹם (chalowm, H2472) — a dream; the content dreamed
- **Object (Aramaic):** חֵלֶם (chelem, H2493) — Daniel's Aramaic dream-noun
- **Decoding:** פִּתְרוֹן (pithrown, H6623) — the interpretation without which a dream is unintelligible (Gen 40:8 "do not interpretations belong to God?")
- **Counterpart (waking):** חָזוֹן (chazown, H2377) — the waking visionary counterpart to the nocturnal dream
- **Institutional pair/foil:** נָבִיא (nabiy, H5030) — prophet; sometimes paired with dreamer as legitimate channels (Num 12:6; Joel 2:28) and sometimes opposed as the threshold between true and false revelation (Deut 13; Jer 23)

## The Two Polarities

The cluster organizes around a binary the biblical writers maintain carefully:

**Legitimate dream-revelation:** Gen 28 (Jacob), Gen 37/40/41 (Joseph), Num 12:6 (prophetic norm), Dan 2/4/7 (court dreams with divinely granted pithrown), Joel 2:28 (eschatological sign).

**Illegitimate / false dream-claim:** Deut 13 (sign-working dreamers who lead astray), Jer 23:25–32 (prophets who fabricate dreams), Jer 29:8 (self-induced dreams), Zech 10:2 (dreamers speak vanity), Jude 1:8 (NT echo of the false-dreamer tradition).

The presence of פִּתְרוֹן (H6623) that "belongs to God" (Gen 40:8; Dan 2:28) is the distinguishing mark of the legitimate stream; the absence of God-given interpretation marks the false.
