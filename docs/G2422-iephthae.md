## Narrative Background (reference only)

The Jephthah of Judg 11–12 is the son of Gilead by a harlot, expelled by his half-brothers, later recalled by the elders of Gilead to lead them against Ammon. He defeats Ammon after his vow concerning whomever first comes out of his house, which results in the sacrifice/dedication of his daughter. He also fights Ephraim (the shibboleth episode) and judges Israel six years. Hebrews 11:32 lists him among exemplars of faith.
