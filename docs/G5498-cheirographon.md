## Lexicon Definition

Neuter compound of χείρ (G5495) and γράφω (G1125); something hand-written — a handwritten document, specifically a handwritten record or acknowledgment. In Greek legal usage, the term refers to a signed note of indebtedness written in the debtor's own hand: a chirograph.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Handwritten Certificate / Document with Legal Force

The word designates a written instrument bearing the handwriting of the one who incurred the obligation — functioning as a legally binding acknowledgment. In Col 2:14 the cheirographon is described as (a) "against us" (καθ' ἡμῶν), (b) "contrary to us" (ὑπεναντίον ἡμῖν), and (c) the object of Christ's cancellation by nailing it to the cross.

- **Col 2:14** — "Blotting out the handwriting of ordinances (τοῖς δόγμασιν) that was against us, which was contrary to us, and took it out of the way, nailing it to his cross"

The accompanying genitive τοῖς δόγμασιν ("of ordinances/decrees," G1378) specifies the content of the document — the regulatory requirements. The three verbal actions applied to the cheirographon (ἐξαλείψας, "blotting out"; ἦρκεν ἐκ τοῦ μέσου, "taken out of the way"; προσηλώσας, "nailing") all describe permanent cancellation of a binding written record.
