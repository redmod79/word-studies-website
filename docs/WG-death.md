# WG: Death, Life, Sin, Hades, Resurrection

## Overview

The NT death-cluster organizes around a five-axis framework: death as noun (thanatos), its verbal causative (thanatoo), the realm of the dead (hades), death's source (hamartia/sin), death's antonym (zoe/life), and death's reversal (anastasis/resurrection). Romans 5–8 is the theological center of gravity where five of the six members converge.

## Roles in the Concept

- **The state:** θάνατος (thanatos) — death as noun: what is experienced, feared, defeated
- **The act:** θανατόω (thanatoo) — to cause death; also "mortify" (Rom 8:13) as spiritual practice
- **The realm:** ᾅδης (hades) — where the dead reside; thanatos and hades always move together in Revelation
- **The cause:** ἁμαρτία (hamartia) — sin generates death (Rom 6:23); removing sin removes death's basis
- **The antonym:** ζωή (zoe) — life is not merely the absence of thanatos but its active opposite (John 5:24)
- **The reversal:** ἀνάστασις (anastasis) — resurrection undoes thanatos; the final enemy destroyed (1 Cor 15:26)
