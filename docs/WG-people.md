## Roles in the Concept

- **Informal gathering:** ochlos (ὄχλος) — a casual crowd with no organizational structure; the throng that gathers around a teacher, healer, or spectacle
- **Constituted people:** laos (λαός) — a people defined by shared identity (Israel, the church); carries covenant overtones
- **Quantified group:** plēthos (πλῆθος) — emphasizes the fullness or large number of the group, often with partitive genitive ("multitude of priests," "multitude of the heavenly host")
- **Civic body:** dēmos (δῆμος) — the public citizenry as a political unit; the formal assembly of a Greek city
- **Crowd-making action:** ochlopoieō (ὀχλοποιέω) — the act of forming a mob, with negative connotation of disturbance

## Key Passages Where Multiple Group Words Appear Together

- **Luke 6:17** — ochlos + plēthos + laos in a single description of the crowds at the Sermon on the Plain
- **Acts 19:33-35** — ochlos (the mob) within the context of the dēmos (the civic assembly of Ephesus)
- **Matt 26:47** — ochlos + laos in the arrest narrative, distinguishing the armed crowd from the people
- **Rev 17:15** — ochlos alongside laos and ethnos in the eschatological vision of Babylon's dominion
