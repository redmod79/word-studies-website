# WG: Fear / Reverence

## Overview
The fear/reverence concept spans both testaments through the yare-phobeo bridge, the strongest verb-to-verb LXX connection in the group (241 co-occurrences). Hebrew distinguishes yare (reverent awe / terror) from pachad (sudden alarm / dread), while Greek unifies most of the range under the phob- family. The word group covers both positive covenant fear of God and negative human terror, with proskyneo (worship) as the behavioral outworking and deilos (cowardly fear) as the condemned counterpart.

## Roles in the Concept

- **Core verb:** phobeo (G5399) — the act of fearing or revering; neutral — context determines whether positive (reverence) or negative (terror)
- **State/disposition:** phobos (G5401) — the condition of fear; also neutral — "fear of God" (positive) vs. "fear of death" (negative)
- **Quality/description:** phoberos (G5398) — "fearful, terrible" — describes the object that provokes fear (Heb 10:27, 31; 12:21)
- **Outward response:** proskyneo (G4352) — worship as the behavioral expression of godly fear (Rev 14:7 links "fear God" directly to "worship him")
- **Cautious piety:** eulabeomai (G2125) — careful reverence, used of Noah (Heb 11:7) — more specific than phobeo
- **Negative counterpart:** deilos (G1169) — cowardly, timid fear — always condemned (Mat 8:26; Mark 4:40; Rev 21:8 lists "the fearful" among the lost)

## Key Passages Where Multiple Group Words Appear Together

- **Rev 14:7** — phobeo + proskyneo: "Fear God, and give glory to him... and worship him that made heaven, and earth"
- **Rev 15:4** — phobeo + proskyneo: "Who shall not fear thee, O Lord... for all nations shall come and worship before thee"
- **Mark 4:40-41** — deilos + phobeo + phobos: "Why are ye so fearful (deilos)?" then "they feared (phobeo) exceedingly" with "great fear (phobos)" — the full spectrum in two verses
- **1 John 4:18** — phobos + phobeo: "perfect love casteth out fear (phobos)... he that feareth (phobeo) is not made perfect in love"
- **Luke 2:9-10** — phobeo + phobos: "they were sore afraid (phobeo with mega phobos)" then "Fear (phobeo) not"
