## Semantic Categories

### 1. Eucharistic Remembrance — The Lord's Supper Command
- **Luke 22:19** — "This is my body which is given for you: this do in remembrance of me."
- **1 Cor 11:24** — "This is my body, which is broken for you: this do in remembrance of me."
- **1 Cor 11:25** — "This cup is the new testament in my blood: this do ye, as oft as ye drink it, in remembrance of me."

All three use the formula τοῦτο ποιεῖτε εἰς τὴν ἐμὴν ἀνάμνησιν ("do this for my remembrance"). The noun functions as the purpose of the repeated act — the bread and cup serve as a means of active recollection.

### 2. Sacrificial Remembrance — OT Offerings Recalling Sin
- **Heb 10:3** — "But in those sacrifices there is a remembrance again made of sins every year."

Here anamnesis is the opposite effect: rather than eliminating the memory of sin, the annual Day of Atonement sacrifices bring sins back to mind — a yearly reminder that the blood of bulls and goats cannot permanently remove sin.
