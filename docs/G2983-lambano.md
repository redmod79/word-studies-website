## Lexicon Definition

A prolonged form of a primary verb, which is used only as an alternate in certain tenses; to take (in very many applications, literally and figuratively).

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Physical Taking / Grasping
Concrete, physical act of picking up or seizing an object.
- **Mat 26:26** — "Jesus took bread, and blessed it, and brake it"
- **Mat 27:30** — "they spit upon him, and took the reed, and smote him on the head"
- **Rev 10:10** — "I took the little book out of the angel's hand, and ate it up"
- **Jhn 13:4** — "He riseth from supper... and took a towel, and girded himself"

### 2. Receiving from Another
Accepting what is given, granted, or transmitted — the passive counterpart of "give" (didomi).
- **Jhn 1:12** — "as many as received him, to them gave he power"
- **Acts 2:33** — "having received of the Father the promise of the Holy Ghost"
- **1Cor 11:23** — "I have received of the Lord that which also I delivered unto you"
- **Rom 1:5** — "by whom we have received grace and apostleship"

### 3. Receiving the Holy Spirit
A specialized use for the reception of the Spirit — a significant theological collocation.
- **Jhn 7:39** — "the Spirit, which they that believe on him should receive"
- **Jhn 20:22** — "Receive ye the Holy Ghost"
- **Acts 8:17** — "they laid their hands on them, and they received the Holy Ghost"
- **Acts 19:2** — "Have ye received the Holy Ghost since ye believed?"

### 4. Taking / Assuming Power or Authority
Used for the assumption of royal power, office, or dominion — often in Revelation.
- **Rev 4:11** — "Thou art worthy, O Lord, to receive glory and honour and power"
- **Rev 5:12** — "Worthy is the Lamb that was slain to receive power, and riches, and wisdom"
- **Rev 11:17** — "thou hast taken to thee thy great power, and hast reigned" (perfect: εἴληφας)
- **Luk 19:12** — "A certain nobleman went into a far country to receive for himself a kingdom"

### 5. Obtaining Reward or Punishment
Receiving consequences — wages, reward, judgment, or damnation.
- **Mat 20:9** — "they received every man a penny"
- **1Cor 3:14** — "he shall receive a reward"
- **Mar 12:40** — "these shall receive greater damnation"
- **Heb 2:2** — "every transgression and disobedience received a just recompence of reward"

### 6. Taking Counsel / Taking Occasion
Idiomatic uses where lambano combines with a noun to form a verbal phrase.
- **Mat 12:14** — "the Pharisees... held [took] a council against him"
- **Rom 7:8** — "sin, taking occasion by the commandment"
- **Mat 22:15** — "the Pharisees... took counsel how they might entangle him"
- **Jas 5:10** — "Take, my brethren, the prophets... for an example"

### 7. Accepting or Refusing Teaching/Testimony
Mental reception — accepting or refusing truth claims.
- **Jhn 3:11** — "we speak that we do know... and ye receive not our testimony"
- **Jhn 3:32-33** — "no man receiveth his testimony... He that hath received his testimony hath set to his seal that God is true"
- **Jhn 12:48** — "He that rejecteth me, and receiveth not my words"
- **2Jo 1:10** — "If there come any unto you, and bring not this doctrine, receive him not"
