## Semantic Categories

### 1. Golden Vials of Incense — Prayers of the Saints
The first use frames the vials as sacred liturgical vessels before the Lamb, holding the intercessions of God's people as incense.

- **Rev 5:8** — "the four beasts and four and twenty elders fell down before the Lamb, having every one of them harps, and **golden vials** full of odours, which are the prayers of saints"

### 2. Seven Golden Vials of the Wrath of God
The dominant usage: vials filled with divine wrath (θυμός), given to seven angels as the final series of judgments.

- **Rev 15:7** — "one of the four beasts gave unto the seven angels **seven golden vials** full of the wrath of God, who liveth for ever and ever"
- **Rev 16:1** — "Go your ways, and pour out the **vials** of the wrath of God upon the earth"

### 3. The Seven Poured-Out Vials — Plagues on the Earth
Each vial, when poured, triggers a distinct plague. The pattern is repeated seven times through ch. 16.

- **Rev 16:2** — "the first went, and poured out his **vial** upon the earth; and there fell a noisome and grievous sore upon the men which had the mark of the beast"
- **Rev 16:3** — "the second angel poured out his **vial** upon the sea; and it became as the blood of a dead man"
- **Rev 16:4** — "the third angel poured out his **vial** upon the rivers and fountains of waters; and they became blood"
- **Rev 16:17** — "the seventh angel poured out his **vial** into the air; and there came a great voice out of the temple of heaven, from the throne, saying, It is done"

### 4. Vial-Bearing Angels as Prophetic Guides
After the plagues, the same angels who carried the vials reveal visions to John — their identity as "vial-angels" authenticates the revelations.

- **Rev 17:1** — "there came one of the **seven angels which had the seven vials**, and talked with me, saying unto me, Come hither; I will shew unto thee the judgment of the great whore"
- **Rev 21:9** — "there came unto me one of the **seven angels which had the seven vials** full of the seven last plagues... Come hither, I will shew thee the bride, the Lamb's wife"
