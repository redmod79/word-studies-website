## Semantic Categories

### 1. Rebellion against YHWH and his command
- **Deu 31:27** — Moses says, "I know thy rebellion," tying the word to Israel's resistance to YHWH.
- **1Sa 15:23** — rebellion is aligned with witchcraft and set against hearing the word of the LORD.
- **Neh 9:17** — the people appoint a captain "in their rebellion" while refusing obedience and forgetting God's wonders.

### 2. Rebellious people, sons, or children as a class
- **Num 17:10** — Aaron's rod is preserved "for a token against the rebels."
- **Isa 30:9** — Judah is "a rebellious people," defined further as children who will not hear the law of the LORD.
- **Eze 44:6** — the word can stand almost as a title: "say to the rebellious, even to the house of Israel."

### 3. Ezekiel's stock formula: "the rebellious house"
- **Eze 2:5** — whether they hear or forbear, they are still "a rebellious house."
- **Eze 3:27** — the hear/forbear formula is framed by the same rebel-house designation.
- **Eze 12:2** — the house has eyes and ears but refuses perception, making rebellion a pattern of spiritual non-response.
- **Eze 17:12** — the formula introduces Ezekiel's interpretation of Jerusalem's political revolt.

### 4. Defiant or embittered disposition
- **Job 23:2** — the unique bitter use shows the term can still express embittered complaint.
- **Pro 17:11** — "An evil man seeketh only rebellion," using the noun for active defiance.
- **Eze 2:8** — Ezekiel is warned not to become rebellious like the house he is sent to address.
