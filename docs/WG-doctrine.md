# WG: Doctrine / Teaching

## Overview
The NT doctrine/teaching concept is built on the διδάσκω (didasko) word family — a root verb with derived nouns for the teacher, the act/content of teaching, the body of established teaching, and compounds for deviant teaching. The qualifier ὑγιαίνω (hugiaino, "be sound/healthy") provides the Pastoral Epistles' distinctive health metaphor for orthodox doctrine.

### Didaskalia vs. Didache — Functional Distinction
Both nouns derive from didasko and both are translated "doctrine" in KJV. Their distributions differ:
- **didache** (G1322) appears across Gospels, Acts, Epistles, and Revelation (11 books). It frequently denotes the freshly delivered content or the act itself ("astonished at his doctrine," Mat 7:28).
- **didaskalia** (G1319) concentrates in the Pastorals (71% of occurrences, 8 books total). It denotes the established body of apostolic teaching as a system to be maintained, guarded, and transmitted.

In Tit 1:9, both words appear in a single verse — didache for the faithful word "as taught" (act/tradition) and didaskalia for "sound doctrine" (the authoritative body of content).

## Roles in the Concept

- **Activity:** didasko (G1321) — the verb of teaching itself
- **Agent:** didaskalos (G1320) — the one who teaches (Master, teacher, doctor)
- **Delivered content:** didache (G1322) — teaching as act or as freshly communicated material
- **Established body:** didaskalia (G1319) — doctrine as a settled system of instruction
- **Quality marker:** hugiaino (G5198) — the health/soundness criterion applied to doctrine
- **Deviance marker:** heterodidaskaleo (G2085) — teaching a different doctrine
- **Teacher quality:** didaktikos (G1317) — the quality of being instructive/apt to teach
