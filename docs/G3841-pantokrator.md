## Semantic Categories

### 1. Liturgical Doxology — "Lord God Almighty"
The fixed phrase κύριος ὁ θεὸς ὁ παντοκράτωρ ("Lord God Almighty") appears as a worship formula:
- **Rev 4:8** — "Holy, holy, holy, Lord God Almighty, which was, and is, and is to come."
- **Rev 11:17** — "We give thee thanks, O Lord God Almighty, which art, and wast, and art to come."
- **Rev 15:3** — "Great and marvellous are thy works, Lord God Almighty; just and true are thy ways, thou King of saints."
- **Rev 16:7** — "Even so, Lord God Almighty, true and righteous are thy judgments."

### 2. Self-Identification / Christological Title
Used in divine self-declarations or as a title identifying God's essential nature:
- **Rev 1:8** — "I am Alpha and Omega, the beginning and the ending, saith the Lord, which is, and which was, and which is to come, the Almighty."
- **2Co 6:18** — "And will be a Father unto you, and ye shall be my sons and daughters, saith the Lord Almighty."

### 3. Eschatological Sovereignty — God Acting in Judgment
The genitive form appears when divine power is exercised in final acts of judgment or cosmic rule:
- **Rev 16:14** — "...to gather them to the battle of that great day of God Almighty."
- **Rev 19:15** — "...he treadeth the winepress of the fierceness and wrath of Almighty God."
- **Rev 19:6** — "Alleluia: for the Lord God omnipotent reigneth."

### 4. Divine Presence Replacing the Temple
- **Rev 21:22** — "And I saw no temple therein: for the Lord God Almighty and the Lamb are the temple of it."
