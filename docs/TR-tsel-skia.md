## OT Usage (Hebrew)

### H6738 — צֵל (tsêl): Shade, Shadow
| Strong's | Word | Occurrences | Primary Meaning |
|----------|------|-------------|-----------------|
| H6738 | צֵל (tsêl) | 47 | shade, shadow — literal shade from sun; metaphor for divine protection |

The dominant OT use of tsêl is **divine protection imagery** — God's shadow sheltering his people:

- **Ps 91:1** — "He that dwelleth in the secret place of the most High shall abide under the shadow of the Almighty"
- **Ps 17:8** — "hide me under the shadow of thy wings"
- **Isa 25:4** — "a refuge from the storm, a shadow from the heat, when the blast of the terrible ones is as a storm against the wall"
- **Isa 49:2** — "in the shadow of his hand hath he hid me"

Also used for the political shadow of nations (Isa 30:2–3 — "trust in the shadow of Egypt") and the literal shade of plants (Jonah 4; Ezek 17:23).

### H6757 — צַלְמָוֶת (tsalmâveth): Shadow of Death
| Strong's | Word | Occurrences | Primary Meaning |
|----------|------|-------------|-----------------|
| H6757 | צַלְמָוֶת (tsalmâveth) | 17 | deep darkness, shadow of death — mortal peril, the grave |

A Hebrew compound (tsêl + mâveth = "shadow of death") or intensive form for "deepest darkness." Used for the domain of death and mortal peril:

- **Ps 23:4** — "Yea, though I walk through the valley of the shadow of death, I will fear no evil"
- **Ps 107:10** — "Such as sit in darkness and in the shadow of death, being bound in affliction and iron"
- **Isa 9:2** — "they that dwell in the land of the shadow of death, upon them hath the light shined"
- **Job 3:5** — "Let darkness and the shadow of death stain it" (Job's birth-day curse)

## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same:**
- Shadow as something real but **derivative** — it requires a substance to produce it; this idea persists from OT tsêl to NT typological σκιά
- The **light/shadow contrast** — OT protection language (shadow sheltering from burning light) and the death-shadow passages both set up the NT redemptive pattern (light breaking into shadow)

**What shifts:**
- **tsalmâveth** (one compound noun in Hebrew) becomes σκιά θανάτου (phrase in Greek) — the LXX regularizes the Hebrew compound; the NT inherits the phrase form
- **New typological sense:** The NT adds a semantic dimension absent from OT tsêl — OT institutions as σκιά of heavenly realities. This is a distinctly Pauline/Hebrews development; the OT uses no equivalent typological formula
- **Protection sense diminished:** OT tsêl frequently means divine protection ("shadow of thy wings"). The NT does not develop this sense with σκιά (though ἐπισκιάζω preserves it at the Transfiguration and Annunciation)
