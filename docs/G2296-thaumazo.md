## Semantic Categories

### 1. Marveling at Jesus' Words / Teaching
Reaction of hearers (crowds, disciples, opponents, Jesus himself at the centurion) to Jesus' verbal authority, answers, or teaching.

- **Matt 8:10 / Luke 7:9** — Jesus marvels at the centurion's faith.
- **Matt 22:22** — "when they had heard [these words], they marvelled, and left him."
- **Mark 12:17** — "and they marvelled at him" (Caesar's coin reply).
- **Luke 20:26** — "they marvelled at his answer, and held their peace."
- **John 7:15** — "the Jews marvelled, saying, How knoweth this man letters, having never learned?"

### 2. Marveling at Miracles / Deeds
Reaction to healings, exorcisms, nature miracles, and raisings.

- **Matt 8:27** — "the men marvelled, saying, What manner of man is this, that even the winds and the sea obey him!"
- **Matt 9:33** — "the multitudes marvelled, saying, It was never so seen in Israel."
- **Matt 15:31** — "the multitude wondered, when they saw the dumb to speak..."
- **Mark 5:20** — "all [men] did marvel" (Decapolis publishes Jesus' deeds).
- **Luke 9:43** — "while they wondered every one at all things which Jesus did."
- **Luke 11:14** — "the people wondered" (dumb demoniac).
- **Acts 2:7** — "they were all amazed and marvelled" (Pentecost tongues).

### 3. Marveling as Unbelief / Perplexity
The wonder is disapproving, unsettled, or reflects a failure to understand; sometimes rebuked by Jesus.

- **Mark 6:6** — "he marvelled because of their unbelief" (Jesus' reaction).
- **John 3:7** — "Marvel not that I said unto thee, Ye must be born again."
- **John 5:28** — "Marvel not at this: for the hour is coming..."
- **John 7:21** — "I have done one work, and ye all marvel."
- **Gal 1:6** — "I marvel that ye are so soon removed from him that called you..." (Paul's rebuke).
- **1 John 3:13** — "Marvel not, my brethren, if the world hate you."

### 4. Marveling at Unexpected / Counter-expectation Events
The wonder arises because what occurred violates expectation — a governor surprised by silence, disciples by an empty tomb, Pharisees by omitted washing.

- **Matt 27:14** — "the governor marvelled greatly" (Jesus' silence before Pilate).
- **Mark 15:5** — "Pilate marvelled" (at Jesus' silence).
- **Mark 15:44** — "Pilate marvelled if he were already dead."
- **Luke 1:21** — "the people... marvelled that he tarried so long in the temple."
- **Luke 1:63** — "they marvelled all" (Zacharias writing "John").
- **Luke 11:38** — "the Pharisee... marvelled that he had not first washed."
- **Luke 24:12** — "departed, wondering in himself at that which was come to pass" (empty tomb).
- **Luke 24:41** — "while they yet believed not for joy, and wondered."
- **John 4:27** — "marvelled that he talked with the woman."
- **Acts 3:12** — "why marvel ye at this?"
- **Acts 4:13** — "they marvelled; and they took knowledge of them, that they had been with Jesus."
- **Acts 7:31** — "Moses... wondered at the sight" (burning bush).

### 5. Wonder Directed at a Divine/Eschatological Object (passive / future)
Used in passive or future constructions where the wonder terminates on a theological object — Christ at his coming, or an apocalyptic figure. Includes the only passive and future-passive forms in the word's NT usage.

- **2 Thess 1:10** — "when he shall come to be glorified in his saints, and **to be admired** (θαυμασθῆναι, aor. pass. inf.) in all them that believe."
- **Rev 13:3** — "all the world **wondered** (ἐθαυμάσθη, aor. pass. 3sg) after the beast." *(the wonder follows the wound-and-healing sequence; verb marks the turn from injury to worldwide reaction.)*
- **Rev 17:6** — "when I saw her, **I wondered** (ἐθαύμασα) with great admiration (θαῦμα μέγα)." *(figura etymologica: cognate accusative intensifies.)*
- **Rev 17:7** — "Wherefore **didst thou marvel** (ἐθαύμασας)?" *(angel to John, responding to 17:6.)*
- **Rev 17:8** — "they that dwell on the earth **shall wonder** (θαυμασθήσονται, fut. pass. 3pl), whose names were not written in the book of life from the foundation of the world, when they behold the beast that was, and is not, and yet is."

### 6. Warning / Rebuke Usage (imperative, esp. negated)
θαυμάζω appears in direct imperatives (often negated) to check a wrong reaction in the hearer.

- **John 3:7** — "Marvel not (μὴ θαυμάσῃς)..."
- **John 5:28** — "Marvel not at this (μὴ θαυμάζετε)..."
- **Acts 3:12** — "why marvel ye (τί θαυμάζετε)?"
- **Acts 13:41** — "Behold, ye despisers, and wonder (θαυμάσατε), and perish" (LXX quotation of Hab 1:5).
- **1 John 3:13** — "Marvel not (μὴ θαυμάζετε), my brethren."

## Revelation Focus — All Five Occurrences

Revelation contains four verses (5 occurrences counting the cognate noun in 17:6) of θαυμάζω, clustered in the beast passages.

| Ref | Greek Form | Parse | Subject | Object / Direction |
|-----|-----------|-------|---------|--------------------|
| Rev 13:3 | ἐθαυμάσθη | aor. pass. 3sg | ὅλη ἡ γῆ ("the whole earth") | ὀπίσω τοῦ θηρίου ("after the beast") — follows the wound-and-healing sequence |
| Rev 17:6 | ἐθαύμασα (+ θαῦμα μέγα) | aor. act. 1sg + cognate noun | John | The woman drunken with blood; cognate-accusative intensification |
| Rev 17:7 | ἐθαύμασας | aor. act. 2sg | John (quoted by angel) | Refers back to 17:6 |
| Rev 17:8 | θαυμασθήσονται | fut. pass. 3pl | οἱ κατοικοῦντες ἐπὶ τῆς γῆς ("those who dwell on the earth") | The beast that "was, is not, and shall be present" (καίπερ ἔσται) |

Both passive forms (13:3, 17:8) take the beast as the focus of wonder and specify the wondering subjects as earth-dwellers. 17:8 is the only future-tense form of θαυμάζω in the NT. The parallel of aorist-passive (13:3) → future-passive (17:8) frames the beast's reception by the same grammatical voice at two temporal points.
