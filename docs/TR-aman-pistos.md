## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same across testaments:**
- The core meaning: being established, firm, reliable — worthy of trust
- The dual semantic range: both *trusting* (active/subjective) and *trustworthy* (passive/objective)
- Application to God's own character: ʼāman and πιστός both describe divine reliability in keeping covenant
- The Abraham paradigm: Gen 15:6's ʼāman becomes the explicit OT anchor for NT πίστις/πιστός (Rom 4, Gal 3)

**What shifts:**
- In the NT, πιστός gains a specifically *ecclesial* sense: "the faithful" = believers/Christians as a community (Eph 1:1, 1 Tim 4:12)
- The "faithful saying" (πιστὸς ὁ λόγος) formula is unique to the Pastoral Epistles — no Hebrew equivalent
- Christ himself becomes the *subject* of πιστός as the Faithful Witness (Rev 1:5, 3:14, 19:11) — a title drawn from OT covenant-faithfulness language applied to Messiah
