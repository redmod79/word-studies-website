## Lexicon Definition

From θλίβω (G2346); *pressure* (literally or figuratively):—afflicted(-tion), anguish, burdened, persecution, tribulation, trouble.

The core image is **pressing or squeezing** — the noun denotes an external force bearing down on a person. It is used exclusively in figurative, experiential senses in the NT.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Eschatological / Cosmic Tribulation
End-time events of unparalleled scope, often marked by the phrase "great tribulation."
- **Matt 24:21** — "For then shall be great tribulation, such as was not since the beginning of the world to this time, no, nor ever shall be"
- **Matt 24:29** — "Immediately after the tribulation of those days shall the sun be darkened..."
- **Mark 13:19** — "For in those days shall be affliction, such as was not from the beginning of the creation which God created"
- **Rev 7:14** — "These are they which came out of great tribulation, and have washed their robes... in the blood of the Lamb"

### 2. Persecution Arising from Faith
Tribulation inflicted on believers because of their confession or proclamation of the word.
- **Matt 13:21** — "when tribulation or persecution ariseth because of the word, by and by he is offended"
- **Acts 11:19** — "they which were scattered abroad upon the persecution that arose about Stephen"
- **1 Thess 1:6** — "ye received the word in much affliction, with joy of the Holy Ghost"
- **Heb 10:33** — "whilst ye were made a gazingstock both by reproaches and afflictions"

### 3. Apostolic and Ministerial Hardship
Suffering belonging to the calling of ministry; associated with Paul's bonds, travels, and mission.
- **Acts 20:23** — "the Holy Ghost witnesseth in every city, saying that bonds and afflictions abide me"
- **2 Cor 1:8** — "our trouble which came to us in Asia, that we were pressed out of measure, above strength"
- **2 Cor 4:17** — "our light affliction, which is but for a moment, worketh for us a far more exceeding and eternal weight of glory"
- **Phil 1:16** — "supposing to add affliction to my bonds"

### 4. Tribulation as Spiritual Catalyst
Suffering that produces patience, deepens faith, and coexists with joy; embraced or gloried in.
- **Rom 5:3** — "we glory in tribulations also: knowing that tribulation worketh patience"
- **Rom 12:12** — "Rejoicing in hope; patient in tribulation; continuing instant in prayer"
- **2 Cor 8:2** — "in a great trial of affliction the abundance of their joy and their deep poverty abounded unto the riches of their liberality"
- **Rev 1:9** — "I John... your companion in tribulation, and in the kingdom and patience of Jesus Christ"

### 5. Divine Retributive Tribulation
Tribulation that God sends as judgment on the disobedient; a form of righteous recompense.
- **Rom 2:9** — "Tribulation and anguish, upon every soul of man that doeth evil, of the Jew first, and also of the Gentile"
- **2 Thess 1:6** — "it is a righteous thing with God to recompense tribulation to them that trouble you"
- **Rev 2:22** — "I will cast her into a bed, and them that commit adultery with her into great tribulation, except they repent"
