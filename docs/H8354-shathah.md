## Lexicon Definition

A primitive root; to imbibe (literally or figuratively) — assuredly, banquet, certainly, drink(-er, -ing), drunk(-ard), surely.

The sense is the simple act of imbibing any liquid — water, wine, strong drink, blood, milk, vinegar, or figuratively "the waters of Sihor" (Jer 2:18) or "the wine of violence" (Pro 4:17). Context alone supplies the beverage. The causative/hiphil sense ("give to drink, cause to drink") is handled by H8248 (שָׁקָה shaqah), not by H8354.

**Pattern:** The verb is broadly distributed — every book from Genesis to Zechariah except Song/Ruth in this data. Highest density in prophetic judgment contexts (Obadiah, Amos, Joel, Habakkuk) where the "cup of wrath" motif dominates, and in narrative court settings (1 Kings, Esther, Judges).
