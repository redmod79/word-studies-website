## Semantic Categories

### 1. Household / Family Objects
In Genesis 31 the teraphim are movable possessions belonging to a household head and kept among domestic goods.

- **Gen 31:19** — "Rachel had stolen the images that [were] her father's."
- **Gen 31:34** — Rachel hides the images "in the camel's furniture."
- **Gen 31:35** — Laban searches but "found not the images."

### 2. Shrine Objects Paired with Ephod and Priest
The Judges material repeatedly places teraphim alongside an ephod, a priest, and other cult objects inside Micah's house shrine.

- **Judg 17:5** — Micah "made an ephod, and teraphim," and consecrated a priest.
- **Judg 18:14** — the spies identify "an ephod, and teraphim, and a graven image, and a molten image."
- **Judg 18:17** — the Danites "took the graven image, and the ephod, and the teraphim, and the molten image."
- **Judg 18:20** — the priest "took the ephod, and the teraphim, and the graven image."

### 3. Human-Sized Domestic Figure
In 1 Samuel 19 the term is applied to a single object that can be laid in a bed and dressed to imitate a person closely enough to delay discovery.

- **1 Sam 19:13** — Michal "took an image, and laid [it] in the bed."
- **1 Sam 19:16** — the messengers discover "an image in the bed, with a pillow of goats' [hair]."

### 4. Standard Idol-Term in Condemnation and Reform
Some uses treat teraphim as a stock member of Israel's anti-idolatry vocabulary.

- **1 Sam 15:23** — "stubbornness [is as] iniquity and idolatry."
- **2 Kgs 23:24** — Josiah puts away "the images, and the idols, and all the abominations."
- **Zech 10:2** — "the idols have spoken vanity."

### 5. Consultation / Divination Setting
The prophetic texts associate teraphim with inquiry, omens, or the absence of cultic apparatus.

- **Ezek 21:21** — the king of Babylon "consulted with images."
- **Hos 3:4** — Israel will remain "without an ephod, and [without] teraphim."
- **Zech 10:2** — the teraphim/idols "have spoken vanity," in parallel with diviners and false dreams.
