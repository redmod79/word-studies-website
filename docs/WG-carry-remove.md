# WG: Carry / Bear / Remove

## Overview
The carry/remove concept in the Greek NT centers on αἴρω (aírō) and its compounds, alongside the semantically adjacent βαστάζω and φέρω. Together they cover the full range from physical lifting to sustained burden-bearing to decisive removal. All three primary members share the Hebrew source H5375 nāśāʾ through LXX translation, creating a direct bridge between OT sin-bearing/removal language and NT usage.

## Relationships

### Word Family (aírō compounds)
aírō (G142) is the root; compounds add directional prefixes:
- **ἀπ-** (away from) → ἀπαίρω (G522): lift off, remove
- **ἐπ-** (upon) → ἐπαίρω (G1869): raise up, lift up (eyes, hands, voice)
- **ὑπερ-** (above) → ὑπεραίρω (G5229): exalt beyond measure
- **μετ-** (change) → μεταίρω (G3332): depart
- **συν-** (together) → συναίρω (G4868): reckon together (settle accounts)
- **ἐξ-** (out) → ἐξαίρω (G1808): remove out, put away

## Roles in the Concept

- **Decisive lifting/removal:** aírō (G142) — the punctiliar act of lifting or removing; broadest term
- **Sustained bearing:** bastázō (G941) — carrying a burden over time; weight, endurance
- **Directional conveyance:** phérō (G5342) — bringing or carrying toward a destination
- **Upward offering:** anaphérō (G399) — carrying upward, specifically sacrificial offering (Heb 7:27, 9:28, 1 Pet 2:24)
- **Downward removal:** kathairéō (G2507) — taking down, pulling down from a position
- **Complete stripping:** periairéō (G4014) — removing all around, total removal (Acts 27:20 hope, 2 Cor 3:16 veil, Heb 10:11 sins)
- **Separation/departure:** apaírō (G522) — lifting away, specifically the bridegroom "taken away" (Matt 9:15, Mark 2:20, Luke 5:35)

## Key Passages Where Multiple Group Words Appear Together
- **Mark 2:3** — aírō (borne) + phérō (bringing): carrying the paralytic
- **John 20:15** — bastázō (borne him) + aírō (take him away): Mary at the tomb
- **Heb 9:28** — anaphérō (to bear the sins of many): sacrificial bearing — compare John 1:29 aírō (taketh away the sin)
