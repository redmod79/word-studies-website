## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same:**
- "White" as color of purity and honor persists from OT to NT — Eccl 9:8 (garments), to Rev 3:4–5 (redeemed)
- "White" as descriptor of the divine: Dan 7:9 (Ancient of Days) → Rev 1:14 (Christ), same word in LXX and NT
- Zechariah's apocalyptic horse visions (white horses) → Revelation's white horse (Rev 6:2; 19:11)

**What shifts:**
- OT dominant usage: natural objects (linen, manna, leprosy marks, hair), livestock (Gen 30), prophetic horses (Zech)
- NT dominant usage (61% in Revelation): eschatological garments of the redeemed, heavenly throne/horse/cloud imagery — white becomes a concentrated symbol of heavenly authority and the victorious state of the saints
- The Levitical leprosy associations (white skin marks) present in LXX entirely absent from NT; NT never uses leukós for skin conditions
