## Lexicon Definition

A primitive root; to fear; morally to revere; causatively to frighten: — affright, be (make) afraid, dread(-ful), (put in) fear(-ful, -fully, -ing), (be had in) reverence(-end), see, terrible (act, -ness, thing).

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Reverent Awe / Worship of God
The dominant OT usage: covenant loyalty expressed as fearing the LORD.
- **Deut 6:13** — "Thou shalt fear the LORD thy God, and serve him, and shalt swear by his name."
- **Deut 10:12** — "What doth the LORD thy God require of thee, but to fear the LORD thy God, to walk in all his ways"
- **Josh 24:14** — "Now therefore fear the LORD, and serve him in sincerity and in truth"
- **Eccl 12:13** — "Fear God, and keep his commandments: for this is the whole duty of man."

### 2. Natural Terror / Human Fear
Emotional fear in response to danger, enemies, or threatening circumstances.
- **Gen 3:10** — "I was afraid, because I was naked; and I hid myself." (first occurrence)
- **1 Sam 17:11** — "When Saul and all Israel heard those words of the Philistine, they were dismayed, and greatly afraid."
- **1 Sam 18:12** — "And Saul was afraid of David, because the LORD was with him"
- **Jon 1:5** — "Then the mariners were afraid, and cried every man unto his god"

### 3. Divine Reassurance ("Fear Not")
God or his messengers commanding the cessation of fear — a recurring prophetic formula.
- **Gen 15:1** — "Fear not, Abram: I am thy shield, and thy exceeding great reward."
- **Isa 41:10** — "Fear thou not; for I am with thee: be not dismayed; for I am thy God"
- **Isa 43:1** — "Fear not: for I have redeemed thee, I have called thee by thy name; thou art mine."
- **Dan 10:12** — "Fear not, Daniel: for from the first day that thou didst set thine heart to understand"

### 4. The Awesome / Terrible (Niphal Participle nora)
God, his name, or his acts described as nora — awe-inspiring, terrible.
- **Exod 15:11** — "Who is like thee, glorious in holiness, fearful in praises, doing wonders?"
- **Deut 10:17** — "The LORD your God is God of gods, and Lord of lords, a great God, a mighty, and a terrible"
- **Psalm 99:3** — "Let them praise thy great and terrible name; for it is holy."
- **Joel 2:31** — "before the great and the terrible day of the LORD come"

### 5. Fearing God as Ethical Foundation
Fear of God as the basis for moral behavior, especially toward the vulnerable.
- **Gen 42:18** — "This do, and live; for I fear God" (Joseph to his brothers)
- **Exod 1:17** — "But the midwives feared God, and did not as the king of Egypt commanded them"
- **Lev 19:14** — "Thou shalt not curse the deaf, nor put a stumblingblock before the blind, but shalt fear thy God"
- **Lev 25:17** — "Ye shall not therefore oppress one another; but thou shalt fear thy God"

### 6. Deterrent Fear (Public / Judicial)
Fear produced by witnessing judgment, deterring others from sin.
- **Deut 13:11** — "And all Israel shall hear, and fear, and shall do no more any such wickedness"
- **Deut 17:13** — "And all the people shall hear, and fear, and do no more presumptuously."
- **Deut 21:21** — "so shalt thou put evil away from among you; and all Israel shall hear, and fear."
- **1 Sam 12:18** — "and all the people greatly feared the LORD and Samuel"

### 7. Syncretistic / Divided Fear
Fearing Yahweh alongside other gods — exposed as contradiction.
- **2 Ki 17:32-33** — "So they feared the LORD, and made unto themselves... priests of the high places... They feared the LORD, and served their own gods"
- **2 Ki 17:34-35** — "they fear not the LORD, neither do they after their statutes... Ye shall not fear other gods"
- **2 Ki 17:39** — "But the LORD your God ye shall fear; and he shall deliver you"
