## Roles in the Concept

- **G2078 éschatos** — the terminal endpoint of a sequence or the lowest point of a rank; also the defining temporal horizon ("last days," "last day")
- **G4413 prōtos** — the originating point of a sequence or the highest point of a rank; prime semantic opposite of éschatos; also means "foremost/chief" in authority
- **G5305 hústeron** — temporally subsequent (comes after something else), but NOT the absolute end; relative, not terminal
- **G1646 eláchistos** — the least in quantity, degree, or value; overlaps with éschatos in the "status/rank" domain (Louw-Nida 087.004) but not in the "sequence" domain (061)

## Key Passages Where Group Words Interact

- **Mat 19:30 / Mar 10:31** — πρῶτοι ἔσχατοι / ἔσχατοι πρῶτοι: "many that are first (prōtoi) shall be last (eschatoi); and the last first" — the kingdom inverts social rank
- **Mat 20:16** — "the last (éschatos) shall be first (prōtos), and the first last" — the great reversal formula
- **Mar 9:35** — "If any man desire to be first (prōtos), the same shall be last (éschatos) of all, and servant of all"
- **Rev 1:17** — "I am the first (prōtos) and the last (éschatos)" — divine self-declaration; direct echo of Isa 44:6
- **Rev 22:13** — "I am Alpha and Omega, the beginning and the end, the first (prōtos) and the last (éschatos)"
- **1Co 15:45–47** — "the first (prōtos) man Adam… the last (éschatos) Adam" — typological axis of history
- **Luk 14:9–10** — "begin with shame to take the lowest (éschatos) room… Go and sit in the lowest (éschatos) room" — social reversal in the dinner parable; éschatos = status domain
