- **Original:** πνεῦμα

- **Pronunciation:** pnyoo'-mah
- **Part of Speech:** Neuter noun
- **Root:** πνέω (pneō) — to blow, breathe
- **Occurrences:** 385 in NT (BLB); 342 indexed in occurrences table (John, James, Philippians, 1–3 John not indexed)
- **First Occurrence:** Matt 1:18 — "she was found with child of the Holy Ghost (pneuma)"
- **Hapax Legomenon:** No

## Lexicon Definition

A current of air, i.e. breath (blast) or a breeze; by analogy or figuratively, a spirit, i.e. (human) the rational soul, (by implication) vital principle, mental disposition, etc., or (superhuman) an angel, demon, or (divine) God, Christ's spirit, the Holy Spirit.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. The Holy Spirit / Spirit of God
The dominant NT usage — the divine Spirit as person, agent, and gift.
- **Matt 3:16** — "he saw the Spirit of God descending like a dove"
- **Acts 2:4** — "they were all filled with the Holy Ghost, and began to speak with other tongues"
- **Rom 8:9** — "if so be that the Spirit of God dwell in you"
- **1 Cor 2:10** — "God hath revealed them unto us by his Spirit: for the Spirit searcheth all things"
- **Gal 5:22** — "the fruit of the Spirit is love, joy, peace, longsuffering"

### 2. Unclean / Evil Spirits
Demonic beings; spirits causing disease or oppression.
- **Matt 12:43** — "When the unclean spirit is gone out of a man"
- **Mark 1:23** — "a man with an unclean spirit; and he cried out"
- **Mark 5:2** — "a man with an unclean spirit"
- **Luke 4:33** — "a man, which had a spirit of an unclean devil"
- **Rev 16:13** — "three unclean spirits like frogs"

### 3. The Human Spirit / Inner Disposition
The immaterial part of a person; seat of emotions, will, and consciousness.
- **Matt 26:41** — "the spirit indeed is willing, but the flesh is weak"
- **Mark 8:12** — "he sighed deeply in his spirit"
- **John 11:33** — "he groaned in the spirit, and was troubled"
- **Acts 17:16** — "his spirit was stirred in him, when he saw the city wholly given to idolatry"
- **Rom 1:9** — "God is my witness, whom I serve with my spirit"

### 4. Life-Force / Animating Breath
The vital principle that sustains life; its departure = death.
- **Matt 27:50** — "Jesus... yielded up the ghost (pneuma)"
- **Luke 23:46** — "Father, into thy hands I commend my spirit"
- **John 19:30** — "he bowed his head, and gave up the ghost"
- **James 2:26** — "the body without the spirit is dead"
- **Rev 11:11** — "the Spirit of life from God entered into them"

### 5. Disembodied Spirits / Spiritual Beings
Spirits of the dead or angelic beings.
- **Luke 24:37** — "they... supposed that they had seen a spirit"
- **Luke 24:39** — "a spirit hath not flesh and bones, as ye see me have"
- **Acts 23:8–9** — "the Sadducees say... neither angel, nor spirit: but the Pharisees confess both"
- **Heb 1:7** — "Who maketh his angels spirits"
- **Heb 12:23** — "the spirits of just men made perfect"

### 6. Wind / Breath (Physical)
Rare in NT; the root meaning from πνέω.
- **John 3:8** — "The wind (pneuma) bloweth where it listeth" (wordplay: wind/Spirit)
- **Heb 1:7** — "Who maketh... his ministers a flame of fire" (context includes wind imagery)

### 7. Figurative / Animating Power
Non-personal life or power given to an object.
- **Rev 13:15** — "he had power to give life (pneuma) unto the image of the beast"
- **1 Cor 15:45** — "the last Adam was made a quickening spirit"
