## Lexicon Definition

A primitive root; to forgive: — forgive, pardon, spare.

## Verb Stem Distribution

| Stem | Count | Typical Meaning |
|------|-------|-----------------|
| Qal | 33 | forgive, pardon (active: God as subject, or prayer requesting forgiveness) |
| Niphal | 13 | be forgiven (passive: the offerer/sinner receives forgiveness) |

**Qal breakdown:** Perfect 16×, Imperfect 10×, Imperative 4×, Infinitive Construct 3×, Participle 1× (Psa 103:3 "who forgiveth").

**Niphal:** All 13 occurrences are Perfect 3ms (nislach), exclusively in the Levitical sacrificial formulae (Lev 4–5, 6:7, 19:22, Num 15:25–28). The formulaic pattern is: "the priest shall make atonement (kaphar) for him... and it shall be forgiven (nislach) him."

**Key observation:** The Niphal passive occurs only in conjunction with sin offerings (chaṭṭaʾah) and trespass offerings (ʾasham) — never with burnt offerings (ʿolah), peace offerings (shelamim), or grain offerings (minchah). When Lev 5:10 mentions a burnt offering in the same verse, the forgiveness formula follows the sin offering portion, not the burnt offering.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Sacrificial Forgiveness Formula (Niphal passive — 13 verses)
The priest makes atonement (kaphar); forgiveness (nislach) follows as the result.
- **Lev 4:20** — "the priest shall make an atonement for them, and it shall be forgiven them"
- **Lev 4:26** — "the priest shall make an atonement for him... and it shall be forgiven him"
- **Lev 5:10** — "the priest shall make an atonement for him for his sin... and it shall be forgiven him"
- **Num 15:25** — "the priest shall make an atonement for all the congregation... and it shall be forgiven them"

### 2. Intercession / Prayer for Forgiveness (Qal imperative/petition — 12 verses)
Moses, Solomon, Daniel, or Amos petitions God to forgive.
- **Exo 34:9** — "pardon our iniquity and our sin, and take us for thine inheritance"
- **1 Ki 8:34** — "Then hear thou in heaven, and forgive the sin of thy people Israel"
- **Dan 9:19** — "O Lord, hear; O Lord, forgive; O Lord, hearken and do"
- **Amo 7:2** — "O Lord GOD, forgive, I beseech thee"

### 3. Divine Promise / Declaration of Forgiveness (Qal perfect/imperfect — 10 verses)
God announces that he has forgiven or will forgive.
- **Num 14:20** — "And the LORD said, I have pardoned according to thy word"
- **Jer 31:34** — "I will forgive their iniquity, and I will remember their sin no more"
- **Jer 33:8** — "I will cleanse them from all their iniquity... and I will pardon all their iniquities"
- **2 Ch 7:14** — "then will I hear from heaven, and will forgive their sin, and will heal their land"

### 4. Conditional / Refused Forgiveness (Qal with negative — 5 verses)
God states conditions or explicitly refuses to forgive.
- **Deu 29:20** — "The LORD will not spare him, but then the anger of the LORD... shall smoke against that man"
- **2 Ki 24:4** — "for the innocent blood that he shed... which the LORD would not pardon"
- **Jer 5:7** — "How shall I pardon thee for this? thy children have forsaken me"
- **Lam 3:42** — "We have transgressed and have rebelled: thou hast not pardoned"

### 5. General Divine Attribute / Habitual Action (Qal participle/infinitive — 3 verses)
- **Psa 103:3** — "Who forgiveth all thine iniquities; who healeth all thy diseases"
- **Isa 55:7** — "let him return unto the LORD, and he will have mercy upon him... for he will abundantly pardon"
- **Psa 25:11** — "For thy name's sake, O LORD, pardon mine iniquity; for it is great"

### 6. Husband Annulling Vows (Qal imperfect — 3 verses)
A unique non-sacrificial usage where YHWH forgives a woman whose husband annulled her vow.
- **Num 30:5** — "the LORD shall forgive her, because her father disallowed her"
- **Num 30:8** — "the LORD shall forgive her"
- **Num 30:12** — "the LORD shall forgive her"
