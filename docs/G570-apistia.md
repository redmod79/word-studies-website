## Semantic Categories

### 1. Unbelief That Limits Divine Power
- **Mat 13:58** — "And he did not many mighty works there because of their unbelief."
- **Mark 6:6** — "And he marvelled because of their unbelief."
- **Mat 17:20** — "Because of your unbelief: for verily I say unto you, If ye have faith as a grain of mustard seed..."

In the Synoptics, apistia names the condition that prevents God from acting. At Nazareth, Jesus's own townspeople limit his works by their unbelief. In Mat 17:20, the disciples' failure to cast out the demon is diagnosed as apistia — even a mustard-seed measure of pistis would have sufficed.

### 2. Unbelief as Rebukable Hardness
- **Mark 9:24** — "Lord, I believe; help thou mine unbelief."
- **Mark 16:14** — "upbraided them with their unbelief and hardness of heart, because they believed not them which had seen him after he was risen"

Here apistia is paired with moral hardness (sklerokardia). The father's cry in Mark 9:24 treats apistia as something that can coexist with partial belief — a condition requiring divine help to overcome.

### 3. Israel's Corporate Unbelief (Romans)
- **Rom 3:3** — "shall their unbelief make the faith of God without effect?"
- **Rom 11:20** — "because of unbelief they were broken off, and thou standest by faith"
- **Rom 11:23** — "if they abide not still in unbelief, shall be graffed in"

Paul uses apistia for Israel's national failure to respond to God's covenant faithfulness. In Rom 3:3, apistia stands opposite "the faith (pistis) of God" — human unfaithfulness versus divine faithfulness. In Rom 11:20-23, apistia is the reason the natural branches were cut off, and its reversal is the condition for regrafting.

### 4. Unbelief as Pre-Conversion State
- **1 Tim 1:13** — "but I obtained mercy, because I did it ignorantly in unbelief"

Paul describes his former persecuting life as conducted "in unbelief" — apistia as the encompassing condition of life before faith. Paired with ignorance (agnoeo, G50).

### 5. Unbelief as Apostasy Warning
- **Heb 3:12** — "lest there be in any of you an evil heart of unbelief, in departing from the living God"
- **Heb 3:19** — "So we see that they could not enter in because of unbelief."

Hebrews applies the wilderness-generation narrative to the church. "An evil heart of unbelief" (kardia ponera apistias) links apistia to the deliberate act of departing (aphistemi, G868) from God. In 3:19, apistia is the final verdict on why Israel failed to enter Canaan.
