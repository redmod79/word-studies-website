## Lexicon Definition

τύπος, τύπου, ὁ (τύπτω), from Aeschylus and Herodotus down:
1. **The mark of a stroke or blow; print** — τῶν ἥλων, the nail marks (John 20:25)
2. **A figure formed by impression; image** — of the images of the gods (Acts 7:43; cf. Amos 5:26)
3. **Form** — διδαχῆς, the teaching which embodies the sum and substance of religion (Romans 6:17); manner/contents of a letter (Acts 23:25)
4. **An example:**
   - α. *Technical* — the pattern in conformity to which a thing must be made: Acts 7:44; Hebrews 8:5 (Exod 25:40)
   - β. *Ethical* — a dissuasive example/warning: 1 Cor 10:6, 11; a model to imitate: Phil 3:17; 1 Tim 4:12; 1 Pet 5:3; 2 Thess 3:9; 1 Thess 1:7; Titus 2:7
   - γ. *Doctrinal/typological* — a person or thing prefiguring a future Messianic person or thing: Adam as τύπος of Christ (Romans 5:14)

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Physical Mark / Impression
The word's most literal sense — the visible imprint left by a blow or nail.
- **John 20:25** — "Except I shall see in his hands the *print* of the nails, and put my finger into the *print* of the nails... I will not believe"

### 2. Image / Idol (Figure Shaped by Impression)
Extending from physical impression → shaped figure → idol image.
- **Acts 7:43** — "Yea, ye took up the tabernacle of Moloch, and the star of your god Remphan, *figures* which ye made to worship them"

### 3. Heavenly Pattern / Architectural Blueprint
The divine archetype shown to Moses that earthly structures must replicate.
- **Acts 7:44** — "Our fathers had the tabernacle of witness... as he had appointed, speaking unto Moses, that he should make it according to the *fashion* that he had seen"
- **Hebrews 8:5** — "Who serve unto the *example* and shadow of heavenly things, as Moses was admonished of God... See that thou make all things according to the *pattern* shewed to thee in the mount" *(cf. Exod 25:40)*

### 4. Doctrinal / Literary Form
A concrete embodiment or formal expression of content.
- **Romans 6:17** — "ye have obeyed from the heart *that form* of doctrine which was delivered you"
- **Acts 23:25** — "And he wrote a letter after *this manner*" *(i.e., in this form/style)*

### 5. Warning Example from OT History
Israel's wilderness failures as written-down cautionary patterns.
- **1 Corinthians 10:6** — "Now these things were our *examples*, to the intent we should not lust after evil things, as they also lusted"
- **1 Corinthians 10:11** — "Now all these things happened unto them for *ensamples*: and they are written for our admonition, upon whom the ends of the world are come"

### 6. Ethical Model to Imitate
A living person whose conduct serves as a concrete pattern for others to follow.
- **Philippians 3:17** — "Brethren, be followers together of me, and mark them which walk so as ye have *us for an ensample*"

- **Titus 2:7** — "In all things shewing thyself *a pattern* of good works"
- **1 Peter 5:3** — "Neither as being lords over God's heritage, but being *ensamples* to the flock"
- **2 Thessalonians 3:9** — "to make ourselves *an ensample* unto you to follow us"
- **1 Thessalonians 1:7** — "So that ye were *ensamples* to all that believe in Macedonia and Achaia"

### 7. Typological Prefigurement (OT → Messianic)
