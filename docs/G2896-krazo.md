## Lexicon Definition

A primary verb; properly, to "croak" (as a raven) or scream, i.e. (generally) to call aloud (shriek, exclaim, intreat). The onomatopoeic origin ("croak") points to a raw, inarticulate vocal quality — a cry that bypasses measured speech.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Demonic Shrieking / Spiritual Confrontation
Unclean spirits recognize and react to Jesus with involuntary screams.
- **Matt 8:29** — "they cried out, saying, What have we to do with thee, Jesus, thou Son of God?"
- **Mark 1:26** — "the unclean spirit had torn him, and cried with a loud voice, he came out of him"
- **Mark 3:11** — "unclean spirits, when they saw him, fell down before him, and cried, saying, Thou art the Son of God"
- **Mark 5:7** — "cried with a loud voice, and said, What have I to do with thee, Jesus, thou Son of the most high God?"

### 2. Desperate Pleas for Mercy / Healing
Afflicted persons cry to Jesus for help, often using the "Son of David" messianic title.
- **Matt 9:27** — "two blind men followed him, crying, and saying, Thou Son of David, have mercy on us"
- **Matt 15:23** — "she crieth after us" (the Canaanite woman)
- **Matt 20:30–31** — "two blind men... cried out... but they cried the more, saying, Have mercy on us, O Lord, thou Son of David"
- **Mark 10:47–48** — "he began to cry out, and say, Jesus, thou Son of David, have mercy on me"

### 3. Crowd Acclaim / Hosanna Cries
Triumphal entry scenes — public acclamation of Jesus as messianic king.
- **Matt 21:9** — "the multitudes... cried, saying, Hosanna to the Son of David"
- **Matt 21:15** — "the children crying in the temple, and saying, Hosanna to the Son of David"
- **Mark 11:9** — "they that went before, and they that followed, cried, saying, Hosanna"
- **John 12:13** — "went forth to meet him, and cried, Hosanna: Blessed is the King of Israel"

### 4. Hostile Mob Cries / Demands for Death
Crowds directing violent demands at authorities.
- **Matt 27:23** — "they cried out the more, saying, Let him be crucified"
- **Mark 15:13–14** — "they cried out again, Crucify him... they cried out the more exceedingly, Crucify him"
- **John 19:12** — "the Jews cried out, saying, If thou let this man go, thou art not Caesar's friend"
- **Acts 19:28, 34** — "they were full of wrath, and cried out, saying, Great is Diana of the Ephesians"

### 5. Jesus' Own Cry — Death and Authoritative Proclamation
Jesus uses krazō both for public teaching proclamation and his final deathbed utterance.
- **Matt 27:50** — "Jesus, when he had cried again with a loud voice, yielded up the ghost"
- **Mark 15:39** — "when the centurion... saw that he so cried out, and gave up the ghost, he said, Truly this man was the Son of God"
- **John 7:28** — "Then cried Jesus in the temple as he taught"
- **John 7:37** — "Jesus stood and cried, saying, If any man thirst, let him come unto me, and drink"

### 6. Adoptive / Spiritual Cry to God
The Spirit enables believers to cry "Abba, Father" — intimate address to God.
- **Rom 8:15** — "ye have received the Spirit of adoption, whereby we cry, Abba, Father"
- **Gal 4:6** — "God hath sent forth the Spirit of his Son into your hearts, crying, Abba, Father"

### 7. Apocalyptic Proclamation / Angelic Cries

- **Rev 6:10** — "they cried with a loud voice, saying, How long, O Lord, holy and true, dost thou not judge"
