## Semantic Categories

### 1. Extended Duration ("Long Time")
The most common usage — measuring how long something has lasted or someone has been somewhere.
- **John 5:6** — "knew that he had been now a long time [in that case]"
- **John 14:9** — "Have I been so long time with you, and yet hast thou not known me, Philip?"
- **Acts 14:28** — "there they abode long time with the disciples"
- **Acts 8:11** — "of long time he had bewitched them with sorceries"

### 2. Fulfilled / Completed Time
Time that arrives, fills up, or runs out — marking transition from one state to another.
- **Luke 1:57** — "Elisabeth's full time came that she should be delivered"
- **Gal 4:4** — "when the fulness of the time was come, God sent forth his Son"
- **Acts 7:17** — "when the time of the promise drew nigh, which God had sworn to Abraham"
- **Rev 10:6** — "there should be time no longer" (eschatological cessation of delay)

### 3. Sovereign Times of History
God's disposal and governance of entire epochs or eras; time as object of divine control.
- **Acts 1:7** — "not for you to know the times or the seasons, which the Father hath put in his own power"
- **Acts 3:21** — "times of restitution of all things, which God hath spoken by the mouth of all his holy prophets"
- **Acts 17:30** — "the times of this ignorance God winked at; but now commandeth all men… to repent"
- **1 Pet 1:20** — "manifest in these last times for you"

### 4. Pre-temporal / Before-Time
Primordial time before the world — God's eternal counsel before creation.
- **Rom 16:25** — "the preaching of Jesus Christ… kept secret since the world began"
- **2 Tim 1:9** — "given us in Christ Jesus before the world began (pro chronōn aiōniōn)"
- **Tit 1:2** — "eternal life, which God… promised before the world began"
- **1 Pet 4:3** — "the time past of our life may suffice us to have wrought the will of the Gentiles"

### 5. Brief Remaining Period / Space for Action
A limited span allotted for something to occur — rest, repentance, tarrying.
- **Rev 2:21** — "I gave her space to repent of her fornication; and she repented not"
- **Rev 6:11** — "rest yet for a little season, until their fellowservants… should be fulfilled"
- **Rev 20:3** — "he must be loosed a little season"
- **1 Cor 16:7** — "I trust to tarry a while with you, if the Lord permit"
