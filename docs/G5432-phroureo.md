## Lexicon Definition

From a compound of πρό (G4253) and ὁράω (G3708); to be a watcher in advance, i.e., to mount guard as a sentinel; figuratively, to hem in, protect.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Military Guarding / Physical Confinement
- **2 Cor 11:32** — "In Damascus the governor under Aretas the king kept the city of the Damascenes with a garrison, desirous to apprehend me"
  - The one clearly literal/military usage. Imperfect active: ongoing surveillance of city exits. The military etymology (sentinel watching in advance) is fully present here.

### 2. Confinement Under the Law (Restrictive Custody)
- **Gal 3:23** — "But before faith came, we were kept under the law, shut up unto the faith which should afterwards be revealed"
  - Imperfect passive: sustained confinement. Paired with συγκλείω (sygkleiō, "shut up"), reinforcing the custodial image. The law functions as a guard that restricts until faith arrives.

### 3. Divine Protective Guarding
- **Phil 4:7** — "And the peace of God, which passeth all understanding, shall keep your hearts and minds through Christ Jesus"
  - Future active: God's peace as sentinel over hearts and minds. The military metaphor is deliberately applied to divine protection — the peace of God garrisons the believer.
- **1 Pet 1:5** — "Who are kept by the power of God through faith unto salvation ready to be revealed in the last time"
  - Present passive participle: continuous divine guarding through faith unto eschatological salvation. The same military word applied to God's protective power.
