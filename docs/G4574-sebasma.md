## Semantic Categories

### 1. Concrete Objects of Pagan Worship
- **Acts 17:23** — "For as I passed by, and beheld your **devotions** [σεβάσματα, plural], I found an altar with this inscription, TO THE UNKNOWN GOD. Whom therefore ye ignorantly worship, him declare I unto you."
  - Paul uses the plural to describe the physical shrines, altars, and images in Athens. The word denotes the tangible objects that receive religious veneration.

### 2. Any Object of Religious Veneration (Abstract / General)
- **2 Thessalonians 2:4** — "Who opposeth and exalteth himself above all that is called God, or **that is worshipped** [σέβασμα, singular]; so that he as God sitteth in the temple of God, shewing himself that he is God."
  - Here the word is used in a broader, more abstract sense: anything that receives worship or religious reverence — whether pagan or true. The Man of Sin exalts himself above every sebasma.
