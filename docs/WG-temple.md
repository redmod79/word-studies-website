## Relationships

### Semantic Distinction: naos vs. hieron vs. oikos
- **naos** — the sanctuary building itself, where God dwells; used for the veil, the inner space, and all metaphorical temple language (believers, Christ's body, heaven)
- **hieron** — the entire temple precinct including courts; used for teaching, commerce, walking, public activity; never used metaphorically in the NT
- **oikos** — general "house"; used for "house of God" in OT quotations (Matt 21:13 quoting Isa 56:7) but not for the sanctuary per se

### Usage Distribution
- **naos** never appears in the same verse as **hieron** — the NT consistently distinguishes them
- **katalyo + oikodomeo** pair appears exclusively in the "destroy/rebuild" sayings about naos, never about hieron
- **oikos** overlaps with hieron in temple contexts (2x) but never with naos — different register

## Roles in the Concept

- **Sacred Space (inner):** naos (ναός) — the dwelling place of deity
- **Sacred Space (outer):** hieron (ἱερόν) — the public temple complex
- **Traditional Designation:** oikos (οἶκος) — "house of God" in OT-quoting contexts
- **Qualifier of Holiness:** hagios (ἅγιος) — marks the space as consecrated
- **Possessor:** theos (θεός) — identifies whose temple it is
- **Destruction:** katalyo (καταλύω) — dismantling the sanctuary
- **Construction:** oikodomeo (οἰκοδομέω) — building/rebuilding
