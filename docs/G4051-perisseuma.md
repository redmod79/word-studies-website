## Semantic Categories

### 1. Inner Overflow — What Fills the Heart Spills Out
- **Matt 12:34** — "for out of the abundance (perisseumatōs) of the heart the mouth speaketh"
- **Luke 6:45** — "for of the abundance (perisseumatōs) of the heart his mouth speaketh"

Both Synoptic sayings use the genitive construction *ek tou perisseumatos tēs kardias* — the surplus stored in the heart is what overflows into speech. The word here is metaphorical: the heart is a container, and perisseuma is what exceeds its capacity.

### 2. Physical Surplus — Leftover Food
- **Mark 8:8** — "they took up of the broken meat that was left (perisseumata) seven baskets"

The only plural occurrence. After the feeding of the 4,000, the surplus fragments are collected — concrete, countable leftovers. This is the most literal use.

### 3. Material Abundance — Financial Surplus for Mutual Supply
- **2 Cor 8:14** — "that now at this time your abundance (perisseuma) may be a supply for their want, that their abundance (perisseuma) also may be a supply for your want: that there may be equality"

Paul uses perisseuma twice in a single verse to describe reciprocal economic surplus. The Corinthians' current material surplus supplies Jerusalem's deficit; Jerusalem's (presumably spiritual) surplus supplies what the Corinthians lack. The word carries the sense of "what you have beyond your own need."
