# WG: Time (Greek NT Vocabulary)

## Overview

The Greek NT vocabulary for "time" involves 4 distinct words that English translations often render identically as "time," "season," or "age." They differ in whether they emphasize measured duration (chronos), appointed occasion (kairos), qualitative epoch (aion), or a brief specific moment (hora). Understanding the distinctions matters for eschatological passages where the nuance of which word is used carries theological weight.

## Relationships

### Semantic Contrast (Key Distinctions)
- **chronos vs. kairos** — chronos measures *how long*; kairos marks *when* the right moment has come. Acts 1:7 uses both together: "the times (χρόνους) or the seasons (καιρούς)" — the duration-spans and the appointed occasions.
- **chronos vs. aion** — chronos = quantitative time as a measurable span; aion = qualitative time as a characterized age/epoch. "Before the world began" (pro chronōn aiōniōn, 2 Tim 1:9) uses both together — literally "before age-times."
- **chronos vs. hora** — hora is the smallest unit; chronos is the broader span. In John 7:33, Jesus says "yet a little while (χρόνον μικρόν)" — a measured span — whereas hora typically marks a decisive turning point.

## Roles in the Concept

- **Duration** — χρόνος (chronos): time measured in length; the span itself
- **Occasion** — καιρός (kairos): the right, appointed, opportune moment within time
- **Epoch** — αἰών (aion): an age characterized by its quality; eternity; "the world" as a time-era
- **Moment** — ὥρα (hora): the immediate hour; a short window; a decisive turning point

## Key Passages Where Multiple Group Words Appear Together

- **Acts 1:7** — "It is not for you to know the *times* (χρόνους) or the *seasons* (καιρούς), which the Father hath put in his own power" — chronos and kairos in direct parallel
- **Rom 13:11** — "knowing the *time* (καιρόν), that now it is high *time* (ὥρα) to awake out of sleep" — kairos and hora in the same sentence
- **2 Tim 1:9** — "given us in Christ Jesus before the world *began* (pro *χρόνων* *αἰωνίων*)" — chronos and aionios together in the "before time" formula
- **1 Th 5:1** — "of the *times* (χρόνων) and the *seasons* (καιρῶν), brethren, ye have no need" — same pairing as Acts 1:7
