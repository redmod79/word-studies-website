# WG: Opening / Access

## Overview
The concept of "opening" in the NT involves a cluster of words describing the action of opening (anoigo, dianoigo), its antonym (kleio, "to shut"), and the primary objects opened (thyra, "door"; sphragis, "seal"; ouranos, "heaven"). These converge especially in Revelation, where opening seals, doors, temples, and heaven itself structures the entire apocalyptic narrative.

## Roles in the Concept

- **Action (opening):** anoigo (G455) — the general verb of opening; dianoigo (G1272) — thorough/complete opening
- **Action (closing):** kleio (G2808) — the antonym; what is opened can be shut, and vice versa
- **Object (portal):** thyra (G2374) — the door/gate that is opened or shut
- **Object (restriction):** sphragis (G4973) — the seal that must be broken/opened to reveal contents
- **Location (access):** ouranos (G3772) — heaven as the destination revealed by opening
