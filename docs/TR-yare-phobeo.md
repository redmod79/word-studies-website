## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same across testaments:**
- Both yare and phobeo span the full range from "terror" to "reverence" — the word is not split into separate fear/worship terms
- "Fear God" as covenant command: Deut 6:13 → 1 Pet 2:17, Rev 14:7
- "Fear not" as divine reassurance: Gen 15:1, Isa 41:10 → angelic and Jesus' "fear not" formulas in the Gospels
- The participle "those who fear God/him" as designation of the faithful: Psa 115:11 → Acts 10:2, 35; 13:16; Rev 11:18; 19:5

**What shifts:**
- The NT develops a technical use absent in the OT: φοβούμενος τὸν θεόν ("God-fearer") becomes a sociological category for Gentile converts in Acts
- The NT intensifies the contrast between fear of God and fear of men (Mat 10:28; Luke 12:4-5) more explicitly than OT usage
- 1 John 4:18 introduces a distinctive claim — "perfect love casteth out fear" — distinguishing servile fear (punishment-based) from mature reverence, a nuance less developed in OT usage
