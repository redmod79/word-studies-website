## Semantic Categories

### 1. Covenant Blood Application
- **Heb 12:24** — "And to Jesus the mediator of the new covenant, and to the blood of sprinkling, that speaketh better things than [that of] Abel."

The "blood of sprinkling" (*haima rhantismou*) describes the application of Christ's blood to ratify the new covenant. The genitive construction ("blood *of* sprinkling") identifies the blood by its function — it is blood meant for sprinkling, i.e., ceremonial application. The verse contrasts this blood with Abel's blood, which cried out for vengeance (Gen 4:10), whereas Christ's blood "speaketh better things."

### 2. Purification of Conscience
- **1 Pet 1:2** — "Elect according to the foreknowledge of God the Father, through sanctification of the Spirit, unto obedience and sprinkling of the blood of Jesus Christ: Grace unto you, and peace, be multiplied."

Here *rhantismos* appears in a Trinitarian formula: the Father's foreknowledge, the Spirit's sanctification, and the Son's blood-sprinkling. The sprinkling is the goal (*eis*, "unto") of the believer's election — obedience and the application of Christ's blood.

The LXX uses ῥαντισμός to translate the concept of purification-water rites in Numbers 19, where the "water of niddah" (water of impurity/separation) was sprinkled on those contaminated by contact with a corpse. This OT background directly informs the NT usage: the blood of Christ replaces the purification water.

## OT Background (Numbers 19)

The lexicon's "Outline of Biblical Usage" lists Num 19:9, 13, 20 — the red heifer purification rite. In the LXX, ῥαντισμός appears in the phrase *hydor rhantismou* ("water of sprinkling/purification") where the MT has *me niddah* ("water of impurity/separation"). This is the primary OT ceremonial context that Hebrews and 1 Peter draw upon when using rhantismos for Christ's blood.
