## Lexicon Definition

Imprecation, execration; an intensified form from kata (down upon) + ara (prayer/imprecation). Denotes a pronounced curse — a formal declaration of doom or condemnation, not a casual insult or personal malediction.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Covenant Curse — Deuteronomic Penalty for Law-Breaking
- **Gal 3:10** — "as many as are of the works of the law are under the curse: for it is written, Cursed is every one that continueth not in all things which are written in the book of the law to do them" (quoting Deut 27:26)
- **Gal 3:13** — "Christ hath redeemed us from the curse of the law, being made a curse for us: for it is written, Cursed is every one that hangeth on a tree" (quoting Deut 21:23)

Both Galatians uses cite Deuteronomy, framing katara as the covenant penalty pronounced on Israel for disobedience — not a personal malediction but a corporate, juridical sentence.

### 2. Agricultural/Land Curse — Barren Ground Under Judgment
- **Heb 6:8** — "But that which beareth thorns and briers is rejected, and is nigh unto cursing; whose end is to be burned"

The curse on unfruitful land echoes Gen 3:17-18 (ground cursed, producing thorns). The agricultural metaphor applies to persons who receive spiritual rain but bear no fruit.

### 3. Speech-Act Curse — Human Imprecation
- **Jas 3:10** — "Out of the same mouth proceedeth blessing and cursing. My brethren, these things ought not so to be"

Here katara is the opposite of eulogia (blessing) as a speech act — words spoken against persons. James treats this as a moral contradiction when directed at those made in God's image.

### 4. Habitual Cursing — Character of False Teachers
- **2 Pet 2:14** — "Having eyes full of adultery, and that cannot cease from sin; beguiling unstable souls: an heart they have exercised with covetous practices; cursed children"

The genitive katara modifies "children" (tekna kataras = "children of a curse"), describing false teachers whose character is defined by the curse they live under and propagate.
