## Lexicon Definition

From H5763 (ʻûl); a suckling — "babe, (young) child, infant, little one."
The term denotes a nursing child / small infant still dependent on the mother,
often paired with its near-synonym יוֹנֵק (yôneq, H3243 — "suckling") as a
hendiadys for "the smallest and most defenseless members of a population."

**Pattern:** Overwhelmingly concentrated in judgment/lament literature. Lamentations
alone accounts for 25% of all uses at the highest density of any book. The prophets
(Jer, Isa, Hos, Joel, Mic, Nah) supply another 40%. The word rarely surfaces outside
contexts of siege, famine, slaughter, or divine wrath — it is the vocabulary of
catastrophe against the most vulnerable. Absent entirely from the Pentateuch (aside
from the Amalek oracle in 1 Sam 15:3, which cites Deut 25), Chronicles, and all
wisdom books except Job 3 and three Psalms.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Victim of Military Slaughter / Herem
The single dominant usage: infants targeted or killed in warfare, siege, or
divine judgment against a city. Typically paired with yôneq (suckling) or with
their mothers.

- **1 Sam 15:3** — "slay both man and woman, infant (ʻôwlêl) and suckling, ox and sheep, camel and ass" (Amalekite herem)
- **1 Sam 22:19** — "smote... both men and women, children (ʻôlêl) and sucklings" (Doeg's slaughter at Nob)
- **Jer 44:7** — "to cut off from you man and woman, child (ʻôlêl) and suckling, out of Judah"
- **Hos 13:16** — "their infants (ʻôlêl) shall be dashed in pieces, and their women with child shall be ripped up"

### 2. Dashed / Torn in Pieces — The Atrocity Formula
A fixed collocation: ʻôwlêl + rṭš (H7376, "dash, shatter") describes infants
killed by being smashed against stones or walls — the stereotyped image of
the worst of siege horrors.

- **Ps 137:9** — "Happy shall he be, that taketh and dasheth thy little ones (ʻôlâl) against the stones"
- **Isa 13:16** — "Their children (ʻôlâl) also shall be dashed to pieces before their eyes"
- **Nah 3:10** — "her young children (ʻôlâl) also were dashed in pieces at the top of all the streets"
- **Hos 13:16** — "their infants (ʻôlêl) shall be dashed in pieces"

### 3. Starving / Dying in Famine and Siege
Infants perishing not by sword but by hunger and drought — the slower atrocity
of blockade and covenant curse.

- **Lam 2:11** — "the children (ʻôlêl) and the sucklings swoon in the streets of the city"
- **Lam 2:19** — "lift up thy hands toward him for the life of thy young children (ʻôlâl), that faint for hunger in the top of every street"
- **Lam 2:20** — "Shall the women eat their fruit, and children (ʻôlâl) of a span long?" (cannibalism in siege)
- **Lam 4:4** — "The tongue of the sucking child (yôneq) cleaveth to the roof of his mouth for thirst: the young children (ʻôlâl) ask bread, and no man breaketh it unto them"

### 4. Exiled or Taken Captive
Infants swept up in deportation — a motif of the totality of the national
disaster, where even those too small to walk are carried off.

- **Lam 1:5** — "her children (ʻôlâl) are gone into captivity before the enemy"
- **2 Kgs 8:12** — "wilt dash their children (ʻôlâl), and rip up their women with child" (Hazael's prophesied atrocities)
- **Jer 6:11** — "I will pour it out upon the children (ʻôlâl) abroad" (wrath poured on every age)

### 5. Cry of Praise / Divine Ordination of Weakness
A rare positive register: infants as the vehicle through which God silences
his enemies. Cited by Jesus in Matt 21:16.

- **Ps 8:2** — "Out of the mouth of babes (ʻôlêl) and sucklings hast thou ordained strength because of thine enemies"

### 6. Infant Mortality / Lament of Existence
The infant as the archetype of the life never lived — Job's counterfactual wish
and the Psalmist's picture of those for whom life is fleeting.

- **Job 3:16** — "Or as an hidden untimely birth I had not been; as infants (ʻôlêl) which never saw light"
- **Ps 17:14** — "they are full of children (ʻôlêl), and leave the rest of their substance to their babes (ʻôlêl)" (wicked prospering, passing on to descendants)

### 7. Gathered into Covenant Assembly
A single positive use: even nursing infants are summoned to solemn assembly,
marking the totality of Israel's repentance.

- **Joel 2:16** — "gather the children (ʻôlêl), and those that suck the breasts"
- **Jer 9:21** — "to cut off the children (ʻôlêl) from without" (inverse image: normally outside at play, now cut off)
- **Mic 2:9** — "from their children (ʻôlêl) have ye taken away my glory for ever" (social-justice oracle)
