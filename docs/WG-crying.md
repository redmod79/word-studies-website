# WG: Crying / Shouting

## Overview
The NT vocabulary for crying and shouting involves a cluster of Greek words that overlap in KJV translation ("cry," "cried," "crying") but carry distinct nuances of volume, emotion, context, and articulation. These words share Hebrew antecedents through the LXX, where zaaq, tsaaq, and qol were rendered by various members of this group.

## Relationships

### Word Family (Cognates)
- **boaō (G994)** → boē (G995, noun "cry"), epiboaō (G1916, compound "cry against"), anaboaō (G310, compound "cry out")

The crying verbs (boaō, krazō, kraugazō) never appear together in the same verse. They are near-synonyms that the NT writers select individually based on the type of cry. However, phōnē ("voice") frequently co-occurs with all three, serving as the paired noun ("with a loud voice," "the voice of one crying").

The LXX treated boaō and krazō as partially interchangeable translations for the Hebrew "cry/shriek" verbs zaaq and tsaaq. The Hebrew qol ("voice/sound") overwhelmingly maps to phōnē, confirming phōnē's role as the acoustic medium rather than the emotional act.

## Roles in the Concept

- **Articulate proclamation:** boaō (G994) — the herald's cry, the cross cry, the prayer cry; implies vocal projection with meaningful content
- **Raw vocalization:** krazō (G2896) — the most frequent and least specific; covers everything from demonic shrieks to messianic acclamation; etymologically "to croak"
- **Group tumult:** kraugazō (G2905) — specifically mob or crowd clamor; Pilate trial scenes (John 18:40, 19:6, 19:12, 19:15)
- **The outcry event:** kraugē (G2906) — noun form marking the cry as a reported event ("there was a cry," "their cry came up")
- **Voice/medium:** phōnē (G5456) — not a cry-word itself but the instrument; "with a loud phōnē" is the standard volume qualifier for all cry verbs
- **Hostile accusation:** epiboaō (G1916) — compound of boaō with ἐπί; "to cry out against someone" (Acts 25:24 only)

## Key Passages Where Multiple Group Words Appear Together

- **Matt 27:46, 50** — Jesus "cried" [boaō, v.46] with a loud voice [phōnē], then "cried" [krazō, v.50] again with a loud voice [phōnē] and yielded up the ghost. Both cry verbs used in the same crucifixion narrative, with boaō for the articulate Psalm 22 quotation and krazō for the final wordless cry.
- **Matt 3:3 / Mark 1:3 / Luke 3:4 / John 1:23** — "The voice [phōnē] of one crying [boaō]" — the boaō + phōnē pairing defines the prophetic herald.
- **Matt 12:19** — "He shall not strive, nor cry [kraugazō]; neither shall any man hear his voice [phōnē] in the streets" — kraugazō + phōnē in a quotation about the Servant's quiet manner.
