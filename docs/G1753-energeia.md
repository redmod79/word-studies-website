## Lexicon Definition

From ἐνεργός ("at work, active"), which is from ἐν + ἔργον: "efficiency," "operation," "active working," "energy." The noun names *operative power actually in motion* — power conceived not as latent capacity but as the exertion or outworking of that capacity. KJV renders: working, effectual working, operation, strong.

## Semantic Categories

The noun always denotes *operative working* — power-in-action rather than power-at-rest. Its theological weight lies in the genitive construction that follows: ἐνέργεια τοῦ X ("working of X") is a **subjective genitive** — X is the source or agent whose power is being exerted. The category below is organized by the identity of that source.

### 1. The Operative Power of God / Christ (resurrection, sanctification, apostolic ministry)
The working of the divine source energizing salvation, resurrection, ministry, and bodily transformation.
- **Eph 1:19** — "what is the exceeding greatness of his power to us-ward who believe, according to the **working** of his mighty power" (κατὰ τὴν ἐνέργειαν τοῦ κράτους τῆς ἰσχύος αὐτοῦ — stacked with three other power words: δύναμις, κράτος, ἰσχύς)
- **Eph 3:7** — "I was made a minister … by the **effectual working** of his power" (κατὰ τὴν ἐνέργειαν τῆς δυνάμεως αὐτοῦ)
- **Col 1:29** — "striving according to his **working**, which worketh in me mightily" (κατὰ τὴν ἐνέργειαν αὐτοῦ τὴν ἐνεργουμένην — noun reinforced by its cognate participle)
- **Col 2:12** — "risen with him through the faith of the **operation** of God, who hath raised him from the dead" (τῆς ἐνεργείας τοῦ θεοῦ)
- **Phil 3:21** — "Who shall change our vile body … according to the **working** whereby he is able even to subdue all things unto himself" (κατὰ τὴν ἐνέργειαν τοῦ δύνασθαι αὐτὸν)

### 2. The Operative Power Distributed through the Body of Christ
The source (Christ/Head) operates *through* the members; ἐνέργεια names the measured outflow of that power into each part.
- **Eph 4:16** — "From whom the whole body fitly joined together and compacted by that which every joint supplieth, according to the **effectual working** in the measure of every part" (κατ' ἐνέργειαν ἐν μέτρῳ ἑνὸς ἑκάστου μέρους)

### 3. The Operative Power of Satan / of Delusion
The same construction is used of Satan as the active source, and then of the deceptive operation God judicially sends. The noun is morally neutral — it names whatever source is actually exerting its power.
- **2 Thess 2:9** — "whose coming is after the **working** of Satan with all power and signs and lying wonders" (κατ' ἐνέργειαν τοῦ Σατανᾶ — the coming of the lawless one is patterned after and energized by the ἐνέργεια of Satan, with the cascade of δύναμις, σημεῖα, τέρατα ψεύδους following in vv. 9–10 as the observable output of that ἐνέργεια)
- **2 Thess 2:11** — "For this cause God shall send them **strong delusion**, that they should believe a lie" (πέμπει αὐτοῖς ὁ θεὸς ἐνέργειαν πλάνης — literally "God sends them a working of deception"; divine judicial action releasing the deceptive operation upon those who rejected the love of the truth)

## Grammatical Pattern — The Subjective Genitive

In seven of eight occurrences ἐνέργεια is qualified by a following genitive, and in every case the genitive identifies the **source/agent** of the working, not its object:

| Reference | Genitive phrase | Agent of the working |
|-----------|-----------------|----------------------|
| Eph 1:19 | τοῦ κράτους τῆς ἰσχύος αὐτοῦ | His mighty power (God) |
| Eph 3:7 | τῆς δυνάμεως αὐτοῦ | His power (God) |
| Eph 4:16 | — (governed by κατ' + μέτρον phrase) | Christ / each member |
| Phil 3:21 | τοῦ δύνασθαι αὐτὸν | His power (Christ) |
| Col 1:29 | αὐτοῦ (+ ἐνεργουμένην) | His (Christ) |
| Col 2:12 | τοῦ θεοῦ | God |
| **2 Thess 2:9** | **τοῦ Σατανᾶ** | **Satan** |
| 2 Thess 2:11 | πλάνης | (deception — object of the sending; the sender is God, v. 11a) |

The seven source-genitives (six divine, one Satanic) establish a fixed Pauline idiom: ἐνέργεια + genitive = "the operative power exerted by X." In 2 Thess 2:11 the governing verb πέμπει with subject ὁ θεός preserves the same logic by grammatical means rather than genitive: God is the sender, πλάνη is what the working consists of.

Five of eight instances occur in the prepositional phrase **κατὰ (τὴν) ἐνέργειαν** ("according to the working of …"), marking the operative power as the *norm or standard* by which a result is produced.

## Historical-Philosophical Note

ἐνέργεια is a technical term in Aristotelian philosophy ("actuality" as opposed to δύναμις = "potentiality"). It does not appear in the canonical LXX and is absent from Classical Hebrew thought categories. Paul — writing in a Hellenistic idiom — imports the word and uses it theologically: not as a metaphysical abstraction but as a concrete designation for *whose power is actually operating* in a given event (God's, Christ's, the risen body's, the members of the church's, or Satan's).
