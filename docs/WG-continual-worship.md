# WG: Continual Worship

## Overview
The "continual" (*tamid*) worship concept in Hebrew Scripture describes the uninterrupted cycle of offerings and sacred elements that formed the daily backbone of Israel's sanctuary service. The word tamid functions as the qualifier that binds together the various sacrificial and ritual terms into a unified system of perpetual worship.

## Roles in the Concept

- **Qualifier:** tamid (תָּמִיד) — marks the offering system as uninterrupted and regular
- **Primary Offering:** olah (עֹלָה) — the twice-daily burnt offering, wholly consumed on the altar
- **Grain Accompaniment:** minchah (מִנְחָה) — flour/oil offering presented alongside the olah
- **Liquid Accompaniment:** nesek (נֶסֶךְ) — wine poured out as drink offering
- **Purification Offering:** chattaah (חַטָּאָה) — sin offering presented "beside the continual"
- **Sacred Bread:** lechem (לֶחֶם) — the showbread set "before the LORD continually"
