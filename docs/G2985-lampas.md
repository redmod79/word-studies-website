## Semantic Categories

### 1. Handheld Lamp for a Procession
Portable oil lamp carried by hand for going out to meet someone; requires oil to stay burning.
- **Mat 25:1** — "ten virgins, which took their lamps, and went forth to meet the bridegroom"
- **Mat 25:3** — "they that were foolish took their lamps, and took no oil with them"
- **Mat 25:7** — "all those virgins arose, and trimmed their lamps"

### 2. Lamp Going Out / Requiring Fuel
The lamp's dependence on oil — burning out is the crisis in the parable.
- **Mat 25:4** — "the wise took oil in their vessels with their lamps"
- **Mat 25:8** — "the foolish said unto the wise, Give us of your oil; for our lamps are gone out"

### 3. Military/Official Torch
A larger, brighter flambeau carried by soldiers for illumination or intimidation.
- **John 18:3** — "Judas then, having received a band of men…cometh thither with lanterns and torches and weapons"
- **Acts 20:8** — "there were many lights in the upper chamber, where they were gathered together"

### 4. Heavenly / Apocalyptic Fire-Lamp
Burning lamp as a symbol of divine presence or a celestial phenomenon in vision.
- **Rev 4:5** — "there were seven lamps of fire burning before the throne, which are the seven Spirits of God"
- **Rev 8:10** — "there fell a great star from heaven, burning as it were a lamp"
