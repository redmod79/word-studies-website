## Lexicon Definition

Ferment (as if boiling up); leaven. Probably from ζέω (zeō, "to boil"). The noun denotes the fermenting agent itself — a lump of old dough retained to start fermentation in a new batch.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Parabolic Image of Pervasive Growth — Kingdom of Heaven
- **Mat 13:33** — "The kingdom of heaven is like unto leaven, which a woman took, and hid in three measures of meal, till the whole was leavened."
- **Luk 13:21** — "It is like leaven, which a woman took and hid in three measures of meal, till the whole was leavened."

The only positive usage. Leaven as an image of hidden, pervasive transformation — a small amount works through an entire batch. Paired with the mustard seed parable (small beginnings → total penetration).

### 2. Warning Against Corrupting Doctrine — Pharisees and Sadducees
- **Mat 16:6** — "Take heed and beware of the leaven of the Pharisees and of the Sadducees."
- **Mat 16:11** — "I spake it not to you concerning bread, that ye should beware of the leaven of the Pharisees and of the Sadducees."
- **Mat 16:12** — "Then understood they how that he bade them not beware of the leaven of bread, but of the doctrine of the Pharisees and of the Sadducees."
- **Mar 8:15** — "Take heed, beware of the leaven of the Pharisees, and of the leaven of Herod."

Jesus explicitly identifies leaven with "doctrine" (Mat 16:12). Mark adds Herod as a third source. The danger is that false teaching, like leaven, silently permeates everything it contacts.

### 3. Warning Against Hypocrisy
- **Luk 12:1** — "Beware ye of the leaven of the Pharisees, which is hypocrisy."

Luke's parallel identifies the leaven specifically as ὑπόκρισις (hypocrisy), narrowing the Matthean "doctrine" to its moral dimension. This usage underlies the Louw-Nida 88.29 domain assignment.

### 4. Moral Contamination in the Congregation
- **1 Cor 5:6** — "Know ye not that a little leaven leaveneth the whole lump?"
- **1 Cor 5:7** — "Purge out therefore the old leaven, that ye may be a new lump, as ye are unleavened. For even Christ our passover is sacrificed for us."
- **1 Cor 5:8** — "Therefore let us keep the feast, not with old leaven, neither with the leaven of malice and wickedness; but with the unleavened bread of sincerity and truth."
- **Gal 5:9** — "A little leaven leaveneth the whole lump."

Paul applies the Passover leaven-purging ritual (Exod 12:15-19) to church discipline. The "old leaven" = pre-conversion moral corruption; "unleavened" = the new-covenant community's purity. The proverbial formula "a little leaven leaveneth the whole lump" appears identically in both 1 Cor 5:6 and Gal 5:9 (in Galatians the contaminating agent is false doctrine about circumcision rather than sexual immorality).
