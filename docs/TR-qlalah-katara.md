## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same across testaments:**
- The formal, juridical character of the curse — a pronounced sentence, not a personal insult
- The Deuteronomic blessing/curse framework (Deut 11:26-28, 28:1-68, 30:1, 19) carries directly into NT usage
- The pairing of katara with eulogia (blessing) as antithetical alternatives appears in both testaments (Deut 11:26 → Jas 3:10)

**What shifts:**
- OT usage is predominantly corporate and national (Israel's covenant blessings and curses); NT usage applies the covenant curse to individual standing before God (Gal 3:10) and to Christ's substitutionary bearing of it (Gal 3:13)
- The OT land-curse imagery (barrenness, thorns) reappears in Heb 6:8 but as spiritual metaphor rather than literal agricultural warning
- 2 Pet 2:14 uses katara as a character descriptor ("children of a curse"), extending beyond the Deuteronomic formula into ethical characterization
