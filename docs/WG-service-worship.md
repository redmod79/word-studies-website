# WG: Service and Worship

## Overview
The Hebrew concept of service-worship is expressed through a cluster of words that together cover the spectrum from manual labor to religious devotion. The anchoring verb ʻâbad (H5647) holds these senses together, while surrounding words differentiate the physical act (shâchâh), the status relationship (shârath), the activity itself (ʻăbôdâh), the generic doing (ʻâsâh), and the Aramaic equivalent (pelach).

## Roles in the Concept

- **Core verb:** ʻâbad (H5647) — the unified work/serve/worship concept, spanning agricultural labor through religious devotion
- **Physical gesture:** shâchâh (H7812) — the bodily act of prostration that accompanies or expresses the service
- **Honorable attendance:** shârath (H8334) — higher-status personal ministry (priests minister; slaves serve)
- **The activity:** ʻăbôdâh (H5656) — the service as a thing; can be positive (tabernacle service) or negative (hard bondage)
- **Aramaic equivalent:** pelach (H6399) — carries the ʻâbad concept into the Aramaic portions of Daniel, where the question "whom do you serve/worship?" is central
- **Generic action:** ʻâsâh (H6213) — supplies "do service" constructions and fills out the Deuteronomic command vocabulary

## Key Passages Where Multiple Group Words Appear Together

- **Num 8:26** — [ʻâbad + shârath + ʻăbôdâh + ʻâsâh] — "minister with their brethren... to keep the charge, and shall do no service" — four group words in one verse distinguishing Levitical roles
- **Num 16:9** — [ʻâbad + shârath + ʻăbôdâh] — "to stand before the congregation to minister unto them" — Moses distinguishes Levitical service from priestly ministry
