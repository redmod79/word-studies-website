# WG: Heard — The Hearing-Obedience Concept Cluster

## Overview
The hearing-obedience concept in Hebrew Scripture involves a core verb (shama) surrounded by synonymous hearing verbs, the content of what is heard (voice, commandments, Torah), and a willingness verb. Together they form the covenant-response vocabulary: God speaks through voice, commandments, and Torah; Israel is to hear/attend/be willing, and responsive hearing constitutes obedience.

## Roles in the Concept

- **Core Action:** shama (שָׁמַע) — the comprehensive hearing-obeying act
- **Intensifiers:** ʼazan (אָזַן) "give ear" + qashab (קָשַׁב) "attend" — sharpen the hearing demand in poetic/prophetic parallelism
- **Willingness:** ʼabah (אָבָה) — the volitional component; negated (loʼ ʼabah) = "would not" = refusal
- **What is Heard:** qowl (קוֹל) "voice" — the medium of divine communication
- **What is Obeyed:** mitsvah (מִצְוָה) "commandment" + towrah (תּוֹרָה) "law/instruction" — the content of what God speaks
