## Lexicon Definition

Accusative case of G1431 (δωρεά) used as adverb; gratuitously (literally or figuratively): freely, without a cause, for nought, in vain.

The word is morphologically an accusative singular noun form that became fixed as an adverb. All 9 NT tokens are parsed [ADV]. It carries the core idea of the gift-noun into adverbial modification: the action it modifies is done "as a gift" — without payment, without merit, or without cause.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Without Cost / As a Gift (Positive — Divine Generosity)
- **Matt 10:8** — "freely ye have received, freely give" — apostolic ministry mirrors divine generosity; both the receiving and giving are dorean
- **Rom 3:24** — "Being justified freely by his grace through the redemption that is in Christ Jesus" — justification received without payment or merit
- **Rev 21:6** — "I will give unto him that is athirst of the fountain of the water of life freely"
- **Rev 22:17** — "And whosoever will, let him take the water of life freely" — eschatological invitation with no cost

### 2. Without Cost (Neutral — No Charge for Labor)
- **2 Cor 11:7** — "because I have preached to you the gospel of God freely?" — Paul charged the Corinthians nothing
- **2 Thess 3:8** — "Neither did we eat any man's bread for nought" — Paul denying he took free meals

### 3. Without Cause / Without Reason (Negative — Groundless Hostility)
- **John 15:25** — "They hated me without a cause" — citing Ps 35:19/69:4 (LXX uses dorean for H2600 chinnam); hostility is unmerited, groundless

### 4. To No Effect / In Vain
- **Gal 2:21** — "if righteousness come by the law, then Christ is dead in vain" — Christ's death rendered purposeless, achieving nothing

## Hebrew Source (via LXX)

The adverb dorean corresponds to the LXX rendering of H2600 חִנָּם (chinnam), which shows the same dual semantic range:

| Hebrew Source | Strong's | LXX Mapping | Notes |
|---------------|----------|-------------|-------|
| חִנָּם (chinnam) | H2600 | G1431 δωρεά 20x co-occ, PMI 9.31 | Primary bridge; chinnam = "gratis, without cost, reason, or advantage" — from H2580 chen "grace/favor" |

The Hebrew chinnam (32 OT verses) carries exactly the same range as dorean: "without cost" (Exod 21:2, 11; Isa 52:3), "without cause" (Ps 35:19, 69:4; 1 Sam 19:5), and "for nothing/in vain" (Job 1:9; Mal 1:10). John 15:25 quotes the LXX of Ps 35:19/69:4, carrying chinnam → dorean into the NT.
