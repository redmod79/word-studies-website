## Semantic Categories

### 1. God's Covenant Loyalty / Enduring Faithfulness
The dominant usage — YHWH's steadfast commitment to his covenant people, often in the refrain "his mercy endureth for ever" (ki le'olam chasdo).
- **Psalm 136:1** — "O give thanks unto the LORD; for he is good: for his mercy endureth for ever."
- **Psalm 100:5** — "For the LORD is good; his mercy is everlasting; and his truth endureth to all generations."
- **Psalm 103:17** — "But the mercy of the LORD is from everlasting to everlasting upon them that fear him, and his righteousness unto children's children."
- **1 Chr 16:34** — "O give thanks unto the LORD; for he is good; for his mercy endureth for ever."

### 2. Divine Attribute in Theophanic Formulas
Checed as a named attribute of God's character, especially in the Exodus 34:6 creedal formula and its echoes.
- **Exo 34:6** — "The LORD, The LORD God, merciful and gracious, longsuffering, and abundant in goodness and truth."
- **Psalm 86:15** — "But thou, O Lord, art a God full of compassion, and gracious, longsuffering, and plenteous in mercy and truth."
- **Psalm 145:8** — "The LORD is gracious, and full of compassion; slow to anger, and of great mercy."
- **Joel 2:13** — "for he is gracious and merciful, slow to anger, and of great kindness, and repenteth him of the evil."

### 3. Paired with Emeth (Truth/Faithfulness) — Covenant Hendiadys
Checed appears with emeth (H571) 33 times, forming a word pair expressing reliable covenant commitment.
- **Gen 24:49** — "And now if ye will deal kindly and truly with my master, tell me."
- **Psalm 25:10** — "All the paths of the LORD are mercy and truth unto such as keep his covenant and his testimonies."
- **Psalm 85:10** — "Mercy and truth are met together; righteousness and peace have kissed each other."
- **Prov 3:3** — "Let not mercy and truth forsake thee: bind them about thy neck; write them upon the table of thine heart."

### 4. Human Covenant Obligation / Interpersonal Loyalty
Checed expected between those in relationship — kinsman, ally, friend, host — arising from covenant or mutual obligation.
- **Ruth 1:8** — "the LORD deal kindly with you, as ye have dealt with the dead, and with me."
- **Ruth 3:10** — "thou hast shewed more kindness in the latter end than at the beginning."
- **1 Sam 20:8** — "Therefore thou shalt deal kindly with thy servant; for thou hast brought thy servant into a covenant of the LORD."
- **2 Sam 9:1** — "Is there yet any that is left of the house of Saul, that I may shew him kindness for Jonathan's sake?"

### 5. Ground of Appeal in Prayer / Petition
The psalmist appeals to God's checed as basis for deliverance and forgiveness.
- **Psalm 51:1** — "Have mercy upon me, O God, according to thy lovingkindness: according unto the multitude of thy tender mercies blot out my transgressions."
- **Psalm 119:88** — "Quicken me after thy lovingkindness; so shall I keep the testimony of thy mouth."
- **Psalm 143:8** — "Cause me to hear thy lovingkindness in the morning; for in thee do I trust."
- **Psalm 69:16** — "Hear me, O LORD; for thy lovingkindness is good: turn unto me according to the multitude of thy tender mercies."

### 6. Ethical Ideal / What God Requires
Checed as something God desires from humans as moral practice.
- **Hos 6:6** — "For I desired mercy, and not sacrifice; and the knowledge of God more than burnt offerings."
- **Mic 6:8** — "He hath shewed thee, O man, what is good; and what doth the LORD require of thee, but to do justly, and to love mercy, and to walk humbly with thy God?"
- **Hos 12:6** — "Therefore turn thou to thy God: keep mercy and judgment, and wait on thy God continually."
- **Zec 7:9** — "Execute true judgment, and shew mercy and compassions every man to his brother."
