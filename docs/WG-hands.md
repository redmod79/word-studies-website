# WG: Hands (χείρ Word Family)

## Overview

The χείρ (cheír) word family in Greek groups words derived from or compounded with the root χείρ ("hand"). These words share the semantic domain of the hand as body part, instrument, agent, and symbol. Together they cover: the physical hand, guiding by hand, things made with hands, documents written by hand, and appointment by raising the hand.

## Relationships

### Shared Roots
All members compound χείρ with a second element:
- G5496/G5497: χείρ + ἀγωγός (leading/guiding) → "hand-leader"
- G5498: χείρ + γράφω (write) → "hand-written document"
- G5499: χείρ + ποιέω (make/do) → "hand-made"
- G5500: χείρ + τείνω (stretch) → "stretch out the hand" (for vote/appointment)

## Roles in the Concept

- **χείρ (G5495):** The foundational term — body part, divine agency, instrument of healing, arrest, labor, and apocalyptic symbolism
- **χειραγωγέω / χειραγωγός (G5496/G5497):** The hand as guide — physical assistance for the blind or helpless; both appear only in Acts in contexts of blindness
- **χειρόγραφον (G5498):** The hand as writer — the signed legal document; in Col 2:14, the debt-bond nailed to the cross
- **χειροποίητος (G5499):** The hand as maker — what humans build with hands (typically contrasted with what God inhabits or creates); Stephen's speech and Hebrews use this to argue against temple-worship
- **χειροτονέω (G5500):** The hand as voter — raised in election/appointment; the NT church appointed elders this way

## Key Distinctions

- **Made with hands vs. not made with hands:** The NT uses χειροποίητος (G5499) repeatedly to contrast human-built structures (temples, idols) with the divine and eternal. The contrast "not made with hands" (ἀχειροποίητος) occurs 3x (Mar 14:58; 2Co 5:1; Col 2:11)
- **χειραγωγέω vs. χείρ:** Both involve the hand, but χειραγωγέω is specifically the act of guiding — always in the context of blindness (Saul's blindness; Elymas's blindness)
- **χειροτονέω:** Though etymologically "to stretch out the hand," by NT usage it means simply "to appoint/elect" — not requiring literal hand gesture
