## Lexicon Definition

From a primary τέλλω (*téllō*, to set out for a definite point or goal): the conclusion of an act or state (termination, result), or the purpose toward which it tends (aim, goal); also used for a tax or toll (as settled payment). The root idea is **reaching a definitive endpoint** — whether temporal, purposive, or transactional.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Temporal Terminus — Endurance to the End
Eschatological context: disciples must persist through tribulation until the definitive endpoint.
- **Matt 10:22** — "he that endureth to the end shall be saved"
- **Matt 24:13** — "he that shall endure unto the end, the same shall be saved"
- **Mark 13:13** — parallel: "he that shall endure unto the end, the same shall be saved"
- **Rev 2:26** — "he that overcometh, and keepeth my works unto the end"

### 2. Eschatological Consummation — The End of the Age
The arrival of the final event-horizon that concludes history or an era.
- **Matt 24:6, 14** — "the end is not yet… then shall the end come"
- **1 Cor 15:24** — "Then cometh the end, when he shall have delivered up the kingdom to God"
- **1 Pet 4:7** — "the end of all things is at hand"
- **Rev 21:6; 22:13** — "I am Alpha and Omega, the beginning and the end"

### 3. Outcome / Result of a Process
τέλος as the final state that a trajectory produces — death or life, destruction or glory.
- **Rom 6:21** — "for the end of those things is death"
- **Rom 6:22** — "the end everlasting life"
- **2 Cor 11:15** — "whose end shall be according to their works"
- **Phil 3:19** — "whose end is destruction"

### 4. Goal / Purpose / Culmination of Something
τέλος as the telos-ic aim or intended fulfillment — what something is ultimately *for*.
- **Rom 10:4** — "Christ is the end of the law for righteousness"
- **1 Tim 1:5** — "the end of the commandment is charity out of a pure heart"
- **1 Pet 1:9** — "Receiving the end of your faith, even the salvation of your souls"
- **Jas 5:11** — "ye have seen the end of the Lord; that the Lord is very pitiful"

### 5. Tax / Tribute (Administrative)
Plural τέλη = toll or tribute paid to authorities; this usage is grounded in the root sense of a "settled, completed payment."
- **Matt 17:25** — "of whom do the kings of the earth take custom (τέλη) or tribute?"
- **Rom 13:7** — "Render… custom to whom custom" (τέλος to the tax authority)

### 6. Adverbial εἰς τέλος — Completely / Persistently
When used in the phrase εἰς τέλος, τέλος functions adverbially: "utterly, completely, to the very end."
- **John 13:1** — "he loved them unto the end" (eis telos = fully, completely, to the uttermost)
- **Luke 18:5** — "her continual coming" (εἰς τέλος = persistently without letup)
- **Heb 3:6, 3:14** — "hold fast... stedfast unto the end"

## Hebrew Source (LXX)

G5056 maps to Hebrew words primarily via the Psalm superscriptions — the LXX rendered the Psalms header לַמְנַצֵּחַ (*laménatsséach*, "for/to the director/chief musician") as *εἰς τέλος* ("to the end/completion"), interpreting the musical term as an eschatological dedication.

| מִזְמוֹר (mizmor) | H4210 | 35 | 6.74 | Psalm type title — co-occurs with נָצַח in headers |
| נֶצַח (netsach) | H5331 | 19 | 6.41 | Noun: perpetuity, victory, forever — LXX reads as "to the end/completion" |
| מַשְׂכִּיל (maskiyl) | H4905 | 9 | 6.92 | Instructive psalm type — co-occurs with נָצַח |

**Significance:** The LXX choice of εἰς τέλος for the Psalm dedication header apparently reflects a reading of נָצַח as "to the victory/completion" (from netsach = enduring triumph) rather than "to the director." Early Christian interpreters (Justin Martyr, Tertullian) exploited this LXX rendering to read the Psalms as pointing forward to Christ's final triumph — a distinctly eschatological hermeneutic.
