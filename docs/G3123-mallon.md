## Semantic Categories

### 1. A Fortiori / "Much More" Reasoning (qal wahomer)
The signature construction: if the lesser case is true, *how much more* the greater. Typically paired with πολύ (polys) or πόσος (posos) to form "much more" / "how much more."
- **Rom 5:9** — "Much more then, being now justified by his blood, we shall be saved from wrath through him."
- **Rom 5:10** — "For if, when we were enemies, we were reconciled to God by the death of his Son, much more, being reconciled, we shall be saved by his life."
- **Rom 5:15** — "much more the grace of God, and the gift by grace, which is by one man, Jesus Christ, hath abounded unto many"
- **Rom 5:17** — "much more they which receive abundance of grace and of the gift of righteousness shall reign in life by one, Jesus Christ"
- **Mat 7:11** — "how much more shall your Father which is in heaven give good things to them that ask him?"
- **Heb 9:14** — "How much more shall the blood of Christ... purge your conscience from dead works"
- **2 Cor 3:9** — "much more doth the ministration of righteousness exceed in glory"
- **2 Cor 3:11** — "much more that which remaineth is glorious"
- **Heb 12:25** — "much more shall not we escape, if we turn away from him that speaketh from heaven"

### 2. Preference / "Rather Than" Contrast
Marks a deliberate choice between two alternatives. Translated "rather" — signals what ought to be chosen, valued, or done instead.
- **Mat 10:28** — "but rather fear him which is able to destroy both soul and body in hell"
- **Acts 5:29** — "We ought to obey God rather than men"
- **John 3:19** — "men loved darkness rather than light, because their deeds were evil"
- **Eph 5:11** — "have no fellowship with the unfruitful works of darkness, but rather reprove them"
- **Heb 11:25** — "Choosing rather to suffer affliction with the people of God, than to enjoy the pleasures of sin for a season"

### 3. Exhortation to Increase / "More and More"
Marks progressive intensification — the believer should keep doing what is already good, but more so.
- **1 Thess 4:1** — "as ye have received of us how ye ought to walk and to please God, so ye would abound more and more"
- **1 Thess 4:10** — "we beseech you, brethren, that ye increase more and more"
- **Phil 1:9** — "this I pray, that your love may abound yet more and more in knowledge"
- **Phil 2:12** — "not as in my presence only, but now much more in my absence, work out your own salvation"

### 4. Intensifying Comparison / "The More"
Describes escalation — the more something happens, the more something else intensifies in response.
- **Mark 7:36** — "the more he charged them, so much the more a great deal they published it"
- **Mark 10:48** — "he cried the more a great deal, Thou Son of David, have mercy on me"
- **Luke 5:15** — "so much the more went there a fame abroad of him"
- **John 5:18** — "the Jews sought the more to kill him"
- **Acts 9:22** — "Saul increased the more in strength"

### 5. Corrective Redirection ("but rather")
Introduces what should be done *instead* — a mild correction redirecting action from one course to another.
- **Mat 10:6** — "go rather to the lost sheep of the house of Israel"
- **1 Cor 14:1** — "desire spiritual gifts, but rather that ye may prophesy"
- **1 Cor 14:5** — "but rather that ye prophesied"
- **Eph 4:28** — "Let him that stole steal no more: but rather let him labour"
- **Rom 14:13** — "judge this rather, that no man put a stumblingblock or an occasion to fall"

### 6. Self-Correction / Rhetorical Refinement
The speaker corrects or sharpens their own statement — "or rather" / "yea rather."
- **Gal 4:9** — "after that ye have known God, or rather are known of God"
- **Rom 8:34** — "It is Christ that died, yea rather, that is risen again"
