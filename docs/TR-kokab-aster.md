**Semantic continuity:** The LXX renders kôwkâb almost exclusively with ἄστρον or ἀστήρ — the two Greek words are near-synonyms (astron slightly favoring collective/constellation, astēr favoring the individual luminary). All four OT symbolic registers (literal creation, covenant multitude, royal/messianic, angelic, eschatological saints) pass through the Greek bridge intact, giving NT writers the full symbolic vocabulary of kôwkâb.

**Semantic shift in translation:** Minimal. The one notable loss: the Hebrew collocation with צָבָא ("host of heaven") becomes somewhat weaker in Greek since Greek lacks a single-word equivalent that preserves the military-and-astral overtones of tsaba.

## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same across testaments:**
- The fivefold symbolic register (literal luminary, covenant multitude, royal Messiah, angelic being, righteous saint) maps 1:1.
- The Day-of-the-LORD darkening oracle (stars not giving light / falling) is preserved verbatim in Olivet and Revelation.
- "Star of Jacob" (Num 24:17) functions as a Messianic nerve across both testaments (Matt 2, Rev 22:16).
- The astral-angelic identification (Job 38:7 "morning stars = sons of God"; Dan 8:10 "host and stars"; Isa 14:13 "stars of God") carries directly into Rev 1:20 (stars = angels) and Rev 12:4 (stars cast down).

**What shifts:**
- NT writers (esp. John in Revelation) intensify the *falling / casting-down* register. The OT has darkening (Joel, Isa) and one casting-down (Dan 8:10); Revelation repeats the motif four distinct times (Rev 6:13; 8:10; 9:1; 12:4), making "falling stars" a structural feature of the Apocalypse.
- The Messianic Star shifts from anticipation (Num 24:17) to identification (Matt 2; Rev 22:16).
- The angelic identification becomes explicit in Rev 1:20 ("the seven stars are the angels") in a way the OT only implies.
- 2 Pet 1:19 and Rev 22:16 introduce a *morning-star* Christological register drawing on shachar + kôwkâb, producing the title "bright and morning star."

**Dan 8:10 as pivot:** The OT verse that most directly feeds Revelation's astral-warfare imagery. Its pairing of "host" + "stars" + "cast down" + "stamped" reappears, expanded, in Rev 12:4 (dragon's tail casts stars; pursues the woman), binding the two apocalyptic canons into one symbolic circuit.
