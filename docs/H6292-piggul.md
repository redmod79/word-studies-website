# H6292 — פִּגּוּל (piggûl)

## Core Data
- **Strong's:** H6292
- **Original:** פִּגּוּל (also spelled פִּגֻּל)
- **Transliteration:** piggûl
- **Pronunciation:** pig-gool
- **Part of Speech:** Masculine noun
- **Root:** From an unused root meaning "to stink" (TWOT 1730a); properly "fetid"
- **Occurrences:** 4 verses across 3 books (Leviticus, Isaiah, Ezekiel)
- **First Occurrence:** Lev 7:18 — peace-offering flesh eaten on the third day declared piggûl
- **Hapax Legomenon:** No (4 occurrences), but extremely rare

## Lexicon Definition

From an unused root meaning to stink; properly, fetid, i.e. (figuratively) unclean (ceremonially): foul thing, refuse. A technical sacrificial term denoting flesh that has become ritually putrid/rejected because it was kept beyond the time permitted by law.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Sacrificial Flesh Kept Beyond the Lawful Time (Leviticus)
The core, definitional usage. The peace offering (שֶׁלֶם, shelem) had a prescribed eating window: thanksgiving offerings must be eaten the same day (Lev 7:15); vow/freewill offerings could extend to the second day (Lev 7:16). Any flesh remaining to the third day became piggûl — physically stale and ritually rejected.

- **Lev 7:18** — "And if any of the flesh of the sacrifice of his peace offerings be eaten at all on the third day, it shall not be accepted, neither shall it be imputed unto him that offereth it: it shall be an abomination [piggûl], and the soul that eateth of it shall bear his iniquity."
  - Three consequences: (1) the sacrifice is "not accepted" (לֹא יֵרָצֶה, Niphal of רצה — God does not find pleasure in it); (2) it is "not imputed" (לֹא יֵחָשֵׁב — not credited to the offerer's account); (3) whoever eats it "bears iniquity" (עֲוֹנָהּ תִּשָּׂא).
  - The infinitive absolute + imperfect construction (הֵאָכֹל יֵאָכֵל, "if it be eaten at all") emphasizes certainty and completion.

- **Lev 19:7** — "And if it be eaten at all on the third day, it is abominable [piggûl]; it shall not be accepted."
  - Same infinitive absolute construction (הֵאָכֹל יֵאָכֵל).
  - Briefer formulation: piggûl + "not accepted" (לֹא יֵרָצֶה).
  - Lev 19:8 adds that the eater "hath profaned the hallowed thing of the LORD" (אֶת־קֹדֶשׁ יְהוָה חִלֵּל) and "shall be cut off" (כָּרַת) — a harsher penalty than Lev 7:18's "bear iniquity."

### 2. Idolatrous Food (Isaiah — Prophetic Extension)
Isaiah extends the sacrificial term metaphorically to describe apostate Israel's pagan food practices.

- **Isa 65:4** — "Which remain among the graves, and lodge in the monuments, which eat swine's flesh, and broth of abominable things [מְרַק פִּגֻּלִים] is in their vessels."
  - The plural פִּגֻּלִים (piggulim) broadens from a single rejected sacrifice to a category of putrid/forbidden food.
  - Paired with swine's flesh (בְּשַׂר הַחֲזִיר) — doubly forbidden: both the animal (Lev 11:7) and the preparation method (piggûl broth) are unclean.
  - Context: necromantic practices (graves, monuments), pagan sacrifices (gardens, brick altars, Isa 65:3), and dietary violations — a catalogue of covenant rebellion.
  - The word מָרָק (mârâq, H4839, "broth/soup") appears only 3 times in the OT (Judg 6:19–20; Isa 65:4), making "broth of piggulim" an exceptionally rare phrase.

### 3. Priestly Conscience and Dietary Purity (Ezekiel)
Ezekiel invokes piggûl in his personal protest against God's command to eat bread baked over human dung.

- **Ezek 4:14** — "Then said I, Ah Lord GOD! behold, my soul hath not been polluted: for from my youth up even till now have I not eaten of that which dieth of itself, or is torn in pieces; neither came there abominable [piggûl] flesh into my mouth."
  - Ezekiel groups three categories of forbidden flesh: (1) נְבֵלָה (nᵉbêlâh, H5038) — an animal that died naturally (Lev 17:15; 22:8); (2) טְרֵפָה (ṭᵉrêphâh, H2966) — an animal torn by beasts (Exod 22:31; Lev 7:24); (3) בְּשַׂר פִּגּוּל — piggûl flesh (stale/putrid sacrificial meat).
  - The triad moves from general dietary law (nebelah, terephah) to specifically sacrificial law (piggûl), showing Ezekiel's comprehensive priestly observance.
  - His soul is "not polluted" (לֹא מְטֻמָּאָה — Pual participle of טמא, H2930) — piggûl is here explicitly linked to the tame'/unclean system.

## Key Syntactic Patterns

1. **Predicate declaration:** "piggûl it-shall-be" (פִּגּוּל יִהְיֶה, Lev 7:18) / "piggûl it [is]" (פִּגּוּל הוּא, Lev 19:7) — the noun functions as a predicate declaring the status of the meat.
2. **Paired with non-acceptance:** In both Levitical occurrences, piggûl is immediately paired with לֹא יֵרָצֶה ("not accepted," Niphal of H7521 רצה). This creates a technical formula: piggûl = not-ratsah.
3. **Construct with בָּשָׂר (flesh):** Ezek 4:14 uses בְּשַׂר פִּגּוּל ("piggûl flesh"), making the noun attributive — modifying the flesh as putrid.
4. **Construct with מָרָק (broth):** Isa 65:4 uses מְרַק פִּגֻּלִים ("broth of piggulim"), extending from solid flesh to liquid preparation.

## Semantic Domains

### Domain Placement
Piggûl occupies a narrow intersection of three domains:
1. **Sacrificial terminology** — exclusively concerns offering flesh and its consumption
2. **Ritual impurity** — produces the state of ṭumʼâh (uncleanness) in the eater
3. **Temporal violation** — the uncleanness arises not from the type of animal or manner of death, but from *elapsed time*

This makes piggûl unique among Hebrew "abomination" words: it is the only one whose trigger is temporal rather than inherent. The flesh was once acceptable; it becomes piggûl by the passage of time past the lawful deadline.

### Relationship to the Abomination Word Group
Within the broader WG-abomination cluster (see [WG-abomination.md](WG-abomination.md)):
- **tô'êbâh** = moral/covenantal abomination (broadest)
- **shiqquwts** = idolatrous cult object
- **sheqets** = inherently unclean creature
- **piggûl** = time-expired sacrificial flesh (narrowest, most technical)

Piggûl is the most specialized member of the group — 4 occurrences versus tô'êbâh's 112.

## Theological Significance

### The Time Boundary as Sacred
Piggûl establishes a principle that God's acceptance of a sacrifice has a temporal dimension. The offering is not merely right in kind (correct animal, correct procedure) but right in time. Once the permitted eating window closes, the very same flesh that was holy becomes fetid — not because it has chemically changed (though decomposition may be implied), but because the divine window of acceptance has closed.

### Connection to Broader Sacrificial Theology
The piggûl law reinforces:
- **Prompt obedience:** The worshipper must act within the appointed time.
- **No hoarding of grace:** The peace offering was a communal meal of fellowship with God; attempting to store it beyond its time perverts the gift into a pollutant.
- **Imputation logic:** Lev 7:18 explicitly says the piggûl sacrifice "shall not be imputed" (לֹא יֵחָשֵׁב) to the offerer — the same verb (חשׁב) used for crediting righteousness (Gen 15:6). A piggûl offering earns no credit.

### Ezekiel's Triad of Forbidden Flesh (Ezek 4:14)
Ezekiel's threefold protest — nebelah + terephah + piggûl — represents the complete spectrum of flesh a faithful Israelite would refuse:
1. **Nebelah** (died naturally) — defective in manner of death
2. **Terephah** (torn by beasts) — defective in manner of death
3. **Piggûl** (time-expired sacrifice) — defective in manner of handling

The first two concern the animal's death; the third concerns human negligence with sacred things. Together they constitute the comprehensive priestly standard.

## Comparative Note: Related Concepts Without H6292

Several passages describe rejected or polluted sacrifices without using the word piggûl:
- **Mal 1:7–8** — "polluted bread" (לֶחֶם מְגֹאָל) and blind/lame offerings: uses H1351 גָּאַל ("stain/pollute"), not piggûl
- **Hos 9:4** — "their sacrifices shall be unto them as the bread of mourners; all that eat thereof shall be polluted": conceptually parallel but uses no piggûl term
- **Isa 1:11–14** — God rejects sacrifices offered with unclean hands: different vocabulary entirely

These passages share the concept of divine rejection of improper sacrifice but use different vocabulary, confirming piggûl's narrow technical scope.
