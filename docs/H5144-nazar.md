## Lexicon Definition

> A primitive root; to hold aloof, i.e. (intransitively) abstain (from food and drink, from impurity, and even from divine worship — i.e. apostatize); specifically, to set apart (to sacred purposes), i.e. devote: — consecrate, separate(-ing, self).

The verb's semantic axis is *separation from something in order to belong to something else*. The same form can describe positive devotion (separation unto YHWH) or negative defection (separation from YHWH toward idols), depending on the object.

The verb is almost entirely a **Pentateuchal cultic term** (7 of 10), heavily concentrated in the Nazirite law (Num 6). Its prophetic reuses (Hos, Ezek, Zech) invert the cultic sense: separation *from* YHWH, or separation as religious performance.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Priestly Separation from Uncleanness (cultic boundary)
The earliest uses cast nâzar as the discipline of keeping holy things holy by standing apart from defilement.
- **Lev 15:31** — "Thus shall ye separate the children of Israel from their uncleanness; that they die not..."
- **Lev 22:2** — "Speak unto Aaron and to his sons, that they separate themselves from the holy things of the children of Israel..."

### 2. Nazirite Vow — Separation Unto YHWH (devotional)
The core legislative use. The person vows a temporary status in which abstentions (wine, corpses, razor) symbolize total belonging to YHWH.
- **Num 6:2** — "When either man or woman shall separate themselves to vow a vow of a Nazarite, to separate themselves unto the LORD"
- **Num 6:5** — "...until the days be fulfilled, in the which he separateth himself unto the LORD, he shall be holy"
- **Num 6:6** — "All the days that he separateth himself unto the LORD he shall come at no dead body."
- **Num 6:12** — "And he shall consecrate unto the LORD the days of his separation..."

### 3. Abstention from Specific Substances
Within the vow, nâzar governs concrete disciplines — especially products of the vine.
- **Num 6:3** — "He shall separate himself from wine and strong drink..."

### 4. Apostasy — Separation Away from YHWH (pejorative)
The same verb describes Israel's defection, using the cultic vocabulary ironically: they "consecrated" themselves to another object.
- **Hos 9:10** — "they went to Baalpeor, and separated themselves unto that shame"
- **Ezek 14:7** — "every one of the house of Israel... which separateth himself from me, and setteth up his idols in his heart"

### 5. Self-imposed Ritual Observance (post-exilic)
A late, non-Nazirite use: to set oneself apart for religious practice (here, mourning-fasting).
- **Zech 7:3** — "Should I weep in the fifth month, separating myself, as I have done these so many years?"
