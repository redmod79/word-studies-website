## Lexicon Definition

Something owed; i.e. (figuratively) a due; morally, a fault. From the alternate of G3784 (opheilō). The -μα noun suffix denotes the thing resulting from the verbal action: what has accrued as owed.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Moral/Spiritual Debt (Sin as Obligation Owed to God)
- **Mat 6:12** — "And forgive us our debts (ὀφειλήματα), as we forgive our debtors (ὀφειλέταις)." The Lord's Prayer uses the debt metaphor for sins — moral obligations owed to God that remain unpaid. The plural "debts" treats individual transgressions as accumulated obligations.

### 2. Financial/Legal Obligation (What Is Owed for Work)
- **Rom 4:4** — "Now to him that worketh is the reward not reckoned of grace, but of debt (ὀφείλημα)." Paul contrasts grace with debt in the context of justification. When someone works, the wage is owed as a legal obligation — it is a debt, not a gift. The word here retains its literal commercial sense to set up the contrast with grace-based righteousness.
