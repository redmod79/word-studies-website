## Semantic Categories

### 1. Violent, Rushing Wind (Physical Phenomenon)
- **Acts 2:2** — "And suddenly there came a sound from heaven as of a rushing mighty wind (πνοή), and it filled all the house where they were sitting."

Here pnoē describes a powerful atmospheric blast — not a gentle breeze. The adjective βίαιος (biaios, "violent") intensifies it. The wind-sound accompanies the Holy Spirit's arrival but is itself described as a physical phenomenon (ἦχος, "sound"; ὥσπερ, "as of"), distinguished from the Spirit (πνεῦμα) that fills the disciples in v. 4.

### 2. Life-Breath Given by God (Creational Gift)
- **Acts 17:25** — "Neither is worshipped with men's hands, as though he needed any thing, seeing he giveth to all life, and breath (πνοή), and all things."

Paul echoes Gen 2:7 (LXX πνοὴν ζωῆς) and Isa 42:5 (God "giveth breath unto the people"). Here pnoē is the creaturely breath God grants — biological respiration as evidence of dependence on the Creator. It stands alongside ζωή (zōē, "life") in a hendiadys: "life, namely breath."

The dominant Hebrew source is nshamah (H5397), not ruach (H7307). This is significant: in the OT, nshamah is the physical life-breath God breathes into creatures (Gen 2:7), while ruach carries the broader spirit/wind/disposition range. The LXX translators consistently reserved pnoē for nshamah's physical-breath sense, using pneuma for ruach's wider meanings.
