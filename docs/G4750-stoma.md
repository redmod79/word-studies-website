## Lexicon Definition

Probably strengthened from a presumed derivative of the base of G5114; the mouth (as if a gash in the face); by implication language (and its relations); by analogy an opening (in the earth); specifically the front or edge (of a weapon).

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Prophetic/Authoritative Speech — "By the Mouth of"
The formula "by/through the mouth of" (διὰ στόματος) marks words as carrying divine authority — spoken by God through a human agent.
- **Act 1:16** — "this scripture must needs have been fulfilled, which the Holy Ghost by the mouth of David spake"
- **Act 3:18** — "which God before had shewed by the mouth of all his prophets"
- **Act 3:21** — "which God hath spoken by the mouth of all his holy prophets since the world began"
- **Act 4:25** — "Who by the mouth of thy servant David hast said"

### 2. Verbal Self-Revelation — What Comes Out of the Mouth
The mouth as the conduit through which the inner person is disclosed — what proceeds from the mouth reveals the heart.
- **Mat 12:34** — "out of the abundance of the heart the mouth speaketh"
- **Mat 15:11** — "that which cometh out of the mouth, this defileth a man"
- **Mat 15:18** — "those things which proceed out of the mouth come forth from the heart"
- **Luk 6:45** — "of the abundance of the heart his mouth speaketh"

### 3. Confession and Praise — The Mouth as Instrument of Worship
The mouth actively employed in confessing faith, praising God, or glorifying him.
- **Rom 10:9** — "if thou shalt confess with thy mouth the Lord Jesus"
- **Rom 10:10** — "with the mouth confession is made unto salvation"
- **Rom 15:6** — "with one mind and one mouth glorify God"
- **Mat 21:16** — "Out of the mouth of babes and sucklings thou hast perfected praise"

### 4. Divine Judgment-Weapon — Sword/Fire from the Mouth
Heavily concentrated in Revelation, where the mouth of Christ or divine agents emits destructive instruments of judgment.
- **Rev 1:16** — "out of his mouth went a sharp twoedged sword"
- **Rev 2:16** — "I will fight against them with the sword of my mouth"
- **Rev 19:15** — "out of his mouth goeth a sharp sword, that with it he should smite the nations"
- **2Th 2:8** — "whom the Lord shall consume with the spirit of his mouth"

### 5. Devouring/Emitting — Monstrous or Supernatural Mouths
The mouth as source of destructive forces — fire, flood, unclean spirits — used of beasts, dragons, and apocalyptic figures.
- **Rev 9:17-18** — "out of their mouths issued fire and smoke and brimstone"
- **Rev 11:5** — "fire proceedeth out of their mouth, and devoureth their enemies"
- **Rev 12:15** — "the serpent cast out of his mouth water as a flood"
- **Rev 16:13** — "three unclean spirits like frogs come out of the mouth of the dragon"

### 6. Blasphemy and Arrogant Speech — The Mouth Given to Speak
The mouth granted or used for blasphemous, boastful, or wicked speech.
- **Rev 13:5** — "there was given unto him a mouth speaking great things and blasphemies"
- **Rev 13:6** — "he opened his mouth in blasphemy against God"
- **Jud 1:16** — "their mouth speaketh great swelling words"
- **Rom 3:14** — "Whose mouth is full of cursing and bitterness"

### 7. Witness and Legal Testimony — The Mouth Establishes Facts
Mouth as the organ through which legal testimony is given, establishing truth by multiple witnesses.
- **Mat 18:16** — "in the mouth of two or three witnesses every word may be established"
- **2Co 13:1** — "In the mouth of two or three witnesses shall every word be established"
- **Luk 22:71** — "we ourselves have heard of his own mouth"
- **Luk 19:22** — "Out of thine own mouth will I judge thee"
