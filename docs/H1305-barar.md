## Semantic Categories

### 1. Moral Purity of Character (Reciprocal Principle)
The Niphal/Hithpael pair: God responds to the pure in kind, revealing His own purity. The contrast with "froward" (H6617, H6141) underscores purity as moral integrity, not ritual status.
- **2 Sam 22:27 / Ps 18:26** — "With the pure (Niphal ptcp) thou wilt shew thyself pure (Hithpael); and with the froward thou wilt shew thyself unsavoury/froward" — both forms in same verse
- **Zeph 3:9** — "For then will I turn to the people a pure (Qal ptcp) language, that they may all call upon the name of the LORD, to serve him with one consent"

### 2. Eschatological Purging and Refining
God actively purging rebels from His people, and the testing/refining of the faithful in the last days — a refiner's fire metaphor.
- **Ezek 20:38** — "And I will purge out (Qal) from among you the rebels, and them that transgress against me"
- **Dan 11:35** — "And [some] of them of understanding shall fall, to try them, and to purge (Piel), and to make [them] white, [even] to the time of the end"
- **Dan 12:10** — "Many shall be purified (Hithpael), and made white, and tried; but the wicked shall do wickedly"
- **Isa 52:11** — "Be ye clean (Niphal), that bear the vessels of the LORD"

### 3. Selection / Choosing the Best
As Qal participle "bârûr" — chosen, selected, the choice ones. Used of elite personnel (warriors, temple servants, Levitical singers) and quality goods.
- **1Chr 7:40** — "All these [were] the children of Asher... choice (Qal ptcp) and mighty men of valour"
- **1Chr 9:22** — "All these [which were] chosen (Qal ptcp) to be porters in the gates [were] two hundred and twelve"
- **1Chr 16:41** — "And with them Heman and Jeduthun, and the rest that were chosen (Qal ptcp)... to give thanks"
- **Neh 5:18** — "one ox and six choice (Qal ptcp) sheep; also fowls were prepared"

### 4. Polishing / Making Bright (Physical)
Hiphil and Qal applied to physical objects — polishing weapons or sharpening arrows, producing a gleaming, refined surface.
- **Isa 49:2** — "he hath made my mouth like a sharp sword... and made me a polished (Qal ptcp) shaft"
- **Jer 51:11** — "Make bright (Hiphil) the arrows; gather the shields: the LORD hath raised up the spirit of the kings of the Medes"

### 5. Examining / Clarifying / Testing
Bringing hidden things to light through examination; making speech or knowledge clear and transparent.
- **Job 33:3** — "My words [shall be of] the uprightness of my heart: and my lips shall utter knowledge clearly (Qal)"
- **Eccl 3:18** — "I said in mine heart... that God might manifest (Qal) them, and that they might see that they themselves are beasts"
- **Jer 4:11** — "A dry wind... not to fan, nor to cleanse (Hiphil)" — winnowing/purification imagery
