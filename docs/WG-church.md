# WG: Church / Assembly

## Overview
The NT "church" concept involves a cluster of words describing the gathered covenant people — the body, its members, their relationships, and its leadership. The central word ἐκκλησία (ekklesia) draws on the OT qahal tradition and is distinguished from the Jewish synagoge.

## Roles in the Concept

- **The body:** ekklesia (G1577) — the assembled/called-out community as a whole
- **The members:** hagios (G40) — their identity as holy ones set apart to God
- **The relationships:** adelphos (G80) — their kinship bond as brothers/sisters
- **The foundation:** apostolos (G652) — the delegate/sent one who plants and constitutes churches
- **The counterpart:** synagoge (G4864) — the parallel Jewish assembly; same Hebrew root, different covenantal context

## Distinction: ekklesia vs. synagoge

The two words share a Hebrew antecedent (H6951 qahal) but NT usage distinguishes them sharply:
- *Ekklesia* is used for Christian assemblies in every NT instance except Acts 19 (secular civic context)
- *Synagoge* is used for Jewish assemblies, almost always with the building/institution sense
- James 2:2 is the sole exception: "your assembly (synagoge)" — possibly early Jewish-Christian usage
- The LXX choice to translate qahal as ekklesia (rather than synagoge) becomes the conceptual bridge for Acts 7:38 and Heb 2:12
