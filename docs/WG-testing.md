# WG: Testing / Examining

## Overview
The Hebrew concept of testing and examining involves four primary verbs that each approach the act of evaluation from a distinct angle: scrutinizing evaluation (bachan), metallurgical refining (tsaraph), experiential ordeal (nasah), and investigative probing (chaqar). These words frequently co-occur — Psalm 26:2 combines three of them in a single verse.

## Roles in the Concept

- **Evaluator:** bachan (בָּחַן) — the assaying judgment itself; determining quality, truth, or character
- **Refiner:** tsaraph (צָרַף) — the purification process; removing impurity through heat and pressure
- **Prover:** nasah (נָסָה) — the experiential ordeal; putting through a trial to reveal what is present
- **Investigator:** chaqar (חָקַר) — the search and penetration; uncovering hidden realities
- **Result:** bochan (בֹּחַן) — what has been tested; the proven/tried product (Isa 28:16)

## Key Passages Where Multiple Group Words Appear Together

- **Psa 26:2** — bachan + nasah + tsaraph: The fullest single-verse expression of the testing concept. David invites all three forms of divine examination.
- **Psa 139:23** — bachan + chaqar: Testing and searching as synonymous acts of divine omniscience.
- **Jer 17:10** — bachan + chaqar: YHWH's self-description as the one who both searches and tests.
- **Psa 17:3** — bachan + tsaraph: Testing and refining as the nighttime visitation of God.
