## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same:**
- The substance and act: fragrant compound burned on the golden altar, smoke ascending before God
- The liturgical context: incense belongs to the sanctuary and its ministers (priests → angels)
- The connection to prayer: Ps 141:2 already linked incense and prayer; Revelation fulfills this architecturally

**What shifts:**
- The officiant changes: OT human Aaronic priest → Rev's angel before the heavenly altar
- The location shifts from earthly temple to heavenly throne room — but the ritual furniture is identical (golden altar, censer)
- Meaning becomes explicitly allegorical: the OT substance *represents* what it always *meant* — the prayers of God's people ascending before him
