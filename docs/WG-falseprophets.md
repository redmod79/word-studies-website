# WG: False Prophets

## Overview
The false prophets concept in the NT clusters around the compound ψευδοπροφήτης and its cognates — a pseudo- prefix attached to the base prophet/Christ roles. Three distinct ideas converge: **the counterfeit identity** (pseudo-), **the legitimate role being imitated** (prophetes/christos), and **the deceptive mechanism** (planao). The Hebrew background lacks a single-word equivalent; the LXX coined the Greek compound to translate Jeremiah's extended warnings.

## Roles in the Concept

- **Counterfeit office:** pseudoprophetes (G5578) — the false prophet claiming prophetic authority
- **Parallel counterfeit:** pseudochristos (G5580) — the false messiah; always paired with G5578 in the Synoptic warnings
- **Legitimate archetype:** prophetes (G4396) — the role being imitated; gives G5578 its deceptive plausibility
- **Root qualifier:** pseudes (G5571) — the adjective of falseness that forms the compound prefix
- **Mechanism:** planao (G4105) — to wander/lead astray; the verb that describes what false prophets do
