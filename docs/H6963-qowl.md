## Semantic Categories

### 1. God's Authoritative Voice / Word — Obedience Formula
The dominant usage: "obey the voice of the LORD" (שׁמע בקול יהוה). The "voice" stands for God's expressed will and commands.
- **Exo 19:5** — "if ye will obey my voice indeed, and keep my covenant"
- **Deu 28:1** — "if thou shalt hearken diligently unto the voice of the LORD thy God"
- **1 Sam 15:22** — "Hath the LORD as great delight in burnt offerings... as in obeying the voice of the LORD?"
- **Jer 7:23** — "Obey my voice, and I will be your God"

### 2. Cry of Lament / Petition to God
Human voice directed upward in distress — prayer, weeping, supplication.
- **Psa 3:4** — "I cried unto the LORD with my voice, and he heard me out of his holy hill"
- **Psa 130:2** — "Lord, hear my voice: let thine ears be attentive to the voice of my supplications"
- **Psa 142:1** — "I cried unto the LORD with my voice; with my voice unto the LORD did I make my supplication"
- **Jonah 2:2** — "I cried by reason of mine affliction unto the LORD, and he heard me"

### 3. God's Theophanic Voice — Thunder, Cosmic Power
The "voice of the LORD" as audible, terrifying manifestation in nature.
- **Psa 29:3–9** — "The voice of the LORD is upon the waters... powerful... full of majesty... breaketh the cedars... divideth the flames of fire... shaketh the wilderness"
- **Exo 19:16, 19** — "the voice of the trumpet sounded long, and waxed louder and louder"
- **Job 37:4–5** — "After it a voice roareth: he thundereth with the voice of his excellency... God thundereth marvellously with his voice"
- **Psa 18:13** — "The LORD also thundered in the heavens, and the Highest gave his voice"

### 4. Sound of Instruments, War, and Events
Auditory phenomena: trumpets, battle noise, rushing water, natural sounds.
- **Josh 6:5, 20** — "when ye hear the sound of the trumpet" / "when the people heard the sound of the trumpet"
- **2 Sam 5:24** — "when thou hearest the sound of a going in the tops of the mulberry trees"
- **Ezek 1:24** — "I heard the noise of their wings, like the noise of great waters, as the voice of the Almighty"
- **Jer 4:19** — "thou hast heard, O my soul, the sound of the trumpet, the alarm of war"

### 5. Voice Raised in Weeping or Emotional Outcry
Lifting the voice in grief, mourning, or intense emotion (often with "lifted up his/their voice and wept").
- **Gen 29:11** — "And Jacob kissed Rachel, and lifted up his voice, and wept"
- **1 Sam 30:4** — "David and the people... lifted up their voice and wept, until they had no more power to weep"
- **2 Sam 19:4** — "the king cried with a loud voice, O my son Absalom"
- **Jer 31:15** — "A voice was heard in Ramah, lamentation, and bitter weeping; Rahel weeping for her children"

### 6. Voice of Joy, Praise, and Celebration
Singing, thanksgiving, festive worship.
- **Psa 42:4** — "I went with them to the house of God, with the voice of joy and praise"
- **Psa 47:1** — "shout unto God with the voice of triumph"
- **Jer 33:11** — "The voice of joy, and the voice of gladness, the voice of the bridegroom, and the voice of the bride"
- **Isa 52:8** — "Thy watchmen shall lift up the voice; with the voice together shall they sing"

### 7. Prophetic and Proclamation Voice
A voice crying out as herald, prophet, or public announcement.
- **Isa 40:3** — "The voice of him that crieth in the wilderness, Prepare ye the way of the LORD"
- **Isa 40:6** — "The voice said, Cry. And he said, What shall I cry?"
- **Isa 58:1** — "Cry aloud, spare not, lift up thy voice like a trumpet"
- **Prov 1:20** — "Wisdom crieth without; she uttereth her voice in the streets"
