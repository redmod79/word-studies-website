## Lexicon Definition

Of uncertain affinity; to abstain from wine (keep sober), i.e. (figuratively) to be discreet, vigilant, watchful, circumspect. The literal sense (abstaining from intoxication) yields the dominant NT figurative use: mental and moral sobriety, a clear-headed alertness especially fitting for those awaiting the end / the Lord's return.

## Hebrew Source

No LXX-mediated Hebrew source. *Nēphō* is not used in the LXX to translate a standard Hebrew word; it enters the biblical vocabulary essentially as an NT figurative-ethical term rooted in the Greek cultural value of not being drunk. (The LXX translators instead used *nēphō*'s cognate field sparingly, e.g., *nēphálios* does not render a single fixed Hebrew term.)

This makes *nēphō* a **Greek-native exhortation vocabulary** — unlike many NT ethical terms, it carries no strong Hebrew substrate.

## Grammatical Note

All NT occurrences are in the **imperative mood** (present active, 2nd person) or hortatory subjunctive:
- 1Th 5:6 — pres. act. subjunctive 1pl (hortatory): "let us be sober"
- 1Th 5:8 — pres. act. subjunctive 1pl (hortatory)
- 2Tim 4:5 — pres. act. imperative 2sg: "watch thou"
- 1Pe 1:13 — aorist act. participle used imperativally / pres. imp.
- 1Pe 4:7 — aorist act. imperative 2pl: "be sober"
- 1Pe 5:8 — aorist act. imperative 2pl: "Be sober"

The verb is always a **command to the community**, never a descriptive statement. No verse uses *nēphō* to describe what someone *is*; every verse calls readers to *become* sober.
