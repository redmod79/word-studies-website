## Lexicon Definition

A primitive root; to commiserate; by implication, to spare:—have compassion, (have) pity, spare.

The word denotes an emotional response (commiseration, pity) that issues in a concrete action of restraint — withholding destruction, sparing from harm, refusing to strike down what one has power to strike. Unlike abstract love-words, *châmal* is almost always measured against the foil of warranted destruction: it is what one does (or pointedly does NOT do) when judgment, wrath, or violence stand ready.

**Pattern:** The word is overwhelmingly concentrated in prophetic-judgment literature (Ezekiel, Jeremiah, Lamentations, the Twelve) and military-narrative settings (1–2 Samuel, 2 Chronicles). Absent from the Pentateuch apart from two instances (Exo 2:6, Deut 13:8), and absent from Psalms — a telling silence given how common compassion-vocabulary is in the Psalter, suggesting *châmal* is reserved for contexts where judgment is on the table.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Refraining from destruction in holy war / ban contexts
The archetypal usage: a commander or warrior withholding the sword against an enemy marked for destruction. Often coupled with חרם (*châram*, devote to destruction) or הרג (*hârag*, slay).
- **1 Sam 15:3** — "smite Amalek, and utterly destroy all that they have, and spare them not"
- **1 Sam 15:9** — "But Saul and the people spared Agag, and the best of the sheep"
- **1 Sam 15:15** — "the people spared the best of the sheep and of the oxen"
- **Jer 50:14** — "shoot at her, spare no arrow"
- **Jer 51:3** — "spare ye not her young men; destroy ye utterly all her host"

### 2. YHWH withholding or executing judgment on His people
God as subject, often in formulaic oracle language: "mine eye shall not spare, neither will I have pity." The negated form dominates; positive occurrences function as striking exceptions.
- **Ezek 7:4** — "mine eye shall not spare thee, neither will I have pity"
- **Ezek 7:9; 8:18; 9:5, 10; 5:11** — the same formulaic pair, clustered in Ezekiel's judgment oracles
- **Jer 13:14** — "I will not pity, nor spare, nor have mercy"
- **Jer 21:7** — "he shall not spare them, neither have pity, nor have mercy"
- **Lam 2:2** — "The Lord hath swallowed up all the habitations of Jacob, and hath not pitied"
- **Lam 2:17; 2:21; 3:43** — recurring refrain of unpitying judgment in the fall of Jerusalem

### 3. YHWH sparing / having compassion (positive usage)
The remnant-reversal: God's pity extended despite deserved judgment. Usually linked to His name, His people, or His covenantal self-interest.
- **2 Chr 36:15** — "the LORD God of their fathers... sent to them... because he had compassion on his people"
- **Ezek 36:21** — "But I had pity for mine holy name"
- **Joel 2:18** — "Then will the LORD be jealous for his land, and pity his people"
- **Mal 3:17** — "I will spare them, as a man spareth his own son that serveth him"

### 4. Sparing in interpersonal / narrative contexts
Human agents showing pity, often weighed against self-interest or duty.
- **Exo 2:6** — Pharaoh's daughter has compassion on the infant Moses
- **2 Sam 12:4** — the rich man "spared to take of his own flock" (Nathan's parable)
- **2 Sam 21:7** — "the king spared Mephibosheth"
- **1 Sam 23:21** — Saul to the Ziphites: "ye have compassion on me"

### 5. Prohibited pity — compassion that must be refused
Legal and wisdom contexts where pity is explicitly forbidden as a betrayal of justice or covenant loyalty.
- **Deut 13:8** — "neither shall thine eye pity him, neither shalt thou spare" (the enticer to idolatry)
- **Prov 6:34** — "therefore he will not spare in the day of vengeance" (the wronged husband)
- **Job 6:10; 16:13; 20:13; 27:22** — Job's adversary (or God-as-adversary in Job's complaint) who "doth not spare"

### 6. Cognate noun — "compassion" / "object of compassion"
Three morphologically related tokens that the parser flags as nouns (*chemlâh / chumlâh / machmal*), not verbs:
- **Gen 19:16** — "the LORD being merciful (חֶמְלַת, chemlath) unto him"
- **Isa 63:9** — "in his love and in his pity (חֶמְלָתוֹ) he redeemed them"
- **Ezek 24:21** — "the desire (מַחְמַל) of your eyes" (what one spares / holds dear)
