# Present Active Indicative (Greek)

## Definition
The present active indicative is the most common Greek verb form, expressing action (active voice) as a real assertion (indicative mood) with imperfective aspect (present tense). Its core force is **internal, ongoing, or unfolding action** — the speaker portrays the action as in progress, repeated, or characteristic, rather than as a completed whole. The "present" label is primarily aspectual, not temporal; context determines whether the action is currently occurring, habitually practiced, or timelessly true.

1. **Progressive/Continuous** — action currently in progress at the time of speaking
2. **Customary/Habitual** — action regularly or characteristically performed
3. **Gnomic** — universally or timelessly true statement
4. **Historical/Narrative** — past event narrated with present tense for vividness
5. **Stative** — ongoing state of being (especially with εἰμί)

## Form Recognition
- **Morph code pattern:** `V-PAI-{person}{number}` — Verb, Present, Active, Indicative
- **Person/number endings:** -ω (1S), -εις (2S), -ει (3S), -ομεν (1P), -ετε (2P), -ουσιν (3P)
- **Contract verbs** (-εω, -αω, -οω) show contracted vowel before ending
- **-μι verbs** (εἰμί, δίδωμι, τίθημι, ἵστημι) use athematic endings
- **Parser search:** `tense=present voice=active mood=indicative`
- **No augment** (distinguishes from imperfect, which adds ε-augment to the same stem)

## Functions with Examples

### 1. Progressive / Continuous
Action portrayed as currently in progress at the time of utterance. The speaker views the action from the inside, as ongoing and unfolding.

| Reference | Greek | Morph Code | KJV Translation |
|-----------|-------|------------|-----------------|
| 1 John 1:7 | καθαρίζει (καθαρίζω G2511) | V-PAI-3S | "cleanseth" |
| 1 John 1:7 | ἔχομεν (ἔχω G2192) | V-PAI-1P | "we have" |
| John 15:5 | φέρει (φέρω G5342) | V-PAI-3S | "bringeth forth" |
| Philippians 4:13 | ἰσχύω (ἰσχύω G2480) | V-PAI-1S | "I can do" |

1 John 1:7: "the blood of Jesus Christ his Son **cleanseth** us from all sin" — καθαρίζει is V-PAI-3S, portraying the cleansing as a continuous, ongoing process rather than a one-time completed event. Contrast with the aorist subjunctive καθαρίσῃ in 1 John 1:9 (V-AAS-3S), which views cleansing as a bounded, summary act.

### 2. Customary / Habitual
Action that is regularly or characteristically performed. Not a single event but a pattern of behavior.

| Reference | Greek | Morph Code | KJV Translation |
|-----------|-------|------------|-----------------|
| Mark 2:18 | νηστεύουσιν (νηστεύω G3522) | V-PAI-3P | "fast" |
| Matthew 23:25 | καθαρίζετε (καθαρίζω G2511) | V-PAI-2P | "ye make clean" |
| 1 Corinthians 15:31 | ἀποθνῄσκω (ἀποθνῄσκω G599) | V-PAI-1S | "I die" |

Mark 2:18: "Why do the disciples of John ... **fast**, but thy disciples **fast** not?" — νηστεύουσιν (V-PAI-3P) describes a customary, habitual practice. 1 Cor 15:31 with καθ' ἡμέραν ("daily") explicitly marks the repeated nature: "I **die** daily."

### 3. Gnomic
States a general truth, universal principle, or timeless reality. Not tied to a particular moment.

| Reference | Greek | Morph Code | KJV Translation |
|-----------|-------|------------|-----------------|
| Romans 8:28 | συνεργεῖ (συνεργέω G4903) | V-PAI-3S | "work together" |
| Matthew 5:15 | λάμπει (λάμπω G2985) | V-PAI-3S | "giveth light" |

Romans 8:28: "all things **work together** for good" — συνεργεῖ (V-PAI-3S) states a timeless principle about divine providence, not a report of a single event.

### 4. Historical / Narrative
A past event narrated using the present tense for vividness and immediacy. Common in Mark's Gospel with λέγει and ἔρχεται.

| Reference | Greek | Morph Code | KJV Translation |
|-----------|-------|------------|-----------------|
| John 1:29 | βλέπει (βλέπω G991) | V-PAI-3S | "seeth" |
| John 1:29 | λέγει (λέγω G3004) | V-PAI-3S | "saith" |
| Mark 1:40 (in 1:41) | λέγει (λέγω G3004) | V-PAI-3S | "saith" |
| Mark 2:18 | ἔρχονται (ἔρχομαι G2064) | V-PNI-3P | "they come" |

John 1:29: "John **seeth** Jesus coming ... and **saith**, Behold the Lamb of God" — βλέπει and λέγει (both V-PAI-3S) narrate a past event with present-tense vividness. The preceding temporal marker Τῇ ἐπαύριον ("the next day") anchors the action in past time. Mark 1:41 uses λέγει (V-PAI-3S) amid aorist narrative (σπλαγχνισθείς V-AOP, ἐκτείνας V-AAP, ἥψατο V-ADI) — the shift to present tense highlights the spoken word.

### 5. Stative
Describes an ongoing state or condition, especially with εἰμί ("to be") and verbs of perception or possession.

| Reference | Greek | Morph Code | KJV Translation |
|-----------|-------|------------|-----------------|
| John 14:6 | εἰμι (εἰμί G1510) | V-PAI-1S | "I am" |
| John 15:5 | εἰμι (εἰμί G1510) | V-PAI-1S | "I am" |
| John 5:24 | ἔχει (ἔχω G2192) | V-PAI-3S | "hath" |
| 1 John 1:9 | ἐστιν (εἰμί G1510) | V-PAI-3S | "is" |

John 14:6: "I **am** the way, the truth, and the life" — εἰμι (V-PAI-1S) expresses an abiding ontological state. John 5:24: "**hath** everlasting life" — ἔχει (V-PAI-3S) expresses present ongoing possession.

## Contrast with Related Forms

| Form | Aspect/Function | Example |
|------|-----------------|---------|
| **Present Active Indicative** | Imperfective: ongoing/internal view | 1 John 1:7 καθαρίζει "cleanseth" (V-PAI-3S) |
| **Aorist Active Indicative** | Perfective: action as bounded whole | Acts 10:15 ἐκαθάρισεν "cleansed" (V-AAI-3S) |
| **Perfect Active Indicative** | Completed action with abiding result | 2 Tim 4:7 τετέλεκα "I have finished" (V-RAI-1S) |
| **Imperfect Active Indicative** | Past imperfective: ongoing action in past time | Mark 2:18 ἦσαν "were" (V-IAI-3P) |

The contrast between present and aorist is especially visible with καθαρίζω across 1 John 1:7–9:
- **1 John 1:7** καθαρίζει (V-**P**AI-3S) — present: ongoing cleansing as a continuous reality
- **1 John 1:9** καθαρίσῃ (V-**A**AS-3S) — aorist subjunctive: cleansing viewed as a complete act in a conditional clause
