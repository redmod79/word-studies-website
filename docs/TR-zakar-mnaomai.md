## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same:**
- Zakar / μνάομαι both carry an active, consequential sense — to remember is to act. God's remembrance results in deliverance (Gen 8:1 → flood recedes; Exo 2:24 → Exodus begins; Luk 1:54 → Incarnation).
- The covenant context (remember + berîyth / diathēkē) is preserved perfectly through the LXX into the NT.

**What shifts:**
- In the NT, μνάομαι is used more for human memory and petition (Luk 23:42 "remember me when thou comest into thy kingdom") — similar to OT prayer usage.
- The proclamation/herald use (Hiphil "make mention") does not carry over strongly to μνάομαι in the NT; that function shifts to μαρτυρέω and εὐαγγελίζω.
