## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same across testaments:**
- God remains the primary tester of hearts and character (Jer 17:10 → 1 Th 2:4)
- The metallurgical metaphor (testing as refining by fire) carries through intact (Pro 17:3, Job 23:10 → 1 Co 3:13, 1 Pe 1:7)
- The ethical demand to test/examine remains (Psa 26:2, Mal 3:10 → 1 Th 5:21, Gal 6:4)

**What shifts:**
- The NT expands the *subject* of testing: believers are commanded to test themselves and all claims (Gal 6:4, 1 Th 5:21), not only God testing humans
- Dokimazo gains a stronger "approve" pole — what passes the test is *approved* (Rom 14:22, 1 Co 16:3), shifting from neutral examination to a test-and-certify model
- The NT word family (dokime, dokimion, dokimos) develops the concept into a technical vocabulary for Christian character formation through trial
