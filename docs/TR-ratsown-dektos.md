**Semantic shift:** The Hebrew râtsôwn covers a wide range — from cultic acceptability to royal will to God's gracious favour. The LXX δεκτός captures the "accepted/acceptable" pole but shifts the emphasis toward a more formal legal/cultic sense of being approved. The εὐδοκία and θέλημα renderings capture the relational and volitional poles respectively.

## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same across testaments:**
- The cultic idiom of an offering or action being "accepted/acceptable before God" persists from Leviticus through Phil 4:18
- The "acceptable time/year" idiom (עֵת רָצוֹן / καιρὸς δεκτός) is directly quoted in NT
- The divine initiative: râtsôwn/δεκτός signals God's pleased acceptance, not earned merit

**What shifts:**
- In the OT, râtsôwn spans cultic, wisdom, royal, and devotional domains; in the NT δεκτός is narrower — mainly the cultic/temporal senses survive in direct quotation
- The "sovereign will" sense (Dan 11: "do according to his will") does not carry into NT via δεκτός — θέλημα (G2307) absorbs that semantic range in the NT
- εὐδοκία (G2107) — the LXX's rendering for God's inner delight/pleasure (7x for râtsôwn) — becomes a major NT word (9x, notably Luke 2:14 "goodwill toward men"), making it the fuller heir to râtsôwn's relational-favour sense
