## Lexicon Definition

From the root concept of being shared/held in common by all; common, i.e. (literally) shared by all or several, or (ceremonially) profane, defiled:

- **Sense 1 (secular):** that which is shared by all, belonging to all in common, communal
- **Sense 2 (ceremonial/ritual):** that which is ordinary/secular (as opposed to set-apart), hence *profane*, *defiled*, *unclean* — what belongs to everyone, not consecrated to God

The dual meaning arises from the same logic: what is *common to all* is, by definition, *not set apart*. Ceremonial holiness required separation from the common sphere.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Shared / Held in Common (communal life)
The positive sense — property, faith, or salvation belonging to all members of a group equally, with no individual claiming exclusive ownership.

- **Acts 2:44** — "And all that believed were together, and had all things common" (early church community of goods)
- **Acts 4:32** — "neither said any of them that ought of the things which he possessed was his own; but they had all things common"
- **Titus 1:4** — "mine own son after the common faith" (a faith shared equally by Paul and Titus)
- **Jude 1:3** — "when I gave all diligence to write unto you of the common salvation" (salvation belonging to all believers)

### 2. Ceremonially Defiled / Ritually Unclean (Mosaic purity context)
The negative religious sense — food, persons, or practices that are "common" (not set apart) and therefore violate Jewish purity boundaries. Repeatedly paired with ἀκάθαρτος (G169, *unclean*).

- **Mark 7:2** — "eat bread with defiled (koinós), that is to say, with unwashen, hands" (Pharisees challenge disciples)
- **Mark 7:5** — "eat bread with unwashen hands" (Pharisees and scribes press the question)
- **Acts 10:14** — "I have never eaten any thing that is common or unclean" (Peter's protest in Cornelius vision)
- **Acts 10:28** — "God hath shewed me that I should not call any man common or unclean" (Peter applies the vision to persons, not just food)
- **Acts 11:8** — "nothing common or unclean hath at any time entered into my mouth" (Peter's retelling to Jerusalem)

### 3. Profane / Ordinary (desecrating what is sacred)
Applied to sacred things treated as if they were merely ordinary — the inverse of consecration. What is holy is made *koinós* by treating it without reverence.

- **Hebrews 10:29** — "hath counted the blood of the covenant, wherewith he was sanctified, an unholy thing (koinón)" (apostasy = treating the covenant blood as merely common)
- **Romans 14:14** — "there is nothing unclean (koinón) of itself: but to him that esteemeth any thing to be unclean, to him it is unclean" (Paul nuances the ceremonial sense — profaneness is relational/conscientious, not intrinsic)
- **Revelation 21:27** — "there shall in no wise enter into it any thing that defileth (koinón)" (nothing profane/common enters the New Jerusalem)
