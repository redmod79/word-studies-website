## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same across testaments:**
- Blood as the life-force — the prohibition on eating blood and the equation blood = life carries into NT (Acts 15:20)
- Blood as covenant-ratifying substance — Exo 24:8 echoed in Mat 26:28 and Heb 9:20
- Innocent blood crying out — martyrs' blood before God's throne (Rev 6:10; cf. Gen 4:10)
- Eschatological blood signs — Joel's blood moon quoted verbatim in Acts 2 and alluded to in Revelation

**What shifts:**
- The sacrificial system is fulfilled and typologically complete in Christ's blood (Heb 9–10); blood of animals gives way to blood of the Lamb
- Multiplicity → singularity: OT has innumerable blood offerings; NT centers on one definitive sacrifice
- Atonement scope expands: OT blood atones for specific sins within Israel; NT blood of Christ is for "many" and extends to all nations
