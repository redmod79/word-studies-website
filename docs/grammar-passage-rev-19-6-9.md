# Grammar Analysis: Revelation 19:6–9

## Overview

The climactic hallelujah-chorus and wedding-announcement of the Apocalypse. Following the fall of Babylon (Rev 17–18) and the double heavenly Hallelujah over her judgment (19:1–5), the scene pivots in v. 6 from *destruction* to *consummation* — from "Babylon the harlot" to "the wife of the Lamb" (γυνὴ αὐτοῦ, v. 7). The passage resolves into the fourth beatitude of the book (v. 9) and a divine-speech authentication formula ("these are the true words of God").

Structural panels:

- **v. 6** — Seer hears a threefold-simile heavenly voice ("as voice of great crowd / as voice of many waters / as voice of mighty thunders") with a participial speech-frame (λεγόντων) introducing the direct-speech Hallelujah and its ὅτι-grounded acclamation: "for the Lord God, the Almighty, has begun-to-reign (ἐβασίλευσεν)".
- **v. 7** — Two first-person-plural present subjunctives (hortatory) + one future indicative summon the heavenly community to rejoice, with ὅτι-clause grounding: the marriage of the Lamb *has come* (ἦλθεν) and his wife *has prepared herself* (ἡτοίμασεν ἑαυτήν — reflexive).
- **v. 8** — Divine passive (ἐδόθη αὐτῇ) + ἵνα-purpose subjunctive (περιβάληται — middle) clothes the bride; a narrator γάρ-gloss (not the voice of v. 6–7) decodes the symbol: "the fine linen is the righteous deeds of the saints".
- **v. 9** — The speech-situation shifts from heard-crowd-voice to a direct interlocutor ("he says to me") — presumably the interpreting angel first introduced in 17:1. Aorist imperative Γράψον commands the seer; makarism predicates blessedness on those *having-been-called* (κεκλημένοι — perfect passive participle) to the marriage supper; a second "he says to me" caps the speech with a divine-speech authentication formula.

Genre: heavenly-liturgy oracle inside an apocalyptic vision-report, with an embedded beatitude and a meta-textual authentication clause.

## Clause Structure

### v. 6 — four clauses
- **Main:** Καὶ ἤκουσα [ὡς φωνὴν ὄχλου πολλοῦ καὶ ὡς φωνὴν ὑδάτων πολλῶν καὶ ὡς φωνὴν βροντῶν ἰσχυρῶν] — aorist-indicative narrative spine (ἤκουσα, the seer's characteristic "I heard" of Revelation). Direct object = threefold ὡς-simile φωνὴν chain.
- **Participial speech-frame:** λεγόντων — genitive plural agreeing by *sense* with the collective-crowd referent (the accusative φωνήν is grammatically feminine singular; ἀνθρώπων/ὄχλων is the implied notional antecedent). See **Key Feature 1** below: concord-by-sense.
- **Direct-speech Hallelujah:** Ἁλληλουϊά (indeclinable Hebrew loanword, morph tag `HEB`).
- **ὅτι-ground:** ἐβασίλευσεν Κύριος ὁ Θεός ἡμῶν ὁ Παντοκράτωρ — the acclamation's reason; single main verb ἐβασίλευσεν with a triple-titled subject in predicative succession.

### v. 7 — three main clauses + one ὅτι-subordinate containing two clauses
- **Main 1 (hortatory subj.):** χαίρωμεν — "let us rejoice"
- **Main 2 (hortatory subj.):** ἀγαλλιῶμεν — "let us be glad"
- **Main 3 (future ind.):** δώσομεν τὴν δόξαν αὐτῷ — "we will give him the glory" (functioning as a *volitive future*; see Key Feature 3)
- **ὅτι-ground containing two clauses:**
  - ἦλθεν ὁ γάμος τοῦ Ἀρνίου — "the wedding of the Lamb has come"
  - ἡ γυνὴ αὐτοῦ ἡτοίμασεν ἑαυτήν — "his wife has prepared herself" (reflexive direct object)

### v. 8 — two clauses joined by γάρ
- **Clause 1:** καὶ ἐδόθη αὐτῇ ἵνα περιβάληται βύσσινον λαμπρὸν καθαρόν — impersonal passive main verb + ἵνα + middle subjunctive (content/purpose). The ἵνα-clause fills the semantic slot of what-was-granted (epexegetic). βύσσινον λαμπρὸν καθαρόν is a substantival adjective string, not a noun phrase with a head noun.
- **Clause 2 (γάρ-explanatory narrator's aside):** τὸ βύσσινον τὰ δικαιώματα τῶν ἁγίων ἐστίν — articular subject + articular predicate nominative plural (τὰ δικαιώματα, pl.) with singular copula ἐστίν. The singular copula with plural predicate is regular when the *subject* (τὸ βύσσινον, neuter sg.) is singular. Classic allegorical-identification formula (X is Y = X *means* Y), paralleling Rev 1:20; 5:6; 5:8; 17:9, 12, 15.

### v. 9 — four clauses
- **Clause 1:** Καὶ λέγει μοι — narrative/historical present λέγει, the signature Apocalyptic speech-introduction formula.
- **Clause 2 (direct speech imperative):** Γράψον — 2 sg. aorist active imperative.
- **Clause 3 (direct speech — the beatitude):** Μακάριοι οἱ εἰς τὸ δεῖπνον τοῦ γάμου τοῦ Ἀρνίου κεκλημένοι — verbless *makarism* formula: Μακάριοι (predicate adjective, nom. pl. m.) + οἱ … κεκλημένοι (articular perfect-passive participle used substantivally). The whole εἰς τὸ δεῖπνον … Ἀρνίου prepositional phrase intrudes *between* the article οἱ and its participle κεκλημένοι — classic Greek attributive-position embedding.
- **Clause 4 (second speech-introduction + authentication formula):** καὶ λέγει μοι Οὗτοι οἱ λόγοι ἀληθινοὶ τοῦ Θεοῦ εἰσιν — nominal-identification clause with demonstrative subject Οὗτοι, articular subject-complement οἱ λόγοι … τοῦ Θεοῦ, anarthrous predicate adjective ἀληθινοὶ, and explicit copula εἰσιν.

## Key Grammatical Features

### 1. λεγόντων — genitive-masculine participle after accusative-feminine noun (v. 6) — concord-by-sense

- **Form:** V-PAP-GPM — present active participle, **genitive plural masculine**, lemma λέγω.
- **Grammatical "problem":** The immediate antecedent φωνήν is accusative feminine singular. The only plural antecedents in the simile chain are ὄχλου (gen. sg. m.), ὑδάτων (gen. pl. n.), βροντῶν (gen. pl. f.) — none gen. pl. m. exactly. Yet the participle is gen. pl. m.
- **Resolution:** *Constructio ad sensum* — the participle agrees by *sense* with the implied referent "the people of the crowd / the mass of speakers" (notionally masculine-plural persons), not with any surface case-matched noun. Rev does this repeatedly: see Rev 4:8 (neuter-sg. ζῷα referred to by masc.-pl. ptcp. λέγοντες in many MSS); Rev 7:9–10 (acc. ὄχλον answered by κράζουσιν); Rev 11:15 (nom.-pl. φωναί followed by masc.-pl. participial agreement); Rev 19:14 (neut. στρατεύματα followed by masc. ἐνδεδυμένοι).
- **Exegetical consequence:** the threefold-ὡς simile (crowd / waters / thunders) is not describing three acoustic phenomena speaking — only the first (the crowd) is the speaker. The gen.-pl.-masc. participle *selects* the human-crowd antecedent from the triad. The waters-and-thunders terms are acoustic comparanda for the volume, not the speakers.
- **Grammar reference:** [concord-shift-neuter-to-masculine](../greek/concord-shift-neuter-to-masculine.md) covers the gender-shift-by-sense category; this verse is the canonical gender-and-case-shift example.

### 2. ἐβασίλευσεν — ingressive aorist (v. 6)

- **Form:** V-AAI-3S of βασιλεύω "to reign, be king". Aorist active indicative.
- **Significance:** The aorist of an inherently *stative* verb (βασιλεύω = "to be king / to reign") characteristically functions as an **ingressive aorist**: "he *began* to reign / has *taken up* his reign / has *entered into* his kingly rule". KJV "reigneth" conceals the aorist; NASB/NET "has begun to reign" / "began to reign" is the grammatically weighted rendering. Compare Rev 11:17 (ἐβασίλευσας in the 7th-trumpet Hallelujah, same form, 2 sg.) — the same ingressive sense anchors both triumph-hymns.
- The ingressive aorist locates the *transition point*: from "the kingdom of the world" (11:15 perf. ἐγένετο) to "the reign is-taken-up". In narrative-apocalyptic frame, it marks the eschatological accession moment.
- **Grammar-library gap:** no canonical entry for "ingressive aorist" yet. Flagged.

### 3. χαίρωμεν / ἀγαλλιῶμεν — hortatory (cohortative) present subjunctives (v. 7)

- **Form:** V-PAS-1P for both. Present active subjunctive, 1 pl. Paired as synonymous "rejoice and be glad" — the standard LXX/NT hendiadys for festive celebration (cf. Matt 5:12 χαίρετε καὶ ἀγαλλιᾶσθε; 1 Pet 1:8; 4:13).
- **Function:** **Hortatory subjunctive** (also called "volitive subjunctive") — the 1st-person-plural subjunctive is Greek's normal way of expressing "let us X"; there is no dedicated cohortative mood in Greek as there is in Hebrew. The present (vs. aorist) tense-stem is aspectually *imperfective* — "let us *be rejoicing / keep on rejoicing*". For a sustained festal action this is the expected stem.
- **Semitic background:** Matches the Hebrew 1cp cohortative form נָגִילָה וְנִשְׂמְחָה "let us rejoice and be glad" (Isa 25:9; Ps 118:24). The chorus in v. 7 is deliberately echoing the OT eschatological-banquet celebration vocabulary in Greek dress.
- **Grammar-library gap:** no canonical "hortatory subjunctive" entry yet. Flagged.

### 4. δώσομεν — future indicative as continuation of hortatory series (v. 7)

- **Form:** V-FAI-1P of δίδωμι — future active indicative, 1 pl.
- **Function:** The future indicative in the *third* member of a volitive series after two hortatory subjunctives is best read as a **volitive future** — "and we will give him the glory" in the sense "and let us give…". This assimilation of futures into volitional series is common NT-Koine usage (cf. Matt 5:48 ἔσεσθε, Rom 6:2 ζήσομεν). Some manuscripts (א A) read δῶμεν (aor. act. subj., 1 pl. — "let us give"), harmonizing the series to three subjunctives; the majority reading δώσομεν is preferred as the *lectio difficilior*.
- **Exegetical consequence:** The textual-variant and the grammatical flexibility both reflect the same reality — the three verbs form a single *volitive triad*, however the final member is morphologically marked.

### 5. ἡτοίμασεν ἑαυτήν — aorist active + reflexive object (v. 7)

- **Form:** V-AAI-3S of ἑτοιμάζω "to prepare" + F-3ASF ἑαυτήν "herself" (reflexive pronoun, 3rd person, acc. sg. fem.).
- **Significance:** Active voice with reflexive object — "she prepared herself". The bride is the **agent** of her own readiness. This is theologically striking in light of v. 8, where the clothing is given by divine passive (ἐδόθη). The grammar holds two complementary truths in adjacent clauses:
  - v. 7 — *the bride prepared herself* (human agency, reflexive).
  - v. 8 — *it was granted to her that she might clothe herself* (divine gift + middle subjunctive).
- The pairing is resolved by v. 8's γάρ-gloss equating the fine linen with τὰ δικαιώματα τῶν ἁγίων — the bride's "self-preparation" *is* the righteous deeds she has done, and those deeds *are themselves* granted by God (ἐδόθη). The grammar mirrors the Phil 2:12–13 pattern ("work out your own salvation… for God is the one working in you").
- **Compare** Greek middle alternative: the writer *could* have written ἡτοιμάσατο "she prepared herself" in the middle voice. He used the active + reflexive instead, which emphasizes the bride as subject-and-object distinctly — her agency is foregrounded more emphatically than a bare middle would have.

### 6. ἐδόθη — divine passive (v. 8)

- **Form:** V-API-3S of δίδωμι — aorist passive indicative, 3 sg. "it was given / it was granted".
- **Function:** Textbook **divine passive** (*passivum divinum*). No expressed agent; God is the implied giver; context (marriage-of-the-Lamb consummation) makes the divine agency unambiguous. ἐδόθη is Revelation's signature divine-passive form — it occurs 22× in the book (e.g. Rev 6:2, 4, 8; 7:2; 9:1, 3, 5; 11:1, 2; 13:5, 7, 14, 15; 16:8; 19:8; 20:4). Each occurrence signals something permitted-by-divine-decree within the apocalyptic drama.
- **Here the construction is impersonal** ("it was granted to her that…"), with the content-purpose being filled by the ἵνα-clause. This is identical in form to Rev 6:4 ἐδόθη αὐτῷ… ἵνα "it was given to him that…".
- **Grammar reference:** [divine-passive](../greek/divine-passive.md) — canonical entry; this is example Rev 19:8. Also [aorist-passive-indicative](../greek/aorist-passive-indicative.md) for the morphology.

### 7. ἵνα περιβάληται — ἵνα + aorist middle subjunctive (v. 8)

- **Form:** ἵνα (G2443) + V-2AMS-3S of περιβάλλω (G4016) "to clothe (around)".
- **Voice:** **Middle** (not passive). περιβάλλομαι in the middle = "I clothe myself / put on (clothing)". Not "she should be clothed" (passive sense) but "she should clothe herself" — she is the agent of her own dressing, though the clothing itself was given to her.
- **Function of ἵνα:** Content/epexegetic ἵνα — the ἵνα-clause fills the semantic content of what was granted. ἵνα with subjunctive here is substantival (the thing-granted) rather than purely telic (in order that). Compare Rev 6:4, 9:5, 13:12, etc., where ἐδόθη + ἵνα fuses "grant-permission" with content-specification.
- **Aspect:** Aorist stem — perfective/constative; the single dressing-event (not an ongoing habit).
- The combination of divine passive (ἐδόθη) + middle subjunctive (περιβάληται) is theologically precise: the bride's dressing-action is her own (middle), but the occasion/possibility/the clothing itself is the divine grant (passive). See also the reflexive ἑαυτήν of v. 7 — three different grammatical strategies, all pointing to the same human-divine cooperation in the bride's readiness.

### 8. τὸ γὰρ βύσσινον τὰ δικαιώματα … ἐστίν — allegorical-identification formula (v. 8)

- **Form:** Articular neuter-singular substantival adjective τὸ βύσσινον (subject, "the fine-linen") + articular neuter-plural predicate nominative τὰ δικαιώματα τῶν ἁγίων + singular copula ἐστίν.
- **Narrator's aside (γάρ):** The voice shifts. vv. 6–7 are spoken by the heavenly multitude; v. 8a continues the narrator's description; v. 8b is a parenthetical γάρ-explanation in John's own interpretive voice. Revelation does this repeatedly — e.g. Rev 1:20 (John's Lord identifies symbols); 5:6 (seer's description + parenthetical identification of horns/eyes); 17:9 ("this calls for wisdom"), 17:12, 15 (explicit symbol-decoding). The γάρ-aside in 19:8 is of this decoding type.
- **Singular verb with plural predicate:** ἐστίν agrees with the *subject* τὸ βύσσινον (sg.), not with the predicate τὰ δικαιώματα (pl.). This is regular Greek. The *effect* is to identify one symbol with one abstract collection ("*the fine linen [as a whole]* is-equivalent-to *the righteous-deeds*").
- **δικαιώματα pl.** — concrete "righteous-deeds / righteous-acts" (plural abstract → concrete), not the abstract "righteousness" (δικαιοσύνη, G1343). The word [G1345-dikaioma](../../word-studies/G1345-dikaioma.md) ranges from "ordinance/legal-statute" (Rom 1:32; 2:26; 8:4; Heb 9:1, 10; Luke 1:6) to "righteous-act" (Rom 5:18; Rev 15:4; 19:8) to "verdict/acquittal-act" (Rom 5:16). Here the plural with τῶν ἁγίων makes it concrete deeds by/of the saints.
- **Genitive τῶν ἁγίων:** ambiguous between subjective ("the righteous-deeds **done-by** the saints") and possessive/pertinence ("the righteous-deeds **pertaining-to / belonging-to** the saints"). Given the reflexive self-preparation of v. 7, the subjective genitive is most natural — deeds done *by* the saints. The divine-passive of v. 8a and the γάρ-aside together require the *source* of these deeds to be God's grant, even while the *doer* is the saint.
- **Word-study:** [G1345-dikaioma](../../word-studies/G1345-dikaioma.md) — canonical entry; this is the climactic Revelation occurrence.

### 9. Γράψον — aorist active imperative (v. 9)

- **Form:** V-AAM-2S of γράφω. Aorist active imperative, 2 sg. — "Write!"
- **Function:** A command for a single act (the writing-down of the beatitude that follows). Aorist imperatives typically command a specific punctiliar action; present imperatives command habitual/ongoing action. Rev uses Γράψον repeatedly as the inspired-dictation formula: Rev 1:11, 19; 2:1, 8, 12, 18; 3:1, 7, 14; 14:13; 19:9; 21:5 — each time a specific declaration follows which John is to inscribe. The formula authenticates the following speech as *canonical / to be written / authoritative*.
- **Grammar reference:** [mood-aorist-imperative](../greek/mood-aorist-imperative.md) — canonical entry covers the punctiliar-command function; Rev 19:9 is a paradigmatic "write this down" dictation-imperative.
- **Word study:** [G1125-grapho](../../word-studies/G1125-grapho.md).

### 10. Μακάριοι … κεκλημένοι — makarism with perfect passive participle (v. 9)

- **Form:** Verbless beatitude. Μακάριοι (A-NPM, predicate adjective, nom. pl. masc.) + οἱ … κεκλημένοι (articular substantival **perfect passive participle**, V-RPP-NPM, nom. pl. masc., lemma καλέω).
- **Beatitude formula:** Standard NT makarism pattern — "Blessed [are] those who …". Revelation has **seven beatitudes** (Rev 1:3; 14:13; 16:15; 19:9; 20:6; 22:7; 22:14); 19:9 is the fourth. Each is verbless predication with Μακάριοι/Μακάριος fronted for emphasis.
- **κεκλημένοι — perfect passive participle:** The perfect-tense stem of the participle denotes a **completed past action with present-enduring result**. "Those who have-been-called (and therefore stand-as-called)". The passive voice conceals the agent — another divine passive: God is the caller. The substantival article οἱ turns the whole construction into a noun phrase ("the ones-who-have-been-called").
- **Attributive position:** The prepositional phrase εἰς τὸ δεῖπνον τοῦ γάμου τοῦ Ἀρνίου sits *between* the article οἱ and its participle κεκλημένοι — classic first-attributive-position embedding. The article's scope gathers the entire prepositional phrase under the participle's headship.
- **Compare the Synoptic wedding-feast parables:** κεκλημένοι is the standard participle used in Matt 22:3, 4, 8; Luke 14:7, 8, 10, 17, 24 for "the invited" to wedding feasts. The form in Rev 19:9 is deliberately echoing Jesus' parables — the ones from the parables who accepted the invitation and made it to the feast.
- **Grammar reference:** [participle-perfect-passive-state](../greek/participle-perfect-passive-state.md) — canonical entry; Rev 19:9 is an explicit example case.
- **Word study:** [G3107-makarios](../../word-studies/G3107-makarios.md) (if exists — flagged as gap otherwise); [G2564-kaleo](../../word-studies/G2564-kaleo.md) (gap); [G1173-deipnon](../../word-studies/G1173-deipnon.md) (gap); [G721-arnion](../../word-studies/G721-arnion.md) — canonical entry exists.

### 11. τὸ δεῖπνον τοῦ γάμου τοῦ Ἀρνίου — triple-stacked genitive chain (v. 9)

- **Form:** N-ASN τὸ δεῖπνον "the supper" + N-GSM τοῦ γάμου "of the wedding" + N-GSN τοῦ Ἀρνίου "of the Lamb". Three nouns, two genitive-dependency layers.
- **Structure:** "The supper [belonging-to the wedding [of the Lamb]]". γάμος is itself genitively dependent on δεῖπνον; Ἀρνίου is genitively dependent on γάμος. Each genitive names the *occasion/owner* of the preceding noun. Compare v. 7 ὁ γάμος τοῦ Ἀρνίου (simple) → v. 9 τὸ δεῖπνον τοῦ γάμου τοῦ Ἀρνίου (extended).
- **Significance:** γάμος (G1062) = the whole marriage-ceremony/wedding-festival-period; δεῖπνον (G1173) = the specific banquet-meal within that festival. The seven-day Jewish wedding-feast (cf. Gen 29:27; Judg 14:10–17; Tob 11:18) had a δεῖπνον as its central meal; γάμος named the whole festive complex. So δεῖπνον τοῦ γάμου = "the *banquet proper* of the wedding" — the culminating meal, not the whole festival.
- **Semitic / OT background:** The triple-stacked genitive ("X of Y of Z") is native Greek idiom but echoes Hebrew construct-chains. The motif of a *banquet of the wedding of the messianic figure* traces back through Isa 25:6 (YHWH's mountain-banquet for all peoples) and Jesus' parables (Matt 22:1–14; Luke 14:15–24).
- **Grammar reference gap:** no canonical "stacked genitive chain" entry.

### 12. Οὗτοι οἱ λόγοι ἀληθινοὶ τοῦ Θεοῦ εἰσιν — authentication formula (v. 9)

- **Form:** Demonstrative-subject nominal clause: Οὗτοι (D-NPM) + οἱ λόγοι (articular N-NPM) + ἀληθινοὶ (anarthrous predicate adjective A-NPM) + τοῦ Θεοῦ (genitive modifier) + εἰσιν (V-PAI-3P copula).
- **Word-order analysis:** The predicate adjective ἀληθινοὶ stands *between* the articular subject-phrase (οἱ λόγοι … τοῦ Θεοῦ) and the copula εἰσιν. This is the anarthrous-predicate-adjective pattern: "These-the-words-are true-of-God". Greek routinely signals the predicate by *not* giving it an article when the subject has one.
- **Function:** An **authentication/authorization formula** — a meta-textual seal placed on the beatitude that precedes it. Revelation uses this formula twice more with minor variation:
  - Rev 21:5 — οὗτοι οἱ λόγοι πιστοὶ καὶ ἀληθινοί εἰσιν (the throne-speech "behold, I make all things new").
  - Rev 22:6 — οὗτοι οἱ λόγοι πιστοὶ καὶ ἀληθινοί (the book-closing statement).
  - Each instance certifies a preceding speech-act as *genuine divine speech, faithfully transmitted*.
- **ἀληθινός (G228) vs ἀληθής (G227):** ἀληθινός = "genuine, real, authentic, corresponding-to-reality" (not merely "not-false"). It denotes the *real thing as opposed to the imitation*. Used of Christ (John 1:9; 6:32; 15:1), of God (John 17:3; 1 Thess 1:9), of the heavenly sanctuary (Heb 8:2; 9:24), and of the divine words (Rev 19:9; 21:5; 22:6).
- **Significance:** The formula does double duty — it certifies the beatitude of v. 9a, and by extension (as a self-reflexive Apocalyptic authentication) it anchors the chain back through the hallelujah oracle of vv. 6–8. The narrator's γάρ-gloss in v. 8b is absorbed under this same authentication umbrella.

## Discourse Structure

| Segment | Speaker | Speech-introduction | Verb type(s) | Function |
|---------|---------|---------------------|--------------|----------|
| v. 6a | Seer | ἤκουσα (main-line) | aor. ind. | vision-report (heard) |
| v. 6b | Heavenly voice | λεγόντων (ptcp. frame) | pres. ptcp. → ὅτι + aor. | acclamation |
| v. 7 | Heavenly voice (continuing) | — | 2× pres. subj. + fut. ind. + ὅτι + aor. | festal summons + ground |
| v. 8a | Narrator | — | aor. pass. + ἵνα + aor. mid. subj. | report of grant |
| v. 8b | John (γάρ-aside) | τὸ γάρ | pres. ind. copula | allegorical gloss |
| v. 9a | Interpreting angel | Καὶ λέγει μοι (hist. pres.) | aor. impv. + makarism + perf. pass. ptcp. | dictation-beatitude |
| v. 9b | Interpreting angel | καὶ λέγει μοι (hist. pres.) | pres. ind. copula | authentication formula |

**Seams:**
- v. 6 → v. 7: continuous voice (crowd-chorus), no new speech-frame needed; the ὅτι-ground of v. 6 transitions into the hortatory 1 pl. of v. 7 without break.
- v. 7 → v. 8a: the narrator's voice takes over; the subject shifts from "we" (chorus) to "her" (the bride); the verb shifts from volitive to divine-passive.
- v. 8a → v. 8b: γάρ-parenthesis by John; copula-ἐστίν allegorical identification.
- v. 8b → v. 9a: Καὶ λέγει μοι — full speaker-change; new interlocutor (interpreting angel); new speech-mode (direct 2 sg. dictation).
- v. 9a → v. 9b: the repeated καὶ λέγει μοι resets the frame for the authentication-formula; it is a separate speech-act, not a continuation of the beatitude.

## Key Word-Level Observations

### Ἁλληλουϊά (G239, v. 6)

Indeclinable Hebrew loanword הַלְלוּיָהּ ("praise [imperative pl.] + Yah"). Morph tag `HEB` (parser's Hebraism category). Occurs in the NT only in Rev 19:1, 3, 4, 6 — four times, clustered in this single oracle. The LXX uses it as a Psalm-title (LXX Psalms 104–106, 110–118, 134–135, 145–150). The concentration of four Hallelujahs in Rev 19 frames the hinge from Babylon-judgment (19:1–5) to Lamb-wedding (19:6–9).

### ὁ Παντοκράτωρ (G3841, v. 6)

"The Almighty / All-Ruler". Articular, in apposition to ὁ Θεός ἡμῶν. LXX's standard rendering of צְבָאוֹת ("of hosts") and of שַׁדַּי ("Shaddai"). Revelation uses it 9×: 1:8; 4:8; 11:17; 15:3; 16:7, 14; 19:6, 15; 21:22. Always articular, always in YHWH-title contexts. The title here bridges v. 6 back to the OT enthronement-hymn tradition (esp. Ps 93, 97, 99; Isa 24:23, which locates YHWH's reign at Mount Zion).

### γάμος (G1062, v. 7, v. 9)

"Wedding, marriage-celebration". Distinct from δεῖπνον — γάμος is the whole festival; δεῖπνον is the banquet-meal inside it. The word-study [G1060-gameo](../../word-studies/G1060-gameo.md) covers the cognate verb; a dedicated γάμος entry is a gap.

### δεῖπνον (G1173, v. 9)

"Banquet, principal meal (typically evening)". NT uses: Matt 23:6 (chief seats at dinners); Mark 6:21 (Herod's birthday δεῖπνον); Mark 12:39; Luke 14:12, 16, 17, 24 (parable-banquet); Luke 20:46; John 12:2; 13:2, 4; 21:20 (Last Supper); 1 Cor 11:20–21 (κυριακὸν δεῖπνον, the Lord's Supper); Rev 19:9, 17 (the marriage supper of the Lamb vs. the "great supper of God" — an anti-banquet in v. 17 where the birds are invited to eat the flesh of kings). The v. 9 δεῖπνον and the v. 17 δεῖπνον are deliberately paralleled-and-contrasted: the Lamb's wedding banquet vs. the vultures' flesh-banquet on those who opposed the Lamb.

### Ἀρνίον (G721, v. 7, v. 9)

"Lamb" (diminutive form). The signature Christological title in Revelation — used 29× of Christ (plus 1× of the second-beast/antichrist figure in Rev 13:11). Revelation prefers ἀρνίον over the Gospels' ἀμνός (John 1:29, 36; Acts 8:32; 1 Pet 1:19). See [G721-arnion](../../word-studies/G721-arnion.md).

### βύσσινος (G1039, v. 8)

"Fine linen". Substantival adjective — no head noun; the adjective stands for "fine-linen (garment)". Used elsewhere in NT only at Rev 18:12, 16 (Babylon's cargo and clothing) and 19:14 (the heavenly armies' clothing). The deliberate echo of 18:16 ("clothed in fine linen") by 19:8 and 19:14 constructs an antithetical pair: Babylon-in-fine-linen (commercial/harlot) vs. Bride-in-fine-linen (righteous-deeds) vs. Armies-in-fine-linen (eschatological). OT background: the priestly and tabernacle bysses (Exod 26:1, 31, 36; 28:5; etc.); Esther's royal garments (Esth 8:15).

## Grammatical Summary

Five grammatical features most tightly carry the exegesis of Rev 19:6–9:

1. **ἐβασίλευσεν (ingressive aorist, v. 6)** — dates the reign's beginning; the acclamation is a *transition-marker*, not a timeless report.
2. **ἡτοίμασεν ἑαυτήν (active + reflexive) vs. ἐδόθη … ἵνα περιβάληται (divine passive + middle subjunctive)** — the passage holds self-preparation and divine grant in adjacent clauses without dissolving either; the γάρ-aside decodes the synthesis: the bride's deeds are her own (reflexive) *and* the clothing is granted (passive) *because* her self-prepared deeds are themselves the gift.
3. **λεγόντων (concord-by-sense, v. 6)** — the gen.-pl.-masc. participle after an acc.-sg.-fem. noun selects the human-crowd antecedent from the triple simile, clarifying that only the crowd (not waters/thunders) speaks.
4. **κεκλημένοι (perfect passive participle, v. 9)** — the beatitude falls on those whose call is a completed-past act with present-enduring result; the perfect tense's grammatical force undergirds the theology of effectual calling.
5. **Οὗτοι οἱ λόγοι ἀληθινοὶ τοῦ Θεοῦ εἰσιν (authentication formula, v. 9)** — the metatextual seal that authenticates the preceding oracle as canonical divine speech, parallel to Rev 21:5; 22:6.

Together these grammatical features construct the passage as: a datable eschatological-accession acclamation (v. 6), a volitive festal summons grounded in wedding-arrival and bride-readiness (v. 7), a dual-agency clothing (divine grant + self-preparation = righteous deeds, v. 8), and a dictation-beatitude for the invited guests sealed with a divine-authenticity formula (v. 9).
