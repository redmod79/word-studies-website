# H4325 — מַיִם (mayim)

## Core Data
- **Strong's:** H4325
- **Original:** מַיִם
- **Transliteration:** mayim
- **Pronunciation:** mah'-yim
- **Part of Speech:** Masculine noun (plural in form, often singular in sense)
- **Root:** Dual of a primitive noun used in a singular sense
- **Occurrences:** 524 unique verses (631 tokens) across 33 OT books
- **First Occurrence:** Gen 1:2 — "darkness was upon the face of the deep. And the Spirit of God moved upon the face of the waters"
- **Hapax Legomenon:** No

## Lexicon Definition

Dual of a primitive noun (but used in a singular sense); water; figuratively, juice; by euphemism, urine, semen. KJV translations: piss, wasting, water(-ing, -course, -flood, -spring).

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Drinking Water — Daily Sustenance, Pilgrim Logistics, Hospitality
- **Gen 24:13–20** — Rebekah at the well drawing water for Abraham's servant and his camels
