## Semantic Categories

### 1. Commanded Silence for Listening (Acts)
A crowd is hushed so a speaker can be heard — the silence is earned, sudden, and purposeful.

- **Acts 21:40** — "And when he had given him licence, Paul stood on the stairs, and beckoned with the hand unto the people. And when there was made a great silence (σιγή), he spake unto them in the Hebrew tongue."

*Pattern:* σιγή + γίνομαι ("great silence came/was made") — the noun is used adjectivally in a genitive construction emphasizing the totality of the hush.

### 2. Cosmic/Eschatological Silence (Revelation)
Heaven itself becomes silent at the opening of the seventh seal — a pregnant pause before the final judgments.

- **Rev 8:1** — "And when he had opened the seventh seal, there was silence (σιγή) in heaven about the space of half an hour."

*Pattern:* σιγή + ἐν τῷ οὐρανῷ — the silence is localized in heaven and defined by duration (ἡμιώριον, "half an hour"), suggesting it is a deliberate, expectant, and solemn pause.
