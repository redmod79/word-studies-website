# G622 — ἀπόλλυμι (apóllymi)

## Core Data
- **Strong's:** G622
- **Original:** ἀπόλλυμι
- **Transliteration:** apóllymi
- **Pronunciation:** ap-ol'-loo-mee
- **Part of Speech:** Verb
- **Root:** ἀπό (G575, "away from") + the base of ὄλλυμι ("to destroy") — "to destroy away," i.e., destroy fully
- **Occurrences:** 86 unique verses (102 tokens) across 15 NT books
- **First Occurrence:** Mat 2:13 — "young child to destroy (apóllymi) him" (Herod seeking to kill Jesus)
- **Hapax Legomenon:** No

## Lexicon Definition

From ἀπό and the base of ὄλλυμι; to destroy fully (reflexively, to perish, or lose), literally or figuratively. Voice distinguishes meaning:
- **Active:** to destroy, ruin, put to death (transitive)
- **Middle/Passive:** to perish, be ruined, be lost; to lose oneself
- **Middle with direct object:** to lose (as in losing one's life or a possession)

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Physical Destruction / Killing (active voice, human agent)
Plotting to kill, military destruction, execution.
- **Mat 2:13** — Herod seeking "to destroy" the young child
- **Mat 12:14 / Mar 3:6 / Mar 11:18 / Luk 19:47** — Pharisees plotting "how they might destroy him"
- **Mat 22:7** — king "destroyed those murderers, and burned up their city"
- **Mat 27:20** — multitude persuaded to "destroy Jesus"
- **Mar 9:22** — demon casting boy into fire "to destroy him"

### 2. Divine Judgment / Eschatological Ruin (active voice, God as agent)
God or Christ destroying the wicked, the unrepentant, or hostile powers.
- **Mat 10:28** — "fear him which is able to destroy both soul and body in hell"
- **Luk 17:27, 29** — flood and Sodom: "destroyed them all"
- **Jas 4:12** — "one lawgiver, who is able to save and to destroy"
- **Jude 1:5** — "afterward destroyed them that believed not"
- **1 Cor 1:19** — "I will destroy the wisdom of the wise"

### 3. Perishing / Being Lost in Eternal Judgment (middle/passive voice)
The standard NT contrast with eternal life or salvation. Often paired with σῴζω.
- **Joh 3:15–16** — "whosoever believeth in him should not perish, but have eternal life"
- **Joh 10:28** — "they shall never perish, neither shall any pluck them out of my hand"
- **Luk 13:3, 5** — "except ye repent, ye shall all likewise perish"
- **Rom 2:12** — "as many as have sinned without law shall also perish without law"
- **1 Cor 1:18** — "the preaching of the cross is to them that perish foolishness"
- **2 Cor 2:15** — "in them that are saved, and in them that perish"
- **2 Pet 3:9** — "not willing that any should perish, but that all should come to repentance"
- **2 Th 2:10** — "in them that perish; because they received not the love of the truth"

### 4. Losing One's Life / Soul (middle voice with object)
The paradox saying — lose/save life. Always voluntary-paradigm contexts.
- **Mat 10:39 / 16:25 / Mar 8:35 / Luk 9:24 / Luk 17:33 / Joh 12:25** — "whosoever will save his life shall lose it"
- **Luk 9:25** — "if he gain the whole world, and lose himself"

### 5. Losing / Finding a Possession (middle voice, mundane object)
The lost-sheep / lost-coin / lost-son parable cluster.
- **Mat 10:6 / 15:24** — "lost sheep of the house of Israel"
- **Luk 15:4, 6, 8, 9, 17, 24, 32** — parables of the lost sheep, coin, prodigal ("this my son... was lost, and is found")
- **Mat 18:11 / Luk 19:10** — "Son of man is come to save that which was lost"
- **Joh 6:12** — "that nothing be lost"
- **Joh 6:39 / 17:12 / 18:9** — "of all which he hath given me I should lose nothing"

### 6. Decay / Perishability (of food, flesh, garments, goods)
Material degradation contrasted with eternal endurance.
- **Joh 6:27** — "Labour not for the meat which perisheth"
- **Heb 1:11** — "They shall perish; but thou remainest"
- **Jas 1:11** — "the grace of the fashion of it perisheth"
- **1 Pet 1:7** — "gold that perisheth"
- **Mat 9:17 / Mar 2:22 / Luk 5:37** — wineskins "perish" / "are marred" when new wine bursts them

### 7. Safety / Preservation (negated)
Reassurance that no harm will come.
- **Luk 21:18** — "there shall not an hair of your head perish"
- **Joh 17:12** — "none of them is lost, but the son of perdition"
- **Acts 27:34** (referenced in Outlook, not Strong's lookup count) — similar preservation promise
