## Semantic Categories

### 1. Ontological Reality / Essential Being
The word denotes the underlying reality or essential nature of a being — what something truly is beneath appearances.
- **Heb 1:3** — "Who being the brightness of his glory, and the express image of his **person** (hupostasis), and upholding all things by the word of his power"
  - Here hupostasis refers to God's essential being. The Son is the exact representation (charakter) of the Father's underlying reality.

### 2. Subjective Confidence / Boldness
In 2 Corinthians, the word describes a confident stance or bold assurance in one's position or claims.
- **2 Cor 9:4** — "Lest haply if they of Macedonia come with me, and find you unprepared, we (that we say not, ye) should be ashamed in this same **confident** (hupostasis) boasting"
- **2 Cor 11:17** — "That which I speak, I speak it not after the Lord, but as it were foolishly, in this **confidence** (hupostasis) of boasting"
  - Both uses pair hupostasis with kauchesis ("boasting"), indicating the confident ground or basis for one's claims.

### 3. Objective Foundation / Substance of Faith
In Hebrews, the word describes the assured ground or foundational reality upon which faith rests, and the firm beginning-point of Christian hope.
- **Heb 11:1** — "Now faith is the **substance** (hupostasis) of things hoped for, the evidence of things not seen"
  - Hupostasis here denotes the objective ground or assured reality that gives faith its content — paired with elenchos ("evidence/proof," G1650).
- **Heb 3:14** — "For we are made partakers of Christ, if we hold the beginning of our **confidence** (hupostasis) stedfast unto the end"
  - Here hupostasis is the firm foundation or confident assurance that believers must maintain; paired with bebaios ("steadfast," G949).
