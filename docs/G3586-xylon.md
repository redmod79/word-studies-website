## Lexicon Definition

Timber (as fuel or material); by implication a stick, club, or tree, or other wooden article or substance. From another form of the base of ξύω (xyo).

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Crucifixion / Hanging on a Tree
- **Acts 5:30** — "whom ye slew and hanged on a tree (xylon)"
- **Acts 10:39** — "whom they slew and hanged on a tree (xylon)"
- **Acts 13:29** — "they took him down from the tree (xylon)"
- **Gal 3:13** — "Cursed is every one that hangeth on a tree (xylon)" (quoting Deut 21:23)
- **1 Pet 2:24** — "bare our sins in his own body on the tree (xylon)"

This is the most theologically concentrated usage. All five passages apply xylon to Christ's death, deliberately echoing the LXX of Deut 21:23 where H6086 (ets) is rendered ξύλον. The apostolic preference for xylon over stauros in these contexts connects the cross to the OT curse-formula.

### 2. Tree of Life (Eschatological)
- **Rev 2:7** — "will I give to eat of the tree (xylon) of life"
- **Rev 22:2** — "the tree (xylon) of life, which bare twelve manner of fruits"

These echo the LXX of Gen 2:9 and 3:22 where the tree of life is ξύλον τῆς ζωῆς. The bookend use — Genesis creation to Revelation restoration — marks the full arc of the concept.

### 3. Wooden Clubs / Staves (Weapons)

### 4. Building Material / Timber
- **1 Cor 3:12** — "gold, silver, precious stones, wood (xylon), hay, stubble"

Here xylon means raw timber as a building material, contrasted with precious and permanent materials in Paul's foundation metaphor.

### 5. Instrument of Restraint

The stocks (τὸ ξύλον) as a wooden restraining device — the punishment/binding sense reflected in Louw-Nida domain 6.004/6.006.

### 6. Green/Dry Tree (Proverbial)
- **Luk 23:31** — "if they do these things in a green tree (xylon), what shall be done in the dry?"

A proverbial use on the road to Calvary. The green tree (living/moist wood) versus dry tree (dead/combustible) creates a parabolic contrast.

### 7. Merchandise / Trade Goods
