- **Hapax Legomenon:** No

## Lexicon Definition

A primitive root; properly, to touch, i.e., lay the hand upon (for any purpose; euphemistically, to lie with a woman); by implication, to reach (figuratively, to arrive, acquire); violently, to strike (punish, defeat, destroy, etc.). KJV: beat, (be able to) bring (down), cast, come (nigh), draw near (nigh), get up, happen, join, near, plague, reach (up), smite, strike, touch.

The root operates across three overlapping senses:
1. **Physical contact** — to touch, lay a hand upon (neutral contact, often ritually charged)
2. **Spatial/temporal arrival** — to reach, extend to, arrive at (extension of contact through space or time)
3. **Violent impact** — to strike, smite, plague (divine or forceful contact)

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Ritual Contact — Contact with Impurity or Holiness
The Levitical heartland of the word. Contact transmits status — defilement or consecration.
- **Lev 5:2–3** — "if a soul touch (tiggaʿ) any unclean thing… he also shall be unclean"
- **Lev 11:24, 26–27, 31, 36, 39** — "whosoever toucheth the carcase shall be unclean"
- **Exod 29:37** — altar "whatsoever toucheth (nōgēaʿ) the altar shall be holy"
- **Num 19:11** — "he that toucheth the dead body of any man shall be unclean seven days"
- **Hag 2:12–13** — consecrated flesh vs. corpse-uncleanness; impurity transmits but holiness does not

### 2. Prohibited Contact — Touch That Would Violate Boundary
- **Gen 3:3** — "neither shall ye touch it, lest ye die" (Eden prohibition)
- **Exod 19:12–13** — mountain: "whosoever toucheth the mount shall be surely put to death"
- **1 Chr 16:22 / Ps 105:15** — "Touch not mine anointed, and do my prophets no harm"
- **Zech 2:8** — "he that toucheth you toucheth the apple of his eye"

### 3. Divine or Angelic Empowering Touch — Prophet/Seer Commissioning
Touch conveys authorization, strength, or cleansing from heaven.
- **Isa 6:7** — the seraph touches Isaiah's lips with the coal: "Lo, this hath touched thy lips; and thine iniquity is taken away"
- **Jer 1:9** — "the LORD put forth his hand, and touched my mouth… Behold, I have put my words in thy mouth"
- **1 Kgs 19:5, 7** — angel touches Elijah twice: "an angel touched him, and said, Arise [and] eat"
- **Dan 8:18** — "he touched me (way-yiggaʿ-bî), and set me upright" (Gabriel strengthens Daniel)
- **Dan 9:21** — Gabriel "being caused to fly swiftly, touched me about the time of the evening oblation"
- **Dan 10:10** — "behold, an hand touched me, which set me upon my knees"
- **Dan 10:16** — "one like the similitude of the sons of men touched my lips" (cf. Isa 6:7)
- **Dan 10:18** — "there came again and touched me one like the appearance of a man, and he strengthened me"

### 4. Violent Contact — Striking, Smiting, Plaguing
Often divine in agent; the transition from "touch" to "strike" is a matter of force.
- **Gen 12:17** — "the LORD plagued (yᵉnaggaʿ, Piel) Pharaoh and his house with great plagues"
- **Gen 32:26, 33** — "he touched the hollow of his thigh" (Jacob wrestling; the touch dislocates)
- **Job 1:11, 19; 2:5** — Satan: "put forth thine hand… and touch all that he hath"
- **2 Kgs 15:5** — "the LORD smote (yᵉnaggaʿ, Piel) the king, so that he was a leper"
- **Isa 53:4, 8** — "stricken (nāgûaʿ), smitten of God" / "for the transgression of my people was he stricken (nega)"
- **Ps 73:5, 14** — "neither are they plagued (yᵉnuggāʿû, Pual) like other men"

### 5. Spatial Extension — Reach, Extend, Arrive At (spatial)
Hiphil-heavy category. Object reaches a place, boundary, or condition.
- **Gen 28:12** — Jacob's ladder, its top "reached (maggîaʿ) to heaven"
- **1 Kgs 6:27** — cherubim wings "touched (nōgaʿat) one another in the midst of the house"
- **Isa 8:8** — overflowing waters "shall reach (yaggîaʿ) [even] to the neck"
- **Zech 14:5** — "the valley of the mountains shall reach (yaggîaʿ) unto Azal"
- **Dan 8:5** — he-goat "touched not the ground" (Qal negated)
- **Dan 8:7** — he-goat "came close unto the ram" (Qal)

### 6. Temporal Arrival — The Time / Turn "Came"
Hiphil idiom; common in post-exilic books.
- **Esth 2:12, 15; 4:14; 6:14; 9:1, 26** — "when the turn of Esther… was come (ba-haggîaʿ)"
- **Ezra 3:1; Neh 7:73** — "when the seventh month was come"
- **Ezek 7:12** — "the time is come (higgîaʿ), the day draweth near"
- **Song 2:12; Eccl 8:14; 12:1** — temporal arrivals
- **Dan 12:12** — "Blessed is he that waiteth, and cometh (yaggîaʿ) to the 1335 days"

### 7. Intimate / Sexual Euphemism
Rare but lexicalized in BDB.
- **Gen 20:6** — "I suffered thee not to touch (nᵉgōaʿ) her" (Abimelech and Sarah)
- **Prov 6:29** — "whoso toucheth (nōgēaʿ) her shall not be innocent" (neighbor's wife)
- **Ruth 2:9** — "charged the young men that they should not touch (nāgaʿ) thee"

The LXX bridge is exceptionally strong: **ἅπτομαι (G680)** renders *nāgaʿ* in the overwhelming majority of passages, including the Gospel accounts where Jesus' ministry consciously echoes the OT touch-typology (Matt 8:3 leper; 9:20 hem; 9:29 eyes; 17:7 Transfiguration — "Jesus came and touched them, and said, Arise, and be not afraid" — an unmistakable verbal echo of Dan 8:18; 10:10; 1 Kgs 19:5, 7).

For the temporal "reach / arrive at" sense, **φθάνω (G5348)** takes over (cf. Matt 12:28 "the kingdom of God is come upon you").
