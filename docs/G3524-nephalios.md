## Lexicon Definition

From G3525 (nēphō, "to be sober"); sober, i.e. (figuratively) circumspect. Literally, abstaining from wine; by extension, clear-headed, self-possessed, watchful, free from intoxicating influences (whether literal or metaphorical). KJV renders "sober" and "vigilant."

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Qualification for the Overseer (Bishop)
- **1 Tim 3:2** — "A bishop then must be blameless, the husband of one wife, vigilant (nēphalios), sober, of good behaviour, given to hospitality, apt to teach"

Here nēphalios stands at the head of a list of positive character traits required of the episkopos. Paired with σώφρων (sōphrōn, "sober" / sound-minded) and κόσμιος (kosmios, "orderly").

### 2. Qualification for Deacons' Wives (or Women Servants)
- **1 Tim 3:11** — "Even so must their wives be grave, not slanderers, sober (nēphalios), faithful in all things"

Paired with σεμνός (semnos, "grave") and πιστός (pistos, "faithful"). The quality is linked with trustworthiness and self-restraint, set against the vice of slander (διάβολος).

### 3. Qualification for Aged Men
- **Titus 2:2** — "That the aged men (presbytes) be sober (nēphalios), grave, temperate, sound in faith, in charity, in patience"

Applied to elder men in the congregation, grouped with σεμνός (grave), σώφρων (temperate/sound-minded), and three theological virtues (faith, love, patience/endurance). The ordering suggests nēphalios functions as a foundational disposition enabling the other virtues.

**Distinction:** σώφρων-group = sound-mindedness, discretion, prudence; nēphalios = unintoxicated, clear-headed, vigilant. The English "sober" merges these; Greek keeps them distinct. nēphalios emphasizes the absence of clouding influence; sōphrōn emphasizes the positive presence of prudent judgment.
