# Biblical Word Studies

A comprehensive library of original-language word studies covering Hebrew, Aramaic, and Greek vocabulary used across the biblical text. Each study is tool-driven, built from Strong's concordance data, morphological analysis, and distribution statistics -- not from commentaries or theological dictionaries.

---

## What's Here

This library contains **430+ word studies** organized into five categories:

### Hebrew (96 entries)

Old Testament word studies keyed to Strong's numbers. Each entry includes lexicon definitions, verb stem distributions, translation statistics, semantic categories with verse examples, and theological significance.

*Examples: [kaphar](H3722-kaphar.md) (atonement), [towrah](H8451-towrah.md) (law/instruction), [ruach](H7307-ruach.md) (spirit/wind/breath)*

### Aramaic (12 entries)

Word studies for the Aramaic portions of Daniel and Ezra. Same methodology as Hebrew entries, with attention to Aramaic-specific grammar and Daniel's apocalyptic vocabulary.

*Examples: [iddan](A5732-iddan.md) (times), [malkuw](A4437-malkuw.md) (kingdom), [shna](A8133-shna.md) (change)*

### Greek (134 entries)

New Testament word studies keyed to Strong's numbers. Includes morphological analysis, translation distributions, semantic domains, and connections to LXX usage that bridge Hebrew concepts into the NT.

*Examples: [nomos](G3551-nomos.md) (law), [pneuma](G4151-pneuma.md) (spirit), [diatheke](G1242-diatheke.md) (covenant)*

### Cross-Testament Traces (88 entries)

These trace a Hebrew word through the Septuagint (LXX) into its Greek NT equivalent, showing how meaning evolves and what theological freight each translation choice carries. Each trace includes PMI co-occurrence scores from the LXX.

*Examples: [owlam to aion](TR-owlam-aion.md) (eternity), [towrah to nomos](TR-towrah-nomos.md) (law), [beriyth to diatheke](TR-beriyth-diatheke.md) (covenant)*

### Word Groups (98 entries)

Thematic studies that gather all related words around a single concept -- across both testaments, all three languages. Shows how terms relate, overlap, and distinguish within a semantic domain.

*Examples: [Atonement](WG-atonement.md), [Law](WG-law.md), [Sabbath](WG-sabbath.md), [Judgment](WG-judgment.md)*

## How to Use This Library

**Starting from a concept:** Browse the Word Groups tab. Find your topic, and the word group will point you to every relevant individual word study and cross-testament trace.

**Starting from a specific word:** Use the Hebrew, Aramaic, or Greek tabs. Find the Strong's number. Each study links to related words, traces, and word groups.

**Tracing a word across testaments:** Use the Cross-Testament Traces tab. Each trace shows the Hebrew-to-Greek pathway through the LXX with statistical co-occurrence data.

---

## Methodology

Every word study follows the same approach:

1. **Strong's concordance lookup** -- base definition, occurrences, part of speech
2. **Morphological analysis** -- verb stems, noun patterns, grammatical forms
3. **Translation distribution** -- how the KJV renders each word, with frequency counts
4. **Distribution by book** -- where the word clusters in the biblical text
5. **Semantic categories** -- grouped usage patterns with representative verses
6. **Cross-references** -- links to related words, traces, and word groups

No commentaries. No systematic theologies. Just the lexical data, organized for study.
