## Lexicon Definition

From G1303 (διατίθεμαι); properly, a disposition, i.e. (especially) a contract (especially a devisory will): — covenant, testament.

The word carries two distinct legal senses in the Greek world: (1) a bilateral *covenant* or compact between parties, and (2) a unilateral *will/testament* (legally valid only at the testator's death). The NT uses both senses — the covenant sense dominates, but Heb 9:16–17 explicitly invokes the testamentary sense to argue that Christ's death ratifies the new covenant.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. The New Covenant in Christ's Blood
The eucharistic formula links the cup directly to a "new covenant" in Christ's blood.
- **Matt 26:28** — "For this is my blood of the new testament, which is shed for many for the remission of sins"
- **Mark 14:24** — "This is my blood of the new testament, which is shed for many"
- **Luke 22:20** — "This cup is the new testament in my blood, which is shed for you"
- **1 Cor 11:25** — "This cup is the new testament in my blood: this do ye, as oft as ye drink it"

### 2. Covenant with the Patriarchs and Israel
NT writers recall the OT covenant made with Abraham and the fathers as the foundation of Israel's identity.
- **Luke 1:72** — "to remember his holy covenant" (Zechariah's Benedictus, recalling Abrahamic covenant)
- **Acts 3:25** — "the covenant which God made with our fathers, saying unto Abraham"
- **Acts 7:8** — "he gave him the covenant of circumcision; and so Abraham begat Isaac"
- **Rom 9:4** — "to whom pertaineth the adoption… and the covenants, and the giving of the law"
- **Eph 2:12** — "strangers from the covenants of promise" (Gentiles outside Israel's covenant heritage)

### 3. The New Covenant Superior to the Old
Hebrews and 2 Corinthians contrast the new covenant (Christ/Spirit) with the old (Sinai/letter).
- **2 Cor 3:6** — "made us able ministers of the new testament; not of the letter, but of the spirit"
- **Heb 8:6** — "he is the mediator of a better covenant, established upon better promises"
- **Heb 8:8–10** — Jeremiah 31 quoted: "I will make a new covenant… not according to the covenant that I made with their fathers"
- **Heb 12:24** — "Jesus the mediator of the new covenant, and to the blood of sprinkling"

### 4. The Covenant as Legal Testament (Will)
Hebrews uniquely uses diathēkē in its Greek legal sense — a will that takes effect only at death.
- **Heb 9:16** — "where a testament is, there must also of necessity be the death of the testator"
- **Heb 9:17** — "a testament is of force after men are dead: otherwise it is of no strength at all while the testator liveth"

### 5. The Ark of the Covenant
The physical ark containing covenant documents appears in the heavenly sanctuary.
- **Heb 9:4** — "the ark of the covenant overlaid round about with gold, wherein was the golden pot that had manna"
- **Rev 11:19** — "there was seen in his temple the ark of his testament"

### 6. Covenant Confirmed to Abraham in Galatians
Paul argues the Abrahamic diathēkē is an irrevocable arrangement the Mosaic law cannot annul.
- **Gal 3:15** — "Though it be but a man's covenant, yet if it be confirmed, no man disannulleth"
- **Gal 3:17** — "the covenant that was confirmed before of God in Christ, the law… cannot disannul"
- **Gal 4:24** — "these are the two covenants; the one from mount Sinai, which gendereth to bondage"
