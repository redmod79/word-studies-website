## Lexicon Definition

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Physical Wound / Weal from a Blow
The core sense: the visible mark — bruise, welt, or stripe — left on flesh by a blow or beating.
- **Exo 21:25** — "Burning for burning, wound for wound, stripe (chabbuwrah) for stripe (chabbuwrah)." Lex talionis: the injury inflicted must match the injury suffered.
- **Pro 20:30** — "The blueness (chabbuwrah) of a wound cleanseth away evil: so do stripes the inward parts of the belly." Disciplinary bruising as moral purification.
- **Psa 38:5** — "My wounds (chabbuwrah) stink and are corrupt because of my foolishness." Physical wounds as consequence of sin.

### 2. Wound in Contexts of Violence or Injury
The word used for bodily harm inflicted by or upon a person.
- **Gen 4:23** — "I have slain a man to my wounding, and a young man to my hurt (chabbuwrah)." Lamech's boast of disproportionate violence.
- **Isa 1:6** — "Wounds, and bruises (chabbuwrah), and putrifying sores: they have not been closed, neither bound up, neither mollified with ointment." Israel's spiritual condition described as untreated bodily wounds.

### 3. Vicarious / Substitutionary Wounding
- **Isa 53:5** — "But he was wounded for our transgressions, he was bruised for our iniquities: the chastisement of our peace was upon him; and with his stripes (chabbuwrah) we are healed." The Servant's wounds as the means of healing for others. This is the passage quoted in 1 Pet 2:24 via LXX μώλωψ (molops).
