# H398 — אָכַל (ʼâkal)

## Core Data
- **Strong's:** H398
- **Original:** אָכַל
- **Transliteration:** ʼâkal
- **Pronunciation:** aw-kal'
- **Part of Speech:** Verb (primitive root)
- **Root:** Primitive root (nominal cognates: H400 אֹכֶל `ʼôkel` "food"; H402 אׇכְלָה `ʼoklâh` "food"; H399 אֲכַל `ʼăkal` Aramaic)
- **Occurrences:** 703 unique verses (947 tokens, 915 parsed forms) across 38 books
- **First Occurrence:** Gen 2:16 — "Of every tree of the garden thou mayest freely eat (אָכֹל תֹּאכֵל)"
- **Hapax Legomenon:** No — one of the most common verbs in the Hebrew Bible

## Lexicon Definition

A primitive root; to eat (literally or figuratively):— at all, burn up, consume, devour(-er, up),
dine, eat(-er, up), feed (with), food, freely, in...wise(-deed, plenty), (lay) meat, quite.

**Weqatal imperatival force (key pattern for covenant-feast commands):**
In Deuteronomy's centralization code, H398 repeatedly appears as a Qal Perfect 2ms/2mp prefixed
with waw-conjunction (weqatal), functioning as a **commanded future** that continues a preceding
imperative or imperfect-of-command. The parser labels these as "Perf" but the syntactic context
converts them to imperatival force — "you shall eat," "you will eat" as covenant obligation:

- **Deut 12:7** — `וַאֲכַלְתֶּם שָׁם` — "and there ye shall eat before the LORD your God" (weqatal continuing 12:5–6 imperatives)
- **Deut 12:18** — `תֹּאכְלֶנּוּ` (Impf 2ms) — "thou shalt eat it before the LORD"
- **Deut 14:23** — `וְאָכַלְתָּ לִפְנֵי יְהוָה` — "and thou shalt eat before the LORD thy God"
- **Deut 14:26** — `וְאָכַלְתָּ שָּׁם לִפְנֵי יְהוָה` — "and thou shalt eat there before the LORD thy God, and thou shalt rejoice"
- **Deut 27:7** — `וְאָכַלְתָּ שָׁם וְשָׂמַחְתָּ לִפְנֵי יְהוָה` — "and thou shalt eat there, and rejoice before the LORD"

These weqatal forms sit in chained strings with other commanded acts — slaughter, bring, rejoice,
bless — all functioning as covenant cultic imperatives. H398 is thus a principal verb of **commanded
covenant-feast participation**, not merely a descriptive future.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Ordinary Eating — Physical Nourishment
The baseline sense: consuming food for sustenance, often in mundane narrative settings.

- **Gen 3:6** — "she took of the fruit thereof, and did eat (וַתֹּאכַל)"
- **Gen 27:25** — "And he said, Bring it near to me, and I will eat (וְאֹכְלָה) of my son's venison"
- **1Sa 1:7** — "therefore she wept, and did not eat (וְלֹא תֹאכַל)"
- **Dan 1:12** — "let them give us pulse to eat (וְנֹאכְלָה), and water to drink"

### 2. Commanded Covenant-Feast Eating — Cultic Participation
H398 is the principal verb of the sanctuary meal: eating *before YHWH* as an act of covenant
participation. Dominantly Deuteronomic; strongly weqatal/imperfect of command (see Verb Stem
Distribution above).

- **Deut 12:7** — "And there ye shall eat (וַאֲכַלְתֶּם) before the LORD your God, and ye shall rejoice"
- **Deut 12:18** — "thou must eat (תֹּאכְלֶנּוּ) them before the LORD thy God in the place which the LORD thy God shall choose"
- **Deut 14:23** — "And thou shalt eat (וְאָכַלְתָּ) before the LORD thy God ... the tithe of thy corn, of thy wine, and of thine oil"
- **Deut 14:26** — "and thou shalt eat (וְאָכַלְתָּ) there before the LORD thy God, and thou shalt rejoice, thou, and thine household"
- **Deut 27:7** — "and thou shalt eat (וְאָכַלְתָּ) there, and rejoice before the LORD thy God"
- **Exo 12:8** — "And they shall eat (וְאָכְלוּ) the flesh in that night, roast with fire" (Passover)
- **Lev 6:16** — "the remainder thereof shall Aaron and his sons eat (יֹאכֵלוּ)... in the holy place"
- **Lev 10:12–13** — priestly eating of the meat-offering as "most holy"

### 3. Food-Law Eating — Clean and Unclean
Dense in Leviticus 11 and Deuteronomy 14; typically Qal Imperfect with לֹא ("ye shall not eat") or
Niphal ("shall not be eaten").

- **Lev 3:17** — "that ye eat (תֹאכֵלוּ) neither fat nor blood" (perpetual statute)
- **Lev 11:2** — "These are the beasts which ye shall eat (תֹּאכֵלוּ)"
- **Lev 11:4** — "ye shall not eat (לֹא תֹאכְלוּ) of them that chew the cud"
- **Deut 14:3** — "Thou shalt not eat (לֹא תֹאכַל) any abominable thing"
- **Gen 2:17** — "thou shalt not eat (לֹא תֹאכַל) of it: for in the day that thou eatest thereof thou shalt surely die"

### 4. Fire Devouring — Judgment Imagery
Fire as agent; extremely frequent in prophetic oracles. Often paired with H784 `ʼêsh` (fire).

- **Deut 4:24** — "For the LORD thy God is a consuming fire (אֵשׁ אֹכְלָה)"
- **Lev 10:2** — "And there went out fire from the LORD, and devoured (וַתֹּאכַל) them"
- **2Ki 1:10** — "let fire come down from heaven, and consume (וְתֹאכַל) thee and thy fifty"
- **Amos 1:4, 7, 10, 12, 14; 2:2, 5** — "I will send a fire ... which shall devour (וְאָכְלָה) the palaces"
- **Joel 1:19** — "the fire hath devoured (אָכְלָה) the pastures of the wilderness"
- **Zep 1:18; 3:8** — "all the earth shall be devoured with the fire of his jealousy"

### 5. Sword / Enemy Devouring — Military Destruction
The sword, enemies, locusts, beasts personified as "eating" Israel or the nations.

- **2Sa 2:26** — "Shall the sword devour (הֲלָנֶצַח תֹּאכַל חֶרֶב) for ever?"
- **Isa 1:20** — "ye shall be devoured (תֵּאָכְלוּ) with the sword"
- **Jer 2:30** — "your own sword hath devoured (אָכְלָה) your prophets"
- **Joel 1:4** — "that which the palmerworm hath left hath the locust eaten (אָכַל)"
- **Hos 7:7** — "they are all hot as an oven, and have devoured (וְאָכְלוּ) their judges"

### 6. Figurative / Metabolic — Eating Words, Sin, Fruit of Deeds
The object of eating is abstract: sin, words, fruit of one's way, violence.

- **Ezk 2:8** — "eat (אֱכוֹל) that I give thee"
- **Ezk 3:1–3** — "eat this roll, and go speak unto the house of Israel ... and I did eat it; and it was in my mouth as honey for sweetness"
- **Hos 4:8** — "They eat up (יֹאכֵלוּ) the sin of my people"
- **Prov 1:31** — "shall they eat (וְיֹאכְלוּ) of the fruit of their own way"
- **Prov 13:2** — "A man shall eat good (יֹאכַל טוֹב) by the fruit of his mouth"
- **Isa 3:10** — "they shall eat the fruit of their doings"

### 7. Idiomatic Emphasis — Infinitive Absolute Doubling
The Qal InfAbs paired with a finite form yields emphatic "surely eat" / "freely eat."

- **Gen 2:16** — "thou mayest freely eat" (אָכֹל תֹּאכֵל — literally "eating, thou shalt eat")
- **Gen 2:17** — "thou shalt surely die" pattern inverted on 3:3 "lest ye die" — but the H398 form in 2:16 is the paradigm infinitive absolute of permission
- **Gen 31:15** — "quite devoured (אָכוֹל) also our money"
