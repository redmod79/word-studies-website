## Lexicon Definition

From H2398; an offence (sometimes habitual sinfulness), and its penalty, occasion,
sacrifice, or expiation; also (concretely) an offender:—punishment (of sin),
purifying(-fication for sin), sin(-ner, offering).

*Note: The word carries a dual semantic weight — both the act of sinning and the
sacrificial remedy that addresses it. Context distinguishes which sense is in view.*

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Sin as Personal Moral Offense
The earliest and most basic sense: a specific act of wrongdoing that places the
offender in legal/moral jeopardy.

- **Gen 4:7** — "if thou doest not well, sin lieth at the door" (sin personified, crouching like a predator)
- **Gen 18:20** — "the cry of Sodom... because their sin is very grievous"
- **Exo 32:30** — "Moses said unto the people, Ye have sinned a great sin; and now I will go up unto the LORD"
- **2 Sam 12:13** — "I have sinned against the LORD. And Nathan said... The LORD also hath put away thy sin"

### 2. Sin Offering (Ritual Sacrifice)
The technical cultic term for the offering prescribed to make atonement for sin.
Chattaah here names both the category of sacrifice and specific ritual animals.

- **Exo 29:36** — "thou shalt offer every day a bullock for a sin offering for atonement"
- **Lev 4:3** — "If the priest that is anointed do sin... then let him bring for his sin... a young bullock without blemish for a sin offering"
- **Lev 4:29** — "he shall lay his hand upon the head of the sin offering, and slay the sin offering in the place of the burnt offering"
- **Lev 16:9,15** — "Aaron shall bring the goat... and offer him for a sin offering... he shall kill the goat of the sin offering that is for the people"

### 3. Corporate / National Sin
In the prophetic and historical books, chattaah names the accumulated moral debt
of Israel and Judah — the spiritual cause behind national catastrophe and exile.

- **Hos 10:8** — "the high places also of Aven, the sin of Israel, shall be destroyed"
- **Mic 1:5** — "For the transgression of Jacob is all this, and for the sins of the house of Israel"
- **1 Ki 16:2** — kings are condemned for making Israel sin (the pattern formula throughout Kings)
- **Eze 18:20** — the soul that sinneth carries its own chattaah

### 4. Sin, Punishment, and Exile
The legal consequence attached to unaddressed sin — sometimes the word itself
carries the sense of penalty or recompense.

- **Lev 26:18,21,24,28** — "I will punish you seven times more for your sins" (repeated four-fold in covenant curse section)
- **Lam 3:39** — man punished for his sins
- **Zec 14:19** — "this shall be the punishment of Egypt... of all nations that come not up"
- **Gen 50:17** — "Forgive... the trespass of thy brethren, and their sin"

### 5. Confession, Forgiveness, and Cleansing
Chattaah appears frequently in petitions for pardon and in divine declarations of
forgiveness — showing the word belongs not only to the problem but to its resolution.

- **Exo 34:7** — God's self-disclosure: "forgiving iniquity and transgression and sin" (triad: avon + pesha + chattaah)
- **Exo 34:9** — Moses: "pardon our iniquity and our sin, and take us for thine inheritance"
- **Exo 32:32** — Moses: "if thou wilt forgive their sin—; and if not, blot me out of thy book"
- **Ps 51:2** — "Wash me throughly from mine iniquity, and cleanse me from my sin"
