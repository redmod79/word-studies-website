## Lexicon Definition

A primitive root; to loosen, i.e. relinquish, permit, etc.: — commit self, fail, forsake, fortify, help, leave (destitute, off), refuse, surely.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Divine Abandonment / God Forsaking Israel
God's act of withdrawing his protective presence as judgment for covenant unfaithfulness.
- **Deut 31:17** — "I will forsake them, and I will hide my face from them"
- **Isa 54:7** — "For a small moment have I forsaken thee; but with great mercies will I gather thee"
- **Jer 12:7** — "I have forsaken mine house, I have left mine heritage"
- **2 Chr 24:20** — "Because ye have forsaken the LORD, he hath also forsaken you"

### 2. Human Forsaking of God / Covenant
Israel's act of abandoning YHWH for other gods — the dominant prophetic accusation.
- **Judg 2:12–13** — "They forsook the LORD God of their fathers... and served Baal and Ashtaroth"
- **Jer 2:13** — "My people have committed two evils; they have forsaken me the fountain of living waters"
- **1 Ki 19:10** — "The children of Israel have forsaken thy covenant, thrown down thine altars"
- **2 Chr 7:22** — "Because they forsook the LORD God of their fathers"

### 3. Cry of Dereliction / Lament of the Forsaken
Personal pleas — the psalmist (or sufferer) experiences or fears divine abandonment.
- **Psa 22:1** — "My God, my God, why hast thou forsaken me?"
- **Psa 27:9** — "Leave me not, neither forsake me, O God of my salvation"
- **Psa 38:21** — "Forsake me not, O LORD: O my God, be not far from me"
- **Psa 71:9** — "Cast me not off in the time of old age; forsake me not when my strength faileth"
- **Isa 49:14** — "But Zion said, The LORD hath forsaken me, and my Lord hath forgotten me"

### 4. Divine Promise Not to Forsake
Assurance of YHWH's continued presence — the theological counterweight.

The LXX strongly prefers ἐγκαταλείπω (G1459) — a compound of ἐν + κατά + λείπω literally "leave down in (a place)" — for the covenantal/relational sense of azab. This word carries the specific overtone of abandonment-in-distress, not merely physical departure. It is the verb quoted by Jesus on the cross (Matt 27:46 / Mark 15:34 ← Psa 22:1 LXX).
