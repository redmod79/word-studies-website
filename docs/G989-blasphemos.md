## Lexicon Definition

Scurrilous, i.e. calumnious (against man), or (specially) impious (against God). From a derivative of βλάπτω and φήμη. Used of speech or persons characterized by injurious, slanderous, or irreverent language.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Blasphemous Speech Against God / Sacred Things
- **Acts 6:11** — "We have heard him speak blasphemous words against Moses, and against God."
- **Acts 6:13** — "This man ceaseth not to speak blasphemous words against this holy place, and the law."

Both occurrences modify ῥήματα (rhemata, "words") — blasphemos describes the character of speech directed against God, Moses, the temple, or the law. The charges are presented through false witnesses, but the adjective itself characterizes speech as irreverent toward the sacred.

### 2. A Blasphemous Person (Substantive Use)
- **1 Tim 1:13** — "Who was before a blasphemer, and a persecutor, and injurious: but I obtained mercy, because I did it ignorantly in unbelief."
- **2 Tim 3:2** — "For men shall be lovers of their own selves, covetous, boasters, proud, blasphemers, disobedient to parents, unthankful, unholy."

Used substantively (without a modified noun) to describe a person characterized by blasphemous behavior. In 1 Tim 1:13, Paul applies it to his pre-conversion self. In 2 Tim 3:2, it appears in a vice list describing people of the "last days."

### 3. Railing / Slanderous Accusation (Against Spiritual Beings)
- **2 Pet 2:11** — "Whereas angels, which are greater in power and might, bring not railing accusation against them before the Lord."

Here blasphemos is translated "railing" and describes an accusation (krisis) — the word characterizes speech that is reviling or slanderous, directed against spiritual authorities (cf. parallel in Jude 1:9 using G988).
