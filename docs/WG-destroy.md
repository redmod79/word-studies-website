# WG: Destroy / Exterminate (Hebrew)

## Overview

Hebrew uses a cluster of verbs to express destruction, removal, and annihilation. Each verb occupies a distinct semantic niche: total extermination (shâmad), ruin/corruption (shâchath), sacred-ban destruction (châram), vanishing/perishing (ʼâbad), cutting off (kârath), and dispossessing (yârash). They converge especially in the Deuteronomic conquest/curse formulas, where several of them pile up in single verses to intensify the total-removal force. In LXX translation, most of them consolidate into Greek ἀπόλλυμι (G622) and ἐξαίρω (G1808).

**What LXX reveals:** The Hebrew group spreads destruction across six distinct verbs with different nuances; Greek collapses most of these into apóllymi, losing the lexical distinctions that Hebrew preserved. This is why NT apollymi must carry the full weight that Hebrew distributed across shâmad (extermination), ʼâbad (perishing), kârath (cutting off), and shâchath (corrupting).

## Roles in the Concept

- **Extermination (total):** shâmad — the *completion* of destruction; no survivors
- **Ruin/Corruption:** shâchath — the *process* of destruction; includes moral decay
- **Sacred Ban:** châram — destruction *as religious devotion* (ḥerem)
- **Vanishing:** ʼâbad — destruction *from the victim's side* (to perish, be lost)
- **Severance:** kârath — destruction *as cutting off* (covenantal excision, family extinction)
- **Dispossession:** yârash — destruction *paired with replacement* (driven out so another takes over)
