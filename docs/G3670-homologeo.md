- **Hapax Legomenon:** No

## Lexicon Definition

From ὁμόλογος (homologos, "agreeing"), compound of ὁμός ("same") and λέγω ("to speak"). From Herodotus onward:

1. **To say the same thing as another** — to agree with, assent (with dative of person)
2. **To concede** — (a) not to refuse, i.e. to promise (Acts 7:17; Matt 14:7); (b) not to deny, i.e. to confess, declare — joined with οὐκ ἀρνεῖσθαι (John 1:20); to admit or declare oneself guilty of what one is accused of: τὰς ἁμαρτίας (1 John 1:9; cf. Sir 4:26)
3. **To profess** — to declare openly, speak out freely; one is said to ὁμολογεῖν that of which he is convinced and which he holds to be true (distinguished from πιστεύειν in John 12:42; Rom 10:9–10)
4. **To praise, celebrate** — a usage unknown to secular Greek writers (Heb 13:15); cf. ἐξομολογέω

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Confession of Sin
- **1 John 1:9** — "If we confess our sins, he is faithful and just to forgive us our sins, and to cleanse us from all unrighteousness."
- **Acts 24:14** — "But this I confess unto thee, that after the way which they call heresy, so worship I the God of my fathers."

The only explicit sin-confession usage in the NT is 1 John 1:9. The verb here is present subjunctive (ὁμολογῶμεν), implying ongoing practice rather than a one-time act. The direct object is τὰς ἁμαρτίας ("the sins"), making confession specific, not vague.

### 2. Christological Confession — Incarnation
- **1 John 4:2** — "Every spirit that confesseth that Jesus Christ is come in the flesh is of God."
- **1 John 4:3** — "And every spirit that confesseth not that Jesus Christ is come in the flesh is not of God."
- **2 John 1:7** — "For many deceivers are entered into the world, who confess not that Jesus Christ is come in the flesh."

Present tense in all three — the confession of the incarnation is a standing, continuous reality, not a past-tense recital. Negated homologeō is the diagnostic for the spirit of antichrist.

### 3. Christological Confession — Lordship / Identity
- **Rom 10:9** — "That if thou shalt confess with thy mouth the Lord Jesus, and shalt believe in thine heart..."
- **Rom 10:10** — "For with the heart man believeth unto righteousness; and with the mouth confession is made unto salvation."
- **John 9:22** — "If any man did confess that he was Christ, he should be put out of the synagogue."
- **1 John 4:15** — "Whosoever shall confess that Jesus is the Son of God, God dwelleth in him, and he in God."
- **1 John 2:23** — "He that acknowledgeth the Son hath the Father also."

The Romans passage pairs homologeō with pisteuo: believing is internal (heart), confessing is external (mouth). The aorist subjunctive in Rom 10:9 marks a decisive act.

### 4. Public Profession of Allegiance
- **Matt 10:32** — "Whosoever therefore shall confess me before men, him will I confess also before my Father which is in heaven."
- **Luke 12:8** — "Whosoever shall confess me before men, him shall the Son of man also confess before the angels of God."
- **Rev 3:5** — "I will confess his name before my Father, and before his angels."
- **1 Tim 6:12** — "...hast professed a good profession before many witnesses."

The ἔμπροσθεν ("before") construction is distinctive — confession happens before a witnessing audience. Christ reciprocates: confess him before men, he confesses the confessor before the Father.

### 5. Assent / Promise / Agreement
- **Matt 14:7** — "Whereupon he promised with an oath to give her whatsoever she would ask."
- **Acts 7:17** — "[The time of the promise] which God had sworn to Abraham."
- **John 1:20** — "And he confessed, and denied not; but confessed, I am not the Christ."
- **Acts 23:8** — "The Pharisees confess both."

These uses preserve the original Greek sense of "agree" or "assent." John 1:20's doubling (ὡμολόγησεν καὶ οὐκ ἠρνήσατο) pairs homologeō with its antonym arneōmai for emphasis.

### 6. Praise / Thanksgiving (Hebraistic)
- **Heb 13:15** — "Let us offer the sacrifice of praise to God continually, that is, the fruit of our lips giving thanks to his name."
- **Heb 11:13** — "These all died in faith... and confessed that they were strangers and pilgrims on the earth."

The Heb 13:15 usage follows the LXX pattern where ὁμολογέω renders Hebrew yadah (to praise/give thanks). This connects the word to the OT thank-offering tradition.

### 7. False / Empty Profession
- **Titus 1:16** — "They profess that they know God; but in works they deny him."
- **John 12:42** — "Many believed on him; but because of the Pharisees they did not confess him."

These passages show the negative space: profession without corresponding works (Titus), and suppressed confession out of fear (John). The Titus passage pairs profess (ὁμολογοῦσιν) with deny (ἀρνοῦνται), marking the gap between words and conduct.
