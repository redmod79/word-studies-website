# WG: Slayer / Homicide / Blood-Guilt

## Overview

The OT legal-theological vocabulary of homicide is built from a small cluster of interlocking Hebrew words that distinguish the act of killing, the killer, the victim, the blood, the avenger, and the institution of refuge. Together these eight words carry the whole legal-ethical framework of Numbers 35, Deuteronomy 19, and Joshua 20, and they supply the lexical substrate for the Decalogue's Sixth Commandment.

## Roles in the Concept

- **The forbidden act (narrow):** ratsach (H7523) — unlawful killing of a fellow human; the Sixth-Commandment verb
- **The broader act:** harag (H2026) — killing in any register; warfare, judicial execution, narrative slaying
- **The blow:** nakah (H5221) — the physical strike that causes death
- **The outcome:** muwth (H4191) — dying, being put to death; grounds the death-penalty formula *môt yumat*
- **The victim:** chalal (H2491) — the slain or pierced one (battlefield and corpse contexts)
- **The evidence:** dam (H1818) — shed blood calls for justice (Gen 4:10); blood-guilt attaches to the killer
- **The avenger:** gaʼal (H1350) — the kinsman-redeemer also functions as *goʼel ha-dam*, avenger of blood
- **The institution:** miqlat (H4733) — city of refuge, asylum for the unintentional slayer
