## Lexicon Definition

From the base of G5088 (τίκτω, to bear); a child (as produced):—child, daughter, son.

Outline of Biblical Usage includes: a child, children (literal offspring); used of disciples or converts as spiritual children of teachers/apostles; used of those who share a nature or quality ("children of light," "children of promise," "children of the devil").

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Biological / Natural Children
Literal offspring — parent-to-child relationship in narrative or legal contexts.

- **Mat 7:11** — "know how to give good gifts unto your children, how much more shall your Father…"

- **Luke 14:26** — "hate not his father, and mother, and wife, and children, and brethren"

### 2. Children of God — Spiritual Birth and Identity
Believers described as born of God, sharing divine nature. Contrasted with "children of the flesh" (Rom 9:8).

- **John 1:12** — "to them gave he power to become the sons of God (τέκνα θεοῦ)"
- **Rom 8:16** — "the Spirit itself beareth witness…that we are the children of God"
- **1 John 3:1** — "that we should be called the sons of God…therefore the world knoweth us not"
- **1 John 3:10** — "the children of God are manifest, and the children of the devil"

### 3. Apostolic Affection — Spiritual Fatherhood
Paul and John address converts as their own "children" born through the gospel, expressing tender pastoral care.

- **1 Cor 4:14** — "as my beloved sons I warn you"
- **Phm 1:10** — "I beseech thee for my son Onesimus, whom I have begotten in my bonds"
- **2 Tim 1:2** — "To Timothy, my dearly beloved son"
- **3 John 1:4** — "I have no greater joy than to hear that my children walk in truth"

### 4. "Children of" — Characterizing by Nature or Allegiance

- **Mat 11:19** — "wisdom is justified of her children"

- **Eph 2:3** — "we…were by nature the children of wrath"

### 5. Household and Church Instruction
