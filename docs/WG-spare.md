# WG: Sparing, Withholding, Compassion

## Overview
A cluster of Hebrew verbs and a Greek counterpart that together map the biblical concept of *refraining from warranted harm* — sparing a life, withholding a blow, feeling tender-mercy that stays the hand, or committed-loyalty that refuses to abandon. The Hebrew words range from action-restraint (*châsak*, withhold) through pity-visual (*chûs*, eye-pity) to enacted-mercy (*châmal*, spare) to visceral-tenderness (*râcham*, womb-mercy) to covenant-loyalty (*chéseḏ*). Greek *pheídomai* collapses the first three into one lexeme; Greek *eleéō / éleos* picks up the last two.

## Relationships

### Shared Roots
No shared roots among the Hebrew members — this is a semantic cluster, not a morphological one. *Cheseḏ* is sometimes linked etymologically to a root meaning "to bow / be gracious," but is unrelated to the other members. *Râcham* derives from רֶחֶם (womb).

### Triple-Member Passages

| Verse | Members | Significance |
|-------|---------|--------------|
| **Jer 13:14** | châmal + chûs + râcham | "I will not pity (châmal), nor spare (chûs), nor have mercy (râcham), but destroy them" — the complete merciless-judgment triad |
| **Jer 21:7** | châmal + chûs + râcham | "he shall not spare (châmal) them, neither have pity (chûs), nor have mercy (râcham)" — same triad, same construction |

These two verses are the densest concentration of the cluster in Scripture, and they establish the stereotyped merciless-judgment formula. The negation of all three verbs together is used when YHWH declares that the full spectrum of mercy has been withdrawn.

**Greek conflation effect:** Hebrew distinguishes *châmal* (withhold destruction, enact sparing) from *chûs* (eye-pity, look with compassion) from *châsak* (hold back generally). LXX *pheídomai* absorbs all three. Hebrew distinguishes *râcham* (visceral tenderness) from *chéseḏ* (covenant loyalty). Greek *eleos / eleéō* blends them. NT readers working from Greek alone lose the fine-grained Hebrew psychology of mercy.

## Roles in the Concept

- **Act of withholding the blow (transitive):** H2550 *châmal* — the specific enactment against a judgment backdrop
- **Broader restraint of hand/word/rod:** H2820 *châsak* — general withholding, not limited to mercy
- **Visual-emotional response prompting restraint:** H2347 *chûs* — "the eye pities," the felt response
- **Visceral / maternal tenderness:** H7355 *râcham* — the womb-deep compassion
- **Covenant-loyal steadfastness:** H2617 *chéseḏ* — the faithful-kindness that refuses to abandon
- **Greek collapsing-point:** G5339 *pheídomai* (action-side), with *eleos* covering the mercy-side

## Key Passages Where Multiple Group Words Appear Together

- **Jer 13:14** — "I will not pity (châmal), nor spare (chûs), nor have mercy (râcham)"
- **Jer 21:7** — "he shall not spare (châmal) them, neither have pity (chûs), nor have mercy (râcham), nor have compassion"
- **Deut 13:8** — "neither shall thine eye pity (chûs) him, neither shalt thou spare (châmal), neither shalt thou conceal (châsak-equivalent)"
- **Isa 54:8** — "with everlasting kindness (chéseḏ) will I have mercy (râcham) on thee" — the positive-mercy counterpart pairing
- **Isa 13:18** — "they shall have no pity (chûs)... shall not spare (châmal was parallel in LXX)... their eye shall not spare (châmal elsewhere)" — the full cluster in a single oracle
