## Lexicon Definition

From Strong's: "of Hebrew origin [H7941]; an intoxicant, i.e. intensely fermented liquor." A loan-word taken directly into Greek from Hebrew shekar without translation — the LXX's standard technical rendering of shekar, inherited by the NT at Luke 1:15.

BDAG: "a strong alcoholic drink, 'strong drink' — a generic term for intoxicating beverages other than wine." Thayer: "strong drink; an intoxicating beverage, different from wine; made from various fruits, or of grain, honey, dates, etc." The Greek transliteration preserves the Hebrew semantic category rather than forcing the word into the oinos (grape-wine) lexicon.

## Semantic Categories

Because sikera occurs only once in the NT, semantic range is reconstructed from (1) the single NT usage, (2) the 13 LXX occurrences where sikera translates Hebrew shekar, and (3) the Louw-Nida beverage neighborhood.

### 1. Abstention Vow / Nazirite-Pattern Separation (NT usage)
- **Luke 1:15** — "he shall drink neither wine (οἶνον, oinos) nor strong drink (σίκερα, sikera); and he shall be filled with the Holy Ghost, even from his mother's womb."

Luke places the oinos + sikera pair in Gabriel's mouth, reproducing the exact Hebrew formula yayin + shekar used in Num 6:3 (Nazirite law) and Jdg 13:4, 7, 14 (Samson's pre-birth instruction) and 1 Sam 1:15 (Hannah's denial). The pairing deliberately frames John as a Samson-/Samuel-type figure consecrated from the womb.

### 2. LXX — Cultic Prohibition to Priests
- **Lev 10:9 LXX** — "Wine and strong drink (σίκερα) ye shall not drink" — the post-Nadab/Abihu restriction on officiating priests.

### 3. LXX — Nazirite Vow
- **Num 6:3 LXX** — "he shall separate himself from wine and strong drink (σίκερα)."
- **Jdg 13:4, 7, 14 LXX** — angelic instruction to Samson's mother.

### 4. LXX — Permitted Festal Use
- **Deut 14:26 LXX** — tithe-feast before the LORD, where sikera may be purchased and consumed in worship.

### 5. LXX — Prophetic Rebuke of Intoxication
- **Isa 28:7 LXX** — "priest and prophet have erred through sikera... they stumble in judgment."
- **Mic 2:11 LXX** — the false-prophet caricature: prophesying "of wine and strong drink."

**Primary bridge:** Sikera is not a Greek word in origin — it is a direct calque of Hebrew shekar. Whenever the LXX encounters shekar it either (a) transliterates as sikera (dedicated category preservation), (b) collapses the yayin+shekar pair into oinos (summary rendering), or (c) renders the state rather than the substance with methē. The NT's single use at Luke 1:15 preserves the dedicated transliteration, signaling that Luke wants the Hebrew Nazirite category intact — not a generic "wine" statement.

## Key Observations

- **Luke's single use is deliberately Septuagintal.** The exact phrase "οἶνον καὶ σίκερα οὐ μὴ πίῃ" in Luke 1:15 matches LXX formula from Num 6:3 and Jdg 13:4 almost verbatim. Luke is invoking the Nazirite legal category by vocabulary.
- **Sikera is never used of a non-intoxicant.** Across all ~13 LXX occurrences and the one NT occurrence, sikera always refers to a drink capable of producing drunkenness — there is no biblical instance of sikera meaning "grape juice" or any unfermented beverage.
- **Sikera is paired, not alternative, to oinos.** The Luke 1:15 structure ("neither oinos nor sikera") covers the two complementary categories — grape wine *and* non-grape intoxicant — so that nothing alcoholic is excluded from John's abstention.
- **The word carries a technical/legal register.** Its Hebrew-transliterated form marks it as specialized cultic-legal vocabulary rather than everyday Greek speech; Luke uses it once, exactly where the legal register is required.
