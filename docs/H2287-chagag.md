## Lexicon Definition

A primitive root (compare H2283, H2328); properly, to move in a circle, i.e. (specifically) to march in a sacred procession, to observe a festival; by implication, to be giddy: — celebrate, dance, (keep, hold) a (solemn) feast (holiday), reel to and fro.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Appointed Festival Observance (Legislative Commands)
The dominant use: commanded observance of Israel's annual pilgrimage festivals.
- **Exo 23:14** — "Three times thou shalt keep a feast unto me in the year."
- **Lev 23:39** — "ye shall keep a feast unto the LORD seven days: on the first day shall be a sabbath, and on the eighth day shall be a sabbath."
- **Lev 23:41** — "And ye shall keep it a feast unto the LORD seven days in the year."
- **Num 29:12** — "ye shall keep a feast unto the LORD seven days"

### 2. Exodus Liberation — Feast as Act of Worship
The first occurrence frames chagag as the purpose of Israel's exodus: worship through festival.
- **Exo 5:1** — "Let my people go, that they may hold a feast unto me in the wilderness."
- **Exo 12:14** — "ye shall keep it a feast to the LORD throughout your generations; ye shall keep it a feast by an ordinance for ever."

### 3. Eschatological Feast-Keeping (Prophetic)
Nations compelled to observe YHWH's festivals in the messianic age.
- **Zec 14:16** — "every one that is left of all the nations... shall even go up from year to year to worship the King, the LORD of hosts, and to keep the feast of tabernacles."
- **Zec 14:18** — "if the family of Egypt go not up... there shall be the plague, wherewith the LORD will smite the heathen that come not up to keep the feast of tabernacles."
- **Zec 14:19** — "the punishment of all nations that come not up to keep the feast of tabernacles."
- **Nah 1:15** — "O Judah, keep thy solemn feasts, perform thy vows"

### 4. Festive Celebration / Dancing (Participial)
The participial form describes joyful, possibly circular movement associated with feasting.
- **1 Sam 30:16** — "they were spread abroad upon all the earth, eating and drinking, and dancing, because of all the great spoil"
- **Psa 42:4** — "I had gone with the multitude, I went with them to the house of God, with the voice of joy and praise, with a multitude that kept holyday."

### 5. Physical Reeling / Staggering
The root sense of circular motion applied to physical disorientation.
- **Psa 107:27** — "They reel to and fro, and stagger like a drunken man, and are at their wits' end."
