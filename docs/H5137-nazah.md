## Semantic Categories

### 1. Priestly Blood Sprinkling — Atonement / Expiation
The dominant use: a priest dips his finger in sacrificial blood and sprinkles it toward the sacred furniture.

- **Lev 16:14** — "And he shall take of the blood of the bullock, and sprinkle it with his finger upon the mercy seat eastward; and before the mercy seat shall he sprinkle of the blood with his finger seven times."
- **Lev 16:15** — "Then shall he kill the goat…and bring his blood within the veil…and sprinkle it upon the mercy seat."
- **Lev 16:19** — "And he shall sprinkle of the blood upon it with his finger seven times, and cleanse it, and hallow it from the uncleanness of the children of Israel."
- **Lev 5:9** — "And he shall sprinkle of the blood of the sin offering upon the side of the altar."

### 2. Purification Rites — Leprosy and Ritual Uncleanness
Sprinkling effects the transition from unclean to clean status.

- **Lev 14:7** — "And he shall sprinkle upon him that is to be cleansed from the leprosy seven times, and shall pronounce him clean."
- **Lev 14:51** — "And he shall take the cedar wood…and dip them in the blood of the slain bird…and sprinkle the house seven times."
- **Num 19:19** — "And the clean person shall sprinkle upon the unclean on the third day, and on the seventh day."
- **Num 8:7** — "And thus shalt thou do unto them, to cleanse them: Sprinkle water of purifying upon them."

### 3. Consecration and Ordination
Sprinkling is also the means of setting apart priests and sacred objects for service.

- **Exo 29:21** — "And thou shalt take of the blood that is upon the altar, and of the anointing oil, and sprinkle it upon Aaron, and upon his garments."
- **Lev 8:11** — "And he sprinkled thereof upon the altar seven times, and anointed the altar and all his vessels."
- **Lev 8:30** — "And Moses took of the anointing oil, and of the blood which was upon the altar, and sprinkled it upon Aaron, and upon his garments."

### 4. Natural / Violent Spattering (Qal)
Outside the sanctuary the Qal describes blood or liquid that spatters involuntarily — neither priestly nor ceremonial.

- **2 Ki 9:33** — "And some of her blood was sprinkled on the wall, and on the horses: and he trode her under foot."
- **Isa 63:3** — "For I will tread them in mine anger…and their blood shall be sprinkled upon my garments."

### 5. Prophetic / Messianic — The Servant Sprinkling Many Nations
The only use outside the legal-historical literature; employs the Hiphil of H5137.

- **Isa 52:15** — "So shall he sprinkle many nations; the kings shall shut their mouths at him."

*Note: The LXX renders this verse θαυμάσονται ("they will marvel"), departing from the MT. Modern translations and the NT's use of Isa 52–53 (e.g., Rom 15:21) follow the MT. If "sprinkle" is read, it evokes priestly sprinkling for expiation on a cosmic scale, applied to the Servant's atoning work.*
