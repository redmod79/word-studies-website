## Key Distinctions

| Word | Core Idea | What It Is NOT |
|------|-----------|----------------|
| gadal | to grow tall, to be elevated in honor | not about quantity |
| gadol | large in scale, great in importance | not a process — stative |
| gedullah | the quality of greatness, majesty | not action |
| rab | many, abundant, much in number | not about height/honor |
| rabah | to multiply, to become many | not about stature or magnification |

**gadal vs. gadol:** Same root. gadal is the verbal process (growing, becoming, making great); gadol is the resulting state (the adjective: large, great). They often appear together: Gen 26:13 — "Isaac grew (gadal) until he became very great (gadol meod)."

**gadal vs. rab:** The KJV translates both as "great" in some contexts, causing overlap. rab/rabah focus on multiplicity and abundance (many nations, much silver, great number), while gadal focuses on elevation, honor, stature, and magnification. Jon 4:11 uses all three together: "that great (gadol) city... more than sixscore thousand persons (rabbim)... also much (rabbah) cattle."

**gedullah:** The noun of gadal's Hiphil. It specifically denotes the quality of royal or divine greatness — used 11 times, mostly of God's majesty, Solomon's splendor, or Mordecai's honor.

## Representative Passages Showing Contrasts

**God's greatness (gadal/gadol) ≠ numerical vastness (rab/rabah):**
- **Ps 48:1** — "Great (gadol) is the LORD, and greatly to be praised" — elevation, honor
- **Deut 7:1** — "seven nations greater (rabbim) and mightier than thou" — size/number contrast

**Magnification in praise:**
- **Ps 34:3** — "O magnify (gadal) the LORD with me" — verbal action of declaring God great
- **Luke 1:46** — "My soul doth magnify (megalynei) the Lord" — direct LXX echo

**Self-magnification (arrogance):**
- **Dan 8:4, 11** — the ram, then little horn "magnified (gadal) himself" — Hithpael/Hiphil
- **Isa 10:15** — "shall the axe magnify itself (yithgaddel) against him that heweth therewith?"
