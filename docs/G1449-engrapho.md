## Lexicon Definition

From G1722 (ἐν, "in") and G1125 (γράφω, "to write"); to "engrave," i.e., inscribe — translated "write (in)" in KJV.

The compound verb intensifies the base graphō by specifying the medium: writing *into* or *onto* a surface (heart, tablet, heaven's register), with the prefix ἐν connoting permanence of inscription.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. The Epistle Written on Human Hearts

Paul calls the Corinthian believers themselves a letter of recommendation — not ink on parchment but Christ's work inscribed inwardly.

- **2 Cor 3:2** — "Ye are our epistle written in our hearts, known and read of all men"
- **2 Cor 3:3** — "written not with ink, but with the Spirit of the living God; not in tables of stone, but in fleshy tables of the heart"

### 2. Names Inscribed in Heaven's Register

The morphological tradition attributes ἐνγέγραπται in Luke 10:20 to this verb, portraying divine enrollment of the redeemed.

- **Luke 10:20** — "rejoice, because your names are written in heaven" (ἐνγέγραπται — Perf Pass Ind)
