## Semantic Categories

### 1. Hail as Theophanic Judgment (Trumpet Plague)
- **Rev 8:7** — "The first angel sounded, and there followed hail and fire mingled with blood, and they were cast upon the earth: and the third part of trees was burnt up, and all green grass was burnt up."
- **Rev 11:19** — "And the temple of God was opened in heaven, and there was seen in his temple the ark of his testament: and there were lightnings, and voices, and thunderings, and an earthquake, and great hail."

In Rev 8:7, hail initiates the first trumpet judgment — mixed with fire and blood, devastating vegetation. In Rev 11:19, hail accompanies the heavenly temple's opening alongside lightning, thunder, and earthquake as signs of divine presence and judgment.

### 2. Hail as Extreme Divine Wrath (Bowl Plague)
- **Rev 16:21** — "And there fell upon men a great hail out of heaven, [every stone] about the weight of a talent: and men blasphemed God because of the plague of the hail; for the plague thereof was exceeding great."

The final and most severe occurrence. The hailstones are described as talent-weight (~75 lbs each), provoking blasphemy rather than repentance. Both the nominative (χάλαζα) and genitive (χαλάζης) forms appear in this verse.
