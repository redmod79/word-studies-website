## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same:**
- The moral wrongdoing sense: both עָוֺן and ἀδικία describe a quality of character and action opposed to God's righteousness
- Communal/corporate application: both appear in contexts of national sin (Israel's iniquity; "unrighteousness of men" in Rom 1)
- The theological pairing: עָוֺן + חַטָּאָה in OT parallels ἀδικία + ἀσέβεια (unrighteousness + ungodliness) in NT (Rom 1:18)

**What shifts:**
- The punishment-sense of עָוֺן largely disappears in ἀδικία; the Greek word focuses on the character/quality of the wrong, not its consequence
- The LXX also uses ἀνομία (lawlessness) for עָוֺן at comparable frequency — NT authors tend to use ἀνομία in eschatological contexts (Matt 24:12; 2 Thess 2:7) where the lawlessness-frame dominates
- The Hebrew avon's guilt-accumulation concept (the weight building until judgment) has no direct Greek equivalent; it gets distributed across ἀδικία, ἀνομία, and ἐνοχή (guilt)
