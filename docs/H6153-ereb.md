## Lexicon Definition

From H6150; **dusk** — day, even(-ing, tide), night.

The core meaning is the transition period as daylight fades — the onset of darkness. In the Hebrew reckoning, *ʻereb* marks the beginning of the new day (sunset to sunset), making it the theologically prior half of the day cycle.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. The Liturgical Day Frame — "Evening and Morning"
The creation days establish *ʻereb* as the opening of each day cycle (evening precedes morning).
- **Gen 1:5** — "And the evening and the morning were the first day"
- **Gen 1:8** — "And the evening and the morning were the second day"
- **Dan 8:14** — "Unto two thousand and three hundred days (lit. evenings-mornings); then shall the sanctuary be cleansed"
- **Dan 8:26** — "And the vision of the evening and the morning which was told is true"

### 2. Ritual Purity Deadline — "Unclean Until the Even"
Leviticus and Numbers use *ʻereb* as the standard endpoint for ritual uncleanness, fixing sunset as the boundary of impurity.
- **Lev 11:24–25** — "whosoever beareth [ought] of the carcase... shall wash his clothes, and be unclean until the even"
- **Deut 23:11** — "But it shall be, when evening cometh on, he shall wash himself with water: and when the sun is down, he shall come into the camp again"
- **Num 19:22** — "whatsoever the unclean person toucheth shall be unclean; and the soul that toucheth it shall be unclean until even"

### 3. Passover and Appointed Feast Timing
The Passover lamb was slain *between the evenings* (H996 + H6153), fixing *ʻereb* as the precise moment of the sacrifice.
- **Exo 12:6** — "the whole assembly of the congregation of Israel shall kill it in the evening"
- **Num 9:3** — "In the fourteenth day of this month, at even, ye shall keep it in his appointed season"
- **Deut 16:6** — "there thou shalt sacrifice the passover at even, at the going down of the sun"

### 4. Morning and Evening Sacrifices (Tamid)
The continual burnt offering required a daily *ʻereb* offering alongside the morning sacrifice.
- **2 Kgs 16:15** — "Upon the great altar burn the morning burnt offering, and the evening meat offering"
- **1 Chr 16:40** — "To offer burnt offerings unto the LORD upon the altar... morning and evening"
- **2 Chr 13:11** — "they burn unto the LORD every morning and every evening burnt sacrifices"
- **Ezra 9:5** — "And at the evening sacrifice I arose up from my heaviness"

### 5. Day's End in Narrative
*ʻEreb* marks the close of battle, mourning, or labor — the natural turning point of a day.
- **Josh 7:6** — "Joshua fell to the earth upon his face before the ark of the LORD until the eventide"
- **2 Sam 1:12** — "they mourned, and wept, and fasted until even, for Saul, and for Jonathan"
- **1 Kgs 17:6** — "the ravens brought him bread and flesh in the morning, and bread and flesh in the evening"
- **1 Sam 30:17** — "And David smote them from the twilight even unto the evening of the next day"

### 6. Figurative and Poetic Use
Poets and prophets use *ʻereb* for foreboding — "evening wolves" prowl at the dangerous hour; "eveningtide trouble" signals sudden reversal.
- **Hab 1:8** — "their horsemen are more fierce than the evening wolves"
- **Zeph 3:3** — "Her princes within her are roaring lions; her judges are evening wolves; they gnaw not the bones till the morrow"
- **Isa 17:14** — "And behold at eveningtide trouble; and before the morning he is not"
- **Prov 7:9** — "In the twilight, in the evening, in the black and dark night"
