## Semantic Categories

### 1. Official Record / Administrative Document
- **Ezr 4:15** — "That search may be made in the book of the records of thy fathers: so shalt thou find in the book of the records, and know that this city is a rebellious city" — Persian officials appeal to written governmental archives to verify Jerusalem's history of rebellion. The phrase *sephar dakranayya* ("book of the records") denotes a state archive.
- **Ezr 6:1** — "Then Darius the king made a decree, and search was made in the house of the rolls, where the treasures were laid up in Babylon" — *beth siphrayya* ("house of the rolls/books") refers to the imperial archive repository where royal decrees were stored.
- **Ezr 6:18** — "And they set the priests in their divisions, and the Levites in their courses, for the service of God, which is at Jerusalem; as it is written in the book of Moses" — *sephar Mosheh* ("book of Moses") — the Pentateuch cited as the authoritative written source for priestly organization.

### 2. Book of Divine Judgment
- **Dan 7:10** — "A fiery stream issued and came forth from before him: thousand thousands ministered unto him, and ten thousand times ten thousand stood before him: the judgment was set, and the books were opened" — *siphrin pethichuw* ("books were opened") in the heavenly court scene. The books serve as the record upon which divine judgment is rendered.
