## Semantic Categories

### 1. General Moral Wickedness / Character of Unrighteousness
- **Luk 13:27** — "depart from me, all ye workers of iniquity" — defines a class of people characterized by adikia
- **Rom 1:29** — "being filled with all unrighteousness, fornication, wickedness" — heads a vice list as the umbrella category
- **2 Tim 2:19** — "Let every one that nameth the name of Christ depart from iniquity" — adikia as the moral condition Christians must abandon
- **1 Jhn 5:17** — "All unrighteousness is sin" — equates adikia with hamartia

### 2. Opposition to Truth / Suppression of Righteousness
- **Rom 1:18** — "who hold the truth in unrighteousness" — adikia as the force that suppresses truth
- **Rom 2:8** — "do not obey the truth, but obey unrighteousness" — truth and adikia as rival authorities
- **2 Th 2:10** — "deceivableness of unrighteousness in them that perish" — adikia as deceptive power
- **2 Th 2:12** — "believed not the truth, but had pleasure in unrighteousness" — choosing adikia over truth
- **1 Cor 13:6** — "rejoiceth not in iniquity, but rejoiceth in the truth" — love's antithesis to adikia

### 3. Adikia Contrasted with God's Righteousness
- **Rom 3:5** — "if our unrighteousness commend the righteousness of God, what shall we say?" — human adikia set against divine dikaiosynē
- **Rom 9:14** — "Is there unrighteousness with God? God forbid." — categorical denial of adikia in God's character
- **Jhn 7:18** — "he that seeketh his glory that sent him, the same is true, and no unrighteousness is in him" — absence of adikia as proof of authenticity

### 4. Instrument of Sin / Moral Servitude
- **Rom 6:13** — "Neither yield ye your members as instruments of unrighteousness unto sin" — adikia as a tool in the service of hamartia
- **Jas 3:6** — "the tongue is a fire, a world of iniquity" — the tongue as a microcosm of adikia

### 5. Reward and Consequence of Wickedness
- **Act 1:18** — "this man purchased a field with the reward of iniquity" — Judas's wages of adikia
- **2 Pet 2:13** — "shall receive the reward of unrighteousness" — eschatological recompense
- **2 Pet 2:15** — "who loved the wages of unrighteousness" — Balaam's pattern of adikia-for-profit
- **Luk 16:8–9** — "the unjust steward... the mammon of unrighteousness" — wealth characterized by adikia

### 6. Object of Divine Forgiveness / Cleansing
- **Heb 8:12** — "I will be merciful to their unrighteousness" — adikia as what God forgives under the new covenant
- **1 Jhn 1:9** — "to cleanse us from all unrighteousness" — adikia as what confession removes

### 7. Bondage and Entrapment
- **Act 8:23** — "thou art in the gall of bitterness, and in the bond of iniquity" — adikia as a binding force
- **Luk 18:6** — "Hear what the unjust judge saith" — adikia characterizing a person in authority
