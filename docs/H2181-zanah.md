## Lexicon Definition

A verb meaning to commit whoredom, play the harlot, go a-whoring, or commit fornication. In figurative prophetic usage it becomes one of the central OT verbs for idolatrous covenant infidelity.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Literal prostitution or harlot-status
- **Gen 34:31** — "deal with our sister as with an harlot?"
- **Jos 2:1** — Rahab is called a harlot.
- **Deu 23:19** — prostitution appears in legal context.

### 2. Covenant people going after other gods
- **Exo 34:15-16** — Israel is warned not to go a-whoring after the gods of the land.
- **Num 25:1** — Israel begins to commit whoredom with Moab.
- **Jdg 2:17** — Israel quickly goes a-whoring after other gods.

### 3. Prophetic metaphor for spiritual adultery
- **Jer 3:6-9** — backsliding Israel commits adultery and plays the harlot.
- **Ezk 16:15-17, 26, 28** — Jerusalem's idolatries are narrated as whoredom.
- **Ezk 23:3, 5, 19, 30** — the two sisters' apostasy is cast in extended harlot imagery.

### 4. Hosea's covenant-lawsuit vocabulary
- **Hos 4:12-15** — whoredom is linked to spirit, idolatry, and Israel's corruption.
- **Hos 5:3** — Ephraim's whoredom defiles Israel.
- **Hos 9:1** — Israel has gone a-whoring from its God.

### 5. Broader metaphor of seductive corruption
- **Nah 3:4** — Nineveh's corrupting power is cast as whoredoms.
- **Mic 1:7** — whoredom language is tied to idol hire and cultic corruption.
