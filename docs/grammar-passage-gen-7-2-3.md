**Hebrew (v.2):** מִכֹּ֣ל׀ הַבְּהֵמָ֣ה הַטְּהֹורָ֗ה תִּֽקַּח־לְךָ֛ שִׁבְעָ֥ה שִׁבְעָ֖ה אִ֣ישׁ וְאִשְׁתֹּ֑ו וּמִן־הַבְּהֵמָ֡ה אֲ֠שֶׁר לֹ֣א טְהֹרָ֥ה הִ֛וא שְׁנַ֖יִם אִ֥ישׁ וְאִשְׁתֹּֽו׃

## Key Grammatical Features

### 1. הַבְּהֵמָה הַטְּהֹורָה / אֲשֶׁר לֹא טְהֹרָה הִוא — adjectival clean/unclean contrast

- **Form:** `בְּהֵמָה` is feminine singular absolute; `טְּהֹורָה / טְהֹרָה` are feminine singular absolute adjectives agreeing with it. The first expression uses attributive adjective syntax with repeated article (`the cattle, the clean`), while the second uses a relative/adjectival clause: `אֲשֶׁר לֹא טְהֹרָה הִוא`, "which is not clean."
- **Significance:** The clean/unclean distinction is carried by adjective agreement and by a relative clause, not by a separate noun-class term. The grammar marks two categories of `בְּהֵמָה`: positively described clean animals and negatively described animals that are not clean.
- **Grammar reference:** [construct-state](../hebrew/construct-state.md), [syntax-waw-conjunction](../hebrew/syntax-waw-conjunction.md).

### 2. שִׁבְעָה שִׁבְעָה — repeated cardinal quantity

- **Form:** Two identical feminine singular absolute cardinal nouns stand together without a preposition.
- **Significance:** The repetition functions as a distributive/enumerative quantity inside the object phrase: the clean animals and the birds are to be taken in "seven, seven" units. The parser treats the whole numerical expression with `אִישׁ וְאִשְׁתּוֹ` / `זָכָר וּנְקֵבָה` as the object of `תִּקַּח` in v.2 or as the object of the elliptical clause in v.3.
- **Grammar reference:** no dedicated cardinal-reduplication entry exists in the grammar library; compare the broader category of distributive marking in [distributive-lamed-ratio](../hebrew/distributive-lamed-ratio.md), though this passage does not use lamed-ratio syntax.

### 3. תִּקַּח־לְךָ — Qal imperfect command-like instruction

- **Form:** `תִּקַּח` is Qal imperfect 2ms of `לקח`, with the 2ms pronominal complement `לְךָ`.
- **Significance:** The imperfect occurs in direct instruction to Noah. The addressee is singular throughout the command frame, while the animals are grouped by coordinated pair language.
- **Grammar reference:** [qal-imperfect](../hebrew/qal-imperfect.md).

### 4. אִישׁ וְאִשְׁתּוֹ / זָכָר וּנְקֵבָה — coordinated pair expressions

- **Form:** `אִישׁ וְאִשְׁתּוֹ` is a coordinated noun pair, literally "man and his woman/wife," used here for animal pairing. Verse 3 uses `זָכָר וּנְקֵבָה`, masculine adjective/noun plus feminine noun.
- **Significance:** Both expressions are syntactic pair markers. They qualify the counted groups as paired male/female units without changing the numeric head.
- **Grammar reference:** [syntax-compound-object-waw](../hebrew/syntax-compound-object-waw.md), [syntax-waw-conjunction](../hebrew/syntax-waw-conjunction.md).

### 5. לְחַיֹּות זֶרַע — infinitive construct purpose clause

- **Form:** `לְ` + Piel infinitive construct `חַיֹּות` governs `זֶרַע` as object.
- **Significance:** The infinitive clause gives the purpose/result of the bird preservation command: keeping seed/offspring alive upon the earth.
- **Grammar reference:** no dedicated Hebrew lamed-infinitive purpose entry exists; compare infinitive treatment in [infinitive-construct-natan-predicative](../hebrew/infinitive-construct-natan-predicative.md).

## Clause Structure

### Genesis 7:2

- **Clause 1:** `xYq0`, question/directive domain. Complement `מִ כֹּל הַ בְּהֵמָה הַ טְּהֹורָה`; predicate `תִּֽקַּח`; supplement `לְךָ`; object `שִׁבְעָה שִׁבְעָה אִישׁ וְאִשְׁתּוֹ`.
- **Clause 2:** Elliptical coordinate clause. Complement `וּמִן הַבְּהֵמָה`; object `שְׁנַיִם אִישׁ וְאִשְׁתּוֹ`. The verb "take" is supplied from Clause 1.
- **Clause 3:** Adjectival relative clause: `אֲשֶׁר לֹא טְהֹרָה הִוא`, with negation, predicate complement adjective, and 3fs subject pronoun.

### Genesis 7:3

- **Clause 1:** Elliptical clause with complement `גַּם מֵעֹוף הַשָּׁמַיִם` and object `שִׁבְעָה שִׁבְעָה זָכָר וּנְקֵבָה`.
- **Clause 2:** Infinitive construct clause: `לְחַיֹּות זֶרַע עַל פְּנֵי כָל הָאָרֶץ`, with infinitive predicate, object, and location.

## Construct Chains

- `כֹּל הַבְּהֵמָה הַטְּהֹורָה` — `כֹּל` in construct governs the following definite noun phrase.
- `עֹוף הַשָּׁמַיִם` — "birds of the heavens/air."
- `פְּנֵי כָל הָאָרֶץ` — "face of all the earth," with nested construct relationship.
