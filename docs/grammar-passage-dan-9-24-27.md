# Grammar Analysis: Daniel 9:24–27

*The 70 Weeks Prophecy — Gabriel's explanation continuing the vision of Daniel 8*

## Key Grammatical Features

### 1. נֶחְתַּךְ (nechtak) — Niphal Perfect 3ms of חתך (v. 24)

- **Form:** Niphal stem, perfect aspect, 3rd masculine singular. The root חתך occurs **only here** in the entire Hebrew Bible (BLB count = 1; BDB/Gesenius: Daniel 9:24 sole occurrence). The verb is a true hapax legomenon in the canonical OT.
- **Lexical semantics:** Strong's H2852 — *châthak*, "a primitive root; properly, to cut off, i.e. (figuratively) to decree: — determine" (BDB). The basic Qal sense is concrete severance ("cut off, divide"); the Niphal here is passive — "is/has-been cut off." The figurative sense "decreed/determined" follows naturally from the image of a fixed portion having been *cut off* (cf. English "allotted, apportioned, decreed"). Later Mishnaic Hebrew and Aramaic attest חתך in exactly this "determine/decree" sense, confirming the semantic bridge.
- **Aspect:** The Hebrew perfect here functions as a stative/resultative ("is determined"), not a past-tense narrative. Gabriel is speaking from the vantage point of a *settled divine decision*: the seventy-week allotment has been cut off and stands decreed. KJV "are determined" captures this well.
- **Subject agreement:** 3ms perfect agrees with the plural-form noun שָׁבֻעִים as a single collective block ("the seventy weeks, [as a unit,] has-been-cut-off"). This is standard with collective-numerical subjects; cf. GKC §145k, Joüon-Muraoka §150c.
- **Lexical resonance with Dan 8:** The cutting verb links conceptually, but not lexically, to Daniel 8. Dan 8:14 speaks of 2,300 evening-mornings; here a seventy-week segment is *cut off* from that larger temporal frame. The word חָתַךְ was deliberately chosen — a unique, otherwise-unused verb — to mark this as a singular, foundational act of measuring-off. See **word-studies/H2852-chathak.md**.
- **Grammar reference:** [hebrew/stem-niphal.md](../hebrew/stem-niphal.md) — Niphal as passive.

### 2. שָׁבֻעִים שִׁבְעִים (shabu'im shib'im) — "Seventy Sevens" (v. 24)

- **Form:** Two *Noun.mp.Abs* forms in juxtaposition: שָׁבֻעִים ("sevened-periods, weeks") + שִׁבְעִים ("seventy"). Both derive from the root שבע ("seven"). The numeral שִׁבְעִים is the plural of "seven" used adjectivally for "seventy"; שָׁבֻעִים is the masculine plural of שָׁבוּעַ — etymologically a *passive participle* of the denominative verb "seven" (Strong's H7620), meaning "sevened [unit], heptad, week."
- **Noun שָׁבוּעַ:** Occurs 20× in the OT (BLB). Three senses attested: (a) a literal 7-day week (Gen 29:27-28 "fulfill her week"; Deut 16:9-10, 16 Feast of Weeks; Jer 5:24); (b) a 7-year sabbatical cycle by implication (Lev 25 contextual framework); (c) a heptad/seven-unit with referent supplied by context. In Daniel 9:24-27 (7×) and Dan 10:2-3 שָׁבֻעִים יָמִים, "weeks of days," is explicitly contrasted with the plain שָׁבֻעִים of ch. 9 — a reader's signal that the ch. 9 שָׁבֻעִים are *not* weeks-of-days. The "sevened" root sense is intentionally open-ended; context, not the word, fixes the time unit.
- **Masculine plural in -ים:** The form שָׁבֻעִים (not feminine שָׁבֻעוֹת as in Exod 34:22 Feast of Weeks) appears chiefly in Dan 9 and Dan 10, and is sometimes taken by commentators as a deliberate variant marking the technical apocalyptic usage.
- **Word order:** Placing the object/subject (שָׁבֻעִים שִׁבְעִים) *before* the verb (נֶחְתַּךְ) is marked fronting — emphasis/topicalization. The "seventy weeks" is the headline of the announcement.
- **Connection to Dan 8:14 day-year framework:** Daniel 8 closes with the 2,300 evening-mornings sanctuary timetable, and 8:26-27 leaves Daniel appalled and "none understood" — setting the narrative trigger for Daniel's prayer (9:1-19) and Gabriel's return (9:21-23). Gabriel's words הָבֵן בַּמַּרְאֶה ("understand the vision," 9:23) reach back deliberately to the unexplained מַרְאֶה of 8:16, 26-27. See **hebrew/day-year-formula.md** for the hermeneutical framework that converts prophetic "day" units into years (Num 14:34; Ezek 4:6); on that reading שָׁבֻעִים שִׁבְעִים = 70 × 7 = 490 prophetic days = 490 years.
- **Word study:** [word-studies/WG-weeks.md](../../word-studies/WG-weeks.md).

### 3. Six Telic Infinitive Construct Clauses (v. 24)

The purpose-clause chain in v. 24 contains six coordinated לְ + infinitive construct forms — the grammatical backbone of the verse's theological content:

| # | Form | Stem | Root | Gloss |
|---|------|------|------|-------|
| 1 | לְכַלֵּא | Piel | כלה | "to finish / put-an-end-to [the transgression]" |
| 2 | וּלְהָתֵם | Hiphil | תמם (Kethib ולחתם חתם "to seal," Qere ולהתם תמם "to complete") | "to seal up / make an end of [sins]" |
| 3 | וּלְכַפֵּר | Piel | כפר | "to atone for [iniquity]" |
| 4 | וּלְהָבִיא | Hiphil | בוא | "to bring in [everlasting righteousness]" |
| 5 | וְלַחְתֹּם | Qal | חתם | "to seal [vision and prophet]" |
| 6 | וְלִמְשֹׁחַ | Qal | משׁח | "to anoint [holy-of-holies]" |

- **Function:** Infinitive construct with prefixed לְ expresses purpose/result ("in order to…") and/or epexegetical expansion of the main verb. Here all six depend on נֶחְתַּךְ and define *what the cutting-off of the seventy weeks is for* — the telic goal of the allotment.
- **Pairing pattern:** 3 + 3 structure — the first three deal with the negative removal of sin (rebellion, sin, iniquity) using the OT's three standard terms for sin (פֶּשַׁע, חַטָּאת, עָוֹן), paralleling Exod 34:7; the last three deal with positive installations (everlasting righteousness, sealing of vision-and-prophet, anointing of Holy-of-Holies).
- **Kethib/Qere at #2:** BHS writes ולחתם (Kethib, "to seal") but the Masoretes mark the Qere as ולהתם (from תמם, "to complete"). The parser returns the Qere reading (Hiphil of תמם). Both readings end up in the sealing/completing semantic field; commentators split, but both ancient traditions agree the object is sin.
- **Grammar reference:** see also [hebrew/stem-niphal.md](../hebrew/stem-niphal.md) for Niphal antecedent; no dedicated infinitive-construct entry exists yet in the library (candidate for future creation).

### 4. יִכָּרֵת מָשִׁיחַ — Niphal Imperfect 3ms of כרת (v. 26)

- **Form:** יִכָּרֵת = Niphal imperfect 3ms from כָּרַת (Strong's H3772), "cut, cut off." Subject: מָשִׁיחַ, "Anointed One," unarticulated. KJV: "shall Messiah be cut off."
- **Niphal of כרת:** The Niphal is the standard passive for this root and covers a lexicalized semantic range: (a) concrete severance (a limb, a branch); (b) violent death, especially judicial or covenantal (Gen 9:11; Lev 7:20-27 the *karet* penalty "cut off from his people"); (c) destruction/annihilation of a line or nation. In contexts involving persons, "cut off" almost always denotes violent death with covenantal/divine overtones. Word study: [word-studies/H3772-karath.md](../../word-studies/H3772-karath.md).
- **Imperfect aspect:** Future-referring from Gabriel's speaking vantage; punctiliar-event reading ("shall be cut off") is natural given a nominal agent + definite terminus (after the 62 weeks).
- **Lexical contrast with חָתַךְ (v. 24):** Both roots mean "cut," but the passage uses them distinctively. נֶחְתַּךְ in v. 24 = *cut off a portion of time* (decree, allotment). יִכָּרֵת in v. 26 = *cut off a person* (violent death). The semantic echo is deliberate: the period that is cut-off (v. 24) reaches its climax in a person being cut-off (v. 26).
- **וְאֵין לֹו:** Verbless/particle clause with אֵין + לְ + pronominal suffix — lit. "and there-is-nothing to-him." Syntactically economical; semantically contested. Options (all grammatically valid): "and [he shall have] nothing/no one," "and none [of his own remains] to-him," "but not for himself" (KJV's interpretive gloss). The brevity is itself a rhetorical feature.
- **Grammar reference:** [hebrew/stem-niphal.md](../hebrew/stem-niphal.md).

### 5. חרץ Niphal Participles — the *Determined* Decree (vv. 26, 27)

- **Forms:** נֶחֱרֶצֶת (v. 26, Niphal Participle fs Abs) and וְנֶחֱרָצָה (v. 27, Niphal Participle fs Abs) from the root חרץ (Strong's H2782).
- **Lexical semantics:** *charats* — "properly, to point sharply, i.e. (literally) to wound; figuratively, to be alert, to decide: — bestir self, decide, decree, determine" (BDB). Occurs 12× in the OT. In Isa 10:22-23, 28:22, and Dan 9:26-27; 11:36 the root is used for a divinely *decreed* judgment/end. Word study: [word-studies/H2782-charats.md](../../word-studies/H2782-charats.md).
- **Niphal Participle function:** The Niphal participle is passive-stative — "[something] being/having-been decreed" — describing an abiding state of divinely fixed certainty. It functions as a quasi-substantive in v. 27 (וְנֶחֱרָצָה, "and that-which-is-decreed") and as a predicate adjective in v. 26 (שֹׁמֵמוֹת נֶחֱרֶצֶת, "desolations are decreed").
- **Three-verb "decree" architecture:** The passage layers three distinct but semantically overlapping "decree/cut" roots — חתך (v. 24, unique), כרת (v. 26, the Messiah), חרץ (vv. 26, 27). Read context-neutrally as a grammatical observation: three different Hebrew roots for cutting/determining cluster in this single oracle, none of them a common Biblical-Hebrew verb for "decree." The more usual words for decree (צוה, אמר, גזר [Aram.], יעד) are absent. The rhetorical effect is a deliberate cutting-motif running through the entire passage.
- **Grammar reference:** [hebrew/stem-niphal.md](../hebrew/stem-niphal.md).

### 6. Bracketing Imperatives/Imperfects of v. 25 — וְתֵדַע וְתַשְׂכֵּל

- **Form:** Pair of 2ms imperfects (Qal of ידע + Hiphil of שׂכל) opening v. 25 with *waw* prefixed. Can be read as (a) future indicatives ("and thou shalt know and shalt understand") or (b) cohortative/jussive-force imperatives ("so know, and understand!"). Given Gabriel's explicit declaration in 9:22-23 that he has come לְהַשְׂכִּילְךָ בִינָה ("to give thee skill-and-understanding") and הָבֵן בַּמַּרְאֶה ("understand the vision"), the imperatival force fits the discourse function: Gabriel is commanding comprehension.
- **Hiphil of שׂכל:** Causative/factitive — "cause to be prudent, give insight, act with insight." The exact lexical pair ידע + שׂכל (Hiphil) appears in Dan 9:25 and Dan 11:33, 12:3, 10 — a Danielic signature collocation binding the end-time instruction motif (מַשְׂכִּילִים, "the wise/insightful ones") to the 70-weeks oracle.
- **Connection to Dan 8:** Gabriel's task in Dan 8:16 was הָבֵן … אֶת־הַמַּרְאֶה ("make *this one* understand the vision"), but 8:27 records Daniel's failure: וְאֵין מֵבִין ("and none understood"). Daniel 9:22-25 is the narrative resumption: the same angel, the same verb of understanding, explicitly re-applied to the unfinished vision. Grammatically this is a cross-chapter resumption chain, executed lexically (שׂכל + בין + מַרְאֶה) rather than by explicit discourse markers.

### 7. Construct-Chain and Numeric Apposition Structures

- **v. 25 מֹצָא דָבָר:** Construct chain ("going-forth of a word/decree"). מֹצָא = Hiphil-form verbal noun of יצא ("cause to go out"), in Cst state — the head term; דָבָר = absolute, the semantic complement. The terminus a quo of the prophetic clock.
- **v. 25 מָשִׁיחַ נָגִיד:** Two absolute-state masculine singulars in apposition — not a construct chain (since both are unarticulated absolutes). Syntactically either (a) title + apposition "Anointed [One], [a] Prince/Leader," or (b) double noun-phrase title ("Anointed Prince"). Same construction type as the similar Dan 8 title formulae.
- **v. 25 שָׁבֻעִים שִׁבְעָה / שָׁבֻעִים שִׁשִּׁים וּשְׁנַיִם:** Noun + cardinal number in apposition — standard BH quantification where the number follows the counted noun. Masculine plural שָׁבֻעִים is here modified by a feminine absolute numeral שִׁבְעָה ("seven") — a well-known gender-disagreement pattern in BH counting (the so-called "reverse gender" rule for cardinals 3-10, Joüon §100d, GKC §97a-c). The 7 + 62 + 1 division (total = 70) is grammatically a simple numeric partition of the already-announced 70 of v. 24. See also [hebrew/day-year-formula.md](../hebrew/day-year-formula.md).
- **v. 26 עַם נָגִיד הַבָּא:** Ambiguous construct chain — "people of a prince, the one-coming." Parsing options depend on whether the articular participle הַבָּא modifies עַם or נָגִיד. Grammatically the definite article's position favors נָגִיד as the head (the article on the participle signals the participle modifies the nearer definite-ish noun in construct; however עַם-constructions are exceptional). The interpretation "the people of the coming prince" (prince = subject of action) and "the coming people of the prince" (people = subject of action) both parse. The article placement is the disputed datum.
- **v. 27 כְּנַף שִׁקּוּצִים:** Construct chain "wing of abominations." שִׁקּוּצִים is the standard OT plural for detestable/idolatrous objects (Deut 29:17; 1 Kgs 11:5-7; 2 Kgs 23:13, 24; Jer 4:1, 7:30; Ezek 5:11). The "wing" image is much disputed (pinnacle of temple? sweep of abominations? side/extremity?). Grammatically this is straightforward Cst-Abs noun pair with no marking resolving the metaphor.

## Structural Observations

### Frontline Verbs Through vv. 24–27

Arranged in sequence, the finite-verb spine of the oracle:

| Verse | Verb | Parsing | Subject | Action |
|-------|------|---------|---------|--------|
| 24 | נֶחְתַּךְ | Niph. Perf 3ms | שָׁבֻעִים שִׁבְעִים | "is-decreed" (stative-resultative) |
| 25 | וְתֵדַע / וְתַשְׂכֵּל | Qal/Hiph. Impf 2ms | Daniel | "know / understand" |
| 25 | תָּשׁוּב | Qal Impf 3fs | Jerusalem (implied) | "shall return/be-rebuilt" |
| 25 | וְנִבְנְתָה | Niph. Perf 3fs (waw-consec.) | Jerusalem | "and shall-be-built" |
| 26 | יִכָּרֵת | Niph. Impf 3ms | מָשִׁיחַ | "shall-be-cut-off" |
| 26 | יַשְׁחִית | Hiph. Impf 3ms | עַם נָגִיד | "shall destroy" |
| 27 | וְהִגְבִּיר | Hiph. Perf 3ms (waw-consec.) | 3ms antecedent (disputed: נָגִיד הַבָּא of v.26 or מָשִׁיחַ) | "shall confirm / cause-to-prevail" |
| 27 | יַשְׁבִּית | Hiph. Impf 3ms | same 3ms | "shall cause-to-cease" |
| 27 | תִּתַּךְ | Qal Impf 3fs | נֶחֱרָצָה ("what-is-decreed") | "shall be-poured" |

Note the pattern: the oracle opens with a stative-resultative perfect (settled divine decree), proceeds through imperfects and waw-consecutive perfects for future eventualities, and closes with another "pouring out of what-is-decreed" — grammatically bracketed by divine decree-language (נֶחְתַּךְ … נֶחֱרָצָה).

### The Antecedent of the 3ms in v. 27

The verb וְהִגְבִּיר ("he shall confirm") has no explicit subject in v. 27. Grammatically the closest 3ms antecedents in v. 26 are:

- מָשִׁיחַ ("[the] Anointed One," v. 26 subject of יִכָּרֵת)
- נָגִיד הַבָּא ("the prince that shall come," v. 26 subject-phrase of יַשְׁחִית via its genitive-head עַם)

Both are 3ms nouns; Hebrew narrative syntax allows a 3ms verb to pick up either by proximity or by discourse-salience. The *grammatical* data does not resolve the ambiguity; the *contextual/theological* resolution is the classical interpretive crux of the passage. Context-neutrally: the ambiguity is real, morphologically unsignaled, and is not reducible to grammar alone.

## Connection to Daniel 8

The grammar itself ties Daniel 9:24-27 back to Daniel 8 in three explicit ways:

1. **Lexical resumption of Gabriel's understanding-mission.** Dan 8:16 הָבֵן … אֶת־הַמַּרְאֶה ("make him understand the vision"); 8:27 וְאֵין מֵבִין ("and none understood"); 9:22 לְהַשְׂכִּילְךָ בִינָה ("to make thee understand"); 9:23 הָבֵן בַּמַּרְאֶה ("understand the vision"); 9:25 וְתֵדַע וְתַשְׂכֵּל ("know and understand"). Same angel, same verbal roots (בין, שׂכל), same object (מַרְאֶה), spanning 19 verses of narrative.

2. **The "cutting" motif.** Daniel 8:14 establishes a 2,300 evening-morning time-frame for sanctuary vindication. Daniel 9:24 uses a root (חתך) that nowhere else in the OT appears, with a figurative sense of *cutting a portion off* — i.e., allotting a shorter piece out of a larger whole. The grammatical datum (a unique hapax verb used specifically for a time-allotment) is compatible with the interpretive reading that the 70 weeks are a sub-portion carved out of the 2,300 of Dan 8:14.

3. **Day-year hermeneutic.** Both Daniel 8:14 (2,300 evening-mornings → 2,300 years on the day-year reading) and Daniel 9:24-27 (70 × 7 "sevened" units → 490 years on the day-year reading) are candidates for the day-for-year formula (Num 14:34; Ezek 4:6). See **[hebrew/day-year-formula.md](../hebrew/day-year-formula.md)** for the context-neutral grammar of the formula and its hermeneutical portability to apocalyptic time-prophecies.

The *grammatical* link is firm; *interpretive* conclusions built on the link belong in passage-level studies, not in this reference entry.
