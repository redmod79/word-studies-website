# WG: Covenant Feast — Eating, Drinking, and Sacrificial Meal Before YHWH

## Overview

The covenant feast is a cluster of verbs and nouns that together describe the central cultic
act of ancient Israel: eating and drinking *before YHWH* in the place He has chosen. The verb
**H398 ʼâkal** sits at the center; it chains with **H8354 shâthâh** (drink) as an eat-and-drink
merism, takes **H3899 lechem** (bread), **H1320 bâsâr** (flesh), and **H4682 matstsâh** (unleavened
bread) as objects, is located by **H6440 pânîym** ("before the face of" YHWH), and is opposed by
**H784 ʼêsh** (fire) — which either cooks the sacrifice, consumes the holy portions on the altar,
or devours transgressors.

This group is the vocabulary backbone of:
- The **Passover** (Exo 12)
- The **priestly eating of holy offerings** (Lev 6–7, 10)
- The **Deuteronomic centralization feasts** ("eat before YHWH in the place He shall choose" —
  Deut 12, 14, 16, 26, 27)
- The **sealing of covenants** (Exo 24:11 — "they saw God, and did eat and drink")
- The **judgment-by-fire antithesis** (Lev 10:2; 2Ki 1:10; Amos 1–2) — the same fire that consumes
  the offering can consume the offerer

## Roles in the Concept

- **The Act (verb):** ʼâkal — eating, the central verb; shâthâh — drinking, always paired
- **The Objects:** lechem (bread, staple); bâsâr (flesh, sacrificial); matstsâh (unleavened, festal)
- **The Place:** "lifnê YHWH" (H6440) — "before the face of" YHWH, specifically at the chosen
  sanctuary
- **The Agent of Fire:** ʼêsh — either (a) the altar-fire that consumes the offering (positive
  covenant-meal sign), or (b) the judgment-fire that devours transgressors (negative)
- **The Controlling Authority:** YHWH — names the meal, fixes its place, issues the imperative

## Weqatal / Commanded-Future Signature

A distinctive grammatical signature of this cluster in Deuteronomy is the **weqatal (waw-consecutive
perfect) chain of covenant-imperatives**: a sequence of 2ms/2mp perfects prefixed with waw, carrying
the force of continuing commands — "and you shall eat ... and you shall rejoice ... and you shall
bless." H398 is one of the principal verbs in these chains:

- **Deut 12:7** — `וַאֲכַלְתֶּם שָׁם ... וּשְׂמַחְתֶּם` — "ye shall eat there ... and ye shall rejoice"
- **Deut 14:23, 26** — `וְאָכַלְתָּ ... וְשָׂמַחְתָּ` — "thou shalt eat ... and thou shalt rejoice"
- **Deut 27:7** — `וְזָבַחְתָּ ... וְאָכַלְתָּ ... וְשָׂמַחְתָּ` — "thou shalt sacrifice ... eat ... rejoice"

This weqatal chain pattern — *eat + rejoice + before-YHWH* — is the syntactic fingerprint of
covenant-feast legislation. When searching for covenant-feast imperative verbs, look for 2ms/2mp
perfects of H398, H8354, H2076 (zâbach — sacrifice), H8055 (śâmach — rejoice), and H1288 (bârak —
bless) chained together with location-phrase `lipnê YHWH`.

- **Exo 12:8** — `[ʼâkal + bâsâr + matstsâh + ʼêsh]` "They shall eat the flesh in that night, roast

- **Exo 24:11** — `[ʼâkal + shâthâh + pânîym]` "they saw God, and did eat and drink" (Sinai covenant-
  ratification meal)

- **Lev 10:2** — `[ʼâkal + pânîym + ʼêsh]` "there went out fire from the LORD, and devoured them ...
  before the LORD" (Nadab & Abihu — inverse of covenant-meal)
- **Deut 9:9, 18** — `[ʼâkal + lechem + shâthâh + pânîym]` "I ... did neither eat bread, nor drink
  water ... before the LORD" (Moses' fast)
- **Deut 12:7** — `[ʼâkal + pânîym]` "ye shall eat before the LORD your God"
- **Deut 14:26** — `[ʼâkal + shâthâh + pânîym]` "and thou shalt eat there before the LORD thy God"
  (the clearest covenant-feast imperative)
- **Deut 16:3** — `[ʼâkal + lechem + matstsâh]` "thou shalt eat no leavened bread with it; seven
  days shalt thou eat unleavened bread"

- **Jdg 6:21** — `[ʼâkal + bâsâr + matstsâh + ʼêsh]` "fire out of the rock, and consumed the flesh
  and the unleavened cakes" (Gideon's offering — prefiguration of consumed-offering covenant sign)
