## Semantic Categories

### 1. Dietary/Cultic Revulsion — Creatures Declared Unclean
- **Lev 11:11** — "They shall be even an abomination unto you; ye shall not eat of their flesh, but ye shall have their carcases in abomination." — Waterborne creatures lacking fins and scales
- **Lev 11:13** — "And these are they which ye shall have in abomination among the fowls; they shall not be eaten, they are an abomination." — Predatory/scavenging birds
- **Lev 11:43** — "Ye shall not make yourselves abominable with any creeping thing that creepeth, neither shall ye make yourselves unclean with them, that ye should be defiled thereby." — Swarming ground creatures

### 2. Clean/Unclean Separation Imperative
- **Lev 20:25** — "Ye shall therefore put difference between clean beasts and unclean, and between unclean fowls and clean: and ye shall not make your souls abominable by beast, or by fowl, or by any manner of living thing that creepeth on the ground, which I have separated from you as unclean." — Summary principle linking shaqats to the badal (H914, "separate/distinguish") imperative

### 3. Idolatry Revulsion — Intensive Doubling Construction
- **Deut 7:26** — "Neither shalt thou bring an abomination into thine house, lest thou be a cursed thing like it: but thou shalt utterly detest it, and thou shalt utterly abhor it; for it is a cursed thing." — Infinitive absolute + imperfect (שַׁקֵּץ תְּשַׁקְּצֶנּוּ) paired with identical ta'ab (H8581) construction for maximum intensification; applied to pagan idols, not dietary uncleanness

### 4. Divine Compassion — Negated Form
- **Psa 22:24** — "For he hath not despised nor abhorred the affliction of the afflicted; neither hath he hid his face from him; but when he cried unto him, he heard." — Only non-Pentateuchal use; God is the subject (elsewhere always humans); negated — God did NOT shaqats the afflicted one; paired with H959 bazah "despise"
