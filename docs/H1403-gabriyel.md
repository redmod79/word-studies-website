## Lexicon Definition

*Strong's:* "from [H1397 geber] and [H410 ʾēl]; man of God; Gabriel, an archangel."

The name is a sentence-name: the noun *geber* ("strong/valiant man") in construct with the divine element *ʾēl*. Traditional glosses are "Man of God," "Mighty One of God," or "God is my strength." In Jewish Second-Temple literature (1 Enoch, 4Q Songs of the Sabbath Sacrifice) Gabriel is reckoned among the chief angels that stand before the divine throne.

## Semantic / Functional Categories

Because Gabriel is a proper name with only two OT tokens, there are no translational categories. Instead the usage falls into **functional patterns** — what Gabriel does in the text.

### 1. Commissioned Interpreter of Visions (Dan 8:16)
- **Dan 8:16** — "I heard a man's voice between [the banks of] Ulai, which called, and said, **Gabriel**, make this [man] to understand the vision."
- Gabriel is summoned by a higher voice and instructed to explain the ram-and-goat vision to Daniel. He is positioned as a mediating, explanatory figure — not the one who gives the vision, but the one who unpacks it.

### 2. Messenger of Chronological Revelation (Dan 9:21)
- **Dan 9:21** — "Yea, whiles I [was] speaking in prayer, even **the man Gabriel**, whom I had seen in the vision at the beginning, being caused to fly swiftly, touched me about the time of the evening oblation."
- Gabriel returns (note: "whom I had seen in the vision at the beginning" — a back-reference to Dan 8) to deliver the Seventy-Weeks oracle. His arrival is synchronized with the time of the evening sacrifice, tying the heavenly messenger to temple rhythms.

### 3. (NT Continuation) — not within H1403 but via the Hebrew-origin Greek form G1043 Γαβριήλ
- **Luke 1:19** — "I am Gabriel, that stand in the presence of God; and am sent to speak unto thee, and to shew thee these glad tidings." (to Zacharias)
- **Luke 1:26** — "the angel Gabriel was sent from God unto a city of Galilee, named Nazareth" (to Mary)
- The NT preserves the same two patterns: Gabriel explains/announces and stands in God's presence. See G1043 and the cross-testament trace file.
