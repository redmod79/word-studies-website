## Semantic Categories

### 1. Relentless Attrition of the Saints
The single usage is figurative — not physical wearing down but psychological and spiritual harassment, grinding down over time.

- **Dan 7:25** — "And he shall speak great words against the most High, and shall wear out (יְבַלֵּא) the saints of the most High, and think to change times and laws: and they shall be given into his hand until a time and times and the dividing of time."

## Related Words

### Word Family / Cognates
- **H1086 בָּלָה (balah)** — Hebrew root: to fail, wear out, decay, become old (physical sense: garments wasting, body aging). Compare Deut 8:4; 29:4; Ps 102:26.
- **H1087 בָּלֶה (baleh)** — adjective: worn out, old
- **H1094 בְּלוֹא (belo)** — noun: worn-out things, rags (hapax: Jer 38:11–12)

The Aramaic verb narrows and intensifies the root: where Hebrew בָּלָה describes slow physical decay, the Pael בְּלָא applies the concept to the act of wearing down persons by relentless pressure.
