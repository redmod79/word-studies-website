# Prophetic Double Address and Corporate Personification (Hebrew)

## Definition

Prophetic discourse can address a ruler, city, kingdom, or representative figure in adjacent or overlapping speech units. Hebrew grammar often uses singular address for a corporate entity and can shift between city, ruler, dynasty, and people without requiring the interpreter to flatten all referents into one level.

1. **Double address** - adjacent oracle headings address different title-forms or targets within one larger judgment complex.
2. **Corporate personification** - a city/nation is addressed with singular pronouns and verbs as though it were one person.
3. **Representative ruler grammar** - a king/prince can stand grammatically for a polity, dynasty, or office.
4. **Referent layering** - grammar may permit multiple levels, but it does not decide their theological hierarchy.

## Form Recognition

- Singular vocative or proper-name city address: `צִידֹון`, `צֹר`, `בַּת־צִיּוֹן`.
- Singular pronominal suffixes for cities/nations: `+2fs`, `+3fs`, `+2ms`, `+3ms`.
- Ruler/title construct chains: `נְגִיד צֹר`, `מֶלֶךְ צֹור`, `מֶלֶךְ בָּבֶל`.
- New unit markers: `וַיְהִי דְבַר־יְהוָה`, `כֹּה אָמַר`, imperatives to speak/prophesy/lament.
- Recognition of corporate shift requires tracking agreement, suffix gender/number, and oracle headings together.

## Diagnostic Rule

Do not flatten referents merely because singular grammar is used. In prophetic address:

- Singular masculine ruler title can represent a named office or royal person.
- Singular feminine city address can represent a city as a corporate body.
- `house of X` can designate a corporate people or dynasty.
- A new speech formula can mark a new address layer inside the same chapter.

Grammar distinguishes these levels; theology and exegesis decide how they relate.
