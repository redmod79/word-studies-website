## Semantic Categories

### 1. Priestly Ordination Sacrifice — "Ram of Consecration"
The dominant usage. *ʼAyil hammilluʼim* ("ram of the consecrations") designates the specific sacrifice offered during priestly ordination. The ritual includes hand-laying, blood application, wave offering, and sacrificial meal.

- **Exo 29:22** — "Also thou shalt take of the ram the fat and the rump... for it is a ram of consecration"
- **Exo 29:26** — "thou shalt take the breast of the ram of Aaron's consecration, and wave it for a wave offering before the LORD"
- **Exo 29:31** — "thou shalt take the ram of the consecration, and seethe his flesh in the holy place"
- **Lev 8:22** — "he brought the other ram, the ram of consecration: and Aaron and his sons laid their hands upon the head of the ram"
- **Lev 8:29** — "of the ram of consecration it was Moses' part; as the LORD commanded Moses"

### 2. Consecration Offerings as Completed Whole
Used collectively for the consecration sacrifices (including bread and portions) as a unified sacred entity.

- **Exo 29:34** — "if ought of the flesh of the consecrations, or of the bread, remain unto the morning, then thou shalt burn the remainder with fire"
- **Lev 8:28** — "they were consecrations for a sweet savour: it is an offering made by fire unto the LORD"
- **Lev 8:31** — "eat it with the bread that is in the basket of consecrations"

### 3. Ordination Period
The seven-day duration of the consecration process.

- **Lev 8:33** — "ye shall not go out of the door of the tabernacle of the congregation in seven days, until the days of your consecration be at an end: for seven days shall he consecrate you"
- **Lev 7:37** — "This is the law of the burnt offering, of the meat offering, and of the sin offering, and of the trespass offering, and of the consecrations, and of the sacrifice of the peace offerings"

### 4. Gem Setting (Literal "Filling")
The original concrete sense of "filling" — gems set into a mount or socket. Appears only in tabernacle/temple material lists.

- **Exo 25:7** — "Onyx stones, and stones to be set in the ephod, and in the breastplate"
- **Exo 35:9** — "onyx stones, and stones to be set for the ephod, and for the breastplate"
- **Exo 35:27** — "the rulers brought onyx stones, and stones to be set, for the ephod, and for the breastplate"
- **1Ch 29:2** — "onyx stones, and stones to be set, glistering stones, and of divers colours, and all manner of precious stones"
