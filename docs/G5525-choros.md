## Semantic Categories

### 1. Communal Celebration / Festive Dancing
- **Luk 15:25** — "he heard musick and dancing" — The elder son hears χορός paired with συμφωνία (symphonia, "music/instruments") as the household celebrates the prodigal's return. The word denotes a group dance as part of joyful festivity.

This is the only NT occurrence, so only one semantic category can be derived from the text. The word's broader semantic range is established through its LXX usage (see Hebrew Source section), where it consistently renders Hebrew words for communal round dance in contexts of:
- Victory celebration (Exod 15:20 — Miriam after the Red Sea crossing)
- Welcoming warriors (Judg 11:34 — Jephthah's daughter)
- Temple worship (Psa 150:4 — "Praise him with the timbrel and dance")
- Loss and mourning for lost joy (Lam 5:15 — "our dance is turned into mourning")

## Hebrew Source

χορός serves as the standard LXX rendering for two closely related Hebrew dance nouns, both derived from the root ח-ו-ל (ch-w-l, "to whirl"):

| Hebrew Source | Strong's | Co-occ | PMI | Notes |
|---------------|----------|--------|-----|-------|
| מְחֹלָה (mecholah) | H4246 | 5 | 10.18 | Feminine noun; "a dance"; 8 OT occurrences — Exod 15:20, 32:19, Judg 11:34, 21:21, 1 Sam 18:6, 21:12, 29:5, Song 7:1 |
| מָחוֹל (machol) | H4234 | 4 | 10.08 | Masculine noun; "a (round) dance"; 6 OT occurrences — Psa 30:12, 149:3, 150:4, Jer 31:4, 31:13, Lam 5:15 |
| תֹּף (toph) | H8596 | 5 | 8.98 | "Tambourine/timbrel"; not a translation equivalent but a frequent contextual companion — dance and timbrel appear together |

The LXX translators consistently chose χορός ("round dance") for both machol and mecholah, confirming that the ancient understanding of these Hebrew words was unambiguously "dance" — a circular, communal physical movement — rather than any musical instrument or pipe. This is significant because both Hebrew nouns derive from the root חוּל (chuwl, H2342), whose primary meaning is "to twist, whirl in a circular manner," and the LXX rendering preserves exactly this choreographic sense.
