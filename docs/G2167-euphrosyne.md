## Lexicon Definition

> From the same as εὔφρων; joyfulness: — gladness, joy. (Strong's)

A noun denoting the state or experience of gladness — an interior cheerfulness that naturally expresses itself in festive and social contexts. In classical and Koine Greek εὐφροσύνη regularly connotes the gladness of a shared meal, a feast, or a celebration (cf. LXX Est 9:17–19; Isa 22:13; Sir 30:16, 22). Distinct from χαρά (chará, G5479), which emphasizes the affective state of joy itself, εὐφροσύνη tends to emphasize its outward, festal, communal expression — the mood of a banquet, not merely the inward condition of the heart.

## Semantic Categories

Because the NT occurrences are only two, categories are best drawn from the combined NT + LXX semantic profile, grouped by the contexts the word fills.

### 1. Eschatological / Resurrection Gladness (the Lukan use)
- **Act 2:28** — Peter, citing Psa 16:11 LXX: "thou shalt make me full of joy (εὐφροσύνης) with thy countenance." Here euphrosynē is the gladness of resurrection life, of being restored to the presence of God.

### 2. Providential Gladness from Common Grace
- **Act 14:17** — Paul at Lystra: God "gave us rain from heaven, and fruitful seasons, filling our hearts with food and gladness (εὐφροσύνης)." The gladness that accompanies food, harvest, and daily provision — a witness to God's goodness to all humanity.

### 3. (LXX) Festival Gladness "before the LORD"
In the LXX, euphrosynē repeatedly translates simchâh (H8057) in the Deuteronomic festival contexts where Israel is commanded to rejoice before the LORD at the central sanctuary:
- **Deu 28:47 LXX** — serving God "with joyfulness (εὐφροσύνῃ) and with gladness of heart"
- **Psa 15:11 LXX (=16:11 MT)** — "in thy presence is fulness of joy (εὐφροσύνης)" — the verse Peter quotes in Act 2:28
- Isa 22:13; 35:10; 51:3, 11; 55:12 LXX — Zion's restoration characterized by euphrosynē

### 4. (LXX) Banquet / Wine Gladness
- **Est 9:17–19 LXX** — Purim celebrated as "days of feasting and euphrosynē"
- **Ecc 9:7 LXX** — "eat thy bread with euphrosynē, and drink thy wine with a merry heart"
- **Sir 31:28 LXX** — "wine… is the euphrosynē of heart"
- **Psa 103:15 LXX (=104:15 MT)** — "wine that maketh glad (εὐφραίνει) the heart of man" (cognate verb)

**Semantic bridge note:** The dominant Hebrew source is H8057 simchâh (72x). This is the word used in the Deuteronomic imperatives and festival narratives — where Israel is commanded to bring tithe/offering to the sanctuary and "rejoice before the LORD." Peter's use in Act 2:28 draws directly on this LXX vocabulary (quoting Psa 16:11 LXX verbatim), and Paul's use in Act 14:17 echoes the same creation/harvest/table gladness that Deut 14:26 and 16:11–15 legislate.
