# WG: Fulfillment / Fullness

## Overview

The Greek NT vocabulary of filling and fulfillment clusters around the root πλήρ- (plēr-) together with two completion verbs (τελειόω, συντελέω) that overlap in the "fulfill" semantic space. The group expresses: physical fullness, divine impartation, completion of prophetic prediction, and eschatological completion.

## Key Distinctions

**teleō vs. teleióō:** teleō (G5055) marks the completion of a specific action or event (a discourse ends, a course is finished, the thousand years expire). teleióō (G5048) brings something to its designed end-state or moral perfection — it emphasizes the *quality* of completion. The high priest's prayers "complete/perfect" believers; the risen Christ was "perfected" through obedience (Heb 5:9).

**teleō vs. plēróō:** both are translated "fulfill" but from different angles. teleō emphasizes reaching the end-point or terminus; plēróō emphasizes filling to capacity or executing a textual prediction. John 19:28–30 shows the contrast: plēróō ("that the scripture might be fulfilled") immediately followed by teleō ("It is finished").

**plēróō vs. synteleō:** synteleō adds the prefix σύν- (together/completely) to τελέω (complete) — it emphasizes total, exhaustive ending of an age or covenant. plēróō emphasizes the content being filled or prediction being enacted. synteleō occurs in "end of the age" contexts; plēróō occurs in "that the scripture might be..." contexts.

**plēróō vs. plēróma:** plēróō is the act; plērōma is the result — the sum total that fills, the state of fullness. Pauline use of plērōma is distinctly theological: the full complement of divinity dwelling in Christ (Col 1:19; 2:9); the corporate fullness of the church as Christ's body (Eph 1:23).

## Key Passages Where Multiple Group Words Appear Together

- **Eph 1:23** — "the fulness (πλήρωμα) of him that filleth (πληρόω) all in all" — verb + noun together
- **John 15:11 / 17:13 / 2 Jhn 1:12** — joy "fulfilled" (πληρόω) + "full" (πλήρης) — verb and adjective in joy contexts
