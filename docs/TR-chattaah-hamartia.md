**Semantic shift in translation:** The LXX translation is highly faithful — G266 ἁμαρτία
preserves the abstract/moral sense of chattaah. However, the LXX handles chattaah's
dual meaning (sin + sin offering) by sometimes rendering the sacrifice sense with
ὁλοκαύτωμα (burnt offering) or omitting the article, losing the precise cultic
specificity that the Hebrew carries. The boundary between chattaah = offense and
chattaah = remedy becomes less sharp in Greek.

## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same:**
- The core meaning of ἁμαρτία in the NT exactly matches chattaah's moral/legal sense — a transgression that creates guilt and requires remedy
- The forensic frame: sin against a holy God who will not clear the guilty (Exo 34:7)
- The need for atonement: chattaah presupposes kaphar (covering); NT ἁμαρτία presupposes ἱλαστήριον (propitiation, Rom 3:25)

**What shifts:**
- In the OT, chattaah explicitly names the sacrifice (the sin offering is itself a chattaah). In the NT, ἁμαρτία rarely names the sacrifice directly — though 2 Cor 5:21 deliberately recovers this double sense when Paul says Christ "was made sin" (not just "died for sin")
- The NT concentrates on sin as an enslaving power (Rom 6–7) more than the OT's focus on specific acts and their sacrificial remedy — a development enabled by the LXX's abstraction of chattaah into ἁμαρτία
- The prophetic triad (chattaah + avon + pesha) dissolves in NT Greek: all three typically collapse into ἁμαρτία or παράπτωμα, losing the Hebrew precision
