## Lexicon Definition

σωφροσύνη — "soundness of mind, i.e. (literally) sanity or (figuratively) self-control." Derived from σώφρων (G4998) via the base σῶς ("safe/sound") + φρήν ("mind"). The noun names the abstract virtue/state of possessing a mind that is intact, self-governed, and appropriately restrained — not merely moral sobriety but the settled inner disposition opposed to μανία (madness) on one side and to ἀσωτία (dissoluteness) and προπέτεια (rashness) on the other.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Sound-Minded Rational Speech (opposed to madness)
- **Acts 26:25** — "I am not mad, most noble Festus; but speak forth the words of truth and soberness (sōphrosýnē)." Here σωφροσύνη is explicitly contrasted with μαίνομαι (G3105, "to be mad/raving"). Paul claims his testimony is not delusional enthusiasm but the disciplined, coherent utterance of a sound mind.

### 2. Outward Modesty Flowing from Inward Self-Mastery
- **1 Tim 2:9** — "that women adorn themselves in modest apparel (ἐν καταστολῇ κοσμίῳ), with shamefacedness (αἰδοῦς) and sobriety (sōphrosýnēs); not with broided hair, or gold, or pearls, or costly array." Paired with αἰδώς (modest reverence) and contrasted with ostentatious display. σωφροσύνη here is the interior disposition that produces modest outward form.

### 3. Abiding Christian Character Paired with Faith, Love, Holiness
- **1 Tim 2:15** — "she shall be saved in childbearing, if they continue in faith and charity and holiness with sobriety (μετὰ σωφροσύνης)." σωφροσύνη is the accompanying qualifier of the triad πίστις / ἀγάπη / ἁγιασμός — not an isolated virtue but the settled temperament that frames the whole triad.

## Lexicon Definition

σώφρων — "safe (sound) in mind, i.e. self-controlled, discreet, temperate." The adjective of the σωφρ- family; describes the *person* who possesses σωφροσύνη (G4997). In Greek ethics, one of the four cardinal virtues (alongside φρόνησις, ἀνδρεία, δικαιοσύνη). In the Pastorals, a required trait of elders/bishops, older men, and older women who then teach the same trait to younger women.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Qualification for Overseers/Elders
- **1 Tim 3:2** — "A bishop then must be blameless... vigilant (νηφάλεος), sober (sōphrōn), of good behaviour (kósmios), given to hospitality, apt to teach." Listed between external alertness and orderly bearing.

### 2. Character of Older Men in the Congregation
- **Titus 2:2** — "That the aged men be sober (nēphaleos), grave (semnos), temperate (sōphrōn), sound in faith, in charity, in patience." Here σώφρων is paired with νηφάλεος (vigilance) and σεμνός (gravitas) as the mature masculine profile.

### 3. Character to Be Trained in Younger Women
- **Titus 2:5** — "[To be] discreet (sōphrōn), chaste, keepers at home, good, obedient to their own husbands." Here σώφρων is what σωφρονίζω (G4994, Tit 2:4) trains into the νέαι — the end product of being "sober-minded-ized" by the older women.
