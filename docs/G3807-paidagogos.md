## Lexicon Definition

From παῖς (G3816) and a reduplicated form of ἄγω (G71); a boy-leader, i.e. a servant whose office it was to take children to school and supervise their conduct — a child-conductor, a tutor or guardian.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Temporary Guardian Leading to Maturity (Galatians)
- **Gal 3:24** — "Wherefore the law was our schoolmaster to bring us unto Christ, that we might be justified by faith."
- **Gal 3:25** — "But after that faith is come, we are no longer under a schoolmaster."

The law functions as a paidagōgos: a supervisory guide whose authority terminates when the child reaches maturity (= the coming of faith in Christ). The role is protective and regulatory, not instructional in the modern sense.

### 2. Contrasted with Fatherhood (1 Corinthians)
- **1Co 4:15** — "For though ye have ten thousand instructors in Christ, yet have ye not many fathers: for in Christ Jesus I have begotten you through the gospel."

The paidagōgos is explicitly set against the patēr. Many can function as supervisors; Paul claims the unique relational role of spiritual father through the gospel. The contrast highlights the paidagōgos as secondary and replaceable versus the singular bond of parentage.
