## Lexicon Definition

ἱλαστήριον (*hil-as-tay-ree-on*); neuter of a derivative of ἱλάσκομαι (G2433); an expiatory (place or thing), i.e., a means of appeasing or expiating. Used in two senses:

1. **The mercy seat** — the golden lid of the Ark of the Covenant in the Holy of Holies, sprinkled with expiatory blood on Yom Kippur (Heb 9:5). The LXX uses ἱλαστήριον to render Hebrew כַּפֹּרֶת (kapporeth) throughout Exodus and Leviticus.

2. **An expiatory sacrifice / propitiatory victim** — the means of expiation itself; that by which divine righteousness is satisfied and sin is covered (Rom 3:25). (Thayer)

## Semantic Categories

### 1. The Mercy Seat (Physical Tabernacle Object)
- **Heb 9:5** — "And over it the cherubims of glory shadowing the mercyseat; of which we cannot now speak particularly."

The author of Hebrews describes the Tabernacle furniture and identifies the golden lid of the Ark flanked by cherubim as the ἱλαστήριον. This matches the LXX usage throughout Exodus and Leviticus (e.g., Exo 25:22: "I will meet with thee from above the mercy seat").

### 2. Christ as the Propitiatory
- **Rom 3:25** — "Whom God hath set forth [to be] a propitiation through faith in his blood, to declare his righteousness for the remission of sins that are past, through the forbearance of God."

Paul applies ἱλαστήριον to Christ himself. The anarthrous form (no article) highlights function: Christ *is* the propitiatory — both the place where divine righteousness is publicly displayed and the means by which sin is covered through blood. The typological chain from the OT mercy seat is intentional: what happened annually by priestly blood-sprinkling at the kapporeth now happens once-for-all publicly at the cross.
