## Semantic Categories

### 1. Great Sin — Egregious Moral Offense
The dominant usage pattern. In 5 of 8 verses, chăṭâʼâh is modified by "great" (gâdôwl H1419) and describes a grave communal or personal offense that provokes divine or human outrage.

- **Gen 20:9** — "thou hast brought on me and on my kingdom a great sin" (Abimelech to Abraham regarding the deception about Sarah)
- **Exo 32:21** — "thou hast brought so great a sin upon them" (Moses confronting Aaron over the golden calf)
- **Exo 32:30** — "Ye have sinned a great sin: and now I will go up unto the LORD; peradventure I shall make an atonement for your sin" (Moses to the people)
- **Exo 32:31** — "this people have sinned a great sin, and have made them gods of gold" (Moses to YHWH)
- **2 Ki 17:21** — "Jeroboam drave Israel from following the LORD, and made them sin a great sin" (Jeroboam's apostasy summarized)

### 2. Sin as Moral-Legal Liability
In judicial and prayer contexts, chăṭâʼâh names the sin that stands as a charge or burden.

- **Psa 32:1** — "Blessed is he whose transgression is forgiven, whose sin is covered" (pesha + chăṭâʼâh paired)
- **Psa 109:7** — "let his prayer become sin" (imprecatory — the condemned man's very prayer is counted against him)

### 3. Sin Offering (Cultic)
One occurrence carries the sacrificial sense shared with H2403.

- **Psa 40:6** — "burnt offering and sin offering hast thou not required" (contrasting ritual sacrifice with obedience; paired with ʻôlâh H5930)
