## Relationships

### Shared Roots
- **mâʻôwz** (H4581), **ʻôz** (H5797), and **ʻaz** (H5794) all derive from the root עוז (to be strong). The mâ- prefix of mâʻôwz marks it as a noun of place — "a place of strength" — while ʻôz names the quality itself and ʻaz describes a subject possessing it.
- **kôach** (H3581) and **tsûwr** (H6697) are from unrelated roots; they are included as easily-confused words sharing KJV "strength" and "rock" translations with the above group.
