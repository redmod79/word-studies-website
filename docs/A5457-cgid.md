## Semantic Categories

### 1. Commanded Worship of an Idol
The decree requiring prostration before the golden image at the sound of music.
- **Dan 3:5** — "ye fall down and worship the golden image that Nebuchadnezzar the king hath set up"
- **Dan 3:10** — "every man that shall hear the sound... shall fall down and worship the golden image"
- **Dan 3:15** — "if ye be ready that at what time ye hear the sound... ye fall down and worship the image which I have made"

### 2. Mass Compliance with Worship Decree
The nations carrying out the command.
- **Dan 3:7** — "all the people, the nations, and the languages, fell down and worshipped the golden image"

### 3. Refusal to Worship
The three Hebrews' defiance — the word occurs in accusations, interrogation, and their own declaration.
- **Dan 3:12** — "these men, O king, have not regarded thee: they serve not thy gods, nor worship the golden image"
- **Dan 3:14** — "do not ye serve my gods, nor worship the golden image which I have set up?"
- **Dan 3:18** — "we will not serve thy gods, nor worship the golden image which thou hast set up"
- **Dan 3:28** — "yielded their bodies, that they might not serve nor worship any god, except their own God"

### 4. Penalty Clause for Non-Worship
The threatened punishment for those who refuse prostration.
- **Dan 3:6** — "whoso falleth not down and worshippeth shall the same hour be cast into the midst of a burning fiery furnace"
- **Dan 3:11** — "whoso falleth not down and worshippeth, that he should be cast into the midst of a burning fiery furnace"

### 5. Homage to a Person
Nebuchadnezzar's prostration before Daniel after the dream revelation — the only non-idol usage.
- **Dan 2:46** — "the king Nebuchadnezzar fell upon his face, and worshipped Daniel"
