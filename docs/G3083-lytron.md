## Semantic Categories

### 1. Substitutionary Price for Life

Both NT occurrences present λύτρον as the price Jesus gives — his own life — in exchange for the release of "many."

- **Mat 20:28** — "Even as the Son of man came not to be ministered unto, but to minister, and to give his life a ransom for many."
- **Mar 10:45** — "For even the Son of man came not to be ministered unto, but to minister, and to give his life a ransom for many."

The construction δοῦναι τὴν ψυχὴν αὐτοῦ λύτρον ἀντὶ πολλῶν ("to give his life a ransom for many") places λύτρον as the payment and ἀντί ("in the place of") as the marker of substitution. Only one semantic category exists for the NT usage. The word's broader semantic range (prices paid for slave manumission, prisoner release, consecration redemption) is attested in the LXX and extrabiblical Greek but not in the NT itself.

### 2. OT Background — Redemption Price (LXX Usage)

The lexicon's outline of biblical usage lists Exodus 21:30, Leviticus 19:20, Numbers 35:31, and Isaiah 45:13 — all LXX occurrences where λύτρον translates Hebrew כֹּפֶר (kopher, H3724) or related terms for a ransom price paid to redeem life:

- **Exo 21:30 (LXX)** — ransom price for the owner of an ox that kills a person
- **Num 35:31 (LXX)** — "ye shall take no ransom for the life of a murderer"
- **Isa 45:13 (LXX)** — "not for price nor reward" (Cyrus's release of Israel)
