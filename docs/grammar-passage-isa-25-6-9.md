# Grammar Analysis: Isaiah 25:6–9

## Overview

A compact eschatological oracle — the climactic "Mountain Banquet" at the center of the Isaianic "Apocalypse" (Isa 24–27). The passage unfolds in four panels:

- **v. 6** — YHWH of hosts prepares a feast for all peoples on "this mountain" (בָּהָר הַזֶּה, retrospective to Isa 24:23 / Zion).
- **v. 7** — YHWH "swallows" (בִלַּע Piel perf.) the shrouding covering over the nations.
- **v. 8** — YHWH "swallows up death forever" (בִּלַּע הַמָּוֶת לָנֶצַח); wipes every tear; removes his people's reproach.
- **v. 9** — The redeemed community's antiphonal *weqatal* response: "Behold, this is our God… we have waited for him."

Genre: apocalyptic-eschatological poetic oracle. Domain tagged `Q` (Quotation/prophetic-direct) across all clauses by the ETCBC parser — the entire block is YHWH's / the prophet's first-order eschatological pronouncement, not embedded narrative.

## Key Grammatical Features

### 1. מִשְׁתֶּה — banquet-noun as construct-head (v. 6, twice)

- **Form:** Noun.ms.Cst; lemma משׁתה (Strong's H4960), verbal noun from שׁתה "to drink" with the מ- preformative of place/instrument/abstraction (GKC §85e).
- **Semantic range:** The noun names an occasion dominated by drinking (cf. Gen 21:8; 29:22; Est 1:3, 5; 5:4; 7:2; 9:17). It is *not* a generic word for meal (אֲרֻחָה) or a sacrificial banquet (זֶבַח); it highlights the **wine-component**, which is why the following construct absolutes are all dairy/oil/wine words.
- **Doubled construct-head:** מִשְׁתֵּה שְׁמָנִים || מִשְׁתֵּה שְׁמָרִים sets up a two-commodity banquet (fats/oils + aged wines), then the second bicolon re-names each commodity with an intensifying participle, building an **ABAʹBʹ chiasmus by expansion**.
- **Significance:** The banquet is a **royal-scale covenant meal** (compare the Sinai covenant meal Exod 24:9–11, where elders "ate and drank" in YHWH's presence). Located "on this mountain" (Zion), it extends the inner-Israel covenant meal to **כָּל־הָעַמִּים** — "all the peoples", a universalizing move that is one of the strongest "nations-included" texts in Isa 24–27.
- **Word study (gap):** No canonical entry yet for H4960 מִשְׁתֶּה / H8354 שׁתה. Candidate for the word-studies library.

### 2. שְׁמָרִים — "dregs / lees" (v. 6)

- **Form:** Noun.mp.Abs; lemma שׁמר (Strong's H8105), *plurale tantum* for wine-lees.
- **Lexical:** שְׁמָרִים are the sediment left when wine matures on its own yeast; "wine on the lees" is **aged, undisturbed, fully-matured wine** — a luxury good in the ANE. Jer 48:11 ("Moab… has settled on his lees") uses the same image for long undisturbed ease. Zeph 1:12 ("men who thicken on their dregs") uses it pejoratively.
- **Paired with מְזֻקָּקִים (Pual ptc., below):** "wines on the lees **well-refined**" — the wine has been aged long enough on the yeast to develop full body, *then* racked / clarified so that the dregs are left behind. This is an oxymoron-by-process: the **best** of aged-with-its-own-dregs wine *without* the dregs actually in the cup. Culturally, this is the highest-quality wine available — the equivalent today of a vintage that has been aged in bottle and decanted.

### 3. מְמֻחָיִם and מְזֻקָּקִים — paired Pual participles (v. 6)

- **Form:** Both are Verb.**Pual**.Ptcp.mp.Abs. Pual is the passive counterpart of Piel — factitive/intensive *passive* ("caused to be X, and X-ness is intense/complete").
  - מְמֻחָיִם — Pual ptc. mp of מחה (lemma gloss "wipe / fill up"; here "filled with marrow"; the verb root carries "rich, juicy fatness") — predicated of שְׁמָנִים; i.e. "fat cuts *thoroughly shot-through with marrow*". Hapax as Pual participle of מחה.
  - מְזֻקָּקִים — Pual ptc. mp of זקק "to refine, strain, purify" — predicated of שְׁמָרִים; i.e. aged-lees wines that have been *thoroughly clarified / strained*. Elsewhere used of metal refining (Mal 3:3 of Levi; Job 28:1; 36:27; 1 Chr 28:18; 29:4; Ps 12:6 — "the words of YHWH are purified words, silver מְזֻקָּק").
- **Significance:** Paired Pual participles in a banquet list are extremely rare. The passive voice matters theologically — the food and wine have been *acted upon* (prepared, refined, perfected) before the banquet begins. The guests find the feast already in its perfected state; the agency is YHWH's (v. 6 opener עָשָׂה יְהוָה "YHWH prepared").
- **Ps 12:6 lexical link:** "the words of YHWH are מְזֻקָּקִים, refined seven times" — same Pual ptc. form, now applied to divine speech. The poetic move in Isa 25:6 is to treat *banquet hospitality* with the same vocabulary normally reserved for *metallurgical perfection of divine utterance* — the banquet is therefore qualitatively divine-grade.
- **Grammar reference gap:** No canonical entry yet for stem-pual. Candidate for the library.

### 4. בִּלַּע — Piel perfect "swallow/engulf" (v. 7, v. 8)

- **Form:** Verb.**Piel.Perf.3ms** of בלע (Strong's H1104). The Piel of בלע is the default attested stem for this verb (Qal is rare); carries factitive/resultative force: "cause-to-be-swallowed = utterly destroy, engulf, annihilate".
- **Twice in two verses** — the rhetorical hinge of the oracle. v. 7 "he has swallowed the covering"; v. 8 "he has swallowed **the Death**".
- **v. 8 opens asyndetically** (no *waw*, clause-type ZQt0) — the absence of the conjunction accelerates the discourse at the climactic line. The single-word predicate בִּלַּע hits first, then the object הַמָּוֶת (with the article — "**the** Death", i.e. Death-as-entity, personified), then the durative adverbial לָנֶצַח.
- **לָנֶצַח** — Prep לְ + נֶצַח (Strong's H5331 "perpetuity, duration, glory, eminence"). Here = "forever, for all time, in perpetuity" (most common sense; e.g. Ps 49:10; Amos 1:11; Job 14:20). The KJV's "in victory" reflects the LXX translator's κατίσχυσεν ὁ θάνατος καταπιών "death prevailed having swallowed-up" — the LXX reversed the Hebrew subject-object relationship and rendered לָנֶצַח as *eis nikos* "unto victory". Paul in **1 Cor 15:54** follows the LXX sense (κατεπόθη ὁ θάνατος εἰς νῖκος), reading it as the *eschatological consummation* of the Hebrew oracle.
- **Significance:** The verb normally used for Death's devouring of humans (cf. the serpent-sea-monster-Sheol mythology of Num 16:30–33; Prov 1:12; Isa 5:14 "Sheol enlarged its appetite and swallowed [בָּלְעָה]"; Jon 2:1/LXX; cognate Ugaritic *bl' "Mot swallows Baal"*) is here **reversed** — YHWH swallows Death itself. This is the most explicit OT reversal of the Death-devourer motif.
- **Word-study gap:** No canonical entry for H1104 בלע. High priority for the library given the NT citation chain (1 Cor 15:54 → Rev 21:4).

### 5. הַלֹּוט / הַמַּסֵּכָה / הַנְּסוּכָה — the covering-veil (v. 7)

- הַלֹּוט — Qal ptc. ms of לוט "enwrap, envelop" (Strong's H3874), used substantively with the article = "the enwrapping(-veil)". The doubling הַלֹּוט ׀ הַלֹּוט (repeated lemma with *paseq* disjunction) is a rare rhetorical intensifier — "the enwrapping, the enwrapping **itself**" / "the utter covering".
- הַמַּסֵּכָה — Noun.fs.Abs (Strong's H4541) "molten image / woven covering". Homograph: (a) "molten-metal idol" (Exod 32:4; Deut 9:12) and (b) "woven covering" (Isa 28:20; here). Context (parallel to הַלֹּוט) = covering/veil.
- הַנְּסוּכָה — Qal passive/stative ptc. fs of נסך "to pour out, spread, weave" (Strong's H5258/H5259); attributive relative to מַסֵּכָה. Parsed by ETCBC as a **separate attributive clause (Ptcp)** — הַנְּסוּכָה עַל־כָּל־הַגֹּויִם "the-one-spread over all the nations".
- **Significance:** Metaphor of a mourning-veil / funereal cloth / shroud stretched over every face of every nation — what YHWH "swallows" in v. 7 is immediately identified in v. 8 as **הַמָּוֶת itself**. The shroud = death's pall. Removing the shroud and abolishing death are **one action viewed twice**.

### 6. יָסִיר — Hiphil imperfect with object-fronting (v. 8)

- **Form:** Verb.Hiphil.Impf.3ms of סור "turn aside" → Hiphil "cause-to-turn-aside = remove".
- **Clause type WxY0 with object fronting:** וְחֶרְפַּת עַמֹּו יָסִיר — the object (חֶרְפַּת עַמֹּו "the reproach of his people") precedes the verb. Object-fronting in a *yiqtol* clause flanked by two *qatal* clauses (בִּלַּע… מָחָה… יָסִיר… דִּבֵּר) produces a **topicalized/contrastive focus** on "his people's reproach" — the audience's own shame is singled out and separately promised removal.
- The *yiqtol* form amid the *qatal* panels is not a tense shift; it's a **pragmatic aspect shift** — "the reproach-of-his-people — *that* he will (keep on) removing, from over all the earth".

### 7. כִּי יְהוָה דִּבֵּר — prophetic signature formula (v. 8)

- **Form:** XQtl clause — causal/assertive כִּי + Subj + Piel perf. דִּבֵּר (Strong's H1696, Piel = "speak decisively, authoritatively utter").
- **Stock formula** closing divine-oracle units in Isaiah: 1:2, 20; 21:17; 22:25; 24:3; 25:8; 40:5; 58:14; (cf. "YHWH of hosts has spoken/purposed" 14:24, 27; 19:4; 22:14). The Piel perfect is performative-completive — "the utterance has been made; it is as good as done".

### 8. קִוִּינוּ לֹו — Piel perfect of קוה (v. 9, twice)

- **Form:** Verb.**Piel.Perf.1cp** of קוה "wait/hope for" (Strong's H6960). Piel = intensive ("wait expectantly, strain toward").
- **Repeated clause-by-clause** (ZQt0 in both clauses 3 and 6) — anaphoric confession of covenant hope. Paired with two demonstrative-*nominal clauses* זֶה אֱלֹהֵינוּ // זֶה יְהוָה (NmCl verbless identification constructions).
- The *qatal* is a **performative present-perfect** ("we have been waiting / we-hereby-declare-our-waiting"); c.f. Isa 8:17, 26:8; Ps 25:3, 5, 21.

### 9. נָגִילָה וְנִשְׂמְחָה — paired 1cp cohortatives (v. 9)

- **Form:** Verb.Qal.Impf.1cp of גיל + שׂמח, both volitional/cohortative ("let us…"). Clause types ZYq0 and WYq0.
- **Double volitional** — the community's self-exhortation to celebrate. Paired synonyms גיל + שׂמח recur in Isa 35:1–2; 65:18–19; 66:10; Ps 14:7; 32:11; 53:7; 118:24. The cohortative closes the oracle on **human response** rather than divine pronouncement — the "feast" has turned into a *celebration with voice*.
- **Grammar reference link:** See [cohortative-1cp-divine-speech](../hebrew/cohortative-1cp-divine-speech.md) for the 1cp cohortative form generally; the Isa 25:9 usage is the *human-communal* mirror of the divine "let us make man" 1cp cohortative. The category in the reference entry is divine-speech; an analogue human-communal-confession category could be added.
- **Word-study link:** [H8055-samach](../../word-studies/H8055-samach.md) covers שׂמח generally.

### 10. וְיֹושִׁיעֵנוּ — Hiphil imperfect with 1cp object-suffix (v. 9)

- **Form:** Verb.Hiphil.Impf.3ms of ישׁע (Strong's H3467) + 1cp object suffix.
- Paired with the cognate noun בִּישׁוּעָתֹו (Noun.fs "his salvation" + 3ms) closing the verse — a **figura etymologica** ("he will save us… in his salvation"): the same root frames the verse's final motion.
- **Grammar reference link:** [hiphil-imperfect](../hebrew/hiphil-imperfect.md).

## Exegetical Assessment — literal new-creation feast, or figurative covenant joy?

The question is a classic crux. The grammar and genre push the reading in a specific direction:

**1. Genre signal.** Isa 24–27 (the "Isaianic Apocalypse") is *proto-apocalyptic*: cosmic-level judgment (24:1–6 "YHWH is emptying the earth"), cosmic-level reversal (24:23 "the moon will be abashed"), feast-on-Zion (25:6–9), resurrection (26:19 "your dead shall live"), and eschatological monster-defeat (27:1 Leviathan). The feast in 25:6–9 sits inside the only OT block that explicitly promises **resurrection + death-annihilation + all-nations-included banquet** together. The surrounding cosmology is not domestic — it is new-creation-scale.

**2. Internal grammatical markers that resist reducing to figurative covenant-joy-only:**
- **לָנֶצַח** (v. 8) — Death is swallowed *forever*, not "in the covenant era". The prep-lamed + נֶצַח combination is temporally unrestricted. Even in Hebrew usage where נֶצַח shades to "glory/completeness", the parallel Hiphil imperfect יָסִיר מֵעַל כָּל־הָאָרֶץ ("from off **all the earth**") universalizes the scope.
- **Universal recipients:** לְכָל־הָעַמִּים (v. 6), עַל־כָּל־הָעַמִּים / עַל־כָּל־הַגֹּויִם (v. 7), כָּל־פָּנִים / כָּל־הָאָרֶץ (v. 8). A purely covenant-renewal reading must shrink "all peoples / all nations / every face / all the earth" — which the text resists lexically and syntactically.
- **הַמָּוֶת with the article** — "the Death" as an entity, not death-events metonymized as sorrow. Personified-entity language (swallowed, with article) outstrips the figurative-covenant-joy register.
- **Pual participles of perfection** (מְמֻחָיִם / מְזֻקָּקִים) — the banquet is depicted in *completed-perfection* state, not as an ordinary covenant feast.

**3. What a figurative-only reading gets right.** The mountain-feast motif *does* draw on the covenant-meal background (Exod 24:9–11; Deut 14:26; the Zion-enthronement imagery of Ps 24, 132). The vocabulary is continuous with covenant-joy vocabulary (גיל / שׂמח / ישׁוּעָה). So the passage is not *discontinuous* from covenant-feast imagery — it is its eschatological *intensification*.

**4. Best reading.** The passage describes a **literal eschatological new-creation banquet** whose imagery is drawn from, and intensifies, the covenant-meal tradition. It is not *either/or*. The figurative covenant-feast vocabulary is the *vehicle*; the *tenor* is the new-creation feast where Death is actually abolished, all nations actually come, and the mourning-shroud is actually removed. Reducing the vehicle by calling it "only figurative covenant joy" leaves v. 8's death-abolition without a referent that matches its grammatical scope.

**5. NT inter-textual confirmation.** The NT reads the oracle as *literal new-creation eschatology*:
- **1 Cor 15:54** quotes Isa 25:8 at the *resurrection* of the dead ("when the mortal puts on immortality, then will come to pass the saying that is written: κατεπόθη ὁ θάνατος εἰς νῖκος"). Paul's citation fixes the referent at the bodily resurrection, not at an intra-historical covenant renewal.
- **Rev 7:17 / 21:4** quote Isa 25:8 twice ("God will wipe away every tear from their eyes"), both in the new-Jerusalem / new-creation context; 21:4 adds "and death shall be no more" — a direct echo of בִּלַּע הַמָּוֶת לָנֶצַח.
- **Rev 19:9** — "Blessed are those invited to the **marriage supper of the Lamb** (τοῦ δείπνου τοῦ γάμου τοῦ ἀρνίου)". The noun δεῖπνον ("banquet-meal") + the nations-included guest list recapitulates Isa 25:6's מִשְׁתֵּה.
- **Matt 8:11** — "many will come from east and west and recline at table with Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob in the kingdom of heaven". The *all-nations-at-the-Zion-feast* structure is Isa 25:6's structure transposed into Jesus' mouth.
- **Matt 22:1–14** — the parable of the marriage-feast of the king's son, with the invitation extending to "as many as you find" after the first guests refuse, is Jesus' re-telling of the Isa 25:6 universal-banquet scene with the twist that the originally-invited nation has excluded itself.
- **Matt 26:29** — "I shall not drink again of this fruit of the vine until that day when I drink it new with you in my Father's kingdom". At the Last Supper Jesus deliberately suspends the wine of the covenant meal until the **eschatological μετά-drinking** — a direct deferral of the Isa 25:6 wine-banquet to the new-creation consummation.

The NT's uniform reading is: Isa 25:6–9 describes the **literal eschatological feast at the consummation**, with the wine-at-the-lees being resumed in the new-creation cup, death actually abolished at resurrection, the shroud actually lifted, and the response actually antiphonally voiced by the redeemed from all nations.
