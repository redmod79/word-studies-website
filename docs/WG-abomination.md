# WG: Abomination

## Overview
The abomination/defilement concept in Scripture involves distinct words that express different facets of what is detestable before God — from physical idols to moral corruption to ritual uncleanness. The Hebrew terms carry into the NT via the LXX βδελ- word family.

## Roles in the Concept

- **Broad moral category:** to'ebah (תּוֹעֵבַה) — the general term for what God finds detestable (moral, social, ritual)
- **Idolatrous object:** shiqquwts (שִׁקּוּץ) — the physically disgusting idol or cult object
- **Worthless idol:** gillul (גִּלּוּל) — the contemptible image (emphasizes futility, from "dung")
- **Ritual uncleanness:** sheqets (שֶׁקֶץ) — the unclean creature or object (dietary/purity laws)
- **Root action (moral):** ta'ab (תַּעָב) — to abhor, make abominable; the verb root of to'ebah — moral/personal loathing
- **Root action (ritual):** shaqats (שָׁקַץ) — to detest, make abominable; the verb root of shiqquwts/sheqets — ritual/dietary repugnance
- **Time-expired sacrifice:** piggûl (פִּגּוּל) — the narrowest term: specifically stale/putrid sacrificial flesh past its lawful eating window
- **Resulting state:** tame' (טָמֵא) — the defilement that results from contact with these things
- **NT abomination noun:** bdelygma (βδέλυγμα) — the LXX catchall rendering of to'ebah/shiqquwts/sheqets; carries Hebrew semantic range into NT (6v)
- **NT abomination adjective:** bdelyktos (βδελυκτός) — "detestable" as a character description (Titus 1:16; 1v)
- **NT abomination verb:** bdelyssomai (βδελύσσομαι) — to abhor, detest; from βδέω "to stink"; visceral physical disgust applied to moral revulsion (2v)
