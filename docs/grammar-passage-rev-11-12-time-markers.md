# Grammar Analysis: Revelation's Prophetic Time-Markers (Rev 11:2, 11:3, 12:6, 12:14, 13:5)

## Scope

A cross-passage grammatical comparison of the five occurrences in Revelation 11–13 that share a single prophetic duration expressed in three equivalent units:

- **42 months** (μῆνας τεσσεράκοντα δύο) — Rev 11:2; Rev 13:5
- **1260 days** (ἡμέρας χιλίας διακοσίας ἑξήκοντα) — Rev 11:3; Rev 12:6
- **time, times, and half a time** (καιρὸν καὶ καιροὺς καὶ ἥμισυ καιροῦ) — Rev 12:14

Cross-referenced with the Aramaic/Hebrew parents of the "time, times, and half" formula in Daniel 7:25 (Aramaic) and Daniel 12:7 (Hebrew).

All Greek parses produced by `D:/bible/tools/greek/greek_parser.py`. Aramaic/Hebrew parses produced by `D:/bible/tools/hebrew/hebrew_parser.py`.

**Clause of interest:** ἐδόθη αὐτῷ ἐξουσία ποιῆσαι μῆνας τεσσεράκοντα δύο — "authority was given to him to act/continue for forty-two months." The governing verbal form for the duration is the aorist infinitive ποιῆσαι, complementing ἐξουσία. The accusative μῆνας τεσσεράκοντα δύο is an **accusative of extent of time** modifying ποιῆσαι. The aorist infinitive here is **constative** — it views the whole span of activity as a single bounded block — not a pointed/punctiliar sense.

## Central Finding: All Five Occurrences Are Durational

Every one of the five Revelation uses of this unit is **grammatically durational** — it describes an event **extended across** the span, not a future-literal repetition beyond it.

Evidence from the parser:

1. **All five duration words are in the accusative** — μῆνας (Rev 11:2, 13:5), ἡμέρας (Rev 11:3, 12:6), καιρὸν / καιροὺς / καιροῦ (Rev 12:14). In Koine Greek, an anarthrous accusative of time with no preposition is the canonical **accusative of extent of time** ("for / throughout / during X time"), not a point-accusative.

2. **All five governing verbs are imperfective-in-span or prospective-in-span**, never punctiliar-beyond-the-span:
   - Rev 11:2 πατήσουσιν — future active indicative (progressive/iterative: "will be treading")
   - Rev 11:3 προφητεύσουσιν — future active indicative (progressive: "will be prophesying")
   - Rev 12:6 τρέφωσιν — **present** active subjunctive (purpose + ongoing)
   - Rev 12:14 τρέφεται — **present** passive indicative (ongoing state)
   - Rev 13:5 ποιῆσαι — aorist infinitive complementing ἐξουσία; constative (whole-span) aorist, not punctiliar. The authority **covers** the span.

3. **None of the five forms is an ingressive aorist or a future punctiliar pointing beyond the span.** There is no grammatical marker ("after X months," "at the end of X days," μετά + acc., ἐν τῇ ἡμέρᾳ τῇ + ordinal, etc.) that would redirect the unit into a point-event at the far end.

4. **Rev 13:5 fits the same pattern as the other four, not a separate pattern.** The governing verbal is an aorist infinitive with an accusative of extent — the same extent-accusative morphology that governs πατήσουσιν (Rev 11:2), προφητεύσουσιν (Rev 11:3), τρέφωσιν (Rev 12:6), and τρέφεται (Rev 12:14). The shared morphology (μῆνας τεσσεράκοντα δύο, identical to Rev 11:2 down to the form) and shared syntactic slot (extent-accusative after a verb of ongoing action) place Rev 13:5 squarely inside the set.

Grammatically, the five forms say: **"for this span, X happens."** They do not say: "after this span, X happens," nor "when this span recurs, X happens."

## Cross-Reference: Rev 12:6 + 12:14 with Rev 13:5–7

Beyond the shared duration, the parser output shows a striking overlap of **actors and actions** between the woman-in-wilderness scenes (Rev 12:6; 12:14) and the beast-warring scene (Rev 13:5–7).

### Shared protagonist/antagonist structure

| Element | Rev 12:6 | Rev 12:14 | Rev 13:5–7 |
|---------|----------|-----------|-------------|
| **Protagonist pursued** | ἡ γυνή (the woman) | τῇ γυναικί (the woman) | τοὺς ἁγίους / οἱ ἅγιοι (the saints) — Rev 13:7 |
| **Antagonist** | (implicit — the dragon of 12:3–4) | ὁ ὄφις (the serpent = dragon, 12:9) | τὸ θηρίον (the beast), but 13:2 — **ἔδωκεν αὐτῷ ὁ δράκων τὴν δύναμιν αὐτοῦ** ("the dragon gave him his power") |
| **Divine passive** ἐδόθη / ἐδόθησαν | τόπον ἡτοιμασμένον ἀπὸ τοῦ Θεοῦ (place prepared by God) | ἐδόθησαν τῇ γυναικὶ αἱ δύο πτέρυγες (two wings were given to the woman) | ἐδόθη αὐτῷ στόμα … ἐδόθη αὐτῷ ἐξουσία (mouth/authority were given to the beast) |
| **Verb of action over span** | τρέφωσιν (Pres Act Subj) — nourished | τρέφεται (Pres Pass Ind) — nourished | ποιῆσαι (Aor Inf) — to act; πολεμῆσαι (Rev 13:7, aor inf) — to make war |
| **Duration** | ἡμέρας χιλίας διακοσίας ἑξήκοντα | καιρὸν καὶ καιροὺς καὶ ἥμισυ καιροῦ | μῆνας τεσσεράκοντα δύο |

### Structural parallels the parser surfaces

1. **Same divine-passive idiom (ἐδόθη / ἐδόθησαν).** Rev 11:2 (ἐδόθη τοῖς ἔθνεσιν), Rev 12:14 (ἐδόθησαν τῇ γυναικί), Rev 13:5 (ἐδόθη αὐτῷ … ἐδόθη αὐτῷ) all use aorist passive of δίδωμι to introduce what occurs over the span. The grammar frames the entire span as a **permitted**/**granted** period on both sides of the conflict: the woman is granted refuge, the nations/beast are granted reach.

2. **Same dragon behind the beast.** Rev 12:14 names the pursuer ὁ ὄφις (serpent), identified at 12:9 as ὁ δράκων ὁ μέγας / ὁ ὄφις ὁ ἀρχαῖος. Rev 13:2 makes the beast the dragon's delegate (ἔδωκεν αὐτῷ ὁ δράκων τὴν δύναμιν αὐτοῦ). The parser shows the same lexical δίδωμι chain tying the dragon's giving (13:2) to the beast's receiving (13:5 ἐδόθη). So grammatically, the "beast acting 42 months" is the outward face of the dragon's activity — the same adversary John has been tracking since chapter 12.

3. **Same duration, three units.** The number 1260 (Rev 12:6) and the phrase "time, times, and half a time" (Rev 12:14) are standardly equivalent (1260 days = 3½ years = 42 months = time+times+half, on a 360-day prophetic year). The parser confirms the Aramaic/Hebrew parent of the "time, times, and half" formula in Dan 7:25 / 12:7. The same 42 months appears at Rev 11:2 and 13:5.

4. **Coextensive description, different angles.** Rev 12:6 / 12:14 describe the span from the **pursued party's** vantage ("she is nourished / kept"); Rev 11:2 / 13:5 describe the same span from the **pursuing party's** vantage ("they tread / he acts"); Rev 11:3 describes it from the **witnesses'** vantage ("they prophesy"). Every vantage uses the same duration, the same ἐδόθη idiom, and the same durational accusative syntax. That is what a single span looks like when five scenes frame it.

No grammatical feature in these five passages signals sequential, non-overlapping spans. The accusatives of extent, the imperfective-in-span verbs, and the shared δίδωμι vocabulary cooperate to present a **single coextensive duration viewed from multiple angles**.

## Key Grammatical Concepts Referenced

- **Accusative of extent of time** — Anarthrous accusative (no preposition) expressing duration; the standard Koine construction for "for X time." Operative in all five Revelation passages on the duration unit itself.
- **Aorist infinitive + accusative of duration (ποιέω idiom)** — At Rev 13:5 (ποιῆσαι μῆνας τεσσεράκοντα δύο) the construction is the ποιέω + accusative-of-time idiom meaning "spend / continue / act throughout" a span (BDAG ποιέω §4.2; cf. Acts 15:33; 18:23; 20:3; 2 Cor 11:25; Jas 4:13). See [aorist-infinitive-accusative-of-duration](../greek/aorist-infinitive-accusative-of-duration.md). This entry is directly relevant to the central finding: the aorist-infinitive + extent-accusative construction is inherently **bounded-span/durational**, not punctiliar-beyond-the-span.
- **Future active indicative (V-FAI)** — Predictive-progressive in prophecy; here describes the span of action, not its start/end point. Used at Rev 11:2 (πατήσουσιν) and 11:3 (προφητεύσουσιν).
- **Present subjunctive in ἵνα clause (V-PAS)** — Ongoing/iterative purpose; used at Rev 12:6 (τρέφωσιν).
- **Present passive indicative (V-PPI)** — State of ongoing reception of action; used at Rev 12:14 (τρέφεται).
- **Constative aorist infinitive (V-AAN)** — Views an action as a whole over a bounded span when complementing a noun like ἐξουσία; used at Rev 13:5 (ποιῆσαι). See also [mood-aorist-imperative](../greek/mood-aorist-imperative.md) for related aorist-aspect discussion.
- **Aorist passive indicative (V-API)** — Perfective/external view of a past event undergone; at Rev 11:2, 12:14, 13:5 framing the grant of the span. See [aorist-passive-indicative](../greek/aorist-passive-indicative.md).
- **Perfect passive participle (V-RPP)** — State resulting from completed action; περιβεβλημένοι (Rev 11:3), ἡτοιμασμένον (Rev 12:6). See [participle-perfect-passive-state](../greek/participle-perfect-passive-state.md).
- **Divine passive (ἐδόθη / ἐδόθησαν)** — Aorist passive of δίδωμι (G1325) with unexpressed agent, idiomatically signaling divine permission; appears at Rev 11:2, 12:14, 13:5 (twice), and 13:7. See [divine-passive](../greek/divine-passive.md).
- **Apocalyptic narrative prolepsis / visionary recursion** — Genre convention in which the same span is viewed from multiple angles across separate καὶ εἶδον tableaux. See [apocalyptic-narrative-prolepsis](../greek/apocalyptic-narrative-prolepsis.md). Directly relevant to the Rev 12 ↔ Rev 13 coextension observed here.
