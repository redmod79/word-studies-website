## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same across testaments:**
- Kardia in the NT retains the full OT semantic range: emotion (joy, grief), will (purpose, intention), intellect (understanding, reasoning), and moral character (evil heart, pure heart)
- The "hardening of the heart" idiom (Pharaoh → Isa 6:10 → NT) carries intact through the lêb → kardia bridge
- Covenant devotion language — "with all your heart" — survives in NT as ἐν ὅλῃ τῇ καρδίᾳ (Matt 22:37 quoting Deut 6:5)

**What shifts:**
- The NT introduces the pneuma (spirit) dimension more prominently alongside kardia; the OT lêb alone carried most of the inner-person semantic load
- NT kardia gains eschatological dimensions (hardened heart as judgment; renewed heart as new covenant gift) that draw on but develop the OT base
- Hebrew lêb was rarely associated with the physical organ; NT kardia similarly is almost entirely figurative
