# Grammar Analysis: Jeremiah 18:7–10 (Conditional Prophecy Formula)

## Overview

Jer 18:7–10 is the locus classicus for the **conditional prophecy formula** in the Hebrew Bible — the metalinguistic statement in which YHWH himself frames every oracle of judgment or promise as conditional on the addressee's moral response. The four verses comprise a **symmetrical twin-oracle diptych** (vv. 7–8 judgment-announcement, vv. 9–10 promise-announcement) built on identical syntactic scaffolding:

1. A **temporal-modal opener** רֶגַע אֲדַבֵּר ("at-an-instant I-am-speaking") — fronted adverbial accusative of time + 1cs Piel *yiqtol*
2. A **purpose-direction complement** עַל־גּוֹי וְעַל־מַמְלָכָה ("upon a nation and upon a kingdom") + 3× לְ + InfCon chain giving the *intended* effect
3. A **weqatal-headed protasis** stating the *reversal* of the nation's posture — וְשָׁב / וְעָשָׂה
4. A **weqatal-headed apodosis** stating YHWH's own reversal — וְנִחַמְתִּי ("and I will relent") — identical in both directions

The grammatical architecture is *direction-neutral*: the same scaffolding carries a threatened nation toward mercy (v. 8) and a favored nation toward disaster (v. 10). The formula therefore generalizes the Jonah-pattern (Jon 3:4, 10) and stands as the prophetic-theological rule the potter-parable (18:1–6) is designed to illustrate.

## Text

### Verse 7 (judgment-announcement opener)
**Hebrew:** רֶ֣גַע אֲדַבֵּ֔ר עַל־גּ֖וֹי וְעַל־מַמְלָכָ֑ה לִנְת֥וֹשׁ וְלִנְת֖וֹץ וּֽלְהַאֲבִֽיד׃
**KJV:** At what instant I shall speak concerning a nation, and concerning a kingdom, to pluck up, and to pull down, and to destroy it;

### Verse 8 (judgment-protasis + apodosis)
**Hebrew:** וְשָׁב֙ הַגּ֣וֹי הַה֔וּא מֵרָ֣עָת֔וֹ אֲשֶׁ֥ר דִּבַּ֖רְתִּי עָלָ֑יו וְנִֽחַמְתִּי֙ עַל־הָ֣רָעָ֔ה אֲשֶׁ֥ר חָשַׁ֖בְתִּי לַעֲשׂ֥וֹת לֽוֹ׃
**KJV:** If that nation, against whom I have pronounced, turn from their evil, I will repent of the evil that I thought to do unto them.

### Verse 9 (promise-announcement opener)
**Hebrew:** וְרֶ֣גַע אֲדַבֵּ֔ר עַל־גּ֖וֹי וְעַל־מַמְלָכָ֑ה לִבְנֹ֖ת וְלִנְטֹֽעַ׃
**KJV:** And at what instant I shall speak concerning a nation, and concerning a kingdom, to build and to plant it;

### Verse 10 (promise-protasis + apodosis)
**Hebrew:** וְעָשָׂ֤ה הָרַע֙ בְּעֵינַ֔י לְבִלְתִּ֖י שְׁמֹ֣עַ בְּקוֹלִ֑י וְנִֽחַמְתִּי֙ עַל־הַטּוֹבָ֔ה אֲשֶׁ֥ר אָמַ֖רְתִּי לְהֵיטִ֥יב אוֹתֽוֹ׃

## Key Grammatical Features

### 1. רֶגַע אֲדַבֵּר — Temporal Accusative + 1cs *Yiqtol* (the "at-what-instant-I-speak" frame)

The opener is a **bare noun in the adverbial accusative of time** (רֶגַע, "[at] an instant, [at] any moment," Noun.ms.Abs) **fronted before** the 1cs Piel imperfect אֲדַבֵּר of דבר. The clause type **xYq0** (non-clause-initial *yiqtol*) marks the noun as a topicalized temporal frame rather than the verb's subject or object.

- **Morphology of אֲדַבֵּר:** Piel *yiqtol* 1cs. The Piel of דבר is the standard "speak" conjugation (cf. [ehyeh-1cs-imperfect.md](../hebrew/ehyeh-1cs-imperfect.md) on the 1cs *yiqtol* in general).
- **Aspect:** imperfective / non-past. KJV "at what instant I shall speak" (future) is defensible, but the *yiqtol* here is better read as **gnomic/habitual-modal** — "whenever I pronounce…" — setting up a general rule, not a single future speech-event.
- **רֶגַע as adverbial:** The bare noun without prefixed *bə-* or *lə-* is the Hebrew idiom for "in an instant, momentarily, suddenly" (cf. Num 16:21, Exod 33:5, Isa 47:9, Jer 4:20). Here it modifies the act of speaking itself: YHWH *at any moment can speak* a verdict that is nevertheless conditional on subsequent response.
- **Fronted word order (xYq0):** Topic-fronting of the temporal adverb places the *conditionality-at-the-instant* in the rhetorical spotlight — the speech-act itself is framed as revisable.

### 2. עַל־גּוֹי וְעַל־מַמְלָכָה — Indefinite Paired Complement

- Both nouns are **anarthrous** (no definite article), generating an unrestricted any-nation / any-kingdom reading.
- **עַל** with verbs of speech = "concerning, against, about" (BDB); in v. 7 the negative purpose-chain makes it adversarial ("against"), in v. 9 the positive purpose-chain makes it benefactive ("concerning/for").
- Identical preposition in both oracles — the preposition does NOT encode direction; only the following purpose-chain does.

### 3. The Three-Verb Destruction Chain (v. 7) — לִנְתוֹשׁ וְלִנְתוֹץ וּלְהַאֲבִיד

Three parallel **לְ + InfCon** clauses expressing purpose/intent:

- **נתשׁ** — Qal InfCon "to pluck up, uproot" (agricultural root-imagery)
- **נתץ** — Qal InfCon "to tear down" (masonry-imagery — walls, altars)
- **אבד Hiphil** — InfCon "to cause to perish / to destroy" (comprehensive-negative)

The three verbs together (plus "demolish" הרס and "afflict" הרע in the fuller Jer 1:10 list) form the **Jeremianic destruction-vocabulary signature**: they recur at Jer 1:10; 12:17; 18:7; 24:6; 31:28; 42:10; 45:4. Jer 1:10 and 31:28 pair them with the build/plant verbs of v. 9, making 18:7–10 the **metalinguistic rule** that governs Jeremiah's entire commissioning mandate.

### 4. The Two-Verb Construction Chain (v. 9) — לִבְנֹת וְלִנְטֹעַ

- **בנה** — Qal InfCon "to build" (architectural-positive)
- **נטע** — Qal InfCon "to plant" (agricultural-positive)

Only **two** verbs, against the three in v. 7. The asymmetry is stylistic, not theological: both chains are exhaustive of their domain.

### 5. וְשָׁב — Weqatal Qal Perfect 3ms of שׁוּב (the Protasis of v. 8)

**A correction to note:** the verb in v. 8 is NOT the Qal *imperfect* of שׁוּב (*yāšûb*) but the **Qal perfect 3ms** שָׁב preceded by conjunctive/consecutive waw (*wə-šāb*) — i.e., a **weqatal**, clause-type **WQtX**. This is load-bearing for the grammar of the conditional formula:

- **Weqatal in a conditional frame** functions as a **hypothetical future** following on the *yiqtol* of v. 7. After a setup-*yiqtol*, a *weqatal* can carry the conditional protasis just as well as an explicit *ʾim*-clause (cf. [syntax-conditional-vow.md](../hebrew/syntax-conditional-vow.md); [weqatal-apodosis-vow.md](../hebrew/weqatal-apodosis-vow.md)).
- **Syndetic (no אִם):** The conditionality is carried entirely by the *sequence* v.7 *yiqtol* → v.8 *weqatal*, without a lexical conditional particle. This is an *unmarked* (particle-less) conditional — a well-attested Hebrew construction (GKC §159b; Gesenius-Kautzsch-Cowley p. 445 notes 2 Kgs 5:10 as a parallel where וְיָשֹׁב is apodosis to a suppressed protasis).
- **שׁוּב as covenantal-return verb:** In Jeremiah שׁוּב + מִן + "evil" is the standard idiom for national repentance (Jer 15:7; 23:14; 23:22; 25:5; 26:3; 35:15; 36:3, 7). The Qal stem (not the Hiphil) puts the grammatical agency on the nation itself.
- **WQtX type** (not WQt0) because the overt subject הַגּוֹי הַהוּא follows the verb. The demonstrative הַהוּא ("that [particular] nation") refers back anaphorically to the *any*-nation of v. 7.

*User-supplied description note:* the prompt called this "Qal imperfect of שׁוּב." The parser (ETCBC/BHSA tagging) identifies the form as Qal **Perfect** 3ms with conjunctive waw. The semantic function (hypothetical future / conditional protasis) is the same either way, but the **morphology is weqatal, not yiqtol**. This is representative of the broader pattern: the whole formula uses weqatal chains, not yiqtol chains, for the protasis-apodosis material.

### 6. וְעָשָׂה … לְבִלְתִּי שְׁמֹעַ — Weqatal + Negative Infinitive Construct (the Protasis of v. 10)

The mirror-protasis in v. 10 is structured identically, with expansion:

- **וְעָשָׂה** — Qal weqatal 3ms of עשׂה, parallel to וְשָׁב of v. 8 (WQt0 clause-type, subject carried over from v. 9).
- **הָרַע בְּעֵינַי** — "the evil in my eyes" (standard Deuteronomistic indictment formula, 2 Kgs 17:17; Jer 7:30; 32:30).
- **לְבִלְתִּי + InfCon** — the **negative infinitive-of-purpose** construction. בִּלְתִּי (H1115) is a negator-particle that combines with לְ + InfCon to form "so as not to / in not" clauses (GKC §114s). Here it glosses the evil-action as *constituted by* disobedience — "doing evil *in that it does not obey my voice.*"
- The protasis is therefore **bipartite** (v. 10a weqatal + v. 10b neg-InfCon) where v. 8's protasis was **monopartite** (v. 8a weqatal alone).

### 7. וְנִחַמְתִּי — Weqatal Niphal Perfect 1cs of נחם (the Identical Apodosis in both v. 8 and v. 10)

This is the **rhetorical hinge** of the whole diptych — the SAME verb-form, morphologically identical to the last diacritic, heads both apodoses:

- **Morphology:** Niphal perfect 1cs with conjunctive/consecutive waw. Clause type **WQt0** (weqatal, no overt subject, since YHWH is the 1cs speaker throughout).
- **Niphal stem:** The Niphal of נחם carries the semantic range "be sorry / relent / change one's mind / be moved to pity / console oneself" (cf. Gen 6:6–7, Exod 32:12–14, 1 Sam 15:11, 29, 35; Joel 2:13–14; Jon 3:9–10; 4:2; Amos 7:3, 6; Jer 4:28; 15:6; 18:8, 10; 20:16; 26:3, 13, 19; 42:10). The Niphal here is the **reflexive/inner-change** sense, not the passive "be comforted."
- **Weqatal after the protasis:** Standard weqatal-apodosis pattern — cf. [weqatal-apodosis-vow.md](../hebrew/weqatal-apodosis-vow.md) for the analogous construction in votive contexts (Jdg 11:31, Num 21:2, 1 Sam 1:11). The formal syntax is the same; only the *speaker* differs (vow = human speaker binding self; 18:7–10 = divine speaker framing contingency).
- **Direction-neutrality:** This is the critical grammatical datum. Whether the announced oracle is disaster (v. 8) or benefit (v. 10), the reversal-verb is *identical*. The form does NOT encode which way the relenting goes; that is determined entirely by the **object of the preposition עַל**: עַל־הָרָעָה (v. 8, "concerning the disaster I had purposed") vs. עַל־הַטּוֹבָה (v. 10, "concerning the good I had said I would do").
- **Apparent tension with 1 Sam 15:29 / Num 23:19:** 1 Sam 15:29 *lōʾ yinnāḥēm kî lōʾ ʾādām hûʾ lə-hinnāḥēm* ("he will not relent, for he is not a man that he should relent") uses the same Niphal root to deny that YHWH relents. The tension is grammatical-theological, not morphological: Jer 18 asserts YHWH's *responsiveness-to-repentance* (contingent); 1 Sam 15:29 denies YHWH's *capriciousness-without-cause*. Both use נחם Niphal.

### 8. אֲשֶׁר-Clauses as Performative-Speech Markers

Each apodosis ends with an אֲשֶׁר-clause that grounds the *reality* of the previously-announced oracle in YHWH's own prior speech-act:

- v. 8: אֲשֶׁר דִּבַּרְתִּי עָלָיו ("which I had spoken against it") + אֲשֶׁר חָשַׁבְתִּי לַעֲשׂוֹת לוֹ ("which I had purposed to do to it")
- v. 10: אֲשֶׁר אָמַרְתִּי לְהֵיטִיב אוֹתוֹ ("which I had said I would benefit it")

These are Qal/Piel **qatal** perfects (דבר Piel perf., חשׁב Qal perf., אמר Qal perf.) — encoding completed speech-acts that the אֲשֶׁר-clause now makes *revisable*. The very act of YHWH's speaking is marked as the thing to be unspoken under changed conditions.

## Genre and Function

- **Genre:** divine metalinguistic discourse (God speaking about how God speaks). Comparable to Num 23:19 / 1 Sam 15:29 (negative pole) and Ezek 18:21–32; 33:12–20 (individual analog of the national-level Jer 18 rule).

## Summary

Jer 18:7–10 canonically establishes the **conditional-prophecy formula** as a grammatical rule: xYq0 opener (*reḡaʿ ʾădabbēr*) → InfCon purpose-chain (destruction or construction) → WQtX/WQt0 protasis (weqatal of שׁוב or עשׂה) → WQt0 apodosis (weqatal Niphal of נחם). The direction (judgment-to-mercy / promise-to-disaster) is encoded NOT in the verbs of the formula itself but in the *purpose-chain* of the opener and the *object of עַל* in the apodosis. The reversal-verb (*wə-niḥamtî*) is morphologically identical in both directions, making the formula a symmetrical diptych whose grammatical architecture enforces the theological claim that every divine oracle stands under the conditionality of creaturely response.
