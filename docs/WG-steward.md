# WG: Stewardship

## Overview
The stewardship concept in the NT involves a cluster of words describing the person who manages, the act of managing, the office itself, and the virtues required. The oikonomos word family (G3623/G3621/G3622) provides the core, with epitropos (G2012) as a near-synonym and pistos (G4103) / phronimos (G5429) as qualifying virtues. Diakonos (G1249) shares the service dimension.

## Roles in the Concept

- **Person:** oikonomos (G3623) — the steward/manager who holds responsibility
- **Action:** oikonomeo (G3621) — the act of managing/stewarding
- **Office:** oikonomia (G3622) — the stewardship, administration, or dispensation entrusted
- **Guardian parallel:** epitropos (G2012) — commissioner/guardian with delegated authority (Gal 4:2 pairs both)
- **Virtue — faithfulness:** pistos (G4103) — the primary quality required (1 Cor 4:2)
- **Virtue — wisdom:** phronimos (G5429) — the practical competence required (Lk 12:42)
- **Service dimension:** diakonos (G1249) — minister/servant, the broader service context (1 Cor 4:1 pairs "ministers" with "stewards")
