**Semantic continuity:** The LXX translators chose φιάλη — a broad, shallow cup — as the precise functional equivalent for the Hebrew sanctuary bowl (mizrâq). Both words describe a wide, open vessel specifically used for holding and pouring liquids in a ritual setting. The form is stable across the translation.

**LXX examples:**
- **Num 7:13** — Each tribal prince brings one silver **mizrâq** (LXX: φιάλη) as an offering at the dedication of the Tabernacle altar — repeated 12 times through the chapter
- **1 Ki 7:40** — The bronze **basons** (mizrâq / φιάλη) of the Temple service
- **Amos 6:6** — "That drink wine in **bowls** (mizrâq / φιάλη)" — used here of luxurious excess, implying these large Temple vessels

## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same across testaments:**
- The vessel type: a wide, shallow bowl used for ritual pouring — exactly the shape and function the LXX preserved
- The sanctuary setting: OT mizrâq lived at the altar and Tabernacle; NT phiálē comes from the heavenly temple (Rev 15:8 — "the temple was filled with smoke from the glory of God")
- The golden material: OT "golden bowls" (Num 7:84–86); NT "seven golden vials" (Rev 15:7)
- The act: sprinkling/pouring out — the core ritual function of the mizrâq becomes the climactic action in Revelation

**What shifts:**
- Content shifts from blood (OT altar, sacrificial rite) → incense/prayers (Rev 5:8) → divine wrath (Rev 15:7; 16:1ff); the vessel is the same, what fills it changes
- Scale: OT mizrâq contained a finite offering; NT phiálē contains the full wrath of God who "liveth for ever and ever" (Rev 15:7)
- Direction: OT sprinkling was toward the altar/curtain/priest; NT pouring is toward the earth, sea, and sky — outward cosmic judgment
