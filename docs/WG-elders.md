# WG — Elders / Presbyter Word Family

## Member Definitions

**G4245 πρεσβύτερος** — Comparative of πρέσβυς (*elderly*); *older*; as noun, a senior; in the NT: an elder in the Jewish Sanhedrin, a church officer, one of the 24 heavenly elders in Revelation. The dominant NT term for ordained leadership. (67 verses)

**G4244 πρεσβυτέριον** — The collective *body* of elders; the "eldership" as an institution or council. Used of the Jewish council that tried Jesus (Luke 22:66), the Sanhedrin body (Acts 22:5), and the body of elders that ordained Timothy (1 Tim 4:14). (3 verses)

**G4246 πρεσβύτης** — Simply "an old man"; no official or titular sense. Zechariah describes himself this way (Luke 1:18); Paul uses it in Philem 1:9 as a self-description ("Paul the aged"). Titus 2:2 addresses "aged men" in the congregation. (3 verses)

**G4243 πρεσβεύω** — "To be a senior" hence to act as an ambassador or envoy. In Paul: "We are ambassadors for Christ" (2 Cor 5:20); "I am an ambassador in bonds" (Eph 6:20). The senior-as-envoy extension. (2 verses)

**G1087 γερουσία** — "The eldership" as a formal institution; specifically the Jewish senate/Sanhedrin. Appears only in Acts 5:21: "all the senate of the children of Israel." A synonym for the Sanhedrin as a whole. (1 verse)

## Key Distinctions

| Contrast | Explanation |
|----------|-------------|
| G4245 vs G4244 | G4245 = individual elder(s); G4244 = the collective body/council as a unit |
| G4245 vs G4246 | G4245 carries official/role sense; G4246 is purely age-descriptive (no leadership implied) |
| G4245 vs G1087 | G1087 = the Sanhedrin senate specifically; G4245 is broader (church, synagogue, Revelation) |
| G4243 vs others | G4243 = the functional extension: the "senior" who acts as envoy; no overlap with leadership role |

## Shared Hebrew Source

All three major members (G4245, G4246, G1087) translate H2205 זָקֵן in the LXX:
- G4245 — 112 co-occurrences (dominant)
- G1087 — 25 co-occurrences (collective body)
- G4246 — 19 co-occurrences (age-descriptive)

This confirms the LXX systematically distributed the one Hebrew word זָקֵן across these Greek lexical items based on whether the individual elder, the collective body, or the age quality was in view.
