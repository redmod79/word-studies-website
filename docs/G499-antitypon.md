## Lexicon Definition

Strong's: "corresponding (antitype), i.e., a representative, counterpart." From ἀντί (corresponding to) + τύπος (type, pattern, impression). The word literally means "struck back" — a counter-impression produced by a die (τύπος). Thus: a thing that corresponds to and answers a prior pattern.

In classical Greek it could describe anything that "strikes back" or answers a blow; in later usage it specialized toward the sense of a copy, image, or counterpart answering to an original mold.

## Semantic Categories

Because the word occurs only twice, the two occurrences constitute its entire semantic range in the NT. They display the word's two possible directions of correspondence.

### 1. Copy / Counterpart of a Heavenly Original (ἀντίτυπα = the copies answering to the true)
- **Heb 9:24** — "For Christ is not entered into the holy places made with hands, [which are] the figures (ἀντίτυπα) of the true; but into heaven itself, now to appear in the presence of God for us."

Here the earthly sanctuary is the ἀντίτυπος *of* the heavenly reality. The earthly is the copy; the heavenly is the original (τὰ ἀληθινά, "the true [things]"). The direction of correspondence runs from earthly copy backward/upward to heavenly archetype. This parallels ὑπόδειγμα ("copy, model") and σκιά ("shadow") in Heb 8:5; 9:23; 10:1.

### 2. Fulfillment / Reality Answering an Earlier Pattern (ἀντίτυπον = the now-corresponding thing)
- **1 Pet 3:21** — "The like figure (ἀντίτυπον) whereunto [even] baptism doth also now save us... by the resurrection of Jesus Christ."

Here baptism is the ἀντίτυπον *of* the Flood / Noah's deliverance by water (v. 20). The direction of correspondence runs from earlier pattern forward to present fulfillment. Baptism is the thing that now answers the ancient event.

**Note on the two directions:** The single Greek word ἀντίτυπος can point to either member of a type/counterpart pair. In Heb 9:24 the antitype is the *earthly copy* of a heavenly original. In 1 Pet 3:21 the antitype is the *present fulfillment* of an ancient pattern. The word itself is bidirectional; context determines which member is the τύπος and which is the ἀντίτυπος.

### Typological Framework in Hebrews
Heb 8:5 uses ὑπόδειγμα + σκιά; Heb 9:9 uses παραβολή; Heb 9:23 uses ὑποδείγματα; Heb 9:24 uses ἀντίτυπα; Heb 10:1 uses σκιάν... οὐκ αὐτὴν τὴν εἰκόνα. These terms function as near-synonyms for the earthly sanctuary's relation to heavenly realities, each highlighting a slightly different facet (specimen, shadow, figure, counterpart, image).

## Hebrew Source

No LXX use of ἀντίτυπος occurs in canonical Hebrew Bible texts (the word is almost entirely post-classical). In the Apocrypha it appears in 2 Macc and 3 Macc; no Hebrew Vorlage is involved. The word enters Scripture's vocabulary only in the NT.

The conceptual background in Hebrew typology (earthly sanctuary patterned on a heavenly original) is carried by תַּבְנִית (tabnít, H8403, "pattern") in Ex 25:9, 40 — rendered by τύπος in the LXX (Ex 25:40 cited at Heb 8:5). Thus ἀντίτυπος in Heb 9:24 implicitly answers to the tabnít/τύπος of Exodus.
