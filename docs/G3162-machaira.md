## Lexicon Definition

μάχαιρα, -ας, ἡ — probably feminine of a presumed derivative of μάχη; "a knife," i.e. a short sword, dirk, or dagger. Classical usage originally denoted a large knife for sacrifice or slaughtering animals; in Hellenistic Greek it broadened to include the short stabbing sword carried by soldiers and civilians alike (cf. the Roman *gladius*). In the NT it is the common, generic term for "sword" in narrative contexts (arrests, executions, warfare) and takes figurative extension for judicial authority, divine judgment, persecution, and the penetrating word of God.

### 4. Warfare / Deadly Peril

- **Heb 11:34** — "escaped the edge of the sword"
- **Rom 8:35** — "shall tribulation… or famine, or nakedness, or peril, or sword, separate us"

### 5. Divine / Messianic Division
- **Mat 10:34** — "I came not to send peace, but a sword" (figurative for the division Christ's coming brings)

### 6. Figurative — Word of God / Spirit
- **Eph 6:17** — "the sword of the Spirit, which is the word of God" (μάχαιραν τοῦ πνεύματος)
- **Heb 4:12** — "the word of God… sharper than any twoedged sword (μαχαίρας διστόμου), piercing even to the dividing asunder of soul and spirit"

### 7. Apocalyptic Judgment (Revelation)
- **Rev 6:4** — red-horse rider "that they should kill one another: and there was given unto him a great sword (μάχαιρα μεγάλη)" — widespread bloodshed, mutual slaughter
- **Rev 13:10** — "he that killeth with the sword (ἐν μαχαίρᾳ) must be killed with the sword" — *lex talionis* formula tied to captivity-and-sword judgment against persecutors
- **Rev 13:14** — "the beast, which had the wound by a sword (τὴν πληγὴν τῆς μαχαίρας), and did live" — the deadly wound that the image of the beast celebrates

Of Revelation's seven "sword" references, three use μάχαιρα (6:4; 13:10; 13:14) and four use ῥομφαία (1:16; 2:12, 16; 6:8; 19:15, 21). In John's apocalyptic vocabulary μάχαιρα tends toward the human-wielded, violent, historical blade (war, persecution, the beast's wound); ῥομφαία is reserved for the sword proceeding from the mouth of Christ and for the Lamb's cosmic judgment. See **Easily Confused Words**.

**Rev 1:16 and Rev 19:15 caveat:** The "sharp two-edged sword" proceeding from Christ's mouth (Rev 1:16; 19:15; cf. 2:12, 16) is **ῥομφαία (G4501)**, not μάχαιρα. The only NT passage that uses μάχαιρα for a two-edged sword in a figurative "word of God" sense is **Heb 4:12** (μαχαίρας διστόμου). **Eph 6:17** ("sword of the Spirit") also uses μάχαιρα. Revelation deliberately restricts the mouth-sword imagery to ῥομφαία.

### KJV / LXX Translation Overlap
- KJV "sword": G3162 (10v), G4501 (6v), H2719 חֶרֶב (many v)
- LXX renders חֶרֶב predominantly with μάχαιρα; ῥομφαία is the second-most-frequent LXX equivalent. Thus English readers conflate three distinct Greek lexemes (μάχαιρα / ῥομφαία / φόνος) under a single gloss.
