## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same across testaments:**
- The core meaning "to take a solemn oath" is identical in both testaments
- God swearing to the patriarchs (land, seed, blessing) is the dominant theological use in both OT and NT
- The formula pattern "swear by [something greater]" persists from Genesis through Hebrews
- Divine oaths are irrevocable in both testaments

**What shifts:**
- The NT introduces explicit prohibition of casual human oath-taking (Mat 5:34, Jas 5:12) — no such blanket prohibition exists in the OT, where human oath-taking is regulated rather than forbidden
- Hebrews develops a contrast between the Aaronic priesthood (established without an oath) and the Melchizedek priesthood (established by divine oath, Heb 7:20–21) — a theological argument that depends entirely on the LXX rendering of Psa 110:4
- The negative use (God's oath of wrath/exclusion in Heb 3–4 from Psa 95) receives extended theological treatment absent in the OT context
