## Lexicon Definition

A primitive root; properly, to weigh out; by implication, to allot or constitute officially; also to enumerate or enroll: — appoint, count, number, prepare, set, tell.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Counting / Enumerating What Is Vast
- **Gen 13:16** — "if a man can number the dust of the earth, [then] shall thy seed also be numbered"
- **Num 23:10** — "Who can count the dust of Jacob, and the number of the fourth [part] of Israel?"
- **Psa 147:4** — "He telleth the number of the stars; he calleth them all by [their] names"
- **1Ch 27:24** — "Joab the son of Zeruiah began to number, but he finished not"

The enumeration sense often carries the nuance of what is beyond human ability to count — dust, stars, covenant descendants. Only God successfully completes such counting (Psa 147:4).

### 2. Census-Taking / Military Numbering
- **2Sa 24:1** — "he moved David against them to say, Go, number Israel and Judah"
- **1Ch 21:1** — "Satan stood up against Israel, and provoked David to number Israel"
- **1Ch 21:17** — "Is it not I that commanded the people to be numbered?"
- **1Ki 20:25** — "number thee an army, like the army that thou hast lost"

Census-taking in this sense is laden with moral weight — David's numbering of Israel provoked divine wrath. Contrasts with purely administrative enumeration.

### 3. Being Beyond Numbering (Niphal Passive)
- **1Ki 3:8** — "a great people, that cannot be numbered nor counted for multitude"
- **1Ki 8:5** — "sheep and oxen, that could not be told nor numbered for multitude"
- **2Ch 5:6** — "sheep and oxen, which could not be told nor numbered for multitude"
- **Ecc 1:15** — "that which is wanting cannot be numbered"

The Niphal passive expresses what exceeds human capacity to reckon — people, sacrificial animals, or deficiencies.

### 4. Divine Appointment / Preparation (Piel)
- **Jon 1:17** — "the LORD had prepared a great fish to swallow up Jonah"
- **Jon 4:6** — "the LORD God prepared a gourd"
- **Jon 4:7** — "God prepared a worm when the morning rose the next day"
- **Jon 4:8** — "God prepared a vehement east wind"

All four Jonah uses share the same structure: God as subject, Piel stem, inanimate natural agent as object. The "preparing" conveys sovereign ordination — God assigns a role or function to a creature.

### 5. Official Appointment / Allotment
- **Dan 1:5** — "the king appointed them a daily provision of the king's meat"
- **Dan 1:10** — "the prince of the eunuchs... who hath appointed your meat and your drink"
- **Dan 1:11** — "whom the prince of the eunuchs had set over Daniel"
- **1Ch 9:29** — "Some of them also were appointed to oversee the vessels"
- **Job 7:3** — "wearisome nights are appointed to me"

Here the Piel expresses authoritative assignment of provisions, roles, or circumstances. The subject is a king, official, or God.

### 6. Reckoning / Numbering Among a Group
- **Isa 53:12** — "he was numbered with the transgressors"
- **Isa 65:12** — "I will number you to the sword"
- **Psa 90:12** — "So teach us to number our days, that we may apply our hearts unto wisdom"

Isa 53:12 (Niphal) — being reckoned as belonging to a category. Isa 65:12 (Qal) — allotting/assigning a destiny. Psa 90:12 (Qal infinitive) — assessing/weighing the significance of something finite.

### 7. Counting Flocks (Pastoral)
- **Jer 33:13** — "the flocks pass again under the hands of him that telleth them"
- **2Ki 12:10** — "they told and put up the money that was found in the house of the LORD" (counting/weighing)

The pastoral and commercial sense connects to the root meaning "to weigh out" — the careful assessment of what one possesses.
