# WG: Commandment-Keeping

## Overview
The NT commandment-keeping concept clusters around 7 words. The core triad — entolē (the commandment), tēreō (keeping it), and agapaō (love) — appears together in the densest Johannine passages. Nomos provides the broader legal framework. Entellomai, entalma, and epitagē define the edges: the cognate verb, the counterfeit form (human tradition), and the apostolic variant.

## Roles in the Concept

- **The charge:** entolē (G1785) — the specific divine or apostolic injunction
- **The action:** tēreō (G5083) — keeping/guarding the commandment
- **The motive:** agapaō (G25) — love both commands and motivates keeping
- **The framework:** nomos (G3551) — the law within which commandments operate
- **The source act:** entellomai (G1781) — the act of commanding that produces entolē
- **The counterfeit:** entalma (G1778) — human tradition masquerading as divine commandment
- **The apostolic form:** epitagē (G2003) — directive issued under apostolic authority
