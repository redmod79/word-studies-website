## Semantic Categories

### 1. The Repentant Harlot — Faith and the Kingdom
Harlots named as examples of unexpected faith and repentance, entering the kingdom ahead of the self-righteous.
- **Matt 21:31** — "the publicans and the harlots go into the kingdom of God before you"
- **Matt 21:32** — "the publicans and the harlots believed him: and ye, when ye had seen it, repented not afterward"

### 2. Rahab — OT Harlot as Faith Exemplar
Rahab explicitly called πόρνη yet commended for faith and works, the only individual named as a harlot in the NT epistles.
- **Heb 11:31** — "By faith the harlot Rahab perished not with them that believed not, when she had received the spies with peace"
- **Jas 2:25** — "was not Rahab the harlot justified by works, when she had received the messengers, and had sent them out another way?"

### 3. Sexual Union — The Body and the Harlot
Paul's argument that physical union with a harlot makes one body with her, violating membership in Christ.
- **1 Cor 6:15** — "shall I then take the members of Christ, and make them the members of an harlot? God forbid"
- **1 Cor 6:16** — "he which is joined to an harlot is one body? for two, saith he, shall be one flesh"

### 4. The Prodigal's Waste
The elder brother's accusation against the prodigal, associating harlots with reckless dissipation.
- **Luke 15:30** — "this thy son was come, which hath devoured thy living with harlots"

### 5. Babylon the Great Whore (Revelation)
Figurative/symbolic usage — Babylon personified as a harlot who seduces nations and kings, then faces divine judgment.
- **Rev 17:1** — "Come hither; I will shew unto thee the judgment of the great whore that sitteth upon many waters"
- **Rev 17:5** — "MYSTERY, BABYLON THE GREAT, THE MOTHER OF HARLOTS AND ABOMINATIONS OF THE EARTH"
- **Rev 17:15** — "The waters which thou sawest, where the whore sitteth, are peoples, and multitudes, and nations, and tongues"
- **Rev 17:16** — "the ten horns…shall hate the whore, and shall make her desolate and naked, and shall eat her flesh, and burn her with fire"
- **Rev 19:2** — "he hath judged the great whore, which did corrupt the earth with her fornication"
