Concentrated in prophetic literature (Isaiah 25v, Jeremiah 25v) and historical narrative (2 Samuel 14v, 2 Kings 14v, 1 Samuel 13v). Prophets use it heavily in both positive (divine promise) and negative (false peace) contexts. The historical books use it frequently in greetings and diplomatic language.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Greeting and Inquiry of Welfare
- **Gen 29:6** — "Is he well? And they said, He is well"
- **Gen 43:27** — "And he asked them of their welfare, and said, Is your father well?"
- **2 Ki 4:26** — "Is it well with thee? is it well with thy husband? is it well with the child?"
- **1 Sa 25:6** — "Peace be both to thee, and peace be to thine house, and peace be unto all that thou hast"

The most common interpersonal usage: asking after or wishing someone's total well-being. The greeting "shalom" encompasses health, safety, and general flourishing.

### 2. Absence of War / Diplomatic Peace
- **Jos 9:15** — "And Joshua made peace with them, and made a league with them"
- **1 Ki 4:24** — "he had peace on all sides round about him"
- **1 Ki 5:12** — "there was peace between Hiram and Solomon"
- **Ecc 3:8** — "a time of war, and a time of peace"
- **Deu 20:10** — "When thou comest nigh unto a city to fight against it, then proclaim peace unto it"

Cessation or absence of military conflict between parties, often formalized in treaties.

### 3. Wholeness, Prosperity, and Well-being
- **Jer 29:11** — "thoughts of peace, and not of evil, to give you an expected end"
- **Jer 29:7** — "seek the peace of the city whither I have caused you to be carried away captives"
- **Psa 122:6-7** — "Pray for the peace of Jerusalem... Peace be within thy walls, and prosperity within thy palaces"
- **Est 10:3** — "seeking the wealth of his people, and speaking peace to all his seed"
- **Psa 73:3** — "I saw the prosperity of the wicked"

Comprehensive well-being including material prosperity, communal flourishing, and social harmony. Broader than mere absence of conflict.

### 4. Safety in Departure and Death
- **Gen 15:15** — "And thou shalt go to thy fathers in peace; thou shalt be buried in a good old age"
- **2 Ki 22:20** — "thou shalt be gathered into thy grave in peace"
- **1 Sa 20:42** — "Go in peace, forasmuch as we have sworn both of us in the name of the LORD"
- **Isa 57:2** — "He shall enter into peace: they shall rest in their beds"
- **Jdg 18:6** — "Go in peace: before the LORD is your way"

A blessed departure — whether leaving on a journey or dying. Implies divine protection and a settled, complete state.

### 5. Covenant Relationship with God
- **Num 25:12** — "Behold, I give unto him my covenant of peace"
- **Eze 34:25** — "I will make with them a covenant of peace"
- **Eze 37:26** — "I will make a covenant of peace with them; it shall be an everlasting covenant"
- **Mal 2:5** — "My covenant was with him of life and peace"
- **Lev 26:6** — "I will give peace in the land"
- **Num 6:26** — "The LORD lift up his countenance upon thee, and give thee peace"

Shalom as a covenant gift from God — the state of right relationship with YHWH. The "covenant of peace" appears as a specific divine arrangement (Phinehas, Ezekiel's new covenant).

### 6. Soundness and Completeness (Physical/Spiritual)
- **Isa 26:3** — "Thou wilt keep him in perfect peace, whose mind is stayed on thee" (lit. shalom shalom)
- **Psa 38:3** — "There is no soundness in my flesh... neither is there any rest in my bones"
- **Psa 119:165** — "Great peace have they which love thy law: and nothing shall offend them"
- **Isa 48:18** — "then had thy peace been as a river, and thy righteousness as the waves of the sea"
- **Psa 85:10** — "righteousness and peace have kissed each other"

Inner wholeness and moral/spiritual completeness. Often linked with righteousness (tsedaqah) as paired attributes.

### 7. False Peace / Peace Denied
- **Jer 6:14** — "saying, Peace, peace; when there is no peace"
- **Jer 8:11** — "saying, Peace, peace; when there is no peace"
- **Eze 13:10** — "they have seduced my people, saying, Peace; and there was no peace"
- **Isa 48:22** — "There is no peace, saith the LORD, unto the wicked"
- **Isa 57:21** — "There is no peace, saith my God, to the wicked"
- **Deu 29:19** — "I shall have peace, though I walk in the imagination of mine heart"

Prophetic polemic against false assurance. The wicked cannot possess shalom; prophets who proclaim it falsely are condemned.

### 8. Messianic and Eschatological Peace
- **Isa 9:6** — "his name shall be called... The Prince of Peace"
- **Isa 9:7** — "Of the increase of his government and peace there shall be no end"
- **Isa 53:5** — "the chastisement of our peace was upon him"
- **Mic 5:5** — "And this man shall be the peace"
- **Zec 9:10** — "he shall speak peace unto the heathen"
- **Isa 54:10** — "neither shall the covenant of my peace be removed"

The Messiah as both the source and embodiment of shalom. Isa 53:5 makes shalom the result of vicarious suffering — the chastisement that produces our wholeness/well-being fell upon him.
