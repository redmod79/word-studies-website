## Lexicon Definition

From παρασκευάζω (G3903); readiness, preparation. In the NT used exclusively as a calendrical term for the day of preparation (Friday), the day before the Sabbath.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. The Day Before the Sabbath (Friday in Passion Week)

The Synoptic Gospels use παρασκευή to mark the day of Jesus's crucifixion as the Friday before the Sabbath.

- **Mar 15:42** — "And now when the even was come, because it was the preparation, that is, the day before the sabbath"
- **Luk 23:54** — "And that day was the preparation, and the sabbath drew on"
- **Mat 27:62** — "Now the next day, that followed the day of the preparation, the chief priests and Pharisees came together unto Pilate"

### 2. The Passover Preparation Day in John

John uses παρασκευή three times, each in the Passover context, stressing the time pressure governing the burial of Jesus.

- **Jhn 19:14** — "And it was the preparation of the passover, and about the sixth hour: and he saith unto the Jews, Behold your King!"
- **Jhn 19:31** — "The Jews therefore, because it was the preparation, that the bodies should not remain upon the cross on the sabbath day"
- **Jhn 19:42** — "There laid they Jesus therefore because of the Jews' preparation [day]; for the sepulchre was nigh at hand"
