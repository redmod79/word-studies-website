# WG: Gate — Entries and Openings

## Overview
The NT gate/door concept uses three Greek words with overlapping but distinct semantic ranges. All three translate Hebrew "gate/door" words in the LXX but divide the concept along architectural lines: πύλη is the city or camp gate, πυλών is the gateway or porch structure, and θύρα is the door of a house or room.

## Roles in the Concept

- **Monumental Entry:** πύλη (pyle) — the gate of a city, camp, or sacred precinct; the boundary between public and private, inside and outside, sacred and profane
- **Gateway Structure:** πυλών (pylon) — the architectural passage or porch through which one walks; the vestibule space between outside and inside
- **Household Entry:** θύρα (thyra) — the door of a building, room, or enclosure; smaller scale, domestic or figurative ("I am the door," John 10:7, 9)

## Key Passages Where Multiple Group Words Appear Together

- **Acts 12:10-13** — Peter escapes through the "iron gate" (πύλη, v.10) of the prison, arrives at Mary's house and knocks at "the door of the gate" (θύρα τοῦ πυλῶνος, v.13) — all three words in a single narrative, each with distinct architectural reference
