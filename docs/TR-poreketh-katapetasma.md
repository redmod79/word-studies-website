## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same across testaments:**
- The katapetasma refers to the same physical object — the inner curtain before the Holy of Holies
- Its function as a barrier separating humanity from the immediate divine presence is assumed in both testaments
- The Day of Atonement restriction (Lev 16:2) underlies every NT reference

**What shifts:**
- OT poreketh is strictly architectural/liturgical — construction materials, placement instructions, blood-sprinkling regulations
- NT katapetasma becomes an event (torn at the crucifixion) and a metaphor (Christ's flesh as the veil)
- The directional language "from the top to the bottom" (Matt 27:51, Mark 15:38) has no OT parallel — it signals divine initiative in removing the barrier
- Hebrews reinterprets the veil-as-barrier into veil-as-opened-way: the same object that once restricted access now marks the consecrated path
