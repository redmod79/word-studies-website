## Key Grammatical Features

### 1. εἶδον with Present Participles (v.1)

- **Form:** εἶδον is V-2AAI-1S, "I saw." καταβαίνοντα and ἔχοντα are present active participles, accusative masculine singular, agreeing with ἄγγελον.
- **Significance:** The finite aorist introduces the seer's vision; the participles describe the angel as "coming down" and "having" the key/chain within the vision scene.
- **Grammar reference:** [vision-introduction-formulas](../greek/vision-introduction-formulas.md); [apocalyptic-narrative-prolepsis](../greek/apocalyptic-narrative-prolepsis.md)

### 2. Aorist Active Indicative Chain (vv.2-3)

- **Form:** ἐκράτησεν, ἔδησεν, ἔβαλεν, ἔκλεισεν, ἐσφράγισεν are all aorist active indicatives, 3rd singular.
- **Significance:** The grammar presents a chain of bounded actions: seizure, binding, casting, shutting, sealing. The verbs advance the narrated action without marking internal duration.
- **Grammar reference:** [aorist-active-indicative-narrative-chain](../greek/aorist-active-indicative-narrative-chain.md)

### 3. Appositional Identification of the Dragon (v.2)

- **Form:** τὸν δράκοντα is accusative object of ἐκράτησεν; ὁ ὄφις ὁ ἀρχαῖος stands in nominative apposition, followed by a relative clause ὅς ἐστιν Διάβολος καὶ Ὁ Σατανᾶς.
- **Significance:** The grammar supplies identity labels for the dragon inside the sentence. The nominative apposition is explanatory rather than a new finite clause.

### 4. ἵνα μὴ πλανήσῃ (v.3)

- **Form:** ἵνα + μή + πλανήσῃ (V-AAS-3S, aorist active subjunctive of πλανάω) introduces a negative purpose clause.
- **Significance:** The preceding actions are grammatically oriented toward the prevented outcome: "that he should not deceive the nations any longer." The aorist subjunctive views the possible deception as a bounded event/action.
- **Grammar reference:** [purpose-clause-hina-me-aorist-subjunctive](../greek/purpose-clause-hina-me-aorist-subjunctive.md)
- **Word study:** [G4105-planao](../../word-studies/G4105-planao.md)

### 5. δεῖ λυθῆναι αὐτὸν μικρὸν χρόνον (v.3)

- **Form:** δεῖ is V-PAI-3S, an impersonal necessity verb. λυθῆναι is V-APN, aorist passive infinitive of λύω. αὐτόν is accusative subject of the infinitive. μικρὸν χρόνον is accusative of duration/extent.
- **Significance:** The clause means "it is necessary for him to be loosed for a short time." The passive infinitive marks the subject as undergoing release; the accusative time phrase marks extent, not object.
- **Grammar reference:** [aorist-infinitive-accusative-of-duration](../greek/aorist-infinitive-accusative-of-duration.md) for the duration syntax; [divine-passive](../greek/divine-passive.md) as a related passive-voice category where relevant by context.
