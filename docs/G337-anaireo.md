# G337 — ἀναιρέω (anaireō)

## Core Data
- **Strong's:** G337
- **Original:** ἀναιρέω
- **Transliteration:** anaireō
- **Pronunciation:** an-ahee-reh-o
- **Part of Speech:** Verb (v)
- **Root:** Compound of ἀνά (G303, "up") + αἱρέω (G138, "to take, choose")
- **Literal sense:** "to take up"
- **Derived senses:** (1) to take up into one's arms / adopt; (2) to take up, i.e. make away with, destroy, kill; (3) to abolish, set aside (of laws/practices)
- **Occurrences:** 24 tokens across 23 verses in 5 NT books (Acts 7:28 has two tokens)
- **First Occurrence:** Matthew 2:16 — "[Herod] sent forth, and slew all the children that were in Bethlehem"
- **Hapax Legomenon:** No

## Lexicon Definition

From ἀνά + (the active of) αἱρέομαι; to take up, i.e. adopt; by implication, to take away (violently), i.e. abolish, murder:—put to death, kill, slay, take away, take up.

The verb compounds ἀνά ("up") with αἱρέω ("take, choose"). The base sense is "take up from the ground." Greek developed two specialized extensions from this: the middle voice for taking up a child into one's arms (adoption; cf. Acts 7:21) and the active voice idiomatically for "taking off" a life — i.e. killing. Both senses appear in the NT.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Judicial / State Execution
Formal putting to death by political or judicial authority.
- **Matthew 2:16** — Herod "slew all the children" (ἀνεῖλεν)
- **Luke 23:32** — two malefactors "led with him to be put to death" (ἀναιρεθῆναι)
- **Acts 12:2** — Herod "killed James the brother of John with the sword" (ἀνεῖλεν)
- **Acts 13:28** — though no cause of death, "they Pilate that he should be slain" (ἀναιρεθῆναι)
- **Acts 26:10** — "when they were put to death, I gave my voice against them" (ἀναιρουμένων)

### 2. Plotted / Conspired Killing
Lethal intent, often expressed as aorist infinitive after ζητέω / βουλεύομαι / συντίθεμαι ("seek / counsel / agree to kill").
- **Luke 22:2** — chief priests "sought how they might kill him" (ἀνέλωσιν)
- **Acts 5:33** — council "took counsel to slay them" (ἀνελεῖν)
- **Acts 9:23** — Jews "took counsel to kill him" (ἀνελεῖν)
- **Acts 9:24** — "watched the gates day and night to kill him" (ἀνέλωσιν)
- **Acts 9:29** — "they went about to slay him" (ἀνελεῖν)
- **Acts 23:15** — "we... are ready to kill him" (ἀνελεῖν)
- **Acts 23:21** — men bound by oath "till they have killed him" (ἀνέλωσιν)
- **Acts 25:3** — "laying wait in the way to kill him" (ἀνελεῖν)

### 3. Completed Act of Killing (Historical Narrative)
Aorist indicative for past killings.
- **Acts 2:23** — "ye have taken, and by wicked hands have crucified and slain" (ἀνείλατε) — of Christ
- **Acts 5:36** — Theudas "was slain" (ἀνῃρέθη)
- **Acts 7:28** — "Wilt thou kill me, as thou diddest the Egyptian yesterday?" (ἀνελεῖν / ἀνεῖλες) — Moses' challenger
- **Acts 10:39** — "whom they slew and hanged on a tree" (ἀνεῖλαν) — of Christ
- **Acts 22:20** — "them that slew him" (ἀναιρούντων) — Stephen's killers
- **Acts 23:27** — "should have been killed of them" (ἀναιρεῖσθαι) — Paul

### 4. Attempted / Prevented Killing
- **Acts 16:27** — jailer "would have killed himself" (ἀναιρεῖν) — thwarted suicide

### 5. Taking Up (Middle Voice, Adoption Sense)
Only one NT instance preserves the literal "take up" sense.
- **Acts 7:21** — "Pharaoh's daughter took him up, and nourished him for her own son" (ἀνείλατο, aorist middle) — of Moses

### 6. Abolishing / Setting Aside
Metaphorical "taking away" of a prior order.
- **Hebrews 10:9** — "He taketh away the first [covenant/sacrifice], that he may establish the second" (ἀναιρεῖ, present active)

### 7. Eschatological Slaying
The future-tense use unique in the NT.
- **2 Thessalonians 2:8** (critical text) — "whom the Lord shall slay with the breath of his mouth" (ἀνελεῖ, V-2FAI-3S)

## 2 Thessalonians 2:8 — Textual Variant

Two readings stand in the manuscript tradition for the verb describing the Lord's act against ὁ ἄνομος ("the lawless one"):

| Reading | Verb | Lemma | Gloss | Witnesses |
|---------|------|-------|-------|-----------|
| **Critical text** (NA28/UBS5) | ἀνελεῖ | ἀναιρέω (G337) | "will slay / take away" | א A B P Ψ 0278 33 1739 1881 Maj (majority of Greek mss) |
| **Textus Receptus** | ἀναλώσει | ἀναλίσκω (G355) | "will consume / destroy" | D F G and a minority; followed by KJV "shall consume" |

The critical-text reading ἀνελεῖ (future active indicative 3rd singular of ἀναιρέω) is the basis of the Isa 11:4 LXX allusion. The TR reading ἀναλώσει (from ἀναλίσκω, "consume utterly") is semantically close but lexically distinct; it would remove the direct verbal link to the LXX of Isaiah 11:4.

Parallel in the same verse: καὶ καταργήσει τῇ ἐπιφανείᾳ τῆς παρουσίας αὐτοῦ — "and shall destroy/render-inoperative by the appearing of his coming" (καταργέω, G2673). The two verbs (ἀνελεῖ ... καταργήσει) form a parallel couplet: slaying/abolishing by his breath (present/ongoing) and by his appearing (final).

## Isaiah 11:4 LXX Allusion

The critical-text reading in 2 Thess 2:8 echoes verbatim the LXX of Isa 11:4:

> **LXX Isa 11:4b:** καὶ πατάξει γῆν τῷ λόγῳ τοῦ στόματος αὐτοῦ καὶ ἐν πνεύματι διὰ χειλέων ἀνελεῖ ἀσεβῆ.
> "And he shall smite the earth with the word of his mouth, and with breath through his lips he shall slay the ungodly."

> **2 Thess 2:8b (critical):** ὃν ὁ Κύριος Ἰησοῦς ἀνελεῖ τῷ πνεύματι τοῦ στόματος αὐτοῦ.
> "whom the Lord Jesus shall slay with the breath of his mouth."

Both use ἀνελεῖ (V-2FAI-3S) as the finite verb of slaying, and both pair the verb with πνεῦμα + στόμα/χείλη ("breath of the mouth/lips"). The MT of Isa 11:4 has וּבְרוּחַ שְׂפָתָיו יָמִית רָשָׁע — "and with the breath of his lips he shall slay the wicked" (H4191 mûth, "put to death"). The LXX renders the Hebrew יָמִית ("slay") by ἀνελεῖ, and Paul picks up the LXX form directly.

Paul substitutes στόματος ("mouth") for the LXX's χειλέων ("lips") but retains πνεύματι and ἀνελεῖ. This is the tightest verbal allusion in the immediate clause.

## Isaiah 11:4 Lexical Pathway Summary

The allusive chain connecting the OT messianic prophecy to 2 Thess 2:8:

```
MT Isa 11:4  →  LXX Isa 11:4  →  2 Thess 2:8 (critical text)
יָמִית רָשָׁע  →  ἀνελεῖ ἀσεβῆ  →  ἀνελεῖ (obj.: ὁ ἄνομος)
"slay the wicked"   "slay the ungodly"   "slay [the lawless one]"
(בְּרוּחַ שְׂפָתָיו) → (ἐν πνεύματι διὰ χειλέων) → (τῷ πνεύματι τοῦ στόματος αὐτοῦ)
"with the breath of his lips"
```

The future-tense verb ἀνελεῖ occurs only once in the NT (2 Thess 2:8) and once in the LXX of the Major Prophets in this formulation (Isa 11:4). The lexical match + the πνεῦμα + στόμα/χείλη word field establishes the allusion. The TR variant ἀναλώσει ("will consume," from ἀναλίσκω G355) breaks the direct verbal link, though retains the sense.

## Notable Patterns

- **Acts-heavy distribution:** 19 of 24 tokens in Acts reflects Luke's recurring narrative of plots against Paul and other believers, and the legal/political vocabulary of state execution.
- **Aorist dominance:** 20 of 24 tokens are aorist (either indicative of completed killing or infinitive/subjunctive of intended killing); the 4 present tokens are either iterative (Acts 22:20; 26:10), thwarted (Acts 16:27, 23:27), or principial (Heb 10:9).
- **Christ as object:** Acts 2:23; 10:39; 13:28 all use ἀναιρέω for the killing of Christ — a deliberate Lukan echo.
- **Future tense unique:** 2 Thess 2:8 is the only future form of this verb in the NT.
