## Semantic Categories

### 1. Physical Alteration — Change of Garments / External State
- **2Ki 25:29** — "And changed (וְשִׁנָּא, Piel perfect 3ms) his prison garments: and he did eat bread continually before him all the days of his life." Jehoiachin's release from Babylonian captivity — the change of garments marks a change of status from prisoner to royal guest.

### 2. Alteration of Appearance / Countenance
- **Ecc 8:1** — "a man's wisdom maketh his face to shine, and the boldness (עֹז, strength/hardness) of his face shall be changed (יְשֻׁנֶּא, Pual imperfect 3ms)." Wisdom softens what was formerly hard or severe — the inner quality produces an outward alteration.

### 3. Degradation / Loss of Quality
- **Lam 4:1** — "How is the gold become dim! how is the most fine gold changed (יִשְׁנֶא, Qal imperfect 3ms)! the stones of the sanctuary are poured out in the top of every street." Jerusalem's glory is likened to gold that has lost its luster — change here means deterioration, the finest material becoming something lesser.
