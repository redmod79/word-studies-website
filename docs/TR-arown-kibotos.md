## LXX Bridge

| Hebrew | Greek Translation | Count | Notes |
|--------|------------------|-------|-------|
| H727 ʼārôn | κιβωτός (G2787) | 160x | Primary and near-exclusive equivalent; PMI 34.64 |
| H8392 têbâh | κιβωτός (G2787) | 23x | Noah's ark and Moses' basket |

**Semantic shift:** The LXX chose the same κιβωτός for both H727 and H8392, merging two distinct Hebrew objects under one Greek term. In Hebrew, the Ark of the Covenant (aron) and Noah's ark (tevah) are lexically unrelated; in Greek (and therefore in NT typology), they share identity. This is a significant LXX simplification that opens a typological bridge between Noah's salvation and the Mosaic sanctuary.

The other major Greek words co-occurring with H727 in the LXX — διαθήκη (G1242, covenant), ἱλαστήριον (G2435, mercy seat), μαρτύριον (G3142, testimony), πλάξ (G4109, tablets) — show the covenant/sanctuary semantic field surrounding the ark.

## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same:**
- κιβωτός is used exclusively for these two objects in all NT occurrences; there is no semantic drift
- The Ark of the Covenant retains its full OT theological weight: covenant, divine presence, law tablets, and Aaron's rod
- The Hebraic phrase "ark of the covenant/testament" (Heb 9:4; Rev 11:19) directly mirrors the OT formula ʼărôn habberîth

**What shifts:**
- The LXX's lexical fusion (one word for both arks) enables typology that the Hebrew text resists — Noah's salvation through water connects to baptism (1 Pet 3:20–21); Noah's ark as a "saving" vessel (Heb 11:7) implicitly echoes the salvation function of the covenant ark
- In Revelation, the heavenly ark (Rev 11:19) appears as a climactic eschatological disclosure — the covenant object is real, intact, and resident in the heavenly sanctuary; this presupposes the full OT theology of H727
