## Roles in the Concept

- **Primary term (ʻânân H6051):** The nimbus / thunder-cloud; the narrative term for God's presence-cloud in Exodus–Numbers and the judgment-cloud of the prophets. 44% of occurrences are in the wilderness narrative alone.
- **Storm / dark cloud (ʻâb H5645):** The thick, enveloping cloud. Favored in poetic/wisdom texts (Job, Psalms) and in contexts of concealment. Job 26:8 — "He bindeth up the waters in his thick clouds."
- **Cloudiness (ʻănânâh H6053):** Abstract hapax — the state or condition of being cloudy.
- **LXX/NT bridge (nephele G3507):** The primary Greek equivalent. In NT, consistently the theophanic/eschatological cloud — transfiguration, ascension, second coming.
- **Cloud mass (nephos G3509):** Only once in NT (Heb 12:1 — "cloud of witnesses"); metaphorical, not physical.

## Key Passages Where Hebrew Distinction Matters

- **Isa 44:22** — "I have blotted out, as a thick cloud (ʻâb), thy transgressions, and, as a cloud (ʻânân), thy sins" — both words in one verse; ʻâb = the denser, ʻânân = the lighter morning-cloud image
- **Exo 19:9** — "in a thick cloud (ʻânân)" — divine communication uses ʻânân, not ʻâb
- **Job 26:8** — "He bindeth up the waters in his thick clouds (ʻâb)" — meteorological / poetic register
