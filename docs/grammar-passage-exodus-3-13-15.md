# Grammar Analysis: Exodus 3:13-15

**The Divine Name Revealed at the Burning Bush**

Core passage for the self-revelation of YHWH. The grammatical core is the three-fold *ehyeh* (אֶהְיֶה) of v.14 linked to the tetragrammaton יהוה of v.15, with *asher* (אֲשֶׁר) as the relative particle binding them into a single self-predicating formula. Part of the ongoing study "What does YHWH mean — grammar and word study."

**Decisive observation:** In Clause 6, the verb-form *ehyeh* is used *as if it were a proper name*— the predicate-subject of the qatal verb שְׁלָחַנִי ("he sent me"). A finite 1cs imperfect verb stands in the syntactic slot where a divine name is expected. This is the morphological bridge to v.15's יהוה.

## Key Grammatical Features

### 1. *ehyeh* (אֶהְיֶה) — Qal Imperfect 1cs of היה

- **Form:** Qal.Impf.1cs of the verb היה ("to be, become, happen, exist").
- **Morphology:** Prefix א- (1cs marker) + stem -הי- + suffix -ה. The short/jussive equivalent would be אֱהִי; the full yiqtol אֶהְיֶה is the normal long-form imperfect.
- **Semantic range of יהה:** Hebrew imperfect is **non-past aspect** — it covers present-continuous, future, habitual, modal, and "whatever X is/does" generic readings. The verb היה itself ranges from bare existence ("be, exist") through identity-copula ("be X") to eventive ("become, come to pass"). This triple ambiguity is precisely why Ex 3:14 resists a single translation: "I am," "I will be," and "I become whatever I choose to become" are all grammatically licit readings of *ehyeh*.
- **Word study:** [H1961-hayah](../../word-studies/H1961-hayah.md) — comprehensive study of היה including the Exodus 3:14 crux.

### 2. *asher* (אֲשֶׁר) — Relative Particle Functioning as Nominal Head

- **Parsing:** The BHSA codes אֲשֶׁר in 3:14 as `Conj` (conjunction/subordinator); traditional grammars classify it as the pan-purpose **relative particle** (GKC §138, Joüon §158).
- **Function in *ehyeh asher ehyeh*:** אֲשֶׁר introduces a relative clause modifying (and here, substantivizing) the antecedent predicate. Because Hebrew relatives can be **headless**, the whole phrase אֲשֶׁר אֶהְיֶה = "[that which / who] I am."
- **Translation consequences:**
  - **"I AM THAT I AM"** (KJV) — renders the relative as identity-predicate ("I am the-very-one-who I am"); tautological-sounding but faithful to the Hebrew self-referential loop.
  - **"I will be who I will be"** — takes the imperfect as future and *asher* as personal relative ("the one who").
  - **"I am the one who is"** — reads *asher ehyeh* as a headless relative substantivized ("the [one] who is"), matching the LXX ὁ ὤν rendering.
  - **"I am because I am"** / **"I will be whatever I will be"** — emphatic/sovereign-freedom reading (Rashi, von Rad): the relative absorbs a clause of free self-determination.
- **Grammar reference:** The *asher + yiqtol* relative clause is the core grammar here; no dedicated library entry for *asher* yet (candidate for future entry).

### 3. Person-Shift: 1cs *ehyeh* (v.14) → 3ms YHWH (v.15)

- **Morphological bridge:** The tetragrammaton יהוה has traditionally been analyzed as a **3ms form of the same root היה/הוה** ("he is / he will be"), i.e. the third-person counterpart of the first-person *ehyeh*. The parser confirms both belong to the same lemma-family: v.14's אֶהְיֶה is parsed `היה Verb.Qal.Impf.1cs`; v.15's יְהוָה is parsed as `יהוה PropN.ms.Abs` — the name has been nominalized.
- **Grammatical shift:**
  - God speaking of himself → **1cs:** "I am" (אֶהְיֶה)
  - Moses/Israel speaking of him → **3ms:** "He is" (יהוה)
- **Why it matters:** The very same semantic content ("the One who is / will be") is grammaticalized twice — once as a finite verb in God's mouth, once as a proper noun on human lips. This is a deliberate person-switch, not a change of deity. It is the single clearest point in Scripture where the divine name is explained morphologically rather than etymologically.
- **Consonantal arithmetic:** Root ה-י-ה (or the alternate ה-ו-ה) + 3ms prefix י- yields יהוה. The middle waw/yod reflects the weak III-ה / II-waw alternation well-attested in this verb (cf. הֹוֶה "being," Ecc 2:22; הָוָה "falls/happens," Neh 6:6). The 3ms imperfect thus reads phonetically as *yihyeh* / *yahweh* / *yahaweh* — "he is" / "he will be" / "he causes to be" (Hiphil-causative is a minority reading, defended by Albright, contested by most).

### 4. *zeh shemi le-olam* (זֶה־שְּׁמִי לְעֹלָם) — Verbless Predicate-Nominal Naming Formula

- **Parsing:** DemPron.ms זֶה + Noun.ms.Abs.+1cs שְּׁמִי — a zero-copula **predicate-nominal clause** ("this [is] my name"). The parallel וְזֶה זִכְרִי ("and this [is] my memorial") repeats the pattern.
- **Force:** Explicit self-identification formula — the demonstrative זֶה points anaphorically backward to the name just uttered (יהוה in v.15, or the whole *ehyeh*-formula of vv.14–15 together). The 1cs pronominal suffix on שֵׁם locks it as God's own declaration ("*my* name"), not merely a human ascription.
- **Temporal extent:** לְעֹלָם ("to long-duration, forever") + the reduplicated construction-paralleled phrase לְדֹר דֹּר ("to generation [of] generation") declare the name as permanently binding on all subsequent Israel and the whole biblical corpus that follows. The reduplication לְדֹר דֹּר is an idiomatic perpetuity-formula (cf. Ps 45:18; 77:9; 119:90).
- **Grammar reference:** [symbol-decoding-predicate-nominal](../hebrew/symbol-decoding-predicate-nominal.md) — same verbless `DemPron + Noun` predication schema used in vision-decoding (Dan 8:20–21), here deployed for divine self-naming rather than symbol-reference resolution.

### 5. Speech-Frame Architecture: Triple *wayyomer* + Triple *ehyeh*

The pericope is structured around three successive divine speech-introductions (וַיֹּאמֶר, each Qal.Wayq.3ms of אמר) and three occurrences of אֶהְיֶה:

| Speech | Verb | Content |
|--------|------|---------|
| v.13 | Moses speaks | Question: "What is his name?" (מַה־שְּׁמֹו) |
| v.14a | וַיֹּאמֶר אֱלֹהִים | **אֶהְיֶה אֲשֶׁר אֶהְיֶה** (the formula) |
| v.14b | וַיֹּאמֶר (God to Moses) | **אֶהְיֶה** שְׁלָחַנִי אֲלֵיכֶם (the shortened name) |
| v.15 | וַיֹּאמֶר עֹוד אֱלֹהִים | **יְהוָה** אֱלֹהֵי אֲבֹתֵיכֶם... (the permanent name) |

**Progressive revelation pattern:**
1. First utterance: full three-word self-predicating formula (*ehyeh asher ehyeh*)
2. Second: abbreviated to bare *ehyeh* (the finite verb treated as name)
3. Third: person-shifted to the nominal יהוה bound to the patriarchal-covenant titles אֱלֹהֵי אַבְרָהָם / יִצְחָק / יַעֲקֹב

The עֹוד ("moreover") of v.15 signals an additional, amplifying disclosure — not a correction. The name YHWH of v.15 is thus grammatically equated with the *ehyeh* of v.14.

## Translation History of *ehyeh asher ehyeh*

| Version | Rendering | Grammar logic |
|---------|-----------|---------------|
| MT (Hebrew) | אֶהְיֶה אֲשֶׁר אֶהְיֶה | Qal impf. 1cs + rel. + Qal impf. 1cs |
| LXX | Ἐγώ εἰμι ὁ ὤν (Ex 3:14a), ὁ ὤν ἀπέσταλκέν με (3:14b) | 1cs pers. pron. + pres. ind. εἰμι + articular pres. ptcp. ὤν ("the Being-One") — reifies the relative clause as a nominal title |
| Vulgate | Ego sum qui sum | "I am who I am" — relative *qui* for אֲשֶׁר, indicative *sum* for *ehyeh*; preserves present-tense identity-copula |
| Targum Onkelos | אֶהְיֶה אֲשֶׁר אֶהְיֶה (Aramaic transliteration retained — untranslated) | Paraphrase refuses: "The one who said to the world, 'Be!' and it was; who said to it, 'Be!' and it shall be" (modal/future reading) |
| Targum Pseudo-Jonathan | "I am he who is and who will be" | Explicitly expands the imperfect into dual present+future |
| KJV | I AM THAT I AM | Small-caps marks the formula; "that" = archaic relative (= "who/which") |
| Rashi | "I will be with them in this distress as I will be with them in future oppressions" | Reads both *ehyeh*s as modal future of presence (cf. Ex 3:12 *ki-ehyeh immak*) |

**The LXX ὁ ὤν tradition** was absorbed into Christian theology as the ontological self-existence predicate, preserved in Revelation's self-designation formula ὁ ὢν καὶ ὁ ἦν καὶ ὁ ἐρχόμενος (Rev 1:4, 8; 4:8; 11:17; 16:5). Greek εἰμί (G1510) carries this forward; see [G1510-eimi](../../word-studies/G1510-eimi.md).

## Summary

The grammatical core of Ex 3:13–15 is a **three-move self-predication**:

1. **Formula (v.14a):** Qal impf. 1cs + *asher* rel. + Qal impf. 1cs — a self-referential loop of pure being/becoming, grammatically open-ended by design (the imperfect aspect refuses tense-pinning; the relative particle refuses predicate-restriction).
2. **Abbreviation (v.14b):** The bare finite verb אֶהְיֶה functions as a proper name — a 1cs verb-form in the slot of a divine name — binding identity to grammar.
3. **Nominalization (v.15):** The same verb is re-cast in the 3ms as יהוה, shifted from God's own speech into what humans may say of him; then sealed with the zero-copula predicate-nominal *zeh shemi le-olam* ("this is my name forever").

The morphological link *ehyeh* ↔ *YHWH* via the root ה-י-ה / ה-ו-ה is the single load-bearing grammatical fact of the passage; translation traditions (LXX ὁ ὤν; Vulg. *qui sum*; KJV I AM) each preserve only part of the Hebrew's deliberate triple ambiguity between existence, presence, and self-determining becoming.
