**Pattern:** H6842 is a strictly post-exilic / late-Biblical Hebrew word. It occurs nowhere in the Torah, Former Prophets, or pre-exilic Writings. Torah sin-offering texts use H8163 (śāʿîr) or H5795 (ʿēz); the Chronicler and Ezra shift to ṣāp̄îr (matched by the Aramaic ṣəp̄îr in Ezra 6:17). Daniel uses it vision-symbolically. The word's narrow lexical footprint correlates with Second-Temple dating and with the same Aramaic-influenced register found in Daniel.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Sin-Offering Male Goat (Second-Temple Cult)
In all three non-Daniel verses the word appears in the construct chain **ṣəp̄îrê ʿizzîm** — "he-goats of the she-goats" — a fixed late-Hebrew formula for the male of the goat flock designated for sin offering (חַטָּאת).

- **2 Chr 29:21** — "seven bullocks, and seven rams, and seven lambs, and seven he goats (ṣəp̄îrê ʿizzîm), for a sin offering for the kingdom, and for the sanctuary, and for Judah" — Hezekiah's Temple cleansing.
- **Ezra 8:35** — "twelve bullocks... ninety and six rams, seventy and seven lambs, twelve he goats (ṣəp̄îrê) for a sin offering" — Ezra's returning exiles' burnt offering.
- **Ezra 6:17** (Aramaic cognate H6841) — "twelve he goats (ṣəp̄îrê ʿizzîn), according to the number of the tribes of Israel" — dedication of the Second Temple.

### 2. Apocalyptic Symbol of a World Kingdom (Daniel 8)
In Daniel the word moves from cultic referent to visionary animal-symbol, identified explicitly within the text.

- **Dan 8:5** — "behold, an he goat (ṣəp̄îr hā-ʿizzîm) came from the west... and the goat (haṣ-ṣāp̄îr) had a notable horn between his eyes." Here the same construct idiom "ṣəp̄îr hā-ʿizzîm" of the cultic texts is repurposed as a vision-animal.
- **Dan 8:8** — "Therefore the he goat (ṣəp̄îr hā-ʿizzîm) waxed very great: and when he was strong, the great horn was broken."
- **Dan 8:21** — "And the rough goat (haṣ-ṣāp̄îr haś-śāʿîr) is the king of Grecia (melek yāwān): and the great horn that is between his eyes is the first king." This is the interpretive key: ṣāp̄îr qualified by śāʿîr "hairy/shaggy" = the kingdom of Greece.

### Notable Verbal Overlap
H6842 and H8163 share the KJV gloss "goat" but are etymologically unrelated: śāʿîr derives from "hair/shagginess," ṣāp̄îr from "prancing." Their juxtaposition in Dan 8:21 is therefore not tautological but deliberately lexically layered — the post-exilic/vision word qualified by the Torah-sacrificial word.

## Morphological Notes

In all 8 tokens ṣāp̄îr is a masculine noun, singular or plural.
- **Singular absolute** (haṣ-ṣāp̄îr): Dan 8:5b, 8:8a, 8:21a — "the he-goat" as the discourse-topical vision animal.
- **Singular construct** (ṣəp̄îr hā-ʿizzîm): Dan 8:5a — formulaic "he-goat of the goats."
- **Plural construct** (ṣəp̄îrê ʿizzîm / ṣəp̄îrê ḥaṭṭāʾt): 2 Chr 29:21, Ezra 8:35 — the post-exilic sin-offering formula.

Dan 8:21 is lexically the densest token: **וְהַצָּפִיר הַשָּׂעִיר מֶלֶךְ יָוָן** — "and the he-goat, the shaggy-one, is king of Greece." Both haṣ-ṣāp̄îr and haś-śāʿîr carry the definite article; haś-śāʿîr functions attributively.
