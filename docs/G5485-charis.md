## Lexicon Definition

Graciousness (as gratifying), of manner or act (abstract or concrete; literal, figurative or spiritual; especially the divine influence upon the heart, and its reflection in the life; including gratitude). KJV: grace, liberality, pleasure, thanks, worthy.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Epistolary Greeting — "Grace and Peace"
Opening formula in virtually every Pauline letter, linking charis with eirēnē.
- **Rom 1:7** — "Grace to you and peace from God our Father, and the Lord Jesus Christ"
- **Eph 1:2** — "Grace [be] to you, and peace, from God our Father, and [from] the Lord Jesus Christ"
- **1 Tim 1:2** — "Grace, mercy, [and] peace, from God our Father and Jesus Christ our Lord"

### 2. Divine Saving Grace — Unmerited Favor in Justification
The theological core: salvation, justification, and calling are entirely by God's free gift, not works.
- **Rom 3:24** — "Being justified freely by his grace through the redemption that is in Christ Jesus"
- **Eph 2:5** — "by grace ye are saved"
- **Tit 2:11** — "For the grace of God that bringeth salvation hath appeared to all men"
- **2 Tim 1:9** — "not according to our works, but according to his own purpose and grace"

### 3. Appointed Ministry Grace — Grace Given for Service
Grace as specific divine enablement distributed for apostolic or ministerial function.
- **Rom 1:5** — "By whom we have received grace and apostleship"
- **Rom 12:6** — "Having then gifts differing according to the grace that is given to us"
- **1 Cor 3:10** — "According to the grace of God which is given unto me, as a wise masterbuilder"
- **Eph 4:7** — "unto every one of us is given grace according to the measure of the gift of Christ"

### 4. Social Favor — Relational Goodwill (OT-Flavored)
Luke and early Acts preserve the OT idiom of ḥēn: finding favor in someone's sight.
- **Luke 1:30** — "thou hast found favour with God"
- **Luke 2:52** — "Jesus increased in wisdom and stature, and in favour with God and man"
- **Acts 2:47** — "having favour with all the people"
- **Acts 7:10** — "gave him favour and wisdom in the sight of Pharaoh"

### 5. Gratitude / Thanks to God
Charis used as an exclamation of thanks — the gratitude that grace evokes.
- **Rom 6:17** — "God be thanked (charis de tō theō)"
- **1 Cor 15:57** — "thanks be to God, which giveth us the victory"
- **2 Cor 2:14** — "thanks be unto God, which always causeth us to triumph"
- **2 Tim 1:3** — "I thank God, whom I serve from [my] forefathers"

### 6. Epistolary Benediction — Closing Grace Blessing
Virtually every NT letter closes with charis upon the recipients.
- **Rom 16:20** — "The grace of our Lord Jesus Christ [be] with you"
- **1 Cor 16:23** — "The grace of our Lord Jesus Christ [be] with you"
- **Rev 22:21** — "The grace of our Lord Jesus Christ [be] with you all. Amen"
