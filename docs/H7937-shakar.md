## Lexicon Definition

A primitive root; to become tipsy; in a qualified sense, to satiate with a stimulating drink or (figuratively) influence. Translations: "(be filled with) drink (abundantly), (be, make) drunk(-en), be merry." Superlative of shathah (H8354).

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Literal Intoxication from Wine
- **Gen 9:21** — Noah "drank of the wine, and was drunken."
- **Gen 43:34** — Joseph's brothers "drank, and were merry (yishkeru) with him."
- **1 Sam 1:14** — Eli: "How long wilt thou be drunken (tishtakkarin)?" (Hithpael — acting drunk.)
- **2 Sam 11:13** — David "made him (Uriah) drunk (yeshakkerehu)" (Piel).

### 2. Satiation / Being Filled (Qualified Sense)
- **Hag 1:6** — "ye drink, but ye are not filled with drink (shakrah)." Root sense of satisfaction through drink, negated.
- **Song 5:1** — "drink, yea, drink abundantly (shikru), O beloved." Fullness of shared drinking, no drunkenness connotation.

### 3. Divine Judgment — Cup of Wrath
- **Jer 25:27** — "Drink ye, and be drunken (shikru), and spue, and fall... because of the sword."
- **Jer 48:26** — "Make ye him (Moab) drunken (hashkiruhu): for he magnified himself against the LORD."
- **Jer 51:7** — "Babylon... made all the earth drunken (meshakkereth)."
- **Jer 51:39, 57** — "I will make them drunken (hishkartim)... that they may sleep a perpetual sleep."
- **Lam 4:21** — "the cup also shall pass through unto thee: thou shalt be drunken (tishkeri)."
- **Nah 3:11** — "Thou (Nineveh) also shalt be drunken (tishkeri)."
- **Isa 63:6** — "I will tread down the people in mine anger, and make them drunk (ashakkerem) in my fury."

### 4. Drunkenness in Blood / Violence Imagery
- **Deut 32:42** — "I will make mine arrows drunk (ashkir) with blood."
- **Isa 49:26** — "they shall be drunken (shakar) with their own blood, as with sweet wine."

### 5. Stupefaction — Drunkenness Not from Wine
- **Isa 29:9** — "they are drunken (shakeru), but not with wine; they stagger, but not with strong drink." Spiritual/judicial blindness.
- **Isa 51:21** — "thou afflicted, and drunken (shekurath), but not with wine."

### 6. Provocation to Drunkenness (as Sin)
- **Hab 2:15** — "Woe unto him that giveth his neighbour drink... and makest him drunken (shakker) also." Piel infinitive — deliberate inducement condemned.
