## Semantic Categories

### 1. Miraculous Cleansing of Leprosy (Synoptics)
The largest single cluster: Jesus pronounces and effects the purification that Levitical law could only declare.
- **Matt 8:2–3** — "Lord, if thou wilt, thou canst make me clean... I will; be thou clean. And immediately his leprosy was cleansed."
- **Matt 10:8** — "Heal the sick, cleanse the lepers, raise the dead..." (disciples commissioned)
- **Luke 4:27** — "Many lepers were in Israel... none of them was cleansed, saving Naaman the Syrian"
- **Luke 17:14–17** — "Go shew yourselves unto the priests... Were there not ten cleansed? but where are the nine?"

### 2. God Redefining "Clean" — Gentile Inclusion (Acts)
Katharizō used by a divine voice to declare that categories have changed.
- **Acts 10:15** — "What God hath cleansed, that call not thou common"
- **Acts 11:9** — Peter repeats the same word to the Jerusalem council
- **Acts 15:9** — "purifying their hearts by faith" — the inner reality behind the outer declaration

### 3. Ritual / Blood Purification (Hebrews)
The Levitical purification system — then its eschatological fulfillment in Christ's blood.
- **Heb 9:14** — "the blood of Christ... purge your conscience from dead works to serve the living God"
- **Heb 9:22** — "almost all things are by the law purged with blood; and without shedding of blood is no remission"
- **Heb 9:23** — "the patterns of things in the heavens should be purified with these; but the heavenly things themselves with better sacrifices"
- **Heb 10:2** — "worshippers once purged should have had no more conscience of sins"

### 4. Spiritual/Divine Cleansing Through Christ (Epistles)
Christ's redemptive work as the agent and ground of cleansing.
- **Eph 5:26** — "That he might sanctify and cleanse it [the church] with the washing of water by the word"
- **Tit 2:14** — "Who gave himself for us, that he might... purify unto himself a peculiar people"
- **1 John 1:7** — "the blood of Jesus Christ his Son cleanseth us from all sin"
- **1 John 1:9** — "he is faithful and just... to cleanse us from all unrighteousness"

### 5. Ethical / Self-Purification (Call to Action)
Imperatives addressed to believers to purify themselves.
- **2 Cor 7:1** — "let us cleanse ourselves from all filthiness of the flesh and spirit"
- **Jas 4:8** — "Cleanse [your] hands, ye sinners; and purify [your] hearts, ye double minded"

### 6. Hypocritical Outer Cleansing (Jesus' Critique)
Used in Jesus' woe against washing the outside while leaving the inside defiled.
- **Matt 23:25** — "for ye make clean the outside of the cup and of the platter, but within they are full of extortion"
- **Matt 23:26** — "cleanse first that which is within the cup and platter, that the outside of them may be clean also"
- **Luke 11:39** — parallel to Matt 23:25

### 7. Purging — Dietary / Ceremonial (Mark)
One instance of the word in a food/body context.
- **Mark 7:19** — "goeth out into the draught, purging all meats" (parenthetical editorial comment)
