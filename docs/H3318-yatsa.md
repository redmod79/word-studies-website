**Note on animals:** In the ms Qal participle corpus, no instance has an animal as its grammatical subject. Jer 5:6 uses הַיּוֹצֵא for "every one that goeth out thence" (from the besieged city) — the threat is a lion, but the participle refers to the *human* who exits. Animal referents for the root do occur (Gen 8:19, Gen 9:10 יֹצְאֵי — masc. *plural* construct, "those going out of the ark," covering every living creature), but the **masculine-singular** substantival participle across the HB is distributed almost exclusively over human individuals, human groups, human offspring, deity, and inanimate natural/abstract phenomena.

**Formulaic frame "whosoever cometh forth of the doors of X":** Jdg 11:31 (Jephthah) is the only OT instance of הַיּוֹצֵא + "doors of my house" to-meet-me. The closest idiomatic neighbors are:
- 1 Sam 2:33 — "the increase of thine house shall die in the flower of their age" (context: family)
- Prov 7:15 — harlot: "I came forth (יָצָאתִי) to meet thee"
- Phrases of the type "he that goeth out and cometh in" (Num 27:17, Deu 28:6, 1 Ki 3:7, 2 Chr 1:10) invariably describe human leadership/civic life, not livestock movement.

The grammatical pattern יוֹצֵא + לִקְרָאתִי ("coming out to meet me") is itself strongly human — meeting (קָרָא I / לִקְרַאת) is a social act performed by persons, not animals, across its ~100 OT occurrences.
