# WG: Trust

## Overview
The trust/confidence concept in Hebrew Scripture involves a cluster of verbs and nouns describing reliance upon, refuge in, and confident dependence upon God or other objects. These words overlap in the KJV translation "trust" but carry distinct emphases: volitional confidence (batach), flight to refuge (chasah), firm belief (aman), supportive leaning (shaan), and their nominal counterparts for the state or object of trust.

## Roles in the Concept

- **Settled confidence:** batach (בָּטַח) — volitional, deliberate trust; the default OT "trust" verb; implies calm assurance
- **Crisis refuge:** chasah (חָסָה) — fleeing to God for protection in danger; more urgent and physical than batach
- **Firm belief:** aman (אָמַן) — believing God to be reliable and true; trust as intellectual conviction (→ H530 emunah "faithfulness")
- **Dependent leaning:** shaan (שָׁעַן) — supporting oneself upon; trust as physical dependence metaphor
- **Object of trust:** mibtach (מִבְטָח) — what/whom one trusts in; can be God (Jer 17:7) or false confidence (Job 8:14)
- **Place of refuge:** machaceh (מַחֲסֶה) — shelter, hiding place; the spatial metaphor for divine protection
- **Confidence/folly:** kesel (כֶּסֶל) — confidence that may be well-founded or foolish; ambivalent trust
