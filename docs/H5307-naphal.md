# H5307 — נָפַל (nâphal)

## Core Data
- **Strong's:** H5307
- **Original:** נָפַל
- **Transliteration:** nâphal
- **Pronunciation:** naw-fal'
- **Part of Speech:** Verb (primitive root)
- **Occurrences:** 403 unique verses (539 tokens) across 33 books
- **First Occurrence:** Gen 2:21 — "the LORD God caused a deep sleep to fall upon Adam"
- **Hapax Legomenon:** No

## Lexicon Definition

A primitive root; to fall, in a great variety of applications (intransitive or causative, literal or figurative). KJV renders with a wide spread: be accepted, cast (down, self, lots, out), cease, die, divide (by lot), fail, (cause/let/make/ready to) fall (away, down, -en, -ing), fell, fugitive, have (inheritance), inferior, be judged (by mistake for H6419), lay (along), lie down, light (down), be (hast) lost, lying, overthrow, overwhelm, perish, present(-ed, -ing), (make to) rot, slay, smite out, surely, throw down. Core sense: the motion or resultant state of descending by gravity, loss of upright posture, or being overthrown — extended metaphorically into judgment, death, prostration, lot-casting, and allotment.

The Hithpael is the reserved voice of worship-prostration.

Heaviest concentration in the historical books of kingdom warfare (1–2 Sam, 2 Ki, 1 Chr, Judg) and the judgment prophets (Ezek, Jer, Isa, Amos). Esther tops by density because of the idiomatic "Pur" — the lot that "fell." The verb is comparatively scarce in the Torah legal corpus (Lev/Num/Deu) and rare in Ezra and Ruth. Daniel uses it for Nebuchadnezzar's proskynesis (Dan 2:46 via parallel H5308) and for Gabriel's "casting down" of the stars (Dan 8:10).

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Literal Physical Fall
The unmarked gravitational sense — objects, persons, buildings collapse.
- **Gen 14:10** — "they fell there" (kings into slime pits)
- **2 Sam 1:4, 10** — "the people are fallen... he could not live after that he was fallen"
- **Job 1:19** — "a great wind... smote the four corners of the house, and it fell"
- **Amos 3:5** — "Can a bird fall in a snare upon the earth"

### 2. Death in Battle — "Fall by the Sword"
The most frequent idiom: *naphal be-chereb* = "fall by the sword," a formulaic battlefield death.
- **Num 14:29, 32** — carcasses "shall fall in this wilderness"
- **2 Sam 1:25** — "How are the mighty fallen in the midst of the battle!"
- **Isa 3:25** — "Thy men shall fall by the sword, and thy mighty in the war"
- **Jer 20:4; Eze 5:12; 6:7; 17:21; 23:25** — standard judgment-oracle vocabulary

### 3. Prostration in Worship or Homage
The voluntary or awed casting-down of the self before God, an angel, or a superior.
- **Gen 17:3** — "Abram fell on his face, and God talked with him"
- **Josh 5:14** — "Joshua fell on his face to the earth, and did worship"
- **1 Ki 18:39** — "they fell on their faces: and they said, The LORD, he is the God"
- **Ezek 1:28; 3:23; 43:3; 44:4** — Ezekiel's repeated prophetic collapse
- **Dan 8:17** — "I was afraid, and fell upon my face"

### 4. Prostration in Supplication / Petition
Ritual falling-down in prayer or plea — often with Hithpael.
- **Deut 9:18, 25** — "I fell down before the LORD, as at the first, forty days" (Hithpael)
- **Ezra 10:1** — "casting himself down before the house of God" (Hithpael)
- **Jer 36:7; 37:20; 38:26** — "let my supplication... be accepted (fall) before thee"
- **Dan 9:18** — "we do not present our supplications before thee for our righteousnesses"

### 5. Judgment / Overthrow of a City or Nation
Prophetic "fall" of corporate entities — Babylon, Samaria, Israel, Jerusalem.
- **Isa 21:9** — "Babylon is fallen, is fallen" (echoed verbatim in Rev 14:8; 18:2)
- **Isa 3:8** — "Jerusalem is ruined, and Judah is fallen"
- **Jer 51:8** — "Babylon is suddenly fallen and destroyed"
- **Amos 5:2** — "The virgin of Israel is fallen; she shall no more rise"
- **Lam 2:21; 5:16** — "the crown is fallen from our head"

### 6. Casting / Falling of the Lot; Apportionment
Technical use for lot-casting (Hiphil causative) and the resulting inheritance-allotment.
- **Num 34:2** — "the land that shall fall unto you for inheritance"
- **Josh 13:6; 23:4** — "to fall by lot / which I have divided by lot"
- **Neh 10:34; 11:1** — "we cast the lots" / "they cast lots"
- **Jon 1:7** — "the lot fell upon Jonah"
- **Esth 3:7; 9:24** — "they cast Pur, that is, the lot"
- **Prov 1:14** — "Cast in thy lot among us"

### 7. Causative "Cast Down / Fell / Throw Down" (Hiphil)
Subject actively causes the object to fall — trees felled, walls thrown down, stars cast down, deep sleep caused to fall.
- **Gen 2:21** — "the LORD God caused a deep sleep to fall upon Adam"
- **2 Ki 3:19, 25** — "shall fell every good tree" (timber)
- **2 Ki 6:5** — "the axe head fell into the water" (Hiphil — "let fall")
- **Dan 8:10** — "it cast down some of the host and of the stars to the ground"
- **Ps 106:26** — "to overthrow (Hiphil) them in the wilderness"

### 8. Figurative Falling — Countenance, Fear, Sleep, Words
Abstract subjects "fall" onto persons or from persons.
- **Gen 4:5-6** — "his countenance fell... why is thy countenance fallen"
- **Gen 15:12** — "a deep sleep fell upon Abram" / "an horror of great darkness fell upon him"
- **Exod 15:16** — "Fear and dread shall fall upon them"
- **Josh 2:9** — "your terror is fallen upon us"
- **1 Sam 3:19** — "did let none of his words fall to the ground"
- **1 Sam 26:12** — "a deep sleep from the LORD was fallen upon them"
