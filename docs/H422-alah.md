A primitive root; properly, to adjure, i.e. (usually in a bad sense) imprecate: — adjure, curse, swear.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Sworn Imprecation / Cursing
- **Jdg 17:2** — "The eleven hundred [shekels] of silver that were taken from thee, about which thou cursedst, and spakest of also in mine ears, behold, the silver [is] with me; I took it. And his mother said, Blessed [be thou] of the LORD, my son."
  - Micah's mother uttered a sworn curse (ʼalah) over stolen silver; confession triggers reversal to blessing (barak H1288).
- **Hos 4:2** — "By swearing, and lying, and killing, and stealing, and committing adultery, they break out, and blood toucheth blood."
  - ʼalah heads a list of covenant violations paralleling the Decalogue; the infinitive absolute form emphasizes habitual cursing/false swearing.

### 2. Formal Adjuration — Placing Under Oath
- **1Sa 14:24** — "And the men of Israel were distressed that day: for Saul had adjured the people, saying, Cursed [be] the man that eateth [any] food until evening, that I may be avenged on mine enemies."
  - Saul uses the Hiphil of ʼalah to bind the army under an oath-curse; the adjuration is paired with ʼarar (H779) "cursed be the man."
- **1Ki 8:31** — "If any man trespass against his neighbour, and an oath be laid upon him to cause him to swear, and the oath come before thine altar in this house:"
  - Solomon's temple prayer envisions a judicial oath-ceremony: the Hiphil of ʼalah describes the formal imposition of an oath upon a suspect, with the noun ʼalah (H423) appearing as "the oath" in the same verse.
- **2Ch 6:22** — "If a man sin against his neighbour, and an oath be laid upon him to make him swear, and the oath come before thine altar in this house;"
  - Parallel to 1Ki 8:31; identical judicial oath-ceremony language.

### 3. False / Covenant-Breaking Swearing
- **Hos 10:4** — "They have spoken words, swearing falsely in making a covenant: thus judgment springeth up as hemlock in the furrows of the field."
  - ʼalah + shav' ("falsely") describes oath-treachery: entering a covenant with no intention of keeping it. The oath-curse rebounds upon the false swearer.
