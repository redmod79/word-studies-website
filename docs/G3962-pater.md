## Lexicon Definition

From Strong's: "apparently a primary word; a 'father' (literally or figuratively, near or more remote)." The word denotes (1) a biological male parent, (2) any male ancestor or forefather, (3) a founder of a nation or tribe (Abraham), (4) a spiritual or vocational progenitor ("fathers in the faith"), (5) an elder or honoured man (vocative of respect), (6) an archetype or originator of a behaviour ("father of lies"), and (7) God as Father — whether of Israel, of creation, of Jesus in a uniquely filial sense, or of believers by adoption.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Biological Father / Male Parent
Nearest-kin living male parent in narrative and ethical contexts.
- **Mat 2:22** — "his father Herod" (Archelaus)
- **Luk 15:12** — "the younger of them said to his father, Father, give me the portion"
- **Heb 12:9** — "we have had fathers of our flesh which corrected us"

### 2. Forefathers / Ancestors (corporate / national)
Plural use denoting the patriarchal line, covenant ancestors, or the preceding generation collectively.
- **Act 7:2** — "our father Abraham, when he was in Mesopotamia"
- **Rom 9:5** — "whose are the fathers, and of whom as concerning the flesh Christ came"
- **Heb 1:1** — "God, who at sundry times… spake in time past unto the fathers by the prophets"
- **1Co 10:1** — "all our fathers were under the cloud"

### 3. Archetypal / Founding Father (spiritual paternity)
Abraham as father of the circumcised and uncircumcised believing line; typological originator.
- **Rom 4:11** — "the father of all them that believe, though they be not circumcised"
- **Rom 4:16** — "Abraham; who is the father of us all"
- **Mat 3:9** — "We have Abraham to our father"

### 4. "Father" as Metaphor of Origination — Good and Evil
Any being may be called *patēr* of that which proceeds from him: God as Father of lights; the devil as father of lies.
- **Jas 1:17** — "cometh down from the Father of lights"
- **Jhn 8:44** — "Ye are of your father the devil… he is a liar, and the father of it"
- **2Co 1:3** — "the Father of mercies, and the God of all comfort"

### 5. God as Father of Israel / Father of All (OT-rooted covenantal sense)
Continues the OT pattern (Deut 32:6, Mal 2:10, Isa 63:16) where YHWH is Father of the covenant people.
- **Jhn 8:41** — "we have one Father, even God" (claim of Jesus' opponents)
- **Mal 2:10 (LXX backdrop)** — "Have we not all one father? hath not one God created us?"
- **Eph 4:6** — "One God and Father of all, who is above all, and through all, and in you all"
- **1Co 8:6** — "to us there is but one God, the Father, of whom are all things"

### 6. God as Father of Believers (through adoption / regeneration)
The Christian "our Father" — a new-covenant filial relation established by the Spirit of adoption, expressed through the Aramaic *Abba*.
- **Mat 6:9** — "Our Father which art in heaven, Hallowed be thy name"
- **Rom 8:15** — "ye have received the Spirit of adoption, whereby we cry, Abba, Father"
- **Gal 4:6** — "God hath sent forth the Spirit of his Son into your hearts, crying, Abba, Father"
- **1Jo 3:1** — "what manner of love the Father hath bestowed upon us, that we should be called the sons of God"

### 7. God as Father of Jesus Christ — Jesus' Distinctive "My Father" (ὁ πατήρ μου)
Jesus' characteristic self-expression throughout the Synoptics and especially John: a filial relation categorically different from that of any other person. Whereas Jesus teaches the disciples to pray "*our* Father," he himself never joins them in that prayer — he speaks of "*my* Father" and "*your* Father" as two distinct relations (Jhn 20:17).
- **Mat 11:27 / Luk 10:22** — "All things are delivered unto me of my Father: and no man knoweth the Son, but the Father; neither knoweth any man the Father, save the Son"
- **Luk 2:49** — "wist ye not that I must be about my Father's business?" (first recorded saying of Jesus)
- **Jhn 5:17** — "My Father worketh hitherto, and I work"
- **Jhn 5:18** — "the Jews sought the more to kill him, because he not only had broken the sabbath, but said also that God was his Father, making himself equal with God"
- **Jhn 8:54** — "it is my Father that honoureth me; of whom ye say, that he is your God"
- **Jhn 10:30** — "I and my Father are one"
- **Jhn 14:9** — "he that hath seen me hath seen the Father"
- **Jhn 20:17** — "I ascend unto my Father, and your Father; and to my God, and your God" (the two relations deliberately kept distinct)

Key observation from the tool data: across the Greek NT, "*my* Father" (πατήρ μου) occurs as Jesus' own expression dozens of times; it is effectively Jesus' proprietary usage. In the KJV tagging, 68 verses containing G3962 also contain G3450 (μου, "my") as a co-occurring Strong's — a distribution mostly clustered in the Synoptics and John and almost exclusively on Jesus' lips. The Jewish reaction in Jhn 5:18 and the blasphemy charge in Jhn 10:33 indicate that Jesus' first-century hearers understood this "*my* Father" language as a claim to unique filial status — "making himself equal with God" — not merely the generic covenantal fatherhood God has toward Israel.

### 8. Vocative of Respect / Honour
Used in direct address to honoured elders or as a respectful title.
- **Act 7:2** — "Men, brethren, and fathers, hearken" (Stephen addressing the Sanhedrin)
- **Act 22:1** — "Men, brethren, and fathers, hear ye my defence" (Paul)
- **Luk 16:24** — "Father Abraham, have mercy on me"
