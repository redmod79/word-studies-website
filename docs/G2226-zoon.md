## Lexicon Definition

A live thing, i.e., a living creature or animal. Neuter derivative of ζάω (zaō, to live). In NT contexts, used both of ordinary animals and of the four supernatural living beings surrounding God's throne in Revelation.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. The Four Heavenly Living Creatures (Revelation)
The most frequent use — the four ζῶα (living beings) that surround God's throne, each with a distinctive face (lion, calf, man, eagle), six wings, and full of eyes. They lead worship in the heavenly sanctuary and announce the seal judgments.

- **Rev 4:6–7** — "in the midst of the throne, and round about the throne, were four beasts… And the first beast was like a lion, and the second beast like a calf, and the third beast had a face as a man, and the fourth beast was like a flying eagle."
- **Rev 4:8** — "And the four beasts had each of them six wings about him; and they were full of eyes within: and they rest not day and night, saying, Holy, holy, holy, Lord God Almighty."
- **Rev 5:8** — "And when he had taken the book, the four beasts and four and twenty elders fell down before the Lamb."
- **Rev 6:1** — "And I saw when the Lamb opened one of the seals, and I heard, as it were the noise of thunder, one of the four beasts saying, Come and see."

### 2. Sacrificial/Ritual Animals
Used for animals whose blood or bodies are involved in the sacrificial system — physical, mortal creatures distinguished by their use in worship.

- **Heb 13:11** — "For the bodies of those beasts, whose blood is brought into the sanctuary by the high priest for sin, are burned without the camp."

### 3. Brute/Irrational Animals (Moral Comparison)
Used to describe the animal nature of those who act without reason or spiritual understanding — the instinctive, unreasoning creature.

- **2 Pet 2:12** — "But these, as natural brute beasts, made to be taken and destroyed, speak evil of the things that they understand not."
- **Jude 1:10** — "But what they know naturally, as brute beasts, in those things they corrupt themselves."
