## Lexicon Definition

From πρός (G4314) and ἄγω (G71); to lead towards, i.e. (transitively) to conduct near (bring, draw near). Used both in the physical sense of bringing a person before someone and in the theological sense of bringing persons to God.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Bringing Before an Authority (Judicial/Official)
- **Matt 18:24** — "one was brought (prosēchthē) unto him, which owed him ten thousand talents" — a debtor led before a king for accounting
- **Acts 16:20** — "brought (prosagagontes) them to the magistrates" — Paul and Silas dragged before civic rulers at Philippi

### 2. Navigational Approach (Maritime)
- **Acts 27:27** — "the shipmen deemed that they drew near (prosagein) to some country" — a ship approaching land in the Adriatic storm

### 3. Bringing to a Person (Imperative/Request)
- **Luke 9:41** — "Bring (prosagagage) thy son hither" — Jesus commanding the father to bring his epileptic son forward for healing

### 4. Access to God (Soteriological)
- **1 Pet 3:18** — "that he might bring (prosagagē) us to God" — Christ's substitutionary death as the means of conducting sinners into God's presence; the purpose clause (hina + subjunctive) frames this as the ultimate goal of Christ's suffering
