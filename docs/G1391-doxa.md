## Lexicon Definition

From the base of δοκέω (G1380); **glory** (as very apparent), in a wide application: dignity, glory, glorious, honour, praise, worship.

The word shifted from classical Greek (where δόξα meant *opinion* or *reputation*) to its LXX sense of visible, luminous majesty — particularly the manifest presence of God (Hebrew כָּבוֹד, kabod). In NT usage it carries both the visible-radiance sense and the relational honor/status sense.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Divine Luminous Presence — Visible Radiance of God
The Shekinah sense: δόξα as visible, radiant manifestation — light that fills space and overwhelms observers.
- **Luke 2:9** — "the glory of the Lord shone round about them: and they were sore afraid"
- **Acts 22:11** — "I could not see for the glory of that light"
- **Rev 15:8** — "the temple was filled with smoke from the glory of God, and from his power"
- **Heb 9:5** — "the cherubims of glory shadowing the mercyseat"

### 2. Christ's Inherent Glory — Pre-existent and Revealed
δόξα as the essential majesty proper to the Son — possessed before incarnation, veiled during it, disclosed at key moments.
- **John 1:14** — "we beheld his glory, the glory as of the only begotten of the Father"
- **John 17:5** — "the glory which I had with thee before the world was"
- **Heb 1:3** — "the brightness of his glory, and the express image of his person"
- **Luke 9:32** — "when they were awake, they saw his glory, and the two men that stood with him"

### 3. Eschatological / Resurrection Glory — Future Transformed State
δόξα as the condition of the resurrected body and the inheritance awaiting believers — contrasted with present suffering.
- **Rom 8:18** — "the glory which shall be revealed in us"
- **1 Cor 15:43** — "it is sown in dishonour; it is raised in glory"
- **Col 3:4** — "When Christ shall appear, then shall ye also appear with him in glory"
- **2 Cor 4:17** — "an eternal weight of glory far more exceeding"

### 4. Doxological Ascription — Liturgical Praise Formula
δόξα as the content of worship directed toward God or Christ — fixed formulas concluding arguments or letters.
- **Rom 11:36** — "to whom be glory for ever. Amen"
- **Rev 7:12** — "Blessing, and glory, and wisdom, and thanksgiving, and honour, and power"
- **Luke 2:14** — "Glory to God in the highest"
- **Gal 1:5** — "to whom be glory for ever and ever. Amen"

### 5. Human Honor / Status
δόξα as reputation, social esteem, or honor accruing to a person — often contrasted with divine glory to expose the inadequacy of human honor-seeking.
- **John 5:41, 44** — "I receive not honour from men... receive honour one of another, and seek not the honour that cometh from God only"
- **1 Thess 2:6** — "Nor of men sought we glory, neither of you, nor yet of others"
- **1 Pet 1:24** — "all the glory of man as the flower of grass"
- **John 12:43** — "they loved the praise of men more than the praise of God"

### 6. Natural Splendor — Brilliance of Created Things
δόξα applied to the visible brightness of heavenly bodies or fine garments — the rhetorical range of the word from cosmological to mundane.
- **1 Cor 15:40–41** — "one glory of the sun, and another glory of the moon, and another glory of the stars"
- **Mat 6:29** — "Solomon in all his glory was not arrayed like one of these"
- **Rev 21:23** — "the glory of God did lighten it, and the Lamb is the light thereof"
