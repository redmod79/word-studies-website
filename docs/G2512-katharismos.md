## Semantic Categories

### 1. Levitical Cleansing after Healing (Mosaic compliance)
- **Mark 1:44** — "offer for thy cleansing (katharismos) those things which Moses commanded, for a testimony unto them"
- **Luke 5:14** — "offer for thy cleansing (katharismos), according as Moses commanded, for a testimony unto them"

Both describe the post-healing leper presenting himself to the priest per Leviticus 14. Jesus heals, then directs compliance with the purification ritual — the katharismos is the prescribed ceremonial process.

### 2. Post-partum Purification (Mosaic Law)
- **Luke 2:22** — "when the days of her purification (katharismos) according to the law of Moses were accomplished, they brought him to Jerusalem"

Refers to Mary's purification period per Leviticus 12. A time-bounded ritual process ("the days of") culminating in temple presentation.

### 3. Jewish Ritual Purification Practice (Custom)
- **John 2:6** — "six waterpots of stone, after the manner of the purifying (katharismos) of the Jews, containing two or three firkins apiece"
- **John 3:25** — "there arose a question between some of John's disciples and the Jews about purifying (katharismos)"

In John's Gospel the word describes Jewish purification practice as a cultural institution. John 2:6 specifies stone vessels designated for ritual washing; John 3:25 introduces a dispute about purification that leads directly into the Baptist's testimony about Christ.

### 4. Christological Purging of Sin (Accomplished atonement)
- **Hebrews 1:3** — "when he had by himself purged (katharismos) our sins, sat down on the right hand of the Majesty on high"
- **2 Peter 1:9** — "hath forgotten that he was purged (katharismos) from his old sins"

The epistolary uses apply the purification concept to Christ's atoning work. Hebrews 1:3 treats it as a completed priestly act; 2 Peter 1:9 treats it as a past event the believer must remember.
