# WG: Transgressions — The OT Sin Triad

## Overview
Hebrew Scripture distinguishes sin with a precision Greek and English flatten. The core vocabulary for human moral failure forms a three-term triad (pesha / chattah / avon) that appears together in the most solemn passages of the OT — the Sinai covenant renewal, the Day of Atonement liturgy, Job's great complaint, and Daniel's prayer. A fourth term (asham) addresses the guilt liability dimension. The root verb pasha anchors the nominal cluster.

## Roles in the Concept

- **pesha (H6588):** The volitional dimension — conscious defection, rebellion, breaking away from authority. Prophetic vocabulary par excellence.
- **chattah (H2403):** The directional dimension — missing the mark, falling short of the target. Covers both the act and the offering that addresses it.
- **avon (H5771):** The consequential dimension — the crooked, distorted state that sin produces; the accumulated weight of guilt.
- **asham (H817):** The legal/liability dimension — guilt that creates an obligation for restitution or offering.
- **pasha (H6586):** The verbal root — the active breaking away from which pesha derives.
