## Lexicon Definition

Third person singular active present of δέω (to bind); also neuter participle form **δέον** (deh-on'). An impersonal verb expressing necessity, compulsion, or obligation. It is necessary; there is need of; it is proper; one must. The force ranges from absolute divine necessity to moral obligation, depending on context.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Messianic Necessity — The Son of Man Must Suffer and Rise
The Gospels use δεῖ to express the divine-necessity of Christ's passion and resurrection. This is Synoptic theology's defining use of the word.
- **Mat 16:21** — "he must go unto Jerusalem, and suffer many things of the elders and chief priests and scribes, and be killed, and be raised again the third day"
- **Mark 8:31** — "the Son of man must suffer many things, and be rejected of the elders, and of the chief priests, and scribes, and be killed"
- **Luke 24:26** — "Ought not Christ to have suffered these things, and to enter into his glory?"
- **John 20:9** — "they knew not the scripture, that he must rise again from the dead"

### 2. Eschatological Necessity — Things That Must Come to Pass
Wars, cosmic signs, and prophetic events carry δεῖ — they are divinely appointed to occur before the end.
- **Mat 24:6** — "all these things must come to pass, but the end is not yet"
- **Mark 13:7** — "such things must needs be; but the end shall not be yet"
- **Rev 1:1** — "things which must shortly come to pass"
- **Rev 4:1** — "things which must be hereafter"

### 3. Theological Necessity — What God Requires for Salvation and Worship
Statements about divinely required conditions for approaching or being accepted by God.
- **John 3:7** — "Ye must be born again"
- **John 4:24** — "they that worship him must worship him in spirit and in truth"
- **Acts 4:12** — "neither is there salvation in any other… whereby we must be saved"
- **Acts 16:30** — "Sirs, what must I do to be saved?"
- **Heb 11:6** — "he that cometh to God must believe that he is"

### 4. Divine Mission / Prophetic Compulsion
Jesus and Paul use δεῖ to express an inescapable calling placed upon them by God.
- **Luke 4:43** — "I must preach the kingdom of God to other cities also: for therefore am I sent"
- **Luke 13:33** — "I must walk to day, and to morrow, and the day following: for it cannot be that a prophet perish out of Jerusalem"
- **John 9:4** — "I must work the works of him that sent me, while it is day"
- **Acts 23:11** — "as thou hast testified of me in Jerusalem, so must thou bear witness also at Rome"

### 5. Ethical Obligation — How Believers and Leaders Must Conduct Themselves
Particularly dense in the Pastorals: qualifications for church office and patterns of Christian living.
- **1 Tim 3:2** — "A bishop then must be blameless, the husband of one wife…"
- **1 Tim 3:7** — "he must have a good report of them which are without"
- **Eph 6:20** — "that therein I may speak boldly, as I ought to speak"
- **2 Th 3:7** — "yourselves know how ye ought to follow us"
- **Heb 2:1** — "we ought to give the more earnest heed to the things which we have heard"

### 6. Cosmic Salvific Necessity — Reign, Resurrection, Judgment
Final eschatological realities that must be fulfilled in salvation history.
- **1 Cor 15:25** — "he must reign, till he hath put all enemies under his feet"
- **1 Cor 15:53** — "this corruptible must put on incorruption, and this mortal must put on immortality"
- **2 Cor 5:10** — "we must all appear before the judgment seat of Christ"
