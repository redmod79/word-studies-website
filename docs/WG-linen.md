## Roles in the Concept

- **Priestly liturgical fabric:** bad (בַּד) — the specific linen of priestly service and Day of Atonement; always white, always sacred
- **Fine woven fabric:** shesh (שֵׁשׁ) — bleached linen for tabernacle furnishings and the glory garments; associated with skilled workmanship and color embroidery
- **Raw textile material:** pishteh (פִּשְׁתֶּה) — flax as fiber/thread; the agricultural product before it becomes a garment
- **Fine court/worship fabric:** buwts (בּוּץ) — white linen in post-exilic usage; Levitical singers and Persian court
- **Garment types:** ephod (H646) and miknas (H4370) are specific garment forms often made from bad; beged (H899) is the generic garment word qualified by bad
