## Lexicon Definition

From κατά (down) and πατέω (to tread); to trample down; figuratively, to reject with disdain. The κατά- prefix intensifies the simple verb πατέω, adding downward force and the connotation of thorough, contemptuous trampling — not incidental stepping, but deliberate crushing underfoot. The figurative extension to contempt and scorn (reject with disdain) is already native to the word; Hebrews 10:29 exploits this dual sense.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Loss of Function — Trodden as Worthless
The trampled object has forfeited its purpose; being walked on signifies its uselessness.

- **Mat 5:13** — "if the salt have lost his savour... it is thenceforth good for nothing, but to be cast out, and to be trodden under foot of men"
- **Luk 8:5** — "some fell by the way side; and it was trodden down, and the fowls of the air devoured it"

### 2. Profaning the Sacred — Trampling the Holy
Swine and dogs, ritually unclean, crush what is holy; the act is at once physical desecration and moral outrage.

- **Mat 7:6** — "Give not that which is holy unto the dogs, neither cast ye your pearls before swine, lest they trample them under their feet"

### 3. Crowding / Physical Crushing (Literal Crowd Pressure)
Pure physical sense — a mob so thick people step on each other.

- **Luk 12:1** — "an innumerable multitude of people, insomuch that they trode one upon another"

### 4. Contempt for the Holy — Apostate Rejection
The strongest figurative use: deliberate, high-handed rejection of Christ is framed as bodily trampling on the Son of God.

- **Heb 10:29** — "who hath trodden under foot the Son of God, and hath counted the blood of the covenant... an unholy thing, and hath done despite unto the Spirit of grace"
