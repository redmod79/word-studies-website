## Semantic Categories

### 1. Execution — Hanging on a Tree (Crucifixion)
The dominant NT usage. Describes the death of Jesus using Deuteronomic "tree" language rather than the standard σταυρόω vocabulary.
- **Acts 5:30** — "The God of our fathers raised up Jesus, whom ye slew and hanged (κρεμάσαντες) on a tree"
- **Acts 10:39** — "whom they slew and hanged (κρεμάσαντες) on a tree"
- **Gal 3:13** — "Cursed is every one that hangeth (κρεμάμενος) on a tree" (quoting Deut 21:23)
- **Luke 23:39** — "one of the malefactors which were hanged (κρεμασθέντων) railed on him"

### 2. Figurative Dependence — Suspended Upon / Resting On
The law and prophets "hang" on two commandments — a metaphor of structural dependence.
- **Matt 22:40** — "On these two commandments hang (κρέμαται) all the law and the prophets"

### 3. Physical Suspension — Literal Hanging
A viper fastened onto Paul's hand.
- **Acts 28:4** — "when the barbarians saw the venomous beast hang (κρεμάμενον) on his hand"

### 4. Hypothetical Punishment — Drowning Preferable
Millstone imagery for causing a little one to stumble.
- **Matt 18:6** — "it were better for him that a millstone were hanged (κρεμασθῇ) about his neck, and that he were drowned in the depth of the sea"
