## Lexicon Definition

Probably from the same (unused root) as in the sense of rolling or in the sense of blazing; a star (as round or as shining); figuratively, a prince — star, stargazer.

**Patterns:** Densest in the minor prophets (Obadiah, Joel, Nahum, Amos) where star imagery drives Day-of-the-LORD rhetoric. Heavy concentration in Job (wisdom/creation reflection) and Genesis (Abrahamic promise). Notably present in both apocalyptic books available to the OT writer — Daniel (8:10; 12:3) — and across Torah in patriarchal covenant contexts.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Literal Astronomical Bodies (Created Luminaries)
- **Gen 1:16** — "he made the stars also" — paired with sun and moon as created lights.
- **Psalm 8:3** — "the moon and the stars, which thou hast ordained."
- **Psalm 147:4** — "He telleth the number of the stars; he calleth them all by [their] names."
- **Jer 31:35** — "the ordinances of the moon and of the stars for a light by night."

### 2. Covenant Multitude (Abrahamic Promise)
- **Gen 15:5** — "tell the stars, if thou be able to number them... So shall thy seed be."
- **Gen 22:17; 26:4** — "I will multiply thy seed as the stars of the heaven."
- **Exod 32:13; Deut 1:10; 10:22; 28:62; Neh 9:23; 1 Chr 27:23; Nah 3:16** — Abrahamic multiplication formula repeatedly invoked.

### 3. Symbolic for Angelic / Heavenly Beings
- **Job 38:7** — "When the morning stars sang together, and all the sons of God shouted for joy" — parallel stichos equates "morning stars" with "sons of God."
- **Judg 5:20** — "They fought from heaven; the stars in their courses fought against Sisera" — celestial combatants acting on Israel's behalf.
- **Isa 14:13** — "I will exalt my throne above the stars of God" — the usurper ascends over God's heavenly court.
- **Dan 8:10** — "cast down [some] of the host and of the stars to the ground" — stars paired with "host of heaven."

### 4. Symbolic for a Ruler / Prince (Royal Figure)
- **Num 24:17** — "there shall come a Star out of Jacob, and a Sceptre shall rise out of Israel" — Balaam's oracle; "Star" parallels "Sceptre" denoting a royal deliverer.
- **Amos 5:26** — "the star of your god, which ye made to yourselves" — a rival deity/cultic figure borne in procession.

### 5. Symbolic for Saints / Wise Ones
- **Dan 12:3** — "they that be wise shall shine as the brightness of the firmament; and they that turn many to righteousness as the stars for ever and ever" — the righteous likened to stars for eschatological glory.

### 6. Cosmic Signs — Day-of-the-LORD Darkening
- **Isa 13:10** — "the stars of heaven and the constellations thereof shall not give their light."
- **Ezek 32:7** — "I will cover the heaven, and make the stars thereof dark."
- **Joel 2:10; 3:15** — "the stars shall withdraw their shining."
- **Job 3:9; 9:7; Eccl 12:2** — stars darkened as reversal of Gen 1 order.

### 7. Height / Pride Metaphor
- **Obad 1:4** — "though thou set thy nest among the stars, thence will I bring thee down."
- **Isa 14:13** — usurper's boast (also Category 3).
- **Job 22:12** — "behold the height of the stars, how high they are!"

### 8. Purity / Brightness Standard
- **Job 25:5** — "yea, the stars are not pure in his sight" — even stars fall short of divine holiness.
- **Ps 148:3** — "stars of light" — summoned to praise.

### 9. Astrologers / Occult Practitioners
- **Isa 47:13** — "the astrologers, the stargazers (hōḇrē šāmayim / hakkôwkāḇîm), the monthly prognosticators" — Babylonian celestial divination condemned.

### 10. Calendrical / Temporal Marker
- **Neh 4:21** — "from the rising of the morning till the stars appeared" — stars mark the working day.
