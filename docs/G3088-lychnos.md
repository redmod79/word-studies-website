## Lexicon Definition

λύχνος, -ου, ὁ — a lamp, candle (that is placed on a stand or candlestick). The Septuagint's primary rendering of Hebrew נֵר (nêr, H5216). A portable oil lamp used for household illumination, distinguished from φῶς (the light itself) and λυχνία (the lampstand that holds it). In metaphorical usage: the eye as the lamp of the body (illuminating the person from within), OT prophecy as a lamp shining in darkness, a teacher compared to a burning light, and Christ as the lamp of the heavenly city.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Literal Household Lamp — Placed on a Stand
The lamp's basic function: light a room by placing it on a lampstand (λυχνία), not hiding it.

- **Mat 5:15** — "Neither do men light a candle, and put it under a bushel, but on a candlestick; and it giveth light unto all that are in the house"
- **Mark 4:21** — "Is a candle brought to be put under a bushel, or under a bed? and not to be set on a candlestick?"
- **Luke 15:8** — "doth not light a candle, and sweep the house, and seek diligently till she find it?"

### 2. The Eye as Lamp of the Body
The eye illuminates the whole person; spiritual "singleness" of eye keeps the body full of light.

- **Mat 6:22** — "The light of the body is the eye: if therefore thine eye be single, thy whole body shall be full of light"
- **Luke 11:34** — "The light of the body is the eye: therefore when thine eye is single, thy whole body also is full of light"
- **Luke 11:36** — "If thy whole body therefore be full of light, having no part dark, the whole shall be full of light, as when the bright shining of a candle doth give thee light"

### 3. Metaphorical Lamp — Person or Prophecy
A person or body of writing that functions as a lamp, giving light before the fuller revelation arrives.

- **John 5:35** — "He was a burning and a shining light: and ye were willing for a season to rejoice in his light" (John the Baptist)
- **2 Pet 1:19** — "ye do well that ye take heed, as unto a light that shineth in a dark place, until the day dawn, and the day star arise in your hearts" (OT prophecy)

### 4. Vigilant Readiness — Lamps Burning
Disciples as lamp-bearers awaiting their master; readiness expressed through kept-burning lamps.

- **Luke 12:35** — "Let your loins be girded about, and your lights burning"

### 5. Eschatological Absence — No Lamp Needed
Revelation's three uses all concern the end state where lamps become unnecessary (Babylon's destruction; New Jerusalem; eternal day).

- **Rev 18:23** — "the light of a candle shall shine no more at all in thee" (Babylon's permanent desolation)
- **Rev 21:23** — "the city had no need of the sun, neither of the moon, to shine in it: for the glory of God did lighten it, and the Lamb is the light thereof"
- **Rev 22:5** — "they need no candle, neither light of the sun; for the Lord God giveth them light"
