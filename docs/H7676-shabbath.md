## Semantic Categories

### 1. Weekly Seventh-Day Rest
The foundational use: the seventh day of the week, set apart from labor and ordinary activity.
- **Exo 20:8** — "Remember the sabbath day, to keep it holy."
- **Exo 20:10** — "But the seventh day is the sabbath of the LORD thy God: in it thou shalt not do any work."
- **Exo 16:26** — "Six days ye shall gather it; but on the seventh day, which is the sabbath, in it there shall be none."
- **Deu 5:14–15** — "The seventh day is the sabbath of the LORD thy God... And remember that thou wast a servant in the land of Egypt."

### 2. Covenant Sign Between God and Israel
The sabbath functions as a treaty-sign (אוֹת) marking Israel's unique relationship to Yahweh.
- **Exo 31:13** — "Verily my sabbaths ye shall keep: for it is a sign between me and you."
- **Eze 20:12** — "Moreover also I gave them my sabbaths, to be a sign between me and them, that they might know that I am the LORD."
- **Eze 20:20** — "Hallow my sabbaths; and they shall be a sign between me and you, that ye may know that I am the LORD your God."

### 3. Practical Regulations (Commerce, Labor, Gates)
Nehemiah and the prophets address specific violations — commerce, agriculture, and city gates.
- **Jer 17:21** — "Take heed to yourselves, and bear no burden on the sabbath day, nor bring it in by the gates of Jerusalem."
- **Neh 13:15–16** — Nehemiah sees treading of winepresses, laden donkeys, and Tyrian merchants selling fish on the sabbath.
- **Neh 13:19** — "When the gates of Jerusalem began to be dark before the sabbath, I commanded that the gates should be shut."
- **Amos 8:5** — "Saying, When will the new moon be gone, that we may sell corn? and the sabbath, that we may set forth wheat?"

### 4. Annual Solemn Rest Days
Beyond the weekly cycle, shabbâth also designates special cessation days within the festival calendar.
- **Lev 16:31** — "It shall be a sabbath of rest unto you, and ye shall afflict your souls" (Day of Atonement).
- **Lev 23:32** — "It shall be unto you a sabbath of rest, and ye shall afflict your souls" (Feast of Tabernacles).
- **Lev 23:3** — "The seventh day is the sabbath of rest, an holy convocation."

### 5. Sabbatical Year — Land Sabbath
Every seventh year the agricultural land itself observes a sabbath.
- **Lev 25:4** — "But in the seventh year shall be a sabbath of rest unto the land, a sabbath for the LORD."
- **Lev 26:34** — "Then shall the land enjoy her sabbaths, as long as it lieth desolate."
- **2Ch 36:21** — "To fulfil the word of the LORD by the mouth of Jeremiah, until the land had enjoyed her sabbaths."

### 6. Sabbath Profanation as Covenant Breach
Ezekiel and Nehemiah treat sabbath violation as symptomatic of total covenant unfaithfulness.
- **Eze 20:13** — "They walked not in my statutes, and they despised my judgments... and my sabbaths they greatly polluted."
- **Eze 22:8** — "Thou hast despised mine holy things, and hast profaned my sabbaths."
- **Neh 13:17–18** — "What evil thing is this that ye do, and profane the sabbath day?... ye bring more wrath upon Israel by profaning the sabbath."
- **Lam 2:6** — "He hath caused the solemn feasts and sabbaths to be forgotten in Zion."

### 7. Eschatological Universal Sabbath
Isaiah projects sabbath observance outward to foreigners and forward to the new creation.
- **Isa 56:2** — "Blessed is the man... that keepeth the sabbath from polluting it."
- **Isa 56:4–6** — Eunuchs and foreigners who keep the sabbath are included in the covenant.
- **Isa 58:13** — "If thou turn away thy foot from the sabbath... and call the sabbath a delight."
- **Isa 66:23** — "From one sabbath to another, shall all flesh come to worship before me."
