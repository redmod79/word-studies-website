## Semantic Categories

### 1. Demonic Recognition of Jesus' Identity
- **Mark 1:24** — "Saying, Let us alone; what have we to do with thee, thou Jesus of Nazareth? art thou come to destroy us? I know thee who thou art, the Holy One of God."
- **Luke 4:34** — "Saying, Let us alone; what have we to do with thee, thou Jesus of Nazareth? art thou come to destroy us? I know thee who thou art; the Holy One of God."

Both are nearly identical accounts of an unclean spirit in the Capernaum synagogue addressing Jesus by this title in the vocative, paired immediately with "the Holy One of God." The geographic identifier contrasts with the supernatural confession.

### 2. Human Identification of Jesus
- **Mark 14:67** — "And when she saw Peter warming himself, she looked upon him, and said, And thou also wast with Jesus of Nazareth."
- **Mark 16:6** — "And he saith unto them, Be not affrighted: Ye seek Jesus of Nazareth, which was crucified: he is risen; he is not here: behold the place where they laid him."

The servant girl at Peter's denial (14:67) and the angel at the empty tomb (16:6) both use it as a straightforward geographic identifier — "Jesus, the one from Nazareth."
