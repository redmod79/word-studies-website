## Lexicon Definition

A primitive root; to be guilty; by implication to be punished or perish: certainly, be(-come, made) desolate, destroy, greatly, be(-come, found, hold) guilty, offend (acknowledge offence), trespass.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Legal/Cultic Guilt — Becoming Liable Before God
The dominant sense in Leviticus: a person incurs guilt by violating a commandment, often unwittingly, and must bring an offering.
- **Lev 4:13** — "the whole congregation of Israel sin through ignorance... and are guilty (asham)"
- **Lev 5:2** — "if a soul touch any unclean thing... and it be hidden from him; he also shall be unclean, and guilty (asham)"
- **Lev 5:4** — "if a soul swear, pronouncing with his lips to do evil, or to do good... and it be hid from him; when he knoweth of it, then he shall be guilty (asham)"
- **Lev 5:17** — "if a soul sin, and commit any of these things which are forbidden... though he wist it not, yet is he guilty (asham)"

### 2. Moral/Relational Offense — Sinning Against God or Neighbor
Used outside ritual law for offenses against God's covenant or against other people.
- **Jer 2:3** — "all that devour him shall offend (asham); evil shall come upon them"
- **Jer 50:7** — "their adversaries said, We offend (asham) not, because they have sinned against the LORD"
- **Hos 13:1** — "when Ephraim spake trembling, he exalted himself in Israel; but when he offended (asham) in Baal, he died"
- **Hab 1:11** — "then shall his mind change, and he shall pass over, and offend (asham), imputing this his power unto his god"

### 3. Acknowledging Guilt — Confession and Accountability
Used for recognizing or being found in a state of guilt.
- **Hos 5:15** — "till they acknowledge their offence (asham), and seek my face"
- **Hos 10:2** — "now shall they be found faulty (asham)"
- **Zec 11:5** — "whose possessors slay them, and hold themselves not guilty (asham)"
- **Pro 30:10** — "Accuse not a servant unto his master, lest he curse thee, and thou be found guilty (asham)"

### 4. Desolation as Consequence of Guilt
A secondary sense where guilt results in devastation — the verb shades from "be guilty" into "bear the consequences (desolation)."
- **Isa 24:6** — "therefore hath the curse devoured the earth, and they that dwell therein are desolate (asham)"
- **Hos 13:16** — "Samaria shall become desolate (asham); for she hath rebelled against her God"
- **Eze 6:6** — "the cities shall be laid waste, and the high places shall be desolate (asham)"
- **Joel 1:18** — "the flocks of sheep are made desolate (asham)" [Niphal — the only passive form]

### 5. Imprecatory/Judicial Condemnation
Rare Hiphil use: causative — to hold or declare guilty, to condemn.
- **Ps 5:10** — "Destroy them (ha'ashimem), O God; let them fall by their own counsels" [Hiphil imperative — "declare/hold them guilty"]
- **Ps 34:21** — "evil shall slay the wicked: and they that hate the righteous shall be desolate (asham)"
- **Ps 34:22** — "none of them that trust in him shall be desolate (asham)"
