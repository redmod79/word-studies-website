## Lexicon Definition

Comparative of ἔσω (G2080); interior: — inner, within. The comparative degree of the adverb ἔσω, functioning as a substantivized adjective denoting the more inward space relative to an outer boundary.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Physical Interior Space (Confinement)
- **Acts 16:24** — "Who, having received such a charge, thrust them into the inner (ἐσωτέραν) prison, and made their feet fast in the stocks."
  The comparative force is active: not merely "the prison" but the more interior cell, further from exit, maximizing security. Modifies φυλακή (G5438).

### 2. Sacred Interior Space (Sanctuary Access)
- **Heb 6:19** — "Which [hope] we have as an anchor of the soul, both sure and stedfast, and which entereth into that within (ἐσώτερον) the veil."
  Substantivized neuter with the article (τὸ ἐσώτερον τοῦ καταπετάσματος) = "the interior [space] of the veil" — the Most Holy Place. The comparative marks a spatial boundary crossing: from the holy place through the curtain into the holier interior.
