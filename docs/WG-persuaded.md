# WG: Persuaded (Trust / Obedience from Conviction)

## Overview

This word group centers on πείθω (peitho) and its derivatives, capturing the semantic chain from persuasion through trust to obedience. Unlike the broader WG-faith group (which centers on pistis), this group focuses on the peitho root and the words that compete with it in the "obey" and "trust" translation spaces. The key insight is that Greek treats obedience as the natural outcome of being persuaded — a conceptual unity that English obscures by translating the same word as both "trust" and "obey."

## Roles in the Concept

- **Root Process:** peitho (πείθω) — the act of being persuaded, producing trust, yielding obedience
- **Faith Commitment:** pisteuo (πιστεύω) — the cognitive/volitional response of belief
- **Structural Obedience:** hupakouo (ὑπακούω) — obedience framed as subordinate hearing
- **Settled Confidence:** pepoithesis (πεποίθησις) — the noun for the state of trust
- **Negation (verb):** apeitheo (ἀπειθέω) — refusal to be persuaded, willful disbelief
- **Negation (noun):** apeitheia (ἀπείθεια) — the state of obstinate unbelief/disobedience

## Key Passages Where Multiple Group Words Appear Together

- **Rom 2:8** — peitho + apeitheo: "them that are contentious, and do not obey (apeitheo) the truth, but obey (peitho) unrighteousness" — the only verse pairing peitho with its own negated form
- **Rom 10:16** — pisteuo + hupakouo: "they have not all obeyed (hupakouo) the gospel. For Esaias saith, Lord, who hath believed (pisteuo) our report?" — Paul treats obedience and belief as interchangeable
- **Jhn 3:36** — pisteuo + apeitheo: "He that believeth (pisteuo) on the Son hath everlasting life: and he that believeth not (apeitheo) the Son shall not see life" — KJV renders apeitheo as "believeth not," collapsing obedience and belief
- **2 Tim 1:12** — peitho + pisteuo: "I know whom I have believed (pisteuo), and am persuaded (peitho) that he is able to keep that which I have committed" — the two cognates side by side
