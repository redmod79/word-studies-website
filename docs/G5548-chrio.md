## Semantic Categories

### 1. Messianic Commissioning by the Spirit
- **Luke 4:18** — "The Spirit of the Lord is upon me, because he hath anointed me to preach the gospel to the poor; he hath sent me to heal the brokenhearted, to preach deliverance to the captives, and recovering of sight to the blind, to set at liberty them that are bruised"
- **Acts 10:38** — "How God anointed Jesus of Nazareth with the Holy Ghost and with power: who went about doing good, and healing all that were oppressed of the devil; for God was with him"

Both passages identify the instrument of anointing as the Holy Spirit rather than physical oil. The anointing is linked directly to empowerment for a specific ministry task.

### 2. Divine Appointment of Jesus to His Office
- **Acts 4:27** — "For of a truth against thy holy child Jesus, whom thou hast anointed, both Herod, and Pontius Pilate, with the Gentiles, and the people of Israel, were gathered together"
- **Hebrews 1:9** — "Thou hast loved righteousness, and hated iniquity; therefore God, even thy God, hath anointed thee with the oil of gladness above thy fellows"

Acts 4:27 addresses God directly: "whom thou hast anointed" — the anointing establishes Jesus's identity as the appointed holy servant against whom the nations conspire. Heb 1:9 (quoting Ps 45:7) grounds the anointing in moral qualification: righteousness precedes appointment.

### 3. Anointing Extended to Believers
- **2 Corinthians 1:21** — "Now he which stablisheth us with you in Christ, and hath anointed us, is God"

The only occurrence where chrio applies to persons other than Jesus. God is the agent; believers are anointed alongside their establishment "in Christ." The participial form (chrisas) links this anointing to the confirming and sealing of vv. 21-22.
