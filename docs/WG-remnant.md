# WG: Remnant / Remainder

## Overview

Hebrew has a rich vocabulary for "what is left over." Five words — two verb roots and three derived nouns — cover the spectrum from neutral administrative surplus to theologically charged eschatological survival. Understanding their distinctions prevents reading she'erith's covenantal weight into yether's bureaucratic formula, or flattening both into a single "remnant" concept.

## Root Families

### Family 1 — יתר root (excess, overhang)
- **H3498 יָתַר (yathar)** — the root verb: to jut over, exceed; also to be left over, survive
- **H3499 יֶתֶר (yether)** — derived noun: the overhang itself = surplus, remainder; also "cord" (something hanging free)

The root idea is spatial: what hangs or extends beyond an edge. This produces both the sense of "excess/superiority" (Gen 49:3 — "the excellency of dignity") and "remainder" (administrative "the rest of the acts of king X").

### Family 2 — שׁאר root (survival, persistence)
- **H7604 שָׁאַר (sha'ar)** — root verb: to swell up, persist; to remain, survive
- **H7605 שְׁאָר (she'ar)** — derived noun: remainder, those remaining
- **H7611 שְׁאֵרִית (she'erith)** — abstract noun: residual/surviving portion; carries the theological weight of Israel's covenantal remnant

The שׁאר root emphasizes *persistence through adversity* rather than *excess beyond an edge*. Its noun forms dominate the prophets when speaking of Israel's survival.

## Key Distinctions

**yether vs. she'erith:** The easiest confusion. Both are nouns rendered "remnant/residue" in KJV. yether is the neutral word — it occurs over 60 times in the bureaucratic "rest of the acts" formula. she'erith almost never appears there; it carries the covenantal freight of divine preservation (Isa 10:20–22; Jer 23:3; Mic 2:12; Zep 3:13). Reading she'erith's theological weight into a yether occurrence is an exegetical overclaim.

**yathar vs. sha'ar:** Near-synonymous verbs. yathar's root is "overhang/exceed" (surplus perspective); sha'ar's root is "swell/persist" (survival perspective). In practice they are largely interchangeable for "to remain," but sha'ar's noun forms (she'ar, she'erith) dominate the prophetic remnant passages.

**she'ar vs. she'erith:** Both nouns from the sha'ar root. she'ar (H7605) is the simple "remainder" (25 uses); she'erith (H7611) is the more abstract "residual portion" (66 uses) and carries the explicitly covenantal sense. Jer, Ezek, and Mic use she'erith almost exclusively when referring to Israel's future survival.

## Key Passages with Multiple Group Words

- **Exo 10:5** — yether (H3499) + sha'ar (H7604): "they shall eat the rest (yether) of that which is escaped, which remaineth (sha'ar) to you from the hail"
- **Isa 11:11** — sha'ar (H7604) + she'ar (H7605): "the Lord shall set his hand again the second time to recover the remnant (she'ar) of his people, which shall be left (sha'ar)"
- **Zep 2:9** — yether (H3499) + she'erith (H7611): "the residue (yether) of my people shall spoil them, and the remnant (she'erith) of my nation shall possess them" — rare collocation: the administrative/neutral word alongside the covenantal word in one eschatological oracle

## Cross-Testament

The LXX's primary rendering of yether as **λοιποί** (G3062) carries the "remaining ones" sense into Greek. In Revelation, λοιποί occurs 8× with eschatological weight (Rev 12:17; 20:5) that echoes she'erith more than yether — a case where the Greek word, carrying both Hebrew traditions through a single Greek form, is pressed into service for the covenantal remnant concept.
