## Semantic Categories

### 1. Physical Elevation — Literal Lifting
An object, water, hand, or wings raised upward.
- **Gen 7:17** — "the waters increased, and bare up the ark, and it was lift up above the earth"
- **Exod 7:20** — "he lifted up the rod, and smote the waters"
- **Exod 17:11** — "when Moses held up his hand, Israel prevailed; and when he let down his hand, Amalek prevailed"
- **Exod 14:16** — "lift thou up thy rod, and stretch out thine hand over the sea, and divide it"

### 2. Ritual Lifting — Heave Offering
Priestly elevation of sacrificial portions before the LORD. Consistently collocates with תְּרוּמָה (tᵉrûwmâh, H8641 — "heave offering": 16 co-occ).
- **Num 15:19–20** — "ye shall offer up an heave offering unto the LORD... a cake of the first of your dough for an heave offering"
- **Lev 2:9** — "the priest shall take from the meat offering a memorial thereof, and shall burn it upon the altar"
- **Exod 29:27** — "[the breast of the wave offering] which is heaved up"

### 3. Physical Stature — Tallness
Persons or natural objects described as physically higher than others.
- **Deut 1:28** — "the people is greater and taller than we"
- **Deut 2:10** — "a people great, and many, and tall, as the Anakims"
- **Isa 2:13** — "upon all the cedars of Lebanon, that are high and lifted up"
- **Job 22:12** — "Is not God in the height of heaven? and behold the height of the stars, how high they are"

### 4. Divine Exaltation of the Lowly
God's sovereign act of raising up the humble, poor, or chosen.
- **1 Sam 2:7–8** — "he bringeth low, and lifteth up; he raiseth up the poor out of the dust, lifteth up the beggar from the dunghill"
- **2 Sam 22:49** — "thou also hast lifted me up on high above them that rose up against me"
- **Ps 147:6** — "The LORD lifteth up the meek: he casteth the wicked down to the ground"
- **Ps 75:7** — "God is the judge: he putteth down one, and setteth up another"

### 5. Worship and Praise — Extolling God
Humans lifting God's name in honor and worship; Piel form especially common.
- **Exod 15:2** — "he is my God, and I will extol (rûwm, Piel) him; my father's God, and I will exalt him"
- **Ps 99:5** — "Exalt ye the LORD our God, and worship at his footstool; for he is holy"
- **Neh 9:5** — "stand up and bless the LORD your God...who is exalted above all blessing and praise"
- **Ps 57:5, 11** — "Be thou exalted, O God, above the heavens" (liturgical refrain)

### 6. Human Pride and Arrogance (negative)
Heart or voice "lifted up" against God or in self-glorification; regularly followed by divine judgment.
- **Deut 8:14** — "thine heart be lifted up, and thou forget the LORD thy God"
- **2 Sam 22:28** — "thine eyes are upon the haughty, that thou mayest bring them down"
- **Hos 13:6** — "their heart was exalted (rûwm); therefore have they forgotten me"
- **Dan 11:36** — "the king shall...exalt himself, and magnify himself above every god"

### 7. Royal and Eschatological Exaltation
The king's horn raised in victory; the Servant elevated in the Servant Song.
- **1 Sam 2:1** — "mine horn is exalted in the LORD; my mouth is enlarged over mine enemies"
- **1 Chr 25:5** — "God gave to Heman...sons...to lift up the horn"
- **Isa 52:13** — "my servant shall...be exalted (rûwm) and extolled (nâsâʼ), and be very high (gâbahh)" — three lifting verbs stacked
- **Ps 21:13** — "Be thou exalted, LORD, in thine own strength: so will we sing and praise thy power"
