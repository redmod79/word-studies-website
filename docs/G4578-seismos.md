## Semantic Categories

### 1. Physical Sea Storm / Violent Commotion
- **Matt 8:24** — "there arose a great tempest (seismós) in the sea, insomuch that the ship was covered with the waves"

The word's lexical range includes any violent agitation. Here it denotes a storm-induced shaking of the sea, not a ground earthquake. This is the only "tempest" rendering.

### 2. Eschatological Warning Signs (Olivet Discourse)
- **Matt 24:7** — "there shall be famines, and pestilences, and earthquakes (seismoí), in divers places"
- **Mark 13:8** — "there shall be earthquakes in divers places"
- **Luke 21:11** — "And great earthquakes shall be in divers places, and famines, and pestilences"

All three Synoptics use seismós in Jesus' list of "beginning of sorrows" — paired with λιμός (famine) and λοιμός (pestilence).

### 3. Earthquake at the Passion
- **Matt 27:54** — "the centurion... saw the earthquake (seismós), and those things that were done"
- **Matt 28:2** — "there was a great earthquake (seismós): for the angel of the Lord descended from heaven"

Seismós marks both the death and resurrection of Jesus as earth-shaking divine events. Unique to Matthew's Passion and resurrection accounts.

### 4. Divine Deliverance
- **Acts 16:26** — "suddenly there was a great earthquake (seismós), so that the foundations of the prison were shaken: and immediately all the doors were opened"

God's earthquake breaks open Paul's prison — seismós as instrument of miraculous liberation.

### 5. Apocalyptic Theophany in Revelation
- **Rev 6:12** — "there was a great earthquake; and the sun became black as sackcloth"
- **Rev 8:5** — "the angel... cast [fire] into the earth: and there were voices, and thunderings, and lightnings, and an earthquake"
- **Rev 11:13** — "the same hour was there a great earthquake, and the tenth part of the city fell"
- **Rev 11:19** — "there were lightnings, and voices, and thunderings, and an earthquake, and great hail"
- **Rev 16:18** — "there was a great earthquake, such as was not since men were upon the earth"

Revelation's standard theophanic formula pairs seismós with φωνή (voices), βροντή (thunderings), and ἀστραπή (lightnings) — occurring at the sixth seal and at each of the three judgment series' climaxes (trumpets, bowls, and their intercalations).
