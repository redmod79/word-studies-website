### 1. Eschatological Abundance — Mountains Flowing with New Wine
- **Joel 3:18** — "the mountains shall drop down new wine (asis), and the hills shall flow with milk"
- **Amos 9:13** — "the mountains shall drop sweet wine (asis), and all the hills shall melt"

Both depict a future age of supernatural agricultural abundance where mountains drip with asis — freshly pressed grape juice flowing without human labor.

### 2. Loss Through Judgment — New Wine Cut Off
- **Joel 1:5** — "Awake, ye drunkards, and weep; and howl, all ye drinkers of wine, because of the new wine (asis); for it is cut off from your mouth."

The removal of asis signals divine judgment through agricultural devastation. The drunkards are told to mourn because the supply of fresh juice has ceased.

### 3. Metaphorical Intoxication — Blood as Sweet Wine
- **Isa 49:26** — "they shall be drunken with their own blood, as with sweet wine (asis)"

God's oppressors will be made drunk with their own blood, compared to asis. The simile relies on asis as something potently intoxicating (or overwhelmingly abundant).

### 4. Intimate Gift — Spiced Wine of Pomegranate Juice
- **Song 8:2** — "I would cause thee to drink of spiced wine of the juice (asis) of my pomegranate."

The only non-prophetic use. Asis here is pomegranate juice mixed with spices — offered as a drink of intimacy. This usage confirms asis refers to freshly expressed fruit juice, not exclusively grape.
