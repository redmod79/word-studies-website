# WG: Shortly / Quickly (ταχύς Family)

## Overview

The Greek concept of quickness/swiftness/imminence is expressed through a tightly
unified word family derived from the root adjective **ταχύς** (G5036, "swift").
The family includes a noun, two adverbs, a comparative, and a derived adjective —
all carrying the core idea of brevity in time or swiftness in action. In NT usage,
the cluster frequently describes eschatological urgency (especially in Revelation
and Luke) and moral promptness (in Paul's pastoral letters).

## Relationships

### Shared Root
All six words derive from **G5036 ταχύς** (swift). The inflectional and derivational
chain:

- ταχύς (adj.) → **τάχος** (noun formed from adj. stem)
- ταχύς (adj.) → **ταχέως** (adverb from adj.)
- ταχύς (adj.) → **ταχύ** (neuter of adj. used as adverb)
- ταχύς (adj.) → **ταχινός** (derived adjective)
- ταχέως (adv.) → **τάχιον** (comparative of adverb)

## Roles in the Concept

- **Noun (τάχος):** Always in the idiom ἐν τάχει — "in swiftness" — functioning
  adverbially to describe the nearness of prophetic/divine events. Preferred in
  apocalyptic framing where events are both certain and imminent.
- **Simple adverb (ταχύ):** Most frequent; used especially in Revelation's direct
  commands ("I come quickly") and narrative urgency.
- **Adverb (ταχέως):** Broader NT usage, including moral promptness (laying on of
  hands "hastily," being "soon shaken"). Paul's letters prefer this form.
- **Comparative (τάχιον):** "More quickly / sooner" — epistolary contexts where
  plans or anticipated events are accelerated.
- **Adjective (ταχινός):** "Swift/impending" — used of swift destruction (2 Pet 2:1)
  and the nearness of death (2 Pet 1:14). Intensified adjectival form.

## Key Passages Using This Word Family
