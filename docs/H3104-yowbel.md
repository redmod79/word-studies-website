## Lexicon Definition

The blast of a horn (from its continuous sound); specifically, the signal of the silver trumpets; hence, the instrument itself and the festival thus introduced — **jubile, ram's horn, trumpet**.

The word spans two semantic poles: (1) the physical instrument — a ram's horn blown as a signal, and (2) the institution — the fiftieth-year cycle of liberty, land-return, and economic reset proclaimed by blowing that horn. The instrument names the institution.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. The Ram's Horn Instrument (Joshua 6)
The physical horn blown as a military-liturgical signal at Jericho. Always plural ("trumpets of rams' horns") except in Jos 6:5 where the singular denotes the sustained blast.
- **Jos 6:4** — "seven priests shall bear before the ark seven trumpets of rams' horns"
- **Jos 6:5** — "when they make a long blast with the ram's horn, and when ye hear the sound of the trumpet, all the people shall shout"
- **Jos 6:8** — "the seven priests bearing the seven trumpets of rams' horns passed on before the LORD"
- **Jos 6:13** — "seven priests bearing seven trumpets of rams' horns before the ark of the LORD went on continually"

### 2. The Jubilee Year as Institution (Leviticus 25)
The fiftieth-year cycle proclaimed by trumpet-blast on the Day of Atonement. The word names the year itself — "a jubile shall that fiftieth year be."
- **Lev 25:10** — "ye shall hallow the fiftieth year, and proclaim liberty throughout all the land unto all the inhabitants thereof: it shall be a jubile unto you"
- **Lev 25:11** — "A jubile shall that fiftieth year be unto you: ye shall not sow, neither reap that which groweth of itself"
- **Lev 25:12** — "For it is the jubile; it shall be holy unto you"

### 3. Economic Reset — Land Restoration
In the jubilee year, all ancestral land reverts to its original family. Property sales are really leases counted toward the next jubilee.
- **Lev 25:13** — "In the year of this jubile ye shall return every man unto his possession"
- **Lev 25:28** — "that which is sold shall remain in the hand of him that hath bought it until the year of jubile: and in the jubile it shall go out, and he shall return unto his possession"
- **Lev 27:24** — "In the year of the jubile the field shall return unto him of whom it was bought"
- **Num 36:4** — "when the jubile of the children of Israel shall be, then shall their inheritance be put unto the inheritance of the tribe whereunto they are received"

### 4. Social Reset — Servant Release
Israelite servants go free at jubilee regardless of remaining debt; their service is bounded by the jubilee cycle.
- **Lev 25:40** — "as an hired servant, and as a sojourner, he shall be with thee, and shall serve thee unto the year of jubile"
- **Lev 25:54** — "if he be not redeemed in these years, then he shall go out in the year of jubile, both he, and his children with him"

### 5. Valuation Benchmark
The jubilee year serves as the fixed reference point for calculating the monetary value of vows, field dedications, and redemption prices.
- **Lev 25:15** — "According to the number of years after the jubile thou shalt buy of thy neighbour"
- **Lev 25:50** — "the price of his sale shall be according unto the number of years, according to the time of an hired servant shall it be with him"
- **Lev 27:17** — "If he sanctify his field from the year of jubile, according to thy estimation it shall stand"
- **Lev 27:18** — "if he sanctify his field after the jubile, then the priest shall reckon unto him the money according to the years that remain"

### 6. The Initial Sinai Blast (Exodus 19)
The earliest occurrence — a prolonged horn-blast signals that the people may approach Mount Sinai after YHWH's descent.
- **Exo 19:13** — "when the trumpet soundeth long, they shall come up to the mount"
