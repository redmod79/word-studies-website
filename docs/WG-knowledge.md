# WG: Knowledge / Wisdom / Understanding

## Overview
The Hebrew knowledge concept operates as a cluster of 7 words spanning three semantic roles: the act of knowing (yada'), the content known (da'ath, de'ah, madda'), and the companion virtues of wisdom (chokmah) and understanding (biyn, tabuwn). These words frequently appear together in triadic formulas — especially in Exodus (Bezalel's endowment), Proverbs (wisdom instruction), and Daniel (court training).

## Roles in the Concept

- **Act:** yada' (יָדַע) — the verb of knowing; experiential, relational, cognitive
- **Content:** da'ath (דַּעַת) — the knowledge grasped; what is known
- **Alternate content forms:** de'ah (דֵּעָה) — rare feminine synonym; madda' (מַדָּע) — intelligence/consciousness
- **Companion — Wisdom:** chokmah (חׇכְמָה) — skill in applying knowledge to life
- **Companion — Discernment:** biyn (בִּין) — ability to distinguish and perceive; tabuwn (תָּבוּן) — the resulting insight
