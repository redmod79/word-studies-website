## Overview

## Roles in the Concept

- **Substance:** σάρξ (G4561) — the flesh itself, in all senses (physical, human, moral)
- **Quality:** σαρκικός (G4559) — the adjective ("carnal"), describing what belongs to or is characteristic of flesh
- **Embodiment:** σῶμα (G4983) — the organized body as a whole; overlaps sarx but emphasizes the bodily unit ("body of Christ," resurrection body)
- **Antithesis:** πνεῦμα (G4151) — Spirit, the polar opposite of sarx in Paul's ethical dualism
- **Substance of Life:** αἷμα (G129) — blood, inseparable from flesh in NT usage ("flesh and blood" as idiom for mortal humanity)
- **Drive:** ἐπιθυμία (G1939) — desire/lust, what the flesh "lusts after" (Gal 5:16–17)

## Key Passages Where Multiple Group Words Appear Together

- **Rom 8:13** — σάρξ + σῶμα + πνεῦμα: "if ye live after the flesh, ye shall die: but if ye through the Spirit do mortify the deeds of the body, ye shall live"
