# WG: Interpret / Translate

## Overview
The NT interpretation/translation concept is carried by a tight cognate family built on the root ἑρμηνεύω (hermēneúō), plus one semantically adjacent verb. All share the core idea of rendering meaning accessible — whether across languages, across testaments, or across the human/divine communication gap in charismatic speech.

## Relationships

### Shared Roots
- **ἑρμηνεύω (G2059)** is the root for the entire family:
  - δι-ερμηνεύω (G1329) — δια- prefix adds thoroughness ("through")
  - μεθ-ερμηνεύω (G3177) — μετα- prefix adds transfer ("across")
  - δι-ερμηνευτής (G1328) — agent noun from G1329
  - δυσ-ερμήνευτος (G1421) — δυσ- prefix negates ("difficult to")
- **ἐπιλύω (G1956)** has a separate root (λύω, "loose/solve") but occupies the same semantic space ("explain, expound")

### Co-occurrence
No members co-occur in the same verse. This is characteristic of a complementary-distribution word family — NT authors select one member per context rather than combining them.

## Roles in the Concept

- **Root action:** hermēneúō (G2059) — the basic act of rendering meaning in another language
- **Intensified action:** diermēneúō (G1329) — comprehensive, thorough interpretation working through a body of material
- **Transfer action:** methermēneúō (G3177) — carrying meaning across from one language to another
- **Agent:** diermēneutḗs (G1328) — the person who performs interpretation
- **Negation:** dysermḗneutos (G1421) — what resists interpretation
- **Adjacent:** epilýō (G1956) — resolving a specific interpretive knot

## Key Distinctions

The three compound verbs divide the interpretation domain by *scope*:

1. **hermēneúō** — point translation: single word or name rendered in another language
2. **methermēneúō** — formulaic translation: "which being interpreted means..." (editorial gloss)
3. **diermēneúō** — sustained interpretation: working *through* a text (Luke 24:27) or *through* an utterance (1 Cor 14) to make its full meaning accessible

The δια- prefix is the key differentiator — it signals comprehensiveness, not merely equivalence.
