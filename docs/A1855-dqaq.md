## Semantic Categories

### 1. The Smiting Stone Crushing the Statue (Dan 2)
The divine kingdom-stone destroys the composite statue, symbolizing the replacement of human empires.
- **Dan 2:34** — "a stone was cut out without hands, which smote the image upon his feet of iron and clay, and brake them to pieces"
- **Dan 2:35** — "Then was the iron, the clay, the brass, the silver, and the gold, broken to pieces together, and became like the chaff of the summer threshingfloors"
- **Dan 2:44** — "it shall break in pieces and consume all these kingdoms, and it shall stand for ever"
- **Dan 2:45** — "the stone was cut out of the mountain without hands, and that it brake in pieces the iron, the brass, the clay, the silver, and the gold"

### 2. Iron as the Symbol of Kingdom-Crushing Power (Dan 2:40)
Iron's capacity to break everything is the metaphor for the strength of the fourth earthly kingdom.
- **Dan 2:40** — "forasmuch as iron breaketh in pieces and subdueth all things: and as iron that breaketh all these, shall it break in pieces and bruise"

### 3. The Fourth Beast Devouring and Crushing (Dan 7)
In the night visions, the terrible fourth beast both devours and breaks in pieces — a doublet of destruction.
- **Dan 7:7** — "it devoured and brake in pieces, and stamped the residue with the feet of it"
- **Dan 7:19** — "whose teeth were of iron, and his nails of brass; which devoured, brake in pieces, and stamped the residue with his feet"
- **Dan 7:23** — "The fourth beast shall be the fourth kingdom upon earth... and shall devour the whole earth, and shall tread it down, and break it in pieces"

### 4. Physical Destruction of Human Bodies (Dan 6:24)
The only non-visionary use: lions physically crush bones.
- **Dan 6:24** — "the lions had the mastery of them, and brake all their bones in pieces or ever they came at the bottom of the den"
