## Semantic Categories

### 1. Copula — Identity and Characterization
The most common use: linking a subject to a predicate nominative or adjective, stating what someone or something was.
- **Jhn 1:1** — "In the beginning was the Word, and the Word was with God, and the Word was God."
- **Jhn 1:4** — "In him was life; and the life was the light of men."
- **Jhn 8:44** — "He was a murderer from the beginning"
- **Act 11:24** — "For he was a good man, and full of the Holy Ghost"

### 2. Existential — Something Existed or Was Present
Introduces the existence of a person, place, or thing, often with "there was/were."
- **Mar 1:23** — "And there was in their synagogue a man with an unclean spirit"
- **Luk 16:19** — "There was a certain rich man"
- **Jhn 3:1** — "There was a man of the Pharisees, named Nicodemus"
- **Act 9:10** — "And there was a certain disciple at Damascus, named Ananias"

### 3. Locative — Being in a Place
Describes a subject's ongoing location or spatial presence.
- **Mar 1:13** — "And he was there in the wilderness forty days"
- **Jhn 18:1** — "he went forth... where was a garden"
- **Act 9:28** — "And he was with them coming in and going out at Jerusalem"
- **Luk 2:7** — "laid him in a manger; because there was no room for them in the inn"

### 4. Periphrastic — Continuous/Habitual Past Action (ēn + participle)
Combines with a present participle to form an imperfect periphrastic, emphasizing ongoing or habitual action in the past.
- **Mar 1:22** — "he taught (ēn didaskōn) them as one that had authority"
- **Mar 1:39** — "And he preached (ēn kēryssōn) in their synagogues"
- **Luk 4:44** — "And he preached (ēn kēryssōn) in the synagogues of Galilee"
- **Luk 5:16** — "And he withdrew (ēn hypochōrōn) himself into the wilderness, and prayed"

### 5. Temporal Marker — Noting Time or Season
Identifies what time it was or what occasion was occurring.
- **Mar 15:25** — "And it was the third hour, and they crucified him"
- **Jhn 10:22** — "And it was at Jerusalem the feast of the dedication, and it was winter"
- **Jhn 13:30** — "and it was night"
- **Jhn 19:14** — "And it was the preparation of the passover, and about the sixth hour"

### 6. Pre-Existence and Eternal Being
Distinctive Johannine usage for Christ's existence before creation and the eternal nature of divine realities.
- **Jhn 1:1** — "In the beginning was the Word" (ēn ho logos)
- **Jhn 1:2** — "The same was in the beginning with God"
- **Jhn 1:9** — "That was the true Light, which lighteth every man"
- **Jhn 1:10** — "He was in the world, and the world was made by him"

### 7. Revelation's Temporal Formula — "Which Was"
ēn appears in Revelation's distinctive threefold divine title expressing God's eternal nature across all time frames.
