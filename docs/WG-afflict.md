## Roles in the Concept

- **Action (imposed):** anah (H6031) Piel — the deliberate afflicting/humbling done to another
- **Action (voluntary):** anah (H6031) Hithpael — self-humbling before God
- **Condition:** oniy (H6040) — the state of affliction/misery resulting from the action
- **Person:** aniy (H6041) — the afflicted or poor one (the most socially charged member — 72v, overlaps significantly with "poor")
- **Disposition:** anav (H6035) — meekness as a character quality, not just a circumstance
- **Intensified action:** daka (H1792) — crushing, breaking in pieces (stronger than anah)
- **Related action:** kana (H3665) — bending the knee, subduing (more about submission than suffering)

## Key Passages Where Multiple Group Words Appear Together

- **Job 36:15** — oniy (H6040) + aniy (H6041): "He delivereth the poor (aniy) in his affliction (oniy)"

- **Psa 72:4** — aniy (H6041) + daka (H1792): "He shall judge the poor (aniy) of the people... and shall break in pieces (daka) the oppressor"
- **Isa 53:4,7** — anah (H6031) in the context of the suffering servant, the defining passage where affliction becomes redemptive
