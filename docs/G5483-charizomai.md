## Lexicon Definition

Middle voice from G5485 (charis); to grant as a favor, i.e. gratuitously, in kindness, pardon or rescue. To do something pleasant or agreeable (to one), to do a favor to, gratify; to show one's self gracious, kind, benevolent; to grant forgiveness, to pardon; to give graciously, give freely, bestow.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. God's Gracious Giving / Bestowal
- **Rom 8:32** — "He that spared not his own Son, but delivered him up for us all, how shall he not with him also freely give (charizomai) us all things?"
- **1 Cor 2:12** — "that we might know the things that are freely given (charizomai) to us of God"
- **Gal 3:18** — "God gave (charizomai) it to Abraham by promise"
- **Phil 1:29** — "unto you it is given (charizomai) in the behalf of Christ, not only to believe on him, but also to suffer"
- **Phil 2:9** — "God also hath highly exalted him, and given (charizomai) him a name which is above every name"

The emphasis in each case is that the gift is unearned and unmerited — granted as an act of favor, not as payment or obligation.

### 2. Divine Forgiveness of Sins / Trespasses
- **Col 2:13** — "hath he quickened together with him, having forgiven (charizomai) you all trespasses"
- **Eph 4:32** — "forgiving (charizomai) one another, even as God for Christ's sake hath forgiven (charizomai) you"
- **Col 3:13** — "forgiving (charizomai) one another... even as Christ forgave (charizomai) you, so also do ye"

In these passages forgiveness is presented as a gift — God "graces away" the debt of sin. The parallel in Eph 4:32 and Col 3:13 makes divine forgiveness the model for human forgiveness.

### 3. Interpersonal Forgiveness
- **2 Cor 2:7** — "ye ought rather to forgive (charizomai) him, and comfort him"
- **2 Cor 2:10** — "To whom ye forgive any thing, I forgive also: for if I forgave any thing, to whom I forgave it, for your sakes forgave I it in the person of Christ"
- **2 Cor 12:13** — "forgive me this wrong" (ironic use)

Paul models interpersonal forgiveness on the pattern of Christ's forgiveness (2 Cor 2:10, "in the person of Christ"), connecting the human act of pardoning back to the divine act.

### 4. Debt Cancellation (Parable)
- **Luke 7:42** — "when they had nothing to pay, he frankly forgave (charizomai) them both"
- **Luke 7:43** — "he, to whom he forgave (charizomai) most"

The parable of the two debtors uses charizomai for outright cancellation of a monetary debt, making explicit the economic metaphor underlying the forgiveness sense.

### 5. Granting / Delivering (Judicial-Administrative)
- **Acts 3:14** — "desired a murderer to be granted (charizomai) unto you"
- **Acts 25:11** — "no man may deliver (charizomai) me unto them"
- **Acts 25:16** — "not the manner of the Romans to deliver (charizomai) any man to die"
- **Acts 27:24** — "God hath given (charizomai) thee all them that sail with thee"

In Acts the word carries a judicial sense: granting custody of a prisoner or granting a person's life as a favor. Even here the core idea is a gratuitous bestowal — giving what is not owed.

### 6. Healing as Gracious Gift
- **Luke 7:21** — "unto many that were blind he gave (charizomai) sight"

Healing is presented not as earned but as graciously bestowed — consistent with the word's root in charis (grace).
