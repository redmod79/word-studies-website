## Lexicon Definition

Captivity. From αἰχμάλωτος (G164). The state of being a prisoner of war; captivity as an abstract condition or collective body of captives.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Captivity Conquered — Messianic Victory
- **Eph 4:8** — "When he ascended up on high, he led captivity (aichmalōsian) captive, and gave gifts unto men."

Quoting Psalm 68:18, Paul depicts Christ's ascension as a triumphal procession. "Captivity" here is personified or treated as an abstract collective — captivity itself is taken captive. The word functions as the object of a cognate verb (aichmalōteuō, G162), creating an intensifying figure: "he captured captivity."

### 2. Captivity as Retributive Consequence
- **Rev 13:10** — "He that leadeth into captivity (aichmalōsian) shall go into captivity (aichmalōsian): he that killeth with the sword must be killed with the sword. Here is the patience and the faith of the saints."

Both tokens occur in this verse. The word describes captivity as a form of divine retribution — those who impose captivity on others will themselves be subjected to it. The lex talionis pattern parallels the sword statement in the same verse.

## Related Words

### Word Family / Cognates
- **G164 αἰχμάλωτος (aichmalōtos)** — adjective/noun: captive, prisoner of war (Luke 4:18 only)
- **G162 αἰχμαλωτεύω (aichmalōteuō)** — verb: to lead captive, capture (Eph 4:8, 2 Tim 3:6)

All four words share the root αἰχμ- (spear/war). G161 is the abstract noun (the state of captivity), G164 is the adjective/noun (the captive person), and G162/G163 are near-synonymous verbs (to make captive).
