## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same:**
- entolē carries the full range of mitsvah — single specific directive, collective law-code, king's decree
- The collocation pattern holds: "keep the commandments" (tēreō + entolē) in the NT directly mirrors "keep the commandments" (shâmar + mitsvah) in Hebrew
- The commandment-keeping formula remains the marker of covenant fidelity: Deut 7:9 → John 15:10 ("If ye keep my commandments, ye shall abide in my love")

**What shifts:**
- John 13:34 introduces a "new commandment" (entolē kainē) — the commandment to love as Christ loved, grounding obedience in Christ's own example rather than Sinai
- Paul in Romans 7 uses entolē analytically to describe the commandment's role in revealing sin — a usage absent from OT mitsvah
- Revelation 12:17; 14:12 places "keeping the commandments of God" alongside "the testimony/faith of Jesus" — a dual covenant marker impossible in OT terms
