H530 emunah is by far the dominant Hebrew source for pistis in the LXX. The ʼ-m-n root family (H530 + H571) accounts for 23 of the LXX's pistis renderings.

**Semantic shift in translation:** The LXX translators split emunah's range across two Greek words: pistis for the faithfulness/fidelity sense and aletheia for the truth/certainty sense. This division means pistis in the LXX carries a strongly relational, covenant-fidelity meaning that maps cleanly onto the NT concept of trust in God. The key moment is Hab 2:4, where the LXX renders "by his emunah" as ek pisteōs mou ("by my faithfulness"), shifting the possessive from the righteous person to God — Paul then reads ek pisteōs as "by faith" without the possessive, universalizing the principle.

## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same across testaments:**
- The core idea of trusting reliance on God as the basis for righteous living
- The pairing of faithfulness with covenant relationship (emunah + chesed in OT; pistis + agape in NT)
- The contrast between faithfulness and falsehood/unbelief

**What shifts:**
- OT emunah primarily describes God's faithfulness (22 of 49 Psalms uses are "thy/my faithfulness" spoken of God); NT pistis primarily describes human faith directed toward God
- OT emunah includes a concrete "steadiness" sense (Exo 17:12) and an administrative "set office" sense (1 Chr 9:22, 26, 31) that pistis does not carry
- The LXX possessive shift at Hab 2:4 (from "his emunah" to "my pistis") opens the door to Paul's reading of faith as a universal principle rather than an individual's personal faithfulness
