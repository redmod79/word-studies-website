## Lexicon Definition

Comparative of πρέσβυς (présbys, elderly); *older*; as noun, **a senior**; specially, an Israelite Sanhedrin member or Christian "presbyter":—elder(-est), old (man, men and…women), senator.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Jewish Religious Authority (Sanhedrin / Tradition)
The "elders" as members of the ruling body alongside chief priests and scribes, guardians of oral tradition and opponents of Jesus.
- **Matt 15:2** — "Why do thy disciples transgress the tradition of the elders?"
- **Matt 26:3** — "Then assembled together the chief priests, and the scribes, and the elders of the people, unto the palace of the high priest"
- **Matt 27:1** — "All the chief priests and elders of the people took counsel against Jesus to put him to death"
- **Acts 4:5** — "It came to pass on the morrow, that their rulers, and elders, and scribes…"

### 2. Church Leadership Officers
Elders appointed to serve and govern local congregations; paired with overseers/bishops in Pastoral Epistles.
- **Acts 14:23** — "When they had ordained them elders in every church, and had prayed with fasting, they commended them to the Lord"
- **Acts 20:17** — "From Miletus he sent to Ephesus, and called the elders of the church"
- **1 Tim 5:17** — "Let the elders that rule well be counted worthy of double honour, especially they who labour in the word and doctrine"
- **Titus 1:5** — "Ordain elders in every city, as I had appointed thee"
- **James 5:14** — "Is any sick among you? let him call for the elders of the church; and let them pray over him"

### 3. Apostolic Council at Jerusalem
"Elders" standing alongside the apostles as the governing body of the early church.
- **Acts 15:2** — "Determined that Paul and Barnabas…should go up to Jerusalem unto the apostles and elders"
- **Acts 15:6** — "The apostles and elders came together for to consider of this matter"
- **Acts 15:22** — "Pleased it the apostles and elders, with the whole church, to send chosen men…"
- **Acts 16:4** — "The decrees…ordained of the apostles and elders which were at Jerusalem"

### 4. The Twenty-Four Elders (Heavenly Throne)
A distinct heavenly council of 24 elders surrounding the throne of God in Revelation; they fall down in worship and cast their crowns before God and the Lamb.
- **Rev 4:4** — "Round about the throne were four and twenty seats: and upon the seats I saw four and twenty elders sitting"
- **Rev 4:10** — "The four and twenty elders fall down before him that sat on the throne, and worship him"
- **Rev 5:8** — "The four beasts and four and twenty elders fell down before the Lamb"
- **Rev 11:16** — "The four and twenty elders, which sat before God on their seats, fell upon their faces, and worshipped God"

### 5. Age / Generational Relationship
The word's base comparative sense: "older" relative to a younger person; used in household and pastoral advice.
- **Luke 15:25** — "His elder son was in the field: and as he came and drew nigh to the house…"
- **John 8:9** — "They went out one by one, beginning at the eldest"
- **1 Tim 5:1–2** — "Rebuke not an elder, but intreat him as a father; and the elder women as mothers"
- **1 Pet 5:5** — "Ye younger, submit yourselves unto the elder"

### 6. Epistolary Self-Designation
The author of 2 and 3 John identifies himself simply as "the elder" without a personal name.
- **2 John 1:1** — "The elder unto the elect lady and her children, whom I love in the truth"
- **3 John 1:1** — "The elder unto the wellbeloved Gaius, whom I love in the truth"

### 7. Ancestors / Forebears of Faith
"The elders" as the OT saints who obtained divine commendation; also prophetic reference to old men receiving visions.
- **Heb 11:2** — "By it the elders obtained a good report"
- **Acts 2:17** — "Your old men shall dream dreams" (quoting Joel 2:28; presbyteros used for "old men")
