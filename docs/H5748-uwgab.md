## Semantic Categories

### 1. Origin of Musical Arts
- **Gen 4:21** — "And his brother's name was Jubal: he was the father of all such as handle the harp and organ."

The ugab appears at the very origin of instrumental music, paired with kinnor (harp). Jubal, a descendant of Cain, is credited as progenitor of both stringed (kinnor) and wind (ugab) instruments.

### 2. Instruments of Festive Joy
- **Job 21:12** — "They take the timbrel and harp, and rejoice at the sound of the organ."
- **Psalm 150:4** — "Praise him with the timbrel and dance: praise him with stringed instruments and organs."

In both verses, ugab accompanies toph (timbrel/tambourine) and other instruments in contexts of celebration. Job 21:12 describes the prosperous wicked; Psalm 150:4 commands doxological praise.

### 3. Instrument of Mourning (Inverted)
- **Job 30:31** — "My harp also is turned to mourning, and my organ into the voice of them that weep."

The ugab, normally associated with joy, is here turned to lament. Job's reversal of the instrument's natural purpose underscores the depth of his suffering.
