## Lexicon Definition

Or (shortened) חֲלֹם (chalom); from H2492 (chalam); a dream. Translated "dream" or "dreamer" throughout the KJV. The Aramaic cognate is H2493 (chelem), used in the Aramaic sections of Daniel.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Divine revelation to patriarchs and kings
God speaks directly through a dream, usually warning or guiding.
- **Gen 20:3** — "God came to Abimelech in a dream by night"
- **Gen 31:24** — "God came to Laban the Syrian in a dream by night"
- **1 Kings 3:5** — "the LORD appeared to Solomon in a dream by night"
- **Num 12:6** — "I the LORD will make myself known unto him in a vision, and will speak unto him in a dream"

### 2. Symbolic prophetic dream requiring interpretation
The dream encodes a message through imagery; it must be told and interpreted.
- **Gen 37:5–10** — Joseph's sheaves and sun/moon/stars dreams
- **Gen 40:5–16** — butler and baker's dreams in prison
- **Gen 41:7, 15, 25, 32** — Pharaoh's twin dreams of cattle and ears
- **Dan 2:1–3** — Nebuchadnezzar's troubling dreams
- **Judg 7:13–15** — Midianite's barley-loaf dream foretelling Gideon's victory

### 3. Channel of genuine prophetic inspiration (in lists with other means)
Paired with visions, Urim, prophets as a legitimate mode of revelation.
- **Num 12:6** — dream contrasted with Moses' face-to-face speech
- **1 Sam 28:6, 15** — "the LORD answered him not, neither by dreams, nor by Urim, nor by prophets"
- **Dan 1:17** — "Daniel had understanding in all visions and dreams"
- **Joel 2:28** — "your old men shall dream dreams, your young men shall see visions"

### 4. False or deceptive dream (prophetic polemic)
Dreams invoked by false prophets; the word becomes a foil for true revelation.
- **Deut 13:1, 3, 5** — "a prophet, or a dreamer of dreams"; test and reject if he leads to other gods
- **Jer 23:27–28, 32** — "they that prophesy false dreams… let him tell a dream; and he that hath my word, let him speak my word faithfully"
- **Jer 27:9; 29:8** — "hearken not… to your dreamers"
- **Zech 10:2** — "the diviners have seen a lie, and have told false dreams"

### 5. Dream as metaphor for the transient and insubstantial
Wisdom literature uses dreams to figure emptiness, vanishing, or vanity.
- **Job 20:8** — "He shall fly away as a dream, and shall not be found"
- **Ps 73:20** — "As a dream when one awaketh"
- **Isa 29:7** — enemies of Ariel "shall be as a dream of a night vision"
- **Eccl 5:3, 7** — "a dream cometh through the multitude of business… in the multitude of dreams and many words there are also divers vanities"

### 6. Night setting / paired with sleep and visions
Dream co-located with "night," "vision," "deep sleep."
- **Gen 20:3; 31:24; 40:5; 1 Kings 3:5** — "in a dream by night"
- **Job 7:14** — "thou scarest me with dreams, and terrifiest me through visions"
- **Job 33:15** — "In a dream, in a vision of the night, when deep sleep falleth upon men"
