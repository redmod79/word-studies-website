## Semantic Categories

### 1. Divine Visitation (Judgment or Mercy)
- **Luke 19:44** — "And shall lay thee even with the ground, and thy children within thee; and they shall not leave in thee one stone upon another; because thou knewest not the time of thy visitation." — Jesus weeps over Jerusalem's failure to recognize God's gracious visitation in his coming; the consequence is destruction.
- **1 Peter 2:12** — "Having your conversation honest among the Gentiles: that, whereas they speak against you as evildoers, they may by your good works, which they shall behold, glorify God in the day of visitation." — A future day when God inspects, and honest conduct will cause even accusers to glorify God.

### 2. Office/Position of Oversight
- **Acts 1:20** — "For it is written in the book of Psalms, Let his habitation be desolate, and let no man dwell therein: and his bishoprick let another take." — Judas's position of oversight (quoting Psalm 109:8 LXX where Hebrew pequddah H6486 is rendered episkope); the vacancy must be filled.
- **1 Timothy 3:1** — "This is a true saying, If a man desire the office of a bishop, he desireth a good work." — The pastoral oversight role in the church, presented as a worthy aspiration.

The Hebrew root paqad carries the same dual semantic range: (1) divine visitation for judgment or mercy, and (2) administrative oversight/appointment.
