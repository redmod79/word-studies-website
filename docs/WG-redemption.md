# WG: Redemption

## Overview
The redemption concept in the Greek NT centers on the λύτρον (lytron) word family — a group of cognates built on the root λύω (lyo, "to loose/release"). Together these words describe the act, agent, price, and result of ransoming someone from bondage. Two additional terms regularly intersect: ἄφεσις (aphesis, "forgiveness/release") as the immediate effect of redemption, and αἷμα (haima, "blood") as the means by which the ransom is paid.

## Roles in the Concept

- **Root action:** λύω (G3089) — the basic act of loosing or releasing from bonds
- **Price/payment:** λύτρον (G3083) — the ransom money; ἀντίλυτρον (G487) — the corresponding ransom given in exchange
- **Act of ransoming:** λύτρωσις (G3085) — the simple act; ἀπολύτρωσις (G629) — the intensified/completed act ("ransom in full")
- **Verb of ransoming:** λυτρόω (G3084) — to perform the ransoming
- **Agent:** λυτρωτής (G3086) — the person who pays the ransom
- **Market metaphor:** ἐξαγοράζω (G1805) — buying someone out of the slave market
- **Means:** αἷμα (G129) — the blood of Christ as the ransom payment
- **Result:** ἄφεσις (G859) — the forgiveness/release that redemption accomplishes
