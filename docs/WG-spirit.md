## Overview
The spirit concept in the NT centers on πνεῦμα (pneuma), which carries the full semantic range from physical wind/breath through human inner life to the divine Holy Spirit. This word group maps pneuma's cognate family, its key collocate (ἅγιος), and the easily confused ψυχή (psychē).

## Roles in the Concept

- **Core entity:** πνεῦμα (pneuma) — the spirit itself, whether divine, human, demonic, or natural (wind)
- **Qualifier:** ἅγιος (hagios) — specifies the divine Spirit as "holy"
- **Contrast:** ψυχή (psychē) — the soul/natural life, distinguished from the spirit in anthropological and eschatological contexts
- **Derivative quality:** πνευματικός (pneumatikos) — "spiritual" as characteristic of Spirit-empowered persons or gifts
- **Root action:** πνέω (pneō) — the underlying physical act (blowing) from which the metaphor extends
- **Physical cognate:** πνοή (pnoē) — breath/wind in purely physical sense

- **John 3:5–8** — "born of... the Spirit (pneuma)... The wind (pneuma) bloweth where it listeth" (wordplay on pneuma as wind and Spirit)
