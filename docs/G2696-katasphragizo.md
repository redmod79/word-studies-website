## Lexicon Definition

From G2596 (κατά, intensifying) and G4972 (σφραγίζω); to seal closely, to seal up completely:—seal.

The κατά- prefix intensifies the action of the base verb σφραγίζω, emphasizing thoroughness or finality of the sealing. BLB lists two biblical occurrences: LXX Job 9:7 (translating H2856 חָתַם) and Rev 5:1.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Completely/Thoroughly Sealed (Intensive Action)

The sole NT usage carries the κατά- intensifier, emphasizing that the book is *fully* sealed — not merely closed but secured with seven seals, making it impenetrable until the worthy Lamb opens it.

- **Rev 5:1** — "And I saw in the right hand of him that sat on the throne a book written within and on the backside, sealed [κατεσφραγισμένον] with seven seals."

**Parsing at Rev 5:1:** Perfect Passive Participle, Accusative Singular Neuter (V-RPP-ASN) — the book *has been sealed* and remains in that sealed state. The perfect aspect underscores the settled, complete nature of the sealing.

**LXX parallel — Job 9:7:** "Who commandeth the sun, and it riseth not; and sealeth up [κατασφραγίζει] the stars." Here God's sovereign shutting of the stars is expressed with the same intensive compound.
