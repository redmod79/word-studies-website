## Semantic Categories

### 1. Decisive, Unrepeatable Sacrifice
The dominant sense (4 of 5 uses). Describes Christ's death or offering as a single, decisive event that cannot and need not be repeated — contrasted with the repetitive Levitical system.

- **Rom 6:10** — "For in that he died, he died unto sin once (ephapax): but in that he liveth, he liveth unto God."
- **Heb 7:27** — "Who needeth not daily, as those high priests, to offer up sacrifice, first for his own sins, and then for the people's: for this he did once (ephapax), when he offered up himself."
- **Heb 9:12** — "Neither by the blood of goats and calves, but by his own blood he entered in once (ephapax) into the holy place, having obtained eternal redemption [for us]."
- **Heb 10:10** — "By the which will we are sanctified through the offering of the body of Jesus Christ once (ephapax) [for all]."

### 2. Simultaneous Occurrence
One use carries a different sense: a large number of people present at one time.

- **1 Cor 15:6** — "After that, he was seen of above five hundred brethren at once (ephapax); of whom the greater part remain unto this present, but some are fallen asleep."
