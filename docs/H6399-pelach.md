## Lexicon Definition

(Aramaic) corresponding to H6398; to serve or worship — KJV: *minister, serve*. BDB: "pay reverence to, serve (deity)." The Aramaic verb has specifically cultic/religious weight; it is the Aramaic counterpart to Hebrew עָבַד (H5647, "serve, worship") when used with a divine object. When the object is a god (true or false), *pelach* means cultic worship, not mere labor. The one non-cultic occurrence (Ezra 7:24) uses the participle substantivally for temple-servants ("ministers of this house of God"), still within the sacred domain.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Cultic Worship of Idols / False Gods
The verb governs accusations, interrogations, and confessions in the golden-image narrative. Its object is a false deity.
- **Dan 3:12** — "they serve not thy gods, nor worship the golden image"
- **Dan 3:14** — "do not ye serve my gods, nor worship the golden image"
- **Dan 3:18** — "we will not serve thy gods, nor worship the golden image"
- **Dan 3:28** — "they might not serve nor worship any god, except their own God"

### 2. Cultic Worship of the True God (YHWH / *'Elaha*)
The same verb — with no semantic shift — denotes exclusive worship of Israel's God.
- **Dan 3:17** — "our God whom we serve is able to deliver us" (participle: *di-'anachna palchin*)
- **Dan 6:16 [MT 6:17]** — "Thy God whom thou servest continually, he will deliver thee" (Darius speaking of Daniel)
- **Dan 6:20 [MT 6:21]** — "O Daniel, servant of the living God, is thy God, whom thou servest continually, able to deliver thee…"

### 3. Eschatological Worship of the Son of Man
The verb describes what "all peoples, nations, and languages" render to the heavenly figure who comes with the clouds. The object is the one like a son of man; the action is the same action the three Hebrews refused to give an idol.
- **Dan 7:14** — "there was given him dominion, and glory, and a kingdom, that all people, nations, and languages, should serve him" (imperfect 3mp *yiphlechun*, with indirect object *leh* "to him")
- **Dan 7:27** — "and all dominions shall serve and obey him" (of the kingdom given to the saints of the Most High / the son of man)

### 4. Cultic Personnel (Substantive Participle)
Masculine-plural construct participle functioning as a noun: "servants-of / ministers-of."
- **Ezra 7:24** — "priests and Levites, singers, porters, Nethinims, or ministers [פָֽלְחֵי] of this house of God" — the construct form *palchei bet elaha* = "servants of the house of God."

**Key point on the Greek witness of Dan 7:14:**
- **Theodotion** (the version dominant in the early Church and followed by most NT citations of Daniel): renders *yiphlechun* with **λατρεύσουσιν** / **δουλεύσουσιν** — aligning Son-of-Man service with technical cultic worship.

- In either tradition, *pelach* in Dan 7:14 moves into a Greek semantic field that the NT and Second-Temple reader would recognize as worship-language rendered to the heavenly figure.

The participle form stresses ongoing, habitual cultic service — the same habitual verb that Dan 7:14 says every nation will render to the Son of Man.

## First Occurrence Notes

The first occurrence is Ezra 7:24 (in Artaxerxes' decree, ca. 458 BC) — but canonically and thematically, the Daniel occurrences (6th century setting, 9 of 10 uses) dominate the word's biblical profile. Within Daniel, the first occurrence is Dan 3:12 on the lips of the Chaldean accusers — the verb enters the Danielic narrative as an accusation of religious nonconformity.

## Notes on Cross-Language Equivalence

The Aramaic *pelach* sits in the same semantic slot as Hebrew עָבַד (H5647) when that Hebrew verb takes a divine object. Hebrew *abad* has a much wider range (slavery, agricultural labor, civic service, cultic service); Aramaic *pelach* is narrower — the contexts almost demand the cultic/reverential reading. This narrowness is what gives Dan 7:14 its weight: the verb chosen for what all nations render to the Son of Man is the same verb Daniel has already used seven times for what is owed to the God of Israel and refused to idols.
