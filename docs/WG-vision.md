# WG: Prophetic Vision

## Overview
The prophetic vision concept in Hebrew Scripture involves a cluster of words describing divinely granted revelatory perception, the persons who receive it, and related acts of seeing. The core word חָזוֹן (chazon) sits at the center, with cognates from the root חזה and semantically related words from the ראה word family.

## Roles in the Concept

- **Content/Oracle:** chazon (חָזוֹן) — the revelatory message itself
- **Action (prophetic):** chazah (חָזָה) — the act of perceiving divine revelation
- **Action (general):** ra'ah (רָאָה) — general seeing, sometimes used for visions
- **Person:** chozeh (חֹזֶה) — the seer who receives visions
- **Office:** nabi (נָבִיא) — the prophet, institutional recipient of chazon
- **Experience:** chizzayon (חִזָּיוֹן) — the visionary experience, especially in dreams
- **Form:** mar'eh (מַרְאֶה) — the visual appearance/form of what is seen

## Key Passages Where Multiple Group Words Appear Together
- **Dan 8:1–2** — chazon (the vision) + mar'eh (the appearance) in the same passage; Daniel receives a chazon and describes what he sees in the mar'eh
- **Eze 12:22–27** — chazon appears 5x in this passage discussing the reliability and timing of prophetic vision
- **Jer 14:14** — chazon + nabi: "The prophets prophesy lies... they prophesy unto you a false vision"
- **2 Chr 32:32** — chazon + chozeh (implied): "written in the vision of Isaiah the prophet"
