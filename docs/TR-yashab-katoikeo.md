## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same across testaments:**
- The core sense of permanent, settled dwelling persists from OT yashab through LXX katoikeo into NT katoikeo. The "inhabitants of [place]" formula (OT prophets) carries directly into Acts (2:5, 1:19).
- Divine dwelling language continues: OT "will God indeed dwell on the earth?" (1 Ki 8:27) → NT "in him should all fulness dwell" (Col 1:19).

**What shifts:**
- The NT extends katoikeo beyond geographical dwelling into spiritual/metaphorical indwelling: "Christ may dwell in your hearts" (Eph 3:17), "righteousness dwelleth" in the new earth (2 Pet 3:13). The OT uses yashab almost exclusively for physical dwelling.
- The "sitting on throne" sense of yashab does not transfer through katoikeo. Instead, the LXX's kathizo/kathemai carries that sense into the NT separately (e.g., Heb 1:3, Rev 3:21 use kathizo for enthronement).
- The participial "inhabitants" usage, dominant in OT prophets (Jeremiah, Zechariah), appears in Acts but is otherwise rare in the epistles, which prefer the spiritual indwelling sense.
