# WG: Bridegroom / Wedding

## Overview
The NT wedding cluster centers on a groom–bride–chamber triad (νυμφίος / νύμφη / νυμφών) paired with the generic marriage vocabulary (γαμέω, γάμος) and the technical term for going out to meet an approaching dignitary (ἀπάντησις). Together they carry both literal wedding scenes (John 2) and the eschatological picture of Christ coming to claim his people.

## Relationships

### Shared Roots
- νυμφίος (G3566), νύμφη (G3565), νυμφών (G3567) — all derived from the νυμφ- stem (originally "veiled one"). Together they form the groom/bride/chamber triad.
- γαμέω (G1060) and γάμος (G1062) share the γαμ- stem (act vs. event).

## Roles in the Concept

- **Groom:** νυμφίος (G3566) — the arriving figure whose presence defines the feast
- **Bride:** νύμφη (G3565) — the counterpart who awaits and receives him
- **Chamber:** νυμφών (G3567) — the physical setting; "sons of the bridechamber" are the groom's attendants
- **Act of marrying:** γαμέω (G1060) — the verb of wedding (either sex)
- **Event / feast:** γάμος (G1062) — the wedding celebration itself
- **Meeting:** ἀπάντησις (G529) — the formal going-out to welcome the approaching groom (also used in 1 Thess 4:17 for meeting the Lord)

## Key Passages Where Multiple Group Words Appear Together

- **Mat 9:15** — νυμφίος + νυμφών: "the children of the bridechamber" cannot mourn while the bridegroom is with them
- **Mat 25:1** — νυμφίος + ἀπάντησις: virgins "went forth to meet the bridegroom"
- **Mat 25:6** — νυμφίος + ἀπάντησις: "Behold, the bridegroom cometh; go ye out to meet him"
- **Mat 25:10** — νυμφίος + γάμος: "the bridegroom came; and they that were ready went in with him to the marriage (γάμος)"
- **Jhn 3:29** — νυμφίος + νύμφη: "He that hath the bride is the bridegroom"
- **Rev 18:23** — νυμφίος + νύμφη: "the voice of the bridegroom and of the bride, shall be heard no more"
