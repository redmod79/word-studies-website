## Lexicon Definition

From δια- (through) and ἑρμηνεύω (to interpret); to explain thoroughly, by implication to translate. The δια- prefix intensifies the root — not merely rendering words but interpreting comprehensively, working *through* a body of material.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Expounding Scripture — Christological Interpretation
- **Luke 24:27** — "And beginning at Moses and all the prophets, he expounded (διερμήνευσεν) unto them in all the scriptures the things concerning himself."

The aorist active indicative marks a completed, decisive act. Jesus works *through* (δια-) the entire scriptural corpus — Moses, prophets, "all the scriptures" — systematically interpreting their messianic content. This is not casual explanation but comprehensive, Scripture-by-Scripture exposition.

### 2. Translating a Name Between Languages
- **Acts 9:36** — "Now there was at Joppa a certain disciple named Tabitha, which by interpretation (διερμηνευομένη) is called Dorcas."

Present passive participle: the name *is being interpreted/translated* from Aramaic (Tabitha) to Greek (Dorcas, "gazelle"). Here the word functions as straightforward linguistic translation — rendering equivalent meaning across languages.

### 3. Interpreting Tongues in Corporate Worship
- **1 Cor 12:30** — "do all speak with tongues? do all interpret (διερμηνεύουσιν)?"
- **1 Cor 14:5** — "greater is he that prophesieth than he that speaketh with tongues, except he interpret (διερμηνεύῃ), that the church may receive edifying."
- **1 Cor 14:13** — "let him that speaketh in an unknown tongue pray that he may interpret (διερμηνεύῃ)."
- **1 Cor 14:27** — "let it be by two, or at the most by three, and that by course; and let one interpret (διερμηνευέτω)."

Four of six occurrences regulate the charismatic gift of tongues-interpretation. The present tense forms (indicative, subjunctive, imperative) indicate an ongoing ministry role, not a one-time event. Paul consistently pairs tongue-speaking with diermēneuō as its necessary complement — without interpretation, tongues do not edify the assembly.
