## Relationships

### Shared Root
- G5218 ὑπακοή, G5219 ὑπακούω, G5255 ὑπήκοος — all from ὑπό + ἀκούω ("to hear under")
- G3876 παρακοή — same base ἀκούω with παρά ("beside/amiss") — "hearing amiss" → disobedience
- G0543 ἀπείθεια, G0544 ἀπειθέω — from ἀ- (not) + πείθω (persuade) — unpersuadable stubbornness

## Roles in the Concept

- **The act:** ὑπακοή (G5218) — obedience as an event or quality ("the obedience of faith")
- **The behavior:** ὑπακούω (G5219) — the verb of obeying ("children, obey your parents")
- **The character:** ὑπήκοος (G5255) — the adjective describing someone as obedient
- **The antonym noun:** παρακοή (G3876) — disobedience as inattentive/wayward hearing
- **The stubborn refusal (noun):** ἀπείθεια (G0543) — obstinate unbelief/non-compliance
- **The stubborn refusal (verb):** ἀπειθέω (G0544) — to actively disbelieve or refuse the gospel

## Key Passages Where Multiple Group Words Appear Together

- **Rom 5:19** — G3876 / G5218: "by one man's *disobedience* (parakoē) many were made sinners; by the *obedience* (hupakoe) of one shall many be made righteous" — the sharpest Adam/Christ contrast in Scripture
- **Rom 6:16** — G5218 / G5219: "servants to obey (hupakouō)...whether of sin...or of *obedience* (hupakoe) unto righteousness"
- **2 Cor 10:5–6** — G5218 / G3876: "bringing every thought captive to the *obedience* of Christ...revenge all *disobedience*, when your obedience is fulfilled"
