## Lexicon Definition

Feminine of H5707 in the original sense of fixture; a stated assemblage (specifically, a concourse, or generally, a family or crowd): assembly, company, congregation, multitude, people, swarm.

Overwhelmingly Pentateuchal: 102/140 verses (73%) occur in Exodus through Numbers. Numbers alone accounts for 54% of all uses. The word is tightly bound to the wilderness-period narrative of Israel as an organized community under Mosaic leadership. ## Semantic Categories

### 1. The Covenant Assembly of Israel
The dominant usage: edah denotes the whole Israelite community as a defined, organized body — the people addressed by Moses, subject to law, accountable before YHWH.
- **Exo 12:3** — "Speak ye unto all the congregation of Israel, saying, In the tenth day of this month they shall take to them every man a lamb"
- **Exo 12:47** — "All the congregation of Israel shall keep it"
- **Num 1:2** — "Take ye the sum of all the congregation of the children of Israel, after their families"
- **Lev 19:2** — "Speak unto all the congregation of the children of Israel, and say unto them, Ye shall be holy"

### 2. The Congregation as Actor — Judicial and Ritual Authority
Edah acts collectively in legal proceedings (judging manslayers, executing capital punishment) and in ritual acts (offering sacrifices, receiving commands).
- **Num 35:24** — "Then the congregation shall judge between the slayer and the revenger of blood"
- **Num 35:25** — "And the congregation shall deliver the slayer out of the hand of the revenger of blood"
- **Lev 4:13** — "And if the whole congregation of Israel sin through ignorance... the assembly shall offer a young bullock"
- **Lev 24:14** — "let all that heard him lay their hands upon his head, and let all the congregation stone him"

### 3. The Rebellious Congregation
Repeatedly in Numbers, edah appears with negative modifiers — "evil congregation," murmuring congregation — during Israel's wilderness failures.
- **Num 14:27** — "How long shall I bear with this evil congregation, which murmur against me?"
- **Num 14:35** — "I will surely do it unto all this evil congregation, that are gathered together against me"
- **Num 16:41** — "all the congregation of the children of Israel murmured against Moses and against Aaron"
- **Num 16:19** — "Korah gathered all the congregation against them unto the door of the tabernacle"

### 4. Korah's Company — A Sub-Group or Faction
In Numbers 16, edah is applied specifically to Korah's rebel faction, showing the word can denote a smaller "company" within the larger assembly.
- **Num 16:5** — "he spake unto Korah and unto all his company"
- **Num 16:6** — "Take you censers, Korah, and all his company"
- **Num 16:11** — "thou and all thy company are gathered together against the LORD"
- **Psa 106:17–18** — "The earth opened and swallowed up Dathan, and covered the company of Abiram. And a fire was kindled in their company"

### 5. Congregation in Worship and Praise
In Psalms and Proverbs, edah extends beyond the wilderness to describe gathered worshipers or the righteous community.
- **Psa 1:5** — "nor sinners in the congregation of the righteous"
- **Psa 82:1** — "God standeth in the congregation of the mighty; he judgeth among the gods"
- **Psa 111:1** — "I will praise the LORD with my whole heart, in the assembly of the upright, and in the congregation"
- **Psa 74:2** — "Remember thy congregation, which thou hast purchased of old"

### 6. Non-Human Swarm
A single usage applies edah to a swarm of bees, showing the root sense of "gathered group" extends beyond human assembly.
- **Judg 14:8** — "behold, there was a swarm of bees and honey in the carcase of the lion"

### 7. Extended/Figurative Uses
Poetic and prophetic uses applying the congregation concept beyond the wilderness narrative.
- **Job 15:34** — "the congregation of hypocrites shall be desolate"
- **Psa 22:16** — "the assembly of the wicked have inclosed me"
- **Psa 86:14** — "the assemblies of violent men have sought after my soul"
- **Jer 30:20** — "their congregation shall be established before me"
