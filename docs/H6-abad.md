## Lexicon Definition

A primitive root; properly, to wander away, i.e. lose oneself; by implication to perish (causative, destroy) — translated: break, destroy(-uction), not escape, fail, lose, (cause to, make) perish, spend, and surely, take, be undone, utterly, be void of, have no way to flee.

The core semantic is *loss* — beginning with simple straying or going missing (animals, paths, counsel) and extending via metaphorical extension to total destruction. The same root covers "the lost sheep" and "the nation that perishes." Qal is intransitive (subject perishes / is lost); Piel and Hiphil are causative (subject destroys something).

The Qal (~61%) dominates: the word's most basic sense is intransitive "perish / be lost." When Hebrew needs to say "X destroys Y" the verb moves into Piel or Hiphil. Unlike H8045 shâmad (almost exclusively causative), ʼâbad is flexible — it describes both the one destroyed and the one destroying, depending on stem.
