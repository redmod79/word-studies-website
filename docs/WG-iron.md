Their pairwise co-occurrences form a tightly clustered group: iron appears with clay 100% of the time, with brass 78%, and with gold or silver 29% each. ## Roles in the Concept

- **דְּהַב (dᵉhab)** — gold appears most broadly (23 verses, including Ezra 5–6 and other Aramaic texts); in Daniel specifically marks Babylon as the head. Used for temple vessels and royal idols as well.
- **כְּסַף (kᵉçaph)** — silver pairs most tightly with gold (85% co-occurrence); applies to temple vessels, tribute, and the image. Also used in Ezra's temple-related Aramaic sections.
- **נְחָשׁ (nᵉchâsh)** — brass/bronze links to both the statue (Dan 2) and the fourth beast's nails (Dan 7:19); also in the tree-stump band of Dan 4:15, 4:23. 9 unique verses, all in Daniel.
- **פַּרְזֶל (parzel)** — iron exclusively in Daniel; the most symbolically developed material: crushing strength (2:40), mixed with clay (2:41–43), iron teeth of the beast (7:7, 7:19), iron band on the stump (4:15, 4:23). 14 unique verses.
- **חֲסַף (chăçaph)** — clay appears only with iron (100% co-occurrence); exclusively in Daniel 2 (feet/toes); symbolizes inherent brittleness preventing the iron kingdom from cohering. 7 unique verses.
