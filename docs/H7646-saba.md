## Lexicon Definition

A primitive root; to sate, i.e. fill to satisfaction (literally or figuratively): have enough, fill (full, self, with), be (to the) full (of), have plenty of, be satiate, satisfy (with), suffice, be weary of.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Physical Satisfaction with Food
The most basic sense — eating and being full.
- **Exo 16:8** — "in the morning bread to the full"
- **Deu 8:10** — "When thou hast eaten and art full, then thou shalt bless the LORD"
- **Ruth 2:14** — "she did eat, and was sufficed, and left"
- **Psa 78:29** — "So they did eat, and were well filled: for he gave them their own desire"

### 2. God Satisfying His People
Divine provision — God as the one who fills and satisfies.
- **Psa 103:5** — "Who satisfieth thy mouth with good things; so that thy youth is renewed like the eagle's"
- **Psa 107:9** — "For he satisfieth the longing soul, and filleth the hungry soul with goodness"
- **Psa 132:15** — "I will abundantly bless her provision: I will satisfy her poor with bread"
- **Psa 145:16** — "Thou openest thine hand, and satisfiest the desire of every living thing"

### 3. Satisfaction of the Soul / Inner Person
Satisfaction extending beyond physical food to deep personal fulfillment.
- **Psa 17:15** — "I shall be satisfied, when I awake, with thy likeness"
- **Psa 63:5** — "My soul shall be satisfied as with marrow and fatness"
- **Isa 53:11** — "He shall see of the travail of his soul, and shall be satisfied"
- **Jer 31:14** — "my people shall be satisfied with my goodness, saith the LORD"

### 4. Insatiable Desire / Never Satisfied
The negative — things that can never be sated, used for judgment or moral observation.
- **Ecc 1:8** — "the eye is not satisfied with seeing, nor the ear filled with hearing"
- **Ecc 5:10** — "He that loveth silver shall not be satisfied with silver"
- **Pro 27:20** — "Hell and destruction are never full; so the eyes of man are never satisfied"
- **Pro 30:15-16** — "There are three things that are never satisfied... The grave; and the barren womb"
- **Hab 2:5** — "who enlargeth his desire as hell, and is as death, and cannot be satisfied"

### 5. Satisfaction Leading to Forgetting God
Fullness producing spiritual complacency — a Deuteronomic warning pattern.
- **Deu 8:12** — "Lest when thou hast eaten and art full, and hast built goodly houses"
- **Deu 31:20** — "when they shall have eaten and filled themselves... then will they turn unto other gods"
- **Hos 13:6** — "According to their pasture, so were they filled; they were filled, and their heart was exalted; therefore have they forgotten me"
- **Neh 9:25** — "they did eat, and were filled, and became fat, and delighted themselves in thy great goodness"

### 6. Filled with Negative Things (Judgment/Suffering)
Sated not with good but with bitterness, reproach, or wrath.
- **Job 7:4** — "I am full of tossings to and fro unto the dawning of the day"
- **Lam 3:15** — "He hath filled me with bitterness"
- **Lam 3:30** — "he is filled full with reproach"
- **Psa 88:3** — "my soul is full of troubles"
- **Psa 123:3-4** — "we are exceedingly filled with contempt... filled with the scorning"

### 7. Covenant-Blessing Satisfaction
Satisfaction as a promised blessing for obedience and faithfulness.
- **Deu 14:29** — "the stranger, and the fatherless, and the widow... shall come, and shall eat and be satisfied"
- **Joel 2:26** — "ye shall eat in plenty, and be satisfied, and praise the name of the LORD"
- **Isa 58:10-11** — "satisfy the afflicted soul... the LORD shall... satisfy thy soul in drought"
- **Psa 22:26** — "The meek shall eat and be satisfied"
