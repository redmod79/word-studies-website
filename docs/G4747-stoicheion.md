Exclusively Pauline epistles plus 2 Peter. Absent from the Gospels, Acts, and all other epistles. The Pauline usage (Galatians, Colossians) centers on "elements/rudiments of the world" as something believers have been freed from; 2 Peter uses it for physical dissolution of the cosmos; Hebrews uses it for basic doctrinal instruction.

## Morphological Note

All 7 NT occurrences appear in the plural (στοιχεῖα / στοιχείων). The singular is never used in the NT. Case distribution: accusative plural 4× (Gal 4:3, 4:9; Col 2:8; Heb 5:12), genitive plural 1× (Col 2:20), nominative plural 2× (2Pe 3:10, 3:12).

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Elemental Principles / Rudimentary Teaching
Basic, elementary instruction — the ABCs of a subject.
- **Heb 5:12** — "ye have need that one teach you again which be the first principles of the oracles of God; and are become such as have need of milk"
  - Here stoicheia = foundational doctrinal elements, contrasted with mature teaching ("strong meat")

### 2. Elemental Forces of the World (Cosmic/Spiritual)
The "elements of the world" from which believers were in bondage — regulatory cosmic or spiritual powers governing pre-Christian existence.
- **Gal 4:3** — "when we were children, were in bondage under the elements of the world"
- **Gal 4:9** — "how turn ye again to the weak and beggarly elements, whereunto ye desire again to be in bondage?"
  - Described as "weak and beggarly" — something believers should not return to
- **Col 2:8** — "Beware lest any man spoil you through philosophy and vain deceit, after the tradition of men, after the rudiments of the world, and not after Christ"
  - Contrasted with Christ — paired with "philosophy," "vain deceit," and "tradition of men"
- **Col 2:20** — "if ye be dead with Christ from the rudiments of the world, why, as though living in the world, are ye subject to ordinances"
  - Dying with Christ frees from stoicheia; linked to subjection to ordinances (δογματίζω G1379)

### 3. Physical Elements of the Cosmos
The material components of the physical universe, subject to eschatological destruction.
- **2Pe 3:10** — "the heavens shall pass away with a great noise, and the elements shall melt with fervent heat, the earth also and the works that are therein shall be burned up"
- **2Pe 3:12** — "the heavens being on fire shall be dissolved, and the elements shall melt with fervent heat"
  - Physical sense: the material substances composing the created order, destroyed by fire at the Day of the Lord
