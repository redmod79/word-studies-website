## Lexicon Definition

A primitive root; to turn back (hence, away) transitively or intransitively, literally or figuratively (not necessarily with the idea of return to the starting point); generally to retreat; often adverbial, *again*. KJV renderings include: return, turn (back/away/again), bring (again/back), restore, come again, answer, render, recompense, recover, deliver, convert, repent, requite, rescue, refresh, withdraw.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Physical Return / Travel Back
The basic, literal sense: a person or group physically returns to a place of origin.
- **Gen 8:9** — "the dove found no rest for the sole of her foot, and she returned unto him into the ark"
- **Gen 22:19** — "So Abraham returned unto his young men"
- **Josh 10:15** — "And Joshua returned, and all Israel with him, unto the camp to Gilgal"
- **Ruth 1:6** — "Then she arose with her daughters in law, that she might return from the country of Moab"

### 2. Spiritual Return / Repentance (Turning to God)
The prophetic call to turn back to YHWH — the theological heart of *shub*. This is the OT's primary vocabulary for repentance.
- **Deut 30:2** — "And shalt return unto the LORD thy God, and shalt obey his voice"
- **Isa 55:7** — "Let the wicked forsake his way... and let him return unto the LORD"
- **Jer 3:12** — "Return, thou backsliding Israel, saith the LORD"
- **Hos 14:1** — "O Israel, return unto the LORD thy God; for thou hast fallen by thine iniquity"
- **Joel 2:13** — "turn unto the LORD your God: for he is gracious and merciful"
- **Mal 3:7** — "Return unto me, and I will return unto you, saith the LORD of hosts"

### 3. Turning Away / Apostasy
The negative mirror of Category 2: turning away from God, His commandments, or righteousness.
- **Num 14:43** — "because ye are turned away from the LORD"
- **Jer 8:5** — "Why then is this people of Jerusalem slidden back by a perpetual backsliding?"
- **Eze 18:24** — "when the righteous turneth away from his righteousness, and committeth iniquity"
- **Eze 33:18** — "When the righteous turneth from his righteousness, and committeth iniquity, he shall even die thereby"

### 4. Restitution / Restoration of Property or Persons (Hiphil)
The Hiphil causative sense of making whole again — returning stolen goods, restoring persons to their position, or giving back what was taken. This is the sense operative in Num 5:7-8.
- **Gen 20:7** — "Now therefore restore the man his wife; for he is a prophet"
- **Gen 42:25** — "Joseph commanded to fill their sacks with corn, and to restore every man's money into his sack"
- **Lev 6:4** — "he shall restore that which he took violently away, or the thing which he hath deceitfully gotten"
- **Num 5:7** — "he shall recompense his trespass with the principal thereof, and add unto it the fifth part thereof"
- **Neh 5:11** — "Restore, I pray you, to them, even this day, their lands, their vineyards"
- **Eze 33:15** — "If the wicked restore the pledge, give again that he had robbed"

### 5. Divine Restoration / Turning Captivity
God as subject: bringing back from exile, reversing judgment, restoring fortunes. The idiom *shub shebuth* ("turn the captivity") appears ~30 times.
- **Deut 30:3** — "the LORD thy God will turn thy captivity, and have compassion upon thee, and will return and gather thee"
- **Jer 29:14** — "I will turn away your captivity, and I will gather you from all the nations"
- **Jer 30:18** — "I will bring again the captivity of Jacob's tents, and have mercy on his dwellingplaces"
- **Eze 39:25** — "Now will I bring again the captivity of Jacob, and have mercy upon the whole house of Israel"
- **Joel 3:1** — "when I shall bring again the captivity of Judah and Jerusalem"
- **Amos 9:14** — "I will bring again the captivity of my people of Israel"

### 6. Return to Dust / Mortality
The cosmic sense: human beings returning to the ground from which they were made.
- **Gen 3:19** — "till thou return unto the ground; for out of it wast thou taken: for dust thou art, and unto dust shalt thou return"
- **Job 34:15** — "All flesh shall perish together, and man shall turn again unto dust"
- **Ecc 3:20** — "All go unto one place; all are of the dust, and all turn to dust again"
- **Ecc 12:7** — "Then shall the dust return to the earth as it was: and the spirit shall return unto God who gave it"

### 7. Rendering / Requiting / Recompensing
God or humans giving back according to deeds — retribution or reward.
- **Deut 32:41** — "I will render vengeance to mine enemies, and will reward them that hate me"
- **Jdg 9:57** — "all the evil of the men of Shechem did God render upon their heads"
- **Psa 28:4** — "Give them according to their deeds... render to them their desert"
- **Pro 24:29** — "I will render to the man according to his work"
- **Joel 3:4** — "will ye render me a recompence?"

### 8. Adverbial "Again" (Repetition)
Used adverbially with another verb to express doing something again — the most frequent single KJV translation (87 verses).
- **Gen 26:18** — "Isaac digged again the wells of water, which they had digged in the days of Abraham"
- **Exo 4:7** — "Put thine hand into thy bosom again"
- **Pro 26:11** — "As a dog returneth to his vomit, so a fool returneth to his folly"
