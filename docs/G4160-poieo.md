## Lexicon Definition

Apparently a prolonged form of an obsolete primary; to make or do (in a very wide application, more or less direct). The word is the broadest Greek term for performing an action or producing a result, covering everything from physical creation to moral conduct to the carrying out of a command.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. General Action / Carrying Out a Command
The most basic usage: performing what one is told or what duty requires.

- **Matt 1:24** — "Then Joseph being raised from sleep *did* as the angel of the Lord had bidden him"
- **Col 3:17** — "And whatsoever ye *do* in word or deed, *do* all in the name of the Lord Jesus"
- **Col 3:23** — "And whatsoever ye *do*, *do* it heartily, as to the Lord, and not unto men"
- **2 Thess 3:4** — "that ye both *do* and will *do* the things which we command you"

### 2. Physical Making / Creating
Bringing something into existence or fabricating an object.

- **Matt 19:4** — "he which *made* them at the beginning *made* them male and female"
- **Acts 17:24** — "God that *made* the world and all things therein"
- **Heb 1:2** — "by whom also he *made* the worlds"
- **Rev 14:7** — "worship him that *made* heaven, and earth, and the sea"

### 3. Performing Signs and Miracles
ποιέω + σημεῖον ("signs") is a recurring NT pattern, especially in John.

- **John 2:11** — "This beginning of miracles *did* Jesus in Cana of Galilee"
- **Acts 6:8** — "And Stephen, full of faith and power, *did* great wonders and miracles among the people"
- **Acts 15:12** — "declaring what miracles and wonders God had *wrought* among the Gentiles"
- **John 14:12** — "the works that I *do* shall he *do* also; and greater works than these shall he *do*"

### 4. Moral / Ethical Conduct (Doing Good or Evil)
Used to describe righteous conduct or sinful action; often paired with ἀγαθός ("good") or κακός ("evil").

- **1 Pet 2:22** — "Who *did* no sin, neither was guile found in his mouth"
- **1 Pet 3:11** — "Let him eschew evil, and *do* good; let him seek peace"
- **John 5:29** — "they that have *done* good, unto the resurrection of life; and they that have *done* evil"
- **John 3:21** — "he that *doeth* truth cometh to the light, that his deeds may be made manifest"

### 5. Committing Sin
A specialized usage in the Johannine letters, ποιέω + ἁμαρτία = "commit sin."

- **John 8:34** — "Whosoever *committeth* sin is the servant of sin"
- **1 John 3:4** — "Whosoever *committeth* sin transgresseth also the law: for sin is the transgression of the law"
- **1 John 3:8** — "He that *committeth* sin is of the devil"

### 6. Fulfilling / Doing the Law
Obedience to Torah or commandment expressed as ποιέω.

- **Rom 2:14** — "the Gentiles, which have not the law, *do* by nature the things contained in the law"
- **Gal 3:12** — "The man that *doeth* them shall live in them"
- **Jas 2:8** — "If ye fulfil the royal law... ye *do* well"
- **Matt 25:40** — "Inasmuch as ye have *done* it unto one of the least of these my brethren, ye have *done* it unto me"

### 7. Divine Redemptive Action
God and Christ as the subject of ποιέω in acts of salvation, appointment, and new creation.

- **Acts 2:36** — "God hath *made* that same Jesus, whom ye have crucified, both Lord and Christ"
- **2 Cor 5:21** — "he hath *made* him to be sin for us, who knew no sin"
- **Rev 1:6** — "hath *made* us kings and priests unto God and his Father"
- **Eph 2:14** — "he is our peace, who hath *made* both one"
