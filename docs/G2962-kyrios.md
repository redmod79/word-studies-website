## Lexicon Definition

From κῦρος (kŷros, "supremacy"); supreme in authority, i.e. (as noun) controller; by implication Master (as a respectful title): God, Lord, master, Sir. In classical Greek: owner, head of household, one with legal authority. In LXX: standard rendering for the divine name YHWH and for אֲדֹנָי (Adonai). In NT: applied to God the Father, to Jesus Christ, and in mundane sense to earthly masters/owners.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Divine Name — Quoting or Alluding to YHWH (LXX Continuity)

The NT inherits the LXX convention of translating יְהוָה (YHWH) with κύριος. Quoted OT passages carry this divine-name sense directly into NT Greek.

- **Mat 1:20** — "the angel of the Lord appeared unto him" (OT theophany idiom)
- **Mar 1:3** — "Prepare ye the way of the Lord" (quoting Isa 40:3)

## Hebrew Source (via LXX)

Kyrios is the LXX's standard rendering for the Tetragrammaton and for אֲדֹנָי. PMI-ranked alignment across the parallel corpus:

| Hebrew Source | Strong's | LXX Co-occurrence | PMI | Score |
|---------------|----------|-------------------|-----|-------|
| יְהוָה (YHWH) | H3068 | 5,107 | 1.81 | 15.48 |
| אֲדֹנָי (Adonai) | H136 | 419 | 1.87 | 11.29 |
| יְהוִה (Yehovih, variant) | H3069 | 290 | 1.88 | 10.65 |
| אָדוֹן (adon) | H113 | 271 | 1.86 | 10.45 |
| יָהּ (Yah) | H3050 | (via formulae) | — | — |

**LXX convention:** By the 3rd-2nd cent. BCE, Hebrew scribal reverence for the Tetragrammaton led readers to pronounce "Adonai" wherever YHWH appeared; Greek translators rendered both with κύριος. This is why the NT can quote OT "YHWH says" passages as "the Lord says" and apply them directly to Jesus without lexical seam — the same Greek word that carried the divine name in the Greek scriptures the apostles read is the word they use for the risen Christ.

**Rom 10:13 / Joel 2:32 bridge:** Paul quotes "whosoever shall call upon the name of the Lord (kyrios) shall be saved," applying a YHWH-invocation text directly to Jesus (Rom 10:9, "Lord Jesus"). The LXX convention supplies the exegetical vocabulary.

**Phil 2:11 / Isa 45:23 bridge:** "Every knee shall bow...every tongue confess" in Isaiah is spoken by YHWH ("unto me every knee shall bow"); Phil 2:10-11 applies it to Jesus and names him kyrios "to the glory of God the Father" — ascribing YHWH's unique prerogative to the exalted Christ without displacing the Father.
