# WG: Forever / Eternal Duration

## Overview
The concept of eternal or limitless duration in Scripture is expressed by a cluster of Hebrew and Greek words, each with a distinct angle: ʻôwlâm (the boundless time horizon), ʻad (the terminus that intensifies ʻôwlâm), netsach (perpetuity as decisive finality), αἰών and αἰώνιος (the LXX/NT equivalents), and the Aramaic ʻâlam (cognate in Daniel/Ezra). Together they cover past antiquity, present perpetuity, and future endless existence.

## Roles in the Concept

- **The horizon** — ʻôwlâm (H5769): time as it recedes beyond sight; the word for "eternity" in its most open-ended form
- **The endpoint** — ʻad (H5703): the terminus or goal; combined with ʻôwlâm to express "for ever and ever" (eternity with an intensifying endpoint)
- **The finality** — netsach (H5331): perpetuity as decisive, complete, victorious outcome; often in lament or judicial contexts ("forgotten for ever?")
- **The age** — αἰών (G165): the Greek equivalent, adding cosmological nuance ("this age" vs. "the coming age")
- **The attribute** — αἰώνιος (G166): the adjectival form expressing "eternal life," "eternal covenant," "eternal fire"
- **The Aramaic cognate** — ʻâlam (H5957): appears in Daniel's court narratives and doxologies ("O king, live for ever")
