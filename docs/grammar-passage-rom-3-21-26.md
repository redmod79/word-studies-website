## Text

## Clause Structure and Syntactic Flow

The six-verse paragraph is a single period — one of the most complex syntactic structures in the Pauline corpus. The main clause is in v.21 with every subsequent element dependent on it through participial, relative, prepositional, and infinitival subordination. The structural skeleton:

```
MAIN CLAUSE (v.21):
  Νυνὶ δὲ ... δικαιοσύνη Θεοῦ πεφανέρωται
  [Subject: δικαιοσύνη Θεοῦ | Verb: πεφανέρωται (perf. pass. ind.)]

  ├── PARTICIPIAL MODIFIER (v.21b):
  │     μαρτυρουμένη ὑπὸ τοῦ νόμου καὶ τῶν προφητῶν
  │     [pres. pass. ptcp., adjectival, modifying δικαιοσύνη]
  │
  ├── APPOSITIONAL RESTATEMENT (v.22a):
  │     δικαιοσύνη δὲ Θεοῦ διὰ πίστεως Ἰησοῦ Χριστοῦ
  │     [verbless, restates subject with instrumental qualifier]
  │
  ├── EXTENSION (v.22b): εἰς πάντας τοὺς πιστεύοντας
  │     [articular substantival ptcp. = recipients]
  │
  ├── EXPLANATORY γάρ CLAUSES (vv.22c-23):
  │     οὐ γάρ ἐστιν διαστολή· πάντες γὰρ ἥμαρτον
  │     καὶ ὑστεροῦνται τῆς δόξης τοῦ Θεοῦ
  │     [two γάρ clauses grounding the "unto all" claim]
  │
  ├── PARTICIPIAL CONTINUATION (v.24):
  │     δικαιούμενοι δωρεὰν τῇ αὐτοῦ χάριτι
  │     διὰ τῆς ἀπολυτρώσεως τῆς ἐν Χριστῷ Ἰησοῦ
  │     [pres. pass. ptcp. NPM, linked to πάντες of v.23]
  │
  ├── RELATIVE CLAUSE (v.25a):
  │     ὃν προέθετο ὁ Θεὸς ἱλαστήριον
  │     [antecedent = Χριστῷ Ἰησοῦ from v.24]
  │
  │     ├── INSTRUMENTAL (v.25b): διὰ πίστεως
  │     ├── LOCATIVE (v.25c): ἐν τῷ αὐτοῦ αἵματι
  │     │
  │     ├── PURPOSE (v.25d): εἰς ἔνδειξιν τῆς δικαιοσύνης αὐτοῦ
  │     │     └── CAUSAL (v.25e): διὰ τὴν πάρεσιν τῶν προγεγονότων ἁμαρτημάτων
  │     │           └── TEMPORAL (v.26a): ἐν τῇ ἀνοχῇ τοῦ Θεοῦ
  │     │
  │     ├── PURPOSE RESTATED (v.26b): πρὸς τὴν ἔνδειξιν τῆς δικαιοσύνης αὐτοῦ
  │     │     └── TEMPORAL (v.26c): ἐν τῷ νῦν καιρῷ
  │     │
  │     └── RESULT / PURPOSE (v.26d): εἰς τὸ εἶναι αὐτὸν δίκαιον
  │           καὶ δικαιοῦντα τὸν ἐκ πίστεως Ἰησοῦ
  │           [articular infinitive = telic/ecbatic outcome]
```

The paragraph flows from one main verb (πεφανέρωται) through layers of participial, prepositional, and infinitival expansion. There is no second finite main verb until the new sentence at v.27. This single-period architecture compresses the entire logic of justification — its source (God's righteousness), channel (faith), ground (Christ's blood), mechanism (propitiation), occasion (the justice problem of the ages), and outcome (God shown to be just and justifier) — into one syntactically unified statement.

## Key Grammatical Features

### 1. πεφανέρωται (v.21) — Perfect Passive Indicative of φανερόω

- **Form:** V-RPI-3S — perfect tense, passive voice, indicative mood, third person singular. Reduplication πε- + stem φανερ- + passive ending -ωται.
- **Significance:** This is the sole finite main verb governing the entire six-verse period. The **perfect tense** (see [tense-perfect](../greek/tense-perfect.md)) fuses two temporal frames: (1) a past event — God's righteousness was revealed at a point in time — and (2) a present state — it now stands manifested and remains so. The Νυνὶ δέ ("but now") temporal marker exploits this dual reference: the "now" is not merely the moment of the event but the inaugurated era in which the revealed state persists.
- **Parser-verified contrast:** Of 20+ NT occurrences of φανερόω, **only Rom 3:21 uses the perfect tense**. All other occurrences are aorist (ἐφανέρωσεν/ἐφανερώθη, punctiliar event) or present/future. Paul's choice of perfect over aorist here is deliberate — he is not narrating a past event (aorist would serve) but asserting a new standing reality. Compare Rom 16:26 φανερωθέντος (aorist passive participle) where Paul narrates the historical event of revelation; here the perfect marks the abiding state produced by that event.
- **Passive voice:** The unexpressed agent is God (cf. [divine-passive](../greek/divine-passive.md)). God's righteousness is the grammatical subject, and God himself is the implied agent of the manifestation — a layered divine-passive where God both manifests and is the content of what is manifested.
- **Grammar reference:** [tense-perfect](../greek/tense-perfect.md), [divine-passive](../greek/divine-passive.md)

### 2. δικαιούμενοι (v.24) — Present Passive Participle of δικαιόω

- **Form:** V-PPP-NPM — present tense, passive voice, participle, nominative plural masculine. This is the **adverbial participle** modifying the subject πάντες from v.23.
- **Significance:** The present tense of this participle is grammatically striking against the backdrop of the aorist ἥμαρτον ("sinned," constative aorist covering the whole history of human sin) and present ὑστεροῦνται ("fall short," ongoing present reality). The present passive participle δικαιούμενοι sits between the past-completed universal sin and the ongoing present deficit, characterizing the *manner* in which the "all" who sinned now relate to God's manifested righteousness — they are "being justified" as an ongoing, present-tense reality.
- **Syntactic link:** The NPM (nominative plural masculine) concords with πάντες (v.23 A-NPM), creating a participial bridge from v.23 back to the main clause of v.21. The "all who sinned" are the same "all who are being justified" — the universality of sin is grammatically matched by the universality of the justification offer.
- **Adverbial modifiers:** Three instrumental/means qualifiers stack under this single participle:
  - δωρεὰν (ADV) — "freely / as a gift" — adverb of manner
  - τῇ αὐτοῦ χάριτι (dative of means) — "by his grace" — the ground
  - διὰ τῆς ἀπολυτρώσεως (genitive after διά) — "through the redemption" — the mechanism
- **Pauline dikaioo morphology:** Parser search reveals Paul uses δικαιόω across every tense-voice combination: present passive participle (Rom 3:24), present active participle (Rom 3:26; 4:5; 8:33), aorist passive participle (Rom 5:1, 9), future active indicative (Rom 3:30), future passive indicative (Rom 2:13; Gal 2:16), aorist passive indicative (Rom 4:2; 1 Cor 6:11), perfect passive indicative (Rom 6:7; 1 Cor 4:4). The morphological variety itself is theologically productive: justification appears as past event (aorist), present process (present), and future consummation (future).
- **Word study link:** [G1344-dikaioo](../../word-studies/G1344-dikaioo.md)

### 3. προέθετο (v.25) — Aorist Middle Indicative of προτίθημι

- **Form:** V-2AMI-3S — second aorist, middle voice, indicative mood, third person singular. Subject: ὁ Θεός (nominative, explicitly stated).
- **Significance:** The middle voice of προτίθημι carries two possible nuances, both parser-verified across its 3 NT occurrences (Rom 1:13; 3:25; Eph 1:9):
  - (a) **"Set forth publicly"** — middle with display/presentation sense: God publicly exhibited Christ as ἱλαστήριον. The middle conveys that God did this *for his own purposes / of his own initiative*.
  - (b) **"Purposed / planned beforehand"** — middle with deliberative-reflexive sense (cf. Rom 1:13 προεθέμην "I purposed"; Eph 1:9 προέθετο "he purposed"). God determined within himself to present Christ.
- **Double accusative construction:** ὃν (relative pronoun, accusative — direct object, antecedent = Christ Jesus) + ἱλαστήριον (predicate accusative) — God set forth *him* [as] *a propitiation/mercy-seat*. The ἱλαστήριον functions as an object complement.
- **ἱλαστήριον (G2435):** Parser-verified NT usage: only Rom 3:25 and Heb 9:5. In Heb 9:5 it explicitly denotes the mercy seat (כַּפֹּרֶת, the gold lid of the Ark where blood was sprinkled on the Day of Atonement, Lev 16:14-15). Whether Paul uses the term as "mercy seat" (cultic furniture metaphor) or "means of propitiation" (abstract) is an exegetical question, but the lexical link to the Day of Atonement ritual is grammatically unavoidable given the Heb 9:5 parallel and the LXX use of ἱλαστήριον for כַּפֹּרֶת (Exo 25:17-22; Lev 16:2, 13-15).
- **Word study link:** [G2435-hilasterion](../../word-studies/G2435-hilasterion.md), [G129-haima](../../word-studies/G129-haima.md)

| εἰς + acc. | εἰς ἔνδειξιν (v.25) | Accusative | Purpose — "for a demonstration" |
| **διά + acc.** | **διὰ τὴν πάρεσιν** (v.25) | **Accusative** | **Cause — "because of the passing over"** |
| ἐν + dat. | ἐν τῇ ἀνοχῇ τοῦ Θεοῦ (v.26) | Dative | Temporal/circumstantial — "in/during God's forbearance" |
| πρός + acc. | πρὸς τὴν ἔνδειξιν (v.26) | Accusative | Purpose — "with a view to the demonstration" |
| ἐν + dat. | ἐν τῷ νῦν καιρῷ (v.26) | Dative | Temporal — "at the present time" |
| εἰς + acc. | εἰς τὸ εἶναι (v.26) | Accusative | Purpose/result — "so that he might be" |
| ἐκ + gen. | ἐκ πίστεως Ἰησοῦ (v.26) | Genitive | Source/basis — "of faith in Jesus" |

**Critical grammatical distinction — διά + genitive vs. διά + accusative:**
Paul uses διά four times in this passage. Three times with the **genitive** (διὰ πίστεως ×2, διὰ τῆς ἀπολυτρώσεως) denoting means/instrumentality. Once with the **accusative** (διὰ τὴν πάρεσιν, v.25) denoting cause/reason. This is a standard Koine distinction (Wallace 368-369; BDF §222-223), but it is exegetically load-bearing here: the πάρεσις is the **cause that necessitated** the demonstration of God's righteousness, not the means by which it was accomplished. The grammar encodes the justice problem directly.

### 5. πάρεσις (v.25) — NT Hapax and the Justice Problem

- **Form:** N-ASF — accusative singular feminine of πάρεσις (G3929). Parser-verified as **NT hapax legomenon** — it occurs only in Rom 3:25.
- **Significance:** πάρεσις must be distinguished from the more common ἄφεσις (G859, "forgiveness/remission," 17 NT occurrences). πάρεσις is not forgiveness but *passing over* — a temporary letting-go or overlooking without full settlement. The KJV renders it "remission" but this obscures the lexical distinction Paul is drawing. God's πάρεσις of sins committed in former times (τῶν προγεγονότων ἁμαρτημάτων, with perfect active participle προγεγονότων [V-2RAP-GPN of προγίνομαι G4266] marking sins as having-occurred-and-standing-as-historical-fact) created a justice problem: if sins were merely "passed over" rather than dealt with, then God's δικαιοσύνη was open to question.
- **Grammatical encoding of the problem:** The causal prepositional phrase (διὰ + accusative = "because of") structurally subordinates πάρεσις to the purpose clause εἰς ἔνδειξιν τῆς δικαιοσύνης αὐτοῦ. The syntax reads: *[God set forth Christ as ἱλαστήριον] for the purpose of demonstrating his righteousness, because of the passing over of formerly committed sins*. The ἔνδειξις (demonstration, proof, display — G1732, parser-verified 4 NT occurrences, 2 in this very passage) was *necessitated by* the πάρεσις. The justice problem generated the atonement, not the other way around.
- **Word study link:** [G1732-endeixis](../../word-studies/G1732-endeixis.md)

### 6. εἰς τὸ εἶναι αὐτὸν δίκαιον καὶ δικαιοῦντα (v.26) — Articular Infinitive + Participial Climax

- **Form:** εἰς (PREP) + τὸ (T-ASN, neuter article) + εἶναι (V-PAN, present active infinitive of εἰμί) + αὐτόν (P-ASM, accusative subject of infinitive) + δίκαιον (A-ASM, predicate adjective) + καί (CONJ) + δικαιοῦντα (V-PAP-ASM, present active participle of δικαιόω, accusative agreeing with αὐτόν).
- **Significance:** The articular infinitive (εἰς τὸ + infinitive) is a standard Pauline purpose/result construction (BDF §402; Wallace 590-592). It provides the telic climax of the entire paragraph — the ultimate aim of God's setting forth Christ as ἱλαστήριον.
- **The double predication:** The climax consists of two predications joined by καί:
  - **δίκαιον** (adjective, accusative) — "just/righteous" — God's own character is vindicated
  - **δικαιοῦντα** (present active participle, accusative) — "justifying / the one who justifies" — God's saving action toward believers
  
  The grammar requires both predicates to be simultaneously true of the same subject (αὐτόν = God). This is the resolution of the justice problem identified by πάρεσις: the articular infinitive clause asserts that the cross enables God to be *both* just (his righteousness vindicated) *and* justifier (his mercy extended) without contradiction. The coordinating καί is not merely additive but resolves what appeared to be a disjunction.
- **δίκαιος / δικαιόω paronomasia:** The adjective δίκαιον (G1342) and the verb δικαιοῦντα (G1344) share the same root δικ-, creating a rhetorical wordplay (paronomasia/polyptoton): *the just one and the justifier*, *the righteous one and the one who makes-righteous*. This wordplay is present in the Greek and irreproducible in most translations.
- **τὸν ἐκ πίστεως Ἰησοῦ** — the direct object of δικαιοῦντα is an articular prepositional phrase functioning as a substantive: "the one [who is] of faith in Jesus." The article τὸν nominalizes the entire ἐκ πίστεως Ἰησοῦ phrase, identifying the class of persons whom God justifies.
- **Word study links:** [G1342-dikaios](../../word-studies/G1342-dikaios.md), [G1344-dikaioo](../../word-studies/G1344-dikaioo.md), [G4102-pistis](../../word-studies/G4102-pistis.md)

### 7. ἥμαρτον / ὑστεροῦνται (v.23) — Aorist-Present Tense Pair

- **Forms:** ἥμαρτον = V-2AAI-3P (second aorist, active, indicative, 3rd plural of ἁμαρτάνω G264); ὑστεροῦνται = V-PPI-3P (present, passive, indicative, 3rd plural of ὑστερέω G5302).
- **Significance:** The shift from aorist to present within the same clause is aspectually significant. The **constative/summary aorist** ἥμαρτον ("sinned") views the totality of human sin as a completed historical reality — a single action encapsulating all human transgression from Adam forward. The **present passive** ὑστεροῦνται ("fall short / are lacking") then shifts to an ongoing state: the result of having sinned is a present, continuing deficit. The aorist looks backward; the present looks at the current condition. Together they establish the problem that justification must solve: a past deed (sin) producing a present state (deficiency before God's glory).
- **Genitive object:** τῆς δόξης τοῦ Θεοῦ — genitive after ὑστερέω (standard genitive of separation or objective genitive with verbs of lacking: "fall short *of* the glory *of* God"). The double genitive chain (δόξης → Θεοῦ) identifies the standard against which the deficit is measured.
- **Word study link:** [G1391-doxa](../../word-studies/G1391-doxa.md)

### 8. μαρτυρουμένη (v.21b) — Present Passive Participle of μαρτυρέω

- **Form:** V-PPP-NSF — present tense, passive voice, participle, nominative singular feminine, agreeing with δικαιοσύνη.
- **Significance:** This adjectival participle modifying δικαιοσύνη creates a grammatical paradox with the main clause: the righteousness of God is manifested *apart from law* (χωρὶς νόμου) yet is simultaneously *being witnessed by the law* (μαρτυρουμένη ὑπὸ τοῦ νόμου). The preposition ὑπό + genitive marks the law and prophets as the **agent** of the witnessing — an expressed agent, making this a standard passive with agent rather than a divine passive. The present tense of the participle (vs. the perfect of the main verb) suggests the witnessing is *ongoing* — the OT Scriptures continue to testify to this reality.
- **Word study link:** [G3140-martyreo](../../word-studies/G3140-martyreo.md)

## Discourse Architecture: The δικ- Root Saturation

The passage is saturated with the δικ- word-group, creating a tight lexical chain that binds the entire paragraph together:

| Verse | Form | Lemma | Function |
|-------|------|-------|----------|
| 3:21 | δικαιοσύνη | G1343 | Subject of main clause |
| 3:22 | δικαιοσύνη | G1343 | Appositional restatement |
| 3:24 | δικαιούμενοι | G1344 | Present passive participle — the act of justifying |
| 3:25 | δικαιοσύνης | G1343 | Object of ἔνδειξις — what is demonstrated |
| 3:26 | δικαιοσύνης | G1343 | Repeated in purpose restatement |
| 3:26 | δίκαιον | G1342 | Predicate adjective — God's attribute |
| 3:26 | δικαιοῦντα | G1344 | Present active participle — God's activity |

Seven δικ- words in six verses. Three members of the word-family appear: the noun (δικαιοσύνη, God's righteousness as attribute), the adjective (δίκαιος, God as righteous person), and the verb (δικαιόω, God's act of declaring righteous). The passage moves from the *attribute* (v.21-22) through the *crisis* (v.23-25) to the *resolution* that holds attribute and action together (v.26).
