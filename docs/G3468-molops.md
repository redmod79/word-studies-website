## Lexicon Definition

**Thayer's:** μώλωψ, μώλωπος, ὁ (Hesychius: τραῦμα καί ὁ ἐκ αἱματώδης τόπος ἤ καί τά ἐξερχόμενα τῶν πληγῶν ὕδατα) — a bruise, wale, wound that trickles with blood. 1 Peter 2:24, from Isaiah 53:5 (where KJV renders "stripes"). Also found in Genesis 4:23; Exodus 21:25; Isaiah 1:6 (LXX). Classical usage in Aristotle, Plutarch, Anthologia.

**Key distinction:** Unlike πληγή (G4127), which denotes the blow or stroke itself, μώλωψ specifically names the visible bruise-mark or weal left behind — the wound as evidence of beating endured.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Vicarious Wound / Substitutionary Suffering
- **1 Pet 2:24** — "by whose stripes (μώλωπι) ye were healed"

This is the sole NT usage. Peter quotes Isaiah 53:5 (LXX) to describe Christ's atoning wounds. The dative singular μώλωπι is instrumental — healing comes *by means of* the wound-mark Christ bore. The paradox is deliberate: a wound that heals, a bruise that restores.

### 2. LXX Usage (Outside NT Canon)
The word appears in the LXX in several passages where it translates H2250 חַבּוּרָה (chabbuwrah):
- **Gen 4:23** (LXX) — Lamech's song: "I have slain a man to my wounding (μώλωπα)"
- **Exod 21:25** (LXX) — lex talionis: "stripe for stripe (μώλωπα ἀντὶ μώλωπος)"
- **Isa 1:6** (LXX) — Israel's moral condition: "wounds (μώλωπες) and bruises"
- **Isa 53:5** (LXX) — the Suffering Servant: "by his stripe (τῷ μώλωπι αὐτοῦ) we are healed"
