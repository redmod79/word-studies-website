## Lexicon Definition

From H7200 (ra'ah); a view (the act of seeing); also an appearance (the thing seen),
whether (real) a shape (especially if handsome, comeliness; often plural the looks),
or (mental) a vision:— apparently, appearance(-reth), as soon as beautiful(-ly),
countenance, fair, favoured, form, goodly, to look (up) on (to), look(-eth), pattern,
to see, seem, sight, visage, vision.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Physical Attractiveness of Persons
The phrase *tob mar'eh* ("good of appearance" → "well favoured") expresses outward beauty.
Applied to women, men, livestock — any visually assessed subject.
- **Gen 12:11** — "Behold now, I know that thou [art] a fair woman to look upon"
- **Gen 29:17** — "Rachel was beautiful and well favoured"
- **Gen 41:2** — "seven well favoured kine and fatfleshed"
- **Esther 2:7** — "the maid [was] fair and beautiful" (Esther before the king)

### 2. Facial Countenance
The look or expression of a person's face — assessed visually, carrying social/spiritual weight.
- **Judg 13:6** — "his countenance [was] like the countenance of an angel of God, very terrible"
- **1 Sam 16:7** — "Look not on his countenance... for the LORD looketh on the heart"
- **Dan 1:13** — "let our countenances be looked upon before thee"
- **Song 2:14** — "let me see thy countenance, let me hear thy voice; for... thy countenance is comely"

### 3. Clinical Visual Inspection (Leviticus Skin Laws)
Ten occurrences in Leviticus 13 use *mar'eh* as a technical term for the visual depth and
color of a skin lesion as assessed by the priest. Entirely a physical, diagnostic sense.
- **Lev 13:3** — "the hair in the plague is turned white, and the plague in sight [be] deeper than the skin"
- **Lev 13:4** — "if the bright spot [be] white... and in sight [be] not deeper than the skin"
- **Lev 13:30** — "if it [be] in sight deeper than the skin; [and there be] in it a yellow thin hair"
- **Lev 14:37** — "plague [be] in the walls... with hollow strakes, greenish or reddish, which in sight [are] lower"

### 4. Theophanic / Angelic Appearances
Divine beings and glory appear in a visible form described with *mar'eh*. Ezekiel uses it
repeatedly to describe what the vision *looked like*, always with comparative hedging.
- **Exod 24:17** — "the sight of the glory of the LORD [was] like devouring fire"
- **Num 9:16** — "the appearance of fire by night" over the tabernacle
- **Ezek 1:26–28** — "the appearance of the brightness round about... as the appearance of the bow"
- **Dan 10:6** — "his face as the appearance of lightning, and his eyes as lamps of fire"

### 5. Prophetic Visions (Daniel and Ezekiel)
*Mar'eh* used for the content of a prophetic vision — what was seen, the thing revealed.
Interchangeable with *chazon* (H2377) in these contexts, but *mar'eh* emphasizes the
visual impression received.
- **Ezek 8:4** — "the glory of the God of Israel [was] there, according to the vision that I saw in the plain"
- **Dan 8:15–16** — "when I, [even] I Daniel, had seen the vision... Gabriel, make this [man] to understand the vision"
- **Dan 8:26** — "the vision of the evening and the morning which was told [is] true"
- **Dan 9:23** — "I am come to shew [thee]; for thou [art] greatly beloved... understand the vision"
