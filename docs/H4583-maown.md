## Lexicon Definition

From the same root as H4585; an abode, of God (the Tabernacle or the Temple), men (their home) or animals (their lair); hence, a retreat (asylum): — den, dwelling((-) place), habitation.

Alternate form: מָעִין (maʻiyn) in 1 Chronicles 4:41.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. God's Heavenly Dwelling
- **Deu 26:15** — "Look down from thy holy habitation (maʻown), from heaven, and bless thy people Israel"
- **2Ch 30:27** — "their prayer came up to his holy dwelling place (maʻown), even unto heaven"
- **Jer 25:30** — "The LORD shall roar from on high, and utter his voice from his holy habitation (maʻown)"
- **Zec 2:13** — "he is raised up out of his holy habitation (maʻown)"
- **Psa 68:5** — "A father of the fatherless, and a judge of the widows, is God in his holy habitation (maʻown)"

The dominant usage. The phrase "holy habitation" (maʻown qodesh) appears 5 times, always referring to heaven as YHWH's dwelling. Often paired with divine action directed earthward — blessing, hearing prayer, roaring in judgment.

### 2. God as Personal Refuge
- **Psa 90:1** — "Lord, thou hast been our dwelling place (maʻown) in all generations"
- **Psa 91:9** — "thou hast made the LORD, which is my refuge, even the most High, thy habitation (maʻown)"
- **Psa 71:3** — "Be thou my strong habitation (maʻown), whereunto I may continually resort"

A shift from location to relationship: God himself becomes the maʻown rather than occupying one. The refuge/asylum sense of the lexicon definition is clearest here.

### 3. God's Earthly Dwelling (Tabernacle/Temple)
- **Psa 26:8** — "LORD, I have loved the habitation (maʻown) of thy house, and the place where thine honour dwelleth"
- **1Sa 2:29** — "which I have commanded in my habitation (maʻown)"
- **1Sa 2:32** — "thou shalt see an enemy in my habitation (maʻown)"
- **2Ch 36:15** — "he had compassion on his people, and on his dwelling place (maʻown)"

Applied to the Tabernacle at Shiloh (1 Samuel) and the Temple (2 Chronicles). In 1 Samuel, the sanctuary as maʻown is threatened by priestly corruption.

### 4. Den/Lair of Wild Animals (Desolation)
- **Jer 9:11** — "I will make Jerusalem heaps, and a den (maʻown) of dragons"
- **Jer 10:22** — "to make the cities of Judah desolate, and a den (maʻown) of dragons"
- **Jer 49:33** — "Hazor shall be a dwelling (maʻown) for dragons, and a desolation for ever"
- **Jer 51:37** — "Babylon shall become heaps, a dwellingplace (maʻown) for dragons"
- **Nah 2:11** — "Where is the dwelling (maʻown) of the lions?"

Judgment imagery: cities reduced to animal lairs. The word tanniyn (H8577, "dragon/jackal") collocates 4 times in these verses (24% of all occurrences). This usage inverts the nobility of categories 1-3: what was once a human or divine dwelling becomes an animal lair.
