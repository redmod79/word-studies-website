## Semantic Categories

### 1. Historical Narrative Moment ("at that time")
The most common usage: a deictic pointer to a historical moment in narrative, often introduced by "at that time" (בָּעֵת הַהִיא bāʻêth hahîʼ).

- **Deut 1:9** — "And I spake unto you at that time, saying, I am not able to bear you myself alone"
- **Josh 5:2** — "At that time the LORD said unto Joshua, Make thee sharp knives, and circumcise again the children of Israel"
- **1 Kgs 8:65** — "And at that time Solomon held a feast, and all Israel with him"
- **1 Chr 12:32** — "And of the children of Issachar, which were men that had understanding of the times, to know what Israel ought to do"

### 2. Appointed / Proper Time
ʻêth used for a set, expected, or fitting occasion — divinely appointed or naturally determined.

- **Gen 18:14** — "At the time appointed I will return unto thee, according to the time of life, and Sarah shall have a son"
- **Jer 8:7** — "Yea, the stork in the heaven knoweth her appointed times; and the turtle and the crane and the swallow observe the time of their coming; but my people know not the judgment of the LORD"
- **2 Sam 24:15** — "the LORD sent a pestilence upon Israel from the morning even to the time appointed"
- **Eccl 3:1** — "To every [thing there is] a season, and a time to every purpose under the heaven"

### 3. Natural Seasons and Cycles
ʻêth applied to recurring natural time periods (harvest, rain, evening, tides).

- **Gen 8:11** — dove returned "in the evening" (lit. at the time of evening)
- **Zech 10:1** — "Ask ye of the LORD rain in the time of the latter rain"
- **Hos 2:9** — "I will return, and take away my corn in the time thereof, and my wine in the season thereof"
- **Job 5:26** — "Thou shalt come to thy grave in a full age, like as a shock of corn cometh in in his season"

### 4. Time of Trouble / Crisis
A dominant prophetic pattern: ʻêth + trouble/distress, marking a moment of acute need or judgment.

- **Judg 10:14** — "let them deliver you in the time of your tribulation"
- **Neh 9:27** — "in the time of their trouble, when they cried unto thee, thou heardest them from heaven"
- **Jer 14:8** — "O the hope of Israel, the saviour thereof in time of trouble, why shouldest thou be as a stranger in the land"
- **Dan 12:1** — "there shall be a time of trouble, such as never was since there was a nation even to that same time"

### 5. Eschatological / End Time
In Daniel especially, ʻêth refers to the culminating prophetic moment of history — "the time of the end."

- **Dan 8:17** — "Understand, O son of man: for at the time of the end shall be the vision"
- **Dan 11:35** — "even to the time of the end: because it is yet for a time appointed"
- **Joel 3:1** — "in those days, and in that time, when I shall bring again the captivity of Judah and Jerusalem"
- **Ezek 7:7** — "the time is come, the day of trouble is near, and not the sounding again of the mountains"

### 6. Wisdom about the Right Moment
ʻêth used reflexively in wisdom literature and prophetic speech: discerning, seizing, or missing the appropriate time.

- **Eccl 3:2–8** — "A time to be born, and a time to die; a time to plant, and a time to pluck up..."
- **Esth 4:14** — "if thou altogether holdest thy peace at this time, then shall there enlargement and deliverance arise to the Jews from another place"
- **Amos 5:13** — "the prudent shall keep silence in that time; for it is an evil time"
- **Psa 119:126** — "It is time for thee, LORD, to work: for they have made void thy law"
