# WG: Exceeding / Surpassing

## Overview
The NT "surpassing" concept is expressed through a family of ὑπέρ (huper) compounds and related περισσ- (periss-) words. These are paradigmatic alternatives — they occupy the same slot in Paul's vocabulary for divine super-abundance but never co-occur in the same verse. All are exclusively or predominantly Pauline.

## Relationships

### Shared Roots
- **ὑπέρ (huper, G5228)** — preposition "beyond, over": root of G5235, G5236, G5248, G5250
- **βάλλω (ballo, G906)** — "to throw": root of G5235, G5236
- **περισσ-** — "surplus, excess": root of G4052, G4053; also in G5248 (huper + perisseuo)
- **πλεονάζω (pleonazo, G4121)** — "to multiply, increase": root of G5250

### Co-occurrence (appear in same verses)

No pairwise co-occurrences. These words are paradigmatic alternatives — Paul selects one per context rather than combining them. This makes them substitution-class synonyms: each expresses "beyond ordinary measure" with a different shade.

## Roles in the Concept

- **Qualitative Surpassing:** huperballo (G5235) — the thing itself exceeds measurement
- **Abstract Concept:** huperbole (G5236) — the state or degree of surpassing
- **Intensified Overflow:** huperperisseuo (G5248) — abounding amplified beyond normal abounding
- **Intensified Multiplication:** huperpleonazo (G5250) — multiplying amplified beyond normal multiplying
- **Base-Level Abundance:** perisseuo (G4052) — the standard "abound" that the huper- compounds intensify
- **Adjectival Surplus:** perissos (G4053) — describing something as exceeding normal degree
