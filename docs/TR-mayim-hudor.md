**Semantic continuity:** ὕδωρ inherits the full Hebrew semantic range of mayim — drinking water, ritual washing water, riverine/oceanic water, cosmic/creation waters, figurative living water. The LXX translators used ὕδωρ as an almost one-to-one substitute. Where Hebrew specifies a body of water (river, spring, well, flood), Greek often disambiguates with ποταμός/πηγή/ἄβυσσος, but ὕδωρ remains the default.

## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same across testaments:**
- Water as the default beverage for human sustenance — drunk, drawn, rationed, begged for (OT: Gen 24; 1 Kgs 13, 17, 22; Amos 4:8 / NT: Matt 10:42; Mark 9:41)
- Water as ritual purifier (OT: Lev 14–15; Num 19; Ezek 36:25 / NT: Matt 3:11; Eph 5:26; Heb 10:22)
- Water as cosmic/chaos agent (OT: Gen 1:2; flood; Exodus sea / NT: 2 Pet 3:5–6; Rev 12:15)
- Water as figurative abundance, life, salvation (OT: Isa 12:3; 55:1; Jer 2:13 / NT: John 4; 7:38; Rev 22:1)

**What shifts:**
- In the NT, water becomes heavily sacramentally loaded (baptism, eucharistic contrast to wine) in a way the OT only typologically anticipates
- The OT "living waters" prophetic image becomes explicitly christologically and pneumatologically located (John 4:14; 7:38–39 glossed as "the Spirit")
- Physical drinking water retreats as a named topic (the NT has far fewer narrative drinking-water scenes than the OT's pilgrim/siege/wilderness contexts), though it remains assumed as the default (John 4 at the well; Matt 10:42 cup of cold water)
