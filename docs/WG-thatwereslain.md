## Roles in the Concept

- **The slaughter act (verb):** σφάζω (G4969) — the precise act of butchering; used of the Lamb, martyrs, and Cain's murder
- **The slaughter as event (noun):** σφαγή (G4967) — "brought as a lamb to the slaughter" (Acts 8:32 quotes Isa 53:7)
- **The slaughter victim (noun):** σφάγιον (G4968) — the animal being offered; hapax in Acts 7:42
- **The slaughtered Lamb (image):** ἀρνίον (G721) — Revelation's central sacrificial symbol; 28x, almost all in Revelation
- **The broader sacrifice (verb):** θύω (G2380) — sacrifice/offer; covers the full act from killing to presenting; includes the Passover (1 Cor 5:7)

## Key Passages Where Group Words Appear Together

- **Rev 5:6** — ἀρνίον (G721) + ἐσφαγμένον (G4969): "a Lamb as it had been slain" — the two words that define the concept's center
- **Rev 5:12** — ἀρνίον + ἐσφαγμένον: "Worthy is the Lamb that was slain to receive power"
- **Rev 13:8** — ἀρνίον + ἐσφαγμένου: "the Lamb slain from the foundation of the world"
- **Acts 8:32** (quotes Isa 53:7) — σφαγή (G4967): "he was led as a sheep to the slaughter"
