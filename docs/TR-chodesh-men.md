**Semantic split in translation:** The LXX translators systematically divided chodesh into two Greek words based on sense. When chodesh refers to a calendar month (date formulas, durations), they used μήν. When it refers to the new-moon festival or observance, they used νουμηνία. This split confirms that ancient readers understood chodesh as carrying both an astronomical and a cultic meaning — the Greek language required two words where Hebrew used one.

## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same across testaments:**
- The calendar-month sense carries through without change: Hebrew chodesh → LXX μήν → NT μήν all denote a month as a time unit
- The new-moon cultic observance is recognized in both testaments (OT new moon sacrifices → Col 2:16 noumēnia)

**What shifts:**
- The NT uses μήν exclusively as a neutral time unit (durations, prophetic periods). The cultic "new moon" sense is entirely captured by νουμηνία (G3561) in the single occurrence at Col 2:16
- The rich OT texture — where chodesh simultaneously evokes astronomical observation, calendar reckoning, and cultic celebration — narrows in the NT to a simple temporal measurement word
- The prophetic eschatological vision of Isa 66:23 ("from one new moon to another shall all flesh come to worship") has no direct NT quotation, though the concept of ongoing worship is carried by other vocabulary
