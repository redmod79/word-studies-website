## Semantic Categories

### 1. Lifting the Eyes / Voice / Hand (Gestural Idioms)
The most common idiom is wayyiśśāʾ ... ʾet-ʿênāyw ("and he lifted up his eyes"), used for deliberate visual survey. Lifting the voice = weeping or crying aloud. Lifting the hand = swearing an oath.
- **Gen 13:14** — "Lift up now thine eyes, and look from the place where thou art" (territorial survey)
- **Gen 21:16** — "she lifted up her voice, and wept" (crying aloud)
- **Exo 6:8** — "I did swear (lit. I lifted my hand) to give it to Abraham" (oath-swearing)
- **Lam 3:41** — "Let us lift up our heart with our hands unto God in the heavens" (posture of prayer)

### 2. Physical Transport and Carrying
Bearing physical objects — the ark, burdens, provisions, bodies — by servants, Levites, and laborers.
- **Num 1:50** — "the Levites... shall bear the tabernacle" (Levitical carrying duty)
- **1 Kgs 5:15** — "Solomon had threescore and ten thousand that bare burdens" (labor force)
- **2 Sam 2:32** — "they took up Asahel, and buried him" (carrying the dead)
- **Josh 3:6** — "Take up the ark of the covenant, and pass over before the people" (priestly command)

### 3. Bearing Consequences and Burdens (Moral/Legal)
Nāśāʾʾāwōn / nāśāʾ ḥēṭ — to "bear iniquity/sin" means to suffer the consequence or bear legal liability. The sinner bears his own guilt; alternatively, a substitute or God bears it away.
- **Gen 4:13** — "My punishment [is] greater than I can bear" (Cain bearing consequences)
- **Lev 5:1, 17** — "he shall bear his iniquity" (legal formula: liability falls on the sinner)
- **Isa 53:12** — "he bare the sin of many" (Servant bearing others' guilt — substitutionary)
- **Num 14:34** — "ye shall bear your iniquities, even forty years" (covenant penalty)

### 4. Forgiveness / Pardoning Sin
When God is the subject, nāśāʾ + ʿāwōn/peśaʿ/ḥaṭṭāʾ means to "lift off" guilt — to forgive, absolve, pardon. The same verb used for bearing sin is used for removing it.
- **Exo 10:17** — "forgive, I pray thee, my sin" (Pharaoh pleading)
- **Psa 32:5** — "thou forgavest the iniquity of my sin" (David's confession answered)
- **Mic 7:18** — "Who is a God like unto thee, that pardoneth iniquity?" (doxology)
- **Exo 34:7** — "forgiving iniquity and transgression and sin" (divine self-disclosure)

### 5. Exaltation and Honor
Niphal and Hithpael stems frequently describe being raised to honor or exalting oneself. The prophetic literature pairs nāśāʾ with rûm (H7311) for the "high and lofty" exaltation of God.
- **Isa 2:2** — "the mountain of the LORD'S house shall be established... exalted above the hills"
- **Isa 52:13** — "he shall be exalted and extolled, and be very high" (Servant's vindication)
- **Ezek 1:19–21** — the living creatures and wheels "lifted up from the earth" (theophanic vision)
- **Est 3:1** — "Ahasuerus... advanced Haman" (royal promotion)

### 6. Taking a Census ("Lifting the Head")
A specialized idiom: nāśāʾ ʾet-rōʾš ("lift the head of") means to take a census, count, or muster. Used throughout Numbers.
- **Num 1:2** — "Take ye the sum (lit. lift the head) of all the congregation"
- **Num 1:49** — "thou shalt not number the tribe of Levi, neither take the sum"
- **Exo 30:12** — "when thou takest the sum of the children of Israel"
