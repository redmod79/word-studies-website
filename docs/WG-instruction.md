# WG: Instruction

## Overview
The OT instruction concept cluster centers on the act of authoritative teaching/directing (yarah), its product (towrah), and the parallel vocabulary of learning and knowing that forms the complete wisdom-instruction field. These words frequently co-occur with derek ("the way"), forming a recurring formula: divine/priestly instruction directs people in the right path.

## Roles in the Concept

- **Source Act:** yarah (יָרָה) — the authoritative pointing/directing; the teacher aims the learner at the target
- **Product:** towrah (תּוֹרָה) — the codified instruction; what results from yarah
- **Receptive Act:** lamad (לָמַד) — the learner's response; to be goaded into knowledge
- **Resulting Knowledge:** yada (יָדַע) — experiential, relational knowing acquired through instruction
- **Practical Outcome:** sakal (שָׂכַל) — acting wisely; successful application of what was taught
- **Acquired Character:** chakam (חָכַם) — wisdom as settled capacity
- **Spatial Metaphor:** derek (דֶּרֶךְ) — "the way" in which one is taught to walk
