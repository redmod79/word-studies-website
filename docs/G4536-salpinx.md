## Semantic Categories

### 1. Eschatological Gathering / Second Coming
The trumpet as the signal for Christ's return and the gathering of believers.
- **Matt 24:31** — "he shall send his angels with a great sound of a trumpet, and they shall gather together his elect from the four winds"
- **1 Thess 4:16** — "the Lord himself shall descend from heaven with a shout, with the voice of the archangel, and with the trump of God"

### 2. The Resurrection Signal
The trumpet specifically marking the moment of resurrection at the last day.
- **1 Cor 15:52** — "In a moment, in the twinkling of an eye, at the last trump: for the trumpet shall sound, and the dead shall be raised incorruptible"

### 3. The Voice of God / Divine Communication
A sound like a trumpet representing God's voice or a heavenly summons — echoing the Sinai theophany.
- **Heb 12:19** — "the sound of a trumpet, and the voice of words; which voice they that heard intreated that the word should not be spoken to them any more" (Sinai)
- **Rev 1:10** — "I was in the Spirit on the Lord's day, and heard behind me a great voice, as of a trumpet"
- **Rev 4:1** — "the first voice which I heard was as it were of a trumpet talking with me"

### 4. The Seven Apocalyptic Trumpets
The seven trumpets given to angels in Revelation, each signaling a successive eschatological judgment.
- **Rev 8:2** — "to them were given seven trumpets"
- **Rev 8:6** — "the seven angels which had the seven trumpets prepared themselves to sound"
- **Rev 8:13** — an angel crying woe at the sound of the remaining trumpets
- **Rev 9:14** — "Saying to the sixth angel which had the trumpet, Loose the four angels..."

### Easily Confused Words

| Word | Strong's | Shared Translation | Key Difference |
|------|----------|--------------------|----------------|
| שׁוֹפָר (shôwphâr) | H7782 | trumpet | The Hebrew *shofar* — a ram's horn; G4536 is its primary LXX/NT equivalent |
| חֲצֹצְרָה (chătsôtsᵉrâh) | H2689 | trumpet | The Hebrew silver *chatsotsrah* — the priestly signaling trumpet of Num 10; also renders as G4536 in LXX |

The Greek σάλπιγξ conflates both distinct OT instruments in LXX translation. The *shofar* was made of ram's horn (cultic, covenantal, Jubilee); the *chatsotsrah* was a straight silver trumpet (priestly, liturgical).
