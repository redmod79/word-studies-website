## Lexicon Definition

The lexicon table gives the short definition: "to smear out, i.e. obliterate." The fuller Thayer entry in the same tool data gives two main senses:

1. To anoint, wash, besmear, whitewash, plaster, or overlay a surface.
2. To wipe off, wipe away, obliterate, erase, wipe out, or blot out.

Thayer lists the NT forms: future **ἐξαλείψω**, first aorist participle **ἐξαλείψας**, and first aorist passive infinitive **ἐξαλειφθῆναι**. It explicitly connects the LXX usage with Hebrew **מָחָה** (H4229), especially for blotting out sins, names, and tears.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Blotting Out Sins

- **Acts 3:19** — "Repent ye therefore, and be converted, that your sins may be blotted out, when the times of refreshing shall come from the presence of the Lord"

Here the direct object is **ἁμαρτίας** (G266, "sins"). The verb is aorist passive infinitive: the sins are the object acted upon.

### 2. Cancelling or Removing a Written Record

- **Col 2:14** — "Blotting out the handwriting of ordinances that was against us, which was contrary to us, and took it out of the way, nailing it to his cross"

The direct object is **χειρόγραφον** (G5498, "handwriting"). The same clause also includes **δόγμα** (G1378, "ordinances/decrees"), **αἴρω** (G142, "took it out of the way"), and **προσηλόω** (G4338, "nailing").

### 3. Non-Erasure from the Book of Life

- **Rev 3:5** — "He that overcometh, the same shall be clothed in white raiment; and I will not blot out his name out of the book of life, but I will confess his name before my Father, and before his angels."

The object is **ὄνομα** (G3686, "name"), with the source phrase **ἐκ τῆς βίβλου τῆς ζωῆς** ("out of the book of life"). The negative particles **οὐ μή** mark the promised non-removal.

### 4. Wiping Away Tears

- **Rev 7:17** — "and God shall wipe away all tears from their eyes"
- **Rev 21:4** — "And God shall wipe away all tears from their eyes; and there shall be no more death, neither sorrow, nor crying, neither shall there be any more pain"

Both uses pair G1813 with **δάκρυον** (G1144, "tear") and **ὀφθαλμός** (G3788, "eye"), matching the physical "wipe away" sense.
