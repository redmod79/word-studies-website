# Grammar Analysis: Elijah's 3.5-Year Drought (1 Kgs 17:1; 18:1; Luke 4:25; James 5:17)

## Classification
**LITERAL 3.5 years** — a narrative duration reported in Kings and specifically
quantified as *three years and six months* by two independent NT witnesses
(Jesus, James). **TYPOLOGICAL** (not prophetic-time) significance for the
Daniel/Revelation 3.5 apocalyptic unit: the typology runs from Elijah-historical
to Daniel-apocalyptic, not the reverse. No seal-command, no apocalyptic genre
markers, no day-for-year formula attached. Elijah's drought is a *real
historical duration* of roughly 3.5 solar years that later apocalyptic literature
appears to have patterned itself after (cf. Rev 11:6 where the two witnesses
"shut up heaven" in explicit Elijah-imitation).

## 1. 1 Kings 17:1 — Elijah's Oath: "These Years"

### Text
**Hebrew:** וַיֹּאמֶר֩ אֵלִיָּ֨הוּ הַתִּשְׁבִּ֜י מִתֹּשָׁבֵ֣י גִלְעָד֮ אֶל־אַחְאָב֒ חַי־יְהוָ֞ה אֱלֹהֵ֤י יִשְׂרָאֵל֙ אֲשֶׁ֣ר עָמַ֣דְתִּי לְפָנָ֔יו אִם־יִהְיֶ֛ה הַשָּׁנִ֥ים הָאֵ֖לֶּה טַ֣ל וּמָטָ֑ר כִּ֖י אִם־לְפִ֥י דְבָרִֽי׃

**KJV:** And Elijah the Tishbite, [who was] of the inhabitants of Gilead, said
unto Ahab, [As] the LORD God of Israel liveth, before whom I stand, there shall
not be dew nor rain these years, but according to my word.

### Key Grammatical Features

**1. חַי־יְהוָה (ḥay-YHWH) — oath formula + אִם (ʾim) oath-negation**
- **Form:** Predicate adjective `חַי` ("[as] YHWH lives") + `אֲשֶׁר עָמַדְתִּי לְפָנָיו`
  (relative xQt0 clause), then `אִם` introducing the content of the oath.
- **Significance:** This is a classic Hebrew oath formula. Clause 2 (`AjCl`
  "YHWH-liveth") is the invocation; clause 4 (`xYqX` with `אִם`) is the
  self-imprecation — syntactically a conditional protasis whose apodosis
  ("…then may I suffer") is suppressed. `אִם` in an oath context therefore
  functions as an **emphatic negation**: "there shall *not* be dew or rain."
- **Grammar reference:** [syntax-conditional-vow.md](../hebrew/syntax-conditional-vow.md)

**2. הַשָּׁנִים הָאֵלֶּה (ha-šānîm hā-ʾēlleh) — "these years" (definite plural + demonstrative)**
- **Form:** Articular feminine plural noun `שָׁנִים` "years" + articular plural
  demonstrative `אֵלֶּה` "these." Plain, unmarked plural of `שָׁנָה`. The
  parser flags the noun as `fp.Abs` (feminine plural absolute). Functions
  syntactically as `[Time]` adjunct in clause 4.
- **Significance:** No prophetic-symbol vocabulary. No `עִדָּן` (Aramaic
  "time"), no `מוֹעֵד` ("appointed time"), no fractional expression ("time,
  times, and half a time"). The word `שָׁנִים` is the ordinary Hebrew noun for
  calendar years, used here with the proximal demonstrative ("these, the
  current ones"). The duration is left **indefinite** in Elijah's own speech
  — he does not stipulate "three and a half years"; he says "until I say
  otherwise." The specific length is only disclosed retrospectively by 18:1
  and by the NT witnesses.
- **Contrast with apocalyptic markers:** Dan 7:25 / 12:7 use `עִדָּן וְעִדָּנִין
  וּפְלַג עִדָּן` (Aramaic "time, times, and half a time"); Rev 11:2/13:5 use
  `μῆνας τεσσεράκοντα δύο` (42 months); Rev 11:3/12:6 use
  `ἡμέρας χιλίας διακοσίας ἑξήκοντα` (1260 days). None of these idioms appear
  in 1 Kgs 17:1.

**3. אִם־יִהְיֶה … טַל וּמָטָר — imperfect under oath-negation**
- **Form:** Qal imperfect 3ms of `היה` "to be," with compound subject `טַל
  וּמָטָר` ("dew and rain"). Parser returns clause type `xYqX` (extraposed
  element + yiqtol + subject), domain `Q` (direct speech).
- **Significance:** The imperfect + `אִם` locks the statement into the future
  indefinite: "there shall by no means be…" Note that `יִהְיֶה` is 3ms even
  though the subject is compound; this is standard verb-first agreement with
  the nearest singular element.
- **Grammar reference:** [qal-imperfect.md](../hebrew/qal-imperfect.md)

**4. לְפִי דְבָרִי (lə-p̄î dəḇārî) — "according to my word"**
- **Form:** Construct chain `פִי … דְבָרִי` ("the mouth of my word"), idiomatic
  for "at my command/utterance." Final nominal clause (`NmCl`) functions
  adverbially.
- **Significance:** The drought is explicitly tied to Elijah's *speech-act
  authority*, not to a calendrical countdown. It ends when Elijah (on YHWH's
  word, 18:1) announces its end — not when a fixed prophetic period expires.
  This is the decisive literary tell that 1 Kgs 17:1 is *not* a time-prophecy.
  A time-prophecy terminates by clock; a vow terminates by the vow-maker's
  releasing word.

Clauses 2–5 are all **domain Q (direct speech)** — the narrator (Clause 1) is
quoting Elijah's oath. No prophetic-vision framing, no "I looked," no seal
command, no angel-interpreter. Pure prophetic speech-act.

## 2. 1 Kings 18:1 — "In the Third Year"

### Text
**Hebrew:** וַיְהִי֙ יָמִ֣ים רַבִּ֔ים וּדְבַר־יְהוָ֗ה הָיָה֙ אֶל־אֵ֣לִיָּ֔הוּ בַּשָּׁנָ֥ה הַשְּׁלִישִׁ֖ית לֵאמֹ֑ר לֵ֚ךְ הֵרָאֵ֣ה אֶל־אַחְאָ֔ב וְאֶתְּנָ֥ה מָטָ֖ר עַל־פְּנֵ֥י הָאֲדָמָֽה׃

**KJV:** And it came to pass [after] many days, that the word of the LORD came
to Elijah in the third year, saying, Go, shew thyself unto Ahab; and I will send
rain upon the earth.

### Key Grammatical Features

**1. יָמִים רַבִּים (yāmîm rabbîm) — "many days"**
- **Form:** Indefinite masculine plural noun + adjective. Functions as `[Time]`
  in clause 1 (`Way0`, narrative domain).
- **Significance:** A stock Hebrew idiom for an extended but **unspecified**
  duration. The narrator is not giving a prophetic time-count; he is
  signalling "a long stretch of time passed." This is the *narrative* tempo
  marker; the calendrical marker follows in the next clause.

**2. בַּשָּׁנָה הַשְּׁלִישִׁית (ba-šānāh ha-šəlîšîṯ) — "in the third year"**
- **Form:** Preposition `בְּ` + articular noun `שָׁנָה` (fs.Abs "year") +
  articular ordinal adjective `שְׁלִישִׁית` ("third"). Standard ordinal-date
  construction. Functions as `[Time]` adjunct in clause 2 (`WXQt`,
  disjunctive X-qatal giving the specific calendar reference).
- **Significance:** The clause type `WXQt` is the parser's code for a
  **disjunctive waw-X-qatal** construction, which in narrative regularly
  marks circumstantial / scene-setting information off the main `wayyiqtol`
  line. Here it provides the precise date: **the third (rainless) year**.
  From which event is this "third year" counted? Hebrew ordinal-year
  formulae in Kings count from the inauguration of the reference event; the
  natural antecedent is Elijah's oath in 17:1 — meaning the drought has now
  been underway **into its third year** when YHWH speaks. Depending on
  whether years are counted inclusively (Hebrew standard), this can comport
  with the NT's "three years and six months" if the drought began well
  before the famine was recognized (the 6 extra months cover the pre-drought
  dry season or the regnal-year reckoning offset).
- **Grammar reference:**
  [syntax-wx-qatal-circumstantial.md](../hebrew/syntax-wx-qatal-circumstantial.md)

**3. הֵרָאֵה (hērāʾēh) — Niphal imperative 2ms**
- **Form:** Niphal imperative from `ראה` "to see/appear." The Niphal of `ראה`
  carries reflexive/middle force: "show yourself, let yourself be seen."
- **Significance:** God commands Elijah to reverse his 17:1 hiddenness — the
  drought-speech required him to vanish (17:3), the end-speech requires him
  to reappear. The drought's beginning and end are both speech-act framed,
  not time-clock framed.
- **Grammar reference:** [stem-niphal.md](../hebrew/stem-niphal.md),
  [imperative-mood.md](../hebrew/imperative-mood.md)

**4. וְאֶתְּנָה (wə-ʾettənāh) — 1cs cohortative**
- **Form:** Waw + Qal cohortative/imperfect 1s `נתן` "to give." Cohortative
  ending in `-āh` expresses resolve/intention.
- **Significance:** "And I will (be pleased to) send rain." YHWH's cohortative
  resolution matches Elijah's 17:1 vow-negation: the same divine speech
  authority that honored Elijah's withholding now authorizes release.
- **Grammar reference:** [cohortative-1cp-divine-speech.md](../hebrew/cohortative-1cp-divine-speech.md)

### Clause Structure (from parser)

```
Clause 1 (Way0, N): וַיְהִי יָמִים רַבִּים       [main narrative verb, N-domain]
  [Pred] יְהִי | [Time] יָמִים רַבִּים
Clause 2 (WXQt, N): וּדְבַר-יְהוָה הָיָה אֶל-אֵלִיָּהוּ בַּשָּׁנָה הַשְּׁלִישִׁית
  [Conj] וּ | [Subj] דְבַר-יְהוָה | [Pred] הָיָה | [Cmpl] אֶל-אֵלִיָּהוּ | [Time] בַּשָּׁנָה הַשְּׁלִישִׁית
Clause 3 (InfC, N): לֵאמֹר    [infinitive-construct introducing speech]
Clause 4 (ZIm0, Q): לֵךְ       [imperative, Q-domain opens]
Clause 5 (ZIm0, Q): הֵרָאֵה אֶל-אַחְאָב
Clause 6 (WYq0, Q): וְאֶתְּנָה מָטָר עַל-פְּנֵי הָאֲדָמָה
```

Again: ordinary prose narrative, ordinary ordinal date. No apocalyptic framing,
no sealed vision, no day-for-year formula.

## 3. Luke 4:25 — Jesus' Testimony: ἐπὶ ἔτη τρία καὶ μῆνας ἕξ

### Text
**Greek:** ἐπ' ἀληθείας δὲ λέγω ὑμῖν, πολλαὶ χῆραι ἦσαν ἐν ταῖς ἡμέραις Ἠλείου
ἐν τῷ Ἰσραήλ, ὅτε ἐκλείσθη ὁ οὐρανὸς **ἐπὶ ἔτη τρία καὶ μῆνας ἕξ**, ὡς ἐγένετο
λιμὸς μέγας ἐπὶ πᾶσαν τὴν γῆν.

**KJV:** But I tell you of a truth, many widows were in Israel in the days of
Elias, when the heaven was shut up three years and six months, when great
famine was throughout all the land.

### Word-by-Word Parsing (key words)

|---|------|-------|----------|---------|
| 1 | λέγω | λέγω | G3004 | V-PAI-1S (present active indicative) |
| 2 | ὑμῖν | σύ | G4771 | P-2DP (dative plural) |
| 3 | ἦσαν | εἰμί | G1510 | V-IAI-3P (imperfect active indicative) |
| 4 | ἐκλείσθη | κλείω | G2808 | **V-API-3S (aorist passive indicative)** |
| 5 | ὁ οὐρανὸς | οὐρανός | G3772 | T-NSM + N-NSM ("the heaven") |
| 6 | ἐπὶ | ἐπί | G1909 | PREP (+ accusative = **extent of time**) |
| 7 | ἔτη | ἔτος | G2094 | **N-APN (accusative plural neuter)** — "years" |

| 12 | ἐγένετο | γίνομαι | G1096 | V-2ADI-3S (aorist middle indicative) |
| 13 | λιμὸς μέγας | λιμός / μέγας | G3042 / G3173 | N-NSM + A-NSM — "a great famine" |

### Key Grammatical Features

**1. ἐπὶ ἔτη τρία καὶ μῆνας ἕξ — accusative of extent of time ("for 3 years and 6 months")**
- **Form:** Preposition `ἐπί` + accusative noun phrase. Both `ἔτη` and `μῆνας`
  are **accusative plural**; numerals agree in case, gender, and number
  (`τρία` A-APN agrees with `ἔτη` neuter; `ἕξ` is indeclinable but
  distributes over `μῆνας`).
- **Significance:** The accusative-of-extent is the standard, unambiguous
  Greek way of saying "for a duration of X." There is nothing symbolic or
  elliptical about it. Two distinct time-units (ἔτος "year" and μήν "month")
  are added together — **this is arithmetic, not prophetic symbol.**
  Prophetic time-units in apocalyptic (ἡμέρας, μῆνας forty-two as a *unit*)
  are never added to years in the NT; they stand alone as sealed
  time-designators. Here Jesus *does* math: 3 years + 6 months = 3.5 years.
- **Grammar reference:** [aorist-infinitive-accusative-of-duration.md](../greek/aorist-infinitive-accusative-of-duration.md)
  (same construction-type, accusative duration).

**2. ἐκλείσθη — aorist passive indicative 3s ("was shut up")**
- **Form:** Aorist passive indicative of `κλείω` "to shut."
- **Significance:** Classic **divine passive** — the heaven is shut by God,
  in response to Elijah's prayer. The aorist reports the whole event as a
  completed whole; it does *not* narrate it moment-by-moment. That Jesus
  treats the whole 3.5-year closure as a single aorist action is consistent
  with a historical, bounded event — not a symbolic unit still unfolding.
- **Grammar reference:** [aorist-passive-indicative.md](../greek/aorist-passive-indicative.md),
  [divine-passive.md](../greek/divine-passive.md)

**3. ἐπ' ἀληθείας … λέγω ὑμῖν — "truly I say to you"**
- **Form:** Prepositional phrase `ἐπί + gen ἀληθείας` ("on [the basis of]
  truth") + present active indicative `λέγω`.
- **Significance:** Jesus prefaces the number with a **truth-asseveration
  formula**. He is reporting a *factual historical duration*, not retailing
  an apocalyptic cipher. If Elijah's drought were a symbolic 1260-day unit,
  Jesus' most natural phrasing would be some variant of "a time, times, and
  half a time" or "1260 days" or "forty-two months" — the LXX/NT apocalyptic
  vocabulary. He uses none of it. He gives calendar arithmetic.

## 4. James 5:17 — James' Testimony: ἐνιαυτοὺς τρεῖς καὶ μῆνας ἕξ

### Text
**Greek:** Ἠλείας ἄνθρωπος ἦν ὁμοιοπαθὴς ἡμῖν, καὶ προσευχῇ προσηύξατο τοῦ μὴ
βρέξαι, καὶ οὐκ ἔβρεξεν ἐπὶ τῆς γῆς **ἐνιαυτοὺς τρεῖς καὶ μῆνας ἕξ**.

**KJV:** Elias was a man subject to like passions as we are, and he prayed
earnestly that it might not rain: and it rained not on the earth by the space
of three years and six months.

| 10 | ἐνιαυτοὺς | ἐνιαυτός | G1763 | **N-APM (accusative plural masc)** — "years" |
| 11 | τρεῖς | τρεῖς | G5140 | A-APM — "three" |
| 12 | μῆνας | μήν | G3376 | N-APM — "months" |
| 13 | ἕξ | ἕξ | G1803 | A-NUI — "six" |

### Key Grammatical Features

**1. ἐνιαυτοὺς τρεῖς καὶ μῆνας ἕξ — accusative of extent**
- **Form:** Same construction-type as Luke 4:25 but **without** the
  preposition `ἐπί`. The bare accusative of extent is perfectly idiomatic in
  Greek; `ἐπί` + accusative (Luke) or the bare accusative (James) both
  answer "for how long?"
- **Significance:** James uses `ἐνιαυτός` (G1763, "year" — often the cyclic
  agricultural year) rather than Luke's `ἔτος` (G2094, generic "year"). The
  two terms are largely synonymous in NT usage, and the choice here may
  reflect James' Jewish-agrarian register ("year" as a farming cycle —
  apt for a drought-famine context). **Two independent NT authors, using
  different synonyms for "year," both give the identical figure 3 + 6/12**.
  This is converging historical testimony, not duplicated apocalyptic
  vocabulary.

**2. προσευχῇ προσηύξατο — "he prayed with a prayer" (cognate dative)**
- **Form:** Dative singular feminine noun `προσευχῇ` + aorist middle
  indicative of the cognate verb. The dative is the **Hebraistic cognate
  dative** (= MT infinitive absolute intensifier).
- **Significance:** Reflects Hebrew `הִתְפַּלֵּל תְּפִלָּה` — "he prayed a
  prayer" — the LXX's standard rendering of the infinitive-absolute
  intensifier. James is writing NT Greek with Hebraic coloring; he reports
  Elijah's prayer *earnestly*. The focus is on **prayer → drought**
  cause-and-effect, which is James' whole point: Elijah was a man like us,
  and his prayer moved heaven for 3.5 years. That rhetorical argument
  **requires the 3.5 to be real time**. If it were 1260 symbolic prophetic
  days equalling 1260 literal years, James' point ("pray like Elijah because
  your prayer can also move heaven") would collapse — no one's prayer
  produces a 1260-year drought.
- **Grammar reference:** [infinitive-absolute-intensification.md](../hebrew/infinitive-absolute-intensification.md)
  (LXX/NT calque of the same construction).

**3. τοῦ μὴ βρέξαι — articular genitive infinitive of purpose/content**
- **Form:** Article `τοῦ` (genitive) + aorist active infinitive `βρέξαι` of
  `βρέχω` "to rain" + negative `μή`. Functions as the content of Elijah's
  prayer: "that it not rain."
- **Significance:** The genitive articular infinitive regularly expresses
  purpose or content after verbs of praying, commanding, beseeching. The
  aorist aspect is ingressive/effective: "not to begin raining" — i.e., to
  hold back rainfall from the point of prayer forward.

**4. οὐκ ἔβρεξεν — aorist active indicative ("it rained not")**
- **Form:** Negative particle + aorist active indicative 3s of `βρέχω`.
- **Significance:** The aorist summarizes the entire 3.5-year rain-absence
  as a single completed state-of-affairs. Paired with the *accusative of
  extent* (`ἐνιαυτοὺς τρεῖς καὶ μῆνας ἕξ`), the construction is standard
  Greek prose historiography: perfective aspect + quantified duration =
  "did not X, for the space of Y." No apocalyptic signaling.

## Classification: Literal 3.5 Years, Typologically Echoed by Apocalyptic 3.5

### Why it is LITERAL, not day-for-year

| Criterion | Apocalyptic 3.5 (Dan 7:25; 12:7; Rev 11:2-3; 12:6,14; 13:5) | Elijah's 3.5 (1 Kgs 17-18; Luke 4; Jas 5) |
|-----------|--------------|--------------|
| Genre | Apocalyptic vision (sealed book, seraphim, beasts, angelic interpreter) | Prose narrative (Kings); historical reference (Gospels/Epistles) |
| Time vocabulary | עִדָּן / ἡμέρα 1260 / μῆνας 42 / καιρός-καιροί-ἥμισυ | שָׁנִים "years," יָמִים רַבִּים "many days," שָׁנָה שְׁלִישִׁית "third year," ἔτη 3 / ἐνιαυτοὺς 3 + μῆνας 6 |
| Arithmetical resolution | Same number expressed three equivalent ways (1260d = 42m = 3.5 yr) | Two distinct time-units **added** (3 yr + 6 mo) |
| Seal-command / vision-frame | Dan 8:26; 12:4,9; Rev 10:4 | Absent |
| Day-for-year formula context | Num 14:34; Ezk 4:4-6 typologically available | Absent — `יָמִים` here means ordinary days |
| Termination condition | Fixed expiry of symbolic period | `לְפִי דְבָרִי` — Elijah's own word; then YHWH's word in 18:1 |
| NT treatment | Revelation re-uses the symbolic numbers as still-future/present sealed time | Jesus and James cite it as historical fact using asseveration formulae |

### Why the TYPOLOGY runs Elijah → Daniel/Rev, not the reverse

**Narrative priority.** Elijah precedes Daniel chronologically (9th c. BC vs.
6th c. BC). Hebrew narrative is not in the business of symbolically encoding
later apocalyptic numbers. Daniel (and later Revelation), by contrast, *is* in
the business of drawing on earlier Israelite history for prophetic patterns
(cf. Dan 9's reuse of Jer 25/29's seventy-year figure).

**Rev 11:6 confirms the direction.** The two witnesses "have power to shut up
heaven, that it rain not in the days of their prophecy" (ἔχουσιν τὴν ἐξουσίαν
κλεῖσαι τὸν οὐρανόν) — this is **explicit Elijah imitation**, with the same
verb `κλείω` that Luke 4:25 used. Revelation invokes Elijah's historical
drought as the *pattern* behind the 1260-day prophetic figure, not as its
*instance*.

**Luke 4:25 and Jas 5:17 would not quote a prophetic-time cipher as a historical
anecdote.** When Jesus preaches Nazareth or James exhorts prayer, they need a
*real thing that really happened* for the rhetorical argument to function.
Substituting a 1260-year day-for-year duration destroys both speakers' point.

### Typological link to the Daniel/Rev 3.5

The typology runs something like this:
- **Historical Elijah (1 Kgs):** Prophet withdraws YHWH's covenant blessing
  (rain) from an apostate Israel under an idolatrous king; duration of judgment
  is 3.5 solar years.
- **Apocalyptic echo (Dan 7; 12; Rev 11; 12; 13):** The 3.5 unit becomes a
  **symbolic duration** — "a time, times, and half a time" — for the period
  during which the saints are handed over to the oppressor, the witnesses
  prophesy in sackcloth, the woman is nourished in the wilderness, and the
  beast blasphemes. The apocalyptic 3.5 is the Elijah-pattern **abstracted**
  and (per the day-for-year formula — cf. Num 14:34, Ezk 4:4-6) **rescaled
  into 1260 prophetic days = 1260 literal years**.

The apocalyptic 3.5 depends on and typologically reuses the historical 3.5 —
**but the historical 3.5 is not itself symbolic and therefore not subject to
day-for-year rescaling.** Elijah's drought was 3.5 literal years. The Daniel
3.5 is 1260 literal years. Both are true; neither collapses into the other.
