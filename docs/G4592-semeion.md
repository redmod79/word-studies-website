## Lexicon Definition

Neuter of a presumed derivative of the base of σημαίνω; *an indication*, especially ceremonially or supernaturally — a sign, miracle, token, wonder. A σημεῖον is not merely a wonder in itself but a *pointer*: something observable that signifies a deeper meaning, identity, or authority behind it. The word denotes the meaning-bearing character of a display, in contrast to its sheer power (δύναμις) or its capacity to astonish (τέρας).

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Authenticating Signs of Jesus the Messiah (Johannine "Signs")
In John's Gospel, σημεῖον designates Jesus' miracles as revelatory pointers to his identity and glory, eliciting (or failing to elicit) belief.
- **John 2:11** — "This beginning of miracles (sēmeiōn) did Jesus in Cana of Galilee, and manifested forth his glory; and his disciples believed on him"
- **John 6:14** — "when they had seen the miracle (sēmeion) that Jesus did, said, This is of a truth that prophet"
- **John 12:37** — "though he had done so many miracles (sēmeia) before them, yet they believed not"
- **John 20:30** — "many other signs (sēmeia) truly did Jesus in the presence of his disciples"

### 2. Demanded Sign (Hostile / Testing Context)
A σημεῖον sought by adversaries as proof; Jesus typically refuses or points only to "the sign of Jonah."
- **Mat 12:38-39** — "Master, we would see a sign (sēmeion) from thee… An evil and adulterous generation seeketh after a sign"
- **Mat 16:1, 4** — Pharisees and Sadducees "tempting desired him that he would shew them a sign from heaven"
- **Mark 8:11-12** — "seeking of him a sign from heaven, tempting him… Why doth this generation seek after a sign?"
- **1 Cor 1:22** — "For the Jews require a sign (sēmeion), and the Greeks seek after wisdom"

### 3. Eschatological Sign (The Sign of the End / of the Son of Man)
A σημεῖον that marks or heralds a future event — particularly the coming of the Son of Man and the consummation.
- **Mat 24:3** — "what shall be the sign (sēmeion) of thy coming, and of the end of the world?"
- **Mat 24:30** — "then shall appear the sign (sēmeion) of the Son of man in heaven"
- **Luke 21:11, 25** — "great signs (sēmeia) shall there be from heaven… signs in the sun, and in the moon, and in the stars"
- **Mark 13:4** — "what shall be the sign (sēmeion) when all these things shall be fulfilled?"

### 4. Apostolic Confirming Signs (Authenticating the Gospel)
Signs wrought by apostles (and associated believers) to bear God's witness to the preached word.
- **Mark 16:17, 20** — "these signs (sēmeia) shall follow them that believe… confirming the word with signs following"
- **Acts 2:43; 4:30; 5:12** — "many wonders and signs were done by the apostles"
- **Acts 14:3** — God "gave testimony unto the word of his grace, and granted signs (sēmeia) and wonders to be done by their hands"
- **Heb 2:4** — "God also bearing them witness, both with signs (sēmeiois) and wonders, and with divers miracles"
- **2 Cor 12:12** — "the signs (sēmeia) of an apostle were wrought among you in all patience, in signs, and wonders, and mighty deeds"

### 5. Deceptive / Lying Signs of False Prophets and the Adversary
Parallel construction: the adversary's agents perform σημεῖα that imitate the authentic, intended to deceive.
- **Mat 24:24 / Mark 13:22** — "there shall arise false Christs, and false prophets, and shall shew great signs (sēmeia) and wonders; insomuch that, if it were possible, they shall deceive the very elect"
- **2 Thess 2:9** — "whose coming is after the working of Satan with all power and signs (sēmeiois) and lying wonders"
- **Rev 13:13-14** — "he doeth great wonders (sēmeia)… And deceiveth them that dwell on the earth by the means of those miracles (sēmeia) which he had power to do"
- **Rev 16:14** — "the spirits of devils, working miracles (sēmeia), which go forth unto the kings of the earth"
- **Rev 19:20** — "the false prophet that wrought miracles (sēmeia) before him, with which he deceived them that had received the mark of the beast"

### 6. Identifying Token / Symbolic Mark
σημεῖον as a designated indicator of identity or covenant — not necessarily miraculous.
- **Mat 26:48** — "gave them a sign (sēmeion), saying, Whomsoever I shall kiss, that same is he"
- **Luke 2:12** — "this shall be a sign (sēmeion) unto you; Ye shall find the babe wrapped in swaddling clothes"
- **Rom 4:11** — "he received the sign (sēmeion) of circumcision, a seal of the righteousness of the faith"
- **2 Thess 3:17** — "the salutation of Paul with mine own hand, which is the token (sēmeion) in every epistle"

### 7. Heavenly / Apocalyptic Sign (Vision Marker)
In Revelation, σημεῖον introduces a symbolic tableau seen in heaven.
- **Rev 12:1** — "there appeared a great wonder (sēmeion) in heaven; a woman clothed with the sun"
- **Rev 12:3** — "there appeared another wonder (sēmeion) in heaven; and behold a great red dragon"
- **Rev 15:1** — "I saw another sign (sēmeion) in heaven, great and marvellous, seven angels"

### LXX / Hebrew Source Overlap (Confusion via Hebrew)
Through LXX, σημεῖον most often renders **H226 אוֹת (ʼôwth)** — the broad Hebrew sign-word (covenant sign, natural sign, divine portent). It also renders **H4159 מוֹפֵת (môwphêth)** — typically rendered τέρας, but sometimes σημεῖον — and less commonly **H5251 נֵס (nēs, "banner/standard")**. The conflation in Greek can flatten distinctions the Hebrew preserved between covenant-marker (ʼôwth) and portent-wonder (môwphêth).

## Structural Observation: The "Signs and Wonders" Formula

σημεῖον and τέρας are bonded in the NT as a fixed idiom (*sēmeia kai terata*, 16× co-occurrence). Often expanded to a triad with δύναμις (power/mighty deed). Distribution of this formula:

- **Authentic / divinely sourced:** Acts 2:19, 22, 43; 4:30; 5:12; 6:8; 7:36; 14:3; 15:12; Rom 15:19; 2 Cor 12:12; Heb 2:4 — authenticates prophet/apostle/Christ
- **Counterfeit / adversarial:** Mat 24:24; Mark 13:22; 2 Thess 2:9 — authenticates false Christ/false prophet

The same vocabulary, the same formula, the same observable phenomenology — distinguished only by source, content of the message, and fruit. The NT nowhere claims that signs are self-authenticating; Deut 13 logic (a sign from a prophet who teaches apostasy is to be rejected) remains operative under the σημεῖον word.
