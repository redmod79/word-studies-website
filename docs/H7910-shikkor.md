## Lexicon Definition

> "Or שִׁכֹּר shikkôr; from [H7937]; intoxicated, as a state or a habit: — drunk(-ard, -en, -en man)."

The adjective denotes both the momentary condition of being intoxicated and the habitual character of a drunkard. English renderings "drunken" (the state) and "drunkard" (the habitual type) both fall within its range.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Literal Intoxication — Narrative Portraits
The word labels real individuals physically incapacitated by wine.
- **1 Samuel 1:13** — "Eli thought she had been drunken" (Hannah mistaken for drunk).
- **1 Samuel 25:36** — "Nabal's heart was merry within him, for he was very drunken."
- **1 Kings 16:9** — "drinking himself drunk in the house of Arza."
- **1 Kings 20:16** — "Benhadad was drinking himself drunk in the pavilions."

### 2. Simile of Reeling / Staggering
The word supplies the vehicle of a simile for disorientation, instability, or dizziness — often divine judgment.
- **Job 12:25** — "he maketh them to stagger like a drunken man."
- **Psalm 107:27** — "They reel to and fro, and stagger like a drunken man."
- **Isaiah 19:14** — "as a drunken man staggereth in his vomit" (Egypt under judgment).
- **Isaiah 24:20** — "The earth shall reel to and fro like a drunkard."
- **Jeremiah 23:9** — "I am like a drunken man... because of the LORD" (prophet's trembling).

### 3. Habitual Drunkard — Typecast Figure
The word names a class of persons characterized by the habit.
- **Proverbs 26:9** — "As a thorn goeth up into the hand of a drunkard, so is a parable in the mouth of fools."
- **Joel 1:5** — "Awake, ye drunkards, and weep" (addressed as a category).

### 4. Corporate Indictment — "Drunkards of Ephraim"
Prophetic usage stigmatizes a whole population as a collective of drunkards, making intoxication the emblem of political pride and coming judgment.
- **Isaiah 28:1** — "Woe to the crown of pride, to the drunkards of Ephraim."
- **Isaiah 28:3** — "The crown of pride, the drunkards of Ephraim, shall be trodden under feet."
