## Semantic Categories

### 1. Righteousness as God's Own Attribute
Tsedaqah as an intrinsic property of Yahweh's character — what he is, not merely what he does.

- **Dan 9:7** — "O Lord, righteousness [belongeth] unto thee, but unto us confusion of faces"
- **Ps 71:16** — "I will go in the strength of the Lord GOD: I will make mention of thy righteousness, even of thine only."
- **Ps 111:3** — "His work [is] honourable and glorious: and his righteousness endureth for ever."
- **Ps 103:17** — "But the mercy of the LORD [is] from everlasting to everlasting upon them that fear him, and his righteousness unto children's children"

### 2. Paired with Mishpat — Governance, Social Order, and Justice
The most frequent collocation (46x). The pair "mishpat and tsedaqah" (or reversed) functions as a hendiadys for comprehensive justice — the judicial verdict and the moral rightness behind it.

- **2 Sam 8:15** — "David executed judgment and justice (mishpat + tsedaqah) unto all his people."
- **Amos 5:24** — "But let judgment run down as waters, and righteousness as a mighty stream."
- **Isa 33:5** — "he hath filled Zion with judgment and righteousness."
- **Ps 72:1,3** — "Give the king thy judgments, O God, and thy righteousness unto the king's son… The mountains shall bring peace to the people, and the little hills, by righteousness."

### 3. Yahweh's Saving Acts of Righteousness (Plural: Righteous Acts)
The plural form (tsidqot/tsidqoth) refers to concrete historical acts of God's deliverance — his interventions on behalf of his people. This usage is particularly common in the historical summaries.

- **Judg 5:11** — "there shall they rehearse the righteous acts of the LORD"
- **1 Sam 12:7** — "I may reason with you before the LORD of all the righteous acts of the LORD"
- **Ps 103:6** — "The LORD executeth righteousness and judgment for all that are oppressed."
- **Mic 6:5** — "[remember] the righteous acts of the LORD" (from Shittim to Gilgal)

### 4. Righteousness as Eschatological Deliverance / Salvation
In Isaiah especially, tsedaqah becomes nearly synonymous with God's salvation — his righteousness *is* his saving intervention, arriving in the future like rain or sunrise.

- **Isa 45:8** — "let the skies pour down righteousness: let the earth open, and let them bring forth salvation, and let righteousness spring up together"
- **Isa 46:13** — "I bring near my righteousness; it shall not be far off, and my salvation shall not tarry"
- **Isa 51:6** — "for the heavens shall vanish away… but my salvation shall be for ever, and my righteousness shall not be abolished."
- **Ps 98:2** — "his righteousness hath he openly shewed in the sight of the heathen."

### 5. Human Moral Conduct and Obedience
Tsedaqah as right-living before God — the practical righteousness of covenant faithfulness, including obedience to commandments.

- **Deut 6:25** — "And it shall be our righteousness, if we observe to do all these commandments before the LORD our God"
- **Job 27:6** — "My righteousness I hold fast, and will not let it go"
- **Prov 14:34** — "Righteousness exalteth a nation: but sin [is] a reproach to any people."
- **Ezek 18:22** — "All his transgressions that he hath committed, they shall not be mentioned unto him: in his righteousness that he hath done he shall live."

### 6. Messianic / Eschatological Righteousness
A righteous reign and branch from David's line, where tsedaqah characterizes the future Messianic kingdom.

- **Jer 23:5** — "I will raise unto David a righteous Branch, and a King shall reign and prosper, and shall execute judgment and righteousness in the land."
- **Jer 33:15** — "I will cause the Branch of righteousness to grow up unto David; and he shall execute judgment and righteousness in the land."
- **Isa 9:7** — "Of the increase of [his] government and peace [there shall be] no end, upon the throne of David… to establish it with judgment and with justice"
- **Mal 4:2** — "But unto you that fear my name shall the Sun of righteousness arise with healing in his wings"

> **Eleemosyne note:** The LXX translation of tsedaqah as ἐλεημοσύνη (almsgiving) in ~7% of cases reflects the concrete, act-based dimension of tsedaqah — the "righteous deed" manifesting as charity. This translation history is the source of the Jewish use of *tzedakah* to mean "almsgiving" in rabbinic literature.
