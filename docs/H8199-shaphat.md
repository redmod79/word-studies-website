## Lexicon Definition

A primitive root; to judge, i.e. pronounce sentence (for or against); by implication, to vindicate or punish; by extension, to govern; passively, to litigate (literally or figuratively): — avenge, that condemn, contend, defend, execute (judgment), (be a) judge(-ment), needs, plead, reason, rule.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Pronouncing Judicial Sentence (Legal / Civil)
The core meaning: hearing cases and rendering verdicts, including administrative governance.

- **Exo 18:13** — "Moses sat to judge the people: and the people stood by Moses from the morning unto the evening"
- **Deut 1:16** — "Hear [the causes] between your brethren, and judge righteously between every man and his brother"
- **1 Kgs 3:9** — "Give therefore thy servant an understanding heart to judge thy people, that I may discern between good and bad"
- **Prov 29:14** — "The king that faithfully judgeth the poor, his throne shall be established for ever"

### 2. God as the Universal Judge
God (Yahweh or Elohim) invoked as the ultimate source of justice, vindicating the righteous and punishing the wicked.

- **Gen 18:25** — "Shall not the Judge of all the earth do right?"
- **Ps 7:8** — "The LORD shall judge the people: judge me, O LORD, according to my righteousness"
- **Ps 7:11** — "God judgeth the righteous, and God is angry [with the wicked] every day"
- **Ps 9:8** — "He shall judge the world in righteousness, he shall minister judgment to the people in uprightness"
- **Ps 58:11** — "Verily he is a God that judgeth in the earth"

### 3. Civil Rulers — The "Judges" of Israel
The Qal active participle functions as a title (שֹׁפֵט *shophet*) for the deliverer-rulers of the pre-monarchic period; also used for any magistrate or official.

- **Judg 2:16** — "The LORD raised up judges, which delivered them out of the hand of those that spoiled them"
- **Judg 2:18** — "When the LORD raised them up judges, then the LORD was with the judge, and delivered them"
- **1 Sam 4:18** — Eli "judged Israel forty years"
- **Dan 9:12** — "our judges that judged us"

### 4. Pleading / Defending a Cause (Litigate)
Shaphat in the context of presenting or adjudicating a legal grievance; can be translated "plead" when one party brings a case.

- **Gen 16:5** — Sarai: "The LORD judge between me and thee"
- **Isa 1:17** — "judge the fatherless, plead for the widow"
- **Lam 3:59** — "O LORD, thou hast seen my wrong: judge thou my cause"
- **Joel 3:2** — "I will plead with them there for my people and for my heritage Israel"

### 5. Divine Judgment on Israel / Nations
God pronouncing sentence of punishment against His own people or against foreign nations; prophetic judgment oracles.

- **Ezek 7:3** — "I will judge thee according to thy ways, and will recompense thee for all thine abominations"
- **1 Sam 3:13** — "I will judge his house for ever for the iniquity which he knoweth"
- **Jer 11:20** — "O LORD of hosts, that judgest righteously, that triest the reins and the heart"

### 6. Eschatological / Universal Judgment
Future judgment of all nations, associated with the end-time coming of the LORD or the Messiah.

- **1 Chr 16:33** — "he cometh to judge the earth"
- **Ps 82:8** — "Arise, O God, judge the earth: for thou shalt inherit all nations"
- **Micah 4:3** — "He shall judge among many people, and rebuke strong nations afar off"
- **Joel 3:12** — "there will I sit to judge all the heathen round about"
- **Obad 1:21** — "saviours shall come up on mount Zion to judge the mount of Esau; and the kingdom shall be the LORD'S"
