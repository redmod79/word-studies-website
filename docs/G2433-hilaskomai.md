## Lexicon Definition

ἱλάσκομαι (*hil-as'-kom-ahee*); middle voice from the same root as ἵλεως (G2436); to conciliate, i.e., (transitively) to make atonement for, expiate; (intransitively/reflexively) to be propitious, show mercy. The middle voice indicates the subject's personal involvement in the action — either the sinner seeking mercy or the priest performing the atoning work.

LXX usage is far more extensive than the NT: the verb renders H5545 סָלַח (salach) "forgive" 6 times and H3722 כָּפַר (kaphar) "make atonement" 4 times, plus occurrences in Exo 32:14, 2 Ki 5:18, Psa 25:11, 78:38, 79:9, Pro 16:14.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Sinner's Plea for Divine Mercy
- **Luke 18:13** — "God be merciful (ἱλάσθητί) to me a sinner."

The publican in Jesus' parable uses hilaskomai as a direct petition to God. The aorist passive imperative (ἱλάσθητί) functions as "be propitiated toward me" — the sinner does not propitiate God but asks God to be propitious. The request is set against the physical posture of contrition: standing far off, eyes downcast, breast-beating. Jesus declares this man "justified" (δεδικαιωμένος) rather than the self-righteous Pharisee.

### 2. Priestly Atonement for the People's Sins
- **Heb 2:17** — "that he might be a merciful and faithful high priest in things pertaining to God, to make reconciliation for (ἱλάσκεσθαι) the sins of the people."

The author of Hebrews uses hilaskomai to describe Christ's high-priestly function. The present passive infinitive (ἱλάσκεσθαι) with the accusative τὰς ἁμαρτίας expresses the purpose of the incarnation: Christ became human so he could expiate the people's sins. The verse pairs the word with "merciful" (ἐλεήμων) and "faithful" (πιστός), framing propitiation as both a compassionate and a reliable priestly act.

## Related Words

### Word Family (hilask- root)
- **G2434 ἱλασμός (hilasmos)** — noun: the propitiation, the atoning act/provision (2x: 1 John 2:2; 4:10). The -μός suffix makes this the abstract result noun: Christ *is* the hilasmos.
- **G2435 ἱλαστήριον (hilasterion)** — noun: the propitiatory place/object; mercy seat (2x: Rom 3:25; Heb 9:5). The -τήριον suffix denotes the place or instrument of the action.
- **G2436 ἵλεως (hileos)** — adjective: propitious, gracious, merciful (2x: Matt 16:22; Heb 8:12). The adjectival form describing God's disposition after propitiation.

Hilaskomai is the process verb from which the entire family derives: the act of propitiating (hilaskomai) produces the propitiation-event (hilasmos) at the propitiation-place (hilasterion) resulting in a propitious God (hileos).
