## Lexicon Definition

A flabby thing, i.e. a carcass or carrion (human or bestial, often collectively); figuratively, an idol. KJV: (dead) body, (dead) carcase, dead of itself, which died, (beast) that (which) dieth of itself.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Carcass of Unclean Animals (purity/dietary context)
The most common usage — dead bodies of ritually unclean creatures whose contact produces defilement.
- **Lev 5:2** — "a carcase of an unclean beast, or a carcase of unclean cattle, or the carcase of unclean creeping things"
- **Lev 11:8** — "Of their flesh shall ye not eat, and their carcase shall ye not touch; they are unclean to you"
- **Lev 11:24-28** — touching the carcass produces uncleanness until evening; bearing it requires washing clothes
- **Deut 14:8** — "ye shall not eat of their flesh, nor touch their dead carcase"

### 2. Carcass of Clean Animals That Die Naturally
A clean animal that dies of itself (rather than being slaughtered) also becomes nebelah — the issue is the manner of death and blood retention, not species.
- **Lev 11:39** — "if any beast, of which ye may eat, die; he that toucheth the carcase thereof shall be unclean until the even"
- **Lev 11:40** — "he that eateth of the carcase of it shall wash his clothes, and be unclean until the even"
- **Lev 7:24** — "the fat of the beast that dieth of itself... may be used in any other use: but ye shall in no wise eat of it"
- **Deut 14:21** — "Ye shall not eat of any thing that dieth of itself: thou shalt give it unto the stranger"

### 3. Human Corpse (narrative and prophetic)
Used for human dead bodies, especially in contexts of judgment, disgrace, or unburied remains.
- **1 Ki 13:24-30** — the disobedient prophet's carcase (6 occurrences in one narrative)
- **2 Ki 9:37** — "the carcase of Jezebel shall be as dung upon the face of the field"
- **Jer 7:33** — "the carcases of this people shall be meat for the fowls of the heaven"
- **Deut 21:23** — "His body shall not remain all night upon the tree... he that is hanged is accursed of God"
- **Deut 28:26** — "thy carcase shall be meat unto all fowls of the air" (covenant curse)

### 4. Prophetic/Figurative — Judgment and Resurrection
- **Isa 5:25** — "their carcases were torn in the midst of the streets" (divine judgment)
- **Isa 26:19** — "Thy dead men shall live, together with my dead body shall they arise" (resurrection prophecy)
- **Jer 16:4,18; 19:7; 34:20; 36:30** — unburied carcasses as marks of God's judgment

### 5. Priestly Prohibition
Priests face stricter standards regarding nebelah.
- **Lev 22:8** — "That which dieth of itself, or is torn with beasts, he shall not eat to defile himself therewith: I am the LORD"
- **Ezek 44:31** — "The priests shall not eat of any thing that is dead of itself, or torn, whether it be fowl or beast"
