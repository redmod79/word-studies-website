## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same across testaments:**
- The kathar- word group carries the full semantic range of taher: ritual, moral, and spiritual cleansing
- Blood remains the primary instrument of deep cleansing (Lev 16 → Heb 9)
- God remains the ultimate agent of spiritual cleansing (Eze 36:25 → 1 John 1:9)
- The priestly declaration pattern persists (Lev 13 → Jesus in Matt 8)

**What shifts:**
- **From ritual to moral/spiritual:** NT usage heavily favors spiritual cleansing over ceremonial; katharizō in the Gospels addresses leprosy (physical) but in the Epistles addresses sin/conscience
- **From priest-mediated to direct:** Jesus himself declares "be thou clean" without the Levitical inspection process
- **From external to internal:** Acts 15:9 — "purifying their hearts by faith" internalizes what was formerly external ritual
- **From repeated to once-for-all:** Heb 10:2 — "the worshippers once purged should have had no more conscience of sins" contrasts with Levitical repetition
