## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same across testaments:**
- The core meaning is stable: treating the sacred as common, crossing the holy/profane boundary without authorization. From Leviticus through Ezekiel through the Pastorals, the concept of profanation is consistent.
- The sabbath remains a prime object of profanation language in both testaments (Eze 20 → Matt 12:5).
- Temple/sanctuary profanation runs from Eze 44:7 straight through Acts 24:6.

**What shifts:**
- Frequency drops dramatically: 61 LXX uses → 2 NT verb uses. The concept narrows in the NT.
- The adjective bebelos (G952) takes over in the Pastorals, applied to *speech* and *persons* rather than cultic objects — "profane babblings," "profane person." The concept shifts from ritual desecration to moral/intellectual trivialization.
- Jesus's Matt 12:5 argument actually *subverts* profanation language: the priests "profane" the sabbath and are guiltless, because something greater than the temple is present. The sacral boundary itself is being redrawn.
