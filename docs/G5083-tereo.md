## Lexicon Definition

From τερός *teros* (a watch; perhaps akin to G2334); to guard (from loss or injury, properly by keeping the eye upon; and thus differing from G5442, which is properly to prevent escaping; and from G2892, which implies a fortress or full military lines of apparatus), i.e. to note (a prophecy; figuratively to fulfil a command); by implication to detain (in custody; figuratively to maintain); by extension to withhold (for personal ends; figuratively to keep unmarried).

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Keeping Commandments, Words, and Law (Obedient Observance)
The dominant usage — faithful, active compliance with a divine charge.
- **Jhn 14:15** — "If ye love me, keep my commandments."
- **1Jo 2:3** — "Hereby we do know that we know him, if we keep his commandments."
- **Rev 14:12** — "Here are they that keep the commandments of God, and the faith of Jesus."
- **Rev 12:17** — "the remnant of her seed, which keep the commandments of God, and have the testimony of Jesus Christ."

### 2. Physical Custody — Guarding a Person or Place
Concrete, literal guarding by soldiers or authorities.
- **Acts 12:5** — "Peter therefore was kept in prison."
- **Acts 16:23** — "charging the jailor to keep them safely."
- **Mat 27:36** — "sitting down they watched him there" (guard at the cross).
- **Mat 28:4** — "the keepers did shake, and became as dead men" (tomb guards).

### 3. Reserving for Future Judgment or Reward
Things (or persons) held in storage for a future appointed moment.
- **1Pe 1:4** — "an inheritance... reserved in heaven for you."
- **2Pe 2:4** — "delivered them into chains... to be reserved unto judgment."
- **2Pe 3:7** — "the heavens and the earth... kept in store, reserved unto fire."
- **2Pe 2:9** — "to reserve the unjust unto the day of judgment."

### 4. Protective Keeping — Shielding from Harm
Jesus or the Father actively watching over believers to preserve them.
- **Jhn 17:11** — "Holy Father, keep through thine own name those whom thou hast given me."
- **Jhn 17:15** — "that thou shouldest keep them from the evil."
- **Rev 3:10** — "I also will keep thee from the hour of temptation."
- **1Th 5:23** — "your whole spirit and soul and body be preserved blameless."

### 5. Maintaining / Preserving a Condition or Quality
Holding something intact over time — a state, a virtue, a relationship.
- **Jhn 2:10** — "thou hast kept the good wine until now."
- **2Ti 4:7** — "I have fought a good fight... I have kept the faith."
- **Eph 4:3** — "Endeavouring to keep the unity of the Spirit in the bond of peace."
- **1Co 7:37** — "hath power over his own will... to keep his virgin."
