## Lexicon Definition

Strong's: "a primitive root; to pour out, especially a libation, or to cast (metal); by analogy, to anoint a king:—cover, melt, offer, (cause to) pour (out), set (up)."

The semantic core is **pouring-out a liquid at a cultic site** (the libation act). Secondary senses radiate from the physical action of pouring: (a) pouring molten metal → casting an image; (b) pouring oil → anointing/installing a king; (c) in Isa 25:7 a by-form meaning "weave/spread over," possibly a homonymous root. The dominant KJV renderings ("pour out," "pour," "cover [with a molten image]," "melt," "offer [a drink offering]," "set up") all trace to the single mental picture of a liquid or molten substance being emptied onto or into a receiving surface.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Prescribed Cultic Libation to YHWH (Pouring-Out a Drink Offering)
The archetypal sense: pouring a measured liquid (wine or *shēkār*/strong drink) at/onto the altar as part of the sacrificial rite.
- **Gen 35:14** — "Jacob... poured (nāsak) a drink offering thereon, and he poured oil thereon" (Bethel pillar).
- **Num 28:7** — "in the holy [place] shalt thou cause the strong wine to be poured (Hiphil *hassēk*) unto the LORD [for] a drink offering (nesek)."
- **2 Sam 23:16** — David "would not drink thereof, but poured it out (nāsak) unto the LORD" — explicit contrast between drinking and pouring-out as libation.
- **2 Ki 16:13** — Ahaz "poured his drink offering" with burnt, meal, and blood rites.

### 2. Illicit / Foreign-Deity Libation
The same physical act directed to another god — condemned in prophetic polemic.
- **Jer 44:17** — "to burn incense unto the queen of heaven, and to pour out (nāsak) drink offerings unto her."
- **Jer 44:18, 19, 25** — women defend their practice of pouring libations to the queen of heaven.
- **Jer 7:18; 19:13; 32:29** — libations poured on rooftops to Baal / the host of heaven.
- **Hos 9:4** — "They shall not offer (nāsak) wine [offerings] to the LORD."
- **Eze 20:28** — on every high hill and under every green tree "poured out (nāsak) there their drink offerings."

### 3. Casting / Molding a Metal Image
Pouring molten metal into a mold — the physical action extended to metallurgy.
- **Isa 40:19** — "The workman melteth (nāsak) a graven image, and the goldsmith spreadeth it over with gold."
- **Isa 44:10** — "Who hath formed a god, or molten (nāsak) a graven image [that] is profitable for nothing?"
- **Isa 30:1** — "that cover (nāsak) with a covering, but not of my spirit" — idiomatic for making a "molten" alliance/treaty overlay.

### 4. Installation by Pouring (Anointing / Setting-Up)
Pouring oil → installing a king; by extension, divinely "setting up/appointing."
- **Ps 2:6** — "Yet have I set (nāsak) my king upon my holy hill of Zion" — royal installation language.
- **Prov 8:23** — "I was set up (Niphal *nissaktî*) from everlasting, from the beginning" — Wisdom's primordial installation.

### 5. Pouring Out as Divine Judgment (Metaphorical)
The libation action metaphorically inverted — God pours out a noxious substance upon the people.
- **Isa 29:10** — "the LORD hath poured out (Piel) upon you the spirit of deep sleep" — punitive pouring.

### 6. Hophal Passive: Liquid Poured From Vessel
The tabernacle table's libation vessels are described by what is poured from them.
- **Exo 25:29; 37:16** — "covers... and bowls thereof, to cover withal [whereby libations] are poured out (Hophal)."
- **Exo 30:9** — prohibition: "neither shall ye pour (Qal) drink offering thereon" (on the incense altar).

### 7. Possibly Separate Root: Weave / Spread Over (Isa 25:7)
- **Isa 25:7** — "the face of the covering cast (Qal Ptcp *n'sûkāh*) over all people" — likely a by-form homonym meaning "woven/spread," unrelated to libation.

**Critical distinction for Num 28:7 interpretation:** nāsak is morphologically and distributively incompatible with a "drinking" sense. The verbs of consumption are שָׁתָה šātāh (H8354, "drink"), אָכַל ʼākal (H398, "eat"), or לָקַק lāqaq (H3952, "lap"). No occurrence of nāsak in the OT describes a liquid being ingested; all 24 verses describe liquid being expelled from a vessel onto a receiving surface, object, or deity.
