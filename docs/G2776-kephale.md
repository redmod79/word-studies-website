## Lexicon Definition

From the primary κάπτω (kaptō, "to seize"); the head (as the part most readily taken hold of). Used literally of the physical body part (human or animal/beast) and figuratively of that which is chief, foremost, preeminent, or supreme — whether a stone ("head of the corner"), a person in authority over another (husband, Christ), or topographical summits (the "seven heads are seven mountains"). In apocalyptic literature, heads on symbolic beasts function as carriers of crowns and as enumerated features of the creature.
