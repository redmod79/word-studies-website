## Lexicon Definition

From ἐν and λόγος (in the sense of account); to reckon in, set to one's account, lay to one's charge, impute. Thayer: "τοῦτο ἐμοὶ ἐλλόγει (ἐλλόγα), charge this to my account, Philemon 1:18; sin the penalty of which is under consideration, Romans 5:13." Attested in inscriptions (Boeckh) and the Edict of Diocletian; variant form ἐλλογάω equivalent to ἐλλογέω.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Theological Imputation — Sin Not Charged
- **Rom 5:13** — "For until the law sin was in the world: but sin is not **imputed** when there is no law."

Paul uses ellogeō in the passive to describe the non-reckoning of sin to a person's account when law is absent. The verb frames sin as a bookkeeping entry that requires a legal standard (nomos) to be formally registered.

### 2. Commercial Account — Debt Charged to a Person
- **Phm 1:18** — "If he hath wronged thee, or oweth thee ought, **put that on mine account**."

Paul uses ellogeō in the active imperative, asking Philemon to transfer Onesimus's debt to Paul's ledger. This is explicit accounting language: crediting one person's liability to another's account.
