## Relationships

### Shared Root
All members derive from the root κρίνω (krinō, "to separate, distinguish, decide"):
- κρίσις (-sis suffix) → the process/act
- κρίμα (-ma suffix) → the product/result
- κριτής (-tēs suffix) → the agent
- κριτήριον (-tērion suffix) → the instrument/place
- κατα-κρίνω (kata- prefix) → intensified ("judge against" → condemn)
- κατά-κριμα → intensified result

## Roles in the Concept

- **Action:** κρίνω (G2919) — the act of judging, deciding, distinguishing
- **Process/Standard:** κρίσις (G2920) — the judicial process, the standard of judgment, the event
- **Result:** κρίμα (G2917) — the verdict, sentence, decree that results from judging
- **Agent:** κριτής (G2923) — the judge who renders the verdict
- **Place:** κριτήριον (G2922) — the court or tribunal
- **Intensified Action:** κατακρίνω (G2632) — to condemn ("judge against")
- **Intensified Result:** κατάκριμα (G2631) — condemnation (the sentence of condemnation)

## Key Passages Where Multiple Group Words Appear Together

- **Rev 19:2** — κρίσις + κρίνω: "true and righteous are his judgments (kriseis): for he hath judged (ekrinen) the great whore"
- **Mat 12:41–42** — κρίσις + κατακρίνω: "rise in judgment (krisei)... and shall condemn (katakrinousin) it"
- **John 5:22, 24, 27, 29, 30** — κρίσις used five times in a single discourse on divine judicial authority
- **Mat 7:1–2** — κρίνω + κρίμα: "Judge (krinete) not... for with what judgment (krimati) ye judge (krinete)"
