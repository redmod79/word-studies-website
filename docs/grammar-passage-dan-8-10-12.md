# Grammar Analysis: Daniel 8:10–12

The little horn's vertical assault on heaven. Three verses densely layered with stem alternation — Qal wayyiqtol narrative foregrounding the horn's ascent (v.10), Hiphil/Hophal active-passive voice-pair at the hinge of the reflexive-intensive *higdîl* "he magnified himself" → *hûram* / *hušlaḵ* "was taken away / was cast down" (v.11), and a Niphal divine passive *tinnāṯēn* "was given over" flanked by Hiphil *tašlēḵ* active casting down of truth (v.12). Two parser-verified construct chains anchor the vertical axis — **צְבָא הַשָּׁמָיִם** "host of the heavens" (v.10) and **מְכוֹן מִקְדָּשׁוֹ** "place of HIS sanctuary" (v.11, 3ms pronominal suffix on the absolute member). The 3ms suffix on *miqdāš* is syntactically bound to the nearest eligible 3ms antecedent **שַׂר־הַצָּבָא** "Prince of the host" — a heavenly figure — so "his sanctuary" is grammatically *the Prince's* sanctuary, not the horn's. The passive cluster *hûram* (Hophal) / *hušlaḵ* (Hophal) / *tinnāṯēn* (Niphal) encodes agentless or divine-agency action in the exact slots where an active verb would name the agent; the one unambiguously active verb with the horn as subject in the triad is v.11's *higdîl* (Hiphil perfect of גדל "he magnified [himself]" — internal-causative/reflexive force).

## Clause Structure

| 2 | Way0 | וַתַּפֵּל אַרְצָה מִן־הַצָּבָא וּמִן־הַכּוֹכָבִים | [Conj] + [Pred] *tappēl* + [Cmpl] *ʾarṣāh* + [Objc] *min-haṣ-ṣāḇāʾ û-min-hak-kôḵāḇîm* |

All three predicates are **3fs wayyiqtol** with continuous subject-chaining from the f.s. referent in v.9 (*qeren* "horn" is grammatically f.s.). Clause 3 is a bare Verb+Objc (3mp suffix *-ēm* = "them"), narratologically climactic — minimal surface for maximal violence.

|--------|------|------|------------------|

| 3 | WQtX | וְהֻשְׁלַךְ מְכוֹן מִקְדָּשׁוֹ | [Conj] + [Pred] *hušlaḵ* + [Subj] *məḵôn miqdāšô* |

The parser codes these as **WxQ** / **WQt** (waw + X + qatal, waw + qatal) — **not** wayyiqtol — which is the Biblical Hebrew signature for **backgrounding / circumstantial / focus-fronted** material breaking out of the foregrounded wayyiqtol narrative of v.10. The result is a discourse shift: v.10 narrates the horn's action on the host; v.11 shifts register to highlight the new target (**ʿaḏ-śar-haṣ-ṣāḇāʾ** fronted) and the resulting passive events (**ʾûram ʾe* ha-tāmîḏ / *hušlaḵ məḵôn miqdāšô*). Cross-link [syntax-wx-qatal-circumstantial](../hebrew/syntax-wx-qatal-circumstantial.md).

The parser tags **Domain D** (discursive — non-narrative, deliberative/prospective/gnomic register), distinct from the narrative Domain N of vv.10–11. The final two clauses *wə-ʿāśəṯāh wə-hiṣlîḥāh* (weqatal pair) echo the stereotyped Danielic success-formula of v.24 (*wə-hiṣlîaḥ wə-ʿāśâ* in inverted order — cross-link [dan-8-23-25](dan-8-23-25.md)).

## Construct Chains (parser-verified)

| Verse | Construct Chain | Translation | Note |
|-------|-----------------|-------------|------|
| 8:10 | צְבָא הַשָּׁמָיִם | "host of the heavens" | **locates the host in heaven**, not on earth |
| 8:11 | שַׂר הַצָּבָא | "prince of the host" | heavenly ruler-figure over the host of 8:10 |
| 8:11 | מְכוֹן מִקְדָּשׁוֹ | "place of his-sanctuary" | two-member chain; 3ms suffix attaches to absolute *miqdāš*, scopes over the whole chain — "**the place of HIS sanctuary**" |

The three construct chains form a vertical ladder: host → prince-over-host → sanctuary-of-prince. Each step is **anchored in heaven** by the first chain (*ṣəḇāʾ haš-šāmayim*) and by the 3ms suffix on the third chain pointing back to the heavenly *śar*. No earthly-sanctuary referent is grammatically available in the passage; **the text supplies only the heavenly antecedent**.

## Key Grammatical Features

### 1. וַתִּגְדַּל עַד — Qal wayyiqtol of גדל + עַד "grew up to / as high as" (v.10, 11)

- **Forms:** v.10 *tigdal* (Qal.Wayq.3fs of גדל H1431, "grew great"); v.11 *higdîl* (Hiphil.Perf.3ms of גדל, "he magnified [himself]").
- **Stem alternation:** Qal wayq (basic, intransitive — "grew") → Hiphil perfect (internal-causative / reflexive-intensive — "**magnified himself**"). The Hiphil of גדל without an overt object is the classic **self-exaltation** register (cf. Isa 10:15 *hityaḡḡēl* / *yiṯnaśśēʾ* of the Assyrian axe; Psa 35:26; 38:17; 55:13 Hiphil *higdîl* of enemy speech against the psalmist). The Hiphil voice silently supplies the reflexive direct object.
- **Preposition עַד "up-to/as-high-as":** spatial vertical-reach marker; occurs 3× in the triad — (a) v.10 *ʿaḏ-ṣəḇāʾ haš-šāmayim* (reaches **the host**), (b) v.11 *ʿaḏ-śar-haṣ-ṣāḇāʾ* (reaches **the Prince of the host**). The preposition climbs the ladder: host → Prince. The horn's self-magnification culminates not at a general altitude but at the specific heavenly figure named in the construct chain.
- **Significance:** grammar distinguishes **growth** (Qal, v.10) from **self-aggrandizement** (Hiphil, v.11). Cross-links: [stem-hiphil](../hebrew/stem-hiphil.md) (causative/internal-causative H-stem), [hiphil-imperfect](../hebrew/hiphil-imperfect.md), [word study H1431-gadal](../../word-studies/H1431-gadal.md), translation-pair [TR-gadal-megaluno](../../word-studies/TR-gadal-megaluno.md).

### 2. וַתַּפֵּל / וַתִּרְמְסֵם — Hiphil wayq of נפל + Qal wayq of רמס with 3mp object-suffix (v.10)

- **Forms:** *tappēl* (Verb.Hiphil.Wayq.3fs of נפל H5307 "cause to fall / cast down") + *tirməsēm* (Verb.Qal.Wayq.3fs of רמס H7429 "trample" + 3mp object-suffix *-ēm* "them").
- **Significance:** *tappēl* = **Hiphil causative** of the common Qal *nāp̄al* "fall"; morphology encodes an external agent causing the falling — the horn is **grammatical causer** of the host's downward motion. Locative *ʾarṣāh* "to-the-ground" (Noun.s.Abs with **locative *-āh* suffix**, specifies endpoint of fall, not a second noun phrase). The 3mp object-suffix on *tirməsēm* grammatically resumes **both** the host *and* the stars as its antecedent (mixed-gender plural defaults to 3mp).
- **Cross-links:** [word study H5307-naphal](../../word-studies/H5307-naphal.md), [TR-naphal-pipto](../../word-studies/TR-naphal-pipto.md), [word study H7429-ramas](../../word-studies/H7429-ramas.md), [word study H3556-kokab](../../word-studies/H3556-kokab.md) (stars), [word study H6635-tsaba](../../word-studies/H6635-tsaba.md) (host), [word study H8064-shamayim](../../word-studies/H8064-shamayim.md) (heavens).

### 3. הוּרַם / הֻשְׁלַךְ — The Hophal passive cluster of v.11

- **Forms:** *hûram* (Verb.Hophal.Perf.3ms of רום H7311 "be-high / be-exalted" → Hophal "was-lifted/taken-away") + *hušlaḵ* (Verb.Hophal.Perf.3ms of שׁלך H7993 "throw" → Hophal "was-cast-down").
- **Hophal stem:** **passive of Hiphil**. Morphology: prefix **הֻ־** (hu- / o-vowel preformative) + stem + perfect afformative. The Hiphil/Hophal pair is Hebrew's lexical active-passive voice alternation for causative verbs — the *Hiphil* says "X caused Y to happen," the *Hophal* says "Y was caused-to-happen (by unspecified/obscured agent)." Hophal is **rare** (~400× vs. Hiphil's ~9,000×) and clusters at precisely these moments of action-without-named-agent. Parallel: no existing grammar-reference entry yet for Hophal — candidate gap for a future canonical entry; related pair Hiphil/Hiphil-internal-causative is documented at [stem-hiphil](../hebrew/stem-hiphil.md) and [hiphil-imperfect](../hebrew/hiphil-imperfect.md).
- **Textual-critical note on הוּרַם vs. הֵרִים:** the Masoretic pointing נִקֻּד gives **Hophal** *hûram* "was-taken-away" (agentless passive). The ETCBC/BHSA parser (reproduced above) tags the consonantal form as הרים with `Verb.Hophal.Perf.3ms`, matching MT pointing. A long minority textual tradition (numerous medieval Hebrew MSS; see BHS apparatus and Collins 1993, *Daniel* Hermeneia 326–27) repoints as **Hiphil** *hērîm* "he-raised / he-took-away" — making the horn the overt subject of the verb. The two readings are **morphologically indistinguishable in the unpointed text** (both הרים), so the dispute is a vocalization-tradition dispute, not a consonantal-text dispute. Consequence: on MT-Hophal the *tāmîḏ* "was-removed" without the horn as grammatical agent (divine passive reading available); on Hiphil repointing the horn actively "took-away" the *tāmîḏ*. The parser follows MT pointing.
- **מִמֶּנּוּ "from him / by him" (v.11):** Prep מִן + 3ms suffix. Two well-attested functions — (a) **ablative of separation** ("away from him"), matching Hophal *hûram* as impersonal-passive "the *tāmîḏ* was-removed from his domain," (b) **personal-agent marker with passive verb** ("by him" as agent of passive — GKC §121f, Joüon-Muraoka §132e list מִן with passive verbs as one of Hebrew's ways of marking agent, though the more common marker is *bə-* or *lə-*). On reading (b) the horn becomes the logical (semantic) agent even while the verb is grammatically passive. KJV "by him" adopts (b); ESV/NRSV "from him" adopts (a).
- **Significance:** the Hophal cluster *hûram / hušlaḵ* positions the sanctuary-assault at a stylistic altitude distinct from v.10's active wayq narration — the grammar **will not name** who took the *tāmîḏ* away or who cast down the sanctuary-place. This restraint is a feature, not a bug: the divine passive reading is grammatically available (cross-link Greek [divine-passive](../greek/divine-passive.md) for the cross-testament phenomenon), but so is the ambiguous-agent reading. What the grammar rules *out* is an unambiguous identification of the horn as the overt grammatical agent of removal.
- **Cross-links:** [word study H7311-ruwm](../../word-studies/H7311-ruwm.md) (rûm "be-high"), [word study H7993-shalak](../../word-studies/H7993-shalak.md) (šālaḵ "throw / cast"), [word study H8548-tamid](../../word-studies/H8548-tamid.md) (tāmîḏ "continual"), [word study H4720-miqdash](../../word-studies/H4720-miqdash.md) (miqdāš "sanctuary"), [word study H4349-makon](../../word-studies/H4349-makon.md) (māḵôn "established-place").

### 4. מְכוֹן מִקְדָּשׁוֹ — Two-member construct chain with 3ms suffix on absolute (v.11)

- **Chain:** *məḵôn* (Noun.ms.Cst of מָכוֹן H4349 "fixed-place / established-site / foundation") bound to *miqdāšô* (Noun.ms.Abs of מִקְדָּשׁ H4720 "sanctuary" + 3ms pronominal suffix *-ô* "his").
- **Genitival relation:** *məḵôn* is the construct head; *miqdāš* is the governed absolute. Classical BH requires the possessive suffix on the **absolute** (last) member even when it semantically binds the whole chain — GKC §127a, §128a, Joüon-Muraoka §140. So the English rendering is **"place of his-sanctuary"** ≈ "his sanctuary's place" / "the site-of-his-sanctuary."
- **Antecedent of the 3ms suffix:** BH resolution rules prefer the nearest eligible 3ms referent. The eligible 3ms antecedents in v.11 are (a) **שַׂר** "prince" (Noun.ms), (b) the implicit 3ms subject of *higdîl* (the horn, but parsed f.s. elsewhere in the vision — *qeren* is feminine; the 3ms of *higdîl* is the exception explained below), (c) the 3ms of *mimmennû*. The horn is grammatically **f.s.** throughout Dan 8 (v.5 *qeren* f.s., v.9 *tigdal* 3fs, v.10 *tigdal/tappēl/tirməsēm* 3fs, v.12 *tinnāṯēn/tašlēḵ/ʿāśəṯāh/hiṣlîḥāh* 3fs); the 3ms of *higdîl* in v.11 has been variously explained as (i) agreement with the (masculine) person behind the symbol (Theodotion and medieval commentators), (ii) constructio ad sensum with *śar* in proximity, or (iii) an impersonal "it magnified" — see Collins 1993, *Daniel* 333. The **nearest 3ms antecedent in the clause chain** for the suffix on *miqdāšô* is **שַׂר־הַצָּבָא** "Prince of the host" (Noun.ms, explicitly 3ms), which v.11's clause 1 has just fronted into focus position. The 3ms suffix is therefore grammatically bound to the heavenly *śar*; "his sanctuary" = the Prince's sanctuary.
- **Significance:** this is the **load-bearing grammatical point** of the passage for the heavenly-sanctuary thesis. The construct-chain + 3ms-suffix architecture does not permit a reading in which the sanctuary belongs to the horn (the horn is f.s. throughout the vision's verb-agreement); the suffix's only eligible 3ms antecedent in the near discourse is the heavenly Prince. Combined with construct chain #1 *ṣəḇāʾ haš-šāmayim* (host **of the heavens**), the spatial vector of the passage is **upward into heaven**, and the sanctuary that gets "cast down" is syntactically bound to that heavenly register.

### 5. תִּנָּתֵן — Niphal imperfect 3fs of נתן: divine passive "was-given-over" (v.12)

- **Form:** *tinnāṯēn* (Verb.Niphal.Impf.3fs of נתן H5414). Niphal morphology: **תִּ־** prefix + **נָ־** infix + stem + afformative; Niphal of נתן is the standard passive "be-given." The imperfect tense-form in this WXYq clause functions **iteratively / progressively / past-in-prophetic-vision** ("was [repeatedly/continually] given over"), not as a simple future — a register typical of vision-description (cross-link [qal-imperfect](../hebrew/qal-imperfect.md) §3 on imperfect aspectual range).
- **Divine-passive function:** Niphal is Hebrew's default passive-of-Qal. In contexts like this where no overt agent is supplied, the form regularly carries **divine-passive / theological-passive** force — the unnamed agent is understood to be God or the divine council. Parallel: the Greek NT's ἐδόθη / ἐπιτραπῇ pattern in Revelation (Rev 6:2, 4, 8; 13:5, 7, 14–15) is the direct LXX-calque of this Hebrew Niphal-of-*nāṯan*. Cross-links [stem-niphal](../hebrew/stem-niphal.md), [niphal-imperfect-divine-denial](../hebrew/niphal-imperfect-divine-denial.md), [divine-passive](../greek/divine-passive.md), [aorist-passive-indicative](../greek/aorist-passive-indicative.md).
- **Subject of *tinnāṯēn*:** צָבָא *ṣāḇāʾ* "host" (Noun.ms.Abs) — **fronted and topicalized** in WXYq slot. But note: the verb is **3fs** *tinnāṯēn*, not 3ms. *ṣāḇāʾ* is morphologically ambiguous for gender (standardly m, but occasionally takes f. agreement when construed as a collective abstract; GKC §122s, §145b). The 3fs agreement here aligns *ṣāḇāʾ* with the continuous 3fs verb-chain of the horn-referent (v.10 *tigdal/tappēl/tirməsēm*; v.12 cl.2–4 *tašlēḵ/ʿāśəṯāh/hiṣlîḥāh*). Several commentators read *ṣāḇāʾ* in v.12 as referring not to the heavenly host of v.10 but to "an [additional] host [given to the horn]," hence KJV supplies "[him]" in brackets (cf. BDB *ṣāḇāʾ* §1.b, Collins 1993 *Daniel* 333–34). The grammar allows both: (a) the heavenly host is *given over* (passive handed-over into the horn's power), (b) an army is *given over* (i.e. appointed) against the *tāmîḏ*.
- **ʿal-hat-tāmîḏ + bə-p̄āšaʿ (v.12 cl.1 adjuncts):**
  - **עַל** can mean "against" (hostile direction) or "together-with / in-addition-to" (accompaniment — BDB *ʿal* II.1.e, GKC §119aa). Reading 1: "a host was given over **against** the *tāmîḏ* **through/by** transgression." Reading 2: "a host was given over **together with** the *tāmîḏ* **in** transgression." Reading 1 construes the *tāmîḏ* as the target of assault; reading 2 construes it as co-victim.
  - **בְּ־פָשַׁע** "in/by/through transgression" (Prep *bə-* + Noun.ms.Abs *pešaʿ* H6588). The *bə-* is the classical ambiguity — (a) instrumental/causal *bə-* ("by means of / because of transgression" — the transgression is the cause of the handover), (b) circumstantial *bə-* ("in [a state of] transgression" — the handover takes place in a condition of transgression), (c) partitive-modal *bə-* ("with transgression" — transgression attends the handover). GKC §119i–o. Combined with reading 1 of *ʿal*, the most common English rendering is "against the daily [sacrifice] because of transgression" (KJV, NKJV, NASB, ESV, NRSV).
- **Cross-links:** [word study H5414-nathan](../../word-studies/H5414-nathan.md) (nāṯan "give"), [word study H6588-pesha](../../word-studies/H6588-pesha.md) (pešaʿ "transgression/rebellion"), [word study H6635-tsaba](../../word-studies/H6635-tsaba.md) (ṣāḇāʾ "host / army"), [word study H8548-tamid](../../word-studies/H8548-tamid.md) (tāmîḏ "continual").

### 6. וְתַשְׁלֵךְ אֱמֶת אַרְצָה — Hiphil active casting-down of truth (v.12 cl.2)

- **Form:** *tašlēḵ* (Verb.Hiphil.Impf.3fs of שׁלך H7993 "throw / cast") + *ʾĕmeṯ* (Noun.fs.Abs אֱמֶת H571 "truth / trustworthiness / faithfulness") + *ʾarṣāh* (Noun.s.Abs + locative *-āh* "to-the-ground").
- **Voice contrast with v.11:** this is the **matching active Hiphil** to v.11's passive Hophal *hušlaḵ*. Same root (שׁלך), same surface gesture (casting down, with *ʾarṣāh* locative in both), opposite voice. The grammar arranges a deliberate voice-chiasm:
  - v.10: active **Hiphil** *tappēl* + *ʾarṣāh* (horn casts down **host**)
  - v.11: passive **Hophal** *hušlaḵ* (sanctuary-place **is cast down**, agent unnamed)
  - v.12: active **Hiphil** *tašlēḵ* + *ʾarṣāh* (horn casts down **truth**)
- The root שׁלך brackets the passage like a refrain, shifting voice at the hinge of v.11's passive cluster.
- **Cross-links:** [word study H7993-shalak](../../word-studies/H7993-shalak.md) (šālaḵ), [TR-emeth-aletheia](../../word-studies/TR-emeth-aletheia.md) (ʾĕmeṯ → LXX/NT ἀλήθεια).

### 7. וְעָשְׂתָה וְהִצְלִיחָה — The weqatal success-pair (v.12 cl.3–4)

- **Forms:** *ʿāśəṯāh* (Verb.Qal.Perf.3fs of עשׂה H6213 "do / make / act") + *hiṣlîḥāh* (Verb.Hiphil.Perf.3fs of צלח H6743 "prosper / succeed / advance"). Both with initial וְ conjunction — parsed as **weqatal** chain (waw + perfect, WQt0 clause type).
- **Weqatal function in Domain D:** in discursive/prospective contexts the weqatal propagates modal/iterative force from a preceding *yiqtol* (here clause 2's *tašlēḵ*). The force is **"and [she will/did] keep doing-and-prospering"** — open-ended iterative-continuous action without fixed temporal endpoint. Cross-link [weqatal-legal-imperatival](../hebrew/weqatal-legal-imperatival.md) for the related Deuteronomic function (different pragmatic force, same morphology).
- **Parallel with Dan 8:24:** identical Hiphil of צלח + Qal of עשׂה occurs in inverted order at v.24 *wə-hiṣlîaḥ wə-ʿāśâ* (3ms there — the horn-king now grammatically singular-masculine at the interpretation layer). The formulaic pair brackets the vision and its angelic interpretation. Cross-link [dan-8-23-25](dan-8-23-25.md).

## The Sequential Destruction Cascade — Syntactic Ladder

The grammar organizes the horn's action as a five-rung vertical ladder, each rung marked by a specific clause-type / verb-stem signature:

| Rung | Target | Verse | Verb(s) | Voice/stem |
|------|--------|-------|---------|------------|
| 1 | *ṣəḇāʾ haš-šāmayim* (heavenly host) | 8:10 | *tigdal* / *tappēl* / *tirməsēm* | Qal wayq + **Hiphil wayq** (active causer) |
| 2 | *śar-haṣ-ṣāḇāʾ* (Prince of host) | 8:11a | *higdîl* | **Hiphil perf** (self-magnification, reflexive-internal causative) |
| 3 | *hat-tāmîḏ* (continual [offering]) | 8:11b | *hûram* | **Hophal perf** (agentless / divine passive) |
| 4 | *məḵôn miqdāšô* (place of HIS sanctuary) | 8:11c | *hušlaḵ* | **Hophal perf** (agentless / divine passive) |
| 5 | *ʾĕmeṯ* (truth) | 8:12b | *tašlēḵ* | **Hiphil impf** (active casting-down) |

Three observations fall out of the grammar:

1. **The two passive-Hophal rungs (3 and 4) flank the Prince rung (2)** — exactly the point at which the text refuses to name the horn as grammatical agent. The Hophal is not an accidental stylistic choice; it is the voice Hebrew uses to describe action-without-named-agent. The vision narrates the outcome (removal, casting-down) without subjecting the heavenly Prince's *tāmîḏ* and sanctuary-place to direct horn-agency at the surface-syntax level.
2. **The two Hiphil active rungs (1 and 5) bracket the Hophal middle** — the horn is unambiguously Hiphil-active causer against the host (rung 1) and against truth (rung 5). These are the horn's unambiguous direct assaults at the syntactic surface.
3. **Rung 2 is the hinge** — *higdîl* "he-magnified-himself" is the horn's only unambiguous active verb against the Prince, and it is reflexive-internal-causative, not transitive — the horn's action is self-exaltation, not direct attack. The claim against the Prince is ontological ("as-high-as the Prince"), not physical.
