## Semantic Categories

### 1. Diligence / Industriousness
The most frequent sense in Proverbs, where charuwts describes the character trait of sharp, determined effort — contrasted with the sluggard (atsel H6102) and the slack/deceitful (remiyyah H7423).

- **Pro 10:4** — "He becometh poor that dealeth [with] a slack hand: but the hand of the diligent maketh rich."
- **Pro 12:24** — "The hand of the diligent shall bear rule: but the slothful shall be under tribute."
- **Pro 13:4** — "The soul of the sluggard desireth, and [hath] nothing: but the soul of the diligent shall be made fat."
- **Pro 21:5** — "The thoughts of the diligent [tend] only to plenteousness; but of every one [that is] hasty only to want."

### 2. Fine Gold / Precious Metal
Used as a noun for gold (as something cut/mined from the earth). Appears in value-comparison formulas where wisdom, instruction, or knowledge is declared more precious than charuwts.

- **Pro 3:14** — "For the merchandise of it [is] better than the merchandise of silver, and the gain thereof than fine gold."
- **Pro 8:19** — "My fruit [is] better than gold, yea, than fine gold; and my revenue than choice silver."
- **Pro 16:16** — "How much better [is it] to get wisdom than gold! and to get understanding rather to be chosen than silver!"
- **Psa 68:13** — "Though ye have lien among the pots, [yet shall ye be as] the wings of a dove covered with silver, and her feathers with yellow gold."
- **Zec 9:3** — "And Tyrus did build herself a strong hold, and heaped up silver as the dust, and fine gold as the mire of the streets."

### 3. Threshing Instrument / Sharp Agricultural Tool
Refers to the threshing sledge — a heavy board fitted with sharp stones or iron teeth dragged over grain. Both literal agricultural and figurative judgment imagery.

- **Isa 28:27** — "For the fitches are not threshed with a threshing instrument, neither is a cart wheel turned about upon the cummin."
- **Isa 41:15** — "Behold, I will make thee a new sharp threshing instrument having teeth: thou shalt thresh the mountains, and beat [them] small."
- **Amo 1:3** — "Thus saith the LORD; For three transgressions of Damascus, and for four, I will not turn away [the punishment] thereof; because they have threshed Gilead with threshing instruments of iron."

### 4. Physical Sharpness
The base adjectival sense: something incised, pointed, or cutting.

- **Job 41:30** — "Sharp stones [are] under him: he spreadeth sharp pointed things upon the mire."

### 5. Decision / Determination
Abstract figurative sense from the root meaning "to cut" → "to decide." Used for the eschatological "valley of decision."

- **Joel 3:14** — "Multitudes, multitudes in the valley of decision: for the day of the LORD [is] near in the valley of decision."

### 6. Wall / Moat / Trench (Fortification)
Something cut or dug — a defensive trench or moat. Paired with rechob (open plaza/street) in Daniel's prophecy of Jerusalem's rebuilding.

- **Dan 9:25** — "the street shall be built again, and the wall, even in troublous times."

*In the Hebrew text, Dan 9:25 reads rechob wechârûts — "plaza and moat/trench." The KJV renders charuwts as "wall" here. The Hebrew parser confirms the word appears as a masculine noun absolute in this verse.*
