## Key Grammatical Features

### 1. הִזָּה / יַזֶּה (nazah) — Hiphil of נזה: Two-Phase Blood Application

- **Form:** Two Hiphil forms of נזה in v.14: (a) הִזָּה (Hiphil perfect 3ms) in clause 2, and (b) יַזֶּה (Hiphil imperfect 3ms) in clause 3. These describe two distinct sprinkling actions.
- **Distribution:** The verb נזה occurs 24 times in the OT (parser-verified). 20 of those are Hiphil; 3 are Qal (Lev 6:20 x2, 2 Kgs 9:33, Isa 63:3); 1 Hiphil imperfect in Isa 52:15. In Leviticus 16, it appears 4 times (vv.14 x2, 15, 19).
- **Two-phase sequence in v.14:**
  - Phase 1: וְהִזָּה בְאֶצְבָּעוֹ **עַל־פְּנֵי הַכַּפֹּרֶת** קֵדְמָה — spatter with his finger **upon the face/surface of the kapporeth**, eastward. Preposition עַל marks contact with the surface itself.
  - Phase 2: וְ**לִפְנֵי** הַכַּפֹּרֶת יַזֶּה שֶׁבַע־פְּעָמִים מִן־הַדָּם — **before the face of** the kapporeth he shall spatter seven times from the blood. Preposition ל + פְנֵי marks a position in front of the object, not on its surface.
- **Tense-form shift:** The perfect הִזָּה (clause 2) shifts to the imperfect יַזֶּה (clause 3). The clause-type also changes from WQt0 (waw+qatal sequential) to WxY0 (waw + non-verbal constituent + yiqtol). The fronted complement לִפְנֵי הַכַּפֹּרֶת before the verb signals a new focus/emphasis on the changed location.
- **Comparison with Lev 4:6:** In Lev 4:6, the same verb הִזָּה appears with sprinkling **לִפְנֵי יְהוָה אֶת־פְּנֵי פָּרֹכֶת הַקֹּדֶשׁ** — "before YHWH, toward the face of the curtain of the holy place." The location in Lev 4:6 is the holy place, before the veil. In Lev 16:14, the location has moved deeper: past the veil, onto and before the kapporeth itself. The verb and the ritual gesture (finger-sprinkling, seven times) are identical; only the spatial depth changes from the first compartment to the innermost.
- **Grammar reference:** [stem-hiphil](../hebrew/stem-hiphil.md)

### 2. עַל־פְּנֵי vs. לִפְנֵי — Spatial Prepositions Distinguishing Two Sprinkling Locations

- **Form:** Both use the construct פְּנֵי (Noun.mp.Cst, "face of") from the lemma פנה, but with different governing prepositions.
- **עַל־פְּנֵי הַכַּפֹּרֶת** (v.14a): עַל ("upon") + פְּנֵי + הַכַּפֹּרֶת. This marks contact with the upper surface. The blood is applied directly onto the kapporeth. The directional adverb קֵדְמָה ("eastward/frontward") further specifies which part of the surface.
- **לִפְנֵי הַכַּפֹּרֶת** (v.14b, v.15): ל ("to/before") + פְנֵי + הַכַּפֹּרֶת. This marks spatial position in front of the object. The blood is sprinkled in the area before the kapporeth — i.e., on the ground in front of the ark.
- **v.15 ellipsis:** Clause 7 of v.15 is classified as Ellp (elliptical) by the clause parser. It reads וְלִפְנֵי הַכַּפֹּרֶת with no verb — the verb הִזָּה from clause 6 is gapped/elided. This compressed syntax depends on the established pattern from v.14: "and [he shall spatter it] before the kapporeth."
- **Grammar reference:** [construct-state](../hebrew/construct-state.md)

### 3. כִפֶּר עַל־הַקֹּדֶשׁ (v.16) — Piel of כפר with Locative Object

- **Form:** Piel perfect 3ms of כפר, with the complement עַל הַקֹּדֶשׁ ("upon/for the holy place").
- **Significance:** The Piel of כפר is the dominant atonement verb (92 of 102 occurrences of the root — parser-verified). Here the direct complement is not a person but a place: הַקֹּדֶשׁ, "the holy (place)." The preposition עַל introduces the entity that is the target of the purging/atoning action. The text states that the holy place itself requires atonement — it has been contaminated by Israel's impurities and must be ritually cleansed.
- **Parallel in v.16b:** וְכֵן יַעֲשֶׂה לְאֹהֶל מֹועֵד — "and thus he shall do for the tent of meeting." The adverb כֵן ("thus/so") back-references the entire blood application sequence of vv.14-15, and the imperfect יַעֲשֶׂה indicates this same procedure applies to the outer compartment. The construct chain אֹהֶל מֹועֵד ("tent of meeting/appointment") designates the tabernacle as a whole or its outer compartment.
- **Grammar reference:** [stem-piel](../hebrew/stem-piel.md); **word studies:** [H3722-kaphar](../../word-studies/H3722-kaphar.md), [H6944-qodesh](../../word-studies/H6944-qodesh.md), [WG-atonement](../../word-studies/WG-atonement.md)

### 4. Three מִן-Prepositions in v.16: מִטֻּמְאֹת / וּמִפִּשְׁעֵיהֶם / לְכָל־חַטֹּאתָם

- **Form:** The clause-analysis tags the entire phrase מִטֻּמְאֹת בְּנֵי יִשְׂרָאֵל וּמִפִּשְׁעֵיהֶם לְכָל־חַטֹּאתָם as the [Adju] (adjunct) of the verb כִפֶּר. It contains three prepositional elements specifying the cause/source of the contamination requiring atonement:

  1. **מִטֻּמְאֹת בְּנֵי יִשְׂרָאֵל** — מִן + טֻּמְאֹת (fp.Cst, "uncleannesses of") + בְּנֵי יִשְׂרָאֵל. The preposition מִן here has a causal/separative sense: "because of / away from the uncleannesses of the sons of Israel." The noun טֻמְאָה appears in the feminine plural construct (טֻּמְאֹת), indicating multiple or various kinds of ritual impurity.

  2. **וּמִפִּשְׁעֵיהֶם** — וּ + מִן + פִּשְׁעֵיהֶם (mp.Abs + 3mp suffix, "their transgressions/rebellions"). The coordinating waw + repeated מִן creates a parallel causal phrase. The noun פֶּשַׁע (H6588) denotes willful rebellion or breaking away from authority, distinct from ritual impurity.

  3. **לְכָל־חַטֹּאתָם** — ל + כָל (Noun.ms.Cst) + חַטֹּאתָם (fp.Abs + 3mp suffix, "their sins"). The switch from מִן to ל marks a syntactic shift. The preposition ל here functions as a specification/respect preposition: "with respect to all their sins." The noun חַטָּאת (H2403) is the broadest term for sin/missing the mark.

- **Three-term sin taxonomy:** The same three categories appear in v.21 in the confession formula (see [lev-16-21-sin-terms](lev-16-21-sin-terms.md)). In v.16, the three terms are arranged as the cause from which the holy place must be purged:
  - טֻמְאֹת — ritual/ceremonial uncleannesses (broadest category of defilement)
  - פְּשָׁעִים — deliberate rebellions/transgressions (willful covenant violations)
  - חַטֹּאת — sins (the comprehensive catch-all term, governed by כָל "all")

- **Prepositional logic:** The two מִן-phrases identify the source/cause of contamination (causal מִן: "because of uncleannesses ... and because of rebellions"), while the ל-phrase identifies the comprehensive scope ("with respect to all their sins"). The structure moves from specific categories to a totalizing summary.

- **Word studies:** [H6588-pesha](../../word-studies/H6588-pesha.md), [WG-transgressions](../../word-studies/WG-transgressions.md)

### 5. הַשֹּׁכֵן אִתָּם בְּתוֹךְ טֻמְאֹתָם — Participle as Circumstantial-Causal Relative Clause

- **Form:** הַ + שֹּׁכֵן (Qal active participle ms.Abs of שׁכן, "dwell") + אִתָּם (Prep + 3mp suffix, "with them") + בְּתוֹךְ טֻמְאֹתָם (Prep + Noun.ms.Cst "midst of" + Noun.fp.Abs + 3mp suffix "their uncleannesses").
- **Significance:** The article הַ preceding the participle signals a relative clause modifying אֹהֶל מֹועֵד: "the tent of meeting, **the one dwelling with them in the midst of their uncleannesses**." The participle שֹׁכֵן is durative/stative — it describes God's ongoing habitation among them. The double locative complement (אִתָּם + בְּתוֹךְ טֻמְאֹתָם) reinforces the paradox: the divine dwelling persists **in the midst of** the very uncleannesses that require purging.
- **Construct chain:** תוֹךְ (Noun.ms.Cst) + טֻמְאֹתָם (Noun.fp.Abs + 3mp) — "the midst of their uncleannesses." This is a construct chain expressing the spatial relationship of the tabernacle (and the divine presence) to Israel's impurities.
- **Inclusio with v.16a:** The verse opens with atonement being made **because of** Israel's uncleannesses (מִטֻּמְאֹת) and closes with God dwelling **in the midst of** their uncleannesses (בְּתוֹךְ טֻמְאֹתָם). The same root טמאה frames the verse, providing the ground for both the necessity and the purpose of the blood ritual.
- **Grammar reference:** [construct-state](../hebrew/construct-state.md)

## Comparison: Lev 4:6-7 vs. Lev 16:14-16 Blood Application

| Feature | Lev 4:6 (Holy Place) | Lev 16:14 (Most Holy Place) |
|---------|---------------------|---------------------------|
| **Verb** | הִזָּה (Hiphil perf 3ms of נזה) | הִזָּה / יַזֶּה (Hiphil perf/impf of נזה) |
| **Instrument** | finger (אֶצְבָּעוֹ) | finger (אֶצְבָּעוֹ) |
| **Repetition** | seven times (שֶׁבַע פְּעָמִים) | seven times (שֶׁבַע פְּעָמִים) |
| **Source** | from the blood (מִן הַדָּם) | from the blood (מִן הַדָּם) |
| **Location** | before YHWH (לִפְנֵי יְהוָה), toward the face of the curtain of the holy (אֶת פְּנֵי פָּרֹכֶת הַקֹּדֶשׁ) | upon the face of the kapporeth (עַל פְּנֵי הַכַּפֹּרֶת) + before the kapporeth (לִפְנֵי הַכַּפֹּרֶת) |
| **Spatial depth** | First compartment — outside the veil | Second compartment — past the veil, at the ark |
| **Surface contact** | No (sprinkling before the veil) | Yes (first application directly onto the kapporeth) |
| **Additional action** | Blood on altar-of-incense horns (v.7: וְנָתַן ... עַל קַרְנֹות מִזְבַּח) | No altar-of-incense step described here (but cf. Lev 16:18-19 for the outer altar) |

The ritual verb, instrument, number of applications, and partitive source construction (מִן הַדָּם) are identical between Lev 4:6 and Lev 16:14. What changes is the location: Lev 4:6 operates in the first compartment before the veil; Lev 16:14 operates in the second compartment at the kapporeth itself. The Day of Atonement moves the same purification gesture into the innermost sacred space.
