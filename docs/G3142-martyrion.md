## Lexicon Definition

Neuter of a presumed derivative of G3144 (μάρτυς); something evidential, i.e. (genitive case) evidence given or (specifically) the Decalogue (in the sacred Tabernacle): — to be testified, testimony, witness.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Priestly Confirmation after Healing
The healed person is sent to the priest with a gift "for a testimony to them" — the ritual act serves as public, official evidence of the healing.
- **Matt 8:4** — "shew thyself to the priest, and offer the gift that Moses commanded, for a testimony unto them"
- **Mark 1:44** — "offer for thy cleansing those things which Moses commanded, for a testimony unto them"
- **Luke 5:14** — "offer for thy cleansing, according as Moses commanded, for a testimony unto them"

### 2. Public Witness Before Rulers and Nations
Being brought before governors and kings "for a testimony against them" or "to the Gentiles" — the persecution itself becomes a public declaration.
- **Matt 10:18** — "ye shall be brought before governors and kings for my sake, for a testimony against them and the Gentiles"
- **Mark 13:9** — "in the synagogues ye shall be beaten: and ye shall be brought before rulers and kings for my sake, for a testimony against them"
- **Luke 21:13** — "it shall turn to you for a testimony"

### 3. Missionary Declaration (Gospel Preached / Dust Shaken)
The gospel is the testimony proclaimed to all nations; shaking dust off the feet is a visible testimony against those who reject the message.
- **Matt 24:14** — "this gospel of the kingdom shall be preached in all the world for a witness unto all nations"
- **Mark 6:11** — "shake off the dust under your feet for a testimony against them"
- **Luke 9:5** — "shake off the very dust from your feet for a testimony against them"

### 4. Apostolic Gospel Testimony
The content of apostolic proclamation — the resurrection, Christ himself, or the apostle's conduct — described as a concrete testimony.
- **Acts 4:33** — "with great power gave the apostles witness of the resurrection of the Lord Jesus"
- **1 Cor 1:6** — "the testimony of Christ was confirmed in you"
- **2 Cor 1:12** — "the testimony of our conscience, that in simplicity and godly sincerity...we have had our conversation"
- **2 Thess 1:10** — "to be admired in all them that believe (because our testimony among you was believed)"
- **2 Tim 1:8** — "Be not thou therefore ashamed of the testimony of our Lord"

### 5. Tabernacle of Testimony (OT Sanctuary Echo)
The wilderness tabernacle is called the "tabernacle of witness/testimony" — echoing the LXX rendering of the Hebrew "tent of meeting" where the ark of the testimony was kept.
- **Acts 7:44** — "Our fathers had the tabernacle of witness in the wilderness, as he had appointed, speaking unto Moses"
- **Heb 3:5** — "Moses verily was faithful in all his house, as a servant, for a testimony of those things which were to be spoken after"
- **Rev 15:5** — "the temple of the tabernacle of the testimony in heaven was opened"

### 6. Concrete Material Witness
Physical objects or legal acts that stand as objective evidence — either testifying against someone or establishing legal fact.
- **James 5:3** — "the rust of them shall be a witness against you, and shall eat your flesh"
- **1 Tim 2:6** — "Who gave himself a ransom for all, to be testified in due time"
