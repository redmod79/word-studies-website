## Semantic Categories

### 1. Day of Judgment (Eschatological)
The dominant use: a fixed future reckoning when all deeds and words are accountable.
- **Mat 10:15** — "more tolerable for Sodom and Gomorrha in the day of judgment"
- **Mat 12:36** — "every idle word... they shall give account... in the day of judgment"
- **Heb 9:27** — "appointed unto men once to die, but after this the judgment"
- **1 Jn 4:17** — "boldness in the day of judgment: because as he is, so are we"

### 2. Judicial Process / Legal Setting
κρίσις as the courtroom, tribunal, or the act of adjudication itself.
- **Mat 5:22** — "whosoever is angry with his brother... shall be in danger of the judgment (council)"
- **Jn 5:22** — "the Father... hath committed all judgment unto the Son"
- **Jn 5:27** — "hath given him authority to execute judgment also"
- **1 Tim 5:24** — "some men's sins... going before to judgment"

### 3. Present Cosmic Condemnation
In John, κρίσις describes an ongoing spiritual reality — the world already stands judged by its response to light.
- **Jn 3:19** — "this is the condemnation (κρίσις): that light is come into the world, and men loved darkness"
- **Jn 12:31** — "now is the judgment of this world: now shall the prince of this world be cast out"
- **Jn 16:11** — "of judgment, because the prince of this world is judged"

### 4. Just and Righteous Character of Judgment
God's and Christ's judgment is explicitly qualified as δίκαιος (just/righteous).
- **Jn 5:30** — "my judgment is just; because I seek not mine own will"
- **Jn 7:24** — "judge not according to the appearance, but judge righteous judgment"
- **2 Th 1:5** — "a manifest token of the righteous judgment of God"
- **Rev 19:2** — "true and righteous are his judgments"

### 5. Prophetic Fulfillment (Isaiah)
Matt 12:18–20 quotes Isa 42:1–4 (LXX): the Servant will bring κρίσιν (judgment/justice) to victory — the eschatological establishment of justice.
- **Mat 12:18** — "I will put my spirit upon him, and he shall shew judgment (κρίσιν) to the Gentiles"
- **Mat 12:20** — "till he send forth judgment (κρίσιν) unto victory"
