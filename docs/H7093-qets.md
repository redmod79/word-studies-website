## Lexicon Definition

An extremity; used adverbially (with prepositional prefix) to mean "after." From a root meaning to cut off — hence the point where something is cut short, the terminus or limit of a period or space.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Eschatological/Prophetic End — The Appointed Terminus
The dominant Danielic usage: a divinely fixed endpoint toward which history moves.
- **Dan 8:17** — "at the time of the end [qets] shall be the vision"
- **Dan 11:35** — "to the time of the end [qets]: because it is yet for a time appointed"
- **Dan 11:40** — "at the time of the end [qets] shall the king of the south push at him"
- **Dan 12:4** — "shut up the words, and seal the book, even to the time of the end [qets]"
- **Dan 12:9** — "the words are closed up and sealed till the time of the end [qets]"
- **Hab 2:3** — "the vision is yet for an appointed time, but at the end [qets] it shall speak"

### 2. Judgment Terminus — End as God's Verdict
The end arriving as divine judgment upon a people or person.
- **Gen 6:13** — "The end [qets] of all flesh is come before me"
- **Eze 7:2** — "An end [qets], the end [qets] is come upon the four corners of the land"

- **Amos 8:2** — "The end [qets] is come upon my people of Israel"

- **Dan 11:45** — "he shall come to his end [qets], and none shall help him"

### 3. Temporal Duration Marker — "At the End of [X Time]"
Used with miqqets (מִקֵּץ) to mark completion of a time period.
- **Gen 4:3** — "in process of time" (lit. "at the end of days")
- **Gen 8:6** — "at the end [qets] of forty days, Noah opened the window of the ark"
- **Gen 16:3** — "after Abram had dwelt ten years in the land of Canaan"
- **2 Sam 14:26** — "at every year's end [qets] that he polled it"
- **Dan 11:6** — "in the end [qets] of years they shall join themselves together"

### 4. Limitlessness (with Negation)
With אֵין (ein, "no") to express boundlessness.
- **Isa 9:7** — "Of the increase of his government and peace there shall be no end [qets]"
- **Ecc 4:8** — "yet is there no end [qets] of all his labour"
- **Psa 119:96** — "I have seen an end [qets] of all perfection: but thy commandment is exceeding broad"
- **Job 22:5** — "Is not thy wickedness great? and thine iniquities infinite [ein qets]?"

### 5. Spatial Extremity / Border
Rare usage retaining the root sense of an outer limit.
- **2 Ki 19:23** — "I will enter into the lodgings of his borders [qets]"
- **Isa 37:24** — "I will enter into the height of his border [qets]"
