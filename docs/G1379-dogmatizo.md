## Semantic Categories

### 1. Submission to Regulatory Statutes

The sole NT use is in the passive voice (δογματίζεσθε — 2nd plural present passive indicative): not "you decree" but "you are subjected to ordinances" or "you submit yourselves to regulations." Paul applies it rhetorically to challenge the Colossian church's acceptance of imposed religious rules.

- **Col 2:20** — "Wherefore if ye be dead with Christ from the rudiments of the world, why, as though living in the world, are ye subject to ordinances [δογματίζεσθε]" — the passive framing emphasizes the external imposition; believers who died with Christ should not be submitting to such statutes.

The LXX usages follow the cognate δόγμα pattern of royal decrees with force of law — Esther 3:9 (Haman's royal decree) and Dan 2:13 (Nebuchadnezzar's execution decree). This background gives the word gravitas: to be "dogmatized" is to be placed under statute with legal/coercive force.
