## OT Usage (Hebrew)

| Strong's | Word | Occurrences | Primary Meaning |
|----------|------|-------------|-----------------|
| H3526 | כָּבַס (kabas) | 48v | wash/launder garments (by treading); fuller |

Kabas is the specific term for washing garments or fabric, distinct from rachats (H7364) which washes the body. The root sense is "to trample" — referring to the ancient fulling process of stamping cloth in water. In ritual law, kabas clothes + rachats body form a mandatory pair for restoring purity after contact with uncleanness.

**Key OT passages:**
- **Exo 19:10** — "let them wash (kabas) their clothes" — consecration before Sinai theophany
- **Lev 15:5** — "shall wash (kabas) his clothes, and bathe in water" — standard purification formula
- **Psa 51:2** — "Wash me (kabbeseni) throughly from mine iniquity" — figurative application to moral cleansing

## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same across testaments:**
- Plyno retains the specific garment-washing sense of kabas in its sole NT appearance
- The Levitical association of garment-washing with achieving ritual purity carries into Rev 7:14, where washed robes = access to God's presence
- The pairing with whiteness (Psa 51:7 "whiter than snow"; Rev 7:14 "made them white") bridges the testaments

**What shifts:**
- Kabas in OT law requires literal water; Rev 7:14 replaces water with "the blood of the Lamb" as the cleansing agent — a paradox (blood normally defiles in Levitical law)
- OT kabas is a repeated ritual act; Rev 7:14 presents a once-for-all completed washing (aorist tense eplynan)
- The OT fuller (kobes) as professional tradesman has no NT parallel; Mal 3:2 "fullers' soap" provides the bridge into eschatological purification imagery
