## Lexicon Definition

From an unused root meaning to despatch as a deputy; a messenger; specifically, of God, i.e. an
angel (also a prophet, priest or teacher): ambassador, angel, king, messenger.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. The Angel of the LORD (מַלְאַךְ יְהֹוָה)
The most prominent usage — a divine being who speaks as God in the first person and is identified with God's presence. Appears in key theophanic narratives.

- **Gen 16:7** — "And the angel of the LORD found her by a fountain of water in the wilderness" (first occurrence — to Hagar)
- **Gen 22:11** — "And the angel of the LORD called unto him out of heaven, and said, Abraham, Abraham"
- **Exo 3:2** — "And the angel of the LORD appeared unto him in a flame of fire out of the midst of a bush"
- **Jdg 2:1** — "And an angel of the LORD came up from Gilgal to Bochim, and said, I made you to go up out of Egypt"

### 2. Celestial Angels — Heavenly Servants
Angels as a class of heavenly beings who serve God, execute his commands, and minister to humans. Often used in the plural.

- **Ps 34:7** — "The angel of the LORD encampeth round about them that fear him, and delivereth them"
- **Ps 78:49** — "He cast upon them...trouble, by sending evil angels among them"
- **Ps 103:20** — "Bless the LORD, ye his angels, that excel in strength, that do his commandments"
- **Job 4:18** — "Behold, he put no trust in his servants; and his angels he charged with folly"

### 3. Human Diplomatic Messenger
Political and military envoys sent between rulers and nations. The word bears no divine connotation in these contexts — it simply means "one dispatched."

- **Num 20:14** — "And Moses sent messengers from Kadesh unto the king of Edom"
- **2Sa 2:5** — "And David sent messengers unto the men of Jabeshgilead"
- **2Ki 1:2** — "Ahaziah...sent messengers, and said...Go, enquire of Baalzebub the god of Ekron"
- **1Ch 14:1** — "Now Hiram king of Tyre sent messengers to David, and timber of cedars"

### 4. Ambassador / International Envoy
Formal state-level representative in high-stakes diplomatic missions. The KJV renders these as "ambassador(s)."

- **Isa 18:2** — "That sendeth ambassadors by the sea, even in vessels of bulrushes upon the waters"
- **2Ch 35:21** — "But he sent ambassadors to him, saying, What have I to do with thee, thou king of Judah?"
- **Ezek 17:15** — "He rebelled against him in sending his ambassadors into Egypt, that they might give him horses"
- **Prov 13:17** — "A wicked messenger falleth into mischief: but a faithful ambassador is health"

### 5. Priestly / Prophetic Messenger of God's Word
Humans who function as God's mouthpiece — priests, prophets, and heralds. The same word used for divine angels is applied to these human offices, pointing to the common core: "one sent with a message."

- **Hag 1:13** — "Then spake Haggai the LORD'S messenger in the LORD'S message unto the people"
- **Mal 2:7** — "For the priest's lips should keep knowledge...for he is the messenger of the LORD of hosts"
- **Mal 3:1** — "Behold, I will send my messenger, and he shall prepare the way before me...even the messenger of the covenant"
- **Job 33:23** — "If there be a messenger with him, an interpreter, one among a thousand, to shew unto man his uprightness"
