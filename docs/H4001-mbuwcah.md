## Lexicon Definition

From H947 (bûwç, "to trample"); a trampling, i.e. a thing trodden under foot — rendered "treading (trodden) down (under foot)." Formed on the m-preformative pattern (miqtal), which typically yields a nominal abstract or result-noun from a verbal root. מְבוּסָה denotes either the *act* of being trampled or the *state/condition* of being something that is trampled upon (a subjugated, downtrodden thing).

**Pattern:** The word is an exclusively Isaianic lexeme. Two of the three occurrences (Isa 18:2, 18:7) belong to the oracle concerning Cush/the tall-smooth nation; the third (Isa 22:5) belongs to the "Valley of Vision" oracle against Jerusalem. Isaiah therefore uses the same noun both of a foreign nation *doing* the trampling (or being trampled) and of Yahweh's day of judgment *upon* his own people.

## Semantic Categories

Given only three occurrences, the range clusters into two overlapping uses:

### 1. Epithet of a Nation — a "Trodden-Down People"
Applied to the nation described in the Cush oracle, characterizing it as a people marked by trampling — either aggressively trampling others or suffering trampling themselves (commentators divide on the direction). Paired in both verses with the phrase קַו־קָו ("meted out / measuring line").
- **Isa 18:2** — "...a nation meted out and trodden down (mᵉbûwçâh), whose land the rivers have spoiled."
- **Isa 18:7** — "...a nation meted out and trodden under foot (mᵉbûwçâh), whose land the rivers have spoiled, to the place of the name of the LORD of hosts, the mount Zion."

### 2. A Day of Judgment — Trampling as Divine Visitation
Listed in a triad of disaster-nouns alongside מְהוּמָה (mᵉhûwmâh, "discomfiture/tumult," H4103) and מְבוּכָה (mᵉbûwkâh, "perplexity/confusion," H3998), describing the character of the "day" that Adonai YHWH of hosts brings.
- **Isa 22:5** — "For [it is] a day of trouble, and of treading down (mᵉbûwçâh), and of perplexity by the Lord GOD of hosts in the valley of vision..."

Both categories share the semantic core of *subjugation under the foot* — either as a standing description of a nation or as an eschatological/judicial event.
