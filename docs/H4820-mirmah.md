## Lexicon Definition

From רָמָה (ramah) in the sense of deceiving; fraud: — craft, deceit(-ful, -fully), false, feigned, guile, subtilly, treachery.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Deceit of the Tongue / Speech
The largest cluster. Mirmah is repeatedly associated with the tongue, lips, and mouth as instruments of deception.
- **Psa 10:7** — "His mouth is full of cursing and deceit and fraud: under his tongue is mischief and vanity."
- **Psa 34:13** — "Keep thy tongue from evil, and thy lips from speaking guile."
- **Psa 50:19** — "Thou givest thy mouth to evil, and thy tongue frameth deceit."
- **Psa 52:4** — "Thou lovest all devouring words, O thou deceitful tongue."
- **Jer 9:8** — "Their tongue is as an arrow shot out; it speaketh deceit."

### 2. Fraudulent Commerce / False Balances
A concrete, economic application — falsified weights and measures.
- **Pro 11:1** — "A false balance is abomination to the LORD: but a just weight is his delight."
- **Pro 20:23** — "Divers weights are an abomination unto the LORD; and a false balance is not good."
- **Hos 12:7** — "He is a merchant, the balances of deceit are in his hand: he loveth to oppress."
- **Amos 8:5** — "making the ephah small, and the shekel great, and falsifying the balances by deceit"

### 3. Deceit as Inner Character / Counsel
Mirmah as what fills the heart or governs the plans of the wicked.
- **Pro 12:5** — "The thoughts of the righteous are right: but the counsels of the wicked are deceit."
- **Pro 12:20** — "Deceit is in the heart of them that imagine evil."
- **Pro 14:8** — "The wisdom of the prudent is to understand his way: but the folly of fools is deceit."
- **Pro 26:24** — "He that hateth dissembleth with his lips, and layeth up deceit within him."

### 4. Political / Military Treachery
Deliberate, strategic deception in statecraft and warfare.
- **2 Ki 9:23** — "And Joram turned his hands, and fled, and said to Ahaziah, There is treachery, O Ahaziah."
- **Dan 8:25** — "And through his policy also he shall cause craft to prosper in his hand."
- **Dan 11:23** — "And after the league made with him he shall work deceitfully."
- **Gen 34:13** — "And the sons of Jacob answered Shechem and Hamor his father deceitfully."

### 5. Absence of Deceit (the Righteous / the Innocent)
Where mirmah is negated or denied — marking moral integrity.
- **Psa 17:1** — "Hear the right, O LORD, attend unto my cry, give ear unto my prayer, that goeth not out of feigned lips."
- **Psa 24:4** — "He that hath clean hands, and a pure heart; who hath not lifted up his soul unto vanity, nor sworn deceitfully."
- **Isa 53:9** — "because he had done no violence, neither was any deceit in his mouth"
- **Job 31:5** — "If I have walked with vanity, or if my foot hath hasted to deceit"

### 6. Corporate / National Deceit
Deceit characterizing an entire community or institution.
- **Jer 5:27** — "As a cage is full of birds, so are their houses full of deceit."
- **Jer 9:6** — "Thine habitation is in the midst of deceit; through deceit they refuse to know me, saith the LORD."
- **Hos 11:12** — "Ephraim compasseth me about with lies, and the house of Israel with deceit."
- **Zep 1:9** — "I will punish all those that leap on the threshold, which fill their masters' houses with violence and deceit."
