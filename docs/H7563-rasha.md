## Semantic Categories

### 1. The Wicked as a Moral Class (Contrasted with the Righteous)

The dominant usage: rasha functions as the standard antonym of tsaddiyq (H6662, "righteous"). These two words appear together in 81 verses (33% of all rasha occurrences), forming the fundamental moral binary of Wisdom literature.

- **Gen 18:23** — "Wilt thou also destroy the righteous with the wicked?"
- **Prov 10:3** — "The LORD will not suffer the soul of the righteous to famish: but he casteth away the substance of the wicked."
- **Prov 11:5** — "The righteousness of the perfect shall direct his way: but the wicked shall fall by his own wickedness."
- **Mal 3:18** — "Then shall ye return, and discern between the righteous and the wicked."

### 2. The Fate of the Wicked (Destruction/Judgment)

The wicked are consistently assigned a terminal outcome — being cut off, perishing, consumed, or destroyed.

- **Psa 37:20** — "But the wicked shall perish, and the enemies of the LORD shall be as the fat of lambs: they shall consume; into smoke shall they consume away."
- **Psa 37:38** — "But the transgressors shall be destroyed together: the end of the wicked shall be cut off."
- **Prov 2:22** — "But the wicked shall be cut off from the earth, and the transgressors shall be rooted out of it."
- **Psa 9:17** — "The wicked shall be turned into hell, and all the nations that forget God."

### 3. The Wicked as Violent Oppressors

Rasha describes people who actively harm others — plotting, ambushing, drawing the sword against the poor and needy.

- **Psa 37:14** — "The wicked have drawn out the sword, and have bent their bow, to cast down the poor and needy."
- **Psa 37:32** — "The wicked watcheth the righteous, and seeketh to slay him."
- **Prov 12:6** — "The words of the wicked are to lie in wait for blood."
- **Psa 10:2** — "The wicked in his pride doth persecute the poor."

### 4. God's Active Opposition to the Wicked

God is presented as personally hostile to the wicked — hating, judging, denying peace, cutting them off.

- **Psa 11:5** — "The LORD trieth the righteous: but the wicked and him that loveth violence his soul hateth."
- **Psa 145:20** — "The LORD preserveth all them that love him: but all the wicked will he destroy."
- **Isa 48:22** — "There is no peace, saith the LORD, unto the wicked."
- **Psa 147:6** — "The LORD lifteth up the meek: he casteth the wicked down to the ground."

### 5. The Wicked in Judicial/Legal Contexts

A forensic sense: one found guilty at trial, one who deserves condemnation — the adjective functions as a legal verdict.

- **Exo 23:7** — "the innocent and righteous slay thou not: for I will not justify the wicked."
- **Deut 25:1** — "they come unto judgment... then they shall justify the righteous, and condemn the wicked."
- **1 Ki 8:32** — "condemning the wicked, to bring his way upon his head."
- **Prov 17:15** — "He that justifieth the wicked, and he that condemneth the just, even they both are abomination to the LORD."

### 6. The Wicked Who May Repent (Ezekiel)

Ezekiel uses rasha distinctively in divine appeals for repentance — the wicked person is not beyond hope if they turn.

- **Eze 18:21** — "But if the wicked will turn from all his sins that he hath committed, and keep all my statutes, and do that which is lawful and right, he shall surely live."
- **Eze 33:11** — "I have no pleasure in the death of the wicked; but that the wicked turn from his way and live."
- **Eze 33:14-15** — "when I say unto the wicked, Thou shalt surely die; if he turn from his sin... he shall surely live."
- **Eze 33:19** — "But if the wicked turn from his wickedness, and do that which is lawful and right, he shall live thereby."

### 7. The Temporary Prosperity of the Wicked

Wisdom and prophetic texts repeatedly address the puzzle of wicked people flourishing, treating it as an apparent but short-lived anomaly.

- **Psa 37:35** — "I have seen the wicked in great power, and spreading himself like a green bay tree."
- **Psa 73:3** — "I was envious at the foolish, when I saw the prosperity of the wicked."
- **Job 21:7** — "Wherefore do the wicked live, become old, yea, are mighty in power?"
- **Ecc 8:14** — "there be just men, unto whom it happeneth according to the work of the wicked."
