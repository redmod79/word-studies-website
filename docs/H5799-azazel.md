## Lexicon Definition

From H5795 (עֵז, *goat*) and H235 (אָזַל, *to go away, disappear*); **goat of departure; the scapegoat**. KJV gloss: scapegoat.

BDB analysis: the compound form suggests *complete removal* or *utter dismissal*. The word functions as both a name for the ritual goat and as a description of its role — the goat designated for total removal of sin from the community.

*Note:* Modern translations (ESV, NASB, NIV) often transliterate as **Azazel**, treating it as a proper name — either a desert demon or a rugged cliff. The KJV and the LXX (ἀποπομπαῖος, *one sent away*) both treat it as a descriptive term for the ritual, not a personal name.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. The Lot Designation — One of Two Fates
In the Day of Atonement ritual, Aaron casts lots to assign one goat to the LORD and the other to azazel. The two goats together represent a complete disposal of sin — one by blood, one by removal.

- **Lev 16:8** — "And Aaron shall cast lots upon the two goats; one lot for the LORD, and the other lot for the scapegoat."

### 2. The Living Goat Presented and Sent Away
The azazel-goat is not slaughtered. It is first *presented alive before the LORD* — entering the sacred ceremony — then sent into the wilderness. This distinguishes it from the sin-offering goat (Lev 16:9).

- **Lev 16:10** — "But the goat, on which the lot fell to be the scapegoat, shall be presented alive before the LORD, to make an atonement with him, and to let him go for a scapegoat into the wilderness."

### 3. Ritual Defilement of the Handler
The man who releases the goat must undergo full purification before re-entering the camp. The goat bears the community's sins out, but the contact imparts ritual uncleanness to its handler.

- **Lev 16:26** — "And he that let go the goat for the scapegoat shall wash his clothes, and bathe his flesh in water, and afterward come into the camp."
