## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same across testaments:**
- The quantitative sense "many people/nations" carries directly from OT prophetic usage into NT fulfillment texts
- The scope marker function persists — rab/polys signals the breadth of divine action (judgment or redemption)
- The collocation with "nations" (goyim rabbim -> ethne polla) maintains the universal-scope formula

**What shifts:**
- The OT title/rank sense ("captain," rab as a noun of office) does not carry into the NT through polys
- The OT "enough/suffice" sense drops away; NT polys does not express sufficiency
- The NT intensifies the soteriological application: while OT rab describes scope broadly (many people, many days, great waters), the NT concentrates polys heavily in atonement/justification contexts (Matt 26:28, Mark 10:45, Rom 5:15-19, Heb 9:28) — all echoing the Isaianic Servant's work "for many"
