## Lexicon Definition

From H3559; properly, a fixture, i.e. a basis; generally a place, especially as an abode:—foundation, habitation, (dwelling-, settled) place.

The form is a segholate noun built from the verbal root כּוּן (kûn, "to be firmly established") via the m-prefix pattern characteristic of nouns of place/instrument. The semantic emphasis is not merely locative (a "spot") but *stability-of-location* — a site that has been fixed, founded, or settled. Hence the gloss range: "foundation" (Ps 104:5), "settled place" (1 Kgs 8:13), "dwelling place / habitation" (psalm and prophetic contexts).
