**Semantic shift in translation:**
- **Neutral continuity**: ἐσθίω carries ʼâkal's ordinary eating sense without loss.
- **Split at "devour"**: The Hebrew single verb ʼâkal is split in Greek between ἐσθίω and κατεσθίω
  (G2719) based on context. The Greek language lacks a single verb that covers both ordinary
  eating and hostile consumption, so the κατα- prefix marks judgment/hostile contexts.
- **"Before YHWH" formula**: The LXX preserves the covenant-feast formula `ἔφαγες ἐκεῖ ἐνώπιον
  κυρίου` (Deut 14:26 LXX) mirroring `וְאָכַלְתָּ שָׁם לִפְנֵי יְהוָה`. The weqatal imperatival
  force is rendered by Greek future indicative ("you shall eat"), which in LXX Deuteronomy carries
  the same commanded-future force it has in Hebrew.

**Note on G5315 φάγω:** In Greek, ἐσθίω and φάγω function as a **single suppletive verb** — ἐσθίω
supplies present/imperfect forms, φάγω supplies aorist/future forms. Together they constitute
the full paradigm of "to eat." Many NT "eating" passages show φάγω (aorist/future) rather than
ἐσθίω (present/imperfect), but they belong to the same lexical reality.

## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same across testaments:**
- The **eat-and-drink pair** (H398 + H8354 → ἐσθίω/φάγω + πίνω) is preserved as the idiom for full
  meal/feast participation (Deut 14:26 → Luk 22:15; 1Co 11:26).
- The **"eat before YHWH" covenant-feast formula** carries into the NT as eucharistic language
  ("do this in remembrance of me," "the Lord's supper").
- **Fire-devouring judgment imagery** passes intact: ʼâkal + ʼêsh (H398+H784) → ἐσθίω/κατεσθίω +
  πῦρ (G2068/G2719+G4442). Hebrews 10:27 and Rev 20:9 are direct prophetic continuations.
- **Food-law commands** (H398 + H3808 "shall not eat") continue in Acts 10/15 (κοινός, unclean
  food) and Rom 14/1Co 8–10 (ἐσθίω debates).

**What shifts:**
- **Verb splits into two Greek verbs**: ἐσθίω (neutral/covenantal) and κατεσθίω (hostile/judgment).
  Greek lacks a single verb spanning both polarities that Hebrew carried in one lexeme.
- **Suppletive paradigm**: Greek ἐσθίω borrows aorist/future from φάγω (G5315); NT "eating"
  appears under two Strong's numbers but one verbal concept.
- **Covenant-meal fulfillment shifts agent**: OT commands "**thou** shalt eat before YHWH"
  (weqatal 2ms); NT translates this into eucharistic participation where the risen Christ is the
  meal-host and the covenant-meal becomes Christologically-centered (Luk 22; 1Co 11).
- **Clean/unclean abolished** (Acts 10:15; Mar 7:19): the food-law use of ʼâkal is declared
  fulfilled, while the covenant-feast use is intensified.
