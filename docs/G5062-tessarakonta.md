## Lexicon Definition

The decade of [τέσσαρες]; forty (Strong's). Indeclinable cardinal numeral meaning "forty." Built from τέσσαρες ("four") + the decade suffix ‑κοντα, paralleling τριάκοντα (30), πεντήκοντα (50), ἑξήκοντα (60), etc. Commonly appears in compound numerical expressions: τεσσεράκοντα καὶ δύο ("forty-two"), τεσσεράκοντα τέσσαρες ("forty-four"), ἑκατὸν τεσσεράκοντα τέσσαρες ("one hundred forty-four"). In Rev 7:4; 14:1, 3; 21:17 it forms the multiplier behind 144,000 and 144 cubits.

## Biblical-Theological Distribution of "Forty"

Though this entry lemmatizes the NT Greek cardinal, the numeric motif tessarakonta inherits carries OT weight chiefly through LXX ἀρβαῖμ → τεσσαράκοντα. Canonical touch-points the NT verses re-invoke:

- **Flood** — Gen 7:4, 12, 17; 8:6 — rain/waters "forty days and forty nights."
- **Moses on Sinai (first tablets)** — Exo 24:18; 34:28; Deut 9:9 — "forty days and forty nights" receiving the law.
- **Moses on Sinai (second tablets, intercession)** — Deut 9:18, 25; 10:10 — another "forty days and forty nights."
- **Spies in Canaan** — Num 13:25; 14:34 — "forty days… each day for a year."
- **Wilderness wandering** — Num 14:33–34; 32:13; Deut 2:7; 8:2; 29:5; Jos 5:6; Neh 9:21; Ps 95:10; Amos 2:10; 5:25. Acts 7:36, 42; 13:18; Heb 3:9, 17 cite this span.
- **Elijah's journey to Horeb** — 1Ki 19:8 — "went in the strength of that meat forty days and forty nights" to the mount of God.
- **Jonah's Nineveh preaching** — Jon 3:4 — "Yet forty days, and Nineveh shall be overthrown."
- **Ezekiel lying on his side** — Eze 4:6 — "forty days, each day for a year" bearing Judah's iniquity.
- **Davidic/Solomonic reigns** — 2Sa 5:4; 1Ki 2:11; 11:42; 2Ch 9:30 — David and Solomon each reigned forty years.
- **Temptation of Christ** — Mat 4:2; Mar 1:13; Luk 4:2 — forty days' fast in the wilderness.
- **Post-resurrection appearances** — Act 1:3 — forty days between resurrection and ascension.

Recurring associations across these passages: wilderness, testing, fasting, generation-length chastening, preparatory concealment before commissioning, probation leading to transition.

## Prophetic Time-Idiom: "Forty-Two Months" and Its Equivalents

Revelation itself supplies three parallel measurements attached to the same prophetic span. Each is introduced as the duration of witness / oppression / flight during the period of Gentile/beast dominance:

| Expression | Reference | Literal Value |
|-----------|-----------|---------------|
| μῆνας τεσσεράκοντα [καὶ] δύο ("forty-two months") | Rev 11:2; 13:5 | 42 months |
| ἡμέρας χιλίας διακοσίας ἑξήκοντα ("a thousand two hundred [and] threescore days") | Rev 11:3; 12:6 | 1,260 days |
| καιρὸν καὶ καιροὺς καὶ ἥμισυ καιροῦ ("a time, and times, and half a time") | Rev 12:14 | 3½ "times" |

Arithmetic cross-equivalence as presented in the text:

- 42 months × 30 days = 1,260 days.
- 3½ × 360 = 1,260 days (i.e., 3½ "times" treated as 3½ years of 360 days).
- 1,260 days ÷ 30 = 42 months; 1,260 ÷ 360 = 3½.

Revelation distributes these expressions across paired visions that describe the same interval from different angles:

- **Rev 11:2** — outer court trodden "forty and two months."
- **Rev 11:3** — two witnesses prophesy "a thousand two hundred and threescore days."
- **Rev 12:6** — woman fed in the wilderness "a thousand two hundred and threescore days."
- **Rev 12:14** — same woman nourished "for a time, and times, and half a time."
- **Rev 13:5** — beast given "forty and two months" to continue.

The "time, times, and half a time" formula is drawn from Daniel:

- **Dan 7:25** — "they shall be given into his hand until a time and times and the dividing of time."
- **Dan 12:7** — "for a time, times, and an half."

Daniel 12:11–12 also supplies the parallel day-totals 1,290 and 1,335, and Dan 8:14 gives 2,300 evenings-and-mornings — related but distinct numerical spans. The 1,260 / 42 / 3½ cluster is the one Revelation equates internally.
