## Semantic Categories

### 1. The Created Order / Physical Creation
Refers to the totality of created things — the universe as God's work.
- **Mark 10:6** — "But from the beginning of the creation God made them male and female."
- **Mark 13:19** — "affliction, such as was not from the beginning of the creation which God created"
- **Rom 1:20** — "the invisible things of him from the creation of the world are clearly seen"
- **2 Pet 3:4** — "all things continue as they were from the beginning of the creation"

### 2. The Creation Groaning Under Futility
The personified creation subject to the fall, longing for restoration.
- **Rom 8:19** — "the earnest expectation of the creature waiteth for the manifestation of the sons of God"
- **Rom 8:20** — "the creature was made subject to vanity, not willingly"
- **Rom 8:21** — "the creature itself also shall be delivered from the bondage of corruption"
- **Rom 8:22** — "the whole creation groaneth and travaileth in pain together until now"

### 3. New Creation in Christ
A transformed spiritual state — the individual believer made new.
- **2 Cor 5:17** — "if any man be in Christ, he is a new creature: old things are passed away"
- **Gal 6:15** — "neither circumcision availeth any thing, nor uncircumcision, but a new creature"

### 4. Every Creature / Universal Scope
The entirety of created beings — used with πᾶς (pas, "every/all") for totality.
- **Mark 16:15** — "Go ye into all the world, and preach the gospel to every creature"
- **Col 1:23** — "the gospel, which was preached to every creature which is under heaven"
- **Col 1:15** — "the firstborn of every creature"
- **Rom 8:39** — "nor any other creature, shall be able to separate us from the love of God"

### 5. Individual Creature Before God
A specific created being in relation to the Creator.
- **Rom 1:25** — "worshipped and served the creature more than the Creator"
- **Heb 4:13** — "Neither is there any creature that is not manifest in his sight"

### 6. Institution / Ordinance (Extended Sense)
An established arrangement or institution — the act of founding applied to human structures.
- **1 Pet 2:13** — "Submit yourselves to every ordinance of man for the Lord's sake"

### 7. Christological Title
Christ as the origin-point of creation.
- **Rev 3:14** — "the beginning of the creation of God"
