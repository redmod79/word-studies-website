## Semantic Categories

### 1. Metaphor for Power and Salvation
The OT idiom "horn of salvation" (Ps 18:2; 2 Sam 22:3) enters the NT. A horn raised up signifies strength, victory, and divine deliverance — here applied to the Davidic Messiah.
- **Luk 1:69** — "And hath raised up an horn of salvation for us in the house of his servant David"

### 2. Symbol of Divine Power — The Lamb's Seven Horns
In the throne-room vision, the Lamb bears seven horns, symbolizing complete (7 = fullness) divine power alongside seven eyes (the sevenfold Spirit).
- **Rev 5:6** — "a Lamb... having seven horns and seven eyes, which are the seven Spirits of God sent forth into all the earth"

### 3. Literal Altar Horns
The four projecting corners of the golden incense altar — a structural feature of both the tabernacle and temple (cf. Exo 30:2–3).
- **Rev 9:13** — "I heard a voice from the four horns of the golden altar which is before God"

### 4. Apocalyptic Beast Horns — Composite Powers
Beasts in Revelation bear multiple horns representing a composite of kingdoms or authorities, deliberately echoing Daniel's four-beast vision (Dan 7) and the ram/goat vision (Dan 8).
- **Rev 12:3** — "a great red dragon, having seven heads and ten horns, and seven crowns upon his heads"
- **Rev 13:1** — "a beast rise up out of the sea, having seven heads and ten horns, and upon his horns ten crowns"
- **Rev 13:11** — "another beast... he had two horns like a lamb, and he spake as a dragon"
- **Rev 17:3** — "a woman sit upon a scarlet coloured beast, full of names of blasphemy, having seven heads and ten horns"
- **Rev 17:7** — "the mystery of the woman, and of the beast that carrieth her, which hath the seven heads and ten horns"

### 5. Horns Interpreted as Kings
John's angel gives an explicit interpretation: the ten horns are ten kings who receive authority and act in concert with the beast.
- **Rev 17:12** — "the ten horns which thou sawest are ten kings, which have received no kingdom as yet; but receive power as kings one hour with the beast"
- **Rev 17:16** — "the ten horns which thou sawest upon the beast, these shall hate the whore, and shall make her desolate"
