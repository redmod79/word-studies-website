## Semantic Categories

### 1. Judicial Verdict / Condemnation
- **Acts 25:15** — "the chief priests and the elders of the Jews informed me, desiring [to have] judgment (dikē) against him" — a legal sentence sought from a court
- **2 Thess 1:9** — "Who shall be punished (dikē) with everlasting destruction from the presence of the Lord" — divine judicial penalty

### 2. Retributive Justice / Vengeance
- **Acts 28:4** — "No doubt this man is a murderer, whom, though he hath escaped the sea, yet vengeance (dikē) suffereth not to live" — personified Justice that ensures wrongdoers receive due penalty
- **Jude 1:7** — "Sodom and Gomorrha... are set forth for an example, suffering the vengeance (dikē) of eternal fire" — divine retribution as exemplary consequence

### Usage Pattern
Two categories emerge: (1) forensic/judicial proceedings (Acts 25:15, 2 Thess 1:9) and (2) retributive justice as a cosmic principle (Acts 28:4, Jude 1:7). In Acts 28:4, dikē appears as a personified force (the barbarians treat Justice as a divine agent). In Jude 1:7 and 2 Thess 1:9, it describes the penalty itself rather than the principle.
