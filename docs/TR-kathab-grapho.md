## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same:**
- The core semantic range is identical: physical inscription, legal recording, authoritative textual reference
- Both kâthab and γράφω carry the theological weight of permanent, binding divine commands
- The idea of divine authorship (God writes / commands writing) is continuous from Exo 31:18 through Rev 1:11

**What shifts:**
- NT γράφω adds the **γέγραπται formula** as a stereotyped appeal to OT authority — this becomes the NT's technical idiom for scripture citation (53 of 197 verses)
- The **eschatological register** (book of life, names written in heaven) is more developed in the NT
- The **apostolic correspondence** usage (Paul/John writing letters) is new — kâthab rarely refers to private correspondence
- Heb 8:10 / Jer 31:33 marks a shift: the New Covenant writing is **internal** (on hearts) rather than external (on stone), using ἐγγράφω rather than simple γράφω
