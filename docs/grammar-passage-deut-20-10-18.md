# Grammar Analysis: Deuteronomy 20:10–18 (Two-Tier War Law)

## Passage Overview

Deut 20:10–18 is a bipartite military-casuistic statute with two parallel protases governed by the exclusive-restrictive adverb **רַק** ("only/however"). The structural seam is at v.15 ↔ v.16:

| Block | Verses | Governing adverb | Scope | Outcome |
|-------|--------|------------------|-------|---------|
| A | 10–15 | (default + רַק in v.14 carving out spoil) | **הֶעָרִ֔ים הָרְחֹקֹ֥ת מִמְּךָ֖ מְאֹ֑ד** — "cities very far from you" | Peace-offer → tribute-service if accepted; males-by-sword + non-combatant spoil if refused |
| B | 16–18 | **רַ֗ק** (v.16 initial) | **עָרֵ֤י הָֽעַמִּים֙ הָאֵ֔לֶּה** — "cities of these peoples" (the inheritance) | **לֹ֥א תְחַיֶּ֖ה כָּל־נְשָׁמָֽה** + **הַחֲרֵ֣ם תַּחֲרִימֵ֗ם** — no-breath-survives + absolute-devotion |

The **purpose/negative-final clause of v.18** (`לְמַ֗עַן אֲשֶׁ֨ר לֹֽא־יְלַמְּד֤וּ אֶתְכֶם֙`) supplies the stated rationale for why block B departs from block A: prevention of cultic contagion.

**KJV:**
- **10** When thou comest nigh unto a city to fight against it, then proclaim peace unto it.
- **11** And it shall be, if it make thee answer of peace, and open unto thee, then it shall be, [that] all the people [that is] found therein shall be tributaries unto thee, and they shall serve thee.
- **12** And if it will make no peace with thee, but will make war against thee, then thou shalt besiege it:
- **13** And when the LORD thy God hath delivered it into thine hands, thou shalt smite every male thereof with the edge of the sword:
- **14** But the women, and the little ones, and the cattle, and all that is in the city, [even] all the spoil thereof, shalt thou take unto thyself; and thou shalt eat the spoil of thine enemies, which the LORD thy God hath given thee.
- **15** Thus shalt thou do unto all the cities [which are] very far off from thee, which [are] not of the cities of these nations.
- **16** But of the cities of these people, which the LORD thy God doth give thee [for] an inheritance, thou shalt save alive nothing that breatheth:
- **17** But thou shalt utterly destroy them; [namely], the Hittites, and the Amorites, the Canaanites, and the Perizzites, the Hivites, and the Jebusites; as the LORD thy God hath commanded thee:
- **18** That they teach you not to do after all their abominations, which they have done unto their gods; so should ye sin against the LORD your God.

## Clause Structure (BHSA)

### Verse 10 — casuistic protasis + direct-speech apodosis
1. **xYq0 (Q):** `כִּֽי תִקְרַ֣ב אֶל־עִיר` — "when you approach a city" (*ki* + Qal.Impf.2ms protasis).
2. **InfC (Q):** `לְהִלָּחֵם עָלֶיהָ` — "to fight against it" (purpose infinitive, Niphal reciprocal-reflexive of לחם).
3. **WQt0 (Q):** `וְקָרָ֥אתָ אֵלֶיהָ לְשָׁלוֹם` — **weqatal apodosis** "then you shall call to it for peace."

### Verse 16 — exceptive clause introducing Block B
1. **xYq0 (Q):** `רַ֗ק מֵעָרֵי הָעַמִּים הָאֵלֶּה ... לֹא תְחַיֶּה כָּל־נְשָׁמָה` — fronted **רַק**-adverbial delimitation ([Adju]) + negated Piel.Impf.2ms predicate + universal-negated object.
2. **Ptcp (Q):** `אֲשֶׁר יְהוָה אֱלֹהֶיךָ נֹתֵן לְךָ נַחֲלָה` — relative-clause with ptcp-as-PreC: "which YHWH your God is (continually) giving you as inheritance" (*futurum instans* / ongoing gift).

### Verse 17 — intensification + commandment-reference
1. **xYq0 (Q):** `כִּי־הַחֲרֵם תַּחֲרִימֵם הַחִתִּי וְהָאֱמֹרִי ...` — *ki* + **Hiphil InfAbs + Hiphil Impf.2ms+3mp finite** (cognate-intensification: "utterly devote them to destruction") + six gentilic direct objects.
2. **xQtX (Q):** `כַּאֲשֶׁר צִוְּךָ יְהוָה אֱלֹהֶיךָ` — comparative relative with Piel.Perf.3ms+2ms subject יְהוָה ("just-as YHWH your God commanded you").

### Verse 18 — purpose clause + weqatal result
1. **xYq0 (Q):** `לְמַעַן אֲשֶׁר לֹא־יְלַמְּדוּ אֶתְכֶם` — **compound purpose/negative-final conjunction** *lemaʿan asher* + negation + Piel.Impf.3mp + 2mp object. `Conj = לְמַעַן אֲשֶׁר`, `Nega = לֹא`, `Pred = יְלַמְּדוּ`, `Objc = אֶתְכֶם`.
2. **InfC (Q):** `לַעֲשׂוֹת כְּכֹל תּוֹעֲבֹתָם` — complementing InfCon expressing the content they would teach.
3. **xQt0 (Q):** `אֲשֶׁר עָשׂוּ לֵאלֹהֵיהֶם` — relative clause modifying תועבתם with Qal.Perf.3p.
4. **WQt0 (Q):** `וַחֲטָאתֶם לַיהוָה אֱלֹהֵיכֶם` — **weqatal 2mp** "and (so) you would sin against YHWH your God" — consecutive result / second-order purpose-clause outcome.

## Key Grammatical Features

### 1. Casuistic *ki*/*im* architecture of Block A (vv.10–14)
- **Main protasis:** `כִּי־תִקְרַב` (v.10) — *ki* + Qal.Impf.2ms, general casuistic case-opener ("when…").
- **Nested protases:** `אִם־שָׁלוֹם תַּעַנְךָ` (v.11, peace-accepted sub-case) and `וְאִם־לֹא תַשְׁלִים` (v.12, peace-refused sub-case).
- **Apodoses:** all **weqatal** (`וְקָרָאתָ`, `וְצַרְתָּ`, `וְהִכִּיתָ`, `וְאָכַלְתָּ`) — the normal legal-future continuation after the protasis ([syntax-waw-conjunction](../hebrew/syntax-waw-conjunction.md)).
- The Hiphil `תַשְׁלִים` in v.12 is denominative of שלום — idiomatic "make peace/agree to terms"; its Hebrew root connects directly to `שלום` in vv.10–11 (the peace-call) making the refusal lexically mirrored.

### 2. Hiphil *haḥarēm taḥarîmēm* (v.17) — infinitive-absolute intensification of חרם
- Form: **Hiphil InfAbs** `הַחֲרֵם` + **Hiphil Impf.2ms with 3mp object-suffix** `תַּחֲרִימֵם`.
- Construction: the InfAbs preceding the finite verb of the same root intensifies/asseverates the action — the classic paronomastic infinitive (*tautological infinitive*; GKC §113l–r; Joüon–Muraoka §123d–i; W–O §35.3.1).
- Standard gloss-range: "utterly destroy / completely devote-to-destruction / wholly ban."
- **Lexical meaning:** חרם (H2763) in Hiphil means "to place under a ban / devote irrevocably to YHWH, usually by destruction"; the action removes an object from profane/commercial use because it is consecrated-by-destruction.
- **Grammar-reference:** [infinitive-absolute-intensification](../hebrew/infinitive-absolute-intensification.md); [stem-hiphil](../hebrew/stem-hiphil.md)
- **Word studies:** [H2763-charam](../../word-studies/H2763-charam.md), [H2764-cherem](../../word-studies/H2764-cherem.md)
- Form-recognition note: the same Hiphil-paronomastic pattern recurs across the conquest ban (Deut 7:2; 1 Sam 15:3; Josh 11:20) and is the diagnostic formula for the *ḥerem*-war category distinct from ordinary-siege warfare.

### 3. Piel `לֹא תְחַיֶּה כָּל־נְשָׁמָה` (v.16) — negated Piel imperfect + universal-negated object
- Form: **Piel.Impf.2ms** of חיה ("live"). Piel here is **factitive**: "cause to live / keep alive / spare" (W–O §24.1–2; Joüon §52d).
- `כָּל־נְשָׁמָה` is a **universal-negative idiom**: noun.ms.Cst `כֹּל` + ms.Abs `נְשָׁמָה` under governing `לֹא` yields "not any breath / no breathing thing" (GKC §152b; Joüon §160k).
- `נְשָׁמָה` (H5397) is the lexeme for "breath (of life)"; it identifies the object of preservation precisely at the biological-respiratory level — literally "you shall not let any breath live."
- The verse therefore does not merely prohibit spoil-taking (the prohibition of Block A's v.14 spoil-category): it prohibits preservation-of-life itself.
- **Word study:** [H5397-nshamah](../../word-studies/H5397-nshamah.md); cross-language [TR-nshamah-pnoe](../../word-studies/TR-nshamah-pnoe.md).
- This is the lexical/semantic **antonym** to Block A's outcomes: v.11 `יִהְיוּ לְךָ לָמַס וַעֲבָדוּךָ` (all-alive-as-tributaries) and v.14 `רַק הַנָּשִׁים וְהַטַּף וְהַבְּהֵמָה ... תָּבֹז לָךְ` (women/children/cattle-alive-as-spoil).

### 4. The **רַק** exceptive adverb as structural hinge (vv.14, 16)
- `רַק` occurs **twice** in the passage with different scopes:
  - **v.14 רַ֣ק:** restrictive-adverb *within* Block A carving out the positive exception (non-males preserved as spoil) from the preceding prohibition-implication of v.13's "every male by sword."
  - **v.16 רַ֗ק:** clause-initial **adversative-restrictive** introducing Block B as an exception to Block A's whole preceding regime. This is the pivotal structural word of the entire statute.
- The lexical repetition is programmatic: the first רַק spares non-combatants in the distant case; the second רַק **withdraws** that sparing for the inheritance-territory case.
- In BHSA clause-tagging v.16's רַק forms part of the fronted [Adju] (`רַק מֵעָרֵי הָעַמִּים הָאֵלֶּה ... הָאֵלֶּה`), marking the **scope** of the negated verb — "only [= with-respect-to] cities of these peoples, you shall not keep alive any breath."
- This restricted scope is the grammatical basis for limiting the *ḥerem* statute geographically (the inheritance-land seven nations of v.17) and legally (not a general rule of conduct toward all foreigners).

### 5. The purpose clause `לְמַעַן אֲשֶׁר לֹא־יְלַמְּדוּ` (v.18) — compound negative-final conjunction + Piel imperfect
- **Compound conjunction:** `לְמַעַן אֲשֶׁר` (*lemaʿan ʾăšer*). The base preposition `לְמַעַן` + relative `אֲשֶׁר` yields a **purpose/final-clause marker** used commonly in Deuteronomy for stating divinely-intended outcomes (cf. Deut 4:40; 5:16; 6:2; 8:1; 11:21). BHSA glosses `לְמַעַן` as "because of / in order that" (preposition functioning conjunctively when paired with `אֲשֶׁר`); Joüon–Muraoka §169g classifies this as a standard final-clause marker; GKC §165b lists it among the "*final* particles."
- **Negation:** `לֹא` — the clause is **negative-purpose**: the goal is the non-occurrence of teaching/learning, not the occurrence of a positive goal.
- **Predicate:** **Piel.Impf.3mp** `יְלַמְּדוּ` (H3925 למד). Piel of למד = **"teach"** (causative-factitive: "cause [someone] to learn"); Qal of the same root = "learn." The 3mp subject is the implicit antecedent-set — the populations of vv.17 (Hittites … Jebusites).
- **Object:** 2mp suffix on את — `אֶתְכֶם` — "you (plural)"; the addressees are corporate-Israel.
- **Rendering:** "**in order that they should not teach you** to do after all their abominations" (KJV "That they teach you not to do…" — OE/KJV archaism where "that … not" = "lest they teach").
- **Construction-inherent logic (grammar-only):** the negative-purpose clause specifies **non-survival-as-teachers** as the intended outcome. Combined with v.16's `לֹא תְחַיֶּה כָּל־נְשָׁמָה` and v.17's `הַחֲרֵם תַּחֲרִימֵם`, it binds the absolute-destruction commandment to a stated rationale: prevention of cultic-pedagogical contagion. The grammar does not *expand* the ban (e.g., to later generations or to distant cities); it supplies a reason for restricting the ban to Block B's specific referent set.
- **Follow-up weqatal result clause:** `וַחֲטָאתֶם לַיהוָה אֱלֹהֵיכֶם` (v.18d) — Qal.Perf.2mp with consecutive waw. Clause-type **WQt0** ("and-you-shall-sin"). This extends the negative-purpose backward as second-order motivation: learning → doing-abominations → sinning-against-YHWH. The chain is **purpose-clause → InfCon-complement → relative → weqatal-result**, a four-clause cascade stating the endpoint at issue.
- **Grammar-reference:** [qal-imperfect](../hebrew/qal-imperfect.md) (for imperfect mechanics); [syntax-waw-conjunction](../hebrew/syntax-waw-conjunction.md) (for weqatal apodosis in v.18d).

### 6. Block-A participle `הַנִּמְצָא` (v.11) — Niphal middle-passive "the-being-found"
- Form: Art + **Verb.Niphal.Ptcp.ms.Abs** of מצא. BHSA parsing flags the gender as ambiguous-listed ("unknown") due to the attachment pattern, but contextually modifying `הָעָם` (ms.Abs) = "the people that is found (therein)."
- Niphal of מצא = **"be-found / be-present"** (middle-passive stative); an idiomatic marker of census/inventory-scope (cf. Gen 19:15; 1 Sam 13:15–16; Est 4:16).
- Function: restricts the object of tribute-service to the entire resident population, without remainder.
- **Grammar-reference:** [stem-niphal](../hebrew/stem-niphal.md)

### 7. Block-B participle `נֹתֵן` (v.16) — Qal active participle *futurum instans*
- Form: **Verb.Qal.Ptcp.ms.Abs** of נתן. PreC in Ptcp-clause ("which YHWH your-God is giving-you as inheritance").
- The participle frames the gift as **ongoing / imminent / covenantally-operative** at the moment of speech (Deut's Moab-plains address) — not a completed past gift.
- Contrast with v.13's `וּנְתָנָהּ` (Qal.Perf.3ms+3fs with consecutive waw, "and-he-gives/will-give-her") which is future-conditional on the city's war-making.
- The two נתן uses frame the two blocks with identical root: Block A = YHWH *will* give specific hostile-distant cities (contingent on war); Block B = YHWH *is-giving* the inheritance-territory (unconditional, covenantal).
- **Word study:** [H5414-nathan](../../word-studies/H5414-nathan.md)

### 8. Gentilic-adjective series (v.17) — definite + coordinated
- Six `Art+Adj.ms.Abs` gentilics: הַחִתִּי / הָאֱמֹרִי / הַכְּנַעֲנִי / הַפְּרִזִּי / הַחִוִּי / הַיְבוּסִי.
- The singular-with-article pattern is the Hebrew "collective-ethnic" construction (W–O §7.2.2, §14.3.2): "the Hittite(s)" = the Hittite-people as a category.
- The list is a **six-member variant** of the canonical conquest-list (full seven of Deut 7:1 includes הַגִּרְגָּשִׁי). The shorter six-member form here is paralleled at Ex 3:8, 17; 23:23; 33:2; 34:11.
- In the clause structure (Clause 1 of v.17), all six function as direct-object-apposition to the 3mp suffix on `תַּחֲרִימֵם` — they spell out who "them" are.

### 9. `כַּאֲשֶׁר צִוְּךָ יְהוָה אֱלֹהֶיךָ` (v.17b) — comparative relative of prior commandment
- Form: preposition `כְּ` + relative `אֲשֶׁר` + **Piel.Perf.3ms + 2ms suffix** `צִוְּךָ` (subject יהוה אלהיך).
- Piel of צוה (H6680) = "command/charge" (factitive only-in-Piel).
- The comparative-relative anchors this statute to a prior statement — specifically the earlier seven-nations command (Deut 7:1–2) — marking v.17 as re-affirmation, not new legislation. BHSA tags the clause as **xQtX** (x-qatal with fronted conjunction).

### 10. *Ḥîlūq* of categories: *ʿāmîm* (v.16) vs. *gōyîm* (v.15)
- v.15 uses `הַגּוֹיִם` (mp. of גוי, "nations"); v.16 uses `הָעַמִּים` (mp. of עם, "peoples"). The referent is the same (the seven inheritance-nations) but the lexical shift is deliberate:
  - גוי foregrounds political-ethnic identity ("nations");
  - עם foregrounds population-as-kin ("peoples").
- The pair permits the rhetorical contrast: distant *nations* (with whom peace-terms are possible) vs. inheritance *peoples* (with whom integration is categorically blocked on cultic-contagion grounds, v.18).

## Summary of the Two-Tier Structure (grammatical)

| Feature | Block A (vv.10–15, distant cities) | Block B (vv.16–18, inheritance cities) |
|---------|------------------------------------|----------------------------------------|
| Protasis | *ki* + Qal.Impf.2ms (v.10) | *raq* adversative (v.16) |
| Peace-offer | Mandatory: `וְקָרָאתָ ... לְשָׁלוֹם` | **Absent** — no peace-call |
| Conditional sub-cases | Yes: `אִם שלום ... ואם לא` (vv.11–12) | **None** — unconditional |
| Life of non-combatants | Preserved: `רַק הַנָּשִׁים וְהַטַּף ... תָּבֹז` (v.14) | Prohibited: `לֹא תְחַיֶּה כָּל־נְשָׁמָה` (v.16) |
| Outcome verb | Qal.Impf.2ms `תָּבֹז` (plunder) | **Hiphil InfAbs + Impf** `הַחֲרֵם תַּחֲרִימֵם` |
| Rationale | Land-distant → non-threat to cult | **Purpose clause** `לְמַעַן אֲשֶׁר לֹא־יְלַמְּדוּ` — cultic-contagion prevention |
| YHWH-gift verb | Perf.3ms `נָתַן` (v.14, past/completed) | **Ptcp** `נֹתֵן` (v.16, ongoing) |
| Ethnic category | `הַגּוֹיִם` (nations, v.15) | `הָעַמִּים` / gentilic-six (v.16–17) |

The grammar itself draws the boundary: morphology (Piel-vs-Hiphil-paronomastic), syntax (רַק adversative, purpose clause with *lemaʿan asher*), and lexis (`שלום` / `חיה` vs. `חרם` / `נשמה`) cooperate to specify **two distinct legal regimes keyed to two distinct referent-sets**. The *lemaʿan-asher* purpose clause of v.18 is the grammar's own statement of why.
