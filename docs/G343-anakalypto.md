## Lexicon Definition

From ἀνά (G303, in the sense of reversal) and καλύπτω (G2572); to unveil. KJV renderings: "open," "untaken away."

The word denotes the removal of a covering or veil — the reversal of the act of concealment. Both NT uses occur in Paul's veil-of-Moses discourse (2 Cor 3:7–18), where the veil over the old covenant and over the face are contrasted with unveiled access in Christ.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Unveiling the Old Covenant — Spiritual Perception Blocked
- **2 Cor 3:14** — "But their minds were blinded: for until this day remaineth the same vail untaken away (mē anakalyptomenon) in the reading of the old testament; which vail is done away in Christ."
  - Present passive participle with negative μή: the veil is "not being unveiled" — the ongoing state of imperception when the old covenant is read apart from Christ.

### 2. Unveiled Beholding — Transformed Access
- **2 Cor 3:18** — "But we all, with open (anakekalymmenō) face beholding as in a glass the glory of the Lord, are changed into the same image from glory to glory, even as by the Spirit of the Lord."
  - Perfect passive participle: the veil has been removed, and the completed state of unveiled access enables direct beholding of divine glory, resulting in progressive transformation.
