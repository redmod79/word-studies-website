## Lexicon Definition

A primitive root (compare H1794 dakah); to crumble; transitively, to bruise (literally or figuratively): beat to pieces, break (in pieces), bruise, contrite, crush, destroy, humble, oppress, smite.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Physical Destruction / Crushing
- **Job 4:19** — "which are crushed before the moth?" — human frailty, easily destroyed
- **Job 5:4** — "they are crushed in the gate" — the fatherless denied justice
- **Isa 19:10** — "they shall be broken in the purposes thereof" — Egypt's infrastructure destroyed
- **Job 34:25** — "he overturneth them in the night, so that they are destroyed" — God's sovereign judgment on the wicked

### 2. Oppressive Crushing of the Vulnerable
- **Isa 3:15** — "ye beat my people to pieces, and grind the faces of the poor" — social oppression condemned
- **Pro 22:22** — "neither oppress the afflicted in the gate" — prohibition against exploiting the helpless
- **Psa 94:5** — "They break in pieces thy people, O LORD, and afflict thine heritage" — enemies crushing God's people
- **Psa 143:3** — "the enemy hath persecuted my soul; he hath smitten my life down to the ground" — personal persecution

### 3. Messianic Suffering (Vicarious Crushing)
- **Isa 53:5** — "he was bruised for our iniquities" — Pual participle: the Servant is the one crushed
- **Isa 53:10** — "it pleased the LORD to bruise him" — Piel infinitive construct: YHWH is the agent of crushing; the Servant is the object. The Piel intensive stem conveys deliberate, thorough crushing as a purposeful divine act

### 4. Spiritual Contrition / Humility
- **Isa 57:15** — "with him also that is of a contrite and humble spirit, to revive the spirit of the humble, and to revive the heart of the contrite ones" — God dwelling with the dakka (adjective) and the nidka'im (Niphal participle)
- **Psa 34:19** — "Many are the afflictions of the righteous: but the LORD delivereth him out of them all" — dakka as adjective, "contrite ones," used for those near to God
- **Jer 44:10** — "They are not humbled even unto this day" — Pual: failure to be crushed/humbled before God

### 5. Divine Warrior Action
- **Psa 72:4** — "he shall... break in pieces the oppressor" — the messianic king crushing injustice
- **Psa 89:10** — "Thou hast broken Rahab in pieces, as one that is slain" — YHWH's victory over chaos
- **Job 6:9** — "that it would please God to destroy me" — Job invoking God's crushing power upon himself
