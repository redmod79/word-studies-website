# H4725 — מָקוֹם (mâqôwm)

## Core Data
- **Strong's:** H4725
- **Original:** מָקוֹם
- **Transliteration:** mâqôwm (māqôm); also the feminine forms מְקוֹמָה / מְקֹמָה
- **Pronunciation:** maw-kome
- **Part of Speech:** Masculine noun
- **Root:** קוּם (qûm, H6965) — "to arise, stand, stand up." *Māqôm* is a noun of place built on the m-prefix pattern from *qûm*: literally a "standing-place," "spot where something stands / takes its stand."
- **Occurrences:** 379 unique verses (471 tokens) in OT
- **Books:** 33 (nearly the entire OT)
- **First Occurrence:** Gen 1:9 — "Let the waters under the heaven be gathered together unto one place (māqôm)"
- **Hapax Legomenon:** No (one of the most common locative nouns in Hebrew)

## Lexicon Definition

From קוּם (*qûm*, "to stand, arise"); properly, *a standing*, i.e. a spot; but used widely of a locality (general or specific); also (figuratively) of a condition (of body or mind):—country, home, open, place, room, space, whither(-soever).

The semantic core is "where something stands" — the spot a thing occupies. Unlike H4349 *māḵôn* (from *kûn*, "to be firmly established"), *māqôm* does not inherently carry the nuance of divine foundation or stability of establishment. It is the generic, all-purpose locative: any location can be a *māqôm*; only a founded / fixed site is properly a *māḵôn*. See Related Words below for the load-bearing etymological contrast.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Generic spatial location (neutral locative)
The default, untheologized use — any spot, any place.
- **Gen 1:9** — "let the waters... be gathered together unto one place (māqôm)"
- **Neh 2:14** — "there was no place (māqôm) for the beast that was under me to pass"
- **Pro 15:3** — "The eyes of the LORD are in every place (məqôm)"

### 2. Named / commemorative place (place-naming formula)
*Māqôm* is the standard noun in the idiom "he called the name of the place X."
- **Gen 28:19** — "he called the name of that place (māqôm) Bethel"
- **Num 11:3** — "he called the name of the place (māqôm) Taberah"
- **Jdg 2:5** — "they called the name of that place (māqôm) Bochim"
- **1 Chr 13:11** — "that place (māqôm) is called Perezuzza"

### 3. "The place" of sacrificial / cultic action
The site designated for a ritual or for cultic personnel — often the altar-precinct or a "clean place."
- **Lev 1:16** — "cast it beside the altar on the east part, by the place (məqôm) of the ashes"
- **Lev 4:12** — "carry forth without the camp unto a clean place (māqôm)"
- **Lev 4:24** — "in the place (məqôm) where they kill the burnt offering"
- **Lev 6:16** — "in the holy place (māqôm qādôš)"

### 4. "The place which the LORD shall choose" (Deuteronomic formula)
Deuteronomy repeatedly uses *māqôm* for the future central sanctuary — but describes it as the place *YHWH will choose*, not as a pre-existing founded site. The emphasis is on divine election of a location, not on architectural foundation.
- **Deu 12:5** — "the place (māqôm) which the LORD your God shall choose"
- **Deu 12:11** — "there shall be a place (māqôm) which the LORD your God shall choose"
- **Deu 12:14, 12:18, 12:21, 12:26; 14:23–25; 15:20; 16:2, 16:6–7, 16:11, 16:15–16; 17:8, 17:10; 18:6; 26:2; 31:11** — the refrain runs through the book.

### 5. The sanctuary / holy place (cultic site, overlap with H4720)
Concrete locations of worship — both the Jerusalem temple precincts and local altars.
- **Exo 3:5** — "the place (māqôm) whereon thou standest is holy ground"
- **1 Ki 8:29–30** — "toward the place (māqôm) of which thou hast said, My name shall be there" (Solomon's dedication)
- **1 Ki 8:6 / 2 Chr 5:7** — "the priests brought in the ark... unto his place (məqômô), into the oracle"
- **Ezr 9:8** — "a nail in his holy place (məqôm qodšô)"

### 6. "His place" / "her place" — fixed station or origin (from which one departs or to which one returns)
A person's or thing's native location, often in the idiom "return to his place" or "out of his place."
- **1 Sam 3:2** — "Eli was laid down in his place (məqômô)"
- **Jdg 9:55** — "they departed every man unto his place (məqômô)"
- **Job 7:10** — "neither shall his place (məqômô) know him any more"
- **Hos 5:15** — "I will go and return to my place (məqômî)" (YHWH's withdrawal)
- **Mic 1:3** — "the LORD cometh forth out of his place (məqômô)"
- **Ecc 1:5** — "the sun... hasteth to his place (məqômô) where he arose"
- **Isa 13:13** — "the earth shall remove out of her place (məqômâh)"

### 7. Judgment / displacement contexts (prophetic)
Being removed from one's *māqôm* is a standard figure for judgment — an entity uprooted from where it stood.
- **Nah 1:8** — "an utter end of the place (məqômâh) thereof"
- **Zep 1:4** — "I will cut off... from this place (māqôm)"
- **Jer 7:3, 7:6–7** — "cause you to dwell in this place (māqôm)" // threat of expulsion
- **Joe 3:7** — "I will raise them out of the place (māqôm) whither ye have sold them"

### 8. Figurative / idiomatic — condition, room, opportunity
Extended senses: mental or social "space."
- **Ecc 3:16** — "the place (məqôm) of judgment, that wickedness was there"
- **Ecc 10:4** — "leave not thy place (məqômekā)" (idiom: don't abandon your station)
- **Est 4:14** — "enlargement and deliverance arise... from another place (māqôm)" (= source)

### Easily Confused via LXX Overlap
In the LXX, the range *māqôm / māḵôn / māʻôn / nāweh* is often collapsed into a narrow Greek set (*τόπος*, *κατοικητήριον*, *μονή*), flattening the Hebrew distinctions. Translators must be alert to the Hebrew underlying the Greek to preserve the *qûm* / *kûn* distinction.
