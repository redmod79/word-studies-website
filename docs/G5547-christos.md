## Lexicon Definition

From G5548 (chriō); *anointed*, i.e. the Messiah, an epithet of Jesus:—Christ.

The word is a verbal adjective (perfect passive participle of χρίω) that became a proper title. It is the Greek translation of the Hebrew H4899 מָשִׁיחַ (*mashiyach*, Messiah). In the Gospels and Acts it still carries titular force ("the Christ" = the Anointed One); in the Pauline letters it functions as a proper name or compound name ("Jesus Christ," "Christ Jesus").

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Disputed Messianic Title — "Art Thou the Christ?"
The Synoptics and John use *Christos* primarily as the contested title: Is Jesus the expected Anointed One?

- **Mat 16:16** — "Thou art the Christ, the Son of the living God."
- **John 1:41** — "We have found the Messias, which is, being interpreted, the Christ."
- **John 20:31** — "These are written, that ye might believe that Jesus is the Christ, the Son of God."
- **Acts 18:28** — "He mightily convinced the Jews... shewing by the scriptures that Jesus was Christ."

### 2. The Crucified and Risen Christ
Paul and the apostolic preaching define *Christos* by the events of crucifixion and resurrection — "Christ died… Christ is risen."

- **1 Cor 1:23** — "We preach Christ crucified, unto the Jews a stumblingblock, and unto the Greeks foolishness."
- **1 Cor 15:3–4** — "Christ died for our sins according to the scriptures; and… rose again the third day."
- **Rom 5:8** — "While we were yet sinners, Christ died for us."
- **Acts 17:3** — "Christ must needs have suffered, and risen again from the dead; and… this Jesus… is Christ."

### 3. "In Christ" — Union, New Identity, Participation
Paul's distinctive *en Christō* formula (80+ occurrences) expresses incorporation into Christ's death, resurrection, and body.

- **2 Cor 5:17** — "If any man be in Christ, he is a new creature: old things are passed away."
- **Rom 8:1** — "There is therefore now no condemnation to them which are in Christ Jesus."
- **Gal 2:20** — "I am crucified with Christ: nevertheless I live; yet not I, but Christ liveth in me."
- **Eph 1:10** — "That in… the fulness of times he might gather together in one all things in Christ."

### 4. Christ as Head, Lord, and Cosmic Authority
Christ holds authority over the church, creation, and the cosmos; seated at God's right hand.

- **1 Cor 11:3** — "The head of every man is Christ; and the head of the woman is the man; and the head of Christ is God."
- **Eph 5:23** — "Christ is the head of the church: and he is the saviour of the body."
- **Col 3:1** — "If ye then be risen with Christ, seek those things which are above, where Christ sitteth on the right hand of God."
- **Rev 11:15** — "The kingdoms of this world are become the kingdoms of our Lord, and of his Christ."

### 5. Christ's Suffering as Pattern
Christ's suffering is presented as both atoning and exemplary — the template for discipleship.

- **1 Pet 2:21** — "Christ also suffered for us, leaving us an example, that ye should follow his steps."
- **1 Pet 3:18** — "Christ also hath once suffered for sins, the just for the unjust, that he might bring us to God."
- **Heb 9:28** — "Christ was once offered to bear the sins of many; and unto them that look for him shall he appear the second time."
- **Phil 3:10** *(Phl)* — "That I may know him, and the power of his resurrection, and the fellowship of his sufferings."

### 6. The Coming Christ — Eschatological Expectation
Christ's return is consistently linked to his title.

- **1 Cor 15:23** — "Every man in his own order: Christ the firstfruits; afterward they that are Christ's at his coming."
- **1 Thess 4:16** — "The Lord himself shall descend from heaven… and the dead in Christ shall rise first."
- **Titus 2:13** — "Looking for that blessed hope, and the glorious appearing of the great God and our Saviour Jesus Christ."
- **2 Thess 2:1** — "By the coming of our Lord Jesus Christ, and by our gathering together unto him."
