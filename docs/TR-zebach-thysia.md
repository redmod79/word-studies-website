## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same across testaments:**
- The word chain keeps the sacrificial act/victim field: Hebrew zebach and Greek thysia both name sacrifice as ritual act and offered victim.
- Meal/altar participation remains visible: zebach collocates with eating and peace offerings; thysia appears in 1 Cor 10:18 with altar participation.
- Prophetic critique carries forward through the thysia rendering of "mercy, not sacrifice" (Matt 9:13 using Hos 6:6 wording).

**What shifts:**
- Greek thysia is less taxonomically specific than Hebrew sacrificial vocabulary; it can render zebach, minchah, shelem, olah, necek, and qorban.
- NT usage expands the term into ethical/metaphorical applications such as "living sacrifice" (Rom 12:1) and praise/beneficence sacrifice language in Hebrews.
