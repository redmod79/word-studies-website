## Semantic Categories

### 1. Sound/Wholesome Doctrine (Apostolic Teaching as Standard)
- **1Ti 1:10** — "...contrary to sound doctrine"
- **2Ti 4:3** — "...they will not endure sound doctrine"
- **Tit 1:9** — "...able by sound doctrine both to exhort and to convince the gainsayers"
- **Tit 2:1** — "...speak thou the things which become sound doctrine"

The phrase ὑγιαίνουσα διδασκαλία ("sound/healthy doctrine," G5198 + G1319) appears 5 times and is distinctive to the Pastorals. It frames doctrine as something that can be healthy or sick.

### 2. Doctrine as Object of Devoted Attention
- **1Ti 4:13** — "...give attendance to reading, to exhortation, to doctrine"
- **1Ti 4:16** — "Take heed unto thyself, and unto the doctrine; continue in them"
- **Rom 12:7** — "...he that teacheth, on teaching"
- **1Ti 5:17** — "...especially they who labour in the word and doctrine"

Doctrine here is something to be pursued, attended to, and labored in — a sustained object of ministerial effort.

### 3. Doctrine as Nourishment
- **1Ti 4:6** — "...nourished up in the words of faith and of good doctrine, whereunto thou hast attained"

The verb ἐντρέφω ("nourished up," G1789) explicitly pairs with didaskalia, treating doctrine as sustaining food. This is the only verse in the NT using G1789.

### 4. Scripture's Doctrinal Purpose
- **2Ti 3:16** — "All scripture is given by inspiration of God, and is profitable for doctrine, for reproof, for correction, for instruction in righteousness"
- **Rom 15:4** — "...whatsoever things were written aforetime were written for our learning"

Doctrine as the informational content that Scripture yields — its teaching payload.

### 5. False/Human Doctrines
- **Mat 15:9** — "...teaching for doctrines the commandments of men"
- **Mar 7:7** — "...teaching for doctrines the commandments of men"
- **Col 2:22** — "...after the commandments and doctrines of men"
- **1Ti 4:1** — "...giving heed to seducing spirits, and doctrines of devils"
- **Eph 4:14** — "...carried about with every wind of doctrine"

When qualified by human or demonic origin, didaskalia denotes false teaching. The plural form (didaskalias) appears in all five of these verses.

### 6. Doctrine Adorned / Honored
- **Tit 2:10** — "...that they may adorn the doctrine of God our Saviour in all things"
- **1Ti 6:1** — "...that the name of God and his doctrine be not blasphemed"
- **Tit 2:7** — "...in doctrine shewing uncorruptness, gravity, sincerity"

Doctrine can be adorned (cosmetic metaphor) or blasphemed — it carries the reputation of God.

### 7. Paul's Personal Doctrine
- **2Ti 3:10** — "But thou hast fully known my doctrine, manner of life, purpose, faith..."
- **1Ti 6:3** — "...consent not to wholesome words, even the words of our Lord Jesus Christ, and to the doctrine which is according to godliness"

Didaskalia as the body of content an apostle transmits — identifiable, followable, and tied to godly living.
