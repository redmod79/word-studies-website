## Semantic Categories

### 1. Financial Debt Owed to a Creditor
- **Mat 18:32** — "O thou wicked servant, I forgave thee all that debt (opheilē), because thou desiredst me:" — The master cancels the full financial liability of the servant in the parable of the unforgiving debtor.

### 2. Civic Obligation / What Is Owed to Authorities
- **Rom 13:7** — "Render therefore to all their dues (opheilas): tribute to whom tribute is due; custom to whom custom; fear to whom fear; honour to whom honour." — Plural form denoting the various obligations citizens owe to governing authorities: taxes, customs, respect, honor.

### 3. Marital Duty / Conjugal Obligation
- **1 Cor 7:3** — "Let the husband render unto the wife due (opheilēn) benevolence: and likewise also the wife unto the husband." — The obligation of mutual physical affection within marriage, framed as a reciprocal debt each spouse owes the other.
