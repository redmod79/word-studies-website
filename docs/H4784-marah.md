## Semantic Categories

### 1. Defying God's command, word, or voice
- **Num 20:24** — Aaron is barred from the land "because ye rebelled against my word."
- **Num 27:14** — Moses and Aaron "rebelled against my commandment" at Meribah.
- **Deut 1:26** — Israel "rebelled against the commandment of the LORD your God."
- **1 Kgs 13:21** — the man of God is condemned because he "hast disobeyed the mouth of the LORD."

### 2. Describing a stubborn or rebellious person
- **Num 20:10** — Moses addresses the congregation, "Hear now, ye rebels."
- **Deut 21:18** — the legal case of "a stubborn and rebellious son."
- **Ps 78:8** — "a stubborn and rebellious generation."
- **Jer 5:23** — "this people hath a revolting and a rebellious heart."

### 3. Historical provocation in the wilderness and national story
- **Deut 9:7** — Israel "provokedst the LORD thy God to wrath in the wilderness."
- **Ps 78:17** — they sinned more "by provoking the most High in the wilderness."
- **Ps 106:7** — the fathers "provoked [him] at the sea, even at the Red sea."
- **Ezek 20:13** — "the house of Israel rebelled against me in the wilderness."

### 4. Rebellion remembered in confession and lament
- **Lam 1:18** — "The LORD is righteous; for I have rebelled against his commandment."
- **Lam 1:20** — "I have grievously rebelled."
- **Lam 3:42** — "We have transgressed and have rebelled."
- **Neh 9:26** — "they were disobedient, and rebelled against thee."

### 5. Rebellion bringing judgment or reversal
- **Isa 63:10** — "they rebelled, and vexed his holy Spirit: therefore he was turned to be their enemy."
- **Jer 4:17** — invaders surround Jerusalem "because she hath been rebellious against me."
- **Hos 13:16** — Samaria becomes desolate "for she hath rebelled against her God."
- **Ezek 5:6** — Jerusalem "hath changed my judgments into wickedness more than the nations."

### 6. Bitter grief or provocation rather than overt revolt
- **2 Kgs 14:26** — Israel's affliction "was very bitter."
- **1 Sam 30:6** — the people's soul "was grieved."
- **Job 17:2** — Job speaks of continuing "in their provocation."

### 7. Obedience expressed by negating the verb
- **Isa 50:5** — "I was not rebellious, neither turned away back."
- **Ps 105:28** — "they rebelled not against his word."
