## Semantic Categories

### 1. Personal Lack in Discipleship
The word can identify a remaining deficiency in an otherwise observant life.
- **Matt 19:20** — "All these things have I kept from my youth up: what lack I yet?"
- **Mark 10:21** — "One thing thou lackest: go thy way, sell whatsoever thou hast..."

### 2. Material Want, Supply, and Destitution
G5302 often names shortage or deprivation of ordinary resources.
- **Luke 15:14** — "he began to be in want."
- **John 2:3** — "And when they wanted wine..."
- **2 Cor 11:9** — "that which was lacking to me the brethren which came from Macedonia supplied"
- **Phil 4:12** — "both to abound and to suffer need."

### 3. Comparative Inferiority or Disadvantage
The word can mark being "behind," "worse," or lacking relative to another state or group.
- **1 Cor 1:7** — "ye come behind in no gift"
- **1 Cor 8:8** — "if we eat not, are we the worse"
- **2 Cor 11:5** — "I was not a whit behind the very chiefest apostles"
- **2 Cor 12:11** — "in nothing am I behind the very chiefest apostles"

### 4. Falling Short of a Stated Divine Gift, Promise, or Standard
In these texts the lack is measured against glory, promised rest, or grace.
- **Rom 3:23** — "For all have sinned, and come short of the glory of God"
- **Heb 4:1** — "lest... any of you should seem to come short of it"
- **Heb 12:15** — "Looking diligently lest any man fail of the grace of God"
