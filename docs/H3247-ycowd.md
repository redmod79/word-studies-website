## Semantic Categories

### 1. Base of the Sacrificial Altar
The most frequent usage — the physical base/bottom where blood is poured out during offerings. Appears exclusively in Leviticus and Exodus.

- **Exo 29:12** — "thou shalt take of the blood of the bullock, and put it upon the horns of the altar with thy finger, and pour all the blood beside the bottom of the altar"
- **Lev 4:7** — "shall pour all the blood of the bullock at the bottom of the altar of the burnt offering"
- **Lev 5:9** — "the rest of the blood shall be wrung out at the bottom of the altar: it is a sin offering"
- **Lev 8:15** — "poured the blood at the bottom of the altar, and sanctified it, to make reconciliation upon it"

### 2. Foundation of a Building or City (Literal)
The structural base of walls, buildings, or cities — often in contexts of destruction where foundations are exposed or demolished.

- **Eze 13:14** — "I will break down the wall... so that the foundation thereof shall be discovered"
- **Eze 30:4** — "they shall take away her multitude, and her foundations shall be broken down"
- **Mic 1:6** — "I will pour down the stones thereof into the valley, and I will discover the foundations thereof"
- **2Ch 23:5** — "a third part shall be at the gate of the foundation"

### 3. Foundation of a Person or People (Metaphorical)
Used figuratively for the basis or ground of a person's existence, whether stable or destroyed.

- **Pro 10:25** — "the righteous is an everlasting foundation"
- **Job 4:19** — "them that dwell in houses of clay, whose foundation is in the dust"
- **Job 22:16** — "whose foundation was overflown with a flood"
- **Hab 3:13** — "thou woundedst the head out of the house of the wicked, by discovering the foundation unto the neck"

### 4. Foundation as Object of Judgment
In prophetic usage, the uncovering or destroying of foundations signifies total divine judgment — nothing remains hidden or intact.

- **Lam 4:11** — "hath kindled a fire in Zion, and it hath devoured the foundations thereof"
- **Psa 137:7** — "who said, Rase it, rase it, even to the foundation thereof"

### 5. Repairing / Restoration
One occurrence uses yesod in the sense of foundational repair work on the temple.

- **2Ch 24:27** — "the repairing of the house of God"
