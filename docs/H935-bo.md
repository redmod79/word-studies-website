## Lexicon Definition

A primitive root; to go or come (in a wide variety of applications):— abide, apply, attain, be, befall, besiege, bring (forth, in, into, to pass), call, carry, certainly, (cause, let, thing for) to come (against, in, out, upon, to pass), depart, doubtless again, eat, employ, (cause to) enter (in, into, -tering, -trance, -try), be fallen, fetch, follow, get, give, go (down, in, to war), grant, have, indeed, (in-) vade, lead, lift (up), mention, pull in, put, resort, run (down), send, set, (well) stricken (in age), surely, take (in), way.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Simple Physical Motion — come / go / enter
The base sense, covering ordinary arrival, departure, and entering a location.
- **Gen 7:1** — "Come (bôʾ) thou and all thy house into the ark"
- **Exo 3:11** — "Who am I, that I should go (bôʾ) unto Pharaoh"
- **Ruth 1:22** — "they came (bôʾ) to Bethlehem in the beginning of barley harvest"
- **1 Sam 1:19** — "came (bôʾ) to their house to Ramah"

### 2. Causative — bring, lead in, carry in (Hiphil)
Used for transporting people, goods, sacrifices, or news into a place.
- **Gen 4:3** — "Cain brought (bôʾ Hiphil) of the fruit of the ground an offering unto the LORD"
- **Lev 2:2** — "he shall bring (bôʾ) it to Aaron's sons the priests"
- **Exo 26:33** — "thou shalt bring in (bôʾ) thither within the vail the ark"
- **Dan 1:2** — "he brought (bôʾ) the vessels into the treasure house of his god"

### 3. Angelic or Divine Approach / Appearance
A person, messenger, or supernatural being arrives on the scene — often freighted with revelation.
- **Gen 32:6** — "the messengers returned to Jacob, saying, We came (bôʾ) to thy brother Esau"
- **Job 1:6** — "the sons of God came (bôʾ) to present themselves before the LORD, and Satan came (bôʾ) also"
- **Dan 8:17** — "So he came near (wayyāḇōʾ) where I stood"  (Gabriel's approach to Daniel)
- **Dan 9:22** — "he informed me, and talked with me, and said, O Daniel, I am now come forth (bôʾ) to give thee skill"
- **Dan 10:12** — "thy words were heard, and I am come (bôʾ) for thy words"
- **Ezek 1:4** — "a whirlwind came (bôʾ) out of the north"

### 4. The Coming of a Day, Time, or Event
bôʾ is the verb of choice for time-events arriving, especially eschatological or judgment days.
- **Joel 1:15** — "the day of the LORD [is] at hand, and as a destruction from the Almighty shall it come (bôʾ)"
- **Joel 2:1** — "for the day of the LORD cometh (bôʾ), for it is nigh at hand"
- **Amos 4:2** — "the days shall come (bôʾ) upon you, that he will take you away with hooks"
- **Mal 3:1** — "the Lord, whom ye seek, shall suddenly come (bôʾ) to his temple... behold, he shall come (bôʾ)"
- **Mal 4:5** — "before the coming (bôʾ) of the great and dreadful day of the LORD"
- **Isa 13:9** — "the day of the LORD cometh (bôʾ), cruel both with wrath and fierce anger"

### 5. Prophetic Fulfillment — "come to pass"
Used for the actualization of a word, sign, promise, or threat.
- **Deu 13:2** — "the sign or the wonder come to pass (bôʾ)"
- **Jos 23:14** — "not one thing hath failed of all the good things which the LORD... spake concerning you; all are come to pass (bôʾ)"
- **1 Sam 9:6** — "all that he saith cometh surely to pass (bôʾ)"
- **Isa 42:9** — "new things do I declare: before they spring forth I tell you of them" — fulfillment language in Isaiah often pairs with bôʾ for what "is come to pass"
- **Jer 32:24** — "that which thou hast spoken is come to pass (bôʾ); and, behold, thou seest it"

### 6. The Word of the LORD Coming
A prophetic formula: divine speech "arrives" at the prophet.
- **Jer 1:2** — "The word of the LORD came (bôʾ) unto him in the days of Josiah" (construction varies; hāyâh also common)
- **Ezek 1:3** — formula for prophetic call
- **1 Sam 3:7** — "neither was the word of the LORD yet revealed unto him" (related formula)
- **Hag 1:1** — "came (bôʾ) the word of the LORD by Haggai the prophet"

### 7. Idiomatic — Besiege / Invade / Attain / Be Stricken in Age
Lexicon reflects specialized idioms produced by bôʾ + preposition or object.
- **Gen 24:1** — "Abraham was old, [and] well stricken in age (bôʾ bayyāmîm, lit. 'coming into the days')"
- **Deu 20:19** — "when thou shalt besiege (bôʾ) a city a long time"
- **2 Ki 24:10** — "Nebuchadnezzar... came up (bôʾ) against Jerusalem, and the city was besieged"
- **1 Ki 13:33** — "he consecrated him... and he became (bôʾ) [one of] the priests"

### 8. Sexual Euphemism — "go in unto"
A euphemism for sexual intercourse when bôʾ takes an animate object with ʼel.
- **Gen 6:4** — "the sons of God came in (bôʾ) unto the daughters of men"
- **Gen 16:2** — "go in (bôʾ), I pray thee, unto my maid"
- **Gen 38:18** — "he gave [it] her, and came in (bôʾ) unto her"
