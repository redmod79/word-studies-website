## Semantic Categories

### 1. Physical Turning Aside / Changing Direction
The literal, spatial sense — a person physically turns off a path or toward a location.
- **Exo 3:3–4** — "I will now turn aside, and see this great sight... when the LORD saw that he turned aside to see"
- **Judg 4:18** — "Turn in, my lord, turn in to me; fear not. And when he had turned in unto her"
- **Judg 19:12** — "We will not turn aside hither into the city of a stranger"
- **Ruth 4:1** — "Ho, such a one! turn aside, sit down here. And he turned aside, and sat down."

### 2. Departing / Leaving a Place or Person
Movement away from a location or from someone's presence.
- **Gen 49:10** — "The sceptre shall not depart from Judah"
- **1 Sam 16:14** — "But the Spirit of the LORD departed from Saul"
- **1 Sam 28:15** — "God is departed from me, and answereth me no more"
- **Num 12:10** — "the cloud departed from off the tabernacle"

### 3. Removing / Taking Away (Hiphil — Causative)
The dominant Hiphil sense — actively removing objects, people, or conditions.
- **Exo 8:8** — "Intreat the LORD, that he may take away the frogs"
- **Isa 6:7** — "thine iniquity is taken away, and thy sin purged"
- **Ezek 36:26** — "I will take away the stony heart out of your flesh"
- **Zech 3:4** — "Take away the filthy garments from him"

### 4. Putting Away Idols and Foreign Worship
A specialized sub-category of removal, prominent in Deuteronomistic and reform contexts.
- **Gen 35:2** — "Put away the strange gods that are among you"
- **1 Sam 7:3–4** — "put away the strange gods and Ashtaroth... the children of Israel did put away Baalim"
- **2 Ki 18:4** — "He removed the high places, and brake the images, and cut down the groves"
- **2 Chr 33:15** — "he took away the strange gods, and the idol out of the house of the LORD"

### 5. Moral/Spiritual Departure — Turning from God's Way
Used for apostasy, rebellion, or departing from God's commands. Predominantly Qal.
- **Exo 32:8** — "They have turned aside quickly out of the way which I commanded them"
- **Deut 9:12** — "thy people... have turned aside quickly out of the way"
- **2 Ki 10:29** — "from the sins of Jeroboam... Jehu departed not from after them"
- **Dan 9:5** — "We have sinned... even by departing from thy precepts"

### 6. Departing from Evil — Wisdom and Righteousness
A distinct positive usage where sur means avoiding or forsaking evil. Heavy in Proverbs and Job.
- **Job 1:1** — "that man was perfect and upright, and one that feared God, and eschewed evil"
- **Prov 3:7** — "fear the LORD, and depart from evil"
- **Prov 16:6** — "by the fear of the LORD men depart from evil"
- **Prov 16:17** — "The highway of the upright is to depart from evil"

### 7. Ritual/Sacrificial Removal
Technical priestly usage for removing fat, crop, or garments in sacrificial procedure.
- **Lev 1:16** — "he shall pluck away his crop with his feathers"
- **Lev 3:4** — "the caul above the liver, with the kidneys, it shall he take away"
- **Lev 4:31** — "he shall take away all the fat thereof"
- **2 Chr 35:12** — "they removed the burnt offerings"
