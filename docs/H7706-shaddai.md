## Lexicon Definition

Strong's (BDB-linked): "the Almighty" — rendered by KJV exclusively as "Almighty." BDB lists it as a divine name/epithet, commonly appearing in the compound אֵל שַׁדַּי (*El Shaddai*, "God Almighty") in the patriarchal narratives, and as the stand-alone שַׁדַּי in poetry (especially Job).

### Etymology — Unresolved Scholarly Debate

The root is genuinely contested. Major proposals:

1. **From שָׁדַד (shadad, H7703) — "to deal violently, destroy, devastate."** Yields "the Destroyer" or "the Overpowerer." Supported by the paronomasia in Isa 13:6 and Joel 1:15 — "as a destruction (שֹׁד, *shod*) from the Almighty (שַׁדַּי) shall it come" — where Shaddai is explicitly linked with destroying power.
2. **From שַׁד (shad, H7699) — "breast."** Yields "the Breasted One" / "the Nourisher." Appealed to in Gen 49:25 ("blessings of the breasts, and of the womb") where *Shaddai* and *shadayim* (breasts) occur in proximity.
3. **From a Semitic root meaning "mountain" (Akkadian *šadû*).** Yields "God of the Mountain" — fits the epithet parallel to other ancient Near Eastern mountain-god titles.
4. **From שׁ + דַּי — "the one who is sufficient" / "self-sufficient."** Rabbinic/Talmudic derivation (she-dai, "who is enough"). Reflected in LXX renderings *hikanos* ("sufficient") 5x.
5. **From an unused root meaning "to be powerful."** Generic "the Almighty" — the traditional rendering reflected in Vulgate *Omnipotens* and KJV.

No single etymology commands consensus. Translation tradition has settled on "Almighty" despite the root being uncertain.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Patriarchal Covenant Name — El Shaddai
Compound אֵל שַׁדַּי (El Shaddai) is used in every Genesis occurrence and in Exo 6:3. Always in the context of covenant, fruitfulness/multiplication, and land promise.
- **Gen 17:1** — "I am El Shaddai; walk before me, and be thou perfect" (covenant with Abraham)
- **Gen 28:3** — "God Almighty bless thee, and make thee fruitful, and multiply thee" (Isaac blesses Jacob)
- **Gen 35:11** — "I am El Shaddai: be fruitful and multiply; a nation and a company of nations shall be of thee"
- **Gen 43:14** — "God Almighty give you mercy before the man" (Jacob sending sons to Egypt)
- **Gen 48:3** — "El Shaddai appeared unto me at Luz... and blessed me"
- **Gen 49:25** — "by the Almighty, who shall bless thee with blessings of heaven above, blessings of the deep that lieth under, blessings of the breasts (shadayim), and of the womb"
- **Exo 6:3** — "I appeared unto Abraham, unto Isaac, and unto Jacob, by [the name of] El Shaddai, but by my name JEHOVAH (YHWH) was I not known to them"

### 2. Supreme Judicial / Sovereign Power (Job)
Shaddai as the ultimate court of appeal, the judge whose justice cannot be subverted, whose scrutiny is inescapable.
- **Job 5:17** — "despise not thou the chastening of the Almighty"
- **Job 8:3** — "doth the Almighty pervert justice?"
- **Job 11:7** — "canst thou find out the Almighty unto perfection?"
- **Job 34:12** — "neither will the Almighty pervert judgment"
- **Job 37:23** — "Touching the Almighty, we cannot find him out: he is excellent in power, and in judgment"

### 3. Object of Petition, Worship, or Rejection
Shaddai in Job as the one to whom (or from whom) the sufferer turns, addresses, or withdraws.
- **Job 8:5** — "make thy supplication to the Almighty"
- **Job 13:3** — "I would speak to the Almighty"
- **Job 21:15** — "What is the Almighty, that we should serve him?"
- **Job 22:26** — "then shalt thou have thy delight in the Almighty"
- **Job 31:35** — "the Almighty would answer me"

### 4. Source of Destruction / Dread — the shod/Shaddai wordplay
Shaddai invoked precisely in his terrifying, devastating aspect; twice linked by paronomasia with *shod* ("devastation").
- **Isa 13:6** — "as a destruction (shod) from the Almighty (Shaddai) shall it come"
- **Joel 1:15** — "as a destruction (shod) from the Almighty (Shaddai) shall it come"
- **Job 6:4** — "the arrows of the Almighty are within me"
- **Job 21:20** — "he shall drink of the wrath of the Almighty"
- **Ruth 1:20–21** — "the Almighty hath dealt very bitterly with me... the Almighty hath afflicted me"

### 5. Source of Life-Breath and Inspiration
Shaddai as the one who animates and gives understanding.
- **Job 32:8** — "the inspiration (*nishmat*) of the Almighty giveth them understanding"
- **Job 33:4** — "The Spirit of God hath made me, and the breath of the Almighty hath given me life"

### 6. Revelatory Voice and Vision
Shaddai in theophanic or oracular contexts — the one seen in visions, whose voice is heard.
- **Num 24:4, 16** — Balaam: "which saw the vision of the Almighty, falling into a trance"
- **Eze 1:24** — "as the voice of the Almighty"
- **Eze 10:5** — "as the voice of the Almighty God when he speaketh"
- **Psa 68:14; 91:1** — "he that dwelleth in the secret place of the most High shall abide under the shadow of the Almighty"

Note: LXX does **not** consistently use *theos* for Shaddai; it often leaves Shaddai un-rendered as a distinct epithet (*pantokratōr*) or translates it idea-for-idea (*hikanos*, *hupsistos*).
