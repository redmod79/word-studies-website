## Semantic Categories

### 1. Calendar Month (Date Formulas)
The dominant usage (~80%) — specifying a month as part of a date, typically with ordinal numbers and day-of-month.
- **Gen 7:11** — "in the second month, the seventeenth day of the month" (Flood chronology)
- **Exo 40:17** — "in the first month in the second year, on the first day of the month, the tabernacle was reared up"
- **Eze 1:1** — "in the fourth month, in the fifth day of the month" (prophetic date formula)
- **Est 3:7** — "In the first month, that is, the month Nisan" (Persian-era month name paired with ordinal)

### 2. Named Month (Calendar Names)
Chodesh paired with a proper month name — connecting Israelite ordinal months to named calendars.
- **Exo 13:4** — "This day came ye out in the month Abib" (pre-exilic Canaanite month name)
- **1Ki 6:38** — "in the month Bul, which is the eighth month" (Phoenician month name)
- **1Ki 8:2** — "at the feast in the month Ethanim, which is the seventh month"
- **Neh 1:1** — "in the month Chisleu" (post-exilic Babylonian month name)

### 3. New Moon (Cultic/Festival Observance)
The astronomical sense — the appearance of the new moon marking the beginning of a month, observed with sacrifices and assemblies.
- **Num 28:11** — "in the beginnings of your months ye shall offer a burnt offering unto the LORD"
- **1Sa 20:5** — "Behold, to morrow is the new moon, and I should not fail to sit with the king at meat"
- **2Ki 4:23** — "it is neither new moon, nor sabbath" (new moon as a recognized rest/worship day)
- **Eze 46:1,6** — "in the day of the new moon it shall be a young bullock without blemish" (new moon offering)

### 4. New Moon in Prophetic Critique and Eschatology
Prophetic use of new moon observances — either in judgment against empty ritual or in eschatological restoration.
- **Isa 1:13-14** — "the new moons and sabbaths, the calling of assemblies, I cannot away with... Your new moons and your appointed feasts my soul hateth"
- **Hos 2:11** — "I will also cause all her mirth to cease, her feast days, her new moons, and her sabbaths"
- **Amos 8:5** — "When will the new moon be gone, that we may sell corn?" (impatient merchants)
- **Isa 66:23** — "from one new moon to another... shall all flesh come to worship before me" (eschatological worship)

### 5. Duration of Time (Elapsed Months)
Chodesh used to express a period or span of time, rather than a specific calendar date.
- **Gen 29:14** — "he abode with him the space of a month"
- **Num 11:20-21** — "even a whole month, until it come out at your nostrils"
- **2Sa 24:13** — "or wilt thou flee three months before thine enemies?"
- **1Ki 5:14** — "ten thousand a month by courses: a month they were in Lebanon, and two months at home"

### 6. Age Reckoning (From a Month Old)
Used with age thresholds, especially in census and valuation contexts.
- **Num 3:15** — "every male from a month old and upward"
- **Num 18:16** — "those that are to be redeemed from a month old shalt thou redeem"
- **Lev 27:6** — "if it be from a month old even unto five years old, then thy estimation shall be..."

### 7. Allotted Time / Appointed Months
The months of a person's life as measured by God.
- **Job 14:5** — "the number of his months are with thee, thou hast appointed his bounds"
- **Job 21:21** — "when the number of his months is cut off in the midst"
