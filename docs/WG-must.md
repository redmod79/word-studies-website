# WG: Must / Necessity-Obligation

## Overview
The necessity-obligation concept in the NT involves five Greek words expressing different gradations of compulsion — from absolute divine necessity (δεῖ) to moral debt (ὀφείλω) to constraint/distress (ἀνάγκη) to imminent futurity (μέλλω). Together they map the NT's vocabulary for "what must or ought to happen/be done."

### Semantic Gradient
These words form a spectrum of necessity force:
- **δεῖ** — strongest: absolute divine or inherent necessity ("it is binding by nature")
- **ὀφείλω** — relational: moral debt owed to someone ("you are obligated to")
- **ἀνάγκη** — circumstantial: necessity from constraint or distress ("under compulsion")
- **ἀναγκαῖος** — adjectival: descriptively necessary, needful, or essential
- **μέλλω** — temporal: imminent futurity ("about to" happen), not obligation

## Roles in the Concept

- **Absolute necessity:** δεῖ (G1163) — divine compulsion, prophetic must, theological requirement
- **Moral obligation:** ὀφείλω (G3784) — duty owed to God or neighbor
- **Noun of constraint:** ἀνάγκη (G318) — necessity as a noun (distress, compulsion, constraint)
- **Adjectival necessity:** ἀναγκαῖος (G316) — characterizing something as needful or close
- **Imminent futurity:** μέλλω (G3195) — not obligation, but destiny/intention/imminence

## Key Passages Illustrating the Distinction

- **Mat 24:6** — δεῖ + μέλλω in same verse: "all these things must (δεῖ) come to pass, but the end is not yet" — μέλλω signals imminence, δεῖ signals divine appointment
- **Luke 24:26** — "Ought not (δεῖ) Christ to have suffered these things?" — retrospective divine necessity
- **Acts 5:29** — ὀφείλω flavor: "We ought (δεῖ here actually) to obey God rather than men" — moral obligation
- **1 Cor 7:26** — ἀνάγκη: "this is good for the present distress (ἀνάγκη)" — necessity as external constraint
- **Heb 9:16** — ἀνάγκη: "there must of necessity be the death of the testator" — logical/covenantal necessity (uses noun form)
