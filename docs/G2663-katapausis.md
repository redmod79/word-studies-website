## Lexicon Definition

From καταπαύω; *reposing down* (a settling into place); by Hebraism, an **abode** — the place or state of resting. KJV: rest.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. God's Dwelling / Heavenly Resting Place
Heaven as the place where God "rests" or is enthroned — quoting Isaiah 66:1.

- **Acts 7:49** — "Heaven is my throne, and earth is my footstool: what house will ye build me? saith the Lord: or what is the place of my rest?"

### 2. The Oath-Barred Rest (Ps 95:11 Quotation)
The wilderness generation was excluded by divine oath from entering "his rest" — a judgment against unbelief.

- **Heb 3:11** — "So I sware in my wrath, They shall not enter into my rest."
- **Heb 3:18** — "And to whom sware he that they should not enter into his rest, but to them that believed not?"
- **Heb 4:5** — "And in this place again, If they shall enter into my rest."

### 3. The Eschatological Rest Still Open to Believers
The promised rest remains available; the argument of Hebrews 4 is that since Israel failed to enter, the rest still stands for those who believe.

- **Heb 4:1** — "Let us therefore fear, lest, a promise being left us of entering into his rest, any of you should seem to come short of it."
- **Heb 4:3** — "For we which have believed do enter into rest ... although the works were finished from the foundation of the world."
- **Heb 4:11** — "Let us labour therefore to enter into that rest, lest any man fall after the same example of unbelief."

### 4. God's Creation Rest / Cessation from Works
The "rest" is grounded in God's own cessation at the end of creation week, which becomes the pattern for the believer's rest.

- **Heb 4:3** — "the works were finished from the foundation of the world"
- **Heb 4:10** — "For he that is entered into his rest, he also hath ceased from his own works, as God did from his."
