## Semantic Categories

### 1. Divine Imputation — Crediting Righteousness to an Account
The theological center of the word. God "reckons" or "imputes" something to a person's account — treating them as possessing what is credited.
- **Rom 4:3** — "Abraham believed God, and it was counted unto him for righteousness."
- **Rom 4:5** — "But to him that worketh not, but believeth on him that justifieth the ungodly, his faith is counted for righteousness."
- **Rom 4:22** — "And therefore it was imputed to him for righteousness."
- **Gal 3:6** — "Even as Abraham believed God, and it was accounted to him for righteousness."
- **Jas 2:23** — "Abraham believed God, and it was imputed unto him for righteousness."

### 2. Divine Non-Imputation — Not Charging Sin to an Account
The inverse: God deliberately does not reckon trespasses to the offender's ledger.
- **Rom 4:8** — "Blessed is the man to whom the Lord will not impute sin."
- **2 Cor 5:19** — "God was in Christ, reconciling the world unto himself, not imputing their trespasses unto them."
- **2 Tim 4:16** — "At my first answer no man stood with me, but all men forsook me: I pray God that it may not be laid to their charge."

### 3. Evaluative Reckoning — Forming a Considered Judgment
Mental evaluation or deliberate assessment of a situation.
- **Rom 8:18** — "For I reckon that the sufferings of this present time are not worthy to be compared with the glory which shall be revealed."
- **Rom 3:28** — "Therefore we conclude that a man is justified by faith without the deeds of the law."
- **Heb 11:19** — "Accounting that God was able to raise him up, even from the dead."
- **Phil 3:13** — "Brethren, I count not myself to have apprehended."

### 4. Regarding/Esteeming — Treating Something as Being in a Category
Classifying or categorizing a person or thing.
- **Rom 8:36** — "We are accounted as sheep for the slaughter."
- **Rom 9:8** — "The children of the promise are counted for the seed."
- **Mark 15:28** — "He was numbered with the transgressors."
- **Luke 22:37** — "He was reckoned among the transgressors."

### 5. General Thinking/Reasoning — Cognitive Processing
Basic mental activity: supposing, reasoning, thinking through.
- **Mark 11:31** — "And they reasoned with themselves."
- **1 Cor 13:11** — "When I was a child... I thought as a child."
- **Phil 4:8** — "Whatsoever things are true... think on these things."
- **1 Cor 13:5** — "[Love] thinketh no evil."

### 6. Imperative Self-Reckoning — Commanded Assessment
Believers commanded to reckon/regard themselves in a certain way.
- **Rom 6:11** — "Likewise reckon ye also yourselves to be dead indeed unto sin, but alive unto God."
- **1 Cor 4:1** — "Let a man so account of us, as of the ministers of Christ."
- **2 Cor 10:7** — "If any man trust to himself that he is Christ's, let him of himself think this again."
