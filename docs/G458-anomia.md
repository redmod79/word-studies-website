## Lexicon Definition

From ἄνομος (G459) + the α- privative prefix on νόμος (G3551): *illegality, violation of law*. Abstract noun denoting the condition or act of being without law, operating contrary to law, or disregarding divine statute. Sometimes concrete ("a lawless deed"), sometimes abstract ("the state of lawlessness"). In genitive constructions it functions as a descriptive qualifier ("of lawlessness" = "lawless").

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Lawless deeds / acts contrary to God's will (concrete, often plural)
- **Rom 4:7** — "Blessed are they whose iniquities (ἀνομίαι) are forgiven" (quoting Ps 32:1 LXX)
- **Heb 8:12** — "their sins and their iniquities (ἀνομιῶν) will I remember no more" (quoting Jer 31:34)
- **Heb 10:17** — parallel to 8:12 — "their sins and iniquities (ἀνομιῶν) will I remember no more"
- **Titus 2:14** — "redeem us from all iniquity (ἀνομίας)"

### 2. Lawlessness as character / moral condition
- **Matt 23:28** — "within ye are full of hypocrisy and iniquity (ἀνομίας)"
- **Heb 1:9** — "Thou hast loved righteousness, and hated iniquity (ἀνομίαν)" (quoting Ps 45:7)
- **2 Cor 6:14** — "what fellowship hath righteousness with unrighteousness (ἀνομίᾳ)?"

### 3. Lawlessness personified in active agents / workers
- **Matt 7:23** — "depart from me, ye that work iniquity (ἐργαζόμενοι τὴν ἀνομίαν)"
- **Matt 13:41** — "gather out of his kingdom all things that offend, and them which do iniquity (τοὺς ποιοῦντας τὴν ἀνομίαν)"
- **Rom 6:19** — "ye have yielded your members servants to uncleanness and to iniquity unto iniquity (τῇ ἀνομίᾳ εἰς τὴν ἀνομίαν)" (double use — instrument and result)

### 4. Eschatological intensification of lawlessness
- **Matt 24:12** — "because iniquity (τὴν ἀνομίαν) shall abound (πληθυνθῆναι), the love of many shall wax cold"
- **2 Thess 2:7** — "the mystery of iniquity (τὸ μυστήριον τῆς ἀνομίας) doth already work"
- **2 Thess 2:3** — *textual variant:* "man of sin" (ἁμαρτίας, TR/Byzantine) vs. "man of lawlessness" (ἀνομίας, Alexandrian — NA/UBS). The same figure is called ὁ ἄνομος (G459) in v.8.

### 5. Definitional identification with sin
- **1 John 3:4** — "Whosoever committeth sin (ἁμαρτίαν) also committeth lawlessness (τὴν ἀνομίαν ποιεῖ): for sin is lawlessness (ἡ ἁμαρτία ἐστὶν ἡ ἀνομία)" — the only NT text that equates the two terms outright.

The LXX uses ἀνομία as a broad cover term for Hebrew words denoting violation of Torah — with עָוֺן (iniquity/guilt) and פֶּשַׁע (transgression/revolt) as the two strongest bridges. This Septuagintal breadth underlies the NT's use of ἀνομία in quotations drawn from the Psalms and Jeremiah (Rom 4:7 < Ps 32; Heb 1:9 < Ps 45; Heb 8:12; 10:17 < Jer 31).
