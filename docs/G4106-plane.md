## Lexicon Definition

Feminine of πλάνος (used as an abstract noun): objectively, **fraudulence**; subjectively, **a straying from orthodoxy or piety** — deceit, delusion, error. From the same root family as πλανάω ("to cause to wander, go astray"), planē names the resulting state or instrument of wandering: the deception itself, the condition of being misled, or the erroneous teaching/way that produces straying.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Doctrinal/Religious Error — Deviation from Truth
Erroneous teaching or belief as a departure from what is true. Often contrasted with ἀλήθεια (*alētheia*, truth).
- **1 Jn 4:6** — "hereby know we the spirit of truth, and the spirit of error (planē)" — directly paired against ἀλήθεια as the defining antonym.
- **Eph 4:14** — "carried about with every wind of doctrine, by the sleight of men, and cunning craftiness, whereby they lie in wait to deceive (*pros tēn methodeian tēs planēs*)" — planē as the scheme/method of deception.
- **1 Th 2:3** — "For our exhortation was not of deceit (planē), nor of uncleanness, nor in guile" — Paul disclaims planē as a mark of false preaching.

### 2. Eschatological Delusion — Active Deception as Judgment
Planē as an agent-like force sent or permitted, producing belief in a lie.
- **2 Th 2:11** — "for this cause God shall send them strong delusion (*energeian planēs*, 'a working of delusion'), that they should believe a lie" — planē as the operative power God sends on those who "received not the love of the truth." The only NT instance where God is the subject governing planē.
- **Mat 27:64** — "so the last error (planē) shall be worse than the first" — the chief priests fear that the resurrection proclamation will be a second, worse planē after Jesus' first (cognate πλάνος used of Jesus in v.63).

### 3. Moral Corruption — The "Way" of Error
Planē as a lifestyle or path, often coupled with ὁδός (*hodos*, way).
- **Jas 5:20** — "he which converteth the sinner from the error (planē) of his way (*hodou*) shall save a soul from death"
- **2 Pe 2:18** — "those that were clean escaped from them who live in error (*tois en planē anastrephomenous*)" — planē as the sphere one walks/lives in.
- **Jude 1:11** — "ran greedily after the error of Balaam (*tē planē tou Balaam*) for reward" — planē named after the archetypal false prophet who led Israel into sin for profit.

### 4. Consequence / Recompense of Sin
Planē as the just penalty for deviation, received back in one's own person.
- **Rom 1:27** — "receiving in themselves that recompence of their error (planē) which was meet" — the homoerotic act is itself the planē whose wages are paid in the body. Planē here is both the sin and its return.

### 5. Danger to Believers — Being Led Away
Planē as a hazard the faithful must guard against.
- **2 Pe 3:17** — "beware lest ye also, being led away with the error (planē) of the wicked, fall from your own stedfastness" — verbal cognate (*synapachthentes*) makes the "leading astray" sense explicit.
