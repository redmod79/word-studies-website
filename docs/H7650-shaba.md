## Lexicon Definition

A primitive root; properly to be complete, but used only as a denominative from H7651; to seven oneself, i.e. swear (as if by repeating a declaration seven times): — adjure, charge (by an oath, with an oath), feed to the full (by mistake for H7646), take an oath, straitly, (cause to, make to) swear.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Divine Oath to the Patriarchs (Land Promise)
The most frequent use — God swearing to Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob to give the land to their descendants. This formula recurs throughout Deuteronomy and the historical books.
- **Gen 26:3** — "I will perform the oath which I sware unto Abraham thy father"
- **Deu 1:8** — "go in and possess the land which the LORD sware unto your fathers, Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob"
- **Deu 34:4** — "This is the land which I sware unto Abraham, unto Isaac, and unto Jacob"
- **Jos 21:43** — "the LORD gave unto Israel all the land which he sware to give unto their fathers"

### 2. Divine Oath of Judgment or Decree
God swearing by himself, his holiness, or his right hand to announce irrevocable judgment or decree.
- **Gen 22:16** — "By myself have I sworn, saith the LORD, for because thou hast done this thing..."
- **Psa 95:11** — "Unto whom I sware in my wrath that they should not enter into my rest"
- **Psa 110:4** — "The LORD hath sworn, and will not repent, Thou art a priest for ever after the order of Melchizedek"
- **Isa 45:23** — "I have sworn by myself, the word is gone out of my mouth in righteousness, and shall not return"
- **Amo 4:2** — "The Lord GOD hath sworn by his holiness"

### 3. Interpersonal Covenant Oath
Humans swearing to one another to establish or seal an agreement, treaty, or promise.
- **Gen 21:23-24** — Abimelech to Abraham: "swear unto me here by God"... "I will swear"
- **Gen 25:33** — "Jacob said, Swear to me this day; and he sware unto him"
- **Jos 9:15** — "Joshua made peace with them, and made a league with them... and the princes of the congregation sware unto them"
- **1Sa 20:42** — "we have sworn both of us in the name of the LORD"

### 4. Adjuration — Placing Another Under Oath (Hiphil)
Causing someone to swear, administering an oath. Concentrated in the Hiphil stem.
- **Gen 24:3** — "I will make thee swear by the LORD, the God of heaven"
- **Num 5:19** — "the priest shall charge her by an oath"
- **Jos 6:26** — "Joshua adjured them at that time, saying, Cursed be the man..."
- **1Ki 22:16** — "How many times shall I adjure thee that thou tell me nothing but that which is true?"

### 5. Solemn Charge / Entreaty (Song of Solomon)
The distinctive refrain in Song of Solomon where the beloved charges the daughters of Jerusalem, using shaba in a non-oath entreaty formula.
- **Song 2:7** — "I charge you, O ye daughters of Jerusalem, by the roes, and by the hinds of the field, that ye stir not up, nor awake my love"
- **Song 3:5** — same formula repeated
- **Song 5:8** — "I charge you, O daughters of Jerusalem, if ye find my beloved, that ye tell him"
- **Song 8:4** — same formula repeated

### 6. Personal Vow or Oath of Commitment
An individual swearing to God or making a binding personal commitment.
- **Psa 119:106** — "I have sworn, and I will perform it, that I will keep thy righteous judgments"
- **Psa 132:2** — "How he sware unto the LORD, and vowed unto the mighty God of Jacob"
- **Num 30:2** — "If a man... swear an oath to bind his soul with a bond; he shall not break his word"

### 7. False or Forbidden Swearing
Swearing falsely, by false gods, or in prohibited contexts.
- **Lev 19:12** — "ye shall not swear by my name falsely, neither shalt thou profane the name of thy God"
- **Jer 5:2** — "though they say, The LORD liveth; surely they swear falsely"
- **Jer 7:9** — "Will ye steal, murder, and commit adultery, and swear falsely, and burn incense unto Baal?"
- **Hos 4:15** — "come not ye unto Gilgal, neither go ye up to Bethaven, nor swear, The LORD liveth"
- **Zep 1:5** — "them that worship and that swear by the LORD, and that swear by Malcham"
