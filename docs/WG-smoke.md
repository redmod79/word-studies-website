# WG: Smoke (Fire, Smoke, Brimstone, Burning)

## Overview

This word group maps the NT apocalyptic judgment vocabulary centered on visible combustion — fire, smoke, brimstone, and burning. These four words cluster together in Revelation's judgment scenes and share deep roots in OT theophany and divine-wrath imagery. The triad πῦρ + καπνός + θεῖον (fire + smoke + brimstone) appears as a unit in Rev 9:17-18 and reflects the Sodom archetype of catastrophic divine judgment.

## Roles in the Concept

- **Agent:** πῦρ (G4442) — the fire; can be purifying (1 Cor 3:13) or destroying (Rev 20:10); the dominant member (73 verses)
- **Effect:** καπνός (G2586) — the smoke; visible evidence that fire has occurred; also ascends as sign or worship
- **Instrument:** θεῖον (G2303) — sulfur/brimstone; the specific agent linking Revelation judgment to Sodom (Gen 19; Luk 17:29)
- **Process:** πύρωσις (G4451) — the burning as event (Rev 18:9, 18:18: "her burning") or ordeal (1 Pet 4:12: "fiery trial")
