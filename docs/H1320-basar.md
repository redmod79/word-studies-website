**Pattern:** Heaviest concentration in Leviticus (ritual/purity law), with significant presence in Genesis (creation and kinship), Job (human frailty), Isaiah (prophetic "all flesh"), and Ezekiel (spiritual renewal). The word spans every genre — law, narrative, poetry, and prophecy.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Physical Flesh / Body Material
The material substance of living bodies — human and animal. Dominant in Leviticus (skin disease, bodily discharges, priestly garments), Job (the vulnerable body), and Psalms (embodied worship).

- **Gen 2:21** — "he took one of his ribs, and closed up the *flesh* instead thereof"
- **Lev 13:10** — "if there be quick raw *flesh* in the rising" (skin disease diagnostics)
- **Job 19:26** — "yet in my *flesh* shall I see God"
- **Psalm 119:120** — "My *flesh* trembleth for fear of thee; and I am afraid of thy judgments"

### 2. Kinship / Shared Flesh
Used to denote family solidarity — "one flesh" (marriage), "bone and flesh" (blood kinship). This usage reflects the ancient view of family as physically shared substance.

- **Gen 2:23** — "This is now bone of my bones, and *flesh* of my flesh: she shall be called Woman"
- **Gen 2:24** — "they shall be one *flesh*"
- **Gen 37:27** — "let not our hand be upon him; for he is our brother and our *flesh*"
- **2 Sam 5:1** — "Behold, we are thy bone and thy *flesh*" (Israel's covenant with David)

### 3. All Flesh — Universal Living Beings
A fixed phrase idiom (כָּל־בָּשָׂר, kol-bâsâr) meaning all living creatures, all humanity, or all created life. Used in covenantal and eschatological contexts.

- **Gen 9:11** — "neither shall all *flesh* be cut off any more by the waters of a flood"
- **Num 16:22** — "O God, the God of the spirits of all *flesh*, shall one man sin..."
- **Isa 40:5** — "and all *flesh* shall see it together: for the mouth of the LORD hath spoken it"
- **Joel 2:28** — "I will pour out my spirit upon all *flesh*; and your sons and your daughters shall prophesy"
- **Psalm 65:2** — "unto thee shall all *flesh* come"

### 4. Sacrificial / Ritual Meat
Flesh of offerings and animals in liturgical contexts. Concentrated in Leviticus, Exodus, Numbers — governing what may be eaten, how sacrificial flesh is handled, and purification rites.

- **Exo 12:8** — "they shall eat the *flesh* in that night, roast with fire, and unleavened bread"
- **Lev 6:27** — "Whatsoever shall touch the *flesh* thereof shall be holy"
- **Lev 17:14** — "For it is the life of all *flesh*; the blood of it is for the life thereof"
- **Num 11:13** — "Whence should I have *flesh* to give unto all this people?"

### 5. Human Frailty — Flesh vs. God
Flesh as the seat of creaturely weakness, mortality, and dependence on God. Contrasted with the divine power or Spirit. The theological axis of "arm of flesh" passages.

- **2 Chr 32:8** — "With him is an arm of *flesh*; but with us is the LORD our God"
- **Jer 17:5** — "Cursed be the man...that maketh *flesh* his arm, and whose heart departeth from the LORD"
- **Psalm 56:4** — "In God I will praise his word...I will not fear what *flesh* can do unto me"
- **Psalm 78:39** — "he remembered that they were but *flesh*; a wind that passeth away"

### 6. Circumcision in the Flesh
bâsâr used as a technical term for the covenant sign of circumcision — the flesh of the foreskin. Concentrated in Gen 17.

- **Gen 17:11** — "ye shall circumcise the *flesh* of your foreskin"
- **Gen 17:14** — "the uncircumcised man child whose *flesh* of his foreskin is not circumcised"
- **Lev 12:3** — "the *flesh* of his foreskin shall be circumcised"

### 7. Heart of Flesh — Spiritual Renewal
A distinctive prophetic metaphor: replacing the stony heart with a heart of bâsâr — soft, responsive flesh. Used in Ezekiel's new covenant promises.

- **Ezek 11:19** — "I will take the stony heart out of their *flesh*, and will give them an heart of *flesh*"
- **Ezek 36:26** — "I will take away the stony heart out of your *flesh*, and I will give you an heart of *flesh*"
