## Semantic Categories

### 1. Conditional Vow in Distress ("If God will... then I will...")

The paradigmatic biblical vow pattern: a petitioner binds himself to a future act contingent on divine deliverance.

- **Gen 28:20** — "And Jacob vowed a vow, saying, If God will be with me, and will keep me in this way..."
- **Num 21:2** — "And Israel vowed a vow unto the LORD, and said, If thou wilt indeed deliver this people into my hand..."
- **Judg 11:30** — "And Jephthah vowed a vow unto the LORD, and said, If thou shalt without fail deliver the children of Ammon..."
- **1 Sam 1:11** — "And she vowed a vow, and said, O LORD of hosts, if thou wilt indeed look on the affliction of thine handmaid..."

### 2. Legal Regulation of Vow-Making

Priestly and Deuteronomic laws governing who may vow, where vows are paid, and their binding force.

- **Num 6:2** — "When either man or woman shall separate themselves to vow a vow of a Nazarite..."
- **Num 30:2–3** — "If a man vow a vow unto the LORD... he shall not break his word... If a woman also vow a vow unto the LORD, and bind herself by a bond..."
- **Deut 12:11** — "thither shall ye bring... all your choice vows which ye vow unto the LORD"
- **Lev 27:8** — "But if he be poorer than thy estimation... according to his ability that vowed shall the priest value him."

### 3. Imperative to Pay What Is Vowed

Prophetic and wisdom texts emphasizing that vows, once made, must be fulfilled without delay.

- **Deut 23:21** — "When thou shalt vow a vow unto the LORD thy God, thou shalt not slack to pay it"
- **Deut 23:22** — "But if thou shalt forbear to vow, it shall be no sin in thee"
- **Eccl 5:4** — "When thou vowest a vow unto God, defer not to pay it"
- **Eccl 5:5** — "Better is it that thou shouldest not vow, than that thou shouldest vow and not pay"
- **Psa 76:11** — "Vow, and pay unto the LORD your God"

### 4. Vow Retrospectively Invoked or Fulfilled

References back to a vow previously made, often tied to sacrifice or pilgrimage.

- **Gen 31:13** — "where thou vowedst a vow unto me: now arise, get thee out from this land"
- **2 Sam 15:7–8** — "let me go and pay my vow... thy servant vowed a vow while I abode at Geshur"
- **Psa 132:2** — "How he sware unto the LORD, and vowed unto the mighty God of Jacob"
- **Jon 2:9** — "I will sacrifice unto thee with the voice of thanksgiving; I will pay that that I have vowed"

### 5. Gentile and Nominal Vowing

Non-Israelites vowing to YHWH, or vows made insincerely/condemned.

- **Jon 1:16** — "the men feared the LORD exceedingly, and offered a sacrifice unto the LORD, and made vows"
- **Isa 19:21** — "the Egyptians shall know the LORD... and shall vow a vow unto the LORD, and perform it"
- **Jer 44:25** — "Ye and your wives have both spoken with your mouths... saying, We will surely perform our vows that we have vowed" (to the queen of heaven — condemned)
- **Mal 1:14** — "cursed be the deceiver, which hath in his flock a male, and voweth, and sacrificeth unto the Lord a corrupt thing"
