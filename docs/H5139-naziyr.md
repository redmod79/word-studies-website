## Lexicon Definition

> Or נָזִר nâzir; from H5144; separate, i.e. consecrated (as prince, a Nazirite); hence (figuratively from the latter) an unpruned vine (like an unshorn Nazirite): — Nazarite (by a false alliteration with Nazareth), separate(-d), vine undressed.

The noun carries two distinct semantic fields: (1) a person consecrated to YHWH through specific abstentions, and (2) an untrimmed/undressed vine — both sharing the root idea of being left untouched.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. The Nazirite under Vow — Consecrated Person (legislative)
The central usage: a person who has taken the formal vow of separation unto YHWH, governed by the triple abstention (wine, razor, corpse contact).
- **Num 6:2** — "When either man or woman shall separate themselves to vow a vow of a Nazarite, to separate themselves unto the LORD"
- **Num 6:13** — "And this is the law of the Nazarite, when the days of his separation are fulfilled: he shall be brought unto the door of the tabernacle"
- **Num 6:18** — "And the Nazarite shall shave the head of his separation at the door of the tabernacle of the congregation"
- **Num 6:21** — "This is the law of the Nazarite who hath vowed, and of his offering unto the LORD for his separation"

### 2. The Nazirite from Birth — Lifelong Consecration (narrative)
Samson's status as a Nazirite from the womb, announced by the angel of the LORD. Unlike the voluntary, temporary vow of Numbers 6, this is prenatal and permanent.
- **Jdg 13:5** — "for the child shall be a Nazarite to God from the womb"
- **Jdg 13:7** — "for the child shall be a Nazarite to God from the womb to the day of his death"
- **Jdg 16:17** — "I have been a Nazarite unto God from my mother's womb: if I be shaven, then my strength will go from me"

### 3. The Nazarite as Prophetic Symbol — Purity and Judgment
Prophetic references to Nazarites as examples of consecrated purity within the nation, and the national sin of corrupting them.
- **Lam 4:7** — "Her Nazarites were purer than snow, they were whiter than milk, they were more ruddy in body than rubies, their polishing was of sapphire"
- **Amo 2:11** — "And I raised up of your sons for prophets, and of your young men for Nazarites. Is it not even thus, O ye children of Israel? saith the LORD"
- **Amo 2:12** — "But ye gave the Nazarites wine to drink; and commanded the prophets, saying, Prophesy not"

### 4. The Distinguished/Separated One — Patriarchal Blessing
Applied to Joseph as the one "separated" or distinguished among his brothers — using the word in its broader etymological sense rather than the technical Nazirite-vow sense.
- **Gen 49:26** — "they shall be on the head of Joseph, and on the crown of the head of him that was separate from his brethren"
- **Deu 33:16** — "let the blessing come upon the head of Joseph, and upon the top of the head of him that was separated from his brethren"

### 5. The Untrimmed Vine — Agricultural Metaphor
The vine left uncut during sabbatical and jubilee years. The shared root idea with the Nazirite is being left untouched — as the Nazirite's hair is unshorn, so the sabbatical vine is unpruned.
- **Lev 25:5** — "That which groweth of its own accord of thy harvest thou shalt not reap, neither gather the grapes of thy vine undressed"
- **Lev 25:11** — "ye shall not sow, neither reap that which groweth of itself in it, nor gather the grapes in it of thy vine undressed"
