## Semantic Categories

### 1. Ceremonial/Ritual Purification (Jewish Practice)
- **John 11:55** — "many went out of the country up to Jerusalem before the passover, to purify themselves" — pre-Passover ritual purification
- **Acts 21:24** — "Them take, and purify thyself with them, and be at charges with them, that they may shave their heads" — Paul's purification with men under a Nazarite-type vow
- **Acts 21:26** — "Then Paul took the men, and the next day purifying himself with them entered into the temple, to signify the accomplishment of the days of purification" — temple purification ritual with vow completion
- **Acts 24:18** — "Whereupon certain Jews from Asia found me purified in the temple, neither with multitude, nor with tumult" — Paul's completed state of ceremonial purity

### 2. Moral/Spiritual Purification (Interior Transformation)
- **James 4:8** — "Cleanse your hands, ye sinners; and purify your hearts, ye double minded" — paired with καθαρίζω (katharizō, hands); hagnizō applied to the heart, indicating inward moral purification
- **1 Peter 1:22** — "Seeing ye have purified your souls in obeying the truth through the Spirit unto unfeigned love of the brethren" — purification of the soul through obedience to truth, resulting in genuine love
- **1 John 3:3** — "every man that hath this hope in him purifieth himself, even as he is pure" — self-purification motivated by eschatological hope, with Christ as the standard of purity (ἁγνός)
