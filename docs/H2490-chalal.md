## Lexicon Definition

A primitive root; properly, to bore, i.e. (by implication) to wound, to dissolve; figuratively, to profane (a person, place or thing), to break (one's word), to begin (as if by an "opening wedge"); to play (the flute). KJV renderings: begin (men began), defile, break, eat (as common things), first, gather the grape thereof, take inheritance, pipe, player on instruments, pollute, (cast as) profane (self), prostitute, slay (slain), sorrow, stain, wound.

The semantic range divides into three major poles:
1. **To profane / defile / pollute** — treat the sacred as common (Piel stem, dominant)
2. **To begin** — open, make a start (Hiphil stem)
3. **To wound / pierce** — bore through (Qal/Pual, rare in verb form; the adjective H2491 carries this sense)

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Profaning God's Name
The most theologically significant category. God's "name" (shem) represents his character and reputation among the nations.
- **Lev 18:21** — "neither shalt thou profane the name of thy God"
- **Lev 19:12** — "ye shall not swear by my name falsely, neither shalt thou profane the name of thy God"
- **Eze 20:9** — "I wrought for my name's sake, that it should not be polluted before the heathen"
- **Eze 36:20–23** — "they profaned my holy name… I will sanctify my great name, which was profaned among the heathen"
- **Mal 1:12** — "ye have profaned it, in that ye say, The table of the LORD is polluted"

### 2. Profaning Holy Things / Sanctuary
Treating consecrated objects, spaces, or offerings as though they were common.
- **Lev 19:8** — "he hath profaned the hallowed thing of the LORD"

- **Lev 22:15** — "they shall not profane the holy things of the children of Israel"
- **Eze 22:26** — "her priests have profaned mine holy things"
- **Eze 24:21** — "I will profane my sanctuary, the excellency of your strength"
- **Dan 11:31** — "they shall pollute the sanctuary of strength"

### 3. Profaning the Sabbath
- **Eze 20:13, 16, 21, 24** — "polluted my sabbaths" (repeated refrain in Ezekiel's historical review)

### 4. Profaning Persons / Priesthood
