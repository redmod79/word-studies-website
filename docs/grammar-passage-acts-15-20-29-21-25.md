## Key Grammatical Features

### 1. τοῦ ἀπέχεσθαι — genitive articular infinitive in Acts 15:20

- **Form:** `τοῦ` is genitive singular neuter article; `ἀπέχεσθαι` is present middle infinitive of `ἀπέχω`.
- **Significance:** The articular infinitive gives the content/purpose of `ἐπιστεῖλαι`: the written instruction concerns abstaining. Its complements appear in the genitive case after `ἀπέχεσθαι`.
- **Grammar reference:** [greek-articular-infinitive-purpose](../greek/greek-articular-infinitive-purpose.md).

### 2. Genitive complements after ἀπέχεσθαι — Acts 15:20 and 15:29

- **Form:** Acts 15:20 has a coordinated genitive list: `τῶν ἀλισγημάτων τῶν εἰδώλων`, `τῆς πορνείας`, `πνικτοῦ`, `τοῦ αἵματος`. Acts 15:29 has `εἰδωλοθύτων`, `αἵματος`, `πνικτῶν`, `πορνείας`.
- **Significance:** The abstention verb governs a series of genitive complements. The list changes vocabulary between `ἀλισγημάτων τῶν εἰδώλων` ("pollutions of idols") and `εἰδωλοθύτων` ("idol-offered things"), but the syntactic relation remains parallel: genitive items coordinated by `καί`.
- **Grammar reference:** no dedicated Greek genitive-complement-after-ἀπέχω entry exists.

### 3. πνικτοῦ / πνικτῶν beside αἵματος — coordinate syntax, not subordination

- **Form:** `πνικτοῦ` in Acts 15:20 is adjective genitive singular neuter; `πνικτῶν` in Acts 15:29 is adjective genitive plural neuter; `πνικτὸν` in Acts 21:25 is adjective accusative singular neuter. `αἵματος` is genitive singular neuter in Acts 15; `αἷμα` is accusative singular neuter in Acts 21:25.
- **Significance:** In all three passages, `πνικτός` is coordinated with `αἷμα` by `καί`. Grammatically it is not an adjective modifying "blood"; it is its own coordinated list item. Its proximity to blood may be conceptually relevant, but the syntax treats "strangled" and "blood" as adjacent coordinated complements/objects.
- **Word study:** [G4156-pniktos](../../word-studies/G4156-pniktos.md).

### 4. διατηροῦντες ἑαυτούς — participial self-keeping clause in Acts 15:29

- **Form:** `διατηροῦντες` is present active participle nominative plural masculine; `ἑαυτούς` is reflexive accusative plural masculine.
- **Significance:** The participle supplies the condition/means related to `εὖ πράξετε`: keeping yourselves from these things is the action attached to doing well. The relative `ἐξ ὧν` points back to the preceding coordinated list.
- **Grammar reference:** no dedicated entry for this participial construction exists; compare [paired-middle-participles](../greek/paired-middle-participles.md) for participial coordination elsewhere.

### 5. φυλάσσεσθαι + accusative list — Acts 21:25 restatement

- **Form:** `φυλάσσεσθαι` is present middle infinitive; `αὐτούς` is accusative plural masculine subject of the infinitive. The abstention items are accusative objects: `τό τε εἰδωλόθυτον`, `αἷμα`, `πνικτόν`, `πορνείαν`.
- **Significance:** Acts 21:25 restates the decree with a different governing infinitive and a different case frame. Instead of `ἀπέχεσθαι` + genitive, it uses `φυλάσσεσθαι` + accusative object list. The same coordinate list structure remains visible.
- **Grammar reference:** no dedicated Greek middle-infinitive-with-accusative-object entry exists.

## Clause / Syntax Structure

### Acts 15:20

- `ἀλλὰ` marks contrast with the preceding proposal.
- `ἐπιστεῖλαι αὐτοῖς` is the main infinitival action in the reported decision.
- `τοῦ ἀπέχεσθαι` is the articular infinitive construction.
- The abstention complements are genitive and coordinated: idol-pollutions, fornication, strangled, blood.

### Acts 15:29

- `ἀπέχεσθαι` heads the clause.
- Four genitive complements follow in a coordinated list.
- `ἐξ ὧν` resumes the list by relative pronoun.
- `διατηροῦντες ἑαυτούς` is participial, attached to `εὖ πράξετε`.

### Acts 21:25

- `περὶ δὲ τῶν πεπιστευκότων ἐθνῶν` frames the topic: the believing Gentiles.
- `ἡμεῖς ἐπεστείλαμεν κρίναντες` gives the prior written decision with aorist indicative plus aorist participle.
- `φυλάσσεσθαι αὐτούς` introduces the infinitive content.
- The kept-from items are accusative and coordinated: idol-offered thing, blood, strangled thing, fornication.
