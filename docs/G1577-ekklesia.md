## Lexicon Definition

From a compound of G1537 (ek, "out of") and a derivative of G2564 (kaleō, "to call"); a calling out, i.e. a popular meeting, especially a religious congregation (Jewish synagogue or Christian community of members on earth, saints in heaven, or both). KJV: assembly, church.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Local Congregation / Particular Church
The most frequent use — a specific assembly of believers in a city or region.
- **Acts 5:11** — "And great fear came upon all the church, and upon as many as heard these things."
- **1 Cor 1:2** — "Unto the church of God which is at Corinth, to them that are sanctified in Christ Jesus."
- **Acts 14:23** — "And when they had ordained them elders in every church, and had prayed with fasting, they commended them to the Lord."
- **Acts 9:31** — "Then had the churches rest throughout all Judaea and Galilee and Samaria, and were edified."

### 2. Universal / Cosmic Church
Christ as head over the church as his body — the totality of believers transcending any local expression.
- **Eph 1:22** — "And hath put all things under his feet, and gave him to be the head over all things to the church."
- **Eph 5:25** — "Husbands, love your wives, even as Christ also loved the church, and gave himself for it."
- **Col 1:18** — "And he is the head of the body, the church: who is the beginning, the firstborn from the dead."
- **Eph 3:21** — "Unto him be glory in the church by Christ Jesus throughout all ages, world without end."

### 3. Household Church
A congregation meeting in a private home — the smallest unit of ekklesia in the NT.
- **Rom 16:5** — "Likewise greet the church that is in their house."
- **1 Cor 16:19** — "Aquila and Priscilla salute you much in the Lord, with the church that is in their house."
- **Col 4:15** — "Salute the brethren which are in Laodicea, and Nymphas, and the church which is in his house."
- **Phm 1:2** — "And to our beloved Apphia, and Archippus our fellowsoldier, and to the church in thy house."

### 4. Prophetic Address: Seven Churches
Revelation addresses ekklesia as distinct regional assemblies receiving divine evaluation and exhortation.
- **Rev 1:4** — "John to the seven churches which are in Asia: Grace be unto you, and peace."
- **Rev 2:1** — "Unto the angel of the church of Ephesus write."
- **Rev 2:7** — "He that hath an ear, let him hear what the Spirit saith unto the churches."
- **Rev 2:23** — "And all the churches shall know that I am he which searcheth the reins and hearts."

### 5. Historical Assembly of Israel
The LXX rendered Hebrew qahal (assembly) as ekklesia — the NT applies this retroactively to Israel in the wilderness.
- **Acts 7:38** — "This is he, that was in the church in the wilderness with the angel which spake to him in the mount Sina."
- **Heb 2:12** — "Saying, I will declare thy name unto my brethren, in the midst of the church will I sing praise unto thee." (citing Ps 22:22)
- **Heb 12:23** — "To the general assembly and church of the firstborn, which are written in heaven."

### 6. Secular Civil Assembly
In Acts 19, ekklesia refers to the pagan Ephesian civic assembly — demonstrating the word's non-religious range in common Greek.
- **Acts 19:32** — "For the assembly was confused; and the more part knew not wherefore they were come together."
- **Acts 19:39** — "But if ye inquire any thing concerning other matters, it shall be determined in a lawful assembly."
- **Acts 19:41** — "And when he had thus spoken, he dismissed the assembly."
