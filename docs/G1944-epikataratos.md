## Lexicon Definition

From ἐπί (G1909, "upon") and a derivative of καταράομαι (G2672, "to execrate"); imprecated, i.e. accursed, under a curse. The compound intensifies the base: not merely cursed, but one upon whom a curse has been formally placed. Used exclusively in the LXX and NT for translating the Hebrew participle אָרוּר (arur, Qal passive participle of H779 אָרַר).

### 1. Covenant Curse Formula (Deuteronomic)
