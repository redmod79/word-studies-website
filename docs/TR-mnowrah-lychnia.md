**Semantic continuity:** The LXX translators chose λυχνία as an almost 1:1 rendering of מְנוֹרָה. Both words denote the *stand* holding lamps, not the lamp itself. The high PMI score (32.20) confirms this is one of the tightest Hebrew→Greek translation bonds in the sanctuary vocabulary. The NT writers inherited this equation directly — when John and the author of Hebrews write λυχνία, a Jewish reader would hear מְנוֹרָה.

## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same:**
- The lampstand consistently represents the presence and testimony of God's people before him
- The sanctuary setting carries from OT (Exo–Num) through the Hebrews reference into Revelation's heavenly temple imagery
- Gold is the consistent material descriptor (Exo 25, 1 Ki 7, Rev 1)

**What shifts:**
- In the OT the מְנוֹרָה is one (or seven, in Solomon's temple) physical objects in the holy place
- In Revelation the seven λυχνίαι are explicitly interpreted as the seven churches (Rev 1:20) — the concrete furnishing becomes ecclesial symbol
- The synoptic gospels (Mat 5, Mk 4, Lk 8, 11) use λυχνία in a purely domestic sense (household lamp stand), without sanctuary overtones — a semantic range the Hebrew מְנוֹרָה never occupied
