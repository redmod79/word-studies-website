## Semantic Categories

### 1. Intensified Surplus of Grace over Sin
- **Rom 5:20** — "Moreover the law entered, that the offence might abound. But where sin abounded, grace did much more abound [huperperisseuo]"

The aorist active captures a one-time, decisive, overwhelming action: grace not merely matches or exceeds sin's increase, but super-abounds beyond it. The ὑπέρ- prefix intensifies the base περισσεύω (used of sin's abounding via πλεονάζω in the same verse) to express a qualitatively different order of surplus.

### 2. Overflowing Personal Joy in Affliction
- **2 Cor 7:4** — "Great is my boldness of speech toward you, great is my glorying of you: I am filled with comfort, I am exceeding joyful [huperperisseuo] in all our tribulation"

The present middle voice marks ongoing, personally experienced overflowing. Paul's joy does not merely survive tribulation — it super-abounds within it. The middle voice indicates the subject's involvement in the experience of overflow.
