## Lexicon Definition

From H6458 (pacal, "to carve"); an idol: carved (graven) image. The word denotes a physical object that has been carved or chiseled from wood or stone, then set up as an object of worship. The root verb pacal is the same word used for God's command to Moses to "hew" the stone tablets (Exo 34:1, 4; Deu 10:1, 3), making the contrast sharp: the same carving action produces either the tablets of the covenant or a forbidden idol.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. The Prohibited Object — Second Commandment
The primary usage: pecel names the specific thing forbidden in the Decalogue. It appears in every recitation of the image-prohibition.
- **Exo 20:4** — "Thou shalt not make unto thee any graven image, or any likeness of any thing that is in heaven above..."
- **Deu 5:8** — "Thou shalt not make thee any graven image, or any likeness of any thing..."
- **Deu 4:23** — "Take heed... lest ye forget the covenant of the LORD your God... and make you a graven image"
- **Lev 26:1** — "Ye shall make you no idols nor graven image, neither rear you up a standing image"

### 2. The Manufactured Object — Idol Fabrication
Isaiah's idol-polemic uses pecel to emphasize the absurdity of manufacturing a god. The graven image is the product of human craft — melted, carved, overlaid.
- **Isa 40:19** — "The workman melteth a graven image, and the goldsmith spreadeth it over with gold"
- **Isa 40:20** — "he seeketh unto him a cunning workman to prepare a graven image, that shall not be moved"
- **Isa 44:9** — "They that make a graven image are all of them vanity"
- **Isa 44:15** — "he maketh a god, even his graven image: he falleth down unto it, and worshippeth it"

### 3. The Cultic Installation — Placed in a Shrine or Temple
Pecel appears in narratives where an image is physically set up for ongoing worship, whether in a private shrine or in the temple itself.
- **Jdg 17:3-4** — Micah's mother dedicates silver to make "a graven image and a molten image"
- **Jdg 18:30-31** — "the children of Dan set up the graven image... all the time that the house of God was in Shiloh"
- **2Ki 21:7** — "he set a graven image of the grove that he had made in the house [of the LORD]"
- **2Ch 33:7** — "he set a carved image, the idol which he had made, in the house of God"

### 4. The Object of Prophetic Judgment
Prophets declare that pecel is futile, shameful, and destined for destruction.
- **Jer 10:14** — "every founder is confounded by the graven image: for his molten image is falsehood"
- **Nah 1:14** — "out of the house of thy gods will I cut off the graven image and the molten image"
- **Hab 2:18** — "What profiteth the graven image that the maker thereof hath graven it?"
- **Isa 42:17** — "They shall be turned back, they shall be greatly ashamed, that trust in graven images"

### 5. Pre-emptive Attribution — "My Idol Did This"
A distinctive Isaianic usage: God reveals prophecy in advance so Israel cannot credit a pecel with the fulfilled event.
- **Isa 48:5** — "lest thou shouldest say, Mine idol hath done them, and my graven image... hath commanded them"
