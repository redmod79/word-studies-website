## Thematic Continuity

### Gate as Boundary of Sacred/Profane Space
- **OT:** The tabernacle/temple gate separates holy from common ground (Num 4:26; Ezek 44:1-3)
- **NT:** Heb 13:11-12 shifts from "camp" (παρεμβολή) to "gate" (πύλη), connecting the Levitical camp boundary with the physical Jerusalem gate — Jesus suffered "outside" both

### Gate as Authority / Power
- **OT:** "Possess the gate of his enemies" (Gen 22:17) = military/judicial dominion; elders judge "in the gate" (Ruth 4:1; Deut 22:15)
- **NT:** "Gates of Hades shall not prevail" (Mat 16:18) = the power of death cannot overcome the church

### Gate as Entry to Life or Death
- **OT:** "Gates of righteousness" and "gate of the LORD" (Psa 118:19-20); "gates of the grave" (Isa 38:10)
- **NT:** "Strait gate" leading to life vs. "wide gate" leading to destruction (Mat 7:13-14); "gates of Hades" (Mat 16:18)

## What Stays the Same

- The dual literal/metaphorical range: physical entry point and metaphor for authority, access, or boundary
- Gate as threshold between domains (inside/outside, life/death, sacred/profane)
- "Gates of death/Hades/grave" as an idiom spanning both testaments

## What Shifts

- OT sha'ar is predominantly literal (city gate, temple gate) with the judicial/civic dimension dominant (302 occurrences, mostly concrete); NT pyle is predominantly metaphorical (6 of 9 verses use figurative or theologically loaded senses)
- The judicial "sitting in the gate" function (central to OT) has no NT counterpart
- NT adds the eschatological "narrow gate" metaphor (Mat 7:13-14; Luk 13:24) with no direct OT precedent in sha'ar, though Psa 118:19-20 provides the closest conceptual ancestor
