## Lexicon Definition

Active participle of H3547; literally one officiating, a priest; also (by courtesy) an acting priest (although a layman):—chief ruler, own, priest, prince, principal officer.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Officiating Priest — Sacrificial and Ritual Duties
The dominant usage: the priest as the one who performs offerings, sprinkles blood, burns incense, and pronounces ritual verdicts.
- **Lev 1:9** — "the priest shall burn all on the altar, to be a burnt sacrifice"
- **Lev 4:6** — "the priest shall dip his finger in the blood, and sprinkle of the blood seven times before the LORD"
- **Num 15:25** — "the priest shall make an atonement for all the congregation"
- **2 Chr 29:24** — "the priests killed them, and they made reconciliation with their blood upon the altar"

### 2. Priest as Examiner and Judge — Purity Determinations
In Leviticus 13–14 the priest functions as inspector and diagnostic authority on skin diseases and house contamination.
- **Lev 13:3** — "the priest shall look on the plague in the skin of the flesh... the priest shall pronounce him unclean"
- **Lev 13:17** — "the priest shall see him: and, behold, if the plague be turned into white; then the priest shall pronounce him clean"
- **Lev 14:3** — "the priest shall go forth out of the camp; and the priest shall look, and, behold, if the plague of leprosy be healed"
- **Deut 17:9** — "thou shalt come unto the priests the Levites, and unto the judge... and they shall show thee the sentence of judgment"

### 3. Priest as Teacher and Keeper of the Law
The priest as custodian and instructor of Torah.
- **Deut 31:9** — "Moses wrote this law, and delivered it unto the priests the sons of Levi, which bare the ark of the covenant"
- **2 Chr 15:3** — "Israel hath been without the true God, and without a teaching priest, and without law"
- **Mal 2:7** — "the priest's lips should keep knowledge, and they should seek the law at his mouth: for he is the messenger of the LORD of hosts"
- **Ezek 7:26** — "they shall seek a vision of the prophet; but the law shall perish from the priest"

### 4. Named High Priests and Chief Priests — Leadership
Specific individuals named as priest, often in the compound "the high priest" or "chief priest."
- **Gen 14:18** — "Melchizedek king of Salem... the priest of the most high God"
- **2 Ki 22:8** — "Hilkiah the high priest said unto Shaphan the scribe, I have found the book of the law"
- **Hag 1:1** — "Joshua the son of Josedech, the high priest"
- **Zech 3:1** — "Joshua the high priest standing before the angel of the LORD"

### 5. Priests of Foreign or Illegitimate Cults
The word is used without qualification for pagan priests and unauthorized Israelite priests.
- **Gen 41:45** — "Asenath the daughter of Potiphera priest of On"
- **Exo 2:16** — "the priest of Midian had seven daughters"
- **1 Ki 12:31** — "made priests of the lowest of the people, which were not of the sons of Levi"
- **2 Ki 23:20** — "he slew all the priests of the high places that were there upon the altars"

### 6. Prophetic Criticism of Priests
The prophets address priestly failure — corruption, violence, faithlessness.
- **Jer 5:31** — "the priests bear rule by their means; and my people love to have it so"
- **Hos 4:9** — "there shall be, like people, like priest: and I will punish them for their ways"
- **Lam 4:13** — "for the sins of her prophets, and the iniquities of her priests, that have shed the blood of the just"
- **Zeph 3:4** — "her priests have polluted the sanctuary, they have done violence to the law"

### 7. Eschatological and Universal Priesthood
Passages extending the priestly role beyond the Levitical line.
- **Exo 19:6** — "ye shall be unto me a kingdom of priests, and an holy nation"
- **Isa 61:6** — "ye shall be named the Priests of the LORD: men shall call you the Ministers of our God"
- **Isa 66:21** — "I will also take of them for priests and for Levites, saith the LORD"
- **Psa 110:4** — "Thou art a priest for ever after the order of Melchizedek"
