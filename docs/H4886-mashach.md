## Lexicon Definition

A primitive root; to rub with oil, i.e. to anoint; by implication, to consecrate; also to paint. KJV renders: anoint, paint.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Anointing of Sacred Objects
The tabernacle, altar, laver, and all cultic vessels are anointed with oil to consecrate them for service. Anointing makes an object "most holy" (qodesh qodashim).
- **Exo 40:9** — "thou shalt take the anointing oil, and anoint the tabernacle, and all that is therein, and shalt hallow it"
- **Exo 40:10-11** — "thou shalt anoint the altar of the burnt offering… anoint the laver and his foot"
- **Num 7:1** — "Moses had fully set up the tabernacle, and had anointed it, and sanctified it"

### 2. Anointing of Priests
Aaron and his sons are anointed to consecrate them to the priestly office. The act effects their separation from the common and their appointment to stand before YHWH.
- **Exo 28:41** — "thou shalt anoint them, and consecrate them, that they may minister unto me in the priest's office"
- **Lev 8:12** — "he poured of the anointing oil upon Aaron's head, and anointed him, to sanctify him"
- **Num 3:3** — "the sons of Aaron, the priests which were anointed, whom he consecrated to minister"

### 3. Anointing of Kings
The dominant use by frequency. Kings of Israel and Judah — Saul, David, Solomon, Jehu, Joash, Jehoahaz — are installed by anointing, typically performed by a prophet or priest at YHWH's command.
- **1 Sam 16:13** — "Samuel took the horn of oil, and anointed him in the midst of his brethren: and the Spirit of the LORD came upon David"
- **2 Sam 2:4** — "the men of Judah came, and there they anointed David king over the house of Judah"
- **1 Kings 1:39** — "Zadok the priest took an horn of oil… and anointed Solomon. And they blew the trumpet"
- **2 Kings 9:6** — "he poured the oil on his head, and said… Thus saith the LORD, I have anointed thee king over Israel"

### 4. YHWH as Direct Agent
In a subset of uses, YHWH himself is the subject of mashach — he anoints through his Spirit or by direct declaration, elevating the act from human ceremony to divine appointment.
- **Isa 61:1** — "the Spirit of the Lord GOD is upon me; because the LORD hath anointed me to preach good tidings unto the meek"
- **Ps 45:7** — "God, thy God, hath anointed thee with the oil of gladness above thy fellows"
- **Ps 89:20** — "I have found David my servant; with my holy oil have I anointed him"
- **2 Sam 12:7** — "Thus saith the LORD God of Israel, I anointed thee king over Israel"

### 5. Eschatological / Terminal Use
The one instance in prophetic literature pointing beyond the historical anointing cycle to a definitive future act.
- **Dan 9:24** — "to anoint (lim'shoach) the most holy (qodesh qodashim)" — the sixth of six goals of the seventy weeks; the object is a place or person, not a routine king

### 6. Non-Ritual / Secular Uses
A small cluster where mashach describes smearing without consecration — anointing a stone, applying oil to a shield, self-application of perfume, and once painting a wall.
- **Gen 31:13** — "where thou anointedst the pillar" — Jacob's stone pillar at Bethel
- **Isa 21:5** — "arise, ye princes, and anoint the shield" — soldiers oiling leather shields before battle
- **Amos 6:6** — "anoint themselves with the chief ointments: but they are not grieved for the affliction of Joseph"
- **Jer 22:14** — "cieled with cedar, and painted with vermilion" — the lone non-oil use
