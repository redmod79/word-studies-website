# Grammar Analysis: Isaiah 14:12–15 and Ezekiel 28:2–9 — Self-Deification Template

**Scope.** Two OT oracles against earthly rulers (the king of Babylon; the prince/nagid of Tyre) whose shared Hebrew lexicon and syntax form the most developed "I-will-be-God" / "I-sit-in-God's-seat" template in the Hebrew Bible. Both passages are parsed below word-by-word from ETCBC/BHSA via `hebrew_parser.py`. The analysis catalogs the grammatical features — not the referents. Application (earthly king, Satan, eschatological figure, typological layering) is interpretive and left to studies that cite this file.

## Key Grammatical Features (cross-passage)

### 1. The Verbless Nominal Clause: אֵל אָנִי / אֱלֹהִים אָנִי

- **Form:** Predicate-Subject verbless clause (type `NmCl` in ETCBC). No copula. Word order is predicate-first (PreC + Subj), which is the emphatic/classifying order in Biblical Hebrew (cf. Ezek 28:2 clause 6; 28:9 clause 2).
- **Function:** Ascribes a class or essence. "I (am) God" — not a statement of location or activity but of *identity*. Parallel to Exod 3:14's divine self-identification structure (though there the pronoun is first: אָנֹכִי אָנֹכִי... אֱהְיֶה).
- **Significance:** The predicate-fronting (אֵל / אֱלֹהִים preceding אָנִי) throws the weight on the divine title, not the speaker — grammatically equivalent to saying "**God** — that's what I am." The same structure recurs in YHWH's own self-declarations (e.g., "I am YHWH" אֲנִי יְהוָה with subject-predicate order; here the order is *reversed*, placing the divine title in the emphatic slot).
- **Ezek 28 rhetorical frame:** The verbless clause is bracketed by its negation in the same verse (v. 2: וְאַתָּה אָדָם וְלֹא־אֵל; v. 9: וְאַתָּה אָדָם וְלֹא־אֵל). YHWH does not merely contradict the claim; He uses the same verbless syntax to state the counter-proposition ("man — and not God"). The rebuttal mirrors the arrogation word-for-word.

### 2. First-Person Imperfect (Yiqtol 1cs) as Resolve / Intention

- **Isa 14:13–14 sequence:** אֶעֱלֶה (עלה), אָרִים (רום Hiphil), אֵשֵׁב (ישׁב), אֶעֱלֶה (עלה), אֶדַּמֶּה (דמה Hithpael) — five 1cs imperfects.
- **Form:** Qal/Hiphil/Hithpael imperfect, first singular. In direct speech embedded under אָמַרְתָּ בִלְבָבְךָ ("you said in your heart"), the 1cs yiqtol functions as **volitional / resolve** — near cohortative in force (though classical cohortative would add final -ָה suffix, which is absent here).
- **Significance:** Grammar is neutral — these forms can be predictive ("I will...") or volitional ("I intend to..."). Embedded in inner monologue (בִלְבָבְךָ), the volitional reading is grammatically dominant: a resolution formed in the heart. The sequence is not a prediction but a *program* of self-exaltation.
- **Stem escalation:** Qal → Hiphil (causative) → Qal → Qal → Hithpael (reflexive). The climax is the Hithpael אֶדַּמֶּה ("I will make **myself** be like") — a grammatical form that is self-referential by stem, matching the semantic peak of the speech.

### 3. Qatal of Inward State: גָּבַהּ לִבְּךָ and אָמַרְתָּ בִלְבָבְךָ

- **Form:** גָּבַהּ is Qal perfect 3ms ("is/has been high"); the subject is לִבְּךָ ("your heart"). The qatal here expresses a **stative/resultant state** — "your heart has-become/is lifted up." Parallel in Ezek 28:5 (וַיִּגְבַּהּ לְבָבְךָ — wayyiqtol continuing the climb).
- **Isa 14:13** uses אָמַרְתָּ (Qal perf 2ms) + בִלְבָבְךָ with a WXQt clause type (waw + subject-pronoun + qatal; see [syntax-wx-qatal-circumstantial](../hebrew/syntax-wx-qatal-circumstantial.md)). The pronoun אַתָּה is expressed — grammatically redundant since 2ms is in the verb — marking **emphatic contrast** ("as for **you**, you said...").
- **Significance:** Both oracles locate the offense in the **heart** (לֵב / לֵבָב), and both verbalize the heart's posture with qatal (completed/resultant). The grammar frames the sin as a settled inner state, not a momentary slip.

### 4. יַעַן + Qatal / InfCon: Causal-Indictment Clauses

- **Ezek 28:2:** יַעַן גָּבַהּ לִבְּךָ — "because your heart has-been-high" (יַעַן + qatal).
- **Ezek 28:6:** יַעַן תִּתְּךָ אֶת־לְבָבְךָ — "because of your giving your heart..." (יַעַן + **infinitive construct** with 2ms suffix as subject of the infinitive).
- **Form:** יַעַן is morphologically a construct noun ("motive/cause") functioning as a causal particle. It takes either a finite qatal clause (v. 2) or an infinitive construct with pronominal subject (v. 6).
- **Significance:** יַעַן is the **indictment marker** in Ezekielian judgment oracles — it introduces the legal ground on which the verdict (introduced by לָכֵן, "therefore," vv. 6, 7) rests. The two יַעַן clauses (vv. 2, 6) flank vv. 3–5 as a frame, and the two לָכֵן clauses (vv. 6, 7) introduce the verdict and the sentence respectively.
- **Clause-logic skeleton of vv. 2–10:**
  - יַעַן (v. 2) — charge restated: "you said אֵל אָנִי, etc."
  - Evidence (vv. 3–5): wisdom, wealth, lifted heart
  - יַעַן (v. 6) — charge re-restated: "you set your heart as God's heart"
  - לָכֵן (v. 7) — sentence: "I am bringing strangers"
  - Consequence (vv. 7b–8): swords, defilement, pit, death
  - Rhetorical coup de grâce (v. 9): הֶאָמֹר תֹּאמַר... — "will you **still** say 'God I (am)'?"

### 5. Construct Chains of Divine Reference

The passages cluster construct chains that pair divine vocabulary with topographical/political terms — the grammatical form that underwrites the arrogation:

| כּוֹכְבֵי־אֵל | Noun.mp.Cst + Noun.ms.Abs | "stars of God/El" | Isa 14:13 |
| הַר־מוֹעֵד | Noun.ms.Cst + Noun.ms.Abs | "mount of assembly" | Isa 14:13 |
| יַרְכְּתֵי צָפוֹן | Noun.fd.Cst + PropN | "recesses of Zaphon/north" | Isa 14:13 |
| בָּמֳתֵי עָב | Noun.fp.Cst + Noun.s.Abs | "heights of cloud" | Isa 14:14 |
| יַרְכְּתֵי־בוֹר | Noun.fd.Cst + Noun.ms.Abs | "recesses of the pit" | Isa 14:15 |
| מוֹשַׁב אֱלֹהִים | Noun.ms.Cst + Noun.mp.Abs | "seat of God" | Ezek 28:2 |

The two poetic inclusios are striking:
- **Isa 14:** יַרְכְּתֵי צָפוֹן (the mythic summit) → יַרְכְּתֵי בוֹר (the pit). Same construct head יַרְכְּתֵי ("remote parts of"), opposite objects. The grammar executes a chiasm: highest-high → lowest-low.
- **Ezek 28:** בְּלֵב יַמִּים (the enthronement claim, v. 2) → בְּלֵב יַמִּים (the death venue, v. 8). Identical phrase. The place of claimed deity becomes the place of death.

### 6. Passive Reversal: Hophal and Hiphil + 2ms Suffix

The judgment in both passages uses **passive morphology** to signal *who does the acting* — not the speaker, but external agents (including YHWH).

| Passage | Verb | Parsing | Gloss |
|---------|------|---------|-------|
| Isa 14:12 | נִגְדַּעְתָּ | **Niphal** Perf 2ms | "you are cut off" |
| Isa 14:15 | תּוּרָד | **Hophal** Impf 2ms | "you will be brought down" |
| Ezek 28:8 | יוֹרִדוּךָ | **Hiphil** Impf 3mp + 2ms suff. | "they will bring you down" |

The Hophal (passive of Hiphil) of ירד ("go down") at Isa 14:15 is the grammatical inversion of Isa 14:13–14's five active-voice ascent verbs. The self-elevator becomes the passively-lowered. Ezek 28:8 achieves the same semantic result with Hiphil + object suffix — the agents are the זָרִים ("strangers") of v. 7; the grammatical patient is the prince of Tyre.

For the Niphal generally, see [stem-niphal](../hebrew/stem-niphal.md).

### 7. Wayyiqtol of Self-Accusation (Ezek 28:2)

- Two wayyiqtol forms in v. 2b: וַתֹּאמֶר (2ms) and וַתִּתֵּן (2ms).
- **Form:** Wayyiqtol normally drives narrative sequence (3ms/3mp most common). Here both are 2ms, turning the prophetic oracle into a **rehearsal of the addressee's own actions**: "and you said... and you set...".
- **Significance:** Grammatically, YHWH is narrating *back to the prince* what the prince has done. The wayyiqtol chain reconstructs the offense as historical fact under the messenger formula כֹּה אָמַר אֲדֹנָי יְהוִה. The charge is framed not as allegation but as recorded event.

### 8. Infinitive Absolute + Finite Verb (Ezek 28:9): Emphatic Interrogation

- **הֶאָמֹר תֹּאמַר** — He-interrogative + Qal InfAbs (אָמֹר) + Qal Impf 2ms (תֹּאמַר).
- **Form:** The infinitive absolute before a cognate finite verb is the classical Hebrew construction for **emphasis/intensity** ("will you really / still say...?"). The he-interrogative prefix (הֶ-) adds an incredulity question.
- **Significance:** The grammar is maximally loaded: interrogative + InfAbs + Impf = "will-you-indeed-say?" — a rhetorical construction expecting a negative answer. It is the mirror of v. 2's arrogation: the prince said "אֵל אָנִי" with a nominal clause; YHWH asks whether he will still say "אֱלֹהִים אָנִי" (same verbless clause type) while in the hand of his slayer. Grammatically matched, theologically decisive.

### 9. Participles of the Adversaries (Ezek 28:9)

- הֹרְגֶךָ — Qal participle mp + 2ms suffix: "the ones killing you."
- מְחַלְלֶיךָ — **Piel** participle mp + 2ms suffix: "the ones profaning you."
- **Significance:** Two substantive participles with identical suffixation, framing the prince in the passive role of object. The Piel חלל ("profane") is the same root as חָלָל ("slain/pierced" adjective in v. 8) — the grammar binds the death-scene to the action of the slayers.

## Cross-Passage Parallels — The Template

| Feature | Isa 14:12–15 | Ezek 28:2–9 |
|---------|-------------|-------------|
| Addressee | King of Babylon (v. 4 context) | נְגִיד צֹר / prince of Tyre |
| Self-deification formula | אֶדַּמֶּה לְעֶלְיוֹן (Hithpael) "I will make myself like Elyon" | אֵל אָנִי (verbless clause) "God (am) I" — verbless + explicit |
| Enthronement claim | אָרִים כִּסְאִי... אֵשֵׁב (exalt my throne, sit) | מוֹשַׁב אֱלֹהִים יָשַׁבְתִּי (I have sat in God's seat) |
| Location of arrogated seat | הַר־מוֹעֵד / יַרְכְּתֵי צָפוֹן / בָּמֳתֵי עָב (mountain-summit, heights, north-recesses, clouds) | בְּלֵב יַמִּים (heart of the seas) |
| Seat of offense | בִלְבָבְךָ ("in your heart") | לִבְּךָ גָּבַהּ ("your heart is high"), כְּלֵב אֱלֹהִים ("as God's heart") |
| Root for "exalt/high" | רום (Hiphil אָרִים "I will exalt") | גבה (Qal גָּבַהּ "is high"; wayyiqtol וַיִּגְבַּהּ v.5) |
| Root for "sit" | ישׁב (Qal אֵשֵׁב "I will sit") | ישׁב (Qal יָשַׁבְתִּי "I have sat") |
| Judgment verb | ירד (Hophal תּוּרָד "you will be brought down") | ירד (Hiphil יוֹרִדוּךָ "they will bring you down") + מות (Qal מַתָּה "you will die") |
| Destination | שְׁאוֹל / יַרְכְּתֵי־בוֹר (Sheol / recesses of pit) | שַׁחַת (pit) / בְּלֵב יַמִּים (same phrase as the seat-claim) |
| Divine-agent mode | Passive voice (Hophal) | Active agents (זָרִים, strangers) instrumentalized by YHWH (Hiphil הִנְנִי מֵבִיא "behold, I am bringing") |
| Explicit man/God contrast | Implicit (the fall itself) | Explicit verbless negation: וְאַתָּה אָדָם וְלֹא־אֵל (v. 2, v. 9) |

**Shared structural logic (grammar-driven):**
1. **Claim** (qatal/yiqtol in 1cs) — the inner speech of self-elevation.
2. **Indictment** (יַעַן + qatal/infcon) — the charge restated by YHWH.
3. **Verdict** (לָכֵן / אַךְ + finite verb) — the turn.
4. **Sentence** (passive or hostile-agent verbs of descent/death) — grammar reverses subject and patient.
5. **Coup** (verbless rebuttal) — same syntactic frame as the claim, inverted content.

The two passages share every major lexeme of the template: ישׁב (sit), רום/גבה (exalt/be-high), לֵב (heart), אֱלֹהִים / אֵל / עֶלְיוֹן (God-titles), מוֹשַׁב / כִּסֵּא (seat/throne), ירד (brought down). Ezek 28:2 is the **only** OT verse with the explicit verbless formula אֵל אָנִי; Isa 14:14 is the **only** OT verse with the Hithpael אֶדַּמֶּה לְעֶלְיוֹן. The two oracles thus supply, between them, the full grammatical vocabulary of arrogated deity.

### 2 Thess 2:4 Grammatical Correspondences (reference only)

The Greek of 2 Thess 2:4 (καθίσαι εἰς τὸν ναὸν τοῦ Θεοῦ ἀποδεικνύντα ἑαυτὸν ὅτι ἐστὶν Θεός) uses three verbs whose LXX/Hebrew counterparts recur in this template:

| Greek (2 Thess 2:4) | Hebrew counterpart (this template) | Morphological match |

| καθίσαι (aor. inf. of καθίζω, "sit") | ישׁב — Isa 14:13 אֵשֵׁב; Ezek 28:2 יָשַׁבְתִּי | Both roots = "enthrone oneself" |
| ὑπεραιρόμενος (pres. ptcp. of ὑπεραίρω, "exalt oneself over") | רום Hiphil — Isa 14:13 אָרִים; cf. גבה Qal Ezek 28:2, 5 | Hiphil "cause-to-be-high" ‖ ὑπέρ-compound |

## Notes for Studies Citing This File

- The grammatical data is referent-agnostic. Whether either passage (a) addresses only its named earthly ruler, (b) typologically includes Satan as the primal self-exalter, (c) points forward to a final antichristian figure, or (d) any combination — these readings are all compatible with the parsing above, and the choice belongs to exegesis/theology, not grammar.
- The strongest **grammatical** link to 2 Thess 2:4 is the verbless predicate-first nominal clause אֵל אָנִי / אֱלֹהִים אָנִי of Ezek 28:2, 9 — it is the Hebrew construction closest in structure to "ὅτι ἐστὶν Θεός" (ὅτι-clause predicate with fronted divine title).
- The strongest **lexical** link is ישׁב (Qal) / καθίζω: both passages enthrone the claimant; 2 Thess 2:4 has the claimant sit in the temple.
- The strongest **rhetorical** link is the doubled pattern of claim → indictment → reversal, which the Ezekielian יַעַן...לָכֵן structure exhibits with maximum grammatical clarity.
