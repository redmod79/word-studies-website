# WG: Plagues, Stripes, Wounds

## Overview
Three Greek words cover the semantic range of "blow, stripe, wound, plague" in the NT. The core word is πληγή (G4127), which spans from literal beating to Revelation's eschatological plagues. The other two are more specific: μάστιξ (G3148) focuses on the instrument of flogging, and μώλωψ (G3468) names the resulting bruise-mark.

## Roles in the Concept

- **The stroke / judgment:** πληγή (G4127) — the broadest: physical blow, open wound, divinely inflicted plague
- **The whip / scourge:** μάστιξ (G3148) — the instrument; also diseases as God's scourges (Mark 3:10, 5:29, 5:34; Luke 7:21; Heb 11:36)
- **The bruise / weal:** μώλωψ (G3468) — the mark remaining; soteriological focus in 1 Pet 2:24 ("by whose stripes ye were healed")

## Key Passages

- **1 Pet 2:24** — "by whose stripes [μώλωπι, G3468] ye were healed" — citing Isa 53:5; connects Christ's wounds to healing
- **Rev 15:1** — "seven angels having the seven last plagues [πληγάς, G4127]; for in them is filled up the wrath of God"
- **Heb 11:36** — "had trial of cruel mockings and scourgings [μαστίγων, G3148]" — apostolic/martyr sufferings
