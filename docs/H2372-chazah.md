## Lexicon Definition

> A primitive root; to gaze at; mentally to perceive, contemplate (with pleasure); specifically, to have a vision of: — behold, look, prophesy, provide, see.

The verb differs from the more general H7200 רָאָה (ra'ah, "to see") by leaning toward fixed, contemplative gazing and, in the prophetic corpus, revelatory perception. It is the root behind the prophetic vocabulary of "vision" (chazon) and "seer" (chozeh).

**Patterns.** The verb is a **prophetic–poetic word**. It clusters in the Latter Prophets (Isaiah, Ezekiel, the Twelve) and in Wisdom poetry (Job, Psalms, Proverbs). It is nearly absent from the Torah (only Exo 18:21; 24:11; Num 24:4, 16) and the historical narrative corpus (Joshua–Kings, Chronicles, Ezra–Nehemiah, Esther, Ruth). The Numbers occurrences both belong to Balaam's oracles — the single Torah passage framed as prophetic vision.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Prophetic Vision — The Oracle a Prophet "Sees"

chazah is the technical verb for receiving divine revelation through visionary perception. Superscriptions of prophetic books use it to introduce the whole prophetic witness.

- **Isa 1:1** — "The vision of Isaiah the son of Amoz, which he saw (chazah) concerning Judah and Jerusalem"
- **Isa 2:1** — "The word that Isaiah the son of Amoz saw (chazah) concerning Judah and Jerusalem"
- **Isa 13:1** — "The burden of Babylon, which Isaiah the son of Amoz did see (chazah)"
- **Amos 1:1** — "The words of Amos... which he saw (chazah) concerning Israel"
- **Mic 1:1** — "The word of the LORD... which he saw (chazah) concerning Samaria and Jerusalem"
- **Hab 1:1** — "The burden which Habakkuk the prophet did see (chazah)"

### 2. Balaam-Type Visionary Perception — "Who Saw the Vision of the Almighty"

The verb describes the prophet's altered-state reception of a revelation.

- **Num 24:4** — "which saw (chazah) the vision (machazeh) of the Almighty, falling into a trance, but having his eyes open"
- **Num 24:16** — "saw (chazah) the vision of the most High"
- **Eze 12:27** — "The vision that he seeth (chazah) is for many days to come"

### 3. False Vision / Prophesying Lies

A sharply marked sub-use: chazah often labels what false prophets "see" (vanity, lies, vain divination). The verb itself is neutral — content determines truth or falsehood.

- **Isa 30:10** — "Which say to the seers, See not; and to the prophets, Prophesy (chazah) not unto us right things"
- **Lam 2:14** — "Thy prophets have seen (chazah) vain and foolish things for thee"
- **Eze 13:6** — "They have seen (chazah) vanity and lying divination"
- **Eze 13:7** — "Have ye not seen (chazah) a vain vision?"
- **Eze 13:8, 23; 21:29** — repeated "whiles they see (chazah) vanity"
- **Zec 10:2** — "the diviners have seen (chazah) a lie"

### 4. Beatific / Worshipful Gazing upon God

A distinctive poetic use — seeing God, His face, His beauty, His works — carrying connotations of intimacy, longing, or reverent contemplation.

- **Exo 24:11** — "also they saw (chazah) God, and did eat and drink"
- **Job 19:26** — "yet in my flesh shall I see (chazah) God"
- **Job 19:27** — "Whom I shall see (chazah) for myself, and mine eyes shall behold"
- **Psa 11:7** — "his countenance doth behold (chazah) the upright" (also, "they...behold his face")
- **Psa 17:15** — "I will behold (chazah) thy face in righteousness"
- **Psa 27:4** — "to behold (chazah) the beauty of the LORD"
- **Psa 63:2** — "To see (chazah) thy power and thy glory, so as I have seen thee in the sanctuary"

### 5. Contemplative Observation of the World / Persons

In wisdom poetry the verb denotes the sage's discerning observation of human life.

- **Pro 22:29** — "Seest thou (chazah) a man diligent in his business?"
- **Pro 24:32** — "Then I saw (chazah), and considered it well"
- **Pro 29:20** — "Seest thou (chazah) a man that is hasty in his words?"
- **Job 8:17** — "His roots... seeth (chazah) the place of stones"
- **Job 15:17; 27:12; 34:32; 36:25** — Job's appeal to shared human perception

### 6. Seeing Judgment or Vindication

The psalmist/prophet "sees" God's judgment fall on enemies or His deliverance arrive.

- **Psa 46:8** — "Come, behold (chazah) the works of the LORD"
- **Psa 58:10** — "The righteous shall rejoice when he seeth (chazah) the vengeance"
- **Isa 26:11** — "they shall see (chazah), and be ashamed for their envy"
- **Isa 33:17** — "Thine eyes shall see (chazah) the king in his beauty"
- **Isa 33:20** — "thine eyes shall see (chazah) Jerusalem a quiet habitation"
- **Mic 4:11** — "let our eye look (chazah) upon Zion"

### 7. Discerning Selection — "To Look Out / Provide"

A single-occurrence idiom where visual discernment is applied to vetting people.

- **Exo 18:21** — "Moreover thou shalt provide (chazah) out of all the people able men, such as fear God" (Jethro's counsel to Moses)
