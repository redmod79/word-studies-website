## Lexicon Definition

A primitive root; also as denominative from H3836 — to be (or become) white; to make bricks: — make brick, be (made, make) white(-r).

The verb carries two distinct uses derived from the same root: (1) the concrete activity of making bricks (related to *lebênâh*, H3843 — a brick, pale/clay-colored object) and (2) the state or process of becoming/making white (sharing the root with the adjective *lâbân*, H3836). In form, brick-making uses the Qal stem; whitening uses the Hiphil and Hithpael.

**Pattern:** The word splits cleanly by stem and context. The three Qal (brick-making) occurrences are concentrated in the Pentateuch (Tower of Babel and Egyptian slavery narratives). The five whitening usages appear in prophetic/poetic texts — two in Daniel's eschatological purification sequence, one each in Isaiah, Psalms, and Joel. ## Semantic Categories

### 1. Brick-Making — Labor and Construction (Qal)
The Qal stem refers entirely to the physical work of making clay bricks — the raw material of ancient Mesopotamian and Egyptian architecture. Both contexts carry theological weight: Babel's bricks represent human ambition; Egypt's bricks represent oppression.

- **Gen 11:3** — "Go to, let us make brick, and burn them throughly. And they had brick for stone, and slime had they for morter." (Tower of Babel — human defiance)
- **Exo 5:7** — "Ye shall no more give the people straw to make brick, as heretofore: let them go and gather straw for themselves." (Pharaoh's increased oppression)
- **Exo 5:14** — "Wherefore have ye not fulfilled your task in making brick both yesterday and to day, as heretofore?" (Officers beaten for shortfall)

### 2. Moral/Spiritual Whitening — Divine Purification (Hiphil/Hithpael)
The Hiphil and Hithpael forms describe a process of becoming white as a metaphor for purification from sin, or eschatological refinement. This is the dominant usage in prophetic literature and closely linked to refining (H6884 tsaraph) and purging (H1305 barar) — the Daniel 11–12 triad.

- **Isa 1:18** — "Though your sins be as scarlet, they shall be as white as snow; though they be red like crimson, they shall be as wool." (Divine offer of forgiveness)
- **Psa 51:7** — "Purge me with hyssop, and I shall be clean: wash me, and I shall be whiter than snow." (David's penitential prayer)
- **Dan 11:35** — "And some of them of understanding shall fall, to try them, and to purge, and to make them white, even to the time of the end." (Eschatological testing of the faithful)
- **Dan 12:10** — "Many shall be purified, and made white, and tried; but the wicked shall do wickedly." (Hithpael — the people themselves are whitened through the process)

### 3. Physical Whitening — Stripped Bare (Hiphil)
Joel's use is literal: fire or locusts stripping bark from branches leaves them bleached/white. The image reinforces the completeness of devastation.

- **Joel 1:7** — "He hath laid my vine waste, and barked my fig tree: he hath made it clean bare, and cast it away; the branches thereof are made white."
