## Key Grammatical Features

### 1. Third Word-of-YHWH Formula

Ezek 28:20 repeats the same formula as vv.1 and 11: Qal wayyiqtol of היה + construct chain `דְבַר יְהוָה` + `אֵלַי` + infinitive `לֵאמֹר`. This marks the Sidon/restoration material as a new unit, not merely an appendix to the king lament.

### 2. Double Imperative Commission Against Sidon

Ezek 28:21 uses Qal imperative `שִׂים` ("set") with object `פָּנֶיךָ`, then Niphal imperative `הִנָּבֵא` ("prophesy") with feminine suffix `עָלֶיהָ`, agreeing with Sidon as the city-addressee. This is standard prophetic targeting grammar.

### 3. Sidon Judgment Produces Recognition

Ezek 28:22-23 repeats the recognition formula with `וְיָדְעוּ כִּי־אֲנִי יְהוָה`. The judgment verbs are first-person divine actions (`נִכְבַּדְתִּי`, `נִקְדַּשְׁתִּי`, `שִׁלַּחְתִּי`), while the result is third-person plural knowing.

### 4. Israel Restoration Is a Closing Frame

Ezek 28:25-26 shifts from Sidon to `בֵּית יִשְׂרָאֵל`, a construct chain. The grammar moves from divine gathering (`בְּקַבְּצִי`) and sanctification (`וְנִקְדַּשְׁתִּי`) to Israel's plural settlement verbs: `יָשְׁבוּ`, `בָנוּ`, `נָטְעוּ`, `יָשְׁבוּ`. The repeated `לָבֶטַח` frames security as the final state.
