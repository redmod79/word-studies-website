## Lexicon Definition

From G1225 (διαβάλλω); a traducer; specially, Satan (compare H7854): — false accuser, devil, slanderer.

The word's literal force is "one who throws across/against" — i.e., one who lodges accusations, a slanderer or calumniator. In the NT it functions in two registers: (1) as the proper name/title of the chief adversary of God and humanity (always articular or nearly so; always singular), and (2) as an adjective describing humans who are "false accusers" or "slanderers" (1 Tim 3:11; 2 Tim 3:3; Tit 2:3).

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Satan as Tempter (Synoptic Temptation Narrative)
Personal engagement with Jesus in the wilderness — the devil speaks, acts, and ultimately departs.
- **Mat 4:1** — "Then was Jesus led up of the Spirit into the wilderness to be tempted of the devil."
- **Mat 4:5** — "Then the devil taketh him up into the holy city, and setteth him on a pinnacle of the temple."
- **Luke 4:6** — "And the devil said unto him, All this power will I give thee, and the glory of them."
- **Luke 4:13** — "And when the devil had ended all the temptation, he departed from him for a season."

### 2. Satan as Active Adversary of Believers
The devil as an ongoing threat who must be resisted; believers warned to stand against him.
- **Eph 6:11** — "Put on the whole armour of God, that ye may be able to stand against the wiles of the devil."
- **1 Pet 5:8** — "Your adversary the devil, as a roaring lion, walketh about, seeking whom he may devour."
- **Jas 4:7** — "Submit yourselves therefore to God. Resist the devil, and he will flee from you."
- **Eph 4:27** — "Neither give place to the devil."

### 3. Satan as Origin of Sin and Murder
The devil as progenitor of sin, murder, and deceit; humans characterized by whose "father" they follow.
- **John 8:44** — "Ye are of your father the devil...He was a murderer from the beginning...for he is a liar, and the father of it."
- **1 John 3:8** — "He that committeth sin is of the devil; for the devil sinneth from the beginning."
- **Acts 13:10** — "O full of all subtilty and all mischief, thou child of the devil, thou enemy of all righteousness."
- **John 13:2** — "the devil having now put into the heart of Judas Iscariot...to betray him."

### 4. Satan in Eschatological Judgment
The devil bound, cast out, and consigned to final destruction; identified with the dragon and old serpent.
- **Rev 12:9** — "the great dragon was cast out, that old serpent, called the Devil, and Satan, which deceiveth the whole world."
- **Rev 20:2** — "he laid hold on the dragon, that old serpent, which is the Devil, and Satan, and bound him a thousand years."
- **Rev 20:10** — "the devil that deceived them was cast into the lake of fire and brimstone."
- **Mat 25:41** — "Depart from me, ye cursed, into everlasting fire, prepared for the devil and his angels."

### 5. Human Slanderers (Adjectival Use)
The only non-personal uses — describing people who are false accusers/slanderers. No article; functions as predicate adjective.
- **1 Tim 3:11** — "Even so must their wives be grave, not slanderers (διαβόλους), sober, faithful in all things."
- **2 Tim 3:3** — "Without natural affection, trucebreakers, false accusers (διάβολοι), incontinent, fierce."
- **Tit 2:3** — "The aged women likewise...not false accusers (διαβόλους), not given to much wine."
- **John 6:70** — "Jesus answered them, Have not I chosen you twelve, and one of you is a devil (διάβολός)?" (of Judas)
