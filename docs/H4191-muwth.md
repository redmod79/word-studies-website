## Lexicon Definition

A primitive root; to die (literally or figuratively); causatively, to kill:— at all, crying, (be) dead (body, man, one), (put to, worthy of) death, destroy(-er), (cause to, be like to, must) die, kill, necro(-mancer), must needs, slay, surely, very suddenly, in (no) wise.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Natural/Narrative Death
Death as a life event — patriarchal death notices, reports of dying from old age or illness.
- **Gen 5:5** — "And all the days that Adam lived were nine hundred and thirty years: and he died."
- **Gen 25:8** — "Then Abraham gave up the ghost, and died in a good old age, an old man, and full of years."
- **Job 42:17** — "So Job died, being old and full of days."
- **2 Ki 13:20** — "And Elisha died, and they buried him."

### 2. Judicial Death Formula (מוֹת יוּמָת — "surely be put to death")
The infinitive absolute + Hophal imperfect construction for capital sentencing in the Mosaic code.
- **Gen 2:17** — "for in the day that thou eatest thereof thou shalt surely die" (מוֹת תָּמוּת — Qal InfAbs + Qal Impf)
- **Exo 21:12** — "He that smiteth a man, so that he die, shall be surely put to death."
- **Lev 20:10** — "the adulterer and the adulteress shall surely be put to death."
- **Lev 24:16** — "he that blasphemeth the name of the LORD, he shall surely be put to death."

### 3. Death as Divine Judgment / Consequence of Sin
God directly causing death, or death resulting from disobedience.
- **Gen 38:7** — "And Er, Judah's firstborn, was wicked in the sight of the LORD; and the LORD slew him."
- **Lev 10:2** — "And there went out fire from the LORD, and devoured them, and they died before the LORD."
- **Num 16:49** — "Now they that died in the plague were fourteen thousand and seven hundred."
- **2 Sam 6:7** — "the anger of the LORD was kindled against Uzzah; and God smote him there... and there he died."

### 4. Death as a State / the Dead (Participial)
The Qal participle (מֵת) used adjectivally for a corpse or dead person.
- **Gen 23:3** — "Abraham stood up from before his dead, and spake unto the sons of Heth."
- **Num 19:13** — "Whosoever toucheth the dead body of any man that is dead..."
- **Lev 21:11** — "Neither shall he go in to any dead body."
- **Ecc 9:5** — "the living know that they shall die: but the dead know not any thing."

### 5. Causative Killing (Hiphil)
The Hiphil stem used for intentional or commanded killing — humans causing death.
- **Gen 37:18** — "they conspired against him to slay him."
- **1 Sam 2:6** — "The LORD killeth, and maketh alive."
- **Deu 32:39** — "I kill, and I make alive; I wound, and I heal."
- **Jer 26:15** — "if ye put me to death, ye shall surely bring innocent blood upon yourselves."

### 6. Theological/Prophetic Death — Individual Accountability
Ezekiel's extended use of muwth for the principle of personal responsibility before God.
- **Eze 18:4** — "the soul that sinneth, it shall die."
- **Eze 18:20** — "The soul that sinneth, it shall die. The son shall not bear the iniquity of the father."
- **Eze 33:8** — "When I say unto the wicked, O wicked man, thou shalt surely die..."
- **Eze 33:15** — "If the wicked restore the pledge... he shall surely live; he shall not die."

### 7. Death as Universal Human Condition
Wisdom and existential reflections on mortality.
- **Ecc 3:2** — "A time to be born, and a time to die."
- **2 Sam 14:14** — "For we must needs die, and are as water spilt on the ground."
- **Job 14:10** — "But man dieth, and wasteth away: yea, man giveth up the ghost, and where is he?"
- **Hab 1:12** — "Art thou not from everlasting, O LORD my God, mine Holy One? we shall not die."
