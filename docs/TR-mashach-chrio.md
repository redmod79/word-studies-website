**Semantic continuity:** The LXX translators reserved χρίω specifically for the consecrating act (mashach) and used ἀλείφω for ordinary anointing. This distinction carries forward precisely into the NT. The Hebrew verb's two domains — ritual consecration and physical smearing — map cleanly to two different Greek words.

## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same:**
- χρίω consistently renders the *official, divinely initiated* anointing — never ordinary personal grooming
- The pattern "God [subject] anoints [person] for [mission]" is identical in OT mashach and NT χρίω usage
- The anointing is always accompanied by Spirit empowerment in the key passages (1 Sam 16:13; Isa 61:1; Luke 4:18; Acts 10:38)

**What shifts:**
- In the OT, mashach primarily installs kings and priests to institutional offices; in the NT, χρίω shifts toward a pneumatic, direct divine act on the Messiah and also on believers (2 Cor 1:21; 1 John 2:20, 27)
- The noun Χριστός (Christós) becomes a proper name/title rather than a general category — the semantic weight moves from "an anointed one among many" to "the Anointed One"
