## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same across testaments:**
- The figurative sense of "cup as allotted portion" — whether of joy (Ps 16:5; 23:5) or of judgment (Ps 75:8; Isa 51:17) — transfers intact.
- The pairing of cup + wine + wrath (Ps 75:8; Jer 25:15) is quoted directly in Rev 14:10.
- The Babylon-cup figure of Jer 51:7 reappears as Rev 17:4 / 18:6.
- The verb pair "drink the cup" / שׁתה את הכוס (Isa 51:17; Jer 25:28) → πίνω τὸ ποτήριον (Mat 20:22; Mar 10:38; Jhn 18:11) is a direct calque.

**What shifts in the NT:**
- A new figurative use emerges: the **eucharistic cup of blessing** (Mat 26:27; 1Co 10:16; 11:25). The "cup of salvation" of Ps 116:13 is no mere echo — it is filled with Christ's own blood and becomes the covenant sign.
- The **same vessel holds both blessing and wrath** — this is the theological hinge. At the Last Supper Jesus gives the cup of blessing (Mat 26:27); at Gethsemane he asks the Father to remove the cup of wrath (Mat 26:39). In Revelation the cup of wrath is poured out on Babylon (Rev 14:10; 16:19). The cup Christ drinks is the cup his people are spared.
- The "cup and baptism" pairing (Mk 10:38–39) is NT-distinctive — suffering-overwhelm language attached to the Hebrew cup figure.
