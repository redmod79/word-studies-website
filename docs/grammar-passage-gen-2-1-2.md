## Key Grammatical Features

### 1. יְכֻלּוּ (wayekhulu) — Pual Stem, the Only Pual Occurrence of כלה

- **Form:** Verb.Pual.Wayq.3mp of כלה (H3615). Pual stem = passive of Piel. Wayyiqtol (waw-consecutive imperfect) marks narrative sequence.
- **Root:** כלה (k-l-h) — "to complete, finish, bring to an end, consume."
- **Significance:** This is **the only Pual occurrence of כלה in the entire Hebrew Bible** (199 verses, 258 tokens — Pual count: 1). The Pual is the passive-intensive stem: the subject receives the action as a completed state with enduring result. The heavens and earth are not self-completed; they stand as *having been brought to completion* by an unexpressed agent. The passive voice grammatically occludes the agent in v.1, setting up the agent-reveal in v.2.
- **3mp agreement:** The verb is 3rd masculine plural, agreeing with the compound subject "the heavens and the earth and all their host" — a plural entity construed as a unified whole.
- **Grammar reference:** No canonical Pual stem entry exists yet. Pual (= pu''al) is the passive counterpart of the Piel, formed with u-class vowels (quttal pattern). It is the least common of the seven major stems. Diagnostic: u-vowel after first root consonant (e.g., יְכֻלּוּ ← *yequṭṭəlû*).
- **Word study:** [H3615-kalah.md](../../word-studies/H3615-kalah.md)

### 2. יְכַל (wayekhal) — Piel Stem, Active Completion by God

- **Form:** Verb.Piel.Wayq.3ms of כלה (H3615). Piel stem = factitive/resultative active. Wayyiqtol narrative sequential.
- **Root:** Same lemma כלה (k-l-h), but the stem-switch from Pual (v.1) to Piel (v.2) is the grammatical hinge of the passage.
- **Significance:** The Piel stem expresses **active, agent-driven completion**. In v.1 the heavens and earth passively *stand completed* (Pual); in v.2 God actively *completes/brings to an end* (Piel) his work. The Piel is the dominant stem for כלה (141 of 199 occurrences), typically expressing transitive action: bringing something to its end, finishing a task, consuming in judgment. Here the object is מְלַאכְתּוֹ ("his work"), and the explicit subject is אֱלֹהִים — naming the agent that v.1's Pual left unexpressed.
- **Grammar reference:** No canonical Piel stem entry exists yet. Piel (= pi''el) is the factitive/intensive stem, formed with i-vowel after first root consonant and doubled middle radical (qittel pattern). Core function: bringing about a state in the object that corresponds to the Qal's basic meaning. With stative roots like כלה, the Piel is resultative: "to cause X to be complete/ended."
- **Word study:** [H3615-kalah.md](../../word-studies/H3615-kalah.md), [TR-kalah-synteleo.md](../../word-studies/TR-kalah-synteleo.md)

### 3. Pual → Piel Stem-Shift Architecture (vv.1–2)

- **Form:** The same lemma (כלה) appears in two different stems in adjacent verses, in the same narrative tense (wayyiqtol), under the same conjunction (waw-consecutive). The shift is grammatically deliberate.
- **Significance:** The Pual of v.1 states creation's completedness as a passive fact — the cosmos simply *is* finished. The Piel of v.2 identifies the agent and the work that was completed. Together they form a grammatical chiasm:
  - **v.1:** [passive-result] heavens-and-earth **were-completed** (Pual)
  - **v.2:** [active-agent] God **completed** (Piel) his work
  - **v.2b:** [active-agent] God **ceased** (Qal) from his work
- **Theological relevance:** This stem architecture grounds the NT "it is finished" language (John 19:30 τετέλεσται, perfect passive of τελέω G5055). The perfect passive in Greek echoes the Hebrew Pual: a completed action whose result endures. The LXX renders v.1 with συνετελέσθησαν (aorist passive of συντελέω G4931, the dominant LXX equivalent of כלה), and v.2 with συνετέλεσεν (aorist active of συντελέω) — preserving the passive→active stem shift. The cross-testament bridge is: H3615 כלה (Pual) → G4931 συντελέω (passive) → G5055 τελέω (passive) at the cross.
- **Cross-references:** [H3615-kalah.md](../../word-studies/H3615-kalah.md), [TR-kalah-synteleo.md](../../word-studies/TR-kalah-synteleo.md), [G5055-teleo.md](../../word-studies/G5055-teleo.md), [G4931-synteleo.md](../../word-studies/G4931-synteleo.md)

### 4. Wayyiqtol Tense — Narrative Sequential

- **Form:** Both key verbs (יְכֻלּוּ and יְכַל) are in the wayyiqtol (waw-consecutive imperfect) form. Also וַיִּשְׁבֹּת (v.2b).
- **Significance:** Wayyiqtol is the main narrative tense of Biblical Hebrew, carrying the storyline forward in sequence. Both verbs are prefixed with וַ + short imperfect. The Pual wayyiqtol in v.1 is morphologically notable: the u-class theme vowel of the Pual stem combines with the wayyiqtol prefix to produce *wayekhullû* (וַיְכֻלּוּ), carrying the doubled middle radical (dagesh in ל) characteristic of the Pual/Piel.
- **Grammar reference:** No canonical wayyiqtol entry exists. Wayyiqtol is the primary narrative tense form: prefix-conjugation with waw-consecutive (וַ + shortened imperfect). It marks sequential past action ("and X happened... and then Y happened").

### 5. Compound Subject and Construct Chain in v.1

- **Form:** הַשָּׁמַיִם וְהָאָרֶץ וְכָל־צְבָאָם — a three-member compound subject (two articular nouns + one construct chain), all governed by the single predicate יְכֻלּוּ. The construct chain כָל־צְבָאָם (ms.Cst + ms.Abs.+3mp) with the 3mp pronominal suffix referring back to both heavens and earth.
- **Significance:** The compound-subject syntax unites heavens, earth, and their host as a single grammatical entity receiving the predicate "were completed." The 3mp suffix on צְבָאָם ("their host") binds the host to both heavens and earth, not to one alone. The construct chain כָל ("all/whole of") distributes totality over the host noun.
