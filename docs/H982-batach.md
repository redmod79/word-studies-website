## Lexicon Definition

A primitive root; properly, to hie for refuge (but not so precipitately as H2620 chasah); figuratively, to trust, be confident or sure: — be bold (confident, secure, sure), careless (one, woman), put confidence, (make to) hope, (put, make to) trust.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Trusting in YHWH — Positive Command and Declaration
The primary and most frequent use: placing confident reliance upon God.
- **Psa 37:3** — "Trust in the LORD, and do good; so shalt thou dwell in the land"
- **Psa 115:9-11** — "O Israel, trust thou in the LORD: he is their help and their shield" (threefold call to Israel, Aaron, God-fearers)
- **Pro 3:5** — "Trust in the LORD with all thine heart; and lean not unto thine own understanding"
- **Isa 26:4** — "Trust ye in the LORD for ever: for in the LORD JEHOVAH is everlasting strength"

### 2. Personal Testimony of Trust
Individual psalmists declaring their own act of trusting, often in crisis.
- **Psa 56:3-4** — "What time I am afraid, I will trust in thee. In God I will praise his word, in God I have put my trust; I will not fear"
- **Psa 13:5** — "But I have trusted in thy mercy; my heart shall rejoice in thy salvation"
- **Psa 28:7** — "The LORD is my strength and my shield; my heart trusted in him, and I am helped"
- **Psa 31:14** — "But I trusted in thee, O LORD: I said, Thou art my God"

### 3. Misplaced Trust — Prophetic Condemnation
Trust directed toward inadequate objects: wealth, military power, false gods, human allies.
- **Jer 17:5** — "Cursed be the man that trusteth in man, and maketh flesh his arm"
- **Isa 31:1** — "Woe to them that go down to Egypt for help; and stay on horses, and trust in chariots"
- **Psa 49:6** — "They that trust in their wealth, and boast themselves in the multitude of their riches"
- **Pro 11:28** — "He that trusteth in his riches shall fall"
- **Jer 48:7** — "For because thou hast trusted in thy works and in thy treasures, thou shalt also be taken"

### 4. Trust Contrasts — Blessing vs. Curse Formulae
Explicit contrast between trusting in YHWH and trusting elsewhere.
- **Jer 17:5, 7** — "Cursed be the man that trusteth in man... Blessed is the man that trusteth in the LORD"
- **Psa 118:8-9** — "It is better to trust in the LORD than to put confidence in man. It is better to trust in the LORD than to put confidence in princes"
- **Psa 146:3** — "Put not your trust in princes, nor in the son of man, in whom there is no help"

### 5. Security / Dwelling Safely — Stative Sense
The word carries a secondary sense of settled, undisturbed security (rendered "secure," "careless," "safely").
- **Jdg 18:7** — "how they dwelt careless, after the manner of the Zidonians, quiet and secure"
- **Pro 31:11** — "The heart of her husband doth safely trust in her"
- **Isa 32:9-11** — "ye women that are at ease... ye careless daughters... ye careless ones" (participial form)
- **Pro 14:16** — "the fool rageth, and is confident"

### 6. The Hezekiah Narrative — Extended Case Study
The densest concentration outside Psalms: the Rabshakeh's challenge to Hezekiah's trust.
- **2Ki 18:5** — "He trusted in the LORD God of Israel; so that after him was none like him"
- **2Ki 18:19-22** — "What confidence is this wherein thou trustest?... thou trustest upon the staff of this bruised reed, even upon Egypt"
- **2Ki 19:10** — "Let not thy God in whom thou trustest deceive thee"
- **Isa 36:4-7** — Parallel account with identical language
