# Grammar Analysis: Revelation 21:1-22:5

Final dwelling and unveiled sanctuary. The passage moves from new creation (21:1-8), to the bride-city shown from the high mountain (21:9-21), to the explicit sanctuary climax: no separate ναός is seen, because the Lord God Almighty and the Lamb are the city's sanctuary; no created lamp or sun is needed, because divine glory and the Lamb illumine it; the river and tree of life proceed from the throne of God and the Lamb; God's servants render priestly service, see His face, bear His name, and reign forever (21:22-22:5).

## Key Grammatical Features

### 1. καὶ εἶδον / καὶ ἤκουσα / ἔδειξέν μοι — vision-report mainline

- **Forms:** εἶδον appears at 21:1, 21:2, and 21:22 (`V-2AAI-1S`); ἤκουσα at 21:3 (`V-AAI-1S`); ἔδειξέν μοι at 21:10 and 22:1 (`V-AAI-3S` + dative pronoun).
- **Function:** The aorist indicatives present the seer's received scenes as bounded vision units. They do not, by grammar alone, require that every image is chronologically subsequent to the previous one; they mark the visionary report's mainline.
- **Passage significance:** 21:22 is framed with the same seer-verb as 21:1-2: "I saw no temple." The absence of a separate sanctuary is not an aside; it is a reported object of the final vision.
- **Grammar references:** [vision-introduction-formulas](../greek/vision-introduction-formulas.md); [aorist-active-indicative-narrative-chain](../greek/aorist-active-indicative-narrative-chain.md).

### 2. ἀπήνεγκέν με ἐν Πνεύματι ἐπὶ ὄρος — final visionary transposition

- **Form:** ἀπήνεγκέν (`V-AAI-3S`) + με (`P-1AS`) + ἐν Πνεύματι (`PREP` + `N-DSN`) + ἐπὶ ὄρος (`PREP` + `N-ASN`).
- **Function:** This is the fourth Revelation "in Spirit" macro-frame. The seer is transported in the visionary state to the high mountain, paralleling the wilderness transposition of 17:3 but reversing the vision-object from harlot-city to bride-city.
- **Passage significance:** The high mountain vantage introduces the holy Jerusalem as an eschatological sanctuary-city. Grammar marks the scene as visionary transposition, not ordinary geographical travel.
- **Grammar references:** [en-pneumati-visionary-transposition](../greek/en-pneumati-visionary-transposition.md); [vision-introduction-formulas](../greek/vision-introduction-formulas.md).

### 3. καταβαίνουσαν ... ἡτοιμασμένην ... κεκοσμημένην — the city as descending prepared bride

- **Forms:** καταβαίνουσαν (`V-PAP-ASF`) modifies τὴν πόλιν / Ἱερουσαλὴμ; ἡτοιμασμένην (`V-RPP-ASF`) and κεκοσμημένην (`V-RPP-ASF`) describe her as already prepared and adorned.
- **Function:** The present participle καταβαίνουσαν portrays the city in the act/state of descent within the vision. The perfect passive participles foreground resultant state: the city stands prepared and adorned because of prior action done to her.
- **Passage significance:** The bride-city is not presented as self-made. Its readiness is grammatically passive and stative. The sanctuary-city descends "out of heaven from God" (ἐκ τοῦ οὐρανοῦ ἀπὸ τοῦ Θεοῦ), grounding its origin in divine action.
- **Grammar reference:** [participle-perfect-passive-state](../greek/participle-perfect-passive-state.md).
- **Word studies:** [G2537-kainos](../../word-studies/G2537-kainos.md); gap flagged for G2597 καταβαίνω, G2090 ἑτοιμάζω, and G2885 κοσμέω.

### 4. ἡ σκηνὴ τοῦ Θεοῦ μετὰ τῶν ἀνθρώπων ... σκηνώσει — tabernacle as final divine dwelling

- **Forms:** ἡ σκηνὴ (`T-NSF + N-NSF`) stands in a verbless presentative clause after Ἰδοὺ; τοῦ Θεοῦ is a genitive of possession/source/association; μετὰ τῶν ἀνθρώπων marks accompaniment; σκηνώσει is future active indicative (`V-FAI-3S`).
- **Function:** The noun σκηνή and verb σκηνόω form a lexical pair: "the tabernacle of God [is] with men, and he will tabernacle/dwell with them." The future verb extends the presentative claim into permanent covenantal realization.
- **Passage significance:** In sanctuary-service synthesis, 21:3 is the thesis statement for the whole final scene. The sanctuary's end is not merely access to a holy building but God's own tent-presence with humanity.
- **Word studies:** [G4633-skene](../../word-studies/G4633-skene.md); [G4637-skenoo](../../word-studies/G4637-skenoo.md); [G444-anthropos](../../word-studies/G444-anthropos.md).

### 5. Γέγοναν ... ποιῶ πάντα καινά — completed new-creation decree and ongoing divine making

- **Forms:** Γέγοναν (`V-2RAI-3P`) is perfect active indicative, "they have come to be / are done"; ποιῶ (`V-PAI-1S`) is present active indicative; καινά πάντα are accusative neuter plural objects.
- **Function:** The perfect Γέγοναν presents the decree as accomplished with abiding result. The present ποιῶ portrays the throne-sitter's action as active and immediate within the announcement: "I make all things new."
- **Passage significance:** The new heaven/new earth and the final sanctuary-city are not merely future scenery. Grammatically, the throne speech declares the consummation as accomplished and the divine new-making as the controlling present reality.
- **Grammar references:** [tense-perfect-indicative](../greek/tense-perfect-indicative.md); [tense-present-active-indicative](../greek/tense-present-active-indicative.md).
- **Word studies:** [G1096-ginomai](../../word-studies/G1096-ginomai.md); [G2537-kainos](../../word-studies/G2537-kainos.md); [G4160-poieo](../../word-studies/G4160-poieo.md).

### 6. ναὸν οὐκ εἶδον ... Κύριος ὁ Θεὸς ὁ Παντοκράτωρ ναὸς αὐτῆς ἐστιν, καὶ τὸ Ἀρνίον

- **Forms:** ναὸν (`N-ASM`) is fronted before οὐκ εἶδον (`PRT-N + V-2AAI-1S`); ναὸς (`N-NSM`) is predicate nominative with ἐστιν (`V-PAI-3S`); the divine title cluster is articular and nominative; τὸ Ἀρνίον (`T-NSN + N-NSN`) is coordinated after the predicate clause.
- **Function:** The first clause negates a seen object: "a temple I did not see in her." The γάρ clause gives the explanation: the city's sanctuary is the Lord God Almighty, with the Lamb joined in the same sanctuary identity. The singular ἐστιν does not diminish the Lamb; Greek can use singular agreement where a compound subject is conceived as a unity or where the second nominative is appended epexegetically.
- **Passage significance:** Grammatically, ναός is not abolished as a concept; it is relocated from structure to divine presence. The final sanctuary is personal: God and the Lamb are the city's sanctuary.
- **Word studies:** [G3485-naos](../../word-studies/G3485-naos.md); [G721-arnion](../../word-studies/G721-arnion.md).

### 7. ἡ δόξα τοῦ Θεοῦ ἐφώτισεν ... ὁ λύχνος αὐτῆς τὸ Ἀρνίον ... φωτίσει

- **Forms:** 21:23 uses οὐ χρείαν ἔχει (`V-PAI-3S`) plus genitives τοῦ ἡλίου / τῆς σελήνης; ἵνα φαίνωσιν is present active subjunctive (`V-PAS-3P`) after ἵνα; ἐφώτισεν is aorist active indicative (`V-AAI-3S`); ὁ λύχνος αὐτῆς τὸ Ἀρνίον is an elliptical nominal clause. 22:5 repeats the no-need construction and shifts to φωτίσει (`V-FAI-3S`).
- **Function:** The created light-bearers are syntactically unnecessary for the city. The explanatory γάρ/ὅτι clauses identify the true illumination: God's glory has illumined it, the Lamb is its lamp, and the Lord God will illumine His servants.
- **Passage significance:** This is sanctuary-light language transposed to the final dwelling. The lampstand/created luminaries yield to God's glory and the Lamb as the direct source and vessel of light.
- **Word studies:** [G1391-doxa](../../word-studies/G1391-doxa.md); [G5457-phos](../../word-studies/G5457-phos.md); [G721-arnion](../../word-studies/G721-arnion.md).

### 8. ποταμὸν ὕδατος ζωῆς ... ἐκπορευόμενον ἐκ τοῦ θρόνου τοῦ Θεοῦ καὶ τοῦ Ἀρνίου

- **Forms:** ποταμὸν (`N-ASM`) is the object of ἔδειξέν; ὕδατος ζωῆς is a nested genitive chain; ἐκπορευόμενον (`V-PNP-ASM`) modifies ποταμὸν; ἐκ τοῦ θρόνου marks source; τοῦ Θεοῦ καὶ τοῦ Ἀρνίου are coordinated genitives depending on θρόνου.
- **Function:** The present participle ἐκπορευόμενον portrays the river as proceeding from the throne in the vision. The single throne is genitively related to both God and the Lamb.
- **Passage significance:** The water of life is not merely located in the city; it issues from the shared throne. Sanctuary, kingship, life, and Lamb theology converge in one genitive chain.
- **Word studies:** [G2362-thronos](../../word-studies/G2362-thronos.md); [G2222-zoe](../../word-studies/G2222-zoe.md); [G721-arnion](../../word-studies/G721-arnion.md).
- **Gaps:** canonical word studies for G4215 ποταμός, G5204 ὕδωρ, and G1607 ἐκπορεύομαι would help future studies.

### 9. ξύλον ζωῆς ποιοῦν ... ἀποδιδοῦν ... εἰς θεραπείαν τῶν ἐθνῶν

- **Forms:** ξύλον ζωῆς is nominative with genitive ζωῆς; ποιοῦν (`V-PAP-NSN`) and ἀποδιδοῦν (`V-PAP-NSN`) are present active participles agreeing with ξύλον; εἰς θεραπείαν is a purpose/result prepositional phrase; τῶν ἐθνῶν is an objective/recipient genitive with θεραπείαν.
- **Function:** The tree is characterized by continuous fruit-bearing and fruit-yielding. The leaves are directed "unto healing" for the nations.
- **Passage significance:** Edenic tree imagery is placed inside the final sanctuary-city and tied to the nations' healing. The grammar stresses ongoing provision, not a one-time act.
- **Word studies:** [G3586-xylon](../../word-studies/G3586-xylon.md); [G2222-zoe](../../word-studies/G2222-zoe.md).

### 10. οὐ μὴ κλεισθῶσιν / οὐ μὴ εἰσέλθῃ — emphatic negation at the gates

- **Forms:** 21:25 οὐ μὴ κλεισθῶσιν combines οὐ μή with aorist passive subjunctive (`V-APS-3P`); 21:27 οὐ μὴ εἰσέλθῃ combines οὐ μή with second-aorist active subjunctive (`V-2AAS-3S`).
- **Function:** οὐ μή + subjunctive is emphatic future-oriented denial: the gates shall certainly not be shut; nothing common/defiling shall certainly enter.
- **Passage significance:** The city is permanently open and permanently pure. Access and exclusion are both stated with the strongest negation pattern in the passage.
- **Grammar gap:** no canonical οὐ μή + subjunctive entry is present yet. Existing related contrast note appears in [purpose-clause-hina-me-aorist-subjunctive](../greek/purpose-clause-hina-me-aorist-subjunctive.md), but Revelation 21:25, 27 need their own emphatic-negation reference.

### 11. λατρεύσουσιν αὐτῷ ... ὄψονται τὸ πρόσωπον αὐτοῦ ... τὸ ὄνομα αὐτοῦ

- **Forms:** λατρεύσουσιν (`V-FAI-3P`) takes singular dative αὐτῷ; ὄψονται (`V-FDI-3P`) takes τὸ πρόσωπον αὐτοῦ; τὸ ὄνομα αὐτοῦ is nominative in an verbless clause with ἐπὶ τῶν μετώπων αὐτῶν.
- **Function:** The servants render future cultic service/worship to a singular referent, see His singular face, and bear His singular name. In immediate context, that singular reference follows "the throne of God and of the Lamb."
- **Passage significance:** The grammar joins priestly service, beatific sight, covenant ownership, and royal reign. In the final sanctuary, servants are not outside the veil: they see His face.
- **Word studies:** gap flagged for G3000 λατρεύω and G4383 πρόσωπον; [G2362-thronos](../../word-studies/G2362-thronos.md); [G721-arnion](../../word-studies/G721-arnion.md).

### 12. βασιλεύσουσιν εἰς τοὺς αἰῶνας τῶν αἰώνων — servants as eternal reigners

- **Forms:** βασιλεύσουσιν (`V-FAI-3P`) is future active indicative; εἰς τοὺς αἰῶνας τῶν αἰώνων is the standard accusative + genitive idiom of endless duration.
- **Function:** The subjects are the servants who serve, see, and bear the name. The passage ends by identifying them also as those who reign.
- **Passage significance:** Final sanctuary service is not servile exclusion from rule; it is priestly-royal participation under the throne of God and the Lamb.
- **Word study gap:** G936 βασιλεύω would be useful for the royal dimension of final sanctuary service.

## Clause Structure

### New Creation and Covenant Dwelling (21:1-8)

1. **Vision object:** καὶ εἶδον + double accusative object: οὐρανὸν καινὸν / γῆν καινήν.
2. **Grounding explanation:** γάρ explains the newness by the passing away of the first heaven and earth.
3. **Bride-city object:** τὴν πόλιν ... εἶδον + participial modifiers: καταβαίνουσαν, ἡτοιμασμένην, κεκοσμημένην.
4. **Auditory proclamation:** ἤκουσα φωνῆς ... λεγούσης introduces direct divine/celestial speech.
5. **Covenant formula:** ἡ σκηνὴ ... μετὰ τῶν ἀνθρώπων + σκηνώσει + λαοὶ ... ἔσονται + Θεὸς ... ἔσται.
6. **Negated former order:** οὐκ ἔσται ἔτι repeated with death, mourning, crying, pain; ὅτι explains with τὰ πρῶτα ἀπῆλθαν.
7. **Throne speech:** ὁ καθήμενος ... εἶπεν + present making (ποιῶ), aorist command (Γράψον), perfect consummation (Γέγοναν), and future gift/inheritance.

### Bride-City Shown from the Mountain (21:9-21)

1. **Angel introduction:** εἷς ἐκ τῶν ἑπτὰ ἀγγέλων + participial descriptors τῶν ἐχόντων / τῶν γεμόντων.
2. **Speech invitation:** ἐλάλησεν ... λέγων + Δεῦρο, δείξω.
3. **Visionary transport:** ἀπήνεγκέν με ἐν Πνεύματι ἐπὶ ὄρος.
4. **City display:** ἔδειξέν μοι τὴν πόλιν ... καταβαίνουσαν ... ἔχουσαν.
5. **Name and structure:** repeated ἔχουσα clauses, perfect passive ἐπιγεγραμμένα, foundation/gate lists, and repeated numerals.
6. **Measurement:** ἵνα μετρήσῃ purpose clause, followed by finite aorists ἐμέτρησεν and nominal dimension clauses.
7. **Cubic statement:** τὸ μῆκος καὶ τὸ πλάτος καὶ τὸ ὕψος αὐτῆς ἴσα ἐστίν. Grammatically this is the passage's strongest geometric statement. It supports sanctuary-service comparison with the Most Holy Place as a theological synthesis, while the grammar itself only asserts equal length, breadth, and height.

### No Temple, Divine Light, Open Gates, Pure Entry (21:22-27)

1. **Negated vision:** ναὸν οὐκ εἶδον ἐν αὐτῇ.
2. **Explanatory predicate:** γάρ + nominative divine title cluster + predicate ναὸς + ἐστιν + coordinated Lamb.
3. **No-need light clause:** πόλις οὐ χρείαν ἔχει + genitives of sun/moon + ἵνα φαίνωσιν.
4. **True light explanation:** γάρ + δόξα τοῦ Θεοῦ ἐφώτισεν + nominal clause identifying the Lamb as lamp.
5. **Nations and kings:** future περιπατήσουσιν and present/future φέρουσιν / οἴσουσιν bringing glory into the city.
6. **Gate negation:** οὐ μὴ κλεισθῶσιν.
7. **Purity negation:** οὐ μὴ εἰσέλθῃ + πᾶν κοινὸν / ὁ ποιῶν, except οἱ γεγραμμένοι in the Lamb's book of life.

### River, Tree, Throne, Face, Name, Reign (22:1-5)

1. **Showing verb:** ἔδειξέν μοι resumes the angelic display from 21:10.
2. **River object:** ποταμὸν ὕδατος ζωῆς + adjectival λαμπρόν + simile ὡς κρύσταλλον.
3. **Source participle:** ἐκπορευόμενον ἐκ τοῦ θρόνου τοῦ Θεοῦ καὶ τοῦ Ἀρνίου.
4. **Tree clause:** ξύλον ζωῆς + present participles ποιοῦν / ἀποδιδοῦν + εἰς θεραπείαν.
5. **No curse:** πᾶν κατάθεμα οὐκ ἔσται ἔτι.
6. **Throne located:** θρόνος ... ἐν αὐτῇ ἔσται.
7. **Servants' future:** λατρεύσουσιν, ὄψονται, name upon foreheads, φωτίσει, βασιλεύσουσιν.

## Sanctuary-Service Synthesis

The grammar of Revelation 21:1-22:5 supports a final-sanctuary reading by accumulation rather than by one isolated word.

- **Dwelling thesis:** ἡ σκηνὴ τοῦ Θεοῦ μετὰ τῶν ἀνθρώπων and σκηνώσει state that God's tabernacle and tabernacling are now with humanity.
- **Structural transformation:** ναὸν οὐκ εἶδον does not negate God's sanctuary presence; the γάρ clause explains that the Lord God Almighty and the Lamb are the sanctuary.
- **Most-Holy geometry:** τετράγωνος and the equal μῆκος / πλάτος / ὕψος give the city a cubic shape. Grammar establishes the cube; sanctuary theology recognizes the Most Holy Place resonance.
- **Light replacement:** created light sources are grammatically unnecessary because God's glory and the Lamb's lamp-function illumine the city.
- **Throne source:** the river proceeds from the throne of God and the Lamb. Life flows from the center of rule and worship.
- **Priestly access:** λατρεύσουσιν αὐτῷ plus ὄψονται τὸ πρόσωπον αὐτοῦ moves servants from mediated worship to direct face-to-face service.
- **Name-bearing:** τὸ ὄνομα αὐτοῦ ἐπὶ τῶν μετώπων αὐτῶν marks covenant ownership and priestly identity in the final state.
- **Royal outcome:** βασιλεύσουσιν closes the sanctuary scene with reign, not mere residence.
