# Grammar Analysis: Hebrews 10:1-25

**Focus:** canonical passage grammar reference for `sanctuary-service-unified-account`, with attention to shadow/image language, once-for-all sacrifice, perfected/sanctified forms, access through the veil, and priestly ministry implications.

## Text

**Greek (N1904 parser text):**
- **v1:** Σκιὰν γὰρ ἔχων ὁ νόμος τῶν μελλόντων ἀγαθῶν, οὐκ αὐτὴν τὴν εἰκόνα τῶν πραγμάτων, κατ’ ἐνιαυτὸν ταῖς αὐταῖς θυσίαις ἃς προσφέρουσιν εἰς τὸ διηνεκὲς οὐδέποτε δύναται τοὺς προσερχομένους τελειῶσαι·
- **v2:** ἐπεὶ οὐκ ἂν ἐπαύσαντο προσφερόμεναι, διὰ τὸ μηδεμίαν ἔχειν ἔτι συνείδησιν ἁμαρτιῶν τοὺς λατρεύοντας ἅπαξ κεκαθαρισμένους;
- **v3:** ἀλλ’ ἐν αὐταῖς ἀνάμνησις ἁμαρτιῶν κατ’ ἐνιαυτόν·

- **v5:** Διὸ εἰσερχόμενος εἰς τὸν κόσμον λέγει Θυσίαν καὶ προσφορὰν οὐκ ἠθέλησας, σῶμα δὲ κατηρτίσω μοι·
- **v6:** ὁλοκαυτώματα καὶ περὶ ἁμαρτίας οὐκ εὐδόκησας.
- **v7:** τότε εἶπον Ἰδοὺ ἥκω, ἐν κεφαλίδι βιβλίου γέγραπται περὶ ἐμοῦ, τοῦ ποιῆσαι ὁ Θεός, τὸ θέλημά σου.
- **v8:** ἀνώτερον λέγων ὅτι Θυσίας καὶ προσφορὰς καὶ ὁλοκαυτώματα καὶ περὶ ἁμαρτίας οὐκ ἠθέλησας οὐδὲ εὐδόκησας, αἵτινες κατὰ νόμον προσφέρονται,

- **v10:** ἐν ᾧ θελήματι ἡγιασμένοι ἐσμὲν διὰ τῆς προσφορᾶς τοῦ σώματος Ἰησοῦ Χριστοῦ ἐφάπαξ.
- **v11:** Καὶ πᾶς μὲν ἱερεὺς ἕστηκεν καθ’ ἡμέραν λειτουργῶν καὶ τὰς αὐτὰς πολλάκις προσφέρων θυσίας, αἵτινες οὐδέποτε δύνανται περιελεῖν ἁμαρτίας·
- **v12:** οὗτος δὲ μίαν ὑπὲρ ἁμαρτιῶν προσενέγκας θυσίαν εἰς τὸ διηνεκὲς ἐκάθισεν ἐν δεξιᾷ τοῦ Θεοῦ,

- **v16:** Αὕτη ἡ διαθήκη ἣν διαθήσομαι πρὸς αὐτοὺς μετὰ τὰς ἡμέρας ἐκείνας, λέγει Κύριος· διδοὺς νόμους μου ἐπὶ καρδίας αὐτῶν, καὶ ἐπὶ τὴν διάνοιαν αὐτῶν ἐπιγράψω αὐτούς,

**KJV:**
- **v1:** For the law having a shadow of good things to come, [and] not the very image of the things, can never with those sacrifices which they offered year by year continually make the comers thereunto perfect.
- **v2:** For then would they not have ceased to be offered? because that the worshippers once purged should have had no more conscience of sins.
- **v3:** But in those [sacrifices there is] a remembrance again [made] of sins every year.
- **v4:** For [it is] not possible that the blood of bulls and of goats should take away sins.
- **v5:** Wherefore when he cometh into the world, he saith, Sacrifice and offering thou wouldest not, but a body hast thou prepared me:
- **v6:** In burnt offerings and [sacrifices] for sin thou hast had no pleasure.
- **v7:** Then said I, Lo, I come (in the volume of the book it is written of me,) to do thy will, O God.
- **v8:** Above when he said, Sacrifice and offering and burnt offerings and [offering] for sin thou wouldest not, neither hadst pleasure [therein]; which are offered by the law;
- **v9:** Then said he, Lo, I come to do thy will, O God. He taketh away the first, that he may establish the second.
- **v10:** By the which will we are sanctified through the offering of the body of Jesus Christ once [for all].
- **v11:** And every priest standeth daily ministering and offering oftentimes the same sacrifices, which can never take away sins:
- **v12:** But this man, after he had offered one sacrifice for sins for ever, sat down on the right hand of God;
- **v13:** From henceforth expecting till his enemies be made his footstool.
- **v14:** For by one offering he hath perfected for ever them that are sanctified.
- **v15:** [Whereof] the Holy Ghost also is a witness to us: for after that he had said before,
- **v16:** This [is] the covenant that I will make with them after those days, saith the Lord, I will put my laws into their hearts, and in their minds will I write them;
- **v17:** And their sins and iniquities will I remember no more.
- **v18:** Now where remission of these [is, there is] no more offering for sin.
- **v19:** Having therefore, brethren, boldness to enter into the holiest by the blood of Jesus,
- **v20:** By a new and living way, which he hath consecrated for us, through the veil, that is to say, his flesh;
- **v21:** And [having] an high priest over the house of God;
- **v22:** Let us draw near with a true heart in full assurance of faith, having our hearts sprinkled from an evil conscience, and our bodies washed with pure water.
- **v23:** Let us hold fast the profession of [our] faith without wavering; (for he [is] faithful that promised;)
- **v24:** And let us consider one another to provoke unto love and to good works:
- **v25:** Not forsaking the assembling of ourselves together, as the manner of some [is]; but exhorting [one another]: and so much the more, as ye see the day approaching.

|------|---------|--------------------------|
| 10:1-4 | Main contrast between **Σκιὰν** (shadow) and **εἰκόνα** (image), with the law as nominative subject (**ὁ νόμος**) and inability stated by **οὐδέποτε δύναται ... τελειῶσαι**. | Establishes that repeated Levitical sacrifices are typological and incapable of bringing worshipers to the goal expressed by **τελειόω**. |

## Key Grammatical Features

### 1. Σκιὰν ... οὐκ αὐτὴν τὴν εἰκόνα - shadow, not the image itself

- **Forms:** **Σκιὰν** = accusative singular feminine noun (G4639); **εἰκόνα** = accusative singular feminine noun (G1504); **ἔχων** = present active participle nominative singular masculine modifying **ὁ νόμος**; **τῶν μελλόντων ἀγαθῶν** = genitive plural phrase, with **μελλόντων** as present active participle functioning substantivally/adjectivally.
- **Syntax:** The law is the nominative subject with **ἔχων** supplying its characterized possession: it has **a shadow** of the coming good things, not **the very image** of the realities. The negative contrast is sharpened by **οὐκ αὐτὴν τὴν εἰκόνα**.
- **Significance:** Grammatically, Hebrews 10:1 does not make the sacrificial system the final referent. It makes it a shadow-bearing system whose repeated sacrifices cannot bring the worshipers to the intended goal.
- **Word studies:** [G4639 σκιά](../../word-studies/G4639-skia.md), [G1504 εἰκών](../../word-studies/G1504-eikon.md), [WG-shadow](../../word-studies/WG-shadow.md), [WG-image](../../word-studies/WG-image.md), [WG-typology](../../word-studies/WG-typology.md).

### 2. οὐδέποτε δύναται ... τελειῶσαι / οὐδέποτε δύνανται περιελεῖν - repeated offerings lack completing/removing power

- **Forms:** **δύναται** (10:1) = present middle/passive indicative 3rd singular; **τελειῶσαι** = aorist active infinitive of τελειόω; **δύνανται** (10:11) = present middle/passive indicative 3rd plural; **περιελεῖν** = second aorist active infinitive; **οὐδέποτε** negates both clauses.
- **Syntax:** In 10:1, **οὐδέποτε δύναται ... τελειῶσαι** states categorical inability: the law, by the same repeated yearly sacrifices, can never perfect the ones drawing near. In 10:11, **αἵτινες οὐδέποτε δύνανται περιελεῖν ἁμαρτίας** applies the same grammar to the repeated sacrifices themselves: they can never remove sins.
- **Significance:** The infinitives name the outcome the system cannot accomplish. The issue is not merely frequency; the present-tense ability verbs plus **οὐδέποτε** make inability characteristic, while the aorist infinitives view the needed result as a whole act: perfecting/removing.
- **Grammar references:** [Present Active Indicative](../greek/tense-present-active-indicative.md) for present-tense aspect; [Perfect Tense - Indicative](../greek/tense-perfect-indicative.md) for the contrast with 10:14.
- **Word studies:** [G5048 τελειόω](../../word-studies/G5048-teleioo.md), [G4374 προσφέρω](../../word-studies/G4374-prosphero.md), [G2378 θυσία](../../word-studies/G2378-thysia.md), [G266 ἁμαρτία](../../word-studies/G266-hamartia.md), [WG-perfection](../../word-studies/WG-perfection.md), [WG-sacrifice](../../word-studies/WG-sacrifice.md), [WG-offerup](../../word-studies/WG-offerup.md).

### 3. ἡγιασμένοι ἐσμὲν and τοὺς ἁγιαζομένους - sanctified state and sanctified people

- **Forms:** **ἡγιασμένοι** (10:10) = perfect passive participle nominative plural masculine; **ἐσμὲν** = present active indicative 1st plural of εἰμί; **ἁγιαζομένους** (10:14) = present passive participle accusative plural masculine.
- **Syntax:** Hebrews 10:10 uses a periphrastic construction (**perfect passive participle + εἰμί**) to state the believers' standing: "we are sanctified," with the perfect participle foregrounding the resultant state. Hebrews 10:14 then identifies the perfected group as **τοὺς ἁγιαζομένους**, the sanctified/being-sanctified ones.
- **Significance:** The passage holds together accomplished consecration and the identity/process of the people consecrated. The grammar supports a real standing produced **διὰ τῆς προσφορᾶς τοῦ σώματος Ἰησοῦ Χριστοῦ ἐφάπαξ** while still describing the recipients with a present passive participle in v14.
- **Grammar references:** [Greek Perfect Passive Participle](../greek/participle-perfect-passive-state.md), [Perfect Tense - Indicative](../greek/tense-perfect-indicative.md), [Divine Passive](../greek/divine-passive.md).

### 4. μίαν ... θυσίαν / μιᾷ ... προσφορᾷ ... τετελείωκεν εἰς τὸ διηνεκές - one offering with abiding result
