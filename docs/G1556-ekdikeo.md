## Semantic Categories

### 1. Plea for Vindication (Human Appeal to a Judge)
- **Luke 18:3** — "And there was a widow in that city; and she came unto him, saying, Avenge me of mine adversary."
- **Luke 18:5** — "Yet because this widow troubleth me, I will avenge her, lest by her continual coming she weary me."

The widow's cry is for judicial vindication — she asks the judge to grant her justice against her opponent. The verb here carries the sense of obtaining a legal ruling on behalf of the wronged party.

### 2. Prohibition Against Self-Vindication
- **Romans 12:19** — "Dearly beloved, avenge not yourselves, but rather give place unto wrath: for it is written, Vengeance is mine; I will repay, saith the Lord."

The present participle ἐκδικοῦντες with the negative forbids the ongoing practice of personal retaliation. The right to avenge is reserved for God.

### 3. Apostolic Disciplinary Authority
- **2 Corinthians 10:6** — "And having in a readiness to revenge all disobedience, when your obedience is fulfilled."

Here the verb denotes corrective punishment of disobedience within the church community, exercised by apostolic authority. The aorist infinitive frames it as a decisive future action.

### 4. Divine Eschatological Vengeance
- **Revelation 6:10** — "And they cried with a loud voice, saying, How long, O Lord, holy and true, dost thou not judge and avenge our blood on them that dwell on the earth?"
- **Revelation 19:2** — "For true and righteous are his judgments: for he hath judged the great whore, which did corrupt the earth with her fornication, and hath avenged the blood of his servants at her hand."

The martyrs' plea (Rev 6:10, present tense) finds its answer in divine action (Rev 19:2, aorist — completed act). In both cases, αἷμα (blood) co-occurs, linking the verb to blood-justice.
