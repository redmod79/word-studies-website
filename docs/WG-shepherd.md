# WG: Shepherd

## Overview
The shepherd concept in the NT encompasses the person (poimēn), the activity (poimainō, boskō), the object of care (poimnē, poimnion), the supreme title (archipoimen), and the related oversight role (episkopos). Together these words map the full scope of pastoral care — from literal animal husbandry to Christ's self-designation as the Good Shepherd to the church leadership gift.

## Roles in the Concept

- **Person:** poimēn (ποιμήν) — the shepherd himself, whether literal, christological, or ministerial
- **Activity:** poimainō (ποιμαίνω) — the act of shepherding; extends to ruling (Rev 2:27, 12:5, 19:15)
- **Feeding:** boskō (βόσκω) — specifically the feeding/grazing function; Jesus to Peter: "Feed my sheep" (John 21:15-17)
- **Flock:** poimnē (ποίμνη) — the flock as a collective unit
- **Little flock:** poimnion (ποίμνιον) — diminutive, used for the church community (Luke 12:32; Acts 20:28-29; 1 Pet 5:2-3)
- **Supreme shepherd:** archipoimen (ἀρχιποίμην) — Christ as chief shepherd over all under-shepherds (1 Pet 5:4)
- **Parallel office:** episkopos (ἐπίσκοπος) — overseer/bishop; paired with poimēn in 1 Pet 2:25 and with poimainō in Acts 20:28, showing the offices overlap
