## Lexicon Definition

Feminine of H2706, and meaning substantially the same: an enactment, appointed statute. KJV renderings: *appointed, custom, manner, ordinance, site, statute*.

Derived from H2710 (חָקַק), which means to cut in or inscribe — evoking a statute as something engraved, permanent, and fixed. As the feminine form of choq (H2706), chuqqâh shares the same core meaning but is the grammatically marked variant; in practice both appear in the same collocations and formulae. Chuqqâh is the dominant form in the priestly literature's "perpetual statute" formula (לְחֻקַּת עוֹלָם).

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Covenant Statutes — God's Law as Covenant Obligation
The most common use: God's statutes given to Israel as the binding terms of the covenant. Typically paired with mishpat (H4941) and mitsvah (H4687) in legal summaries.

- **Lev 18:5** — "Ye shall therefore keep my statutes, and my judgments: which if a man do, he shall live in them: I am the LORD your God."
- **Lev 26:3** — "If ye walk in my statutes, and keep my commandments, and do them..."
- **Deut 6:2** — "That thou mightest fear the LORD thy God, to keep all his statutes and his commandments, which I command thee, thou, and thy son, and thy son's son..."
- **Ezek 20:11** — "And I gave them my statutes, and shewed them my judgments, which if a man do, he shall even live in them."

### 2. Cultic and Ritual Ordinances — Specific Liturgical Laws
Used as the technical term for specific liturgical prescriptions: the Passover, Day of Atonement, feasts, red heifer, and priestly regulations.

- **Exo 12:14** — "And this day shall be unto you for a memorial; and ye shall keep it a feast to the LORD throughout your generations; ye shall keep it a feast by an ordinance for ever."
- **Exo 12:43** — "And the LORD said unto Moses and Aaron, This is the ordinance of the passover: There shall no stranger eat thereof."
- **Num 19:2** — "This is the ordinance of the law which the LORD hath commanded, saying, Speak unto the children of Israel, that they bring thee a red heifer without spot..."
- **Lev 16:29** — "And this shall be a statute for ever unto you: that in the seventh month, on the tenth day of the month, ye shall afflict your souls..." (Day of Atonement)

### 3. Perpetual Statutes — "לְחֻקַּת עוֹלָם" (For All Generations)
A distinctive formula using chuqqâh + olam (H5769) or "throughout your generations" (לְדֹרֹתֵיכֶם). This marks laws as permanently binding, not time-limited.

- **Lev 3:17** — "It shall be a perpetual statute for your generations throughout all your dwellings, that ye eat neither fat nor blood."
- **Lev 16:34** — "And this shall be an everlasting statute unto you, to make an atonement for the children of Israel for all their sins once a year."
- **Num 19:21** — "And it shall be a perpetual statute unto them, that he that sprinkleth the water of separation shall wash his clothes."
- **Lev 23:31** — "Ye shall do no manner of work: it shall be a statute for ever throughout your generations in all your dwellings." (Feast of Tabernacles)

### 4. Statutes of the Nations — Foreign Practices Condemned
Chuqqâh is applied to pagan customs as a foil — what Israel must not do. These are the "statutes" of Egypt, Canaan, the heathen, and apostate Israel.

- **Lev 18:3** — "After the doings of the land of Egypt, wherein ye dwelt, shall ye not do: and after the doings of the land of Canaan...ye shall not do: neither shall ye walk in their ordinances."
- **2 Ki 17:8** — "And walked in the statutes of the heathen, whom the LORD cast out from before the children of Israel, and of the kings of Israel, which they had made."
- **2 Ki 17:19** — "Also Judah kept not the commandments of the LORD their God, but walked in the statutes of Israel which they had made."
- **Mic 6:16** — "For the statutes of Omri are kept, and all the works of the house of Ahab, and ye walk in their counsels; that I should make thee a desolation..."

### 5. Ordinances of Creation — Cosmic Laws
A small but theologically significant use: chuqqâh extends to the fixed ordinances of the natural order — God's decrees built into creation itself.

- **Job 38:33** — "Knowest thou the ordinances of heaven? canst thou set the dominion thereof in the earth?"
- **Jer 31:35** — "Thus saith the LORD, which giveth the sun for a light by day, and the ordinances of the moon and of the stars for a light by night..."
- **Jer 33:25** — "Thus saith the LORD; If my covenant be not with day and night, and if I have not appointed the ordinances of heaven and earth..."

### 6. Walking in Statutes — The Covenant Obedience Formula
The verb הָלַךְ (halak, "walk") + בְּחֻקֹּתַי ("in my statutes") is a recurring covenantal formula describing the life of covenant faithfulness, especially prominent in Ezekiel.

- **1 Ki 6:12** — "Concerning this house which thou art in building, if thou wilt walk in my statutes, and execute my judgments, and keep all my commandments to walk in them..."
- **Ezek 18:9** — "Hath walked in my statutes, and hath kept my judgments, to deal truly; he is just, he shall surely live, saith the Lord GOD."
- **Ezek 33:15** — "If the wicked restore the pledge, give again that he had robbed, walk in the statutes of life, without committing iniquity; he shall surely live..."
- **Ezek 37:24** — "And David my servant shall be king over them; and they all shall have one shepherd: they shall also walk in my judgments, and observe my statutes, and do them."
