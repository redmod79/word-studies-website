## Lexicon Definition

The right hand or side (leg, eye) of a person or other object (as the stronger and more dexterous); locally, the south. KJV renderings: left-handed (in idiom), right (hand, side), south.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Divine Power and Deliverance
The right hand of YHWH as the instrument of his saving power, military victory, and sovereign action.
- **Exo 15:6** — "Thy right hand, O LORD, is become glorious in power: thy right hand, O LORD, hath dashed in pieces the enemy"
- **Psa 98:1** — "his right hand, and his holy arm, hath gotten him the victory"
- **Psa 118:15–16** — "The right hand of the LORD doeth valiantly. The right hand of the LORD is exalted"
- **Isa 48:13** — "my right hand hath spanned the heavens"

### 2. Divine Protection and Guidance
God's right hand holding, upholding, or shielding the faithful.
- **Psa 16:8** — "he is at my right hand, I shall not be moved"
- **Psa 63:8** — "thy right hand upholdeth me"
- **Psa 73:23** — "thou hast holden me by my right hand"
- **Isa 41:13** — "I the LORD thy God will hold thy right hand, saying unto thee, Fear not; I will help thee"
- **Psa 121:5** — "The LORD is thy shade upon thy right hand"
- **Psa 139:10** — "thy right hand shall hold me"

### 3. Position of Honor and Authority
The right hand/side as the seat of honor, royal dignity, or favored status.
- **Psa 110:1** — "Sit thou at my right hand, until I make thine enemies thy footstool"
- **Psa 45:9** — "upon thy right hand did stand the queen in gold of Ophir"
- **1 Ki 2:19** — "a seat set for the king's mother, and she sat on his right hand"
- **Psa 80:17** — "Let thy hand be upon the man of thy right hand"

### 4. Moral Straightness — Neither Right Nor Left
The idiom of not turning to the right or left, meaning strict obedience to God's commands.
- **Deut 5:32** — "ye shall not turn aside to the right hand or to the left"
- **Deut 17:20** — "that he turn not aside from the commandment, to the right hand, or to the left"
- **Josh 1:7** — "turn not from it to the right hand or to the left, that thou mayest prosper"
- **Prov 4:27** — "Turn not to the right hand nor to the left: remove thy foot from evil"

### 5. Physical/Bodily Right Hand
The literal right hand or right side of a person or object in narrative.
- **Gen 48:13–14** — Jacob crosses his hands to lay his right hand on Ephraim's head
- **Judg 3:21** — Ehud "took the dagger from his right thigh"
- **Judg 5:26** — Jael's "right hand to the workmen's hammer"
- **Dan 12:7** — the man clothed in linen "held up his right hand... and sware"

### 6. Sacrificial/Priestly Usage
The right shoulder, thumb, or toe in consecration and offering rituals.
- **Lev 7:32** — "the right shoulder shall ye give unto the priest"
- **Lev 9:21** — "the right shoulder Aaron waved for a wave offering"
- **Num 18:18** — "as the wave breast and as the right shoulder are thine"

### 7. Directional/Geographic — South
When facing east (the default Hebrew orientation), the right hand indicates south.
- **Psa 89:12** — "The north and the south thou hast created them"
- **1 Sam 23:19** — "on the south of Jeshimon" (yamiyn rendered directionally)
- **1 Sam 23:24** — "in the wilderness of Maon, in the plain on the south of Jeshimon"
