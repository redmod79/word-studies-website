## Semantic Categories

### 1. Military trampling of enemies (victory motif)
- **Psa 44:5** — "Through thee will we push down our enemies: through thy name will we tread them under that rise up against us."
- **Psa 60:12** / **Psa 108:13** — "Through God we shall do valiantly: for he it is that shall tread down our enemies."
- **Zec 10:5** — "And they shall be as mighty men, which tread down their enemies in the mire of the streets in the battle."

### 2. YHWH treading down in wrath (divine warrior)
- **Isa 14:25** — "That I will break the Assyrian in my land, and upon my mountains tread him under foot."
- **Isa 63:6** — "And I will tread down the people in mine anger, and make them drunk in my fury."

### 3. Being trodden — humiliation, desecration, defeat
- **Isa 14:19** — "But thou art cast out of thy grave like an abominable branch … as a carcase trodden under feet." (Hophal — the king of Babylon)
- **Isa 63:18** — "our adversaries have trodden down thy sanctuary."
- **Jer 12:10** — "Many pastors have destroyed my vineyard, they have trodden my portion under foot."

### 4. Figurative — polluted / weltering in blood
- **Eze 16:6** — "when I passed by thee, and saw thee polluted in thine own blood." (Hithpael participle)
- **Eze 16:22** — "thou wast polluted in thy blood." (Hithpael participle)
(Here the root carries a reflexive sense — writhing/wallowing, with the KJV reading "polluted" because trampled blood defiles.)

### 5. Figurative — the full soul loathes
- **Pro 27:7** — "The full soul loatheth an honeycomb."
(Unique metaphorical extension: a satisfied appetite "tramples on" a honeycomb — i.e., spurns / treads down in contempt.)
