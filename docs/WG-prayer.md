They appear together most densely in 1 Tim 2:1 and Php 4:6, where Paul stacks multiple terms to indicate comprehensive prayer life.

- δέησις (G1162) and δέομαι (G1189) — both from δέω "to lack, be in need"; the noun is the action-result of the verb

## Roles in the Concept

- **General address:** προσευχή (proseuchē) — prayer as worship-address to God; the umbrella term
- **Need-based petition:** δέησις (deēsis) — supplication arising from specific need or lack
- **Bold intercession:** ἔντευξις (enteuxis) — approaching God on behalf of others
- **Solemn wish/vow:** εὐχή (euchē) — prayer shading into vow or solemn wish
- **Suppliant's entreaty:** ἱκετηρία (hiketēria) — the posture of desperate appeal
- **Content requested:** αἴτημα (aitēma) — the specific thing asked for
- **Verbal act:** δέομαι (deomai) — the action of beseeching/begging
