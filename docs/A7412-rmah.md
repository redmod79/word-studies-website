## Lexicon Definition

(Aramaic) corresponding to H7411; to throw, set, (figuratively) assess:—cast (down), impose.

**BDB Outline:**
- **Peal** Perfect 3mp רְמוֹ (Dan 6:17, 6:25); 1p רְמֵינָא (Dan 3:24); Infinitive לְמִרְמֵא (Dan 3:20; Ezr 7:24) — (1) cast, accusative of person + ל location (Dan 3:24; 6:25); accusative omitted (Dan 6:17; 3:20). (2) Figurative: throw (burden of) tribute, עַל person (Ezr 7:24).

### 3. Placing/Setting in Position (Thrones)
- **Dan 7:9** — "I beheld till the thrones were cast down (rmiyw), and the Ancient of days did sit"

The Peil form here means "were set in place / were placed" rather than violently overturned. The context — the Ancient of Days sitting, robed in white, on a flame-throne — describes a judicial installation scene. The LXX renders the broader context with ἐτέθησαν ("were placed"), and the BDB lexicon explicitly cites the parallel to Jer 1:15 where the same root carries the sense of setting/placing thrones.

### 4. Imposing a Burden (Figurative)
- **Ezr 7:24** — "it shall not be lawful to impose (lmirme) toll, tribute, or custom, upon them"

The figurative extension: "throwing" a tax burden upon someone. The preposition עַל ("upon") governs the indirect object, distinguishing this from the literal casting with ל ("into").
