## Lexicon Definition

From H6942; sacred (ceremonially or morally); used as noun for God (by eminence), an angel, a saint, a sanctuary. KJV renderings: holy (One), saint.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Divine Title — "The Holy One" (of Israel)
The most distinctive use of qadowsh: a substantive title for God, emphasizing his absolute otherness and moral purity. Concentrated in Isaiah.
- **Isa 6:3** — "Holy, holy, holy, is the LORD of hosts: the whole earth is full of his glory."
- **Isa 43:3** — "For I am the LORD thy God, the Holy One of Israel, thy Saviour."
- **Isa 57:15** — "For thus saith the high and lofty One that inhabiteth eternity, whose name is Holy."
- **Hab 1:12** — "Art thou not from everlasting, O LORD my God, mine Holy One?"

### 2. God's Intrinsic Holiness
Declarations that God himself is holy — the ground of all other holiness. Not a title but a predicate describing his nature.
- **1 Sam 2:2** — "There is none holy as the LORD: for there is none beside thee."
- **Josh 24:19** — "Ye cannot serve the LORD: for he is an holy God; he is a jealous God."
- **Ps 99:9** — "Exalt the LORD our God, and worship at his holy hill; for the LORD our God is holy."
- **Isa 5:16** — "God that is holy shall be sanctified in righteousness."

### 3. Commanded Holiness of God's People
Israel called to be holy because God is holy — the ethical/covenantal demand for separation and moral purity.
- **Lev 11:44** — "Ye shall therefore sanctify yourselves, and ye shall be holy; for I am holy."
- **Lev 19:2** — "Ye shall be holy: for I the LORD your God am holy."
- **Lev 20:26** — "Ye shall be holy unto me: for I the LORD am holy, and have severed you from other people."
- **Deut 7:6** — "For thou art an holy people unto the LORD thy God."

### 4. Sacred Place / Cultic Space
The adjective modifying a location where offerings must be handled, priests must eat sacrificial portions, or sacred rites occur.
- **Exo 29:31** — "Take the ram of the consecration, and seethe his flesh in the holy place."
- **Lev 6:16** — "The remainder thereof shall Aaron and his sons eat... in the holy place."
- **Lev 6:26** — "The priest that offereth it for sin shall eat it: in the holy place shall it be eaten, in the court of the tabernacle."
- **Lev 7:6** — "Every male among the priests shall eat thereof: it shall be eaten in the holy place."

### 5. Holy Persons (Saints / Consecrated Individuals)
Persons set apart — whether heavenly beings, the Nazirite, or the covenant community collectively called "saints."
- **Num 6:5** — "Until the days be fulfilled... he is holy unto the LORD."
- **Num 16:5** — "The LORD will shew who are his, and who is holy."
- **Ps 16:3** — "But to the saints that are in the earth, and to the excellent, in whom is all my delight."
- **Ps 34:9** — "O fear the LORD, ye his saints: for there is no want to them that fear him."
- **Dan 8:13** — "Then I heard one saint speaking, and another saint said unto that certain saint."

### 6. Holy Time / Holy Day
The adjective applied to sacred days, particularly sabbaths and feast days.
- **Neh 8:9** — "This day is holy unto the LORD your God; mourn not, nor weep."
- **Neh 8:10** — "For this day is holy unto our LORD."
- **Isa 58:13** — "If thou turn away thy foot from the sabbath... and call the sabbath a delight, the holy of the LORD."

### 7. Contagious / Transferable Holiness
Holiness as a quality that transmits to whatever contacts a holy object — a distinctly ritual concept.
- **Lev 6:27** — "Whatsoever shall touch the flesh thereof shall be holy."
- **Num 18:8** — "I also have given thee the charge of mine heave offerings... the hallowed things."
