## Roles in the Concept

- **The people as body:** edah (עֵדָה) — the standing, defined community; can act, judge, sin, murmur, worship
- **The act of assembling:** qahal (קָהָל) — the calling together; more dynamic, event-focused
- **The appointed meeting:** moed (מוֹעֵד) — the fixed time and place of encounter (especially with God)
- **The physical location:** machaneh (מַחֲנֶה) — the camp where the community lives and moves
- **The inner circle:** sod (סוֹד) — the intimate council; used for divine counsel and close deliberation

## Key Passages Where Multiple Group Words Appear Together

- **Exo 12:6** — edah + qahal: "the whole assembly (qahal) of the congregation (edah) of Israel shall kill it in the evening"
- **Lev 8:3** — edah + moed: "gather thou all the congregation (edah) together unto the door of the tabernacle of the congregation (moed)"
- **Num 14:5** — edah + qahal: "Moses and Aaron fell on their faces before all the assembly (qahal) of the congregation (edah)"
- **Lev 24:14** — edah + machaneh: "bring forth him that hath cursed without the camp (machaneh); and let all the congregation (edah) stone him"

## Distinctive Usage Patterns

**Edah vs. Qahal:** The most important distinction. Edah is the community as it exists — a permanent, defined body. Qahal is the community as it assembles — the act of coming together. When both appear in the same verse (Exo 12:6, Num 14:5), qahal functions as the gathered event and edah as the identity of those gathered. In the LXX, this maps to synagoge (edah) vs. ekklesia (qahal), a distinction that carries forward into NT terminology.

**Gender:** Edah is feminine (collective noun — the community as a whole). Qahal is masculine. This matters for Hebrew agreement patterns: edah takes feminine verbs and adjectives when treated as a unit.
