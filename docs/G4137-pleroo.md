## Lexicon Definition

To make replete; (literally) to cram (a net), level up (a hollow); (figuratively) to furnish fully, imbue, diffuse, influence, satisfy; (specifically) to execute an office, finish a period or task, verify or coincide with a prediction.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Prophetic Fulfillment Formula
The dominant use: Scripture or prophecy "being fulfilled" by an event. Nearly always passive aorist subjunctive (πληρωθῇ) in purpose clauses.
- **Mat 1:22** — "Now all this was done, that it might be fulfilled which was spoken of the Lord by the prophet"
- **Luk 4:21** — "This day is this scripture fulfilled in your ears"
- **John 19:36** — "For these things were done, that the scripture should be fulfilled, A bone of him shall not be broken"
- **Acts 1:16** — "This scripture must needs have been fulfilled, which the Holy Ghost by the mouth of David spake"

### 2. Temporal Completion — A Period "Filling Up"
Time, days, or years reaching their appointed end; pleroo used where English says "expired," "ended," "when he was forty."
- **Mar 1:15** — "The time is fulfilled, and the kingdom of God is at hand"
- **Acts 7:23** — "When he was full forty years old, it came into his heart to visit his brethren"
- **Acts 7:30** — "When forty years were expired, there appeared to him...an angel"
- **Acts 9:23** — "After that many days were fulfilled, the Jews took counsel to kill him"

### 3. Fulfilling Law, Righteousness, or a Mission
Active sense: completing an obligation, carrying out a charge, or exhausting the full demand of the law.
- **Mat 3:15** — "Thus it becometh us to fulfil all righteousness"
- **Mat 5:17** — "I am not come to destroy, but to fulfil"
- **Rom 13:8** — "He that loveth another hath fulfilled the law"
- **Acts 13:25** — "As John fulfilled his course, he said, Whom think ye that I am?"

### 4. Being Filled with a Quality, Spirit, or Emotion
Passive: a person filled with wisdom, the Spirit, joy, knowledge, or (negatively) unrighteousness or sorrow.
- **Luk 2:40** — "The child grew, and waxed strong in spirit, filled with wisdom"
- **Acts 13:52** — "The disciples were filled with joy, and with the Holy Ghost"
- **Rom 1:29** — "Being filled with all unrighteousness, fornication, wickedness, covetousness"
- **Eph 5:18** — "Be not drunk with wine...but be filled with the Spirit"
- **Col 1:9** — "That ye might be filled with the knowledge of his will in all wisdom"

### 5. Joy Fulfilled / Made Complete
A distinct Johannine pattern: joy reaching fullness as an eschatological gift, often in farewell discourse context.
- **John 15:11** — "That my joy might remain in you, and that your joy might be full"
- **John 17:13** — "That they might have my joy fulfilled in themselves"
- **1 John 1:4** — "These things write we unto you, that your joy may be full"
- **2 John 1:12** — "I trust to come unto you...that our joy may be full"

### 6. Cosmic / Universal Filling (Pauline)
Christ as the one who fills all things; believers as complete or fully supplied in him.
- **Eph 1:23** — "The fulness of him that filleth all in all"
- **Eph 4:10** — "He that descended is the same also that ascended...that he might fill all things"
- **Col 2:10** — "Ye are complete in him, which is the head of all principality and power"
- **Phil 4:19** — "My God shall supply all your need according to his riches in glory by Christ Jesus"
