H2181 is a verb root meaning to commit fornication or play the harlot. In the OT it carries both a literal sense (prostitution: Gen 34:31, Josh 2:1) and a dominant figurative sense (Israel's spiritual unfaithfulness to God: Judg 2:17, Ezek 16, Hosea). The figurative "whoring after other gods" accounts for the bulk of OT usage.

**Key OT passages:**

## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same across testaments:**
- The word retains both literal (actual prostitution: 1 Cor 6:15–16, Luke 15:30) and figurative (spiritual unfaithfulness: Rev 17–19) senses, mirroring the OT dual usage of zânâh.
- Rahab's identification as πόρνη is stable from LXX Josh 2:1 through Heb 11:31 and Jas 2:25 — no softening of the label.

**What shifts:**
- The OT "whoring after other gods" is almost always applied to Israel as a corporate metaphor. In the NT, the figurative harlot is no longer Israel but "Babylon the Great" — an external enemy of God's people rather than a wayward covenant partner.
- The NT adds a positive dimension entirely absent from the OT verb: πόρνη becomes a category of person who can repent and enter the kingdom (Matt 21:31–32), and whose faith is commended (Heb 11:31, Jas 2:25). The OT uses zânâh almost entirely as condemnation.
