## Semantic Categories

### 1. Estate Steward — Manager of Property and Workers
- **Mat 20:8** — "So when even was come, the lord of the vineyard saith unto his steward, Call the labourers, and give them their hire, beginning from the last unto the first."

The epitropos acts as the vineyard owner's authorized representative, managing workers and distributing wages. He exercises delegated authority on behalf of an absent or withdrawn master.

### 2. Royal/Household Steward — Administrative Official
- **Luk 8:3** — "And Joanna the wife of Chuza Herod's steward, and Susanna, and many others, which ministered unto him of their substance."

Chuza held the position of epitropos to Herod Antipas — a high-ranking administrative role managing royal household affairs or finances. The term here denotes an official appointment with real authority.

### 3. Legal Guardian — Custodian of a Minor
- **Gal 4:2** — "But is under tutors and governors until the time appointed of the father."

Paired with οἰκονόμος (oikonomos, "governors"), the epitropos here refers to the legal guardian of an underage heir. The role is temporary — it lasts only "until the time appointed of the father." Paul uses this to illustrate the believer's pre-faith status under the law.
