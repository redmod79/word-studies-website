# WG: Revelation and Disclosure (Greek)

## Overview
The revelation/disclosure concept in the Greek NT involves a cluster of words describing God's act of making hidden truth known. At the center is the apokalyptō/apokalypsis word pair (uncovering what was veiled), with phaneroō/phanerōsis (making visible/manifest) as the most frequent near-synonym. Mystērion (mystery) represents the content being disclosed, and chrēmatismos represents a specific divine oracle.

## Roles in the Concept

- **Action (uncovering):** apokalyptō (G601) — the divine act of removing the veil
- **Result/Event (revelation):** apokalypsis (G602) — the disclosure itself, or the future eschatological unveiling
- **Action (making visible):** phaneroō (G5319) — the broader act of making apparent
- **Result (manifestation):** phanerōsis (G5321) — the making plain
- **Content (mystery):** mystērion (G3466) — what was hidden and is now disclosed
- **Oracle (specific response):** chrēmatismos (G5538) — a divine answer to inquiry (Rom 11:4 only)

## Semantic Distinctions

- **apokalypsis vs. phaneroō:** apokalypsis implies prior divine concealment and a dramatic unveiling (often eschatological); phaneroō is broader — simply making something visible that already exists
- **apokalypsis vs. mystērion:** These are complementary — mystērion is the hidden content, apokalypsis is the act of disclosing it. Paul uses them together: "the revelation of the mystery" (Rom 16:25)
- **apokalypsis vs. chrēmatismos:** chrēmatismos is a specific divine oracle (answer from God); apokalypsis covers broader disclosure including visions, eschatological events, and spiritual gifts

## Key Passages Where Multiple Group Words Appear Together
- **Rom 16:25-26** — apokalypsis (G602) + mystērion (G3466): "the revelation of the mystery, which was kept secret since the world began, but now is made manifest"
- **Col 1:26** — phaneroō (G5319) + mystērion (G3466): "the mystery which hath been hid from ages and from generations, but now is made manifest"
- **Dan 2:19 (LXX/Theodotion)** — raz (H7328) + gelah (H1541): the Aramaic source passage where "the secret was revealed," foundational for the NT mystery-revelation language
