## Semantic Categories

### 1. Idiomatic Non-Movement ("not move the tongue")
The Qal is used in a fixed idiom — "not a dog shall move (chârats) his tongue" — denoting complete passivity or absence of opposition.

- **Exo 11:7** — "But against any of the children of Israel shall not a dog move his tongue, against man or beast"
- **Jos 10:21** — "none moved his tongue against any of the children of Israel"

### 2. Be Stirred / Bestir Oneself (Qal — swift decisive action)
Used for a person prompted to act, to move quickly at a signal.

- **2Sa 5:24** — "when thou hearest the sound of a going in the tops of the mulberry trees, that then thou shalt bestir thyself"

### 3. Decide / Render Judgment (Qal — legal/judicial sense)
The active Qal can carry the sense of rendering a verdict or deciding a case.

- **1Ki 20:40** — "And the king of Israel said unto him, So shall thy judgment be; thyself hast decided it"

### 4. What Is Divinely Fixed / Appointed (Qal participle — limits set by God)
The participial form denotes something already set by divine authority — days, limits, times.

- **Job 14:5** — "Seeing his days are determined, the number of his months are with thee, thou hast appointed his bounds"
- **Isa 10:22** — "a remnant of them shall return: the consumption decreed shall overflow with righteousness"

### 5. The Decreed End / Consummation (Niphal — divinely fixed judgment, prophetic)
The Niphal participle נֶחֱרָצָה / נֶחֱרֶצֶת becomes a near-technical term in prophetic literature for God's irrevocable determination — especially of judgment or end-time events.

- **Isa 10:23** — "the Lord GOD of hosts shall make a consumption, even determined, in the midst of all the land"
- **Isa 28:22** — "for I have heard from the Lord GOD of hosts a consumption, even determined upon the whole earth"
- **Dan 9:26** — "unto the end of the war desolations are determined"
- **Dan 9:27** — "even until the consummation, and that determined shall be poured upon the desolate"
- **Dan 11:36** — "for that that is determined shall be done"

### 6. Physical Wounding / Defect (Qal — literal maiming)
One occurrence applies the "wound/cut" sense to a physical defect disqualifying a sacrificial animal.

- **Lev 22:22** — "Blind, or broken, or maimed (chârats), or having a wen... ye shall not offer these unto the LORD"
