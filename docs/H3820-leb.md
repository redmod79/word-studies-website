## Lexicon Definition

A form of H3824; the heart; also used (figuratively) very widely for the
feelings, the will and even the intellect; likewise for the centre of
anything:—care for, comfortably, consent, considered, courag(-eous),
friend(-ly), ((broken-), (hard-), (merry-), (stiff-), (stout-),
double) heart(-ed), heed, I, kindly, midst, mind(-ed), regard(-ed),
themselves, unawares, understanding, well, willingly, wisdom.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Seat of Emotion / Inner Experience
The leb experiences grief, joy, fear, gladness, and sorrow — the full range of affective states.
- **Gen 6:6** — "it grieved him at his heart" (God's grief over human evil)
- **1 Sam 2:1** — "My heart rejoiceth in the LORD, mine horn is exalted"
- **1 Sam 4:13** — "his heart trembled for the ark of God"
- **Esther 1:10** — "when the heart of the king was merry with wine"

### 2. Seat of Will / Intention / Purpose
Leb is the deciding faculty — where plans are formed and purposes set.
- **Gen 8:21** — "the LORD said in his heart, I will not again curse the ground"
- **1 Sam 1:13** — "Hannah, she spake in her heart; only her lips moved"
- **Dan 1:8** — "Daniel purposed in his heart that he would not defile himself"
- **Neh 4:6** — "the people had a mind to work"

### 3. Seat of Intellect / Understanding / Wisdom
Leb functions as the cognitive center — the place of reflection, reason, and wisdom.
- **1 Kgs 3:9** — "Give therefore thy servant an understanding heart to judge thy people"
- **1 Kgs 4:29** — "God gave Solomon wisdom and…largeness of heart, even as the sand"
- **Prov 2:2** — "apply thine heart to understanding"
- **Ecc 1:13** — "I gave my heart to seek and search out by wisdom"
- **Dan 10:12** — "from the first day that thou didst set thine heart to understand"

### 4. Moral / Spiritual Condition
The leb is the seat of moral character — it can be evil, hardened, pure, or perfect.
- **Gen 6:5** — "every imagination of the thoughts of his heart was only evil continually"
- **Exo 4:21** — "I will harden Pharaoh's heart, and multiply my signs"
- **Obad 1:3** — "The pride of thine heart hath deceived thee"
- **Ezek 2:4** — "impudent children and stiffhearted"

### 5. Devotion / Religious Orientation
The heart as the organ of covenant relationship — turning toward or away from God.
- **Jer 3:10** — "hath not turned unto me with her whole heart, but feignedly"
- **Jer 3:15** — "I will give you pastors according to mine heart"
- **2 Chr 6:38** — "return to thee with all their heart and with all their soul"
- **Mal 4:6** — "he shall turn the heart of the fathers to the children"
- **Hos 2:14** — "I will…speak comfortably unto her" (lit. *to her heart*)

### 6. Center / Midst (Spatial)
Occasionally leb denotes the physical center of something (fire to the midst of heaven, heart of the sea).
- **Deut 4:11** — "the mountain burned with fire unto the midst of heaven"
- **Deut 28:65** — "LORD shall give thee there a trembling heart"
