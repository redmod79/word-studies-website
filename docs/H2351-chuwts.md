## Lexicon Definition

Or (shortened) חֻץ chuts; (both forms feminine in the plural) from an unused root meaning to sever; properly, separate by a wall, i.e. outside, outdoors:—abroad, field, forth, highway, more, out(-side, -ward), street, without.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Outside the Camp — Cultic Exclusion and Disposal
The most distinctive use: "without the camp" (*michuts lamachaneh*) designates the space outside the Israelite encampment for burning sin-offering remains, expelling the unclean, and executing offenders.
- **Lev 4:12** — "Even the whole bullock shall he carry forth without the camp unto a clean place, where the ashes are poured out, and burn him"
- **Lev 4:21** — "And he shall carry forth the bullock without the camp, and burn him as he burned the first bullock: it is a sin offering for the congregation"
- **Num 5:3** — "Both male and female shall ye put out, without the camp shall ye put them; that they defile not their camps"
- **Lev 24:14** — "Bring forth him that hath cursed without the camp; and let all that heard him lay their hands upon his head"

### 2. Outside a City or Building — Spatial Boundary
General spatial "outside" relative to a city wall, gate, building, or defined interior.
- **Gen 24:11** — "And he made his camels to kneel down without the city by a well of water"
- **Gen 24:31** — "Come in, thou blessed of the LORD; wherefore standest thou without?"
- **2 Ki 23:6** — "And he brought out the grove from the house of the LORD, without Jerusalem"
- **Ezek 40:5** — "And behold a wall on the outside of the house round about"

### 3. Streets of a City — Public Urban Space
Plural *chutsoth* functions as "streets" — the open passages and public areas of a city — distinct from H7339 rechob (broad plaza) and H7784 shuq (market lane).
- **Jer 5:1** — "Run ye to and fro through the streets of Jerusalem, and see now"
- **Lam 2:21** — "The young and the old lie on the ground in the streets"
- **Prov 1:20** — "Wisdom crieth without; she uttereth her voice in the streets"
- **Nah 2:4** — "The chariots shall rage in the streets, they shall justle one against another"

### 4. Abroad / Out from a Place — Movement Outward
Denotes going out from an enclosed space, often with motion verbs (yatsa H3318).
- **Gen 15:5** — "And he brought him forth abroad, and said, Look now toward heaven"
- **Exo 12:46** — "thou shalt not carry forth ought of the flesh abroad out of the house"
- **Deut 24:11** — "Thou shalt stand abroad, and the man to whom thou dost lend shall bring out the pledge abroad unto thee"
- **Psa 41:6** — "when he goeth abroad, he telleth it"

### 5. Inner/Outer Contrast — Architectural and Moral Pairing
Paired with "within" (*bayith* or *pniymah*) to describe spatial completeness or inner/outer duality.
- **Gen 6:14** — "shalt pitch it within and without with pitch"
- **Exo 25:11** — "within and without shalt thou overlay it"
- **Deut 32:25** — "The sword without, and terror within, shall destroy both the young man and the virgin"
- **Ezek 7:15** — "The sword is without, and the pestilence and the famine within"

### 6. Open Fields / Outdoors — Unbuilt Space
Occasionally denotes open country, fields, or uncultivated land beyond settlement boundaries.
- **Prov 8:26** — "While as yet he had not made the earth, nor the fields"
- **Job 5:10** — "Who giveth rain upon the earth, and sendeth waters upon the fields"
- **Prov 24:27** — "Prepare thy work without, and make it fit for thyself in the field"
