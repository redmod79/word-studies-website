# WG: Blessedness

## Overview
The blessedness concept in Hebrew Scripture revolves around the declared-happy state of the person who walks rightly before God. Seven words form the core cluster: the beatitude noun and its verbal root, the active blessing verb, and the three conduct-words (way, walk, keep) that describe the conditions for blessedness.

## Roles in the Concept

- **Declaration:** esher (אֶשֶׁר) — the exclamatory formula declaring someone's blessedness
- **Root verb:** ashar (אָשַׁר) — the verbal form: "to pronounce blessed"
- **Active counterpart:** barak (בָּרַךְ) — blessing as an act conferred by God or a person (distinct from the declared state)
- **Source:** YHWH (יְהֹוָה) — the God whose relationship produces blessedness
- **Path metaphor:** derek (דֶּרֶךְ) — the "way" one walks to qualify for the beatitude
- **Conduct verb:** halak (הָלַךְ) — "walking" as the ongoing conduct of the blessed person
- **Obedience verb:** shamar (שָׁמַר) — "keeping" commandments, judgments, testimonies as the concrete expression of that walk

## Key Passages Where Multiple Group Words Appear Together

- **Deut 28:9** — [YHWH + derek + halak + shamar] "The LORD shall establish thee an holy people unto himself, as he hath sworn unto thee, if thou shalt keep the commandments of the LORD thy God, and walk in his ways"
- **Deut 30:16** — [barak + YHWH + derek + shamar] "I command thee this day to love the LORD thy God, to walk in his ways, and to keep his commandments... that the LORD thy God may bless thee"
- **Psalm 1:1** — [esher + derek + halak] "Blessed is the man that walketh not in the counsel of the ungodly, nor standeth in the way of sinners"
- **Psalm 119:1-2** — [esher + derek + shamar] "Blessed are the undefiled in the way... Blessed are they that keep his testimonies"
- **Psalm 128:1** — [esher + YHWH + derek + halak] "Blessed is every one that feareth the LORD; that walketh in his ways"

The blessedness concept forms a coherent theological cluster: YHWH is the source, the derek is the prescribed conduct, halak and shamar describe the living-out of that conduct, and esher declares the resulting state of the person who does so. Barak operates as the active complement — God's conferring of blessing on those who walk and keep.
