# Grammar Analysis: Judges 11:37-40

**The Daughter's Response, the Fulfillment, and the Annual Commemoration**

## Text

## Key Grammatical Features

### 1. בְּתוּלַי / בְּתוּלֶיהָ — "my/her virginity"
- **Lemma:** בְּתוּלִים (bethulim) — plural form; not the noun בְּתוּלָה (bethulah, "a virgin").
- **Form:** **Noun.fp.Abs.+1cs** (11:37) and **+3fs** (11:38). Morphologically a *plural of abstraction / condition*: "virginity, state of being a virgin." The plural -îm ending here does not mean multiple virgins; it signals an abstract state (compare נְעוּרִים "youth," זְקֻנִים "old age").
- **Syntax:** Object of verb בכה ("weep") via preposition עַל ("for, over") — the stereotyped formula "to weep *over* one's bethulim." The construction appears twice (stated by daughter, executed in narration), binding the lament tightly to the condition, not to a person.
- **Significance:** The narrator repeats the noun verbatim in vv. 37 and 38. Whatever she mourns is a *state*, not a loss of life per se. The grammatical form itself is one of the strongest data points in the passage: בְּתוּלַי lexicalizes "my unmarried/childless condition" — see word study for the distinction from בְּתוּלָה.
- **Word study:** [H1330-bthuwlah](../../word-studies/H1330-bthuwlah.md)

### 2. וַיַּ֣עַשׂ לָ֔הּ אֶת־נִדְרֹ֖ו אֲשֶׁ֣ר נָדָ֑ר — "he did to her his vow which he had vowed"
- **Form:** וַיַּעַשׂ is Qal wayyiqtol 3ms (narrative preterite) of עשׂה, followed by indirect-object לָהּ, then direct-object marker אֶת + נִדְרוֹ ("his vow" + 3ms suffix). The relative clause אֲשֶׁר נָדָר is a perfect-aspect internal object construction (cognate accusative): the verb נָדַר and the noun נֶדֶר share the same root.
- **Clause pattern:** Type `Way0` (main-line narrative) followed by type `xQt0` (relative-particle + qatal), per clause parser. The qatal נָדָר in the relative clause is anterior to the wayyiqtol וַיַּעַשׂ — the vow had already been uttered (vv. 30-31) before being "performed."
- **Crucial syntactic ambiguity:** The verb עשׂה here takes `לָהּ` (to/for her) + direct object "his vow." Hebrew עשׂה with ל + person most often means "do to/for someone" in a neutral sense (cf. Gen 12:18 "What is this thou hast *done unto me* [עָשִׂ֣יתָ לִּ֔י]"). The construction *does not lexically specify* killing. The grammar alone is consistent with either (a) carrying out a vow of dedication (life-long service) or (b) carrying out a vow of sacrifice. The narrator deliberately reports the *fulfillment* by reference back to the *vow-text*, forcing the reader to reconstruct what "doing the vow" means from 11:30-31.
- **Cognate-accusative intensification:** "vowed his vow which he vowed" (נדר…נדרו…אשר נדר) is an emphatic construction. Biblical Hebrew uses cognate-object pairs (e.g., חלם חלום, חטא חטאה) to underline the solemnity/irrevocability of the act. Here it emphasizes the legal binding force under which Jephthah acts.
- **Word study:** [H5087-nadar](../../word-studies/H5087-nadar.md)

### 3. וְהִיא֙ לֹא־יָדְעָ֣ה אִ֔ישׁ — "and she had not known a man"
- **Form:** Qal perfect 3fs of ידע. Preceded by disjunctive waw + pronoun (וְהִיא) + negation (לֹא).
- **Clause type:** `WXQt` — circumstantial/backgrounded waw-X-qatal clause (clause parser confirms). This is the standard BH construction for a *parenthetical / explanatory* statement breaking the wayyiqtol chain.
- **Aspect / sequence:** The qatal after the wayyiqtol וַיַּעַשׂ is **not** sequential ("and then she knew no man"). It is **anterior / stative** relative to the main line — "now she, she had not known a man." The fronted pronoun הִיא adds contrastive topicalization ("as for her…").
- **Idiom:** ידע + אישׁ (with man/woman as object) is the standard BH euphemism for sexual relations — the *feminine* counterpart of the masculine idiom ידע אשׁה (Gen 4:1, 17, 25; 24:16). The feminine formulation is rare; Num 31:17, 18, 35 are the only other clear cases, all of which concern Midianite women who either had or had not "known a man by lying with him." The vocabulary is loaded with sexual meaning.
- **Interpretive weight:** This circumstantial clause is the narrator's *own comment* on what "doing the vow" entailed. Precisely what the narrator chooses to tell us is not "she died" but "she had not known a man" — a statement about her *sexual/marital state*, grammatically parallel to her earlier lament over בְּתוּלִים. The syntactic parallel (lament of her virginity → she had not known a man) frames the outcome in celibacy-language rather than death-language.
- **Grammar reference:** See [stem-niphal.md](../hebrew/stem-niphal.md) for the Qal/Niphal contrast; Qal ידע is the standard active-knowing stem.

### 4. לְתַנּוֹת — "to recount / to lament"
- **Form:** Piel Infinitive Construct of **תנה** (Strong's H8567), with prefixed לְ (purpose/result). Parser code: `Verb.Piel.InfCon`.
- **Rarity:** Only **two** clear attestations of this verb in the entire Hebrew Bible: **Judg 5:11** (יְתַנּוּ, Piel impf. 3mp, "they rehearse the righteous acts of the LORD") and **Judg 11:40** (לְתַנּוֹת, Piel InfCon). The parser confirms these are the sole verbal occurrences — all other hits for root תנה are the unrelated noun מַתָּנָה ("gift"), the 2fp pronoun אַתֵּנָה, or the place-name Mattanah.
- **Semantic range:** Because of the scarcity, glossing is debated. Two main options from lexica: (1) *recount / rehearse / commemorate* (cognate to Aramaic תְּנָא "repeat, teach" — this is the Judg 5:11 sense: reciting the LORD's deeds). (2) *lament* (traditional sense, following LXX θρηνεῖν and Vulgate *plangere*). Piel stem intensifies — whatever the base action, it is repeated/ceremonial.
- **Syntactic frame:** InfCon with לְ encodes *purpose* of the main verb תֵּלַכְנָה ("they would go…**in order to** tanneh"). The indirect object לְבַת־יִפְתָּח ("for/to the daughter of Jephthah") uses ל, not עַל; one typically mourns *for* (עַל) the dead but *recounts to/for* (ל) a subject of memory. This dative-of-interest construction with ל favors the "recount, commemorate" reading — the rite is *about her*, not *over her corpse*.
- **Clause type:** `InfC` in domain D (discursive) — the infinitival clause specifies the customary purpose of the yearly pilgrimage established as a חֹק ("statute/custom") in v. 39.
- **Word study:** [H8567-tanah](../../word-studies/H8567-tanah.md)

### 5. Additional noted forms
- **יֵעָשֶׂה (v. 37):** Niphal imperfect 3ms of עשׂה — "let it be done." Passive/jussive. Polite/formal way for the daughter to request; she does not command her father with an active imperative. See [stem-niphal.md](../hebrew/stem-niphal.md).
- **הַרְפֵּה מִמֶּנִּי (v. 37):** Hiphil imperative 2ms of רפה — lit. "let (yourself) loose from me," i.e. "leave me alone / grant me respite." Idiomatic Hiphil meaning "release, desist."
- **וְאֵלְכָה (v. 37):** Qal cohortative 1cs of הלך (-āh ending). Cohortative expresses volitional self-exhortation ("let me go"). Paired with weqatal וְיָרַדְתִּי (Qal perfect 1cs with consecutive waw), which continues the same modal force.
- **מִיָּמִים יָמִימָה (v. 40):** Distributive / iterative idiom: *"from days to days-ward"* (noun + same noun with -āh of direction). Standard BH for "yearly, from year to year" (cf. Exod 13:10; 1 Sam 1:3; 1 Sam 2:19 — the only other attestations of this exact formula).

## Clause Structure Summary

### v. 37 — The Daughter's Speech (Direct Discourse, Domain Q)
| Clause | Type | Function |
|--------|------|----------|
| 1 | Way0 (narrative) | speech-frame: "And she said to her father" |
| 2 | ZYqX (jussive) | "Let this thing be done for me" |
| 3 | ZIm0 (imperative) | "Leave me alone two months" |
| 4 | WYq0 (cohortative) | "that I may go" |
| 5 | WQt0 (weqatal) | "and descend on the mountains" |
| 6 | WYqX (cohortative) | "and weep for my virginity, I and my companions" |

Shift from jussive → imperative → 1cs cohortative chain: formal politeness (v.37a) yields to personal petition (v.37b-d). Grammatically the *daughter controls the discourse* of this scene.

### v. 38 — Jephthah's Consent + Narrative Execution (Domain N)
| Clause | Type | Function |
|--------|------|----------|
| 1 | Way0 | "And he said" |
| 2 | ZIm0 | "Go" (2fs imperative) |
| 3 | Way0 | "and he sent her away two months" |
| 4 | WayX | "and she went, she and her companions" |
| 5 | Way0 | "and she wept for her virginity on the mountains" |

Tight wayyiqtol chain — rapid narrative execution. Notable: the narrator re-uses **בְּתוּלֶיהָ** to describe what she did, matching her own phrasing in v. 37 exactly.

### v. 39 — The Return and the Vow's Fulfillment (Domain N)
| Clause | Type | Function |
|--------|------|----------|
| 1 | Way0 | temporal frame: "at end of two months" |
| 2 | Way0 | "and she returned to her father" |
| 3 | Way0 | **"and he did to her his vow"** |
| 4 | xQt0 | relative clause: "which he had vowed" |
| 5 | **WXQt** | **circumstantial: "now as for her, she had not known a man"** |
| 6 | Way0 | result/consequence: "and it became a statute in Israel" |

Clause 5 (WXQt) is the hinge. A wayyiqtol chain (3→6) is interrupted by one backgrounded X-qatal clause — the narrator's one-line commentary on what the "doing of the vow" entailed. The commentary is in *sexual/marital terms*, not *mortality terms*.

### v. 40 — The Etiology (Domain D = Discursive/Habitual)
| Clause | Type | Function |
|--------|------|----------|
| 1 | xYqX | "Yearly the daughters of Israel would go" |
| 2 | InfC | purpose: "to *tanneh* the daughter of Jephthah" |

Domain shift from N (narrative) to D (discursive/habitual) marks the transition from event-time to institution-time. The yiqtol תֵּלַכְנָה is iterative/habitual. The passage closes as an etiological explanation of an ongoing Israelite custom.

## Exegetical Observations from the Grammar

Three grammatical facts are especially load-bearing:

1. **The double use of בְּתוּלִים (vv. 37, 38)** — noun of *abstract condition*, not simply "virgin" — frames the entire lament as over a *state*, and the narrator redoubles it by reusing the word.

2. **The circumstantial WXQt clause "she had not known a man" (v. 39)** is the narrator's interpretive gloss on "he did to her his vow." Syntactically, this clause is *not sequential* to the vow's fulfillment; it is an explanatory aside. Its content is *sexual/marital*, aligning with the daughter's own lament-vocabulary (בְּתוּלִים).

3. **לְתַנּוֹת (v. 40)** — a Piel infinitive of a verb used elsewhere only in Judg 5:11, where it means "rehearse the righteous acts of YHWH." If the same sense applies here (with ל of benefit, not mourning), the annual rite *recounts / commemorates* rather than *bewails*. The syntactic frame (ל of interest rather than עַל of object-of-mourning) leans this direction.

The grammatical evidence does not settle whether Jephthah's vow was fulfilled by (a) a literal burnt offering or (b) perpetual dedication to celibate sanctuary service. But the vocabulary the narrator *chooses* — בְּתוּלִים, לֹא יָדְעָה אִישׁ, לְתַנּוֹת — consistently belongs to the semantic field of *unmarried / celibate life*, not of *death and mourning the dead*. The reader is given grammar that is at minimum consistent with, and arguably biased toward, the dedication reading.
