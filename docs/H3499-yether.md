remainder; also a small rope (as hanging free): — abundant, cord, exceeding,
excellency(-ent), what they leave, that hath left, plentifully, remnant, residue,
rest, string, with.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Regnal Archive Formula ("the rest of the acts")
The single dominant use — an administrative closure formula in Kings and Chronicles marking the end of each king's reign and directing readers to fuller records.

- **1 Kgs 11:41** — "And the rest of the acts of Solomon, and all that he did, and his wisdom, [are] they not written in the book of the acts of Solomon?"
- **2 Kgs 10:34** — "Now the rest of the acts of Jehu, and all that he did, and all his might, [are] they not written in the book of the chronicles of the kings of Israel?"
- **2 Chr 20:34** — "Now the rest of the acts of Jehoshaphat, first and last, behold, they [are] written in the book of Jehu..."
- **2 Kgs 25:11** — "Now the rest of the people [that were] left in the city, and the fugitives that fell away to the king of Babylon, with the remnant of the multitude..."

### 2. Remaining People / Survivors
Groups of people left after battle, deportation, or divine judgment. Used for remnants of enemy nations and surviving populations.

- **Josh 23:12** — "if ye do in any wise go back, and cleave unto the remnant of these nations, [even] these that remain among you"
- **Neh 4:14** — "I said unto the nobles, and to the rulers, and to the rest of the people, Be not ye afraid of them"
- **Hab 2:8** — "all the remnant of the people shall spoil thee; because of men's blood"
- **Zech 14:2** — "the city shall be taken... but the residue of the people shall not be cut off from the city"

### 3. Remaining Portion of Things
Leftover quantities of physical items: oil, prey, territory, food, wood, years of life.

- **Lev 14:17** — "of the rest of the oil that [is] in his hand shall the priest put upon the tip of the right ear"
- **Joel 1:4** — "that which the palmerworm hath left hath the locust eaten; and that which the locust hath left hath the cankerworm eaten"
- **Isa 44:19** — "the residue thereof shall I make for an abomination"
- **Isa 38:10** — "I am deprived of the residue of my years"
- **Psa 17:14** — "they are full of children, and leave the rest of their [substance] to their babes"

### 4. Excellence / Surpassing Degree
The root sense of "overhang/excess" used metaphorically for superiority or exceptional quality. Rare but early.

- **Gen 49:3** — "Reuben, thou [art] my firstborn, my might... the excellency of dignity, and the excellency of power"
- **Prov 17:7** — "Excellent speech becometh not a fool: much less do lying lips a prince"
- **Job 4:21** — "Doth not their excellency [which is] in them go away? they die, even without wisdom"
- **Dan 8:9** — "a little horn, which waxed exceeding great, toward the south, and toward the east"

### 5. Physical Cord or String
The literal "hanging free" sense: bowstrings, animal cords, restraining ropes.

- **Judg 16:7** — "If they bind me with seven green withs that were never dried, then shall I be weak"
- **Judg 16:8–9** — seven green withs used to bind Samson; he broke them
- **Psa 11:2** — "they make ready their arrow upon the string, that they may privily shoot at the upright"
- **Job 30:11** — "Because he hath loosed my cord, and afflicted me"
