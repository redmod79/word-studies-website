### Relationship: skotos (G4655) vs. skotia (G4653)

Both mean "darkness" but differ in usage pattern:
- **skotos** (neuter, 31 vv): Broader NT usage across 16 books; preferred by Paul, Synoptics; used for "outer darkness" (eschatological punishment), "power of darkness" (domain of evil), and physical cosmic darkness
- **skotia** (feminine, 12 vv): Concentrated in Johannine literature (John 6x, 1 John 4x); preferred for the light/darkness dualism in John's prologue and epistles
- They never co-occur in the same verse (0 shared verses)
- Both share the same Louw-Nida domains (14.007 Darkness, 88.015 Bad/Evil)
- The distinction is primarily authorial preference, not semantic
