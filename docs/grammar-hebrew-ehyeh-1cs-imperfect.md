# אֶהְיֶה *ehyeh* — 1cs Qal Imperfect of היה (H1961)

## Definition

*ehyeh* (אֶהְיֶה) is the **first-person common singular Qal imperfect** of the root היה "to be / become / happen" (H1961, III-he). As a finite verb-form it is morphologically unambiguous (no gender distinction in 1cs), but its semantic range is exceptionally wide because Biblical Hebrew's "imperfect" (*yiqtol*) is aspect/mood-driven rather than tense-locked and because היה itself spans copular "be," existential "exist," and fientive "become / come to pass." The form is the headline of Exo 3:14 *ehyeh asher ehyeh* but occurs 20× in the Hebrew Bible and is most often parsed as a covenant formula "I will be with you."

1. **Predictive future** — "I will be / I will become"
2. **Durative / habitual / ongoing present** — "I am / I keep being"
3. **Modal / volitive (cohortative nuance)** — "let me be / may I be"
4. **Gnomic or essential predication** — "I am (in essence)"
5. **Covenantal accompaniment formula** — idiomatic אֶהְיֶה + עִם + 2ms "I will be with you"

## Form Recognition

- **Aleph-prefix (א)** — the 1cs *yiqtol* preformative; always vocalised *seghol* (אֶ-) or *hateph-seghol* under the laryngeal before the following *shewa*/*hateph*
- **No gender suffix** — 1cs *yiqtol* is common-gender (same form for male/female speaker), so אֶהְיֶה never takes a feminine counterpart
- **III-he root behaviour** — the original final *he* of היה surfaces as a plene **segolate ending -ה** (yod + seghol + he) in the non-jussive imperfect; contrast the jussive/wayyiqtol which apocopates to יְהִי / וַיְהִי (h elided, short vowel)
- **Full pointing:** אֶהְיֶה = א (seghol) + ה (hateph-seghol under laryngeal, rendered vocal shewa in printed texts) + י (shewa/silent) + ֶ (seghol) + ה (final mater)
- **Parser codes:** `Verb.Qal.Impf.1s` (ETCBC gloss "be"); search string `sp=verb vs=qal vt=impf ps=p1 nu=sg` with lemma filter `היה`
- **Lemma:** היה, Strong's **H1961**
- **Contrast with jussive form** יְהִי ("let there be," Gen 1:3) — same root, same stem, different person (3ms) and mood (jussive/short); the *yod*-prefix + apocopated *he* is the telltale difference from א-prefix + full *-eh* ending

### 3. Absolute / Essential Predication (Exo 3:14)

| Reference | Hebrew clause | Parsing | KJV |
|-----------|---------------|---------|-----|

| Exo 3:14b | אֶֽהְיֶ֖ה שְׁלָחַ֥נִי אֲלֵיכֶֽם | Qal.Impf.1s | "I AM hath sent me unto you" |

**Syntax of *ehyeh asher ehyeh*** — an **idem-per-idem construction**: two finite clauses (each a complete verbless-verbal sentence with built-in 1cs subject) joined by the relative particle אֲשֶׁר. The two *ehyeh*s are grammatically symmetrical; אֲשֶׁר functions as a generalising relative ("that / which / whatever") rather than a standard restrictive relative. Parallel formulae include Exo 33:19 *ḥannōtî ʾet-ʾăšer ʾāḥōn* ("I will be gracious to whom I will be gracious") and Ezk 12:25, confirming the idiom as a Hebrew rhetorical device of deliberate self-definition by self-reference. The 1cs subject is embedded in the verb; no independent pronoun is required.

In v.14b the form is re-used as a **proper name-substitute** (subject of שְׁלָחַנִי "has sent me"), showing the verb-form can itself be nominalised as a self-designation.

## The Translation Range

Because Biblical Hebrew *yiqtol* does not grammaticalise tense in the Indo-European sense, *ehyeh* is compatible with all of the following glosses — the resolution depends on clausal context, not morphology:

| Gloss | Aspectual/Modal force | Contextual trigger |
|-------|----------------------|--------------------|
| "I will be" | future/predictive | commissioning/promise contexts (Exo 3:12; Jos 1:5) |
| "I am" | durative-present / essential | verbless-style existential assertion (Exo 3:14) |
| "I am being" / "I keep being" | continuous / habitual | repeated-action or idem-per-idem frames |
| "I become / I am becoming" | fientive / ingressive | contexts of state-change (cf. Qal *hāyāh* "come to pass") |
| "Let me be" / "may I be" | volitive / modal | hypothetical/conditional contexts (some wisdom/narrative) |

Standard grammars (Joüon–Muraoka §113; GKC §107) classify *yiqtol* as encoding **imperfective / non-past / modal** aspect. The form *ehyeh* thus legitimately bears any one of "I am / I will be / I am being / I keep being / I become" — Exo 3:14 is ambiguous at the morphological level by design. LXX resolves with present indicative Ἐγώ εἰμι ὁ ὤν; Aquila and Theodotion read future Ἔσομαι ὃς ἔσομαι; the Vulgate reads present "ego sum qui sum."

**Relation to יִהְיֶה and phonological proximity to יהוה (YHWH):** the 3ms imperfect of היה is **yihyeh** (vocalised יִהְיֶ֣ה), consonantally יהיה. The Tetragrammaton **יהוה** (YHWH) shares the same root-consonants י-ה-ה plus waw (or is formed from an older *הוה* by-form). Whether יהוה is best analysed as a Qal 3ms imperfect ("he is / he will be / he causes to be") or a Hiphil ("he causes to be / he brings into existence") is a long-standing morphological debate; either way, יהוה is formally and etymologically adjacent to 3ms יִהְיֶה and, by reflex, to 1cs אֶהְיֶה. The Exo 3:14 → 3:15 sequence exploits exactly this relation: God self-names in v.14 with the 1cs *ehyeh* ("I will be"), then in v.15 supplies the external 3ms name *YHWH* ("he will be / he is") that others will use. The 1cs↔3ms pairing is a single semantic core (אֶהְיֶה "I will be" ↔ *YHWH* "He-will-be / He-is") rotated to first person for divine self-reference and third person for the covenant name.
