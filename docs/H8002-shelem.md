## Lexicon Definition

Properly, requital, i.e. a (voluntary) sacrifice in thanks. From H7999 shalam. KJV rendering: peace offering.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Legislative Regulation (Leviticus 3, 7)
The core legal definition of the offering — types of animals, fat portions for YHWH, flesh portions for the offerer, time limits for eating.
- **Lev 3:1** — "And if his oblation be a sacrifice of peace offering, if he offer it of the herd; whether it be a male or female, he shall offer it without blemish before the LORD"
- **Lev 7:11** — "And this is the law of the sacrifice of peace offerings, which he shall offer unto the LORD"
- **Lev 7:15** — "And the flesh of the sacrifice of his peace offerings for thanksgiving shall be eaten the same day"
- **Lev 19:5** — "And if ye offer a sacrifice of peace offerings unto the LORD, ye shall offer it at your own will"

### 2. Paired with Burnt Offering (olah) in Worship Events
The most common collocation — shelem and olah appear together in 37 verses, representing the complete worship sequence: total dedication (olah) plus communal fellowship meal (shelem).
- **Exo 20:24** — "shalt sacrifice thereon thy burnt offerings, and thy peace offerings"

### 3. National/Covenant-Making Occasions
Offered at pivotal moments establishing or renewing Israel's relationship with God — at Sinai, on Ebal, at coronations, and at temple dedications.
- **Exo 24:5** — "offered burnt offerings, and sacrificed peace offerings of oxen unto the LORD" (Sinai covenant ratification)
- **Jos 8:31** — "offered thereon burnt offerings unto the LORD, and sacrificed peace offerings" (Ebal ceremony)
- **1 Sam 11:15** — "there they sacrificed sacrifices of peace offerings before the LORD; and there Saul and all the men of Israel rejoiced greatly" (Saul's coronation)
- **2 Chr 7:7** — "Solomon hallowed the middle of the court... for there he offered burnt offerings, and the fat of the peace offerings"

### 4. Dedicatory/Tribal Offerings (Numbers 7)
The twelve tribal chieftains each bring identical shelem offerings at the tabernacle dedication — a formulaic repetition emphasizing equal participation.
- **Num 7:17** — "And for a sacrifice of peace offerings, two oxen, five rams, five he goats, five lambs of the first year"
- **Num 7:88** — "And all the oxen for the sacrifice of the peace offerings were twenty and four bullocks, the rams sixty, the he goats sixty, the lambs of the first year sixty"

### 5. Nazirite Completion and Vow Fulfillment

- **Num 6:14** — "one he lamb... for a burnt offering... one ewe lamb... for a sin offering, and one ram... for peace offerings"
- **Num 6:17** — "And he shall offer the ram for a sacrifice of peace offerings unto the LORD"
- **Lev 22:21** — "whosoever offereth a sacrifice of peace offerings unto the LORD to accomplish his vow, or a freewill offering"

### 6. Eschatological/Restored Temple (Ezekiel)
Ezekiel's temple vision reinstates the shelem as part of the prince's worship responsibilities.
- **Eze 43:27** — "the priests shall make your burnt offerings upon the altar, and your peace offerings; and I will accept you"
- **Eze 46:2** — "the prince shall enter... and the priests shall prepare his burnt offering and his peace offerings"
