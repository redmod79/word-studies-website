## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same across testaments:**
- Spatial superimposition ("upon," "on," "over") is stable from Hebrew ʿal through LXX ἐπί into NT ἐπί.
- Adversative sense ("against") remains intact in the Greek tradition.
- Authority sense ("over," "in charge of") is preserved.

**What shifts:**
- Hebrew ʿal functions as a conjunction ("and," "because," "therefore") roughly 8% of the time; Greek ἐπί is almost exclusively prepositional. The NT uses γάρ, ὅτι, and οὖν for these logical connections.
- The sheer frequency drops: ʿal appears in ~27% of OT verses, while ἐπί appears in ~11% of NT verses, reflecting the distribution of prepositional needs across the corpora.
