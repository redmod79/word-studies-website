# WG: Fat / Best Portion

## Overview
This cluster covers cheleb (fat/best portion) in its sacrificial setting: the fat parts are removed, handled with kidneys/caul, burned on the altar, and repeatedly paired with blood. The same Hebrew word also extends figuratively to the "best" or richest portion, so the group distinguishes ritual fat from blood, meat/flesh, and general clean/unclean food categories.

## Roles in the Concept

- **Core substance / quality:** cheleb (חֶלֶב) — fat itself and, by extension, the best or choice part
- **Narrow fat term:** peder (פֶּדֶר) — suet/fat in a small number of burnt-offering texts
- **Ritual action:** qatar (קָטַר) — make smoke/burn sacrificial material
- **Ritual place:** mizbeach (מִזְבֵּחַ) — altar where fat is burned
- **Paired but distinct substance:** dam (דָּם) — blood, often named beside fat but not identical with it
- **Anatomical removal terms:** kilyah (כִּלְיָה) and yothereth (יֹתֶרֶת) — kidneys and caul/lobe repeatedly handled with the fat portions
- **Sacrificial frame:** zebach (זֶבַח) — sacrifice/slaughter to which these fat portions belong
