**Semantic split in translation:** The LXX cleanly separates kabad's two semantic poles. When kabad means "be heavy, burden, harden" (physical weight), βαρύνω is used (e.g., Pharaoh's hardened heart, Exod 8:15, 32; 9:7, 34). When kabad means "honor, glorify" (relational weight/importance), δοξάζω is used (e.g., "honour thy father," "glorify the LORD"). This split is systematic — the semantic range of kabad was judged too wide for a single Greek verb.

## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same across testaments:**
- The core meaning of rendering honor, ascribing weight/importance to God, persists from kabad → doxazō
- The ethical command pattern ("honor your father," "glorify God") carries directly
- The retributive principle of 1 Sam 2:30 — those who honor God will be honored — reappears in John 12:26

**What shifts:**
- In the OT, kabad covers both physical heaviness and relational honor; the LXX split isolates the honor sense into doxazō, making the NT usage more focused
- The NT develops a mutual glorification theology absent from the OT: Father glorifies Son, Son glorifies Father, Spirit glorifies Son (John 13:31-32; 16:14; 17:1-5)
- The passive "being glorified" takes on an eschatological dimension (Rom 8:30) beyond the OT usage pattern
