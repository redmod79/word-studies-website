# Seal-Command Syntax in Daniel — סְתֹם / חֲתֹם (Qal Impv 2ms) and Paired Lexeme Syntax

## Definition

A closely related pair of Hebrew verbs, **סתם H5640** "stop up / shut up / hide" and **חתם H2856** "seal / affix a seal," are deployed together in Daniel's apocalyptic closures (Dan 8:26; 9:24; 12:4, 9) in a **fixed volitive construction** — Qal imperative (or Qal infinitive construct of purpose, or Qal passive participle) + definite direct object + optional temporal limit `ʿad ʿēṯ qēṣ` ("until the time of the end"). The construction is a genre-marker of prophetic *deferred-disclosure*: the receiving prophet is ordered to package the vision so that it is *retained intact yet inaccessible* until a designated horizon.

1. **Simple shut-up command** — *sətōm* + definite object (Dan 8:26): seal one lexeme, one object, no temporal limit expressed in the sealing verb itself (it is transferred to the explanatory *kî lə-yāmîm rabbîm* "for many days" clause).
2. **Dual shut-up + seal command** — *sətōm* X *wa-ḥăṯōm* Y + *ʿad ʿēṯ qēṣ* (Dan 12:4): two coordinated Qal.Impv.2ms, two distinct objects (*dəbārîm* / *sēper*), explicit temporal end-boundary.
3. **Dual passive participle declaration** — *sətumîm wa-ḥăṯumîm* + definite subject + *ʿad ʿēṯ qēṣ* (Dan 12:9): same lexeme pair rotated from active command to resultative-passive state; the sealing has already happened and persists.
4. **Infinitive-of-purpose sealing** — *lə-* + *ḥătōm* + double object (Dan 9:24): the sealing is listed as one of six purpose-clauses governed by *neḥtaḵ* ("are determined") defining what the seventy weeks are *for*.

## Form Recognition

- **סתם (sātam)** — root consonants ס‑ת‑ם; H5640; ETCBC gloss "stop up"; attested 13× canonically (Gen 26:15, 18; 2Kgs 3:19, 25; Neh 4:1; Psa 51:8; Ezek 28:3; Dan 8:26; 12:4; 12:9; 2Chr 32:3, 4, 30); Qal + one Piel cluster (Gen 26) + one Niphal (Neh 4:1); physical meaning = "stop up (a well, a water-source, a mouth)."
- **חתם (ḥātam)** — root consonants ח‑ת‑ם; H2856; ETCBC gloss "seal"; attested 27× canonically; Qal dominant with Piel (Job 24:16), Niphal (Esth 3:12; 8:8), Hiphil (Lev 15:3 bodily-flow sense) peripheral; physical meaning = "close with a seal / signet / bulla."
- **Parser codes (for the Daniel cluster):**
  - `sp=verb vs=qal vt=impv ps=2 gn=m nu=sg` — Qal.Impv.2ms (Dan 8:26 סְתֹם; Dan 12:4 סְתֹם, חֲתֹם; Isa 8:16 צוֹר, חֲתוֹם)
  - `sp=verb vs=qal vt=ptca gn=m nu=pl st=a` — Qal passive participle mp Abs (Dan 12:9 סְתֻמִים, חֲתֻמִים)
  - `sp=verb vs=qal vt=infc` — Qal Infinitive Construct (Dan 9:24 לַחְתֹּם)
- **Pointing tell for passive participle of these roots:** u-class theme-vowel *qətulîm* (סְתֻמִים / חֲתֻמִים) — same vocalization as e.g. Gen 41:3 רָעוֹת. ETCBC tags these as `Ptcp` without a separate passive feature; the u-vocalization + resultative sense identifies the passive reading.

### 1. Single-Lexeme Imperative: *sətōm* + definite object

### 2. Dual-Lexeme Imperative Chain: *sətōm* X *wa-ḥăṯōm* Y *ʿad ʿēṯ qēṣ*

| Reference | Hebrew | Parsing | KJV |
|-----------|--------|---------|-----|
| Dan 12:4 | סְתֹם הַדְּבָרִים וַחֲתֹם הַסֵּפֶר עַד־עֵת קֵץ | Impv.2ms סתם + Art+Noun.mp + Conj+Impv.2ms חתם + Art+Noun.ms + Prep + Noun.s.**Cst** + Noun.ms | "shut up the words, and seal the book, [even] to the time of the end" |

Pragmatics: two Qal imperatives coordinated by consecutive *wə-* (*wa-ḥăṯōm* with *patah* under waw + *šewaʾ* — standard impv-chain pointing, not wayyiqtol — because an imperative cannot be wayyiqtol). Two objects **distinguished by lexeme** — *dəbārîm* (plural, the verbal content) bound to *sātam*, *sēper* (singular, the physical scroll) bound to *ḥātam*. Time-bound by construct-chain PP *ʿad ʿēṯ qēṣ* "until a time of end" (*ʿēṯ* fem.sg.Cst of עת H6256, *qēṣ* Noun.ms.Abs H7093).

### 4. Infinitive of Purpose (Nested in Six-Clause Purpose Chain)

Parser-verified chain of six *lə-* + infinitives under the governing Niphal *neḥtaḵ* "is determined" (Dan 9:24 opening): *ləḵallēʾ* / *lə-hātēm* / *lə-ḵappēr* / *lə-hāḇîʾ* / *la-ḥtōm* / *li-mšōaḥ*. ## Contrast with Related Forms (Sealing Outside Daniel)

### A. Isa 8:16 — *ṣôr təʿûḏâ ḥăṯôm tôrâ* (closest syntactic parallel to Dan 12:4)

| Reference | Hebrew | Parsing | KJV |
|-----------|--------|---------|-----|
| Isa 8:16 | צוֹר תְּעוּדָה חֲתוֹם תּוֹרָה בְּלִמֻּדָי | Qal.Impv.2ms of צרר ("bind") + Noun.fs + Qal.**Impv.2ms** of חתם + Noun.fs + Prep+Noun.mp.+1s | "Bind up the testimony, seal the law among my disciples" |

Same paired-imperative shape as Dan 12:4, but the first verb is **צרר** (bind/wrap) rather than **סתם** (stop up), and there is **no** *ʿad ʿēṯ qēṣ* terminus. Isaiah's pair binds testimony into an accessible closed group (*bə-limmuḏay*, "among my disciples"); Daniel's pair consigns words to a future horizon. Same sealing verb חתם, different sealing *destination*.

### B. Isa 29:11 — *ha-sēp̄er he-ḥāṯûm* (sealed-book topos)

| Reference | Hebrew | Parsing | KJV |
|-----------|--------|---------|-----|
| Isa 29:11 | כְּדִבְרֵי הַסֵּפֶר הֶחָתוּם ... כִּי חָתוּם הוּא | Prep+Noun.mp.Cst + Art+Noun.ms + Art+Verb.Qal.**Ptcp.ms.Abs** חתם | "as the words of a book that is sealed ... for it [is] sealed" |

Anchors *sēper ḥāṯûm* as a formed topos before Daniel. Qal passive participle (identical morphology to Dan 12:9 but singular) used adjectivally with article. Sealed=illegible, even to the literate ("he saith, I cannot; for it is sealed"). This Isaianic background is presupposed by Daniel's *sētōm ha-dəbārîm wə-ḥăṯōm ha-sēper*.

### C. Jer 32:10–14 — Legal Sealing of a Deed

| Reference | Hebrew | Parsing | KJV |
|-----------|--------|---------|-----|
| Jer 32:10 | וָאֶכְתֹּב בַּסֵּפֶר וָאֶחְתֹּם וָאָעֵד עֵדִים | Qal.**Wayq.1s** of כתב + Qal.Wayq.1s of חתם + Hiph.Wayq.1s of עוד | "I subscribed the evidence, and sealed [it], and took witnesses" |

*ḥātam* deployed in first-person wayyiqtol narrative — legal-transactional register. Seal authenticates a **document that is meant to be opened later** in accordance with stipulated conditions (Jer 32:14 "put them in an earthen vessel, that they may continue many days"). Semantic overlap with Daniel on the *time-deferred* axis; contrast on the *hiddenness* axis (Jeremiah's deed is not shut up to prevent reading — it is sealed to prove authenticity when unsealed).

## Semantic Distinction: סתם vs. חתם

| Axis | סתם (H5640) | חתם (H2856) |
|------|-------------|-------------|
| Prototype physical action | stop up a well / block a water source (Gen 26:15, 18; 2 Kgs 3:19, 25; 2 Chr 32:3–4, 30) | affix a seal with signet-ring to a scroll, letter, decree (1 Kgs 21:8; Jer 32:10, 44; Esth 3:12; 8:8; Dan 6:18; Neh 10:1–2) |
| Agent instrumentality | physical plugging / filling | stamp/impression of seal-ring (ʿizqāh / ḥôṯām) |
| Effect on access | **blocks** — nothing passes in or out | **authenticates + closes** — object remains intact, but opening = breaking the seal |
| Effect on content | content becomes unreadable / unreachable (source of water becomes unavailable) | content is protected from tampering; legally binding on reading |
| Who may reverse | the same stopper-up (2 Chr 32 Hezekiah stops; Jer 2:13 broken-cistern inversion) | authorized seal-holder only (Dan 6:18 "that the purpose might not be changed"); Rev 5 "who is worthy to open the book and loose the seals thereof?" |
| Metaphor extension in Daniel | the **words** are shut up — cognitive inaccessibility | the **book** is sealed — document-integrity until a rightful opener |

Dan 12:4 uses **both** because the two verbs operate on different objects with different functions: *sətōm ha-dəbārîm* ("make the words inaccessible") + *ḥăṯōm ha-sēper* ("lock the book until its authorized opener"). Dan 8:26 uses only *sətōm* because the object is *ha-ḥāzôn* (the vision-event as cognitive content) — there is no physical scroll-document yet to receive a seal. Dan 9:24 uses only *ḥātam* because the infinitive-of-purpose frame is sketching what the seventy weeks *accomplish* — they **authenticate/ratify** *ḥāzôn wə-nāḇîʾ* by bringing them to determined fulfillment (the ratification sense of חתם, cf. Dan 6:18 irrevocability), not by making them inaccessible.

## The *ʿad ʿēṯ qēṣ* Terminus

- Construct chain: Prep עַד H5704 + Noun.s.Cst עֵת H6256 + Noun.ms.Abs קֵץ H7093 — "unto time-of end."
- Distribution: 5× in Daniel in the sealed-vision strand (Dan 8:17; 11:35; 12:4; 12:9; cf. Dan 12:6 עַד מָתַי קֵץ variant "how long until the end").
- The terminus is **asymmetric to the command** — a sealing command without *ʿad ʿēṯ qēṣ* (Isa 8:16 "among my disciples") defers to an addressable group; a sealing command with *ʿad ʿēṯ qēṣ* (Dan 12:4) defers to a calendrical horizon. Dan 8:26 uses the semantic equivalent in declarative form (*kî lə-yāmîm rabbîm* "because [it is] for many days"); this is not the *ʿēṯ qēṣ* construct-chain but functions as its informal variant.
- Syntactic scope: in Dan 12:4 *ʿad ʿēṯ qēṣ* modifies the entire *sətōm … wa-ḥăṯōm* conjoined verbal phrase (not only the nearer verb). In Dan 12:9 it modifies the *sətumîm wa-ḥăṯumîm* resultative state.
