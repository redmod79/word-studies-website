## Semantic Categories

### 1. God's Presence Dwelling in the Sanctuary

The foundational theological use: YHWH choosing to shakan (encamp, settle) in the midst of his people via the tabernacle/temple. The verb carries the sense of divine condescension — the transcendent God taking up tent-dwelling among mortals.

- **Exod 25:8** — "And let them make me a sanctuary; that I may dwell among them."
- **Exod 29:45** — "And I will dwell among the children of Israel, and will be their God."
- **Num 35:34** — "Defile not therefore the land...wherein I dwell: for I the LORD dwell among the children of Israel."
- **1 Ki 6:13** — "And I will dwell among the children of Israel, and will not forsake my people Israel."

### 2. The Divine Cloud Settling

The shekinah glory-cloud (כָּבוֹד, H3519) rests (shakan) on specific locations — Sinai, the tabernacle. The verb captures the dynamic of the divine presence descending and remaining, directing Israel's movements.

- **Exod 24:16** — "And the glory of the LORD abode upon mount Sinai, and the cloud covered it six days."
- **Num 9:17** — "And when the cloud was taken up from the tabernacle...and in the place where the cloud abode, there the children of Israel pitched their tents."
- **Psa 68:16** — "Why leap ye, ye high hills? this is the hill which God desireth to dwell in; yea, the LORD will dwell in it for ever."

### 3. Deuteronomic: God's Name Dwelling in a Chosen Place

Deuteronomy develops a distinctive theology of the divine name (שֵׁם, H8034) being caused to dwell in the chosen sanctuary. The Hiphil form is concentrated here. Rather than YHWH dwelling bodily, his "name" is the presence that settles.

- **Deut 12:11** — "Then there shall be a place which the LORD your God shall choose to cause his name to dwell there."
- **Deut 14:23** — "And thou shalt eat before the LORD thy God, in the place which he shall choose to place his name there."
- **Psa 15:1** — "LORD, who shall abide in thy tabernacle? who shall dwell in thy holy hill?"
- **Psa 65:4** — "Blessed is the man whom thou choosest...that he may dwell in thy courts."

### 4. Prophetic / Eschatological: "I Will Dwell"

The Prophets project shakan forward as an eschatological promise: YHWH will return to Zion and take up permanent residence. This future-tense "I will dwell" marks the climax of covenant restoration.

- **Zech 2:10** — "Sing and rejoice, O daughter of Zion: for, lo, I come, and I will dwell in the midst of thee, saith the LORD."
- **Zech 8:3** — "Thus saith the LORD; I am returned unto Zion, and will dwell in the midst of Jerusalem."
- **Joel 3:17** — "So shall ye know that I am the LORD your God dwelling in Zion, my holy mountain."
- **Jer 23:6** — "In his days Judah shall be saved, and Israel shall dwell safely: and this is his name whereby he shall be called, THE LORD OUR RIGHTEOUSNESS."

### 5. Human Settlement in the Land

The covenant promise that Israel will dwell (shakan) securely in the land given to the fathers. Here the word carries its mundane sense of permanent, settled habitation — but the theological context of land-grant covenant is always near.

- **Psa 37:3** — "Trust in the LORD, and do good; so shalt thou dwell in the land, and verily thou shalt be fed."
- **Psa 37:29** — "The righteous shall inherit the land, and dwell therein for ever."
- **Pro 2:21** — "For the upright shall dwell in the land, and the perfect shall remain in it."
- **Deut 33:12** — "The beloved of the LORD shall dwell in safety by him."

### 6. Desolation — Creatures Inhabiting Ruins

Judgment oracles invert the dwelling promise: where Israel once shakan in the land, desolate creatures will shakan in the ruins. The same word used for divine indwelling is used for birds and beasts colonizing devastated cities.

- **Isa 13:20–21** — "It shall never be inhabited...but wild beasts of the desert shall lie there; and their houses shall be full of doleful creatures; and owls shall dwell there."
- **Isa 34:11** — Similar desolation: cormorant and bittern dwell where God once acted.
- **Nah 3:18** — "Thy nobles shall dwell in the dust."
- **Job 3:5** — "Let darkness and the shadow of death stain it; let a cloud dwell upon it."
