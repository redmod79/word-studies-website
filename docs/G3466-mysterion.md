# G3466 — μυστήριον (mystērion)

## Core Data
- **Strong's:** G3466
- **Original:** μυστήριον
- **Transliteration:** mystērion
- **Pronunciation:** moos-tay'-ree-on
- **Part of Speech:** Neuter noun
- **Root:** from μύω (myō) — to shut the mouth; hence "that into which one is initiated"
- **Occurrences:** 27 unique verses across 10 books
- **First Occurrence:** Mat 13:11 — "it is given unto you to know the mysteries of the kingdom of heaven"
- **Hapax Legomenon:** No

## Lexicon Definition

From a derivative of μύω (myō — to shut the mouth); a secret or "mystery" (kept silent), i.e. a divine secret that is not discernible by human reason alone but requires divine revelation for its disclosure.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Mysteries of the Kingdom (Synoptic Parables)
Jesus uses the plural to explain why he speaks in parables — insiders receive the secret, outsiders only the surface.
- **Mat 13:11** — "it is given unto you to know the mysteries of the kingdom of heaven, but to them it is not given"
- **Mark 4:11** — "Unto you it is given to know the mystery of the kingdom of God: but unto them that are without, all these things are done in parables"
- **Luke 8:10** — "it is given to know the mysteries of the kingdom of God: but to others in parables"

### 2. God's Pre-temporal Secret Now Disclosed
The dominant Pauline usage — something hidden through all prior ages but now brought to light in the gospel proclamation.
- **1 Cor 2:7** — "we speak the wisdom of God in a mystery, even the hidden wisdom, which God ordained before the world unto our glory"
- **Col 1:26** — "the mystery which hath been hid from ages and from generations, but now is made manifest to his saints"
- **Rom 16:25** — "the revelation of the mystery, which was kept secret since the world began"
- **Eph 3:9** — "the mystery, which from the beginning of the world hath been hid in God"

### 3. The Mystery of Christ Among the Gentiles
In Ephesians and Colossians the content of the mystery is specified — Christ himself, and the Gentiles' inclusion in the covenant.
- **Col 1:27** — "what is the riches of the glory of this mystery among the Gentiles; which is Christ in you, the hope of glory"
- **Eph 3:3–4** — "by revelation he made known unto me the mystery... my knowledge in the mystery of Christ"
- **Eph 1:9** — "Having made known unto us the mystery of his will, according to his good pleasure which he hath purposed in himself"
- **Eph 5:32** — "This is a great mystery: but I speak concerning Christ and the church"

### 4. Eschatological and Apocalyptic Mysteries
End-time disclosures — things about to be completed or already beginning to unfold.
- **1 Cor 15:51** — "Behold, I shew you a mystery; We shall not all sleep, but we shall all be changed"
- **2 Thess 2:7** — "the mystery of iniquity doth already work"
- **Rev 10:7** — "in the days of the voice of the seventh angel... the mystery of God should be finished"
- **Rev 1:20** — "The mystery of the seven stars... and the seven golden candlesticks"

### 5. Encoded Symbolic Meaning
Revelation uses mystērion to flag that a name or image carries a hidden symbolic referent.
- **Rev 17:5** — "upon her forehead was a name written, MYSTERY, BABYLON THE GREAT"
- **Rev 17:7** — "I will tell thee the mystery of the woman, and of the beast"
- **1 Tim 3:9** — "Holding the mystery of the faith in a pure conscience"
- **1 Tim 3:16** — "great is the mystery of godliness: God was manifest in the flesh"
