## Lexicon Definition

Feminine of מָנוֹחַ (H4494); repose or (adverbially) peacefully; figuratively, consolation (specifically, matrimony); hence (concretely) an abode: — comfortable, ease, quiet, rest(-ing place), still.

The word carries two interlocking senses: the *state* of rest/repose, and the *place* where rest is found (a settled abode or resting place). Both senses run through its biblical usage.

Notably it sits in poetic and prophetic literature as much as in narrative, and in narrative it tends to mark the giving of "rest" as the goal of the land/inheritance (Deut, 1 Kings, 1 Chronicles).

### 1. Rest in the Promised Land / Inheritance
The settled rest God gives Israel after wandering and conquest — the land itself as the resting place.
- **Deut 12:9** — "ye are not as yet come to the rest (mᵉnûwchâh) and to the inheritance"
- **1 Kings 8:56** — "the LORD, that hath given rest (mᵉnûwchâh) unto his people Israel"
- **Psalm 95:11** — "they should not enter into my rest (mᵉnûwchâh)"
- **Micah 2:10** — "this is not your rest (mᵉnûwchâh): because it is polluted"

### 2. The Resting Place of the Ark / God's Dwelling
A fixed dwelling-place — for the ark, and for the LORD himself.
- **Num 10:33** — "the ark of the covenant... went before them... to search out a resting place (mᵉnûwchâh) for them"
- **Psalm 132:8** — "Arise, O LORD, into thy rest (mᵉnûwchâh); thou, and the ark of thy strength"
- **Psalm 132:14** — "This is my rest (mᵉnûwchâh) for ever: here will I dwell"
- **Isaiah 66:1** — "where is... the place of my rest (mᵉnûwchâh)?"

### 3. Inner Repose / Comfort of the Soul
Rest as a personal, restorative condition — quietness, consolation, refreshment.
- **Psalm 23:2** — "he leadeth me beside the still (mᵉnûwchâh) waters"
- **Psalm 116:7** — "Return unto thy rest (mᵉnûwchâh), O my soul"

- **Jeremiah 45:3** — "I fainted in my sighing, and I find no rest (mᵉnûwchâh)"

### 4. A State of Peace, Security, and Ease
Settled tranquility, freedom from war or threat — often promised or contrasted with turmoil.
- **Gen 49:15** — "he saw that rest (mᵉnûwchâh) was good"
- **1 Chron 22:9** — "he shall be a man of rest (mᵉnûwchâh)... I will give him rest from all his enemies"
