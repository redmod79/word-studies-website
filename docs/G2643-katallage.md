## Lexicon Definition

From καταλλάσσω (G2644); exchange (figuratively, adjustment), i.e. restoration to
[divine favor]:

1. **Exchange** — of the business of money-changers, exchanging equivalent values (Aristotle et al.)
2. **Adjustment of a difference; reconciliation; restoration to favor** (from Aeschylus on); in the NT, of the restoration of the favor of God to sinners that repent and put their trust in the expiatory death of Christ — 2 Cor 5:18f.; with the genitive of the one received into favor, τοῦ κόσμου (opposed to ἀποβολή), Rom 11:15; καταλλαγήν ἐλάβομεν — "we received the blessing of the recovered favor of God," Rom 5:11.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. The Received State of Reconciliation
The noun denotes the restored relationship believers have already received — a completed gift, not an ongoing process.

- **Rom 5:11** — "And not only [so], but we also joy in God through our Lord Jesus Christ, by whom we have now received the atonement [katallagēn]."

### 2. Reconciliation in Scope — Rejection and Receiving of Israel
Katallagē describes the cosmic/international dimension of God's redemptive economy involving Israel and the Gentiles.

- **Rom 11:15** — "For if the casting away of them [be] the reconciling [katallagē] of the world, what [shall] the receiving [of them be], but life from the dead?"

### 3. The Ministry and Word of Reconciliation
Katallagē names both the task entrusted to Paul (and believers) and its content — the message to be proclaimed.

- **2 Cor 5:18** — "...who hath reconciled us to himself by Jesus Christ, and hath given to us the ministry [diakonian] of reconciliation [katallagēs];"
- **2 Cor 5:19** — "...and hath committed unto us the word [logon] of reconciliation [katallagēs]."
