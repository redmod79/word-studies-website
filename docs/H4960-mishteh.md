## Lexicon Definition

From שָׁתָה (shathah, "to drink"): drink, by implication, drinking (the act); also (by implication) a banquet or (generally) feast. KJV: banquet, drank, drink, feast((-ed), -ing).

The underlying sense is a social drinking-occasion. Unlike חַג (chag, H2282), which denotes a calendrical/religious festival, mishteh denotes an ad-hoc social event centered on shared eating and especially drinking.

**Pattern:** Massive concentration in Esther (44% of all uses, density ~20x the next book) — the book of Esther is structurally built around a sequence of mishteh-scenes. Secondary clusters in Genesis patriarchal narratives (weddings, weanings, covenant meals) and in Daniel (Babylonian court banqueting). Entirely absent from Torah legal/cultic material, Psalms, and most of the Prophets — this is a **narrative-setting** word, not a cultic one.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Covenant / Hospitality Feast
A meal hosted to welcome, honor, or seal relations with guests.
- **Gen 19:3** — Lot "made them a feast" for the two angelic visitors
- **Gen 26:30** — Isaac makes a feast for Abimelech at the Beersheba covenant
- **2 Sam 3:20** — David makes a feast for Abner and his twenty men
- **1 Kings 3:15** — Solomon "made a feast to all his servants" after the Gibeon dream

### 2. Life-Cycle Celebration (Weaning, Wedding, Birthday)
Feasts tied to family/royal milestone events.
- **Gen 21:8** — Abraham makes "a great feast" on the day Isaac was weaned
- **Gen 29:22** — Laban gathers all the men and "made a feast" for Jacob's wedding
- **Gen 40:20** — Pharaoh's birthday feast "unto all his servants"
- **Judg 14:10, 12, 17** — Samson's seven-day wedding feast

### 3. Royal / Imperial Banquet (Esther, Daniel)
State banquets that display the king's wealth and serve as the stage for political drama.
- **Esth 1:3, 5** — Ahasuerus's 180-day feast for his princes, then 7-day feast for all Shushan
- **Esth 5:4–6; 7:2, 7–8** — Esther's sequence of banquets trapping Haman
- **Dan 1:5** — "a daily provision of the king's meat, and of the wine which he drank (mishteh)"

### 4. Drinking-Session / "Banquet of Wine"
The specialized idiom מִשְׁתֵּה הַיַּיִן ("banquet of wine") — a session whose defining feature is the wine itself.
- **Esth 5:6; 7:2, 7, 8** — "banquet of wine" (four times, all in the Esther/Haman climax)
- **Isa 5:12** — "the harp, and the viol... and wine, are in their feasts (mishteh)"
- **Jer 51:39** — "I will make their feasts, and I will make them drunken"

### 5. Feasting as Ethical Contrast (Wisdom Literature)
In Job, Proverbs, and Ecclesiastes mishteh functions as a moral foil — either a cause of worry or a lesser good than sober reflection.
- **Job 1:4–5** — the sons' rotating feasts that prompt Job's sin-offerings
- **Prov 15:15** — "he that is of a merry heart hath a continual feast"
- **Eccl 7:2** — "better to go to the house of mourning, than to go to the house of feasting"

### 6. Eschatological Feast of YHWH
One distinctive prophetic re-use: YHWH himself hosts a mishteh for all peoples.
- **Isa 25:6** — "In this mountain shall the LORD of hosts make unto all people a feast (mishteh) of fat things, a feast of wines on the lees"
