# WG: Confess / Profess / Deny

## Overview
The confession concept in the NT involves words spanning the act of publicly declaring (or refusing to declare) one's convictions, sins, or allegiance. The group includes the core confession verb and its cognates, the intensified compound form, the antonym pair for denial, and the belief verb that confession externalizes.

### Semantic Contrast Pairs
- **homologeō ↔ arneomai** — The primary confess/deny axis. John 1:20 uses both in a single sentence: "he confessed, and denied not." The Titus 1:16 indictment reverses them: "they profess... but in works they deny."
- **pisteuō ↔ homologeō** — Internal conviction vs. external declaration. Rom 10:9–10 distinguishes them precisely: "with the heart man believeth... with the mouth confession is made."
- **homologeō ↔ exomologeō** — Both translate "confess" but exomologeō dominates in LXX praise/thanksgiving contexts (rendering Hebrew yadah), while homologeō dominates in NT christological and soteriological confession.

## Roles in the Concept

- **Internal conviction:** pisteuō (G4100) — believing in the heart
- **External declaration:** homologeō (G3670) — confessing with the mouth
- **Intensified declaration:** exomologeō (G1843) — full acknowledgment, praise
- **The declaration itself:** homologia (G3671) — the confession/profession as a noun
- **Refusal to declare:** arneomai (G720) — denial, disowning
- **Emphatic refusal:** aparneomai (G533) — utter denial (Peter's denial of Christ)
