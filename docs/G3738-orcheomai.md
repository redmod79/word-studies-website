## Semantic Categories

### 1. Responsive Joy — Expected but Refused (Parable)
- **Mat 11:17** — "We have piped unto you, and ye have not danced; we have mourned unto you, and ye have not lamented."
- **Luk 7:32** — "They are like unto children sitting in the marketplace, and calling one to another, and saying, We have piped unto you, and ye have not danced; we have mourned unto you, and ye have not wept."

Dancing here represents the joyful response that should follow joyful news. Paired antithetically with mourning/lamenting (θρηνέω G2354). The parable criticizes those who refuse to respond appropriately to either John's ascetic ministry or Jesus's celebratory one.

### 2. Performance Before Royalty — Seductive Display
- **Mat 14:6** — "But when Herod's birthday was kept, the daughter of Herodias danced before them, and pleased Herod."
- **Mar 6:22** — "And when the daughter of the said Herodias came in, and danced, and pleased Herod and them that sat with him..."

Dance as a courtly performance that "pleased" (ἀρέσκω G700) Herod — leading directly to the rash oath and John the Baptist's execution. The collocations with θυγάτηρ (daughter), Ἡρώδης (Herod), Ἡρωδιάς (Herodias), and ἀρέσκω (to please) mark this as a distinct, darker context.

## Hebrew Source (via LXX)

| Hebrew Source | Strong's | Co-occurrences | PMI | Notes |

| רָקַד (raqad) | H7540 | 3 | 10.30 | Primary: to stamp, spring about, leap for joy; 9v OT |
| שָׂחַק (sachaq) | H7832 | 3 | 8.30 | Secondary: to laugh, play, make merry; 36v OT |

The LXX uses ὀρχέομαι to render רָקַד (raqad) in 2 Sam 6:21 and 1 Chr 15:29, both describing David's dance before the ark. The Hebrew raqad emphasizes vigorous, leaping movement — "properly, to stamp, to spring about wildly or for joy" — while orcheomai carries the sense of ordered, circular movement. The LXX also uses orcheomai for שָׂחַק (sachaq) in 1 Chr 15:29, where sachaq means "to play/make merry" more broadly.
