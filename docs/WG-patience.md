# WG: Patience, Endurance, Perseverance

## Overview
The NT patience concept clusters around five words: the noun ὑπομονή (hupomone) and its cognate verb ὑπομένω (hupomeno) for active bearing-under-pressure; μακροθυμία (makrothymia) for longanimity toward persons; and the situational pair of ἐλπίς (elpis, hope) and θλῖψις (thlipsis, tribulation) that define the field patience operates in.

## Roles in the Concept

- **Active endurance:** ὑπομονή / ὑπομένω (G5281/G5278) — bearing up *under circumstances*; the image is structural (staying beneath a load)
- **Longanimity:** μακροθυμία (G3115) — bearing with *persons*; slow to avenge, not retaliating; fruit of the Spirit (Gal 5:22)
- **Context:** θλῖψις (G2347) — the tribulation/pressure that occasions patience; without it the category has no reference
- **Goal:** ἐλπίς (G1680) — hope anchors endurance forward; patience without hope is mere stoicism

## Semantic Contrast: hupomone vs. makrothymia

Both are rendered "patience" or "longsuffering" in KJV and often appear together, but they differ:

| Feature | ὑπομονή (G5281) | μακροθυμία (G3115) |
|---------|-----------------|---------------------|
| Orientation | Toward *circumstances* | Toward *persons* |
| Image | Standing firm under a weight | A long fuse; slow to boil |
| Usage context | Tribulation, the race, keeping commandments | Forbearance with people; divine patience with sinners |
| LN domain | 025.015 | 025.015 |
| Fruit of Spirit? | No | Yes (Gal 5:22) |
