## Lexicon Definition

From μιαίνω (G3392, "to defile"); (morally) foulness — properly, the effect or result of defilement. Strong's defines it as "(morally) foulness (properly, the effect):—pollution." Thayer adds: "that which defiles; defilement; metaphorically, vices the foulness of which contaminates one in his contact with the ungodly mass of mankind" (2 Pet 2:20).

The word denotes the pollution-substance itself — the contaminating residue left by moral defilement — as distinct from the act of defiling (μιασμός, G3394) or the verb of contamination (μιαίνω, G3392).

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Moral Pollution from Worldly Contact
- **2 Pet 2:20** — "For if after they have escaped the pollutions (μιάσματα) of the world through the knowledge of the Lord and Saviour Jesus Christ, they are again entangled therein, and overcome, the latter end is worse with them than the beginning."

This is the sole NT occurrence. The plural μιάσματα denotes the accumulated moral contaminations that characterize the world (κόσμος, G2889). The context describes persons who had genuinely escaped these pollutions through ἐπίγνωσις (G1922, "full knowledge") of Christ, then returned to them. The grammatical structure is significant:
- ἀποφυγόντες (aorist active participle of ἀποφεύγω, G668) — a decisive, completed escape
- ἐμπλακέντες (aorist passive participle of ἐμπλέκω, G1707) — becoming entangled again
- ἡττῶνται (present passive indicative of ἡσσάομαι, G2274) — being overcome, a continuing state of defeat

The "pollutions" are thus the defiling substance of the world's moral corruption — not the act of sinning itself (that would be μιασμός, G3394) but the contaminating residue that clings to those who participate in worldly vice.

### LXX Background
The lexicon's outline of biblical usage notes Lev 7:8 and Jer 32:34 as LXX references. In the LXX, μίασμα carries the same sense of defiling substance — the contamination itself, particularly in contexts of ritual and idolatrous pollution. This links the NT usage to the Levitical purity system, where contact with contaminating substances rendered a person unfit for sacred participation.

### Word Family / Cognates
- **G3392 μιαίνω (miaínō)** — root verb: to sully, taint, contaminate (4v NT). The parent verb from which μίασμα derives.
