# H6635 — צָבָא (tsâbâʼ)

## Core Data
- **Strong's:** H6635
- **Original:** צָבָא (also feminine צְבָאָה tsᵉbâʼâh)
- **Transliteration:** tsâbâʼ
- **Pronunciation:** tsaw-baw'
- **Part of Speech:** Masculine noun
- **Root:** from the primitive root צָבָא (to mass for war, to serve, esp. in worship)
- **Occurrences:** 492 tokens in 462 unique OT verses, across 27 books
- **First Occurrence:** Gen 2:1 — "Thus the heavens and the earth were finished, and all the host (ṣāḇāʾ) of them."
- **Hapax Legomenon:** No

## Lexicon Definition

From the primitive root צָבָא; a mass of persons (or figuratively, things), especially regularly organized for war (an army); by implication, a campaign, literally or figuratively (specifically, hardship, worship): — appointed time, (+) army, (+) battle, company, host, service, soldiers, waiting upon, war(-fare).

The word covers four overlapping spheres of reference: (1) a military mass (army / troop / warfare), (2) the organized "host" of heavenly bodies and celestial beings, (3) the ordered service of Levites at the sanctuary, and (4) by extension a fixed term of hard labor or appointed time. Across all uses the core idea is an *ordered, mustered, corporate body under a commander* — never random assembly.

**Pattern notes:** The word is massively over-represented in the post-exilic prophets (Malachi, Haggai, Zechariah) — driven almost entirely by the title *YHWH ṣəḇāʾôṯ* ("LORD of hosts"). Numbers concentrates the word around mustering and Levitical service. Jeremiah and Isaiah carry the divine-title and heavenly-host uses. Daniel's 5 occurrences are all narrow: four clustered in Dan 8:10–13, plus Dan 8:8 (kingdom context). Absent from most NT-typological books (Song, Ruth, Ecclesiastes, Lamentations, Esther, Proverbs, Ezekiel, Ezra, Jonah, Joel, Obadiah).

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Heavenly Host — Angels, Stars, and Cosmic Bodies
The organized celestial retinue, sometimes pictured as stars (inanimate) and sometimes as God's court/army (animate). Paired with שָׁמַיִם (*šāmayim*, heaven).
- **Gen 2:1** — "the heavens and the earth were finished, and all the host (ṣāḇāʾ) of them."
- **Deut 4:19** — "the sun, and the moon, and the stars, even all the host of heaven."
- **Neh 9:6** — "thou hast made heaven... with all their host... and the host of heaven worshippeth thee."
- **Isa 40:26** — "who hath created these things, that bringeth out their host by number: he calleth them all by names."
- **1 Ki 22:19 / 2 Chr 18:18** — "I saw the LORD sitting on his throne, and all the host of heaven standing by him on his right hand and on his left." (court/council)
- **Isa 34:4** — "all the host of heaven shall be dissolved... all their host shall fall down, as the leaf falleth off from the vine." (cosmic dissolution)

### 2. Illicit Worship of the Heavenly Host
A specific polemic subset: idolatrous veneration of sun, moon, stars, constellations.
- **Deut 4:19; 17:3** — prohibition against being "driven to worship" the host of heaven.
- **2 Ki 17:16** — "worshipped all the host of heaven, and served Baal."
- **Jer 19:13** — rooftop incense "unto all the host of heaven."
- **Zeph 1:5** — "them that worship the host of heaven upon the housetops."

### 3. Earthly Military Host — Army, Warfare, Battle
Organized human forces mustered for war; also the act/duration of warfare.
- **Num 31:14** — "the officers of the host (ṣāḇāʾ), the captains over thousands, and captains over hundreds, which came from the battle."
- **1 Sam 17:45** — "I come to thee in the name of the LORD of hosts, the God of the armies (ṣiḇʾôṯ) of Israel."
- **Josh 5:14–15** — "as captain of the host (ṣāḇāʾ) of the LORD am I now come." (the theophanic Commander)
- **Isa 13:4** — "the LORD of hosts mustereth the host of the battle."
- **2 Sam 2:8** — "Abner the son of Ner, captain of Saul's host."

### 4. Cultic / Levitical Service at the Tabernacle
A technical usage in Numbers where ṣāḇāʾ denotes ordered priestly/Levitical duty — the Levites "muster" for sanctuary service as soldiers muster for war.
- **Num 4:3** — "all that enter into the host (ṣāḇāʾ), to do the work in the tabernacle of the congregation."
- **Num 4:23** — "all that enter in to perform the service (liṣbōʾ ṣāḇāʾ), to do the work."
- **Num 4:30** — "every one that entereth into the service" (same construction).
- **Num 8:24** — "they shall go in to wait upon the service of the tabernacle."

### 5. Appointed Time / Hardship / Fixed Term of Labor
An extension from "term of military service" to any appointed period of hard duty, including human life.
- **Job 7:1** — "Is there not an appointed time (ṣāḇāʾ) to man upon earth?"
- **Job 14:14** — "all the days of my appointed time will I wait, till my change come."
- **Isa 40:2** — "her warfare (ṣəḇāʾāh) is accomplished" (Jerusalem's term of hardship fulfilled). [noted as cognate feminine צְבָאָה]
- **Dan 10:1** — "the time appointed was long" (same nuance, though form is contested).

### 6. Yhwh Ṣəḇāʾôṯ — "LORD of Hosts" (Divine Epithet)
The single most frequent construction (272 of 462 verses, 58.9%). Almost entirely in prophets + Samuel–Kings.
- **1 Sam 1:3** — "worship and to sacrifice unto the LORD of hosts in Shiloh." (first canonical use of the title)
- **Isa 1:9; 1:24; 6:3** — "Holy, holy, holy, is the LORD of hosts."
- **Zech 1:3; 4:6** — programmatic post-exilic usage.
- **Mal 1:10; 1:14; 3:1** — closing OT note.

### 7. The Host Associated with Saints / Sanctuary (Daniel 8 cluster)
Dan 8:10–13 uses ṣāḇāʾ four times in contested pairing with stars (v.10), with a "prince of the host" (v.11), with the daily sacrifice and "sanctuary" (v.12), and with "saints" in v.13 (qᵉḏōšîm). The term here bridges the heavenly-host and the covenant-community senses.
- **Dan 8:10** — "it waxed great, even to the host of heaven; and it cast down some of the host and of the stars to the ground."
- **Dan 8:11** — "he magnified himself even to the prince of the host (śar-haṣṣāḇāʾ)."
- **Dan 8:12** — "an host was given him against the daily sacrifice by reason of transgression."
- **Dan 8:13** — "to give both the sanctuary and the host to be trodden under foot."

### Domain Overview
The four Hebrew "host/army" terms divide the conceptual space roughly:
- **ṣāḇāʾ** — the organized, mustered body (+ its term of service, + heavenly parallel)
- **maḥăneh** — the camp as spatial arrangement
- **ḥayil** — force, valor, power, the resource
- **milḥāmāh** — the battle event itself

**Four bridge words matter for NT theology:**
1. *pantokrátōr* — carries the divine-warrior title into Revelation (9× in Rev: 1:8; 4:8; 11:17; etc.).
2. *sabaōth* — preserved as a loan-word twice (Rom 9:29; Jas 5:4).
3. *dýnamis* — links "host of heaven" to "powers of heaven" shaken at the end (Matt 24:29 || Mark 13:25 || Luke 21:26), and to the angelic/cosmic "powers" in Paul and 1 Pet 3:22.
4. *stratiá* — bridges to the "multitude of the heavenly host" in Luke 2:13 and to "the host of heaven" language in Acts 7:42.
