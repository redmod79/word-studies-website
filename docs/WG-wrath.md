## Roles in the Concept

- **Passionate eruption:** θυμός — the boiling-over fury, the flash of intense rage (from "breathing hard")
- **Settled disposition:** ὀργή — the abiding anger, judicial displeasure, the deliberate verdict
- **Provocation:** παροργίζω / παροργισμός — what triggers anger, being stirred to wrath
- **Action:** θυμόω — to put into a passion; ὀργίζω — to be in a state of anger
- **Extreme expression:** θυμομαχέω — to fight in a fury (thumos + machē)
- **Vice-list companions:** ἐριθεία (factional strife), κακία (malice) — the social sins that cluster with anger

## Semantic Distinction: θυμός vs. ὀργή

These two words appear together 6 times, always in a specific pattern:
- In **vice lists** (Eph 4:31, Col 3:8): listed as separate sins to mortify — thumos is the impulsive outburst, orgē is the harbored resentment
- In **divine judgment** (Rom 2:8, Rev 14:10, 16:19, 19:15): combined to convey both the fierce intensity (thumos) and the judicial authority (orgē) of God's eschatological wrath
- The KJV typically renders orgē as "anger" and thumos as "wrath" in vice lists, but reverses this in Revelation (orgē = "wrath," thumos = "fierceness")
