## Semantic Categories

### 1. Divine Wrath Poured Out (Judgment Formula)
The most frequent usage — God's fury as the active force behind judgment, typically with verbs of pouring (shaphak), accomplishing (kalah), or causing to rest (nuach).
- **Jer 7:20** — "mine anger and my fury shall be poured out upon this place"
- **Eze 7:8** — "Now will I shortly pour out my fury upon thee, and accomplish mine anger"
- **Eze 5:13** — "I will cause my fury to rest upon them, and I will be comforted"
- **Lam 4:11** — "The LORD hath accomplished his fury"

### 2. The Cup / Wine of Fury
A distinct metaphorical cluster where chemah is liquid poured into a cup and drunk by nations under judgment.
- **Isa 51:17** — "drunk at the hand of the LORD the cup of his fury"
- **Isa 51:22** — "I have taken out of thine hand the cup of trembling, even the dregs of the cup of my fury"
- **Jer 25:15** — "Take the wine cup of this fury at my hand, and cause all the nations... to drink it"
- **Isa 63:6** — "I will... make them drunk in my fury"

### 3. Burning Heat (Physical Metaphor)
The root meaning surfaces where chemah is paired with fire, burning, or whirlwind imagery — heat as both cause and consequence.
- **Deu 29:23** — "brimstone, and salt, and burning... his anger, and in his wrath (chemah)"
- **Isa 66:15** — "render his anger with fury (chemah), and his rebuke with flames of fire"
- **Nah 1:6** — "his fury is poured out like fire"
- **Jer 4:4** — "lest my fury come forth like fire"

### 4. Human Rage (Royal, Wisdom, Interpersonal)
Chemah applied to human subjects — often kings or angry men, with the connotation of violent or dangerous temper.
- **Gen 27:44** — Esau's fury toward Jacob
- **Est 1:12** — "the king was very wroth, and his anger burned in him (chemah)"
- **Pro 16:14** — "The wrath (chemah) of a king is as messengers of death"
- **Pro 27:4** — "Wrath (chemah) is cruel, and anger is outrageous"

### 5. Poison / Venom
A specialized figurative use where the "heat" of chemah is applied to serpent venom or poisoned weapons.
- **Deu 32:24** — "I will also send the teeth of beasts upon them, with the poison (chemah) of serpents"
- **Deu 32:33** — "Their wine is the poison (chemah) of dragons"
- **Job 6:4** — "the arrows of the Almighty... the poison (chemah) whereof drinketh up my spirit"
- **Psa 58:4** — "Their poison (chemah) is like the poison of a serpent"

### 6. Wrath Appeased / Turned Away
Chemah as the object of relenting — turned away, pacified, made to rest.
- **Num 25:11** — "hath turned my wrath (chemah) away from the children of Israel"
- **Pro 15:1** — "A soft answer turneth away wrath (chemah)"
- **Pro 21:14** — "a reward in the bosom strong wrath (chemah)"
- **Eze 16:42** — "So will I make my fury toward thee to rest"

### 7. Stacked Intensifier with Other Wrath Words
Chemah appears beside aph (H639) or charon (H2740) in a high-frequency doublet pattern, especially in prophetic judgment oracles.
- **Deu 9:19** — "the anger (aph) and hot displeasure (chemah)"
- **Jer 21:5** — "in anger (aph), and in fury (chemah), and in great wrath"
- **Isa 42:25** — "the fury (chemah) of his anger (aph)"
- **Mic 5:15** — "vengeance in anger (aph) and fury (chemah)"
