## Lexicon Definition

A primitive root; to slaughter (in sacrifice or massacre) — kill, offer, shoot out, slay, slaughter.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Sacrificial Slaughter — Levitical Offerings
The dominant usage. The offerer (or priest) slaughters the animal as the prescribed first act after laying hands on its head.
- **Lev 1:5** — "And he shall kill (shachat) the bullock before the LORD"
- **Lev 4:24** — "And he shall lay his hand upon the head of the goat, and kill (shachat) it in the place where they kill the burnt offering"
- **Lev 16:15** — "Then shall he kill (shachat) the goat of the sin offering, that is for the people"
- **Lev 9:12** — "And he slew (shachat) the burnt offering; and Aaron's sons presented unto him the blood"

### 2. Passover Slaughter
Specifically the killing of the Passover lamb, often by Levites in later periods.
- **Exo 12:6** — "the whole assembly of the congregation of Israel shall kill (shachat) it in the evening"
- **Exo 12:21** — "Draw out and take you a lamb... and kill (shachat) the passover"
- **2 Chr 30:15** — "Then they killed (shachat) the passover on the fourteenth day"
- **2 Chr 35:11** — "And they killed (shachat) the passover, and the priests sprinkled the blood"

### 3. Purification Ritual Slaughter
Specific to the cleansing rituals of Leviticus 14 (leper cleansing) and Numbers 19 (red heifer).
- **Lev 14:5** — "the priest shall command that one of the birds be killed (shachat) in an earthen vessel over running water"
- **Lev 14:25** — "And he shall kill (shachat) the lamb of the trespass offering"
- **Num 19:3** — "one shall slay (shachat) her before his face"

### 4. Non-Cultic Killing of Animals
Slaughter of animals for food or other non-sacrificial purposes.
- **Gen 37:31** — "they... killed (shachat) a kid of the goats, and dipped the coat in the blood"
- **1 Sam 14:32** — "the people... took sheep, and oxen, and calves, and slew (shachat) them on the ground"
- **1 Sam 14:34** — "Bring me hither every man his ox... and slay (shachat) them here"
- **Isa 22:13** — "slaying (shachat) oxen, and killing sheep, eating flesh"

### 5. Killing of Persons — Judgment and Violence
Extended from the slaughter vocabulary to human killing, always violent and often tied to conquest or punishment.
- **Gen 22:10** — "Abraham stretched forth his hand, and took the knife to slay (shachat) his son"
- **Jdg 12:6** — "they... slew (shachat) him at the passages of Jordan"
- **2 Ki 25:7** — "they slew (shachat) the sons of Zedekiah before his eyes"
- **Jer 39:6** — "the king of Babylon slew (shachat) the sons of Zedekiah in Riblah"

### 6. Idolatrous Sacrifice — Child Sacrifice
Prophetic condemnation of child sacrifice to idols, using the sacrificial vocabulary to heighten the horror.
- **Eze 16:21** — "That thou hast slain (shachat) my children, and delivered them to cause them to pass through the fire"
- **Eze 23:39** — "For when they had slain (shachat) their children to their idols"
- **Isa 57:5** — "slaying (shachat) the children in the valleys under the cliffs of the rocks"

### 7. Metaphorical / Deceptive "Slaughter"
- **Jer 9:8** — "Their tongue is as an arrow shot out (shachat); it speaketh deceit" (the tongue as a slaughtering weapon)
- **Hos 5:2** — "the revolters are profound to make slaughter (shachat)" (nominal form — deep in apostasy)
