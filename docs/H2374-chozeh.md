## Semantic Categories

### 1. Royal/Court Seer — Personal Visionary Advisor to the King
- **2 Sam 24:11** — "the word of the LORD came unto the prophet Gad, David's seer"
- **1 Chr 21:9** — "the LORD spake unto Gad, David's seer"
- **1 Chr 25:5** — "the sons of Heman the king's seer in the words of God"
- **2 Chr 29:25** — "according to the commandment of David, and of Gad the king's seer, and Nathan the prophet"

The chozeh functions as an officially recognized visionary attached to the royal court — paired with but distinguished from the nabiy (prophet). Gad is the paradigmatic example, identified as "David's seer" in multiple passages.

### 2. Recorder of Prophetic History
- **1 Chr 29:29** — "written in the book of Samuel the seer, and in the book of Nathan the prophet, and in the book of Gad the seer"
- **2 Chr 9:29** — "in the visions of Iddo the seer against Jeroboam"
- **2 Chr 12:15** — "written in the book of Shemaiah the prophet, and of Iddo the seer"
- **2 Chr 33:18** — "the words of the seers that spake to him in the name of the LORD"

Chronicles cites seers as authoritative historiographers whose written records document the acts of kings.

### 3. False or Silenced Seers — Negative Usage
- **Isa 29:10** — "the LORD hath poured out upon you the spirit of deep sleep, and hath closed your eyes: the prophets and your rulers, the seers hath he covered"
- **Isa 30:10** — "Which say to the seers, See not; and to the prophets, Prophesy not unto us right things"
- **Eze 13:9** — "mine hand shall be upon the prophets that see vanity"
- **Mic 3:7** — "then shall the seers be ashamed, and the diviners confounded"

These passages use chozeh for those who claim visionary status but prophesy falsely, or whose vision is divinely shut down as judgment.

### 4. Compact / Agreement (Non-Visionary Sense)
- **Isa 28:15** — "with hell are we at agreement (chozeh)"

Unique usage: the word denotes a compact or agreement — something "looked upon with approval." This is the only occurrence of this semantic sense.

### 5. Stargazer (Babylonian Astrologer)
- **Isa 47:13** — "the astrologers, the stargazers (chozeh), the monthly prognosticators"

Applied pejoratively to Babylonian sky-watchers. The compound "stargazers" (chozeh + kokab) extends the "gazer/beholder" root meaning to celestial observation.

### Synonyms
- **H5030 נָבִיא (nabiy)** — prophet, inspired man (316 occurrences). The dominant term for prophet. Nabiy emphasizes the role of speaking forth God's message; chozeh emphasizes the mode of reception through visionary seeing.
- **H7203 רֹאֶה (roeh)** — seer (from H7200 raah, "to see"). An older term for seer; 1 Sam 9:9 explicitly notes "he that is now called a Prophet (nabiy) was beforetime called a Seer (roeh)." Roeh derives from the common verb "to see" (raah), while chozeh derives from the more specialized "to gaze/perceive in vision" (chazah).
