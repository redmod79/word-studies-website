## Lexicon Definition

From γεννάω (G1080, "to beget, bring forth"). Meaning: **offspring**; by analogy, **produce** (literally or figuratively) — **that which has been born or produced**. Neuter of the verbal form, it designates the *product* of generation: either biological offspring (idiomatic in "brood/generation of vipers") or the yield/produce of the earth or a plant. The LXX uses it for agricultural produce (e.g., Gen 41:34; Ezek 36:30; Hos 10:12; Lev 25:3), paralleling Hebrew תְּבוּאָה (tevua), פְּרִי (peri), יְבוּל (yevul). It differs from καρπός (G2590, karpos, "fruit as plucked") in emphasizing the *thing generated/born* rather than the plucked fruit itself.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Offspring / Brood (idiom: "brood of vipers")
- **Matt 3:7** — "O generation (génnēma) of vipers, who hath warned you to flee from the wrath to come?" (John the Baptist)
- **Matt 12:34** — "O generation (génnēma) of vipers, how can ye, being evil, speak good things?" (Jesus)
- **Matt 23:33** — "Ye serpents, ye generation (génnēma) of vipers, how can ye escape the damnation of hell?" (Jesus)
- **Luke 3:7** — parallel to Matt 3:7 (John the Baptist)

Always paired with ἔχιδνα (G2191, echidna, "viper") as genitive of origin — "that which has been produced by vipers."

### 2. Fruit / Produce of the Vine (Last Supper)
- **Matt 26:29** — "I will not drink henceforth of this fruit (génnēma) of the vine (ámpelos), until that day when I drink it new with you in my Father's kingdom."
- **Mark 14:25** — "I will drink no more of the fruit (génnēma) of the vine, until that day that I drink it new in the kingdom of God."
- **Luke 22:18** — "For I say unto you, I will not drink of the fruit (génnēma) of the vine, until the kingdom of God shall come."

All three Synoptic evangelists use the identical construction τοῦ γενήματος τῆς ἀμπέλου ("the produce of the vine") — a traditional Jewish liturgical phrase (cf. the Passover berakhah *borei peri ha-gafen*, "who creates the fruit of the vine"). Notably, none of the Synoptics uses οἶνος (G3631, oinos, "wine") in Jesus' cup-saying itself.

### 3. Agricultural Produce / Yield
- **Luke 12:18** — "there will I bestow all my fruits (génnēma) and my goods" (rich fool parable — harvest stored in barns)
- **2 Cor 9:10** — "multiply your seed sown, and increase the fruits (génnēma) of your righteousness"

Produce as the natural yield of sowing/growing — echoes LXX usage (Gen 41:34; Lev 25:3; Ezek 36:30; Hos 10:12), where génnēma renders Hebrew תְּבוּאָה (tevua, "yield/increase") and related agricultural terms.

The LXX usage centers on **agricultural yield**, reinforcing that génnēma in the Last Supper saying points to the vine's *produce* — the grape juice/liquid it generates — rather than specifically denoting a fermented or finished product (which would be oinos).

## Synoptic Uniformity Note

A distinctive feature: in the Last Supper cup-saying, all three Synoptic evangelists (Matt 26:29, Mark 14:25, Luke 22:18) independently preserve τοῦ γενήματος τῆς ἀμπέλου rather than the more common οἶνος (oinos, G3631). This matches the wording of the Jewish Passover berakhah over the cup (*borei peri ha-gafen*, "creator of the fruit of the vine"), suggesting a preserved liturgical formula. The terminology describes the substance by its botanical origin (the vine's generative produce) rather than by its fermentation state. For comparison of the semantic range of what "fruit of the vine" could denote in first-century Jewish usage, see WG-wine.md and TR-yayin-oinos.md.
