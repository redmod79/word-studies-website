## Semantic Categories

### 1. Divine Abhorrence — God's Moral Revulsion
God as subject, expressing active rejection of sin, sinners, or his own people in judgment.
- **Psa 5:6** — "Thou shalt destroy them that speak leasing: the LORD will abhor the bloody and deceitful man"
- **Psa 106:40** — "Therefore was the wrath of the LORD kindled against his people, insomuch that he abhorred his own inheritance"
- **Deu 7:26** — "thou shalt utterly detest it, and thou shalt utterly abhor it; for it is a cursed thing" (command to Israel to mirror God's own revulsion toward idolatrous objects)

### 2. Interpersonal Abhorrence — Social Rejection and Contempt
Humans abhorring other humans; the experience of being socially despised.
- **Job 19:19** — "All my inward friends abhorred me: and they whom I loved are turned against me"
- **Job 30:10** — "They abhor me, they flee far from me, and spare not to spit in my face"
- **Job 9:31** — "Yet shalt thou plunge me in the ditch, and mine own clothes shall abhor me"
- **Isa 49:7** — "Thus saith the LORD... to him whom the nation abhorreth, to a servant of rulers"

### 3. Prophetic Indictment — Leaders Who Abhor Justice
Prophets condemning rulers who reject righteousness.
- **Mic 3:9** — "ye heads of the house of Jacob, and princes of the house of Israel, that abhor judgment, and pervert all equity"
- **Amo 5:10** — "They hate him that rebuketh in the gate, and they abhor him that speaketh uprightly"

### 4. Doing Abominably — Causative (Hiphil) Action
Making oneself or one's actions abominable through idolatry or moral corruption.
- **1Ki 21:26** — "And he [Ahab] did very abominably in following idols, according to all things as did the Amorites"
- **Psa 14:1** — "The fool hath said in his heart, There is no God. They are corrupt, they have done abominable works"
- **Psa 53:1** — "Corrupt are they, and have done abominable iniquity: there is none that doeth good"
- **Eze 16:52** — "thou hast committed more abominable than they"

### 5. State of Being Abhorred — Passive (Niphal)
The condition of being loathed or made loathsome.
- **Isa 14:19** — "But thou art cast out of thy grave like an abominable branch" (king of Babylon)
- **Job 15:16** — "How much more abominable and filthy is man, which drinketh iniquity like water?"
- **1Ch 21:6** — "the king's word was abominable to Joab"

### 6. Commanded Non-Abhorrence — Restraint of National Contempt
The one exceptional use: prohibition against despising a kinsman-nation.
- **Deu 23:7** — "Thou shalt not abhor an Edomite; for he is thy brother: thou shalt not abhor an Egyptian; because thou wast a stranger in his land"

### 7. Righteous Abhorrence of Falsehood
The psalmist's self-directed loathing of sin.
- **Psa 119:163** — "I hate and abhor lying: but thy law do I love"
