## Lexicon Definition

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Interpreting / Explaining Spiritual Things
- **1 Cor 2:13** — "Which things also we speak, not in the words which man's wisdom teacheth, but which the Holy Ghost teacheth; comparing spiritual things with spiritual."

The participle πνευματικοῖς πνευματικὰ συγκρίνοντες can be read as "interpreting spiritual things by spiritual [means/words]" or "combining spiritual things with spiritual [things]." The LXX background strongly favors the interpretive sense (see Hebrew Source below).

### 2. Comparing / Measuring Against
- **2 Cor 10:12** — "For we dare not make ourselves of the number, or compare ourselves with some that commend themselves: but they measuring themselves by themselves, and comparing themselves among themselves, are not wise."

Here the word clearly means "compare" — self-measuring against a wrong standard. The aorist infinitive (συνκρῖναι) denotes the act of comparison, while the present participle (συνκρίνοντες) describes the ongoing activity.

### LXX Dream-Interpretation Connection

The LXX uses συγκρίνω as the standard rendering of H6622 פָּתַר ("interpret") in the Joseph dream narratives:

- **Gen 40:8** (LXX) — "there is no interpreter (ὁ συγκρίνων) of it" — Joseph offers to interpret the dreams
- **Gen 40:16** — "the interpretation (ἡ σύγκρισις) was good" — the chief baker sees the favorable reading
- **Gen 40:22** — "as Joseph had interpreted (συνέκρινεν) to them"
- **Gen 41:12** — "he interpreted (συνέκρινεν) to us our dreams"
- **Gen 41:15** — "thou canst understand a dream to interpret (συγκρῖναι) it"
- **Judg 7:15** — Gideon hears "the interpretation (τὴν σύγκρισιν)" of the barley-cake dream
- **Dan 5:12** — Daniel described as one skilled in "interpreting (συγκρίνων) dreams"

This LXX usage reveals the word's core logic: to interpret is to *place things side by side and judge their correspondence* — comparing the dream-symbol with the reality it signifies. The interpreter discerns meaning by matching one thing to another.

Paul's usage in 1 Cor 2:13 (πνευματικοῖς πνευματικὰ συγκρίνοντες) draws on this LXX semantic field: the Spirit-taught interpreter explains spiritual realities by placing spiritual things alongside spiritual things — interpreting scripture by scripture, revelation by revelation, rather than by human wisdom.
