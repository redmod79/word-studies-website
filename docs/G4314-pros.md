## Lexicon Definition

A strengthened form of G4253; a preposition of direction; forward to, i.e. toward (with the genitive case, the side of, i.e. pertaining to; with the dative case, by the side of, i.e. near to; usually with the accusative case, the place, time, occasion, or respect, which is the destination of the relation, i.e. whither or for which it is predicated): — about, according to, against, among, at, because of, before, between, (where-)by, for, at thy house, in, for intent, nigh unto, of, which pertain to, that, to (the end that), together, to (you)-ward, unto, with(-in). In the comparative case, it denotes essentially the same applications, namely, motion towards, accession to, or nearness at.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Direction / Movement Toward (Physical or Relational)
The primary sense — motion toward a person, place, or entity.
- **Mat 3:13** — "Then cometh Jesus from Galilee to Jordan unto John, to be baptized of him."
- **Jhn 6:35** — "he that cometh to me shall never hunger"
- **Act 9:32** — "he came down also to the saints which dwelt at Lydda"
- **Rev 10:9** — "And I went unto the angel, and said unto him, Give me the little book."

### 2. Communication / Speaking To
Introduces the addressee of speech — the most frequent NT pattern by raw count.
- **Mat 3:15** — "And Jesus answering said unto him, Suffer it to be so now"
- **Luk 1:13** — "But the angel said unto him, Fear not, Zacharias"
- **Act 2:38** — "Then Peter said unto them, Repent, and be baptized"
- **Heb 1:8** — "But unto the Son he saith, Thy throne, O God, is for ever and ever"

### 3. Presence / Face-to-Face Proximity
Being "with" or "in the presence of" someone — the accusative often carries this nuance.
- **Jhn 1:1** — "the Word was with God" (ὁ λόγος ἦν πρὸς τὸν θεόν)
- **Jhn 1:2** — "The same was in the beginning with God"
- **1Th 3:4** — "when we were with you, we told you before"
- **2Co 5:8** — "willing rather to be absent from the body, and to be present with the Lord"

### 4. Opposition / Against
Directional force turned hostile — "toward" as "against."
- **Act 6:1** — "there arose a murmuring of the Grecians against the Hebrews"
- **Eph 6:11** — "stand against the wiles of the devil"
- **Eph 6:12** — "we wrestle not against flesh and blood, but against principalities"
- **Col 3:13** — "if any man have a quarrel against any"

### 5. Reciprocal Interaction
"Among themselves," "one to another" — πρός marks mutual or internal discourse.
- **Mar 4:41** — "said one to another, What manner of man is this"
- **Mar 8:16** — "they reasoned among themselves"
- **Luk 2:15** — "the shepherds said one to another, Let us now go even unto Bethlehem"
- **Jhn 6:52** — "The Jews therefore strove among themselves"

### 6. Purpose / Reference / Pertaining To
