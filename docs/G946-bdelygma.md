## Lexicon Definition

From βδελύσσομαι (bdelyssomai, G948): a detestation; specifically, an idol or idolatrous object; by extension, anything morally or religiously loathsome — "abomination." In the Septuagint it renders Hebrew תּוֹעֵבָה (to'ebah) and שִׁקּוּץ (shiqquts), the standard OT terms for what is ritually or morally abhorrent to God, especially idolatry and associated pagan practice.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. The Eschatological "Abomination of Desolation"
Technical phrase `βδέλυγμα τῆς ἐρημώσεως` drawn from Daniel (LXX Dan 9:27; 11:31; 12:11), pointing to a defiling presence in the holy place that triggers desolation.
- **Mat 24:15** — "When ye therefore shall see the abomination of desolation, spoken of by Daniel the prophet, stand in the holy place"
- **Mark 13:14** — "But when ye shall see the abomination of desolation... standing where it ought not"

### 2. Idolatrous / Defiling Contents of Babylon's Cup
βδέλυγμα as the cumulative output of the harlot-system: idolatry, fornication (spiritual and literal), and moral filth concentrated in a single vessel.
- **Rev 17:4** — "having a golden cup in her hand full of abominations and filthiness of her fornication"
- **Rev 17:5** — "MYSTERY, BABYLON THE GREAT, THE MOTHER OF HARLOTS AND ABOMINATIONS OF THE EARTH"

### 3. What God Loathes vs. What Men Esteem
Ethical reversal — something humans exalt can be βδέλυγμα before God.
- **Luke 16:15** — "that which is highly esteemed among men is abomination in the sight of God"

### 4. Exclusion from the New Jerusalem
Final purity boundary: no βδέλυγμα enters the eschatological city.
- **Rev 21:27** — "there shall in no wise enter into it any thing that defileth, neither whatsoever worketh abomination"
