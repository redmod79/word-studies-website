**Semantic continuity in translation:** The LXX largely preserves both senses of H4390 — physical filling and temporal/prophetic fulfillment — under a single Greek word (πληρόω). This sets up the NT usage where plēróō carries both: "filled with the Spirit" (from divine-glory-filling tradition) and "that the scripture might be fulfilled" (from prophetic-time-filling tradition).

## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same across testaments:**
- The verb carries both "fill a container" (physical) and "bring to intended completion" (purposive) in both OT and NT
- The divine-glory-filling pattern (tabernacle / temple / earth) continues in Paul's "fill all things" theology
- Temporal completion ("days being full") is consistent from OT (Jer 29:10) through NT (Mark 1:15; Acts 7:23)

**What shifts:**
- In the OT, the physical sense (fill nets, fill hands, fill pots) is common; in the NT this nearly disappears in favor of the purposive and prophetic senses
- The NT develops a new dominant use: the fulfillment formula (πληρωθῇ ἵνα) applied to specific OT texts — this is NT-specific and has no exact OT parallel
- Paul expands the Shekinah-glory theme cosmically: Christ does not merely fill the temple but "fills all things" (Eph 4:10; 1:23), applying H4390's eschatological promise (Isa 11:9) to the ascended Christ
