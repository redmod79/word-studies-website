**Semantic narrowing in translation:** Hebrew chashab carries the full range from literal weaving to mental calculation to theological imputation. The LXX splits this: logizomai takes the cognitive-evaluative and imputation senses; byssos/kokkinos/porphyra take the textile-craft senses. The NT inherits only the narrowed logizomai range — reckoning, imputing, and thinking — with no trace of the weaving/craftsmanship meaning.

## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same across testaments:**
- The core accounting metaphor: placing something to a person's ledger (credit or debit). Gen 15:6 establishes the pattern; Paul inherits it intact through the LXX.
- The divine passive: God is the one who "reckons" — the human is the recipient. This holds in both testaments.
- The non-imputation concept: not charging sin to a person's account (Psa 32:2 → Rom 4:8; 2 Cor 5:19).

**What shifts:**
- OT chashab has a wider semantic range including craftsmanship/weaving, devising evil plans, and general mental activity. The NT logizomai drops the craft sense entirely.
- The NT concentrates the word in the faith-righteousness argument (30% of all NT uses cluster in Rom 4), elevating the imputation-of-righteousness meaning to theological prominence it does not hold as strongly in the OT, where the Gen 15:6 formula appears only once.
- Paul introduces the imperative self-reckoning ("reckon yourselves dead to sin" — Rom 6:11), which has no direct OT precedent with chashab.
