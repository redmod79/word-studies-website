## Lexicon Definition

Three semantically distinct senses distinguished in BDB:
1. **Iniquity** — the wrongful act itself; moral perversity or crookedness
2. **Guilt** of iniquity — the culpable state resulting from the act
3. **Punishment / consequence** of iniquity — the penalty borne by the offender

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Moral Iniquity — the Act of Perversity
The dominant sense: the wrongful deed itself, emphasizing moral crookedness or perversity rather than legal status.

- **Gen 15:16** — "the iniquity of the Amorites is not yet full" (iniquity accumulates toward judgment)
- **Isa 1:4** — "a people laden with iniquity, a seed of evildoers"
- **Hosea 13:12** — "The iniquity of Ephraim is bound up; his sin is hid"
- **Jer 2:22** — "thine iniquity is marked before me, saith the Lord GOD"

### 2. Guilt of Iniquity — the Culpable State
The standing or condition of being guilty; the weight of iniquity resting on a person or community.

- **Gen 44:16** — "God hath found out the iniquity of thy servants"
- **Num 5:31** — "this woman shall bear her iniquity"
- **Ps 51:5** — "Behold, I was shapen in iniquity; and in sin did my mother conceive me"
- **Ps 38:4** — "mine iniquities are gone over mine head; as a heavy burden they are too heavy for me"

### 3. Punishment / Consequence of Iniquity
BDB identifies a distinct sense where עָוֺן = the penalty borne, not the act itself (cf. Gen 4:13).

- **Gen 4:13** — "My punishment [is] greater than I can bear" (Cain, after the murder)
- **Isa 40:2** — "her iniquity is pardoned: for she hath received of the LORD'S hand double" (punishment served)
- **Jer 31:30** — "every one shall die for his own iniquity"
- **Ezek 18:20** — "The son shall not bear the iniquity of the father" (individual accountability)

### 4. Bearing / Carrying Iniquity — Priestly and Vicarious Contexts
The formula נָשָׂא עָוֺן (*nâsâʼ ʻâvôn*, "bear iniquity") appears 37× — used of priests bearing communal guilt, of the scapegoat ritual, and of the Suffering Servant.

- **Lev 16:21** — Aaron confesses "all the iniquities of the children of Israel" over the scapegoat
- **Ezek 4:4–5** — Ezekiel lies on his side to "bear the iniquity of the house of Israel"
- **Isa 53:6** — "the LORD hath laid on him the iniquity of us all"
- **Isa 53:11** — "he shall bear their iniquities"

### 5. Confession, Forgiveness, and Removal of Iniquity
Penitential and prophetic passages where עָוֺן is the object of God's forgiveness, covering, or atonement.

- **Exo 34:7** — "forgiving iniquity and transgression and sin"
- **Ps 32:5** — "thou forgavest the iniquity of my sin"
- **Ps 103:3** — "Who forgiveth all thine iniquities; who healeth all thy diseases"
- **Dan 9:24** — "to make reconciliation for iniquity, and to bring in everlasting righteousness"
