## Semantic Categories

### 1. The Realm of the Dead — Universal Destination
Sheol receives all who die; described as below the earth and contrasted with heaven above.

- **Gen 37:35** — "I will go down into the grave unto my son mourning"
- **Ps 89:48** — "What man is he that liveth, and shall not see death? shall he deliver his soul from the hand of the grave?"
- **Eccl 9:10** — "there is no work, nor device, nor knowledge, nor wisdom, in the grave, whither thou goest"
- **Ps 141:7** — "Our bones are scattered at the grave's mouth, as when one cutteth and cleaveth wood upon the earth"

### 2. Descent — The Directional Language of Sheol
Sheol is invariably below; people "go down" (yārad, 26x) into it. The spatial metaphor is consistent throughout all genres.

- **Num 16:33** — "They...went down alive into the pit, and the earth closed upon them"
- **Job 7:9** — "he that goeth down to the grave shall come up no more"
- **Isa 14:15** — "thou shalt be brought down to hell, to the sides of the pit"
- **Job 17:16** — "They shall go down to the bars of the pit, when our rest together is in the dust"

### 3. Sheol and Death as Paired Terms
Sheol (H7585) and māvet (H4194) appear in synonymous parallelism in 15 verses, functioning as a hendiadys for the full reality of death.

- **2 Sam 22:6** — "The sorrows of hell compassed me about; the snares of death prevented me"
- **Prov 5:5** — "Her feet go down to death; her steps take hold on hell"
- **Ps 18:5** — "The sorrows of hell compassed me about: the snares of death prevented me"
- **Hos 13:14** — "I will ransom them from the power of the grave; I will redeem them from death: O death, I will be thy plagues; O grave, I will be thy destruction"

### 4. Fate of the Wicked
The wicked go to sheol as a consequence of judgment; descent is premature or violent.

- **Ps 9:17** — "The wicked shall be turned into hell, and all the nations that forget God"
- **Ps 55:15** — "let them go down quick into hell: for wickedness is in their dwellings"
- **Prov 9:18** — "the dead are there; and her guests are in the depths of hell"
- **Deut 32:22** — "a fire is kindled in mine anger, and shall burn unto the lowest hell"

### 5. Rescue and Redemption from Sheol
The Psalms especially express prayer for deliverance from sheol and celebrate God's power to raise from it.

- **Ps 16:10** — "thou wilt not leave my soul in hell; neither wilt thou suffer thine Holy One to see corruption"
- **Ps 30:3** — "O LORD, thou hast brought up my soul from the grave: thou hast kept me alive"
- **Ps 86:13** — "thou hast delivered my soul from the lowest hell"
- **Hos 13:14** — "I will ransom them from the power of the grave; I will redeem them from death"

### 6. Sheol as Insatiable
Sheol is personified as ever-hungry, never satisfied — a poetic figure for insatiable appetite or ambition.

- **Prov 27:20** — "Hell and destruction are never full; so the eyes of man are never satisfied"
- **Isa 5:14** — "hell hath enlarged herself, and opened her mouth without measure"
- **Hab 2:5** — "who enlargeth his desire as hell, and is as death, and cannot be satisfied"
- **Song 8:6** — "jealousy is cruel as the grave: the coals thereof are coals of fire"
