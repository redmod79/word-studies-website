## Lexicon Definition

A primitive root; to trample or thresh:—break, tear, thresh, tread out (down), at grass (Jeremiah 50:11 — KJV "at grass" rendering considered a textual mistake by Strong's for a different root).

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Literal threshing of grain (agricultural)
- **Deu 25:4** — "Thou shalt not muzzle the ox when he treadeth out [the corn]." (humane case law; cited by Paul in 1 Cor 9:9 / 1 Tim 5:18)
- **1 Ch 21:20** — "Now Ornan was threshing wheat." (the threshing-floor that becomes the temple site)
- **Isa 28:28** — "Bread [corn] is bruised; because he will not ever be threshing it, nor break [it with] the wheel of his cart." (measured threshing as a picture of divine wisdom)
- **Hos 10:11** — "Ephraim [is as] an heifer [that is] taught, [and] loveth to tread out [the corn]." (ox treading grain as metaphor for easy work)

### 2. Threshing as military conquest / judgment on nations
- **Mic 4:13** — "Arise and thresh, O daughter of Zion: for I will make thine horn iron, and I will make thy hoofs brass: and thou shalt beat in pieces many people."
- **Hab 3:12** — "Thou didst march through the land in indignation, thou didst thresh the heathen in anger."
- **Amos 1:3** — "because they have threshed Gilead with threshing instruments of iron" (Damascus oracle — war-crime imagery)
- **Isa 41:15** — "Behold, I will make thee a new sharp threshing instrument having teeth: thou shalt thresh the mountains." (Israel as eschatological threshing sledge)

### 3. Being trodden down / humiliated (passive — Niphal/Hophal)
- **Isa 25:10** — "Moab shall be trodden down under him, even as straw is trodden down for the dunghill." (Niphal — double use; the classic image of ignominious defeat)
- **Isa 28:27** — "the fitches are not threshed with a threshing instrument" (Hophal — divine restraint matched to the crop)

### 4. Punitive tearing / crushing (figurative extension)
- **Jdg 8:7** — "then I will tear your flesh with the thorns of the wilderness" (Gideon's threat to Succoth — threshing-sledge imagery transferred to human punishment)
- **2 Ki 13:7** — "the king of Syria had destroyed them, and had made them like the dust by threshing." (Hazael's oppression of Jehoahaz)
- **Job 39:15** — "the foot may crush them, or that the wild beast may break them." (ostrich eggs)
