## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same across testaments:**
- The full semantic range transfers intact: physical falling, prostration in worship, destruction of powers, death in judgment, cosmic descent
- The "is fallen, is fallen" formula for Babylon passes from Isa 21:9 through LXX into Rev 14:8/18:2 with the same doubled-verb emphasis
- Prostration before God as worship (Deu 9:18 → Rev 4:10, 7:11, 11:16) uses the same word across both testaments

**What shifts:**
- The NT concentrates pipto heavily in Revelation (20 of 85 uses = 24%) compared to naphal's broad OT distribution across 37+ books, giving the word a more apocalyptic/liturgical focus
- Compounds specialize in NT: ekpipto takes on a distinct "fall away from faith" meaning (Gal 5:4) that naphal's hiphil handled in OT
