## Semantic Categories

### 1. National/Messianic Redemption (Hope for Israel's Deliverance)
- **Luke 24:21** — "But we trusted that it had been he which should have redeemed Israel: and beside all this, to day is the third day since these things were done."
  The Emmaus disciples express dashed hope that Jesus was the one to redeem Israel — a political-messianic expectation of national liberation.

### 2. Redemption from Sin through Christ's Self-Giving
- **Titus 2:14** — "Who gave himself for us, that he might redeem us from all iniquity, and purify unto himself a peculiar people, zealous of good works."
  Christ's self-sacrifice is the ransom price. The redemption is from ἀνομία (lawlessness/iniquity), and the purpose clause links redemption to purification and the creation of a covenant people.

### 3. Redemption by Costly Blood (Not Corruptible Things)
- **1 Peter 1:18** — "Forasmuch as ye know that ye were not redeemed with corruptible things, as silver and gold, from your vain conversation received by tradition from your fathers;"
  The marketplace metaphor is explicit: redemption costs something. Silver and gold are insufficient; the implied payment (stated in v. 19) is "the precious blood of Christ, as of a lamb without blemish."
