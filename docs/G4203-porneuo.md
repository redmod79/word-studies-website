## Lexicon Definition

From πόρνη (pornē); to act the harlot, i.e. (literally) indulge unlawful lust (of either sex), or (figuratively) practise idolatry:—commit (fornication).

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Bodily Sin Against Oneself (Pauline Ethics)
Paul singles out porneuō as uniquely self-damaging — the only sin described as being "against one's own body."
- **1 Cor 6:18** — "Flee fornication. Every sin that a man doeth is without the body; but he that committeth fornication sinneth against his own body."

### 2. Historical Warning — Israel's Wilderness Sin
The verb in aorist narrates Israel's literal sexual sin with Moabite women (Num 25:1–9), used as a warning to the church.
- **1 Cor 10:8** — "Neither let us commit fornication, as some of them committed, and fell in one day three and twenty thousand."

### 3. Idolatrous Seduction — Balaam/Jezebel (Letters to the Churches)
In the messages to Pergamos and Thyatira, porneuō describes the seduction of believers into sexual sin linked with idol worship — echoing the Balaam/Baal-Peor pattern.
- **Rev 2:14** — "thou hast there them that hold the doctrine of Balaam, who taught Balac to cast a stumblingblock before the children of Israel, to eat things sacrificed unto idols, and to commit fornication."
- **Rev 2:20** — "thou sufferest that woman Jezebel, which calleth herself a prophetess, to teach and to seduce my servants to commit fornication, and to eat things sacrificed unto idols."

### 4. Political-Spiritual Harlotry — Kings and Babylon
In the Babylon judgment chapters, porneuō describes the complicity of the "kings of the earth" in Babylon's idolatrous system — a metaphorical/political fornication.
- **Rev 17:2** — "With whom the kings of the earth have committed fornication, and the inhabitants of the earth have been made drunk with the wine of her fornication."
- **Rev 18:3** — "the kings of the earth have committed fornication with her, and the merchants of the earth are waxed rich through the abundance of her delicacies."
- **Rev 18:9** — "the kings of the earth, who have committed fornication and lived deliciously with her, shall bewail her, and lament for her."
