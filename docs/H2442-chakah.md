## Lexicon Definition

A primitive root (apparently akin to H2475 through the idea of piercing); properly, to adhere to; hence, to await: — long, tarry, wait.

## Related Words

### Synonyms
- **H6960 קָוָה (qavah)** — to wait, hope, expect (49v). The most common Hebrew "wait" verb. Emphasizes expectant tension — literally "to bind together / be taut." More frequent and broader than chakah; dominant in Psalms.
- **H7663 שָׂבַר (sabar)** — to hope, wait, expect (8v). Emphasizes scrutinizing observation while waiting — "to watch expectantly."
- **H3176 יָחַל (yachal)** — to wait, hope (46v). Emphasizes hope as a disposition — patient endurance with confidence. Common in Job and Psalms.
- **H1826 דָּמַם (damam)** — to be still, cease, wait (27v). Waiting expressed as silence or stillness before God (Psa 37:7 "rest in the LORD, and wait patiently").
