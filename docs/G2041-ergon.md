## Lexicon Definition

From Thayer: *Work, i.e.*
1. **Business, employment, that with which anyone is occupied** — the work committed by God to Christ (John 17:4); the work of apostles and Christian teachers (Acts 13:2; 1 Thess 5:13; 2 Tim 4:5).
2. **Any product whatever, anything accomplished by hand, art, industry, or mind** — works of creation visible in the world (Heb 1:10); τά ἔργα τοῦ διαβόλου, sins and all misery springing from them (1 John 3:8).
3. **An act, deed, thing done** — contrasted with λόγος (word) in Luke 24:19; Rom 15:18; 2 Thess 2:17; 1 John 3:18. Applied to conduct measured by religion and righteousness — whether evil (Matt 23:3; John 3:20) or good (John 3:21; Jas 2:14–26). In Paul's writings ἔργα νόμου = works demanded by and agreeing with the law (Rom 3:20, 28; Gal 2:16; 3:2, 5, 10).

*Derives from ἔργω érgō; the Septuagint renders פֹּעַל, עֲבֹדָה, מְלָאכָה, and especially מַעֲשֶׂה with this word.*

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Works of God and Christ (Signs, Creation, Acts of Power)
- **John 5:36** — "the works which the Father hath given me to finish, the same works that I do, bear witness of me"
