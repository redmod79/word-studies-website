## Semantic Categories

### 1. The Slaughtered Lamb (Christological)
Christ's death expressed through the sacrificial slaughter vocabulary drawn from the LXX rendering of שָׁחַט (shachat). The Perfect Passive Participle emphasizes the Lamb's permanent, ever-present identity as the one who has been slain.
- **Rev 5:6** — "stood a Lamb as it had been slain (ἐσφαγμένον), having seven horns and seven eyes"
- **Rev 5:9** — "thou wast slain (ἐσφάγης), and hast redeemed us to God by thy blood"
- **Rev 5:12** — "Worthy is the Lamb that was slain (ἐσφαγμένον)"
- **Rev 13:8** — "the Lamb slain (ἐσφαγμένου) from the foundation of the world"

### 2. Martyrs Slaughtered
Believers killed for their testimony are described with the same slaughter vocabulary as Christ — their deaths placed in the same sacrificial register.
- **Rev 6:9** — "souls of them that were slain (ἐσφαγμένων) for the word of God, and for the testimony which they held"
- **Rev 18:24** — "the blood of prophets, and of saints, and of all that were slain (ἐσφαγμένων) upon the earth"

### 3. Literal Killing / Violence
Physical slaughter of persons — both historical murder and eschatological war.
- **1 John 3:12** — "Cain...slew (ἔσφαξεν) his brother"
- **Rev 6:4** — "power was given to him...that they should kill (σφάξουσιν) one another"

### 4. The Beast's Counterfeit Wound
The beast's mortal wound mimics the slain Lamb using the identical vocabulary — a deliberate parody.
- **Rev 13:3** — "one of his heads as it were wounded (ἐσφαγμένην) to death; and his deadly wound was healed"
