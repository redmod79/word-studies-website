**Semantic shift in translation:** None — the LXX transliterates rather than translates, preserving the Hebrew sound. This is unusual among LXX renderings. It shows the LXX translators regarded the Sabbath as a proper institution with an untranslatable name, not merely a "day of rest" (which might have been ἀνάπαυσις or ἀργία).

## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same:**
- The word itself: σάββατον IS שַׁבָּת, phonologically preserved
- Seventh-day timing: NT consistently treats the sabbath as the seventh day
- Synagogue worship pattern: OT establishment → NT continuation (Acts 13, 15, 17, 18)
- Holiness framing: both OT and NT treat it as a marked day requiring intentional observance

**What shifts:**
- OT: sabbath defined primarily by prohibition (no work, no commerce, no fire)
- NT: Jesus reframes sabbath around purpose — human flourishing, mercy, healing
- OT: sabbath as covenant sign between Israel and YHWH (Exo 31:13)
- NT: sabbath as arena for eschatological controversy — who is Lord of the day?
- H7676 in OT: frequently plural ("my sabbaths," "your sabbaths") — covenant obligation to a nation
- G4521 in NT: mostly singular — the specific day encountered week by week
