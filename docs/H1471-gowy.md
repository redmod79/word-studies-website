# H1471 — גּוֹי (gôwy)

## Core Data

- **Original:** גּוֹי (also shortened גֹּי)
- **Transliteration:** gôwy (goy)
- **Pronunciation:** go'-ee
- **Part of Speech:** Masculine noun (occasionally proper noun in place-names)
- **Root:** Apparently from the same root as גֵּוָה (gēvâh, "body/back"), in the sense of "massing"; i.e. a people grouped together as a corporate body
- **Occurrences:** 511 unique verses (607 tokens) across 34 OT books

- **Hapax Legomenon:** No — the standard OT word for "nation"

## Lexicon Definition

From the same root as גֵּוָה (in the sense of massing); a foreign nation; hence, a Gentile; also (figuratively) a troop of animals, or a flight of locusts — **Gentile, heathen, nation, people**.

The word is overwhelmingly prophetic: the twelve minor prophets and Isaiah/Jeremiah/Ezekiel dominate by density. Ezekiel and Jeremiah together account for ~31% of all tokens (158/511). Deuteronomy leads the Torah (41×) as the legal framework for Israel's relationship to the surrounding gôyim. Obadiah has the highest density (19 per 100 verses) because its entire subject is Edom as one of the gôyim. Absent from Ruth, Esther, Song of Solomon, and most of the wisdom literature — the word belongs to covenantal, historical, and prophetic discourse, not sapiential reflection.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Israel as a Nation (The Covenant Goy)
Despite later connotations, gôwy is used of Israel itself — sometimes in distinction from the gôyim, sometimes alongside them.

- **Gen 12:2** — "And I will make of thee a great nation (gôwy gâdôl)" — the Abrahamic promise
- **Exod 19:6** — "ye shall be unto me a kingdom of priests, and an holy nation (gôwy qâdôsh)"
- **Deut 4:6–8** — "what nation is there so great, who hath God so nigh unto them... statutes and judgments so righteous"
- **2 Sam 7:23** — "what one nation in the earth is like thy people, even like Israel"

### 2. The Nations in General (Ethnopolitical Groups)
Neutral usage: peoples defined by language, territory, lineage, or kingship.

- **Gen 10:5, 20, 31, 32** — the Table of Nations: gôyim sorted "after their tongues... after their families... in their lands"
- **Gen 17:4–5** — Abraham as "father of many nations"
- **Gen 25:23** — "Two nations are in thy womb" (Jacob/Esau)
- **Deut 32:8** — "When the most High divided to the nations their inheritance"

### 3. Foreign/Hostile Nations (Adversaries of Israel)
The gôyim as military and political threats — nations God will drive out, or nations that oppress Israel.

- **Deut 7:1** — "hath cast out many nations before thee... seven nations greater and mightier than thou"
- **Jer 5:15** — "I will bring a nation upon you from far, O house of Israel... it is a mighty nation, it is an ancient nation"
- **Hab 1:6** — "I raise up the Chaldeans, that bitter and hasty nation"
- **Obad 1:1** — "an ambassador is sent among the heathen"

### 4. Morally/Religiously Other ("The Heathen")
Nations characterized by idolatry, abomination, uncircumcision, or ignorance of YHWH; the KJV often renders this register as "heathen."

- **Lev 18:24** — "the nations are defiled which I cast out before you"
- **Lev 26:33** — "I will scatter you among the heathen"
- **2 Kgs 17:8, 11** — "walked in the statutes of the heathen, whom the LORD cast out"
- **Jer 10:2** — "Learn not the way of the heathen"
- **Ezek 36:23** — "I will sanctify my great name, which was profaned among the heathen"

### 5. Nations as Objects of Divine Judgment
Prophetic oracles where gôyim are the direct targets of YHWH's wrath — the "day of the LORD" extending universally.

- **Joel 3:2, 12** — "I will also gather all nations... let the heathen be wakened, and come up to the valley of Jehoshaphat"
- **Zeph 3:8** — "my determination is to gather the nations, that I may assemble the kingdoms"
- **Isa 34:1–2** — "Come near, ye nations, to hear... the indignation of the LORD is upon all nations"
- **Zech 14:2–3** — "I will gather all nations against Jerusalem to battle... Then shall the LORD go forth and fight against those nations"

### 6. Nations as Objects of Redemption / Eschatological Blessing
The counter-current: gôyim as witnesses to or sharers in YHWH's salvation.

- **Gen 22:18** — "in thy seed shall all the nations of the earth be blessed"
- **Isa 2:2–4** — "all nations shall flow unto it... he shall judge among the nations"
- **Isa 49:6** — "I will also give thee for a light to the Gentiles, that thou mayest be my salvation unto the end of the earth"
- **Isa 60:3** — "the Gentiles shall come to thy light"
- **Zech 8:22–23** — "Yea, many people and strong nations shall come to seek the LORD"
- **Mal 1:11** — "my name shall be great among the Gentiles... from the rising of the sun even unto the going down of the same"

### 7. Israel Exiled / Scattered Among the Nations
A distinct covenantal pattern: the scattering of Israel *among* the gôyim as curse, followed by regathering.

- **Lev 26:38** — "ye shall perish among the heathen"
- **Deut 4:27** — "the LORD shall scatter you among the nations"
- **Deut 28:64–65** — "the LORD shall scatter thee among all people... among these nations shalt thou find no ease"
- **Ezek 36:19, 24** — "I scattered them among the heathen... I will take you from among the heathen"
- **Jer 29:14; 30:11** — "I will gather you from all the nations... I make a full end of all nations whither I have scattered thee"

**Key distinction (ʻam vs. gôwy):** Deut 32:21 plays on this: "they have moved me to jealousy with that which is not God... God's relationship to Israel is expressed through ʻam; his relationship to outsiders through gôwy.

The LXX virtually locks **gôwy → ἔθνος (éthnos)**. This equation is the most consistent ethno-theological bridge in the entire LXX. Because of this, NT authors using *ethnos* are drawing on the full OT theology of *haggôyim* — nations as "other," as judged, and as ultimately blessed.
