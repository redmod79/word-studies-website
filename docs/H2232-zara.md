## Lexicon Definition

> A primitive root; to sow; figuratively, to disseminate, plant, fructify: — bear, conceive seed, set with, sow(-er), yield.

The verb denotes the act of producing or distributing seed — either agriculturally (sowing grain, planting a field) or biologically (bearing seed, conceiving offspring). The same root underlies the noun *zeraʻ* ("seed, offspring, posterity," H2233), and the two are frequently paired in the same clause (cognate accusative "sow seed").

The **Hiphil participle** *mazriaʻ* ("seed-producing / yielding seed") is the specifically productive-causative form used in Gen 1:11–12 of plants that generate their own seed, and in Lev 12:2 of a woman who "conceives seed." The Qal participle *zoreaʻ* ("sower / one sowing") dominates proverbial and wisdom contexts (Gen 1:29; Prov 11:18; 22:8; Ps 126:5; Ecc 11:4; Jer 50:16).

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Plants producing their own seed (Hiphil, Edenic diet language)
The Gen 1 Priestly account employs the Hiphil participle *mazriaʻ* to designate vegetation that internally generates seed — self-propagating plants — and the Qal participle *zoreaʻ* for seed-producing herbs given as food.
- **Gen 1:11** — "Let the earth bring forth grass, the herb yielding seed (ʻeseb mazriaʻ zeraʻ), [and] the fruit tree yielding fruit"
- **Gen 1:12** — "the earth brought forth grass, [and] herb yielding seed after his kind"
- **Gen 1:29** — "every herb bearing seed (ʻeseb zoreaʻ zeraʻ)... and every tree, in the which [is] the fruit of a tree yielding seed (zoreaʻ zeraʻ); to you it shall be for meat"

### 2. Literal agricultural sowing
Planting seed in a field; the base agronomic sense.
- **Gen 26:12** — "Then Isaac sowed in that land, and received... an hundredfold"
- **Exo 23:10** — "Six years thou shalt sow thy land, and shalt gather in the fruits thereof"
- **Isa 28:24** — "Doth the plowman plow all day to sow?"
- **Ecc 11:6** — "In the morning sow thy seed, and in the evening withhold not thine hand"

### 3. Sabbatical / covenantal land legislation
The verb is the legal hinge of the sabbath-year and jubilee statutes — prohibitions or commands regulating when land may/may not be sown.
- **Lev 25:3–4** — "Six years thou shalt sow thy field... but in the seventh year... thou shalt neither sow thy field"
- **Lev 25:11, 20, 22** — jubilee prohibition and promise of the eighth-year sowing
- **Lev 19:19** — "Thou shalt not sow thy field with mingled seed"
- **Deu 22:9** — "Thou shalt not sow thy vineyard with divers seeds"

### 4. Conception / bearing of seed (human reproduction)
Niphal and Hiphil of a woman who conceives / is made fruitful — metaphor drawn from the agricultural domain onto human generation.
- **Lev 12:2** — "If a woman have conceived seed (tazriaʻ), and born a man child"
- **Num 5:28** — "then she shall be free, and shall conceive seed (nizreʻah zaraʻ)"

### 5. Prophetic futility / reversal oracles
A recurrent prophetic motif: "sow but not reap" — Yahweh's covenant curse reversed against disobedient Israel.
- **Mic 6:15** — "Thou shalt sow, but thou shalt not reap"
- **Hag 1:6** — "Ye have sown much, and bring in little"
- **Lev 26:16** — "ye shall sow your seed in vain, for your enemies shall eat it"
- **Jer 12:13** — "They have sown wheat, but shall reap thorns"
- **Hos 8:7** — "they have sown the wind, and they shall reap the whirlwind"

### 6. Moral / wisdom sowing-and-reaping
Metaphorical extension: conduct is "sown" and a corresponding harvest is "reaped."
- **Job 4:8** — "they that plow iniquity, and sow wickedness, reap the same"
- **Pro 11:18** — "to him that soweth righteousness [shall be] a sure reward"
- **Pro 22:8** — "He that soweth iniquity shall reap vanity"
- **Hos 10:12** — "Sow to yourselves in righteousness, reap in mercy"
- **Psa 126:5** — "They that sow in tears shall reap in joy"

### 7. Divine sowing / scattering of Israel
Yahweh himself as the sower — either scattering (exile) or replanting (restoration).
- **Hos 2:23** — "And I will sow her (uzraʻtîhâ) unto me in the earth"
- **Jer 31:27** — "I will sow the house of Israel and the house of Judah with the seed of man, and with the seed of beast"
- **Zec 10:9** — "And I will sow them among the people"

### 8. Negated "not sown" as desolation formula
The land or place "shall not be sown" as a marker of total covenant judgment.
- **Deu 29:23** — "it is not sown, nor beareth"
- **Jer 2:2** — "in a land that was not sown"
- **Isa 40:24** — "yea, they shall not be sown" (Pual)
