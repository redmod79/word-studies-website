## Key Grammatical Features

### 1. wayyaben -- Hiphil Wayyiqtol 3ms of biyn (H995)

- **Form:** Hiphil wayyiqtol 3rd masculine singular of biyn. The Hiphil stem is causative: "he caused [me] to understand" / "he gave understanding." The wayyiqtol places it in narrative sequence -- this is what Gabriel did upon arriving.
- **Significance:** This is the opening verb of the entire passage and it is a biyn verb in the Hiphil. Gabriel's first action upon reaching Daniel is to cause understanding. The KJV renders this "he informed [me]" -- the bracketed pronoun is supplied because the Hiphil of biyn here is used transitively with an implied direct object. This is the same root (H995) that saturates the Dan 8-12 understanding chain (Dan 8:5, 8:15, 8:16, 8:17, 8:27, 9:2, 9:22, 9:23, 10:1, 10:11, 10:12, 10:14, 11:33, 12:8, 12:10).
- **Grammar reference:** [stem-hiphil](../hebrew/stem-hiphil.md)
- **Word study:** [H995-biyn](../../word-studies/H995-biyn.md), [H998-biynah](../../word-studies/H998-biynah.md)

### 2. lehaskilkha binah -- Hiphil Infinitive Construct of sakal (H7919) + Object binah (H998)

- **Form:** Preposition le + Hiphil infinitive construct of sakal with 2ms pronominal suffix. The Hiphil of sakal means "to cause to be prudent/skillful," "to give insight." The suffix is the indirect object -- "to cause you to have insight." The direct object of this causing is the noun binah (H998), "understanding."
- **Significance:** This is a purpose clause -- Gabriel has "come forth" (yatsati, Qal perfect 1cs of yatsa) in order to give Daniel sakal-type insight, and the content of that insight is binah-type understanding. The two understanding-words from distinct roots (sakal and biyn) are yoked together: Gabriel uses sakal (prudential/practical insight) to deliver binah (discerning comprehension). The Hiphil infinitive construct with pronominal suffix and a following noun object is a compact construction expressing Gabriel's entire mission in three words.
- **Grammar reference:** [stem-hiphil](../hebrew/stem-hiphil.md)
- **Word study:** [H7919-sakal](../../word-studies/H7919-sakal.md), [H998-biynah](../../word-studies/H998-biynah.md)

### 3. chamudoth attah -- Predicate Nominal Clause with Passive Participle

- **Form:** chamudoth is the feminine plural absolute of a passive participle/adjective from the root chamad (H2530, "to desire/delight in"). It functions as a predicate adjective in a verbless nominal clause: chamudoth (predicate) + attah (subject, 2ms pronoun). Clause type: NmCl (nominal clause).
- **Significance:** The clause means "you [are] desirable things" -- rendered by KJV as "greatly beloved." The plural form intensifies: Daniel is not merely desired but is "desirabilities" in the abstract-plural sense. The passive orientation of the root (chamad) indicates Daniel is desired/delighted-in -- the agent (God) is implied. This same term recurs at Dan 10:11 (ish chamudoth "man of desirabilities") and Dan 10:19 (ish chamudoth), forming a bracket around Gabriel's interactions with Daniel. The ki introducing this clause makes it causal: Gabriel has come to declare because Daniel is chamudoth -- his status as divinely desired is the ground for receiving the revelation.
- **Word study:** No canonical entry yet for H2530/chamudoth -- gap flagged.

### 4. ubin ... wehaben -- Paired Imperatives: Qal and Hiphil of biyn (H995)

- **Form:** Two consecutive imperatives of the same root biyn, in different stems:
  - **ubin** -- Qal imperative 2ms: "understand!" (basic stem -- direct, unmediated comprehension)
  - **wehaben** -- Hiphil imperative 2ms: "gain/cause-yourself insight!" (causative stem -- actively produce understanding)
- **Significance:** This is the climactic biyn concentration. Gabriel issues back-to-back commands using the same root in two different stems, each with a distinct prepositional object:
  - Qal biyn + baddabar ("understand the word/matter") -- direct comprehension of what is said
  - Hiphil haben + bammareh ("gain insight into the vision") -- active, causative processing of what was seen

  The stem shift from Qal to Hiphil within a single breath is significant. The Qal is receptive: take in the word. The Hiphil is productive: work the vision until you have extracted understanding from it. The two objects correspond to two modes of revelation: dabar (verbal/propositional content -- what Gabriel is about to say in 9:24-27) and mareh (visual revelation -- the vision of Dan 8 that was left uninterpreted at 8:27). Gabriel is explicitly reconnecting to the unfinished business of chapter 8.
- **Grammar reference:** [stem-hiphil](../hebrew/stem-hiphil.md), [imperative-mood](../hebrew/imperative-mood.md)
- **Word study:** [H995-biyn](../../word-studies/H995-biyn.md)

### 5. yatsa dabar -- Qal Perfect 3ms of yatsa with Subject dabar

- **Form:** Qal perfect 3rd masculine singular of yatsa ("go out / come forth") with the subject dabar ("word" / "commandment"). The clause type is xQtX (non-verb-initial qatal clause) because the temporal adjunct is fronted.
- **Significance:** A dabar "came forth" at the very beginning of Daniel's supplications. The fronted temporal phrase emphasizes the immediacy of the divine response -- the word was issued before Daniel finished praying. The verb yatsa is the same root used of Gabriel himself in v.22 (yatsati, "I have come forth"), creating a verbal parallel: the word came forth and Gabriel came forth, both triggered by Daniel's prayer. The nature of this dabar (whether it is the decree of Dan 9:24-27 or a divine command dispatching Gabriel) is left grammatically open -- the noun itself means simply "word/matter/thing."

## Clause Structure

### Verse 22 -- Six Clauses

| # | Type | Domain | Function | Text |
|---|------|--------|----------|------|
| 1 | Way0 | N (narrative) | Main | wayyaben -- "and he caused [me] to understand" |
| 2 | Way0 | N | Coordinate | wayedabber immi -- "and he spoke with me" |
| 3 | Way0 | N | Coordinate | wayyomar -- "and he said" |
| 4 | Voct | Q (speech) | Vocative | daniyyel -- "O Daniel" |
| 5 | xQt0 | Q | Main speech | attah yatsati -- "now I have come forth" |
| 6 | InfC | Q | Purpose | lehaskilkha binah -- "to give you understanding" |

**Note:** The three wayyiqtol clauses (Way0) form a rapid narrative chain: Gabriel understood, spoke, said. The shift from narrative domain (N) to speech domain (Q) occurs at the vocative. Within speech, Gabriel states his completed action (yatsati, perfect -- "I have come forth," fait accompli) and then its purpose (infinitive construct clause).

### Verse 23 -- Six Clauses

| # | Type | Domain | Function | Text |
|---|------|--------|----------|------|
| 1 | xQtX | Q | Main | bitchillat tachanunekha yatsa dabar -- "at the beginning of your supplications a word came forth" |
| 2 | WXQt | Q | Coordinate | waani bati -- "and I have come" |
| 3 | InfC | Q | Purpose | lehaggiyd -- "to declare" |
| 4 | NmCl | Q | Causal | ki chamudoth attah -- "for you are greatly beloved" |
| 5 | WIm0 | Q | Command | ubin baddabar -- "so understand the word" |
| 6 | WIm0 | Q | Command | wehaben bammareh -- "and gain insight into the vision" |

**Note:** The clause-type sequence tells the story: xQtX (fronted temporal -- what happened when Daniel prayed) to WXQt (fronted subject ani emphasizing Gabriel's personal coming) to InfC (purpose of his coming) to NmCl (ground for receiving revelation) to WIm0 + WIm0 (double imperative -- the actual commands). The two WIm0 imperative clauses are the rhetorical climax.

## Construct Chain

**v.23:** techillat tachanunekha -- "beginning-of your-supplications"
- techillat (fs.Cst of techillah, "beginning") + tachanunekha (mp.Abs of tachanun, "supplication" + 2ms suffix)
- Standard construct chain with pronominal suffix on the absolute noun

## The Biyn Chain: Dan 8-12 Reconnection Point

Daniel 9:22-23 contains the highest concentration of biyn (H995) language in the entire book, with four instances of the root in just two verses:

1. **wayyaben** (v.22) -- Hiphil wayyiqtol 3ms: Gabriel "caused understanding" (narrative action)
2. **binah** (v.22) -- Noun fs: "understanding" as the object Gabriel delivers
3. **ubin** (v.23) -- Qal imperative 2ms: "understand!" (direct command)
4. **wehaben** (v.23) -- Hiphil imperative 2ms: "gain insight!" (causative command)

This reconnects directly to Dan 8:15-17 where Daniel "sought understanding" (binah, 8:15), Gabriel was commanded to "make this one understand" (haben, Hiphil imperative, 8:16), and Gabriel addressed Daniel with "understand" (biyn, Qal imperative, 8:17). The chapter 8 understanding sequence ended in failure -- Daniel "did not understand" (eyn mebiyn, 8:27 participle). Chapter 9:22-23 is Gabriel's return to complete that unfinished mission, announced with the same verb forms (Hiphil wayyiqtol, Qal imperative, Hiphil imperative) deployed in the original commissioning.
