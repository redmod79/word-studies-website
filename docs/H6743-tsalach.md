## Lexicon Definition

A primitive root; **to push forward**, in various senses (literal or figurative, transitive or intransitive): — break out, come (mightily), go over, be good, be meet, be profitable, (cause to, effect, make to, send) prosper(-ity, -ous, -ously).

Note: Strong's lists H6743 (Hebrew) separately from H6744 (Aramaic צְלַח, used in Dan 3:30; 6:28; Ezra 5:8; 6:14). Both share the core sense "to prosper/advance successfully."

**Pattern:** Densest in Daniel and Chronicles. Chronicles concentrates the "prosperity of covenant-faithful kings" motif (Solomon, Asa, Jehoshaphat, Uzziah, Hezekiah). Daniel concentrates the verb's darker ironic edge (the little horn, the willful king). Genesis anchors the positive paradigm around Abraham's servant and Joseph. Judges/Samuel carry the distinctive Qal of the Spirit "rushing" on charismatic leaders (Samson, Saul, David).

## Semantic Categories

### 1. YHWH grants success on a commissioned journey or task
- **Gen 24:21** — "whether the LORD had made his journey prosperous"
- **Gen 24:40, 42, 56** — Abraham's servant; divinely guided mission
- **Neh 1:11; 2:20** — "The God of heaven, he will prosper us"
- **Isa 55:11** — "it shall prosper in the thing whereto I sent it" (God's word)

### 2. Human prospering tied to covenant obedience
- **Jos 1:8** — "thou shalt make thy way prosperous, and then thou shalt have good success"
- **1 Chr 22:11, 13** — Solomon will prosper if he keeps the statutes
- **2 Chr 26:5** — "as long as he sought the LORD, God made him to prosper"
- **2 Chr 31:21; 32:30** — Hezekiah "prospered" in all his works
- **Pro 28:13** — "He that covereth his sins shall not prosper"

### 3. Failed prospering — divine prevention of wicked plans
- **Num 14:41** — "it shall not prosper" (going up without the Lord)
- **Jer 2:37; 22:30** — Judah's disloyalty will not prosper
- **Jer 32:5** — "though ye fight with the Chaldeans, ye shall not prosper"
- **Eze 17:9, 10, 15** — rhetorical "shall it prosper?" (Zedekiah's rebellion)
- **Isa 54:17** — "No weapon formed against thee shall prosper"

### 4. The Spirit of YHWH "rushing" on a person (Qal only)
- **Jdg 14:6, 19; 15:14** — "the Spirit of the LORD came mightily upon" Samson
- **1 Sam 10:6, 10; 11:6** — upon Saul (prophesying, kindled anger)
- **1 Sam 16:13** — upon David "from that day forward"
- **1 Sam 18:10** — even an "evil spirit from God" rushed upon Saul (same verb, same construction)

Distinctive Qal idiom: *tsalach al* ("rush upon / come mightily upon") — sudden divine empowerment. This is not prosperity in the financial sense but forceful breakthrough.

### 5. Prospering of the wicked (unresolved tension)
- **Jer 5:28; 12:1** — "wherefore doth the way of the wicked prosper?"
- **Psa 37:7** — "because of him who prospereth in his way"
- **Psa 73-style theodicy** — foil for the question "why do the wicked succeed?"

### 6. Prospering applied to tyrannical powers (Daniel)
- **Dan 8:12** — of the little horn: *hiṣlîḥāh* "and it practiced, and prospered" (Hiphil Perf 3fs)
- **Dan 8:24** — "he shall destroy wonderfully, and shall prosper, and practise"
- **Dan 8:25** — "he shall cause craft to prosper in his hand"
- **Dan 11:27** — "it shall not prosper" (Qal, of the kings' lying conference)
- **Dan 11:36** — "he shall prosper till the indignation be accomplished"

In Daniel, the verb's normally positive sense is held in ironic tension: the horn/king genuinely *succeeds* (the verb is never negated there), yet the success is bounded ("till the indignation be accomplished") and morally disordered. The Hebrew does not carry the moral judgment — context supplies it.

### 7. Messianic and servant contexts
- **Isa 53:10** — "the pleasure of the LORD shall prosper in his hand" (Qal, Suffering Servant)
- **Isa 48:15** — "I have brought him, and he shall make his way prosperous" (Cyrus)
- **Psa 45:4(5)** — "in thy majesty ride prosperously" (royal / messianic king)
- **Psa 118:25** — "O LORD, send now prosperity" (Hosanna Psalm)

### 8. "Break out / go over" (extended physical sense)
- **2 Sam 19:17(18)** — the people "went over" Jordan
- **Amos 5:6** — "lest he break out like fire in the house of Joseph"
