## Lexicon Definition

To render (i.e., show or regard as) just or innocent; to declare righteous, acquit, vindicate. From δίκαιος (just). KJV renders: justify (-ier), be righteous, be freed.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Forensic Declaration (God declares the believer righteous)
The dominant Pauline use: a legal verdict rendered by God, not a moral transformation.
- **Rom 3:24** — "Being justified freely by his grace through the redemption that is in Christ Jesus"
- **Rom 3:28** — "a man is justified by faith without the deeds of the law"
- **Gal 2:16** — "a man is not justified by the works of the law, but by the faith of Jesus Christ"
- **Rom 8:30** — "whom he justified, them he also glorified"

### 2. Justification by Faith vs. Works (Paul–James tension)
Paul argues faith apart from works; James insists faith demonstrated by works — same verb, different vantage points.
- **Rom 5:1** — "being justified by faith, we have peace with God"
- **Gal 3:24** — "the law was our schoolmaster unto Christ, that we might be justified by faith"
- **Jas 2:21** — "Was not Abraham our father justified by works, when he had offered Isaac?"
- **Jas 2:24** — "by works a man is justified, and not by faith only"

### 3. Vindication (showing someone to be right/wise)
Used outside the atonement framework to mean proving or demonstrating righteousness.
- **Mat 11:19** — "wisdom is justified of her children" (vindicated by its results)
- **Luk 7:29** — "the people justified God, being baptized" (acknowledged God's rightness)
- **Luk 7:35** — "wisdom is justified of all her children"
- **1Ti 3:16** — "God was manifest in the flesh, justified in the Spirit" (Christ vindicated by resurrection)

### 4. Self-Justification (human attempt to appear righteous)
Always used negatively — attempting to appear right before others rather than before God.
- **Luk 10:29** — "willing to justify himself, said unto Jesus, And who is my neighbour?"
- **Luk 16:15** — "Ye are they which justify yourselves before men; but God knoweth your hearts"

### 5. Acquittal / Liberation from Sin
- **Acts 13:39** — "by him all that believe are justified from all things, from which ye could not be justified by the law"
- **Rom 6:7** — "he that is dead is freed from sin" (only KJV instance of "freed")
- **Rom 3:26** — "that he might be just, and the justifier of him which believeth in Jesus"
