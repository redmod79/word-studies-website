## Lexicon Definition

From H8175; shaggy; as noun, a he-goat; by analogy, a faun (hairy woodland creature): — devil, goat, hairy, kid, rough, satyr.

Also written שָׂעִר (sair). The base meaning is "hairy/shaggy." The nominal use denotes a male goat (the shaggy one). By extension, a shaggy demon-figure or satyr-like creature inhabiting desolate places.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Sacrificial He-Goat — Sin Offering
The dominant usage. The saiyr is the prescribed male goat for sin offerings throughout the cultic calendar.
- **Lev 4:23** — "he shall bring his offering, a kid of the goats for a sin offering"
- **Num 7:16** — "One kid of the goats for a sin offering"
- **Num 28:15** — "And one kid of the goats for a sin offering unto the LORD"
- **Num 29:5** — "And one kid of the goats for a sin offering, to make an atonement for you"

### 2. Day of Atonement Goats (Yom Kippur)
The saiyr in Lev 16 carries the most theologically loaded usage — the two goats of the Day of Atonement, one sacrificed and one sent to Azazel.
- **Lev 16:5** — "he shall take of the congregation of the children of Israel two kids of the goats for a sin offering"
- **Lev 16:8** — "And Aaron shall cast lots upon the two goats; one lot for the LORD, and the other lot for the scapegoat"
- **Lev 16:10** — "But the goat, on which the lot fell to be the scapegoat, shall be presented alive before the LORD"
- **Lev 16:21-22** — "And Aaron shall lay both his hands upon the head of the live goat, and confess over him all the iniquities... And the goat shall bear upon him all their iniquities unto a land not inhabited"

### 3. Physical Description — Hairy/Shaggy
The adjectival root sense, used to describe Esau.
- **Gen 27:11** — "Esau my brother is a hairy man, and I am a smooth man"
- **Gen 27:23** — "his hands were hairy, as his brother Esau's hands"

### 4. Demons / False Worship Objects
A distinct semantic shift: the shaggy goat-figure becomes an object of illicit worship — goat-demons or satyr-like beings.
- **Lev 17:7** — "they shall no more offer their sacrifices unto devils, after whom they have gone a whoring"
- **2 Chr 11:15** — "he ordained him priests for the high places, and for the devils, and for the calves which he had made"

### 5. Wild Creatures of Desolation
In prophetic judgment oracles, saiyr denotes eerie creatures inhabiting ruins — translated "satyr" in KJV.
- **Isa 13:21** — "owls shall dwell there, and satyrs shall dance there" (Babylon's desolation)
- **Isa 34:14** — "the satyr shall cry to his fellow" (Edom's desolation)

### 6. Geopolitical Symbol
- **Dan 8:21** — "the rough goat is the king of Grecia: and the great horn that is between his eyes is the first king"

### 7. Congregational Sin Offering (Festival Calendar)
The repetitive Numbers 29 cycle — one saiyr per festival day across Feast of Trumpets, Day of Atonement, and Tabernacles.
- **Num 29:11** — "One kid of the goats for a sin offering; beside the sin offering of atonement"
- **Num 29:16** — "And one kid of the goats for a sin offering; beside the continual burnt offering"
- **Eze 45:23** — "seven days of the feast he shall prepare... a kid of the goats daily for a sin offering"
