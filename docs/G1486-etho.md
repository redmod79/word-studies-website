## Lexicon Definition

A primary verb; to be used (by habit or conventionality). The word expresses settled habitual behavior — not a one-time act but a regular, established practice. In all four NT occurrences the perfect/pluperfect form emphasizes the state of being accustomed, treating the habit as a completed-and-ongoing condition.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Habitual Sabbath / Synagogue Practice

- **Luke 4:16** — "as his custom was, he went into the synagogue on the sabbath day, and stood up for to read"
- **Acts 17:2** — "Paul, as his manner was, went in unto them, and three sabbath days reasoned with them out of the scriptures"

Both uses describe a regular pattern of synagogue attendance and scriptural engagement on the sabbath.

### 2. Habitual Teaching Activity

- **Mark 10:1** — "the people resort unto him again; and, as he was wont, he taught them again"

Describes Jesus's established pattern of teaching crowds wherever he traveled, not a special event but a recognized habit.

### 3. Official / Political Custom

- **Matt 27:15** — "at that feast the governor was wont to release unto the people a prisoner, whom they would"

Describes an established civic/political practice of the Roman governor — a custom of governance, not personal piety.
