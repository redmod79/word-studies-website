## Lexicon Definition

A primitive root; to grudge, i.e. avenge or punish: — avenge(-r, self), punish, revenge (self), surely, take vengeance.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Divine Retributive Justice — God as the Avenger
- **Nah 1:2** — "God is jealous, and the LORD revengeth (noqem); the LORD revengeth, and is furious; the LORD will take vengeance on his adversaries"
- **Deut 32:43** — "he will avenge the blood of his servants, and will render vengeance to his adversaries"
- **Jer 46:10** — "this is the day of the Lord GOD of hosts, a day of vengeance, that he may avenge him of his adversaries"
- **Isa 1:24** — "I will ease me of mine adversaries, and avenge me of mine enemies"

### 2. God's Judicial Self-Questioning — "Shall Not My Soul Be Avenged?"
- **Jer 5:9** — "Shall I not visit for these things? saith the LORD: and shall not my soul be avenged on such a nation as this?"
- **Jer 5:29** — "Shall I not visit for these things? saith the LORD: shall not my soul be avenged on such a nation as this?"
- **Jer 9:9** — "Shall I not visit them for these things? saith the LORD: shall not my soul be avenged on such a nation as this?"

*All three use the Hithpael stem. The repeated formula functions as a rhetorical declaration that divine patience has a limit.*

### 3. Human Blood-Vengeance and Warfare
- **Num 31:2** — "Avenge the children of Israel of the Midianites"
- **Jos 10:13** — "the sun stood still... until the people had avenged themselves upon their enemies"
- **Est 8:13** — "to avenge themselves on their enemies"
- **2Ki 9:7** — "that I may avenge the blood of my servants the prophets"

### 4. Personal Vengeance (Prohibited or Cautioned)
- **Lev 19:18** — "Thou shalt not avenge, nor bear any grudge against the children of thy people, but thou shalt love thy neighbour as thyself"
- **1Sa 24:12** — "The LORD judge between me and thee, and the LORD avenge me of thee: but mine hand shall not be upon thee"

*Lev 19:18 is the sole explicit prohibition — personal naqam among covenant members is forbidden, while divine naqam and authorized national vengeance continue.*

### 5. Primitive Blood-Law and Talion
- **Gen 4:15** — "whosoever slayeth Cain, vengeance shall be taken on him sevenfold" (Hophal)
- **Gen 4:24** — "If Cain shall be avenged sevenfold, truly Lamech seventy and sevenfold" (Hophal)
- **Exo 21:20-21** — "he shall be surely punished" / "he shall not be punished" (slave-law regulation of naqam)

### 6. The Avenger as Divine Attribute (Participial)
- **Psa 99:8** — "thou wast a God that forgavest them, though thou tookest vengeance of their inventions"
- **Psa 8:2** — "that thou mightest still the enemy and the avenger"
- **Psa 44:16** — "by reason of the enemy and avenger"

*The Qal participle noqem and Hithpael participle mitnaqem function as quasi-nominal characterizations — "the one who avenges" as a standing attribute.*

## Related Words

### Word Family / Cognates
- **H5359 נָקָם (naqam)** — masculine noun: vengeance, revenge (27v). The nominal form appearing in prophetic judgment oracles: "day of vengeance" (Isa 34:8, 61:2, 63:4), "garments of vengeance" (Isa 59:17).
- **H5360 נְקָמָה (neqamah)** — feminine noun: revenge, vengeance, avengement (17v). Often paired with H5358 verb or H5359 noun. Ezekiel uses it for hostile human vengeance (25:12, 15).

### Synonyms
- **H5162 נָחַם (nacham)** — comfort, repent, avenge. In Isa 1:24, nacham Niphal ("ease myself") pairs with naqam — nacham describes the emotional release, naqam the retaliatory act.
- **H5201 נָטַר (natar)** — keep, reserve, bear a grudge. Paired with naqam in Lev 19:18 and Nah 1:2; natar is the holding of resentment, naqam the acting on it.
- **H6485 פָּקַד (paqad)** — visit, punish, appoint. In Jer 5:9/29, 9:9, paqad ("visit/punish") stands in parallel with naqam Hithpael — paqad frames the divine inspection, naqam the resulting judgment.
- **H7378 רִיב (riyb)** — contend, strive, plead. Legal dispute verb; naqam is the execution, riyb the litigation.
