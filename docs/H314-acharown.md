## Lexicon Definition

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Temporal: "Last" in a Sequence (first…last)
Used as antithesis of H7223 riʾshôwn ("first") in a paired formula.
- **Isa 41:4** — "I the LORD, the first, and with the last; I am he."
- **Isa 44:6** — "I am the first, and I am the last; and beside me there is no God."
- **Isa 48:12** — "I am he; I am the first, I also am the last."
- **2Sa 23:1** — "Now these be the last words of David."
- **1Ch 23:27** — "For by the last words of David the Levites were numbered."
- **Neh 8:18** — "from the first day unto the last day, he read in the book of the law."

### 2. Chronicler's Formula: "First and Last" (acts of a king)
A fixed phrase in 2 Chronicles for royal histories: "the acts of X, first and last."
- **1Ch 29:29** — "the acts of David the king, first and last"
- **2Ch 9:29** — "the rest of the acts of Solomon, first and last"
- **2Ch 16:11** — "the acts of Asa, first and last"
- **2Ch 26:22** — "the rest of the acts of Uzziah, first and last, did Isaiah…write"

### 3. Temporal: Future Generations / Time to Come
Refers to a later generation or subsequent era, often with דּוֹר (dôwr, "generation").
- **Deu 29:22** — "the generation to come of your children…and the stranger"
- **Psa 78:4** — "telling to the generation to come the praises of the LORD"
- **Psa 78:6** — "that the generation to come might know them"
- **Psa 102:18 [MT 102:19]** — "This shall be written for the generation to come"
- **Isa 30:8** — "write it…in a book, that it may be for the time to come"

### 4. Temporal: "Latter" (comparative contrast)
Contrasts a later state or person with an earlier one.
- **Exo 4:8** — "they will believe the voice of the latter sign"
- **Deu 24:3** — "if the latter husband hate her"
- **Hag 2:9** — "The glory of this latter house shall be greater than of the former"
- **Dan 11:29** — "it shall not be as the former, or as the latter"
- **Rth 3:10** — "thou hast shewed more kindness in the latter end than at the beginning"
- **Job 19:25** — "I know that my redeemer liveth, and that he shall stand at the latter day upon the earth"

### 5. Spatial: Rear / Western Position
The literal sense of "behind" — the rear of a formation, or the western direction (facing east).
- **Gen 33:2** — "Rachel and Joseph hindermost"
- **Num 2:31** — "they shall go hindmost with their standards"
- **1Sa 29:2** — "David and his men passed on in the rereward"
- **Deu 11:24** — "from the wilderness and Lebanon…unto the uttermost sea" (= western/Mediterranean sea)
- **Deu 34:2** — "all the land of Judah, unto the utmost sea"
- **Zec 14:8** — "half of them toward the hinder sea" (= western/Mediterranean)
- **Joe 2:20** — "his hinder part toward the utmost sea"

### 6. Temporal: Subsequent People / Those Who Come After
Refers to later people, not a specific generation, but posterity in general.
- **Job 18:20** — "They that come after him shall be astonied at his day"
- **Ecc 1:11** — "neither shall there be any remembrance of things that are to come"
- **Ecc 4:16** — "they also that come after shall not rejoice in him"
- **Pro 31:25** — "she shall rejoice in time to come"
