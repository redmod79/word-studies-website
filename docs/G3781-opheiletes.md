## Lexicon Definition

An ower, i.e. person indebted; figuratively, a delinquent; morally, a transgressor (against God). From G3784 (opheiló).

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Moral/Spiritual Debtor — One Who Owes God Forgiveness
- **Matt 6:12** — "And forgive us our debts, as we forgive our debtors." (Lord's Prayer — debt as metaphor for sin requiring divine pardon)
- **Luke 13:4** — "Or those eighteen, upon whom the tower in Siloam fell, and slew them, think ye that they were sinners above all men that dwelt in Jerusalem?" (KJV renders opheiletes as "sinners" — moral culpability before God)

### 2. Financial/Parabolic Debtor — One Who Owes a Monetary Debt
- **Matt 18:24** — "And when he had begun to reckon, one was brought unto him, which owed him ten thousand talents." (Parable of the Unforgiving Servant — literal financial debt as parabolic vehicle for spiritual obligation)

### 3. Obligation Debtor — One Who Owes a Duty or Service
- **Rom 1:14** — "I am debtor both to the Greeks, and to the Barbarians; both to the wise, and to the unwise." (Gospel obligation — Paul's sense of commissioned duty)
- **Rom 8:12** — "Therefore, brethren, we are debtors, not to the flesh, to live after the flesh." (Ethical obligation — negated; believers do not owe the flesh obedience)
- **Rom 15:27** — "It hath pleased them verily; and their debtors they are. For if the Gentiles have been made partakers of their spiritual things, their duty is also to minister unto them in carnal things." (Reciprocal obligation — spiritual blessings received create material obligation)
- **Gal 5:3** — "For I testify again to every man that is circumcised, that he is a debtor to do the whole law." (Legal obligation — circumcision binds one to the entire Mosaic code)
