## Semantic Categories

### 1. The Earth / The World (cosmic whole)
The broadest sense: the entire terrestrial realm, often paired with heaven.
- **Gen 1:1** — "In the beginning God created the heaven and the earth."
- **Gen 6:11** — "The earth also was corrupt before God, and the earth was filled with violence."
- **Psalm 24:1** — "The earth is the LORD's, and the fulness thereof; the world, and they that dwell therein."
- **Isaiah 66:1** — "Thus saith the LORD, The heaven is my throne, and the earth is my footstool."

### 2. A Land / Country (geographic territory)
A specific region, nation, or political domain — the most frequent usage.
- **Gen 12:1** — "Get thee out of thy country, and from thy kindred, and from thy father's house."
- **Gen 12:5** — "Abram took Sarai his wife, and Lot... into the land of Canaan."
- **Exodus 3:8** — "I am come down to deliver them... unto a good land and a large."
- **Jeremiah 50:3** — "out of the north there cometh up a nation against her, which shall make her land desolate."

### 3. The Ground / Soil (surface, dust)
The physical surface of the earth, especially in contexts of falling, bowing, or agricultural ground.
- **Genesis 4:12** — "When thou tillest the ground, it shall not henceforth yield unto thee her strength."
- **Exodus 4:3** — "Cast it on the ground. And he cast it on the ground, and it became a serpent."
- **1 Samuel 5:3** — "Dagon was fallen upon his face to the ground before the ark of the LORD."
- **Psalm 141:7** — "Our bones are scattered at the grave's mouth, as when one cutteth and cleaveth wood upon the earth."

### 4. The Promised Land (theological)
The land promised to Abraham and his seed, later identified with Israel/Canaan.
- **Genesis 12:7** — "Unto thy seed will I give this land."
- **Exodus 6:4** — "give them the land of Canaan, the land of their pilgrimage, wherein they were strangers."
- **Joshua 1:2** — "go over this Jordan, thou, and all this people, unto the land which I do give to them."
- **Ezekiel 36:28** — "And ye shall dwell in the land that I gave to your fathers; and ye shall be my people."

### 5. Directional Suffix — ʾarṣāh (-âh, "to/toward the earth/ground")
The noun takes the directional/locative suffix **-âh** (Hebrew ה locale, often with compensatory lengthening and vowel change: אֶרֶץ → אֲרָצָה / אַרְצָה, ʾarṣāh), meaning "to the earth/ground" or "downward." This form appears ~90× across narrative, poetic, and prophetic texts, overwhelmingly with verbs of bowing, falling, casting, or coming down.

- **Genesis 18:2** — "ran to meet them... and bowed himself toward the ground" (וַיִּשְׁתַּחוּ אָֽרְצָה)
- **Genesis 33:3** — "bowed himself to the ground seven times, until he came near to his brother" (וַיִּשְׁתַּחוּ אֲרָצָה)
- **Exodus 4:3** — "Cast it on the ground" (הַשְׁלִיכֵהוּ אַ֔רְצָה)
- **Exodus 34:8** — "Moses made haste, and bowed his head toward the earth, and worshipped" (וַיִּקֹּד אַ֖רְצָה)
- **Ruth 2:10** — "she fell on her face, and bowed herself to the ground" (וַתִּשְׁתַּחוּ אָ֑רְצָה)
- **Daniel 8:18** — "I was in a deep sleep on my face toward the ground" (עַל־פָּנַי אָ֑רְצָה)
- **2 Chronicles 7:3** — "bowed themselves with their faces to the ground upon the pavement" (וַיִּכְרְעוּ אַפַּ֤יִם אַ֤רְצָה)

**Morphological note:** The directional form is distinct from the 3fs pronominal suffix *-āh* (her land), which is spelled with mappiq in the final he (אַרְצָהּ, e.g., Deut 29:22; Jer 50:3; Ezek 12:19). The directional suffix lacks the mappiq and carries locative sense ("toward the ground/earth"), while the pronominal suffix means "her land."
