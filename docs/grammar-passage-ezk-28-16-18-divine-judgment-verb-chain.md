## Key Grammatical Features

### 1. Piel `chalal`: Divine Profaning and Addressee Defiling

The root חלל appears twice as Piel: v.16 `אֶחַלֶּלְךָ` (1cs + 2ms object) and v.18 `חִלַּלְתָּ` (2ms). The grammar creates a reversal: the addressee defiles sanctuaries; YHWH profanes/removes the addressee from the divine mountain sphere.

### 2. Piel `abad`: Direct Divine Destruction

`אַבֶּדְךָ` is Piel wayyiqtol 1cs with a 2ms object suffix. The form places YHWH as grammatical subject and the addressee as object/patient.

### 3. Hiphil `shalak` and `yatsa`: Causative Judgment

`הִשְׁלַכְתִּיךָ` is Hiphil perfect 1cs + 2ms suffix: "I caused/cast you onto the earth." `אֹוצִא` is Hiphil wayyiqtol 1cs: "I brought forth fire." Both are causative H-stem forms with explicit divine first-person subjects.

Grammar reference: [stem-hiphil](../hebrew/stem-hiphil.md).

### 4. Qal `akal`: Fire as Grammatical Subject

`אֲכָלַתְךָ` is Qal perfect 3fs + 2ms suffix. Its subject is the feminine pronoun `הִיא`, referring to `אֵשׁ` ("fire," feminine in this clause). The chain briefly shifts from YHWH as subject to fire as the immediate grammatical agent.

### 5. Qal `natan`: Public Exposure and Final Result

`נְתַתִּיךָ` (v.17) and `אֶתֶּנְךָ` (v.18) are Qal 1cs forms with 2ms suffixes. In v.17 the complement is `לְרַאֲוָה` ("for seeing/spectacle"); in v.18 the complement is `לְאֵפֶר` ("to ashes"). The same basic verb frames exposure and final reduction.

## Verb-Chain Summary

`sin` -> `I profaned you` -> `I destroyed you` -> `I cast you` -> `I set you for spectacle` -> `you defiled` -> `I brought out fire` -> `it devoured you` -> `I gave you to ashes`.

The grammar alternates indictment verbs addressed to the second person with first-person divine judgment verbs. This is a structural observation only; referent identification belongs to the study.
