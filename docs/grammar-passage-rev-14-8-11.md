# Grammar Analysis: Revelation 14:8-11

## Text

**Greek (Rev 14:8):**
Καὶ ἄλλος ἄγγελος δεύτερος ἠκολούθησεν λέγων Ἔπεσεν ἔπεσεν Βαβυλὼν ἡ μεγάλη, ἣ ἐκ τοῦ οἴνου τοῦ θυμοῦ τῆς πορνείας αὐτῆς πεπότικεν πάντα τὰ ἔθνη.

**Greek (Rev 14:9):**
Καὶ ἄλλος ἄγγελος τρίτος ἠκολούθησεν αὐτοῖς λέγων ἐν φωνῇ μεγάλῃ Εἴ τις προσκυνεῖ τὸ θηρίον καὶ τὴν εἰκόνα αὐτοῦ, καὶ λαμβάνει χάραγμα ἐπὶ τοῦ μετώπου αὐτοῦ ἢ ἐπὶ τὴν χεῖρα αὐτοῦ,

καὶ αὐτὸς πίεται ἐκ τοῦ οἴνου τοῦ θυμοῦ τοῦ Θεοῦ τοῦ κεκερασμένου ἀκράτου ἐν τῷ ποτηρίῳ τῆς ὀργῆς αὐτοῦ, καὶ βασανισθήσεται ἐν πυρὶ καὶ θείῳ ἐνώπιον ἀγγέλων ἁγίων καὶ ἐνώπιον τοῦ Ἀρνίου.

**Greek (Rev 14:11):**
καὶ ὁ καπνὸς τοῦ βασανισμοῦ αὐτῶν εἰς αἰῶνας αἰώνων ἀναβαίνει, καὶ οὐκ ἔχουσιν ἀνάπαυσιν ἡμέρας καὶ νυκτός οἱ προσκυνοῦντες τὸ θηρίον καὶ τὴν εἰκόνα αὐτοῦ, καὶ εἴ τις λαμβάνει τὸ χάραγμα τοῦ ὀνόματος αὐτοῦ.

**KJV (14:8):** And there followed another angel, saying, Babylon is fallen, is fallen, that great city, because she made all nations drink of the wine of the wrath of her fornication.

**KJV (14:9):** And the third angel followed them, saying with a loud voice, If any man worship the beast and his image, and receive his mark in his forehead, or in his hand,

**KJV (14:10):** The same shall drink of the wine of the wrath of God, which is poured out without mixture into the cup of his indignation; and he shall be tormented with fire and brimstone in the presence of the holy angels, and in the presence of the Lamb:

**KJV (14:11):** And the smoke of their torment ascendeth up for ever and ever: and they have no rest day nor night, who worship the beast and his image, and whosoever receiveth the mark of his name.

## Key Grammatical Features

### 1. Ἔπεσεν ἔπεσεν — Aorist Repetition (Rev 14:8)

- **Form:** Second aorist active indicative, 3rd singular, of πίπτω ("fall"). The verb is repeated identically back-to-back.
- **Significance:** The aorist views the fall as a single, complete event. The repetition is not linguistic tautology but a Hebraism of emphatic proclamation, modeled on Isa 21:9 LXX (πέπτωκεν πέπτωκεν Βαβυλών). The aorist locates the fall in completed action — the angel announces it as an accomplished reality even though the full outworking appears later (Rev 18). This is a case of prophetic or proclamatory aorist: the decree is issued; the outcome is certified.
- **Grammar reference:** [aorist-passive-indicative.md](../greek/aorist-passive-indicative.md) (for aorist aspect in general; this verse is active voice, but the aspect discussion applies)

### 2. πεπότικεν — Perfect Active Indicative (Rev 14:8)

- **Form:** Perfect active indicative, 3rd singular, of ποτίζω ("cause to drink"). Reduplication πε- + stem ποτικ- + primary active ending -εν.
- **Significance:** The perfect encodes a past completed action with continuing result. Babylon "has made (and continues as one who has made) all nations drink." Grammatically this explains the aorist "fell, fell": Babylon's fall is announced because her career of seduction has reached its completed, standing state. The perfect locks the condition of the nations as an abiding consequence, not merely a single past sip.
- **Contrast:** Had John used an aorist (ἐπότισεν), the action would be flat past. The perfect dramatizes the enduring state of the intoxicated nations at the moment judgment is announced.

### 3. τοῦ οἴνου τοῦ θυμοῦ τῆς πορνείας αὐτῆς — Stacked Genitive Chain (Rev 14:8)

- **Form:** Four-link nominal chain, all genitive: "of the wine — of the wrath — of the fornication — of her." Each article τοῦ / τῆς agrees with its head noun.
- **Significance:** The genitives are not all the same type. Most naturally:
  - τοῦ οἴνου τοῦ θυμοῦ — genitive of apposition / epexegetical ("the wine, namely the wrath" — i.e., the wine IS the wrath)
  - τοῦ θυμοῦ τῆς πορνείας — genitive of source or attributive ("the wrath that her fornication produces/incurs")
  - τῆς πορνείας αὐτῆς — possessive ("her fornication")
- **Exegetical weight:** The ambiguity is load-bearing. Is this the wrath produced BY her fornication (the intoxicating seduction she pours out) or the wrath resulting FROM her fornication (the divine wrath earned by it)? In 14:8 the first sense fits (Babylon is the actor making nations drink); in 14:10 the second sense surfaces (those who drank her wrath now drink God's wrath in response). The parallel construction in v. 10 is deliberate: the drinker drinks twice, by two different suppliers.
- **Grammar reference:** [genitive-of-quality-apposition.md](../greek/genitive-of-quality-apposition.md)

### 4. τοῦ κεκερασμένου ἀκράτου — Perfect Passive Participle (Rev 14:10)

- **Form:** Perfect passive participle, genitive singular masculine, from κεράννυμι ("to mix"). Modifies τοῦ θυμοῦ (articular adjectival participle). Paired with adjective ἀκράτου ("unmixed, undiluted").
- **Significance:** This is deliberate Greek oxymoron. κεράννυμι normally means "mix (wine with water to dilute it)" — the standard Greek practice. The perfect passive captures completed action with abiding state: "having been mixed (= prepared)." But the predicate adjective ἄκρατος ("un-mixed") negates the expected dilution. Literally: "which has been mixed unmixed." The paradox conveys: the cup has been carefully prepared, yet without the customary mercy-dilution of water. God's wrath is served at full concentration.
- **Why the perfect:** Passive perfect indicates the cup stands prepared. The preparation is complete and its state endures — the cup is ready now. This is also a candidate for the **divine passive** (agent implied as God himself).
- **Grammar reference:** [participle-perfect-passive-state.md](../greek/participle-perfect-passive-state.md), [divine-passive.md](../greek/divine-passive.md)

### 5. πίεται … βασανισθήσεται — Future Middle / Future Passive Pair (Rev 14:10)

- **Form:** πίεται — future middle indicative (deponent; πίνω uses middle forms for future active sense) 3rd singular "shall drink." βασανισθήσεται — future passive indicative 3rd singular "shall be tormented."
- **Significance:** The shift from middle to passive is pointed. The worshipper actively drinks (middle — personal engagement; the subject drinks for himself). He then is passively tormented — the agent is no longer himself but the judgment imposed upon him. The grammar encodes the reversal of agency: the one who chose to drink is now the object acted upon.
- **Divine passive candidate:** βασανισθήσεται has no expressed agent. Given the context — the presence of the angels and the Lamb — the implied agent is divine judgment. This follows the Revelation pattern of passive verbs concealing the divine actor.
- **Grammar reference:** [divine-passive.md](../greek/divine-passive.md)

### 6. οἱ προσκυνοῦντες — Articular Present Participle as Substantival Subject (Rev 14:11)

- **Form:** Present active participle, nominative plural masculine, of προσκυνέω ("worship"), with definite article οἱ. "The ones (who are) worshiping."
- **Significance:** The articular participle functions as a substantive (= a noun phrase) and names the subject of ἔχουσιν ("they have no rest"). The present tense is imperfective: it views worship as ongoing habitual action, not a single past act. Grammar defines the class: those characterized by persistent worship of the beast. The participle's aspect matters — John does not say "those who once worshiped" (aorist) but "those who are worshiping" (present). The torment is linked to a continuing condition, not a past lapse.
- **Contrast with εἴ τις … λαμβάνει:** The participial clause gives the general class; the conditional clause adds an extension ("and if anyone receives…"). The pairing ensures nobody escapes the description by claiming their participation was only passive mark-reception, not active worship.

### 7. εἰς αἰῶνας αἰώνων — Semitic Intensifying Genitive (Rev 14:11)

- **Form:** Preposition εἰς + accusative plural αἰῶνας ("ages") + genitive plural αἰώνων ("of ages"). The construction stacks the same noun in two cases.
- **Significance:** A Semitic superlative-by-repetition pattern (like "King of kings," "holy of holies"). Literally "unto ages of ages" = "unto the uttermost ages." The genitive plural of the same noun intensifies rather than specifies. It measures duration by compounding — ages extending through ages. Paired with the present ἀναβαίνει ("ascends"), the smoke is pictured as continuously rising through these compounded ages.
- **Exegetical note:** The grammar expresses maximal duration in the lexical stock available; theological questions about what "ages of ages" entails (unending, age-lasting, or some other category) are resolved by broader canonical usage, not by this construction alone.

### 8. Εἴ τις προσκυνεῖ — First-Class Condition (Rev 14:9)

- **Form:** εἰ + present active indicative. First-class conditional ("if, and assume it's so for argument's sake").
- **Significance:** The first-class condition does not assert that the protasis is true in reality — it asserts truth "for the sake of argument." Here it sets up the legal frame: "Given that anyone does worship the beast and receive the mark, the consequence is…" The grammar sets a universal category: any person who falls under this description falls under this judgment. The present tense in both protasis and the verbs of v. 11 (λαμβάνει, προσκυνοῦντες) keeps the category generic, not time-bound.

## Clause Structure (Greek)

**v. 8 structure:**
- Main clause: ἄλλος ἄγγελος … ἠκολούθησεν ("another angel followed")
- Attendant participle: λέγων (introduces direct speech)
- Direct speech: Ἔπεσεν ἔπεσεν Βαβυλὼν ἡ μεγάλη (proclamation)
- Relative clause: ἣ … πεπότικεν πάντα τὰ ἔθνη (gives the ground/explanation — NOT causal ὅτι but explanatory relative)

**v. 9-10 structure:**
- Main clause: ἄλλος ἄγγελος τρίτος ἠκολούθησεν … λέγων ἐν φωνῇ μεγάλῃ
- Direct speech opens with first-class conditional protasis (v. 9): Εἴ τις προσκυνεῖ … καὶ λαμβάνει …
- Apodosis in v. 10a-b: καὶ αὐτὸς πίεται … καὶ βασανισθήσεται
- Note: καί + αὐτὸς at the head of the apodosis is emphatic — "he himself (in turn) shall drink"

**v. 11 structure:**
- Main clause 1: ὁ καπνὸς … ἀναβαίνει
- Main clause 2: οὐκ ἔχουσιν ἀνάπαυσιν (with articular participle as subject: οἱ προσκυνοῦντες)
- Coordinated conditional: καὶ εἴ τις λαμβάνει τὸ χάραγμα …

**Coordination pattern:** Throughout, John uses paratactic καί (coordinating conjunction) rather than subordinating structures. This is typical of Revelation's Semitic-tinged Greek. Logical dependence must be inferred from context, not from subordinating conjunctions.

## Intertextual Grammar Notes

- **Isa 21:9 LXX:** πέπτωκεν πέπτωκεν Βαβυλών — perfect, not aorist. John's aorist (Ἔπεσεν) is sharper: the completed single event rather than the resultant state. The shift tightens the proclamation to a decreed fact.
- **Jer 25:15-16 LXX:** cup of wine of this wrath (τοῦ οἴνου τοῦ ἀκράτου τούτου). The phrase "unmixed wine" feeds directly into Rev 14:10.
- **Ps 75:8 (74:9 LXX):** ποτήριον ἐν χειρὶ Κυρίου, οἴνου ἀκράτου πλῆρες κεράσματος — cup in Yahweh's hand, full of unmixed wine. This is the single closest lexical and conceptual parallel to Rev 14:10.
