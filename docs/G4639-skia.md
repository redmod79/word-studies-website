## Lexicon Definition

Thayer's: *shade* caused by the interception of light (literal), or *shadow* in the typological sense — an image cast by an object representing the form of that object.

(a) Literal shadow: Mark 4:32; Acts 5:15. In the set phrase **σκιά θανάτου** ("shadow of death") — "the densest darkness," tropically, the thick darkness of error and spiritual death: Matt 4:16; Luke 1:79 (from Isa 9:1, where the Hebrew is צַלְמָוֶת).

(b) Typological shadow: an outline or adumbration of the reality — contrasted with σῶμα (the body/substance itself) in Col 2:17, and with εἰκών (the express likeness/image itself) in Heb 10:1.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Physical Shade
Literal shadow cast by an object blocking light.

- **Mark 4:32** — "so that the fowls of the air may lodge under the shadow of it" (mustard tree)
- **Acts 5:15** — "that at the least the shadow of Peter passing by might overshadow some of them"

### 2. Shadow of Death (σκιά θανάτου)
Set phrase for deep gloom, mortal peril, or spiritual darkness. OT background: Isa 9:1 / Ps 23:4 (LXX σκιά θανάτου = Heb צַלְמָוֶת).

- **Matt 4:16** — "to them which sat in the region and shadow of death light is sprung up"
- **Luke 1:79** — "To give light to them that sit in darkness and in the shadow of death, to guide our feet into the way of peace"

### 3. Typological Shadow (OT as foreshadowing of Christ)
The Law, festivals, and tabernacle worship are a shadow cast by the coming substance — Christ and heavenly realities.

- **Col 2:17** — "Which are a shadow of things to come; but the body is of Christ" (τὸ δὲ σῶμα τοῦ Χριστοῦ)
- **Heb 8:5** — "Who serve unto the example and shadow of heavenly things" (tabernacle as σκιά of the heavenly sanctuary)
- **Heb 10:1** — "For the law having a shadow of good things to come, and not the very image of the things, can never with those sacrifices which they offered year by year continually make the comers thereunto perfect"

The "shadow of death" phrase (σκιά θανάτου) is the LXX rendering of the Hebrew compound צַלְמָוֶת (tsalmâveth), literally "deep darkness" or "death-shadow" (cf. Ps 23:4 Heb; Job 3:5; Isa 9:1). The ordinary word for physical shadow (צֵל, tsêl) underlies most other σκιά occurrences.
