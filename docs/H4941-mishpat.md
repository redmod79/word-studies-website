## Lexicon Definition

Properly, a verdict (favorable or unfavorable) pronounced judicially — especially a sentence or formal decree (human or divine), including the act, the place, the suit, the crime, and the penalty. Abstractly: justice, including a participant's right or privilege (statutory or customary), or even a style/custom.

KJV renders as: judgment, judgments, manner, right, ordinance, cause, lawful, sentence, custom, worthy, fashion, order, and others.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Covenant Legislation / Divine Statutes
Mishpat as God's ordinances given to Israel — the specific laws of the covenant. Commonly paired with חֻקִּים (chuqqim, statutes) as a formula for the whole body of Mosaic law.

- **Lev 18:4** — "Ye shall do my judgments, and keep mine ordinances, to walk therein: I am the LORD your God."
- **Deut 4:5** — "Behold, I have taught you statutes and judgments, even as the LORD my God commanded me."
- **Ezek 20:11** — "And I gave them my statutes, and shewed them my judgments, which if a man do, he shall even live in them."
- **Ps 119:137** — "Righteous art thou, O LORD, and upright are thy judgments."

### 2. Judicial Process / Legal Hearing
The formal act of appearing before a judge; a legal case being heard. Mishpat here = the proceedings themselves, not just the verdict.

- **Deut 1:17** — "Ye shall not respect persons in judgment; but ye shall hear the small as well as the great."
- **Deut 17:8** — "If there arise a matter too hard for thee in judgment, between blood and blood, between plea and plea..."
- **Josh 20:6** — "He shall dwell in that city, until he stand before the congregation for judgment, and until the death of the high priest."
- **Job 9:32** — "For he is not a man, as I am, that I should answer him, and we should come together in judgment."

### 3. Legal Verdict / Sentence
The decision or sentence rendered by a judge — the outcome of a judicial process.

- **1 Ki 3:28** — "And all Israel heard of the judgment which the king had judged; and they feared the king."
- **2 Ki 25:6** — "So they took the king, and brought him up to the king of Babylon to Riblah; and they gave judgment upon him."
- **Deut 17:11** — "According to the sentence of the law which they shall teach thee, and according to the judgment which they shall tell thee, thou shalt do."
- **Jer 4:12** — "Now also will I give sentence against them." (mishpat = sentence)

### 4. Social Justice / Rights of the Vulnerable
The just treatment owed to the poor, widow, fatherless, and stranger. Mishpat here = the legal right that protects the weak, and the obligation to uphold it.

- **Deut 10:18** — "He doth execute the judgment of the fatherless and widow, and loveth the stranger, in giving him food and raiment."
- **Isa 1:17** — "Learn to do well; seek judgment, relieve the oppressed, judge the fatherless, plead for the widow."
- **Jer 22:3** — "Execute ye judgment and righteousness, and deliver the spoiled out of the hand of the oppressor."
- **Mic 6:8** — "What doth the LORD require of thee, but to do justly (mishpat), and to love mercy, and to walk humbly with thy God?"

### 5. Divine Justice as Attribute of God's Rule
Mishpat as the foundation of God's reign — what makes his governance righteous and trustworthy.

- **Deut 32:4** — "He is the Rock, his work is perfect: for all his ways are judgment: a God of truth and without iniquity, just and right is he."
