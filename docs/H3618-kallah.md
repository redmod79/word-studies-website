## Lexicon Definition

From כָּלַל ("complete, perfect"); a bride (as if perfect); hence, a son's wife: — bride, daughter-in-law, spouse.

The noun spans two kinship senses held together by a single idea: the young woman brought into a household through marriage. In narrative prose she is almost always "daughter-in-law" (the bride viewed from the groom's family); in poetic/prophetic contexts she is "bride" (the young woman in her wedding role) — and Song of Solomon uniquely uses it as a tender address, rendered "spouse."
