## Semantic Categories

### 1. Bitter Taste — Nature Illustration (Literal)
- **James 3:11** — "Doth a fountain send forth at the same place sweet [water] and bitter?"

The literal sense: acrid, unpleasant to taste. James draws on the physical impossibility of a single spring producing both sweet (glykys G1099) and bitter (pikros) water simultaneously. The adjective here describes water quality — a natural, observable property.

### 2. Bitter Envying — Moral Quality (Figurative)
- **James 3:14** — "But if ye have bitter envying and strife in your hearts, glory not, and lie not against the truth."

The figurative sense: harsh, resentful, malicious. "Bitter envying" (pikron zelon) describes jealousy characterized by acrid hostility — a disposition that is internally corrosive and relationally destructive. The adjective modifies zelos (G2205, zeal/jealousy), marking it as the malicious variety rather than righteous zeal.
