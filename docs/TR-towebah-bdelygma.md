## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same across testaments:**
- Divine evaluative verdict: "abomination **to / before** God" formula is preserved (Deut/Pro usage → Luke 16:15)
- Moral and cultic senses bundled together — βδέλυγμα inherits towebah's broad ethical scope
- Pairing with sexual / idolatrous / commercial corruption persists (Pro 6 list → Rev 17's Babylon catalog)

**What shifts:**
- NT βδέλυγμα becomes far rarer in raw count (6x vs. towebah's 112x) — the LXX's dominant indictment-word is not a high-frequency NT term
- The NT concentrates bdelygma in two registers: (1) eschatological quotation of Daniel (Matt 24 / Mark 13, which actually echo shiqquwts) and (2) Revelation's apocalyptic Babylon imagery (17:4–5; 21:27). The broad Deuteronomic-sapiential usage of towebah appears only in Luke 16:15
- Where towebah catalogs dozens of specific acts (weights, lying lips, idolatry, sexual sin, bloody city), NT bdelygma is typically totalized into a single symbolic referent ("the abomination of desolation," "mother of abominations")
