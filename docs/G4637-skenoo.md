## Lexicon Definition

**Thayer:** to fix one's tabernacle, have one's tabernacle, abide (or live) in a tabernacle (or tent), tabernacle (often in Xenophon, Demosthenes). Of God — σκηνώσει ἐπ' αὐτούς: will spread his tabernacle over them, so that they may dwell in safety and security under its cover and protection (Rev 7:15). Universally equivalent to "to dwell": followed by ἐν with dative of place (Rev 12:12; 13:6); ἐν ἡμῖν, among us (John 1:14); μετά τίνος, with one (Rev 21:3). Compare: ἐπισκηνόω, κατασκηνόω.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. God / Christ Dwelling Among Humanity

The verb's primary pattern: the divine taking up tent-dwelling with people. Parallels the wilderness tabernacle where YHWH's glory-presence dwelt (שָׁכַן, H7931) in the midst of Israel — but now embodied and eschatologically fulfilled.

- **John 1:14** — "And the Word was made flesh, and dwelt [eskenōsen] among us, (and we beheld his glory, the glory as of the only begotten of the Father,) full of grace and truth."
- **Revelation 7:15** — "he that sitteth on the throne shall dwell among them."
- **Revelation 21:3** — "he will dwell with them, and they shall be his people, and God himself shall be with them, and be their God."

### 2. Heavenly Inhabitants Dwelling in Heaven

Used of those — angels or the redeemed — who occupy the heavenly realm, contrasted with earth-dwellers (κατοικέω, G2730).

- **Revelation 12:12** — "Therefore rejoice, ye heavens, and ye that dwell in them. Woe to the inhabiters of the earth and of the sea!"
- **Revelation 13:6** — "And he opened his mouth in blasphemy against God, to blaspheme his name, and his tabernacle, and them that dwell in heaven."

## Hebrew Source

No LXX alignment data available for G4637 (this verb does not appear in the LXX corpus in the DB). The theological background is the Hebrew שָׁכַן (shākan, H7931) — YHWH's tent-dwelling in the midst of Israel — and the cognate noun מִשְׁכָּן (mishkān, H4908, "tabernacle"). NT authors deploy σκηνόω as the Greek equivalent carrying this OT dwelling-presence theology. The LXX's primary rendering of shākan is the cognate verb G2681 κατασκηνόω (46x); σκηνόω itself appears as a theologically loaded NT choice by authors aware of the LXX connection.
