## Semantic Categories

### 1. Messianic Title — "Lamb of God"
- **Jhn 1:29** — "Behold the Lamb of God, which taketh away the sin of the world."
- **Jhn 1:36** — "And looking upon Jesus as he walked, he saith, Behold the Lamb of God!"

John the Baptist's declaration identifying Jesus as the sacrificial lamb. Both uses pair amnos with "of God" (τοῦ θεοῦ), forming a unique christological title found nowhere else in the NT.

### 2. Sacrificial Innocence — Lamb Led to Slaughter
- **Act 8:32** — "He was led as a sheep to the slaughter; and like a lamb dumb before his shearer, so opened he not his mouth:"

A quotation of Isaiah 53:7 (LXX) applied to Jesus by the Ethiopian eunuch's reading. Emphasizes the lamb's silence and submission — its innocence under violence.

### 3. Unblemished Offering — Redemptive Blood
- **1Pe 1:19** — "But with the precious blood of Christ, as of a lamb without blemish and without spot:"

Evokes Passover/sacrificial imagery (cf. Exod 12:5). The lamb's blood is the means of redemption, qualified by ἄμωμος (amomos, unblemished) and ἄσπιλος (aspilos, spotless).
