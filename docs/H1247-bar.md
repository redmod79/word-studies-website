## Lexicon Definition

(Aramaic) corresponding to [H1121 בֵּן]; a son, grandson, etc. — "old, son."

Aramaic noun used exactly as Hebrew *ben*: literal offspring, descendant, membership in a class ("son of X" = one characterized by X), or age designation ("son of N years" = N years old). Construct form dominant; appears in phrases `bar + PN` (patronymic) and `bar + noun` (classifying idiom).

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Literal Son / Patronymic ("son of X")
- **Ezra 5:1** — "Zechariah the son (bar) of Iddo, prophesied unto the Jews"
- **Ezra 5:2** — "Zerubbabel the son (bar) of Shealtiel, and Jeshua the son (bar) of Jozadak"
- **Ezra 6:14** — "Zechariah the son (bar) of Iddo"
- **Dan 5:22** — "And thou his son (bar), O Belshazzar, hast not humbled thine heart"

Standard Aramaic genealogical formula, direct parallel to Hebrew *ben*.

### 2. Age Designation ("son of N years" = N years old)
- **Dan 5:31** — "Darius the Median took the kingdom, [being] about threescore and two years old (bar shenin shittin wetharteyn)"

Idiomatic use: *bar* + number + "years" = the person is that many years old. Matches Hebrew idiom (*ben shanim*).

### 3. Member of a Class / Qualitative "Son of" (idiom)
- **Dan 3:25** — "the form of the fourth is like the Son (bar) of God" — Aramaic *bar-elahin*; KJV capitalizes "Son" but the syntax is the classifying idiom ("one belonging to the category of deity / a divine being"). Nebuchadnezzar is describing divine likeness as he perceives it.
- **Dan 7:13** — "[one] like the Son (bar) of man came with the clouds of heaven" — Aramaic *kebar enash*; the classifying idiom ("one belonging to the category of human / humanlike figure"), sharpened by the preposition *ke-* ("like"). The figure appears human in form while arriving on clouds of heaven — an attribute the OT elsewhere reserves to deity (Ps 104:3; Isa 19:1).

This third category is where the word does its heaviest theological lifting. *Bar enash* / *bar elahin* both function as class-membership idioms ("a human one," "a divine one"), not genealogical.

## Dan 7:13 — *kebar enash* Close Reading

**Text:** כְּבַר אֱנָשׁ — *ke-bar enash*

- **כְּ (ke-)** — preposition "like / as"
- **בַּר (bar)** — construct "son of" (H1247)
- **אֱנָשׁ (enash)** — "mankind, human" (H606, Aramaic counterpart to Hebrew *enosh* H582)

**Literal:** "like a son of man" — i.e., "like a human figure" / "like one of humankind."

**Grammatical force of *ke-*:** The *ke-* of comparison does not deny humanity; it marks visionary mode ("I saw what looked like…"), standard apocalyptic idiom (cf. Ezek 1:26 Hebrew *ke-demuth ke-mareh adam*; Dan 10:16 *kidemuth beney adam*). The figure has human form, but the vision genre requires the *ke-* marker.

**Construct *bar enash*:** In Biblical Aramaic, *bar* + collective/abstract noun = member of that class. *Bar enash* is idiomatic for "a human being" (cf. Ps 146:3 Hebrew *ben-adam*). Collocation with Hebrew *ben-adam* (Ezek's 93x address) and Aramaic *bar enash* (Dan 7:13 only) is direct.

**What makes Dan 7:13 unique is not *bar enash* itself — the phrase would be an ordinary way to say "a human" — but the context:**
- arrives "with the clouds of heaven" (Aram. *im-ananey shemayya*) — cloud-coming elsewhere belongs to YHWH (Ps 104:3; Isa 19:1; Deut 33:26)
- is "brought near before" (Haphel of *qrb*, H7127 root) the Ancient of Days
- receives "dominion, and glory, and a kingdom, that all people, nations, and languages should serve him" (Dan 7:14) — *pelach* (serve), used elsewhere in Daniel for worship of God (Dan 3:12, 17, 18; 6:16, 20; 7:27)
- his kingdom is "everlasting" and "shall not be destroyed" (Dan 7:14)

The word *bar* itself is mundane; the referent is not.

**NT uptake:** Jesus' self-designation "the Son of Man" (ὁ υἱὸς τοῦ ἀνθρώπου, always articular in the Gospels) draws directly on Dan 7:13. Mark 14:62 quotes Dan 7:13 + Ps 110:1 at the Sanhedrin trial; Matt 26:64; Rev 1:7, 13; 14:14 quote/allude. LXX of Dan 7:13 renders *kebar enash* as ὡς υἱὸς ἀνθρώπου / ὡς υἱὸν ἀνθρώπου (with manuscript variation on accusative vs nominative and on preposition).

## Notes on Usage Patterns

- Confined to Biblical Aramaic sections (Ezra 4:8–6:18; Dan 2:4b–7:28). Does not appear in Hebrew portions.
- Ezra uses are exclusively patronymic ("X son of Y"); theologically neutral.
- Daniel uses span four distinct idioms: patronymic (5:22), age (5:31), *bar-elahin* "son of deity" (3:25), *bar enash* "son of man" (7:13).
- Dan 7:13 is the only OT occurrence of the exact phrase *bar enash* and is the lexical anchor for all NT "Son of Man" language.
