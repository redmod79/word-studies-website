## Semantic Categories

### 1. Physical Horn of an Animal
The literal bony projection on a ram, bull, wild ox, or goat.

- **Gen 22:13** — "a ram caught in a thicket by his horns"
- **Deut 33:17** — "his horns [are like] the horns of unicorns [wild ox]: with them he shall push the people"
- **Ezek 34:21** — "ye have thrust...and pushed all the diseased [sheep] with your horns, till ye have scattered them"
- **1 Kgs 22:11** — "Zedekiah...made him horns of iron: and he said, With these shalt thou push the Syrians"

### 2. Altar Horns (Ritual)
The four projecting corners of the tabernacle/temple altar, used in sin-offering blood rites and as a place of asylum.

- **Exod 27:2** — "thou shalt make the horns of it upon the four corners thereof: his horns shall be of the same"
- **Lev 4:7** — "the priest shall put [some] of the blood upon the horns of the altar of sweet incense before the LORD"
- **1 Kgs 1:50** — "Adonijah...caught hold on the horns of the altar" (seeking sanctuary)
- **Amos 3:14** — "the horns of the altar shall be cut off, and fall to the ground" (judgment on Israel's shrines)

### 3. Oil Flask / Container
A hollow animal horn repurposed as a vessel for anointing oil.

- **1 Sam 16:1** — "the LORD said unto Samuel...fill thine horn with oil, and go, I will send thee to Jesse"
- **1 Sam 16:13** — "Samuel took the horn of oil, and anointed him in the midst of his brethren"
- **1 Kgs 1:39** — "Zadok the priest took an horn of oil out of the tabernacle, and anointed Solomon"

### 4. Figurative: Power, Honor, Might
Used metaphorically for strength, national power, personal honor, or royal dignity. "Exalting the horn" = rising to power; "cutting off the horn" = defeat or humiliation.

- **1 Sam 2:1** — "mine horn is exalted in the LORD" (Hannah's prayer of triumph)
- **Ps 75:4–5** — "Lift not up the horn...Lift not up your horn on high: speak [not with] a stiff neck"
- **Ps 89:24** — "in my name shall his horn be exalted" (promise to the Davidic king)
- **Ps 148:14** — "He also exalteth the horn of his people"
- **Lam 2:3** — "He hath cut off in his fierce anger all the horn of Israel"
- **Jer 48:25** — "The horn of Moab is cut off, and his arm is broken, saith the LORD"

### 5. Prophetic / Apocalyptic Symbol
Horns represent kingdoms, rulers, or conquering world powers in prophetic visions.

- **Zech 1:18–19, 21** — "I saw four horns...These are the horns which have scattered Judah, Israel, and Jerusalem"
- **Dan 8:3** — "a ram which had two horns...the two horns were high; but one was higher than the other"
- **Dan 8:8–9** — "the great horn was broken; and for it came up four notable ones...out of one of them came forth a little horn"
- **Dan 8:20–21** — "The ram...the kings of Media and Persia...the great horn...is the first king [of Grecia]"

### 6. Peak / Ray (Geometric Sense)
A projecting point — applied to a mountain summit or beams of radiant light.

- **Isa 5:1** — "My wellbeloved hath a vineyard in a very fruitful hill [qeren]" (brow/peak of a fertile hill)
- **Hab 3:4** — "he had horns [qarnayim] coming out of his hand: and there was the hiding of his power" (rays of glory emanating from God)
