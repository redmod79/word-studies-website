## Lexicon Definition

A primitive root; properly, to bind firmly, i.e. (by implication) to be (causatively to make) plump; also (through the figurative sense of dumbness) to dream:— (cause to) dream(-er), be in good liking, recover.

The word carries two related but distinct senses: (1) the concrete/physiological idea of being firm, plump, or in good health; (2) the figurative idea of dreaming, evidently through the notion of the "dumbness" or stillness of sleep. The dreaming sense dominates usage (~90%); the "be healthy/recover" sense survives in a few texts (Job 39:4, Isa 38:16).

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Revelatory / Divine Dream
Dreaming as a channel for divine communication — the patriarchal and royal-court pattern.
- **Gen 28:12** — "And he dreamed, and behold a ladder set up on the earth…" (Jacob's theophanic dream)
- **Gen 37:5** — "And Joseph dreamed a dream, and he told it his brethren"
- **Gen 41:1, 5** — Pharaoh's paired dreams of kine and ears
- **Dan 2:1, 3** — "Nebuchadnezzar dreamed dreams, wherewith his spirit was troubled"

### 2. Ordinary / Narrative Dream
Dreams recounted for narrative purposes without explicit theophanic framing.
- **Gen 40:5, 8** — the butler and baker "dreamed a dream both of them"
- **Gen 41:11** — "And we dreamed a dream in one night"
- **Judg 7:13** — Midianite soldier: "Behold, I dreamed a dream"

### 3. False / Deceptive Prophetic Dream
Dreams claimed as revelation by false prophets — uniformly condemned.
- **Deut 13:1, 3, 5** — "a prophet, or a dreamer (חֹלֵם) of dreams… shall be put to death"
- **Jer 23:25** — "I have heard what the prophets said, that prophesy lies in my name, saying, I have dreamed, I have dreamed"
- **Jer 29:8** — "Let not your prophets and your diviners… nor hearken to your dreams which ye cause to be dreamed" (Hiphil: self-induced / fabricated dreams)

### 4. Eschatological / Spirit-Outpouring Dream
Dreaming as a sign of the Spirit poured out in the last days.
- **Joel 2:28** — "your old men shall dream dreams" (quoted in Acts 2:17 using G1797 ἐνυπνιάζομαι)

### 5. Illusion / Vanishing Dream (Simile)
The dream used as a figure of unreality or fleeting experience.
- **Ps 126:1** — "we were like them that dream"
- **Isa 29:8** — "as when an hungry man dreameth, and, behold, he eateth; but he awaketh, and his soul is empty"

### 6. Health / Vigor / Recovery (Non-Dream Sense)
The root's concrete meaning "to be plump / firm / well."
- **Job 39:4** — wild asses' young "are in good liking (יַחְלְמוּ), they grow up with corn"
- **Isa 38:16** — Hezekiah: "make me to live (תַחֲלִימֵנִי)… so wilt thou recover me"
