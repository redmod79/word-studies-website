# ἄνθρωπος (G444) + Article: Individual vs. Class/Type Referent (Greek)

## Definition

Greek `ἄνθρωπος` ("man, human") is a common noun whose **referential scope depends heavily on article usage, attributive modifiers, and surrounding context**. A singular articular or anarthrous `ἄνθρωπος` can refer to (a) a specific historical individual, (b) a class representative / generic human type, (c) a prophesied future individual, or (d) a typological figure embodying a principle where the "one" stands in for (or is head of) a corporate whole. Parallel usage with the Aramaic idiom `כְּבַר אֱנָשׁ` / Greek `ὡς υἱὸς ἀνθρώπου` ("as [a] son of man") and the LXX symbolic equation `τέσσαρες ἄνθρωποι = four kingdoms` shows that singular "man" terminology can encode a **succession or corporate entity**, not exclusively a lone historical person.

1. **Historical individual** — ὁ/ὁ-less ἄνθρωπος with proper name in apposition; unique referent.
2. **Class representative (generic)** — ὁ ἄνθρωπος + theological/ethical genitive; any qualifying member of the class.
3. **Prophesied future individual** — ὁ ἄνθρωπος + vice-genitive in apocalyptic context; definite eschatological figure.
4. **Typological / corporate-head figure** — εἷς ἄνθρωπος or bare ἄνθρωπος functioning as head of a group whose actions are imputed to the whole; the "one" is simultaneously a real individual and the representative of a corporate body.

## Form Recognition

- Lemma: `ἄνθρωπος` (Strong's **G444**); declines as regular 2nd-declension masc.
- Singular nominative: `ἄνθρωπος` `[N-NSM]`; genitive `ἀνθρώπου` `[N-GSM]`; vocative `ἄνθρωπε` `[N-VSM]`.
- Article `ὁ` `[T-NSM]` (or oblique `τοῦ/τῷ/τόν`) attached to singular `ἄνθρωπος` flags a *definite* reading; absence of the article in prophetic / sentential contexts often signals *qualitative / generic* force.
- **Diagnostic modifiers:**
  - Proper-name apposition (`Χριστὸς Ἰησοῦς`) → historical individual.
  - Qualitative genitive (`τοῦ Θεοῦ`, `τῆς ἀνομίας`, `τῆς ἀπωλείας`) → type OR eschatological title; decided by context.
  - Numeral `εἷς` ("one") in contrast structures (ὥσπερ … οὕτως) → typological head.
  - Simile particle `ὡς / κατά` + anarthrous form → qualitative/symbolic, not individuating.
- **Parser search:** `lemma=ἄνθρωπος case=nominative number=singular gender=masculine`
- **Morph code pattern:** `N-NSM` (singular nom.), `N-GSM`, `N-VSM`.

## Functions with Examples

### 1. Specific Historical Individual

Article + ἄνθρωπος + proper name in apposition; the referent is uniquely identified by the appositive.

| Reference | Greek | Morph | KJV |
|-----------|-------|-------|-----|
| 1 Tim 2:5 | εἷς … μεσίτης Θεοῦ καὶ ἀνθρώπων, **ἄνθρωπος Χριστὸς Ἰησοῦς** | N-NSM + N-NSM + N-NSM | "the man Christ Jesus" |
| John 19:5 | καὶ λέγει αὐτοῖς Ἰδοὺ **ὁ ἄνθρωπος** | T-NSM N-NSM | "Behold the man!" |

Note 1 Tim 2:5 is technically *anarthrous* (`ἄνθρωπος Χριστὸς Ἰησοῦς`) — the proper-name apposition alone individuates; article not required when the identifier is a rigid designator. John 19:5 uses the article deictically ("this man right here").

### 2. Class Representative / Generic Type

Article + ἄνθρωπος + qualitative genitive describing a *kind* of person; any person meeting the qualification satisfies the reference.

| Reference | Greek | Morph | KJV |
|-----------|-------|-------|-----|
| 2 Tim 3:17 | ἵνα ἄρτιος ᾖ **ὁ τοῦ Θεοῦ ἄνθρωπος** | T-NSM T-GSM N-GSM N-NSM | "that the man of God may be perfect" |
| 1 Tim 6:11 | Σὺ δέ, ὦ **ἄνθρωπε Θεοῦ**, ταῦτα φεῦγε | N-VSM N-GSM | "O man of God" |

Vocative `ἄνθρωπε Θεοῦ` (1 Tim 6:11) is anarthrous and generic — the expression is a class title that Timothy instantiates; the phrase applied equally to OT prophets (cf. LXX 1 Kgdms 2:27; 9:6).

### 3. Prophesied Future Individual (apocalyptic idiom)

Article + ἄνθρωπος + "evil-attribute" genitive naming an eschatological figure; reading takes the expression as a unique future person.

| Reference | Greek | Morph | KJV |
|-----------|-------|-------|-----|
| 2 Thess 2:3 | ἀποκαλυφθῇ **ὁ ἄνθρωπος τῆς ἀνομίας, ὁ υἱὸς τῆς ἀπωλείας** | T-NSM N-NSM T-GSF N-GSF / T-NSM N-NSM T-GSF N-GSF | "that man of sin be revealed, the son of perdition" |

The doubled articular title (`ὁ ἄνθρωπος … ὁ υἱὸς …`) and the passive `ἀποκαλυφθῇ` (apocalyptic unveiling) support a definite eschatological referent under this reading.

### 4. Typological / Corporate-Head Figure (one-for-many)

`εἷς ἄνθρωπος` (with or without article) placed in ὥσπερ … οὕτως parallels; the single named figure is head of a corporate class whose destiny is imputed through him.

| Reference | Greek | Morph | KJV |
|-----------|-------|-------|-----|
| Rom 5:12 | δι' **ἑνὸς ἀνθρώπου** ἡ ἁμαρτία εἰς τὸν κόσμον εἰσῆλθεν | A-GSM N-GSM | "by one man sin entered into the world" |
| Rom 5:19 | διὰ τῆς παρακοῆς **τοῦ ἑνὸς ἀνθρώπου** ἁμαρτωλοὶ κατεστάθησαν οἱ πολλοί | T-GSM A-GSM N-GSM | "by one man's disobedience many were made sinners" |

The shift in Rom 5:19 from anarthrous `ἑνὸς ἀνθρώπου` (5:12) to articular `τοῦ ἑνὸς ἀνθρώπου` specifies *that* one man previously named (Adam), while the parallel `τοῦ ἑνός` (without `ἀνθρώπου`) points to the second Adam. The "one" is simultaneously historical AND corporate-representational — hence the symmetry `ἥμαρτον` (Adam→all) / `δίκαιοι κατασταθήσονται οἱ πολλοί` (Christ→many).

### 5. Corporate / Successional Referent in Apocalyptic Simile

Anarthrous ἄνθρωπος preceded by `ὡς / κατά` or functioning inside a symbolic tableau; the "man" language denotes a corporate entity, a succession of persons, or a kingdom.

| Reference | Aramaic / Greek | Parsing | Rendering |
|-----------|-----------------|---------|-----------|
| Dan 7:13 MT | **כְּבַר אֱנָשׁ** אָתֵה הֲוָה | Prep + Noun.ms.Cst + Noun.ms.Abs | "[one] like the Son of man came" |
| Dan 7:13 LXX/Θ | **ὡς υἱὸς ἀνθρώπου** ἐρχόμενος | ADV + N-NSM + N-GSM | "as a son of man" |
| Dan 7:17 MT | **אַרְבְּעָה מַלְכִין** יְקוּמוּן | Adj + Noun.mp.Abs + Verb.peal.Impf.3mp | "four kings shall arise" |
| Dan 7:17 LXX-OG | τέσσαρες **ἄνθρωποι** (for MT מַלְכִין) | N-NPM | "four men" (= four kingdoms/kings) |
| Dan 7:22 MT | מַלְכוּתָא הֶחֱסִנוּ **קַדִּישִׁין** | Verb.haf.Perf.3mp + Adj.mp.Abs | "the saints possessed the kingdom" |
| Dan 7:27 MT | יְהִיבַת **לְעַם קַדִּישֵׁי עֶלְיוֹנִין** | Verb.peil.Perf.3fs + Prep+Noun.Cst + Adj.mp.Cst + Adj.mp.Abs | "given to the people of the saints of the most High" |

Two structural facts emerge from the parser data:

1. **Dan 7:17 LXX-OG uses singular-like human terminology (`ἄνθρωποι` / MT `מַלְכִין`) for non-human corporate entities** (four successive kingdoms — vv.3-7 beasts). Singular "man" terminology can symbolize a **kingdom or its sequence of rulers**, not a single historical person.
2. **The "one like a son of man" of Dan 7:13 receives the kingdom in v.14, and the identical kingdom is given to "the saints" (plural) in vv.22, 27.** Within one vision the singular human-figure and the corporate saints are assigned the same dominion by the same judicial act — supporting a reading where `כְּבַר אֱנָשׁ / ὡς υἱὸς ἀνθρώπου` is at once an individual Messiah AND the corporate head whose receipt of the kingdom is shared with the saints.

The simile particle `ὡς` in Dan 7:13 LXX + anarthrous forms (`υἱὸς ἀνθρώπου`) is the key morphological signal: this is **qualitative / symbolic ("something manlike, something human-kingdom-like")**, parallel to the qualitative `ὡς λέων / ὡς ἄρκος` of the preceding beasts (vv.4-7). The grammar itself neither forces nor forbids individual vs. corporate reading — genre (apocalyptic vision) + internal parallel (v.13 ↔ vv.22, 27) drives the exegesis.

## Criteria for Distinguishing Functions

A decision procedure for any given `ἄνθρωπος`-expression:

| Criterion | Individual (1, 3) | Class / Type (2) | Corporate / Successional (4, 5) |
|-----------|------------------|------------------|----------------------------------|
| Definite article | Usually present (deictic `ὁ`) | Present (generic `ὁ`) | Often anarthrous or with `ὡς`/`εἷς` |
| Modifier type | Proper name apposition | Qualitative genitive (`τοῦ Θεοῦ`) | Contrast numeral `εἷς` or simile `ὡς` |
| Verbs attributed | Historical acts (ἐξῆλθεν, εἶπεν) | Gnomic / hortatory (φεῦγε, ᾖ ἄρτιος) | Receives/transfers (δίδοται, κατεστάθησαν, ἐρχόμενος + eschatological verbs) |
| Parallel passages | Narrative confirmation | Generic reuse (OT/NT) | Corporate reuse in same pericope (Dan 7:13 ↔ 7:22, 27; Rom 5 "one" ↔ "many") |
| Genre | Narrative / epistle | Paraenesis / wisdom | Apocalyptic vision, Adam–Christ typology |
| Plurality of acts | Single series of historical events | Repeatable pattern | Succession across time OR one act with imputed effects |

### Application to 2 Thess 2:3 (context-neutral)

The grammar admits **both readings** and does not by itself decide:

**Reading A — Prophesied future individual (function 3):**
- Doubled articular title `ὁ ἄνθρωπος … ὁ υἱὸς …` parallel to `ὁ ἄνθρωπος Χριστὸς Ἰησοῦς` (title-structure of 1 Tim 2:5).
- Passive `ἀποκαλυφθῇ` ("be revealed") echoes messianic-unveiling vocabulary and suggests a definite person at a definite moment.
- Singular `καθίσαι εἰς τὸν ναὸν τοῦ Θεοῦ` (v.4) describes a specific historical seating act.

**Reading B — Typological / corporate-successional figure (function 4/5):**
- Parallel with Dan 7's apocalyptic idiom where singular "son of man" language maps onto a corporate entity (saints / kingdom) across the same vision (vv.13, 22, 27) — Paul is demonstrably drawing on Daniel (cf. v.4 "sits in the temple" ~ Dan 11:31–36; v.8 "consume with the breath of his mouth" ~ Isa 11:4 via Dan 7 imagery).
- Qualitative genitives (`τῆς ἀνομίας`, `τῆς ἀπωλείας`) function like Dan 7's beast-by-quality typing; such genitives elsewhere type a *class* (2 Tim 3:17 `ὁ τοῦ Θεοῦ ἄνθρωπος` = any godly man, not one person).
- The "mystery of lawlessness is already at work" (v.7, present `ἐνεργεῖται`) indicates the same entity spans from Paul's day to eschaton — more natural for a succession / corporate body than a single lifespan individual.
- Dan 7:17 LXX precedent: singular human terminology explicitly decoded as a corporate-successional realia (four kings = four kingdoms). Singular "man" in apocalyptic register ≠ necessarily one person.

The grammar does not settle the question; **morphology alone permits both**. Decision criteria: (a) weight given to Danielic source-grammar, (b) whether `ὁ ἄνθρωπος + evil-genitive` is read as proper-title (like 1 Tim 2:5) or as class-designation (like 2 Tim 3:17), (c) temporal span implied by `ἐνεργεῖται` (v.7).

## Note on Dan 7 Source-Grammar

The underlying Aramaic of Dan 7:13 is `כְּבַר אֱנָשׁ` — prep `כְּ` ("like") + construct `בַר` ("son") + `אֱנָשׁ` ("mankind"). Greek renders this `ὡς υἱὸς ἀνθρώπου` (LXX-OG, Θ). Key points:

- Both the MT and LXX are **anarthrous** on "man" — the phrase is qualitative, not individuating.
- NT articular `ὁ υἱὸς τοῦ ἀνθρώπου` (Gospels, Rev) adds double article not present in Daniel, individuating the figure by reference to "that known Danielic figure." The Gospel usage presupposes an identification, but does not remove the corporate substrate Daniel supplies.
- The verb sequence in Dan 7 — v.13 "came … was brought near" (`הַקְרְבוּהִי` Haphel 3mp) / v.14 "given dominion" (`יְהִב` peil) — is reused verbatim for the *saints* in v.22 `וּמַלְכוּתָא הֶחֱסִנוּ קַדִּישִׁין` ("the saints possessed the kingdom") and v.27 `יְהִיבַת לְעַם קַדִּישֵׁי עֶלְיוֹנִין` ("given to the people of the saints"). Same judicial act, same verb roots (`יהב` / receipt of kingdom), one singular receiver (v.13) and one plural receiver (vv.22, 27). The grammar presents them as co-referential OR as corporate-head-plus-body — both readings fit the morphology.
