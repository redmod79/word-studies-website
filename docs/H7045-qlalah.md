## Lexicon Definition

From H7043 (qalal); vilification — (ac-)curse(-d, -ing).

The noun denotes the state or content of being cursed. Derived from the root qalal ("to be light, trifling, contemptible"), it carries the sense of making someone or something insignificant, despised, or subject to divine disfavor. Distinguished from H423 alah (oath-curse / imprecation) and H3994 meerah (execration).

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Covenant Curse — Conditional Consequence of Disobedience
The dominant usage. In the Deuteronomic framework, qelalah is the negative pole of the blessing-curse alternative placed before Israel.
- **Deut 11:26** — "Behold, I set before you this day a blessing and a curse"
- **Deut 11:28** — "And a curse, if ye will not obey the commandments of the LORD your God"
- **Deut 28:15** — "all these curses shall come upon thee, and overtake thee"
- **Deut 30:19** — "I have set before you life and death, blessing and cursing: therefore choose life"

### 2. Status of Disgrace — Becoming a Byword Among Nations
Jeremiah uses qelalah as a term of national shame: Israel or other nations become "a curse" — an object lesson of what divine judgment looks like.
- **Jer 24:9** — "a reproach and a proverb, a taunt and a curse, in all places whither I shall drive them"
- **Jer 26:6** — "I will make this city a curse to all the nations of the earth"
- **Jer 42:18** — "ye shall be an execration, and an astonishment, and a curse, and a reproach"
- **Jer 44:22** — "your land a desolation, and an astonishment, and a curse, without an inhabitant"

### 3. Divine Reversal — Curse Turned to Blessing
A recurrent motif where God overrides an intended or operative curse.
- **Deut 23:5** — "the LORD thy God turned the curse into a blessing unto thee, because the LORD thy God loved thee"
- **Neh 13:2** — "our God turned the curse into a blessing"
- **Zech 8:13** — "as ye were a curse among the heathen... so will I save you, and ye shall be a blessing"

### 4. Personal Curse — Interpersonal Vilification
Used for curses between individuals, including the patriarchal narrative and Shimei's abuse of David.
- **Gen 27:12** — "I shall bring a curse upon me, and not a blessing"
- **Gen 27:13** — "Upon me be thy curse, my son"
- **1 Ki 2:8** — "Shimei... which cursed me with a grievous curse"
- **2 Sam 16:12** — "the LORD will requite me good for his cursing this day"

### 5. Curse as Moral Principle
Wisdom and liturgical uses treating curse as a general principle.
- **Prov 26:2** — "the curse causeless shall not come"
- **Prov 27:14** — "He that blesseth his friend with a loud voice... it shall be counted a curse to him"
- **Psa 109:17** — "As he loved cursing, so let it come unto him"

### 6. Theological Liability — The Hanged
The unique Deut 21:23 declaration that the exposed corpse is "accursed of God."
- **Deut 21:23** — "he that is hanged is accursed of God; that thy land be not defiled"
