## Lexicon Definition

A primitive root; to do or make, in the broadest sense and widest application: accomplish, advance, appoint, apt, be at, become, bear, bestow, bring forth, bruise, be busy, certainly, have the charge of, commit, deal (with), deck, displease, do, (ready) dress(-ed), (put in) execute(-ion), exercise, fashion, feast, (fight-) ing man, finish, fit, fly, follow, fulfill, furnish, gather, get, go about, govern, grant, great, hinder, hold (a feast), indeed, be industrious, journey, keep, labour, maintain, make, be meet, observe, be occupied, offer, officer, pare, bring (come) to pass, perform, practise, prepare, procure, provide, put, requite, sacrifice, serve, set, shew, sin, spend, surely, take, thoroughly, trim, very, vex, be (warr-) ior, work(-man), yield, use.

Asah is the most semantically generic Hebrew verb of action. Its meaning is almost entirely determined by its object and context. Alongside בָּרָא (bara, create ex nihilo) and יָצַר (yatsar, form/shape), asah is one of three foundational "making" verbs of the Hebrew Bible — but where bara reserves agency for God and yatsar emphasizes craftsmanship, asah is the workhorse verb that covers any purposeful activity.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Divine Creative Action
Asah denotes God's creative and fashioning work — distinct from bara (create) but frequently paralleled with it.
- **Gen 1:7** — "And God **made** the firmament"
- **Gen 1:16** — "And God **made** two great lights"
- **Gen 1:26** — "Let us **make** man in our image"
- **Gen 1:31** — "God saw every thing that he had **made**, and, behold, it was very good"
- **Gen 2:2** — "God ended his work which he had **made**"

### 2. Human Craftsmanship and Construction
Building, fabricating, fashioning artifacts — dominant in tabernacle/temple texts.
- **Gen 6:14-16** — "**Make** thee an ark of gopher wood… thus shalt thou **make** it"
- **Ex 25:8** — "Let them **make** me a sanctuary"
- **Ex 25:10** — "they shall **make** an ark of shittim wood"
- **1 Kgs 7:23** — "he **made** a molten sea" (Solomon's temple)

### 3. Keeping/Observing Ritual — "Make" a Feast or Offering
Asah carries the sense of "perform" or "keep" a covenant observance.
- **Ex 12:48** — "will **keep** the passover to the LORD"
- **Num 9:3** — "ye shall **keep** it in his appointed season"
- **Deu 16:10** — "thou shalt **keep** the feast of weeks"
- **Lev 23:12** — "ye shall **offer** that day… a he lamb"

### 4. Moral/Ethical Action (Doing Good or Evil)
The most theologically loaded usage — what one "does" defines covenant loyalty.
- **Gen 18:19** — "that they shall keep the way of the LORD, to **do** justice and judgment"
- **Gen 18:25** — "Shall not the Judge of all the earth **do** right?"
- **Deu 6:18** — "thou shalt **do** that which is right and good in the sight of the LORD"
- **Pro 2:14** — "who rejoice to **do** evil"
- **Eccl 3:12** — "to **do** good in his life"

### 5. Royal Evaluation Formula ("did right/evil in the sight of the LORD")
A stereotyped idiom dominating Kings and Chronicles.
- **1 Kgs 11:6** — "Solomon **did** evil in the sight of the LORD"
- **1 Kgs 15:11** — "Asa **did** that which was right in the eyes of the LORD"
- **2 Kgs 12:2** — "Jehoash **did** that which was right"
- **2 Kgs 21:2** — "Manasseh **did** that which was evil"

### 6. Covenant Action — God Acts on Behalf of His People
Asah names God's covenantal deeds and kindnesses.
- **Gen 21:1** — "The LORD **did** unto Sarah as he had spoken"
- **Deu 10:21** — "he is thy praise… who hath **done** for thee these great and terrible things"
- **2 Sam 7:23** — "the great things… which thou hast **done** for your land"
- **Psa 98:1** — "he hath **done** marvellous things"

### 7. Agricultural/Productive Action — "Yielding," "Dressing," "Bringing Forth"
Asah extends to the productive work of the land and its fruits.
- **Gen 1:11-12** — "the fruit tree **yielding** fruit" (asah governs the plant's activity)
- **Gen 2:15** — "to **dress** it and to keep it" (Eden)
- **Lev 25:21** — "it shall **bring forth** fruit for three years"
- **Isa 5:2** — "it should **bring forth** grapes" (vineyard parable)
- **Isa 37:30** — "ye shall sow, and reap, and plant vineyards, and eat the fruit thereof"

### 8. Passive — "It Shall Be Done / Made" (Niphal)
The Niphal stem expresses result, decree, or completion.
- **Gen 29:26** — "It must not be so **done** in our country"
- **Lev 4:2** — "commandments of the LORD concerning things which ought not to be **done**"
- **Est 6:9** — "thus shall it be **done** to the man whom the king delighteth to honour"
