## Lexicon Definition

From G4059 (περιτέμνω, to cut around); **circumcision** — the rite, the condition, or the people.

Outline of Biblical Usage: The covenant sign commanded in Genesis 17; the physical rite performed on the eighth day; the state of being circumcised; used collectively for the Jewish people (as distinct from ἀκροβυστία, uncircumcision / Gentiles); spiritually reapplied by Paul to inner transformation of the heart.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. The Physical Rite and Covenant Sign
Circumcision as the commanded act — eight-day rite, Abrahamic covenant marker, Mosaic observance:
- **Acts 7:8** — "he gave him the covenant of circumcision: and so Abraham begat Isaac, and circumcised him the eighth day"
- **John 7:22** — "Moses therefore gave unto you circumcision; (not because it is of Moses, but of the fathers;)"
- **Philippians 3:5** — "Circumcised the eighth day, of the stock of Israel, of the tribe of Benjamin"
- **Romans 4:11** — "he received the sign of circumcision, a seal of the righteousness of the faith which he had yet being uncircumcised"

### 2. Circumcision as Covenant Boundary Condition
"Being in circumcision" = being within the Abrahamic covenant; Paul tests whether this status is what counts with God:
- **Romans 2:25** — "circumcision verily profiteth, if thou keep the law: but if thou be a breaker of the law, thy circumcision is made uncircumcision"
- **Romans 4:10** — "when he was in circumcision, or in uncircumcision? Not in circumcision, but in uncircumcision"
- **Romans 3:1** — "What advantage then hath the Jew? or what profit is there of circumcision?"
- **Galatians 5:11** — "if I yet preach circumcision, why do I yet suffer persecution?"

### 3. "The Circumcision" — Collective for the Jewish People
The articular noun used as a group label, contrasted with "the uncircumcision" (Gentiles):
- **Galatians 2:7–8** — "the gospel of the uncircumcision was committed unto me... the apostleship of the circumcision"
- **Romans 15:8** — "Jesus Christ was a minister of the circumcision for the truth of God"
- **Acts 10:45** — "they of the circumcision which believed were astonished"
- **Titus 1:10** — "many unruly and vain talkers and deceivers, specially they of the circumcision"

### 4. Circumcision Spiritualized and Relativized in Christ
Paul redefines circumcision as inward heart-transformation, then declares it irrelevant to standing before God:
- **Romans 2:28–29** — "he is a Jew, which is one inwardly; and circumcision is that of the heart, in the spirit, and not in the letter"
- **1 Corinthians 7:19** — "Circumcision is nothing, and uncircumcision is nothing, but the keeping of the commandments of God"
- **Galatians 6:15** — "in Christ Jesus neither circumcision availeth any thing, nor uncircumcision, but a new creature"
- **Philippians 3:3** — "we are the circumcision, which worship God in the spirit, and rejoice in Christ Jesus, and have no confidence in the flesh"
- **Colossians 2:11** — "circumcised with the circumcision made without hands, in putting off the body of the sins of the flesh by the circumcision of Christ"

## Hebrew Source

No LXX bridge found by tools — περιτομή is a NT coinage not attested as a noun in the LXX. The LXX uses the verb περιτέμνω rather than the noun form. The OT rite corresponds to Hebrew **מוּל** (H4135, mûl = to circumcise) and the related noun **מוּלָה** (H4139, mûlāh = circumcision). The covenant command in Genesis 17 establishes the rite Paul is engaging throughout his letters.
