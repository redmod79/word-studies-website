## Semantic Categories

### 1. Post-Execution Display on a Tree
The primary and most frequent usage — hanging a body (or living person) on a tree/pole as public display after execution or as the mode of execution itself.
- **Gen 40:19** — "shall Pharaoh lift up thy head from off thee, and shall hang thee on a tree" (baker's execution)
- **Deut 21:22** — "if a man have committed a sin worthy of death, and he be to be put to death, and thou hang him on a tree"
- **Deut 21:23** — "he that is hanged is accursed of God" — the Deuteronomic law requiring same-day burial
- **Jos 8:29** — "the king of Ai he hanged on a tree until eventide"
- **Jos 10:26** — "hanged them on five trees: and they were hanging upon the trees until the evening"
- **2 Sam 4:12** — "hanged them up over the pool in Hebron" (Ish-bosheth's assassins)
- **2 Sam 21:12** — "the Philistines had hanged them" (Saul and Jonathan's bodies)

### 2. Gallows Execution (Esther)
The Esther corpus uses talah specifically for impalement or hanging on the gallows (*ets* = "tree/wood/gallows").
- **Est 2:23** — "they were both hanged on a tree" (Bigthan and Teresh)
- **Est 5:14** — "let Mordecai be hanged thereon" (Zeresh's counsel)
- **Est 7:9–10** — "So they hanged Haman on the gallows that he had prepared for Mordecai"
- **Est 9:13–14** — "let Haman's ten sons be hanged" (posthumous display)
- **Est 9:25** — "he and his sons should be hanged upon the gallows"

### 3. Suspending / Hanging Objects
A secondary, non-execution sense — suspending objects from a support.
- **Eze 15:3** — "will men take a pin of it to hang any vessel thereon?"
- **Eze 27:10–11** — "they hanged the shield and helmet in thee" (military display on Tyre's walls)
- **Song 4:4** — "Thy neck is like the tower of David... whereon there hang a thousand bucklers"
- **Isa 22:24** — "they shall hang upon him all the glory of his father's house"

### 4. Cosmic Suspension
A unique metaphorical use for God's creative act.
- **Job 26:7** — "hangeth the earth upon nothing" — divine suspension of the earth in space

### 5. Lament / Cessation
Hanging instruments as a symbol of grief.
- **Psa 137:2** — "We hanged our harps upon the willows in the midst thereof"

### 6. Bodily Exposure as Disgrace
- **Lam 5:12** — "Princes are hanged up by their hand" — public humiliation of leaders
- **2 Sam 18:10** — "I saw Absalom hanged in an oak" (caught by his hair, suspended alive)
