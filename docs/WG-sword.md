# WG: Sword

## Overview
The "sword" concept in the NT draws on two distinct Greek words — rhomphaia and machaira — along with descriptors and associated verbs that form the "sword from Christ's mouth" image in Revelation. The group captures both the physical weapon and its metaphorical force as the divine word of judgment.

## Relationships

### Shared Hebrew Source
Both rhomphaia (G4501) and machaira (G3162) primarily translate חֶרֶב (chereb, H2719) from the LXX. They overlap in representing the same Hebrew word, yet the NT authors choose between them deliberately:
- machaira = ordinary sword, including figurative uses (Peter's sword, the Spirit's sword in Eph 6:17)
- rhomphaia = the Revelation sword from Christ's mouth; the sole Lukan use (soul-piercing prophecy)

## Roles in the Concept

- **Instrument (large):** rhomphaia (ῥομφαία) — the apocalyptic broadsword, the word of divine judgment proceeding from Christ
- **Instrument (common):** machaira (μάχαιρα) — the everyday short sword, also used for the Spirit's sword in Eph 6
- **Quality 1:** oxys (ὀξύς) — sharp, emphasizing the cutting power of the judgment-word
- **Quality 2:** distomos (δίστομος) — two-edged, emphasizing that the sword cuts in every direction (no escape)
- **Source:** stoma (στόμα) — the mouth, the origin from which the sword issues
- **Action:** ekporeuomai (ἐκπορεύομαι) — the proceeding out, the active going-forth of the word
