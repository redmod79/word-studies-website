## Lexicon Definition

From H3648; properly, **an ascetic** (as if shrunk with self-maceration), i.e. **an idolatrous priest**. The KJV handles the word three different ways across its three occurrences: "the idolatrous priests," "the priests thereof," and "the Chemarims."

Across all three uses, *kâmâr* does not refer to Israel's ordinary priesthood in general. It denotes cult personnel attached to condemned worship settings: high places, calf worship, and Baal-linked shrine service.

## Morphology

- The lexical form is singular, but the lexicon notes the noun is used **only in the plural** in biblical usage.
- All three occurrences refer to a class of priests rather than to an individual priest.
- The noun is specialized, not a general synonym for **H3548 כֹּהֵן (kôhên)**.

## Semantic Categories

With only three occurrences, each verse effectively supplies its own usage category:

### 1. Royally Supported High-Place Priests
- **2 Ki 23:5** — "And he put down the idolatrous priests, whom the kings of Judah had ordained to burn incense in the high places..."

Here *kâmâr* names cult officials installed for unlawful incense worship at local shrines and in astral/Baal-related worship settings.

### 2. Priests Serving the Beth-aven Calf Shrine
- **Hos 10:5** — "The inhabitants of Samaria shall fear because of the calves of Bethaven... and the priests thereof [that] rejoiced on it..."

The word identifies shrine personnel bound up with the calf-cult of Beth-aven/Bethel. The verse emphasizes their dependence on the image's "glory," which is about to depart.

### 3. A Priestly Name Marked for Erasure
- **Zeph 1:4** — "I will cut off the remnant of Baal from this place, [and] the name of the Chemarims with the priests"

In Zephaniah the term stands in a judgment formula: not merely the men, but even the remembered **name** of this priestly class is to be cut off.
