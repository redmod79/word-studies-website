## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same:**
- Divine sovereignty framing — God's kingdom is eternal and universal (Psa 145:13 → Rev 11:15, 2 Pet 1:11)
- The kingdom is given, not seized — both OT (God establishes Solomon's kingdom) and NT (the Father gives the kingdom, Luke 12:32)
- Kingdom associated with throne, glory, and everlasting duration

**What shifts:**
- OT: ~27% of malkuwth uses are temporal regnal date formulas ("in the Nth year of the reign") — this usage is entirely absent from NT basileía
- OT: adjectival "royal" uses (Esther's "royal apparel," "crown royal") — absent from NT
- NT introduces "kingdom of heaven" (Matthew's Semitic circumlocution for "kingdom of God") — no direct OT parallel
- Shift from dynastic succession concerns (contested kingdoms, divided realms) to eschatological arrival: the kingdom "is at hand," "cannot be shaken," comes in power
