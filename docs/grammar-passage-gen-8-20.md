## Key Grammatical Features

### 1. וַיִּבֶן / וַיִּקַּח / וַיַּעַל — wayyiqtol narrative chain

- **Form:** Three finite verbs occur as wayyiqtol forms: Qal wayyiqtol 3ms `וַיִּבֶן`, Qal wayyiqtol 3ms `וַיִּקַּח`, and Hiphil wayyiqtol 3ms `וַיַּעַל`.
- **Significance:** The clause sequence presents three narrated actions in order: altar-building, selection from clean animals/birds, and offering of burnt offerings. The chain is narrative grammar, not legal instruction grammar.
- **Grammar reference:** [syntax-waw-conjunction](../hebrew/syntax-waw-conjunction.md), [stem-hiphil](../hebrew/stem-hiphil.md).

### 2. מִכֹּל הַבְּהֵמָה הַטְּהֹורָה / מִכֹּל הָעֹוף הַטָּהֹר — repeated "from every clean..." complements

- **Form:** Each phrase begins with `מִן` + `כֹּל` in construct, followed by a definite noun and an agreeing adjective: feminine `הַבְּהֵמָה הַטְּהֹורָה`; masculine `הָעֹוף הַטָּהֹר`.
- **Significance:** The clean category is grammatically applied to both beasts and birds, with adjective gender agreement tracking the head noun. The two source phrases are coordinated and together form the complement of `וַיִּקַּח`.
- **Grammar reference:** [construct-state](../hebrew/construct-state.md), [syntax-compound-object-waw](../hebrew/syntax-compound-object-waw.md).

### 3. וַיַּעַל עֹלֹת — Hiphil offering idiom

- **Form:** `וַיַּעַל` is Hiphil wayyiqtol 3ms of `עלה`, "cause to go up / bring up / offer." Its object is `עֹלֹת`, feminine plural absolute, "burnt offerings."
- **Significance:** The offering action is expressed by a causative verb plus the burnt-offering noun. Grammatically, Noah is the subject causing the offerings to ascend on the altar.
- **Grammar reference:** [stem-hiphil](../hebrew/stem-hiphil.md).

### 4. בַּמִּזְבֵּחַ — altar as governed location/complement

- **Form:** `בַּ` combines preposition `ב` plus article `ה`, followed by `מִזְבֵּחַ`, "the altar."
- **Significance:** The altar appears first as the direct object of `וַיִּבֶן` and finally as the location/complement of the offering action. The repeated noun frames the verse.
- **Grammar reference:** no dedicated altar syntax entry exists.

## Clause Structure

- **Clause 1:** `WayX`: `וַיִּבֶן` predicate; `נֹחַ` subject; `מִזְבֵּחַ` object; `לַיהוָה` complement.
- **Clause 2:** `Way0`: `וַיִּקַּח` predicate; coordinated source complement `מִכֹּל הַבְּהֵמָה הַטְּהֹורָה וּמִכֹּל הָעֹוף הַטָּהֹר`.
- **Clause 3:** `Way0`: `וַיַּעַל` predicate; `עֹלֹת` object; `בַּמִּזְבֵּחַ` complement.

## Construct Chains

- `כֹּל הַבְּהֵמָה הַטְּהֹורָה` — "all/every of the clean beast."
- `כֹּל הָעֹוף הַטָּהֹר` — "all/every of the clean bird."
