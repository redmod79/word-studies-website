## Lexicon Definition

From a presumed derivative of βδέω (bdeo, "to stink"). Active sense: to render foul, to cause to be abhorred, to defile, pollute. Middle/passive (deponent) sense: to turn oneself away from on account of the stench; metaphorically, to abhor, to detest. The word carries strong connotations of physical disgust transformed into moral revulsion, particularly regarding idolatry and moral corruption.

Thayer distinguishes two senses: (1) active — "to render foul, to cause abhorrence"; (2) middle (deponent) — "to turn oneself away from on account of the stench; to abhor, detest." Both NT occurrences use the middle/passive form.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Active Moral Revulsion Toward Idolatry
- **Rom 2:22** — "thou that abhorrest (βδελυσσόμενος) idols, dost thou commit sacrilege?" — The present middle participle describes a Jewish interlocutor who claims to detest idols yet contradicts that stance through temple-robbing (ἱεροσυλέω). The abhorrence is presented as professed but hypocritical.

### 2. Settled State of Moral Abomination (Eschatological Vice List)
- **Rev 21:8** — "the fearful, and unbelieving, and the abominable (ἐβδελυγμένοις), and murderers..." — The perfect passive participle describes persons who have become defined by abominable character. They appear in a vice list alongside the cowardly, faithless, murderers, sexually immoral, sorcerers, idolaters, and liars — all excluded from the New Jerusalem.
