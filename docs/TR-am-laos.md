## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same across testaments:**
- The covenant formula "I will be their God, and they shall be my people" runs from Lev 26:12 through Jer 31:33 to 2 Cor 6:16 and Rev 21:3, carried by the am → laos bridge without any semantic disruption
- Laos retains am's corporate/collective sense: the people as a unified body, not merely a numerical aggregate
- The possessive construction ("my people") remains the dominant covenant-identity marker in both testaments
- The "peculiar people" election formula (Exo 19:5, Deu 14:2) transfers verbatim into NT application (Tit 2:14, 1 Pet 2:9)

**What shifts:**
- The referent expands: in the OT, am/laos is predominantly ethnic Israel; in the NT (especially Acts 15:14, Rom 9:25, 1 Pet 2:10), laos comes to include Gentile believers — "which in time past [were] not a people, but [are] now the people of God"
- The Hosea reversal (lo-ammi → ammi) becomes the theological mechanism for this expansion: Paul quotes Hosea's "not my people / my people" language to explain Gentile inclusion (Rom 9:25–26)
- The am/laos vs. gowy/ethnos distinction partially collapses in the NT as ethnos-peoples are incorporated into the laos of God (Acts 15:14)
