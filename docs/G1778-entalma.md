## Semantic Categories

### 1. Human Religious Precepts Contrasted with Divine Worship
- **Mat 15:9** — "But in vain they do worship me, teaching for doctrines the commandments (entalmata) of men."
- **Mar 7:7** — "Howbeit in vain do they worship me, teaching for doctrines the commandments (entalmata) of men."

Both are Jesus quoting Isa 29:13 (LXX), contrasting genuine worship of God with human-originated religious rules.

### 2. Perishable Human Regulations
- **Col 2:22** — "Which all are to perish with the using; after the commandments (entalmata) and doctrines of men?"

Paul warns against submitting to human regulations ("touch not; taste not; handle not") that have no lasting authority — they perish with use.

All three occurrences share the same semantic profile: human-originated religious commands that lack divine authority and are set in opposition to genuine worship or freedom in Christ.

## Related Words

### Word Family / Cognates
- **G1781 ἐντέλλομαι (entellomai)** — root verb: to command, enjoin, charge (9v)
- **G1785 ἐντολή (entolē)** — injunction, commandment, authoritative precept (60v)

All three derive from the same ἐν + τέλλω root. The key distinction: ἐντολή is the standard NT word for "commandment" (divine or human), while ἔνταλμα appears exclusively for human-originated precepts contrasted negatively with God's will.
