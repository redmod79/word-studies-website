## Lexicon Definition

From ἐνεργής (energēs, G1756): "to be active, efficient; to put forth power, be operative, work, effect." Strong's: "to be active, efficient — do, (be) effectual (fervent), be mighty in, shew forth self, work (effectually in)." The compound ἐν + ἔργον carries the nuance of working *in* or working *through* something — activity that is internal, effective, and producing a result.

**Voice note:** Traditional grammar debates whether middle-voice forms (ἐνεργεῖται, ἐνεργουμένη, ἐνεργουμένην) are middle ("works itself, is at work") or passive ("is made to work, is being energized"). Many modern grammarians (e.g., J. Armitage Robinson, Ephesians) argue these are consistently middle — an intransitive "to be at work" — since Paul reserves active forms for personal agents (God, Spirit, the evil one in Eph 2:2) and middle forms for impersonal forces, processes, or abstractions (faith, death, word of God, affliction, mystery of lawlessness).
