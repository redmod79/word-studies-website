## Semantic Categories

### 1. Mixed / Spiced Wine as a Beverage (Prov 23:30)
The wine of the heavy drinker — prepared, blended, sought after. The mixing is with water, spices, or aromatics to intensify flavor or strength. The word occurs in a catalog of woe ("Who hath woe? who hath sorrow?") describing those who "tarry long at the wine."

- **Prov 23:30** — "They that tarry long at the wine; they that go to seek mixed wine (mamçâk)."

Here the mixture is the object of pursuit — the drunkard hunts blended wine for its potency. The parallelism sets `mamçâk` alongside simple `yayin` (wine) as its intensified counterpart.

### 2. Mingled Libation Poured to a Pagan Deity (Isa 65:11)
The same word names a cultic preparation — mixed wine poured out to the gods Gad (Fortune) and Meni (Destiny). The act is described in ritual verbs: "prepare a table… furnish the drink offering (mamçâk)."

- **Isa 65:11** — "But ye are they that forsake the LORD, that forget my holy mountain, that prepare a table for that troop (Gad), and that furnish the drink offering (mamçâk) unto that number (Meni)."

The KJV's `drink offering` here is unusual — H5262 (necek) is the standard word for drink offering. The choice of `mamçâk` in Isa 65:11 emphasizes the mixing itself — a blended, prepared libation for foreign gods, not the ordinary poured offering prescribed in Torah.
