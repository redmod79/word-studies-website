## Semantic Categories

### 1. Soteriological Redemption — Buying Out from Under the Law's Curse
- **Gal 3:13** — "Christ hath redeemed [ἐξηγόρασεν] us from the curse of the law, being made a curse for us: for it is written, Cursed is every one that hangeth on a tree"
- **Gal 4:5** — "To redeem [ἐξαγοράσῃ] them that were under the law, that we might receive the adoption of sons"

Both Galatians uses are aorist active with Christ as subject. The ἐκ- prefix emphasizes extraction — buying someone *out of* a condition. In 3:13, the condition is the curse (κατάρα) of the law; in 4:5, it is subjection *under* (ὑπό) the law. The purpose clause in 4:5 specifies the positive result: υἱοθεσία (adoption as sons).

### 2. Practical Wisdom — Buying Up the Opportune Time
- **Eph 5:16** — "Redeeming [ἐξαγοραζόμενοι] the time, because the days are evil"
- **Col 4:5** — "Walk in wisdom toward them that are without, redeeming [ἐξαγοραζόμενοι] the time"

Both uses are present middle participle with believers as subject. The object is τὸν καιρόν (the opportune time). The marketplace metaphor shifts: rather than extracting a person from bondage, one "buys up" every available opportunity. The middle voice suggests personal investment. Colossians pairs it with σοφία (wisdom) and evangelistic conduct toward outsiders.

## Related Words

### Word Family / Cognates
- **G59 ἀγοράζω (agorazō)** — root verb: to purchase in the marketplace (31 occurrences). The simple form without the ἐκ- prefix. Used of buying goods (Matt 13:44, 14:15), buying a field (Matt 27:7), and redemptive purchase (1 Cor 6:20 "ye are bought with a price"; Rev 5:9 "hast redeemed us... by thy blood").
- **G58 ἀγορά (agora)** — the marketplace itself; the noun from which agorazō derives.
- **G1537 ἐκ (ek)** — preposition "out of," denoting origin or extraction. The prefix that distinguishes exagorazō from agorazō, adding the sense of buying *out of* a condition.
