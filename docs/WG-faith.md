# WG — Faith / Belief

## Concept Overview

The faith word group centers on πίστις (pistis) and its cognates, covering the full semantic range of believing, trusting, and being faithful. In the NT, this cluster appears most densely in Paul's justification theology (Romans, Galatians) and the Hebrews 11 faith catalogue.

### Theological Companions
- **ἀγάπη (agapē)** — love; paired with pistis in the Pauline triad (1 Cor 13:13) and as the expression of faith (Gal 5:6 "faith which worketh by love")
- **ἐλπίς (elpis)** — hope; the third member of the triad; faith is present trust, hope is future expectation
- **δικαιοσύνη (dikaiosynē)** — righteousness; the result received through faith (Rom 9:30 "the righteousness which is of faith")

### Contrastive
- **ἀπιστία (apistia)** — the direct antonym; absence of faith (Rom 3:3 "shall their unbelief make the faith of God without effect?")
- **νόμος (nomos, G3551)** — law; the other basis for righteousness, set in opposition to pistis (Rom 3:27-28; Gal 3:11-12). See [WG-law](WG-law.md).
