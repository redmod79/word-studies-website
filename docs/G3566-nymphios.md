## Semantic Categories

### 1. Jesus as the Bridegroom Whose Presence Defines the Feast
In the Synoptic "children of the bridechamber" saying, Jesus identifies himself as the bridegroom. His physical presence with the disciples makes fasting inappropriate; his removal signals a new season.
- **Mat 9:15** — "Can the children of the bridechamber mourn, as long as the bridegroom is with them? but the days will come, when the bridegroom shall be taken from them, and then shall they fast."
- **Mar 2:19–20** — parallel saying.
- **Luk 5:34–35** — parallel saying.

### 2. The Coming Bridegroom in Parable
In the parable of the ten virgins the bridegroom's arrival is delayed, midnight-timed, and decisive — entry to the marriage feast depends on readiness at his coming.
- **Mat 25:1** — virgins "went forth to meet the bridegroom."
- **Mat 25:5** — "While the bridegroom tarried, they all slumbered and slept."
- **Mat 25:6** — "Behold, the bridegroom cometh; go ye out to meet him."
- **Mat 25:10** — "the bridegroom came; and they that were ready went in with him to the marriage."

### 3. The Bridegroom at a Literal Wedding (Cana)
John 2 uses the word in its ordinary sense — the groom hosting the wedding at Cana. This is the only literal, non-figurative use in the NT narrative.
- **Jhn 2:9** — "the governor of the feast called the bridegroom."

### 4. John the Baptist's Self-Definition as the Bridegroom's Friend
John uses νυμφίος to identify Jesus as the true bridegroom while casting himself as the best-man who rejoices at the groom's voice.
- **Jhn 3:29** — "He that hath the bride is the bridegroom: but the friend of the bridegroom, which standeth and heareth him, rejoiceth greatly because of the bridegroom's voice."

### 5. Silence of the Bridegroom as Sign of Judgment
In Revelation, the absence of the bridegroom's voice marks the extinguished joy of a fallen city — reversing the standard prophetic pairing of bridegroom/bride with festive sound.
- **Rev 18:23** — "the voice of the bridegroom and of the bride, shall be heard no more at all in thee."
