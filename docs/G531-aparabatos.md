## Lexicon Definition

From α- (as a negative particle) and a derivative of παραβαίνω (G3845): "not passing away, not to be passed on to another, inviolable, unchangeable." The alpha-privative negates the verbal root's sense of "stepping aside" or "crossing over." In the context of priesthood, it means an office that cannot be transferred to a successor — it does not pass from one holder to another. The word belongs to a cluster of alpha-privative adjectives in Hebrews that describe the permanent, irrevocable character of Christ's work: ἀμετάθετος G276 "immutable" (Heb 6:17-18), ἀκατάλυτος G179 "indestructible" (Heb 7:16), and ἀθέτησις G115 "annulment" (Heb 7:18, the concept aparabatos negates).

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Permanence of Christ's Priesthood — Non-Transferable Office
- **Heb 7:24** — "But this [man], because he continueth ever, hath an unchangeable priesthood."

This is the sole occurrence. The word modifies ἱερωσύνη (hierōsynē, G2420, "priesthood") and functions as the theological anchor of the Heb 7:23-25 argument: Levitical priests were many because death prevented them from continuing (v. 23), but Christ holds a priesthood that is aparabatos — it does not pass to a successor — because he lives forever (v. 24). The consequence is stated in v. 25: he is able to save "to the uttermost" those who come to God through him, because he "ever liveth to make intercession."

The semantic force is debated between two readings:
- **"Unchangeable / permanent"** — the priesthood does not change in character or cease
- **"Non-transferable / inviolable"** — the priesthood cannot be handed to another holder

Both senses derive from the alpha-privative negation of παραβαίνω. The contextual argument in Heb 7:23-24 (many priests vs. one priest who continues forever) favors the "non-transferable" nuance: the office cannot pass to another because its holder never dies.

## Morphological Note

The single occurrence is parsed as **ἀπαράβατον** [A-ASF] — accusative singular feminine, agreeing with ἱερωσύνην (priesthood, also accusative singular feminine). The -τος verbal adjective suffix typically carries passive or potential meaning: "not-able-to-be-transgressed."
