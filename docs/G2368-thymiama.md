## Semantic Categories

### 1. Temple Liturgy — The Hour of Incense
The daily burning of incense at the golden altar within the Holy Place, accompanying the prayers of the people gathered outside.

- **Luke 1:10** — "And the whole multitude of the people were praying without at the time of incense."
- **Luke 1:11** — "And there appeared unto him an angel of the Lord standing on the right side of the altar of incense."

### 2. Heavenly Worship — Incense as the Prayers of the Saints
In Revelation's throne room, incense is explicitly identified as the prayers of God's people, mediating between earthly intercession and the heavenly altar before God.

- **Rev 5:8** — "...golden vials full of odours, which are the prayers of saints."
- **Rev 8:3** — "...there was given unto him much incense, that he should offer [it] with the prayers of all saints upon the golden altar which was before the throne."
- **Rev 8:4** — "And the smoke of the incense, [which came] with the prayers of the saints, ascended up before God out of the angel's hand."

### 3. Commercial Commodity — Babylon's Luxury Trade
Listed among imported spices and ointments in the merchant inventory of the great city, showing incense as a prized trade good of the ancient world.

- **Rev 18:13** — "And cinnamon, and odours, and ointments, and frankincense, and wine, and oil, and fine flour..."
