## Lexicon Definition

From G2819 (kleros) and the base of G3551 (nomos, in its original sense of partitioning). An heir — one who receives a lot or portion, i.e. one who inherits by allotment.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. The Rightful Son Rejected (Synoptic Parable)
- **Mat 21:38** — "This is the heir; come, let us kill him, and let us seize on his inheritance."
- **Mar 12:7** — "This is the heir; come, let us kill him, and the inheritance shall be ours."
- **Luk 20:14** — "This is the heir: come, let us kill him, that the inheritance may be ours."

All three Synoptics apply "heir" to the son in the vineyard parable — a Christological title identifying Jesus as the legitimate heir whom the tenants (Israel's leaders) reject.

### 2. Abrahamic Promise — Heir by Faith, Not Law
- **Rom 4:13** — "For the promise, that he should be the heir of the world, [was] not to Abraham, or to his seed, through the law, but through the righteousness of faith."
- **Rom 4:14** — "For if they which are of the law [be] heirs, faith is made void, and the promise made of none effect."
- **Gal 3:29** — "And if ye [be] Christ's, then are ye Abraham's seed, and heirs according to the promise."
- **Gal 4:7** — "Wherefore thou art no more a servant, but a son; and if a son, then an heir of God through Christ."

Paul's central argument: heirship comes through faith-union with Christ, not through Torah observance. The heir receives what was promised to Abraham.

### 3. Believers as God's Heirs
- **Rom 8:17** — "And if children, then heirs; heirs of God, and joint-heirs with Christ; if so be that we suffer with [him], that we may be also glorified together."
- **Tit 3:7** — "That being justified by his grace, we should be made heirs according to the hope of eternal life."
- **Jam 2:5** — "Hath not God chosen the poor of this world rich in faith, and heirs of the kingdom which he hath promised to them that love him?"

Believers are described as heirs of God, of the kingdom, and of eternal life. Rom 8:17 uniquely links heirship with suffering — co-heirs share both glory and affliction.

### 4. Christ as Appointed Heir
- **Heb 1:2** — "His Son, whom he hath appointed heir of all things, by whom also he made the worlds."

The Son's heirship is cosmic in scope ("all things") and grounded in his role as creator-agent. This is the fullest christological use.

### 5. Heir by Faith (OT Examples)
- **Heb 6:17** — "God, willing more abundantly to shew unto the heirs of promise the immutability of his counsel, confirmed [it] by an oath."
- **Heb 11:7** — "By faith Noah... became heir of the righteousness which is by faith."

Hebrews applies "heir" retroactively to OT figures, demonstrating that faith-based heirship predates the Mosaic covenant.

### 6. Heir vs. Servant — Legal Status Contrast
- **Gal 4:1** — "The heir, as long as he is a child, differeth nothing from a servant, though he be lord of all."
- **Gal 4:7** — "Thou art no more a servant, but a son; and if a son, then an heir."

Paul draws on Greco-Roman inheritance law: the minor heir has no practical advantage over a slave until the father's appointed time.
