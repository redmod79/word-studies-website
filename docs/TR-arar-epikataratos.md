## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same across testaments:**
- The epikataratos/arur formula retains its covenant-legal character — both OT and NT uses declare a state resulting from covenant violation
- The Deuteronomic framework (failure to keep the whole law = cursed) carries directly into Gal 3:10 without semantic reinterpretation

**What shifts:**
- In the OT, arur is always a declaration by God, a prophet, or a covenant community; in NT usage, Paul applies it reflexively to show that the curse applies universally to all under law (Gal 3:10) and then announces its resolution (Gal 3:13)
- The NT epikataratos passages are exclusively Christological/soteriological in application, whereas OT arur covers creation-curses, covenant sanctions, ordeal formulae, and prophetic denunciations
