## Lexicon Definition

Valley of (the son of) Hinnom; a ravine south of Jerusalem historically used for child sacrifice (2 Chr 28:3; 33:6) and later as a refuse dump with perpetually burning fires. Adopted in Second Temple Judaism and by Jesus as a term for the place of final fiery punishment for the wicked.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Fiery Place of Destruction
Geenna is consistently associated with unquenchable fire — the dominant collocate is πῦρ (fire, 6x). The valley's perpetual burning provided the image.

- **Matt 5:22** — "whosoever shall say, Thou fool, shall be in danger of hell fire"
- **Matt 18:9** — "rather than having two eyes to be cast into hell fire"
- **Mark 9:43** — "it is better for thee to enter into life maimed, than having two hands to go into hell, into the fire that never shall be quenched"
- **Mark 9:47** — "it is better for thee to enter into the kingdom of God with one eye, than having two eyes to be cast into hell fire"

### 2. Self-Denial to Escape It
Six of the 12 occurrences appear in sayings about cutting off an offending member — a hyperbolic call to radical self-denial. The pattern is: *it is better to enter life diminished than to be cast whole into geenna*.

- **Matt 5:29** — "it is profitable for thee that one of thy members should perish, and not that thy whole body should be cast into hell"
- **Matt 5:30** — "it is profitable for thee that one of thy members should perish, and not that thy whole body should be cast into hell"
- **Mark 9:45** — "it is better for thee to enter halt into life, than having two feet to be cast into hell, into the fire that never shall be quenched"

### 3. Destruction of Soul and Body
One saying specifies that geenna is where *both* soul and body are destroyed — the only NT text that directly associates geenna with the soul.

- **Matt 10:28** — "fear not them which kill the body, but are not able to kill the soul: but rather fear him which is able to destroy both soul and body in hell"

### 4. God's Judicial Judgment
Geenna appears in contexts of formal condemnation — escaping or receiving the verdict of geenna.

- **Matt 23:33** — "how can ye escape the damnation of hell?"
- **Luke 12:5** — "Fear him, which after he hath killed hath power to cast into hell; yea, I say unto you, Fear him"

### 5. Moral Corruption (James)
James uses geenna metaphorically as the source and symbol of destructive, defiling power — the tongue is "set on fire of hell."

- **Jas 3:6** — "the tongue is a fire... it defileth the whole body... and it is set on fire of hell"

### 6. Character / Destiny Descriptor
Geenna can describe a person's final orientation or judgment identity.

- **Matt 23:15** — "when he is made, ye make him twofold more the child of hell than yourselves"

### Word Family / Cognates
No Greek cognates — geenna is a transliteration, not a native Greek word. The underlying Hebrew toponym:
