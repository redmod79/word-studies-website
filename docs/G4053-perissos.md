## Semantic Categories

### 1. Exceeding / Beyond What Is Expected
That which surpasses a norm or standard — the "more than" that distinguishes.
- **Matt 5:37** — "whatsoever is more than these cometh of evil" — beyond yes/no is surplus, and here surplus is suspect
- **Matt 5:47** — "what do ye more [than others]? do not even the publicans so?" — exceeding the baseline of common courtesy
- **Luke 12:4** — "after that have no more that they can do" — nothing beyond bodily harm remains

### 2. Superabundant Quantity or Degree
Expressing surplus that overflows ordinary measure.
- **John 10:10** — "I am come that they might have life, and that they might have it more abundantly" — life that exceeds mere existence
- **1 Cor 12:23** — "upon these we bestow more abundant honour" — compensatory surplus of honor on less honorable members
- **1 Cor 12:24** — "having given more abundant honour to that part which lacked" — God's design of surplus provision for deficiency

### 3. Superior Status or Preeminence
A qualitative superiority — something greater than its class.
- **Matt 11:9** — "A prophet? yea, I say unto you, and more than a prophet" — John exceeds the prophetic category
- **Luke 7:26** — "much more than a prophet" — same declaration with intensifier πολύ
- **Rom 3:1** — "What advantage then hath the Jew?" — the surplus benefit of Jewish identity (τὸ περισσόν as substantive)

### 4. Intensified Emotion or Action
Adverbial force — vehemence, excess of feeling.
- **Mark 6:51** — "they were sore amazed in themselves beyond measure" — astonishment exceeding capacity
- **Mark 14:31** — "he spake the more vehemently" — Peter's intensified insistence
- **2 Cor 2:7** — "lest perhaps such a one should be swallowed up with overmuch sorrow" — grief beyond what is restorative

### 5. Unnecessary / Superfluous
That which exceeds need and is therefore redundant.
- **2 Cor 9:1** — "it is superfluous for me to write to you" — writing beyond what is necessary
- **2 Cor 10:8** — "though I should boast somewhat more of our authority" — surplus boasting
