# WG: Forgiveness

## Overview
The NT forgiveness concept operates through several Greek words that approach the same reality from different angles: release from debt/bondage (aphesis/aphiēmi), gracious pardon (charizomai), propitiation/mercy (hilaskomai), and redemptive liberation (apolytrōsis). One near-synonym (paresis) marks the distinction between temporary divine forbearance and permanent forgiveness.

## Roles in the Concept

- **Action:** ἀφίημι (G863) — the act of sending away / releasing sins
- **Result:** ἄφεσις (G859) — the state of being released / forgiven
- **Mechanism:** ἀπολύτρωσις (G629) — the ransom/redemption that makes forgiveness possible
- **Godward basis:** ἱλάσκομαι (G2433) — the propitiation that satisfies divine justice
- **Grace motive:** χαρίζομαι (G5483) — the free generosity behind forgiveness
- **Contrast:** πάρεσις (G3929) — the *passing over* of sins (temporary tolerance), distinguished from true *aphesis* (permanent release)

## Key Passages Where Multiple Group Words Appear Together

- **Eph 1:7** — ἀπολύτρωσις + ἄφεσις: "In whom we have redemption through his blood, the forgiveness of sins"
- **Col 1:14** — ἀπολύτρωσις + ἄφεσις: "In whom we have redemption through his blood, even the forgiveness of sins"
- **Rom 3:24–25** — Near-context cluster: δικαιόω (v.24) + ἀπολύτρωσις (v.24) + ἱλαστήριον (v.25, cognate of G2433) + πάρεσις (v.25) — the full theological framework in three verses
