## Semantic Categories

### 1. Military / Political Prevailing
Describes one party overpowering another in conflict — a hand or force being too strong to resist.
- **Judg 3:10** — "his hand prevailed against Chushanrishathaim" (Othniel after the Spirit of the LORD came upon him)
- **Judg 6:2** — "the hand of Midian prevailed against Israel" (oppressive domination)
- **Dan 11:12** — "he shall cast down many ten thousands: but he shall not be strengthened by it" (military victory without lasting power)

### 2. Divine Strength / Appeal for God's Power
Prayers and declarations concerning God's inherent might or requests that God exercise power.
- **Psa 9:19** — "Arise, O LORD; let not man prevail" (prayer that human power not triumph over God)
- **Psa 68:28** — "Strengthen, O God, that which thou hast wrought for us" (imperative petition for divine empowerment)
- **Psa 89:13** — "Thou hast a mighty arm: strong is thy hand, and high is thy right hand" (declaration of God's sovereign strength)

### 3. Wisdom and Moral Strengthening
Wisdom or righteousness as a source of genuine power and capacity.
- **Prov 8:28** — "when he strengthened the fountains of the deep" (God's creative power establishing natural order)
- **Ecc 7:19** — "Wisdom strengtheneth the wise more than ten mighty men" (wisdom as superior to brute force)

### 4. Negative Boldness / Self-Hardening
The Hiphil stem used for brazen audacity or stubborn self-reliance — always negative.
- **Prov 7:13** — "with an impudent face said unto him" (the adulteress; literally "she hardened her face")
- **Prov 21:29** — "A wicked man hardeneth his face" (obstinate defiance)
- **Psa 52:7** — "strengthened himself in his wickedness" (Doeg trusting in evil rather than God)
- **Isa 30:2** — "to strengthen themselves in the strength of Pharaoh" (Israel seeking foreign military aid instead of trusting God)
