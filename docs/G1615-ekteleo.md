## Lexicon Definition

## Semantic Categories

With only 2 verses (both in the same immediate context), G1615 exhibits a single coherent usage pattern.

### 1. Completing a Project / Finishing What Was Begun

Both verses occur in Jesus' twin parables of counting the cost before beginning a project (Luke 14:28-33). The aorist infinitive ἐκτελέσαι appears in the negative construction "not able to finish" (μὴ ἰσχύοντος ἐκτελέσαι / οὐκ ἴσχυσεν ἐκτελέσαι), describing the inability to bring a building project to completion after laying the foundation.

- **Luk 14:29** — "Lest haply, after he hath laid the foundation, and is not able to finish [it], all that behold [it] begin to mock him"
- **Luk 14:30** — "Saying, This man began to build, and was not able to finish."

The compound ἐκτελέω heightens the sense of bringing to *full* completion — not merely stopping work but failing to reach the intended end. The repetition of the same word-form in consecutive verses forms a rhetorical bracket around the mockers' taunt, emphasizing the folly of beginning without the resources to finish.
