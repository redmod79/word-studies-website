## Lexicon Definition

From an unused root meaning "to effervesce"; wine (as fermented); by implication, intoxication:—banqueting, wine, wine(-bibber).

The morphology itself is load-bearing: yayin is formed from a root denoting effervescence/fermentation. The lexicon explicitly qualifies the word as "fermented" and notes "intoxication" as a secondary metonymic extension. Every canonical lexicon (BDB, HALOT, TWOT, Strong's) treats yayin as the fermented product of the grape — it is not a term for fresh juice.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Cultic Drink Offering (Nesek)
The regulated libation accompanying sacrifices. Explicitly fermented wine is poured out to the LORD as a "sweet savour."
- **Exod 29:40** — "the fourth part of an hin of wine for a drink offering"
- **Num 15:5, 15:7, 15:10, 28:14** — graded hin-measures of wine for lamb, ram, bull offerings
- **Deut 32:38** — "drank the wine of their drink offerings"

### 2. Priestly / Nazirite Prohibition
Wine is banned for those in sacred duty — presupposing that ordinary yayin is intoxicating enough to impair judgment.
- **Lev 10:9** — "Do not drink wine nor strong drink, thou, nor thy sons with thee, when ye go into the tabernacle"
- **Num 6:3–4, 6:20** — Nazirite separated from wine, strong drink, vinegar of wine, grapes, raisins
- **Judg 13:4, 13:7, 13:14** — Samson's mother forbidden wine during pregnancy
- **Ezek 44:21** — "Neither shall any priest drink wine, when they enter into the inner court"
- **Jer 35:6, 35:8, 35:14** — Rechabites' vow: "We will drink no wine"
- **Dan 1:8, 1:16, 10:3** — Daniel declines the king's wine

### 3. Intoxication / Drunkenness (Explicit)
Yayin directly produces drunkenness in the narrative — the first occurrence sets the paradigm.
- **Gen 9:21, 9:24** — Noah "drank of the wine, and was drunken… awoke from his wine"
- **Gen 19:32–35** — Lot's daughters make their father drink wine to incapacitate him
- **1 Sam 1:14** — Eli thinks Hannah is drunk: "put away thy wine from thee"
- **1 Sam 25:37** — "when the wine was gone out of Nabal" (sobering up the morning after)
- **Prov 20:1** — "Wine is a mocker, strong drink is raging: and whosoever is deceived thereby is not wise"
- **Prov 23:30–31** — "they that tarry long at the wine… look not thou upon the wine when it is red… it moveth itself aright"
- **Isa 28:7** — "they also have erred through wine… the priest and the prophet have erred through strong drink"
- **Isa 5:11, 5:22** — woes on those "mighty to drink wine"
- **Hos 4:11** — "Whoredom and wine and new wine take away the heart"
- **Hab 2:5** — "he transgresseth by wine"

### 4. Blessing / Covenant Provision / Joy
Yayin is gift, abundance, covenant blessing, and source of gladness — the same fermented substance.
- **Gen 14:18** — Melchizedek brings forth "bread and wine" to Abram
- **Gen 27:25** — Isaac eats and drinks wine before blessing Jacob
- **Gen 49:11–12** — Judah's blessing: "he washed his garments in wine… his eyes shall be red with wine"
- **Deut 14:26** — tithe feast: "wine, or for strong drink… thou shalt rejoice"
- **Psa 104:15** — "wine that maketh glad the heart of man" (indexed but text not in KJV extract)
- **Eccl 9:7** — "drink thy wine with a merry heart; for God now accepteth thy works"
- **Eccl 10:19** — "wine maketh merry"
- **Isa 55:1** — "come, buy wine and milk without money"
- **Zech 10:7** — "their heart shall rejoice as through wine"

### 5. Royal / Banquet Setting
Wine defines the formal feast (mishteh). Esther's six uses cluster here.
- **Est 1:7, 1:10, 5:6, 7:2, 7:7, 7:8** — "banquet of wine" (mishteh ha-yayin); "when the heart of the king was merry with wine"
- **2 Sam 13:28** — "when Amnon's heart is merry with wine"
- **Dan 1:5, 5 (implied)** — Babylonian court provision of wine
- **Neh 2:1, 5:18** — Persian court wine service

### 6. Economic / Agricultural Commodity
Wine as stored produce, tithe, tribute, trade good.
- **1 Chr 27:27** — "wine cellars"

- **Ezek 27:18** — Damascus trades "wine of Helbon"

### 7. Judgment / Wrath Imagery
Wine as metaphor for divine wrath — the cup poured out on nations. Draws on intoxication's disorienting, overthrowing power.
- **Psa 60:3, 75:8** — "wine of astonishment"; "in the hand of the LORD there is a cup, and the wine is red"
- **Jer 25:15** — "Take the wine cup of this fury at my hand, and cause all the nations… to drink it"
- **Jer 51:7** — "Babylon hath been a golden cup… the nations have drunken of her wine"
- **Deut 32:33** — "Their wine is the poison of dragons"
- **Prov 4:17** — "the wine of violence"

**Critical bridge for NT study:** With 115 LXX co-occurrences at PMI 6.95, yayin → oinos is one of the tightest cross-testament lexical equivalences in the whole Hebrew-Greek bridge. This means: when a first-century Greek-speaking Jew or Christian heard οἶνος, the semantic content they imported was exactly the range catalogued above — fermented, capable of intoxication, covenant blessing, cultic drink offering, royal banquet, joy-producing, prohibition-worthy in priestly service.
