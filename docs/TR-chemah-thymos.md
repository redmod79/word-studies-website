### What chemah brings into thymos

**Semantic convergence:** The LXX translators consistently grouped the hotter, more passionate Hebrew wrath words (chemah, charon, charah) under θυμός, reserving ὀργή for the broader/more judicial senses (often paired with aph). The pairing θυμός/ὀργή in the LXX and NT mirrors the Hebrew stacked-intensifier pattern (aph+chemah).

## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same:**
- **Heat/burning metaphor** preserved through θυμός (from thyo, "to seethe/breathe hard") — the physical passion remains
- **"Pour out" formula** — Hebrew shaphak-chemah becomes Greek ekcheo-thymos (Rev 16:1)
- **Cup-of-wrath imagery** — Isa 51 / Jer 25 chemah-cup becomes Rev 14/16/19 thymos-cup, unbroken
- **Winepress imagery** — Isa 63 chemah-winepress becomes Rev 14/19 thymos-winepress
- **Stacked intensifier pattern** — OT "aph + chemah" becomes NT "orge + thymos" (Rev 14:10; 16:19; 19:15)

**What shifts:**
- **Chemah = 117 OT uses; thymos = 18 NT uses.** The Hebrew word's frequency drops by ~85% in the NT — the NT uses orge more broadly, reserving thymos for the most intense/eschatological moments
- **Venom/poison sense disappears** — the Deu 32:24-33 "poison" usage of chemah has no NT echo under thymos; that semantic branch is pruned
- **Royal-rage domain** — thymos in Acts 19:28 and Heb 11:27 preserves the human-king-rage sense (cf. Est 1:12; Pro 16:14)
- **Eschatological concentration** — 7 of 18 NT thymos uses are in Revelation, all applying the OT chemah-of-God judgment formula; the other NT uses are ethical (Eph, Col, Gal) or narrative (Luk, Act)
