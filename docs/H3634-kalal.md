## Semantic Categories

### 1. Perfecting / Completing to a State of Beauty and Wholeness
Both uses describe artistic or constructive perfection — making something visibly complete and beautiful. The object is always "thy beauty" (יפי, yophi), and the agents are Tyre's builders (Eze 27:4) and warriors (Eze 27:11). The meaning carries an aesthetic/excellence dimension: not merely finishing a task, but bringing something to its full potential of splendor.

- **Eze 27:4** — "Thy borders are in the midst of the seas, thy builders have perfected thy beauty."
- **Eze 27:11** — "The men of Arvad with thine army were upon thy walls round about, and the Gammadims were in thy towers: they hanged their shields upon thy walls round about; they have made thy beauty perfect."

*Context: Ezekiel 27 is a dirge over Tyre portrayed as a magnificent ship. The word kalal frames Tyre's beauty as something humans deliberately perfected — builders constructed her, warriors adorned her. Both verses use Qal Perfect 3p with yophi (beauty) as the direct object, forming a repeated refrain.*
