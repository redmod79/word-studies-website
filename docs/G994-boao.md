## Semantic Categories

### 1. Prophetic Herald Crying (Isaiah 40:3 Quotation)
- **Matt 3:3** — "The voice of one crying [boōntos] in the wilderness, Prepare ye the way of the Lord, make his paths straight."
- **Mark 1:3** — "The voice of one crying [boōntos] in the wilderness, Prepare ye the way of the Lord, make his paths straight."
- **Luke 3:4** — "The voice of one crying [boōntos] in the wilderness, Prepare ye the way of the Lord, make his paths straight."
- **John 1:23** — "I am the voice of one crying [boōntos] in the wilderness, Make straight the way of the Lord"

This is the dominant usage (4 of 11 verses, 36%). All four Gospels use boaō (present participle) for the Isaiah 40:3 herald voice — a sustained, proclamatory cry announcing divine arrival. John the Baptist applies it to himself in John 1:23.

### 2. Cry of Anguish / Intense Appeal
- **Mark 15:34** — "And at the ninth hour Jesus cried [eboēsen] with a loud voice, saying, Eloi, Eloi, lama sabachthani?"
- **Luke 18:38** — "And he cried [eboēsen], saying, Jesus, thou Son of David, have mercy on me."

Aorist indicative marks a single, powerful cry. In Mark 15:34, this is the specific verb chosen for Jesus's cry from the cross — a raw, anguished shout. In Luke 18:38, the blind man's desperate appeal for mercy.

### 3. Persistent Crying to God
- **Luke 18:7** — "And shall not God avenge his own elect, which cry [boōntōn] day and night unto him?"

Present participle depicting ongoing, sustained crying — a continuous appeal to God for justice. The parable of the persistent widow uses boaō for the elect's unceasing prayer.

### 4. Crowd Crying / Public Outcry
- **Acts 17:6** — "They drew Jason and certain brethren unto the rulers of the city, crying [boōntes], These that have turned the world upside down are come hither also"
- **Acts 21:34** — "And some cried [eboōn] one thing, some another, among the multitude"

Public shouting in crowd/mob contexts — accusatory declarations before authorities.

### 5. Spirit Crying / Exorcism
- **Acts 8:7** — "For unclean spirits, crying [boōnta] with loud voice, came out of many"

Demons shrieking as they are expelled — an involuntary, compelled cry.

### 6. Prophetic Command to Cry (OT Quotation)
- **Gal 4:27** — "Break forth and cry [boēson], thou that travailest not" (quoting Isa 54:1)

Aorist imperative — a commanded shout of joy/triumph. The only use of boaō as a joyful cry rather than distress or proclamation.
