## Lexicon Definition

A crowd, a casual collection of people; a multitude of men who have flocked together in some place, a throng. Used in the NT only in the historical books (Gospels and Acts) and five times in Revelation. In classical Greek from Pindar and Aeschylus onward. Can denote: (1) a casual gathering or throng; (2) the common people as opposed to rulers and leading men; (3) with contempt, the ignorant multitude, the populace (John 7:49); (4) universally, a multitude with a genitive of class (e.g., "a great company of publicans" Luke 5:29, "the company of his disciples" Luke 6:17). The Septuagint renders chiefly הָמוֹן (hamon).

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Crowds Following / Gathering Around Jesus
The dominant usage: large groups flock to Jesus, follow him, press upon him.

- **Mat 4:25** — "And there followed him great multitudes of people from Galilee"
- **Mat 8:1** — "When he was come down from the mountain, great multitudes followed him"
- **Mark 5:24** — "And Jesus went with him; and much people followed him, and thronged him"
- **Luke 5:1** — "as the people pressed upon him to hear the word of God"

### 2. Object of Jesus' Compassion and Teaching
Jesus ministers to the crowd: healing, feeding, teaching parables.

- **Mat 9:36** — "when he saw the multitudes, he was moved with compassion on them, because they fainted"
- **Mat 14:14** — "saw a great multitude, and was moved with compassion toward them, and he healed their sick"
- **Mark 8:2** — "I have compassion on the multitude, because they have now been with me three days, and have nothing to eat"
- **Mat 13:34** — "All these things spake Jesus unto the multitude in parables"

### 3. Crowd as Reactive Audience
The crowd responds to Jesus' words and deeds with astonishment, wonder, division, or murmuring.

- **Mat 7:28** — "the people were astonished at his doctrine"
- **Mat 9:33** — "the multitudes marvelled, saying, It was never so seen in Israel"
- **John 7:12** — "there was much murmuring among the people concerning him"
- **John 7:43** — "So there was a division among the people because of him"

### 4. Crowd as Political Force
Religious leaders fear the crowd; the crowd is manipulated at the trial of Jesus.

- **Mat 14:5** — "when he would have put him to death, he feared the multitude, because they counted him as a prophet"
- **Mat 21:46** — "they feared the multitude, because they took him for a prophet"
- **Mat 27:20** — "the chief priests and elders persuaded the multitude that they should ask Barabbas"
- **Mark 15:11** — "the chief priests moved the people, that he should rather release Barabbas"

### 5. Crowd as Physical Obstacle ("press")
The dense crowd prevents access to Jesus; used when translated "press."

- **Mark 2:4** — "when they could not come nigh unto him for the press"
- **Mark 5:30** — "turned him about in the press, and said, Who touched my clothes?"
- **Luke 8:19** — "could not come at him for the press"
- **Luke 19:3** — "could not for the press, because he was little of stature"

### 6. General Assembly / Numbered Group
Non-Synoptic usage in Acts (church or civic gatherings) and Revelation (eschatological multitudes).

- **Acts 1:15** — "the number of names together were about an hundred and twenty"
- **Acts 6:7** — "a great company of the priests were obedient to the faith"
- **Rev 7:9** — "a great multitude, which no man could number, of all nations"
- **Rev 19:6** — "the voice of a great multitude, and as the voice of many waters"

### 7. Crowd Dismissed / Sent Away
A recurring narrative pattern where Jesus sends the crowd away before private teaching or prayer.

- **Mat 13:36** — "Then Jesus sent the multitude away, and went into the house"
- **Mat 14:22** — "Jesus constrained his disciples to get into a ship... while he sent the multitudes away"
- **Mark 4:36** — "when they had sent away the multitude"
- **Mat 15:39** — "he sent away the multitude, and took ship"
