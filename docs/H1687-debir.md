## Lexicon Definition

> Or (shortened) דְּבִר dᵉbir; from דָּבַר (apparently in the sense of *oracle*); the shrine or innermost part of the sanctuary: — oracle.

The term designates the Most Holy Place of Solomon's temple — the rearmost, windowless cube (20 × 20 × 20 cubits per 1 Ki 6:20) housing the ark of the covenant between the two cherubim. Rendered "oracle" by KJV following the Vulgate *oraculum*, which reflects an ancient etymological link to *dabar* ("to speak") — the place from which God spoke (Exod 25:22; Num 7:89). Modern lexica generally prefer "inner sanctuary" / "Most Holy Place."

**Pattern:** Nearly all occurrences cluster in the Solomonic temple-construction narratives (1 Ki 6–8; 2 Ch 3–5). The lone poetic use is Ps 28:2. The word is absent from the Pentateuch (which uses קֹדֶשׁ הַקֳּדָשִׁים for the tabernacle's inner room), absent from the prophets' temple visions (Isaiah, Jeremiah, Ezekiel use other terminology), and absent from post-exilic temple texts (Ezra, Nehemiah, Haggai, Zechariah). This makes debir a specifically **Solomonic-temple term**.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Architectural Designation — the Inner Sanctuary Room
References to the debir as the physical windowless cube at the rear of the temple.
- **1 Ki 6:5** — chambers built "against the walls of the house round about, both of the temple and of the oracle"
- **1 Ki 6:20** — "the oracle in the forepart was twenty cubits in length, and twenty cubits in breadth, and twenty cubits in the height thereof"
- **1 Ki 6:16** — "he built twenty cubits on the sides of the house… and he built it within, even for the oracle, even for the most holy place"
- **2 Ch 4:20** — "the candlesticks… should burn after the manner before the oracle"

### 2. Resting Place of the Ark
The debir as destination/position of the ark of the covenant when the temple was dedicated.
- **1 Ki 6:19** — "the oracle he prepared in the house within, to set there the ark of the covenant of the LORD"
- **1 Ki 8:6** — "the priests brought in the ark of the covenant of the LORD unto his place, into the oracle of the house"
- **2 Ch 5:7** — parallel to 1 Ki 8:6
- **1 Ki 8:8** / **2 Ch 5:9** — staves of the ark seen "before the oracle"

### 3. Adorned/Overlaid Sacred Space
The debir as object of gold-overlay, cherub-making, and partition construction.
- **1 Ki 6:21** — "he made a partition by the chains of gold before the oracle; and he overlaid it with gold"
- **1 Ki 6:22** — "the altar that was by the oracle he overlaid with gold"
- **1 Ki 6:23** — "within the oracle he made two cherubims of olive tree, each ten cubits high"
- **1 Ki 6:31** — "for the entering of the oracle he made doors of olive tree"

### 4. Spatial Reference Point ("before the oracle")
Used to locate other temple furniture relative to the Most Holy Place.
- **1 Ki 7:49** — candlesticks "five on the right side, and five on the left, before the oracle"
- **2 Ch 3:16** — chains "as in the oracle" (pattern reference)
- **2 Ch 4:20** — candlesticks "before the oracle"

### 5. Direction of Prayer / Direction of God's Presence
The single poetic/devotional usage.
- **Ps 28:2** — "Hear the voice of my supplications, when I cry unto thee, when I lift up my hands toward thy holy oracle (דְּבִיר קָדְשֶׁךָ)"

Here debir transcends architecture and becomes theological orientation: the direction of God's dwelling, the place whence He speaks and hears prayer.
