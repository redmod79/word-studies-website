## Lexicon Definition

Force (literally or figuratively); especially miraculous power, usually by implication a miracle itself. From δύναμαι (to be able).

## Semantic Categories

### 1. God's Inherent Power
The word describes God's essential, self-existent force — not merely an attribute but an expression of who he is.
- **Rom 1:16** — "it is the power of God unto salvation to every one that believeth"
- **1 Cor 1:24** — "Christ the power of God, and the wisdom of God"
- **2 Pet 1:3** — "According as his divine power hath given unto us all things that pertain unto life and godliness"
- **Rev 4:11** — "Thou art worthy, O Lord, to receive glory and honour and power: for thou hast created all things"

### 2. The Spirit's Power in Ministry
Dynamis as the active force behind apostolic proclamation, miracles, and transformation — closely paired with πνεῦμα (Spirit).
- **Acts 1:8** — "ye shall receive power, after that the Holy Ghost is come upon you"
- **Luke 1:35** — "the power of the Highest shall overshadow thee"
- **Luke 4:14** — "Jesus returned in the power of the Spirit into Galilee"
- **1 Thess 1:5** — "our gospel came not unto you in word only, but also in power, and in the Holy Ghost"

### 3. Miraculous Works (Mighty Works)
Dynamis as a concrete act — a miracle or mighty deed, especially in the Synoptic accounts of Jesus's ministry.
- **Matt 11:20** — "the cities wherein most of his mighty works were done"
- **Acts 2:22** — "Jesus of Nazareth, a man approved of God among you by miracles and wonders and signs"
- **Heb 2:4** — "with signs and wonders, and with divers miracles, and gifts of the Holy Ghost"
- **2 Cor 12:12** — "the signs of an apostle were wrought among you...in signs and wonders, and mighty deeds"

### 4. Cosmic / Supernatural Rulers
In the plural and in eschatological contexts, δυνάμεις refers to heavenly or supernatural powers — either angelic or demonic.
- **Eph 1:21** — "Far above all principality, and power (dynamis), and might, and dominion"
- **Rom 8:38** — "nor angels, nor principalities, nor powers (dynameis)...shall separate us from the love of God"
- **Matt 24:29** — "the powers of the heavens shall be shaken"
- **1 Pet 3:22** — "angels and authorities and powers being made subject unto him"

### 5. Human Capacity / Ability
Dynamis used of finite human resources — a person's strength, financial means, or ability.
- **Matt 25:15** — "to every man according to his several ability (dynamis)"
- **2 Cor 8:3** — "to their power (dynamis), yea, and beyond their power, they were willing"
- **Rev 3:8** — "thou hast a little strength (dynamis), and hast kept my word"
- **2 Tim 3:5** — "Having a form of godliness, but denying the power thereof"
