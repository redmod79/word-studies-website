**Semantic narrowing in translation:** Hebrew bad has a wide semantic range (separation → isolation, exclusivity, addition, physical poles/branches, strength). The LXX splits this across multiple Greek words. Monos captures only the "alone/only" senses — the "beside/apart from" (millibad) sense goes to plen and choris, and the concrete "staves/branches" sense is rendered by entirely different vocabulary. The NT inherits the narrower monos meaning.

## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same across testaments:**
- The core sense of isolation/aloneness transfers directly: Gen 2:18 "not good to be alone" → Mark 6:47 "he alone on the land"
- The exclusivity sense ("only God") transfers directly: Psalm 86:10 "thou art God alone" → John 5:44 "the only God"
- Direct quotations (Deut 8:3 → Matt 4:4; 1 Ki 19 → Rom 11:3) preserve the identical meaning

**What shifts:**
- The "beside/in addition to" (millibad) sense of bad has no counterpart in monos — the NT uses choris or other prepositions for "apart from"
- The concrete senses (staves, branches, bars, strength) disappear entirely from the monos word family
- Monos in the NT picks up theological weight in christological contexts (John 17:3 "the only true God") that goes beyond bad's OT usage
