# WG: Possess / Inherit / Dispossess

## Overview

This cluster covers the Hebrew Bible's vocabulary for territorial and patrimonial possession — how land and property are **taken**, **received as a share**, **held as heritage**, and (conversely) how prior occupants are **driven out** or **devoted to destruction**. These words dominate the conquest narratives (Deuteronomy–Judges) and the tribal-allotment system (Joshua), and together they define Israel's relationship to the land as both *given* and *seized*.

Two parallel morphological families, one built on *take-over* semantics, one on *receive-as-share* semantics.

## Roles in the Concept

- **Aggressive acquisition:** *yârash* (Qal + Hiphil) — takes the land, drives out the prior occupants.
- **Receptive acquisition:** *nâchal* — receives as allotted share (the tribal-portion frame).
- **Expulsion (sans succession):** *gârash* — pure ejection; applies to persons as well (divorce, exile).
- **Sacral destruction:** *châram* — puts under the ban; dispossession by annihilation rather than displacement.
- **The inheritance as thing:** *nachălâh* (most common noun, 191x) — the standard term for a tribe's allotted land.
- **The possession as thing:** *yerushshâh, môwrâshâh* — nominal products of *yârash*; emphasize conquest-origin.

The cluster's internal tension — between *nâchal*'s "receive what is given" and *yârash*'s "take by displacing" — is the theological seam of the conquest narratives: the land is *both* gift *and* seizure, because YHWH gives it by driving out its prior inhabitants.
