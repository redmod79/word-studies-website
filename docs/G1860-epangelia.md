## Semantic Categories

### 1. Abrahamic / Patriarchal Promise
The dominant usage — God's covenant commitment to Abraham and his descendants regarding land, seed, and blessing.
- **Rom 4:13** — "For the promise, that he should be the heir of the world, was not to Abraham, or to his seed, through the law, but through the righteousness of faith"
- **Gal 3:16** — "Now to Abraham and his seed were the promises made"
- **Gal 3:29** — "And if ye be Christ's, then are ye Abraham's seed, and heirs according to the promise"
- **Heb 11:9** — "By faith he sojourned in the land of promise, as in a strange country"

### 2. Promise of the Holy Spirit
The Father's promise identified with the gift of the Spirit, particularly at Pentecost.
- **Luk 24:49** — "I send the promise of my Father upon you"
- **Act 1:4** — "wait for the promise of the Father"
- **Act 2:33** — "having received of the Father the promise of the Holy Ghost"
- **Gal 3:14** — "that we might receive the promise of the Spirit through faith"

### 3. Promise of Life / Eternal Life
The eschatological content of the promise — life in Christ.
- **2Ti 1:1** — "according to the promise of life which is in Christ Jesus"
- **1Ti 4:8** — "godliness is profitable unto all things, having promise of the life that now is, and of that which is to come"
- **1Jo 2:25** — "this is the promise that he hath promised us, even eternal life"
- **Heb 9:15** — "they which are called might receive the promise of eternal inheritance"

### 4. Messianic Promise Fulfilled
The promise to the fathers realized in the coming of Jesus as Savior.
- **Act 13:23** — "Of this man's seed hath God according to his promise raised unto Israel a Saviour, Jesus"
- **Act 13:32** — "the promise which was made unto the fathers"
- **Act 26:6** — "I stand and am judged for the hope of the promise made of God unto our fathers"
- **Rom 15:8** — "Jesus Christ was a minister of the circumcision... to confirm the promises made unto the fathers"

### 5. Promise vs. Law Contrast
Paul's theological argument that promise operates on a different principle than law.
- **Rom 4:14** — "if they which are of the law be heirs, faith is made void, and the promise made of none effect"
- **Gal 3:17** — "the law... cannot disannul [the covenant], that it should make the promise of none effect"
- **Gal 3:18** — "if the inheritance be of the law, it is no more of promise: but God gave it to Abraham by promise"
- **Gal 3:21–22** — "Is the law then against the promises of God? God forbid... the promise by faith of Jesus Christ might be given to them that believe"

### 6. Gentile Inclusion in the Promise
The extension of God's promise beyond Israel to the Gentiles through Christ.
- **Act 2:39** — "the promise is unto you, and to your children, and to all that are afar off"
- **Eph 2:12** — "strangers from the covenants of promise"
- **Eph 3:6** — "the Gentiles should be fellowheirs... and partakers of his promise in Christ"

### 7. Second Coming / Eschatological Promise
The promise of Christ's return and the new creation.
- **2Pe 3:4** — "Where is the promise of his coming?"
- **2Pe 3:9** — "The Lord is not slack concerning his promise"
- **Heb 10:36** — "after ye have done the will of God, ye might receive the promise"

## Hebrew Source (via LXX)

This reflects the fact that ἐπαγγελία is not a standard LXX translation word — the Hebrew OT expresses "promise" through verbs (דָּבָר dabar "speak," אָמַר amar "say," שָׁבַע shaba "swear") rather than a single abstract noun for "promise." The concept crystallized as a distinct Greek theological term in the NT.
