## Lexicon Definition

A primitive root; to smite with deadly intent. KJV translations: destroy, out of hand, kill, murder(-er), put to (death), make (slaughter), slay(-er), surely. The core notion is intentional, lethal violence against a person (occasionally a beast) — a broader "put to death by violence" rather than the sacral slaughter of H7819 (shachat) or the neutral striking of H5221 (nakah).

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Fratricide / Murder Among Kin
The paradigm use — beginning with Cain and recurring in royal successions.
- **Gen 4:8** — "Cain rose up against Abel his brother, and slew him"
- **Judg 9:5** — Abimelech "slew his brethren the sons of Jerubbaal... threescore and ten persons, upon one stone"
- **2 Chr 21:4** — Jehoram "slew all his brethren with the sword"
- **2 Chr 24:22** — Joash "slew [Zechariah] the son of Jehoiada"

### 2. Personal Threat / Fear of Being Killed
A recurring narrative anxiety — fugitives and weaker parties fearing death.
- **Gen 12:12** — "they will kill me, but they will save thee alive"
- **Gen 27:42** — Esau "doth comfort himself, purposing to kill thee"
- **Exod 2:15** — "Pharaoh... sought to slay Moses"
- **1 Sam 16:2** — "if Saul hear it, he will kill me"

### 3. War / Mass Killing in Battle
Large-scale slaughter of combatants or conquered populations.
- **Num 31:7-8** — "they slew all the males... and the kings of Midian"
- **Josh 8:24** — "Israel had made an end of slaying all the inhabitants of Ai"
- **2 Sam 10:18** — "David slew of the Syrians seven thousand... men"
- **2 Chr 28:6** — "Pekah... slew in Judah an hundred and twenty thousand in one day"

### 4. Judicial / Sanctioned Execution
Death penalty imposed by law, king, or covenant authority.
- **Exod 32:27** — "slay every man his brother... his companion... his neighbour" (after golden calf)
- **Lev 20:15-16** — beast and bestialist "shall slay"
- **Num 25:5** — "Slay ye every one his men that were joined unto Baalpeor"
- **Deut 13:9** — "thou shalt surely kill him" (for enticement to idolatry)

### 5. Divine Slaying / Judgment of God
YHWH as subject, or the sword/wrath of YHWH as instrument.
- **Exod 13:15** — "the LORD slew all the firstborn in the land of Egypt"
- **Ps 78:31** — "the wrath of God came upon them, and slew the fattest of them"
- **Ps 135:10** — "smote great nations, and slew mighty kings"
- **Hos 6:5** — "I have slain them by the words of my mouth"
- **Lam 2:21, 3:43** — "thou hast slain, thou hast not pitied"
- **Amos 9:1, 9:4** — "I will slay... the sword shall slay them"

### 6. Killing of Prophets / Righteous
A standing indictment against Israel and its queens/kings.
- **1 Sam 22:21** — "Saul had slain the LORD'S priests"
- **1 Kgs 18:13** — "Jezebel slew the prophets of the LORD"
- **1 Kgs 19:10, 14** — "slain thy prophets with the sword"
- **Neh 9:26** — "slew thy prophets which testified against them"
- **2 Sam 4:11** — "wicked men have slain a righteous person"

### 7. Figurative / Personified Agent Kills
Abstractions — folly, envy, wrath, a harlot — as slayers.
- **Job 5:2** — "wrath killeth the foolish man, and envy slayeth the silly one"
- **Prov 1:32** — "the turning away of the simple shall slay them"
- **Prov 7:26** — "many strong men have been slain by her" (the strange woman)
- **Ps 10:8** — "in the secret places doth he murder the innocent"

### 8. Universal Edict / Genocide
Sweeping commands to exterminate a people — Esther's hinge.
- **Esth 3:13** — "to destroy, to kill, and to cause to perish, all Jews"
- **Esth 7:4** — "we are sold... to be destroyed, to be slain, and to perish"
- **Esth 8:11** — Jews "to slay, and to cause to perish, all the power of the people"
- **Esth 9:6, 9:11, 9:12, 9:15, 9:16** — reversal: Jews slay their enemies
