## Semantic Categories

### 1. Sacrificial Acceptance (Levitical/Cultic)
God accepting or refusing offerings based on their fitness. The Niphal passive is characteristic here.
- **Lev 1:4** — "it shall be accepted for him to make atonement for him"
- **Lev 7:18** — "it shall not be accepted" (peace offering eaten on the third day)
- **Lev 22:25** — "they shall not be accepted for you" (blemished animals from foreigners)
- **Lev 22:27** — "from the eighth day and thenceforth it shall be accepted for an offering"
- **Ezek 43:27** — "I will accept you, saith the Lord GOD" (restored altar worship)

### 2. Divine Pleasure in Persons
God taking pleasure in people, their character, or their ways — not sacrificial objects but relational delight.
- **Psalm 44:3** — "thy right hand... and the light of thy countenance, because thou hadst a favour unto them"
- **Psalm 147:11** — "The LORD taketh pleasure in them that fear him, in those that hope in his mercy"
- **Psalm 149:4** — "the LORD taketh pleasure in his people: he will beautify the meek with salvation"
- **1 Chr 28:4** — "he liked me to make me king over all Israel" (David chosen)

### 3. Divine Pleasure in Conduct/Worship
God's acceptance or rejection of worship, works, and moral behaviour — evaluative rather than relational.
- **Psalm 51:16** — "thou delightest not in burnt offering" (God desires contrition, not ritual)
- **Psalm 147:10** — "He delighteth not in the strength of the horse"
- **Prov 16:7** — "When a man's ways please the LORD, he maketh even his enemies to be at peace with him"
- **Mal 1:10** — "I have no pleasure in you, saith the LORD of hosts, neither will I accept an offering"

### 4. Prophetic Rejection of False Worship
God withholding ratsah from Israel's offerings due to sin — the negative of category 1.
- **Jer 14:10** — "the LORD doth not accept them; he will now remember their iniquity"
- **Jer 14:12** — "when they offer burnt offering and an oblation, I will not accept them"
- **Hos 8:13** — "the LORD accepteth them not; now will he remember their iniquity"
- **Amos 5:22** — "I will not accept them: neither will I regard the peace offerings"

### 5. Sabbath Rest / Debt Satisfaction
The land "enjoying" its sabbaths — a specialized sense of satisfying an obligation or paying off a debt.
- **Lev 26:34** — "then shall the land enjoy her sabbaths, as long as it lieth desolate"
- **Lev 26:43** — "the land... shall enjoy her sabbaths, while she lieth desolate without them"
- **2 Chr 36:21** — "until the land had enjoyed her sabbaths"
- **Isa 40:2** — "her warfare is accomplished, that her iniquity is pardoned" (Niphal: debt satisfied)

### 6. Human Acceptance / Favour
People accepting, approving, or being pleased with others — interpersonal rather than divine.
- **Gen 33:10** — "receive my present at my hand" (Jacob to Esau)
- **Deut 33:24** — "let him be acceptable to his brethren"
- **Est 10:3** — "Mordecai... accepted of the multitude of his brethren"
- **1 Sam 29:4** — "wherewith should he reconcile himself unto his master?" (David among Philistines)

### 7. Human Affection / Delight
Personal delight or pleasure directed toward something or someone.
- **1 Chr 29:3** — "I have set my affection to the house of my God"
- **1 Chr 29:17** — "thou triest the heart, and hast pleasure in uprightness"
- **Psalm 50:18** — "When thou sawest a thief, then thou consentedst with him"
- **Psalm 62:4** — "they delight in lies"
