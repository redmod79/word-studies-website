## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same across testaments:**
- Famine as divine judgment instrument — both OT prophets and NT apocalyptic (Revelation) present famine as sent or permitted by God
- The triad pattern — sword, famine, pestilence in OT becomes sword, hunger, death in Rev 6:8
- Famine as eschatological sign — prophetic warnings in Jeremiah/Ezekiel parallel Jesus's end-time discourse in Matt 24

**What shifts:**
- Scale of usage drops sharply: 88 OT verses to 12 NT verses — famine is primarily an OT concept
- NT adds personal-suffering dimension: Rom 8:35 and 2 Cor 11:27 use limos for individual hunger/deprivation, not national calamity
- The metaphorical "famine of the word" (Amos 8:11) has no explicit NT parallel, though the concept informs Jesus's "man shall not live by bread alone" (Matt 4:4)
