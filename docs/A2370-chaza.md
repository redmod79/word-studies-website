## Lexicon Definition

> (Aramaic) corresponding to H2372; to gaze upon; mentally to dream, be usual (i.e. seem): — behold, have (a dream), see, be wont.

The BDB Aramaic entry distinguishes four senses: (1) have sense of sight (Dan 5:23); (2) see/behold a person or thing directly (Dan 3:25, 27; 5:5; Ezr 4:14); (3) behold in dream or vision, the dominant usage — often with periphrastic participle + הֲוָה (Dan 2:31, 34; 4:10, 13; 7:2, 4, 6, 7, 9, 11, 13, 21); (4) passive = customary/seemly (Dan 3:19).

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Beholding in Prophetic Vision (Dream-Vision Narrative)
The dominant usage. Typically in the periphrastic construction חָזֵה הֲוֵית ("I was seeing/beholding"), which functions as a past continuous providing the narrative frame for apocalyptic visions.
- **Dan 2:31** — "Thou, O king, sawest (chazeh hawaytha), and behold a great image"
- **Dan 7:2** — "I saw (chazeh haweyth) in my vision by night, and, behold, the four winds of the heaven strove upon the great sea"
- **Dan 7:9** — "I beheld (chazeh haweyth) till the thrones were cast down, and the Ancient of days did sit"
- **Dan 7:13** — "I saw (chazeh haweyth) in the night visions, and, behold, one like the Son of man came with the clouds of heaven"

### 2. Beholding with Temporal Continuation ("I beheld till...")
A subset of the vision formula where chaza + עַד ("until") marks the seer watching continuously until a climactic event occurs.
- **Dan 2:34** — "Thou sawest (chazeh hawaytha) till that a stone was cut out without hands"
- **Dan 7:4** — "I beheld (chazeh haweyth) till the wings thereof were plucked"
- **Dan 7:9** — "I beheld (chazeh haweyth) till the thrones were cast down"
- **Dan 7:11** — "I beheld (chazeh haweyth) even till the beast was slain"

### 3. Direct Physical Sight (Seeing Events or Objects)
Ordinary visual perception of real-time events, not revelatory seeing.
- **Dan 3:25** — "Lo, I see (chazeh) four men loose, walking in the midst of the fire"
- **Dan 3:27** — "the princes...saw (chazayin) these men, upon whose bodies the fire had no power"
- **Dan 5:5** — "the king saw (chazeh) the part of the hand that wrote"

### 4. Having a Dream (Object = Dream)
With חֵלֶם (chelem, "dream") as direct object, denoting the experience of a dream.
- **Dan 2:26** — "Art thou able to make known unto me the dream which I have seen (chazeyth)?"
- **Dan 4:5** — "I saw (chazeyth) a dream which made me afraid" (Nebuchadnezzar)
- **Dan 7:1** — "Daniel had a dream (chelem chazah) and visions of his head upon his bed"

### 5. Incapacity of Sight (Negated: Idols Cannot See)
- **Dan 5:23** — "the gods of silver...which see (chazayin) not, nor hear, nor know"

### 6. Seemly/Customary (Passive Participle)
A unique semantic extension: the passive form means "what is seen" → "what is customary/fitting."
- **Dan 3:19** — "commanded that they should heat the furnace one seven times more than it was wont (chazeh) to be heated"
