## Lexicon Definition

From ἀπό (apo) + ἵημι (hiēmi, "to send"; an intensive form of εἶμι, "to go"); literally **"to send away / dismiss / let go."** The verb's meaning spans a wide arc from physical release (dismiss a crowd, leave a place, let go of an object) through social/legal release (remit a debt, divorce a wife, permit an action) into the theological climax of **releasing a person from sin — forgiveness.** The semantic thread is consistent: something that was held is released.

The LXX maps ἀφίημι primarily onto **סָלַח (salach)** — the standard priestly-ritual verb for God's pardon in Leviticus/Numbers (e.g., Lev 4:20, 26, 31, 35; Num 14:19-20; 1 Kings 8:30, 34, 36, 39, 50). This is decisive: when Jesus says "thy sins be forgiven" (Mar 2:5), he deploys the Greek word that in the LXX translates the Hebrew verb **reserved for divine pardon** — never spoken of a human forgiving another's sin against God. The scribes' reaction in Mar 2:7 ("who can forgive sins but God only?") reflects the Hebrew/LXX background: *salach* has only one subject in Scripture, and he is Yahweh.

The secondary bridge to כָּפַר (kaphar, "cover/atone") ties forgiveness to the sacrificial system; the H2403/H817 bridges tie it to the technical sin-/trespass-offering vocabulary. Together these three Hebrew strands make ἀφίημι the natural Greek word for **cultic divine release.**

## Morphological Notes

In the 50 parsed occurrences sampled, ἀφίημι appears most frequently as:
- **Aorist active imperative 2nd-singular** — Ἄφες ("Let!" / "Forgive!" / "Suffer!") — command form, e.g., Mat 6:12
- **Present passive indicative 3rd-person** — ἀφίενταί ("are forgiven") — the performative declarative in Mat 9:2, 9:5; Mar 2:5, 2:9; Luk 5:20, 7:48
- **Future passive indicative** — ἀφεθήσεται ("shall be forgiven") — Mat 12:31-32 (blasphemy sayings)
- **Aorist active participle** — ἀφέντες ("having left") — Mat 4:20, 22 (calling narratives)

The **present passive** construction "thy sins are-being-forgiven" in the healing narratives is grammatically striking: not a prayer ("may they be forgiven") nor a future promise ("shall be forgiven") but a **performative declaration** that the release is accomplished in the moment of utterance. This is the form that triggers the blasphemy charge in Mar 2:7.
