## Key Grammatical Features

### 1. Niphal Infinitive Construct With Suffix

Both v.13 and v.15 use Niphal infinitive construct forms of ברא with a 2ms suffix: `הִבָּרַאֲךָ / הִבָּרְאָךְ`. Grammatically, the suffix supplies the subject/patient of the infinitive: "your being created." The form is passive/intransitive in profile, not an active self-creating verb.

Grammar reference: [stem-niphal](../hebrew/stem-niphal.md).

### 2. Pual Establishment in v.13

`כֹּונָנוּ` is parsed Pual perfect 3p of כון. It reports the prepared/established state of the workmanship listed in the verse. The passive/intensive Pual differs from the Niphal creation infinitive: the verse uses more than one passive-result form.

### 3. Niphal Perfect `nimtsa` as Endpoint

`נִמְצָא` in v.15 is Niphal perfect 3ms of מצא, "was found." Its subject is `עַוְלָתָה` ("wickedness/unrighteousness"). The grammar does not say "until you found iniquity"; it says iniquity "was found" in the addressee.

### 4. `min ... ʿad` Bounded Interval

The temporal frame `מִיֹּום ... עַד` creates a bounded interval: from the creation-day to the found-iniquity point. The adjective `תָּמִים` is predicated over the interval, not over the whole oracle independently of that temporal frame.
