## Semantic Categories

### 1. Reciprocal Judgment — The Standard You Apply Returns to You
- **Matt 7:2** — "with what measure ye mete, it shall be measured to you again"
- **Mark 4:24** — "with what measure ye mete, it shall be measured to you"
- **Luke 6:38** — "with the same measure that ye mete withal it shall be measured to you again"

All three Synoptic uses share this proverbial formula: the act of measuring becomes reflexive. The measure applied to others determines the measure received. Present tense indicates habitual/characteristic action.

### 2. Divine Command to Measure Sacred Space
- **Rev 11:1** — "Rise, and measure the temple of God, and the altar, and them that worship therein"
- **Rev 11:2** — "the court which is without the temple leave out, and measure it not"

Aorist imperative — a decisive, commanded act. Measuring here distinguishes what is included from what is excluded, what belongs to God from what is given to the Gentiles. The act of measuring defines sacred boundaries.

### 3. Eschatological Measurement of the New Jerusalem
- **Rev 21:15** — "had a golden reed to measure the city, and the gates thereof, and the wall thereof"
- **Rev 21:16** — "he measured the city with the reed, twelve thousand furlongs"
- **Rev 21:17** — "he measured the wall thereof, an hundred and forty and four cubits"

An angel measures the completed city. The results are given in specific dimensions. The measuring here verifies and reveals perfection — the city is perfectly symmetrical (length = breadth = height).

### 4. Self-Evaluation / Comparison
- **2 Cor 10:12** — "they measuring themselves by themselves, and comparing themselves among themselves, are not wise"

The only figurative/metaphorical use. Here μετρέω means to assess or evaluate, using oneself as the standard — a usage Paul condemns as foolish.
