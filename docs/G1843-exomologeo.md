## Lexicon Definition

From ek (G1537) and homologeo (G3670); to acknowledge or agree fully — to confess, to profess, to give thanks/praise. Carries the intensifying force of the ek- prefix: not merely to agree (homologeo) but to acknowledge openly, fully, publicly.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Confession of Sin
- **Matt 3:6** — "And were baptized of him in Jordan, confessing their sins."
- **Mark 1:5** — "were all baptized of him in the river of Jordan, confessing their sins."
- **Acts 19:18** — "And many that believed came, and confessed, and shewed their deeds."
- **James 5:16** — "Confess your faults one to another, and pray one for another, that ye may be healed."

Present middle participle in the baptism contexts (ongoing action concurrent with baptism). James uses present middle imperative — a standing instruction for mutual confession.

### 2. Thanksgiving / Praise to God
- **Matt 11:25** — "I thank thee, O Father, Lord of heaven and earth, because thou hast hid these things from the wise..."
- **Luke 10:21** — "I thank thee, O Father, Lord of heaven and earth, that thou hast hid these things from the wise..."

Jesus addresses the Father with exomologeo in the sense of grateful acknowledgment — "I openly acknowledge you, Father." The parallel accounts use identical wording.

### 3. Public Confession / Acknowledgment of Lordship
- **Phil 2:11** — "And that every tongue should confess that Jesus Christ is Lord, to the glory of God the Father."
- **Rom 14:11** — "every knee shall bow to me, and every tongue shall confess to God."

Eschatological/universal acknowledgment. Phil 2:11 (aorist middle subjunctive) presents confession as a decisive future act. Rom 14:11 quotes Isa 45:23 where the Hebrew has H3034 yadah.

### 4. Praise / Acknowledgment Among the Nations
- **Rom 15:9** — "For this cause I will confess to thee among the Gentiles, and sing unto thy name."

Quoting Psa 18:49 (LXX 17:50) where Hebrew yadah is rendered exomologeo. The parallel with "sing" confirms the praise/thanksgiving sense.

### 5. Promise / Consent
- **Luke 22:6** — "And he promised, and sought opportunity to betray him unto them."

The only active voice occurrence. Judas "agreed fully" (exomologeo in the sense of open consent/promise). This is the outlier usage — consent rather than confession or praise.

### 6. Confession of Name
- **Rev 3:5** — "I will confess his name before my Father, and before his angels."

Christ's public acknowledgment of the overcomer — reciprocal to the believer's confession of Christ.
