## Relationships

### Shared Roots
- G3022 λευκός and G3021 λευκαίνω share the same root (λύκη, lykē — light); leukós is the adjective, leukaínō the verb

### Etymology Note
- λευκός derives from λύκη ("light") — white as "light-colored." This explains the Louw-Nida dual placement in Color (079.007) AND Light (014.006) domains.
- λαμπρός derives from λάμπω (lampō, "to shine") — radiance/brilliance. The two words overlap when applied to heavenly garments but are semantically distinct.

## Roles in the Concept

- **Core color term:** λευκός — the primary NT word for white (23 vv)
- **Verbal cognate:** λευκαίνω — the act of making/becoming white (Mark 9:3; Rev 7:14)
- **Luminous overlap:** λαμπρός — "bright/shining" (sometimes translated "white" for heavenly garments in Rev 15:6; 19:8)
- **Primary contrast:** μέλας — black, the opposite of white (Matt 5:36)
- **Apocalyptic contrast:** κόκκινος (scarlet) and πυρρός (fiery red) — the colors of the opposing powers in Revelation

## Key Passages Where Multiple Group Words Appear

- **Matt 5:36** — λευκός + μέλας ("white or black") — the only NT verse pairing these antonyms
- **Mark 9:3** — λευκός + λευκαίνω together in the Transfiguration: "exceeding white...so as no fuller on earth can white them"
- **Rev 6:2–8** — The four horsemen: white (λευκός) → red (πυρρός) → black (μέλας) → pale — a deliberate color sequence
- **Rev 7:14** — λευκαίνω applied to the robes of the great multitude: "made them white in the blood of the Lamb"
