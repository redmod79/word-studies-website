## Lexicon Definition

Neuter singular of G5036 (ταχύς), used adverbially; shortly, i.e. without delay, soon — or (by surprise) suddenly. KJV renders as "quickly" (11×) and "lightly" (1×).

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Eschatological Promise (Christ's Return)
The dominant use — Christ's repeated declaration "I come quickly" in Revelation's closing visions. Functions as both promise and warning.

- **Rev 2:16** — "Repent; or else I will come unto thee quickly, and will fight against them with the sword of my mouth."
- **Rev 3:11** — "Behold, I come quickly: hold that fast which thou hast, that no man take thy crown."
- **Rev 22:7** — "Behold, I come quickly: blessed is he that keepeth the sayings of the prophecy of this book."
- **Rev 22:12** — "And, behold, I come quickly; and my reward is with me, to give every man according as his work shall be."
- **Rev 22:20** — "He which testifieth these things saith, Surely I come quickly. Amen. Even so, come, Lord Jesus."

### 2. Eschatological Event Imminence
Applied not to Christ's coming itself but to the arrival of prophesied woes.

- **Rev 11:14** — "The second woe is past; and, behold, the third woe cometh quickly."

### 3. Resurrection Narrative (Urgent Physical Movement)
Used to describe the prompt, urgent departure of women from the empty tomb and Mary's response to Jesus' summons — action demanded by the urgency of the moment.

- **Matt 28:7** — "And go quickly, and tell his disciples that he is risen from the dead."
- **Matt 28:8** — "And they departed quickly from the sepulchre with fear and great joy."
- **Mark 16:8** — "And they went out quickly, and fled from the sepulchre."
- **John 11:29** — "As soon as she heard that, she arose quickly, and came unto him."

### 4. Practical / Moral Promptness
Prompt settlement before a matter escalates; prompt response before opportunity closes.

- **Matt 5:25** — "Agree with thine adversary quickly, whiles thou art in the way with him."
- **Mark 9:39** — "there is no man which shall do a miracle in my name, that can lightly [= readily/quickly] speak evil of me."
