## Roles in the Concept

- **Action:** παιδεύω (paideuo) — the central discipline/training act itself
- **Process:** παιδεία (paideia) — the ongoing state or experience of being trained
- **Agent:** παιδευτής (paideutes) — the one who administers discipline (father, God)
- **Method:** γυμνάζω (gymnazo) — the training exercise that produces endurance
- **Verbal component:** ἐλέγχω (elegcho) — the reproof/conviction element within discipline
- **Cognitive component:** διδάσκω (didasko) — the instructional/teaching element

## Key Passages Where Multiple Group Words Appear Together

- **Heb 12:5-11** — The definitive discipline passage. Contains paideuo (3×), paideia (4×), paideutes (1×), gymnazo (1×), and elegcho (1×). All five of the non-didasko members co-occur in this single passage.
- **Rev 3:19** — paideuo + elegcho paired: "I rebuke (elegcho) and chasten (paideuo)"
- **2 Tim 3:16** — paideia in context with didasko/didaskalia: "profitable for doctrine (didaskalia), for reproof (elegmos), for correction, for instruction (paideia) in righteousness"
