# WG: Life

## Overview
The NT life-concept involves a cluster of words that distinguish kinds and qualities of life — divine/eternal vs. biographical/material vs. soul-life — and define life against its antithesis, death.

## Roles in the Concept

- **Divine quality:** ζωή (G2222) — life as God's own attribute, given through the Son
- **Verb:** ζάω (G2198) — the act of living, being alive
- **Personal soul:** ψυχή (G5590) — the individual's life-self (risked, saved, lost)
- **Material/biographical:** βίος (G979) — earthly lifespan, livelihood, possessions
- **Qualifier:** αἰώνιος (G166) — eternal, age-lasting; transforms ζωή into the eschatological promise
- **Antithesis:** θάνατος (G2288) — death; defines ζωή by contrast throughout Paul and John

## Key Passages Where Multiple Members Appear Together

- **John 5:24** [ζωή + θάνατος + αἰώνιος] — "hath everlasting life, and shall not come into condemnation; but is passed from death unto life"
- **John 12:25** [ζωή + ψυχή + αἰώνιος] — "He that loveth his life (ψυχή) shall lose it...shall keep it unto life eternal (ζωὴν αἰώνιον)" — the clearest NT distinction between the two words
- **Rom 5:21** [ζωή + θάνατος + αἰώνιος] — "as sin hath reigned unto death, even so might grace reign...unto eternal life"
- **Rom 6:23** [ζωή + θάνατος + αἰώνιος] — "the wages of sin is death; but the gift of God is eternal life"
- **2 Cor 4:11** [ζωή + θάνατος + ζάω] — "delivered unto death for Jesus' sake, that the life also of Jesus might be made manifest in our mortal flesh"
