## Lexicon Definition

Prolongation from a primary ἔνος (enos, "a year"); a year. Distinguished from ἔτος (etos, G2094) by carrying cyclical, liturgical, or annual-recurrence emphasis rather than simple duration.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Liturgical/Ritual Annual Cycle
The dominant NT usage — annual repetition of religious observances.

- **Heb 9:7** — "But into the second [went] the high priest alone once every year (eniautos), not without blood"
- **Heb 9:25** — "as the high priest entereth into the holy place every year (eniautos) with blood of others"
- **Heb 10:1** — "those sacrifices which they offered year by year (eniautos) continually"
- **Heb 10:3** — "in those [sacrifices there is] a remembrance again [made] of sins every year (eniautos)"

### 2. Prophetic/Messianic Year
"The acceptable year of the Lord" — the jubilee concept applied eschatologically.

- **Luke 4:19** — "To preach the acceptable year (eniautos) of the Lord" (quoting Isa 61:2)

### 3. Designating a Specific Calendar Year
Identifying a particular year by official position.

- **John 11:49** — "Caiaphas, being the high priest that same year (eniautos)"
- **John 11:51** — "being high priest that year (eniautos), he prophesied"

### 4. Duration of Activity or Waiting
A period of one year marking a span of activity.

- **Acts 11:26** — "a whole year (eniautos) they assembled themselves with the church"
- **Acts 18:11** — "he continued [there] a year (eniautos) and six months"
- **James 4:13** — "continue there a year (eniautos), and buy and sell"
