## Lexicon Definition

From G2049 (ἐρημόω); **despoliation** — desolation.

The word denotes the result of a devastating act: a place or community rendered empty, ruined, and abandoned. In the NT it carries a specifically prophetic sense, citing the Danielic formula of sanctuary desecration.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. The Abomination of Desolation (Danielic Formula)
Jesus explicitly cites the "abomination of desolation spoken of by Daniel" — a fixed prophetic phrase rooted in Dan 9:27, 11:31, and 12:11. The phrase functions as a warning marker for an identifiable event.

- **Matt 24:15** — "When ye therefore shall see the abomination of desolation, spoken of by Daniel the prophet, stand in the holy place, (whoso readeth, let him understand)"
- **Mark 13:14** — "But when ye shall see the abomination of desolation, spoken of by Daniel the prophet, standing where it ought not, (let him that readeth understand,) then let them that be in Judaea flee to the mountains"

### 2. The Desolation of Jerusalem (Prophetic Fulfillment)
Luke's parallel passage replaces the Danielic formula with the visible sign of armies surrounding Jerusalem — identifying the event in concrete, historical terms.

- **Luke 21:20** — "And when ye shall see Jerusalem compassed with armies, then know that the desolation thereof is nigh"
