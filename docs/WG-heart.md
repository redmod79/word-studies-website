## Roles in the Concept

- **Core term:** lêb (H3820) — the standard word for heart across prose, poetry, and wisdom
- **Emphatic form:** lebab (H3824) — intensified/fuller form; favored in Deuteronomy's "with all your heart and all your soul" and in Chronicles
- **Feminine form:** libbah (H3826) — rare alternate spelling; same meaning
- **Wider inner person:** nephesh (H5315) — the whole living being; frequently paired with lêb in devotion formulas
- **Dynamic power:** ruach (H7307) — the animating force; lêb is the stable seat, ruach the moving energy

## Key Passages Where Multiple Group Words Appear Together

- **Deut 6:5** — "love the LORD thy God with all thine heart (lebab) and with all thy soul (nephesh) and with all thy might" — the Shema uses lebab, not lêb
- **1 Chr 28:9** — "serve him with a perfect heart (lêb) and with a willing mind (lebab + nephesh)" — all three leb-words in near proximity
- **2 Chr 6:38** — "return to thee with all their heart (lêb) and with all their soul (nephesh)" — the covenantal pair
- **Jer 32:41** — (implied promise) — God will give them one heart/lêb and ruach to fear him
