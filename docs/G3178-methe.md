## Lexicon Definition

From Strong's: "Apparently a primary word; an intoxicant, i.e. (by implication) intoxication — drunkenness." The noun denotes the state or act of being intoxicated with strong drink, not merely the beverage itself. Verbal cognate μεθύω (G3184) = "to be drunk"; agent noun μέθυσος (G3183) = "a drunkard"; causative μεθύσκω (G3182) = "to make drunk, become drunk."

## Semantic Categories

All three NT occurrences fall into a single semantic register: μέθη is cited in vice-list or ethical-warning contexts. Sub-categorization reflects the rhetorical frame.

### 1. Eschatological Vigilance — drunkenness as spiritual dulling
- **Luke 21:34** — "And take heed to yourselves, lest at any time your hearts be overcharged with surfeiting, and drunkenness, and cares of this life, and so that day come upon you unawares."

Here μέθη is paired with κραιπάλη (surfeiting, over-indulgence) and βιωτικαὶ μέριμναι (cares of this life) as things that "weigh down" (βαρύνω G925) the heart and cause the Day of the Lord to come unexpectedly. The concern is spiritual stupor, not merely physical intoxication.

### 2. Conduct Becoming Believers — drunkenness as night-behavior
- **Romans 13:13** — "Let us walk honestly, as in the day; not in rioting and drunkenness, not in chambering and wantonness, not in strife and envying."

Paired with κῶμος (revelling), κοίτη (chambering/sexual immorality), ἀσέλγεια (wantonness), ἔρις (strife), ζῆλος (envy). The paired-opposites structure (day/night walking) frames μέθη as darkness behavior incompatible with the believer's eschatological "day."

### 3. Works of the Flesh — drunkenness as fleshly output
- **Galatians 5:21** — "Envyings, murders, drunkenness, revellings, and such like: of the which I tell you before, as I have also told you in time past, that they which do such things shall not inherit the kingdom of God."

Listed among the ἔργα τῆς σαρκός (works of the flesh, Gal 5:19-21). Paired with κῶμος (revellings), φθόνος (envyings), φόνος (murders). Paul explicitly attaches an inheritance-exclusion formula (οὐ κληρονομήσουσιν, G2816): those who practice it will not inherit God's kingdom.

Across all three, μέθη is never a neutral descriptor of a physical condition — it is always a moral-ethical category warranting warning.
