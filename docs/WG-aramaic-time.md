## Roles in the Concept

- **Time-Period:** ʻiddân (עִדָּן) — the set time or year; the primary duration word
- **Appointed Occasion:** zᵉmân (זְמָן) — the scheduled moment or season; complements ʻiddân
- **Temporal Action:** shᵉnâʼ (שְׁנָא) — God changes times; times are changed
- **Temporal Motion:** chălaph (חֲלַף) — times "pass over" someone (marking elapsed duration)
- **Quantifier:** shibʻâh (שִׁבְעָה) — "seven" times; the definite count
- **Sovereign Agent:** ʻillay (עִלַּי) — the Most High who rules over time

## Key Passages Where Multiple Group Words Appear Together

- **Dan 2:21** — ʻiddân + zᵉmân + shᵉnâʼ: "He changeth the **times** (ʻiddânayâʼ) and the **seasons** (zimnayâʼ)" — the fullest statement of divine temporal sovereignty
- **Dan 4:25** — ʻiddân + shibʻâh + chălaph + ʻillay: "**Seven times** (shibʻâh ʻiddânîn) shall **pass over** (yachlᵉphûn) thee, till thou know that the **Most High** (ʻillay) ruleth"
- **Dan 7:25** — ʻiddân (3x) + shᵉnâʼ + zᵉmân: "think to **change** (shᵉnâʼ) **times** (zimnîn)... until a **time** and **times** and the dividing of **time** (ʻiddân)"
