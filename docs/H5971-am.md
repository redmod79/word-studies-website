## Lexicon Definition

From an unused root; a people (as a congregated unit); specifically, a tribe (as
those of Israel); hence (collectively) troops or attendants; figuratively, a
flock:—folk, men, nation, people.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Covenant People of God — "My People" Formula
The possessive *ʻammî* ("my people") is the primary covenant-identity marker. God names Israel as his own; prophets use it in accusation, comfort, and promise.
- **Exo 3:10** — "that thou mayest bring forth my people the children of Israel out of Egypt"
- **Exo 5:1** — "Let my people go, that they may hold a feast unto me in the wilderness"
- **2 Chr 7:14** — "If my people, which are called by my name, shall humble themselves, and pray"
- **Isa 40:1** — "Comfort ye, comfort ye my people, saith your God"
- **Hos 1:9** — "ye [are] not my people, and I will not be your [God]" — covenant revocation
- **Hos 2:23** — "I will say to [them which were] not my people, Thou [art] my people" — covenant restoration

### 2. Elected / Holy People — Corporate Identity
Am designates Israel as a chosen collective, set apart from other peoples.

### 3. The National Populace / Body Politic

## Grammatical Notes: Constructio ad Sensum

Am is a grammatically masculine singular collective noun that frequently triggers constructio ad sensum — the verb or pronoun switches between singular (treating the people as a unit) and plural (treating the people as individuals). Compare:
- **Gen 11:6** — "the people **[is]** one" (singular: unified body)
- **Exo 32:9** — "I have seen this people, and, behold, **it [is]** a stiffnecked people" (singular: corporate entity)
- **Jdg 5:2** — "when the people **willingly offered themselves**" (plural: individual actors)
- **Neh 4:6** — "the people **had** a mind to work" (singular: collective will)

This oscillation is not inconsistent grammar but a feature of Hebrew collective nouns: the speaker can foreground either the group's unity or its constituent members.
