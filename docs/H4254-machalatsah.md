## Lexicon Definition

From H2502 (chalats); a mantle (as easily drawn off): — changeable suit of apparel, change of raiment.

The etymology ties directly to the root idea of "drawing off" or "stripping away" — a garment designed to be removed and replaced, hence "change of raiment." The noun form with the ma- prefix indicates the product or instrument of the root action: the garment that is changed/exchanged.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Luxury / Festival Garments (Secular Context)
- **Isa 3:22** — "The changeable suits of apparel, and the mantles, and the wimples, and the crisping pins,"

Part of a 21-item catalogue (Isa 3:18–24) of luxury goods to be stripped from the daughters of Zion. Listed alongside mantles (H4595 maataphah), wimples (H4304 mitpachath), and crisping pins (H2754 chariyt). The machalatsah here denotes high-quality garments kept as wardrobe changes — fine clothing reserved for festive or formal occasions.

### 2. Divinely Bestowed Clean Garments (Sacred Context)
- **Zec 3:4** — "And he answered and spake unto those that stood before him, saying, Take away the filthy garments from him. And unto him he said, Behold, I have caused thine iniquity to pass from thee, and I will clothe thee with change of raiment."

The angel commands removal of filthy (tsow, H6674 — excremental soiling) garments from Joshua the high priest and replaces them with machalatsah. The exchange is explicitly equated with the removal of iniquity (avon, H5771). The "change of raiment" here functions as the clean replacement garments in a divine investiture ceremony.
