## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same across testaments:**
- The core physical sense of lifting/carrying transfers intact: OT Levites carry the ark (nāśāʾ); NT disciples lift the lame (aírō).
- The idiom "lift the eyes" appears in both testaments using the corresponding word in each language.
- The bearing-of-sin motif carries directly into NT theology (Isa 53 → John 1:29; Heb 9:28).

**What shifts:**
- NT αἴρω strongly favors the "take away" direction over the "bear/endure" direction — 17 of 98 NT uses are explicitly about removal or elimination.
- The forgiveness dimension (nāśāʾ ʿāwōn = God lifts off guilt) in the OT is expressed in NT through compound words like ἀφίημι (aphíēmi, G863) rather than bare αἴρω, though John 1:29 uses aírō for the Lamb's sin-removal.
- The census idiom ("lift the head") has no NT equivalent — αἴρω never functions that way in the NT.
