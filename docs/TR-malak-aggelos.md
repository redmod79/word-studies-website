## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same:**
- The dual sense (divine messenger and human envoy) is preserved across Hebrew → LXX → NT
- The phrase "angel of the Lord" (מַלְאַךְ יְהֹוָה → ángelos Kyríou) is a direct calque
- The servant/dispatch function — one sent by a superior to carry a message or execute a task

**What shifts:**
- In the NT, ággelos becomes almost exclusively a supernatural being; the "human messenger" sense largely passes to apóstolos (G652) and diákonos (G1249)
- The NT develops an angelology with ranks and names (Michael, Gabriel) that the OT largely avoids
- Hebrews 1–2 explicitly argues for the superiority of Christ over ángelos, elevating the category's theological prominence
