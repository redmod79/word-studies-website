## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same across testaments:**
- Grass/herb remains ordinary vegetation: provision for animals, a visible field plant, and an image of temporality.
- Withering/drying imagery continues as a metaphor for human frailty and passing glory.

**What shifts:**
- NT chortos can mean grass in a place where people sit (John 6:10), blade growth in a parable (Mark 4:28), hay as building material (1 Cor 3:12), and grass destroyed in judgment (Rev 8:7).
- The Greek bridge merges eseb with other Hebrew vegetation words, so NT chortos should not be mapped one-to-one back to eseb without context.
