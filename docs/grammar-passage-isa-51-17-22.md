# Grammar Analysis: Isaiah 51:17-22

## Text

**Hebrew (v.17):** הִתְעֹורְרִי הִתְעֹורְרִי קוּמִי יְרוּשָׁלִַם אֲשֶׁר שָׁתִית מִיַּד יְהוָה אֶת־כֹּוס חֲמָתֹו אֶת־קֻבַּעַת כֹּוס הַתַּרְעֵלָה שָׁתִית מָצִית׃

**KJV:**
- **51:17** — Awake, awake, stand up, O Jerusalem, which hast drunk at the hand of the LORD the cup of his fury; thou hast drunken the dregs of the cup of trembling, [and] wrung [them] out.
- **51:18** — [There is] none to guide her among all the sons [whom] she hath brought forth; neither [is there any] that taketh her by the hand of all the sons [that] she hath brought up.
- **51:19** — These two [things] are come unto thee; who shall be sorry for thee? desolation, and destruction, and the famine, and the sword: by whom shall I comfort thee?
- **51:20** — Thy sons have fainted, they lie at the head of all the streets, as a wild bull in a net: they are full of the fury of the LORD, the rebuke of thy God.
- **51:21** — Therefore hear now this, thou afflicted, and drunken, but not with wine:
- **51:22** — Thus saith thy Lord the LORD, and thy God [that] pleadeth the cause of his people, Behold, I have taken out of thine hand the cup of trembling, [even] the dregs of the cup of my fury; thou shalt no more drink it again.

## Key Grammatical Features

### 1. Hithpael Imperative doubled — הִתְעֹורְרִי הִתְעֹורְרִי (v.17)

- **Form:** Verb.Hithpael.Imperative.2fs (from עור, "rouse"), repeated for intensification.
- **Function:** The Hithpael stem carries a reflexive/iterative nuance — "rouse yourself!" The doubled imperative is an emphatic call for Jerusalem to shake off the stupor caused by the cup. It pairs with קוּמִי (Qal Impv.2fs, "arise") to form a three-fold rousing call.
- **Significance:** The stem itself signals that Jerusalem has been passive — knocked down by the cup. The Hithpael places the action of rising back upon her own agency ("wake *yourself*"). The same formula recurs in Isa 52:1 (עוּרִי עוּרִי to Zion), binding this passage to the exile-reversal oracle that follows.
- **Grammar reference:** (no dedicated Hithpael stem entry yet); see `grammar-reference/hebrew/imperative-mood.md` for imperative functions.

### 2. Qal Perfect 2fs chain — שָׁתִית … שָׁתִית מָצִית (v.17)

- **Form:** Three Qal Perfect 2fs verbs addressed to Jerusalem: שָׁתִית ("you have drunk"), שָׁתִית ("you have drunk"), מָצִית ("you have drained").
- **Function:** The perfect aspect in Hebrew prophetic discourse presents the action as completed from the speaker's vantage — Jerusalem's drinking of the cup is a done deed. The second שָׁתִית immediately followed by מָצִית ("drained") is a rhetorical intensification: she has not merely sipped — she has drunk to the bottom.
- **Significance:** The verbal sequence is asyndetic (no waw between שָׁתִית and מָצִית) — the terse juxtaposition creates a cumulative weight. The parsing highlights the completeness of the judgment already endured, which sets up the reversal in v.22 (לָקַחְתִּי Qal Perf.1s, "I have taken").

### 3. Double construct chain — קֻבַּעַת כֹּוס הַתַּרְעֵלָה (vv.17, 22)

- **Form:** Three-member construct chain: קֻבַּעַת (fs.Cst) → כֹּוס (fs.Cst) → הַתַּרְעֵלָה (fs.Abs, articular).
- **Function:** Parallel construct chains cannot normally both take the article, but the article on the absolute noun at the end of the chain definitizes the whole phrase: "the goblet of the cup of the reeling." This is a pleonastic doublet — קֻבַּעַת (a rare word for a deep bowl/goblet) and כֹּוס (the standard word for cup) are stacked for vivid emphasis.
- **Significance:** The rare קֻבַּעַת (only 4 OT occurrences, concentrated in this passage) intensifies the imagery. A construct like this is not redundant — it specifies both the vessel and its contents: the staggering-drink comes in a deep goblet. In v.22 the same chain appears with חֲמָתִי ("my wrath") as the absolute, forming an exact inverse parallelism with v.17's חֲמָתֹו ("his wrath") — the first-person suffix in v.22 signals the divine speaker's direct claim.
- **Grammar reference:** `grammar-reference/hebrew/construct-state.md`, `grammar-reference/hebrew/construct-chain-suffix.md`

### 4. Messenger formula + Participial epithets — כֹּה אָמַר … יָרִיב עַמֹּו (v.22)

- **Form:** Prophetic messenger formula (כֹּה אָמַר + subject) with compound subject (אֲדֹנַיִךְ יְהוָה וֵאלֹהַיִךְ) followed by a yiqtol clause (יָרִיב עַמֹּו) functioning as an epithet: "your God [who] contends for his people."
- **Function:** יָרִיב is Qal Imperfect 3ms from ריב. In this slot it is not a future-tense verb but a relative/characterizing imperfect — "the one who pleads / contends." The imperfect captures the habitual, ongoing character of YHWH's advocacy for his covenant people. The 2fs suffixes on אֲדֹנַיִךְ and אלֹהַיִךְ personalize the address: "*your* Lord, *your* God."
- **Significance:** The clause frames the cup-removal in v.22 not as arbitrary clemency but as covenant advocacy — the same God whose wrath filled the cup (v.17 חֲמָתֹו) is the one who pleads the cause of his people. The 3ms suffix on עַמֹּו binds Jerusalem to him: she is *his* people.
- **Grammar reference:** `grammar-reference/hebrew/qal-imperfect.md`

### 5. Hiphil Imperfect 2fs with negative + Infinitive Construct — לֹא תֹוסִיפִי לִשְׁתֹּותָהּ (v.22)

- **Form:** לֹא + תֹוסִיפִי (Verb.Hiphil.Impf.2fs from יסף "add/continue") + לִשְׁתֹּותָהּ (Verb.Qal.InfCon from שׁתה + 3fs suffix + ל preposition) + עֹוד ("again").
- **Function:** The Hiphil of יסף in the idiom לֹא תֹוסִיפִי + infinitive construct means "you shall not continue/keep on [doing X]" or "you shall no more [do X]." The negated imperfect here is a promissory prohibition — a divine guarantee, not a command. The 3fs suffix on the infinitive (-ָהּ) refers back to the cup (כֹּוס, fs): "to drink *it*."
- **Significance:** This is the hinge of the reversal. In v.17 Jerusalem drank (שָׁתִית Qal.Perf.2fs) — completed, done. In v.22 she will no longer continue to drink — permanent cessation. The idiom תֹוסִיפִי + infinitive frames the prohibition in terms of repetition: the cup has been drunk once (past perfect), and the pattern ends now.
- **Grammar reference:** `grammar-reference/hebrew/hiphil-imperfect.md`

## Clause Structure

### v.17 — Asyndetic imperative cluster + relative clause
- Three ZIm0 clauses (bare imperative, Q-domain / direct speech): `[Pred] הִתְעֹורְרִי` × 2, then `[Pred] קוּמִי`.
- One Voct clause: `[Voct] יְרוּשָׁלִַם` — Jerusalem as the vocative addressee.
- One xQt0 relative clause: `[Rela] אֲשֶׁר [Pred] שָׁתִית [Adju] מִיַּד יְהוָה [Objc] אֶת־כֹּוס חֲמָתֹו` — the relative ties the vocative to the accusation.
- Followed by another xQt0 completing the drinking imagery (the "goblet of the cup of reeling" as fronted object).

### v.22 — Messenger formula + Pendens + Promise
- Clause 1 (xQtX): `[Modi] כֹּה [Pred] אָמַר [Subj] אֲדֹנַיִךְ יְהוָה וֵאלֹהַיִךְ` — standard prophetic messenger formula.
- Clause 2 (ZYq0): `[Pred] יָרִיב [Objc] עַמֹּו` — characterizing yiqtol epithet.
- Clause 3 (xQt0): `[Intj] הִנֵּה [Pred] לָקַחְתִּי [Cmpl] מִיָּדֵךְ [Objc] אֶת־כֹּוס הַתַּרְעֵלָה` — the performative declaration ("I have taken").
- Clause 4 (CPen = **Casus Pendens / fronted element**): `[Frnt] אֶת קֻבַּעַת כֹּוס חֲמָתִי` — the goblet is fronted for emphasis.
- Clause 5 (xYq0): `[Nega] לֹא [Pred] תֹוסִיפִי …` — the promissory prohibition.

The CPen (casus pendens) structure in v.22 is exegetically significant: by fronting "the goblet of the cup of my wrath" as an independent element, the text underlines that *this specific vessel* — the one Jerusalem drained in v.17 — is the thing YHWH is now removing. The fronting binds v.22 back to v.17 as a deliberate reversal.

## Construct Chain Summary

| Verse | Chain | Meaning |
|-------|-------|---------|
| 17 | יַד יְהוָה | hand of YHWH (source of the cup) |
| 17 | כֹּוס חֲמָתֹו | cup of his wrath |
| 17 | קֻבַּעַת כֹּוס הַתַּרְעֵלָה | goblet of the cup of the reeling |
| 22 | כֹּוס הַתַּרְעֵלָה | cup of the reeling |
| 22 | קֻבַּעַת כֹּוס חֲמָתִי | goblet of the cup of my wrath |

Notice the **chiastic substitution**: in v.17 the cup is "his wrath / the reeling"; in v.22 the phrases are reversed — "the reeling / my wrath." The second member of each pair is bonded by suffix to YHWH (3ms in v.17, 1s in v.22), which signals the shift from third-person prophetic description to first-person divine speech.

## Exegetical Flow Across the Unit

- **vv.17-20** — Qal Perfects (שָׁתִית, מָצִית, עֻלְּפוּ, שָׁכְבוּ, יָלָדָה, גִּדֵּלָה) paint completed devastation. Jerusalem has drunk, her sons have fainted, no one guides her. The Pual עֻלְּפוּ (v.20) is a passive intensive — the sons have been *made* to faint; the cup's agency did this to them.
- **v.21** — לָכֵן ("therefore") pivots. The Qal Imperative שִׁמְעִי־נָא addresses the same 2fs subject (Jerusalem) now named as עֲנִיָּה ("afflicted") and שְׁכֻרַת וְלֹא מִיָּיִן ("drunken, but not from wine") — a construct adjective phrase identifying the source of the stupor as the cup, not wine.
- **v.22** — The messenger formula introduces YHWH's direct first-person speech. הִנֵּה + Qal Perfect 1s (לָקַחְתִּי) is a performative perfect — the act of taking occurs in the speaking ("Behold, I hereby remove"). The negated Hiphil imperfect + infinitive construct seals the act: never again.

## Summary

The unit is a chiastic drinking-oracle. v.17 opens with three imperatives demanding Jerusalem rouse herself from the stupor caused by drinking YHWH's cup; the Qal perfects document that drinking as a completed act. vv.18-20 describe the aftermath (sons fainted, streets full of dead, no comforter) in cumulative perfects. v.21 pivots with לָכֵן and a new imperative (שִׁמְעִי). v.22 reverses the oracle through the messenger formula, a performative 1s perfect (לָקַחְתִּי), a fronted casus pendens that reactivates the exact construct chain from v.17, and a promissory negated Hiphil imperfect sealing the never-again. The grammar itself embodies the theological movement from judgment-completed to judgment-removed.
