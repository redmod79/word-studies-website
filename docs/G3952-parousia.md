## Lexicon Definition

From the present participle of πάρειμι (pareimi); a being near, i.e. advent (often, return; specifically of Christ to punish Jerusalem, or finally the world): coming, presence. The word carries the technical sense in Hellenistic Greek of the official visit of a king or high dignitary — an arrival that transforms the situation by the presence of a person of authority.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. The Return of Christ at the End of the Age (Eschatological Parousia)
The dominant use — Christ's final return to judge and gather his people:
- **Matt 24:3** — "what shall be the sign of thy coming, and of the end of the world?"
- **Matt 24:27** — "as the lightning cometh out of the east…so shall also the coming of the Son of man be"
- **1 Thess 4:15** — "we which are alive and remain unto the coming of the Lord shall not prevent them which are asleep"
- **2 Pet 3:12** — "Looking for and hasting unto the coming of the day of God"

### 2. Sanctification and Accountability at His Coming
Parousia is the moment of presentation before Christ — moral preparedness is tied to it:
- **1 Thess 3:13** — "stablish your hearts unblameable in holiness before God…at the coming of our Lord Jesus Christ with all his saints"
- **1 Thess 5:23** — "your whole spirit and soul and body be preserved blameless unto the coming of our Lord Jesus Christ"
- **1 John 2:28** — "abide in him; that, when he shall appear, we may have confidence, and not be ashamed before him at his coming"
- **1 Cor 15:23** — "Christ the firstfruits; afterward they that are Christ's at his coming"

### 3. Denial and Scoffing of the Parousia
Parousia as a contested theological claim — opponents deny it:
- **Matt 24:37–39** — "as the days of Noe were, so shall also the coming of the Son of man be…they knew not until the flood came"
- **2 Pet 3:4** — "Where is the promise of his coming? for since the fathers fell asleep, all things continue as they were"
- **2 Thess 2:1–3** — caution against false claims that the parousia has already occurred

### 4. Physical Presence / Personal Arrival of a Messenger
Used without eschatological force for bodily presence or personal visit:
- **1 Cor 16:17** — "I am glad of the coming of Stephanas and Fortunatus and Achaicus"
- **2 Cor 7:6** — "God…comforted us by the coming of Titus"
- **Phil 1:26** — "your rejoicing may be more abundant in Jesus Christ for me by my coming to you again"
- **Phil 2:12** — "not as in my presence only, but now much more in my absence" (parousia vs. apousia)

### 5. The Counterfeit Parousia of the Antichrist
One occurrence deliberately mirrors Christ's parousia with Satan's:
- **2 Thess 2:8–9** — "the Lord shall consume [the Wicked] with…the brightness of his coming (v.8); [Even him,] whose coming is after the working of Satan with all power and signs and lying wonders (v.9)"

## Hebrew Source

παρουσία does not appear in the Septuagint (LXX). It is a pure NT Greek word with no direct Hebrew equivalent. Its background is Hellenistic royal protocol — the technical term for the official visit of a king, emperor, or deity to a city.

The closest Hebrew concept is **בּוֹא (bôwʼ, H935)** — to come, to arrive — which the LXX translates with ἔρχομαι (G2064), not parousía. See [WG-came.md](WG-came.md) for the full "coming" word group that includes both H935 and its NT Greek equivalents.
