## Semantic Categories

### 1. Israel's Unbelief in God's Mercy Plan (Romans)
- **Rom 11:30** — "For as ye in times past have not believed God, yet have now obtained mercy through their unbelief [apeitheia]"
- **Rom 11:32** — "For God hath concluded them all in unbelief [apeitheia], that he might have mercy upon all"

Paul uses apeitheia to describe Israel's collective rejection of the gospel, but within a framework of divine purpose — their apeitheia opened the door for Gentile mercy, and God "shut up" (synekleisen) all in apeitheia so that mercy might be universal. Dative (11:30) and accusative (11:32) usage.

### 2. The "Children of Disobedience" Under Wrath (Ephesians / Colossians)
- **Eph 2:2** — "the spirit that now worketh in the children of disobedience [apeitheia]"
- **Eph 5:6** — "for because of these things cometh the wrath of God upon the children of disobedience [apeitheia]"
- **Col 3:6** — "For which things' sake the wrath of God cometh on the children of disobedience [apeitheia]"

The formula "children of disobedience" (huioi tēs apeitheias) characterizes a settled condition rather than individual acts. These are persons whose defining trait is apeitheia. The phrase echoes Semitic idiom ("son of X" = person characterized by X). In all three occurrences, divine wrath (orgē) is the stated consequence.

### 3. Exclusion from God's Rest (Hebrews)
- **Heb 4:6** — "they to whom it was first preached entered not in because of unbelief [apeitheia]"
- **Heb 4:11** — "Let us labour therefore to enter into that rest, lest any man fall after the same example of unbelief [apeitheia]"

Hebrews applies apeitheia to the wilderness generation who heard the gospel (euangelisthentes) but failed to enter Canaan. The word appears alongside eiserchomai ("enter") and katapausis ("rest"), framing apeitheia as the barrier to promised rest. Heb 4:11 uses it as a warning "example" (hypodeigma) for the Christian community.
