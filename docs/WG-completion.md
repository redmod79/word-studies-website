## Roles in the Concept

- **Inherent completeness:** tamam (תָּמַם) — reaching wholeness/totality as a natural endpoint; intransitive
- **Cessation/exhaustion:** kalah (כָּלָה) — bringing to an end; can be destructive; both transitive and intransitive
- **Perfecting action:** kalal (כָּלַל) — making something perfect (very rare, 2v); transitive with direct object
- **Transactional completion:** shalam (שָׁלַם) — making whole by repayment/restitution; relational
- **Abrupt termination:** suph (סוּף) — snatching away, cutting off; sudden and final
- **Disappearance:** aphes (אָפֵס) — ceasing to exist, becoming nothing; emphasizes the result

## Key Passages Where Multiple Group Words Appear Together

- **Isa 16:4** — [tamam + kalah + aphes] — "the extortioner is at an end (aphes), the spoiler ceaseth (kalah), the oppressors are consumed (tamam)" — three completion verbs in one verse, each capturing a distinct shade
- **Deu 31:24** — [tamam + kalah] — "when Moses had made an end (kalah) of writing... until they were finished (tamam)" — kalah marks the action of finishing; tamam marks the resulting state of completion
- **Gen 47:15** — [tamam + aphes] — "when money failed (tamam)... money failed (aphes)" — synonymous pairing for resource exhaustion
