## Semantic Categories

### 1. Teaching as Substance Given / Received
The core meaning: doctrine or instruction conceived as a thing handed over from teacher to learner. The etymological link to *laqach* ("take/receive") treats teaching not as an activity but as a transferable substance.

- **Deu 32:2** — "My doctrine shall drop as the rain, my speech shall distil as the dew, as the small rain upon the tender herb, and as the showers upon the grass"
- **Pro 4:2** — "For I give you good doctrine, forsake ye not my law"
- **Isa 29:24** — "They also that erred in spirit shall come to understanding, and they that murmured shall learn doctrine"
- **Job 11:4** — "For thou hast said, My doctrine is pure, and I am clean in thine eyes"

### 2. Learning as Capacity Increased
Used with יָסַף (yasaph, "to increase/add") — the wise person adds to their store of received instruction. Learning is presented as something that grows quantitatively.

- **Pro 1:5** — "A wise man will hear, and will increase learning; and a man of understanding shall attain unto wise counsels"
- **Pro 9:9** — "Give instruction to a wise man, and he will be yet wiser: teach a just man, and he will increase in learning"
- **Pro 16:23** — "The heart of the wise teacheth his mouth, and addeth learning to his lips"

### 3. Persuasion / Fair Speech (Negative Sense)
A single use where the "something received" is seductive persuasion — teaching twisted into enticement. The lexicon notes this "active and sinister sense."

- **Pro 7:21** — "With her much fair speech she caused him to yield, with the flattering of her lips she forced him"

### 4. Wisdom Communicated Through Lips / Speech
Leqach linked to the mouth, lips, or speech as its delivery channel — reinforcing the substance metaphor (teaching is something carried on the lips).

- **Pro 16:21** — "The wise in heart shall be called prudent: and the sweetness of the lips increaseth learning"
- **Pro 16:23** — "The heart of the wise teacheth his mouth, and addeth learning to his lips"
