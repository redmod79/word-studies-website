## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same:**
- The core semantic field of elevation — literal height, raising an object, conferring honor — transfers intact from Hebrew rûwm to Greek ὑψόω
- The divine-sovereignty pattern ("God brings low and raises up") is consistent from Hannah (1 Sam 2) through Mary's Magnificat (Luke 1) to James 4:10 and 1 Peter 5:6
- The worship formula "be exalted" (Ps 57:5, 11; 108:5) finds NT counterpart in the Christ-hymn of Philippians 2:9–11
- Human self-exaltation as pride inviting divine reversal (Deut 8:14; Hos 13:6; Dan 11:36) continues in Matt 23:12: "whosoever shall exalt himself shall be abased"

**What shifts:**
- NT ὑψόω concentrates on *Christological* exaltation — the cross and resurrection as the supreme and definitive "lifting up" — a theological development extending beyond OT rûwm's range
- The heave-offering usage (~20 OT verses where rûwm = ritual elevation of a sacrificial portion) has no NT counterpart in ὑψόω; that sacrificial imagery migrates into other NT vocabulary
- In John 3:14; 8:28; 12:32, crucifixion itself becomes the supreme ὑψόω event, inverting ordinary honor-elevation into suffering-then-glory — a paradox without direct precedent in the OT uses of rûwm
