## Lexicon Definition

A primitive root (compare H2342, H2490); properly, to be rubbed or worn; hence (figuratively) to be weak, sick, afflicted; or (causatively) to grieve, make sick; also to stroke (in flattering), entreat: — beseech, (be) diseased, (put to) grief, be grieved, (be) grievous, infirmity, intreat, lay to, put to pain, pray, make prayer, be (fall, make) sick, sore, be sorry, make suit (supplication), woman in travail, be (become) weak, be wounded.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Physical Sickness / Illness (Qal, Niphal, Hophal)
- **Gen 48:1** — "Behold, thy father is sick" (Jacob's final illness)
- **1 Ki 14:1** — "At that time Abijah the son of Jeroboam fell sick"
- **2 Ki 20:1** — "In those days was Hezekiah sick unto death"
- **2 Ki 13:14** — "Now Elisha was fallen sick of his sickness whereof he died"

### 2. Weakness / Loss of Strength (Qal)
- **Judg 16:7** — "If they bind me with seven green withs... then shall I be weak, and be as another man"
- **Judg 16:11, 17** — Samson repeating the pattern to Delilah: "then shall I be weak"
- **Isa 14:10** — "Art thou also become weak as we? art thou become like unto us?" (taunt against fallen king of Babylon; Pual)

### 3. Entreaty / Supplication (Piel — "to smooth the face of")
- **Exo 32:11** — "And Moses besought the LORD his God" (after golden calf)
- **Psa 119:58** — "I intreated thy favour with my whole heart"
- **Zech 8:21-22** — "Let us go speedily to pray before the LORD... to pray before the LORD of hosts"
- **Mal 1:9** — "And now, I pray you, beseech God that he will be gracious unto us"

### 4. Emotional / Psychological Distress (Qal, Niphal)
- **2 Sam 13:2** — "Amnon was so vexed, that he fell sick for his sister Tamar" (lovesick)
- **Song 2:5** — "Stay me with flagons, comfort me with apples: for I am sick of love"
- **Song 5:8** — "Tell him, that I am sick of love"
- **Amos 6:6** — "They are not grieved for the affliction of Joseph"

### 5. Wound / Battle Injury (Hophal, Qal)
- **1 Ki 22:34** — "A certain man drew a bow at a venture, and smote the king of Israel... I am wounded" (Ahab)
- **2 Chr 35:23** — "The archers shot at king Josiah; and the king said... I am sore wounded"
- **Jer 30:12** — "Thy bruise is incurable, and thy wound is grievous" (Niphal participle)

### 6. Causative: Inflicting Sickness/Grief (Hiphil)
- **Isa 53:10** — "Yet it pleased the LORD to bruise him; he hath put him to grief" (hecheli — Hiphil: the LORD caused his sickness/suffering)
- **Mic 6:13** — "Therefore also will I make thee sick in smiting thee"
- **Hos 7:5** — "In the day of our king the princes have made him sick with bottles of wine"

### 7. Feigned Sickness (Hithpael — reflexive)
- **2 Sam 13:5** — "Lay thee down on thy bed, and make thyself sick" (Jonadab's counsel to Amnon)
- **2 Sam 13:6** — "So Amnon lay down, and made himself sick"
