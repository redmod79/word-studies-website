**Semantic shift in translation:** The LXX splits mo'ed's unified concept (appointed meeting) into distinct Greek words: ἑορτή for festivals, καιρός for timing, and notably reinterprets "tent of meeting" as "tent of testimony" (μαρτύριον), shifting from appointment to witness. The Hebrew chag (H2282), not mo'ed, is the primary source for ἑορτή in the LXX (50x vs. 34x).

No direct quotations where mo'ed/ἑορτή is the quoted word. The connection is conceptual: the OT mo'adim (Lev 23) establish the festival calendar that ἑορτή references throughout the Gospels.

## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same across testaments:**
- ἑορτή in the NT consistently refers to the same divinely appointed festivals established in the OT mo'adim of Leviticus 23
- The feasts retain their character as appointed times of sacred assembly

**What shifts:**
- Mo'ed's broader range (meeting place, appointed time, assembly) narrows to only the festival meaning in ἑορτή
- The LXX's reinterpretation of "tent of meeting" as "tent of testimony" severs the spatial-meeting sense from the feast word entirely
- In Col 2:16, ἑορτή appears in a list (holyday, new moon, sabbath) that echoes the OT pattern (Hos 2:11) but in a context discussing their applicability
