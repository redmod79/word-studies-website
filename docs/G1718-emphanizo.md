## Semantic Categories

### 1. Legal Accusation / Judicial Report (Acts)
The dominant NT usage: to formally bring charges or report information to authorities.
- **Acts 24:1** — "a certain orator named Tertullus, who informed the governor against Paul"
- **Acts 25:2** — "the high priest and the chief of the Jews informed him against Paul"
- **Acts 25:15** — "the chief priests and the elders of the Jews informed me, desiring to have judgment against him"
- **Acts 23:15** — "ye with the council signify to the chief captain that he bring him down"

### 2. Personal Disclosure / Reporting (Acts)
To disclose information privately to someone in authority — not a public accusation but a confidential report.
- **Acts 23:22** — "tell no man that thou hast shewed these things to me" (Paul's nephew reports the ambush plot to the tribune)

### 3. Divine Self-Manifestation (John)
Christ's promise to make himself personally visible/known to those who love him — intimate theophanic disclosure.
- **John 14:21** — "I will love him, and will manifest myself to him"
- **John 14:22** — "Lord, how is it that thou wilt manifest thyself unto us, and not unto the world?"

### 4. Appearing in the Presence of God (Hebrews)
Christ's priestly presentation of himself before God on behalf of humanity — visible appearance in the heavenly sanctuary.
- **Heb 9:24** — "but into heaven itself, now to appear in the presence of God for us"

### 5. Post-Resurrection Visible Appearing (Matthew)
Physical, bodily appearance to human witnesses.
- **Matt 27:53** — "went into the holy city, and appeared unto many"

### 6. Open Declaration (Hebrews)
To make something unmistakably clear through words — verbal manifestation rather than visual.
- **Heb 11:14** — "they that say such things declare plainly that they seek a country"
