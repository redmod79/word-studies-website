## Lexicon Definition

From G863 ἀφίημι (aphiēmi); freedom; (figuratively) pardon — deliverance, forgiveness, remission. Literally "a sending away" or "release." The noun captures the completed act or state that the verb ἀφίημι performs: the release itself.

The LXX data reveals that aphesis is fundamentally a **Jubilee/release word** in the OT. It translates the institutional vocabulary of economic and social liberation (debt remission, slave release, land reversion). The NT theological application — forgiveness of sins — builds on this Jubilee theology: sin is a debt to be released, and Christ proclaims the ultimate Jubilee (cf. Luke 4:18–19 quoting Isa 61:1–2).
