## Lexicon Definition

A revelation, expectation (specifically) by dream; a vision — typically a night-vision. Formed with the intensive/nominalizing -ôwn suffix on the root חזה (chazah). Distinguished from its more common cognate חָזוֹן (chazon, H2377) by its narrower semantic field: chizzayon leans toward dream-visions, night-visions, and the frightening/ecstatic mode of revelation, rather than the prophet's formal oracle.

**Patterns:** Concentrated in Job (50% of all occurrences), where it consistently refers to **night-visions** accompanying dreams and deep sleep. Unlike its cognate chazon (H2377) — which saturates Daniel, Ezekiel, and the prophets — chizzayon is absent from Daniel and the major prophetic corpora except for two special uses in Isaiah ("valley of vision," 22:1, 5) and Joel's eschatological outpouring (2:28). The word belongs to the vocabulary of **experiential, dream-mediated revelation** rather than the formal prophetic oracle.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Night-Vision / Dream-Vision
The dominant Joban use: revelation granted during sleep, deep sleep, or dreams. Paired repeatedly with חֲלוֹם (chalom, "dream") and לַיְלָה (laylah, "night").
- **Job 4:13** — "In thoughts from the visions of the night, when deep sleep falleth on men" (Eliphaz's terrifying encounter)
- **Job 7:14** — "Then thou scarest me with dreams, and terrifiest me through visions"
- **Job 20:8** — "He shall fly away as a dream, and shall not be found: yea, he shall be chased away as a vision of the night"
- **Job 33:15** — "In a dream, in a vision of the night, when deep sleep falleth upon men, in slumberings upon the bed" (Elihu on God's methods of speaking)

### 2. Formal Prophetic Revelation
A single occurrence in which chizzayon stands in for the content of Nathan's divinely-received word to David — here functionally equivalent to chazon.
- **2 Sam 7:17** — "According to all these words, and according to all this vision, so did Nathan speak unto David" (the Davidic covenant oracle)

### 3. Eschatological Outpouring
Paired with dreams and prophesying in the Day-of-the-Lord promise of the Spirit.
- **Joel 2:28** — "your sons and your daughters shall prophesy, your old men shall dream dreams, your young men shall see visions" (quoted by Peter at Pentecost, Acts 2:17, with the LXX form)

### 4. Geographic/Symbolic Designation
A place-name phrase, "valley of vision" — a symbolic designation for Jerusalem as the city of revelation now facing judgment.
- **Isa 22:1** — "The burden of the valley of vision. What aileth thee now, that thou art wholly gone up to the housetops?"
- **Isa 22:5** — "a day of trouble, and of treading down, and of perplexity... in the valley of vision"

### 5. Discredited Prophetic Vision
A single occurrence describing the false prophets' shame over their discredited visions in the messianic cleansing.
- **Zec 13:4** — "the prophets shall be ashamed every one of his vision, when he hath prophesied; neither shall they wear a rough garment to deceive"
