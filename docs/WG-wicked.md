## Roles in the Concept

- **Person:** rasha (H7563) — the morally guilty individual; the standard substantive for "the wicked" as a class
- **Quality:** reshaʻ (H7562) — the abstract wickedness that characterizes the person
- **Action:** rashaʻ (H7561) — the verb: to act wickedly, or forensically to condemn as guilty
- **Broad evil:** raʻ (H7451) — the wider field of badness (natural and moral) within which rasha operates
- **Social dissolution:** beliyyaʻal (H1100) — "sons of Belial" — worthlessness leading to antisocial conduct
- **Impiety:** chaneph (H2611) — profaneness, pollution with sin, godless hypocrisy
- **Injustice:** avval (H5767) — moral crookedness, perverted judgment

## Key Distinctions

- **rasha vs. raʻ:** rasha is always about a morally culpable person; raʻ covers natural evil (calamity, disaster) as well as moral evil
- **rasha vs. beliyyaʻal:** rasha is the generic "wicked person"; beliyyaʻal emphasizes worthlessness and social harm ("sons of Belial" = destructive troublemakers)
- **rasha vs. chaneph:** rasha is morally wrong; chaneph is specifically profane/godless — soiled with impiety
- **rasha vs. avval:** rasha is the broad term; avval focuses on injustice and perverted judgment
