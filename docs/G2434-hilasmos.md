## Lexicon Definition

ἱλασμός (*hil-as-mos'*); from ἱλάσκομαι (G2433); the means or act of propitiation, expiation — the provision by which sin is dealt with and wrath averted. The -μός suffix denotes the action or result of the verb, making hilasmos the abstract noun for the propitiatory act or event itself.

Louw-Nida gloss: "the means of forgiveness, expiation."

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Christ as the Propitiation for Sin
- **1 John 2:2** — "And he is the propitiation (ἱλασμός) for our sins: and not for ours only, but also for [the sins of] the whole world."
- **1 John 4:10** — "Herein is love, not that we loved God, but that he loved us, and sent his Son [to be] the propitiation (ἱλασμόν) for our sins."

Both occurrences identify Christ himself as the hilasmos. In 2:2 the nominative (ἱλασμός) predicates Christ's identity; in 4:10 the accusative (ἱλασμόν) describes the purpose for which the Son was sent. The first places hilasmos in the context of advocacy and ongoing intercession; the second places it in the context of divine love as the initiating cause.

### 2. LXX Usage — Atonement/Forgiveness as a State or Event
The LXX uses ἱλασμός to translate Hebrew concepts of atonement and forgiveness:
- **Lev 25:9** (LXX) — "day of atonement" (ἡμέρᾳ τοῦ ἱλασμοῦ), translating H3725 כִּפֻּר (kippur)
- **Num 5:8** (LXX) — "ram of the atonement" (κριὸς τοῦ ἱλασμοῦ), translating H3725 kippur
- **Psa 130:4** (LXX 129:4) — "there is forgiveness with thee" (ὁ ἱλασμός ἐστιν), translating H5547 סְלִיחָה (selichah)
- **Dan 9:9** (LXX) — "mercies and forgivenesses" (ἱλασμοί), translating H5547 selichah
