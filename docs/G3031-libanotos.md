## Lexicon Definition

From G3030 (λίβανος, frankincense); frankincense, i.e. (by extension) a censer for burning.

*Outline of Biblical Usage:* a censer (the vessel used to hold and burn incense, named after the substance it contained). Referenced in the heavenly sanctuary scene (Revelation 8).

*Note:* The word derives its name from the incense itself (frankincense/λίβανος), making the censer etymologically "the frankincense-thing." The parent word G3030 appears in Matt 2:11 (gift of the Magi) and Rev 18:13 (cargo of Babylon).

## Semantic Categories

### 1. The Heavenly Censer — Offering Incense with Prayers

The censer is held by an angel standing at the heavenly altar. It receives much incense to offer with the prayers of all saints before God's throne.

- **Rev 8:3** — "And another angel came and stood at the altar, having a golden censer (*libanōtón*); and there was given unto him much incense, that he should offer [it] with the prayers of all saints upon the golden altar which was before the throne."

### 2. The Heavenly Censer — Filled with Fire and Cast to Earth

After the offering of incense, the same censer is filled with fire from the altar and hurled to the earth, triggering cosmic upheaval.

- **Rev 8:5** — "And the angel took the censer (*libanōtón*), and filled it with fire of the altar, and cast [it] into the earth: and there were voices, and thunderings, and lightnings, and an earthquake."

*Both uses are accusative singular masculine (N-ASM), referring to the same vessel within a single dramatic scene. The censer functions first as a vessel of intercessory offering, then as an instrument of divine judgment.*
