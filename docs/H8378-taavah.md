## Lexicon Definition

From H183 (ʼâvâh, "to wish for"); a longing; by implication, a delight (subjectively, satisfaction; objectively, a charm): — dainty, desire, exceedingly, greedily, lust(ing), pleasant.

Noun form of the verb אָוָה (ʼâvâh). Whereas the verb denotes the act of craving/longing, taʼăvâh denotes either (a) the craving itself as a subjective state, (b) the object craved, or (c) the satisfaction/delight produced when the craving is met. Semantically neutral at the lexical level — evaluation is supplied by context and modifiers (e.g., "desire of the righteous" vs. "desire of the wicked").

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Neutral / Aesthetic Desirability (Attractive Quality of an Object)
The object is "desirable" as a quality intrinsic to it.
- **Gen 3:6** — "a tree to be desired (taʼăvâh) to make one wise" (the fruit's appeal)
- **Prov 13:12** — "when the desire cometh, [it is] a tree of life"

### 2. Legitimate Human Longing (Answered by God)
The speaker's desire is heard or granted by YHWH; context treats the longing as proper.
- **Psa 10:17** — "thou hast heard the desire of the humble"
- **Psa 21:2** — "Thou hast given him his heart's desire"
- **Psa 38:9** — "Lord, all my desire [is] before thee"
- **Isa 26:8** — "the desire of [our] soul [is] to thy name"

### 3. Desire of the Righteous (Wisdom-Literature Antithesis)
Proverbs repeatedly contrasts "desire of the righteous" with "expectation/desire of the wicked."
- **Prov 10:24** — "the desire of the righteous shall be granted"
- **Prov 11:23** — "The desire of the righteous [is] only good"
- **Prov 13:19** — "The desire accomplished is sweet to the soul"
- **Prov 19:22** — "The desire of a man [is] his kindness"

### 4. Desire of the Wicked / Slothful (Destructive)
Same lexeme, opposite moral valence — derived from the subject, not the word.
- **Psa 10:3** — "the wicked boasteth of his heart's desire"
- **Psa 112:10** — "the desire of the wicked shall perish"
- **Prov 21:25** — "The desire of the slothful killeth him"
- **Prov 21:26** — "He coveteth greedily all the day long"

### 5. Wilderness Craving (Numbers 11 / Psalm 78 / Psalm 106)
A specific narrative complex: Israel's demand for meat at Kibroth-hattaavah ("graves of craving"). This is where KJV reaches for "lust."
- **Num 11:4** — "the mixt multitude… fell a lusting"
- **Psa 78:29–30** — "he gave them their own desire… they were not estranged from their lust"
- **Psa 106:14** — "But lusted exceedingly in the wilderness"

### 6. Physical Appetite (Food)
Concrete appetite — bread, meat, dainty food.
- **1 Sam 2:16** — "as much as thy soul desireth" (priests' share of sacrifice)
- **Job 33:20** — "his soul [abhorreth] dainty meat"
- **Psa 78:29** — satiating the appetite for meat

### 7. Reflective / Introspective Reach
- **Prov 18:1** — "Through desire a man, having separated himself, seeketh [and] intermeddleth with all wisdom"
