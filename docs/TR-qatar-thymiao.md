## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same across testaments:**
- The cultic incense-burning meaning carries intact from OT through LXX into Luke 1:9
- The priestly context is preserved — Zechariah serves as kohen burning incense in the temple, exactly as Aaron did in Exo 30:7–8
- The temple/altar setting remains constant

**What shifts:**
- The word is extremely rare in the NT (1x verb, 6x noun) compared to 112x in OT — reflecting the shift from temple worship to the Christian era
- The NT emphasizes incense as metaphor — Rev 5:8 identifies incense with "the prayers of the saints"; Rev 8:3–4 angel offers incense with prayers
- The Hiphil sacrificial-fat-burning sense (dominant in Leviticus) has no NT parallel — the NT verb retains only the incense sense
