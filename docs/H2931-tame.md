## Semantic Categories

### 1. Unclean Animals (Dietary Law)
Quadrupeds, birds, and sea creatures that violate cud-chewing/split-hoof criteria are declared unclean, prohibiting consumption.
- **Lev 11:7** — "the swine, though he divide the hoof... yet he cheweth not the cud; he [is] unclean unto you"
- **Lev 11:29** — "These also [shall be] unclean unto you among the creeping things"
- **Deut 14:10** — "whatsoever hath not fins and scales ye may not eat; it [is] unclean unto you"

### 2. Unclean through Disease (Leprosy / Skin Conditions)
The priest examines and formally pronounces a person unclean; the declaration carries legal and social consequences.
- **Lev 13:11** — "It [is] an old leprosy... the priest shall pronounce him unclean"
- **Lev 13:44–46** — "He is a leprous man, he [is] unclean: the priest shall pronounce him utterly unclean... he shall dwell alone"
- **Lev 14:57** — "To teach when [it is] unclean, and when [it is] clean: this [is] the law of leprosy"

### 3. Unclean through Contact with Death
Touching a human corpse or the carcase of an unclean animal transmits uncleanness; purification requires specific rites.
- **Num 9:6–7** — "certain men... defiled by the dead body of a man, that they could not keep the passover"
- **Num 19:15** — "every open vessel, which hath no covering bound upon it, [is] unclean"
- **Hag 2:13** — "If [one that is] unclean by a dead body touch any of these, shall it be unclean?"

### 4. Unclean through Bodily Discharges
Seminal emissions and menstrual flows render a person unclean; the state is temporary, resolved by washing and waiting.
- **Lev 5:2** — "if a soul touch any unclean thing, whether [it be] a carcase of an unclean beast"
- **Lev 15:25–26** — "if a woman have an issue of her blood many days... every bed whereon she lieth... shall be unto her as the bed of her separation"
- **Num 5:2** — "put out of the camp every leper, and every one that hath an issue"

### 5. The Clean/Unclean Binary (Governing Distinction)
The most fundamental application: Israel is called to discern and maintain the boundary between these two states.
- **Lev 10:10** — "that ye may put difference between holy and unholy, and between unclean and clean"
- **Lev 11:47** — "To make a difference between the unclean and the clean, and between the beast that may be eaten and the beast that may not be eaten"
- **Eze 44:23** — "they shall teach my people [the difference] between the holy and profane, and cause them to discern between the unclean and the clean"

### 6. Moral/Spiritual Uncleanness (Prophetic Extension)
Prophetic and wisdom literature extend the ritual term to describe moral corruption, personal guilt, and national apostasy.
- **Isa 6:5** — "Woe [is] me! for I [am] a man of unclean lips... I dwell in the midst of a people of unclean lips"
- **Isa 64:6** — "we are all as an unclean [thing], and all our righteousnesses [are] as filthy rags"
- **Job 14:4** — "Who can bring a clean [thing] out of an unclean? not one"
- **Lam 4:15** — "They cried unto them, Depart ye; [it is] unclean; depart, depart, touch not"

### 7. Unclean Land / Geographic Extension
The adjective applies beyond persons and objects to territory: land that is ritually or morally defiled.
- **Jos 22:19** — "if the land of your possession [be] unclean, [then] pass ye over unto the land of the possession of the LORD"
- **Hos 9:3** — "They shall not dwell in the LORD'S land; but Ephraim shall return to Egypt, and they shall eat unclean [things]"
- **Amos 7:17** — Amos told he will die "in a polluted land" (same word טָמֵא)
