## Lexicon Definition

(Aramaic) From a root corresponding to that of H5708; a set time; technically, a year. BDB notes: noun masculine, time. Perhaps a loan-word from Assyrian *adannu* (fixed, appointed, or definite time). Absolute עִדָּן (Dan 7:12+); emphatic עִדָּנָא (Dan 2:8+); plural absolute עִדָּנִין (Dan 4:13+); emphatic עִדָּנַיָּא (Dan 2:21).

Two primary senses: (1) time in general — as duration (Dan 2:8; 7:12) or involving specific conditions (Dan 2:9, 21; 3:5, 15); (2) definite time = year — שִׁבְעָה עִדָּנִין = seven years (Dan 4:13, 20, 22, 29); the prophetic formula עִדָּן וְעִדָּנִין וּפְלַג עִדָּן (Dan 7:25) = 3 1/2 years.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Duration / General Time Span
- **Dan 2:8** — "I know of certainty that ye would gain **the time**, because ye see the thing is gone from me"
- **Dan 2:9** — "ye have prepared lying and corrupt words to speak before me, till **the time** be changed"
- **Dan 7:12** — "the rest of the beasts, they had their dominion taken away: yet their lives were prolonged for a season and **time**"

### 2. Seasons and Times (Divine Sovereignty)
- **Dan 2:21** — "And he changeth **the times** and the seasons: he removeth kings, and setteth up kings"

Here ʻiddân is paired with its Aramaic synonym זְמָן (zᵉmân), emphasizing God's control over all temporal order.

### 3. Point in Time / "When"
- **Dan 3:5** — "at what **time** ye hear the sound of the cornet, flute, harp... ye fall down and worship the golden image"
- **Dan 3:15** — "Now if ye be ready that at what **time** ye hear the sound of the cornet... ye fall down and worship the image"

Used with the Aramaic particle דִּי (dî) to form a temporal clause: "at [the] time that..."

### 4. Definite Time = Year (Nebuchadnezzar's Madness)
- **Dan 4:16** — "let seven **times** pass over him"
- **Dan 4:23** — "till seven **times** pass over him"
- **Dan 4:25** — "seven **times** shall pass over thee, till thou know that the most High ruleth"
- **Dan 4:32** — "seven **times** shall pass over thee, until thou know that the most High ruleth"

In all four occurrences, שִׁבְעָה עִדָּנִין (seven ʻiddânîn) describes the duration of Nebuchadnezzar's beast-like exile. BDB and most lexicographers interpret ʻiddân here as "year," making this a seven-year period.

### 5. Prophetic Time Formula
- **Dan 7:25** — "they shall be given into his hand until **a time** and **times** and the dividing of **time**"

The formula עִדָּן וְעִדָּנִין וּפְלַג עִדָּן (1 + 2 + 0.5 = 3.5 ʻiddânîn) parallels Dan 12:7 (which uses the Hebrew מוֹעֵד). This is the only verse where ʻiddân appears three times.
