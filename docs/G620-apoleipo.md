## Lexicon Definition

Strong's: "from [ἀπό] and [λείπω]; to leave behind (passively, remain); by [extension to forsake]." Abbott-Smith / UBS: active = "to leave behind, leave remaining"; passive (ἀπολείπεται) = "it remains, is reserved." The compound intensifies the simple verb λείπω: the prefix ἀπό marks separation, so the active sense is to leave something *behind* in a place upon departing, and the passive sense is for something to *be left over / remain* after others are accounted for.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Active — To Leave an Object Behind in a Place
The personal subject departs and an object stays where it was set down.
- **2 Timothy 4:13** — "The cloke that I left (apoleípō) at Troas with Carpus, when thou comest, bring with thee"

### 2. Active — To Leave a Person Behind
A traveling subject leaves a companion behind, often for a task or because of circumstance.
- **2 Timothy 4:20** — "Trophimus have I left (apoleípō) at Miletum sick"
- **Titus 1:5** — "For this cause left (apoleípō) I thee in Crete, that thou shouldest set in order the things that are wanting"

### 3. Active — To Abandon / Forsake One's Proper Place
The departure is culpable; the subject leaves behind what should have been kept.
- **Jude 1:6** — "the angels which kept not their first estate, but left (apoleípō) their own habitation"

### 4. Passive — Something Remains / Is Left Over
With no personal agent in view, the verb states that something is still in force, still available, or still outstanding after a reckoning.
- **Hebrews 4:6** — "Seeing therefore it remaineth (apoleípō) that some must enter therein"
- **Hebrews 4:9** — "There remaineth (apoleípō) therefore a rest to the people of God"
- **Hebrews 10:26** — "there remaineth (apoleípō) no more sacrifice for sins"
