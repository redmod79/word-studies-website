# WG: Giving

## Overview
The giving concept in the Greek NT centers on δίδωμι (didomi) and its compound verbs, plus the cognate noun family (dorea, doron) and the semantically adjacent charizomai (grant graciously). Together these words map the full range from general giving to specific nuances: handing over, giving back, sharing, bestowing freely, and granting as favor.

## Relationships

### Shared Root (didomi compounds)
didomi (G1325) is the root; the compounds add directional prefixes:
- **para-** (alongside/over) → paradidomi: hand over, deliver, betray
- **apo-** (from/back) → apodidomi: give back, repay, render what is owed
- **epi-** (upon/to) → epididomi: hand to, give over by hand
- **meta-** (with/sharing) → metadidomi: share, impart to another
- **dia-** (through/throughout) → diadidomi (G1239, not in group): distribute among many
- **ana-** (up) → anadidomi (G325, not in group): deliver up

### Cognate Noun Family (doron root)
- **doron** (G1435) — a gift or offering (concrete)
- **dorea** (G1431) — a free gift, gratuity (abstract, emphasizing grace)
- **doreomai** (G1433) — to bestow gratuitously (verbal form)
- **dorema** (G1434, not in group) — a bestowment

## Roles in the Concept

- **General action:** didomi (δίδωμι) — the broadest giving verb, used for God giving, human giving, granting authority, yielding fruit
- **Surrender/transfer:** paradidomi (παραδίδωμι) — handing over to another's power (betrayal, tradition, delivery)
- **Obligation/return:** apodidomi (ἀποδίδωμι) — giving back what is owed (repay, render, reward)
- **Physical handoff:** epididomi (ἐπιδίδωμι) — handing something to someone directly
- **Sharing:** metadidomi (μεταδίδωμι) — imparting a portion to another
- **Free gift (noun):** dorea (δωρεά) — gift given freely, especially God's gifts
- **Free gift (verb):** doreomai (δωρέομαι) — the act of bestowing freely
- **Offering/present:** doron (δῶρον) — gift as concrete object, especially temple offerings
- **Gracious grant:** charizomai (χαρίζομαι) — granting as a favor, with overtones of grace and forgiveness
