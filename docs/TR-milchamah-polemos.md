## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same across testaments:**
- Both milchamah and polemos cover the full spectrum from literal military engagement to organized campaign
- The collocation with kingship (kings going to war) persists from OT narrative into NT parables (Luk 14:31) and apocalyptic (Rev 16:14; 19:19)
- "War" as divine necessity ("must come to pass") bridges OT prophetic expectation and the Olivet Discourse

**What shifts:**
- The NT dramatically expands polemos into supernatural and cosmic domains: war in heaven (Rev 12:7), the beast's war against saints (Rev 13:7) — milchamah in the OT is overwhelmingly terrestrial
- Figurative/interpersonal use emerges: James 4:1 uses polemos for internal community conflicts driven by desire, a usage without clear OT milchamah precedent
- Concentration shifts from historical narrative (OT) to apocalyptic vision (NT): 9 of 16 NT uses are in Revelation
