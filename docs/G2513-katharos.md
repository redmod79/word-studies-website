## Semantic Categories

### 1. Moral/Spiritual Purity of Heart and Conscience
The dominant NT usage — katharos describes an inner state of moral cleanness, applied to the heart, conscience, and faith.
- **Mat 5:8** — "Blessed [are] the pure in heart: for they shall see God."
- **1Ti 1:5** — "Now the end of the commandment is charity out of a pure heart, and [of] a good conscience, and [of] faith unfeigned."
- **1Ti 3:9** — "Holding the mystery of the faith in a pure conscience."
- **2Ti 1:3** — "I thank God, whom I serve from [my] forefathers with pure conscience."
- **2Ti 2:22** — "follow righteousness, faith, charity, peace, with them that call on the Lord out of a pure heart."

### 2. Ritual/Declarative Cleanness
Used in contexts where cleanness is pronounced or conferred — the state resulting from cleansing.
- **Jhn 13:10** — "He that is washed needeth not save to wash [his] feet, but is clean every whit: and ye are clean, but not all."
- **Jhn 13:11** — "For he knew who should betray him; therefore said he, Ye are not all clean."
- **Jhn 15:3** — "Now ye are clean through the word which I have spoken unto you."
- **Heb 10:22** — "having our hearts sprinkled from an evil conscience, and our bodies washed with pure water."

### 3. All Things Declared Clean (Food/General)
Katharos in universalizing statements about created things being inherently clean.
- **Luk 11:41** — "But rather give alms of such things as ye have; and, behold, all things are clean unto you."
- **Rom 14:20** — "All things indeed [are] pure; but [it is] evil for that man who eateth with offence."
- **Tit 1:15** — "Unto the pure all things [are] pure: but unto them that are defiled and unbelieving [is] nothing pure."

### 4. Innocence/Guiltlessness from Responsibility
Applied to persons who are "clean" from blood-guilt or moral responsibility for others.
- **Act 18:6** — "Your blood [be] upon your own heads; I [am] clean: from henceforth I will go unto the Gentiles."
- **Act 20:26** — "I [am] pure from the blood of all [men]."

### 5. Physical Purity of Materials and Garments
Describes literal purity of objects — linen, gold, water, crystal — predominantly in Revelation's eschatological imagery.
- **Mat 27:59** — "he wrapped it in a clean linen cloth."
- **Rev 15:6** — "clothed in pure and white linen."
- **Rev 19:8** — "arrayed in fine linen, clean and white: for the fine linen is the righteousness of saints."
- **Rev 19:14** — "clothed in fine linen, white and clean."
- **Rev 21:18** — "the city [was] pure gold, like unto clear glass."
- **Rev 21:21** — "the street of the city [was] pure gold, as it were transparent glass."
- **Rev 22:1** — "a pure river of water of life, clear as crystal."

### 6. Pure Religion
- **Jas 1:27** — "Pure religion and undefiled before God and the Father is this, To visit the fatherless and widows in their affliction."
