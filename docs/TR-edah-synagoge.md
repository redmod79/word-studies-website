## OT Usage (Hebrew)

| Strong's | Word | Occurrences | Primary Meaning |
|----------|------|-------------|-----------------|
| H5712 | עֵדָה (edah) | 140 | congregation, assembly, company |

Edah denotes the covenant community of Israel as an organized body. Concentrated in Numbers (75x) and the Pentateuch overall (102x). Used for the entire assembly addressed by Moses, the body that judges legal cases (Num 35:24–25), and occasionally rebel factions (Korah's company, Num 16).

**Key OT passages:**
- **Exo 12:3** — "Speak ye unto all the congregation of Israel" — first occurrence; Passover institution
- **Num 27:17** — "that the congregation of the LORD be not as sheep which have no shepherd"
- **Psa 74:2** — "Remember thy congregation, which thou hast purchased of old"

**Semantic shift in translation:** The LXX synagoge faithfully preserves edah's meaning of "gathered assembly/congregation." The Greek word literally means "a bringing together" (syn + ago). In the LXX period, synagoge carried the full weight of the covenant-community concept. The shift toward a building/institution meaning ("synagogue" as a place of worship) developed during the Second Temple period, after the LXX translation.

Edah is the dominant Hebrew source for synagoge (120 vs. 36 from qahal), whereas qahal is the dominant source for ekklesia. This created a meaningful LXX distinction: edah → synagoge (the existing community) vs. qahal → ekklesia (the called-out assembly). Both Hebrew words overlap in meaning, but the LXX translators consistently distinguished them.

## NT Usage (Greek)

| Strong's | Word | Occurrences | Primary Meaning |

By NT times, synagoge has narrowed from "the assembled congregation" to primarily mean the Jewish synagogue as a place of worship. Of 57 NT uses, the vast majority refer to the physical synagogue building.

**Key NT passages:**

## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same across testaments:**
- The core meaning of a gathered group of people persists from edah through synagoge
- The term retains its association with the covenant community and its identity markers
- Judicial/deliberative function echoes in Acts synagogue scenes (Acts 6:9, 9:2)

**What shifts:**
- OT edah = the whole people of Israel as an organized body, portable and mobile (wilderness), with no fixed building
- LXX synagoge = faithful rendering of this communal sense, still primarily about the people
- NT synagoge = the institution and its building; the community aspect narrows to a local gathering place
- The NT church (ekklesia, from qahal) takes over the role edah once held — the covenant assembly of God's people
- Rev 2:9 and 3:9 ("synagogue of Satan") echo the OT "evil congregation" language of Numbers 14:27, 35
