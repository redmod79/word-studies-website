## Lexicon Definition

A primitive root; properly, to push forth; (but used only figuratively) to lash, i.e. (the sea with oars) to row; by implication, to travel: — go (about, through, to and fro), mariner, rower, run to and fro.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Physical Traversal / Going Through a Territory
- **Num 11:8** — "the people **went about**, and gathered it" — collecting manna
- **2Sa 24:2** — "**Go** now **through** all the tribes of Israel, from Dan even to Beersheba"
- **2Sa 24:8** — "when they had **gone through** all the land, they came to Jerusalem"

The Qal stem describes systematic physical movement through a defined area.

### 2. Roaming / Ranging Over the Earth
- **Job 1:7** — Satan: "From **going to and fro** in the earth, and from walking up and down in it"
- **Job 2:2** — Satan: "From **going to and fro** in the earth, and from walking up and down in it"

Paired with halak (walk up and down), shuwt describes comprehensive, restless ranging without a stated objective. The Qal infinitive construct emphasizes continuous activity.

### 3. Divine Surveillance — Eyes Ranging Over the Earth
- **2Ch 16:9** — "the eyes of the LORD **run to and fro** throughout the whole earth, to shew himself strong"
- **Zec 4:10** — "they are the eyes of the LORD, which **run to and fro** through the whole earth"

The Polel participle describes God's omniscient oversight — comprehensive, active, purposeful scanning.

### 4. Urgent Searching / Seeking in Desperation
- **Dan 12:4** — "many shall **run to and fro**, and knowledge shall be increased"
- **Amo 8:12** — "they shall **run to and fro** to seek the word of the LORD, and shall not find it"
- **Jer 5:1** — "**Run ye to and fro** through the streets of Jerusalem... if ye can find a man"
- **Jer 49:3** — "lament, and **run to and fro** by the hedges"

The Polel/Piel and Hithpael forms in prophetic contexts describe desperate, restless searching — often with a stated object of search (knowledge, the word of the LORD, a righteous man). Dan 12:4 and Amo 8:12 share this pattern: Polel imperfect 3mp, people running to and fro to seek something.

### 5. Seafaring — Rowing / Sailing
- **Eze 27:8** — "the inhabitants of Zidon and Arvad were thy **mariners**" (Qal participle)
- **Eze 27:26** — "thy **rowers** have brought thee into great waters"

The participial form describes those who "push forth" through the sea — rowers and sailors. This may be closest to the literal root meaning ("to push forth").
