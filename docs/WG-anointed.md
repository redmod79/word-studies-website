# WG: Anointed One (Priest / King / Messiah)

## Overview

The "anointed" concept in Hebrew Scripture involves oil consecrating a person to a specific office before YHWH. Five words form the core cluster: the noun for the consecrated individual (mashiyach), its root verb (mashach), and the three offices the anointed occupies — king (melek), priest (kohen), and prince/commander (nagiyd).

## Roles in the Concept

- **The Act:** mashach (מָשַׁח) — the physical anointing with oil that effects consecration
- **The Office:** mashiyach (מָשִׁיחַ) — the person so consecrated; "the anointed one"
- **Royal form:** melek (מֶלֶךְ) — king; the dominant role mashiyach occupies (David, Solomon, Saul, Cyrus)
- **Priestly form:** kohen (כֹּהֵן) — priest; the role in Leviticus (anointed high priest)
- **Leadership form:** nagiyd (נָגִיד) — designated leader/prince; paired with mashiyach in Dan 9:25 ("Messiah the Prince")

## Key Passages Where Multiple Members Appear Together

- **Lev 4:3** — "the priest (kohen) that is anointed (mashiyach)" — priest as mashiyach
- **1 Sam 2:10** — "exalt the horn of his anointed (mashiyach)... his king (melek)" — anointed = king in parallel
- **1 Kings 1:39** — "Zadok the priest (kohen)... anointed (mashach) him; and they blew with the trumpet; and all the people said, God save king (melek) Solomon"
- **Dan 9:25** — "unto the Messiah (mashiyach) the Prince (nagiyd)"
