## Lexicon Definition

Evidence given (judicially or generally); testimony, witness, record. From μάρτυς (a witness). Used for both formal court testimony and the broader act of declaration about what one has seen or knows.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Legal / Judicial Testimony
Formal testimony in a court or quasi-legal setting; the validity and consistency of evidence.

- **Mark 14:55** — "the council sought for witness against Jesus to put him to death; and found none"
- **Mark 14:56** — "many bare false witness against him, but their witness agreed not together"
- **Luke 22:71** — "What need we any further witness? for we ourselves have heard of his own mouth"
- **John 8:17** — "it is written in your law, that the testimony of two men is true"

### 2. The Johannine Self-Testimony Debate
John's Gospel uses μαρτυρία to explore the legitimacy of self-testimony vs. external corroboration.

- **John 5:31–32** — "If I bear witness of myself, my witness is not true. There is another that beareth witness of me"
- **John 8:13–14** — "Thou bearest record of thyself; thy record is not true. Though I bear record of myself, yet my record is true: for I know whence I came"
- **John 5:36** — "I have greater witness than that of John: for the works which the Father hath given me"

### 3. Apostolic and Divine Witness
The testimony of eyewitnesses and the testimony of God himself — grounding belief in reliable declaration.

- **John 19:35** — "he that saw it bare record, and his record is true… that ye might believe"
- **John 21:24** — "this is the disciple which testifieth of these things… we know that his testimony is true"
- **1 John 5:9–10** — "if we receive the witness of men, the witness of God is greater: for this is the witness of God which he hath testified of his Son"
- **1 John 5:11** — "this is the record, that God hath given to us eternal life, and this life is in his Son"

### 4. Testimony of Jesus Christ in Revelation
In Revelation μαρτυρία shifts to a quasi-technical phrase — "the testimony of Jesus" — marking those who hold Christ's testimony as the defining characteristic of the faithful.

- **Rev 1:2** — "who bare record of the word of God, and of the testimony of Jesus Christ"
- **Rev 6:9** — "souls of them that were slain for the word of God, and for the testimony which they held"
- **Rev 12:11** — "they overcame him by the blood of the Lamb, and by the word of their testimony"
- **Rev 19:10** — "the testimony of Jesus is the spirit of prophecy"

### 5. Character Reference / Community Report
Reputation or report about a person's moral standing, attested by others.

- **1 Tim 3:7** — "moreover he must have a good report of them which are without"
- **Tit 1:13** — "this witness is true. Wherefore rebuke them sharply"
- **3 John 1:12** — "Demetrius hath good report of all men, and of the truth itself"
