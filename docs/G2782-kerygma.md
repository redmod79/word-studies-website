## Lexicon Definition

(Thayer) That which is promulgated by a herald or public crier, a proclamation by herald. In the NT: the message or proclamation by the heralds of God or Christ — (1) the proclamation of the necessity of repentance made by the prophet Jonah (Matt 12:41; Luke 11:32; cf. Jonah 3:4); (2) the announcement of salvation procured by Christ and to be had through him (1 Cor 1:21; Titus 1:3); with the genitive of the subject = made by one (1 Cor 2:4; 15:14); with the genitive of the object = concerning Jesus Christ (Rom 16:25); the act of publishing (2 Tim 4:17).

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Prophetic Proclamation Calling to Repentance
- **Matt 12:41** — "The men of Nineveh shall rise in judgment with this generation, and shall condemn it: because they repented at the preaching of Jonas; and, behold, a greater than Jonas is here."
- **Luke 11:32** — "The men of Nineve shall rise up in the judgment with this generation, and shall condemn it: for they repented at the preaching of Jonas; and, behold, a greater than Jonas is here."

Both refer to Jonah's kērygma — a divine proclamation that produced repentance. The word denotes the authoritative content proclaimed, not the act of speaking itself.

### 2. The Apostolic Proclamation of Christ (Gospel Content)
- **Rom 16:25** — "Now to him that is of power to stablish you according to my gospel, and the preaching of Jesus Christ, according to the revelation of the mystery"
- **1 Cor 1:21** — "it pleased God by the foolishness of preaching to save them that believe"
- **1 Cor 15:14** — "And if Christ be not risen, then is our preaching vain, and your faith is also vain"

Here kērygma refers to the proclaimed message about Christ — its content, not merely the activity. In 1 Cor 15:14, the validity of the kērygma depends entirely on the factual reality of the resurrection.

### 3. The Act and Manner of Proclaiming
- **1 Cor 2:4** — "And my speech and my preaching was not with enticing words of man's wisdom, but in demonstration of the Spirit and of power"
- **2 Tim 4:17** — "the Lord stood with me, and strengthened me; that by me the preaching might be fully known, and that all the Gentiles might hear"
- **Titus 1:3** — "But hath in due times manifested his word through preaching, which is committed unto me according to the commandment of God our Saviour"

In these uses kērygma shades toward the act of proclamation — the heralding itself as a divine instrument. In 1 Cor 2:4, Paul contrasts the manner of his kērygma (Spirit-empowered) with rhetorical persuasion.
