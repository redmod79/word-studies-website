## Lexicon Definition

Something portioned (i.e. fashioned) out, as a shape; by extension phantom, embodiment, or manifestation (of favor). KJV renderings cluster tightly around **image**, **likeness**, and **similitude**.

The BDB data in the lexicon table keeps the OT usage narrow: a **representation / form / semblance**, especially in four settings visible in the corpus itself: forbidden likenesses in the image-commandment texts, the unseen form at Horeb, the LORD's form beheld by Moses, and the form of a nocturnal apparition.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Forbidden Visual Representation
- **Exo 20:4** — "Thou shalt not make unto thee any graven image, or any likeness..."
- **Deu 4:16** — "make you a graven image, the similitude of any figure"
- **Deu 4:23** — "make you a graven image, [or] the likeness of any [thing]"
- **Deu 4:25** — "make a graven image, [or] the likeness of any [thing]"
- **Deu 5:8** — "Thou shalt not make thee [any] graven image, [or] any likeness..."

Here *temunah* names the visual representation itself. It is not yet the idol-object term (*pecel*), but the likeness or form that the idol attempts to capture.

### 2. No Visible Form at Horeb
- **Deu 4:12** — "ye heard the voice of the words, but saw no similitude"
- **Deu 4:15** — "ye saw no manner of similitude on the day that the LORD spake unto you in Horeb"

These verses supply the logic behind the prohibition: revelation at Horeb was auditory, not visual. Israel heard a voice but was given no divine form to reproduce.

### 3. Granted Sight of the LORD's Form
- **Num 12:8** — "the similitude of the LORD shall he behold"
- **Psa 17:15** — "I shall be satisfied, when I awake, with thy likeness"

Outside the prohibition passages, *temunah* can denote a true manifestation or form belonging to the LORD rather than a fabricated representation of him.

### 4. Apparition / Visionary Form
- **Job 4:16** — "I could not discern the form thereof: an image [was] before mine eyes"

In Job 4:16 the word refers neither to an idol nor to covenant revelation, but to the visible form of a night apparition.
