## Lexicon Definition

> Or עוֹלָה (ʻôwlâh); feminine active participle of [עָלָה]; a step or (collectively, stairs, as ascending); usually a holocaust (as going up in smoke):—ascent, burnt offering (sacrifice), go up to.

The name derives from the root עלה "to go up/ascend" — the sacrifice is so-called because it (and/or its smoke) "goes up" entirely to God. Unlike other offerings, no portion is retained by priest or worshipper; the whole animal is consumed on the altar.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. The Whole Burnt Offering — Cultic Sacrifice Wholly Consumed
The dominant sense: an animal (bullock, ram, lamb, kid, bird) entirely burned on the altar to YHWH, as a "sweet savour" (ריח ניחוח).
- **Gen 8:20** — Noah's first post-flood worship: burnt offerings of every clean beast and fowl.
- **Gen 22:2, 7–8, 13** — Abraham instructed to offer Isaac as an ʻôlâh; ram substituted.
- **Lev 1:3–17** — the founding torah of the burnt offering: male without blemish, hand-laying, flaying, washing of inwards and legs, all burned.
- **Lev 6:9** — "This is the law of the burnt offering: It is the burnt offering, because of the burning upon the altar all night."

### 2. Continual / Daily Burnt Offering (ʻôlâh tāmîd)
The perpetual morning-and-evening sacrifice that structures Israel's cultic time.
- **Exo 29:42** — "a continual burnt offering throughout your generations at the door of the tabernacle."
- **Num 28:3–6, 10, 23, 31** — twice-daily lambs plus added ʻôlôt for sabbaths, new moons, feasts.
- **Num 29:6, 11, 16, 19, 22, 25, 28, 31, 34, 38** — the refrain "beside the continual burnt offering."
- **Ezr 3:5** — the returned exiles resume the "continual burnt offering."

### 3. Festival and Occasional Burnt Offerings
Additional ʻôlôt for new moons, feasts (Passover, Weeks, Trumpets, Atonement, Tabernacles), royal accessions, and temple dedications.
- **Num 28:11; 29:2, 8, 13, 36** — festival schedules.
- **1 Ki 3:4** — Solomon's thousand burnt offerings at Gibeon.
- **1 Ki 8:64; 2 Chr 7:1, 7** — Solomon's temple-dedication ʻôlôt.
- **2 Chr 29:27–35** — Hezekiah's reform offerings.

### 4. Individual and Voluntary Burnt Offerings
A worshipper's personal offering — often paired with sin offering for purification, or brought as vow/freewill.
- **Lev 12:6, 8; 14:19–20, 22, 31; 15:15, 30** — postpartum and purification offerings.
- **Job 1:5; 42:8** — Job's intercessory ʻôlôt for his children and friends.
- **Jdg 13:16, 23** — Manoah and the Angel of YHWH.
- **Eze 46:12** — the prince's "voluntary burnt offering."

### 5. Burnt Offering with Peace / Meat / Sin Offerings (Covenant Worship Sequence)
ʻôlâh collocates heavily with zebach shelamim (peace/fellowship offering), minchah (meal/tribute), and chattaʼth (sin offering) — forming a ritual grammar: atonement → ascent → communion.
- **Exo 20:24** — "shalt sacrifice thereon thy burnt offerings, and thy peace offerings."
- **Lev 9:22** — Aaron's inaugural sequence: sin, burnt, peace.
- **2 Sam 6:17–18; 24:25** — David's burnt + peace offerings at the ark and threshing floor.
- **1 Chr 16:1–2, 40; 29:21** — Davidic liturgy.

### 6. Prophetic Critique — Burnt Offerings Without Obedience
The prophets repeatedly subordinate ʻôlâh to covenant fidelity, mercy, and justice; burnt offerings are rejected when divorced from the heart.
- **1 Sam 15:22** — "Hath the LORD as great delight in burnt offerings and sacrifices, as in obeying the voice of the LORD?"
- **Isa 1:11** — "I am full of the burnt offerings of rams."
- **Jer 7:21–22** — "Put your burnt offerings unto your sacrifices, and eat flesh."
- **Hos 6:6** — "I desired mercy, and not sacrifice; and the knowledge of God more than burnt offerings."
- **Mic 6:6–7** — "Shall I come before him with burnt offerings?"
- **Psa 40:6; 50:8; 51:16–19** — YHWH delights not in ʻôlâh but in a broken spirit / righteousness.

### 7. Non-Cultic "Ascent / Step" Sense (Rare)
The root meaning "going up" surfaces where the noun denotes a literal ascent or stairway.
- **Eze 40:26** — "seven steps to go up to it" (ʻôlâh as stairway in the temple-vision gate).
- Some commentators also read 2 Sam 15:30 and Ezek 40 terminology in this line.
