## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same across testaments:**
- Dual "bride / daughter-in-law" semantic range carries cleanly from Hebrew kallâh into Greek nýmphē.
- The paired formula "bride and bridegroom" (kallâh + châthân → nýmphē + nymphíos) survives intact; Rev 18:23 quotes Jeremiah almost word-for-word.
- The covenant-marriage figure (God / Zion / people) is already present in Isaiah, Jeremiah, and Hosea; Revelation extends it to Christ and the church.

**What shifts:**
- NT usage drops the literal "daughter-in-law" / kinship-law contexts that dominate the Pentateuch and historical books; only the synoptic division sayings preserve that sense.
- Revelation narrows nýmphē to one referent: the eschatological people of God presented to the Lamb. The OT bride imagery was applied to Zion / Israel; Revelation applies it to New Jerusalem / the church.
- The bridegroom identity shifts: in the OT, YHWH is the husband of Israel; in the NT, the Lamb (Christ) is the bridegroom.
