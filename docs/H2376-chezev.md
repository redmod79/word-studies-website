## Lexicon Definition

(Aramaic) From חֲזָא (H2370, "to gaze"); a sight, vision, appearance. BDB: emphatic חֶוְוָא (Dan 2:19); suffixal forms וִי־ (Dan 7:2), ־וִהּ (Dan 7:20); plural construct חֶזְוֵי (Dan 2:28+). Two senses: (1) **vision as mode of revelation** — Dan 2:19, 2:28, 4:5, 4:9, 4:10, 4:13, 7:1, 7:2, 7:7, 7:13, 7:15; (2) **appearance** — Dan 7:20 ("whose look"). The word is the Aramaic counterpart to Hebrew חָזוֹן (chazon, H2377) and חִזָּיוֹן (chizzayon, H2384). In every revelatory use in Daniel it functions as a technical term for the visionary content perceived in prophetic disclosure, typically occurring "by night" (בְּלֵילְיָא) or "of the head" (רֵאשִׁי) upon the bed.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Night Vision as Medium of Secret-Revealing
- **Dan 2:19** — "Then was the secret revealed unto Daniel in a **night vision (chêzêv)**. Then Daniel blessed the God of heaven."
- **Dan 2:28** — "there is a God in heaven that revealeth secrets... thy dream, and **the visions (chezvê) of thy head** upon thy bed, are these"

chêzêv is the channel by which the "secret" (רָז, raz) is disclosed — paired directly with the verb "reveal" (גְּלָא, gelaʼ) and the noun "secret."

### 2. Visions of the Head Upon the Bed (Formulaic)
- **Dan 4:5** — "I saw a dream which made me afraid... and **the visions of my head** troubled me"
- **Dan 4:10** — "Thus were **the visions of mine head** in my bed"
- **Dan 4:13** — "I saw **in the visions of my head** upon my bed, and, behold, a watcher"
- **Dan 7:1** — "Daniel had a dream and **visions of his head** upon his bed"
- **Dan 7:15** — "I Daniel was grieved in my spirit... and **the visions of my head** troubled me"

The stock formula חֶזְוֵי רֵאשִׁי ("visions of my head") + עַל־מִשְׁכְּבִי ("upon my bed") frames chêzêv as the experiential content received during sleep-revelation. Recurring effect: "troubled" (בְּהַל, behal) the dreamer.

### 3. Visions Paired With the Dream
- **Dan 4:9** — "tell me **the visions of my dream** that I have seen, and the interpretation thereof"
- **Dan 7:1** — "Daniel had a dream **and visions** of his head upon his bed"

chêzêv is distinguished from chêlem (A2493, "dream") as the seen content within the dream-event. The dream is the experience; the chezvê are the discrete visual scenes perceived.

### 4. Night-Vision Apocalyptic Sequence (Daniel 7)
- **Dan 7:2** — "I saw **in my vision (chêzvî)** by night, and, behold, the four winds of the heaven strove upon the great sea"
- **Dan 7:7** — "After this I saw **in the night visions (chezvê lêylyâʼ)**, and behold a fourth beast"
- **Dan 7:13** — "I saw **in the night visions**, and, behold, one like the Son of man came with the clouds of heaven"

The triadic formula "I saw in the night visions" structures Daniel 7 into three apocalyptic scenes (winds/sea; fourth beast; Son of man). chêzêv here is a literary-structural marker of apocalyptic disclosure.

### 5. Appearance / Outward Look (Non-Revelatory)
- **Dan 7:20** — "that horn... **whose look (chezvêh)** was more stout than his fellows"

Sole non-revelatory use. The word denotes the observed appearance of the little horn relative to the others — sense 2 of BDB. Same noun, shifted from "what is seen in prophecy" to "how a thing appears to the eye."

**Key distinction:** chêzêv and châzôwn are the same semantic category in two dialects. The book of Daniel uses chêzêv in its Aramaic chapters (2, 4, 7) and châzôwn in its Hebrew chapters (8–12). Within Daniel 2–7, chêlem names the dream-event while chêzêv names the seen content; the two are juxtaposed, not equated.
