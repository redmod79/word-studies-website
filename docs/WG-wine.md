## Roles in the Concept

- **General wine:** oinos (G3631) — covers fermented grape wine in all contexts (literal, parabolic, apocalyptic)
- **Fresh wine:** gleukos (G1098) — sweet new wine, possibly unfermented or lightly fermented (Acts 2:13)
- **Sour wine:** oxos (G3690) — cheap vinegar-wine; offered at the cross (Mat 27:48, Jhn 19:29-30)
- **Strong drink:** sikera (G4608) — fermented beverages other than grape wine (Lk 1:15 only)
- **Medicinal wine:** smyrnizo (G4669) — wine drugged with myrrh as analgesic (Mk 15:23 only)
- **Drunkard label:** oinopotes (G3630) — accusation of being a wine-drinker (Mat 11:19, Lk 7:34)
- **Drunken excess:** oinophlygia (G3632) — state of wine-debauchery (1Pe 4:3)
- **Produce of the vine:** gennēma (G1081) — the vine's generated yield; chosen by all three Synoptics over oinos for Jesus' Last Supper cup-saying (Mat 26:29, Mar 14:25, Luk 22:18), matching the Jewish Passover berakhah *borei peri ha-gafen*

## Key Passages Where Multiple Group Words Appear Together

- **Luk 1:15** — oinos + sikera: "shall drink neither wine nor strong drink" (John the Baptist's Nazirite-like abstinence)
- **Mar 15:23** — oinos + smyrnizo: "wine mingled with myrrh" (offered to Jesus at crucifixion, refused)
