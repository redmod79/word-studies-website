# Grammar Analysis: Leviticus 18:24–30 (The "Land Vomited Them Out" Paradigm)

## Overview

Lev 18:24–30 is the **paraenetic coda** (epilogue) to the Holiness-Code catalogue of sexual and cultic *ʿervôt* (Lev 18:6–23). It is the locus classicus for the **land-as-active-agent of covenant-judgment** topos in the Pentateuch: the land (הָאָרֶץ), not YHWH directly, is the grammatical subject that defiles itself (Qal), is visited (Qal), and **vomits out** (Hiphil) its inhabitants. The pericope is near-identical in architecture to its twin Lev 20:22–24, and it is presupposed by Ezra 9:10–12, Jer 2:7, Ps 106:38, and 2 Kgs 17:7–23.

The paragraph is built on **four load-bearing grammatical devices** that together encode a corporate-ecological theology of land-defilement:

1. **The קיא verb-pair** (vv. 25, 28) — a **Hiphil wayyiqtol / Hiphil yiqtol / Qal qatal** triad, morphologically distinguishing prior-nation action, causative expulsion, and the threatened repetition against Israel.
2. **The טמא concentric spiral** (vv. 24, 25, 27, 28, 30) — the same root in **five different stems and forms** (Hithpael impf., Niphal perf., Qal wayyiqtol, Piel infCon, Hithpael impf.) marking the contagion-logic of impurity.
3. **The corporate-ecological subject switch** — human-agent plural verbs (vv. 26–27: "you … they") yielding to a **feminine-singular land-agent** (vv. 25, 28), then to the **Niphal passive** (v. 29 — "souls shall be cut off") and the **plenary command** (v. 30 — "you shall keep").
4. **The prohibitive infinitive construct with בִּלְתִּי** (v. 30) — לְבִלְתִּי עֲשׂוֹת, the negative-purpose construction binding "keep my charge" to "not-doing" the abominations.

The rhetorical effect of the grammatical architecture is to *displace* the expected grammar of divine punishment onto the land itself: the covenant is not enforced by an external deity smiting the transgressor but by an animate-construed ecology that *expels the defiler* as a body rejects emetic poison. This is the grammatical-theological template for the Exile, read back into Israel's proleptic placement at Sinai.

## Text

### Verse 24
**Hebrew:** אַל־תִּֽטַּמְּא֖וּ בְּכָל־אֵ֑לֶּה כִּ֤י בְכָל־אֵ֨לֶּה֙ נִטְמְא֣וּ הַגֹּויִ֔ם אֲשֶׁר־אֲנִ֥י מְשַׁלֵּ֖חַ מִפְּנֵיכֶֽם׃
**KJV:** Defile not ye yourselves in any of these things: for in all these the nations are defiled which I cast out before you:

### Verse 25
**Hebrew:** וַתִּטְמָ֣א הָאָ֔רֶץ וָאֶפְקֹ֥ד עֲוֹנָ֖הּ עָלֶ֑יהָ וַתָּקִ֥א הָאָ֖רֶץ אֶת־יֹשְׁבֶֽיהָ׃
**KJV:** And the land is defiled: therefore I do visit the iniquity thereof upon it, and the land itself vomiteth out her inhabitants.

### Verse 26
**Hebrew:** וּשְׁמַרְתֶּ֣ם אַתֶּ֗ם אֶת־חֻקֹּתַי֙ וְאֶת־מִשְׁפָּטַ֔י וְלֹ֣א תַעֲשׂ֔וּ מִכֹּ֥ל הַתֹּועֵבֹ֖ת הָאֵ֑לֶּה הָֽאֶזְרָ֔ח וְהַגֵּ֖ר הַגָּ֥ר בְּתֹוכְכֶֽם׃
**KJV:** Ye shall therefore keep my statutes and my judgments, and shall not commit any of these abominations; neither any of your own nation, nor any stranger that sojourneth among you:

### Verse 27
**Hebrew:** כִּ֚י אֶת־כָּל־הַתֹּועֵבֹ֣ת הָאֵ֔ל עָשׂ֥וּ אַנְשֵֽׁי־הָאָ֖רֶץ אֲשֶׁ֣ר לִפְנֵיכֶ֑ם וַתִּטְמָ֖א הָאָֽרֶץ׃
**KJV:** (For all these abominations have the men of the land done, which were before you, and the land is defiled;)

### Verse 28
**Hebrew:** וְלֹֽא־תָקִ֤יא הָאָ֨רֶץ֙ אֶתְכֶ֔ם בְּטַֽמַּאֲכֶ֖ם אֹתָ֑הּ כַּאֲשֶׁ֥ר קָאָ֛ה אֶת־הַגֹּ֖וי אֲשֶׁ֥ר לִפְנֵיכֶֽם׃
**KJV:** That the land spue not you out also, when ye defile it, as it spued out the nations that were before you.

### Verse 29
**Hebrew:** כִּ֚י כָּל־אֲשֶׁ֣ר יַעֲשֶׂ֔ה מִכֹּ֥ל הַתֹּועֵבֹ֖ות הָאֵ֑לֶּה וְנִכְרְת֛וּ הַנְּפָשֹׁ֥ות הָעֹשֹׂ֖ת מִקֶּ֥רֶב עַמָּֽם׃
**KJV:** For whosoever shall commit any of these abominations, even the souls that commit them shall be cut off from among their people.

### Verse 30
**Hebrew:** וּשְׁמַרְתֶּ֣ם אֶת־מִשְׁמַרְתִּ֗י לְבִלְתִּ֨י עֲשֹׂ֜ות מֵחֻקֹּ֤ות הַתֹּֽועֵבֹת֙ אֲשֶׁ֣ר נַעֲשׂ֣וּ לִפְנֵיכֶ֔ם וְלֹ֥א תִֽטַּמְּא֖וּ בָּהֶ֑ם אֲנִ֖י יְהוָ֥ה אֱלֹהֵיכֶֽם׃
**KJV:** Therefore shall ye keep mine ordinance, that ye commit not any one of these abominable customs, which were committed before you, and that ye defile not yourselves therein: I am the LORD your God.

| 5 | NmCl | Q | **Self-introduction formula** | אֲנִי יְהוָה אֱלֹהֵיכֶם |

## Key Grammatical Features

### 1. קיא "vomit" — The Rare Verb and its Morphological Triad (vv. 25, 28)

The root קיא occurs only **11 times** in the Hebrew Bible (9 verbal + 2 nominal; parser count via `--lemma "קיא"`), distributed across Lev 18:25, 28; Lev 20:22; Isa 19:14; 28:8; Jer 48:26; Jon 2:11; Job 20:15; Prov 23:8; 25:16. In the *verbal* domain it has only two active stems: **Qal** (intransitive "vomit") and **Hiphil** (causative / transitive "cause to vomit → spew out"). Lev 18 deploys all three morphological forms:

| Verse | Form | Parsing | Force |
|-------|------|---------|-------|
| 25b | וַתָּקִא | **Hiphil Wayq.3fs** | narrative-past: land *spewed out* prior nations |
| 28a | תָקִיא | **Hiphil Impf.3fs** | prospective-negative: land *might spew out* Israel |
| 28b | קָאָה | **Qal Perf.3fs** | comparative-past: land *vomited* (intrans.) the nation |

The **Hiphil** is morphologically unambiguous: pre-formative *tāqîʾ* (3fs) preserves the -î- vowel under Ia-Aleph / III-Aleph conditioning, and the wayyiqtol *wat-tāqīʾ* shows the expected shortened form. The Hiphil in Lev 18 is *transitive-causative*: the land is the grammatical subject and the displaced people are the direct object (אֶת־יֹשְׁבֶיהָ, אֶתְכֶם, אֶת־הַגּוֹי).

The **Qal qatal** in v. 28b (קָאָה) appears to be used *interchangeably* with the Hiphil of v. 25b (both 3fs; both take אֶת + object) — but BHSA parses it as Qal Perfect. This is the rare Qal-transitive of an emetic verb: the land "vomits" (Qal) with the displaced-nation as a secondary-accusative object, grammatically parallel to Prov 23:8 *qēʾaḵā ʾăšer-ʾāḵaltā təqîʾennâ* where the Hiphil also takes a direct object. The interchange underscores that the grammatical distinction here is **not causative-vs-simple** but rather **tense-aspect alternation** for stylistic variation between the two comparison-members (*was vomited / will be vomited* on the Hiphil future vs. *vomited* on the Qal past-comparative).

**Subject animacy:** הָאָרֶץ "the land" is feminine singular; the 3fs verb-forms (תָּקִא / תָקִיא / קָאָה / תִּטְמָא) bind the verbs to the land itself as the *agent*. This is the load-bearing grammatical choice — not "God cast them out" but "**the land vomited them out.**" YHWH's only 1cs verb in the pericope is וָאֶפְקֹד ("I reckoned," v. 25a) — a **juridical** act (setting the accounts) — but the *expulsion* itself is carried out by the land. The grammar encodes a *mediated agency*: YHWH reckons, the land expels.

**Semantic field:** The verb belongs to the semantic field of **involuntary bodily rejection** — the same verb is used for Jonah spewed out by the fish (Jon 2:11, Hiphil), the drunkard's vomit (Isa 19:14; 28:8; Jer 48:26; Prov 25:16; Job 20:15), and the rich man's ill-gotten-wealth-regurgitation (Job 20:15). The ecological metaphor thus construes the land as a *stomach* that cannot tolerate abominations and **involuntarily expels** them by physiological necessity, not by moral deliberation.

### 2. טמא "defile" — The Five-Stem Concentric Spiral (vv. 24, 25, 27, 28, 30)

The same root טמא appears **seven times** in this pericope in **five different morphological forms**, creating a concentric spiral that tracks the contagion-logic:

| Verse | Form | Parsing | Subject → Object | Voice-diathesis |
|-------|------|---------|------------------|-----------------|
| 24a | תִּטַּמְּאוּ | **Hithpael Impf.2mp** | Israel → themselves | **Reflexive-self-defiling** |
| 24b | נִטְמְאוּ | **Niphal Perf.3p** | nations (past) | **Passive-have-been-defiled** |
| 25a | תִּטְמָא | **Qal Wayq.3fs** | land (past-narrative) | **Stative/ingressive "became unclean"** |
| 27b | תִּטְמָא | **Qal Wayq.3fs** | land (past-narrative, reprise) | **Stative/ingressive** |
| 28a | טַמַּאֲכֶם | **Piel InfCon.+2mp** | Israel → land (prospective) | **Piel transitive-causative: "your defiling (it)"** |
| 30a | תִטַּמְּאוּ | **Hithpael Impf.2mp** | Israel → themselves | **Reflexive-self-defiling** (closure) |

Note on the count: the table lists six finite occurrences plus one infinitive — seven surface occurrences of the root across five morphological paradigms (Hithpael impf., Niphal perf., Qal wayq., Piel infCon, Hithpael impf. closure).

**What the stem-alternation encodes:**

- **Hithpael** (vv. 24a, 30a) = *self-defilement* — the agent and the patient are the same (Israel). Frame-sets the *vetitive* (v. 24) and *concluding command* (v. 30).
- **Niphal** (v. 24b) = *became-defiled* (passive or middle) — the prior nations are the subject; the abominations did the defiling *to* them. The Niphal here is not merely passive but *medio-passive*: the nations suffered defilement as the natural consequence of their abominations.
- **Qal** (vv. 25a, 27b) = *stative-ingressive* of the land itself — intransitive, "it became unclean" / "it entered a state of uncleanness." The Qal stem for the land (vs. the Hithpael for people, and the Piel for action upon the land) grammatically constructs the land as **patient** when acted on and **agent** (through the קיא Hiphil) when reacting.
- **Piel** (v. 28a, InfCon *ṭammaʾăḵem* "your defiling") = *transitive-causative* — Israel's action (inf + 2mp suff as subject) *upon* the land (אֹתָהּ as direct object). This is the middle-step: *you* (Piel) make *it* (land) unclean, so *it* (Qal) becomes unclean, so *it* (Hiphil of קיא) expels *you*.

**The grammatical chain as theological argument:**

```
Hithpael (you defile SELVES, v. 24)
    ↓ (extends contact-impurity)
Piel     (you defile the LAND, v. 28)
    ↓ (land enters unclean state)
Qal      (the LAND is-defiled, vv. 25, 27)
    ↓ (land reacts)
Hiphil of קיא  (LAND vomits YOU out, vv. 25, 28)
```

This is a **grammatical sorites** — the stem-progression itself carries the theological argument that self-defilement necessarily propagates to land-defilement and necessarily produces land-expulsion. The root טמא is the *medium* of the contagion; the stems are the *stages* of its propagation.

**Cross-references:** See [H2931-tame](../../word-studies/H2931-tame.md) for the lexical data on this root and [stem-niphal](../hebrew/stem-niphal.md) for the Niphal-passive dynamics in v. 24b.

### 3. Corporate–Ecological Agency: The Feminine-Singular Land as Subject (vv. 25, 27, 28)

הָאָרֶץ is morphologically **feminine singular** (Noun.s.Abs, governed by 3fs verb-concord). In Lev 18:24–30 it appears **five times** as a grammatical subject or object of animate-level action:

1. v. 25a — subject of Qal wayyiqtol (*wattiṭmāʾ*): "it became defiled"
2. v. 25b — subject of Hiphil wayyiqtol (*wattāqiʾ*): "it vomited out"
3. v. 27b — subject of Qal wayyiqtol (*wattiṭmāʾ*): "it became defiled" (reprise)
4. v. 28a — subject of Hiphil yiqtol (*tāqîʾ*): "it shall vomit out"
5. v. 28b — subject of Qal qatal (*qāʾâ*): "it vomited" + obj marker אֹתָהּ referring back to it as defiled patient

The land is thus construed both as **patient of defilement** (by the inhabitants' abominations) and as **agent of expulsion** (of those same inhabitants). This grammatical double-construal is the formal machinery of a **corporate-ecological** juridical subject: the land is a *party to the covenant* who can suffer injury (defilement) and can execute sanction (vomiting).

**The land–inhabitants concord:** In v. 25 הָאָרֶץ (3fs) governs the verb *wattāqiʾ* (3fs) with *yōšəḇeyhā* "her inhabitants" as object. The 3fs pronominal suffix on יֹשְׁבֶיהָ binds the land to its population possessively — they are *hers*, and yet she vomits them. This is the grammar of a body rejecting its own tissue.

See [corporate-solidarity](../hebrew/corporate-solidarity.md) for the collective-singular pattern operating in parallel with גּוֹי (v. 28b) and נְפָשׁוֹת (v. 29). The land-agent pattern here is the **ecological extension** of the corporate-solidarity pattern — a land, like a people, is a single juridical subject.

### 4. The Wayyiqtol "Narrative" Domain Embedded in "Discourse" (vv. 25, 27)

The parser tags clauses by **domain: Q (quotation/discourse)** or **N (narrative)**. The whole of Lev 18:24–30 is YHWH's direct discourse to Israel (domain Q throughout). But **vv. 25a, 25b, 25c and 27c are tagged domain N** — narrative-domain clauses embedded within the divine discourse. This is because the three clauses of v. 25 and the third clause of v. 27 are **wayyiqtol chains** (`WayX` / `Way0`) — and wayyiqtol is a narrative-domain past form.

The grammatical effect is **proleptic-retrospective narration**: YHWH, while commanding Israel in the Sinai present, narrates the *past-and-future* expulsion of the Canaanite nations as if it were a completed past event. The wayyiqtol chain here is a *divine retrospective* that relativises the prospective audience's situation — *from YHWH's point of view the Canaanites are already vomited out, and Israel's identical fate under identical conditions is already scripted*.

The shift back to domain Q for v. 26 (weqatal command) marks the return from divine retrospective to direct paraenetic address; the shift forward to domain Q for v. 28 (yiqtol-prospective) marks the projection of the same script onto Israel's future.

### 5. לְבִלְתִּי עֲשׂוֹת — The Negative Infinitive-of-Purpose (v. 30)

The InfC clause *ləḇiltî ʿăśôṯ* in v. 30 is the Hebrew **negative infinitive-of-purpose** construction — a particle-governed negative-purpose prepositional phrase built from:

1. **לְ** — purpose-marking preposition (lamed of purpose)
2. **בִּלְתִּי** — an **archaic negator particle** (H1115; etymologically from בלה "waste/cease"), functioning as a "not"-marker only in construct-state (hence parser: `Noun.s.Cst`); it is *never* used as a free-standing adverb, only in bound construction with לְ or עַד
3. **עֲשׂוֹת** — Qal **Infinitive Construct** of עשׂה, serving as a verbal-noun object of the preposition

The full construction לְבִלְתִּי + InfCon is the **standard Biblical-Hebrew idiom for "in order not to do X"** (GKC §114s; Joüon-Muraoka §124e, §160i). It is used extensively in legal-paraenetic contexts — cf. Deut 17:20; 20:18; Jer 7:6; 17:24; 23:14; 27:18; Jer 18:10 (in the conditional-prophecy diptych, see [jer-18-7-10](jer-18-7-10.md) §6).

**The clause hierarchy in v. 30:**

```
  WQt0:   וּשְׁמַרְתֶּם אֶת מִשְׁמַרְתִּי        (positive command)
            ↓ lə-biltî + InfCon ↓
  InfC:   לְבִלְתִּי עֲשׂוֹת מֵחֻקֹּות הַתֹּועֵבֹת  (negative purpose)
            ↓ asher-qatal ↓
  xQt0:   אֲשֶׁר נַעֲשׂוּ לִפְנֵיכֶם             (rel modifier)
  WxY0:   וְלֹא תִטַּמְּאוּ בָּהֶם              (parallel neg command)
  NmCl:   אֲנִי יְהוָה אֱלֹהֵיכֶם              (self-introduction)
```

The positive command *ušəmartem* (keep my charge) is **teleologically bound** to the negative infinitive *ləḇiltî ʿăśôṯ* (so as not to do): the keeping is *for the purpose of* the not-doing. The grammar encodes an asymmetric command structure — the "keep" is the *means*, the "not-do" is the *end*. Apotropaic legal observance (keeping חוק and משפט) is *prophylactic against abomination*.

**The triple-negative closure:** v. 30 stacks three negations:
1. *ləḇiltî ʿăśôṯ* — negative-purpose infinitive
2. *wə-lōʾ tiṭṭammǝʾû* — negative yiqtol (parallel to the opening negative yiqtol of v. 24)
3. The implicit negation of "I am YHWH your God" (i.e., *no other gods, no other statutes*)

This creates a **grammatical inclusio** with v. 24 (*ʾal-tiṭṭammǝʾû*): the paragraph opens with a vetitive *ʾal* + Hithpael impf. ("do not defile yourselves") and closes with a parallel *wə-lōʾ* + Hithpael impf. ("and you shall not defile yourselves"). The root טמא and the Hithpael stem both recur at the frame-positions, tying the whole paragraph together morphologically.

### 6. וְנִכְרְתוּ הַנְּפָשׁוֹת — The Karet Formula (v. 29)

V. 29 deploys the standard **karet-formula** of the Holiness Code: a third-person conditional-casuistic structure ("whoever does X, the soul[s] shall be cut off from their people") built on:

1. **CPen** (casus pendens): *kî kol* — the topicalised protasis-head
2. **xYq0** (asher + yiqtol): *ʾăšer yaʿăśeh* — the indefinite offender "whoever shall do"
3. **WQtX** (weqatal + subj): *wə-niḵrəṯû han-nəp̄āšôṯ* — the apodosis-sanction
4. Ptcp (rel): *hā-ʿōśōṯ* — "the doing-ones" (substantival, limits the subject)
5. *mi-qereḇ ʿammām* — "from among their people"

**Morphology:** The apodosis-verb *wə-niḵrəṯû* is **Niphal perfect 3p with conjunctive/consecutive waw** — a **weqatal** ("and shall be cut off"). The Niphal stem is here a **divine-passive**: YHWH is the implied agent of the cutting-off, but the surface grammar hides the agent (contrast the explicit 1cs *wə-hiḵrattî* "I will cut off" Hiphil of Lev 20:3, 5, 6). The Niphal *niḵrat* in karet-formulas is formulaic: it occurs 17× in the Pentateuch alone, always with נֶפֶשׁ as subject and מִקֶּרֶב עַמָּהּ / עַמָּיו / בְּנֵי עַמָּיו as source-complement.

**The number-mismatch:** *kol ʾăšer yaʿăśeh* is 3ms (singular indefinite offender), but *wə-niḵrəṯû han-nəp̄āšôṯ* is 3p (plural "souls"). The apodosis shifts to **plural-distributive** construal: the individual offender's *nepeš* is counted among a class of *nəp̄āšôṯ* (plural) who have likewise offended. This is a grammatical instance of the distributive construal — a singular violator is adjudicated as one of a class, and the sanction applies to the class.

**Cross-reference:** [H3772-karath](../../word-studies/H3772-karath.md) for the full lexical-semantic dossier on the karet sanction and its Niphal/Hiphil alternation.

### 7. תּוֹעֵבֹת "abominations" — The Semantic Anchor (vv. 26, 27, 29, 30)

The noun תּוֹעֵבָה (fem. sg., plural תּוֹעֵבֹת) appears **four times** in the paragraph (vv. 26, 27, 29, 30). It is the **technical term** for cultic-sexual-ethical violations that evoke divine revulsion. Its four occurrences are **categorially coordinated** with the paragraph's four legal moments:

| Verse | Phrase | Function |
|-------|--------|----------|
| 26 | מִכֹּל הַתּוֹעֵבֹת הָאֵלֶּה | **Prohibition's object** (neg yiqtol) |
| 27 | אֶת כָּל הַתּוֹעֵבֹת הָאֵל | **Causal-ground's object** (qatal, prior nations) |
| 29 | מִכֹּל הַתּוֹעֵבוֹת הָאֵלֶּה | **Karet's trigger** (yiqtol) |
| 30 | מֵחֻקּוֹת הַתּוֹעֵבֹת | **Infinitive-of-purpose's object** (negative inf.) |

The four occurrences tie the four grammatical moments (prohibition / ground / sanction / closing command) into a single tightly-woven paraenetic fabric. The plural *tôʿēḇōṯ* collectivises the catalogue of vv. 6–23: each specific *ʿervâ* is now enumerated as one instance of the generic category *tôʿēḇâ*.

**The construct chain in v. 30** (*ḥuqqôṯ hat-tôʿēḇōṯ*, "statutes of the abominations") is morphologically striking: *ḥuqqôṯ* is **fem. pl. Construct**, bound to the absolute *hat-tôʿēḇōṯ* (fem. pl. Absolute). The construct relation forms an **ironic construct** — "statutes of [= belonging to / prescribed by] abominations" — inverting the positive *ḥuqqōṯay* of v. 26 ("my statutes"). Israel must keep *my statutes* (v. 26, 30a) lest it observe *the abominations' statutes* (v. 30b): two competing *ḥuqqah*-codes, and Israel must choose one. This is **grammatical polemic** — the very word חֻקָּה is contested between YHWH and the prior-nations' cult-system.

**Cross-reference:** [H8441-towebah](../../word-studies/H8441-towebah.md) for the full lexical-theological profile of *tôʿēḇâ*.

### 8. וָאֶפְקֹד — The Divine Reckoning (v. 25a)

The one 1cs verb in the pericope is *wā-ʾep̄qōḏ* "**and I visited/reckoned**" (Qal wayyiqtol of פקד) — embedded between the two 3fs land-verbs of v. 25 (*wat-tiṭmāʾ* … *wāʾep̄qōḏ* … *wat-tāqiʾ*). The placement is theologically load-bearing:

- **v. 25a:** *wattiṭmāʾ hā-ʾāreṣ* — "and the land became defiled" (3fs subject)
- **v. 25a-mid:** *wāʾep̄qōḏ ʿăwōnâh ʿāleyhā* — "and **I** reckoned its iniquity upon it" (1cs subject, YHWH)
- **v. 25b:** *wat-tāqiʾ hā-ʾāreṣ ʾeṯ-yōšəḇeyhā* — "and the land vomited out her inhabitants" (3fs subject)

The 1cs verb is **sandwiched** between the two 3fs verbs of the land. This grammatical positioning encodes the theological structure: the land's defilement happens, then YHWH **judicially assigns** the iniquity (פקד = "muster / reckon / visit" in judicial sense), and then the land *executes* the expulsion. YHWH is **not** the expeller; YHWH is the **reckoner**. The expulsion is the land's *emetic response* to the reckoned-iniquity. This grammatical division of labour between 1cs (reckon) and 3fs (expel) keeps YHWH juridically responsible but ecologically mediated.

**עָלֶיהָ** "upon it" is a 3fs suffix referring back to הָאָרֶץ: the iniquity is reckoned **upon the land** (not upon the inhabitants). This is striking — the *land* bears the iniquity as a deposit (cf. Num 5:31 for *nāśāʾ ʿāwōn* as bearing guilt). The land, in effect, is *guilt-credited* on behalf of its inhabitants, and then discharges the credit by the emetic response.

### 9. The Holiness-Code Signature: Native-and-Sojourner (v. 26)

V. 26b explicitly extends the command to *hā-ʾezrāḥ wə-hag-gēr hag-gār bəṯôḵəḵem* "the native and the sojourner who sojourns in your midst." This is a **Holiness-Code signature formula** (cf. Lev 16:29; 17:15; 18:26; 19:34; 24:16, 22; Num 15:16, 29). The command is *territorially* (not ethnically) scoped: anyone dwelling *in the land* is bound by the *ḥuqqôṯ / mišpāṭîm* because the *land* is the one that reacts to defilement, and the land does not distinguish native from foreigner. The grammatical universalisation of the subject (*ʾezrāḥ + gēr*) confirms the ecological-not-ethnic logic of the expulsion-sanction.

The participial *hag-gār* (Qal Ptcp.ms.Abs) functions as a **relative-attributive substantive** ("the-one-who-sojourns"), specifying the gēr as the dwelling-gēr (not the transient visitor). The root play *gēr … gār* (noun + cognate participle) is stylistic but carries the force: "the resident-alien who [actually] resides."

### 10. Symmetric Inclusio: vv. 24 and 30

The paragraph is framed by two structurally symmetric verses:

| Feature | v. 24 (opening) | v. 30 (closing) |
|---------|-----------------|------------------|
| Negative form | **אַל**-vetitive + Hithpael impf. | **לֹא**-vetitive + Hithpael impf. (v. 30b) |
| Root | טמא Hithpael 2mp | טמא Hithpael 2mp |
| Ground | כִּי + Niphal perf. (prior nations defiled) | אֲשֶׁר + Niphal perf. (prior abominations done) |
| Prior-nation reference | הַגֹּויִם | לִפְנֵיכֶם |
| Positive counterpart | — | וּשְׁמַרְתֶּם אֶת־מִשְׁמַרְתִּי (v. 30a) |
| Divine self-reference | אֲנִי מְשַׁלֵּחַ (Piel Ptcp — "I am expelling") | אֲנִי יְהוָה אֱלֹהֵיכֶם (nominal formula) |

The inclusio's rhetorical point: the opening gives the *ground* (the nations already defiled), the closing gives the *remedy* (keep the charge, do not defile yourselves). The root טמא Hithpael 2mp is the **exact lexical-morphological frame** for the whole paragraph — you do not defile yourselves (v. 24) precisely because you shall not defile yourselves (v. 30). The indicative-vetitive becomes the prospective-prohibition, and the paragraph's entire argument is compressed into the semantic equivalence of its frames.

## The Paradigm as a Whole: Grammatical Architecture of Ecological Expulsion

Reading the seven verses as a single grammatical unit, the paradigm exhibits a **chiastic-ecological** architecture:

```
A   v. 24   Vetitive: "do not defile yourselves" + ground: the nations were defiled
  B   v. 25  Narrative: LAND defiled → YHWH reckoned → LAND vomited
    C   v. 26 Positive+negative command: keep my statutes, do not do abominations
    C'  v. 27 Causal-ground echoing v. 26: for the prior-men did abominations, land defiled
  B'  v. 28   Prospective: LAND shall not vomit you when you defile it, as it vomited prior nation
    D   v. 29 Karet-sanction for individual offenders
A'  v. 30   Positive: keep my charge + negative purpose: not-do abominations + not defile selves
```

- **A//A'** = Hithpael 2mp *ṭmʾ* frame-inclusio
- **B//B'** = land-agent Qal/Hiphil *ṭmʾ/qyʾ* sequence (narrative past vs. yiqtol prospective)
- **C//C'** = שׁמר-חוק-משפט command + ground
- **D** = karet-sanction as climactic individual-level judgment, nested inside the corporate-ecological judgment of B'

The grammatical architecture enforces the theological claim that corporate/ecological and individual judgments are *two parallel tracks* with grammatically distinct mechanisms: **the land vomits nations** (corporate, 3fs, Hiphil of קיא) while **YHWH cuts off persons** (individual, Niphal-divine-passive of כרת). The Holiness Code needs both mechanisms because covenant-fidelity is both a corporate and an individual matter.

## Comparative Reference: Lev 20:22–24 (Sibling Passage)

The sibling pericope Lev 20:22–24 recycles the identical grammatical frame:

- Lev 20:22 — *ûšəmartem ʾeṯ kol ḥuqqōṯay wə-ʾeṯ kol mišpāṭay wa-ʿăśîṯem ʾōṯām wə-lōʾ ṯāqîʾ ʾeṯḵem hā-ʾāreṣ* ("keep … and do not defile it, that the land vomit you not out"). The Hiphil *tāqîʾ* is again 3fs with land-subject and 2mp object.
