## Lexicon Definition

A primitive root; to prop (literally or figuratively); reflexively, to lean upon or take hold of (in a favorable or unfavorable sense): bear up, establish, (up-)hold, lay, lean, lie hard, put, rest self, set self, stand fast, stay (self), sustain.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Laying Hands on Sacrificial Animals (Identification/Transfer)
- **Lev 1:4** — "And he shall put his hand upon the head of the burnt offering; and it shall be accepted for him to make atonement for him"
- **Lev 4:29** — "And he shall lay his hand upon the head of the sin offering, and slay the sin offering in the place of the burnt offering"
- **Lev 16:21** — "And Aaron shall lay both his hands upon the head of the live goat, and confess over him all the iniquities of the children of Israel"
- **2 Chr 29:23** — "And they brought forth the he goats for the sin offering before the king and the congregation; and they laid their hands upon them"

This is the dominant usage (20+ occurrences). The fixed formula is samak + yad (hand) + rosh (head) + sacrificial animal. Lev 16:21 is distinctive: both hands are laid on, and verbal confession accompanies the gesture.

### 2. Commissioning / Ordination by Hand-Laying
- **Num 27:18** — "Take thee Joshua the son of Nun, a man in whom is the spirit, and lay thine hand upon him"
- **Num 27:23** — "And he laid his hands upon him, and gave him a charge, as the LORD commanded by the hand of Moses"
- **Deut 34:9** — "And Joshua the son of Nun was full of the spirit of wisdom; for Moses had laid his hands upon him"

The same physical gesture applied to persons rather than animals — transfer of authority and empowerment for office.

### 3. Divine Sustaining / Upholding
- **Psa 37:17** — "For the arms of the wicked shall be broken: but the LORD upholdeth the righteous"
- **Psa 37:24** — "Though he fall, he shall not be utterly cast down: for the LORD upholdeth him with his hand"
- **Psa 145:14** — "The LORD upholdeth all that fall, and raiseth up all those that be bowed down"
- **Psa 3:5** — "I laid me down and slept; I awaked; for the LORD sustained me"

The Qal Participle somek becomes a characteristic divine attribute in Psalms — God as the one who props up, sustains, and prevents collapse.

### 4. Human Leaning / Physical Support
- **Judg 16:29** — "And Samson took hold of the two middle pillars upon which the house stood, and on which it was borne up"
- **2 Ki 18:21** — "thou trustest upon the staff of this bruised reed, even upon Egypt, on which if a man lean, it will go into his hand"
- **Amos 5:19** — "or went into the house, and leaned his hand on the wall, and a serpent bit him"

Physical leaning or propping — the literal sense from which the other uses derive.

### 5. Petition for God's Support
- **Psa 51:12** — "Restore unto me the joy of thy salvation; and uphold me with thy free spirit"
- **Psa 119:116** — "Uphold me according unto thy word, that I may live"
- **Song 2:5** — "Stay me with flagons, comfort me with apples: for I am sick of love"

Imperative/jussive forms requesting sustenance or support.

### 6. Figurative Firmness / Establishment
- **Isa 26:3** — "Thou wilt keep him in perfect peace, whose mind is stayed on thee"
- **Psa 111:8** — "They stand fast for ever and ever, and are done in truth and uprightness"
- **Psa 112:8** — "His heart is established, he shall not be afraid"

The Qal Passive Participle samuk describes a settled, firm, established state — applied to God's precepts, to the trusting mind, and to the steadfast heart.

### 7. Military/Political Upholding
- **Isa 59:16** — "therefore his arm brought salvation unto him; and his righteousness, it sustained him"
- **Isa 63:5** — "And I looked, and there was none to help; and I wondered that there was none to uphold"
- **Eze 30:6** — "They also that uphold Egypt shall fall"

Political alliances and military support described as "propping up" — with the theological point that such human support fails.
