## Lexicon Definition

From H8210 (shaphak); an emptying place, e.g. an ash-heap: — are poured out.

The noun denotes the place where something is poured out or dumped — specifically, the designated location outside the camp where sacrificial ashes (deshen) were deposited. The KJV renders the phrase “where the ashes are poured out” as a relative clause, but the underlying noun is a locative: the pouring-place, the ash-dump.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Designated Disposal Site for Sacrificial Ashes
- **Lev 4:12** — “Even the whole bullock shall he carry forth without the camp unto a clean place, where the ashes are poured out, and burn him on the wood with fire: where the ashes are poured out shall he be burnt.”

The shephek is a specific location — a clean place (maqom tahor) outside the camp — designated for the dumping of altar ashes (deshen, H1880). This same location served as the burning site for the entire bull of the sin offering when the anointed priest or the whole congregation sinned (Lev 4:1-12). The word thus sits at the intersection of two ritual concerns: (1) proper disposal of sacred waste, and (2) the complete burning of the sin offering outside the camp.
