## Semantic Categories

### 1. Proverbial — Fruit as Moral Indicator (Synoptic Teaching)
- **Mat 7:16** — "Ye shall know them by their fruits. Do men gather grapes of thorns, or figs of thistles?"
- **Luk 6:44** — "For every tree is known by his own fruit. For of thorns men do not gather figs, nor of a bramble bush gather they grapes."

Both Synoptic uses pair staphylē with sykon (figs) as examples of good fruit that cannot come from thorny plants. The grape functions as the self-evident standard of a good product matched to its proper source.

### 2. Eschatological — Harvest of Judgment (Revelation)
- **Rev 14:18** — "Thrust in thy sharp sickle, and gather the clusters of the vine of the earth; for her grapes are fully ripe."

Here staphylē appears in an angel's command for eschatological harvest. The ripe grape cluster is destined for the "winepress of the wrath of God" (Rev 14:19). Note that βότρυς (G1009) is used for "clusters" in the same verse, while σταφυλή refers to the individual grapes being ripe.
