**Semantic notes:** The LXX splits H4758's two senses. *Opsis* (G3799) renders the physical
appearance / countenance uses (Genesis beauty language, 1 Samuel, Ezekiel). *Horasis* (G3706)
renders the prophetic vision uses (Daniel, Ezekiel). The Hebrew word holds both senses as one
lexeme; Greek separates them.

## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same:**
- The theological warning against judging by outward *mar'eh/opsis* runs from 1 Sam 16:7 through John 7:24 — same principle, same vocabulary chain
- Divine glory described with visual appearance language in both Ezekiel (mar'eh) and Revelation (opsis)

**What shifts:**
- Hebrew *mar'eh* unifies physical beauty, clinical appearance, theophanic vision, and prophetic revelation into one word
- Greek splits these into *opsis* (outward appearance/face) and *horasis* (prophetic vision) — a semantic specialization
- NT uses of *opsis* are all in John and Revelation; the ordinary "beauty" and "skin inspection" senses (Genesis, Leviticus) are absent from NT usage
