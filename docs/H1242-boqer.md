- **Hapax Legomenon:** No

## Lexicon Definition

From בָּקַר (H1239); properly, **dawn** (as the break of day); generally, **morning**. Rendered variously: "day," "early," "morning," "morrow."

Semantically, *bôqer* denotes the first portion of the daylight period — from dawn's first light through the early hours. In the Hebrew reckoning of the day ("evening and morning," Gen 1:5), *bôqer* completes the 24-hour cycle that begins with *ʻereb* (H6153, evening). Its root in *bâqar* ("to inspect, inquire") suggests the morning as the time when visibility returns and matters can be examined — a nuance that carries into forensic, cultic, and theological usage (e.g., "judge in the morning," "look for him in the morning").

## Semantic Categories

### 1. The Creation Day Formula (evening + morning)
- **Gen 1:5** — "And the evening and the **morning** were the first day"
- **Gen 1:8, 13, 19, 23, 31** — same formula repeated for each creation day

The paradigmatic use: *bôqer* paired with *ʻereb* (evening) to mark a complete 24-hour unit. This pairing reappears in cultic and prophetic contexts below.

### 2. Temporal Marker — "early the next day"
- **Gen 19:27** — "Abraham gat up early in the **morning** to the place where he stood before the LORD"
- **Gen 22:3** — "Abraham rose up early in the **morning**, and saddled his ass"
- **Jos 3:1** — "Joshua rose early in the **morning**; and they removed from Shittim"
- **1 Sam 17:20** — "David rose up early in the **morning**... and went, as Jesse had commanded him"

The narrative idiom *wayyashkêm ... babbôqer* ("he rose early in the morning") marks purposeful, obedient action — often preceding a covenant moment, a journey, or battle.

### 3. Cultic / Continual (tamid) Offering
- **Ex 29:39** — "The one lamb thou shalt offer in the **morning**; and the other lamb thou shalt offer at even"
- **Ex 30:7** — "every **morning**: when he dresseth the lamps, he shall burn incense upon it"
- **Lev 6:12** — "the priest shall burn wood on it every **morning**"
- **Num 28:4** — "The one lamb shalt thou offer in the **morning**, and the other lamb shalt thou offer at even"
- **1 Chr 16:40** — "To offer burnt offerings... continually morning and evening"
- **Ezek 46:13-15** — "a lamb... shalt thou daily prepare... every **morning**"

The morning sacrifice (along with its evening counterpart) frames Israel's daily worship. This is the densest thematic cluster in the word's usage.

### 4. Divine Deliverance / Hope at Dawn
- **Ps 30:5** — "weeping may endure for a night, but joy cometh in the **morning**"
- **Ps 46:5** — "God shall help her, and that right early" (lit. "at the turning of the morning")
- **Ps 90:14** — "O satisfy us early [babbôqer] with thy mercy; that we may rejoice and be glad"
- **Ps 143:8** — "Cause me to hear thy lovingkindness in the **morning**"
- **Lam 3:22-23** — "his compassions fail not. They are new every **morning**: great is thy faithfulness"
- **Isa 33:2** — "be thou their arm every **morning**, our salvation also in the time of trouble"

Morning as the time of God's decisive rescue, renewed mercy, and answered prayer. A strong theological motif, particularly in the Psalms.

### 5. Judgment and Justice at Morning
- **Jer 21:12** — "Execute judgment in the **morning**, and deliver him that is spoiled"
- **Ps 101:8** — "I will early destroy all the wicked of the land"
- **Zeph 3:5** — "every **morning** doth he bring his judgment to light"
- **Job 24:17** — "the **morning** is to them even as the shadow of death" — morning unveils the deeds of the wicked

Linked to the root sense of *bâqar* (inspect): morning is when cases are judged and hidden deeds are exposed.

### 6. Prophetic Time Formulas
- **Dan 8:14** — "Unto two thousand and three hundred days [lit. **evening-morning**]; then shall the sanctuary be cleansed"
- **Dan 8:26** — "the vision of the **evening and the morning** which was told is true"

In Daniel, the singular compound "evening-morning" (*ʻereb-bôqer*) echoes the Gen 1 formula and counts cultic days. *Bôqer* here is genre-bound — prophetic temporal measure, not narrative time-of-day.

### 7. Transience / Fleeting Human Life
- **Ps 90:5-6** — "they are as a sleep: in the **morning** they are like grass which groweth up. In the **morning** it flourisheth, and groweth up; in the evening it is cut down"
- **Job 4:20** — "They are destroyed from **morning** to evening"
- **Hos 6:4** — "your goodness is as a **morning** cloud, and as the early dew it goeth away" (note: 6:4 uses *bôqer* as construct modifier)
- **Hos 13:3** — "as the **morning** cloud, and as the early dew that passeth away"

Morning flourishes but fades quickly — a poetic image of human frailty.

### 8. "Morning by Morning" / Continual Iteration
- **Ex 16:21** — "they gathered it every **morning**, every man according to his eating"
- **Isa 28:19** — "**morning** by **morning** shall it pass over, by day and by night"
- **Isa 50:4** — "he wakeneth **morning** by **morning**, he wakeneth mine ear to hear as the learned"
- **Zeph 3:5** — "every **morning** doth he bring his judgment to light, he faileth not"

The doubled or distributive form expresses unfailing regularity — of manna, of divine instruction, of judgment, of mercies (Lam 3:23).

**Key distinction:** *bôqer* is a **time-period term** (the early portion of the day), not a light-quality term (*ʼôr*), a point-of-first-light term (*shachar*), or an action term (*shâkam*). It functions as the bright, visible, inspectable counterpart to *ʻereb* (evening) — and this binary is foundational to Hebrew time-reckoning.

## First Occurrence

**Gen 1:5** — "And God called the light Day, and the darkness he called Night. And the evening and the morning [בֹּקֶר] were the first day."

The word's inaugural appearance establishes its most enduring function: the second member of the *ʻereb-bôqer* day-unit. Every subsequent use inherits this framework.
