## Lexicon Definition

Apparently from an original sense of superiority in mental action; properly, a pithy maxim, usually of metaphorical nature; hence, a simile (as an adage, poem, discourse): — byword, like, parable, proverb.

The term covers a wide range of wisdom utterances: short proverbial sayings (Proverbs), extended oracular poems (Balaam's oracles), taunts and dirges (Isa 14, Mic 2, Hab 2), riddles and allegories (Ezek 17, 24), and bywords of reproach (Deut 28:37; 1 Kgs 9:7).

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Oracular Utterance / Prophetic Poem
Used seven times in Numbers for Balaam's inspired oracles; also of Job's elevated discourse. The opening idiom is "took up (נָשָׂא) his mashal."
- **Num 23:7** — "And he took up his parable (mashal), and said, Balak the king of Moab hath brought me from Aram..."
- **Num 24:3** — "And he took up his parable, and said, Balaam the son of Beor hath said..."
- **Job 27:1** — "Moreover Job continued his parable, and said..."
- **Job 29:1** — "Moreover Job continued his parable, and said..."

### 2. Proverb / Wisdom Saying
The pithy maxim of the wise — the title-word of the book of Proverbs.
- **Prov 1:1** — "The proverbs (mishlei) of Solomon the son of David, king of Israel"
- **Prov 1:6** — "To understand a proverb, and the interpretation; the words of the wise, and their dark sayings"
- **Prov 10:1** — "The proverbs of Solomon. A wise son maketh a glad father..."
- **1 Kgs 4:32** — "And he spake three thousand proverbs"

### 3. Popular Saying / Current Proverb
A common saying circulating among the people, often quoted and then refuted or affirmed.
- **1 Sam 10:12** — "Therefore it became a proverb, Is Saul also among the prophets?"
- **1 Sam 24:13** — "As saith the proverb of the ancients, Wickedness proceedeth from the wicked"
- **Ezek 12:22** — "what is that proverb that ye have in the land of Israel, saying, The days are prolonged..."
- **Ezek 18:2** — "What mean ye, that ye use this proverb... The fathers have eaten sour grapes, and the children's teeth are set on edge"

### 4. Taunt-Song / Mocking Oracle
A prophetic lament or taunt directed against a foreign nation or oppressor.
- **Isa 14:4** — "thou shalt take up this proverb against the king of Babylon, and say, How hath the oppressor ceased!"
- **Mic 2:4** — "shall one take up a parable against you, and lament with a doleful lamentation"
- **Hab 2:6** — "Shall not all these take up a parable against him, and a taunting proverb against him, and say, Woe to him..."

### 5. Allegory / Riddle-Parable
An extended figurative speech requiring interpretation; often paired with חִידָה (chidah, "riddle").
- **Ezek 17:2** — "Son of man, put forth a riddle, and speak a parable unto the house of Israel"
- **Ezek 20:49** — "Ah Lord GOD! they say of me, Doth he not speak parables?"
- **Ezek 24:3** — "And utter a parable unto the rebellious house... Set on a pot..."
- **Psa 78:2** — "I will open my mouth in a parable: I will utter dark sayings of old"

### 6. Byword / Object of Reproach
A name used as a standing example of shame or ruin.
- **Deut 28:37** — "thou shalt become an astonishment, a proverb, and a byword, among all nations"
- **1 Kgs 9:7** — "and this house... shall be a proverb and a byword among all people"
- **2 Chr 7:20** — "and this house... will I cast out of my sight, and will make it to be a proverb and a byword"
- **Jer 24:9** — "I will deliver them... to be a reproach and a proverb, a taunt and a curse"
- **Psa 44:14** — "Thou makest us a byword among the heathen"

### 7. Simile / Comparison
Used of a comparison-like structure, especially for the incongruous.
- **Prov 26:7** — "The legs of the lame are not equal: so is a parable in the mouth of fools"
- **Prov 26:9** — "As a thorn goeth up into the hand of a drunkard, so is a parable in the mouth of fools"
- **Job 13:12** — "Your remembrances are like unto ashes, your bodies to bodies of clay"
