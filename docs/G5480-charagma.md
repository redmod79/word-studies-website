## Lexicon Definition

A scratch or etching, i.e. stamp (as a badge of servitude), or sculptured figure (statue). From the same root as G5482 (χάραξ), which derives from χαράσσω — to scratch or sharpen to a point.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Engraved / Sculptured Artwork
Human craftsmanship working metal, stone, or wood into a shaped image.

- **Acts 17:29** — "we ought not to think that the Godhead is like unto gold, or silver, or stone, graven (cháragma) by art and man's device"

### 2. The Mark Compulsorily Imposed
The beast causes all people to receive a mark on their right hand or forehead; the mark becomes the condition for commerce.

- **Rev 13:16** — "he causeth all, both small and great, rich and poor, free and bond, to receive a mark in their right hand, or in their foreheads"
- **Rev 13:17** — "no man might buy or sell, save he that had the mark, or the name of the beast, or the number of his name"

### 3. The Mark Linked to Worship
Receiving the mark is paired with worshipping the beast and his image — the two acts appear together.

- **Rev 14:9** — "If any man worship the beast and his image, and receive his mark in his forehead, or in his hand"
- **Rev 14:11** — "who worship the beast and his image, and whosoever receiveth the mark of his name"

### 4. The Mark as Basis for Judgment
Divine wrath falls specifically on those who bear the mark; the false prophet is judged for deceiving people into receiving it.

- **Rev 16:2** — "there fell a noisome and grievous sore upon the men which had the mark of the beast"
- **Rev 19:20** — "the false prophet...with which he deceived them that had received the mark of the beast...cast alive into a lake of fire"
- **Rev 20:4** — "the souls of them...which had not worshipped the beast, neither his image, neither had received his mark...they lived and reigned with Christ a thousand years"

### 5. Victory over the Mark
Those who overcome are described as having conquered the beast *and* his mark — the mark is something to be resisted and overcome.

- **Rev 15:2** — "them that had gotten the victory over the beast, and over his image, and over his mark, and over the number of his name, stand on the sea of glass"
