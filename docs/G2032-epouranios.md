## Lexicon Definition

From ἐπί and οὐρανός; above the sky: — celestial, (in) heaven(-ly), high. Designates that which pertains to, belongs to, or originates from the realm above — heavenly in quality, origin, or location, as distinguished from what is earthly (ἐπίγειος).

**Pattern:** Concentrated in Pauline (esp. Ephesians) and Hebrews. Ephesians uses it as a near-technical term ("the heavenlies" — ἐν τοῖς ἐπουρανίοις, a spatial-spiritual sphere). Hebrews uses it to contrast the heavenly sanctuary/calling/gift with earthly types. Rare in the Gospels (Mat 18:35; John 3:12) and entirely absent from the LXX and the Synoptic parallels apart from Matthew.

## Hebrew Source

No direct Hebrew source — this is a NT-only compound with no LXX usage. The conceptual field it occupies (heavens, heavenly sanctuary, heavenly Jerusalem) corresponds in the OT primarily to שָׁמַיִם (shamayim, H8064), rendered in LXX by οὐρανός. ἐπουράνιος is a distinctively Greek/NT intensification rather than a Septuagintal borrowing.
