## Semantic Categories

### 1. God's Everlasting Kingdom
The divine dominion established by God, contrasted with all human kingdoms — eternal, indestructible.

- **Dan 2:44** — "in the days of these kings shall the God of heaven set up a kingdom, which shall never be destroyed"
- **Dan 4:3** — "his kingdom is an everlasting kingdom, and his dominion is from generation to generation"
- **Dan 7:14** — "there was given him dominion, and glory, and a kingdom, that all people, nations, and languages, should serve him"
- **Dan 7:27** — "the kingdom and dominion, and the greatness of the kingdom under the whole heaven, shall be given to the people of the saints"

### 2. Human Kingdoms — the Four Empire Sequence
The succession of world empires in Daniel 2 and 7; each malkûw is God-given yet transient.

- **Dan 2:37** — "the God of heaven hath given thee a kingdom, power, and strength, and glory"
- **Dan 2:39** — "after thee shall arise another kingdom inferior to thee, and another third kingdom of brass"
- **Dan 2:40** — "the fourth kingdom shall be strong as iron"
- **Dan 7:23** — "The fourth beast shall be the fourth kingdom upon earth, which shall be diverse from all kingdoms"

### 3. Nebuchadnezzar's Kingdom — Humiliation and Restoration
His personal reign is taken, held in suspense, and restored once he acknowledges God's sovereignty.

- **Dan 4:26** — "thy kingdom shall be sure unto thee, after that thou shalt have known that the heavens do rule"
- **Dan 4:31** — "O king Nebuchadnezzar, to thee it is spoken; The kingdom is departed from thee"
- **Dan 4:34** — "his dominion is an everlasting dominion, and his kingdom is from generation to generation"
- **Dan 4:36** — "for the glory of my kingdom, mine honour and brightness returned unto me"

### 4. Belshazzar's Kingdom — Numbered and Divided
The judgment scene of Daniel 5, where malkûw passes from Babylon to the Medes and Persians.

- **Dan 5:18** — "the most high God gave Nebuchadnezzar thy father a kingdom, and majesty, and glory"
- **Dan 5:26** — "MENE; God hath numbered thy kingdom, and finished it"
- **Dan 5:28** — "PERES; Thy kingdom is divided, and given to the Medes and Persians"
- **Dan 5:31** — "Darius the Median took the kingdom, being about threescore and two years old"

### 5. Saints Receiving the Kingdom — Eschatological Transfer
The saints of the Most High inherit the kingdom formerly held by human empires.

- **Dan 7:18** — "the saints of the most High shall take the kingdom, and possess the kingdom for ever"
- **Dan 7:22** — "the time came that the saints possessed the kingdom"
- **Dan 7:27** — "the kingdom and dominion...shall be given to the people of the saints of the most High"

### 6. Administrative Realm — Persian Political Usage
Neutral administrative sense: the political territory governed; found in Ezra and Daniel's court scenes.

- **Ezr 7:13** — "all they of the people of Israel...in my realm"
- **Ezr 4:24** — "second year of the reign of Darius king of Persia"
- **Dan 6:1** — "It pleased Darius to set over the kingdom an hundred and twenty princes"
- **Dan 6:4** — "sought to find occasion against Daniel concerning the kingdom"
