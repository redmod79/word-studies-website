## Lexicon Definition

From G264 (hamartanō); a sin (properly concrete) — sin. The -μα suffix marks the concrete result of the action, distinguishing this from the abstract ἁμαρτία (G266). Where G266 can denote the state, principle, or power of sin, G265 denotes specifically the sinful deed committed.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Sinful Acts Subject to Forgiveness
- **Mark 3:28** — "Verily I say unto you, All sins [hamartēmata] shall be forgiven unto the sons of men, and blasphemies wherewith soever they shall blaspheme"
- **Mark 4:12** — "That seeing they may see, and not perceive; and hearing they may hear, and not understand; lest at any time they should be converted, and [their] sins [hamartēmata] should be forgiven them"

Both Markan uses pair hamartēmata with aphiēmi (G863, "forgive/release"). The concrete acts of sin are forgivable objects — things that can be sent away.

### 2. Past Sinful Acts Covered by Propitiation
- **Romans 3:25** — "Whom God hath set forth [to be] a propitiation through faith in his blood, to declare his righteousness for the remission of sins [hamartēmatōn] that are past, through the forbearance of God"

Paul chose the concrete hamartēma (not the abstract hamartia) to specify the particular past deeds that God's forbearance had passed over. Collocates with hilastērion (G2435, propitiation), haima (G129, blood), and dikaiosynē (G1343, righteousness).

### 3. Sin as a Concrete Bodily Act
- **1 Corinthians 6:18** — "Flee fornication. Every sin [hamartēma] that a man doeth is without the body; but he that committeth fornication sinneth against his own body"

The only singular occurrence. Paul distinguishes between hamartēma (the concrete sinful deed a person *does*) and the verb hamartanō (G264, "sins against his own body"). The concrete-act sense is emphatic: "every sinful deed that a man performs."
