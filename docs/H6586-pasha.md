## Lexicon Definition

A verb meaning to rebel, transgress, break away from just authority, trespass, or apostatize. It can describe political revolt, moral breach, or covenantal defection.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Political revolt from human rule
- **1Ki 12:19** — Israel rebelled against the house of David.
- **2Ki 1:1** — Moab rebelled against Israel.
- **2Ki 8:20, 22** — Edom revolted from under Judah.

### 2. Covenantal transgression against God
- **1Ki 8:50** — Solomon speaks of the people's transgressions against God.
- **Isa 1:2** — children have rebelled against the LORD.
- **Hos 8:1** — they have transgressed God's covenant and trespassed against his law.

### 3. Hardened class of transgressors
- **Isa 48:8** — Judah/Israel are called transgressors from the womb.
- **Dan 8:23** — "when the transgressors are come to the full."
- **Hos 14:9** — transgressors fall in the right ways of the LORD.

### 4. Deliberate intensification of sin
- **Amo 4:4** — "Come to Bethel, and transgress."
- **Zep 3:11** — Judah's shame is tied to its rebellion against God.
