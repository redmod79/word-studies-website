## Lexicon Definition

From H7676; a sabbatism or special holiday: — rest, sabbath.

The ôwn suffix is an intensifying or abstracting suffix in Hebrew (compare שִׁלְטוֹן from שָׁלַט; עִנְיָן from עָנָה). Applied to שַׁבָּת, it produces "sabbath-ness" or "a full sabbath rest." BDB glosses it as a solemn rest.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Weekly Seventh-Day Sabbath
Shabbâthôwn applied to the recurring seventh-day Sabbath, emphasizing its character as a complete rest. It appears in contexts stressing death-penalty enforcement or holiness.

- **Exo 16:23** — "To morrow [is] the rest of the holy sabbath unto the LORD: bake [that] which ye will bake [to day]…"
- **Exo 31:15** — "in the seventh [is] the sabbath of rest, holy to the LORD: whosoever doeth [any] work in the sabbath day, he shall surely be put to death"
- **Lev 23:3** — "the seventh day [is] the sabbath of rest, an holy convocation; ye shall do no work [therein]"

### 2. Day of Atonement (Yom Kippur)
The Day of Atonement bears the full shabbâthôwn designation as a permanent statute, paired with soul-affliction. The only day that combines cessation from work with mandatory fasting.

- **Lev 16:31** — "It [shall be] a sabbath of rest unto you, and ye shall afflict your souls, by a statute for ever"
- **Lev 23:32** — "It [shall be] unto you a sabbath of rest, and ye shall afflict your souls: in the ninth [day] of the month at even, from even unto even, shall ye celebrate your sabbath"

### 3. Annual Festival Convocations
Specific appointed feast-days beyond Yom Kippur are also designated shabbâthôwn — a holy convocation with a rest character, though not the full weekly sabbath.

- **Lev 23:24** — Feast of Trumpets: "in the first [day] of the month, shall ye have a sabbath (shabbâthôwn), a memorial of blowing of trumpets, an holy convocation"
- **Lev 23:39** — Feast of Tabernacles: "on the first day [shall be] a sabbath (shabbâthôwn), and on the eighth day [shall be] a sabbath (shabbâthôwn)" [two tokens in one verse]

### 4. Sabbatical Year — Land Rest
The seventh year when the land itself observes a shabbâthôwn; no sowing, pruning, or reaping of volunteer growth.

- **Lev 25:4** — "in the seventh year shall be a sabbath of rest unto the land, a sabbath for the LORD: thou shalt neither sow thy field, nor prune thy vineyard"
- **Lev 25:5** — "neither gather the grapes of thy vine undressed: [for] it is a year of rest unto the land"
