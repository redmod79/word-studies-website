## Lexicon Definition

A primitive root; to drive out from a possession; especially to expatriate or divorce:—cast up (out), divorced (woman), drive away (forth, out), expel, surely put away, trouble, thrust out.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. God Driving Out the Nations (conquest theology)
By far the most concentrated usage — YHWH expelling Canaanite peoples before Israel.
- **Exo 23:28** — "I will send hornets before thee, which shall drive out the Hivite, the Canaanite, and the Hittite"
- **Exo 23:29–30** — "I will not drive them out from before thee in one year... By little and little I will drive them out"
- **Exo 33:2** — "I will drive out the Canaanite, the Amorite, and the Hittite..."
- **Jos 24:12, 18** — "the LORD drave out from before us all the people, even the Amorites"

### 2. Failed / Incomplete Expulsion
The flipside of category 1 — divine refusal or human inability to drive out.
- **Jdg 2:3** — "I will not drive them out from before you; but they shall be as thorns"
- **Jdg 6:9** — "I... drave them out from before you, and gave you their land"
- **Num 22:6, 11** — Balak's hope: "peradventure I shall prevail, that we may smite them, and that I may drive them out"

### 3. Expulsion of Individuals
Personal driving-away — from household, role, or presence.
- **Gen 3:24** — "So he drove out the man" (first occurrence — Eden)
- **Gen 4:14** — Cain: "thou hast driven me out this day from the face of the earth"
- **Gen 21:10** — Sarah to Abraham: "Cast out this bondwoman and her son"
- **1 Ki 2:27** — "Solomon thrust out Abiathar from being priest"

### 4. Divorce / Putting Away a Wife (legal)
The Qal passive participle גְּרוּשָׁה becomes a technical term for a divorced woman.
- **Lev 21:7** — "neither shall they take a woman put away from her husband"
- **Lev 21:14** — "A widow, or a divorced woman... these shall he not take" (priestly marriage law)
- **Num 30:9** — "every vow of a widow, and of her that is divorced"
- **Ezek 44:22** — "Neither shall they take for their wives a widow, nor her that is put away"

### 5. Thrust Out from Egypt (Exodus-specific idiom)
A cluster around the departure from Egypt — Pharaoh forcibly expelling Israel.
- **Exo 6:1** — "with a strong hand shall he let them go, and with a strong hand shall he drive them out of his land"
- **Exo 11:1** — "when he shall let you go, he shall surely thrust you out hence altogether"
- **Exo 12:39** — "they were thrust out of Egypt, and could not tarry"

### 6. Expulsion by Peers or Enemies
Human-on-human driving out, often of the weak or unwanted.
- **Exo 2:17** — "the shepherds came and drove them away" (Reuel's daughters at the well)
- **Jdg 9:41** — "Zebul thrust out Gaal and his brethren"
- **Jdg 11:2, 7** — "they thrust out Jephthah... did not ye hate me, and expel me out of my father's house?"
- **Mic 2:9** — "The women of my people have ye cast out from their pleasant houses"

### 7. Metaphorical / Figurative
Extended uses — disturbed sea, being cut off from God's sight.
- **Isa 57:20** — "the wicked are like the troubled sea... whose waters cast up mire and dirt"
- **Amos 8:8** — "it shall rise up wholly as a flood; and... be drowned, as by the flood of Egypt" (same verb as Isa 57:20's "cast up")
- **Jon 2:4** — "I am cast out of thy sight; yet I will look again toward thy holy temple"
- **1 Sam 26:19** — "they have driven me out this day from abiding in the inheritance of the LORD"
