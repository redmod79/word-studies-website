## Lexicon Definition

A primitive root; to stun (or intransitively, grow numb), i.e. devastate or (figuratively) stupefy (both usually in a passive sense):—make amazed, be astonied, (be an) astonish(-ment), (be, bring into, unto, lay, lie, make) desolate(-ion, places), be destitute, destroy (self), (lay, lie, make) waste, wonder.

The root carries a dual semantic field: **physical ruination** (land/city devastated) and **psychological stupefaction** (person struck numb with shock). Both senses derive from the core image of being "stunned" — a place or person left paralyzed, emptied, unable to function.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Covenant Curse — Land Made Desolate

The founding context: Moses' covenant curses (Lev 26) introduce שָׁמֵם as the condition of the land when Israel breaks covenant. The land "lies desolate" and at last enjoys its unpaid sabbaths. This frame controls all later prophetic usage.

- **Lev 26:32** — "And I will bring the land into desolation: and your enemies which dwell therein shall be astonished at it."
- **Lev 26:34–35** — "Then shall the land enjoy her sabbaths, as long as it lieth desolate... As long as it lieth desolate it shall rest; because it did not rest in your sabbaths"
- **Jer 12:11** — "They have made it desolate, [and being] desolate it mourneth unto me; the whole land is made desolate, because no man layeth it to heart."
- **2 Chron 36:21** — "To fulfil the word of the LORD by the mouth of Jeremiah, until the land had enjoyed her sabbaths: [for] as long as she lay desolate she kept sabbath"

### 2. Stupefied / Struck Dumb (Personal Numbness)

A person stunned by overwhelming experience — grief, horror, divine encounter, or the spectacle of ruin — is described with שָׁמֵם. The emotional state mirrors the physical state of a ruined city: emptied, unable to move.

- **Ezek 3:15** — "Then I came to them of the captivity at Telabib... and I sat where they sat, and remained there astonished [shâmêm] among them seven days."
- **Isa 52:14** — "As many were astonied at thee; his visage was so marred more than any man, and his form more than the sons of men"
- **Ezra 9:3–4** — "And when I heard this thing, I rent my garment... and sat down astonied [shâmêm]"
- **Job 17:8** — "Upright [men] shall be astonied at this, and the innocent shall stir up himself against the hypocrite."

### 3. Abomination of Desolation (Daniel's Formula)

Daniel concentrates שָׁמֵם into a fixed prophetic formula paired with שִׁקּוּץ (shiqquwts, "abomination"): the desecrating act that leaves the sanctuary desolate. This phrase carries into the NT via LXX ἐρήμωσις (Matt 24:15; Mark 13:14).

- **Dan 8:13** — "How long shall be the vision concerning the daily sacrifice, and the transgression of desolation [shâmêm], to give both the sanctuary and the host to be trodden under foot?"
- **Dan 9:27** — "And for the overspreading of abominations he shall make it desolate [shâmêm]"
- **Dan 11:31** — "They shall take away the daily sacrifice, and they shall place the abomination that maketh desolate [shâmêm]."
- **Dan 12:11** — "From the time that the daily sacrifice shall be taken away, and the abomination that maketh desolate set up"

### 4. Nations and Enemies Brought to Ruin

The prophets apply שָׁמֵם to the judgment of foreign nations — Edom, Babylon, Tyre, Egypt — using it to describe both physical devastation and the shock of passersby who witness it.

- **Jer 49:17** — "Edom shall be a desolation: every one that goeth by it shall be astonished, and shall hiss at all the plagues thereof."
- **Jer 50:13** — "Because of the wrath of the LORD it shall not be inhabited, but it shall be wholly desolate: every one that goeth by Babylon shall be astonished"
- **Ezek 27:35** — "All the inhabitants of the isles shall be astonished at thee [Tyre], and their kings shall be sore afraid"
- **Zeph 3:6** — "I have cut off the nations: their towers are desolate; I made their streets waste, that none passeth by"

### 5. Divine Wonder at Human Failure

Twice in Isaiah, שָׁמֵם describes God himself as "wondering" — struck by the absence of any human intercessor. The same word used of ruined landscapes and stunned bystanders here depicts divine astonishment at Israel's failure.

- **Isa 59:16** — "And he saw that [there was] no man, and wondered [shâmêm] that [there was] no intercessor: therefore his arm brought salvation"
- **Isa 63:5** — "And I looked, and [there was] none to help; and I wondered [shâmêm] that [there was] none to uphold: therefore mine own arm brought salvation"
