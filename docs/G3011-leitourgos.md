## Lexicon Definition

From a derivative of λεῖτος (leitos, "of the people, public") and ἔργον (ergon, "work"); a public servant, i.e. a functionary in the temple or gospel, or (generally) a worshipper (of God) or benefactor (of man).

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Civic/Governmental Service
- **Rom 13:6** — "For for this cause pay ye tribute also: for they are God's ministers, attending continually upon this very thing."

Civil magistrates described as God's leitourgoi — public servants carrying out a divinely appointed governmental function. The term retains its classical Greek civic sense of one who performs a public duty.

### 2. Priestly/Sacerdotal Ministry
- **Rom 15:16** — "That I should be the minister of Jesus Christ to the Gentiles, ministering the gospel of God, that the offering up of the Gentiles might be acceptable, being sanctified by the Holy Ghost."
- **Heb 8:2** — "A minister of the sanctuary, and of the true tabernacle, which the Lord pitched, and not man."

Cultic-priestly register. Paul casts his apostolic mission in sacerdotal terms — he is a leitourgos performing sacred service (hierourgeo) with the Gentiles as his offering. In Hebrews, Christ himself is called leitourgos of the heavenly sanctuary and true tabernacle, the definitive priestly minister.

### 3. Angelic Service
- **Heb 1:7** — "And of the angels he saith, Who maketh his angels spirits, and his ministers a flame of fire."

Angels as God's leitourgoi — quoting Psalm 104:4 (LXX 103:4). The term designates their role as official servants carrying out divine assignments.

### 4. Personal Sacrificial Service
- **Php 2:25** — "Yet I supposed it necessary to send to you Epaphroditus, my brother, and companion in labour, and fellowsoldier, but your messenger, and he that ministered to my wants."

Epaphroditus as leitourgos to Paul's needs — personal service framed with the dignity of official public ministry. The Philippians sent him as their representative to perform this service.
