## Lexicon Definition

A primitive root; to trample; hence, to wash (properly, by stamping with the feet), whether literal (including the fulling process) or figurative: — fuller, wash(-ing).

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Ritual Purification of Garments (Levitical Law)
The dominant usage. The person who contacts uncleanness must kabas their garments as part of the prescribed cleansing protocol. Always paired with rachats (body washing) and the "unclean until evening" formula.
- **Lev 11:25** — "whosoever beareth ought of the carcase of them shall wash (kabas) his clothes, and be unclean until the even"
- **Lev 15:5** — "whosoever toucheth his bed shall wash (kabas) his clothes, and bathe himself in water"
- **Lev 16:26** — "he that let go the goat for the scapegoat shall wash (kabas) his clothes, and bathe his flesh in water"
- **Num 19:7** — "the priest shall wash (kabas) his clothes, and he shall bathe his flesh in water"

### 2. Garment Inspection for Plague/Leprosy
Applied to garments themselves as objects that may harbor contamination. The priest commands the garment to be washed and then reinspects.
- **Lev 13:54** — "the priest shall command that they wash (kabas) the thing wherein the plague is"
- **Lev 13:55** — "the priest shall look on the plague, after that it is washed (hukabbas)"
- **Lev 13:58** — "the garment... which thou shalt wash (tekabbes), if the plague be departed... it shall be washed (kubbas) the second time"

### 3. Consecration Preparation
Washing garments before an encounter with God's presence — not cleansing from defilement but preparation for holiness.
- **Exo 19:10** — "sanctify them to day and to morrow, and let them wash (kabas) their clothes"
- **Exo 19:14** — "Moses... sanctified the people; and they washed (kabas) their clothes"
- **Num 8:7** — "let them shave all their flesh, and let them wash (kabas) their clothes, and so cleanse themselves" (Levite consecration)

### 4. Figurative / Metaphorical Washing
The prophets and psalmist apply the ritual term to moral/spiritual cleansing, drawing on its cultic resonance.
- **Psa 51:2** — "Wash me (kabbeseni) throughly from mine iniquity, and cleanse me from my sin"
- **Psa 51:7** — "wash me (tekabbeseni), and I shall be whiter than snow"
- **Jer 4:14** — "O Jerusalem, wash (kabbesi) thine heart from wickedness"
- **Jer 2:22** — "though thou wash thee (tekabbesi) with nitre, and take thee much soap, yet thine iniquity is marked"

### 5. Fuller (Professional Tradesman)
The Qal participle *kobes* designates the professional washer/launderer — a trade important enough to name a geographic landmark.
- **2 Ki 18:17** — "the conduit of the upper pool, which is in the highway of the fuller's (kobes) field"
- **Isa 7:3** — "at the end of the conduit of the upper pool in the highway of the fuller's (kobes) field"
- **Mal 3:2** — "he is like a refiner's fire, and like fullers' (mekabbesim) soap"

### 6. Poetic / Figurative Abundance
- **Gen 49:11** — "he washed (kibbes) his garments in wine, and his clothes in the blood of grapes" — Jacob's blessing on Judah; grape abundance so great that wine serves for laundry
