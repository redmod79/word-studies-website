## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same across testaments:**
- The root concept of wholeness/completeness persists — shelem (from shalam "be complete") becomes soterion (from sozo "save/make whole")
- Both testaments connect the concept to God's initiative toward people — a communal, relational act rather than an isolated legal transaction

**What shifts:**
- In the OT, soterion/shelem is a specific cultic offering involving animal sacrifice, shared meals, and priestly mediation
- In the NT, soterion loses all sacrificial/cultic associations and becomes abstract — the tangible "salvation" that God provides, particularly in Christ (Luke 2:30, 3:6)
- The communal meal aspect of the shelem (offerer eats the flesh before YHWH) has no direct NT parallel through soterion, though the Lord's Supper carries forward the fellowship-meal theology through different vocabulary
- The NT soterion passages emphasize universal scope ("all men," "the Gentiles") — expanding far beyond Israel's cultic community
