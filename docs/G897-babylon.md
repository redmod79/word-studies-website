## Lexicon Definition

Of Hebrew origin (H894); Babylon, the capital of Chaldaea (literally or figuratively, as a type of tyranny). In the NT the name designates (a) the historical Mesopotamian city of the Jewish exile, (b) a symbolic/prophetic city-entity in the apocalyptic visions of Revelation, and (c) a probable cipher for Rome in apostolic usage (1 Pet 5:13).

**Pattern:** More than half of NT usage (6 of 11 verses) concentrates in Revelation 14–18, where Babylon functions as a prophetic-symbolic entity. Matthew's three uses are historical-genealogical (the Exile), Acts 7:43 is an OT citation (Amos 5:27 with "Damascus" replaced by "Babylon"), and 1 Peter 5:13 is a greeting datum whose referent has been debated since antiquity. The word never appears in the Gospels of Mark, Luke, or John, nor in any Pauline epistle.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Historical Mesopotamian City — Place of Exile
- **Matt 1:11** — "carried away to Babylon" (μετοικεσίας Βαβυλῶνος)
- **Matt 1:12** — "after they were brought to Babylon"
- **Matt 1:17** — "from David until the carrying away into Babylon... from the carrying away into Babylon unto Christ"

Here the word denotes the literal sixth-century-BC destination of the Judean deportation, used as a genealogical landmark.

### 2. OT Citation Extending the Exile Motif
- **Acts 7:43** — "I will carry you away beyond Babylon" (Stephen quoting Amos 5:27 LXX, which reads "beyond Damascus"; Stephen updates the name to the more famous exile-city)

### 3. Apostolic Greeting — Place of Writing (Debated Referent)
- **1 Pet 5:13** — "The [church that is] at Babylon, elected together with [you], saluteth you"

Could designate literal Babylon on the Euphrates, the Egyptian Babylon (a Roman fort), or — following the later Jewish and Patristic reading — Rome as a cipher. The word itself is neutral; context decides.

### 4. Apocalyptic City — Fallen Harlot
- **Rev 14:8** — "Babylon is fallen, is fallen, that great city, because she made all nations drink of the wine of the wrath of her fornication"
- **Rev 18:2** — "Babylon the great is fallen, is fallen, and is become the habitation of devils"

The doubled "fallen, fallen" cry echoes Isa 21:9 LXX and announces a coming judgment.

### 5. Apocalyptic City — Cup-Bearer of Wrath
- **Rev 16:19** — "great Babylon came in remembrance before God, to give unto her the cup of the wine of the fierceness of his wrath"
- **Rev 17:5** — "upon her forehead [was] a name written, MYSTERY, BABYLON THE GREAT, THE MOTHER OF HARLOTS AND ABOMINATIONS OF THE EARTH"

Babylon both gives a cup to the nations (14:8) and receives one from God (16:19). The name stands as a titulus on the harlot's forehead, marking her as a μυστήριον (mystery, G3466).

### 6. Apocalyptic City — Object of Final Destruction
- **Rev 18:10** — "Alas, alas, that great city Babylon, that mighty city! for in one hour is thy judgment come"
- **Rev 18:21** — "Thus with violence shall that great city Babylon be thrown down, and shall be found no more at all"

The millstone-into-sea image draws directly on Jer 51:63–64 LXX, cementing the literary dependence on OT Babylon oracles.

## Notes on Usage

- **Proper noun, not Louw-Nida–classified.** Louw-Nida does not index proper names as semantic-domain items; G897 has no domain entry.
- **All 11 NT occurrences are nominative/genitive/accusative of the same feminine singular lemma** — Βαβυλών / Βαβυλῶνος / Βαβυλῶνα / Βαβυλῶνι. There is no morphological variation of theological interest.
- **Literary dependencies.** Revelation's Babylon oracles draw heavily on Isa 13, Isa 21, Isa 47, Jer 50–51, and Ezek 26–27. The doubled "fallen, fallen" comes from Isa 21:9 LXX; the millstone-cast-into-sea from Jer 51:63–64; the cup imagery from Jer 25:15–17 and Jer 51:7; the harlot/mother motif partially from Jer 51:13 and Isa 47:5–9.
- **Rev 17:5 titulus** is the only place in the NT the word is written as a formal inscription ("a name written... BABYLON THE GREAT"), marking it as a symbolic/cryptic designation (μυστήριον).
