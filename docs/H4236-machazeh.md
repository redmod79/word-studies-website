## Semantic Categories

### 1. Authentic Prophetic Vision — Divine Revelation Received
- **Gen 15:1** — "After these things the word of the LORD came unto Abram in a vision (machazeh), saying, Fear not, Abram: I am thy shield, and thy exceeding great reward."
- **Num 24:4** — "He hath said, which heard the words of God, which saw the vision (machazeh) of the Almighty, falling into a trance, but having his eyes open"
- **Num 24:16** — "He hath said, which heard the words of God, and knew the knowledge of the most High, which saw the vision (machazeh) of the Almighty, falling into a trance, but having his eyes open"

In all three, machazeh denotes the act or mode of receiving divine communication. Gen 15:1 uses it as the medium through which God’s word arrives. Num 24:4, 16 pair it with Shaddai to describe Balaam’s ecstatic prophetic state.

### 2. False / Vain Vision — Condemned Prophetic Claim
- **Eze 13:7** — "Have ye not seen a vain vision (machazeh), and have ye not spoken a lying divination, whereas ye say, The LORD saith it; albeit I have not spoken?"

The sole negative usage. Ezekiel applies machazeh to the false prophets’ fabricated claims, pairing it with "lying divination" (qecem kazab). The word itself is neutral — the falsity lies in the prophets, not the word.
