## Semantic Categories

### 1. Resurrection Transformation: The Body Raised Incorruptible
- **1 Cor 15:42** — "It is sown in corruption; it is raised in incorruption"
- **1 Cor 15:50** — "neither doth corruption inherit incorruption"
- **1 Cor 15:53** — "this corruptible must put on incorruption"
- **1 Cor 15:54** — "when this corruptible shall have put on incorruption... Death is swallowed up in victory"

The dominant category (4 of 8 occurrences). Aphtharsia describes the condition of the resurrected body — a state contrasted with phthora (corruption/decay). Something "put on" at resurrection, not presently possessed.

### 2. Object of Aspiration: Immortality Sought or Revealed
- **Rom 2:7** — "To them who by patient continuance in well doing seek for glory and honour and immortality, eternal life"
- **2 Tim 1:10** — "who hath abolished death, and hath brought life and immortality to light through the gospel"

Here aphtharsia is something humans *seek* (Rom 2:7) and something Christ *brought to light* (2 Tim 1:10). Both verses frame it as external to the seeker — obtained through pursuit or revealed through the gospel.

### 3. Ethical Purity / Uncorruptedness
- **Eph 6:24** — "Grace be with all them that love our Lord Jesus Christ in sincerity"
- **Tit 2:7** — "in doctrine shewing uncorruptness, gravity, sincerity"

Extended metaphorical sense: love or doctrine that is "un-decayed," i.e., pure and genuine. The underlying image of incorruptibility applies to character rather than body.
