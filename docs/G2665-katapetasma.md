## Lexicon Definition

From a compound of κατά (kata) and a congener of πετάννυμι (petannymi); something spread thoroughly, i.e. the door-Loss of the inner Temple chamber, a curtain:—veil.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. The Inner Temple Curtain (Torn at Christ's Death)
- **Matt 27:51** — "And, behold, the veil of the temple was rent in twain from the top to the bottom; and the earth did quake, and the rocks rent"
- **Mark 15:38** — "And the veil of the temple was rent in twain from the top to the bottom"
- **Luke 23:45** — "And the sun was darkened, and the veil of the temple was rent in the midst"

All three Synoptics record the tearing (σχίζω, schizo) of the katapetasma. Matthew and Mark specify "from the top to the bottom" (ἄνωθεν ἕως κάτω), emphasizing divine agency (top-down, not bottom-up). Luke places it alongside the solar darkening and uses "in the midst" (μέσον). Luke positions the veil-tearing before Jesus' final cry ("Father, into thy hands..."), while Matthew and Mark place it immediately after.

### 2. The Barrier Before the Holy of Holies (Tabernacle Furniture)
- **Heb 9:3** — "And after the second veil, the tabernacle which is called the Holiest of all"

Identifies the katapetasma as the "second veil" separating the Holy Place from the Most Holy Place, distinguishing it from the outer curtain (the "first tabernacle" entrance). This is the OT poreketh — the curtain before the ark.

### 3. The Opened Way Into God's Presence
- **Heb 6:19** — "Which hope we have as an anchor of the soul, both sure and stedfast, and which entereth into that within the veil"
- **Heb 10:20** — "By a new and living way, which he hath consecrated for us, through the veil, that is to say, his flesh"

Hebrews transforms the katapetasma into a theological metaphor. Hope enters "within" (ἐσώτερον) the veil; the "new and living way" passes "through" (διά) the veil, which Hebrews identifies with Christ's flesh. The veil that once barred access now marks the opened path.
