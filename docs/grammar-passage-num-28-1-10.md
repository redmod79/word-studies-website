# Grammar Analysis: Numbers 28:1-10 (Daily & Sabbath Tamid — with the שֵׁכָר Libation Clause)

## Overview

Numbers 28:1-10 is YHWH's prescription of the *tamid* (continual) burnt-offering: the **daily pair of lambs** (vv.3-8) and the **Sabbath supplement** (vv.9-10). Every action-verb in the unit is an *offering*-verb (הקריב "bring near," עשׂה "make/prepare," נסך Hiphil "pour out [as libation]"). **No verb of human consumption — no שׁתה "drink," no אכל "eat," no לקח "take" — occurs anywhere in the passage.** The אִשֶּׁה / רֵיחַ נִיחֹחַ / לַיהוָה refrain (vv.2, 6, 8) frames every element as cultic material directed *to YHWH*, never to the worshipper.

The central exegetical crux is v.7b — בַּקֹּדֶשׁ הַסֵּךְ נֶסֶךְ שֵׁכָר לַיהוָה ("in the holy [place] pour out a libation of *shekar* to YHWH"). The ETCBC/BHSA parser and clause-analyzer resolve this unambiguously as a **pouring-out libation directed to YHWH**, not a command for human drinking.

**Central verification (confirmed):**
- The only verb governing שֵׁכָר in the clause is **הַסֵּךְ** (Hiphil infinitive absolute of **נסך** "pour out"), the cultic libation-verb.
- שֵׁכָר is in **construct apposition** to **נֶסֶךְ** — "a libation [consisting] of *shekar*" — it is the *material poured*, not an object of drinking.
- The complement **לַיהוָה** ("to YHWH") marks the **recipient of the pouring**, exactly as לַיהוָה marks the recipient of the אִשֶּׁה fire-offering in vv.6 and 8.
- The locative **בַּקֹּדֶשׁ** ("in the sanctuary") specifies that this pouring takes place at the altar, the domain of priestly sacrificial action, not a consumption-space.
- No form of the drinking-verb שׁתה, the eating-verb אכל, or any consumption verb appears anywhere in Num 28:1-10.

## Central Deliverable: Verification of "וְנֵסֶךְ שֵׁכָר" as Libation-Offering, Not Human-Consumption Command

### 1. The verb is הַסֵּךְ — Hiphil of נסך "pour out" (cultic libation-verb)

The parser assigns הַסֵּךְ to lemma **נסך** (Strong's H5258), stem **Hiphil**, form **infinitive absolute**:

> הַסֵּךְ — Verb.Hiphil.InfAbs — "pour"

This is the causative stem of the root נסך whose Qal/Hiphil uses are morphologically and semantically **the pouring-out of liquid as a ritual offering onto or beside the altar**. Every BHSA-tagged occurrence of the verb in the Pentateuch is a libation-pouring context:

| Reference | Form | Parsing | Semantic Field |
|-----------|------|---------|----------------|
| Gen 35:14 | וַיַּסֵּךְ | Hiphil.Wayq.3ms | Jacob pours libation on pillar |
| Exod 25:29 | יֻסַּךְ | Hophal.Impf.3ms | passive: libation-vessels |
| Exod 30:9 | תִסְּכוּ | Qal.Impf.2mp | "you shall not pour" (on incense altar) |
| Exod 37:16 | יֻסַּךְ | Hophal.Impf.3ms | libation-vessels |
| **Num 28:7** | **הַסֵּךְ** | **Hiphil.InfAbs** | **pour libation** |

**The verb never means "give to drink" or "drink" in sacrificial contexts.** The verb of human consumption is שׁתה (*shatah*), which appears nowhere in Num 28:1-10.

The **Hiphil infinitive absolute** used imperativally is a sharp, formulaic cultic command — see `hebrew/infinitive-absolute-intensification.md`. Its function here is the same as in Lev 6:14 parallels: a non-finite *impersonal* directive, marking the act as a priestly prescription, not a personal action of the addressee-qua-consumer.

### 2. שֵׁכָר is the *material* of the libation, in construct apposition to נֶסֶךְ

The parser identifies a **construct chain**:

> **Construct Chain Found:**
>   נֶסֶךְ (libation) — CONSTRUCT
>   שֵׁכָר (strong drink) — ABSOLUTE

**נֶסֶךְ שֵׁכָר** = "a libation [consisting] of *shekar*" — an appositional/material genitive.

Here שֵׁכָר functions as the **content-specifier** of the libation-noun. Grammatically it is a noun in the absolute state answering "what substance is the libation composed of?" — not an object of any drinking-verb. שֵׁכָר here does not stand free as an object of consumption; it is **embedded inside** the construct phrase that names the offering.

The parallel construction appears in Num 15:5, 15:7, 15:10 where נֶסֶךְ יַיִן ("libation of wine") uses exactly the same grammatical pattern: libation-noun + material-absolute.

### 3. לַיהוָה marks the recipient of the pouring

The clause-analyzer assigns לַיהוָה as the **Cmpl** (Complement) of the pouring clause:

> Clause 2 (InfA):
>   [Loca] בַּקֹּדֶשׁ — "in the sanctuary"
>   [Pred] הַסֵּךְ — "pour"
>   [Objc] נֶסֶךְ שֵׁכָר — "a libation of shekar"
>   [Cmpl] **לַיהוָה** — **"to YHWH"**

The preposition לְ here is the **lamed of directed offering / dative of recipient** — the same role לַיהוָה plays three other times in this very passage:

- v.3 — תַּקְרִיבוּ לַיהוָה — "you shall offer *to YHWH*"
- v.6 — אִשֶּׁה לַיהוָה — "a fire-offering *to YHWH*"
- v.7 — נֶסֶךְ שֵׁכָר **לַיהוָה** — "a libation of shekar *to YHWH*"
- v.8 — רֵיחַ נִיחֹחַ **לַיהוָה** — "a scent of appeasement *to YHWH*"

The absolute parallelism between v.6's אִשֶּׁה לַיהוָה ("fire-offering to YHWH" — where no human eats it; it is burnt up) and v.7's נֶסֶךְ שֵׁכָר לַיהוָה ("libation of shekar to YHWH") is structurally decisive: **the shekar goes to the same addressee, in the same way, as the burning flesh.** It is not handed to a worshipper; it is poured out at the altar.

### 4. בַּקֹּדֶשׁ localizes the action to the sanctuary

The locative **בַּקֹּדֶשׁ** ("in the holy [place]") situates the pouring spatially at the sanctuary — the domain of sacrificial activity, not a domestic or festal eating-drinking setting. The preposition ב + הַקֹּדֶשׁ fronted before the verb is the clause-analyzer's **[Loca]** role. This is the grammatical equivalent of "at the altar," placing the act squarely inside priestly-cultic space.

## Key Grammatical Features

### 1. הַסֵּךְ — Hiphil infinitive absolute of נסך (v.7)

- **Form:** Verb.Hiphil.InfAbs of נסך "pour out (as libation)"
- **Function:** Infinitive absolute used imperativally/prescriptively — a standard Pentateuchal cultic formula. The Hiphil of נסך = "cause [liquid] to be poured out [as offering]."
- **Significance:** This is *the* dedicated libation-verb of the Hebrew sacrificial lexicon. It never denotes human consumption; it denotes the priestly act of emptying a liquid vessel at the altar. (Cf. Gen 35:14 וַיַּסֵּךְ עָלֶיהָ נֶסֶךְ — Jacob pours a libation *on* the stone.)
- **Cross-reference:** Word study `word-studies/H5258-nasak.md`; grammar entry `hebrew/stem-hiphil.md`; `hebrew/infinitive-absolute-intensification.md`.

### 2. נֶסֶךְ שֵׁכָר — construct chain "libation of shekar" (v.7)

- **Form:** Noun.ms.Cst (נֶסֶךְ) + Noun.ms.Abs (שֵׁכָר) — classic construct chain.
- **Function:** **Appositional/material genitive** — the absolute noun specifies the substance of the construct. "A libation [made of] shekar."
- **Significance:** שֵׁכָר is not an object of drinking; it is *embedded* as the content-term inside the name of the offering. Grammatically identical to נֶסֶךְ יַיִן ("libation of wine") in Num 15:5, 7, 10.
- **Cross-reference:** Word study `word-studies/H5262-necek.md` (the libation-noun); `word-studies/H7941-shekar.md` (the material-noun).

### 3. לַיהוָה — lamed of recipient / dative of offering (vv.3, 6, 7, 8)

- **Form:** Prep לְ + proper noun YHWH (with definite assimilation לַ + יהוה).
- **Function:** Marks the **addressee/recipient** of the sacrificial action. Classified by the clause-analyzer as **Cmpl** (Complement) in v.7.
- **Significance:** The four-fold refrain in vv.3, 6, 7, 8 (לַיהוָה at the tail of each prescribed act) puts *the libation of shekar* in the same syntactic slot as *the burnt offering* and *the fire offering*. Grammar alone forbids re-interpreting v.7's לַיהוָה as anything other than "to YHWH as recipient."

### 4. בַּקֹּדֶשׁ — locative fronting (v.7)

- **Form:** Prep בְּ + Art הַ + Noun קֹדֶשׁ ("the holy [place] / sanctuary").
- **Function:** **[Loca]** (locative) constituent, fronted before the verb הַסֵּךְ — an emphatic topicalization of the *place* of the act.
- **Significance:** The fronted locative confines the action to sanctuary-space. This is a priestly altar-action, not a domestic or banquet action. It seals the cultic interpretation.

### 5. תַּעֲשֶׂה — Qal imperfect 2ms of עשׂה, the technical sacrificial "make/prepare" (vv.4, 8)

- **Form:** Verb.Qal.Impf.2ms of עשׂה.
- **Function:** In sacrificial legislation, עשׂה + accusative-of-offering is the technical idiom for "to prepare / offer (a sacrifice)" — not general "make/do." (Cf. Lev 9:7, 16:9.)
- **Significance:** Every 2ms verb directed at the worshipper in this passage is a **sacrifice-preparation verb** (עשׂה, הקריב, נסך). None is a consumption verb.

### 6. אִשֶּׁה רֵיחַ נִיחֹחַ לַיהוָה — formulaic offering-refrain (vv.2, 6, 8)

- **Form:** Noun.ms (אִשֶּׁה "fire-offering") + Cst-phrase רֵיחַ נִיחֹחַ ("scent of appeasement") + לַיהוָה.
- **Function:** The standard Pentateuchal terminus-formula marking a sacrifice as **consumed by fire and ascending to YHWH** — never eaten/drunk by humans.
- **Significance:** v.6 joins this formula to v.7's libation by structural equivalence. What applies to the אִשֶּׁה applies to the נֶסֶךְ: destined for YHWH, not for the priest's or worshipper's consumption.

The clause-analyzer's **structural verdict is unambiguous**:
- The verb is pouring (הַסֵּךְ).
- The object is the libation-phrase (נֶסֶךְ שֵׁכָר).
- The recipient is YHWH (לַיהוָה).
- The location is the sanctuary (בַּקֹּדֶשׁ).

There is no syntactic slot for a human drinker, nor any verb of drinking. The clause is a sacrificial libation-prescription, full stop.

## Summary Conclusion

**Morphologically, syntactically, and semantically, Num 28:7 commands a priestly act of pouring out fermented drink at the altar as an offering to YHWH.** The verb is נסך Hiphil (the dedicated libation-verb of the Pentateuchal cult). The noun-phrase is the construct chain נֶסֶךְ שֵׁכָר ("a libation of shekar"), where שֵׁכָר is the *material specifier* of the offering, not the direct object of any consumption-verb. The complement לַיהוָה marks YHWH as recipient of the pouring, paralleling the identical לַיהוָה at the tail of the fire-offering in vv.3, 6, and 8. The locative בַּקֹּדֶשׁ fixes the action to sanctuary space.

**No verb in Num 28:1-10 commands, permits, or depicts a human drinking, eating, or tasting of anything.** The passage's entire verbal inventory is sacrificial preparation and priestly pouring.

The text is grammatically incapable of supporting a reading of v.7 as a directive for human consumption of *shekar*. It prescribes a libation — a liquid offering poured out at the altar and belonging entirely to YHWH.
