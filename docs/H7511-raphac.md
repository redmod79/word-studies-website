## Lexicon Definition

*Strong's:* "A primitive root; to trample, i.e. prostrate: — humble self, submit self."

*BDB (on the Aramaic cognate H7512):* "verb tread, trample (compare Biblical Hebrew H7511 (late))." The core physical image is stamping or treading under foot. The two Hebrew occurrences appear in reflexive/middle stems, which shift the sense from "trample another" to "prostrate oneself / be stamped down" — a self-lowering act of submission.

Both biblical occurrences are in the Hithpael (reflexive/iterative) stem. The Hithpael of a "trample" root yields "cause oneself to be trampled" → "humble self, grovel, submit." ## Semantic Categories

Given only two occurrences, the word carries two adjacent shades:

### 1. Forced Submission of an Enemy (with tribute)
- **Psa 68:30** — "Rebuke the company of spearmen, the multitude of the bulls, with the calves of the people, [till every one] submit himself (מִתְרַפֵּס) with pieces of silver: scatter thou the people [that] delight in war."
  - Participle picturing foreign peoples forced down to their faces, bearing silver as tribute to YHWH's king. The "trample" root colors the submission as coerced — they are stamped into homage.

### 2. Urgent Self-Abasement before a Peer / Creditor
- **Pro 6:3** — "Do this now, my son, and deliver thyself, when thou art come into the hand of thy friend; go, humble thyself (הִתְרַפֵּס), and make sure thy friend."
  - Imperative to a young man who has put himself under bond (surety) for another. He must grovel — lower himself to the dust — to be released. The verb pairs with רָהַב (râhab, "press/importune," H7292) in the next clause: self-abasement plus importunate pressing.

The two contexts share the common thread of **lowering oneself to the ground** in response to a superior power — whether divine judgment (Psa 68) or a looming legal/financial claim (Pro 6).
