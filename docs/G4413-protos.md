## Lexicon Definition

Contracted superlative of πρό (G4253, "before"). Foremost (in time, place, order, or importance). The superlative form of a word meaning "before," hence: that which stands at the head — temporally (earliest, initial), spatially (front), sequentially (first in a series), or hierarchically (chief, foremost, most prominent). The word does not inherently specify which axis is in view; context determines whether ordinal sequence, temporal priority, or rank/primacy is meant.

## Semantic Categories

Seven recurring usage patterns emerge from the 99 verse corpus.

### 1. Ordinal Position in an Enumerated Series (Sequence)
Identifies the first member of a counted or listed group. Purely sequential; no temporal or value claim.
- **Mat 10:2** — "The first, Simon, who is called Peter, and Andrew his brother" (list of twelve)
- **Mat 22:25** — "the first, when he had married a wife, deceased" (first of seven brothers)
- **Luk 14:18** — "The first said unto him, I have bought a piece of ground" (first of excuse-makers)
- **Rev 21:19** — "The first foundation was jasper" (first of twelve foundation stones)

### 2. Temporal Priority — "Earlier in Time" (Time)
The event or state occurring before a later one; may be qualified by adverbial phrases.
- **Jhn 1:41** — "He first findeth his own brother Simon"
- **Mat 12:45** — "the last state of that man is worse than the first"
- **1Ti 2:13** — "For Adam was first formed, then Eve"
- **Act 20:18** — "from the first day that I came into Asia"

### 3. Backward-Referring "the First X" — Prior Member of a Paired/Sequenced Set
A distinctive construction: πρῶτος with a concrete noun, pointing back to an earlier-mentioned member of the same category, contrasted implicitly or explicitly with a later/second/new counterpart. Functions as a retrospective label rather than an ordinal count.
- **Rev 13:12** — "he exerciseth all the power of the first beast before him" (refers back to the sea-beast of Rev 13:1, distinguishing it from the earth-beast introduced in 13:11)
- **Rev 20:5-6** — "This is the first resurrection… Blessed and holy is he that hath part in the first resurrection" (paired with the implied later/second resurrection of 20:12-13; labeled before the "second death" counterpart is named)
- **Rev 21:1** — "the first heaven and the first earth were passed away" (retrospective label for the heaven and earth of Rev 20:11, now contrasted with the "new heaven and new earth")
- **Rev 2:4** — "thou hast left thy first love" (the love previously held, contrasted with the present diminished state; paired with "first works" in v. 5)
- **Heb 8:7** — "if that first covenant had been faultless" (backward reference to the Sinai covenant, contrasted with the "new" of 8:8, 13)
- **Heb 9:2** — "there was a tabernacle made; the first, wherein was the candlestick" (first compartment, paired with "the second" in 9:3)
- **Heb 10:9** — "He taketh away the first, that he may establish the second"

The pattern always operates retrospectively: the author has already introduced or will immediately introduce the contrasting second/new member, and "first" labels the prior member of the pair. This is distinct from ordinal counting in a longer series.

### 4. Rank / Primacy / Chief (Status)
Foremost in status, honor, or importance rather than in sequence.
- **Mat 20:27** — "whosoever will be chief (prōtos) among you, let him be your servant"
- **Mar 6:21** — "his lords, high captains, and chief estates of Galilee"
- **Act 13:50** — "the chief men of the city"
- **Act 28:7** — "the chief man of the island, whose name was Publius"
- **1Ti 1:15** — "Christ Jesus came into the world to save sinners; of whom I am chief (prōtos)"

### 5. Reversal Sayings — "First / Last" Paradox
Jesus' rhetorical inversion of expected rank using πρῶτος / ἔσχατος (eschatos).
- **Mat 19:30** — "many that are first shall be last; and the last shall be first"
- **Mat 20:16** — "So the last shall be first, and the first last"
- **Mar 9:35** — "If any man desire to be first, the same shall be last of all"
- **Mar 10:31** — "But many that are first shall be last; and the last first"
- **Luk 13:30** — "there are last which shall be first, and there are first which shall be last"

### 6. Titular "the First and the Last" — Divine Designation
Christological title in Revelation, pairing πρῶτος with ἔσχατος as an absolute couplet.
- **Rev 1:11** — "I am Alpha and Omega, the first and the last"
- **Rev 1:17** — "Fear not; I am the first and the last"
- **Rev 2:8** — "the first and the last, which was dead, and is alive"
- **Rev 22:13** — "I am Alpha and Omega, the beginning and the end, the first and the last"

### 7. Primacy of Importance — "First [Commandment]" / "First of All"
Used to mark the topmost item in a list where rank rather than sequence is in view.
- **Mat 22:38** — "This is the first and great commandment"
- **Mar 12:28-29** — "Which is the first commandment of all?... The first of all the commandments"
- **Eph 6:2** — "the first commandment with promise"
- **1Co 15:3** — "I delivered unto you first of all that which I also received"

### Distinguishing Ordinal (Time/Sequence) from Rank (Primacy)

|-----------|----------------------|----------------|

| Paired with ἔσχατος as a pairing-couplet | possibly | yes (as title) |

The backward-referring use (Category 3) operates on the sequence axis — it presupposes that a second or new counterpart exists or is about to be named — but semantically emphasizes the relationship between the pair rather than the position in a longer list.
