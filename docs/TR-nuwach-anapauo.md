**Semantic shift in translation:** The LXX splits Hebrew nuach between two Greek families. Anapauo captures the refreshment/repose side (Jesus' "Come unto me... I will give you rest" = anapauo). Katapauo captures the cessation/settlement side (the Hebrews 3–4 "enter into rest" = katapausis). The single Hebrew root's breadth is divided into these two Greek channels, each carrying a different connotation into the NT.

## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same across testaments:**
- The core idea of divinely granted rest from opposition/labor persists from the conquest narratives through Jesus' invitation (Matt 11:28) and Revelation's promise (Rev 14:13)
- The Spirit "resting upon" someone as an endowment of divine power runs from Num 11:25 and Isa 11:2 through to 1 Pet 4:14

**What shifts:**
- In the OT, nuach predominantly describes national/political rest (from enemies, in the land); in the NT, anapauo shifts toward personal/spiritual refreshment and eschatological rest
- The katapauo branch (not anapauo) carries the more theological "Sabbath rest" argument in Hebrews 3–4, creating a distinct channel that nuach feeds but that is not represented by anapauo in the NT
