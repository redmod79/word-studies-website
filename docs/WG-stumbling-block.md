# WG: Stumbling-Block / Scandal / Offence

## Overview
The NT σκανδαλ- / προσκομμ- cluster denotes anything that trips up a person morally, spiritually, or in faith — whether an external bait, a fixed obstacle, or the act of causing the fall. KJV's choice of "offend" / "offence" / "stumblingblock" obscures the underlying imagery: a snare's trigger-stick (σκάνδαλον), a stub one's foot strikes (πρόσκομμα), and the verb of tripping (σκανδαλίζω / προσκόπτω). The concept spans three interlocking uses: (a) Christ as the stone over which unbelievers trip; (b) dominical warnings against causing "little ones" to fall; (c) Pauline ethics of liberty — the strong must not set a stumbling-block before the weak (Rom 14; 1 Cor 8).

## Relationships

### Shared Roots
- σκανδαλ- stem: σκανδαλίζω (G4624) ← σκάνδαλον (G4625). These two are a tight verb-noun pair unique to biblical / Hellenistic Jewish Greek.
- προσκ- stem: πρόσκομμα (G4348), προσκοπή (G4349), προσκόπτω (G4350), ἀπρόσκοπος (G0677). All derive from πρός + κόπτω ("strike against").
- The two Greek stems are not etymologically related but are semantically merged in NT usage.

## Roles in the Concept

- **Agent of tripping (the trap / the stone):** σκάνδαλον (G4625), πρόσκομμα (G4348). These name the *thing* that causes fall.
- **Causative action:** σκανδαλίζω (G4624). To *place* or *be* the trap — transitive / passive.
- **Collision action:** προσκόπτω (G4350). To strike against the obstacle — intransitive.
- **Abstract occurrence:** προσκοπή (G4349). The event/fact of offence being given.
- **Negation:** ἀπρόσκοπος (G0677). A life or conduct that sets no stumbling-block (Acts 24:16; 1 Cor 10:32; Phil 1:10).
- **Resulting lapse:** παράπτωμα (G3900). The fall itself, the transgression — the outcome when the stumbling is successful.
- **Self-trip (no external agent):** πταίω (G4417). To stumble of one's own failing.

## Three Streams of NT Usage

### A. Christological — Christ as Stumbling-Stone
A fixed formula built on Isa 8:14 + Isa 28:16, drawn into Rom 9:32–33; 1 Cor 1:23; Gal 5:11; 1 Pet 2:8. Christ himself (and the cross specifically) is the σκάνδαλον / πρόσκομμα over which unbelieving Israel trips. This is divine placement — God *lays* the stone.

### B. Dominical — Warnings Against Causing Fall
Jesus' teaching contains the gravest warnings in the NT attached to this field: the millstone saying (Matt 18:6–7; Mark 9:42; Luke 17:1–2) and the self-amputation sayings (Matt 5:29–30; 18:8–9; Mark 9:43–47). Here the concern is for the "little ones" and for one's own integrity of faith.

### C. Pauline Ethical — Liberty and the Weak Brother
Paul confines the field to a tight ethical argument: Rom 14:13, 20, 21 and 1 Cor 8:9, 13. The strong believer's liberty (eating meat, drinking wine) can become a σκάνδαλον / πρόσκομμα to the weaker conscience. Rom 14:13 stacks both nouns; Rom 14:21 stacks the verb σκανδαλίζω with προσκόπτω and ἀσθενέω ("stumbleth, or is offended, or is made weak"). 1 Cor 8:9–13 repeats the logic. Paul's prescription: rather forgo the liberty than set the stumbling-block.
