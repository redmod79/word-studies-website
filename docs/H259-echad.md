# H259 — אֶחָד (ʼechâd)

## Core Data
- **Strong's:** H259
- **Original:** אֶחָד
- **Transliteration:** ʼechâd
- **Pronunciation:** ekh-awd'
- **Part of Speech:** Adjective / numeral (also used substantivally and as an ordinal)
- **Root:** Related to אַחַד (H258, "to unify"); most lexicographers take אֶחָד as the primary numeral from which the verbal root is derived.
- **Occurrences:** 739 unique verses (980 tokens) across 34 OT books
- **First Occurrence:** Gen 1:5 — "the evening and the morning were the first (ʼechâd) day"
- **Hapax Legomenon:** No (one of the most frequent numerals in the OT)

## Lexicon Definition

A numeral; properly, **united**, i.e. **one**; or (as an ordinal) **first**:—a, alike, alone, altogether, and, any(-thing), apiece, a certain, (daily), each (one), eleven, every, few, first, highway, a man, once, one, only, other, some, together.

Strong's explicitly glosses the core sense as "properly, united, i.e. one." The word functions as (1) the cardinal numeral "one," (2) the ordinal "first," (3) an indefinite article "a / a certain," and (4) a noun of unity when used absolutely.

**Pattern:** Density peaks in enumeration-heavy books (Numbers, 1 Kings, Ezekiel, Exodus, Leviticus, Joshua) where the numeral tallies offerings, measurements, tribes, days, and architectural parts. Sparse in poetry (Psalms, Proverbs). Genesis (43x) sets many of the theologically freighted uses: "one day," "one place," "one flesh," "one people," "one language."

## Semantic Categories

The word shows a wide but coherent semantic range. Usage falls into the following patterns (all examples drawn from KJV text in the verse output):

### 1. Simple numerical "one" (cardinal)

- **Num 7:15** — "One (ʼechâd) young bullock, one (ʼechâd) ram, one (ʼechâd) lamb"
- **1 Ki 4:22** — "Solomon's provision for one (ʼechâd) day"
- **Gen 22:2** — "upon one (ʼechâd) of the mountains which I will tell thee of"
- **Judg 16:28** — "that I may be at once (ʼechâd) avenged"

This is the dominant use: a countable unit in enumerations, offerings, measurements, and time markers.

### 2. Ordinal "first"

- **Gen 1:5** — "the evening and the morning were the first (ʼechâd) day"
- **Gen 8:13** — "in the first (ʼechâd) month, the first (ʼechâd) day of the month"
- **Ezra 1:1** — "in the first (ʼechâd) year of Cyrus"
- **Ezek 26:1** — "in the eleventh year, in the first (ʼechâd) day of the month"

Hebrew uses the cardinal ʼechâd rather than a distinct ordinal for "first day," especially in calendar/date formulas.

### 3. Indefinite "a / a certain" (one of a class)

- **1 Sam 1:1** — "there was a certain (ʼechâd) man of Ramathaimzophim"
- **Judg 13:2** — "there was a certain (ʼechâd) man of Zorah"
- **Dan 10:5** — "a certain (ʼechâd) man clothed in linen"
- **1 Ki 19:4** — "sat down under a juniper tree (ʼechâd)"

Functions like the English indefinite article, introducing a new but unspecified individual.

### 4. "One of" (partitive) and "the one… the other" (distributive)

- **Gen 3:22** — "become as one (ʼechâd) of us"
- **Exod 1:15** — "the name of the one (ʼechâd) was Shiphrah, and the name of the other was Puah"
- **1 Sam 1:2** — "the name of the one (ʼechâd) was Hannah, and the name of the other Peninnah"
- **Eccl 4:9** — "Two are better than one (ʼechâd)"

Used in contrastive pairs with שֵׁנִי (H8145, "second") or אַחֵר (H312, "other") to distinguish members of a pair.

### 5. Compound / collective unity — a single entity composed of parts

- **Gen 2:24** — "they shall be one (ʼechâd) flesh" (two persons → one bāśār)
- **Num 13:23** — "one (ʼechâd) cluster of grapes" (many grapes → one eshkol)
- **Gen 11:6** — "the people is one (ʼechâd), and they have all one (ʼechâd) language"
- **Gen 34:16** — "we will become one (ʼechâd) people"
- **Exod 26:6** — "couple the curtains together… and it shall be one (ʼechâd) tabernacle" (many curtains → one miškān)
- **Ezek 37:17** — "join them one to another into one (ʼechâd) stick; and they shall become one in thine hand"
- **Ezek 37:22** — "I will make them one (ʼechâd) nation… and one (ʼechâd) king"
- **2 Chr 30:12** — "the hand of God was to give them one (ʼechâd) heart"

This is the well-known "compound unity" usage: ʼechâd describes the singleness of a whole that is made up of plural constituents.

### 6. Absolute / unified "one" of God

- **Deut 6:4** — "Hear, O Israel: The LORD our God is one (ʼechâd) LORD" (the Shema)
- **Zech 14:9** — "in that day shall there be one (ʼechâd) LORD, and his name one (ʼechâd)"
- **Mal 2:10** — "Have we not all one (ʼechâd) father? hath not one (ʼechâd) God created us?"
- **Mal 2:15** — "And did not he make one (ʼechâd)? Yet had he the residue of the spirit"

The form of the adjective in the Shema is the same word used elsewhere for compound and numerical oneness; the text does not select a grammatically distinct "absolute" form. Interpretation of what KIND of oneness is meant is an exegetical question about the referent (YHWH), not a lexical property of ʼechâd itself.

### 7. Emphatic unity of action or will ("as one man")

- **Judg 6:16** — "thou shalt smite the Midianites as one (ʼechâd) man"
- **Judg 20:1, 8, 11** — "all the congregation… as one (ʼechâd) man"
- **1 Sam 11:7** — "they came out with one (ʼechâd) consent"
- **Ezra 2:64; Neh 7:66** — "the whole congregation together (ʼechâd) was forty and two thousand"

Idiom for corporate solidarity: plural subjects acting with a single purpose.

### 8. Temporal "once / at once / in one moment"

- **2 Sam 17:12** — "and of all the men that are with him there shall not be left so much as one (ʼechâd)"
- **Isa 66:8** — "shall a nation be born at once (ʼechâd)?"
- **Hag 2:6** — "Yet once (ʼechâd), it is a little while, and I will shake the heavens"
