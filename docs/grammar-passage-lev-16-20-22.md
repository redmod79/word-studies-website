## Key Grammatical Features

### 1. וְכִלָּה מִכַּפֵּר (v.20)

- **Form:** Piel perfect 3ms כִלָּה followed by מִן + Piel infinitive construct כַּפֵּר.
- **Significance:** The phrase marks completion of the preceding atonement action before the live goat is brought forward. Grammatically, מִן + infinitive construct functions temporally/ablatively: "when he has finished from/with making atonement."
- **Grammar reference:** [infinitive-construct-natan-predicative](../hebrew/infinitive-construct-natan-predicative.md) is a different InfCon construction; no exact entry yet for מִן + InfCon completion.
- **Word study:** [H3722-kaphar](../../word-studies/H3722-kaphar.md)

### 2. Ritual Verb Sequence (vv.20-22)

- **Form:** finite perfect-conjugation verbs chained with waw: וְכִלָּה, וְהִקְרִיב, וְסָמַךְ, וְהִתְוַדָּה, וְנָתַן, וְשִׁלַּח, וְנָשָׂא, וְשִׁלַּח.
- **Significance:** In legal/ritual instruction, waw + perfect forms sequence prescribed actions. The KJV renders them prospectively ("shall bring," "shall lay," "shall send," "shall bear") because the discourse is procedural, not past narrative.
- **Grammar reference:** [weqatal-legal-imperatival](../hebrew/weqatal-legal-imperatival.md)

### 3. Coordinated Objects of Confession (v.21)

- **Form:** הִתְוַדָּה governs a compound object phrase: אֶת־כָּל־עֲוֹנֹת ... וְאֶת־כָּל־פִּשְׁעֵיהֶם לְכָל־חַטֹּאתָם.
- **Significance:** Repeated אֶת marks two direct-object components; לְכָל־חַטֹּאתָם attaches the third sin term with לְ, producing a coordinated comprehensive formula.
- **Grammar reference:** [syntax-compound-object-waw](../hebrew/syntax-compound-object-waw.md); [construct-state](../hebrew/construct-state.md)
- **Word studies:** [H5771-avon](../../word-studies/H5771-avon.md); [H6588-pesha](../../word-studies/H6588-pesha.md); [H2403-chattaah](../../word-studies/H2403-chattaah.md)

### 4. עַל־רֹאשׁ הַשָּׂעִיר (vv.21)

- **Form:** preposition עַל + construct chain רֹאשׁ הַשָּׂעִיר.
- **Significance:** The phrase is a locative complement: the hands are laid upon the goat's head, and the confessed items are put upon the goat's head. The repeated construct phrase binds both ritual actions to the same location.
- **Grammar reference:** [construct-state](../hebrew/construct-state.md)

### 5. אֶל־אֶרֶץ גְּזֵרָה (v.22)

- **Form:** אֶל + construct chain אֶרֶץ גְּזֵרָה.
- **Significance:** The phrase is a destination complement of וְנָשָׂא: the goat bears the iniquities "to" the named destination. The preposition governs the entire construct chain.
