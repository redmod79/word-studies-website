## Semantic Categories

### 1. Military Defeat — to smite an army, city, or nation in battle
The largest usage cluster. Nakah is the standard verb for battlefield victory.
- **Gen 14:5** — Chedorlaomer "smote (nakah) the Rephaims in Ashteroth Karnaim"
- **Josh 10:40** — "So Joshua smote (nakah) all the country of the hills"
- **1 Sam 17:50** — David "smote (nakah) the Philistine, and slew him"
- **1 Kgs 20:29** — "the children of Israel slew (nakah) of the Syrians an hundred thousand footmen in one day"

### 2. Divine Judgment — God smites with plague, pestilence, or calamity
Nakah is the standard verb for Yahweh's punitive striking, especially in the Exodus plagues and covenant curses.
- **Exo 12:29** — "the LORD smote (nakah) all the firstborn in the land of Egypt"
- **Num 14:12** — "I will smite (nakah) them with the pestilence, and disinherit them"
- **Deut 28:22** — "The LORD shall smite (nakah) thee with a consumption, and with a fever"
- **2 Sam 12:15** — "the LORD struck (nakah) the child that Uriah's wife bare unto David"

### 3. Judicial / Punitive Striking — blows as penalty, discipline, or execution
Includes capital punishment, corporal discipline, and judicial blows.
- **Exo 2:12** — "he slew (nakah) the Egyptian, and hid him in the sand"
- **Lev 24:17** — "he that killeth (nakah) any man shall surely be put to death"
- **Prov 17:26** — "to punish the just is not good, nor to strike (nakah) princes for equity"
- **Deut 25:2** — the judicially prescribed stripes (related usage in this chapter)

### 4. Physical Assault — one person striking another
Often an insulting or dishonoring blow rather than a killing stroke.
- **Num 22:28** — Balaam's ass: "thou hast smitten (nakah) me these three times"
- **1 Kgs 22:24** — "Zedekiah... smote (nakah) Micaiah on the cheek"
- **Lam 3:30** — "He giveth his cheek to him that smiteth (nakah) him"
- **Neh 13:25** — Nehemiah "smote (nakah) certain of them, and plucked off their hair"

### 5. Striking an Object or Element — non-personal targets
Nakah is used for striking rocks, waters, ground, dust, trees — often in prophetic sign-acts.
- **Exo 8:16** — "Stretch out thy rod, and smite (nakah) the dust of the land"
- **Exo 17:6** — Moses smites the rock at Horeb (nakah)
- **2 Kgs 2:8** — Elijah "smote (nakah) the waters, and they were divided"
- **Jonah 4:7** — the worm "smote (nakah) the gourd that it withered"

### 6. Figurative / Internal Striking — of conscience, heart, or affliction
A smaller but important category where the blow is emotional, psychological, or moral.
- **1 Sam 24:5** — "David's heart smote (nakah) him"
- **2 Sam 24:10** — "David's heart smote (nakah) him after that he had numbered the people"
- **Isa 1:5** — "Why should ye be stricken (nakah) any more?"
- **Psa 121:6** — "The sun shall not smite (nakah) thee by day, nor the moon by night"

### 7. The Smitten Servant — prophetic striking of a representative figure
A theologically loaded minor category in the Prophets.
- **Isa 53:4** — "we did esteem him stricken, smitten (nakah) of God, and afflicted"
- **Zech 13:7** — "smite (nakah) the shepherd, and the sheep shall be scattered"
- **Mic 5:1** — "they shall smite (nakah) the judge of Israel with a rod upon the cheek"
