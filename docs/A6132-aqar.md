## Semantic Categories

### 1. Forcible Uprooting / Violent Displacement

The sole usage describes three of ten horns being violently dislodged "from before" the rising little horn. The passive Hithpeel stem emphasizes that the horns are acted upon — they do not fall of themselves.

- **Dan 7:8** — "I considered the horns, and, behold, there came up among them another little horn, before whom there were three of the first horns plucked up by the roots [אֶתְעֲקַרָה]: and, behold, in this horn [were] eyes like the eyes of man, and a mouth speaking great things."

*The image of "plucked up by the roots" draws on agricultural language: complete removal, nothing remaining. The Hebrew cognate H6131 uses the same root for houghing horses (removing from service) and uprooting vines (Zeph 2:4), confirming the core idea of total, violent displacement.*
