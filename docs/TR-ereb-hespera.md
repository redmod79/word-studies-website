## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same:**
- The core referent — the onset of darkness, dusk — is identical in Hebrew and Greek
- G2073 ἑσπέρα faithfully carries the LXX sense directly into NT Luke and Acts (both by Luke)
- The day-boundary significance (evening begins the new day) is presupposed in NT Passover/Sabbath timing texts (though ὄψιος is used more often there)

**What shifts:**
- In the NT, ὄψιος (G3798) largely displaces ἑσπέρα for "evening" in narrative contexts (used ~15x vs ἑσπέρα's 3x)
- The dense Levitical ritual function ("unclean until evening") does not carry into NT Greek, since purity law purification language shifts to different vocabulary
- The Hebrew "evening-morning" (*ʻereb-boqer*) compound unit of Dan 8:14 has no Greek NT parallel phrasing
