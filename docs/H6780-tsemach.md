## Semantic Categories

### 1. Messianic Title — The Righteous Branch
- **Jer 23:5** — "I will raise unto David a righteous Branch, and a King shall reign and prosper, and shall execute judgment and justice in the earth"
- **Jer 33:15** — "I will cause the Branch of righteousness to grow up unto David; and he shall execute judgment and righteousness in the land"
- **Zec 3:8** — "I will bring forth my servant the BRANCH"
- **Zec 6:12** — "Behold the man whose name is The BRANCH; and he shall grow up out of his place, and he shall build the temple of the LORD"

In these four passages tsemach functions as a proper title for a future Davidic ruler. Jeremiah pairs it with righteousness (tsedaqah) and kingship; Zechariah pairs it with temple-building and priestly investiture.

### 2. Divine Blessing and Glory
- **Isa 4:2** — "In that day shall the branch of the LORD be beautiful and glorious, and the fruit of the earth shall be excellent and comely for them that are escaped of Israel"
- **Psa 65:10** — "Thou waterest the ridges thereof abundantly... thou blessest the springing thereof"

Used for what God causes to flourish — either eschatological (Isa 4:2) or agricultural blessing from God (Psa 65:10).

### 3. Natural Vegetation / Growth
- **Gen 19:25** — "that which grew upon the ground" (destroyed in Sodom's overthrow)
- **Isa 61:11** — "as the earth bringeth forth her bud, and as the garden causeth the things that are sown in it to spring forth"
- **Eze 16:7** — "I have caused thee to multiply as the bud of the field"

Literal botanical sense: what sprouts from the earth. Often serves as the base image for theological metaphor.

### 4. Failed or Withered Growth (Judgment)
- **Eze 17:9** — "shall he not pull up the roots thereof, and cut off the fruit thereof... it shall wither in all the leaves of her spring"
- **Eze 17:10** — "shall it not utterly wither, when the east wind toucheth it? it shall wither in the furrows where it grew"
- **Hos 8:7** — "the bud shall yield no meal: if so be it yield, the strangers shall swallow it up"

Growth that fails or is judged — the opposite of the Messianic "Branch" that prospers. Ezekiel 17 uses it in the vine/eagle allegory of Zedekiah's failed kingship; Hosea uses it for Israel's fruitless apostasy.

*Tsemach* is distinct from all of these in that it is the only "branch" word used as a Messianic proper title. It emphasizes organic *sprouting from the ground/root* rather than a limb on an existing tree. Netser (H5342) comes closest in Messianic connotation (Isa 11:1) but emphasizes the shoot from a cut stump, while tsemach emphasizes the fresh growth that God causes to spring up.
