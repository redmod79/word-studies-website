# WG: Law

## Overview
The law concept cluster in the NT centers on νόμος (nomos) and its relationships with sin, faith, Christ, God's will, and specific commandments. These words form the theological vocabulary of Paul's justification theology and the broader NT discussion of Torah's role.

## Roles in the Concept

- **The Code:** nomos (νόμος) — the law system itself, from Torah to abstract principle
- **The Specific Precept:** entolē (ἐντολή) — individual commandment within the law
- **What Law Exposes:** hamartia (ἁμαρτία) — sin, which the law makes known (Rom 3:20; 7:7)
- **The Alternative Basis:** pistis (πίστις) — faith, set in contrast to works of law (Gal 2:16)
- **The Law's Telos:** Christos (Χριστός) — the end/goal of the law (Rom 10:4)
- **The Expert:** nomikos (νομικός) — one skilled in law; the adjective "legal"
- **The Negation:** anomos (ἄνομος) — without law, lawless

## Key Passages Where Multiple Group Words Appear Together
- **Rom 3:20-28** — nomos + hamartia + pistis: "by the deeds of the law there shall no flesh be justified... the righteousness of God without the law... justified by faith"
- **Rom 7:7-12** — nomos + hamartia + entolē: "I had not known sin, but by the law... the commandment holy, and just, and good"
- **Gal 2:16** — nomos + pistis + Christos: "not justified by the works of the law, but by the faith of Jesus Christ"
- **Gal 3:23-24** — nomos + pistis + Christos: "the law was our schoolmaster to bring us unto Christ, that we might be justified by faith"
