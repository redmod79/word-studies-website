## Lexicon Definition

> A primitive root; probably to brighten up, i.e. (figuratively) be (causatively, make) blithe or gleesome: — cheer up, be (make) glad, (have, make) joy(-ful), be (make) merry, (cause to, make to) rejoice, very.

The root carries both the intransitive sense ("to be glad, rejoice") in the Qal and the transitive causative sense ("to gladden, cause to rejoice, cheer") in the Piel and (rarely) Hiphil. The underlying image is of brightening — a face lighting up, a heart warmed — rather than the leaping/exulting motion of גִּיל (gîyl, H1523) or שׂוּשׂ (sûws, H7797).

**Observations:** The verb is concentrated in Psalms (52 uses — the single largest reservoir), Proverbs/Ecclesiastes (wisdom), and Deuteronomy (covenant feast). It is rare in the Pentateuchal narrative outside of Deuteronomy (one use each in Exo/Lev) and rare in the historical books relative to poetic/prophetic material. The prophets use it frequently in oracles against the nations ("rejoice not, O my enemy") and in restoration promises.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Covenant Festal Joy (Deuteronomic)
Joy before YHWH at the central sanctuary, with family, servants, Levite, and stranger — commanded as the proper response to God's provision.
- **Deu 12:7** — "there ye shall eat before the LORD your God, and ye shall rejoice in all that ye put your hand unto"
- **Deu 12:12** — "ye shall rejoice before the LORD your God, ye, and your sons, and your daughters"
- **Deu 16:11** — "thou shalt rejoice before the LORD thy God"
- **Deu 26:11** — "thou shalt rejoice in every good thing which the LORD thy God hath given"

### 2. Rejoicing in YHWH Himself
The worshipper's delight in God's person, salvation, and reign — the dominant Psalms usage.
- **1Sa 2:1** — "My heart rejoiceth in the LORD, mine horn is exalted in the LORD"
- **Psa 5:11** — "let all those that put their trust in thee rejoice"
- **Psa 33:21** — "For our heart shall rejoice in him, because we have trusted in his holy name"
- **Psa 97:1** — "The LORD reigneth; let the earth rejoice"

### 3. Piel — God or an Agent Gladdens Another
The causative: something or someone is the cause of joy.
- **Jdg 9:13** — "my wine, which cheereth (Piel ptcp) God and man"
- **Psa 104:15** — "wine that maketh glad (Piel impf) the heart of man"
- **Psa 19:8** — "The statutes of the LORD are right, rejoicing (Piel ptcp) the heart"
- **Psa 86:4** — "Rejoice (Piel impv) the soul of thy servant: for unto thee, O Lord, do I lift up my soul"
- **Psa 90:15** — "Make us glad (Piel impv) according to the days wherein thou hast afflicted us"

### 4. Piel — Domestic / Wisdom Joy
A wise son, a good word, or a friend's counsel gladdens.
- **Pro 10:1** — "A wise son maketh a glad father (Piel impf)"
- **Pro 12:25** — "a good word maketh it [the heart] glad (Piel impf)"
- **Pro 15:30** — "The light of the eyes rejoiceth (Piel impf) the heart"
- **Pro 27:9** — "Ointment and perfume rejoice (Piel impf) the heart"

### 5. Eschatological / Restoration Joy
Promised joy at God's future intervention — often paired with the reversal of mourning.
- **Isa 25:9** — "we will be glad and rejoice in his salvation"
- **Isa 66:10** — "Rejoice ye with Jerusalem, and be glad with her, all ye that love her"
- **Jer 31:13** — "Then shall the virgin rejoice in the dance... I will turn their mourning into joy"
- **Zep 3:14** — "Sing, O daughter of Zion... be glad and rejoice with all the heart"
- **Zec 2:10** — "Sing and rejoice, O daughter of Zion: for, lo, I come"

### 6. Celebratory Joy at Deliverance or Enthronement
Historical narrative uses around political/military triumph.
- **1Sa 11:15** — "Saul and all the men of Israel rejoiced greatly"
- **1Ki 5:7** — "when Hiram heard the words of Solomon, he rejoiced greatly"
- **2Ki 11:20** — "all the people of the land rejoiced" (after Athaliah's overthrow)
- **2Ch 29:36** — "Hezekiah rejoiced, and all the people" (at restoration of temple service)

### 7. Forbidden / Condemned Joy (Malicious or Wrongly-Placed)
The verb frequently appears negated in prophetic oracles: rejoice not over an enemy's fall, rejoice not in iniquity.
- **Pro 24:17** — "Rejoice not when thine enemy falleth"
- **Oba 1:12** — "thou shouldest not have rejoiced over the children of Judah"
- **Mic 7:8** — "Rejoice not against me, O mine enemy"
- **Eze 7:12** — "let not the buyer rejoice, nor the seller mourn"
- **Hos 9:1** — "Rejoice not, O Israel, for joy, as other people: for thou hast gone a whoring from thy God"

### 8. Sapiential Joy — The Limits and Goods of Mirth
Wisdom literature evaluates joy: commended as a gift, interrogated as vanity.
- **Ecc 3:12** — "I know that there is no good in them, but for a man to rejoice, and to do good in his life"
- **Ecc 3:22** — "a man should rejoice in his own works"
- **Ecc 11:9** — "Rejoice, O young man, in thy youth"
- **Pro 23:15** — "My son, if thine heart be wise, my heart shall rejoice, even mine"
