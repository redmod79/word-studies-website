## Semantic Categories

### 1. Marital / Divorce Exception (Synoptic)
Porneia as the sole stated ground permitting divorce in the Matthean exception clauses.
- **Matt 5:32** — "whosoever shall put away his wife, saving for the cause of fornication, causeth her to commit adultery"
- **Matt 19:9** — "Whosoever shall put away his wife, except it be for fornication, and shall marry another, committeth adultery"

### 2. Vice List — Works of the Flesh
Porneia regularly appears first or near first in NT sin catalogs, marking it as a leading item.
- **Matt 15:19** — "out of the heart proceed evil thoughts, murders, adulteries, fornications, thefts, false witness, blasphemies"
- **Mark 7:21** — "out of the heart of men, proceed evil thoughts, adulteries, fornications, murders"
- **Gal 5:19** — "the works of the flesh are manifest: Adultery, fornication, uncleanness, lasciviousness"
- **Rom 1:29** — "filled with all unrighteousness, fornication, wickedness, covetousness, maliciousness"

### 3. Sexual Ethics in the Church (Pauline)
Paul addresses actual cases and gives instruction on avoiding porneia within church communities.
- **1 Cor 5:1** — "fornication among you, and such fornication as is not so much as named among the Gentiles"
- **1 Cor 6:18** — "Flee fornication. Every sin that a man doeth is without the body; but he that committeth fornication sinneth against his own body"
- **1 Cor 7:2** — "to avoid fornication, let every man have his own wife"
- **1 Thess 4:3** — "this is the will of God, your sanctification, that ye should abstain from fornication"

### 4. Jerusalem Council Decree (Acts)
Porneia listed among the four abstentions required of Gentile believers — alongside blood, things strangled, and idol-meats.
- **Acts 15:20** — "abstain from pollutions of idols, and from fornication, and from things strangled, and from blood"
- **Acts 15:29** — "abstain from meats offered to idols, and from blood, and from things strangled, and from fornication"
- **Acts 21:25** — council confirms these as the standing requirements for Gentiles

### 5. Spiritual / Metaphorical Harlotry (Revelation — Babylon)
Porneia used figuratively for the corrupting spiritual influence of Babylon, which seduces nations into idolatry and apostasy.
- **Rev 14:8** — "Babylon is fallen…because she made all nations drink of the wine of the wrath of her fornication"
- **Rev 17:2** — "kings of the earth have committed fornication" with her
- **Rev 17:4** — the woman holds "a golden cup full of abominations and filthiness of her fornication"
- **Rev 18:3** — "all nations have drunk of the wine of the wrath of her fornication"
- **Rev 19:2** — judgment on "the great whore, which did corrupt the earth with her fornication"

### 6. Impenitence / Refusal to Repent
Porneia appears in contexts where repentance from it is refused, linking it to final judgment.
- **Rev 2:21** — "I gave her space to repent of her fornication; and she repented not"
- **Rev 9:21** — "Neither repented they of their murders, nor of their sorceries, nor of their fornication, nor of their thefts"

### 7. Illegitimate Birth (John)
A single occurrence where porneia refers to birth outside proper marriage — used as an insult.
- **John 8:41** — "We be not born of fornication; we have one Father, even God"
