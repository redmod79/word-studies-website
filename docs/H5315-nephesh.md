## Semantic Categories

### 1. Breathing Creature / Any Animate Being
Nephesh applies to all breathing creatures, not only humans.
- **Gen 1:20** — "the moving creature that hath life" (*kol nephesh chayyah*) — sea creatures
- **Gen 1:24** — "Let the earth bring forth the living creature after his kind" — land animals
- **Gen 9:10** — "every living creature (*nephesh chayyah*)" in Noah's covenant — all vertebrate life

### 2. The Whole Person / Legal Individual
In law and census, nephesh = the individual human being as a countable unit.
- **Exo 1:5** — "all the souls that came out of the loins of Jacob were seventy souls"
- **Lev 4:2** — "If a soul shall sin through ignorance against any of the commandments"
- **Num 5:6** — "When a man or woman shall commit any sin... to do a trespass against the LORD"

### 3. Life / Physical Vitality at Risk
Nephesh = the life one can lose, risk, or have taken.
- **Job 2:4** — "all that a man hath will he give for his life"
- **Josh 2:14** — "Our life for yours, if ye utter not this our business"
- **Jdg 5:18** — "Zebulun... a people that jeoparded their lives unto the death"
- **1 Ki 1:29** — "the LORD... that hath redeemed my soul out of all distress"

### 4. Inner Self / Seat of Desire, Appetite, and Emotion
Nephesh as the locus of hunger, longing, grief, and inner experience.
- **1 Sam 1:10** — "she was in bitterness of soul, and prayed unto the LORD"
- **Pro 2:10** — "knowledge is pleasant unto thy soul"
- **Ec 6:2** — "he wanteth nothing for his soul of all that he desireth"
- **Hab 2:5** — "he is a proud man... who enlargeth his desire as hell"

### 5. Wholehearted Devotion to God
"With all thy soul" is a fixed covenant formula calling for undivided commitment.
- **Deu 4:29** — "seek the LORD thy God... with all thy heart and with all thy soul"
- **2 Chr 6:38** — "if they return to thee with all their heart and with all their soul"
- **1 Chr 22:19** — "set your heart and your soul to seek the LORD your God"

### 6. Reflexive Pronoun — Self
Nephesh frequently serves as an emphatic reflexive: "he himself", "by herself."
- **Jer 3:11** — "The backsliding Israel hath justified herself more than treacherous Judah"
- **Amos 6:8** — "The Lord GOD hath sworn by himself, saith the LORD"

### 7. Corpse / Ritual Uncleanness
Contact with a dead nephesh conveys defilement — nephesh can refer to a dead body.
- **Num 6:6** — "he shall come at no dead body" (nephesh meth — lit. a dead soul)
- **Hag 2:13** — "If one that is unclean by a dead body touch any of these, shall it be unclean?"
