# WG: Superabundance (περισσός Word Family)

## Overview
The superabundance concept in Greek Scripture is expressed through the περισσός (perissos) word family — a tightly knit group of cognates from the root περί (peri, "around/beyond"), all conveying the idea of surplus, excess, or going beyond what is expected. The related adjective πλείων (pleion) overlaps in translation ("more") but comes from a different root and emphasizes numerical quantity rather than qualitative surplus.

## Roles in the Concept

- **Quality:** perissos (G4053) — the adjectival description: something is superabundant or exceeding
- **Action:** perisseuo (G4052) — the verbal process: to overflow, to have more than enough
- **State:** perisseia (G4050) — the abstract condition of surplus
- **Result:** perisseuma (G4051) — the concrete surplus itself (fragments left over, overflow of the heart)
- **Manner (comparative):** perissoteros (G4056) — acting in a more superabundant way
- **Manner (positive):** perissos adv (G4057) — acting exceedingly, beyond measure
- **Quantity contrast:** pleion (G4119) — more in number (overlaps in KJV "more" but lacks the beyond-the-norm connotation)

## Key Passages Where Multiple Group Words Appear Together

- **2 Cor 8:2** — perisseia (G4050) + perisseuo (G4052): "the abundance of their joy and their deep poverty abounded unto the riches of their liberality"
- **2 Cor 4:15** — perisseuo (G4052) + pleion (G4119): "that the abundant grace might through the thanksgiving of many redound to the glory of God"

## Semantic Distinction: perissos vs. pleion

Both are translated "more" in KJV, creating easy confusion. The distinction:
- **perissos** = qualitative surplus, beyond what is expected or necessary ("more than a prophet," "what do ye more than others?")
- **pleion** = quantitative comparison, greater in amount or number ("more than twelve legions of angels," "more than five hundred brethren")
