### Semantic Distinctions
- **ἔρχομαι** — neutral movement; the most common NT verb; spans all categories from physical travel to the Second Coming
- **ἥκω** — resultative/stative: emphasizes that the arrival is complete and the person is now present; implies the period of expectation is over
- **παρουσία** — official/royal arrival; used predominantly for Christ's Second Advent as a technical theological term; also used of Paul's personal presence vs. his letters
- **ἐπέρχομαι** — directional compound: "come upon" (from ἐπί + ἔρχομαι); used of the Spirit (Luke 1:35, Acts 1:8), of wrath (Eph 2:7), of tribulation
- **παραγίνομαι** — formal appearance on the scene; preferred for official figures making their debut: John the Baptist (Matt 3:1), Jesus (Matt 3:13), Paul (Acts 9:26)

## Roles in the Concept

- **General action:** ἔρχομαι — the unmarked verb covering all types of coming and going
- **Achieved presence:** ἥκω — "I have come / I am here"; completion and presence foregrounded
- **Official event:** παρουσία — the arrival as an occasion (esp. royal/divine); the Parousia
- **Coming upon:** ἐπέρχομαι — descending or supervening arrival (Spirit, wrath, trouble)
- **Making an appearance:** παραγίνομαι — formal public debut on the scene of history

## Key Passages Where Multiple Group Words Appear

- **Luke 13:35** — "Ye shall not see me, until the time come when ye shall say, Blessed is he that cometh [ἔρχομαι] in the name of the Lord" — Jesus quotes Ps 118:26 and ties it to the eschatological coming
- **Matt 24:37–39** — The coming [παρουσία] of the Son of Man...until the flood came [ἔρχομαι] — both terms in eschatological discourse
- **Acts 1:11** — "this same Jesus...shall so come [ἔρχομαι] in like manner" — érchomai used for the Second Coming
- **Heb 10:37** — "he that shall come [ἔρχομαι] will come, and will not tarry" — quoting LXX; emphasizes the certainty of the arrival
