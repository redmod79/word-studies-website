## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same across testaments:**
- The core meaning — smoke from fire — is identical in Hebrew, LXX, and NT
- The divine presence / theophany association (Sinai → Isa 6 → Rev 15:8) carries through
- The judgment pairing of fire and smoke (2 Sam 22:9; Ps 18:8 → Rev 9:17-18; Acts 2:19)
- The ascending motion: smoke rising upward as sign, witness, or worship (Lev 1:9 → Rev 8:4; 14:11)

**What shifts:**
- OT ʻâshân includes poetic and proverbial uses — "as smoke to the eyes" (Prov 10:26); "as smoke is driven away" (Ps 68:2) — NT kapnós has no such figurative uses; it is strictly literal
- OT usage spreads across historical, poetic, and prophetic books (24 verses, multiple books); NT kapnós concentrates almost entirely in Revelation (10 of 11 uses), showing its role has narrowed to eschatological discourse
- The eternal-smoke motif (Isa 34:10) present in seed form in the OT becomes explicit and repeated in Revelation (Rev 14:11; 19:3)
