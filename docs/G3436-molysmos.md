## Lexicon Definition

**Strong's:** From G3435; a stain, i.e. (figuratively) immorality — filthiness.

**BLB Outline of Biblical Usage:** Referenced in Jer 1; 23:15 (LXX) and 2 Cor 7:1.

The word denotes the stain or contamination itself — not the act of defiling (which is the verb molynō G3435) but the resulting filthiness, the residue of defilement that clings to a person.

### 2. LXX Background: Prophetic Pollution
The LXX uses μολυσμός in prophetic contexts of corruption and contamination:
- **Jer 23:15 (LXX)** — moral corruption proceeding from the false prophets of Jerusalem
- **1 Esdras 8:80 (LXX)** — the pollution of the land by the peoples' practices

This LXX background shows molysmos as prophetic vocabulary for the contamination that comes from associating with pagan practices — the same context as 2 Cor 6:14–7:1.
