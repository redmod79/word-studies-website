## Lexicon Definition

A primitive root; to test (especially metals); generally and figuratively, to investigate: — examine, prove, tempt, try (trial).

## Semantic Categories

### 1. God Testing the Human Heart
The dominant use: YHWH as subject, the heart (leb) and reins (kilyah) as object. God's testing penetrates inward character and motive.
- **Psa 7:9** — "for the righteous God trieth the hearts and reins"
- **Psa 11:5** — "The LORD trieth the righteous: but the wicked and him that loveth violence his soul hateth"
- **Jer 11:20** — "O LORD of hosts, that judgest righteously, that triest the reins and the heart"
- **Jer 17:10** — "I the LORD search the heart, I try the reins"
- **1 Chr 29:17** — "I know also, my God, that thou triest the heart, and hast pleasure in uprightness"

### 2. The Refining/Metallurgical Test
Testing compared to the assaying of metals — silver and gold refined by fire. Overlaps heavily with tsaraph (H6884).
- **Psa 66:10** — "For thou, O God, hast proved us: thou hast tried us, as silver is tried"
- **Zec 13:9** — "will refine them as silver is refined, and will try them as gold is tried"
- **Pro 17:3** — "The fining pot is for silver, and the furnace for gold: but the LORD trieth the hearts"
- **Job 23:10** — "when he hath tried me, I shall come forth as gold"

### 3. The Petition for Personal Examination
The psalmist invites God to examine — a posture of transparency and integrity.
- **Psa 26:2** — "Examine me, O LORD, and prove me; try my reins and my heart"
- **Psa 139:23** — "Search me, O God, and know my heart: try me, and know my thoughts"
- **Psa 17:3** — "Thou hast proved mine heart; thou hast visited me in the night; thou hast tried me, and shalt find nothing"

### 4. Commissioned Testing of Others
A human agent appointed to test people or claims.
- **Jer 6:27** — "I have set thee for a tower and a fortress among my people, that thou mayest know and try their way"
- **Jer 9:7** — "Behold, I will melt them, and try them; for how shall I do for the daughter of my people?"

### 5. Proving Claims or Persons
Testing to verify truthfulness or character — the earliest use (Joseph testing his brothers).
- **Gen 42:15** — "Hereby ye shall be proved: By the life of Pharaoh ye shall not go forth hence"
- **Gen 42:16** — "let him fetch your brother, and ye shall be kept in prison, that your words may be proved"
- **Mal 3:10** — "prove me now herewith, saith the LORD of hosts"

### 6. Human Faculties as Testing Instruments
The ear and discernment as natural instruments of evaluation.
- **Job 12:11** — "Doth not the ear try words? and the mouth taste his meat?"
- **Job 34:3** — "For the ear trieth words, as the mouth tasteth meat"

### Synonyms
- **H5254 נָסָה (nasah)** — to test, attempt, try (34v). More experiential — testing by putting through an ordeal or experience. God testing Abraham (Gen 22:1), Israel testing God (Psa 78:18, 95:9). Broader range including "attempt/venture."
- **H6884 צָרַף (tsaraph)** — to refine, smelt, test (29v). Specifically metallurgical — fusing and purifying metals. When paired with bachan, tsaraph supplies the physical process while bachan names the evaluative purpose.
- **H2713 חָקַר (chaqar)** — to search out, examine, investigate (26v). Intellectual probing, thorough investigation. More about discovering hidden facts than evaluating quality.
