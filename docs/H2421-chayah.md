## Lexicon Definition

A primitive root; to live, whether literally or figuratively; causatively, to revive:— keep (leave, make) alive, certainly, give (promise) life, (let, suffer to) live, nourish up, preserve (alive), quicken, recover, repair, restore (to life), revive, (God) save (alive, life, lives), surely, be whole.

**Patterns:** Highest density in Genesis (antediluvian genealogies "and X lived N years") and Ezekiel (prophetic "live/die" formulae in chs. 3, 18, 33). Habakkuk's density is an artifact of small book size but includes the theologically weighty 2:4 ("the just shall live by his faith"). Wisdom literature (Job, Ecclesiastes, Proverbs) uses it reflectively; Psalms concentrates the "quicken me" petitions (Ps 119). Absent or rare in cultic literature (Leviticus, Chronicles).

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Simple Biological Life / Lifespan (Qal)
- **Gen 5:3** — "Adam lived an hundred and thirty years, and begat a son"
- **Gen 5:5** — "all the days that Adam lived were nine hundred and thirty years"
- **Gen 25:7** — "the days of the years of Abraham's life which he lived"
- **2 Chr 25:25** — "Amaziah... lived after the death of Joash... fifteen years"

### 2. Remain Alive / Not Die (Qal, opposed to muwth H4191)
- **Gen 42:2** — "buy for us... that we may live, and not die"
- **Num 4:19** — "that they may live, and not die, when they approach unto the most holy things"
- **Deut 4:33** — "hear the voice of God speaking out of the midst of the fire... and live"
- **Ezek 18:21** — "if the wicked will turn from all his sins... he shall surely live, he shall not die"

### 3. Revive / Recover from Sickness or Near-Death (Qal / Piel / Hiphil)
- **Gen 45:27** — "the spirit of Jacob their father revived"
- **Judg 15:19** — "he had drunk, his spirit came again, and he revived"
- **2 Kgs 1:2** — "enquire... whether I shall recover of this disease"
- **2 Kgs 8:8–14** — Ben-hadad / Hazael dialogue on recovery from sickness
- **Isa 38:9** — Hezekiah's writing after he "had been sick, and was recovered"

### 4. Resurrection / Restoration to Life (Qal / Piel — the strong theological sense)
- **1 Kgs 17:22** — "the soul of the child came into him again, and he revived"
- **2 Kgs 13:21** — "the man... revived, and stood up on his feet"
- **Job 14:14** — "if a man die, shall he live again?"
- **Isa 26:19** — "Thy dead men shall live, together with my dead body shall they arise"
- **Ezek 37:3, 5, 6, 9, 10, 14** — the dry-bones vision: "shall these bones live?... I will cause breath to enter into you, and ye shall live"
- **Hos 6:2** — "after two days will he revive us: in the third day he will raise us up, and we shall live"

### 5. Preserve Alive / Save from Death (Piel / Hiphil — causative)
- **Gen 12:12** — "they will kill me, but they will save thee alive"
- **Gen 45:7** — "to save your lives by a great deliverance"
- **Gen 50:20** — "to save much people alive"
- **Exod 1:17–22** — midwives "saved the men children alive"
- **Josh 2:13; 6:25** — Rahab "saved alive"
- **1 Sam 2:6** — "The LORD killeth, and maketh alive: he bringeth down to the grave, and bringeth up"

### 6. Torah Life-Formula: Obey and Live (Qal imperfect, Deuteronomic)
- **Lev 18:5** — "which if a man do, he shall live in them" (quoted Ezek 20:11, 13, 21; Neh 9:29; Gal 3:12; Rom 10:5)
- **Deut 4:1** — "hearken... unto the statutes... that ye may live"
- **Deut 8:3** — "by every word that proceedeth out of the mouth of the LORD doth man live"
- **Deut 30:16, 19** — "choose life, that both thou and thy seed may live"
- **Amos 5:4, 6, 14** — "Seek ye me, and ye shall live"
- **Prov 4:4; 7:2; 9:6** — wisdom-parenesis "keep my commandments, and live"
- **Hab 2:4** — "the just shall live by his faith"

### 7. Liturgical "Quicken Me" — Psalm Petitions (Piel)
- **Ps 80:18** — "quicken us, and we will call upon thy name"
- **Ps 119:25, 37, 40, 88, 93, 107, 149, 154, 156, 159** — repeated petition "quicken me" (eleven times in Ps 119 alone)
- **Ps 143:11** — "quicken me, O LORD, for thy name's sake"
- **Hab 3:2** — "O LORD, revive thy work in the midst of the years"

### 8. Royal Acclamation "Let the King Live!" (Qal jussive)
- **1 Sam 10:24** — "God save the king" (yᵉḥî hammelek — "may the king live")
- **2 Sam 16:16; 1 Kgs 1:25, 31, 34, 39** — "God save king Solomon / David / Adonijah"
- **2 Kgs 11:12; Neh 2:3** — "Let the king live for ever"
