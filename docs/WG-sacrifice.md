## Roles in the Concept

- **Total dedication:** olah (עֹלָה) — entirely consumed by fire, nothing returned to offerer; represents complete surrender to God
- **Fellowship/thanksgiving:** shelem (שֶׁלֶם) — offerer eats a portion, the only offering involving a communal meal; represents restored relationship and gratitude
- **General sacrifice:** zebach (זֶבַח) — the slaughter-act itself; often paired with shelem in the construct *zebach shelamim*
- **Presentation/approach:** qorban (קׇרְבָּן) — generic term for anything "brought near" to God; an umbrella category
- **Grain tribute:** minchah (מִנְחָה) — non-blood offering of flour, oil, and frankincense; the only bloodless offering type
- **Purification from defilement:** chattaah (חַטָּאָה) — addresses sin-contamination and ritual impurity; blood applied to purify sacred spaces
- **Restitution for violation:** asham (אָשָׁם) — addresses specific trespass with measurable damage; uniquely requires repayment plus 20% surcharge
