## Morphological Note

All three occurrences appear in the dative singular neuter form μεσουρανήματι (mesouranēmati), always preceded by ἐν ("in") — yielding the prepositional phrase "in the midst of heaven" (ἐν μεσουρανήματι).

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Angelic Proclamation from the Zenith
- **Rev 8:13** — "And I beheld, and heard an angel flying through the midst of heaven, saying with a loud voice, Woe, woe, woe, to the inhabiters of the earth by reason of the other voices of the trumpet of the three angels, which are yet to sound!"
- **Rev 14:6** — "And I saw another angel fly in the midst of heaven, having the everlasting gospel to preach unto them that dwell on the earth, and to every nation, and kindred, and tongue, and people"

Both passages use mesouranema to position an angel at the zenith — the point of maximum visibility and audibility — for a universal proclamation. The flying motion through this space emphasizes ongoing, active broadcast.

### 2. Cosmic Gathering Point
- **Rev 19:17** — "And I saw an angel standing in the sun; and he cried with a loud voice, saying to all the fowls that fly in the midst of heaven, Come and gather yourselves together unto the supper of the great God"

Here the birds (ὄρνεον, orneon) occupy the mesouranema. The zenith position again conveys universal scope — every flying creature across the whole sky is addressed.
