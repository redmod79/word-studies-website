## Lexicon Definition

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Agricultural / Literal Seed
Used in parables and physical contexts for plant seed that is sown or grows.

- **Mat 13:24** — "The kingdom of heaven is likened unto a man which sowed good seed in his field"
- **Mat 13:32** — "Which indeed is the least of all seeds: but when it is grown, it is the greatest among herbs"
- **Mark 4:31** — "like a grain of mustard seed, which, when it is sown in the earth, is less than all the seeds that be in the earth"
- **1 Cor 15:38** — "God giveth it a body as it hath pleased him, and to every seed his own body"

### 2. Offspring / Progeny (Levirate and Physical)
Refers to biological children; especially in levirate-marriage contexts (Deut 25 law applied).

- **Mat 22:24–25** — "If a man die, having no children, his brother shall marry his wife, and raise up seed unto his brother"
- **Mark 12:20–22** — "the first took a wife, and dying left no seed... the second... died, neither left he any seed"
- **Heb 11:11** — "Sara herself received strength to conceive seed, and was delivered of a child"

### 3. Seed of Abraham (Covenant Lineage)
The patriarchal promise to Abraham that his descendants would inherit the land and bless the nations. Contested in John 8 between ethnic and spiritual senses.

- **Luke 1:55** — "As he spake to our fathers, to Abraham, and to his seed for ever"
- **John 8:33, 37** — "We be Abraham's seed... I know that ye are Abraham's seed; but ye seek to kill me"
- **Acts 3:25** — "unto Abraham, saying, And in thy seed shall all the kindreds of the earth be blessed"
- **Rom 9:7–8** — "In Isaac shall thy seed be called... the children of the promise are counted for the seed"

### 4. Messianic Seed of David
Identifies Jesus as fulfilling the promise of a royal line from David (2 Sam 7:12 trajectory).

- **John 7:42** — "Christ cometh of the seed of David, and out of the town of Bethlehem"
- **Rom 1:3** — "his Son Jesus Christ our Lord, which was made of the seed of David according to the flesh"
- **Acts 13:23** — "Of this man's seed hath God according to his promise raised unto Israel a Saviour, Jesus"
- **2 Tim 2:8** — "Remember that Jesus Christ of the seed of David was raised from the dead"

### 5. The Singular Seed as Christ (Gal 3)
Paul's exegetical argument that σπέρμα in Gen 12/17 is grammatically singular, pointing to Christ as the ultimate heir of the Abrahamic promise.

- **Gal 3:16** — "He saith not, And to seeds, as of many; but as of one, And to thy seed, which is Christ"
- **Gal 3:19** — "It was added because of transgressions, till the seed should come to whom the promise was made"
- **Gal 3:29** — "And if ye be Christ's, then are ye Abraham's seed, and heirs according to the promise"

### 6. Spiritual / Divine Seed
Metaphorical: God's word or nature implanted in believers; also the woman's seed in apocalyptic conflict.

- **1 John 3:9** — "Whosoever is born of God doth not commit sin; for his seed remaineth in him: and he cannot sin, because he is born of God"
- **Rev 12:17** — "the dragon was wroth with the woman, and went to make war with the remnant of her seed, which keep the commandments of God"
- **2 Cor 9:10** — "he that ministereth seed to the sower both minister bread for your food, and multiply your seed sown"
