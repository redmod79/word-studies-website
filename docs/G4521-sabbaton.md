## Lexicon Definition

Of Hebrew origin (H7676); the Sabbath (i.e. Shabbath), or day of weekly repose from secular avocations (also the observance or institution itself); by extension, a se'nnight, i.e. the interval between two Sabbaths; likewise the plural in all the above applications:—sabbath (day), week.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. The Weekly Day of Rest — Institution and Commandment
The sabbath as a divinely ordained institution to be observed.

- **Luke 23:56** — "they rested the sabbath day according to the commandment"
- **Acts 15:21** — "Moses of old time hath in every city them that preach him, being read in the synagogues every sabbath day"
- **Col 2:16** — "Let no man therefore judge you... in respect of an holyday, or of the new moon, or of the sabbath days"

### 2. Jesus' Authority Over the Sabbath — "Lord of the Sabbath"
Controversy about permitted actions; Jesus claims dominion over the day.

- **Mark 2:27–28** — "The sabbath was made for man, and not man for the sabbath: Therefore the Son of man is Lord also of the sabbath"
- **Mat 12:2** — "thy disciples do that which is not lawful to do upon the sabbath day"
- **Mat 12:8** — "the Son of man is Lord even of the sabbath day"

### 3. Sabbath Healing — Mercy as Sabbath Work
Jesus heals on the sabbath, reframing the day around restoration rather than restriction.

- **Luke 13:14–16** — synagogue ruler protests; Jesus responds: "ought not this woman, being a daughter of Abraham... be loosed from this bond on the sabbath day?"
- **Luke 14:3** — "Is it lawful to heal on the sabbath day?"
- **John 9:14, 16** — "it was the sabbath day when Jesus made the clay"; Pharisees: "This man is not of God, because he keepeth not the sabbath day"

### 4. Sabbath in the Synagogue — Regular Teaching Practice
Sabbath as the weekly venue for Torah reading, teaching, and prayer.

- **Mark 1:21** — "straightway on the sabbath day he entered into the synagogue, and taught"
- **Acts 13:14** — "went into the synagogue on the sabbath day and sat down"
- **Acts 18:4** — "he reasoned in the synagogue every sabbath, and persuaded the Jews and the Greeks"

### 5. "First Day of the Week" — σάββατον as a Time Unit
In the plural genitive (σαββάτων), used as a unit to mark the first day of a new seven-day period.

- **Mat 28:1** — "toward the first [day] of the week (μίαν σαββάτων), came Mary Magdalene"
- **Acts 20:7** — "upon the first [day] of the week, when the disciples came together to break bread"
- **1 Cor 16:2** — "Upon the first [day] of the week let every one of you lay by him in store"
