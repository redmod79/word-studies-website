## Relationships

### Word Families
- **therap- family:** therapeuo (G2323, verb), therapeia (G2322, noun), therapon (G2324, noun) — all from root meaning "to attend to, serve"
- **ia- family:** iaomai (G2390, verb), iatros (G2395, noun), iama (G2386, noun), iasis (G2392, noun) — all from root meaning "to cure"
- **kathar- family:** katharizo (G2511) — related by function (cleansing as healing-adjacent action)

Despite being near-synonyms, therapeuo (G2323) and iaomai (G2390) never appear in the same verse — the Gospel writers use one or the other for any given healing narrative, never both together.

## Roles in the Concept

- **Primary action (serve/heal):** therapeuo (G2323) — the dominant healing verb in the Synoptics; retains undertone of service/attendance
- **Primary action (cure):** iaomai (G2390) — focuses on the cure itself; occasionally metaphorical
- **Related action (cleanse):** katharizo (G2511) — purification, especially leprosy; paired with therapeuo in commissioning passages
- **Agent:** iatros (G2395) — the physician; used proverbially ("Physician, heal thyself")
- **Activity/household:** therapeia (G2322) — double sense: household servants (Mat 24:45, Luk 12:42) and healing activity (Luk 9:11, Rev 22:2)
- **Gift:** iama (G2386) — spiritual gift of healing (1 Cor 12:9, 28, 30)
- **Result:** iasis (G2392) — the cure as outcome (Luk 13:32, Act 4:22, 30)
- **Root servant:** therapon (G2324) — the original "attendant" meaning; used only of Moses (Heb 3:5)

## Key Passages Where Multiple Group Words Appear Together

- **Luk 4:23** — therapeuo + iatros: "Ye will surely say unto me this proverb, Physician, heal thyself"
- **Luk 9:11** — iaomai + therapeia: "healed them that had need of healing"
- **Mat 10:8** — therapeuo + katharizo (adjacent): "Heal the sick, cleanse the lepers"
- **Rev 22:2** — therapeia: "the leaves of the tree were for the healing of the nations" (uses the noun form)
