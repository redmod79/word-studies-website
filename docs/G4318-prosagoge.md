## Lexicon Definition

From πρός (G4314) and ἄγω (G71), compare προσάγω (G4317); admission, access. The -αγωγή suffix indicates the act or means of leading/bringing. In classical Greek the word could mean "approach," "introduction" (to a person of rank), or the right of audience with a king. All three NT occurrences use it for believers' access to God or to divine grace.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Access to Grace Through Christ
- **Rom 5:2** — "By whom also we have access by faith into this grace wherein we stand, and rejoice in hope of the glory of God."

Christ is the agent ("by whom"), faith is the means ("by faith"), and grace is the destination ("into this grace"). Access here is the result of justification (5:1) — the standing position believers occupy.

### 2. Access to the Father Through Christ and the Spirit
- **Eph 2:18** — "For through him we both have access by one Spirit unto the Father."

Trinitarian structure: through Christ, by the Spirit, unto the Father. "Both" (ἀμφότεροι, G297) = Jews and Gentiles. Access collapses the dividing wall of partition (2:14) — formerly excluded Gentiles now share the same approach to God.

### 3. Access with Boldness and Confidence
- **Eph 3:12** — "In whom we have boldness and access with confidence by the faith of him."

Paired with παρρησία (parrhēsia, G3954 "boldness/freedom of speech") and πεποίθησις (pepoithēsis, G4006 "confidence/trust"). Access here is not tentative or fearful but characterized by confident openness — the opposite of the barrier, distance, or dread that would characterize an unauthorized approach.

## Hebrew Source (via LXX)

No Hebrew sources found for G4318 in the LXX alignment data. The word does not appear in the LXX. Its cognate verb G4317 prosagō maps primarily to H7126 קָרַב (qārab) "to approach, bring near" (82 co-occurrences, PMI 5.81) — the Levitical approach verb for presenting offerings before God. This cultic background informs the NT noun: prosagōgē is the right of approach that the Levitical system restricted and Christ's work opened.
