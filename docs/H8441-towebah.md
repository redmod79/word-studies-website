## Lexicon Definition

Feminine active participle of H8581 taʻab. Properly "something disgusting (morally)," i.e. (as noun) an abhorrence; especially idolatry or (concretely) an idol — "abominable (custom, thing), abomination." The term carries a strong evaluative sense: what is intrinsically loathsome before God (or, by extension, before a given culture, Gen 43:32; 46:34; Exo 8:26). In the prophets and wisdom literature it becomes a covenantal verdict — the thing YHWH hates (Deut 12:31; Pro 6:16).

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Cultural/Ritual Taboo (pre-covenantal sense)
The earliest uses are sociological, not yet theological — a thing a given people finds loathsome.
- **Gen 43:32** — eating with Hebrews was "an abomination unto the Egyptians"
- **Gen 46:34** — "every shepherd is an abomination unto the Egyptians"
- **Exo 8:26** — Israel would "sacrifice the abomination of the Egyptians" (Egyptian sacred animals)

### 2. Idolatry and the Worship of Foreign Gods
The dominant prophetic and Deuteronomic usage: idols, idolatrous practices, and the nations' cultic systems.
- **Deut 7:25–26** — graven images of their gods called "an abomination"
- **Deut 12:31** — "every abomination to the LORD, which he hateth, have they done unto their gods"
- **2 Ki 16:3; 21:2** — kings walked "after the abominations of the heathen"
- **Eze 16:36** — "all the idols of thy abominations"
- **Jer 32:35** — building "high places of Baal... to cause their sons... to pass through the fire"

### 3. Sexual and Social Transgressions
In the Holiness Code and Ezekiel, forbidden sexual unions are labeled towebah.
- **Lev 18:22** — male same-sex intercourse: "it is abomination"
- **Lev 18:26–30** — the whole catalog of forbidden unions: "these abominations"
- **Lev 20:13** — repetition with capital sanction
- **Deut 22:5** — cross-dressing "is abomination unto the LORD"
- **Deut 24:4** — remarriage after defilement "is abomination before the LORD"
- **Eze 22:11** — adultery "committed abomination with his neighbour's wife"

### 4. Dietary and Cultic Uncleanness
- **Deut 14:3** — "thou shalt not eat any abominable thing" (heading the clean/unclean list)
- **Lev 18:29; Deut 17:1** — blemished sacrifices are abomination
- **Isa 1:13** — "incense is an abomination unto me" when divorced from righteousness
- **Eze 22:2; 44:6–7** — uncircumcised in sanctuary

### 5. Commercial and Judicial Dishonesty (Proverbs)
A distinctive wisdom cluster: God's hatred of false weights and perverse speech.
- **Pro 11:1** — "A false balance is abomination to the LORD"
- **Pro 12:22** — "Lying lips are abomination to the LORD"
- **Pro 17:15** — "He that justifieth the wicked... both are abomination to the LORD"
- **Pro 20:10, 23** — "Divers weights... are alike abomination to the LORD"

### 6. Character and Disposition — "Abomination to the LORD"
The Proverbs formula turns towebah inward: it is persons and dispositions, not merely acts.
- **Pro 3:32** — "the froward is abomination to the LORD"
- **Pro 6:16** — "six things doth the LORD hate: yea, seven are an abomination unto him"
- **Pro 11:20** — "they that are of a froward heart are abomination"
- **Pro 15:8–9, 26; 16:5** — sacrifice, way, thoughts, and proud heart of the wicked
- **Pro 28:9** — prayer of one who turns from the law is "abomination"

### 7. Covenant Apostasy (Ezekiel's Programmatic Usage)
Ezekiel uses towebah as the comprehensive indictment-term for Judah's covenant breach, often in catalog form.
- **Eze 5:9, 11** — unparalleled judgment "because of all thine abominations"
- **Eze 8:6, 9, 13, 15, 17** — the escalating temple-tour of "greater abominations"
- **Eze 16:2** — "cause Jerusalem to know her abominations"
- **Eze 33:26, 29** — abomination as covenant-breach warranting exile
- **Eze 44:6–7, 13** — priestly abominations in the restored temple vision

### LXX/Greek Overlap Confusion
Through the LXX, towebah and shiqquwts are both routinely translated by G946 βδέλυγμα, so NT readers of "abomination" (e.g., Matt 24:15) stand at a point where both Hebrew streams converge. Towebah carries the broader moral / covenantal sense; shiqquwts is the sharper cult-object sense.
