## Lexicon Definition

Compound of G473 (ἀντί, "opposite/instead of") and G5547 (Χριστός, "Christ/Messiah"). An opponent of the Messiah — one who stands against or in place of Christ. The ἀντί prefix carries dual force: both *opposition* to Christ and *substitution* for Christ.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. The Expected Eschatological Figure
- **1 John 2:18a** — "ye have heard that antichrist shall come" — a singular figure associated with "the last time" (ἐσχάτη ὥρα); something the audience had been taught to expect
- **1 John 4:3** — "this is that spirit of antichrist, whereof ye have heard that it should come" — repeats the tradition of an expected coming figure

### 2. Present-Tense Manifestations (Plural)
- **1 John 2:18b** — "even now are there many antichrists; whereby we know that it is the last time" — multiple current persons embody the antichrist principle; their appearance signals eschatological timing
- **2 John 1:7** — "many deceivers are entered into the world... This is a deceiver and an antichrist" — the label applies to contemporaries who deny the incarnation

### 3. Christological Denial as Defining Mark
- **1 John 2:22** — "Who is a liar but he that denieth that Jesus is the Christ? He is antichrist, that denieth the Father and the Son" — the specific criterion: denying Jesus as Messiah and denying the Father-Son relationship
- **1 John 4:3** — "every spirit that confesseth not that Jesus Christ is come in the flesh... this is that spirit of antichrist" — denying the incarnation is the diagnostic marker
