# WG: Destroy / Demolish (Greek)

## Overview

Greek has a rich cluster of verbs for destruction, each occupying a distinct niche: taking *down* (kathairéō / kathaíresis), full ruin (apóllymi), corruption/decay (diaphtheírō), making *desolate* (erēmóō), and utter extirpation (exolothreúō). The member words are highly synonymous by gloss but almost never co-occur — each author/context selects one verb and rarely reaches for a sibling. Together they map the semantic space of Louw-Nida domain 20 (Violence, Harm, Destroy, Kill) and subdomain 20.003 (Destroy).

## Roles in the Concept

- **Motion + Removal:** kathairéō / kathaíresis — *take down* (a body, a ruler, a stronghold, a structure). The act is visible and spatial.
- **Total Loss:** apóllymi — the broad workhorse for "destroy, perish, lose." Covers spiritual destruction, physical ruin, and the lost/perishing state.
- **Decay / Corruption:** diaphtheírō — destruction from within; moral corruption; rotting. Used of moths (Luk 12:33) and of corrupt minds.
- **Desolation:** erēmóō — a place/kingdom left waste; the ἐρήμωσις abomination word family. Eschatological and geographic.
- **Extirpation:** exolothreúō — being cut off from a people, LXX-style (Acts 3:23 quoting Deut 18:19).

## Key Passages Where Multiple Group Words Appear Together

No single NT verse contains two members of this group. The closest proximity:

- **2Co 10:4–5** — Paul uses kathaíresis (G2506) in v.4 ("weapons... mighty through God to the pulling down of strong holds") and kathairéō (G2507) in v.5 ("Casting down imaginations"). Adjacent verses, cognate forms, same argument.
- **Act 13:19 / Act 13:29** — Both use kathairéō in the same sermon (conquest destruction + taking Christ down from the tree), but no other cluster members.
