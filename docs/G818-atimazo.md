## Semantic Categories

### 1. Shameful Treatment of God's Messengers
- **Mark 12:4** — "at him they cast stones, and wounded him in the head, and sent him away shamefully handled" — vineyard tenants dishonor the owner's servant
- **Luke 20:11** — "they beat him also, and entreated him shamefully, and sent him away empty" — parallel parable of the wicked husbandmen

### 2. Dishonoring God Directly
- **John 8:49** — "I honour my Father, and ye do dishonour me" — Jesus contrasts his honoring of the Father with the crowd's dishonoring of him
- **Romans 2:23** — "through breaking the law dishonourest thou God?" — law-breaking as active dishonor toward the Lawgiver

### 3. Suffering Dishonor for Christ's Name
- **Acts 5:41** — "rejoicing that they were counted worthy to suffer shame for his name" — apostles after being beaten by the Sanhedrin; passive voice (ἀτιμασθῆναι) — they received dishonor

### 4. Bodily Degradation as Divine Judgment
- **Romans 1:24** — "God also gave them up to uncleanness through the lusts of their own hearts, to dishonour their own bodies between themselves" — middle/passive infinitive; self-inflicted dishonoring of the body as consequence of idolatry

### 5. Social Contempt
- **James 2:6** — "ye have despised the poor" — the church actively dishonors those whom God has chosen
