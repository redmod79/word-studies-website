## Semantic Categories

### 1. Physical Enclosure / Catching
- **Luke 5:6** — "And when they had this done, they inclosed [syneklēsan] a great multitude of fishes: and their net brake."

The literal sense: to shut together on all sides, to enclose within a boundary. The net surrounded and contained the fish, leaving no way of escape.

### 2. Divine Consignment Under a Condition
- **Romans 11:32** — "For God hath concluded [synekleisen] them all in unbelief, that he might have mercy upon all."
- **Galatians 3:22** — "But the scripture hath concluded [synekleisen] all under sin, that the promise by faith of Jesus Christ might be given to them that believe."

The figurative sense: God or Scripture has shut all humanity together under a common condition (sin, unbelief) so that no one can escape by their own merit. The purpose clause (hina) in both verses makes the enclosure purposive — it serves to channel all toward mercy and faith.

### 3. Confinement Under Law Pending Faith
- **Galatians 3:23** — "But before faith came, we were kept under the law, shut up [synkleiomenoi] unto the faith which should afterwards be revealed."

The passive participle describes the ongoing state of being hemmed in. The law functioned as a boundary keeping Israel confined until the era of faith arrived. The present tense participle emphasizes the continuous, sustained nature of this confinement during the pre-faith era.
