# Grammar Analysis: John 2:13–22 (The Temple Cleansing)

## Overview

The second pericope of John 2, placed by the evangelist as the immediate literary sequel to the Cana sign (2:1–11). Where Cana moved through **aorist-imperative → aorist-indicative → perfect-participle** (command → punctiliar execution → abiding-result), the temple pericope is built on a different architecture:

- **Aorist indicatives** (vv.13–16) — a mainline chain of punctiliar actions (ἀνέβη, εὗρεν, ἐξέβαλεν, ἐξέχεεν, ἀνέτρεψεν, εἶπεν)
- **Present participles in the accusative** (v.14) — a present-aspect *tableau* of what Jesus walks into (τοὺς πωλοῦντας, καθημένους)
- **Aorist participle ποιήσας** (v.15) — coincident/antecedent "having-made (a whip)"
- **Aorist imperative + present imperative** contrast (v.16) — Ἄρατε (punctiliar) vs. μὴ ποιεῖτε (cease/do-not-continue)
- **Perfect passive periphrastic** γεγραμμένον ἐστίν (v.17) — "it stands written"
- **Aorist imperative + future indicative** (v.19) — Λύσατε…ἐγερῶ, a conditional imperative answered by a prophetic future
- **Aorist passive** οἰκοδομήθη (v.20) and **aorist passive** ἠγέρθη (v.22) — framing the two temples
- **Imperfect ἔλεγεν** (v.21) — narrator's interpretive aside in durative aspect
- **Inclusio of μιμνῄσκομαι + aorist passive** — v.17 ἐμνήσθησαν (disciples remember *during* the event) and v.22 ἐμνήσθησαν (disciples remember *after* the resurrection) bracket the pericope

Two key content words drive the theological grammar: **ναός** (G3485) vs. **ἱερόν** (G2411) — John uses both within a span of five verses with rigorous lexical discipline, and **ἐγείρω** (G1453) — appearing three times in vv.19–22 as Jesus' future active, the Jews' future active quotation, and the narrator's aorist passive.

## Text

### Verses 13–22 (Greek / KJV)

**v.13** — Καὶ ἐγγὺς ἦν τὸ πάσχα τῶν Ἰουδαίων, καὶ ἀνέβη εἰς Ἱεροσόλυμα ὁ Ἰησοῦς.
*And the Jews' passover was at hand, and Jesus went up to Jerusalem,*

**v.14** — καὶ εὗρεν ἐν τῷ ἱερῷ τοὺς πωλοῦντας βόας καὶ πρόβατα καὶ περιστερὰς καὶ τοὺς κερματιστὰς καθημένους,
*And found in the temple those that sold oxen and sheep and doves, and the changers of money sitting:*

**v.15** — καὶ ποιήσας φραγέλλιον ἐκ σχοινίων πάντας ἐξέβαλεν ἐκ τοῦ ἱεροῦ τά τε πρόβατα καὶ τοὺς βόας, καὶ τῶν κολλυβιστῶν ἐξέχεεν τὰ κέρματα καὶ τὰς τραπέζας ἀνέτρεψεν,
*And when he had made a scourge of small cords, he drove them all out of the temple, and the sheep, and the oxen; and poured out the changers' money, and overthrew the tables;*

**v.16** — καὶ τοῖς τὰς περιστερὰς πωλοῦσιν εἶπεν Ἄρατε ταῦτα ἐντεῦθεν, μὴ ποιεῖτε τὸν οἶκον τοῦ Πατρός μου οἶκον ἐμπορίου.
*And said unto them that sold doves, Take these things hence; make not my Father's house an house of merchandise.*

**v.17** — ἐμνήσθησαν οἱ μαθηταὶ αὐτοῦ ὅτι γεγραμμένον ἐστίν Ὁ ζῆλος τοῦ οἴκου σου καταφάγεταί με.
*And his disciples remembered that it was written, The zeal of thine house hath eaten me up.*

**v.18** — ἀπεκρίθησαν οὖν οἱ Ἰουδαῖοι καὶ εἶπαν αὐτῷ Τί σημεῖον δεικνύεις ἡμῖν, ὅτι ταῦτα ποιεῖς;
*Then answered the Jews and said unto him, What sign shewest thou unto us, seeing that thou doest these things?*

**v.19** — ἀπεκρίθη Ἰησοῦς καὶ εἶπεν αὐτοῖς Λύσατε τὸν ναὸν τοῦτον, καὶ ἐν τρισὶν ἡμέραις ἐγερῶ αὐτόν.
*Jesus answered and said unto them, Destroy this temple, and in three days I will raise it up.*

**v.20** — εἶπαν οὖν οἱ Ἰουδαῖοι Τεσσεράκοντα καὶ ἓξ ἔτεσιν οἰκοδομήθη ὁ ναὸς οὗτος, καὶ σὺ ἐν τρισὶν ἡμέραις ἐγερεῖς αὐτόν;
*Then said the Jews, Forty and six years was this temple in building, and wilt thou rear it up in three days?*

**v.21** — ἐκεῖνος δὲ ἔλεγεν περὶ τοῦ ναοῦ τοῦ σώματος αὐτοῦ.
*But he spake of the temple of his body.*

**v.22** — ὅτε οὖν ἠγέρθη ἐκ νεκρῶν, ἐμνήσθησαν οἱ μαθηταὶ αὐτοῦ ὅτι τοῦτο ἔλεγεν, καὶ ἐπίστευσαν τῇ γραφῇ καὶ τῷ λόγῳ ὃν εἶπεν ὁ Ἰησοῦς.
*When therefore he was risen from the dead, his disciples remembered that he had said this unto them; and they believed the scripture, and the word which Jesus had said.*

The aorist participle **ποιήσας** (Aor Act Ptcp Nom Sg M) is **antecedent/coincident** to the main aorist verb ἐξέβαλεν — "*having made* a whip, he drove [them] all out." Three parallel aorist indicatives (ἐξέβαλεν / ἐξέχεεν / ἀνέτρεψεν), each V-2AAI-3S or V-AAI-3S, form a perfective action-chain viewed as discrete completed events. The pronoun `πάντας` is masculine plural — grammatically applicable to **persons** (the sellers and money-changers), not to neuter πρόβατα or common-gender βόας; the epexegetical phrase `τά τε πρόβατα καὶ τοὺς βόας` then adds the animals as a further clarifying direct object ("*all [of them] — both the sheep and the oxen*"). Punctuation decisions in the Greek text can tilt this either way; cf. §3 below.

### v.16 — The Command (Aorist Imperative vs. Present Prohibition)

| Word | Lemma | Strong's | Parsing | Gloss |
|------|-------|----------|---------|-------|
| τοῖς τὰς περιστερὰς πωλοῦσιν | πωλέω | G4453 | **V-PAP-DPM** (articular Pres Act Ptcp) | to those selling the doves |
| εἶπεν | λέγω | G3004 | V-2AAI-3S | he said |
| Ἄρατε | αἴρω | G142 | **V-AAM-2P** (Aor Act Impv) | Take away! / Remove! |
| ταῦτα | οὗτος | G3778 | D-APN | these things |
| ἐντεῦθεν | ἐντεῦθεν | G1782 | ADV | from here |
| μή | μή | G3361 | PRT-N | not |
| ποιεῖτε | ποιέω | G4160 | **V-PAM-2P** (Pres Act Impv) | do not continue making |
| τὸν οἶκον | οἶκος | G3624 | N-ASM | the house |
| τοῦ Πατρός μου | πατήρ | G3962 | N-GSM + P-1GS | of my Father (possessive gen. + gen. 1s pronoun) |
| οἶκον ἐμπορίου | οἶκος / ἐμπόριον | G3624/G1712 | N-ASM + N-GSN | a house of merchandise (double-accusative: object + predicate) |

Classic aspectual contrast in the imperative mood: **Ἄρατε** (V-AAM-2P, punctiliar "do it once — remove these things") + **μὴ ποιεῖτε** (V-PAM-2P with μή, "stop making / do not keep making" — the standard force of a present prohibition addressed to ongoing activity). Taken together: one-shot clearance order + ongoing-activity cease-and-desist.

The construction `ποιεῖτε τὸν οἶκον…οἶκον ἐμπορίου` is a **double accusative of object-complement** — "do not make [the Father's house] [a house of merchandise]." The genitive `ἐμπορίου` (G1712, NT hapax — "merchandise / emporium") qualifies the predicate accusative. Note also the explicit divine-filiation claim embedded syntactically in `τοῦ Πατρός μου` — "**my** Father" (P-1GS), not "our Father"; the same relational grammar Jesus will use with intensified provocation at John 5:17–18.

Lexical note: here Jesus calls the temple `τὸν οἶκον τοῦ Πατρός μου` (G3624 οἶκος). He has not yet used the technical `ναός`. The shift from οἶκος (v.16) → ἱερόν (v.15) → ναός (vv.19, 20, 21) across the pericope is deliberate, culminating in the double-entendre of v.19 (§2).

### v.17 — Scripture Remembered (Perfect Passive Periphrastic)

| Word | Lemma | Strong's | Parsing | Gloss |
|------|-------|----------|---------|-------|
| ἐμνήσθησαν | μιμνῄσκω | G3403 | **V-API-3P** (Aor Pass Ind) | they remembered / were caused to remember |
| οἱ μαθηταί | μαθητής | G3101 | N-NPM | the disciples |
| αὐτοῦ | αὐτός | G846 | P-GSM | of him |
| ὅτι | ὅτι | G3754 | CONJ (recitative) | that [introducing written content] |
| γεγραμμένον | γράφω | G1125 | **V-RPP-NSN** (Perf Pass Ptcp Nom Sg N) | written |
| ἐστίν | εἰμί | G1510 | V-PAI-3S | is |
| ὁ ζῆλος τοῦ οἴκου σου | ζῆλος / οἶκος | G2205/G3624 | N-NSM / N-GSM + P-2GS | the zeal of your house (subjective gen.) |
| καταφάγεταί | κατεσθίω | G2719 | **V-FDI-3S** (Fut Mid/Dep Ind) | will consume / eat up |
| με | ἐγώ | G1473 | P-1AS | me |

The phrase **γεγραμμένον ἐστίν** is a **perfect passive periphrastic** (perfect passive participle + present indicative of εἰμί = periphrastic perfect passive indicative) — the Johannine variant of the more common single-word γέγραπται. Both encode the same aspectual force: **"it stands written" — completed writing-action with an abiding state of being-on-the-record**. See [participle-perfect-passive-state](../greek/participle-perfect-passive-state.md).

The quotation `Ὁ ζῆλος τοῦ οἴκου σου καταφάγεταί με` is from **LXX Psalm 68:10** (MT Ps 69:10) `ὅτι ὁ ζῆλος τοῦ οἴκου σου κατέφαγέν με`. John's citation has shifted the verb from LXX aorist `κατέφαγεν` (V-2AAI-3S, "consumed [past]") to **future middle-deponent** `καταφάγεταί` (V-FDI-3S) — a tense-change the disciples' "remembering" reads the temple scene *prophetically forward* rather than narratively backward. This is the first of the aorist-passive ἐμνήσθησαν inclusio closed at v.22.

The opening of the quotation `ὁ ζῆλος τοῦ οἴκου σου` — articular nominative + genitive — is a **subjective genitive**: "the zeal [that belongs to / is directed toward] your house."

The **ὅτι-clause** here is causal ("*because/seeing that* you are doing these things") rather than recitative — the Jews are not quoting; they are citing the cleansing as the ground on which a validating sign is owed. `σημεῖον` (G4592) is the **same technical Johannine term** the evangelist uses programmatically at 2:11 of Cana and 20:30–31 of the whole book's σημεῖα-selection. The Jews are demanding a second-order validation of Jesus' first public action in Jerusalem.

### v.19 — Jesus' Answer (Aorist Imperative + Future Active)

| Word | Lemma | Strong's | Parsing | Gloss |
|------|-------|----------|---------|-------|
| ἀπεκρίθη | ἀποκρίνομαι | G611 | V-ADI-3S | answered |
| Ἰησοῦς | Ἰησοῦς | G2424 | N-NSM | Jesus |
| εἶπεν | λέγω | G3004 | V-2AAI-3S | said |
| αὐτοῖς | αὐτός | G846 | P-DPM | to them |
| **Λύσατε** | λύω | G3089 | **V-AAM-2P** (Aor Act Impv) | Destroy! / Loose! (imperative, 2 pl) |
| τὸν ναὸν τοῦτον | ναός / οὗτος | G3485/G3778 | N-ASM + D-ASM | **this sanctuary** |
| ἐν τρισὶν ἡμέραις | τρεῖς / ἡμέρα | G5140/G2250 | A-DPF + N-DPF | in three days (dat. of time-within-which) |
| **ἐγερῶ** | ἐγείρω | G1453 | **V-FAI-1S** (Fut Act Ind) | I will raise up |
| αὐτόν | αὐτός | G846 | P-ASM | it [masc. — agreeing with ναόν] |

**Λύσατε** is an **aorist active imperative 2nd plural** (V-AAM-2P). Grammatically it is a genuine command-form, but pragmatically the syntax is that of a **conditional/concessive imperative** — "[go ahead and] destroy…[if you do,] I will raise it up." Cf. the parallel Jn 13:27 to Judas `ὃ ποιεῖς, ποίησον τάχιον` (same construction: permissive-imperative with expected outcome). See [mood-aorist-imperative](../greek/mood-aorist-imperative.md).

The response-clause **ἐγερῶ αὐτόν** is **future active indicative 1st singular** of ἐγείρω. Pronominal **αὐτόν** is masculine singular accusative, grammatically anchored to the masculine ναόν — a deliberately ambiguous referent (the *temple*? some other τοῦτον / this-one?). The ambiguity is resolved by the narrator at v.21.

Crucially, Jesus uses **ναόν** (G3485, the *inner sanctuary*) here — NOT ἱερόν (G2411, the *temple complex* including outer courts where the commerce occurred in vv.14–15). The two words are not synonymous. See §2.

### v.20 — The Jews' Objection (Aorist Passive + Future Active)

| Word | Lemma | Strong's | Parsing | Gloss |
|------|-------|----------|---------|-------|
| εἶπαν | λέγω | G3004 | V-2AAI-3P | said |
| οὖν | οὖν | G3767 | CONJ | therefore |
| οἱ Ἰουδαῖοι | Ἰουδαῖος | G2453 | A-NPM | the Jews |
| Τεσσεράκοντα καὶ ἕξ | — | G5062/G1803 | A-NUI (numeric) | forty and six |
| ἔτεσιν | ἔτος | G2094 | **N-DPN** (dat. of time) | years (dative of duration — "over 46 years") |
| **οἰκοδομήθη** | οἰκοδομέω | G3618 | **V-API-3S** (Aor Pass Ind) | was built |
| ὁ ναὸς οὗτος | ναός / οὗτος | G3485/G3778 | N-NSM + D-NSM | this sanctuary |
| σύ | σύ | G4771 | **P-2NS** (emphatic) | *you* |
| ἐγερεῖς | ἐγείρω | G1453 | **V-FAI-2S** (Fut Act Ind) | will raise up |
| αὐτόν | αὐτός | G846 | P-ASM | it |

The **aorist passive οἰκοδομήθη** (V-API-3S) is an ordinary historical-aorist passive ("was built"); the dative `Τεσσεράκοντα καὶ ἕξ ἔτεσιν` is a **dative of time-extent** answering "for/over how long?" — Herodian temple construction begun c. 20/19 BCE + 46 years ≈ c. 27/28 CE (a standard chronological datum for dating Jesus' first Passover).

Most importantly, the Jews **accept Jesus' lexical choice**: they answer back with the same `ναός` (ὁ ναὸς οὗτος). They do not hear "the temple complex"; they hear "this sanctuary," which is precisely why they find the claim absurd (you cannot re-erect the *inner sanctuary* in three days any more than you could the complex). Their grammar confirms that the ναός/ἱερόν distinction was live in their dialect too.

The emphatic fronted pronoun **σύ** (P-2NS, nominative) with the second-singular future `ἐγερεῖς` is derisive — "and *you* will raise it up?" The verb `ἐγείρω` is now being used transitively of **building construction** (≈ "erect"), which is a natural extension of the verb's range but NOT the sense John intends in Jesus' own use at v.19; John's narrator will retrieve the *resurrection* sense at v.22 (§4).

### v.21 — The Narrator's Interpretation (Imperfect + Anaphoric Genitive)

| Word | Lemma | Strong's | Parsing | Gloss |
|------|-------|----------|---------|-------|
| ἐκεῖνος | ἐκεῖνος | G1565 | D-NSM (emphatic: *that one*) | **he** (contrastive demonstrative) |
| δέ | δέ | G1161 | CONJ (mild adversative) | but / however |
| **ἔλεγεν** | λέγω | G3004 | **V-IAI-3S** (Impf Act Ind) | was speaking / was meaning (iterative/durative) |
| περί | περί | G4012 | PREP + gen. | concerning |
| τοῦ ναοῦ | ναός | G3485 | N-GSM | the sanctuary |
| τοῦ σώματος αὐτοῦ | σῶμα / αὐτός | G4983/G846 | N-GSN + P-GSM | of his body |

Two grammatical points carry the interpretive weight of the narrator's aside:

1. **ἐκεῖνος** (D-NSM, "that one") — the *distal* demonstrative pronoun, used contrastively with the nearer `Ἰουδαῖοι` of v.20. In Johannine usage ἐκεῖνος frequently marks the emphatic "*he* [= Jesus, God, the Spirit, etc., as opposed to the other party]" (cf. Jn 1:8, 18; 5:35, 39, 46; 14:26; 15:26). The grammar is already prejudging the interpretive priority: *they* said one thing, but *that one* (Jesus) meant another.

2. **ἔλεγεν** (V-IAI-3S, *imperfect*) — NOT the historic aorist εἶπεν one might expect for a reported saying. The imperfect here is **iterative/referential-durative** — "the whole discourse of v.19 was [all the while] being-spoken about…" The imperfect recharacterizes the aorist εἶπεν Λύσατε…ἐγερῶ of v.19 as *continuously meaning* the body-referent across its utterance. The same imperfect recurs at v.22 `τοῦτο ἔλεγεν`.

The double articular genitive `τοῦ ναοῦ τοῦ σώματος αὐτοῦ` is a **genitive of apposition** (= epexegetical / explanatory genitive): "the sanctuary — [namely,] his body." Wallace GGBB 95–100. The construction equates **ναός** with **σῶμα** directly — the ναός-referent *is* Jesus' body, not merely a symbolic reflection of it.

| τῇ γραφῇ | γραφή | G1124 | N-DSF | the scripture (dat. of person believed) |
| καί τῷ λόγῳ | λόγος | G3056 | N-DSM | and the word |
| ὃν εἶπεν ὁ Ἰησοῦς | ὅς / λέγω / Ἰησοῦς | G3739/G3004/G2424 | R-ASM + V-2AAI-3S + N-NSM | which Jesus said |

Three aorist indicatives form a **causal/temporal result-chain** mirroring v.11's triple aorist (ἐποίησεν…ἐφανέρωσεν…ἐπίστευσαν):

1. **ἠγέρθη** (V-API-3S) — aorist passive of ἐγείρω, "he was raised." The passive here is the **agentless divine passive** — Jesus is raised by the Father; cf. [divine-passive](../greek/divine-passive.md).
2. **ἐμνήσθησαν** (V-API-3P) — aorist passive of μιμνῄσκω, "they remembered / were caused-to-remember." Same form as v.17, closing the inclusio.
3. **ἐπίστευσαν** (V-AAI-3P) — aorist active of πιστεύω, "they believed." Same form as v.11 (ἐπίστευσαν εἰς αὐτόν).

The grammatical progression: *raising (divine passive) → remembering (aorist passive) → believing (aorist active)*. The disciples' faith is **retro-caused**: the cross–resurrection becomes the epistemic key that unlocks the saying spoken *before* the event. Cf. 12:16, 13:7, 14:26 for the same Johannine pattern of delayed comprehension.

Verb `ἐπίστευσαν` takes **dative objects** here (`τῇ γραφῇ` + `τῷ λόγῳ`) — "they believed the scripture and the word." This is πιστεύω + dative of **thing/proposition believed** (Wallace 172–175, BDF §187), distinct from the Johannine programmatic πιστεύω εἰς + accusative **of person believed into** (cf. 2:11, 3:16, 20:31). The disciples' belief in v.22 is *propositional* (that the written Psalm and the spoken λόγος corresponded to the event), not yet primarily relational-commitment — though that relational sense is of course entailed.

The relative clause `τῷ λόγῳ ὃν εἶπεν ὁ Ἰησοῦς` — R-ASM `ὃν` (accusative) attracts to its antecedent case-wise loosely (the head λόγῳ is dative, but ὅς takes the case demanded by its own clause: εἶπεν + accusative object). This is a standard NT pattern (not case-attraction proper; the relative takes its own clause's case).

## Key Grammatical Features

### 1. ἀνέβη (v.13) — Pilgrimage Verb and the Discourse Framing

- **Form:** V-2AAI-3S, aorist active indicative of ἀναβαίνω (G305), "went up."
- **Significance:** ἀναβαίνω / καταβαίνω is the fixed Jerusalem-pilgrimage word-pair, calqued from Hebrew עָלָה / יָרַד (LXX consistently renders אעלה → ἀναβαίνω). The phrase `ἀνέβη εἰς Ἱεροσόλυμα` + Passover-marker `τὸ πάσχα τῶν Ἰουδαίων` is a formal pilgrimage-ascent setting that John reuses four times in his Gospel to structure the Passovers (2:13; 6:4; 11:55; 12:1), with `ἀνέβη εἰς Ἱεροσόλυμα` appearing in 2:13; 5:1 (unnamed feast); 7:14 (Tabernacles); 11:55; 12:20 (Greeks at the Passover). The grammar is liturgical before it is topographical.
- **Contrastive tense note:** The *imperfect* `ἦν` in v.13a describes background state (Passover was-at-hand — durative), while the *aorist* `ἀνέβη` punctualizes Jesus' discrete pilgrimage act against that backdrop. Standard narrative tense-pairing.

### 2. ἱερόν (G2411) vs. ναός (G3485) — The Disciplined Lexical Split

John uses **both** temple-words within five verses, and the split is grammatically clean:

| Verse | Word | Strong's | Parsing | Gloss | Referent |
|-------|------|----------|---------|-------|----------|
| 2:14 | ἐν τῷ ἱερῷ | G2411 | N-DSN | in the temple | whole temple precincts (Court of Gentiles, where commerce occurred) |
| 2:15 | ἐκ τοῦ ἱεροῦ | G2411 | N-GSN | out of the temple | whole temple precincts |
| 2:16 | τὸν οἶκον τοῦ Πατρός | G3624 | N-ASM | the Father's house | the temple as *oikos-of-God* (LXX idiom) |
| 2:17 | τοῦ οἴκου σου | G3624 | N-GSM | of your house | citation of Ps 69:10 LXX — same οἶκος lexeme |
| 2:19 | τὸν ναόν | G3485 | N-ASM | the sanctuary | inner sanctuary; re-referred in v.21 to Jesus' body |
| 2:20 | ὁ ναός | G3485 | N-NSM | the sanctuary | Jews' reply — accepts Jesus' lexical choice |
| 2:21 | τοῦ ναοῦ | G3485 | N-GSM | of the sanctuary | narrator's identification with τοῦ σώματος αὐτοῦ |

- **ἱερόν** (G2411) — grammatically neuter (N-DSN / N-GSN / N-ASN); substantival use of the neuter adjective ἱερός ("sacred [place]"). Covers the *entire sacred enclosure* including the outer courts where buying, selling, teaching, and Gentile access occurred. NT parser-verified 72× across the Gospels; all the cleansing-commerce terms (βόες, πρόβατα, περιστεραί, τράπεζαι) occur *in the ἱερόν*, never *in the ναός*.
- **ναός** (G3485) — grammatically masculine (N-ASM / N-NSM / N-GSM); denotes properly the *inner sanctuary* — the Holy Place + Most Holy Place, where only priests could enter. NT parser-verified 45×.
- **οἶκος τοῦ Πατρός μου** (v.16) — Jesus' initial word-choice, drawn from the Psalm he is quoting (`τοῦ οἴκου σου` v.17 = Ps 69:10 LXX). οἶκος is the broader LXX idiom for the sanctuary-as-God's-dwelling, without the architectural specificity of either ἱερόν or ναός.

**Grammatical consequence:** the pun of v.19 **requires** the ναός/ἱερόν distinction. Jesus has just cleansed the *ἱερόν* (vv.14–15), but when asked for a sign, he speaks of destroying **τὸν ναὸν τοῦτον** — the *sanctuary*, not the *complex*. The demonstrative τοῦτον ("*this* sanctuary") is grammatically open: it could point at the building around them *or* at himself. The Jews (v.20) interpret it architecturally; the narrator (v.21) interprets it somatologically (τοῦ σώματος αὐτοῦ). The double meaning is *generated by* the lexical and deictic grammar, not imposed on it. Cf. John's later use of the same distinction: ἱερόν of the temple precincts (7:14, 28; 8:2, 20, 59; 10:23; 11:56), ναός never of the physical temple in John *except here* (2:19–21) where it equates to Jesus' body; Revelation then expands the ναός-Christological metaphor (Rev 11:19; 21:22 *ναὸν οὐκ εἶδον ἐν αὐτῇ· ὁ γὰρ Κύριος ὁ Θεὸς ὁ Παντοκράτωρ ναὸς αὐτῆς ἐστιν, καὶ τὸ Ἀρνίον*).

Word studies: [G3485-naos](../../word-studies/G3485-naos.md), [G2411-hieron](../../word-studies/G2411-hieron.md), [TR-heykal-naos](../../word-studies/TR-heykal-naos.md).

### 3. κερματιστής (v.14) vs. κολλυβιστής (v.15) — The Money-Changer Lexeme Shift

Two different Greek nouns for the same profession appear in adjacent verses:

- **κερματιστής** (G2773, v.14) — N-APM, **parser-verified NT hapax** (only here in the entire NT). Formed from κέρμα (G2772 "small coin") + agent-suffix -της = "small-coin-person." Used when John describes what Jesus *found*.
- **κολλυβιστής** (G2855, v.15) — N-GPM (genitive of possession: "of the money-changers"), NT parser-verified 3× total (Matt 21:12 ‖ Mark 11:15 ‖ John 2:15). Formed from κόλλυβος "rate-of-exchange / premium" = "exchange-commission-charger." Used when John describes whose coins Jesus *poured out*.

The shift from κερματιστής (v.14) → κολλυβιστής (v.15) is not mere variation — it is **angle-of-description**:
- v.14 (trade-description): what the traders *sell* = small-coinage, so `κερματιστής`.
- v.15 (profit-description): what the traders *pocket* = exchange-commission, so `κολλυβιστής`.

κέρμα (G2772, v.15 `τὰ κέρματα`) is itself also a **parser-verified NT hapax** — the small-coinage that was `ἐξέχεεν`-poured-out. Three hapaxes cluster in vv.14–15 (κερματιστής, κέρμα, φραγέλλιον), reflecting the technical specificity of the temple-commerce vocabulary and the physical-violence vocabulary ("cord-whip").

### 4. ἐγείρω (G1453) Across vv.19–22 — Three Voices, Three Speakers, Two Meanings

The verb ἐγείρω appears three times in the pericope, each in a different voice and on different lips:

| Verse | Form | Parsing | Speaker | Lexical sense | Object |
|-------|------|---------|---------|---------------|--------|
| 2:19 | ἐγερῶ | **V-FAI-1S** | Jesus | "raise-up" (resurrection-sense Jesus intends) | αὐτόν (= σῶμα per v.21) |
| 2:20 | ἐγερεῖς | **V-FAI-2S** | the Jews (quoting Jesus) | "raise-up / re-erect" (architectural-sense they hear) | αὐτόν (= ναός architecturally) |
| 2:22 | ἠγέρθη | **V-API-3S** | narrator | "was raised" (resurrection-sense, event-accomplished) | — (Jesus is subject) |

- The **future active `ἐγερῶ` / `ἐγερεῖς`** (1s / 2s) share the same stem and tense; the lexical meaning divides along the ναός/σῶμα axis of §2. Jesus' grammar *permits* both senses; the Jews narrow it to one; the narrator resolves it to the other.
- The **aorist passive `ἠγέρθη`** at v.22 is the standard NT resurrection-formula (parser-verified: Matt 27:64; 28:6–7; Mark 16:6; Luke 24:6, 34; 1 Cor 15:4 *ἐγήγερται* perf. pass. variant, etc.). The passive is **agentless** in the clause but theologically presupposes God the Father as agent — the **divine passive** pattern. Cf. [divine-passive](../greek/divine-passive.md) and [aorist-passive-indicative](../greek/aorist-passive-indicative.md).
- The voice-progression **active-future (promise) → active-future (quotation/mockery) → passive-aorist (fulfillment)** encodes the narrative-theological arc grammatically: Jesus *undertakes*; the Jews *doubt*; the event *is accomplished [by God]*.

### 5. μιμνῄσκομαι (G3403) + Aorist Passive Inclusio — vv.17 ↔ 22

Two identical forms bracket the pericope:

| Verse | Form | Parsing | Object-of-remembrance |
|-------|------|---------|-----------------------|
| 2:17 | ἐμνήσθησαν | **V-API-3P** | Ps 69:10 ὁ ζῆλος τοῦ οἴκου σου |
| 2:22 | ἐμνήσθησαν | **V-API-3P** | Jesus' own λόγος of v.19 + τῇ γραφῇ |

- **Form:** Aorist passive indicative 3rd plural of μιμνῄσκομαι. Morphologically passive, functionally middle-reflexive = "brought to mind / came to remember." The lexical-passive "be reminded" and middle-reflexive "remember" are both available; the passive here carries a **suggestive divine-reminding** overtone consistent with the parallel memory-passives Luke 24:6, 8 and John 12:16.
- **Inclusio function:** v.17 marks remembrance *during* the cleansing (scripture-to-event mapping); v.22 marks remembrance *after the resurrection* (word-and-scripture to resurrection mapping). The two bracket the central speech-act Λύσατε…ἐγερῶ of v.19. Cf. 12:16 `ταῦτα οὐκ ἔγνωσαν αὐτοῦ οἱ μαθηταὶ τὸ πρῶτον, ἀλλ᾽ ὅτε ἐδοξάσθη Ἰησοῦς, τότε ἐμνήσθησαν…` — same grammatical form (ἐμνήσθησαν V-API-3P) for the same delayed-comprehension pattern.
- The **v.22 object-clause** pairs `τῇ γραφῇ καὶ τῷ λόγῳ ὃν εἶπεν ὁ Ἰησοῦς` — scripture + spoken-word *in parallel* as joint objects of ἐπίστευσαν. This is the **earliest Gospel passage** (narratively) in which disciples' post-resurrection faith is said to rest on scripture and the pre-passion logia *together*; it defines a hermeneutical pattern the evangelist will rely on (cf. 20:9, where even on resurrection-morning οὐδέπω…ᾔδεισαν τὴν γραφὴν).

### 6. Ἄρατε / μὴ ποιεῖτε (v.16) — Aorist-vs-Present Imperative Aspectual Contrast

- **Form:** `Ἄρατε` is V-AAM-2P (aorist active imperative 2nd plural of αἴρω G142); `μὴ ποιεῖτε` is V-PAM-2P (present active imperative 2nd plural of ποιέω G4160) with the negative particle μή.
- **Aspectual force:**
  - Aorist imperative = **punctiliar / global command** to do the act as a whole. "Take these away!" (do it, now, as one event).
  - Present imperative + μή = **cease / do not continue** an activity already in progress. "Stop making my Father's house a merchandise-house!" — presupposing the activity is ongoing.
- **Standard grammatical rule:** aorist prohibitions (μή + AAS) typically mean "do not start"; present prohibitions (μή + PAI/PAM) typically mean "stop / do not keep on" (Wallace GGBB 724–725, BDF §336). v.16 fits the latter pattern precisely — the traders *are already* selling; Jesus commands them to *cease* the commercialization of the οἶκος.
- Cross-reference: [mood-aorist-imperative](../greek/mood-aorist-imperative.md).

### 7. γεγραμμένον ἐστίν (v.17) — Perfect Passive Periphrastic

- **Form:** V-RPP-NSN (perfect passive participle, neuter nom. sg.) + V-PAI-3S of εἰμί = **periphrastic perfect passive indicative** of γράφω (G1125).
- **Aspectual force:** Completed writing-event + abiding written-state. The scripture **stands written** — on record, binding, not retractable.
- **Comparison with γέγραπται:** The synchronic synthetic-perfect γέγραπται (V-RPI-3S, parser-verified ~67× in NT) is more common; the analytic periphrastic `γεγραμμένον ἐστίν` is a stylistic variant with the same aspectual meaning. John uses both (γέγραπται at 6:31, 45; 8:17; 10:34; 12:14, 16; 15:25; 19:19; 20:31; periphrastic at 2:17; 6:31v.l.; 12:14; cf. [participle-perfect-passive-state](../greek/participle-perfect-passive-state.md) for the theological register of the "written-and-remaining" participle).
- The disciples' ὅτι-clause introduces not a new thought but a **scriptural citation-formula** — the verb's perfect-passive stativity marks this as authoritative torah-like quotation, not paraphrase.
- Word studies: [G1125-grapho](../../word-studies/G1125-grapho.md), [TR-kathab-grapho](../../word-studies/TR-kathab-grapho.md).

## Clause Structure Summary

Johannine sentence-architecture across vv.13–22:

- **vv.13–16 narrative cleansing (short paratactic καί-clauses):** ἦν…ἀνέβη [v.13] → εὗρεν…[v.14] → ποιήσας…ἐξέβαλεν…ἐξέχεεν…ἀνέτρεψεν [v.15] → εἶπεν Ἄρατε…μὴ ποιεῖτε [v.16]. Mainline is a chain of aorist indicatives with one participial subordination (ποιήσας).
- **v.17 interjected scriptural commentary (ὅτι-periphrastic-perfect-passive + LXX-quote):** ἐμνήσθησαν…ὅτι γεγραμμένον ἐστίν · [quote]. The evangelist interrupts the narrative mainline.
- **vv.18–20 dialogue (aorist indicatives of speech-act + direct-discourse imperative/future):** ἀπεκρίθησαν…εἶπαν · Τί σημεῖον…[v.18] → ἀπεκρίθη…εἶπεν · Λύσατε…ἐγερῶ [v.19] → εἶπαν · …οἰκοδομήθη…σὺ ἐγερεῖς [v.20].
- **v.21 narrator's interpretive aside (δέ-contrastive + imperfect):** ἐκεῖνος δὲ ἔλεγεν περὶ…
- **v.22 closing temporal-result clause (ὅτε-οὖν + aorist passives + aorist indicative result):** ὅτε οὖν ἠγέρθη…ἐμνήσθησαν…ἐπίστευσαν.

The evangelist structures the whole pericope as **action (vv.13–16) → scriptural interpretation (v.17) → dialogue (vv.18–20) → authorial interpretation (v.21) → delayed faith-result (v.22)** — the same interpretive-delay pattern that defines the Cana sign (2:11 `καὶ ἐπίστευσαν εἰς αὐτὸν οἱ μαθηταὶ αὐτοῦ`) but here *doubled* with an anticipatory-faith (v.17) and a retrospective-faith (v.22) flanking the sign-claim.
