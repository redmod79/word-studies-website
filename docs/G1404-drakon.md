## Lexicon Definition

From an alternate form of δέρκομαι (dérkomai, to look); a fabulous kind of serpent — a dragon.
KJV translation: dragon (13x).

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Identified with Satan, the Devil, and the ancient serpent
The dragon is explicitly equated with the devil, Satan, and the Eden serpent by the text itself.

- **Rev 12:9** — "And the great dragon was cast out, that old serpent, called the Devil, and Satan, which deceiveth the whole world"
- **Rev 20:2** — "And he laid hold on the dragon, that old serpent, which is the Devil, and Satan, and bound him a thousand years"

### 2. Heavenly adversary with apocalyptic form
The dragon appears with symbolic features — seven heads, ten horns, seven crowns — and initiates cosmic warfare.

- **Rev 12:3** — "behold a great red dragon, having seven heads and ten horns, and seven crowns upon his heads"
- **Rev 12:4** — "his tail drew the third part of the stars of heaven, and did cast them to the earth"
- **Rev 12:7** — "there was war in heaven: Michael and his angels fought against the dragon; and the dragon fought and his angels"

### 3. Persecutor of the woman and her seed
After being cast to earth, the dragon actively persecutes the woman and makes war on her offspring.

- **Rev 12:13** — "when the dragon saw that he was cast unto the earth, he persecuted the woman which brought forth the man child"
- **Rev 12:16** — "the earth... swallowed up the flood which the dragon cast out of his mouth"
- **Rev 12:17** — "the dragon was wroth with the woman, and went to make war with the remnant of her seed, which keep the commandments of God"

### 4. Source of the beast's power and object of worship
The dragon delegates authority to the first beast; humanity worships the dragon through the beast.

- **Rev 13:2** — "the dragon gave him his power, and his seat, and great authority"
- **Rev 13:4** — "they worshipped the dragon which gave power unto the beast, saying, Who is like unto the beast?"
- **Rev 13:11** — "another beast coming up out of the earth... he spake as a dragon"

### 5. Channel of deception through unclean spirits
The dragon's mouth is a source of deceptive spirits in the final conflict.

- **Rev 16:13** — "I saw three unclean spirits like frogs come out of the mouth of the dragon, and out of the mouth of the beast, and out of the mouth of the false prophet"
