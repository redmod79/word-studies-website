# Perfect Tense — Indicative Mood (Greek)

## Definition

The Greek perfect indicative denotes a completed action whose results persist into the present moment of speaking. It combines two temporal ideas: a past event and a present state arising from that event. The indicative mood asserts this as a factual reality.

1. **Intensive/Resultative** — emphasis falls on the present state produced by the completed action
2. **Consummative** — emphasis falls on the completed action itself, viewed as brought to its finish
3. **Stative (present-force)** — the perfect has essentially lost its past-event component and functions as a present state (especially οἶδα "I know," ἕστηκα "I stand")

## Form Recognition

- **Reduplication** of the initial consonant + ε (e.g., γέγραπται from γράφω; τετέλεσται from τελέω; πεπίστευκα from πιστεύω)
- **Active endings:** -κα, -κας, -κεν, -καμεν, -κατε, -κασιν (first perfect); some verbs use second-perfect without κ (γέγονεν from γίνομαι, ἕστηκα from ἵστημι)
- **Middle/Passive endings:** -μαι, -σαι, -ται, -μεθα, -σθε, -νται
- **Parser search:** `tense=perfect mood=indicative` (all voices), add `voice=active|passive|middle` to narrow
- **Morph code patterns:** `V-RAI-3S` (perfect active indicative 3rd sg), `V-RPI-3S` (perfect passive indicative 3rd sg), `V-RMI-2S` (perfect middle indicative 2nd sg)

## Functions with Examples

### 1. Intensive/Resultative Perfect

The past action recedes; the focus is on the present state that endures.

| Reference | Greek | Morph Code | KJV Translation |
|-----------|-------|------------|-----------------|
| John 19:30 | Τετέλεσται | V-RPI-3S | "It is finished" |
| Mark 1:15 | Πεπλήρωται | V-RPI-3S | "The time is fulfilled" |
| Galatians 2:19 | συνεσταύρωμαι | V-RPI-1S | "I am crucified with Christ" |
| Romans 3:21 | πεφανέρωται | V-RPI-3S | "is manifested" |
| 2 Corinthians 5:17 | γέγονεν | V-2RAI-3S | "are become new" |

John 19:30 Τετέλεσται (τελέω, perfect passive indicative 3rd sg) — the action of finishing is past; the state of completion abides. Mark 1:15 Πεπλήρωται (πληρόω) — the time has been filled and stands full. Galatians 2:19 συνεσταύρωμαι (συσταυρόω) — the co-crucifixion is past; the crucified-state is Paul's present reality. Romans 3:21 πεφανέρωται (φανερόω) — the righteousness of God has been revealed and stands openly disclosed.

### 2. Consummative Perfect

The emphasis falls on the completed action, often summarizing a life, mission, or effort brought to its end.

| Reference | Greek | Morph Code | KJV Translation |
|-----------|-------|------------|-----------------|
| 2 Timothy 4:7 | τετέλεκα | V-RAI-1S | "I have finished [my] course" |
| 2 Timothy 4:7 | τετήρηκα | V-RAI-1S | "I have kept the faith" |
| Hebrews 10:14 | τετελείωκεν | V-RAI-3S | "he hath perfected for ever" |
| John 17:4 | δέδωκάς | V-RAI-2S | "thou gavest me" |
| 1 John 1:1 | ἑωράκαμεν | V-RAI-1P-ATT | "we have seen" |

2 Timothy 4:7 τετέλεκα and τετήρηκα (τελέω, τηρέω) — Paul sums up his ministry as a completed whole. Hebrews 10:14 τετελείωκεν (τελειόω) — Christ's single offering has accomplished perfection permanently (εἰς τὸ διηνεκές reinforces the perfect's abiding force). 1 John 1:1 ἑωράκαμεν (ὁράω) — the apostolic witness is a completed act whose testimony persists.

### 3. Stative Perfect (Present-Force)

Certain verbs have lost the sense of a prior act and function as simple present-state verbs.

| Reference | Greek | Morph Code | KJV Translation |
|-----------|-------|------------|-----------------|
| Matthew 6:8 | οἶδεν | V-RAI-3S | "knoweth" |
| Romans 8:38 | πέπεισμαι | V-RPI-1S | "I am persuaded" |
| 2 Timothy 1:12 | πεπίστευκα | V-RAI-1S | "I have believed" |
| Revelation 3:20 | ἕστηκα | V-RAI-1S | "I stand" |
| Romans 5:2 | ἑστήκαμεν | V-RAI-1P | "wherein we stand" |

οἶδα (from *εἴδω, "see") is the paradigm case — a completed act of seeing has produced a present state of knowing; it is consistently translated as a simple present "I know." ἕστηκα (ἵστημι) functions the same way: "I stand" rather than "I have stood." πέποιθα (πείθω) = "I am confident / I trust."

## Contrast with Related Forms

| Form | Aspect/Function | Example |
|------|-----------------|---------|
| **Perfect indicative** | completed action with abiding result | John 19:30 Τετέλεσται "It is finished" (and remains so) |
| **Aorist indicative** | completed action viewed as a whole event | Rev 5:5 ἐνίκησεν "hath prevailed" (event itself) |
| **Present indicative** | ongoing/progressive action | John 3:16 ἀγαπᾷ "loves" (continuous) |
| **Imperfect indicative** | ongoing past action | Luke 4:22 ἐθαύμαζον "were wondering" (past process) |

The aorist views a past action externally as a simple event; the perfect views a past action through the lens of its continuing result. Compare the aorist ἐνίκησεν in Rev 5:5 ("he conquered" — the event) with the perfect τετελείωκεν in Heb 10:14 ("he has perfected" — the event and its standing outcome).

## Active vs. Passive Voice in the Perfect

The perfect appears in all three voices. Active perfects emphasize what the subject has done and the state that results (τετέλεκα "I have finished," 2 Tim 4:7). Passive perfects emphasize what has been done to the subject and the resulting condition (Τετέλεσται "it has been finished / it stands finished," John 19:30; γέγραπται "it stands written," Matt 4:4). Middle perfects are rare and emphasize the subject's self-involvement (ἐπικέκλησαι "you have appealed," Acts 25:12; προῄρηται "he has purposed for himself," 2 Cor 9:7).
