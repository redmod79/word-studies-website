## Lexicon Definition

A good message, i.e. the gospel. From the same root as G2097 (εὐαγγελίζω, euangelizo — to announce good news). The noun denotes the content of the proclamation rather than the act of proclaiming.

## Hebrew Source (via LXX)

No direct LXX mapping exists for G2098 as a noun. However, the cognate verb G2097 (euangelizo) translates Hebrew **H1319 בָּשַׂר (basar)** — "to bring good tidings" — in key OT passages (Isa 40:9; 52:7; 61:1; Pss 40:9; 96:2). The noun euangelion itself appears in the LXX only at 2 Sam 4:10 and 18:20-25, where it translates the Hebrew noun **בְּשׂוֹרָה (besorah)** meaning "reward for good tidings" or "good news."
