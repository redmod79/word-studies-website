## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same across testaments:**
- God remains the primary agent of healing in both OT and NT usage
- Physical and metaphorical/spiritual healing coexist in both testaments
- The prayer-healing connection persists (Num 12:13 → Jas 5:16)
- The wound-heal antithesis carries over (Deut 32:39 "I wound and I heal" → 1 Pet 2:24 "stripes... healed")

**What shifts:**
- In the NT, Jesus performs the healing that YHWH claimed as his exclusive prerogative (Exo 15:26 "I am the LORD that healeth thee" → Acts 10:38 Jesus "healing all")
- The NT expands iaomai's participial noun family: iama (gift of healing, 1 Cor 12:9) has no OT equivalent — healing becomes a distributed spiritual gift
- The Qal participle *ropheʼ* ("physician") mapped to iatros in both LXX and NT, but the NT physician is contrasted negatively with divine healing (Mark 5:26 "suffered many things of many physicians")
