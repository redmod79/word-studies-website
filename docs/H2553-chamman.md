## Lexicon Definition

The local Strong's lexicon gives the basic sense: **"a sun-pillar: idol, image."** The fuller BDB entry in the same lexicon table sharpens that: H2553 is used only for an idolatrous cult object, always in plural forms, and always in anti-idolatry contexts. It is regularly paired with other forbidden worship fixtures such as **high places**, **altars**, **Asherah poles/groves**, and **graven images**.

Two lexical points matter for the canonical entry:

1. The KJV translates the word generically as **images / idols**, so the English surface form hides the word's more specific solar/cultic nuance.
2. Every biblical occurrence is hostile. H2553 is never neutral, decorative, or legitimate; it is always something to destroy, cut down, break, or refuse.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Covenant-Curse Target
The first occurrence places H2553 in the sanctions of covenant judgment: these cult objects are among the things YHWH Himself will destroy when Israel persists in rebellion.
- **Lev 26:30** — "I will destroy your high places, and cut down your images"

### 2. Royal Reform Object Removed from Judah
In Chronicles the word belongs to reform narratives where faithful kings dismantle illicit worship installations throughout the land.
- **2 Chr 14:5** — Asa "took away... the high places and the images"
- **2 Chr 34:4** — Josiah's men cut down "the images" above the Baal altars
- **2 Chr 34:7** — after breaking altars and beating graven images to powder, Josiah "cut down all the idols"

### 3. Object of Repentance and Reorientation to YHWH
Isaiah uses H2553 in calls to stop looking at hand-made worship objects and to remove them as part of sin's purging.
- **Isa 17:8** — the repentant man will not respect what his fingers made, "either the groves, or the images"
- **Isa 27:9** — Jacob's sin is removed when "the groves and images shall not stand up"

### 4. Prophetic Judgment Against Local Shrine Worship
Ezekiel places H2553 in the full collapse of illicit worship sites: altars ruined, idols broken, and worshippers slain before the objects they served.
- **Ezek 6:4** — "your altars shall be desolate, and your images shall be broken"
- **Ezek 6:6** — "your images may be cut down, and your works may be abolished"

Across all eight verses, the semantic profile is stable: H2553 denotes a forbidden cult object associated with elevated or altar-centered worship and marked for destruction.
