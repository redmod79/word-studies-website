## Lexicon Definition (Thayer)

ζόφος, -ου, ὁ — darkness, blackness. Akin to γνόφος, δνόφος, νέφος, κνέφας (Buttmann, *Lexilogus* 2, p. 266). Used as in Homer (*Iliad* 15:191; 21:56) of the darkness of the nether world (cf. Grimm on Wis. 17:14): 2 Peter 2:4; Jude 1:6. The compound **ζόφος τοῦ σκότους** — "the blackness of (i.e., the densest) darkness" — cf. Heb. חֹשֶׁךְ אֲפֵלָה (Exod 10:22): 2 Peter 2:17; Jude 1:13. Also attested at Heb 12:18 (L T Tr WH). [Cf. Trench, §c.]

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Atmospheric / Elemental Darkness
Dense, palpable gloom — the thick murk of storm or theophany. Attested in some manuscripts at Sinai.

- **Heb 12:18** (L T Tr WH) — "ye are not come unto the mount that might be touched...nor unto blackness [ζόφῳ], and darkness, and tempest"

### 2. The Darkness of the Netherworld — Prison of Fallen Angels
ζόφος as the condition and place of divine punishment where fallen supernatural beings await final judgment. Draws on Homer's usage of ζόφος for the murky underworld.

- **2Pe 2:4** — "cast [them] down to hell, and delivered [them] into chains of darkness [ζόφου], to be reserved unto judgment"
- **Jude 1:6** — "he hath reserved in everlasting chains under darkness [ζόφον] unto the judgment of the great day"

### 3. The Blackness of Darkness — Final Eschatological Doom
The compound **ζόφος τοῦ σκότους** ("blackness of darkness") intensifies the darkness imagery to its extreme. A Hebraism (cf. חֹשֶׁךְ אֲפֵלָה, the plague darkness of Exod 10:22). Reserved as the eternal lot of the ungodly.

- **2Pe 2:17** — "to whom the mist of darkness [ζόφος τοῦ σκότους] is reserved for ever"
- **Jude 1:13** — "wandering stars, to whom is reserved the blackness of darkness [ζόφος τοῦ σκότους] for ever"
