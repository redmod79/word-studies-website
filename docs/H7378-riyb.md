## Lexicon Definition

Or רוּב ruwb; a primitive root; properly, to toss, i.e. grapple; mostly figuratively, to wrangle, i.e. hold a controversy; (by implication) to defend: — adversary, chide, complain, contend, debate, ever, lay wait, plead, rebuke, strive, thoroughly.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Divine Covenant Lawsuit (God as Plaintiff)
God contends with His people over covenant violations — the prophetic *riyb* pattern.
- **Jer 2:9** — "Wherefore I will yet plead with you, saith the LORD, and with your children's children will I plead"
- **Hos 2:2** — "Plead with your mother, plead: for she is not my wife, neither am I her husband"
- **Mic 6:1** — "Arise, contend thou before the mountains, and let the hills hear thy voice"
- **Isa 3:13** — "The LORD standeth up to plead, and standeth to judge the people"

### 2. God as Advocate / Defender (Pleading for the Vulnerable)
God contends on behalf of His people or the oppressed — the redemptive *riyb*.
- **Jer 50:34** — "Their Redeemer is strong; the LORD of hosts is his name: he shall throughly plead their cause"
- **Lam 3:58** — "O Lord, thou hast pleaded the causes of my soul; thou hast redeemed my life"
- **Pro 22:23** — "For the LORD will plead their cause, and spoil the soul of those that spoiled them"
- **Isa 49:25** — "I will contend with him that contendeth with thee, and I will save thy children"

### 3. Petition for God to Plead (Psalmist's Appeal)
The worshiper asks God to take up their legal case.
- **Psa 35:1** — "Plead my cause, O LORD, with them that strive with me: fight against them that fight against me"
- **Psa 43:1** — "Judge me, O God, and plead my cause against an ungodly nation"
- **Psa 119:154** — "Plead my cause, and deliver me: quicken me according to thy word"
- **Psa 74:22** — "Arise, O God, plead thine own cause: remember how the foolish man reproacheth thee daily"

### 4. Interpersonal Strife / Quarrel
Physical or verbal conflict between people over tangible disputes.
- **Gen 26:20** — "And the herdmen of Gerar did strive with Isaac's herdmen, saying, The water is ours"

- **Gen 31:36** — "And Jacob was wroth, and chode with Laban"

### 6. Contending with God / The Almighty
