# WG: Hebrew Righteousness (צדק word family)

## Overview
The צדק (tsadaq) word family in the OT forms the core vocabulary for righteousness and justice. The verb (tsadaq, 41x), adjective (tsaddiyq, 206x), and two nouns (tsedeq, 119x; tsedaqah, 157x) together appear 523 times. These cognates regularly co-occur with judgment vocabulary (mishpat, shaphat) and are contrasted with wickedness (rasha'). This group covers forensic, moral, covenantal, and cosmic dimensions of rightness.

## Roles in the Concept

- **Action:** tsadaq (צָדַק) — the verb of being/becoming/declaring righteous
- **Person:** tsaddiyq (צַדִּיק) — the one who is righteous
- **Standard:** tsedeq (צֶדֶק) — righteousness as the norm or measure ("a just weight," "judge in righteousness")
- **Quality/Act:** tsedaqah (צְדָקָה) — righteousness as attribute or concrete act of justice
- **Context:** mishpat (מִשְׁפָּט) — the judgment setting where righteousness is determined
- **Process:** shaphat (שָׁפַט) — the act of judging that produces the verdict
- **Antonym-Person:** rasha' (רָשָׁע, adj) — the wicked one, contrasted with the righteous
- **Antonym-Action:** rasha' (רָשַׁע, verb) — to condemn / act wickedly, opposite of tsadaq
