# WG: Habitation / Dwelling Place

## Overview
Hebrew uses distinct nouns for "dwelling place," each with a different semantic emphasis. Where English collapses these into "habitation," "dwelling," or "place," the Hebrew terms distinguish between shelter/refuge (maʻown), established foundation (makon), generic location (maqom), and pastoral home (naveh).

## Roles in the Concept

- **Shelter/Refuge:** maʻown (מָעוֹן) — the dwelling as protection; God's heavenly abode or an animal's lair
- **Foundation/Establishment:** makon (מָכוֹן) — the dwelling as permanently fixed; God's throne or the Temple's foundation
- **Location:** maqom (מָקוֹם) — the broadest term; any definite place, from a campsite to the Temple mount
- **Home/Pasture:** naveh (נָוֶה) — the dwelling as pleasant or pastoral; shepherds' camps, green pastures, or a family home
