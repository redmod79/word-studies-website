## Lexicon Definition

From a root in the sense of prominence; **splendor** (as conspicuous): — beautiful(-ty), glorious(-ry), goodly, pleasant, roe(-buck).

**Note on homonymy:** Standard Hebrew lexica (BDB, HALOT) treat ṣəḇî as **two distinct homonymous nouns** that share the same form but differ in meaning:
1. **ṣəḇî I** = "beauty, splendor, glory, honor" (abstract; object of desire / pride / glory)
2. **ṣəḇî II** = "gazelle, roe, roebuck" (the animal — named either because it is "beautiful/graceful" or from a separate root of similar form)

Strong's collapses them under one entry ("splendor, as conspicuous"). The KJV translation distribution reflects both senses, with context determining which is in view.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. The Gazelle / Roebuck (the animal — ṣəḇî II)
Clean, edible, swift-footed wild ruminant. Food-law texts + poetic similes of swiftness.
- **Deut 12:15** — "thou mayest eat… as the **roebuck (ṣəḇî)**, and as the hart"
- **Deut 14:5** — listed among clean game animals
- **2 Sam 2:18** — "Asahel was as light of foot as a wild **roe (ṣəḇî)**" (swiftness)
- **Prov 6:5** — "Deliver thyself as a **roe (ṣəḇî)** from the hand of the hunter"
- **Isa 13:14** — "as the chased **roe (ṣəḇî)**" (hunted fugitive imagery)
- **1 Kings 4:23** — Solomon's table provisions: "harts, and **roebucks**, and fallowdeer"

### 2. Beauty / Splendor as a Quality (ṣəḇî I — abstract)
Conspicuous splendor attaching to persons, cities, or nations — often ironically, as what will fall.
- **2 Sam 1:19** — "The **beauty (ṣəḇî)** of Israel is slain upon thy high places" (David over Saul/Jonathan)
- **Isa 4:2** — "the branch of the LORD shall be beautiful and **glorious (ṣəḇî)**"
- **Isa 28:1** — Ephraim's "**glorious (ṣəḇî)** beauty is a fading flower"
- **Isa 28:4–5** — LORD himself becomes "a crown of **glory (ṣəḇî)**" to the remnant (reversal of 28:1)
- **Ezek 7:20** — "the **beauty (ṣəḇî)** of his ornament, he set it in majesty" (Temple ornaments turned to idols)

### 3. Glory of a Pagan City / Kingdom (marked for judgment)
Applied to Gentile imperial pride that God will overthrow.
- **Isa 13:19** — "Babylon, the **glory (ṣəḇî)** of kingdoms, the beauty of the Chaldees' excellency"
- **Isa 23:9** — "to stain the pride of all **glory (ṣəḇî)**" (Tyre oracle)
- **Ezek 25:9** — Moab's frontier cities called the "**glory (ṣəḇî)** of the country"
- **Ezek 26:20** — Tyre: "I shall set **glory (ṣəḇî)** in the land of the living" (removed from her)

### 4. The Glorious / Pleasant Land (Israel / Promised Land / Jerusalem)
ṣəḇî collocated with *ʼerets* (land) or *har* (mountain) as a title for the covenant land — specifically as the land God gave Israel, distinguished from surrounding territories.
- **Jer 3:19** — "give thee a **pleasant (ṣəḇî)** land, a goodly heritage of the hosts of nations"
- **Ezek 20:6** — "to bring them forth of the land of Egypt into a land… flowing with milk and honey, which is the **glory (ṣəḇî)** of all lands"
- **Ezek 20:15** — same phrase repeated: "the **glory (ṣəḇî)** of all lands"
- **Dan 8:9** — the little horn waxes great "toward the south, and toward the east, and toward the **pleasant land** (*hāṣ-ṣəḇî*, lit. 'the glory')"
- **Dan 11:16** — "he shall stand in the **glorious land** (*ʼereṣ-haṣ-ṣəḇî*)"
- **Dan 11:41** — "he shall enter also into the **glorious land** (*ʼereṣ haṣ-ṣəḇî*)"
- **Dan 11:45** — "between the seas in the **glorious holy mountain** (*har-ṣəḇî-qōḏeš*)"

**Text-internal referent:** Ezek 20:6, 15 supplies the definitive identification — ṣəḇî of the land is explicitly the destination of the Exodus, "flowing with milk and honey," i.e., Canaan/Israel. Jer 3:19 parallels "pleasant land" with "goodly heritage," the covenant inheritance. Dan 11:45 locates the ṣəḇî at a "holy mountain" between the seas (Zion / Jerusalem). Within the Hebrew canon ṣəḇî + ʼerets / har-qodesh is a fixed designation for the covenant-land of Israel, with Zion/Jerusalem as its center.

### 5. Substantival Absolute (with definite article, location)
Dan 8:9's *hāṣ-ṣəḇî* stands alone ("toward **the glory**") without the word *ʼerets*. The parallel directional triplet — south, east, *hāṣ-ṣəḇî* — treats "the Glory [land]" as a fourth geographical term on the compass. Dan 11:16, 41, 45 then make the referent explicit by adding *ʼerets* or *har*. The elliptical Dan 8:9 usage is resolved text-internally by the fuller Dan 11 phrases and by Ezek 20:6, 15 / Jer 3:19.
