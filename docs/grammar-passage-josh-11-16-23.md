## Clause Structure

The pericope's discourse skeleton (from `hebrew_parser.py --clause`) shows a deliberate alternation between **wayyiqtol foreground** (mainline narrative steps) and **WX-QATAL / xQt background** (summary, parenthetical, explanation). The pattern is what Niccacci calls the "summary + circumstantial" closing block of a narrative unit.

| 22 | **xQtX** (לֹֽא־נֹותַ֣ר עֲנָקִ֔ים…) + **xQt0** (רַ֗ק בְּעַזָּ֛ה בְּגַ֥ת וּבְאַשְׁדֹּ֖וד נִשְׁאָֽרוּ) | N | Double negated/restrictive qatal — background summary of the Anakim episode |
| 23 | **WayX** (וַיִּקַּ֨ח יְהֹושֻׁ֜עַ…) + **xQtX** (אֲשֶׁ֨ר דִּבֶּ֣ר יְהוָה֮…) + **WayX** (וַיִּתְּנָהּ֩…) + **WXQt** (וְהָאָ֥רֶץ שָׁקְטָ֖ה מִמִּלְחָמָֽה) | N | Final foreground doublet (וַיִּקַּ֨ח + וַיִּתְּנָהּ֩) bracketing the pericope back to v. 16, closed by **WXQt** circumstantial ("and the land rested from war") |

**Construct chains:** אֶ֣רֶץ הַגֹּ֔שֶׁן (land of Goshen, v. 16); בִּקְעַ֣ת הַלְּבָנֹ֔ון (valley of Lebanon, v. 17); הַר־חֶרְמֹ֑ון (Mount Hermon, v. 17); הַר יִשְׂרָאֵ֖ל (mountain of Israel, vv. 16, 21); בְּנֵ֣י יִשְׂרָאֵ֔ל (sons of Israel, vv. 19, 22); הַ֣ר יְהוּדָ֔ה / הַ֣ר יִשְׂרָאֵ֑ל (hill-country of Judah/Israel, v. 21); אֶ֖רֶץ בְּנֵ֣י יִשְׂרָאֵ֑ל (land of the sons of Israel, v. 22).

## Key Grammatical Features

### 1. The wayyiqtol "לקח — לקח — בוא — כרת — לקח — נתן" chain as narrative backbone (vv. 16, 21, 23)

- **Form:** Four mainline wayyiqtols distributed across the pericope — וַיִּקַּ֨ח (v. 16, Qal.Wayq.3ms of לקח), וַיָּבֹ֨א (v. 21, Qal.Wayq.3ms of בוא), וַיַּכְרֵ֤ת (v. 21, Hiphil.Wayq.3ms of כרת), וַיִּקַּ֨ח (v. 23, Qal.Wayq.3ms of לקח) + וַיִּתְּנָהּ֩ (v. 23, Qal.Wayq.3ms.+3fs of נתן). In v. 17 the embedded doublet וַיַּכֵּ֖ם / וַיְמִיתֵֽם (Hiphil.Wayq.3ms.+3mp of נכה / מות) continues the foreground within the geographic section.
- **Function:** Waltke–O'Connor (*IBHS* §33.2.1) and Niccacci call this the **foreground narrative register** — the wayyiqtol is the "waw-relative" prefix form whose sole discourse function is to advance the mainline. Every other clause type in vv. 16–23 is pragmatically background relative to these wayyiqtols.
- **Inclusio by repeated verb:** The first and final wayyiqtols are the **same verb** — וַיִּקַּ֨ח יְהֹושֻׁ֜עַ אֶת־כָּל־הָאָ֣רֶץ (v. 16) ↔ וַיִּקַּ֨ח יְהֹושֻׁ֜עַ אֶת־כָּל־הָאָ֗רֶץ (v. 23). This is a deliberate lexical inclusio: the pericope opens and closes with the same three-word clause (*and-took Joshua DO all the-land*), with everything between them serving as expansion, qualification, and theological comment on that single claim. The two wayyiqtols of v. 23 then add the redistribution (וַיִּתְּנָהּ֩) before the final **WXQt** closural formula וְהָאָ֥רֶץ שָׁקְטָ֖ה מִמִּלְחָמָֽה.
- **Geographic scope:** The wayyiqtol verb takes six consecutive object-phrases in v. 16 (הָאָ֣רֶץ הַזֹּ֗את / הָהָ֤ר / הַנֶּ֨גֶב֙ / אֶ֣רֶץ הַגֹּ֔שֶׁן / הַשְּׁפֵלָ֖ה / הָעֲרָבָ֑ה / הַ֥ר יִשְׂרָאֵ֖ל + וּשְׁפֵלָתֹֽה) — a cartographic tiling of the land, with the 3fs pronominal suffix on וּשְׁפֵלָתֹֽה ("its lowland") folding the final region back onto הַ֥ר יִשְׂרָאֵ֖ל. The conjunction waw + אֵת/אֶת string (וְאֶת…וְאֵת…וְאֶת…וְאֶת…וְאֶת) is the stock Hebrew **syndetic list idiom** for a closed enumeration (GKC §154a n. 1; Joüon–Muraoka §177n).
- **Significance:** The wayyiqtol chain, by itself, would tell a complete military story ("Joshua took the land … came at that time … cut off the Anakim … took the whole land … and gave it"). Everything the narrator inserts between those wayyiqtols — the durational xQtX of v. 18, the Gibeonite-exception of v. 19, the divine-hardening parenthesis of v. 20, the Anakim-remnant of v. 22 — is **grammatically subordinated** to this chain, even when it occupies the majority of the verses.
- **Grammar reference:** [syntax-waw-conjunction](../hebrew/syntax-waw-conjunction.md); foreground vs. background distinction in [syntax-wx-qatal-circumstantial](../hebrew/syntax-wx-qatal-circumstantial.md).

### 2. The xQtX durational summary: יָמִ֣ים רַבִּ֗ים עָשָׂ֧ה יְהֹושֻׁ֛עַ (v. 18)

- **Form:** Fronted adverbial-accusative יָמִ֣ים רַבִּ֗ים ("many days") + Qal.Perf.3ms עָשָׂ֧ה + Subj יְהֹושֻׁ֛עַ + Objc כָּל־הַמְּלָכִ֥ים הָאֵ֖לֶּה + cognate object מִלְחָמָֽה. BHSA tags this as a single **xQtX** clause with `[Time]…[Pred]…[Subj]…[Cmpl]…[Objc]` slots — a fronted-time qatal, not a wayyiqtol.
- **Function:** Niccacci classifies the fronted-time + qatal construction as a **summary / recapitulative background clause** (Niccacci §23). The break from wayyiqtol register is the signal: the narrator is not advancing the storyline to a new event, but **compressing a long span** into a single durational remark. The KJV rendering "*a long time*" tracks this — it is not "then, on a day, Joshua made war" but "over many days Joshua was engaged in making war."
- **Cognate-accusative object:** עשׂה + מִלְחָמָה is a stock Hebrew **cognate-noun idiom** for "wage war" (Gen 14:2; Deut 20:10, 12, 19–20; Josh 11:18; 1 Chr 22:8) — lexically parallel to the paronomastic-nedher construction in Jdg 11:30 ([jdg-11-30-31](jdg-11-30-31.md)). The pairing of עשׂה ("make/do") with מִלְחָמָה ("war") treats warfare as a durative action-nominal, naturally compatible with the durational יָמִ֣ים רַבִּ֗ים.
- **Significance:** V. 18 is the first moment the narrator slows the wayyiqtol camera. After v. 17's breathless wayyiqtol chain of verbs of capture/strike/kill, the xQtX steps back to say "this was not a single day — this took many days." The construction prepares the reader to receive v. 19's exception and v. 20's theological explanation, both of which are also backgrounded.

### 3. The Gibeonite exception: לֹֽא־הָיְתָ֣ה עִ֗יר אֲשֶׁ֤ר הִשְׁלִ֨ימָה֙ (v. 19)

- **Form:** Clause-1 **xQtX** with fronted negation — Neg לֹֽא + Qal.Perf.3fs הָיְתָ֣ה + Subj עִ֗יר + Adju בִּלְתִּ֥י הַחִוִּ֖י יֹשְׁבֵ֣י גִבְעֹ֑ון. Clause-2 **xQt0** with relative — Rela אֲשֶׁ֤ר + Hiphil.Perf.3fs הִשְׁלִ֨ימָה֙ + Cmpl אֶל־בְּנֵ֣י יִשְׂרָאֵ֔ל. Clause-3 **xQt0** with fronted object — Objc אֶת־הַכֹּ֖ל + Qal.Perf.3p לָקְח֥וּ + Cmpl בַמִּלְחָמָֽה.
- **Key lexemes:** הִשְׁלִ֨ימָה֙ is Hiphil perfect 3fs of שׁלם (H7999), "to make peace, enter into a peace-covenant" (Deut 20:12; Josh 9:15; 10:1, 4; 11:19; 2 Sam 10:19; 1 Kgs 22:44). The Hiphil is causative — "cause (a treaty) to be complete / made peace-with." The subject is feminine (עִ֗יר "city") and the feminine perfect matches.
- **The exception particle בִּלְתִּ֥י:** ETCBC tags בלת as a Noun.s.Cst of the lexeme בלת ("failure, not-being"), but functionally in this slot it operates as a **preposition/conjunction of exception** — "except, save for" (GKC §163c; Joüon–Muraoka §160m; BDB 116a "except"). The parser's "failure" gloss is a lexical-root reconstruction (the same lexeme underlies v. 20's לְבִלְתִּ֥י "so that not") — pragmatically בִּלְתִּ֥י governs הַחִוִּ֖י יֹשְׁבֵ֣י גִבְעֹ֑ון as an **exceptive phrase** tied to the negated universal "there was not a city." Compare Num 32:12 בִּלְתִּ֥י כָּלֵ֣ב בֶּן־יְפֻנֶּ֗ה, with the same syntactic profile (negative universal + בִּלְתִּ֥י + named exception).
- **Three-clause rhetorical symmetry:** Clause-1 states the universal negation ("no city made peace"), clause-2 expands the subject's action via relative ("[a city] that made-peace with Israel"), clause-3 fronts the object אֶת־הַכֹּ֖ל ("the whole") and asserts the positive counterpart ("they took all-of-it in battle"). The three qatals (הָיְתָ֣ה / הִשְׁלִ֨ימָה֙ / לָקְח֥וּ) are all **background** — none is wayyiqtol — so the entire v. 19 is a parenthetical qualification of v. 16's wayyiqtol וַיִּקַּ֨ח, not a fresh narrative step.
- **The Gibeonite datum as grammatical exception, not plot-event:** Joshua 9 narrated the Gibeonite treaty as a *deceived* covenant that Israel nonetheless honored (Josh 9:15, 19, 26–27). In Josh 11:19 that prior narrative is not re-narrated but **cited as a grammatical exception**: the Gibeonites stand outside the negative universal "no city made peace" precisely because Josh 9 already established they *did* make peace, by subterfuge. The xQt0 בִּלְתִּ֥י-phrase is the grammar doing the theological housekeeping — the author acknowledges the anomaly without disturbing v. 16's universal claim וַיִּקַּ֨ח…אֶת־כָּל־הָאָ֣רֶץ.
- **Significance:** The Gibeonite clause is **grammatically marked as exception** and **pragmatically marked as background**, both at once. It is the single crack in the otherwise sealed universal of conquest, and the narrator signals that status by (a) refusing to put it in wayyiqtol and (b) flagging it with the exceptive particle. Theological readings that invoke Josh 11:19 for a doctrine of peace-as-option cannot do so without noting that the construction is *negative* and *exceptive* — a denied universal with one named exception — not a generic invitation to peace.

### 4. The WX-QATAL theological parenthesis: כִּ֣י מֵאֵ֣ת יְהוָ֣ה הָיְתָ֡ה לְחַזֵּ֣ק אֶת־לִבָּם (v. 20)

- **Form:** The verse's spine is **xQt0** — Conj כִּ֣י + PreC מֵאֵ֣ת יְהוָ֣ה + Pred הָיְתָ֡ה (Qal.Perf.3fs of היה, feminine because the null subject is "the-thing" / "it"). The predicate-complement מֵאֵ֣ת יְהוָ֣ה ("from-with YHWH") is fronted before the qatal verb. This is a **כִּי-headed explanatory xQt0** — a background causal clause, not a wayyiqtol. BHSA lists it as Clause 1, type `xQt0`, domain N.
- **Infinitival purpose scaffold:** Five successive InfC clauses specify the content and purpose of what "was from YHWH" —
  1. **InfC** לְחַזֵּ֣ק אֶת־לִבָּם֩ (Prep+Piel.InfCon of חזק + Objc) — "to strengthen their heart"
  2. **InfC** לִקְרַ֨את הַמִּלְחָמָ֤ה אֶת־יִשְׂרָאֵל֙ (Prep+Qal.InfCon of קרא + Objc) — "to meet the war-with-Israel"
  3. **InfC** לְמַ֣עַן הַֽחֲרִימָ֔ם (Prep+Hiphil.InfCon of חרם + 3mp suffix) — "in order to devote them to destruction"
  4. **InfC** לְבִלְתִּ֥י הֱיֹות־לָהֶ֖ם תְּחִנָּ֑ה (Prep+בלת+Qal.InfCon of היה + Prep.+3mp + Noun) — "so that there be no favor/supplication to them"
  5. **InfC** כִּ֚י לְמַ֣עַן הַשְׁמִידָ֔ם (Conj+Prep+Hiphil.InfCon of שׁמד + 3mp suffix) — "for in order to destroy them"
- **Closed by a xQtX comparative clause:** כַּ אֲשֶׁ֛ר צִוָּ֥ה יְהוָ֖ה אֶת־מֹשֶֽׁה (Clause 7, type `xQtX`) — "just as YHWH commanded Moses." The Piel.Perf.3ms צִוָּ֥ה anchors the whole divine program in the already-given Mosaic instruction.
- **"Hardening of the heart" as Piel לְחַזֵּ֣ק אֶת־לִבָּם֩:** The verb is **חזק in Piel** (H2388, factitive/causative "cause to be strong"). This is the classic Exodus lexeme for pharaoh-hardening (Ex 4:21; 9:12; 10:20, 27; 11:10; 14:4, 8, 17 — all Piel חזק with YHWH as subject or implicit agent). The Piel InfCon לְחַזֵּ֣ק + direct-object אֶת־לִבָּם֩ (Noun.ms.+3mp) makes the idiom **morphologically identical** to Ex 14:4 וְחִזַּקְתִּ֣י אֶת־לֵב־פַּרְעֹ֗ה. The narrator is quoting the Exodus hardening-formula and applying it to the Canaanite coalition kings. This is *the* theological index-verse by which Joshua's conquest is grammatically bound to the Exodus plague cycle.
- **Why this is a WX-QATAL / xQt0 and not a wayyiqtol:** The כִּי-headed clause with fronted PreC and qatal verb is **by construction** non-sequential. It explains *why* the universal-conquest claim of v. 19 held — the explanation is logical and theological, not temporal. Niccacci (*Syntax of the Verb* §§17–23) treats כִּי + fronted-phrase + qatal as the **standard Hebrew causal-background construction** in narrative. Had the narrator written וַיְהִי מֵאֵ֣ת יְהוָ֣ה לְחַזֵּ֣ק, he would have turned divine hardening into a narrative step ("and there came to pass, from YHWH, a hardening…"); instead the xQt0 treats it as the **reason-frame behind** the events of vv. 16–19.
- **The nested negative-purpose לְבִלְתִּ֥י:** לְבִלְתִּ֥י + Qal.InfCon הֱיֹות (from היה) + לָהֶ֖ם + תְּחִנָּ֑ה is "so that there not come to be, to them, supplication/favor." The nominal תְּחִנָּ֑ה (H8467) from חנן ("show favor") is the abstract for the favor-act. Joüon–Muraoka §160l and GKC §165b treat לְבִלְתִּ֥י + InfCon as the stock **negative-purpose clause** ("lest, so that…not"). This is the third explicit rejection-of-mercy construction in the infinitival chain, sandwiched between the two לְמַ֣עַן clauses of active destruction.
- **Double לְמַ֣עַן + Hiphil.InfCon of חרם / שׁמד:** The framing purpose verbs are both causative-Hiphil infinitives with 3mp suffixes — הַחֲרִימָ֔ם ("devote-them-to-destruction," Hiphil of חרם H2763; see [H2763-charam](../../word-studies/H2763-charam.md), [H2764-cherem](../../word-studies/H2764-cherem.md)) and הַשְׁמִידָ֔ם ("destroy-them," Hiphil of שׁמד H8045). These are the two technical-theological verbs of the *ḥērem* ban (Deut 7:2, 23–26; 20:16–18; 1 Sam 15:3, 8, 9, 15, 18, 20, 21 — see [1sa-15-1-33](1sa-15-1-33.md)). Their appearance as infinitival complements of the divine-hardening clause explicitly subordinates the military extermination to YHWH's prior decision to make the kings fight.
- **Significance:** V. 20 is the pericope's theological linchpin. Grammatically it is a **background parenthesis**, not a narrative step — but the background is load-bearing. The conquest's near-universal success (v. 16, v. 23) *and* its peculiar absence of peace-treaties (v. 19) are both explained by a single xQt0 clause whose predicate-complement מֵאֵ֣ת יְהוָ֣ה ("from-with YHWH") places YHWH as the causal source. The construction answers the question v. 19 would otherwise leave hanging ("why did no city make peace?") with the Piel-חזק Exodus-hardening lexeme + the Deuteronomic *ḥērem* vocabulary + a Mosaic-command anchor (כַּ אֲשֶׁ֛ר צִוָּ֥ה יְהוָ֖ה אֶת־מֹשֶֽׁה).
- **Grammar reference:** [syntax-wx-qatal-circumstantial](../hebrew/syntax-wx-qatal-circumstantial.md) — background-qatal halts the wayyiqtol chain to supply circumstance/cause; [stem-hiphil](../hebrew/stem-hiphil.md) (for הַחֲרִימָ֔ם, הַשְׁמִידָ֔ם).
- **Word study cross-refs:** [H2763-charam](../../word-studies/H2763-charam.md), [H2764-cherem](../../word-studies/H2764-cherem.md).

### 5. The Anakim episode as internal wayyiqtol sub-unit (vv. 21–22)

- **Form:** V. 21 resumes the **WayX** register with temporal hook בָּעֵ֣ת הַהִ֗יא ("at that time") — a stock Hebrew discourse device for embedding a sub-episode without breaking from foreground narrative (cf. Gen 21:22; Deut 10:1; Josh 6:26; 1 Kgs 8:65; 14:1). The two wayyiqtols וַיָּבֹ֨א + וַיַּכְרֵ֤ת carry the Anakim extermination.
- **Closing xQtX:** Clause 3 of v. 21 is xQtX — fronted PP עִם־עָרֵיהֶ֖ם + Hiphil.Perf.3ms.+3mp הֶחֱרִימָ֥ם + redundant Subj יְהֹושֻֽׁעַ. The verb הֶחֱרִימָ֥ם is the *same ḥērem Hiphil* that appeared as infinitive in v. 20's divine-hardening chain — here in its finite form, performed by Joshua. Grammar thus **executes** in v. 21 what v. 20 announced as YHWH's purpose.
- **Niphal-remnant background in v. 22:** לֹֽא־נֹותַ֣ר עֲנָקִ֔ים (xQtX with Niphal.Perf.3ms of יתר "be-left") + רַ֗ק בְּעַזָּ֛ה בְּגַ֥ת וּבְאַשְׁדֹּ֖וד נִשְׁאָֽרוּ (xQt0 with Niphal.Perf.3p of שׁאר "remain"). The two Niphals are the classic **passive/middle of state** — "none was-left … only they remained." The negation + restriction (לֹא … רַק) is the standard **gnomic "none … except" idiom** (GKC §159cc; Joüon §172e).
- **Significance:** The Anakim sub-unit is a *pericope-within-the-pericope* with its own wayyiqtol opening (v. 21) and xQtX/Niphal background closing (vv. 21c–22). This structurally mirrors the larger pericope (v. 16 wayyiqtol opening → v. 23 wayyiqtol inclusio with intermediate background). The two Niphal perfects of v. 22 soften the universal claim — the Anakim are *not* totally extirpated; they survive in Gaza, Gath, Ashdod — setting up the Goliath-of-Gath narrative of 1 Sam 17. The grammar flags the exception in the same way the Gibeonite exception of v. 19 is flagged: by **background-register qatal**, not by wayyiqtol.
- **Grammar reference:** [stem-niphal](../hebrew/stem-niphal.md) (for נֹותַ֣ר / נִשְׁאָֽרוּ as passive/medio-passive state); [syntax-wx-qatal-circumstantial](../hebrew/syntax-wx-qatal-circumstantial.md) (for xQtX + לֹא as negated-background summary).

### 6. Closural formula: וְהָאָ֥רֶץ שָׁקְטָ֖ה מִמִּלְחָמָֽה (v. 23)

- **Form:** Clause 4 of v. 23 is **WXQt** (BHSA) — Conj וְ + Subj הָ אָ֥רֶץ + Pred שָׁקְטָ֖ה (Qal.Perf.3fs of שׁקט, "be quiet, at rest") + Cmpl מִ מִּלְחָמָֽה. Word order waw + X + qatal is **WX-QATAL** — a background circumstantial clause.
- **Function:** This is a **closural formula** — a standard Hebrew discourse device marking the end of a narrative unit by shifting from foreground wayyiqtol to state-describing WX-qatal. The same formula closes Josh 14:15 (הָאָ֥רֶץ שָׁקְטָ֖ה מִמִּלְחָמָֽה) and the thematically parallel rest-formulas of Judg 3:11, 3:30, 5:31, 8:28 (וַתִּשְׁקֹ֥ט הָאָ֖רֶץ… in Judges use a wayyiqtol form of the same verb, but still clausing a state of rest).
- **Qal שׁקט as perfect of state:** Waltke–O'Connor §30.3 and §30.5.3 classify stative-root qatals as **"perfect of state"** — the suffix-conjugation of a stative root denotes a resultant state that obtains at the narrative's reference time. שָׁקְטָ֖ה thus is not "and the land (became) quiet (on a day)" but "and the land was (already) at rest / was in a state of rest." The WX-fronting of הָאָ֥רֶץ + stative qatal + *min*-of-separation מִמִּלְחָמָֽה ("from war") yields a stative resultant predicate.

The same noun מִלְחָמָה frames the pericope's middle-to-end movement: the long durational war of v. 18 terminates in the state of rest of v. 23.
- **Significance:** The entire pericope is grammatically contoured as **mainline conquest → background explanation → mainline execution → state of rest**. The final WX-QATAL is not an event but the *absence* of events — the narrator closes the Conquest panel of Joshua by stepping out of wayyiqtol altogether and reporting the resultant state.
