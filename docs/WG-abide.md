# WG: Abide / Dwell / Remain

## Overview
The concept of remaining, dwelling, and abiding in the NT is expressed through a family of verbs built on the root μένω (meno) plus related οἰκ- (oik-) dwelling words. These range from simple physical staying to deep relational/spiritual indwelling, with compound prefixes modifying the base meaning in distinct directions.

## Roles in the Concept

- **Core/General:** meno (μένω) — the unmarked member; covers physical staying, relational abiding, and permanent endurance
- **Permanent Residence:** katoikeo (κατοικέω) — settled, established dwelling in a place
- **Inhabitation:** oikeo (οἰκέω) — occupying a house; when used of the Spirit, implies the believer as a temple
- **Indwelling:** enoikeo (ἐνοικέω) — dwelling within; used exclusively of God/Spirit/word within believers
- **Constancy:** diameno (διαμένω) — remaining without change; what endures through time
- **Persistence:** epimeno (ἐπιμένω) — continuing on beyond what might be expected (positive or negative persistence)
- **Endurance:** hupomeno (ὑπομένω) — remaining under trial; patience under suffering

## Key Passages Where Multiple Group Concepts Appear Together
- **Rom 8:9-11** — oikeo + enoikeo: "the Spirit of God dwell (oikeo) in you... the Spirit... that dwelleth (enoikeo) in you"
- **Jhn 14:10-17** — meno in rapid succession: Father abides in Christ (v10), Spirit will abide with disciples (v16-17)
- **1Jo 4:12-16** — meno used 7x in 5 verses for mutual indwelling of God and believer through love
