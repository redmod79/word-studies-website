## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same:**
- The word denotes a person set apart by divine appointment through anointing
- Royal and priestly dimensions both present in OT and NT
- The construction "the LORD's anointed" / "his Christ" carries the same inviolability and covenant-loyalty weight
- Opposition to the anointed one (Psa 2) remains the frame for NT persecution narratives

**What shifts:**
- In OT, mashiyach is almost always used as a description (the anointed priest, the anointed king); in NT, Χριστός functions increasingly as a proper name ("Jesus Christ") rather than a title
- Dan 9:25–26 establishes mashiyach as a specific eschatological individual; NT authors treat this as fulfilled in Jesus
- The Aramaic loanword Μεσσίας appears in John 1:41 and 4:25 as a transliteration, showing the Jewish community preserved the Hebrew term alongside the Greek calque
