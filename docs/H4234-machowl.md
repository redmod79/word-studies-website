## Lexicon Definition

From H2342 (chuwl); a (round) dance: — dance(-cing).

**Etymology note:** Strong's derives machowl from H2342 חוּל (chuwl), "to twist or whirl (in a circular or spiral manner), i.e. to dance." The feminine cognate H4246 מְחֹלָה (mechowlah), "a dance," is explicitly identified as "feminine of H4234." Both nouns share the mem-preformative pattern (מ) indicating a noun of action or place derived from a verbal root.

The alternative derivation from H2490 חָלַל (chalal), "to bore, pierce; to play (the flute)," would yield a meaning of "pipe" or "wind instrument" (cf. H2485 חָלִיל, chaliyl, "a flute, as perforated," which *is* derived from chalal). However, the standard lexical tradition (BDB, Strong's, Gesenius) uniformly assigns machowl to the chuwl root, not the chalal root. The distinction is significant: H2485 chaliyl ("pipe/flute"), the actual instrument word from the chalal root, exists as a separate lexeme with its own 6 OT occurrences (1 Sam 10:5; 1 Kgs 1:40; Isa 5:12; 30:29; Jer 48:36).

It never substitutes for an instrument in a list; rather, it appears as the bodily activity done *with* instruments.

The LXX consistently renders machowl with choros (G5525), a word meaning "round dance" (whence English "chorus/choir"). This confirms the dance meaning rather than any instrumental sense: the LXX translators, working centuries before the Masoretic vowel pointing, understood machowl as denoting circular dance. G5525 choros appears in the NT only at Luke 15:25 (prodigal son parable: "he heard music and dancing").

**Reverse mapping (G5525 → Hebrew sources):** The Greek choros maps back to H4246 mechowlah (5 co-occ.) and H4234 machowl (4 co-occ.), confirming these two cognate Hebrew words as the semantic core of "round dance" in the OT.
