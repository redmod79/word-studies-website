## Semantic Categories

### 1. Official Archival Records (Plural Emphatic — דָּכְרָנַיָּא)
- **Ezra 4:15** — "That search may be made in the book of the records of thy fathers: so shalt thou find in the book of the records, and know that this city is a rebellious city, and hurtful unto kings and provinces, and that they have moved sedition within the same of old time: for which cause was this city destroyed."

The phrase סְפַר דָּכְרָנַיָּא (sᵉphar dâkrânayâʼ) = "book of the records" — a royal archive of historical memoranda. Used twice in this verse, referring to Persian/Babylonian administrative records that document past political events.

### 2. Single Written Memorandum (Emphatic — דִּכְרוֺנָה)
- **Ezra 6:2** — "And there was found at Achmetha, in the palace that is in the province of the Medes, a roll, and therein was a record thus written:"

Here dikrôwnâh (emphatic singular) denotes the specific memorandum of Cyrus's decree found at Ecbatana. Paired with מְגִלָּה (mᵉgillâh, H4040) "roll/scroll" — the physical container bearing the official record.
