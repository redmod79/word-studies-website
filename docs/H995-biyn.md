## Semantic Categories

### 1. Mental Discernment / Comprehension
The core meaning: perceiving, grasping, understanding truth or reality.
- **Dan 9:2** — "I Daniel understood (biyn, Qal) by books the number of the years"
- **Dan 12:8** — "I heard, but I understood (biyn, Qal) not"
- **Dan 12:10** — "none of the wicked shall understand; but the wise shall understand"
- **Isa 6:9** — "Hear ye indeed, but understand (biyn, Qal) not"

### 2. Causing Understanding / Revelation (Hiphil)
God or an angel imparts understanding to a human recipient.
- **Dan 8:16** — "Gabriel, make this man to understand (biyn, Hiphil) the vision"
- **Dan 10:14** — "I am come to make thee understand (biyn, Hiphil) what shall befall thy people"
- **Dan 9:22** — "he informed me (biyn, Hiphil), and talked with me, and said, O Daniel, I am now come forth to give thee skill and understanding"
- **Neh 8:8** — "gave the sense, and caused them to understand (biyn, Hiphil) the reading"

### 3. Careful Consideration / Attentive Study (Hithpael)
Deliberate, sustained mental engagement with something complex.
- **Dan 8:5** — "as I was considering (biyn, Hiphil participle), behold, an he goat came"
- **Jer 2:10** — "send unto Kedar, and consider diligently"
- **Job 37:14** — "stand still, and consider the wondrous works of God"
- **Psa 119:95** — "the wicked have waited for me to destroy me: but I will consider thy testimonies"

### 4. Discerning Character / Quality of a Person (Niphal Participle)
The niphal participle nabown functions as an adjective: "discerning, prudent."
- **Gen 41:33** — "let Pharaoh look out a man discreet (nabown) and wise"
- **1 Sam 16:18** — David described as "prudent (nabown) in matters"
- **Pro 16:21** — "the wise in heart shall be called prudent (nabown)"
- **Isa 5:21** — "Woe unto them that are wise in their own eyes, and prudent in their own sight!"

### 5. Instructing / Teaching Others
The causative use where one person enables another's understanding.
- **Dan 11:33** — "they that understand (biyn, Hiphil) among the people shall instruct many"
- **Neh 8:7** — "the Levites, caused the people to understand the law"
- **1 Chr 25:8** — "the teacher as the scholar" (biyn = teacher)
- **Deut 32:10** — "he instructed him (biyn, Piel), he kept him as the apple of his eye"

### 6. Attending / Regarding
Directing attention toward something; the opposite is failing to attend.
- **Job 30:20** — "I stand up, and thou regardest me not"
- **Dan 11:37** — "Neither shall he regard the God of his fathers, nor the desire of women, nor regard any god"
- **Pro 29:7** — "the wicked regardeth not to know it"
