Neshamah carries three main registers in the OT: the breath God breathes into man at creation (Gen 2:7),
God's destructive blast in judgment (Job 4:9; Ps 18:15; Isa 30:33), and the inner spirit that enables
human wisdom and consciousness (Prov 20:27; Job 32:8).

**Key OT passages:**
- **Gen 2:7** — "breathed into his nostrils the breath (neshamah) of life; and man became a living soul" — paradigmatic creation use
- **Job 32:8** — "the inspiration (neshamah) of the Almighty giveth them understanding" — neshamah as the illuminating inner spark
- **Isa 30:33** — "the breath (neshamah) of the LORD, like a stream of brimstone, doth kindle it" — divine destructive force

## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same:**
- The breath as a direct divine gift — both OT and NT associate pnoē with God as its source
- The physical, concrete character — pnoē retains the "respiration" nuance throughout

**What shifts:**
- In the OT, neshamah can be wrathful/destructive (God's blast). NT pnoē lacks this edge — it appears only in life-giving or creative contexts
- The rare NT usage (2x) suggests pnoē is eclipsed by the broader πνεῦμα (pneuma) for "spirit," just as neshamah is the narrower breath word while rûwach is the broader Hebrew spirit word
