## Lexicon Definition

From πείθω (G3982); persuasion, i.e. credence; moral conviction (of religious truth, or the truthfulness of God or a religious teacher), especially reliance upon Christ for salvation; abstractly, constancy in such profession; by extension, the system of religious (Gospel) truth itself.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Active Trust / Confidence in God
Personal reliance on God's character and promises.
- **Matt 8:10** — "I have not found so great faith, no, not in Israel"
- **Mark 11:22** — "Have faith in God"
- **Luke 7:50** — "Thy faith hath saved thee; go in peace"
- **Rom 10:17** — "So then faith cometh by hearing, and hearing by the word of God"

### 2. Justifying Faith (Means of Righteousness)
Faith as the instrument through which justification/salvation is received.
- **Rom 3:28** — "a man is justified by faith without the deeds of the law"
- **Rom 5:1** — "being justified by faith, we have peace with God"
- **Gal 2:16** — "not justified by the works of the law, but by the faith of Jesus Christ"
- **Eph 2:8** — "by grace are ye saved through faith; and that not of yourselves"

### 3. The Body of Belief ("The Faith")
The objective content of Christian teaching as a system.
- **Acts 6:7** — "a great company of the priests were obedient to the faith"
- **Acts 14:22** — "exhorting them to continue in the faith"
- **1 Tim 4:1** — "some shall depart from the faith"
- **Jude 1:3** — "contend for the faith which was once delivered unto the saints"

### 4. Faithfulness / Fidelity
The quality of being trustworthy or loyal.
- **Gal 5:22** — "the fruit of the Spirit is love, joy, peace... faith" (KJV; faithfulness)
- **Titus 2:10** — "shewing all good fidelity" (only KJV instance as "fidelity")
- **Rev 2:13** — "thou holdest fast my name, and hast not denied my faith"

### 5. Exemplary Faith (Hebrews 11 Catalogue)
Historical demonstration of faith through obedience.
- **Heb 11:1** — "faith is the substance of things hoped for, the evidence of things not seen"
- **Heb 11:7** — "By faith Noah... prepared an ark"
- **Heb 11:17** — "By faith Abraham, when he was tried, offered up Isaac"
- **Heb 12:2** — "Looking unto Jesus the author and finisher of our faith"

### 6. Assurance / Proof
Objective evidence or ground for confidence.
- **Acts 17:31** — "he hath given assurance unto all men, in that he hath raised him from the dead"

### 7. Spiritual Gift
Extraordinary faith as a specific charism.
- **1 Cor 12:9** — "To another faith by the same Spirit"
- **1 Cor 13:2** — "though I have all faith, so that I could remove mountains"
- **1 Cor 13:13** — "now abideth faith, hope, charity, these three"
