# WG: Family / Kinship

## Overview
The Hebrew family cluster covers the core vocabulary of the immediate family unit —
offspring, parent, and stages of childhood. Five words form the basic grid:
ben (son) and bath (daughter) as the child pair; ab (father) as the generating
parent; and yeled and naʻar marking early stages of life from birth to youth.
Together they appear in every family narrative, genealogy, covenant formula,
and wisdom address in the OT.

## Relationships

### Shared Roots
- **H1121 ben, H1323 bath, H1004 bayit (house)** — all derive from H1129 בָּנָה (banah, to build): the family as "that which is built"; a son as "builder of the family name"
- **H3206 yeled** — from H3205 יָלַד (yalad, to bear/bring forth): the child as "the one born"
- **H5288 naʻar** — possibly from a root meaning "to shake out, be active": the restless, active youth

## Roles in the Concept

- **Primary offspring (male):** ben (H1121) — covers son from birth through adulthood, guild membership, covenant designation, and messianic sonship
- **Primary offspring (female):** bath (H1323) — parallel to ben; same range but feminine
- **Generating parent:** ab (H1) — the father who names and transmits covenant and lineage; the correlative of both ben and bath
- **Stage 1 — Birth/infancy:** yeled (H3206) — the newly born; often used of infants and small children; Moses in the basket (Exo 2:6, 10)
- **Stage 2 — Youth/active boy:** naʻar (H5288) — the older boy or young man; can be a servant; Joseph at 17 (Gen 37:2); Saul's young attendant (1Sa 14:1)

## Key Passages Where Multiple Group Words Appear Together

- **Gen 37:35** — "all his sons (ben) and all his daughters (bath) rose up to comfort him [Jacob/ab]" — the full family cluster
- **Exo 10:9** — "we will go with our young (naʻar) and with our old, with our sons (ben) and with our daughters (bath)" — complete family enumeration
- **Isa 9:6** — "unto us a child (yeled) is born, unto us a son (ben) is given: and the government shall be upon his shoulder: and his name shall be called...The everlasting Father (ab)" — three group words in one messianic announcement
- **Exo 2:10** — "the child (yeled) grew, and she brought him unto Pharaoh's daughter (bath), and he became her son (ben)" — Moses narrative using all three child/offspring words
- **Gen 9:18** — "the sons (bene) of Noah...were Shem, and Ham, and Japheth: and Ham is the father (ab) of Canaan"
