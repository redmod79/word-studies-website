## Lexicon Definition

A prolonged form of a primary στάω stáō (of the same meaning, and used as an alternate in certain tenses); to stand (transitively or intransitively), used in various applications (literally or figuratively):
- **(intransitive)** to stand, be standing, stand still, stand firm
- **(transitive)** to set, place, present; to appoint; to establish, confirm, make to stand
- **(passive)** to be set, be placed; to be established, be confirmed

The **tense/voice** carries the semantic weight: perfect active = intransitive "is/was standing"; aorist active = transitive "set, placed" or punctiliar "stood up"; aorist/future passive = "was positioned, will be established."

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Physical Posture — Occupying a Location
The most literal use: a person, object, or being is stationary in an upright position.
- **Matt 6:5** — "they love to pray standing in the synagogues and in the corners of the streets"
- **Luke 1:11** — "there appeared unto him an angel of the Lord standing on the right side of the altar of incense"
- **John 20:14** — "she turned herself back, and saw Jesus standing, and knew not that it was Jesus"
- **Acts 9:7** — "the men which journeyed with him stood speechless, hearing a voice"

### 2. To Set / Place (Causative Transitive)
The aorist active is transitive: to cause someone to stand, to place or present.
- **Matt 4:5** — "the devil taketh him up... and setteth him on a pinnacle of the temple"
- **Matt 18:2** — "Jesus called a little child unto him, and set him in the midst of them"
- **Acts 4:7** — "when they had set them in the midst, they asked, By what power..."
- **Acts 5:27** — "when they had brought them, they set them before the council"

### 3. To Stop / Stand Still
Bringing motion to cessation; halting.
- **Matt 20:32** — "Jesus stood still, and called them, and said, What will ye..."
- **Luke 7:14** — "they that bare him stood still. And he said, Young man, I say unto thee, Arise"
- **Acts 8:38** — "he commanded the chariot to stand still: and they went down both into the water"

### 4. To Stand Firm / Not Fall (Figurative Stability)
Spiritual or moral steadfastness; structural cohesion; not being overthrown.
- **Mark 3:24–26** — "that kingdom cannot stand... that house cannot stand... he cannot stand"
- **Rom 5:2** — "this grace wherein we stand, and rejoice in hope of the glory of God"
- **1 Cor 10:12** — "let him that thinketh he standeth take heed lest he fall"
- **Eph 6:13–14** — "that ye may be able to withstand in the evil day... Stand therefore"

### 5. To Establish / Confirm / Make Valid
Legal or covenantal force — rendering something authoritative, binding, enduring.
- **Matt 18:16** — "in the mouth of two or three witnesses shall every word be established"
- **Rom 3:31** — "Do we then make void the law through faith? God forbid: yea, we establish the law"
- **Heb 10:9** — "He taketh away the first, that he may establish the second"
- **2 Cor 13:1** — "In the mouth of two or three witnesses shall every word be established"

### 6. Standing Before Authority (Forensic)
Formal presentation or accountability before rulers, councils, or divine judgment.
- **Matt 27:11** — "Jesus stood before the governor: and the governor asked him"
- **Acts 25:10** — "I stand at Caesar's judgment seat, where I ought to be judged"
- **Acts 26:6** — "I stand and am judged for the hope of the promise made of God unto our fathers"
- **Rev 20:12** — "I saw the dead, small and great, stand before God; and the books were opened"

### 7. Heavenly / Angelic Standing (Vision Language)
Beings stationed before the divine throne — prominent in Acts and Revelation.
- **Acts 7:55–56** — "saw the glory of God, and Jesus standing on the right hand of God"
- **Rev 5:6** — "a Lamb stood in the midst of the throne and of the four beasts"
- **Rev 7:1** — "four angels standing on the four corners of the earth, holding the four winds"
- **Rev 8:2** — "the seven angels which stood before God; and to them were given seven trumpets"
