The primary Hebrew source is H7462 רָעָה (ra'ah), which is the root verb meaning "to tend a flock, pasture." The LXX consistently renders ra'ah participle forms (ro'eh = "one who shepherds") as poimēn. H6629 צֹאן (tso'n, "flock/sheep") and H5739 עֵדֶר ('eder, "flock/herd") co-occur because shepherd passages naturally mention the flock.
