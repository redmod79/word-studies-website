## Lexicon Definition

Verbal adjective from παρακαλέω (parakaleō, G3870); one called alongside to aid, an intercessor, advocate, helper, comforter. Used in classical Greek legal contexts for one who appears on another's behalf in court — a legal assistant or advocate. The KJV translates as "Comforter" (4x) and "advocate" (1x).

## Semantic Categories

### 1. The Spirit as Divine Helper (John 14–16)
- **John 14:16** — "And I will pray the Father, and he shall give you another Comforter, that he may abide with you for ever"
- **John 14:26** — "But the Comforter, which is the Holy Ghost, whom the Father will send in my name, he shall teach you all things, and bring all things to your remembrance, whatsoever I have said unto you."
- **John 15:26** — "But when the Comforter is come, whom I will send unto you from the Father, even the Spirit of truth, which proceedeth from the Father, he shall testify of me"
- **John 16:7** — "Nevertheless I tell you the truth; It is expedient for you that I go away: for if I go not away, the Comforter will not come unto you; but if I depart, I will send him unto you."

In these four uses, the paraklētos is identified as the Holy Spirit / Spirit of truth, sent by the Father and the Son, who teaches, reminds, testifies, and convicts.

### 2. Christ as Legal Advocate (1 John 2:1)
- **1 John 2:1** — "My little children, these things write I unto you, that ye sin not. And if any man sin, we have an advocate with the Father, Jesus Christ the righteous"

Here the paraklētos is Jesus Christ himself, functioning as an intercessor/advocate before the Father on behalf of sinners. The legal/forensic dimension of the word is prominent.
