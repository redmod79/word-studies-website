**Semantic shift in translation:** The Hebrew distinguishes three nuances — nokriy (foreign person), zuwr (deviant/estranged), nekar (foreignness as quality). The LXX collapses all three into allotrios ("belonging to another"), losing the distinction between ethnic foreignness (nokriy) and moral estrangement (zuwr). The ger/sojourner concept is kept separate, mapped to πάροικος/προσήλυτος instead.

## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same across testaments:**
- The core sense of "not belonging to the community" persists — allotrios in the NT still marks what is outside one's own household, flock, or people
- Property/ownership framing is consistent: nokriy = "not your brother," allotrios = "not your own"

**What shifts:**
- The NT broadens allotrios beyond ethnic boundaries — in John 10:5, the "stranger" is anyone whose voice the sheep do not recognize (spiritual discernment, not nationality)
- Heb 9:25 uses allotrios for sacrificial blood that is "not one's own" — a cultic-metaphorical application absent from the OT ethnic usage
- The "strange woman" semantic category (dominant in Proverbs) has no NT parallel through allotrios
