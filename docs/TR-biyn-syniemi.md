## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same across testaments:**
- Both biyn and syniemi describe analytical mental comprehension -- discerning truth from falsehood, putting pieces together
- Both are used in contexts of divine revelation requiring human reception (Daniel's visions / Jesus' parables)
- The failure-to-understand motif runs from Isaiah through Daniel into the Synoptic parable discourses

**What shifts:**
- In the OT, biyn covers a broader semantic range including "attend to," "regard," "be prudent" -- uses that syniemi does not carry into the NT
- NT usage concentrates on the understand/not-understand binary (especially in the parables and Rom 3:11), whereas OT usage includes the Hithpael sense of careful study and sustained attention
- Daniel's "understanding progression" (8:27 no understanding -> 9:2 understood by books -> 9:22-23 angelic instruction -> 12:10 the wise shall understand) provides the bridge concept that the NT applies eschatologically
