The Hebrew batach family (trust/security/confidence) is the common OT root behind both peitho and elpis in the LXX. Parrhesia itself has no Hebrew equivalent — it enters biblical theology as a distinctly Greek concept (Athenian democratic free speech) theologized by NT authors for access-to-God confidence.

## Roles in the Concept

- **Central term:** parrhesia (G3954) — the confident openness itself, whether in speech or in approaching God
- **Verbal expression:** parrhesiazomai (G3955) — the act of speaking with parrhesia
- **Ground of confidence:** peitho (G3982) — the persuasion/trust that produces boldness
- **Settled reliance:** pepoithesis (G4006) — confidence as established state
- **Foundation underneath:** hypostasis (G5287) — the substantial assurance supporting confidence
- **Motivating expectation:** elpis (G1680) — hope that grounds present boldness
- **Inner courage:** tharreo (G2292) — the courage felt within
- **Action under risk:** tolmao (G5111) — boldness that dares consequences
