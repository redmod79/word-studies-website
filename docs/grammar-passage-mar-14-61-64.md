# Grammar Analysis: Mark 14:61–64

## Text

**Greek (v. 61):** ὁ δὲ ἐσιώπα καὶ οὐκ ἀπεκρίνατο οὐδέν. πάλιν ὁ ἀρχιερεὺς ἐπηρώτα αὐτὸν καὶ λέγει αὐτῷ Σὺ εἶ ὁ Χριστὸς ὁ Υἱὸς τοῦ Εὐλογητοῦ;

**Greek (v. 62):** ὁ δὲ Ἰησοῦς εἶπεν Ἐγώ εἰμι, καὶ ὄψεσθε τὸν Υἱὸν τοῦ ἀνθρώπου ἐκ δεξιῶν καθήμενον τῆς δυνάμεως καὶ ἐρχόμενον μετὰ τῶν νεφελῶν τοῦ οὐρανοῦ.

**Greek (v. 63):** ὁ δὲ ἀρχιερεὺς διαρρήξας τοὺς χιτῶνας αὐτοῦ λέγει Τί ἔτι χρείαν ἔχομεν μαρτύρων;

**Greek (v. 64):** ἠκούσατε τῆς βλασφημίας· τί ὑμῖν φαίνεται; οἱ δὲ πάντες κατέκριναν αὐτὸν ἔνοχον εἶναι θανάτου.

**KJV (v. 61):** But he held his peace, and answered nothing. Again the high priest asked him, and said unto him, Art thou the Christ, the Son of the Blessed?
**KJV (v. 62):** And Jesus said, I am: and ye shall see the Son of man sitting on the right hand of power, and coming in the clouds of heaven.
**KJV (v. 63):** Then the high priest rent his clothes, and saith, What need we any further witnesses?
**KJV (v. 64):** Ye have heard the blasphemy: what think ye? And they all condemned him to be guilty of death.

## Key Grammatical Features

### 1. v. 61 — Imperfect pair ἐσιώπα … ἐπηρώτα: durative silence vs. repeated questioning

- **Forms:** σιωπάω G4623 **V-IAI-3S** + ἐπερωτάω G1905 **V-IAI-3S**, flanked by aor.mid.ind. ἀπεκρίνατο (G611) in the middle of the verse.
- **Significance:** The imperfect of σιωπάω views Jesus's silence as an *ongoing state* during the interrogation (cf. Isa 53:7 LXX οὐκ ἀνοίγει τὸ στόμα αὐτοῦ); the point-aorist ἀπεκρίνατο οὐδέν sharply seals that silence as a completed non-answer to *all prior* charges. Then the second imperfect ἐπηρώτα portrays the high priest's questioning as *iterative/conative* — "was pressing him again" — setting up the decisive question that finally breaks the silence. The aspectual shift (impf → aor → impf → hist.pres. λέγει) is Markan narrative texture: the `λέγει` historic present then zooms to Caiaphas' face-to-face charge.
- **Reinforcement:** Mark piles up three negatives (οὐκ … οὐδέν) for emphatic non-answer ("not … nothing whatever").

### 2. v. 61 — Caiaphas' question framing: Σὺ εἶ ὁ Χριστὸς ὁ Υἱὸς τοῦ Εὐλογητοῦ;

- **Form:** emphatic-pronoun **Σύ** (G4771 P-2NS) + PAI-2S **εἶ** of εἰμί + double articular pred-nominative **ὁ Χριστὸς ὁ Υἱὸς τοῦ Εὐλογητοῦ** with a genitive-of-source/relation **τοῦ Εὐλογητοῦ** (G2128, substantival adj.).
- **Significance:** Greek does not require the subject pronoun with a finite verb; fronted **Σύ** is emphatic — "are *you* the Christ…?" The articular pred-nominative (Colwell context) marks *identification*, not classification: Caiaphas is asking whether Jesus identifies himself with *the* eschatological titles. **τοῦ Εὐλογητοῦ** is a Jewish circumlocution for God (cf. *ha-Baruk*; m. Ber. 7:3; 2 Cor 11:31 ὁ ὢν εὐλογητὸς εἰς τοὺς αἰῶνας) — Mark preserves the Semitic reverential substitution for the divine Name, which makes the following self-designation in v. 62 ("right hand of **the Power**") stylistically consistent and pragmatically sharp. The appositional "Son of the Blessed" stacks Messianic kingship (Χριστός, Psa 2:2 LXX) onto divine-Sonship (2 Sam 7:14; Psa 2:7; 4QFlor; Dead Sea *Son of God* text 4Q246), so the question is legally loaded even before Jesus answers.
- **Grammar reference:** [genitive-of-quality-apposition](../greek/genitive-of-quality-apposition.md) for substantival-adjective genitive; [son-of-X-semitic-idiom](../greek/son-of-X-semitic-idiom.md) for υἱός + divine-descriptor patterns.

### 3. v. 62 — Ἐγώ εἰμι: emphatic self-predication

- **Form:** fronted **Ἐγώ** (G1473 P-1NS) + **εἰμί** (G1510 V-PAI-1S). Redundant subject-pronoun with a finite verb in Greek — always emphatic, focal, or contrastive (Wallace 321; BDF §277).
- **Significance:** At minimum this is an unambiguous affirmative answer to Caiaphas' Σὺ εἶ ("are *you*…?" — "*I* am"). But Mark's wording is also the exact LXX divine-self-designation phrase used by God in the Pentateuch and Isaiah (**ἐγώ εἰμι** = אֲנִי הוּא / אֲנִי יהוה of Exo 3:14; Deut 32:39; Isa 41:4; 43:10, 13, 25; 46:4; 48:12; 51:12). Whether Mark intends it as a full divine-self-predication or only as an emphatic affirmative (or both) is an exegetical question the grammar does not force; the form is compatible with either and will be read in light of the immediately following double-prophecy (which makes the theophanic reading hard to escape). Matthew's parallel softens with *Σὺ εἶπας* (Matt 26:64, "you said [it]") — Mark's starker ἐγώ εἰμι is the form the Sanhedrin hears.
- **Word study:** [G1510-eimi](../../word-studies/G1510-eimi.md).

### 4. v. 62 — Double-prophecy: participle-pair καθήμενον / ἐρχόμενον (Dan 7:13 + Psa 110:1 fusion)

- **Form:** future-middle-indicative (deponent) **ὄψεσθε** (G3708 V-FDI-2P) introduces a vision-content complement ὁ υἱὸς τοῦ ἀνθρώπου (acc.sg.m.) modified by **two coordinated present middle/passive (deponent) participles in concord**: **καθήμενον** (V-PNP-ASM, κάθημαι G2521) + **ἐρχόμενον** (V-PNP-ASM, ἔρχομαι G2064), joined by καί.
- **Significance:** The ASM pair shares a single antecedent (τὸν υἱὸν τοῦ ἀνθρώπου) — one enthroned subject doing two simultaneous, ongoing actions ("sitting … and coming"). The two participles fuse two distinct OT texts onto one figure:
  - **Psa 110:1 LXX** κάθου ἐκ δεξιῶν μου / MT שֵׁב לִימִינִי → **καθήμενον ἐκ δεξιῶν τῆς δυνάμεως**;
  - **Dan 7:13** MT כְּבַר אֱנָשׁ עִם־עֲנָנֵי שְׁמַיָּא אָתֵה הֲוָה / LXX-OG + Theodotion μετὰ τῶν νεφελῶν τοῦ οὐρανοῦ → **ἐρχόμενον μετὰ τῶν νεφελῶν τοῦ οὐρανοῦ**.
- The Dan 7 figure (royal investiture before the Ancient of Days, recipient of universal dominion) is *united with* the Psa 110 figure (YHWH's right-hand co-regent addressed as ʾăḏōnāy) — both predicates attach to the Son of Man title. The **future-tense** ὄψεσθε (addressed 2nd-pl. to the *judges themselves*) turns the passage from a self-defense into a direct counter-verdict: "*you* will see…". Present-aspect participles mark the state-actions as *durative/ongoing* rather than instantaneous (he will not merely "sit down" and "arrive" — he will be found *enthroned* and *en route*). Mark's θεωρέω/ὄψομαι vision-verb pairs this with apocalyptic *see-language*: cf. Rev 1:7 *ἰδοὺ ἔρχεται μετὰ τῶν νεφελῶν καὶ ὄψεται αὐτὸν πᾶς ὀφθαλμός* which citationally chains Dan 7:13 + Zech 12:10 around this same triad (**coming / with clouds / they will see**).
- **Word-order variant:** Mark places καθήμενον **before** its genitive governor (ἐκ δεξιῶν καθήμενον τῆς δυνάμεως) — hyperbaton splitting δυνάμεως off from δεξιῶν for focus-weighting on *sitting*; Matt 26:64 and Luke 22:69 normalize order. Mark's τῆς δυνάμεως (articular, absolute) is another divine circumlocution matching v. 61 τοῦ Εὐλογητοῦ (cf. rabbinic *ha-Gevurah* / b. Sanh. 43a, 56b; Exo 15:6, Deut 9:29 LXX; 1 Macc 3:19) — Jesus adopts the priest's reverential register to name the One at whose right hand he sits.
- **Grammar reference:** [paired-middle-participles](../greek/paired-middle-participles.md) for two coordinated M/P participles under a single subject.
- **Word studies:** [G5207-huios](../../word-studies/G5207-huios.md), [G444-anthropos](../../word-studies/G444-anthropos.md), [G2521-kathizo](../../word-studies/G2523-kathizo.md) (related κάθημαι; cognate G2523), [G2064-erchomai](../../word-studies/G2064-erchomai.md), [G1411-dynamis](../../word-studies/G1411-dynamis.md), [TR-anan-nephele](../../word-studies/TR-anan-nephele.md), [TR-bo-erchomai](../../word-studies/TR-bo-erchomai.md), [TR-shamayim-ouranos](../../word-studies/TR-shamayim-ouranos.md), [TR-ben-huios](../../word-studies/TR-ben-huios.md), [TR-mashiyach-christos](../../word-studies/TR-mashiyach-christos.md).

### 5. v. 63 — Aorist participle διαρρήξας + historic-present λέγει: Caiaphas' ritual-judicial reaction

- **Form:** **διαρρήξας** (διαρρήγνυμι G1284, **V-AAP-NSM** aor.act.ptcp., "having torn apart") expresses *antecedent* action to **λέγει** (pres.act.ind.3s, historic present). Plural object τοὺς χιτῶνας αὐτοῦ ("his tunics/garments").
- **Significance:** Aorist adverbial participle preceding main verb is the standard Greek "having X-ed, he does Y" frame (BDF §339; Wallace 623–628 — *antecedent/attendant circumstance*); Mark's historic-present λέγει gives the scene narrative *vividness*. The tearing of clothes (קְרִיעָה *qəri'ah*) is the legally prescribed reaction of a judge/witness who has heard blasphemy (m. Sanh. 7:5; cf. 2 Kgs 18:37; 19:1; Acts 14:14); the plural **χιτῶνας** (inner + outer garments or plural-for-intensification; *distributive* or *inclusive* plural) may emphasize the completeness of the ritual gesture. The rhetorical question Τί ἔτι χρείαν ἔχομεν μαρτύρων; ("why do we still need witnesses?") *inverts* v. 55–59 where Mark has just reported the Sanhedrin's *failure* to produce consistent witnesses — Caiaphas now treats Jesus' own words as sufficient self-incrimination, converting the defendant into his own prosecution witness.

### 6. v. 64 — Aorist ἠκούσατε + verdict κατέκριναν ἔνοχον εἶναι θανάτου

- **Forms:** **ἠκούσατε** (ἀκούω G191, V-AAI-2P) + genitive-complement τῆς βλασφημίας (genitive of the thing heard — Wallace 133; BDF §173); **κατέκριναν** (κατακρίνω G2632, V-AAI-3P) + accusative object αὐτόν + predicate-accusative **ἔνοχον** (G1777) + **εἶναι** (pres.act.inf., epexegetical/content inf.) + **θανάτου** (genitive of penalty governed by ἔνοχος — BDAG ἔνοχος §2.b, "liable/subject to").
- **Significance:** The aorist ἠκούσατε presents the blasphemy as a completed, self-evident event ("you have heard [it just now]"); the genitive is the characteristic case with ἀκούω for the *source-content* of hearing, fitting the forensic register where a judgement is delivered about *what was heard*. The verdict-construction is classical Greek legal-idiom: **κατακρίνω τινά + ἔνοχον εἶναι + gen. of penalty** = "condemn someone as liable-to [death]." The infinitive εἶναι carries the predication ("[declared him] to be liable to death"); θανάτου is **genitive of the punishment** to which the defendant is now bound. The expression is formulaic — cf. Matt 26:66 ἔνοχος θανάτου ἐστίν; Deut 19:6 LXX; Lev 24:16 LXX (blasphemy→death); Num 35:31 (death-penalty law). οἱ δὲ **πάντες** (G3956, A-NPM) stresses *unanimity* — the condemnation is not a majority but a whole-body verdict (contrast Luke 23:51 where Joseph of Arimathea had not consented to Sanhedrin action, which Mark does not here qualify for this particular ruling). **φαίνεται** (G5316 V-PEI-3S, middle/passive present) in the prior clause ("how does it appear to you?") uses the standard formula for soliciting a judicial opinion (cf. Matt 22:17, 42; 26:66).
