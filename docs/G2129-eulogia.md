## Lexicon Definition

Fine speaking, i.e. elegance of language; by extension: (1) praise, laudation; (2) a blessing, benediction; (3) consecration; (4) a concrete benefit, bounty, generous gift.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Divine / Covenantal Blessing
The primary sense: God's benediction and its content — favor, promises, salvific benefit.
- **Gal 3:14** — "That the blessing of Abraham might come on the Gentiles through Jesus Christ"
- **Rom 15:29** — "I shall come in the fulness of the blessing of the gospel of Christ"
- **Eph 1:3** — "who hath blessed us with all spiritual blessings in heavenly places in Christ"
- **Heb 6:7** — "the earth... bringeth forth herbs... receiveth blessing from God"

### 2. Inherited / Patriarchal Blessing
Blessing as a transmitted inheritance, connected to birthright and promise.
- **Heb 12:17** — "when he would have inherited the blessing, he was rejected" (Esau)
- **1 Pet 3:9** — "knowing that ye are thereunto called, that ye should inherit a blessing"

### 3. Doxological Praise
Ascription of praise to God or to Christ — the "upward" direction of blessing.
- **Rev 5:12** — "Worthy is the Lamb... to receive power, and riches, and wisdom, and strength, and honour, and glory, and blessing"
- **Rev 5:13** — "Blessing, and honour, and glory, and power, be unto him that sitteth upon the throne"
- **Rev 7:12** — "Blessing, and glory, and wisdom, and thanksgiving, and honour, and power, and might, be unto our God"

### 4. Generous Gift / Bounty
Concrete material benefit — a gift freely given.
- **2 Cor 9:5** — "make up beforehand your bounty... as a matter of bounty, and not as of covetousness"
- **2 Cor 9:6** — "he which soweth bountifully shall reap also bountifully"

### 5. Speech — Benediction vs. Flattery
The word's etymological sense of "fine speaking" surfaces in two contrasting directions.
- **Jas 3:10** — "Out of the same mouth proceedeth blessing and cursing"
- **1 Cor 10:16** — "The cup of blessing which we bless" (liturgical benediction)
- **Rom 16:18** — "by good words and fair speeches deceive the hearts of the simple" (negative: persuasive rhetoric used to mislead)
