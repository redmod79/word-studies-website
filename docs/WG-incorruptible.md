# WG: Incorruptible / Corruption

## Overview
The corruption/incorruption word group in the Greek NT centers on the root verb φθείρω (phtheiro) and its derivatives. Two alpha-privative adjectives negate corruption (aphthartos, "imperishable") and death (athanasia, "immortality"). Together, these six words frame the biblical contrast between perishable creation and imperishable divine reality.

## Relationships

### Shared Roots
- **φθείρω (phtheiro, G5351)** is the root verb, from which derive:

- **ἀθανασία (athanasia, G110)** is from a different root (ἀ- + θάνατος, death) but functions in the same semantic field

## Roles in the Concept

- **Root action:** φθείρω (phtheiro) — the process of corrupting, destroying, or decaying
- **Present condition:** φθαρτός (phthartós) — the current perishable state of creation and mortal bodies
- **Outcome of decay:** φθορά (phthora) — corruption as a force or destination (Rom 8:21, Gal 6:8)
- **Negated quality:** ἄφθαρτος (aphthartos) — the divine attribute and promised future state: immune to decay
- **Negated state:** ἀφθαρσία (aphtharsia) — the abstract condition of imperishability; what believers "put on"
- **Deathlessness:** ἀθανασία (athanasia) — overlapping concept focused on inability to die rather than inability to decay
