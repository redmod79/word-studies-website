## Semantic Categories

### 1. Ceremonial / Ritual Defilement
- **John 18:28** — "they themselves went not into the judgment hall, lest they should be defiled (μιανθῶσιν, aor pass subj); but that they might eat the passover"
  The Jewish leaders avoid entering the Gentile praetorium to preserve Passover purity. Ironic: they guard ceremonial cleanness while condemning the innocent.

### 2. Moral / Conscience Defilement
- **Titus 1:15** — "Unto the pure all things are pure: but unto them that are defiled (μεμιαμμένοις, perf pass ptcp) and unbelieving is nothing pure; but even their mind and conscience is defiled (μεμίανται, perf pass ind)"
  Both the state ("them that are defiled") and the ongoing result ("mind and conscience is defiled") use the perfect tense — a settled condition, not a momentary act.

### 3. Corporate / Community Defilement
- **Hebrews 12:15** — "lest any root of bitterness springing up trouble you, and thereby many be defiled (μιανθῶσιν, aor pass subj)"
  One person's apostasy contaminates the whole community. The same form (μιανθῶσιν) as John 18:28, but the defilement is communal and moral rather than ritual.

### 4. Physical / Fleshly Defilement
- **Jude 1:8** — "Likewise also these filthy dreamers defile (μιαίνουσιν, pres act ind) the flesh, despise dominion, and speak evil of dignities"
  The only active-voice occurrence in the NT. These false teachers actively pollute their own bodies — paired with rejection of authority and blasphemy.
