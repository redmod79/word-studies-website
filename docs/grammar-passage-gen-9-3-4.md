## Key Grammatical Features

### 1. כָּל־רֶמֶשׂ — construct totality phrase

- **Form:** `כָּל` is masculine singular construct governing `רֶמֶשׂ`, masculine singular absolute.
- **Significance:** The phrase forms the subject of `יִהְיֶה`: "every/all moving thing." The totalizing force is supplied by construct `כָּל`, while the lexical head is `רֶמֶשׂ`.
- **Grammar reference:** [construct-state](../hebrew/construct-state.md).
- **Word study:** [H7431-remes](../../word-studies/H7431-remes.md). Related but non-occurring lexical contrast: [H8318-sherets](../../word-studies/H8318-sherets.md).

### 2. אֲשֶׁר הוּא־חַי — relative adjectival clause

- **Form:** `אֲשֶׁר` introduces a relative clause. `הוּא` is 3ms subject pronoun; `חַי` is masculine singular absolute adjective.
- **Significance:** The relative clause qualifies `רֶמֶשׂ` by living status. The parser treats it as an adjectival clause, not as the main predication of the verse.
- **Word study:** [H2416-chay](../../word-studies/H2416-chay.md).

### 3. יִהְיֶה לְאָכְלָה — Qal imperfect with predicate lamed

- **Form:** `יִהְיֶה` is Qal imperfect 3ms of `היה`; `לְאָכְלָה` is lamed + noun, functioning as predicate complement ("for food").
- **Significance:** The living moving thing is predicated as becoming/being for food for the addressed plural `לָכֶם`. The grammatical subject remains singular because `רֶמֶשׂ` is singular, though the reference is distributive.
- **Grammar reference:** [qal-imperfect](../hebrew/qal-imperfect.md).
- **Word study:** [H402-oklah](../../word-studies/H402-oklah.md).

### 4. כְּיֶרֶק עֵשֶׂב — comparison phrase

- **Form:** `כְּ` introduces comparison; `יֶרֶק` and `עֵשֶׂב` are adjacent masculine singular nouns. The parser treats the phrase as an adjunct to `נָתַתִּי`.
- **Significance:** The permission/grant is compared with the prior plant-food grant by an explicit comparative preposition, not by a finite verbal parallel.
- **Word studies:** [H3418-yereq](../../word-studies/H3418-yereq.md), [H6212-eseb](../../word-studies/H6212-eseb.md).

### 5. אַךְ ... לֹא תֹאכֵלוּ — restrictive prohibition with fronted object

- **Form:** `אַךְ` is a restrictive adverb; `לֹא תֹאכֵלוּ` is negation plus Qal imperfect 2mp of `אכל`. The object phrase `בָּשָׂר בְּנַפְשׁוֹ דָמוֹ` stands before the negative predicate.
- **Significance:** Verse 4 is syntactically restrictive: the prohibition is not a new subject-predicate statement about blood alone, but a fronted object phrase followed by a plural addressee prohibition. `נַפְשׁוֹ` and `דָמוֹ` both carry 3ms suffixes referring back to `בָּשָׂר`/the living creature.
- **Grammar reference:** [qal-imperfect](../hebrew/qal-imperfect.md).
