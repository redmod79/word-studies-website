## Direct OT-NT Quotations

No direct quotations of cheleq verses appear in the NT using meris. However, the concept of "having a part/portion" carries through:
- **Acts 8:21** echoes the OT formula "no part nor inheritance" (cf. Deut 10:9, Neh 2:20)
- **Col 1:12** uses meris alongside kleronomia, mirroring the Hebrew pairing of cheleq with nachalah

## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same across testaments:**
- The core sense of "one's rightful share in something larger" persists from OT through NT
- The concept of exclusion from a portion ("no part nor lot") carries unchanged from Deut 10:9 to Acts 8:21

**What shifts:**
- In the OT, cheleq is primarily territorial (land allotment) and existential (one's lot in life). In the NT, meris becomes relational and spiritual — one's share in the community of believers (Col 1:12) or one's chosen way of life (Luke 10:42)
- The Ecclesiastes "portion under the sun" usage (temporary, earthly reward) has no NT parallel; the NT emphasizes eternal inheritance instead
