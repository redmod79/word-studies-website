# WG: Doing / Making / Working

## Overview
The Greek vocabulary for action and work clusters around five interlocking words. ποιέω is the broadest all-purpose verb (do, make, create), while πράσσω stresses habitual practice, ἐργάζομαι emphasizes labor with effort, κατεργάζομαι focuses on bringing to full completion, and ἔργον is the noun for the resulting work or deed. These words regularly appear together in ethical, eschatological, and Christological contexts.

## Roles in the Concept

- **Default action:** ποιέω (G4160) — the all-purpose "do/make" covering any agent and any action
- **Habitual practice:** πράσσω (G4238) — emphasizes ongoing or repeated conduct; often used for sin or trade
- **Effortful labor:** ἐργάζομαι (G2038) — working as a tradesperson or with physical effort; "work the works"
- **Full accomplishment:** κατεργάζομαι (G2716) — bringing an action through to its completed result; "work out your salvation"
- **The resulting deed:** ἔργον (G2041) — the noun for any act or work; "good works," "works of the law," "dead works"
