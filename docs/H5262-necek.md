## Lexicon Definition

From H5258 (nāsak, "to pour out"); **a libation** (poured-out offering of liquid, typically wine or strong drink, upon/at an altar or cultic object); by metonymy from the same root **a cast/molten idol** (poured metal). KJV: *drink offering, cover, molten image.*

The noun is built directly on the verbal action of ritual pouring. The overwhelmingly dominant sense (≥90% of occurrences) is the cultic liquid libation; the minor sense ("molten image," used ~5 times in Isaiah/Jeremiah) is an independent semantic extension of the root's casting/pouring-of-metal idea, not a separate homonym.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Prescribed Cultic Libation (Poured to YHWH)
The dominant usage: a measured quantity of wine (or in Num 28:7, *shekhar*/strong drink) poured out at the altar as a required accompaniment to burnt and meal offerings.
- **Num 28:7** — "the drink offering (nesek) thereof [shall be] the fourth [part] of an hin for the one lamb: in the holy [place] shalt thou cause the strong wine to be poured (nāsak H5258) unto the LORD [for] a drink offering (nesek)."
- **Num 15:5** — "the fourth [part] of an hin of wine for a drink offering shalt thou prepare with the burnt offering"
- **Lev 23:13** — "the drink offering thereof [shall be] of wine, the fourth [part] of an hin"
- **Exo 29:40–41** — daily lamb with flour, oil, and "the fourth part of an hin of wine for a drink offering"

### 2. Patriarchal/Foundational Libation
- **Gen 35:14** — Jacob at Bethel "poured a drink offering thereon, and he poured oil thereon" on a stone pillar — the earliest canonical nesek, establishing the pouring-onto-a-cultic-object pattern.

### 3. Royal/Altar Libation in Historical Narrative
- **2 Ki 16:13** — Ahaz "burnt his burnt offering and his meat offering, and poured his drink offering, and sprinkled the blood" — the full sacrificial quartet.
- **2 Ki 16:15** — Ahaz's standing directive coupling burnt, meal, drink offerings with blood-sprinkling.
- **1 Chr 29:21; 2 Chr 29:35** — drink offerings listed with peace/burnt offerings at temple-establishment moments.

### 4. Illicit / Foreign-Deity Libation
The same cultic act directed to another god is condemned in prophetic polemic.
- **Jer 44:17–19, 25** — women "pour out drink offerings unto her" (queen of heaven).
- **Jer 7:18; 19:13; 32:29** — libations poured on rooftops to Baal / host of heaven.
- **Isa 57:6** — "thou hast poured a drink offering" to the smooth stones of the stream.
- **Eze 20:28** — on "every high hill... poured out there their drink offerings."
- **Ps 16:4** — "their drink offerings of blood will I not offer" — explicit rejection.

### 5. Cessation of Libation as Covenant Judgment
- **Joel 1:9, 13; 2:14** — "the meat offering and the drink offering is cut off from the house of the LORD" — loss of nesek signifies broken cultic relationship.

### 6. Prohibition of "Strange" Drink Offering
- **Exo 30:9** — on the golden incense altar "neither shall ye pour drink offering thereon" — the altar's sanctity excludes even otherwise legitimate liquid pouring there.

### 7. Metonymic Extension: Molten/Cast Image
Same root's casting-of-metal sense yields a noun for the poured image.
- **Isa 41:29** — "their molten images are wind and confusion."
- **Isa 48:5** — "my graven image, and my molten image, hath commanded them."
- **Jer 10:14; 51:17** — "his molten image is falsehood, and there is no breath in them."

**Observation for the libation-vs-consumption question:** In all 13 co-occurrences with the root verb H5258 *nāsak*, the action predicated of the liquid is **pouring**, never drinking. The word is lexically and collocationally bound to the act of pouring the liquid out at the altar; it never appears in a consumption context. The figura etymologica *hassēk nesek* ("pour-out a poured-thing") in Num 28:7 is tautologically explicit about the mode of disposal.
