The strong PMI bridge is mishteh — not a dedicated wedding word but the broader "feast" term. Hebrew has no single-word equivalent for "wedding event" in the way Greek gámos consolidates it; OT speaks of "the feast" (mishteh) or "the days of the wedding" (שִׁבְעַת יְמֵי הַמִּשְׁתֶּה, Gen 29:27). Christian Greek inherits gámos from classical/secular usage.
