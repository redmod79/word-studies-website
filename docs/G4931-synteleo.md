## Semantic Categories

### 1. Completing a Period of Time
- **Luk 4:2** — "Being forty days tempted of the devil. And in those days he did eat nothing: and when they were ended (synteléō)..."
- **Act 21:27** — "And when the seven days were almost ended (synteléō), the Jews which were of Asia, when they saw him in the temple, stirred up all the people"

The passive voice marks the natural expiration of a fixed duration — the forty days of testing, the seven days of purification. The word emphasizes that the entire period ran to its full conclusion.

### 2. Completing a Discourse or Activity
- **Mat 7:28** — "And it came to pass, when Jesus had ended (synteléō) these sayings, the people were astonished at his doctrine"
- **Luk 4:13** — "And when the devil had ended (synteléō) all the temptation, he departed from him for a season"

Active voice with a personal subject who brings an activity to its complete conclusion. In both cases "all" (πᾶς) appears in the context, reinforcing the thoroughness of the ending.

### 3. Eschatological / Prophetic Consummation
- **Mar 13:4** — "Tell us, when shall these things be? and what shall be the sign when all these things shall be fulfilled (synteléō)?"
- **Rom 9:28** — "For he will finish (synteléō) the work, and cut it short in righteousness: because a short work will the Lord make upon the earth"

Marks the consummation of prophetic events. The disciples ask about the culmination of all end-time events. Paul quotes the LXX of Isaiah 10:22-23, where the Hebrew כָּלָה (kalah) "to end/complete/consume" underlies synteléō — God decisively finishing his judicial work.

### 4. Covenant-Making as Consummation
- **Heb 8:8** — "Behold, the days come, saith the Lord, when I will make (synteléō) a new covenant with the house of Israel and with the house of Judah"

Quoting LXX Jeremiah 31:31 (38:31 LXX), where the Hebrew כָּרַת (karath, "cut") stands. The LXX translators chose synteléō rather than a cutting/establishing verb, framing covenant-making as bringing something to full consummation — completing or perfecting a relationship.
