## Lexicon Definition

A signet (as fencing in or protecting from misappropriation); by implication, the stamp impressed as a mark of genuineness or the thing that fastens or secures. Used both literally (a signet ring or its impression) and figuratively (a confirming mark, token of authentication, divine ownership, or protection).

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Authentication — Seal as Proof or Certification
The seal confirms a truth, validates a claim, or certifies identity already established. The mark is the evidence, not the source.

- **Rom 4:11** — "he received the sign of circumcision, a seal of the righteousness of the faith which he had yet being uncircumcised" — circumcision is the confirming mark of prior faith, not its cause
- **1 Cor 9:2** — "ye are the seal of mine apostleship in the Lord" — the Corinthian community as living proof of Paul's apostolic authority
- **2 Tim 2:19** — "the foundation of God standeth sure, having this seal, The Lord knoweth them that are his" — God's foundation bears an inscribed motto as its authenticating seal

### 2. The Sealed Heavenly Scroll — Divine Concealment Awaiting Authorization
A scroll in God's right hand, sealed shut with seven seals, inaccessible until the worthy one opens it.

- **Rev 5:1** — "a book written within and on the backside, sealed with seven seals"
- **Rev 5:2** — "Who is worthy to open the book, and to loose the seals thereof?"
- **Rev 5:5, 9** — the Lion of Judah / the Lamb alone has authority to open the seals

### 3. Sequential Seal-Openings — Progressive Unveiling of Judgment
Each seal the Lamb opens releases a corresponding cosmic event or divine disclosure; six seals in Revelation 6, the seventh in Revelation 8.

- **Rev 6:1, 3, 5, 7** — first through fourth seals: four horsemen released
- **Rev 6:9, 12** — fifth seal: martyred souls; sixth seal: cosmic upheaval
- **Rev 8:1** — seventh seal: silence in heaven before the trumpet sequence begins

### 4. The Seal of the Living God — Divine Ownership and Protection
A seal held by an angel from the east, marking God's servants as protected from eschatological harm.

- **Rev 7:2** — "another angel ascending from the east, having the seal of the living God" — sealing God's servants before harm is released
- **Rev 9:4** — "those men which have not the seal of God in their foreheads" — the unsealed are exposed to the locust plague
