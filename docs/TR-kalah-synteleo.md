## Trace Significance

1. **Creation to New Creation:** kalah opens the Bible (Gen 2:1–2) describing the completion of creation. συντέλεια (the noun cognate) closes the age in NT eschatology (Mt 13:39 "consummation of the age").

2. **Speaking / Discourses:** Both words structure narrative transitions: "when he had made an end of speaking" (kalah in OT) / "when Jesus had ended these sayings" (synteleo in NT: Mt 7:28).

3. **Divine Completion:** The pattern of God completing what He begins (creation, tabernacle, temple) runs through both testaments: Gen 2:1 → Exo 39:32 → 1Ki 6:38 → Rom 9:28 ("he will finish the work").

4. **Covenant Language:** In Heb 8:8 (quoting Jer 31:31), synteleo translates the act of God "making/consummating" the new covenant — echoing kalah's use in OT covenant-keeping contexts.
