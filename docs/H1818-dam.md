## Lexicon Definition

From (compare H1826); blood (as that which when shed causes death) of man or an animal; by analogy,
the juice of the grape; figuratively (especially in the plural) bloodshed (i.e. drops of blood):
—blood(-y, -guiltiness), (-thirsty), innocent.

Strong presence in Ezekiel (prophetic condemnation of bloodshed) and the Minor Prophets. Notably absent from wisdom literature beyond Proverbs, and sparse in narrative texts relative to its cultic weight. The Psalms uses are mostly judicial (innocent blood, blood of servants).

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Blood of Sacrifice — Cultic / Priestly Use
The dominant usage in Leviticus and ritual texts: blood received, sprinkled, poured at the altar.

- **Lev 1:5** — "the priests, Aaron's sons, shall bring the blood, and sprinkle the blood round about upon the altar"
- **Lev 17:11** — "the life of the flesh is in the blood: and I have given it to you upon the altar to make an atonement for your souls: for it is the blood that maketh an atonement for the soul"
- **Exo 29:16** — "thou shalt slay the ram, and thou shalt take his blood, and sprinkle it round about upon the altar"
- **Num 19:4** — "Eleazar the priest shall take of her blood with his finger, and sprinkle of her blood directly before the tabernacle"

### 2. Blood as the Life-Force
The explicit equation: dam = life. Foundation of the blood prohibition.

- **Gen 9:4** — "flesh with the life thereof, which is the blood thereof, shall ye not eat"
- **Lev 17:14** — "the life of all flesh is the blood thereof: whosoever eateth it shall be cut off"
- **Deut 12:23** — "the blood is the life; and thou mayest not eat the life with the flesh"
- **Deut 12:16** — "ye shall not eat the blood; ye shall pour it upon the earth as water"

### 3. Murder / Bloodshed / Violence
dâm used for the act of killing and its consequences; often with shaphak (pour/shed).

- **Gen 4:10** — "the voice of thy brother's blood crieth unto me from the ground"
- **Num 35:33** — "blood it defileth the land: and the land cannot be cleansed of the blood that is shed therein"
- **Isa 1:15** — "your hands are full of blood"
- **Jer 7:6** — "shed not innocent blood in this place"

### 4. Blood Guilt — Responsibility for Death
"Blood upon your head/hands" — the formula for moral culpability.

- **Josh 2:19** — "his blood shall be upon his head, and we will be guiltless"
- **2 Sam 1:16** — "thy blood be upon thy head; for thy mouth hath testified against thee"
- **Ezek 3:18** — "his blood will I require at thine hand"
- **Ezek 33:6** — "his blood will I require at the watchman's hand"

### 5. Innocent Blood — Prophetic Indictment
Shedding innocent blood as covenant violation; major theme in Isaiah, Jeremiah, Ezekiel.

- **Jer 2:34** — "in thy skirts is found the blood of the souls of the poor innocents"
- **Lam 4:13** — "for the sins of her prophets, and the iniquities of her priests, that have shed the blood of the just"
- **Prov 1:16** — "their feet run to evil, and make haste to shed blood"
- **Ezek 22:4** — "thou art become guilty in thy blood that thou hast shed"

### 6. Covenant Blood
Blood as the ratifying substance of covenant relationship.

- **Zech 9:11** — "as for thee also, by the blood of thy covenant I have sent forth thy prisoners out of the pit"
- **Exo 24:8** — "Moses took the blood, and sprinkled it on the people, and said, Behold the blood of the covenant"

### 7. Eschatological / Cosmic Blood
Blood as a sign of divine judgment; Joel's blood moon carried into NT.

- **Joel 2:30–31** — "blood, and fire, and pillars of smoke. The sun shall be turned into darkness, and the moon into blood, before the great and terrible day of the LORD"
- **Micah 3:10** — "they build up Zion with blood, and Jerusalem with iniquity"
- **Hab 2:12** — "Woe to him that buildeth a town with blood"
