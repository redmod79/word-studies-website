## Lexicon Definition

From ἀνήρ (man) and ὤψ (the countenance/face, from ὀπ- "to see"); literally "man-faced," i.e. a human being — any member of the human race, irrespective of sex or age. Strong's: "man-faced, i.e. a human." Generic term for a human being in distinction from animals, God, or angels; when contrasted with ἀνήρ (male adult), anthrōpos emphasizes humanity as such rather than maleness. Frequently serves as the LXX equivalent of both אָדָם (ʼâdâm, humanity/Adam) and אִישׁ (ʼîysh, individual man).

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Generic Human Being (individual or class)
Used for any person without emphasis on sex, status, or identity.
- **Matt 4:4** — "Man shall not live by bread alone"
- **Mark 2:27** — "The sabbath was made for man (anthrōpon), and not man for the sabbath"
- **John 2:25** — "He knew what was in man (anthrōpōi)"
- **Heb 9:27** — "It is appointed unto men (anthrōpois) once to die"

### 2. "The Son of Man" (ho huios tou anthrōpou)
The single most frequent idiom in the Gospels — appears in ~65 verses. A Semitic construction (Aramaic bar ʼĕnâsh, Hebrew ben-ʼâdâm) rendering "human one" or drawing on Dan 7:13. In the NT this phrase is used almost exclusively by Jesus of himself.
- **Matt 8:20** — "The Son of man hath not where to lay his head"
- **Mark 2:10** — "The Son of man hath power on earth to forgive sins"
- **Matt 26:64** — "Ye shall see the Son of man sitting on the right hand of power, and coming in the clouds of heaven" (Dan 7:13 allusion)
- **Rev 1:13** — "one like unto the Son of man, clothed with a garment down to the foot"
- **Rev 14:14** — "one sat like unto the Son of man, having on his head a golden crown"

### 3. Humanity in Contrast to God (anthrōpos vs. theos)
Anthrōpos frequently stands in antithesis to θεός — what belongs to humans contrasted with what belongs to God. Co-occurs with θεός 103x (21% of uses).
- **Acts 5:29** — "We ought to obey God rather than men (anthrōpois)"
- **Gal 1:10** — "Do I now persuade men, or God?"
- **1 Cor 1:25** — "The foolishness of God is wiser than men"
- **1 Tim 2:5** — "One God, and one mediator between God and men (anthrōpōn), the man (anthrōpos) Christ Jesus"

### 4. Anthrōpos + Attributive Genitive (Semitic "man of X" construction)
A Hebraism in which anthrōpos followed by a descriptive genitive functions as a characterization — the genitive specifies what type of person is in view. The phrase may denote a particular individual, a class, or a representative figure.
- **2 Tim 3:17** — "that the man of God (anthrōpos tou theou) may be perfect, throughly furnished unto all good works" (cf. 1 Tim 6:11)
- **2 Thess 2:3** — "that man of sin (ho anthrōpos tēs hamartias / tēs anomias) be revealed, the son of perdition"
- **John 18:29** — "What accusation bring ye against this man?"
- **2 Tim 3:8** — "men of corrupt minds (anthrōpoi katephtharmenoi ton noun), reprobate concerning the faith"

### 5. Anthrōpos as Representative / Type (individual standing for class)
Occasionally anthrōpos denotes a single figure whose action or nature is determinative for a whole group — an archetypal or corporate representative. Most prominent in Paul's Adam–Christ typology.
- **Rom 5:12** — "by one man (di' henos anthrōpou) sin entered into the world"
- **Rom 5:19** — "by one man's disobedience many were made sinners"
- **1 Cor 15:21** — "by man (di' anthrōpou) came death, by man (di' anthrōpou) came also the resurrection"
- **1 Cor 15:45** — "The first man (ho prōtos anthrōpos) Adam was made a living soul; the last Adam (ho eschatos Adam) was made a quickening spirit"
- **1 Cor 15:47** — "The first man (ho prōtos anthrōpos) is of the earth, earthy: the second man (ho deuteros anthrōpos) is the Lord from heaven"

### 6. Inward / Outward / Old / New anthrōpos (anthropological aspect)
Paul uses anthrōpos with qualifying adjectives to describe distinct modes or aspects of a single person — not two different people but the old covenantal identity vs. the renewed identity, or the observable self vs. the hidden self.
- **Rom 6:6** — "Our old man (ho palaios hēmōn anthrōpos) is crucified with him"
- **Rom 7:22** — "I delight in the law of God after the inward man (kata ton esō anthrōpon)"
- **2 Cor 4:16** — "Though our outward man (ho exō hēmōn anthrōpos) perish, yet the inward man (ho esō) is renewed day by day"
- **Eph 4:22-24** — "put off the old man... put on the new man (ton kainon anthrōpon), which after God is created in righteousness"
- **Col 3:9-10** — "put off the old man with his deeds... put on the new man"
- **1 Cor 2:14** — "the natural man (psychikos anthrōpos) receiveth not the things of the Spirit"

### 7. Vocative / Direct Address ("O man")
Used in rhetorical address, often pejoratively or as indefinite indictment.
- **Rom 2:1** — "O man (ō anthrōpe), whosoever thou art that judgest"
- **Rom 9:20** — "O man, who art thou that repliest against God?"
- **Jas 2:20** — "O vain man (ō anthrōpe kene)"
- **Luke 22:60** — "Man (anthrōpe), I know not what thou sayest"

## Grammatical / Idiomatic Notes

- **Articular vs. anarthrous:** ὁ ἄνθρωπος ("the man") often points to a specific individual in view; anarthrous ἄνθρωπος ("a man / man") can be generic or indefinite. In the formula ὁ υἱὸς τοῦ ἀνθρώπου both the head noun and the genitive are articular, making it definite and titular.
- **Attributive genitive ("man of X"):** ἄνθρωπος + genitive of quality is a Semitic construction equivalent to Hebrew ʼîysh + construct noun (e.g., ʼîysh-ha-ʼĕlôhîm, "man of God"). The genitive can denote:
  - a characteristic or dominant quality ("man of God" = godly man; "man of sin" = person characterized by lawlessness)
  - membership in a class (could describe an individual OR a type)
  - representation (one who embodies the genitive quality for a group)
  The construction by itself does not disambiguate individual from class — context determines whether a specific historical figure or a representative type is in view.
- **Individual-as-type:** In Pauline theology (Rom 5, 1 Cor 15), a single anthrōpos (Adam, Christ) can be both a historical person and a corporate head whose act is determinative for those "in" him. Individual and class meanings are not mutually exclusive.
