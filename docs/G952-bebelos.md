## Lexicon Definition

From the base of βαίνω (to walk) and βηλός bēlós (a threshold); accessible (as by crossing the threshold), i.e., not sacred — profane, common, unhallowed. The word describes what lacks consecration or has been removed from the sphere of the holy. The LXX uses it consistently for H2455 חֹל (chol), the technical priestly term for "common" as opposed to "holy" (קֹדֶשׁ qodesh).

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Profane Persons — Vice-List Usage
- **1 Tim 1:9** — "for the lawless and disobedient, for the ungodly and for sinners, for unholy and **profane**, for murderers of fathers and murderers of mothers, for manslayers"
- **Heb 12:16** — "Lest there be any fornicator, or **profane person**, as Esau, who for one morsel of meat sold his birthright"

In both passages, bebelos describes people who treat sacred things as common. In 1 Tim 1:9 it pairs with ἀνόσιος (anosios, "unholy") to form a doublet; in Heb 12:16, Esau is the paradigmatic profane person — he valued the covenant birthright no higher than ordinary food.

### 2. Profane Speech — Empty Religious Talk
- **1 Tim 4:7** — "But refuse **profane** and old wives' fables, and exercise thyself rather unto godliness"
- **1 Tim 6:20** — "O Timothy, keep that which is committed to thy trust, avoiding **profane** and vain babblings, and oppositions of science falsely so called"
- **2 Tim 2:16** — "But shun **profane** and vain babblings: for they will increase unto more ungodliness"

In all three Pastoral passages, bebelos modifies speech that is religiously pretentious but actually devoid of sacred content. The collocation with κενοφωνία (kenophonia, "vain babblings") occurs in 2 of these 3 verses. Such speech is profane precisely because it mimics the form of sacred teaching while lacking its substance — it belongs to the common sphere, not the holy.

The LXX data confirms that bebelos is the Greek rendering of the fourfold priestly classification in Lev 10:10 and Eze 44:23: holy (qodesh/hagios) vs. common (chol/bebelos), clean (tahowr/katharos) vs. unclean (tame/akathartos). The holy/common axis is distinct from the clean/unclean axis — something can be common without being impure.
