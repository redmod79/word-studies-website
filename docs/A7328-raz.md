## Lexicon Definition

(Aramaic) from an unused root probably meaning to attenuate, i.e. (figuratively) hide; a mystery:—secret.

The word is a loan from Old Persian *rāza-* ("secret"), taken into Imperial Aramaic and from there into later Hebrew/Rabbinic usage. In Daniel the term denotes not merely a hidden fact but a divinely concealed content — a matter sealed in God's counsel that can be known only by divine disclosure.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. The Dream-Content Concealed in God's Counsel
The râz is the substance of Nebuchadnezzar's dream — a hidden content that only God can disclose.
- **Dan 2:18** — "desire mercies of the God of heaven concerning this secret"
- **Dan 2:19** — "Then was the secret revealed unto Daniel in a night vision"
- **Dan 2:30** — "this secret is not revealed to me for any wisdom that I have"

### 2. What Human Wisdom Cannot Reach
The râz lies beyond the competence of the Babylonian wisdom class. It marks the boundary between human divination and divine revelation.
- **Dan 2:27** — "The secret which the king hath demanded cannot the wise men, the astrologers, the magicians, the soothsayers, shew unto the king"
- **Dan 4:9** — "no secret troubleth thee" (Nebuchadnezzar to Daniel, contrasting him with the failed magicians)

### 3. Disclosed by the God Who Reveals
The word is consistently bound to a participle of גְּלָה (gᵉlâh, "to reveal") — God is characterized as the *revealer of secrets*.
- **Dan 2:28** — "there is a God in heaven that revealeth secrets"
- **Dan 2:29** — "he that revealeth secrets maketh known to thee"
- **Dan 2:47** — "your God is... a revealer of secrets, seeing thou couldest reveal this secret"

### 4. Eschatological Content ("What Shall Be in the Latter Days")
In Dan 2, the râz is explicitly identified as the future course of world empires culminating in the kingdom of God.
- **Dan 2:28** — "revealeth secrets, and maketh known... what shall be in the latter days"
- **Dan 2:29** — "what should come to pass hereafter"
