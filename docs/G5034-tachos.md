## Lexicon Definition

A brief space (of time); with the preposition ἐν: "in speed" = speedily, quickly,
shortly. The word functions exclusively in the fixed idiom **ἐν τάχει** (dative
singular, lit. "in speed/swiftness"), which is then rendered adverbially.

## Morphological Note

All 7 confirmed occurrences are in a single inflected form: **τάχει** — dative
singular neuter (N-DSN). The word never appears in nominative, accusative, or
genitive in the NT. It is a lexicalized idiom:

> ἐν τάχει = "with swiftness" → shortly / quickly / speedily

This frozen construction makes τάχος functionally equivalent to the adverbs
ταχέως (G5030) and ταχύ (G5035), though differing in register and nuance.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Prophetic / Eschatological Imminence
The phrase ἐν τάχει frames divine revelatory events that "must" (δεῖ) soon occur.

- **Rev 1:1** — "to shew unto his servants things which must shortly come to pass (ἐν τάχει γενέσθαι)"
- **Rev 22:6** — "the Lord God… sent his angel to shew unto his servants the things which must shortly be done"
- **Luke 18:8** — "I tell you that he will avenge them speedily (ἐν τάχει)"

### 2. Urgent / Miraculous Departure
Commands to leave or act immediately in narrative contexts.

- **Acts 12:7** — the angel commands Peter: "Arise up quickly (ἐν τάχει)" as chains fall off
- **Acts 22:18** — Jesus to Paul in a vision: "Make haste, and get thee quickly out of Jerusalem"

### 3. Near-Future Temporal Expectation
Human or apostolic plans expected in the near term; also eschatological anticipation.

- **Acts 25:4** — "Festus answered… that he himself would depart shortly (ἐν τάχει)"
- **Romans 16:20** — "the God of peace shall bruise Satan under your feet shortly (ἐν τάχει)"
