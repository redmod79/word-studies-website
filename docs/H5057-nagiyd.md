## Lexicon Definition

A commander (as occupying the front), civil, military or religious; generally (abstractly, plural), honorable themes:—captain, chief, excellent thing, (chief) governor, leader, noble, prince, (chief) ruler.

From H5046 נָגַד — "to front, i.e. stand boldly out opposite; to manifest, announce." A נָגִיד is literally *the one standing out front* — the visibly prominent, publicly designated leader.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Divinely Designated Leader over Israel
The most frequent pattern: God (not men) identifies and designates someone as nâgîyd over His people. The verb of appointment is typically "I have made/anointed thee" — emphasizing divine initiative and selection.

- **1Sa 9:16** — "thou shalt anoint him to be captain (nâgîyd) over my people Israel, that he may save my people out of the hand of the Philistines"
- **1Sa 10:1** — "the LORD hath anointed thee to be captain (nâgîyd) over his inheritance"
- **2Sa 5:2** — "the LORD said to thee, Thou shalt feed my people Israel, and thou shalt be a captain (nâgîyd) over Israel"
- **1Ki 16:2** — "I exalted thee out of the dust, and made thee prince (nâgîyd) over my people Israel"

### 2. Military and Civil Officers
nâgîyd applied to appointed leaders at various ranks — tribal chieftains, military commanders, administrative officials. These may be divinely or royally appointed.

- **1Ch 12:27** — "Jehoiada was the leader (nâgîyd) of the Aaronites, and with him were three thousand and seven hundred"
- **1Ch 13:1** — "David consulted with the captains of thousands and hundreds, and with every leader (nâgîyd)"
- **2Ch 32:21** — "the LORD sent an angel, which cut off all the mighty men of valour, and the leaders (nâgîydîm) and captains in the camp of the king of Assyria"
- **2Ki 20:5** — "Turn again, and tell Hezekiah the captain (nâgîyd) of my people, Thus saith the LORD, the God of David thy father"

### 3. Temple/Priestly Overseer
A specialized category: nâgîyd applied to the head of the priestly establishment or temple administration — the "nagid of the house of God." This is a cultic office, not merely civil.

- **1Ch 9:11** — Azariah the son of Hilkiah... nâgîyd of the house of God
- **Jer 20:1** — "Pashur the son of Immer the priest, who was also chief governor (nâgîyd) in the house of the LORD"
- **2Ch 31:12** — "over which Cononiah the Levite was ruler (nâgîyd)" — over tithes and dedicated things
- **Neh 11:11** — "Seraiah the son of Hilkiah, the son of Meshullam... nâgîyd of the house of God"

### 4. Prophetic and Messianic Prince
The eschatological uses — where nâgîyd designates a coming divine figure. Daniel 9 provides the most theologically charged occurrences: "Messiah the Prince" (nâgîyd Mâshîyach) and "the prince that shall come."

- **Dan 9:25** — "from the going forth of the commandment to restore and to build Jerusalem unto the Messiah the Prince (nâgîyd) shall be seven weeks, and threescore and two weeks"
- **Dan 9:26** — "the people of the prince (nâgîyd) that shall come shall destroy the city and the sanctuary"
- **Dan 11:22** — "yea, also the prince (nâgîyd) of the covenant"
- **Isa 55:4** — "Behold, I have given him for a witness to the people, a leader (nâgîyd) and commander to the people"

### 5. Noble/Excellent (Abstract)
Two uses where nâgîyd conveys nobility of bearing or excellence of speech — the quality associated with one who "stands out front."

- **Prov 8:6** — "Hear; for I will speak of excellent things (nâgîydîm); and the opening of my lips shall be right things"
- **Job 31:37** — "I would declare unto him the number of my steps; as a prince (nâgîyd) would I go near unto him"
- **Ps 76:12** — "He shall cut off the spirit of princes (nâgîydîm): he is terrible to the kings of the earth"
