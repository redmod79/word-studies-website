# WG: Defile (NT Defilement Vocabulary)

## Overview
The NT uses several distinct Greek words for "defilement," each carrying different nuances: ritual profanation, moral contamination, physical soiling, corruption/destruction, and staining. Together they cover the full spectrum of what makes a person or thing unfit for God's purposes.

## Roles in the Concept

- **Ritual contamination:** miaino (μιαίνω) — the Levitical defilement verb; renders unfit for sacred participation
- **Category violation:** koinoo (κοινόω) — makes sacred things common/profane; the clean/unclean food debate word (Matt 15, Mark 7, Acts 10)
- **Moral soiling:** molyno (μολύνω) — stains the conscience or garments; figurative dirt (Rev 3:4, 14:4)
- **Defilement-substance:** molysmos (μολυσμός) — the filthiness/stain itself; noun form of molyno; uniquely qualifies both flesh and spirit (2 Cor 7:1)
- **Visible blemish:** spiloo (σπιλόω) — spots or stains that are externally visible (Jas 3:6, Jude 1:23)
- **Uncontaminated state:** amiantos (ἀμίαντος) — the positive ideal: undefiled priesthood (Heb 7:26), marriage (Heb 13:4), inheritance (1 Pet 1:4), religion (Jas 1:27)

## Key Passages Where Multiple Group Concepts Appear

- **Jude 1:8, 23** — miaino (v.8 "defile the flesh") and spiloo (v.23 "hating even the garment spotted by the flesh") appear in the same epistle, distinguishing deep contamination from surface staining
- **Titus 1:15** — miaino paired with katharos (G2513, "pure") — the defile/pure antonym pair; conceptually linked to koinoo's clean/common framework but using the Levitical verb instead
