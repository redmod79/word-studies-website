## Lexicon Definition

Of uncertain affinity; **new (especially in freshness)**; while νέος (G3501) is properly new with respect to *age*, καινός is new with respect to *nature or quality* — something unprecedented, qualitatively different, not merely recently made.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Qualitative Incompatibility with the Old (Wineskins Parable)
The parable of new wine and old containers establishes καινός as *qualitatively incompatible* with what is worn out — the point is not chronology but the nature of what is new.
- **Mat 9:17** — "Neither do men put new wine into old bottles: else the bottles break, and the wine runneth out, and the bottles perish"
- **Mar 2:21–22** — "No man also seweth a piece of new cloth on an old garment... And no man putteth new wine into old bottles"
- **Luk 5:36–38** — "No man putteth a piece of a new garment upon an old... But new wine must be put into new bottles"

### 2. New Covenant / Testament
The dominant theological use. καινός marks the covenant inaugurated by Christ's blood as qualitatively superior to the Mosaic covenant — not simply a renewed version but an unprecedented arrangement foretold in Jer 31:31.
- **Mat 26:28** — "For this is my blood of the new testament, which is shed for many for the remission of sins"
- **Luk 22:20** — "This cup is the new testament in my blood, which is shed for you"
- **2Co 3:6** — "Who also hath made us able ministers of the new testament; not of the letter, but of the spirit"
- **Heb 8:8** — "Behold, the days come, saith the Lord, when I will make a new covenant with the house of Israel" (quoting Jer 31:31)
- **Heb 9:15** — "He is the mediator of the new testament, that by means of death... they which are called might receive the promise"

### 3. New Commandment
Jesus introduces a commandment as "new" not because loving one another was unprecedented, but because the sacrificial model of his love gives it an unprecedented quality. The Johannine letters debate whether it is truly "new" or an old one heard from the beginning.
- **Jhn 13:34** — "A new commandment I give unto you, That ye love one another; as I have loved you"
- **1Jo 2:7–8** — "I write no new commandment unto you, but an old commandment... Again, a new commandment I write unto you"
- **2Jo 1:5** — "not as though I wrote a new commandment unto thee, but that which we had from the beginning"

### 4. New Creation / New Person
In Paul, καινός marks the eschatological reality of regeneration — the believer is not improved but qualitatively recreated. The "new man" is not the old man renovated.
- **2Co 5:17** — "Therefore if any man be in Christ, he is a new creature: old things are passed away; behold, all things are become new"
- **Gal 6:15** — "neither circumcision availeth any thing, nor uncircumcision, but a new creature"
- **Eph 2:15** — "to make in himself one new man, so making peace"
- **Eph 4:24** — "put on the new man, which after God is created in righteousness and true holiness"

### 5. New Eschatological Realities (Song, Heaven, Earth, Jerusalem)
In Revelation, καινός describes the entire renewed cosmic order — new song, new heaven, new earth, new Jerusalem. All are qualitatively unprecedented, not merely fresh versions of existing things.
- **Rev 5:9** — "they sung a new song, saying, Thou art worthy"
- **Rev 21:1** — "I saw a new heaven and a new earth: for the first heaven and the first earth were passed away"
- **Rev 21:2** — "And I John saw the holy city, new Jerusalem, coming down from God out of heaven"
- **Rev 21:5** — "Behold, I make all things new"

### 6. New Teaching / Doctrine
Outsiders respond to Jesus and Paul's message as something qualitatively new — unprecedented in their experience.
- **Mar 1:27** — "What thing is this? what new doctrine is this? for with authority commandeth he even the unclean spirits"
- **Act 17:19** — "May we know what this new doctrine, whereof thou speakest, is?"
- **Act 17:21** — "all the Athenians... spent their time in nothing else, but either to tell or to hear some new thing"
