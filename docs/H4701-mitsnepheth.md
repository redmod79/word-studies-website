## Lexicon Definition

From H6801 (tsanaph); a tiara, i.e. official turban (of a king or high priest): — diadem, mitre.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Prescribed Priestly Garment
The mitsnepheth appears in the initial garment list and its construction instructions.
- **Exo 28:4** — "a breastplate, and an ephod, and a robe, and a broidered coat, a mitre, and a girdle: and they shall make holy garments for Aaron"
- **Exo 28:39** — "thou shalt make the mitre of fine linen, and thou shalt make the girdle of needlework"
- **Exo 39:28** — "And a mitre of fine linen, and goodly bonnets of fine linen"

### 2. Platform for the Gold Plate (Nezer)
The mitsnepheth serves as the base onto which the gold plate ("HOLINESS TO THE LORD") is fastened with a blue lace.
- **Exo 28:37** — "thou shalt put it on a blue lace, that it may be upon the mitre; upon the forefront of the mitre it shall be"
- **Exo 39:31** — "they tied unto it a lace of blue, to fasten it on high upon the mitre; as the LORD commanded Moses"
- **Exo 29:6** — "thou shalt put the mitre upon his head, and put the holy crown upon the mitre"

### 3. Investiture and Day of Atonement
The mitsnepheth is placed on the high priest during ordination and worn on the Day of Atonement.
- **Lev 8:9** — "he put the mitre upon his head; also upon the mitre, even upon his forefront, did he put the golden plate, the holy crown"
- **Lev 16:4** — "with the linen mitre shall he be attired: these are holy garments; therefore shall he wash his flesh in water"

### 4. Removal as Prophetic Judgment
Ezekiel uses the mitsnepheth metaphorically: stripping the high-priestly turban signals the end of the Judean order.
- **Eze 21:26** — "Remove the diadem, and take off the crown: this shall not be the same: exalt him that is low, and abase him that is high"
