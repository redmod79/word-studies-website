# Grammar Analysis: Hebrews 9:11-22 (The Blood of Christ and Covenantal Blood-Purification)

**Mode:** Passage unit analysis (context-neutral)
**Scope:** Heb 9:11-22 as a coherent argumentative unit — the greater mediation of Christ the high priest (vv.11-12), the *a fortiori* purification of conscience (vv.13-14), the covenant-death logic (vv.15-17), and the Sinai-covenant blood-ritual paradigm (vv.18-22).
**Aim:** Verse-by-verse parsing; tense/mood/voice map; documentation of the load-bearing grammatical features: (a) the paired διά-constructions in vv.11-12 (instrumental vs. locative), (b) παραγενόμενος as antecedent/circumstantial aor.mid.ptcp., (c) εἰσῆλθεν + ἐφάπαξ (aor. of once-for-all entry) paired with εὑράμενος (aor.mid.ptcp. "having obtained"), (d) the *a fortiori* (πόσῳ μᾶλλον) correlation in vv.13-14, (e) the attenuated future καθαριεῖ with future-purpose articular infinitive εἰς τὸ λατρεύειν, (f) the διαθήκη / διαθέμενος wordplay of vv.15-17 (covenant vs. last-will / testator), (g) the genitive absolute λαληθείσης πάσης ἐντολῆς (v.19), (h) the perfect passive ἐγκεκαίνισται (v.18) as resultant-state, (i) the gnomic-present maxim χωρὶς αἱματεκχυσίας οὐ γίνεται ἄφεσις (v.22). Grammar only; theological conclusions deferred.

**Verbal frame:** nominative subject Χριστός fronted, followed by an antecedent/circumstantial aor.mid.ptcp. (παραγενόμενος). The ptcp. is temporally antecedent ("having arrived/appeared"), not concessive. ἀρχιερεύς is predicate-nominative apposition pinned to Χριστός via παραγενόμενος ("Christ arrived *as* high priest"). The second attributive ptcp. τῶν γενομένων ἀγαθῶν is adnominal to a suppressed head — idiomatic shorthand for "the good things that have come to pass" (variant reading μελλόντων, "about to come," flips to a future orientation; see BDF §339). Two comparative adjectives (μείζονος, τελειοτέρας) modify σκηνῆς in a chiastic flank around καί. Note: **διά + genitive here is locative/spatial**, not strictly instrumental — "through (the venue of)" the greater tabernacle (see §3 below). See [greek/paired-middle-participles](../greek/paired-middle-participles.md) for the stacked middle participles, and [greek/genitive-of-quality-apposition](../greek/genitive-of-quality-apposition.md) for the attributive genitive.

**Verbal frame:** the main clause finally lands on εἰσῆλθεν (aor.act.ind.), flanked by two adverbial elements — the negated δι' αἵματος τράγων καὶ μόσχων and the positive διὰ δὲ τοῦ ἰδίου αἵματος. **Verbal frame:** a **first-class conditional** (εἰ + pres.ind. in protasis) — treated as a concrete, granted premise for argumentation (Wallace *GGBB* 690-694). **Verbal frame:** the *a fortiori* (qal wa-ḥomer / *kal va-ḥomer*) axis lands here — πόσῳ μᾶλλον is a classical **dative of measure** used comparatively (BDF §195). The relative clause ὃς … προσήνεγκεν is an **aorist of past event** (the historical self-offering), syntactically subordinate but theologically load-bearing. διὰ Πνεύματος αἰωνίου is **instrumental** διά + gen.; the **anarthrous** Πνεύματος is debated (divine Spirit / Christ's own eternal spirit), but morphosyntactically the collocation αἰωνίου modifying πνεῦμα is a genitive-of-quality attributive adjective (cf. the adjacent αἰωνίαν λύτρωσιν in v.12 and αἰωνίου κληρονομίας in v.15 — the αἰών- cluster is a cohesion marker across the pericope). The main verb καθαριεῖ carries the **Attic-future** contraction (parser tag `V-FAI-3S-ATT`): the uncontracted *καθαρίσει would be expected; the Att. fut. is the older verbal-stem contraction surviving into Koine for liquid/nasal stems and for καθαρίζω (BDF §74). ἄμωμον is **predicative/double accusative** with προσφέρω ("offered himself *as* unblemished") — LXX sacrificial technical term (Lev 1:3; 3:1 etc.). The articular inf. εἰς τὸ λατρεύειν is a classic **εἰς τό + inf. of purpose** (Wallace *GGBB* 590-592; BDF §402-403) — tied to the purified συνείδησις, not to ἡμῶν independently.

**Verbal frame:** διὰ τοῦτο (causal διά + accusative) marks an inferential hinge. **Verbal frame:** impersonal ἀνάγκη + inf. construction — classical Greek idiom ("it is necessary for X to be brought"). The acc. θάνατον is **accusative-of-respect subject** of φέρεσθαι; the gen. τοῦ διαθεμένου is **genitive of possession** ("the death *of the testator*"). The shift from διαθήκη (covenant-cutting in LXX / Hebrews 9:15 etc.) to διαθέμενος (the one making a διαθήκη — more naturally glossed "testator") exploits Hellenistic semantic overlap. The parser tag `V-2AMP` (2nd aor.mid.ptcp.) of διατίθημι is critical: the middle voice matches both LXX covenant idiom (διατίθεσθαι διαθήκην = כָּרַת בְּרִית "cut a covenant," always LXX middle) and Hellenistic will-making. Grammar alone is genuinely ambiguous between the two senses.

## 3. The Two Senses of διά + Genitive in vv.11-12

The parser tags διά identically in v.11 and v.12 (`PREP` + following gen.), but the syntactic/semantic function differs:

| Verse | Phrase | διά + gen. function | Paraphrase |
|-------|--------|--------------------|------------|
| 11 | διὰ τῆς μείζονος … σκηνῆς | **Locative / spatial-path** ("through, via, by way of") | "passing through the greater … tabernacle" |
| 12a | δι' αἵματος τράγων καὶ μόσχων | **Instrumental / means** ("by means of, with") | "by means of blood of goats and calves" |
| 12b | διὰ δὲ τοῦ ἰδίου αἵματος | **Instrumental / means** | "by means of his own blood" |

**Why the distinction matters grammatically:** διά + gen. has two separable uses in classical and Koine Greek (Wallace *GGBB* 368-369; BDF §223):
- **Spatial/agentive intermediate** — "through (a space or a mediator)"; stresses transit or the channel.
- **Instrumental** — "by means of"; stresses efficient cause.

Verse 11's σκηνῆς is a location (a structure), not a means, so the spatial reading dominates — matching the high-priest typology of walking through the sanctuary structure in the Yom Kippur rite (Lev 16). Verse 12's αἵματος is a substance applied as efficient means, matching the instrumental reading. The shift is not arbitrary: it marks the transition from the **venue of ministry** (where? — the "greater tabernacle") to the **instrument of ministry** (by what? — "his own blood"). Hebrews leverages the same morpheme διά twice for distinct syntactic-semantic loads, relying on head-noun semantics to disambiguate.

### 4.1 Verbal Tense/Aspect Spine

| 12 | εὑράμενος | Aor Mid Ptcp | Coincident — "having obtained" |

| 15 | λάβωσιν | Aor Act Subj | Purpose — "that they may receive" |

| 21 | ἐράντισεν | Aor Act Ind | Repeated historical act |
| 22 | καθαρίζεται, γίνεται | Pres Pass / M/P Ind | **Gnomic universal maxim** |

**Three load-bearing finite verbs** stand out for their aspectual marking:
1. **εἰσῆλθεν (v.12)** — historical aorist punctiliar of Christ's definitive entry.
2. **καθαριεῖ (v.14)** — Attic future; the *a fortiori* prospective claim about the present and ongoing purification of conscience. Against this backdrop, the gnomic presents of the surrounding verses are subordinate premises.
3. **ἐνκεκαίνισται (v.18)** — perfect passive for the enduring inaugurated state of the first covenant, matching the perfect-of-state function of the perfect tense in Hebrews generally (cf. Heb 7:23-25; 10:14).

Wallace *GGBB* 414-430) or for lexically fossilized mid. deponents (γίνομαι, διατίθεμαι). **Perfect passive** participles (vv.13, 15) function substantivally, picking out classes of people defined by their enduring state (the defiled; the called).

### 4.3 Comparative Correlation (πόσῳ μᾶλλον, v.14)

The hinge of the pericope is the **πόσῳ μᾶλλον** (*a fortiori*) construction — a **dative of measure** (BDF §195-196). Grammatically: πόσῳ (Q-DSN, interrogative-intensive dat. sg. neut.) + μᾶλλον (comparative adv.) + implicit main clause. The construction presupposes that:
- A (protasis, v.13): "the blood of bulls and goats and ashes of a heifer sanctifies to purity of flesh" — an **admitted premise** in the εἰ (first-class) conditional.
- B (apodosis, v.14): "the blood of Christ will purge your conscience" — the **greater-scope claim**.

The grammatical logic is classical Semitic *qal wa-ḥomer* (light-and-heavy, "how much more") rendered into idiomatic Greek. The future καθαριεῖ in v.14 completes the dative-of-measure rhetorical move: if flesh-purity from the lesser blood is conceded, then conscience-purity from the greater blood follows *by the same measure or more*.

### 4.4 Covenant / Testament Wordplay (vv.15-17)

The grammatical anchor of vv.15-17 is διαθήκη (G1242) + διατίθημι (G1303). Morphology:
- **διαθήκη**: noun, feminine, regular -η declension ← root διά + τίθημι.
- **διατίθημι**: verb, root-final -η- aorist (second aorist middle διαθεμ-), all middle-voice in the relevant forms.
- **διαθέμενος / διαθεμένου**: aor.mid.ptcp. substantivized.

In LXX, διατίθημι + διαθήκη is the **stereotyped rendering of כָּרַת + בְּרִית** ("cut a covenant") — always middle voice (διαθέσθαι διαθήκην). In Hellenistic secular usage, διατίθημι alone (with no διαθήκη) means "arrange, dispose, make a will" — and the substantival participle ὁ διαθέμενος = "the testator." The grammatical ambiguity is genuine; the author exploits the polysemy, using covenant-language from v.15 and testament-logic in vv.16-17 under the cover of a single morpheme. Neither sense is imposed on the grammar; both fit. For the specific middle-voice nuance, cf. [greek/paired-middle-participles](../greek/paired-middle-participles.md).
