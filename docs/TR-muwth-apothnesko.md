## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same across testaments:**
- Death as the consequence of sin: Gen 2:17 (muwth) → Rom 5:12, 6:23 (apothnesko/thanatos) — the same cause-effect holds
- The universal scope of dying: "all die" continuity from Genesis 5 death register through 1 Cor 15:22
- The God-who-kills-and-makes-alive motif: Deu 32:39 (Hiphil of muwth + chayah) → Rom 4:17 (God who "quickeneth the dead")
- Judicial death for sin: Levitical מוֹת יוּמָת formula → Rom 1:32 "worthy of death"

**What shifts:**
- The NT applies apothnesko specifically to Christ's substitutionary death ("Christ died for us" — Rom 5:8), a usage without direct OT parallel for muwth
- NT introduces "dying with Christ" (Rom 6:8, 2 Tim 2:11) as a positive spiritual metaphor; OT muwth is overwhelmingly literal or judicial
- The "sting of death" reversal (1 Cor 15:55–56) reframes death from OT finality toward eschatological defeat
