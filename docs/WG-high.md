## Roles in the Concept

- **Primary verb of elevation:** rûwm (H7311) — the most versatile: physical lift, ritual heave, divine honor, praise, and pride
- **Lifting/bearing verb:** nâsâʼ (H5375) — carries weight to a higher position; used with "eyes," "voice," "hand," "soul" lifted
- **Soaring loftiness:** gâbahh (H1361) — physical height or arrogance; "high and lifted up" (Isa 2:12–13)
- **Inaccessible safety:** sâgab (H7682) — too high to be reached (as a secure refuge); "the LORD...his name is exalted (sâgab)"
- **Divine title:** ʻelyôwn (H5945) — the Most High; absolute superlative used of God as ruler over all nations
- **The high place / lofty abode:** mârôwm (H4791) — height as location or dwelling; "from on high he sent fire" (Lam 1:13); "the high and lofty One that inhabiteth eternity" (Isa 57:15)
