# WG: Vow (Promise, Bond, and Payment to God)

## Overview

The Hebrew concept of the vow is built on a tight lexical cluster: a cognate noun/verb pair for the vow itself (neder / nadar), a contrastive partner for unprompted giving (nedabah), a legal pair for the self-imposed binding (esar / asar), a verb of fulfillment (shalam), and the ritual category the vowed thing often belongs to (zebach). These seven words co-occur so densely — especially in Num 30, Lev 7, Deut 12, and the Psalms — that they form a single legal-liturgical vocabulary for voluntary obligation to the LORD.

## Roles in the Concept

- **Initiation (the act):** *nadar* (H5087) — the speech-act of promising to God
- **Content (the thing):** *neder* (H5088) — what is promised (offering, person, animal, property)
- **Binding mode:** *esar* (H632) + *asar* (H631) — the self-imposed prohibition or abstinence
- **Contrast / voluntary sibling:** *nedabah* (H5071) — unpromised, spontaneous giving
- **Fulfillment:** *shalam* (H7999) — "pay" what was vowed, making it שָׁלֵם (complete)
- **Ritual category:** *zebach* (H2077) — the sacrifice class most vows are discharged through

## Continuity into NT

The entire cluster collapses in the LXX onto **εὐχή (G2171)** and **εὔχομαι (G2172)**. In the NT the cluster surfaces only thinly: Acts 18:18 and 21:23 preserve the Nazirite-vow practice; Jas 5:15 shifts εὐχή toward "prayer." The legal-ritual weight of Num 30 has no NT counterpart.
