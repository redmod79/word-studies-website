### Complementary Distribution: Stauros vs. Xylon

The most significant relationship in this group is the non-overlapping distribution of stauros and xylon when referring to the cross:

- **Stauros** is used by Matthew, Mark, Luke, John, and Paul (in his epistles) for the physical cross and its theological significance
- **Xylon** ("tree") is used exclusively in passages that echo Deut 21:23 ("cursed is he that hangeth on a tree"):
  - Acts 5:30, 10:39, 13:29 (apostolic preaching to Jewish audiences)
  - Gal 3:13 (Paul quoting the curse text directly)
  - 1 Pet 2:24 (sin-bearing on the tree)

These two words never appear in the same verse — the choice between them signals whether the author is invoking OT curse-theology (xylon) or speaking of the cross as event/object/message (stauros).

## Roles in the Concept

- **Instrument/Event:** stauros (σταυρός) — the cross itself and the event of crucifixion viewed theologically
- **Action:** stauroo (σταυρόω) — the act of crucifying, both literal and figurative ("crucify the flesh")
- **Curse-Link:** xylon (ξύλον) — the cross viewed through the lens of Deut 21:23, connecting Christ's death to the covenant curse
- **Corporate Participation:** sustauroo (συσταυρόω) — being crucified with Christ (Rom 6:6, Gal 2:20)
- **Apostasy Warning:** anastauroo (ἀνασταυρόω) — crucifying the Son of God afresh (Heb 6:6)

## Key Passages Where Multiple Group Words Appear Together
