## Semantic Categories

### 1. Physical / Sensory Hearing
Simple auditory perception — hearing sounds, voices, news, or reports.
- **Gen 3:8** — "And they heard the voice of the LORD God walking in the garden"
- **Gen 14:14** — "And when Abram heard that his brother was taken captive, he armed his trained servants"
- **1 Sam 4:6** — "And when the Philistines heard the noise of the shout, they said, What meaneth the noise of this great shout"
- **Jer 4:19** — "my heart maketh a noise in me; I cannot hold my peace, because thou hast heard, O my soul, the sound of the trumpet"

### 2. Obedience / Covenantal Response
Hearing that issues in active compliance — the hinge verb of covenant fidelity. The formula "hearken to the voice of the LORD" (shama beqol YHWH) is the conditional verb for blessings and curses.
- **Deut 28:1** — "if thou shalt hearken diligently unto the voice of the LORD thy God, to observe and to do all his commandments... all these blessings shall come on thee"
- **Deut 28:15** — "if thou wilt not hearken unto the voice of the LORD thy God... all these curses shall come upon thee"
- **Exo 19:5** — "if ye will obey my voice indeed, and keep my covenant, then ye shall be a peculiar treasure unto me"
- **1 Sam 15:22** — "Hath the LORD as great delight in burnt offerings and sacrifices, as in obeying the voice of the LORD? Behold, to obey is better than sacrifice"

### 3. Refusal to Hear / Covenant Rebellion
The negated form — Israel's characteristic failure, especially in prophetic indictment. Often paired with "inclined not their ear" and "stiffened their neck."
- **Jer 7:24** — "But they hearkened not, nor inclined their ear, but walked in the counsels and in the imagination of their evil heart"
- **Jer 11:8** — "Yet they obeyed not, nor inclined their ear, but walked every one in the imagination of their evil heart"
- **Isa 42:24** — "Who gave Jacob for a spoil... did not the LORD, he against whom we have sinned? for they would not walk in his ways, neither were they obedient unto his law"
- **Ezek 3:7** — "the house of Israel will not hearken unto thee; for they will not hearken unto me"

### 4. God Hearing Prayer / Cries
God as the subject of shama — hearing the cries, prayers, and petitions of his people. A foundational theme of biblical prayer theology.
- **Gen 21:17** — "And God heard the voice of the lad; and the angel of God called to Hagar out of heaven"
- **Exo 2:24** — "And God heard their groaning, and God remembered his covenant with Abraham"
- **Deut 26:7** — "And when we cried unto the LORD God of our fathers, the LORD heard our voice"
- **Isa 65:24** — "And it shall come to pass, that before they call, I will answer; and while they are yet speaking, I will hear"

### 5. Proclamation / Making Heard (Hiphil)
Causative usage — to cause others to hear, i.e., to proclaim, announce, publish, or declare.
- **Jer 4:5** — "Declare ye in Judah, and publish in Jerusalem; and say, Blow ye the trumpet"
- **Isa 52:7** — "How beautiful upon the mountains are the feet of him that bringeth good tidings, that publisheth peace"
- **Deut 4:36** — "Out of heaven he made thee to hear his voice, that he might instruct thee"
- **Jer 31:7** — "shout among the chief of the nations: publish ye, praise ye, and say, O LORD, save thy people"

### 6. Understanding / Discernment
Hearing in the sense of comprehension — grasping meaning, not just sound. Overlaps with biyn (understand).
- **Gen 11:7** — "let us go down, and there confound their language, that they may not understand one another's speech"
- **Gen 42:23** — "And they knew not that Joseph understood them; for he spake unto them by an interpreter"
- **Isa 6:9** — "Hear ye indeed, but understand not" (judicial hardening: hearing without comprehension)
- **Deut 29:4** — "Yet the LORD hath not given you an heart to perceive, and eyes to see, and ears to hear, unto this day"

### 7. Wisdom Reception
Proverbs-specific usage — hearing instruction, counsel, and reproof as the mark of wisdom versus folly.
- **Prov 1:5** — "A wise man will hear, and will increase learning"
- **Prov 1:33** — "But whoso hearkeneth unto me shall dwell safely, and shall be quiet from fear of evil"
- **Prov 8:33** — "Hear instruction, and be wise, and refuse it not"
- **Prov 12:15** — "he that hearkeneth unto counsel is wise"
