## OT Usage (Hebrew)

| Strong's | Word | Occurrences | Primary Meaning |
|----------|------|-------------|-----------------|
| H7364 | רָחַץ (rachats) | 71 | wash, bathe (the whole or a part of the body) |

Rachats is the OT verb for laving with water — covering full-body immersion (priestly purification, Lev 16:4), partial washing (feet, Gen 18:4; hands, Exo 30:19), and washing sacrificial parts (inwards and legs, Lev 1:9). Leviticus accounts for 37% of occurrences. Typically paired with kabas (H3526, garment washing) in the standard purification formula: "wash his clothes and bathe his flesh in water."

**Key OT passages:**
- **Exo 29:4** — "shalt wash them with water" (priestly consecration)
- **Lev 16:4** — "shall he wash his flesh in water" (Day of Atonement)
- **Isa 1:16** — "Wash you, make you clean" (moral/figurative cleansing)

## LXX Bridge

| Hebrew | Greek Translation | Co-occ | PMI | Notes |

| rachats (H7364) | λούω (louo) G3068 | 40 | 30.82 | Primary — full-body bathing |
| rachats (H7364) | νίπτω (nipto) G3538 | 19 | 24.74 | Partial washing — hands, feet |
| rachats (H7364) | πλύνω (plyno) G4150 | 25 | 24.21 | Washing objects (some overlap with kabas) |

**Semantic differentiation in translation:** Hebrew rachats covers all washing — whole body, partial body, and sacrificial parts — without distinction. The LXX translators split this single Hebrew verb into three Greek verbs based on what is being washed: louo for full-body bathing, nipto for partial washing (hands/feet), and plyno for objects. This threefold Greek distinction carries directly into the NT, where louo and nipto are carefully distinguished (see John 13:10).

## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same across testaments:**
- The concept of washing/bathing as preparation for approaching God persists: OT priests bathed before entering sacred space (Lev 16:4); NT believers are described as having bodies "washed with pure water" before drawing near (Heb 10:22)
- Water remains the medium: OT "bathe in water," NT "washed with pure water"
- The rachats/louo action consistently denotes thoroughgoing cleansing of the whole person, not superficial rinsing

**What shifts:**
- The LXX's three-way split of rachats (louo/nipto/plyno) introduces precision absent in Hebrew; the NT inherits this distinction, especially in John 13:10 where the louo/nipto contrast carries theological weight
- Physical ritual washing (OT) becomes metaphorical in the NT: washing from sins (Rev 1:5), washing of regeneration (Titus 3:5 uses loutron, the cognate noun), bodies washed as spiritual preparation (Heb 10:22)
- OT rachats is overwhelmingly cultic-ritual (Leviticus 37% of uses); NT louo is overwhelmingly figurative/spiritual (4 of 6 uses)
