## Semantic Categories

### 1. Divorce and Remarriage (Jesus's Legal Teaching)
Paired with ἀπολύω (put away/divorce) and μοιχάω (commit adultery); defines the legal and moral consequences of remarriage after divorce.
- **Matt 5:32** — "whosoever shall put away his wife... causeth her to commit adultery: and whosoever shall marry her that is divorced committeth adultery"
- **Matt 19:9** — "Whosoever shall put away his wife, except it be for fornication, and shall marry another, committeth adultery"
- **Mark 10:11–12** — bilateral statement: equally applies to husband and wife who divorce and remarry
- **Luke 16:18** — "whosoever putteth away his wife, and marrieth another, committeth adultery"

### 2. Eschatological Non-Marriage (The Resurrection State)
Used to define what the resurrected life is *not* — marriage belongs to the present age, not the age to come.
- **Matt 22:30** — "in the resurrection they neither marry, nor are given in marriage, but are as the angels of God in heaven"
- **Mark 12:25** — "when they shall rise from the dead, they neither marry, nor are given in marriage; but are as the angels"
- **Luke 20:34–35** — "The children of this world marry... But they which shall be accounted worthy to obtain that world... neither marry, nor are given in marriage"

### 3. Normal Life Before Judgment (Days of Noah Pattern)
Used to describe unremarkable, ordinary activity that characterizes life before sudden divine intervention.
- **Matt 24:38** — "as in the days before the flood they were eating and drinking, marrying and giving in marriage, until the day that Noe entered into the ark"
- **Luke 17:27** — "They did eat, they drank, they married wives, they were given in marriage, until the day that Noe entered into the ark"

### 4. Pastoral Counsel on Whether to Marry (Paul)
Paul weighs the practical and spiritual value of marriage vs. singleness; the word is used in command, permission, and warning.
- **1 Cor 7:9** — "if they cannot contain, let them marry: for it is better to marry than to burn"
- **1 Cor 7:28** — "if thou marry, thou hast not sinned; and if a virgin marry, she hath not sinned"
- **1 Tim 4:3** — forbidding to marry listed as a doctrine of devils (ascetic error)
- **1 Tim 5:14** — "I will therefore that the younger women marry, bear children, guide the house"

### 5. Historical and Narrative Use
Simple factual statement of marriage entering into a narrative context.
- **Mark 6:17** — "Herod himself had... bound [John] in prison for Herodias' sake, his brother Philip's wife: for he had married her"
- **Matt 22:25** — "the first, when he had married a wife, deceased" (the levirate marriage scenario posed to Jesus)
- **Luke 14:20** — "I have married a wife, and therefore I cannot come" (excuses for not attending the feast)
