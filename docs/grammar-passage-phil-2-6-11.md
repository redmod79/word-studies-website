## Key Grammatical Features

### 1. ὑπάρχων (v.6) — present active participle of ὑπάρχω as pre-existent stative frame
- **Form:** Pres Act Ptcp Nom Sg M, masc sg agreeing with relative ὅς (antecedent = Χριστὸς Ἰησοῦς v.5).
- **Function:** concessive / causal **adverbial participle** scoping the main verb ἡγήσατο; lexically ὑπάρχω denotes **antecedent, settled, ongoing state of existence / possession** (classical "to exist beforehand, be already at hand"; cf. Luke 16:14; Acts 2:30; 1 Cor 11:7; Gal 2:14). The present-tense aspect does not locate the state temporally but predicates **abiding, characteristic existence** relative to the main verb's time-frame.
- **Significance:** establishes the **subject-state** presupposed by the main verb: ὅς‿ὑπάρχων‿ἐν‿μορφῇ‿Θεοῦ = "who, [already] being in [the] form of God"; the participle does not *assert* the pre-existence but presupposes it as the background from which the ἡγήσατο-decision is made. Rendered either concessively ("though existing in the form of God") or causally ("because he was existing in the form of God") depending on how one reads the ἀλλά-reversal at v.7.
- **Grammar reference:** see `greek/participle-perfect-passive-state.md` (analogous state-aspect analysis) and `greek/genitive-of-quality-apposition.md` (on μορφή Θεοῦ below).

### 2. μορφῇ Θεοῦ (v.6) ‖ μορφὴν δούλου (v.7) — construct-chain inclusio with genitive of quality/source
- **Form:** `μορφή` (G3444; NT 3× — Mark 16:12; Phil 2:6, 7) governs anarthrous genitives Θεοῦ (v.6 N-GSM) and δούλου (v.7 N-GSM); ETCBC/Nestle parser tags v.6 μορφῇ as Dat Sg F (locative ἐν μορφῇ) and v.7 μορφήν as Acc Sg F (object of λαβών).
- **Genitive classification:** the Θεοῦ / δούλου genitives are open between (a) **possessive / source** ("form belonging to / proceeding from God / a servant"), (b) **attributive / quality** ("divine form / servile form" = Semitic "form of X" = "X-characterized form"), or (c) **descriptive / definitional apposition**. Wallace 78–100, BDF §§162–168 — see library entry on the Hebraistic "X of Y" = "Y-characterized X" pattern at [genitive-of-quality-apposition](../greek/genitive-of-quality-apposition.md).
- **Lexical note on μορφή:** denotes the **outward form expressing inward nature** (classical contrast with σχῆμα = outward fashion / configuration without ontological commitment, the very term Paul uses in v.7c σχήματι εὑρεθεὶς ὡς ἄνθρωπος); BDAG gloss "form, outward appearance, shape"; Lightfoot (1868) analysed μορφή as specific form-expressing-essence, σχῆμα as changeable fashion — a lexical distinction the two-noun juxtaposition within the hymn exploits, whether or not Lightfoot's categorical distinction holds across all Greek usage.
- **Inclusio syntax:** v.6 ἐν μορφῇ Θεοῦ ὑπάρχων (PREP + dat. + PAP-NSM) ↔ v.7 μορφὴν δούλου λαβών (acc. + aor.act.ptcp NSM). The syntactic mirror (both head-participles modifying the same ὅς-subject) predicates **two morphai** of the same grammatical subject across the hymn's hinge ἀλλά.
- **Significance:** the two μορφή-clauses are the hymn's frame — whatever analytic content μορφή carries at v.6 (divine/ontological vs. royal-glory appearance vs. existential-condition), the **same word** recurs at v.7 where it unambiguously governs δούλου. Grammar does not adjudicate between "μορφή = οὐσία" (ontological equivalence) and "μορφή = external manifestation of nature" readings; grammar **does** refuse any reading that allows μορφή Θεοῦ to drop in register below μορφή δούλου, since the two are syntactically parallel predications of the same subject.
- **Grammar reference:** [genitive-of-quality-apposition](../greek/genitive-of-quality-apposition.md).
- **Word study:** [G2316-theos](../../word-studies/G2316-theos.md).

### 3. οὐχ ἁρπαγμὸν ἡγήσατο τὸ εἶναι ἴσα Θεῷ (v.6) — "did not regard equality with God as ἁρπαγμός"
- **Head verb:** ἡγήσατο (V-ADI-3S of ἡγέομαι, aor. mid. dep. "consider, regard") — double-accusative construction "regard X as Y" with **X = τὸ εἶναι ἴσα Θεῷ** (articular infinitive as accusative of respect / object) and **Y = ἁρπαγμόν** (predicate accusative, fronted by negation οὐχ before ἁρπαγμόν).
- **ἁρπαγμός (G725, NT hapax):** parser-verified unique occurrence at Phil 2:6. Formed from ἁρπάζω "seize, snatch." The **-μος** nominal suffix is classically *resultative* ("the thing seized," *res rapta*) vs. active/abstract **-σις** ("the act of seizing," *raptus*). Four lexical-interpretive options standardly surveyed (context-neutral — grammar alone does not decide):
  1. **Res rapienda — "a thing to be grasped/obtained"**: equality with God is something Christ did not presently possess but refused to snatch (kenosis-as-restraint-from-aspiration; favoured by Hoover/Wright idiom study when paired with ἡγέομαι-idiom analysis below).
  2. **Res rapta — "a thing already seized/robbery"**: treating equality as booty unjustly held and clung to; hence KJV "thought it not robbery."
  3. **Res retinenda — "a thing to be held onto / clung to"**: Christ did not regard equality with God as something to exploit for his own advantage (NRSV/ESV line; Hoover 1971 "harpagmon hegeisthai" as fixed idiom "regard X as something to take advantage of").
  4. **Advantage / prize** (privilege-possessed-and-exploited): the thing already possessed but not leveraged selfishly.
- **Idiomatic reading (Hoover):** the construction *ἁρπαγμόν / ἅρπαγμα ἡγεῖσθαι* appears in patristic and some classical texts as a **fixed idiom** meaning "regard as something to take advantage of" / "treat as a windfall to be exploited" — in which case the ontological question of whether equality was already possessed drops out of the idiom's surface semantics and must be carried by the surrounding ὑπάρχων-participle and the μορφή Θεοῦ phrase.
- **Articular infinitive τὸ εἶναι:** the neuter article τό (T-ASN) substantivises the infinitive εἶναι, creating an accusative-of-specification that functions as the X-slot in the double-accusative; εἶναι = Pres Act Inf of εἰμί, state-verb. Articular infinitives in this position are classically nominal (Wallace 220–221, BDF §399); here it functions as the *object* of ἡγήσατο, parallel in case to ἁρπαγμόν.
- **ἴσα (A-APN, Acc Pl N, adverbial):** neuter plural **adverbial** accusative of ἴσος ("equally") — *not* a predicate nominative/adjective agreeing with any noun. Cf. Job 11:12 LXX ἴσα ὄνῳ, Wis 7:3. Construes with εἶναι + dative Θεῷ: "to be on a par with God" / "to be equally with God" / "to exist in a mode of equality with God." Parser cross-verifies this reading: ἴσα A-APN (neuter plural) cannot agree with masculine singular subject ὅς, so the form is necessarily adverbial here (contrast John 5:18 ἴσον A-ASM agreeing with ἑαυτόν).
- **Significance:** the clause predicates of Christ a **refusal of an exploitative stance** toward τὸ εἶναι ἴσα Θεῷ. Whether "being-equal-with-God" is Christ's antecedent possession (expressed retrospectively from μορφῇ Θεοῦ ὑπάρχων) or a status he could have grasped, the grammar binds the refusal to the same subject who performs the kenosis of v.7.

### 4. ἑαυτὸν ἐκένωσεν (v.7) — aorist active of κενόω with reflexive object
- **Form:** ἐκένωσεν = V-AAI-3S of κενόω (G2758 "to empty"); ἑαυτόν = F-3ASM, third-person reflexive pronoun in accusative, *direct object* of the transitive verb.
- **Voice:** active indicative + reflexive pronoun (Christ is both grammatical subject and object of the action). This is a **morphological active + lexical reflexivity** construction — distinct from a middle-voice "emptied-himself-for-his-own-advantage" construal. Greek had other options (true reflexive middle, or the alternative at Phil 2:8 same-pattern ἑαυτὸν ἐταπείνωσεν "humbled himself") — Paul consistently chooses active + ἑαυτόν for both verbs.
- **Aorist aspect:** perfective, bounding the kenosis as a **completed single event** viewed as a whole (classically point-action in this context, though aorist does not intrinsically denote instantaneity; Wallace 554–565; BDF §318); the aorist frame clamps kenosis to the incarnation-moment rather than spreading it over Christ's earthly life.
- **κενόω NT profile (parser-verified, 5×):** Rom 4:14 κεκένωται (Perf Pass "has been made void"); 1 Cor 1:17 κενωθῇ (Aor Pass Subj, "be made void"); 1 Cor 9:15 κενώσει (Fut Act, "make void"); 2 Cor 9:3 κενωθῇ (Aor Pass Subj); Phil 2:7 ἐκένωσεν (Aor Act). In **every other NT occurrence** κενόω carries abstract metaphorical sense "nullify / render void / empty of force" — the Phil 2:7 use is the **only NT instance with a personal reflexive accusative direct object**, which makes the lexical question of *what* was emptied (of *what* content) exegetically open:
  1. **Subtraction kenosis:** Christ emptied himself *of* something (divine attributes, glory, status-exercise) — this would ordinarily require a partitive genitive (κενόω + gen.-of-separation) which is **absent here**; Paul provides no explicit object-of-emptying.
  2. **Self-referential / metaphorical kenosis:** the object of the emptying *is* himself ("he poured himself out" — cf. Isa 53:12 LXX παρεδόθη εἰς θάνατον ἡ ψυχὴ αὐτοῦ, Heb. הֶעֱרָה לַמָּוֶת נַפְשׁוֹ, the Servant "poured out his soul to death") — in which case ἐκένωσεν is idiomatic for self-giving / self-expenditure, and the lexical content is filled not by subtraction but by the **participial clauses following**.
  3. **Status-exchange kenosis:** the emptying is expressed not by subtraction of a quality but by the **two accompanying aorist participles** μορφὴν δούλου λαβών (taking a slave-form) + ἐν ὁμοιώματι ἀνθρώπων γενόμενος (coming to be in human likeness) — i.e., the "how" of the emptying is coincident-aorist participial: he emptied himself *by taking* and *by becoming*. The kenosis is the incarnation, not a prior subtraction.
- **λαβών + γενόμενος (aorist participles, v.7):** V-2AAP-NSM of λαμβάνω and V-2ADP-NSM of γίνομαι, both masc sg agreeing with the subject of ἐκένωσεν. **Coincident-action aorist participles** (not strictly antecedent) — Greek standardly uses aorist participles to predicate attendant/definitive action that constitutes the main verb (Wallace 624, Fanning 388). These are the grammatical answer to "by what means did he empty himself?": by taking slave-form, by becoming in human likeness.
- **Significance:** the refusal to adjudicate between subtraction and self-giving kenosis is itself a grammatical observation, not evasion. Paul's syntax supplies **no genitive-of-emptying** and **two coincident participles of self-identification**. The kenosis-clause is therefore lexically determined not by an implied object-of-subtraction but by the syntactic chain that follows.

### 5. Aorist participial cascade and copulative chain (vv.7–8)
- **Sequence of aorist participles** (all NSM agreeing with hymn-subject):
  - λαβών (V-2AAP-NSM, λαμβάνω) — "having taken"
  - γενόμενος (V-2ADP-NSM, γίνομαι, v.7) — "having come to be"
  - εὑρεθείς (V-APP-NSM, εὑρίσκω, v.7c) — "being found" (**passive**)
  - γενόμενος (V-2ADP-NSM, γίνομαι, v.8) — "becoming / having come to be"
- **εὑρεθείς (aor. pass.):** the only **passive** participle in the sequence. Divine-passive register ([divine-passive](../greek/divine-passive.md)): Christ **was found** (by observers / from the human angle) ὡς ἄνθρωπος — the passive shifts viewpoint from agent to observer, reinforcing σχῆμα (external configuration / fashion) as **how he appeared** to human perception.
- **σχῆμα vs. μορφή:** σχήματι (v.7, Dat Sg N of σχῆμα, G4976) — *outward configuration / appearance / fashion* — deliberately chosen alongside μορφή rather than as its synonym. The hymn moves from μορφή Θεοῦ → μορφή δούλου → ὁμοίωμα ἀνθρώπων → σχῆμα ὡς ἄνθρωπος, a sequence that progressively narrows from ontic-status-expression to external-appearance. σχῆμα is the term applied to what is humanly observable; μορφή is the term applied to both divine and servile status-expression.
- **Finite-verb mainline:** the participial chain is anchored to two aorist-active-indicative mainline verbs in consecutive clauses — ἐκένωσεν (v.7 head) + ἐταπείνωσεν (v.8 head), both V-AAI-3S with reflexive ἑαυτόν object. This is a **paired morphological pattern** (same tense, voice, mood, person, with same reflexive object) that syntactically couples the two self-actions. The vertical descent proceeds: *emptied himself → humbled himself → became obedient μέχρι θανάτου → [θανάτου δὲ] σταυροῦ* (v.8 closing appositional genitive-of-specification θανάτου σταυροῦ as the nadir).

### 6. διὸ καί + ὑπερύψωσεν + ἐχαρίσατο (v.9) — inferential reversal and superlative-prefix hapax
- **διό (G1352):** illative/inferential conjunction "wherefore, for this reason" — marks v.9 as the **divine response** grounded in the self-humiliation of vv.6–8. Semantically loaded conjunction (cf. Rom 1:24; 15:7; 2 Cor 4:13; Heb 3:7): the exaltation is not additional to the humiliation but **consequent upon** it.
- **ὑπερύψωσεν (V-AAI-3S of ὑπερυψόω, G5251):** parser-verified **NT hapax**. Morphology = prefix ὑπέρ (over/above/beyond) + ὑψόω (lift up / exalt). The ὑπέρ-prefix is classically **intensive / superlative** ("exalt to the highest degree") rather than merely comparative ("exalt over [someone]"). Paul uses ὑπέρ-compound intensives elsewhere (ὑπερεκπερισσοῦ 1 Thess 3:10; ὑπερπερισσεύω Rom 5:20; ὑπερνικῶμεν Rom 8:37; ὑπερβάλλω 2 Cor 3:10). The form is the **maximal exaltation-verb available to Paul** — "super-exalted / exalted to the highest possible status."
- **ἐχαρίσατο (V-ADI-3S of χαρίζομαι, G5483):** aorist middle deponent "graciously gave, granted." Pairs with ὑπερύψωσεν in a coordinate conjunction (καί) — a single two-verb divine action: the exaltation *is* the granting of the Name.
- **τὸ ὄνομα τὸ ὑπὲρ πᾶν ὄνομα:** article-noun-article-prepositional-phrase — **second-attributive position** (the repeated article τό before the qualifier). Wallace 306. The construction specifies "the Name, namely *the* [one] above every name" — **the Name** (definite, singular, supreme). What Name? The hymn itself discloses it at v.11: ΚΥΡΙΟΣ.
- **Grammar reference:** [divine-passive](../greek/divine-passive.md) (for the grammatical theology of God-as-subject / agent-of-exaltation).
- **Word study:** [G3686-onoma](../../word-studies/G3686-onoma.md); [TR-shem-onoma](../../word-studies/TR-shem-onoma.md); [TR-ruwm-hupsoo](../../word-studies/TR-ruwm-hupsoo.md) — Hebrew רוּם / גָּדַל semantic field behind ὑψόω / ὑπερυψόω.

### 7. Isa 45:23 allusion and the κύριος / ὁμολογία logic (vv.10–11)
- **Purpose-clause architecture:** ἵνα + two aorist subjunctives — κάμψῃ (V-AAS-3S of κάμπτω, v.10) and ἐξομολογήσηται (V-AMS-3S of ἐξομολογέω, v.11) — **telic / purpose clause** after ἐχαρίσατο (v.9): the name-granting has as its purpose the universal knee-bowing and tongue-confessing. The two subjunctives are coordinate under a single ἵνα, unified in purpose.
- **Phil 2:10b–11a ‖ Isa 45:23 — direct LXX echo.** Isa 45:23 LXX reads **ὅτι ἐμοὶ κάμψει πᾶν γόνυ καὶ ἐξομολογήσεται πᾶσα γλῶσσα τῷ θεῷ** (lexical match: κάμπτω + γόνυ + πᾶς; ἐξομολογέω + γλῶσσα + πᾶς). MT Isa 45:23 `כִּי־לִי תִּכְרַע כָּל־בֶּרֶךְ תִּשָּׁבַע כָּל־לָשׁוֹן` — Qal.Impf.3fs תִּכְרַע (כרע, kneel) + Niphal.Impf.3fs תִּשָּׁבַע (שׁבע, swear). MT Hebrew שׁבע = "swear (oath)" (Niphal); LXX translates with ἐξομολογήσεται ("confess, acknowledge openly" — the normal LXX rendering of הוֹדָה / יָדָה) — a translational shift from Hebrew oath-swearing to Greek public acknowledgement. Paul's Phil 2:10–11 lexically matches the **LXX** (κάμπτω + γόνυ + πᾶς; ἐξομολογέω + γλῶσσα + πᾶς), confirming LXX-dependence.
- **Isa 45 context (the source):** Isa 45:21–23 is the canonical monotheistic climax of the Isaiah 40–48 "YHWH alone is God" oracle-series. In Isa 45:22–23 YHWH's own speech — `אֲנִי־אֵל וְאֵין עוֹד` / "I am God, and there is none else" — is immediately followed by the oath-formula `בִּי נִשְׁבַּעְתִּי` ("I have sworn by myself," Niphal.Perf.1cs of שׁבע, first-person reflexive-dative construction with בִּי): the universal knee-bowing and tongue-swearing in v.23 are directed exclusively to **YHWH himself as the sole proper object of monotheistic worship**. The MT v.24 (quoted in Rom 14:11, where Paul explicitly applies Isa 45:23 LXX to eschatological judgement before God) confirms the exclusive-worship reading.
- **Paul's redirection:** Phil 2:10 substitutes **ἐν τῷ ὀνόματι Ἰησοῦ** (in the name of Jesus) for the MT's `לִי` / LXX's **ἐμοί** (to me [= YHWH]). The Isa 45:23 universal-worship scene — originally YHWH-exclusive in its monotheistic setting — is **redeployed with Jesus as the referent of the knee-bowing and tongue-confessing.** Three grammatical markers compound the redirection:
  1. **κύριος (v.11):** the standard LXX rendering of the Tetragrammaton **יהוה** (e.g., Isa 45:23 LXX MT-`בִּי` → LXX-`κατ' ἐμαυτοῦ`; MT-`לִי` → LXX-`ἐμοί` with implicit divine-name referent; **but Isa 45:24 immediately uses κύριος for YHWH: `ἐν τῷ κυρίῳ`**). When Paul writes **Κύριος Ἰησοῦς Χριστός** as the confessional content, the title that the LXX uses to render YHWH is being predicated of Jesus.
  2. **τὸ ὄνομα τὸ ὑπὲρ πᾶν ὄνομα (v.9b) + ἐν τῷ ὀνόματι Ἰησοῦ (v.10a):** the transferred "Name above every name" — which the hymn does not lexicalise until v.11 — is resolved by v.11 as **κύριος**, i.e., the divine Name. Patristic and modern commentators concur: the Name given to Jesus at the exaltation is YHWH's own Name (κύριος/יהוה), not the human proper name "Jesus" (which he had from birth). Jewish tradition's reluctance to pronounce the Tetragrammaton and standard substitution of אֲדֹנָי / κύριος (Kyrios) in liturgical reading makes the background unavoidable.
  3. **Universal tripartite scope ἐπουρανίων καὶ ἐπιγείων καὶ καταχθονίων (v.10b):** three-tier cosmology (A-GPM + A-GPN + A-GPM) dependent on πᾶν γόνυ — the scope of the knee-bowing is the whole cosmos, matching Isa 45:23's `כָּל־בֶּרֶךְ` / `πᾶν γόνυ`; the scope that in Isaiah is YHWH-exclusive is here Jesus-inclusive.
- **ἐξομολογήσηται (v.11, V-AMS-3S, aor mid subj):** middle voice of ἐξομολογέω — "make public acknowledgement." Parser profile (10× NT): divided between confess-sins contexts (Mark 1:5; Matt 3:6; Acts 19:18; Jas 5:16) and public-acknowledgement-of-God contexts (Matt 11:25 ‖ Luke 10:21 of thanksgiving to the Father; Rom 14:11 [quoting Isa 45:23]; Rom 15:9; Phil 2:11). Phil 2:11 and Rom 14:11 are the **two NT instances applying Isa 45:23 eschatologically** — Rom 14:11 explicitly quotes "ζῶ ἐγώ λέγει κύριος, ὅτι ἐμοὶ κάμψει πᾶν γόνυ καὶ πᾶσα γλῶσσα ἐξομολογήσεται τῷ θεῷ" and applies the pronoun **ἐμοί** to God (τῷ θεῷ) at the judgement-seat (v.10 τῷ βήματι τοῦ θεοῦ [TR τοῦ Χριστοῦ]); Phil 2:11 redirects the same LXX echo so that the referent of the knee-bowing is **Ἰησοῦς** and the content of the tongue-confession is **Κύριος Ἰησοῦς Χριστός**.
- **εἰς δόξαν Θεοῦ Πατρός (v.11b):** purpose / result phrase with εἰς (G1519). The exaltation of Jesus to κύριος-status and the universal confession terminate **εἰς** the glory of the Father. Grammar here establishes a two-tier theological frame within a single sentence: the Son receives the Name and the universal confession, **and** the Father is glorified by it — a non-competitive structure that preserves monotheistic coherence while grammatically locating the receiving-end of knee-bowing with Jesus. Whether this frame entails ontic equality (classical pro-Nicene reading) or a functional-lordship delegation (adoptionist / subordinationist / some Messianic readings) is not decided by the grammar of v.11 in isolation, but the **Isa 45:23 allusion forces the reader to account for the fact that the language of sole-YHWH-worship is applied to Jesus**.

### 8. ὅτι (v.11) — recitative vs. content-explanation
- **Form:** ὅτι (G3754, CONJ) after ἐξομολογήσηται — classically ambiguous between (a) **recitative ὅτι** introducing direct discourse ("every tongue shall confess: 'Jesus Christ [is] Lord'"), and (b) **content / declarative ὅτι** introducing an indirect-discourse clause ("every tongue shall confess *that* Jesus Christ is Lord").
- **Verbless predication:** the clause has no copula — κύριος / Ἰησοῦς / Χριστός are all Nom Sg M. The standard construal treats κύριος as the predicate nominative and Ἰησοῦς Χριστός as the subject: "Jesus Christ [is] Lord." The alternative "Lord Jesus Christ" as a fixed triple-title formula (cf. Rom 1:7; 1 Cor 1:2–3; countless salutations) is morphologically possible — but Paul's consistent placement of κύριος as fronted predicate (cf. Rom 10:9 ὅτι Κύριον Ἰησοῦν — with Ἰησοῦν in ACC as object of ὁμολογήσῃς, cf. 1 Cor 12:3) supports the predicate reading.
- **Significance:** the hymn's terminal confession IS the Isa 45:23 LXX content predicated of Jesus. Whichever ὅτι-reading one takes, the content is identical: **Jesus is Lord**, and that confession is the universal tongue-content fulfilling the Isaianic prophecy.

## Clause Structure

- vv.6–8: single sentence anchored on two paired finite-verb clauses (ἐκένωσεν + ἐταπείνωσεν), each with reflexive ἑαυτόν, each followed by aorist participial subordinate clauses.
- v.9 opens a new sentence with διὸ καί — **consecutive / inferential pivot** from humiliation to exaltation; two coordinate V-AAI-3S mainlines (ὑπερύψωσεν + ἐχαρίσατο) share ὁ Θεός as grammatical subject.
- vv.10–11: telic ἵνα-clause with two coordinate aorist subjunctives (κάμψῃ, ἐξομολογήσηται), each with its own πᾶς-subject (πᾶν γόνυ, πᾶσα γλῶσσα).
- Result: the hymn moves **descent (Christ-subject, self-verbs) → ascent (God-subject, God-verbs of exaltation) → universal response (all-subject, purpose-verbs)** across three grammatical acts linked by διό.

**Voice / subject chiasm:**
- vv.6–8: **Christ as grammatical subject** of active verbs (ἡγήσατο, ἐκένωσεν, ἐταπείνωσεν) with reflexive objects + concessive / adverbial participles.
- v.9: **God as grammatical subject** of active verbs (ὑπερύψωσεν, ἐχαρίσατο) with Christ as object (αὐτόν, αὐτῷ).
- vv.10–11: **cosmos as grammatical subject** (πᾶν γόνυ, πᾶσα γλῶσσα) with telic-subjunctive verbs directed toward / concerning Christ (ἐν τῷ ὀνόματι Ἰησοῦ; Κύριος Ἰησοῦς Χριστός).

This voice-and-subject chiasm encodes the hymn's inner logic at the grammatical level: self-emptying Christ → God-acting-upon-Christ → cosmos-acknowledging-Christ.

## Context-Neutrality Note

This entry documents the grammar of the hymn without adjudicating between the interpretive traditions the grammar licenses:
- **μορφή Θεοῦ** — divine-essence reading (Nicene / pro-Chalcedonian) vs. royal-glory-appearance reading (Dunn / Murphy-O'Connor adapted Adam Christology) vs. existential-status reading: all compatible with the genitive-construct grammar; syntactic inclusio with μορφὴν δούλου constrains but does not force.
- **ἁρπαγμός** — *res rapta* vs. *res rapienda* vs. *res retinenda* vs. fixed-idiom "exploit-for-advantage" (Hoover): grammar alone does not decide; lexical/idiomatic history does not decide; the cumulative construction with ὑπάρχων + ἴσα Θεῷ constrains but does not force.
- **ἐκένωσεν** — subtraction kenosis (classical kenotic Christology) vs. self-giving / metaphorical kenosis (contemporary consensus) vs. status-exchange kenosis (the coincident-participle-cascade reading): grammar supplies no partitive genitive and therefore does not force subtraction; equally, does not forbid it.
- **Κύριος Ἰησοῦς Χριστός + Isa 45:23 echo** — pre-existence-and-ontological-divinity Christology (classical) vs. messianic-exaltation-to-Lordship Christology (some modern / some NT-scholarship readings) vs. monotheistic-redefinition / two-powers-in-heaven Second-Temple background readings: the LXX echo and Name-transfer are grammatically / lexically secure; what they entail ontologically is not adjudicated by grammar alone. **What is grammatically unavoidable**: the universal worship-language of Isa 45:23 (in its MT/LXX monotheistic setting) is applied to Jesus by Paul.
