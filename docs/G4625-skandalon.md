## Lexicon Definition

Originally the trigger-stick of a trap or snare — the bent stick on which the bait rested, whose disturbance released the mechanism. By metonymy: any obstacle or inducement that catches someone unawares and brings them down. In biblical Greek (LXX, NT) the word is figurative from the start — a stumbling-block, a cause of sin or apostasy, an occasion of moral ruin. The English word "scandal" derives from this term.

## Pauline Context — Rom 14:13; 1 Cor 8:9 (paired with G4348)

In Paul's weak-brother ethics, σκάνδαλον (Rom 14:13) and πρόσκομμα (Rom 14:13, 20; 1 Cor 8:9) function as interchangeable labels for what a mature believer's exercise of liberty can become to the conscience-weak. Rom 14:13 stacks them: "no man put a *proskomma* or *skandalon* in his brother's way." The two nouns together cover the full semantic field — obstacle plus trap.
