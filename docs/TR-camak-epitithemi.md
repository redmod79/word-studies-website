## Direct OT→NT Continuity

| Usage | OT Pattern (samak) | NT Pattern (epitithemi) |
|-------|--------------------|-----------------------|
| Ordination / Commissioning | Moses lays hands on Joshua (Num 27:18-23, Deut 34:9) — transfers authority and spirit of wisdom | Apostles lay hands on deacons (Acts 6:6), Paul and Barnabas commissioned (Acts 13:3) |
| Identification / Transfer | Offerer lays hand on sacrifice's head — identification and sin transfer (Lev 1:4, 4:4, 16:21) | No direct NT sacrifice parallel, but Jesus "bears our sins" (1 Pet 2:24) uses different vocabulary |
| Healing / Blessing | Not prominent in OT samak usage | Jesus and apostles lay hands for healing (Mark 6:5, Luke 4:40, Acts 28:8) — a new dominant usage |
| Spirit-conferral | Moses → Joshua: "full of the spirit of wisdom" (Deut 34:9) | Apostles → believers: "they received the Holy Ghost" (Acts 8:17, 19:6) |

## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same across testaments:**
- The physical gesture of placing hands on a head persists from Levitical sacrifice through apostolic practice
- The commissioning/ordination function transfers directly: Moses → Joshua becomes apostles → deacons/elders
- The concept that hand-laying creates a real connection between the one laying hands and the recipient

**What shifts:**
- The sacrificial hand-laying (samak's largest OT category) has no direct NT ritual counterpart — Christ's sacrifice replaces the entire system
- Healing through hand-laying, rare in OT samak usage, becomes a dominant NT pattern
- Spirit-conferral, present in one OT instance (Moses → Joshua), becomes a major NT category (Acts 8:17, 19:6)
- The NT adds the warning against premature ordination (1 Tim 5:22) — recognizing that hand-laying creates liability
