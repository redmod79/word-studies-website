A primitive root; to send away, for, or out (in a great variety of applications): any wise, appoint, bring (on the way), cast (away, out), conduct, earnestly, forsake, give (up), grow long, lay, leave, let depart (down, go, loose), push away, put (away, forth, in, out), reach forth, send (away, forth, out), set, shoot (forth, out), sow, spread, stretch forth (out).

**Pattern:** Heaviest in historical narrative (1-2 Sam, 1-2 Kings, Judges) where messengers, armies, and spies are dispatched. Exodus concentrates the "let go / release" sense in the Pharaoh confrontation. Jeremiah uses it both for prophetic commissioning ("I sent my servants the prophets") and judgment dispatching ("I will send the sword, famine, pestilence"). Low in wisdom literature except Job and Proverbs.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Dispatching Persons on a Mission (Messengers, Envoys, Prophets)
- **Gen 32:3** — "Jacob sent messengers before him to Esau his brother"

- **Isa 6:8** — "Whom shall I send, and who will go for us?"
- **Jer 7:25** — "I have even sent unto you all my servants the prophets, rising early and sending them"

### 2. Releasing / Letting Go (Piel dominant)
