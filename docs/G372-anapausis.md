## Semantic Categories

### 1. Spiritual Rest Offered by Christ
- **Mat 11:29** — "Take my yoke upon you, and learn of me; for I am meek and lowly in heart: and ye shall find rest unto your souls."

The only positive use of anapausis in the NT. Jesus offers rest (anapausis) for the soul as the result of taking his yoke and learning from him. The phrase "rest unto your souls" echoes Jer 6:16 LXX (where the concept uses a different word).

### 2. Rest Sought by Unclean Spirits (Denied)
- **Mat 12:43** — "When the unclean spirit is gone out of a man, he walketh through dry places, seeking rest, and findeth none."
- **Luk 11:24** — "When the unclean spirit is gone out of a man, he walketh through dry places, seeking rest; and finding none, he saith, I will return unto my house whence I came out."

Parallel passages describing an unclean spirit's futile search for anapausis in waterless places. The demon seeks a settled resting state but cannot find it apart from a human host.

### 3. Ceaseless Activity (Absence of Rest)
- **Rev 4:8** — "And the four beasts had each of them six wings about him; and they were full of eyes within: and they rest not day and night, saying, Holy, holy, holy, Lord God Almighty, which was, and is, and is to come."

Here "they rest not" (ouk echousin anapausin, literally "they have no rest/cessation") describes the unceasing praise of the four living creatures. The absence of anapausis is portrayed positively — perpetual worship.

### 4. Absence of Rest as Punishment
- **Rev 14:11** — "And the smoke of their torment ascendeth up for ever and ever: and they have no rest day nor night, who worship the beast and his image, and whosoever receiveth the mark of his name."

The same construction "they have no rest" (ouk echousin anapausin) as Rev 4:8, but applied negatively — tormented worshippers of the beast receive no cessation from their suffering. The "day and night" formula in both Rev 4:8 and 14:11 creates a deliberate contrast between ceaseless worship and ceaseless torment.

## Related Words

### Word Family / Cognates
- **G373 ἀναπαύω (anapauo)** — cognate verb: to cause to rest, give rest, refresh (12 occurrences). Active = give rest to another; middle = rest oneself. Much broader usage than the noun.
- **G3973 παύω (pauo)** — root verb: to cease, stop, leave off. The base from which anapausis is derived via the prefix ἀνά.

### Synonyms
- **G425 ἄνεσις (anesis)** — relaxation, relief from pressure (5v). More about loosening of tension than settled rest.
- **G2663 κατάπαυσις (katapausis)** — rest, resting place (8v in Acts and Hebrews). Hebraistic term for God's rest / promised land rest. More theological freight; connected to Sabbath rest theology in Heb 3-4.
- **G4520 σαββατισμός (sabbatismos)** — Sabbath rest, sabbath-keeping (hapax, Heb 4:9). Distinct from both anapausis and katapausis; refers specifically to a Sabbath-type rest remaining for the people of God.
