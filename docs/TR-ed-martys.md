## OT Usage (Hebrew)

| Strong's | Word | Occurrences | Primary Meaning |

| H5707 | עֵד (ʻêd) | 60 | witness (person); testimony (abstract) |

עֵד is the standard Hebrew legal witness — the person who testifies from direct knowledge. It appears throughout the covenant legal structure: in laws requiring multiple witnesses for capital verdicts (Deut 17:6; 19:15), in covenant-making ceremonies where God or heaven and earth are called as witnesses (Deut 31:19; Josh 24:27), and in prophetic declarations where Israel is commissioned as God's witnesses (Isa 43:10, 12).

**Key OT passages:**
- **Deut 17:6** — "At the mouth of two witnesses, or three witnesses, shall he that is worthy of death be put to death; but at the mouth of one witness he shall not be put to death"
- **Isa 43:10** — "Ye are my witnesses, saith the LORD, and my servant whom I have chosen: that ye may know and believe me"
- **Isa 55:4** — "Behold, I have given him for a witness (ʻêd) to the people, a leader and commander to the people" (the Davidic/Messianic witness)

**Semantic continuity:** The LXX rendering is highly consistent. μάρτυς carries the full legal-evidentiary sense of עֵד — the witness who has direct personal knowledge and speaks from it. The Greek word introduces no theological shift; it is a transparent equivalence. This linguistic bridge means that wherever NT authors use μάρτυς, educated readers steeped in LXX would hear the OT legal/covenantal resonance of עֵד directly.

**The false witness strand:** The Louw-Nida data shows that עֵד also appears frequently in "false witness" (עֵד שָׁקֶר / ʻêd sheqer) contexts, which the LXX renders with ψευδής (G5571) or ψευδομαρτυρέω (G5576) — the negative form that also survives into NT usage.

## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same across testaments:**
- The legal/evidentiary core: two or three witnesses required (Deut 17:6 → Mat 18:16; Heb 10:28)
- Direct personal knowledge as the basis of testimony
- God/YHWH as the ultimate witness called upon in oaths and solemn affirmations
- Israel/the apostles commissioned as God's witnesses to the nations

**What shifts in the NT:**
- **Christological focus:** μάρτυς is applied to Christ himself as "the faithful witness" — a title drawn from Isa 55:4 / Ps 89:37, now fulfilled in the person of Jesus (Rev 1:5; 3:14)
- **Martyr extension:** The word acquires the specialized meaning "one who dies for their testimony" — this semantic development appears embryonically at Acts 22:20 (Stephen) and crystallizes in Revelation. This shift has no Hebrew OT parallel; it emerges from the Christian experience of witnessing under persecution
- **Eschatological scope:** The NT witness is commissioned to "the uttermost part of the earth" (Acts 1:8) — universal scope that transcends the OT covenantal/national setting
- **Cloud of witnesses:** Heb 12:1 uses the word for OT saints whose *lives* of faith constitute testimony — an abstract extension beyond the forensic/judicial sense
