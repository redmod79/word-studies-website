## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same across testaments:**
- Both beged and himation serve as the generic, all-purpose garment term — the default word when no specific garment type is in view
- Both carry symbolic weight in ritual contexts — priestly investiture, purity, and the garment-rending gesture of grief or indignation
- The metaphorical use of garments as moral/spiritual condition carries through (Isa 61:10 "garments of salvation" → Rev 3:4–5 "white garments")
- Garment contamination and washing as purity language persists (Lev 11–15 → Rev 7:14 "washed their robes")

**What shifts:**
- The NT himation emphasizes the outer cloak specifically (distinguished from chiton, the inner tunic), while OT beged covers garments of any type
- Purity-law washing of beged (dominant OT usage, ~30% of occurrences) has no direct NT counterpart — the concept transfers to spiritual cleansing
- The OT etymological link between beged (garment) and bagad (treachery/covering) has no Greek parallel — himation carries no connotation of deception
