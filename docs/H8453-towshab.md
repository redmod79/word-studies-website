## Lexicon Definition

A dweller but not outlandish (H5237 nokriy); especially as distinguished from a native citizen (active participle of H3427 yashab = H249 ezrach) and a temporary inmate (H1616 ger) or mere lodger (H3885 luwn): resident alien — foreigner, inhabitant, sojourner, stranger.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Legal Status: Exclusion from Sacred Meals
The towshab is barred from the Passover and from priestly holy food — a restriction that distinguishes this status from the ger, who can participate in Passover after circumcision (Exo 12:48).

- **Exo 12:45** — "A foreigner (towshab) and an hired servant shall not eat thereof."
- **Lev 22:10** — "a sojourner (towshab) of the priest, or an hired servant, shall not eat of the holy thing."

### 2. Legal Status: Economic Protections and Labor Classification
In Leviticus 25, the towshab appears in economic legislation — classified alongside the hired servant (sakiyr) as a temporary laborer, entitled to sabbatical-year produce, and subject to jubilee release from debt servitude.

- **Lev 25:6** — "the sabbath of the land shall be meat for you; for thee... and for thy hired servant, and for thy stranger (towshab) that sojourneth with thee"
- **Lev 25:40** — "as an hired servant, and as a sojourner (towshab), he shall be with thee, and shall serve thee unto the year of jubile"
- **Lev 25:35** — "if thy brother be waxen poor... though he be a stranger, or a sojourner (towshab); that he may live with thee"
- **Lev 25:47** — "if a sojourner (towshab) or stranger wax rich by thee, and thy brother... wax poor, and sell himself unto the stranger or sojourner (towshab)"

### 3. Legal Status: Cities of Refuge Access
Like the ger and native Israelite, the towshab has access to the cities of refuge for cases of accidental homicide.

- **Num 35:15** — "These six cities shall be a refuge, both for the children of Israel, and for the stranger, and for the sojourner (towshab) among them"

### 4. Acquisition of Non-Israelite Dependents
The towshab's children and family members can be acquired as permanent servants — a distinction from Israelite debt servants who are released at jubilee.

- **Lev 25:45** — "of the children of the strangers (towshab) that do sojourn among you, of them shall ye buy, and of their families... and they shall be your possession"

### 5. Theological Metaphor: Israel Before God
The towshab category is appropriated to express Israel's (and the individual's) dependent, non-proprietary status before God — the land belongs to YHWH, and Israel dwells as resident aliens by divine permission.

- **Lev 25:23** — "the land is mine; for ye are strangers and sojourners (towshab) with me"
- **1 Chr 29:15** — "we are strangers before thee, and sojourners (towshab), as were all our fathers"
- **Ps 39:12** — "I am a stranger with thee, and a sojourner (towshab), as all my fathers were"

### 6. Geographical Usage: Native Inhabitant
One occurrence uses towshab in a non-legal sense, referring to Elijah as simply an inhabitant of a place.

- **1 Ki 17:1** — "Elijah the Tishbite, who was of the inhabitants (towshab) of Gilead"
