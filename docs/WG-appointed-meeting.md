## Overview

## Roles in the Concept

- **Appointment:** mo'ed (מוֹעֵד) — the divinely fixed time, season, or meeting
- **Place:** ohel (אֹהֶל) — the tent where the appointment is kept
- **Access:** pethach (פֶּתַח) — the entrance/threshold of encounter
- **Activity:** avodah (עֲבֹדָה) — the service performed at the meeting
- **Personnel:** kohen (כֹּהֵן) — the priest who mediates the meeting
- **Character:** qodesh (קֹדֶשׁ) — the holiness that defines the space
- **People (gathered):** edah (עֵדָה) — the appointed assembly
- **People (called):** qahal (קָהָל) — the summoned assembly
- **Celebration:** chag (חַג) — the joyous pilgrimage aspect

## Key Passages Where Multiple Group Words Appear Together
- **Lev 1:1-3** — mo'ed + ohel: God speaks from the tent of meeting; offerings brought to its door

- **Exo 29:4** — mo'ed + ohel + pethach + kohen: Aaron brought to the door of the tent for priestly consecration
- **Lev 23:2-4** — mo'ed + qodesh: the feasts of the LORD proclaimed as holy convocations
