## Semantic Categories

### 1. Sacrificial Blood Dashing on the Altar
The dominant use — priests dash or toss blood against/around the altar after slaughter.
- **Lev 1:5** — "the priests, Aaron's sons, shall bring the blood, and sprinkle the blood round about upon the altar"
- **Lev 3:2** — "kill it at the door of the tabernacle of the congregation: and Aaron's sons the priests shall sprinkle the blood upon the altar round about"
- **Lev 7:2** — "the blood thereof shall he sprinkle round about upon the altar"
- **2 Chr 29:22** — "the priests received the blood, and sprinkled it on the altar: likewise... they sprinkled the blood upon the altar"

### 2. Covenant Ratification Blood Application
Blood dashed on persons or objects to seal a covenant.
- **Exo 24:6** — "Moses took half of the blood, and put it in basons; and half of the blood he sprinkled on the altar"
- **Exo 24:8** — "Moses took the blood, and sprinkled it on the people, and said, Behold the blood of the covenant"

### 3. Priestly Consecration
Blood applied during ordination of Aaron and his sons.
- **Exo 29:16** — "thou shalt take his blood, and sprinkle it round about upon the altar"
- **Exo 29:20** — "take of his blood, and put it upon the tip of the right ear of Aaron... and sprinkle the blood upon the altar round about"
- **Lev 8:19** — "Moses sprinkled the blood upon the altar round about"
- **Lev 8:24** — "Moses... sprinkled the blood upon the altar round about"

### 4. Eschatological / Prophetic Cleansing
God as subject, sprinkling for purification in future restoration.
- **Eze 36:25** — "Then will I sprinkle clean water upon you, and ye shall be clean: from all your filthiness, and from all your idols, will I cleanse you"

### 5. Non-Cultic Scattering / Tossing
Rare uses outside the sacrificial system — scattering material broadly.
- **Exo 9:8** — "let Moses sprinkle it up toward heaven" (ashes producing plague boils)
- **Isa 28:25** — "doth he not... scatter the cummin" (agricultural sowing)
- **Job 2:12** — "sprinkled dust upon their heads toward heaven" (grief/mourning)
- **Hos 7:9** — "gray hairs are here and there upon him" (scattered aging)
