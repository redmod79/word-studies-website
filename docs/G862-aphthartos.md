## Semantic Categories

### 1. Divine Nature — God's Essential Incorruptibility
- **Rom 1:23** — "changed the glory of the uncorruptible God into an image made like to corruptible man"
- **1 Tim 1:17** — "Now unto the King eternal, immortal, invisible, the only wise God"

God is aphthartos by nature — immune to decay, change, and dissolution. In Rom 1:23 the word is contrasted directly with phthartós (G5349), marking the Creator-creature distinction. In 1 Tim 1:17 it appears in a doxology alongside "eternal" (aiōn) and "invisible" (aoratos).

### 2. Eschatological Transformation — The Resurrection Body
- **1 Cor 15:52** — "the dead shall be raised incorruptible, and we shall be changed"

The resurrected body will be aphthartos — no longer subject to decay. This is the climactic use in Paul's resurrection argument, paired with the verb allassō (to change).

### 3. Imperishable Reward — The Believer's Crown and Inheritance
- **1 Cor 9:25** — "they do it to obtain a corruptible crown; but we an incorruptible"
- **1 Pet 1:4** — "an inheritance incorruptible, and undefiled, and that fadeth not away"

Athletic crowns decay; the believer's crown does not. Peter clusters aphthartos with amiantos (G283, undefiled) and amarantos (G263, unfading) to describe the heavenly inheritance — a triple negation of earthly impermanence.

### 4. Imperishable Source — The Living Word as Seed
- **1 Pet 1:23** — "born again, not of corruptible seed, but of incorruptible, by the word of God"

The seed (spora) of regeneration is aphthartos because its source — the word of God — "liveth and abideth for ever." Contrasted with phthartós seed (human origin).

### 5. Imperishable Character — The Inner Person
- **1 Pet 3:4** — "the hidden man of the heart, in that which is not corruptible, even the ornament of a meek and quiet spirit"

Applied to moral/spiritual character rather than physical substance. The "not corruptible" ornament of a gentle spirit has lasting value before God, contrasted with outward adornment (braiding, gold, clothing).
