## Lexicon Definition

A primitive root; properly, to be fresh, i.e. full (rosy, figuratively cheerful); to announce (glad news): — messenger, preach, publish, shew forth, (bear, bring, carry, preach, good, tell good) tidings.

Usage concentrates in two distinct domains: (1) the narrative books of Samuel and Kings, where the word describes battlefield messengers, and (2) the prophetic/worship literature (Isaiah, Psalms, Nahum), where it describes the proclamation of God's salvation. The Isaianic cluster (5 occurrences) is theologically dominant and becomes the foundation for the NT gospel concept.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Battlefield/Royal Messenger — Bringing News of Victory or Defeat
- **1 Sam 4:17** — "And the messenger (mevasser) answered and said, Israel is fled before the Philistines, and there hath been also a great slaughter"
- **1 Sam 31:9** — "they... sent into the land of the Philistines round about, to publish it in the house of their idols"
- **2 Sam 1:20** — "Tell it not in Gath, publish it not in the streets of Askelon; lest the daughters of the Philistines rejoice"
- **2 Sam 18:19-20** — "Let me now run, and bear the king tidings... Thou shalt not bear tidings this day"

This is the earliest and most concrete usage: a runner bearing news from the battlefield to the king or city. The news can be good or bad (Saul's death in 1 Sam 31:9; David's enemy Absalom's death in 2 Sam 18). The Philistine context recurs across 1 Samuel, 2 Samuel, and 1 Chronicles.

### 2. Birth Announcement
- **Jer 20:15** — "Cursed be the man who brought tidings to my father, saying, A man child is born unto thee"

A messenger bearing news of a birth — the only occurrence where the news delivered is cursed rather than welcomed.

### 3. Prophetic Herald of Salvation — The Mevasser
- **Isa 40:9** — "O Zion, that bringest good tidings (mevasseret), get thee up into the high mountain; O Jerusalem, that bringest good tidings, lift up thy voice"
- **Isa 41:27** — "I will give to Jerusalem one that bringeth good tidings (mevasser)"
- **Isa 52:7** — "How beautiful upon the mountains are the feet of him that bringeth good tidings (mevasser), that publisheth peace... that publisheth salvation; that saith unto Zion, Thy God reigneth!"
- **Nah 1:15** — "Behold upon the mountains the feet of him that bringeth good tidings (mevasser), that publisheth peace!"

The defining prophetic usage. The mevasser is no longer a battlefield runner but a divine herald announcing God's reign, salvation, and peace. Isaiah 52:7 and Nahum 1:15 share nearly identical language. This cluster transforms the word from a military/royal term into a theological one.

### 4. Anointed Proclamation to the Afflicted
- **Isa 61:1** — "The Spirit of the Lord GOD is upon me; because the LORD hath anointed me to preach good tidings (levassar) unto the meek"

The culmination of the prophetic usage: the herald is now anointed by God's Spirit to proclaim good news specifically to the poor/meek. Jesus directly applies this passage to himself in Luke 4:18-21.

### 5. Liturgical Proclamation — Declaring God's Salvation in Worship
- **Psalm 40:9** — "I have preached (bissarti) righteousness in the great congregation: lo, I have not refrained my lips, O LORD"
- **Psalm 68:11** — "The Lord gave the word: great was the company of those that published it"
- **Psalm 96:2** — "Sing unto the LORD, bless his name; shew forth his salvation from day to day"
- **1 Chr 16:23** — "Sing unto the LORD, all the earth; shew forth from day to day his salvation"

Worship contexts where basar describes the communal proclamation of God's righteousness and salvation. Psalm 96:2 and 1 Chronicles 16:23 are parallel texts.

### 6. Proclamation of God's Praises Among the Nations
- **Isa 60:6** — "all they from Sheba shall come: they shall bring gold and incense; and they shall shew forth the praises of the LORD"

The nations themselves join in the proclamation — the good news extends beyond Israel.
