## Lexicon Definition

A primitive root; to cover (with a garment); figuratively, to act covertly; by implication, to pillage: — deal deceitfully (treacherously, unfaithfully), offend, transgress(-or), (depart), treacherous (dealer, -ly, man), unfaithful(-ly, man), very.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Covenant Treachery Against YHWH
National Israel or Judah breaking faith with God — the most common prophetic usage.
- **Jer 3:8** — "treacherous Judah... her treacherous sister Judah"
- **Jer 5:11** — "the house of Israel and the house of Judah have dealt very treacherously against me"
- **Hos 5:7** — "they have dealt treacherously against the LORD: for they have begotten strange children"
- **Hos 6:7** — "they like men have transgressed the covenant: there have they dealt treacherously against me"

### 2. Marriage Treachery / Covenant-Breaking Between Spouses
Violation of the marriage covenant, used literally and as metaphor for Israel's apostasy.
- **Mal 2:14** — "the wife of thy youth, against whom thou hast dealt treacherously"
- **Mal 2:15** — "let none deal treacherously against the wife of his youth"
- **Jer 3:20** — "as a wife treacherously departeth from her husband, so have ye dealt treacherously with me"
- **Exo 21:8** — "seeing he hath dealt deceitfully with her" (betrothed woman sold into servitude)

### 3. The Treacherous as a Character Type (Proverbs)
The participle bogdim functions as a substantive noun labeling a moral character type — always negatively.
- **Pro 2:22** — "the transgressors shall be rooted out"

- **Pro 13:15** — "the way of transgressors is hard"

### 4. International / Political Treachery
Nations or political entities acting treacherously against others.
- **Isa 21:2** — "the treacherous dealer dealeth treacherously, and the spoiler spoileth"

### 5. Interpersonal Treachery (Against Community)
Breach of faith between individuals or within a community.
- **Mal 2:10** — "why do we deal treacherously every man against his brother?"
- **Job 6:15** — "my brethren have dealt deceitfully as a brook"
- **Jer 12:6** — "even thy brethren, and the house of thy father, even they have dealt treacherously with thee"
