## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same:**
- The word for grace in LXX = the word for grace in NT — seamless vocabulary transfer
- The basic sense of undeserved divine goodwill toward a petitioner carries across
- The Luke-Acts idiom ("found favour with") directly preserves OT ḥēn usage

**What shifts:**
- OT ḥēn = primarily social/relational favor between persons (or from God); it is always **situational**
- NT cháris = expanded to a cosmic theological concept: the divine disposition underlying all salvation, not merely situational favor
- NT adds: grace as *saving power* (Eph 2:8), grace as *ministerial appointment* (Rom 1:5; 12:6), grace as *thanksgiving* (charis in the thanks idiom), grace as the *content of the gospel* (Acts 20:24: "the gospel of the grace of God")
- NT charis becomes the standard opening and closing formula of letters — entirely absent from OT letter forms
