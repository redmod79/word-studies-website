## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same across testaments:**
- The dual semantic range (without cost + without cause) transfers intact from chinnam to dorean
- Both words function as adverbs modifying the manner or basis of an action
- The "without cause" sense applies to unjust hostility in both testaments (Psalms → John 15:25)
- The "without cost" sense applies to divine generosity in both (Isa 52:3 → Rom 3:24, Rev 21:6/22:17)

**What shifts:**
- In the NT, the "freely/as gift" sense becomes theologically central to Pauline justification doctrine (Rom 3:24), concentrating what was spread across varied OT contexts
- The noun form dorea (G1431) develops a distinctive NT theological usage for "the gift of God" (John 4:10, Acts 2:38, Eph 3:7) beyond what chinnam carried as an adverb
- The cognate family (dorea, dorean, doreomai, dorema) expands in the NT into a full gift-vocabulary cluster, while chinnam stood alone in the OT
