## Core Data

## Lexicon Definition

Properly, desire (as a reaching forth or excitement of the mind); by analogy, violent passion (ire, or justifiable abhorrence); by implication punishment: — anger, indignation, vengeance, wrath.

| θυμός (thumos) | G2372 | "wrath" (thumos 12x, orgē 21x) | thumos = hot, passionate fury (from "breathing hard"); orgē = settled, judicial displeasure |
| παροργισμός (parorgismos) | G3950 | "wrath" (1x) | parorgismos = provoked anger, being stirred to wrath; orgē = the anger itself |

When orgē and thumos appear together (6x), orgē denotes the abiding state and thumos the passionate intensity. In vice lists (Eph 4:31, Col 3:8), they name distinguishable sins; in Revelation's judgment scenes (14:10, 16:19, 19:15), they combine for maximal force.
