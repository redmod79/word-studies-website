## Semantic Categories

### 1. The Judicial Verdict Resulting from Adam's Transgression
- **Rom 5:16** — "for the judgment was by one to condemnation (katakrima), but the free gift is of many offences unto justification"
- **Rom 5:18** — "as by the offence of one judgment came upon all men to condemnation (katakrima); even so by the righteousness of one the free gift came upon all men unto justification of life"

In both verses katakrima names the adverse judicial outcome that followed from Adam's single act. It is the sentence pronounced, not the act of judging itself (which is krima/krisis).

### 2. The Sentence from Which Believers Are Freed
- **Rom 8:1** — "There is therefore now no condemnation (katakrima) to them which are in Christ Jesus"

Here katakrima is negated. The entire force of the word — the adverse verdict that stood over humanity from Adam — is declared absent for those "in Christ Jesus." The negation (ouden) is categorical: not reduced condemnation but none at all.
