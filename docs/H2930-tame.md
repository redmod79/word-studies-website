## Lexicon Definition

A primitive root; to be foul, especially in a ceremonial or moral sense (contaminated): defile (self), pollute (self), be (make, make self, pronounce) unclean, utterly.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Ritual Impurity from Contact (Levitical Purity Laws)
The largest category — physical contact with unclean things produces temporary ceremonial uncleanness, typically lasting "until evening" and resolved by washing.
- **Lev 11:24** — "whosoever toucheth the carcase of them shall be unclean until the even"
- **Lev 11:31** — "whosoever doth touch them, when they be dead, shall be unclean until the even"
- **Lev 15:5** — "whosoever toucheth his bed shall wash his clothes, and bathe himself in water, and be unclean until the even"
- **Num 19:11** — "He that toucheth the dead body of any man shall be unclean seven days"

### 2. Bodily Discharges and Skin Conditions
Specific physiological states that render a person unclean — menstruation, seminal emission, leprosy, childbirth.
- **Lev 12:2** — "If a woman have conceived seed, and born a man child: then she shall be unclean seven days"
- **Lev 15:16** — "if any man's seed of copulation go out from him... he shall be unclean until the even"
- **Lev 15:19** — "if a woman have an issue, and her issue in her flesh be blood, she shall be put apart seven days: and whosoever toucheth her shall be unclean until the even"
- **Lev 13:44** — "He is a leprous man, he is unclean: the priest shall pronounce him utterly unclean"

### 3. Priestly Declaration (Piel — "Pronounce Unclean")
The priest's authoritative declaration of uncleanness, especially in skin-disease diagnosis (Leviticus 13).
- **Lev 13:3** — "the priest shall look on the plague in the skin of the flesh... and the priest shall pronounce him unclean"
- **Lev 13:8** — "the priest shall pronounce him unclean: it is a leprosy"
- **Lev 13:11** — "the priest shall pronounce him unclean, and shall not shut him up: for he is unclean"
- **Lev 13:25** — "the priest shall pronounce him unclean: it is the plague of leprosy"

### 4. Sexual Defilement (Moral Contamination)
Sexual sin — adultery, bestiality, and illicit relations — defiles persons and the land itself.
- **Gen 34:5** — "Jacob heard that he had defiled Dinah his daughter"
- **Lev 18:20** — "thou shalt not lie carnally with thy neighbour's wife, to defile thyself with her"
- **Num 5:13–14** — "and she be defiled... and the spirit of jealousy come upon him, and he be jealous of his wife, and she be defiled"
- **Ezek 18:6** — "neither hath defiled his neighbour's wife"

### 5. Idolatry and Pagan Worship (Hithpael — Self-Defilement)
Voluntary contamination through idolatrous worship — a dominant Ezekiel theme.
- **Lev 18:24** — "Defile not ye yourselves in any of these things: for in all these the nations are defiled"
- **Ezek 20:7** — "Cast ye away every man the abominations of his eyes, and defile not yourselves with the idols of Egypt"
- **Ezek 20:31** — "when ye make your sons to pass through the fire, ye pollute yourselves with all your idols"
- **Ezek 22:4** — "Thou art become guilty in thy blood that thou hast shed; and hast defiled thyself in thine idols"

### 6. Defilement of Sacred Space and Land
The sanctuary, temple, and land of Israel can be defiled by sin, requiring divine judgment.
- **Lev 15:31** — "Thus shall ye separate the children of Israel from their uncleanness; that they die not in their uncleanness, when they defile my tabernacle"
- **Num 19:13** — "whosoever toucheth the dead body of any man... and purifieth not himself, defileth the tabernacle of the LORD"
- **Jer 2:7** — "when ye entered, ye defiled my land, and made mine heritage an abomination"
- **Ezek 5:11** — "because thou hast defiled my sanctuary with all thy detestable things"

### 7. Josiah's Deliberate Desecration (Piel — Active Defilement)
A unique usage: Josiah intentionally defiles pagan high places to render them permanently unusable for worship.
- **2 Kings 23:8** — "he... defiled the high places where the priests had burned incense"
- **2 Kings 23:10** — "he defiled Topheth... that no man might make his son or his daughter to pass through the fire"
- **2 Kings 23:13** — "the high places that were before Jerusalem... did the king of Israel defile"
