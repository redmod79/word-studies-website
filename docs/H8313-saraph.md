## Semantic Categories

### 1. Ritual Burning of Sacrificial Remains
Burning the parts of offerings not placed on the altar — carried outside the camp for complete consumption.
- **Exo 29:14** — "the flesh of the bullock, and his skin, and his dung, shalt thou burn with fire without the camp"
- **Lev 4:12** — "the whole bullock shall he carry forth without the camp... and burn him on the wood with fire"
- **Lev 4:21** — "he shall carry forth the bullock without the camp, and burn him as he burned the first bullock"
- **Lev 16:27** — "the bullock for the sin offering, and the goat for the sin offering... shall one carry forth without the camp; and they shall burn in the fire their skins, and their flesh"
- **Num 19:5** — "one shall burn the heifer in his sight; her skin, and her flesh, and her blood, with her dung, shall he burn"

### 2. Disposal of Unclean or Leftover Consecrated Material
Burning items that must not be kept — leftover sacrificial meat, plague-contaminated garments.
- **Exo 12:10** — "that which remaineth of it until the morning ye shall burn with fire"
- **Lev 7:17** — "the remainder of the flesh of the sacrifice on the third day shall be burnt with fire"
- **Lev 7:19** — "the flesh that toucheth any unclean thing shall not be eaten; it shall be burnt with fire"
- **Lev 13:52** — "He shall therefore burn that garment, whether warp or woof... wherein the plague is"
- **Lev 13:55** — "thou shalt burn it in the fire; it is fret inward"

### 3. Military Destruction of Cities, Fortifications, and Spoil
Burning conquered cities and enemy property as an act of war.
- **Josh 6:24** — "they burnt the city with fire, and all that was therein"
- **Josh 8:28** — "Joshua burnt Ai, and made it an heap for ever"
- **Josh 11:11** — "he burned Hazor with fire"
- **Num 31:10** — "they burnt all their cities wherein they dwelt, and all their goodly castles, with fire"
- **Jdg 18:27** — "came unto Laish... and they burnt the city with fire"

### 4. Destruction of Idols and Pagan Cult Objects
Burning idolatrous images, Asherah poles, and illicit cultic items.
- **Deut 7:5** — "break down their images, and cut down their groves, and burn their graven images with fire"
- **Deut 7:25** — "the graven images of their gods shall ye burn with fire"
- **Exo 32:20** — "he took the calf which they had made, and burnt it in the fire"
- **2Ki 23:6** — "he brought out the grove from the house of the LORD... and burned it at the brook Kidron"
- **2Ki 23:15** — "the altar that was at Bethel... he brake down, and burned the high place"

### 5. Judgment — Burning as Penalty or Divine Wrath
Capital punishment by burning, and prophetic judgment on cities/nations.
- **Gen 38:24** — "Bring her forth, and let her be burnt" (Judah's sentence on Tamar)
- **Lev 20:14** — "if a man take a wife and her mother... they shall be burnt with fire"
- **Lev 21:9** — "the daughter of any priest, if she profane herself... she shall be burnt with fire"
- **Jer 21:10** — "it shall be given into the hand of the king of Babylon, and he shall burn it with fire"
- **Jer 34:2** — "this city... shall be... burned with fire"

### 6. Miscellaneous Burning (Manufacturing, Domestic, Metaphorical)
- **Gen 11:3** — "let us make brick, and burn them throughly" (brick-making)
- **Isa 44:16** — "He burneth part thereof in the fire; with part thereof he eateth flesh" (domestic fuel)
- **Prov 6:27** — "Can a man take fire in his bosom, and his clothes not be burned?" (proverbial)
- **Isa 47:14** — "they shall be as stubble; the fire shall burn them" (metaphor of judgment)

The saraph/qatar distinction is critical in Levitical contexts: qatar describes the altar-smoke offering (a pleasing aroma), while saraph describes total burning of remains outside the camp (disposal/judgment, not worship).
