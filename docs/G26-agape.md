Overwhelmingly a Pauline and Johannine term. The highest density is in the Johannine epistles (1–3 John), where "God is love" theology concentrates. ## Hebrew Source

| Hebrew Source | Strong's | Co-occ | PMI Score |

| שִׂנְאָה (sinʼâh) | H8135 | 3 | 13.94 |

The primary Hebrew equivalent is H160 אַהֲבָה (ʼahăbâh, "love"), a feminine noun from the same root as אָהַב (ʼâhab, H157, "to love"). The LXX chose ἀγάπη to render ʼahăbâh in passages like Song of Songs and Jeremiah 2:2. The co-occurrence with שִׂנְאָה (sinʼâh, "hatred") reflects the love/hate antithesis in the LXX.
