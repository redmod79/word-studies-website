## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same:**
- The ordinal sense (third in sequence) transfers perfectly — same form, same meaning
- The fractional sense (one-third) transfers perfectly — Greek neuter τρίτος mirrors Hebrew feminine שְׁלִישִׁית
- The climactic-repetition sense ("the third time") carries intact from Hebrew idiom

**What shifts:**
- In the NT, τρίτος acquires a concentrated theological weight around *resurrection*: "the third day" becomes a technical creedal phrase (1Co 15:4) that the OT ordinal never bore alone — it required the resurrection event to charge it
- The Revelation "third part" judgment pattern is heavier in proportion than the OT usage, reflecting John's deliberate concentration of fractional-devastation imagery
