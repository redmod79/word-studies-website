## Lexicon Definition

Or חָג (châg); from H2287; a festival, or a victim therefor:—(solemn) feast (day), sacrifice, solemnity.

The root חָגַג (châgag) means "to move in a circle, to march in a sacred procession, to observe a festival." The noun captures both the occasion (the pilgrimage festival) and by extension the sacrificial victim offered at it.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. The Three Annual Pilgrimage Feasts
Chag is the overarching term for the three festivals requiring all male Israelites to appear before the LORD.

- **Exo 23:14–16** — "Three times thou shalt keep a feast unto me in the year. Thou shalt keep the feast of unleavened bread... and the feast of harvest... and the feast of ingathering"
- **Deu 16:16** — "Three times in a year shall all thy males appear before the LORD thy God in the place which he shall choose; in the feast of unleavened bread, and in the feast of weeks, and in the feast of tabernacles"
- **Exo 34:22** — "Thou shalt observe the feast of weeks, of the firstfruits of wheat harvest, and the feast of ingathering at the year's end"

### 2. The Feast of Tabernacles (Sukkot)
The single most prominent individual chag, running seven days from the 15th of the 7th month, with booths (sukkot).

- **Lev 23:34** — "The fifteenth day of this seventh month shall be the feast of tabernacles for seven days unto the LORD"
- **Lev 23:39–41** — "On the fifteenth day of the seventh month, when ye have gathered in the fruit of the land, ye shall keep a feast unto the LORD seven days"
- **Deu 16:13–14** — "Thou shalt observe the feast of tabernacles seven days... and thou shalt rejoice in thy feast"
- **Num 29:12** — "On the fifteenth day of the seventh month ye shall have an holy convocation... ye shall keep a feast unto the LORD seven days"

### 3. Temple Dedication and Royal Assembly Feasts
Chag marks the great national gatherings at the completion of the Temple and under covenant-renewing kings.

- **1 Ki 8:2** — "All the men of Israel assembled themselves unto king Solomon at the feast in the month Ethanim, which is the seventh month"
- **1 Ki 8:65** — "Solomon held a feast, and all Israel with him, a great congregation... seven days and seven days"
- **2 Chr 7:8–9** — "Solomon kept the feast seven days, and all Israel with him... they kept the dedication of the altar seven days, and the feast seven days"
- **Ezr 3:4** — "They kept also the feast of tabernacles, as it is written, and offered the daily burnt offerings"

### 4. Prophetic Rebuke of Corrupted Feast-Keeping
The high density of chag in the Minor Prophets reflects its use in divine condemnation — Israel kept the form while abandoning the covenant.

- **Amos 5:21** — "I hate, I despise your feast days, and I will not smell in your solemn assemblies"
- **Amos 8:10** — "I will turn your feasts into mourning, and all your songs into lamentation"
- **Hos 2:11** — "I will also cause all her mirth to cease, her feast days, her new moons, and her sabbaths, and all her solemn feasts"
- **Mal 2:3** — "Behold, I will corrupt your seed, and spread dung upon your faces, even the dung of your solemn feasts"

### 5. Eschatological Universal Feast of Tabernacles
Zechariah envisions chag expanding from an Israelite obligation to a universal annual observance in the messianic age.

- **Zec 14:16** — "Every one that is left of all the nations which came against Jerusalem shall even go up from year to year to worship the King, the LORD of hosts, and to keep the feast of tabernacles"
- **Zec 14:18–19** — "The punishment of all nations that come not up to keep the feast of tabernacles"
