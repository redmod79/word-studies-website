## Direct OT→NT Quotations

No direct quotation uses G1705 empiplemi. However, Luke 1:53 ("filled the hungry with good things") is a clear allusion to Psalm 107:9 ("satisfieth the longing soul, and filleth the hungry soul with goodness"), and Luke 6:25 ("Woe unto you that are full") inverts the Deuteronomic satisfaction-then-apostasy pattern (Deu 8:12, 31:20).

## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same across testaments:**
- God as the source of true satisfaction (Psa 103:5, 107:9 → Luk 1:53, Acts 14:17)
- Physical food as the primary literal domain, with spiritual satisfaction as extension
- The warning that fullness can lead to spiritual danger (Deu 8:12 → Luk 6:25)

**What shifts:**
- The NT secondary bridge word chortazo (G5526) becomes more common than empiplemi for literal feeding contexts (feeding miracles)
- The metaphorical "satisfied with company/relationship" sense (Rom 15:24) extends beyond the OT range
- Hebrew saba has a rich "insatiable desire" category (Ecc 1:8, Pro 27:20, Hab 2:5) that does not carry into the NT through G1705
