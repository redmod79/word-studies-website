## Lexicon Definition

From H5144; properly, something set apart, i.e. (abstractly) dedication (of a priest or Nazirite); hence (concretely) unshorn locks; also (by implication) a chaplet (especially of royalty): — consecration, crown, hair, separation.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Nazirite Consecration / Separation
The dominant usage (10v, all in Numbers 6). Refers to the entire state of being set apart under the Nazirite vow, covering both the abstract condition and (by metonymy) the outward sign of unshorn hair.
- **Num 6:4** — "All the days of his separation shall he eat nothing that is made of the vine tree"
- **Num 6:5** — "All the days of the vow of his separation there shall no rasor come upon his head"
- **Num 6:7** — "He shall not make himself unclean... because the consecration of his God is upon his head"
- **Num 6:8** — "All the days of his separation he is holy unto the LORD"
- **Num 6:9** — "if any man die very suddenly by him, and he hath defiled the head of his consecration"
- **Num 6:12** — "he shall consecrate unto the LORD the days of his separation"

### 2. Priestly Crown / Diadem
The golden plate (tsiyts) fastened to the high priest's turban, symbolizing holiness unto YHWH. A concrete object marking the priest's consecrated office.
- **Exo 29:6** — "thou shalt put the mitre upon his head, and put the holy crown upon the mitre"
- **Exo 39:30** — "they made the plate of the holy crown of pure gold"
- **Lev 8:9** — "upon the mitre, even upon his forefront, did he put the golden plate, the holy crown"
- **Lev 21:12** — "the crown of the anointing oil of his God is upon him"

### 3. Royal Crown / Diadem
A crown signifying kingly authority, used for coronation and as a symbol of the Davidic dynasty.
- **2 Sam 1:10** — "I took the crown that was upon his head" (Saul's crown, reported to David)
- **2 Ki 11:12** — "he brought forth the king's son, and put the crown upon him" (Joash's coronation)
- **2 Chr 23:11** — "they brought out the king's son, and put upon him the crown" (parallel to 2 Ki 11:12)
- **Psa 89:39** — "thou hast profaned his crown by casting it to the ground" (Davidic lament)
- **Psa 132:18** — "upon himself shall his crown flourish" (messianic promise)

### 4. Figurative / Metaphorical Crown
Extended uses where nezer functions as a metaphor for honor, prosperity, or consecrated status.
- **Pro 27:24** — "doth the crown endure to every generation?" (transience of wealth)
- **Jer 7:29** — "cut off thine hair, O Jerusalem, and cast it away" (Jerusalem's consecration revoked)
- **Zec 9:16** — "they shall be as the stones of a crown, lifted up as an ensign upon his land"
