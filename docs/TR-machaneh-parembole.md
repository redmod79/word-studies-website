## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same across testaments:**
- The core spatial meaning of a community encampment persists. Both OT machaneh and NT parembole denote a bounded, organized community dwelling space.
- The ritual "without the camp" formula carries directly into Hebrews, where it becomes the theological framework for Christ's suffering outside Jerusalem.
- The concept of God's presence in the midst of the camp persists from Deut 23:14 through to Rev 20:9 ("the camp of the saints").

**What shifts:**
- NT adds the meaning "castle/barracks" (Acts 21:34, 37; 22:24; 23:10, 16, 32) — a Roman military fortification, extending the military camp sense into a permanent structure.
- The wilderness camp becomes metaphorical in Hebrews — "the camp" is no longer a literal location but a theological boundary. Going "outside the camp" becomes a call to identification with Christ's rejection.
- In Revelation, parembole is eschatological: the camp of the saints is the final gathering of God's people besieged by Gog and Magog.
