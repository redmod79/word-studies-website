## Lexicon Definition

*From Thayer's Greek Lexicon:* To change one's mind, i.e. to repent; to feel compunction. Specifically, to change one's mind for the better, heartily to amend with abhorrence of one's past sins. Used especially of those who, conscious of their sins and with manifest tokens of sorrow, are intent on obtaining God's pardon. Mental direction may be specified: ἀπό τῆς κακίας (away from wickedness, Acts 8:22); ἐκ τινος (out of something, Rev 2:21; 9:20; 16:11); μετανοεῖν καὶ ἐπιστρέφειν ἐπὶ τὸν θεόν (repent and turn to God, Acts 26:20).

*Etymology:* A compound of two concepts — μετά (after, with, implying change) and νοέω (to exercise the mind, observe, think). The word is fundamentally cognitive and volitional — a deliberate re-orientation of thought and will — not merely emotional remorse.
