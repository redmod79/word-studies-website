## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same:**
- Core meaning: throne = seat of ultimate authority; the one on the throne rules
- Divine throne described as eternal and exalted (Lam 5:19 → Heb 1:8)
- Davidic covenant throne promise (2 Sam 7:13 → Luke 1:32, Acts 2:30)

**What shifts:**
- OT usage spreads across human kings, God, and celestial imagery (124v, 22 books); NT concentrates almost entirely on God/Christ/heavenly court (72% in Revelation)
- NT introduces the "throne of grace" (Heb 4:16) — no direct OT parallel; new NT theological development
- NT adds the throne of the Lamb alongside God's throne (Rev 22:3) — a Christological expansion of the OT divine throne concept
- NT counter-thrones for Satan and the Beast (Rev 2:13; 13:2) — theologically new, no OT equivalent
