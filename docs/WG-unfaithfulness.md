# WG: Unfaithfulness — Covenant Treachery Vocabulary

## Overview
Hebrew Scripture distinguishes several types of unfaithfulness/treachery, each with a distinct semantic emphasis. Where the sin triad (pesha/chattah/avon) covers the broad range of moral failure, this group addresses the specific dimension of covenant betrayal — acting covertly against one to whom loyalty is owed. The two core verbs (maʻal and bagad) share an etymological connection to covering/concealment, while the related terms extend the concept into sexual metaphor (zanah), legal guilt (asham), and open revolt (pasha).

## Roles in the Concept

- **Covert sacrilege:** maʻal (H4603/H4604) — violation of sacred trust, particularly against YHWH's holy things or covenant. Predominantly cultic-legal and Chronicler vocabulary. Always implies something owed to God has been withheld or misappropriated.
- **Personal betrayal:** bagad (H898) — treacherous dealing in relationships. Broader range: marital unfaithfulness (Mal 2:14-16, Jer 3), treaty violations, personal betrayal. More relational and prophetic in distribution.
- **Sexual metaphor:** zanah (H2181) — unfaithfulness depicted as harlotry/prostitution. The dominant prophetic metaphor for Israel's idolatry. Hosea, Jeremiah, Ezekiel.
- **Resulting guilt:** asham (H816) — the state of culpability that unfaithfulness produces. Legal/ritual dimension — triggers the obligation for a guilt offering (H817).
- **Open revolt:** pasha (H6586) — breaking away from just authority. More confrontational than maʻal or bagad. Prophetic term for rebellion.

## Key Passages Where Multiple Group Words Appear Together

- **Num 5:6** — [H4603 + H4604 + H816] "When a man or woman shall commit any sin that men commit, to do a trespass against the LORD, and that person be guilty" — the Levitical trespass formula combining the act of treachery with resulting guilt
- **1 Chr 5:25** — [H4603 + H2181] "they transgressed against the God of their fathers, and went a whoring after the gods of the people of the land" — covenant treachery paired with the harlotry metaphor
- **Isa 48:8** — [H898 + H6586] "thou wouldest deal very treacherously, and wast called a transgressor from the womb" — personal betrayal paired with open revolt
