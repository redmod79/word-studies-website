## Lexicon Definition

A primitive root; to be (causatively, do or declare) wrong; by implication, to disturb, violate: — condemn, make trouble, vex, be (commit, deal, depart, do) wicked(-ly, -ness).

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Forensic Condemnation — Declaring Guilty (Hiphil Declarative)
The largest category. A judge, God, or accuser pronounces a verdict of "guilty/wicked."
- **Deut 25:1** — "the judges may judge them; then they shall justify the righteous, and condemn (hirshiʻu) the wicked"
- **1 Ki 8:32** — "hear thou in heaven, and do, and judge thy servants, condemning (larshiʻa) the wicked"
- **Pro 17:15** — "He that justifieth the wicked, and he that condemneth (marshiʻa) the just, even they both are abomination to the LORD"
- **Job 40:8** — "wilt thou condemn (tarshiʻeni) me, that thou mayest be righteous?"

### 2. Confessional Acknowledgment of Wrongdoing (Qal/Hiphil)
Communal or individual admission of having acted wickedly — penitential formula.
- **1 Ki 8:47** — "We have sinned, and have done perversely, we have committed wickedness (rashaʻnu)"
- **Dan 9:5** — "We have sinned, and have committed iniquity, and have done wickedly (wehirshaʻnu)"
- **Neh 9:33** — "thou hast done right, but we have done wickedly (hirshaʻnu)"
- **Psa 106:6** — "We have sinned with our fathers, we have committed iniquity, we have done wickedly (hirshaʻnu)"

### 3. Behavioral — Acting Wickedly (Qal/Hiphil)
Describing conduct that is morally wrong or in departure from God's ways.
- **2 Sa 22:22** — "I have kept the ways of the LORD, and have not wickedly departed (rashaʻti) from my God"
- **2 Chr 22:3** — "his mother was his counseller to do wickedly (leharshiʻa)"
- **Dan 11:32** — "such as do wickedly (marshiʻey) against the covenant shall he corrupt by flatteries"
- **Dan 12:10** — "the wicked shall do wickedly (hirshiʻu): and none of the wicked shall understand"

### 4. Stative — Being in a State of Wickedness (Qal)
Job's protests about whether he is or is not in a state of guilt.
- **Job 9:29** — "If I be wicked (ʼershaʻ), why then labour I in vain?"
- **Job 10:7** — "Thou knowest that I am not wicked (ʼershaʻ)"
- **Job 10:15** — "If I be wicked (rashaʻti), woe unto me"
- **Ecc 7:17** — "Be not over much wicked (tirshaʻ), neither be thou foolish"

### 5. Causing Trouble / Disturbing (Hiphil Causative)
Rare sense — creating disturbance or harm.
- **1 Sa 14:47** — "Saul... fought against all his enemies... whithersoever he turned himself, he vexed (yarshiʻa) them"
- **Job 34:29** — "when he hideth his face, who then can behold him?... who then can make trouble (yarshiʻa)?"
