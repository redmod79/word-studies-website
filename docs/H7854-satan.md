## Semantic Categories

### 1. Human or Earthly Adversary
The word used without a definite article (or as a common noun) for an earthly opponent — a military or political adversary.

- **Num 22:22** — "the angel of the LORD stood in the way for an adversary (śāṭān) against him"
- **1 Sam 29:4** — "the princes of the Philistines said... lest in the battle he be an adversary (śāṭān) to us"
- **1 Kgs 5:4** — "the LORD my God hath given me rest... neither adversary (śāṭān) nor evil occurrence"
- **1 Kgs 11:14** — "the LORD stirred up an adversary (śāṭān) unto Solomon, Hadad the Edomite"

### 2. The Accuser in the Divine Council (ha-śāṭān)
Used with the definite article — "the adversary/accuser" — designating a specific figure in the divine assembly who challenges and accuses before God.

- **Job 1:6** — "Satan came also among them [the sons of God] to present themselves before the LORD"
- **Job 1:9** — "Then Satan answered the LORD, and said, Doth Job fear God for nought?"
- **Job 2:1** — "Satan came also among them to present himself before the LORD"
- **Zech 3:1** — "Joshua the high priest standing before the angel of the LORD, and Satan standing at his right hand to resist him"
- **Zech 3:2** — "The LORD said unto Satan, The LORD rebuke thee, O Satan"

### 3. Direct Adversary Against God's People
Used of an adversarial figure inciting or opposing Israel — transitional toward proper-name usage.

- **1 Chr 21:1** — "And Satan stood up against Israel, and provoked David to number Israel" (no definite article in Hebrew)
- **Ps 109:6** — "Set thou a wicked man over him: and let Satan stand at his right hand" (imprecatory prayer for a legal accuser)
