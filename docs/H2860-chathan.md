## Lexicon Definition

From H2859; a relative by marriage (especially through the bride); figuratively, a circumcised child (as a species of religious espousal):—bridegroom, husband, son in law.

The word covers the full semantic field of a man joined into a family through the bride: the suitor on his wedding day ("bridegroom"), the husband of one's daughter ("son in law"), and in one unusual figurative use, a circumcised child ("bloody husband"). Context distinguishes the sense.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Son-in-law (relative by marriage)
The man who has married into a family through their daughter; often highlights alliance, loyalty, or genealogical note.
- **Gen 19:12** — "Hast thou here any besides? son in law (châthân), and thy sons, and thy daughters..."
- **Jdg 15:6** — "Samson, the son in law (châthân) of the Timnite, because he had taken his wife"
- **1 Sam 18:18** — "that I should be son in law (châthân) to the king"
- **Neh 6:18** — "he was the son in law (châthân) of Shechaniah the son of Arah"

### 2. Bridegroom on his wedding day
The man in the act or fresh state of marrying; often paired with the bride (kallâh H3618) and with sounds of celebration.
- **Psa 19:5** — "Which is as a bridegroom (châthân) coming out of his chamber, and rejoiceth as a strong man to run a race"
- **Isa 62:5** — "as the bridegroom (châthân) rejoiceth over the bride, so shall thy God rejoice over thee"
- **Jer 7:34** — "the voice of the bridegroom (châthân), and the voice of the bride"
- **Joel 2:16** — "let the bridegroom (châthân) go forth of his chamber, and the bride out of her closet"

### 3. Bridegroom as figure of divine / covenantal joy
The bridegroom's gladness becomes a metaphor for YHWH's delight in His people or — in reverse — for the loss of joy under judgment.
- **Isa 61:10** — "as a bridegroom (châthân) decketh himself with ornaments"
- **Jer 16:9** — "I will cause to cease... the voice of the bridegroom (châthân), and the voice of the bride"
- **Jer 25:10** — "I will take from them... the voice of the bridegroom (châthân), and the voice of the bride"
- **Jer 33:11** — "The voice of joy, and the voice of gladness, the voice of the bridegroom (châthân)"

### 4. Figurative — "bloody husband" (circumcision scene)
Zipporah's enigmatic exclamation applies châthân to the circumcised child or to Moses in the context of blood-rite kinship; the lexicon notes this as a "species of religious espousal."
- **Exo 4:25** — "cast it at his feet, and said, Surely a bloody husband (châthân) art thou to me"
- **Exo 4:26** — "A bloody husband thou art, because of the circumcision"
