## Lexicon Definition

From H3513; properly, **weight**, but only figuratively in a good sense, **splendor or copiousness**:—glorious(-ly), glory, honour(-able).

The root idea is literal heaviness (כָּבַד = "to be heavy"), applied metaphorically to that which has "weight" in a social, moral, or divine sense — substance, importance, radiance.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Divine Presence — Visible Theophany
Kabod as a luminous, tangible manifestation of God's presence, typically associated with cloud, fire, and overwhelming radiance that fills sacred space.

- **Exod 24:16** — "And the glory of the LORD abode upon mount Sinai, and the cloud covered it six days"
- **Exod 40:34** — "the glory of the LORD filled the tabernacle" (priests unable to enter)
- **1 Kgs 8:11** — "the glory of the LORD had filled the house of the LORD"
- **Ezek 43:4** — "the glory of the LORD came into the house by the way of the gate whose prospect is toward the east"

### 2. Divine Identity and Exclusivity
Kabod as the essential, non-transferable attribute of God — His intrinsic worth and majesty that belongs to Him alone and cannot be shared with idols or creatures.

- **Isa 42:8** — "I am the LORD: that is my name: and my glory will I not give to another, neither my praise to graven images"
- **Isa 6:3** — "the whole earth is full of his glory" (seraphim's declaration before the throne)
- **Isa 43:7** — "every one that is called by my name: for I have created him for my glory"
- **Psa 19:1** — "The heavens declare the glory of God; and the firmament sheweth his handywork"

### 3. Liturgical / Doxological Use
Kabod as the proper due of God in worship — giving, declaring, or ascribing glory to His name as an act of acknowledgment and devotion.

- **1 Chr 16:28-29** — "Give unto the LORD glory and strength… give unto the LORD the glory due unto his name"
- **Josh 7:19** — "give, I pray thee, glory to the LORD God of Israel, and make confession"
- **Psa 24:7** — "Lift up your heads, O ye gates… and the King of glory shall come in"
- **Mal 2:2** — "if ye will not lay it to heart, to give glory unto my name, saith the LORD of hosts"

### 4. Human Honour and Wealth
Kabod applied to persons, denoting social prestige, reputation, riches, and the visible marks of high standing — the "weight" a person carries in society.

- **Gen 31:1** — "Jacob hath taken away all that was our father's" (kabod = their possessions/substance)
- **1 Kgs 3:13** — "I have also given thee that which thou hast not asked, both riches, and honour"
- **Prov 22:4** — "by humility and the fear of the LORD are riches, and honour, and life"
- **Esth 1:4** — "the riches of his glorious kingdom and the honour of his excellent majesty"

### 5. Glory Departing or Defiled
Kabod lost, exchanged, or stripped away — whether Israel's national glory removed in judgment, or individuals stripped of honour.

- **1 Sam 4:21-22** — "The glory is departed from Israel: for the ark of God is taken"
- **Jer 2:11** — "my people have changed their glory for that which doth not profit"
- **Hos 4:7** — "I will change their glory into shame"
- **Psa 106:20** — "They changed their glory into the similitude of an ox that eateth grass"

### 6. Eschatological Universal Revelation
Kabod as the goal of history — the future universal filling of creation with the knowledge and visible presence of God's glory.

- **Num 14:21** — "as truly as I live, all the earth shall be filled with the glory of the LORD"
- **Hab 2:14** — "the earth shall be filled with the knowledge of the glory of the LORD, as the waters cover the sea"
- **Isa 40:5** — "the glory of the LORD shall be revealed, and all flesh shall see it together"
- **Hag 2:9** — "The glory of this latter house shall be greater than of the former, saith the LORD of hosts"
