## Hebrew Foundation

**H3499 yether** appears 95× in the OT. Its dominant use is the regnal-archive formula: "Now the rest of the acts of [king X], are they not written in the book of the chronicles?" This is a neutral administrative "remainder" — the bulk of events not narrated in the canonical text.

Secondary uses carry more weight: the remnant of enemy peoples (Josh 23:12), survivors of catastrophe (2 Kgs 25:11), and the poetic/prophetic sense of what is "left over" after divine action (Joel 1:4; Hab 2:8).

**Key OT verse (prophetic survival):**
- **Zech 14:2** — "the residue of the people shall not be cut off from the city" — yether as the preserved group through eschatological crisis.

## LXX Bridge

The translators of 1–2 Kings and Chronicles chose **λοιποί** as their standard rendering for yether in the regnal formula. The word λοιποί (masculine plural of λοιπός) literally means "the remaining/left-over ones." It is semantically transparent: an exact parallel to yether's core sense of "what is left over."

- Co-occurrences: **45x** (PMI 7.20 — dominant and specific)
- Secondary Greek renderings: κατάλοιπος G2645 (17x), ἐπίλοιπος G1954 (4x)

## NT Usage

**G3062** appears **41×** in the NT across 14 books, concentrated in Paul and Revelation.

### Epistle Uses
- **Rom 11:7** — "the election hath obtained it, and the rest were blinded" — Israel and the hardened "remainder" parallel to OT remnant theology (though Israel's remnant there is more shearith/H7611 than yether)
- **1 Thess 4:13** — "that ye sorrow not, even as others which have no hope" — distinguishing the community from "the rest" (those without resurrection hope)
- **Eph 2:3** — "we all had our conversation in times past... even as others" — the non-believing world as "the rest"

### Revelation Uses (Theologically Significant)
- **Rev 12:17** — "the dragon was wroth with the woman, and went to make war with the **remnant** of her seed, which keep the commandments of God" — final eschatological community; the "remaining ones" of the woman's descendants who remain faithful
- **Rev 9:20** — "the **rest** of the men which were not killed by these plagues yet repented not" — survivors of the sixth trumpet who remain unrepentant
- **Rev 11:13** — "the **remnant** were affrighted, and gave glory to the God of heaven" — survivors after the earthquake witness
- **Rev 20:5** — "the **rest** of the dead lived not again until the thousand years were finished" — the wicked dead contrasted with the resurrected saints
- **Rev 19:21** — "the **remnant** were slain with the sword" — final destruction of those who remain after the beast's defeat

## Semantic Shift Across the Bridge

| Feature | OT yether | NT loipoi |
|---------|-----------|-----------|
| Tone | Neutral administrative | Often theologically charged |
| Subject | Kings' records, leftover goods | Groups of people: believers vs. unbelievers |
| Eschatological weight | Low (administrative); moderate (Zech 14) | High (Revelation: final remnant) |
| Marked usage | "excellency" (superiority) | Distinct faithful community (Rev 12:17) |

The LXX translation of yether as loipoi carried the word's survival/remainder sense into Greek, where NT writers—especially the author of Revelation—pressed it into eschatological service. The "remnant of her seed" in Rev 12:17 echoes the same conceptual world as yether in its survival contexts (Josh 23:12; Hab 2:8; Zech 14:2), even though the theological freight is far heavier.
