**Semantic shift in translation:** High fidelity. The Hebrew choshek maps to skotos with remarkable consistency. The LXX translators treated them as near-exact equivalents. The full semantic range of choshek (physical, moral, judgment, chaos) carries through into skotos. The main shift is that the NT adds the "outer darkness" (skotos to exoteron) idiom for eschatological punishment — a concept without direct OT parallel using this word.

## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same across testaments:**
- Darkness as the primordial state before God's light (Gen 1:2 → 2Co 4:6)
- Darkness as judgment (Exo 10:21-22 → Mat 27:45)
- Darkness as the condition needing deliverance (Isa 9:2 → Mat 4:16, Act 26:18)
- The light/darkness antithesis as fundamental moral language

**What shifts:**
- The NT develops "outer darkness" (to skotos to exoteron) as a fixed idiom for eschatological punishment — absent in OT
- The NT personalizes darkness as a domain with "power" (exousia) under Satan (Col 1:13, Luk 22:53)
- John's Gospel and Epistles develop a distinctive Johannine vocabulary using the feminine σκοτία (skotia) rather than the LXX-standard σκότος (skotos) for the dualistic light/darkness theme
