# WG: Love

## Overview

The love concept in the NT centers on the ἀγαπ- word family (agapē noun, agapaō verb, agapētos adjective), which dominates with 276 combined verses. The φιλ- family (phileō verb, philadelphia noun) provides a complementary range emphasizing personal affection and brotherly kindness. These two families overlap in the LXX rendering of Hebrew אָהַב (ʼâhab, H157), where both agapaō and phileō translate the same Hebrew root.

## Roles in the Concept

- **Quality/State:** agapē (G26) — love as a characteristic, virtue, or divine attribute ("God is love")
- **Action:** agapaō (G25) — the act of loving, the verb form ("love one another")
- **Object/Recipient:** agapētos (G27) — the beloved one, the one loved ("dearly beloved")
- **Personal Affection:** phileō (G5368) — fondness, friendship love ("Jesus loved [phileō] Lazarus")
