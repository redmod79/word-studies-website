# WG: Night and Darkness (Hebrew Time/Light Vocabulary)

## Overview

The biblical vocabulary for "night" spans the full darkness cycle — from the onset of dusk (ereb)
through twilight (nesheph) into full night (layil), with darkness (choshek) as the underlying
quality. These words interact with the day vocabulary (yowm, yowmam) in merisms, contrasts, and
creation ordinances. This group complements [WG-day.md](WG-day.md) by focusing on the darkness
side of the day/night pair.

## Semantic Relationships

### Temporal Sequence: ereb → nesheph → layil → boqer
The Hebrew night vocabulary traces a sequence: *ereb* marks dusk (the boundary), *nesheph*
is the transitional twilight, *layil* is the full darkness period, and *boqer* (morning) ends
it. Gen 1:5 establishes the "evening and morning" frame; the intermediate terms fill the
gradations.

### Quality vs. Period: choshek / layil
*Choshek* names the quality (darkness as substance — Gen 1:2 "darkness was upon the face of
the deep") while *layil* names the period (the time when darkness prevails). They co-occur in
only 8 verses (4% of layil), showing they are largely complementary rather than redundant.

### Antithetic Pair: layil / yowm
The "day and night" pair functions as a merism for totality — unbroken, continuous action.
This formula appears in worship (Ps 1:2, 134:1), prayer (1 Ki 8:29, Neh 1:6), suffering
(Ps 42:3), and creation ordinance (Gen 8:22, Jer 33:20-25).
