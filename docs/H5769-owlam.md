## Lexicon Definition

> עוֹלָם or עֹלָם ʻôlâm; from [an unused root]; properly, **concealed**, i.e. the **vanishing point**; generally, **time out of mind** (past or future), i.e. (practically) eternity; frequentatively, adverbial (especially with prepositional prefix) **always**: — alway(-s), ancient (time), any more, continuance, eternal, (for, (n-)) ever(-lasting, -more, of old), lasting, long (time), (of) old (time), perpetual, at any time, (beginning of the) world (without end).

The word's core image is of a **horizon that disappears from sight** — time that recedes beyond the reach of human memory or perception, in either direction.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Eternal Duration of God and His Attributes
God's own being, throne, name, and mercy described as extending infinitely in both directions.

- **Ps 90:2** — "Before the mountains were brought forth...even from everlasting to everlasting, thou art God"
- **Ps 103:17** — "the mercy of the LORD is from everlasting to everlasting upon them that fear him"
- **Ps 29:10** — "the LORD sitteth King for ever"
- **Ps 45:6** — "Thy throne, O God, is for ever and ever"
- **Lam 5:19** — "Thou, O LORD, remainest for ever; thy throne from generation to generation"

### 2. Everlasting Covenant and Promises
The formula בְּרִית עוֹלָם (berît ʻôlâm, "everlasting covenant") marks God's covenantal commitments as permanent and irreversible.

- **Gen 17:7** — "I will establish my covenant...for an everlasting covenant, to be a God unto thee, and to thy seed after thee"
- **Gen 17:13** — "my covenant shall be in your flesh for an everlasting covenant"
- **Ps 89:2** — "Mercy shall be built up for ever: thy faithfulness shalt thou establish in the very heavens"
- **2 Sam 7:13** — "I will stablish the throne of his kingdom for ever"

### 3. Perpetual Cultic Ordinances
Statutes, feasts, and priestly offices established as binding "for ever" (לְעוֹלָם) or "perpetual" throughout Israel's generations.

- **Lev 3:17** — "a perpetual statute for your generations throughout all your dwellings, that ye eat neither fat nor blood"
- **Lev 6:18** — "a statute for ever in your generations"
- **Ex 31:16** — "the children of Israel shall keep the sabbath...for a perpetual covenant"
- **Num 10:8** — "the sons of Aaron shall blow with the trumpets...for an ordinance for ever throughout your generations"

### 4. The Immemorial Past ("of old")
ʻôwlâm can look backward — the hidden antiquity beyond living memory, "time out of mind" in the past direction.

- **Deut 32:7** — "Remember the days of old (ʻôlâm), consider the years of many generations"
- **Isa 51:9** — "awake, as in the ancient days, in the generations of old"
- **Jer 2:20** — "of old time I have broken thy yoke, and burst thy bands"
- **Prov 8:23** — "I was set up from everlasting, from the beginning, or ever the earth was"
- **Mic 5:2** — "whose goings forth have been from of old, from everlasting"

### 5. Eschatological / Age to Come
The future unlimited age — resurrection life, eternal righteousness, the coming kingdom — looking forward from a final judgment horizon.

- **Dan 12:2** — "many of them that sleep in the dust...shall awake, some to everlasting life, and some to shame and everlasting contempt"
- **Dan 9:24** — "to bring in everlasting righteousness, and to seal up the vision and prophecy"
- **Isa 9:6** — "his name shall be called...The everlasting Father, The Prince of Peace"
- **Deut 33:27** — "The eternal God is thy refuge, and underneath are the everlasting arms"

### 6. Permanent Judgment / Irreversible Desolation
Specific places, peoples, or situations permanently altered by divine judgment — "for ever" as a verdict, not a hope.

- **Josh 8:28** — "Joshua burnt Ai, and made it an heap for ever, even a desolation"
- **Jer 25:9** — "I will...make them an astonishment, and an hissing, and perpetual desolations"
- **Obad 1:10** — "thou shalt be cut off for ever"
- **Isa 32:14** — "the forts and towers shall be for dens for ever, a joy of wild asses"
