## Lexicon Definition

From H7937 (shakar, "to be drunken"); **an intoxicant, i.e. intensely alcoholic liquor**: — strong drink, drunkard, strong wine. (BDB: "intoxicating drink, strong drink"; HALOT: a fermented beverage other than grape wine — typically made from grain, date, pomegranate, or honey, distinct from yayin but always clearly inebriating.)

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Cultic Prohibition to Priests
Drinking shekar while officiating is a capital offense; it imperils the ability to distinguish holy from profane.
- **Lev 10:9** — "Do not drink wine nor strong drink... when ye go into the tabernacle of the congregation, lest ye die"
- **Eze 44:21** (cognate prohibition) — echoes this priestly guard (uses yayin alone; shekar implied)

### 2. Nazirite Abstention
The Nazirite vow bars every product of the vine and any shekar — a total separation from inebriants.
- **Num 6:3** — "He shall separate [himself] from wine and strong drink, and shall drink no vinegar of wine, or vinegar of strong drink"
- **Jdg 13:4, 7, 14** — the angel's triple instruction regarding Samson's mother: "drink not wine nor strong drink"
- **1 Sam 1:15** — Hannah's defense: "I have drunk neither wine nor strong drink" (echoing Nazirite language)

### 3. Permitted Festival Use Before the LORD
In the tithe-feast at the central sanctuary, the Israelite may purchase shekar with redeemed tithe money and consume it in worshipful rejoicing.
- **Deut 14:26** — "thou shalt bestow that money for whatsoever thy soul lusteth after, for oxen, or for sheep, or for wine, or for strong drink... and thou shalt eat there before the LORD thy God, and thou shalt rejoice"
- **Num 28:7** — "the strong wine [shekar] to be poured unto the LORD for a drink offering" (daily tamid libation)

### 4. Wilderness Withholding / Covenant Memory
A deliberate deprivation underscoring dependence on YHWH.
- **Deut 29:6** — "Ye have not eaten bread, neither have ye drunk wine or strong drink: that ye might know that I am the LORD your God"

### 5. Prophetic Indictment of Intoxication
Shekar epitomizes self-indulgence and moral/spiritual derangement in leaders and populace.
- **Isa 5:11** — "Woe unto them that rise up early in the morning, that they may follow strong drink"
- **Isa 5:22** — "Woe unto them that are mighty to drink wine, and men of strength to mingle strong drink"
- **Isa 28:7** — "the priest and the prophet have erred through strong drink... they err in vision, they stumble in judgment"
- **Isa 29:9; 56:12** — staggering, greedy drunkenness
- **Mic 2:11** — a false prophet suitable to a people who want prophecies "of wine and of strong drink"

### 6. Wisdom Warnings
Proverbs depicts shekar as both dangerous and, in one narrow case, merciful.
- **Prov 20:1** — "Wine is a mocker, strong drink is raging: and whosoever is deceived thereby is not wise"
- **Prov 31:4** — "It is not for kings... to drink wine; nor for princes strong drink" (impairment of judgment)
- **Prov 31:6** — "Give strong drink unto him that is ready to perish" (palliative, terminal use)

### 7. Community Reproach
Associated with public disgrace and the songs of the dissolute.
- **Psa 69:12** — "I was the song of the drunkards" (shekar-cognate sense)
- **Isa 24:9** — "strong drink shall be bitter to them that drink it" (judgment reversal)

**Key fact for wine/alcohol studies:** Of 20 shekar verses, ~17 also contain yayin (H3196). The two words are used as a hendiadys-like pair ("yayin weshekar") covering the full range of inebriating drink. Whatever moral/legal status is assigned to shekar in a given verse is almost always assigned jointly to yayin — they form a linked pair.
