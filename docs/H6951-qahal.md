**Pattern:** Heaviest concentration in 2 Chronicles (temple worship assemblies under reform kings — Hezekiah, Josiah). Ezekiel uses it distinctively for hostile military companies (Gog, Tyre, executioners). Deuteronomy establishes the exclusion-formula "congregation of the LORD." Absent from 2 Samuel, 2 Kings, most minor prophets, and all wisdom books except Proverbs and Job (one each).

## Semantic Categories

### 1. The Cultic Assembly — Israel Gathered Before God
The dominant usage. Israel assembled as a worshipping body for sacrifice, covenant reading, or festival observance. Often paired with "all" (kol) and "Israel."

- **Lev 16:17** — "there shall be no man in the tabernacle of the congregation when he goeth in to make an atonement in the holy place, and shall come out, and make an atonement... for all the congregation (qahal) of Israel"
- **Deut 5:22** — "These words the LORD spake unto all your assembly (qahal) in the mount out of the midst of the fire"
- **1 Ki 8:14** — "the king turned his face about, and blessed all the congregation (qahal) of Israel: (and all the congregation of Israel stood)"
- **2 Chr 29:28** — "all the congregation (qahal) worshipped, and the singers sang, and the trumpeters sounded"

### 2. The Covenant Assembly — Deliberative and Judicial
The qahal functions as a deliberative body making decisions, passing judgments, or ratifying covenants. Here the collective noun carries corporate agency.

- **Judg 20:2** — "the chief of all the people... presented themselves in the assembly (qahal) of the people of God, four hundred thousand footmen"
- **Judg 21:5** — "Who is there among all the tribes of Israel that came not up with the congregation (qahal) unto the LORD?"
- **Ezra 10:12** — "Then all the congregation (qahal) answered and said with a loud voice, As thou hast said, so must we do"
- **Neh 5:13** — "all the congregation (qahal) said, Amen, and praised the LORD"

### 3. The Congregation of the LORD — Membership Boundary
Deuteronomy uses qahal in a technical sense defining who may or may not "enter into the congregation of the LORD" — a membership/exclusion formula.

- **Deut 23:1** — "He that is wounded in the stones... shall not enter into the congregation (qahal) of the LORD"
- **Deut 23:2** — "A bastard shall not enter into the congregation (qahal) of the LORD; even to his tenth generation"
- **Deut 23:3** — "An Ammonite or Moabite shall not enter into the congregation (qahal) of the LORD"
- **Deut 23:8** — "The children that are begotten of them shall enter into the congregation (qahal) of the LORD in their third generation"

### 4. The Worshipping Assembly — Psalmic Praise
In the Psalms, qahal is the gathered community where praise, testimony, and vows are performed publicly.

- **Psa 22:22** — "I will declare thy name unto my brethren: in the midst of the congregation (qahal) will I praise thee"
- **Psa 22:25** — "My praise shall be of thee in the great congregation (qahal): I will pay my vows before them that fear him"
- **Psa 35:18** — "I will give thee thanks in the great congregation (qahal): I will praise thee among much people"
- **Psa 107:32** — "Let them exalt him also in the congregation (qahal) of the people, and praise him in the assembly of the elders"

### 5. Promised Multitude — Patriarchal Blessing
In Genesis, qahal appears in divine promises of multiplication to the patriarchs — a "company of nations" or "multitude of people."

- **Gen 28:3** — "God Almighty bless thee... that thou mayest be a multitude (qahal) of people"
- **Gen 35:11** — "a nation and a company (qahal) of nations shall be of thee"
- **Gen 48:4** — "I will make of thee a multitude (qahal) of people"

### 6. Hostile Company — Military/Judgment Assembly
Ezekiel uses qahal for hostile military forces or executing companies sent in judgment. This is a distinctive prophetic extension of the word.

- **Eze 16:40** — "They shall also bring up a company (qahal) against thee, and they shall stone thee"
- **Eze 23:46-47** — "I will bring up a company (qahal) upon them... the company (qahal) shall stone them with stones"
- **Eze 38:4** — "I will bring thee forth, and all thine army, horses and horsemen... a great company (qahal)"
- **Jer 50:9** — "I will raise and cause to come up against Babylon an assembly (qahal) of great nations"

### 7. Negative Assembly — The Ungodly Gathered
Qahal occasionally denotes an assembly characterized by evil or death — the "other side" of the concept.

- **Gen 49:6** — "unto their assembly (qahal), mine honour, be not thou united: for in their anger they slew a man"
- **Psa 26:5** — "I have hated the congregation (qahal) of evil doers"
- **Prov 21:16** — "shall remain in the congregation (qahal) of the dead"
- **Prov 5:14** — "I was almost in all evil in the midst of the congregation (qahal) and assembly"

*The qahal-to-ekklesia bridge is one of the most theologically significant LXX mappings. The LXX translators chose ekklesia ("called-out assembly") over synagoge when rendering qahal in contexts of Israel gathered before God — a choice that shaped NT ecclesiology when the early church adopted ekklesia for itself.*
