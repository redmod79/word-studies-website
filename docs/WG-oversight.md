# WG: Oversight / Visitation

## Overview
The oversight/visitation concept in the NT is carried by a cognate family built on the root ἐπί (epi, "upon") + σκοπέω/σκέπτομαι (scopeō/skeptomai, "look at/examine"). Four words cover the full scope: the act of visiting/inspecting (episkeptomai), the ongoing function of oversight (episkope), the activity of overseeing (episkopeō), and the person who oversees (episkopos). All four map back to the Hebrew paqad/pequddah word group through the LXX.

## Relationships

### Shared Root
All four words share the compound root ἐπι-σκοπ-/σκεπ- ("looking upon"). G1980 episkeptomai uses the -skeptomai verbal form; G1983 episkopeō, G1984 episkope, and G1985 episkopos use the -skop- form.

### Co-occurrence (appear in same verses)
No direct co-occurrences — these four words never appear together in the same verse. However, episkopos (G1985) and the shepherd verb poimainō (G4165) appear together in Acts 20:28 ("the Holy Ghost hath made you overseers, to feed the church"), and episkopos pairs with poimēn (G4166) in 1 Pet 2:25 ("the Shepherd and Bishop of your souls").

## Roles in the Concept

- **Act/Event:** episkeptomai (ἐπισκέπτομαι) — the initial visiting, whether divine mercy (Luke 1:68 "he hath visited and redeemed his people") or human care (Matt 25:36 "I was sick, and ye visited me"; Jas 1:27 "to visit the fatherless and widows")
- **Function/Office:** episkope (ἐπισκοπή) — the position of oversight or the decisive moment of divine inspection ("the time of thy visitation" Luke 19:44; "the office of a bishop" 1 Tim 3:1)
- **Activity:** episkopeō (ἐπισκοπέω) — the ongoing exercise of oversight ("taking the oversight" 1 Pet 5:2; "looking diligently" Heb 12:15)
- **Person:** episkopos (ἐπίσκοπος) — the one who holds the oversight position ("the Holy Ghost hath made you overseers" Acts 20:28; "the Shepherd and Bishop of your souls" 1 Pet 2:25)

## Key Passages Where Multiple Group Words or Concepts Appear Together

- **1 Peter 2:12,25** — [episkope v.12 + episkopos v.25] "glorify God in the day of visitation" ... "returned unto the Shepherd and Bishop of your souls" — the same chapter uses both the event (visitation) and the person (Bishop/overseer), both applied to Christ
- **1 Peter 5:2** — [episkopeō] "Feed the flock of God which is among you, taking the oversight thereof" — the oversight activity linked to the shepherd word group (poimainō/poimnion)
- **Acts 20:28** — [episkopos] "the Holy Ghost hath made you overseers, to feed the church of God" — episkopos paired with poimainō, establishing the pastoral-oversight identity
