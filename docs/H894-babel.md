# H894 — בָּבֶל (Bâbel)

## Core Data
- **Strong's:** H894
- **Original:** בָּבֶל
- **Transliteration:** Bâbel
- **Pronunciation:** baw-bel
- **Part of Speech:** Proper locative noun (n-pr-loc)
- **Root/Etymology:** Hebrew lexicon derives the name from *balal* (H1101, "to confound, mix"), giving the folk-etymology "confusion" (Gen 11:9). Akkadian *Bāb-ilu* ("gate of the god") is the native etymology; Scripture reinterprets the name polemically as "confusion."
- **Occurrences:** 264 tokens / 233 unique verses across 14 OT books
- **First Occurrence:** Gen 10:10 — "And the beginning of his kingdom was Babel, and Erech, and Accad, and Calneh, in the land of Shinar."
- **Hapax Legomenon:** No

## Lexicon Definition

From *balal* (to confound); **confusion; Babel (i.e. Babylon), including Babylonia and the Babylonian empire:**—Babel, Babylon. (KJV renders "Babel" only in Gen 10:10 and 11:9; every other occurrence is "Babylon.")

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Origin / Primeval City of Rebellion
Babel as first kingdom after the flood, founded in Shinar by Nimrod; site of the tower whose name the LORD reinterprets as "confusion."
- **Gen 10:10** — "the beginning of his kingdom was Babel... in the land of Shinar"
- **Gen 11:9** — "Therefore is the name of it called Babel; because the LORD did there confound the language of all the earth"

### 2. Imperial Power / Seat of the King
The standard political designation; almost always in the construct *melek Bāvel*, "king of Babylon."
- **2 Kings 20:12** — "Berodachbaladan... king of Babylon, sent letters... unto Hezekiah"
- **Dan 1:1** — "Nebuchadnezzar king of Babylon unto Jerusalem, and besieged it"
- **Jer 25:12** — "I will punish the king of Babylon, and that nation"

### 3. Instrument of Divine Judgment on Judah
Babylon as YHWH's rod against his own covenant people; the exilic destination of the captivity.
- **Jer 29:10** — "after seventy years be accomplished at Babylon I will visit you"
- **2 Chron 36:6** — "Nebuchadnezzar king of Babylon... bound him in fetters, to carry him to Babylon"
- **Micah 4:10** — "thou shalt go forth... thou shalt go even to Babylon; there shalt thou be delivered"

### 4. Proud Oppressor Marked for Divine Judgment
Babylon herself as the object of prophetic oracle — the "golden city," "glory of kingdoms," destined to fall like Sodom.
- **Isa 13:19** — "Babylon, the glory of kingdoms... shall be as when God overthrew Sodom and Gomorrah"
- **Isa 14:4** — "take up this proverb against the king of Babylon... How hath the oppressor ceased!"
- **Isa 21:9** — "Babylon is fallen, is fallen"
- **Isa 47:1** — "O virgin daughter of Babylon... thou shalt no more be called tender and delicate"

### 5. Cup of Wrath / Intoxicating World-Power
Babylon as a vessel in YHWH's hand that makes the nations drunk — the imagery picked up verbatim by Rev 17–18.
- **Jer 51:7** — "Babylon hath been a golden cup in the LORD'S hand, that made all the earth drunken: the nations have drunken of her wine; therefore the nations are mad"
- **Jer 51:8** — "Babylon is suddenly fallen and destroyed"
- **Jer 51:1** — "I will raise up against Babylon... a destroying wind"

### 6. Exile Lament / Place of Captivity
Babylon as the locus of grief, longing, and remembrance for the displaced covenant community.
- **Psa 137:1** — "By the rivers of Babylon, there we sat down... when we remembered Zion"
- **Psa 137:8** — "O daughter of Babylon, who art to be destroyed"
- **Ezra 1:11** — "did Sheshbazzar bring... from Babylon unto Jerusalem"

### 7. Returnee / Genealogical Reference Point
Post-exilic identity markers — those who "came up from Babylon."
- **Ezra 7:6** — "This Ezra went up from Babylon"
- **Neh 7:6** — "children of the province... carried away... unto Babylon"
- **Zech 6:10** — "of the captivity... which are come from Babylon"
