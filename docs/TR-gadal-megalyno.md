**Semantic continuity:** The LXX translators used megalynō specifically for the praise and honor senses of gadal — where God is being magnified or God magnifies someone. The physical growth senses (Qal: children growing) tended to use auxano (G837) or ektrepho (G1625). The arrogance senses (Hithpael: magnifying oneself against God) were also captured by megalynō.

## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same:**
- megalynō preserves gadal's core sense: to declare or make something great, especially in praise of God
- The praise function (Ps 34:3; 2 Sam 7:22 → Luke 1:46; Acts 10:46) carries across unchanged
- Both testaments use the word for God being magnified by human voices

**What shifts:**
- The physical growth sense (children, plants, animals growing) largely drops out in NT — only 8 NT occurrences vs. 111 OT
- The arrogance/self-magnification sense (Dan 8, 11; Jer 48) does not appear in NT megalynō uses — the NT uses are all positive
- G3170 megalynō is rare in NT; the adjective megas (G3173) carries the "great" semantic load in Greek NT
