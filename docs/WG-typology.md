# WG: Typology (Figure, Type, Shadow, Antitype)

## Overview

This word group covers the NT vocabulary of typological correspondence — how a historical person, event, or cultic institution points beyond itself to a heavenly or eschatological reality. The group centers on Louw-Nida subdomain 58.010 (Archetype/Antitype) with secondary ties to 33.003 (Discourse Types) via παραβολή. Its home is the Epistle to the Hebrews, where typos/hypodeigma/skia/antitypon/parabolē form an interwoven terminology; supporting occurrences appear in Paul (Rom 5:14; 1 Cor 10:6; Gal 4:24) and 1 Peter (3:21).

**Companion group:** [WG-figurative-speech.md](WG-figurative-speech.md) handles the wisdom-speech / parable-proverb side of παραβολή. This group handles the figure-pattern-shadow-antitype side.

## Relationships

### Shared Roots

- **τύπος (G5179)** and **ἀντίτυπον (G499)** — same root τυπ-; antitypon = ἀντί ("against/corresponding") + typos, expressing the correlate pair.
- **παραβολή (G3850)** from παραβάλλω (G3846) — παρά ("alongside") + βάλλω ("throw"); a setting-side-by-side. The related NT noun παροιμία (G3942) uses παρά + οἶμος ("way") — "that which runs alongside the road," a wayside saying.
- **ὑπόδειγμα (G5262)** from ὑποδείκνυμι ("show under, exhibit") — a specimen displayed for imitation or warning.
- **ἀλληγορέω (G238)** from ἄλλος ("other") + ἀγορεύω ("speak in assembly") — "say one thing and mean another."

| παραβολή (G3850) | others | 0x | parabolē never co-occurs with the typology cluster — but Heb 9:9 uses parabolē *within the same discourse* (immediately after Heb 8:5), extending the group |

Note on zero co-occurrence: the Synoptic parable vocabulary (parabolē, paroimia, allēgoreō) and the Hebrews typology vocabulary (typos, antitypon, skia, hypodeigma) occupy different NT books, so they never share a verse. But Hebrews **ties them together**: Heb 9:9 calls the first-tabernacle arrangement a *parabolē* in the same rhetorical movement where 8:5 calls it *hypodeigma kai skia*, and 9:24 calls heaven's realities *antitypa* of the holy places. Hebrews treats parabolē as a member of this typological family.

## Roles in the Concept

- **The verbal figure (spoken/told):** παραβολή, παροιμία, ἀλληγορέω — language that means more than it says.
- **The prefigurative pattern (historical):** τύπος — a past event or person that stamps a forward-casting shape.
- **The corresponding reality (the answer):** ἀντίτυπον — that which τύπος pointed toward.
- **The derivative copy (spatial/cultic):** ὑπόδειγμα, σκιά — earthly institutions as shadow-copies of heavenly originals.
- **The bridge:** παραβολή in Heb 9:9; 11:19 — the Synoptic word for verbal figure is redeployed in Hebrews for historical/cultic figure, linking the two registers.

## Key Passages Where Multiple Group Words Appear Together

- **Heb 8:5** — "Who serve unto the example (ὑπόδειγμα) and shadow (σκιά) of heavenly things, as Moses was admonished of God when he was about to make the tabernacle: for, See, saith he, that thou make all things according to the pattern (τύπος) shewed to thee in the mount." *Three group members in one verse — the programmatic statement of Hebrews' typology.*
- **Heb 9:9** — "Which was a figure (παραβολή) for the time then present" — parabolē joins the typological cluster.
- **Heb 9:24** — "figures (ἀντίτυπα) of the true" — Christ has entered the original, not the antitype-copy.
- **Heb 10:1** — "For the law having a shadow (σκιά) of good things to come, and not the very image (εἰκών)" — skia contrasted with the substance.
- **Heb 11:19** — "from whence also he received him in a figure (παραβολή)" — Isaac's return as figure of resurrection.
- **Rom 5:14** — "Adam, who is the figure (τύπος) of him that was to come."
- **1 Pet 3:21** — "The like figure (ἀντίτυπον) whereunto even baptism doth also now save us."
- **Gal 4:24** — "Which things are an allegory (ἀλληγορούμενα)."
