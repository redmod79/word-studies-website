# WG: Speech and Speaking

## Overview
The NT speech/speaking concept involves a cluster of Greek words covering the act of speaking, the content spoken, and the reception of speech. These words are distinguished by whether they emphasize the process of utterance (laleo), the content/declaration (lego, logos), the specific statement (epo, rhema), or the reception (akouo). The word for tongue/language (glossa) connects especially to Spirit-empowered speech.

## Relationships

### Semantic Distinctions
- **laleo** (G2980) vs. **lego** (G3004): laleo emphasizes the act/process of speaking; lego emphasizes what is said (the content). In the same verse, lego typically introduces the quoted words while laleo describes the activity of speaking (48 shared verses).
- **lego** (G3004) vs. **epo** (G2036): epo is used only in definite past tenses; lego covers all tenses. They appear together in 153 verses, often as narrative framing ("he said...saying").
- **logos** (G3056) vs. **rhema** (G4487): logos = the word/message as a whole; rhema = a specific utterance or saying. They share only 4 verses, confirming they occupy distinct niches.
- **laleo + akouo**: The speaking-hearing pair (33 shared verses). "He that speaketh...he that heareth."
- **laleo + glossa**: The tongues-speaking collocation (21 verses, 45% of all glossa occurrences).

## Roles in the Concept

- **Act/Process:** laleo (λαλέω) — the activity of speaking, producing utterance
- **Declaration:** lego (λέγω) — stating or declaring content; introduces direct speech
- **Past statement:** epo (ἔπω) — "he said" in definite past; the aorist speech verb
- **Message:** logos (λόγος) — the word or message as content
- **Utterance:** rhema (ῥῆμα) — a specific word, saying, or matter spoken
- **Reception:** akouo (ἀκούω) — hearing and receiving what is spoken
- **Instrument:** glossa (γλῶσσα) — the tongue; language as the medium of speech
