## Semantic Categories

### 1. Divine Judicial Enthronement
- **Daniel 7:9** — "I beheld till the thrones were cast down, and the Ancient of days did sit, whose garment [was] white as snow, and the hair of his head like the pure wool: his throne [was like] the fiery flame, [and] his wheels [as] burning fire."
- **Daniel 7:10** — "A fiery stream issued and came forth from before him: thousand thousands ministered unto him, and ten thousand times ten thousand stood before him: the judgment was set, and the books were opened."
- **Daniel 7:26** — "But the judgment shall sit, and they shall take away his dominion, to consume and to destroy [it] unto the end."

In Daniel 7, yethib describes the convening of the heavenly court. It is used three ways: the Ancient of Days personally "sitting" (v. 9, peal perfect), the judgment being "set" (v. 10, peal perfect — the court as an institution taking its seat), and the judgment "sitting" in the future (v. 26, peal imperfect — the court resuming or continuing its session to execute the verdict).

### 2. Settlement of Deported Peoples
- **Ezra 4:10** — "And the rest of the nations whom the great and noble Asnappar brought over, and set in the cities of Samaria, and the rest [that are] on this side the river, and at such a time."

The haphel (causative) form: Asnappar forcibly relocated conquered peoples and "settled" them in Samaria. This is the administrative-political sense — causing a population to dwell somewhere.

### 3. Habitual Dwelling / Residing
- **Ezra 4:17** — "[Then] sent the king an answer unto Rehum the chancellor, and [to] Shimshai the scribe, and [to] the rest of their companions that dwell in Samaria, and [unto] the rest beyond the river, Peace, and at such a time."

The peal participle (yathᵉbiyn) functions as a substantive: "the ones dwelling in" — a description of ongoing residence.
