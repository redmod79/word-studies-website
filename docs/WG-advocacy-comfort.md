# WG: Advocacy and Comfort

## Overview
The advocacy/comfort concept in the NT centers on the παρακαλέω (parakaleō) word family — calling alongside for help, comfort, or intercession — and the related terms for mediation and spiritual presence. The group spans legal advocacy, pastoral consolation, and divine indwelling.

## Roles in the Concept

- **Person:** paraklētos (παράκλητος) — the one called alongside: the Holy Spirit (John 14–16) or Christ (1 John 2:1)
- **Action:** parakaleō (παρακαλέω) — the act of calling alongside, beseeching, exhorting, comforting
- **Result:** paraklēsis (παράκλησις) — the comfort, consolation, or exhortation that comes from the advocate's work
- **Related role:** mesitēs (μεσίτης) — mediator; overlaps conceptually but stands *between* parties rather than *alongside* one
- **Identity:** pneuma (πνεῦμα) — the Spirit, the divine person who fulfills the paraklētos role
- **Attribute:** alētheia (ἀλήθεια) — truth, the defining quality of the Spirit-Paraklētos ("Spirit of truth")
