## Semantic Categories

### 1. Sin Multiplying — The Offense Escalating
- **Rom 5:20a** — "Moreover the law entered, that the offence might abound (pleonase)"
- **Rom 6:1** — "Shall we continue in sin, that grace may abound (pleonase)?"

The law's function is to make transgression multiply — not creating sin but exposing and multiplying its manifestation. In 6:1 the same verb frames the rhetorical objection: if sin's increase triggers grace's overflow, why not sin deliberately? Paul's aorist subjunctive marks a hypothetical scenario, not an endorsement.

### 2. Grace Multiplying Through Thanksgiving
- **2 Cor 4:15** — "For all things are for your sakes, that the abundant grace (he charis pleonasasa) might through the thanksgiving of many redound to the glory of God"

Grace is the subject — having abounded (aorist participle), it produces thanksgiving as its effect. The chain runs: suffering → grace multiplied → thanksgiving multiplied → glory to God.

### 3. Material Surplus — Manna Provision
- **2 Cor 8:15** — "He that had gathered much had nothing over (ouk epleonasen); and he that had gathered little had no lack"

Paul quotes Exod 16:18 (LXX uses pleonazo). The verb here means "have surplus/excess" — literal material overflow. This is the only concrete (non-abstract) usage, grounding the word's quantity sense.

### 4. Growth in Love and Virtue
- **1 Thes 3:12** — "And the Lord make you to increase (pleonasai) and abound (perisseusai) in love one toward another, and toward all men"
- **2 Thes 1:3** — "your faith groweth exceedingly ... and the love of every one of you all toward each other aboundeth (pleonazei)"
- **2 Pet 1:8** — "For if these things be in you, and abound (pleonazonta), they make you that ye shall neither be barren nor unfruitful"

In the Thessalonian pair, pleonazo appears as a prayer-wish (optative) and then as a fulfilled reality (present indicative). In 2 Peter, the present participle describes ongoing multiplication of virtues (faith, virtue, knowledge, temperance, patience, godliness, brotherly kindness, love — v. 5-7).

### 5. Fruit Accruing to Account
- **Phil 4:17** — "Not because I desire a gift: but I desire fruit that may abound (pleonazonta) to your account"

Present participle — fruit continuously multiplying (accruing) to the Philippians' spiritual credit. The commercial metaphor (eis logon, "to your account") treats spiritual benefit as accumulating interest.

### Synonyms
- **G4052 περισσεύω (perisseuo)** — to superabound, overflow beyond sufficiency (35v). The most common NT "abound" verb. Both appear together in 1 Thes 3:12 ("increase [pleonazo] and abound [perisseuo]"). Pleonazo emphasizes growth/multiplication in quantity; perisseuo emphasizes overflow beyond what is needed.
- **G4129 πληθύνω (plethuno)** — to multiply, increase in number (12v). More numerical than pleonazo; used for population growth (Acts 6:1, 7:17) and for grace/peace formulas (1 Pet 1:2; 2 Pet 1:2; Jude 1:2).
- **G837 αὐξάνω (auxano)** — to grow, increase (23v). Organic growth metaphor; used for plant growth, body growth, and spiritual maturation. More gradual than pleonazo's decisive multiplication.

### Easily Confused Words

| Word | Strong's | Shared Translation | Key Difference |
|------|----------|--------------------|----------------|
| περισσεύω (perisseuo) | G4052 | "abound" (19v vs 4v) | perisseuo = overflow beyond sufficiency; pleonazo = multiply in quantity |
| ὑπερπερισσεύω (huperperisseuo) | G5248 | "abound much more" | huper-compound of perisseuo; used for grace over sin in Rom 5:20b |
| πληθύνω (plethuno) | G4129 | "abound" (1v), "multiply" | plethuno = numerical multiplication; pleonazo = more general increase |

In the sin-grace escalation of Rom 5:20, Paul uses pleonazo for sin's multiplication ("the offence might abound"), then switches to the intensified huperperisseuo for grace's response ("grace did much more abound"). This verb selection establishes that grace does not merely match sin's increase — it surpasses it with a different, higher-order verb.
