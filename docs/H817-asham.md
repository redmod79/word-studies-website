## Semantic Categories

### 1. The Trespass/Guilt Offering as Ritual Sacrifice
The dominant usage — the ʼasham as a specific sacrificial animal brought to the priest for atonement of guilt, distinct from the sin offering (chaṭṭaʼah).
- **Lev 5:18** — "he shall bring a ram without blemish out of the flock, with thy estimation, for a trespass offering (ʼasham)"
- **Lev 7:1** — "this is the law of the trespass offering (ʼasham): it is most holy"
- **Lev 14:12** — "the priest shall take one he lamb, and offer him for a trespass offering (ʼasham)"
- **Lev 19:21** — "he shall bring his trespass offering (ʼasham) unto the LORD"

### 2. The Guilt/Liability State
Abstract sense — the condition of being guilty, the liability incurred by transgression. Here ʼasham refers not to the offering but to the guilt itself.
- **Gen 26:10** — "thou shouldest have brought guiltiness (ʼasham) upon us" — guilt as social liability
- **Jer 51:5** — "their land was filled with sin (ʼasham) against the Holy One of Israel"
- **Pro 14:9** — "Fools make a mock at sin (ʼasham)"

### 3. Restitution/Compensation for Trespass
The ʼasham as the required recompense — the debt owed. Numbers 5 uses it for the principal plus one-fifth penalty that must be paid.
- **Num 5:7** — "he shall recompense his trespass (ʼasham) with the principal thereof, and add unto it the fifth part"
- **Num 5:8** — "let the trespass (ʼasham) be recompensed unto the LORD"
- **2 Ki 12:16** — "the trespass money and sin money was not brought into the house of the LORD"

### 4. Non-Israelite/Pagan Use of the Concept
The Philistines recognize the need for an ʼasham to appease Israel's God after capturing the ark.
- **1 Sam 6:3** — "return him a trespass offering (ʼasham): then ye shall be healed"
- **1 Sam 6:4** — "What shall be the trespass offering (ʼasham) which we shall return to him?"
- **1 Sam 6:17** — "the golden emerods which the Philistines returned for a trespass offering (ʼasham)"

### 5. The ʼasham as Vicarious/Substitutionary Offering
The singular prophetic application where a person's soul is made the ʼasham.
- **Isa 53:10** — "when thou shalt make his soul an offering for sin (ʼasham), he shall see his seed"

### 6. Eschatological Temple Provision
Ezekiel's future temple vision includes the ʼasham alongside other offerings, showing its enduring place in the sacrificial system.
- **Eze 40:39** — "two tables on this side... to slay thereon the burnt offering and the sin offering and the trespass offering (ʼasham)"
- **Eze 42:13** — "there shall they lay the most holy things, and the meat offering, and the sin offering, and the trespass offering (ʼasham)"
- **Eze 46:20** — "the place where the priests shall boil the trespass offering (ʼasham) and the sin offering"
