## Lexicon Definition

From G756 (ἄρχω). Properly abstract: *a commencement*; or concretely: *chief* (in various applications of order, time, place, or rank). KJV renders: beginning, corner, (at the, the) first (estate), magistrate, power, principality, principle, rule.

Two semantic poles: (1) temporal/ordinal priority — "beginning, first" — and (2) positional priority — "rule, authority, ruler, principality." Both derive from the verbal idea of "going first / taking precedence."

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Temporal Origin of Creation / the World
The moment creation began; often paired with κτίσις (creation) or κόσμος (world).
- **Mat 19:4, 8** — "at the beginning" / "from the beginning it was not so" (marriage in creation)
- **Mar 10:6; 13:19** — "from the beginning of the creation God made them"
- **Joh 1:1, 2** — "In the beginning was the Word"
- **2 Pe 3:4** — "as they were from the beginning of the creation"
- **Heb 1:10** — "Thou, Lord, in the beginning hast laid the foundation of the earth"

### 2. "From the Beginning" of the Gospel / Christian Knowledge
Apostolic formula for original deposit of teaching, witness, or command received at first hearing.
- **Luk 1:2** — "from the beginning were eyewitnesses"
- **1 Jn 1:1** — "that which was from the beginning"
- **1 Jn 2:7, 13, 14, 24; 3:11** — "the old commandment … which ye had from the beginning"
- **2 Jn 1:5, 6** — "not a new commandment … but that which we had from the beginning"
- **Joh 15:27** — "ye have been with me from the beginning"
- **2 Th 2:13** — "God hath from the beginning chosen you to salvation"

### 3. Beginning of a Narrative / Event / Ministry
Starting point of a discourse, event-sequence, or ministry stretch.
- **Mar 1:1** — "The beginning of the gospel of Jesus Christ"
- **Joh 2:11** — "This beginning of miracles did Jesus"
- **Joh 6:64; 8:25; 16:4** — Jesus' knowledge/speech "from the beginning"
- **Act 11:15** — "the Holy Ghost fell on them, as on us at the beginning"
- **Mat 24:8; Mar 13:8** — "the beginning of sorrows" (ἀρχὴ ὠδίνων)

### 4. First Principles / Elementary Teaching
Foundational pedagogical material, the "ABCs" of doctrine.
- **Heb 5:12** — "the first principles of the oracles of God"
- **Heb 6:1** — "leaving the principles of the doctrine of Christ"
- **Heb 3:14** — "hold the beginning of our confidence stedfast"

### 5. Rulers / Principalities (angelic & demonic powers)
Cosmic-authority sense, typically plural; often coupled with ἐξουσία (authorities) and δύναμις (powers).
- **Rom 8:38** — "nor angels, nor principalities, nor powers"
- **Eph 1:21** — "far above all principality, and power, and might"
- **Eph 3:10** — "principalities and powers in heavenly places"
- **Eph 6:12** — "we wrestle not against flesh and blood, but against principalities"
- **Col 1:16** — "thrones, or dominions, or principalities, or powers"
- **Col 2:10, 15** — Christ is "the head of all principality" / "having spoiled principalities"
- **1 Co 15:24** — "shall have put down all rule and all authority"

### 6. Human Rulers / Civil Magistrates
Visible earthly office-holders.
- **Luk 12:11** — "bring you unto the synagogues, and magistrates"
- **Luk 20:20** — "deliver him unto the power and authority of the governor"
- **Tit 3:1** — "be subject to principalities and powers, to obey magistrates"

### 7. Christ as the Beginning / Origin
Christological title combining temporal priority, creational source, and headship.
- **Col 1:18** — "he is the beginning, the firstborn from the dead"
- **Rev 3:14** — "the beginning of the creation of God"
- **Rev 1:8; 21:6; 22:13** — "I am Alpha and Omega, the beginning and the end"

### 8. Assigned Sphere / Original Estate
Apostate angels who abandoned their original position.
- **Jude 1:6** — angels "which kept not their first estate (ἀρχή)"

### 9. Corner / Extremity (concrete object)
The four projecting "beginnings" of a sheet — a spatial edge/corner.
- **Act 10:11; 11:5** — "a great sheet knit at the four corners"
