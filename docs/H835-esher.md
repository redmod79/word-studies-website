## Lexicon Definition

Happiness; only in masculine plural construct as interjection, "how happy!" Translations: blessed, happy.

The word functions almost exclusively as an exclamatory formula introducing a beatitude: "Blessed/Happy is the one who..." The construct-plural form (ashre) is used predicatively rather than as an ordinary noun with articles or prepositions.

### Easily Confused Words

| Word | Strong's | Shared Translation | Key Difference |
|------|----------|--------------------|----------------|
| בָּרַךְ (barak) | H1288 | "blessed" (85v barak vs. 26v esher) | barak = active blessing bestowed by an agent (God blesses someone, someone blesses God); esher = a state of happiness/blessedness declared over someone's condition |
| μακάριος (makarios) | G3107 | "Blessed" (42v in NT) | Greek NT equivalent via LXX; makarios carries identical beatitude-formula function |

The barak/esher distinction is fundamental: barak involves a relational act (blessing conferred or received), while esher describes a condition (the fortunate state of the person who meets certain criteria). Barak takes a direct object; esher introduces a predication about a subject.
