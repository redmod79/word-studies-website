## Lexicon Definition

From ὁμοιόω; a form; abstractly, resemblance. Rendered in KJV as: **made like to, likeness, similitude, shape**. The noun designates the concrete result of making-alike — the form, figure, or representation that resembles something else. It sits between outward appearance (σχῆμα) and inner essence (μορφή), stressing visible resemblance without asserting identity of substance.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Idolatrous Image — a counterfeit resemblance of God or creature
- **Rom 1:23** — "changed the glory of the uncorruptible God into an image made like (homoíōma) to corruptible man, and to birds, and fourfooted beasts"

### 2. Representative Likeness — sharing a pattern or figure without full identity
- **Rom 5:14** — "death reigned from Adam to Moses, even over them that had not sinned after the similitude (homoíōma) of Adam's transgression"
- **Rom 8:3** — "God sending his own Son in the likeness (homoíōma) of sinful flesh" (true flesh, yet not itself sinful — resemblance without identity of sin)

### 3. Union-Likeness — participation in Christ's death and resurrection
- **Rom 6:5** — "if we have been planted together in the likeness (homoíōma) of his death, we shall be also in the likeness of his resurrection"

### 4. Incarnational Form — the visible form Christ took
- **Phil 2:7** — "made himself of no reputation, and took upon him the form of a servant, and was made in the likeness (homoíōma) of men"

### 5. Apocalyptic Shape — the visible figure of a vision-creature
- **Rev 9:7** — "the shapes (homoíōma) of the locusts were like unto horses prepared unto battle"
