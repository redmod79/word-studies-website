## Semantic Categories

### 1. Physical Arrival / Narrative Movement
People and Jesus physically moving to or arriving at locations. The largest category — the workhorse of Gospel narrative.
- **Mat 2:9** — "the star...went before them, till it came and stood over where the young child was"
- **Mark 1:9** — "Jesus came from Nazareth of Galilee, and was baptized of John in Jordan"
- **Luke 2:16** — "they came with haste, and found Mary, and Joseph, and the babe lying in a manger"
- **John 3:31** — "He that cometh from above is above all...he that cometh from heaven is above all"

### 2. Incarnational Coming — Jesus Came with a Purpose
Jesus himself states why he came, marking the theological significance of his arrival in the flesh.
- **Matt 5:17** — "Think not that I am come to destroy the law, or the prophets: I am not come to destroy, but to fulfil"
- **Matt 9:13** — "I am not come to call the righteous, but sinners to repentance"
- **John 1:11** — "He came unto his own, and his own received him not"
- **John 9:39** — "For judgment I am come into this world"
- **1 Tim 1:15** — "Christ Jesus came into the world to save sinners"

### 3. The Coming One — Messianic Identification
A technical use identifying Jesus as the prophesied Messiah who was expected to come (ὁ ἐρχόμενος).
- **Mark 1:7** — "There cometh one mightier than I after me, the latchet of whose shoes I am not worthy"
- **Matt 11:3** — "Art thou he that should come, or do we look for another?"
- **John 11:27** — "I believe that thou art the Christ, the Son of God, which should come into the world"
- **Heb 10:37** — "he that shall come will come, and will not tarry"

### 4. Second Coming / Eschatological Return
The anticipated future return of Jesus to judge and be glorified; the most theologically freighted uses.
- **Acts 1:11** — "this same Jesus...shall so come in like manner as ye have seen him go into heaven"
- **2 Thess 1:10** — "When he shall come to be glorified in his saints...in that day"
- **Rev 1:7** — "Behold, he cometh with clouds; and every eye shall see him"
- **Rev 22:20** — "Surely I come quickly. Amen. Even so, come, Lord Jesus"

### 5. Coming of Spiritual Realities — Wrath, Kingdom, Judgment
Impersonal or abstract subjects: the kingdom, wrath, times of refreshing, deception — events that "come."
- **Matt 6:10** — "Thy kingdom come. Thy will be done in earth, as it is in heaven"
- **Acts 3:19** — "times of refreshing shall come from the presence of the Lord"
- **Eph 5:6** — "cometh the wrath of God upon the children of disobedience"
- **2 Pet 3:3** — "there shall come in the last days scoffers, walking after their own lusts"
