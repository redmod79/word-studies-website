## Semantic Categories

### 1. God's Patience Leading to Repentance
- **Rom 2:4** — "Or despisest thou the riches of his goodness and forbearance and longsuffering; not knowing that the goodness of God leadeth thee to repentance?"

Here anochē appears in a triad with χρηστότης (chrestotes, G5544, "goodness/kindness") and μακροθυμία (makrothumia, G3115, "longsuffering"). The forbearance is God's self-restraint in withholding deserved judgment, purposed toward producing repentance.

### 2. God's Forbearance Over Past Sins
- **Rom 3:25** — "Whom God hath set forth to be a propitiation through faith in his blood, to declare his righteousness for the remission of sins that are past, through the forbearance of God"

Here anochē explains why God appeared to overlook sins committed before the cross. The forbearance is the divine restraint exercised during the period prior to Christ's atonement — God withheld full judgment, and the cross retroactively vindicated that restraint.

### Key Distinctions
- **anochē** = God's self-restraint in withholding judgment (temporary, purposeful delay)
- **makrothumia** = God's patience stretched over long duration (emotional composure under provocation)
- **hupomonē** = steadfast endurance (perseverance through suffering; primarily describes human response, not divine restraint)

### Closely Related (Not Synonyms)
- **G3929 πάρεσις (paresis)** — passing over, praetermission, toleration (1v, hapax — Rom 3:25 only). KJV: "remission." Describes the act itself of letting sins pass unpunished during the forbearance period. Distinguished from G859 ἄφεσις (aphesis, "forgiveness/release," 17v), which is full remission. Paresis is temporary overlooking; aphesis is permanent cancellation.
