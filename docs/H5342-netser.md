## Semantic Categories

### 1. Messianic Shoot / Royal Descendant
- **Isa 11:1** — "And there shall come forth a rod out of the stem of Jesse, and a Branch shall grow out of his roots" — the netser sprouts from the stump (geza) of Jesse's felled dynasty, paired with choter ("rod/twig"); the image is of new growth from an apparently dead line.

### 2. God's Planting / Covenant People as Shoots
- **Isa 60:21** — "Thy people also shall be all righteous: they shall inherit the land for ever, the branch of my planting, the work of my hands, that I may be glorified" — Israel collectively described as God's netser, emphasizing divine origin and permanence.

### 3. Abominable / Rejected Shoot (Negative Reversal)
- **Isa 14:19** — "But thou art cast out of thy grave like an abominable branch, and as the raiment of those that are slain, thrust through with a sword, that go down to the stones of the pit; as a carcase trodden under feet" — the king of Babylon is a netser that is loathsome, cast away — an anti-image inverting the positive "branch" of 11:1.

### 4. Genealogical Offshoot / Dynastic Descendant
- **Dan 11:7** — "But out of a branch of her roots shall one stand up in his estate, which shall come with an army, and shall enter into the fortress of the king of the north, and shall prevail" — netser used in a political-genealogical sense: a descendant from the same dynastic root.
