## Semantic Categories

### 1. Physical Crossing of Geography (River, Territory, Border)
The core, literal sense — crossing a body of water or traversing territory.
- **Gen 31:21** — "he rose up, and passed over the river, and set his face toward the mount Gilead"
- **Jos 3:17** — "the priests... stood firm on dry ground in the midst of Jordan, and all the Israelites passed over on dry ground"
- **2 Sa 17:22** — "they passed over Jordan: by the morning light there lacked not one of them that was not gone over"
- **Num 21:22** — "Let me pass through thy land: we will not turn into the fields"

### 2. Passing By / Passing Along (Movement Past a Point)
Movement past a location or person without stopping or lingering.
- **Exo 34:6** — "And the LORD passed by before him, and proclaimed, The LORD, The LORD God, merciful and gracious"
- **1 Ki 19:11** — "the LORD passed by, and a great and strong wind rent the mountains"
- **2 Ki 4:9** — "this is an holy man of God, which passeth by us continually"
- **Lam 1:12** — "Is it nothing to you, all ye that pass by?"

### 3. Passing Away / Transience (Time, Things, Conditions)
The metaphorical sense of something ceasing, coming to an end, or being over.
- **Job 6:15** — "as the stream of brooks they pass away"
- **Job 30:15** — "my welfare passeth away as a cloud"
- **Psa 144:4** — "Man is like to vanity: his days are as a shadow that passeth away"
- **Jer 8:20** — "The harvest is past, the summer is ended, and we are not saved"

### 4. Transgressing / Crossing a Boundary of Law or Covenant
The figurative sense of overstepping a divine commandment or covenant stipulation.
- **Jos 7:11** — "Israel hath sinned, and they have also transgressed my covenant"
- **Dan 9:11** — "all Israel have transgressed thy law"
- **Hos 6:7** — "they like men have transgressed the covenant"
- **Isa 24:5** — "they have transgressed the laws, changed the ordinance, broken the everlasting covenant"

### 5. Causative: Removing / Putting Away (Hiphil)
The Hiphil "cause to pass from" — used for removal of iniquity, idols, or persons.
- **Zech 3:4** — "I have caused thine iniquity to pass from thee, and I will clothe thee with change of raiment"
- **2 Sa 12:13** — "The LORD also hath put away thy sin; thou shalt not die"
- **2 Chr 15:8** — "he took courage, and put away the abominable idols"
- **Est 8:3** — "besought him with tears to put away the mischief of Haman"

### 6. Causative: Making to Pass Through Fire (Hiphil — Idolatrous Ritual)
A distinct Hiphil usage specifically denoting the pagan practice of passing children through fire to Molech.
- **Lev 18:21** — "thou shalt not let any of thy seed pass through the fire to Molech"
- **2 Ki 16:3** — "made his son to pass through the fire, according to the abominations of the heathen"
- **2 Ki 23:10** — "that no man might make his son or his daughter to pass through the fire to Molech"
- **Eze 20:31** — "when ye make your sons to pass through the fire, ye pollute yourselves"

### 7. Divine Passing Through (Theophany, Judgment)
God himself as the one who "passes through" — in theophanic revelation or judgment.
- **Exo 12:12** — "I will pass through the land of Egypt this night, and will smite all the firstborn"
- **Exo 12:23** — "the LORD will pass through to smite the Egyptians"
- **Exo 33:19** — "I will make all my goodness pass before thee"
- **Amo 5:17** — "in all vineyards shall be wailing: for I will pass through thee, saith the LORD"

### 8. Emotional Overflow: Wrath (Hithpael)
Reflexive sense — "to overflow with feeling," rendered as anger or indignation.
- **Deu 3:26** — "the LORD was wroth with me for your sakes"
- **Pro 14:16** — "the fool rageth, and is confident"
- **Pro 20:2** — "whoso provoketh him to anger sinneth against his own soul"
