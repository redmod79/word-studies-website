## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same across testaments:**
- The day/night merism for totality continues: OT "day and night" (Josh 1:8, Ps 1:2) → NT "night and day" (1 Thes 2:9, 3:10, 2 Tim 1:3, Rev 4:8, 7:15, 12:10, 20:10)
- Night as the setting for divine encounter: OT dreams/visions (Gen 46:2, Job 33:15) → NT angelic visits (Acts 5:19, 16:9, 27:23)
- Night as cover for covert action: OT military movements (Josh 8:3, 2 Ki 25:4) → NT escape/flight (Mat 2:14, Acts 17:10)

**What shifts:**
- The NT develops a stronger figurative/ethical use of night as moral darkness. Paul's "the night is far spent, the day is at hand" (Rom 13:12) and "ye are not of the night" (1 Thes 5:5) extend the OT prophetic metaphor (Isa 21:11-12, Micah 3:6) into sustained ethical exhortation.
- Revelation's eschatological reversal — "there shall be no night there" (Rev 21:25, 22:5) — echoes and consummates the creation pattern of Gen 1:5 where God first named the night. What began in Genesis ends in Revelation with night's abolition.
