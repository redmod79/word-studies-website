# WG: Forsake / Leave Behind

## Overview
The Greek NT uses several compounds of the λειπ- (leip-, "leave") root to express abandonment and desertion. These words are differentiated by their prefixes, which shade the meaning from neutral departure to intensified relational abandonment. The Aramaic σαβαχθάνι appears exclusively alongside ἐγκαταλείπω in the cry of dereliction.

## Relationships

### Shared Root
- G1459 (enkataleipō), G2641 (kataleipō), G620 (apoleipō), G3007 (leipō) — all from root λείπω "to leave"
- G863 (aphiēmi) — from ἵημι "to send," not λείπω, but overlaps in KJV translation
- G4518 (sabachthani) — Aramaic שְׁבַקְתַּנִי (shebaqtani), from שְׁבַק (shebaq, "leave/forsake")

### Shared Hebrew Source (LXX Bridge)
All three λειπ- compounds translate Hebrew H5800 עָזַב (ʻazab, "forsake"):
- G1459 enkataleipō ← H5800 azab: 123 co-occurrences (PMI 31.81)

This shows that G1459 is the *dominant* Greek rendering of azab in the LXX, making it the strongest continuation of OT "forsaking" language into the NT.

## Roles in the Concept

- **Intensified abandonment:** G1459 enkataleipō — relational betrayal, desertion in time of need (Matt 27:46, 2 Tim 4:10, 16)
- **Broad release:** G863 aphiēmi — "let go" in many senses; forsaking is only one use among forgiveness, permission, dismissal
- **Spatial departure:** G2641 kataleipō — "leave behind" physically; neutral, can be positive (Gen 2:24 "leave father and mother")
- **Residual remainder:** G620 apoleipō — emphasis on what is left over; Paul's cloak, a sick companion
- **Aramaic witness:** G4518 sabachthani — preserves the original-language cry; always paired with G1459

## Key Passages Where Multiple Group Words Appear Together
- **Matt 27:46 / Mark 15:34** — G1459 + G4518: "lama sabachthani — why hast thou forsaken me"
