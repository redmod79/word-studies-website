## Semantic Categories

### 1. Imperial Coin Inscription (civic identification)
The Roman denarius bore Caesar's engraved portrait — the question "whose image?" invokes this civic/political meaning.

- **Mat 22:20** — "And he saith unto them, Whose [is] this image and superscription?"
- **Mar 12:16** — "Whose [is] this image and superscription? And they said unto him, Caesar's."
- **Luk 20:24** — "Whose image and superscription hath it? They answered and said, Caesar's."

### 2. Christ as the Image of God (Christological identity)
The Son is the visible expression of the invisible God — not merely resembling God but constituting the full representation of his nature.

- **Col 1:15** — "Who is the image of the invisible God, the firstborn of every creature"
- **2Co 4:4** — "lest the light of the glorious gospel of Christ, who is the image of God, should shine unto them"

### 3. Humanity Bearing and Restored to God's Image (anthropological/soteriological)
Humans were made bearing God's image; the fall distorted it; redemption restores conformity to Christ as the true image.

- **1Co 11:7** — "forasmuch as he is the image and glory of God: but the woman is the glory of the man"
- **1Co 15:49** — "as we have borne the image of the earthy, we shall also bear the image of the heavenly"
- **2Co 3:18** — "we all... are changed into the same image from glory to glory"
- **Col 3:10** — "the new [man], which is renewed in knowledge after the image of him that created him"
- **Rom 8:29** — "predestinate [to be] conformed to the image of his Son"
- **Rom 1:23** — (idolatrous inversion) "changed the glory of the uncorruptible God into an image made like to corruptible man"

### 4. Shadow versus True Image (typological contrast)
The Law gives a shadow of future realities; εἰκών here denotes the full, substantial representation — the reality toward which the shadow pointed.

- **Heb 10:1** — "the law having a shadow of good things to come, [and] not the very image of the things, can never with those sacrifices... make the comers thereunto perfect"

### 5. The Image of the Beast (eschatological worship)
Revelation uses εἰκών for a physical representation of the beast that receives worship, paralleling OT idolatry themes and the Danielic golden image.

- **Rev 13:14** — "they should make an image to the beast, which had the wound by a sword"
- **Rev 13:15** — "he had power to give life unto the image of the beast, that the image of the beast should both speak, and cause that as many as would not worship the image... should be killed"
- **Rev 14:9** — "If any man worship the beast and his image..."
- **Rev 14:11** — "they have no rest day nor night, who worship the beast and his image"
- **Rev 20:4** — "the souls of them that were beheaded... which had not worshipped the beast, neither his image"
