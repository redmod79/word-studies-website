## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same across testaments:**
- The core semantic range is preserved: pathach and anoigo both cover physical opening (doors, gates, eyes, mouths, earth) and metaphorical opening (access, revelation, opportunity)
- The "heaven opened" pattern runs from Gen 7:11 through the prophets into the Gospels and Revelation
- The "open/shut" antonym pair (pathach/sagar → anoigo/kleio) remains stable

**What shifts:**
- The NT dramatically expands the "opening sealed documents" category: pathach is never used for opening scrolls/seals in the OT, but anoigo dominates Revelation's seal-opening sequence (25 of 75 NT verses are in Revelation)
- The "door of opportunity" metaphor (1Co 16:9, 2Co 2:12, Col 4:3) has no close OT parallel with pathach
- Isaiah 22:22 (key of David) becomes christologically loaded in Rev 3:7, transferring the authority to open and shut from a human steward to Christ
