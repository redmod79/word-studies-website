## Roles in the Concept

- **Formal covenant curse:** ʼârar (אָרַר) — the primary, weightiest curse verb; used in divine pronouncements (Gen 3) and covenant formulae (Deut 27-28)
- **Contempt/belittlement curse:** qalal (קָלַל) — to make light of, treat as insignificant; broader semantic range including non-curse uses
- **Piercing-word curse:** qabab (קָבַב) — to scoop out / hollow with words; rare, almost exclusively in the Balaam cycle
- **Oath-curse:** ʼalah (אָלָה) — sworn imprecation, self-invoked; the curse bound to an oath
- **Curse-result:** meʼerah (מְאֵרָה) — the abstract state of being under a curse; derived from ʼârar
- **Indignation/denouncement:** zaʻam (זָעַם) — divine wrath/foaming expressed verbally; more emotional than legal
- **Antonym/counterpart:** barak (בָּרַךְ) — to bless; structurally paired with ʼârar in covenant contexts
