## Semantic Categories

### 1. Humans Glorifying God — Response to Mighty Works
Crowds and individuals glorify God after witnessing healings, miracles, and answered prayer. This is the dominant Synoptic usage.
- **Matt 9:8** — "the multitudes... glorified God, which had given such power unto men"
- **Luke 5:25-26** — healed paralytic "departed to his own house, glorifying God... and they glorified God"
- **Luke 17:15** — healed Samaritan leper "turned back, and with a loud voice glorified God"
- **Luke 23:47** — centurion at the cross "glorified God, saying, Certainly this was a righteous man"
- **Acts 4:21** — "all men glorified God for that which was done"

### 2. Humans Glorifying God — Intentional Obedience and Worship
Believers are commanded to glorify God through their conduct, worship, and bodily life.
- **Matt 5:16** — "see your good works, and glorify your Father which is in heaven"
- **1 Cor 6:20** — "glorify God in your body, and in your spirit, which are God's"
- **Rom 15:6** — "with one mind and one mouth glorify God, even the Father"
- **1 Pet 2:12** — "they may by your good works, which they shall behold, glorify God in the day of visitation"
- **1 Pet 4:16** — "if any man suffer as a Christian... let him glorify God on this behalf"

### 3. God Glorifying the Son / The Son Glorifying the Father
The mutual glorification between Father and Son is the theological center of John's Gospel.
- **John 12:28** — "Father, glorify thy name... I have both glorified it, and will glorify it again"
- **John 13:31-32** — "Now is the Son of man glorified, and God is glorified in him... God shall also glorify him"
- **John 17:1** — "Father, the hour is come; glorify thy Son, that thy Son also may glorify thee"
- **John 17:4-5** — "I have glorified thee on the earth... glorify thou me with thine own self"

### 4. God Glorifying Believers — Eschatological Transformation
God glorifies (transforms, brings to eschatological honor) those he has predestined and called.
- **Rom 8:30** — "whom he justified, them he also glorified" (aorist — proleptic certainty)
- **1 Pet 1:8** — "ye rejoice with joy unspeakable and full of glory (δεδοξασμένῃ)"
- **2 Cor 3:10** — "that which was made glorious had no glory in this respect, by reason of the glory that excelleth"
- **1 Pet 4:14** — "the spirit of glory and of God resteth upon you: on their part he is evil spoken of, but on your part he is glorified"

### 5. The Word of God / Gospel Being Glorified
The message itself is the object of glorification through its spread and reception.
- **Acts 13:48** — "the Gentiles heard this, they were glad, and glorified the word of the Lord"
- **2 Thess 3:1** — "pray for us, that the word of the Lord may have free course, and be glorified"

### 6. Negative / Ironic Uses — Self-Glorification and Failure to Glorify
- **Rom 1:21** — "when they knew God, they glorified him not as God" (deliberate refusal)
- **John 8:54** — "If I honour [glorify] myself, my honour is nothing"
- **Rev 18:7** — "How much she hath glorified herself" (Babylon's self-exaltation)
- **Matt 6:2** — hypocrites doing alms "that they may have glory [be glorified] of men"

The dominant LXX bridge is H3513 כָּבַד (kabad), "to be heavy" → "to honor/glorify." The LXX translators used doxazō to render the *honor* sense of kabad (Piel/Hiphil stems: "honor father and mother," "glorify the LORD"), while βαρύνω captured the *physical weight/hardness* sense ("harden Pharaoh's heart").
