## Lexicon Definition

Or שָׂתַם sâtham (Numbers 24:15), a primitive root; to stop up; by implication, to repair; figuratively, to keep secret: — closed up, hidden, secret, shut out (up), stop.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Physical Blocking of Water Sources
Filling or plugging wells and springs to deny water access — a standard military and territorial tactic.
- **Gen 26:15** — "the Philistines had stopped them, and filled them with earth"
- **Gen 26:18** — "the Philistines had stopped them after the death of Abraham"
- **2 Kgs 3:19** — "shall fell every good tree, and stop all wells of water"
- **2 Kgs 3:25** — "they stopped all the wells of water, and felled all the good trees"
- **2 Chr 32:3** — "He took counsel...to stop the waters of the fountains which were without the city"
- **2 Chr 32:4** — "who stopped all the fountains, and the brook that ran through the midst of the land"
- **2 Chr 32:30** — "Hezekiah also stopped the upper watercourse of Gihon"

### 2. Repair of Breaches
Stopping gaps in a wall — closely related to the physical blocking sense but applied to structural repair.
- **Neh 4:7** — "the walls of Jerusalem were made up, and that the breaches began to be stopped"

### 3. Prophetic Sealing — Shutting Up Revelation Until the End
Sealing words or visions to restrict access until a divinely appointed time. Always paired with חָתַם (chatham) in Daniel.
- **Dan 8:26** — "shut thou up the vision; for it shall be for many days"
- **Dan 12:4** — "shut up the words, and seal the book, even to the time of the end"
- **Dan 12:9** — "the words are closed up and sealed till the time of the end"

### 4. Hiddenness / Secrecy (Figurative)
The participle form used adjectivally to describe concealed or inaccessible knowledge.
- **Psa 51:6** — "in the hidden part thou shalt make me to know wisdom"
- **Eze 28:3** — "thou art wiser than Daniel; there is no secret that they can hide from thee"

### 5. Blocking Access to God
Used once for prayer being shut out — God refusing to hear.
- **Lam 3:8** — "Also when I cry and shout, he shutteth out my prayer"
