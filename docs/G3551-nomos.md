## Lexicon Definition

From a primary νέμω (nemō), to parcel out, especially food or grazing to animals; law (through the idea of prescriptive usage), generally (regulation), specifically (of Moses, including the volume); also of the Gospel, or figuratively (a principle).

## Semantic Categories

### 1. The Mosaic Law / Torah
The commandments and statutes given through Moses at Sinai. The dominant usage.
- **Matt 5:17** — "Think not that I am come to destroy the law, or the prophets: I am not come to destroy, but to fulfil."
- **John 1:17** — "For the law was given by Moses, but grace and truth came by Jesus Christ."
- **Gal 3:24** — "Wherefore the law was our schoolmaster to bring us unto Christ, that we might be justified by faith."
- **Rom 7:12** — "Wherefore the law is holy, and the commandment holy, and just, and good."

### 2. The OT Scriptures Broadly
"The law" as a division of Scripture (the Pentateuch) or the entire OT, sometimes citing Psalms or Prophets.
- **Luke 24:44** — "all things must be fulfilled, which were written in the law of Moses, and in the prophets, and in the psalms"
- **John 10:34** — "Is it not written in your law, I said, Ye are gods?" (quoting Psalm 82:6)
- **John 12:34** — "We have heard out of the law that Christ abideth for ever" (no single Torah source)
- **John 15:25** — "the word might be fulfilled that is written in their law, They hated me without a cause" (quoting Psalm 35:19/69:4)

### 3. A Principle or Operative Rule
Metaphorical use — an operating principle or force, not a legal code.
- **Rom 3:27** — "By what law? of works? Nay: but by the law of faith."
- **Rom 7:23** — "I see another law in my members, warring against the law of my mind"
- **Rom 8:2** — "the law of the Spirit of life in Christ Jesus hath made me free from the law of sin and death"

### 4. The Law of Christ / Royal Law / Law of Liberty
An ethical standard for believers, distinct from Mosaic code.
- **Gal 6:2** — "Bear ye one another's burdens, and so fulfil the law of Christ."
- **James 1:25** — "whoso looketh into the perfect law of liberty, and continueth therein"
- **James 2:8** — "If ye fulfil the royal law according to the scripture, Thou shalt love thy neighbour as thyself"

### 5. Natural / Moral Law
A moral awareness accessible apart from the Mosaic revelation.
- **Rom 2:14** — "the Gentiles, which have not the law, do by nature the things contained in the law, these, having not the law, are a law unto themselves"
- **Rom 2:15** — "Which shew the work of the law written in their hearts"

### 6. Law of Commandments / Ordinances
Specific ceremonial or regulatory requirements, especially in contrast to the new covenant.
- **Eph 2:15** — "Having abolished in his flesh the enmity, even the law of commandments contained in ordinances"
- **Heb 7:16** — "not after the law of a carnal commandment, but after the power of an endless life"
- **Heb 10:1** — "the law having a shadow of good things to come, and not the very image of the things"
