## Semantic Categories

### 1. Prophetic Warning — The Watchman's Duty (Ezekiel)
The overwhelming majority of uses appear in Ezekiel's watchman oracles, where God appoints the prophet as a watchman (tsopheh) to warn Israel of coming judgment.
- **Eze 3:17** — "hear the word at my mouth, and give them warning from me"
- **Eze 3:18** — "thou givest him not warning, nor speakest to warn the wicked"
- **Eze 3:19** — "Yet if thou warn the wicked, and he turn not from his wickedness"
- **Eze 3:21** — "if thou warn the righteous man, that the righteous sin not"
- **Eze 33:3** — "he blow the trumpet, and warn the people"
- **Eze 33:7** — "thou shalt hear the word at my mouth, and warn them from me"
- **Eze 33:8** — "if thou dost not speak to warn the wicked"
- **Eze 33:9** — "if thou warn the wicked of his way to turn from it"

### 2. Receiving Warning — Taking Heed (Niphal)
The reflexive/passive stem conveys the hearer's responsibility to receive and act on a warning.
- **Eze 33:4** — "whosoever heareth the sound of the trumpet, and taketh not warning"
- **Eze 33:5** — "He heard the sound of the trumpet, and took not warning... But he that taketh warning shall deliver his soul"
- **Psalm 19:11** — "Moreover by them is thy servant warned: and in keeping of them there is great reward"
- **Ecc 4:13** — "a poor and a wise child than an old and foolish king, who will no more be admonished"
- **Ecc 12:12** — "by these, my son, be admonished"

### 3. Judicial/Community Warning
Official warning given in civic or legal contexts to prevent bloodguilt or legal error.
- **Exo 18:20** — "thou shalt teach them ordinances and laws, and shalt shew them the way"
- **2 Chr 19:10** — "ye shall even warn them that they trespass not against the LORD"
- **2 Ki 6:10** — "the man of God told him and warned him of, and saved himself there"

### 4. Shining / Radiance (Unique — Dan 12:3)
The sole occurrence preserving the literal root sense "to gleam." The Hiphil here means "give light, radiate brilliance" rather than "give warning."
- **Dan 12:3** — "they that be wise shall shine as the brightness of the firmament"

**Note:** The lexicon defines the root meaning as "to gleam," with the warning sense treated as figurative ("enlighten by caution"). Dan 12:3 is the only verse preserving the literal luminous sense. The cognate noun H2096 זֹהַר (zohar, "brightness") appears in Eze 8:2 and Dan 12:3 confirming the radiance root.
