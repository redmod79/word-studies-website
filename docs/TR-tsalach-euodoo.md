**Semantic continuity and shift:**
- **Continuity:** Both verbs center on "journey/way going well." Hebrew *tsâlach* pushes forward; Greek *euodoō* (eu + hodos) literally "travels a good road." The metaphor is preserved.
- **Shift:** The LXX softens the Qal's raw force of "rushing upon" (Spirit upon Samson/Saul/David). Greek translators typically render those Qal idioms with other verbs (ἅλλομαι "leap," ἐφάλλομαι), reserving εὐοδόω for the "prosper" senses. Result: εὐοδόω inherits tsâlach's prospering register but not its Spirit-rushing register.

## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same across testaments:**
- Core "journey/way-going-well" metaphor (tsâlach "push forward" → euodoō "travel good road").
- Divine agency: the subject causing prosperity is normally God (Gen 24; 39; 2 Chr; 3 John 1:2).
- Conditional / bounded character: prosperity is granted, not intrinsic.

**What shifts:**
- **Frequency collapse:** 64 OT uses → 3 NT uses. The NT does not carry forward the large prophetic-judgment idiom ("shall NOT prosper") that dominates Jeremiah and Ezekiel.
- **Charismatic "rushing" sense is lost.** The Qal "Spirit of the LORD came mightily upon" is not rendered by euodoō and is not picked up by the NT through this word at all. NT Spirit-empowerment language migrates to different verbs (πίμπλημι, ἐπέρχομαι, etc.).
- **Daniel's ironic horn-prospers usage is not echoed in NT εὐοδόω.** Paul/John use euodoō only in pastoral/blessing registers. The "beast-prospers" theme in Revelation uses different vocabulary (νικάω "overcome," ἐξουσία "authority").
- **Narrowing to personal blessing.** OT tsâlach spans nations, kings, armies, crops, journeys, missions. NT εὐοδόω narrows to individual travel, giving, and well-being.
