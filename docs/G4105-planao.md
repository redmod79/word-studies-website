## Lexicon Definition

From the verbal base meaning to "roam" or "wander." Properly, **to cause to roam from safety, truth, or virtue**; passively, **to go astray, wander, be led into error**. Extends from the physical image of straying sheep (Mat 18:12; 1 Pet 2:25) to the figurative sense of deception — being led out of the truth — which dominates NT usage. Frequently middle/passive: "be deceived, err, go astray."
