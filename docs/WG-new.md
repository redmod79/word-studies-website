# WG: New (Newness Concept)

## Overview

The NT concept of "new" involves a small cluster of words centered on the distinction between **qualitative newness** (καινός) and **temporal newness** (νέος), their abstract noun and antonym, and the renewal verbs behind καινός.

### Key Contrast
Both καινός and νέος translate the Hebrew H2319 châdâsh in the OT and are both translated "new" in English. The distinction:
- **καινός** — new in *kind*, fresh in *quality*; the word used for the new covenant (Heb 8:8), new creation (2Co 5:17), new Jerusalem (Rev 21:2), new song (Rev 5:9)
- **νέος** — new in *time* or *age*; the word used for young people (1Ti 5:1), new (recently made) wine alongside old wineskins, the renewed/young mind (Eph 4:23 νεόω)

## Roles in the Concept

- **Qualitative newness:** καινός (G2537) — the theologically dominant term; marks God's eschatological acts as unprecedented, not just recent
- **Temporal newness:** νέος (G3501) — appears in ordinary contexts; also in wineskins parable; overlaps with καινός in some passages
- **Abstract quality:** καινότης (G2538) — "newness" as a state; Paul uses it for the new life of the believer (Rom 6:4, 7:6)
- **Contrast / Antonym:** παλαιός (G3820) — appears in the wineskins parable, Heb 8:13 (Mosaic covenant declared old/obsolete), and leaven of malice (1Co 5:8)

## Key Passages Where Multiple Group Words Appear Together

- **Mat 9:17 / Mar 2:22** — [G2537 + G3501 + G3820] "new (νέος) wine into old (παλαιός) bottles... new (καινός) bottles" — all three appear in the same passage, showing the interplay between the terms
- **Heb 8:13** — [G2537 + G3820] "In that he saith, A new (καινός) covenant, he hath made the first old (παλαιός). Now that which decayeth and waxeth old (παλαιόω) is ready to vanish away"
