## Lexicon Definition

A primitive root; to fold, i.e. duplicate (literally or figuratively); by implication, to transmute (transitive or intransitive): — do (speak, strike) again, alter, double, (be given to) change, disguise, (be) diverse, pervert, prefer, repeat, return, do the second time. *(Strong's)*

The semantic core is **"fold / double."** Two branches radiate out:
1. **Iterative** — "do a second time, repeat, double" (fold the same thing again).
2. **Mutative** — "alter, change, make different, disguise, pervert" (fold into a different shape).

The same verb therefore covers both "doing again" (repetition of the same) and "making different" (transformation into another). Context and verb stem determine which branch is in view.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Divine Constancy — "I do not change / I will not alter"
- **Mal 3:6** — "For I am the LORD, I change not (לֹא שָׁנִיתִי, Qal perfect 1s); therefore ye sons of Jacob are not consumed."
- **Psalm 89:34 (KJV; MT 89:35)** — "My covenant will I not break, nor alter (לֹא אֲשַׁנֶּה, Piel imperfect 1s) the thing that is gone out of my lips."

### 2. Human Instability — people who *do* change (negatively)
- **Prov 24:21** — "meddle not with them that are given to change (שׁוֹנִים)."
- **Jer 2:36** — "Why gaddest thou about so much to change (לְשַׁנּוֹת, Piel inf.) thy way?"
- **Job 14:20** — "thou changest (מְשַׁנֶּה, Piel ptcp.) his countenance, and sendest him away" — death as God-wrought alteration of the human face.

### 3. Doing Again / Repeating
- **Gen 41:32** — "the dream was doubled (הִשָּׁנוֹת, Niphal inf.) unto Pharaoh twice" — doubled = confirmed.
- **1 Ki 18:34** — "Do it the second time (שְׁנוּ). And they did it the second time (וַיִּשְׁנוּ)" — Elijah at Carmel.
- **1 Sam 26:8** — "let me smite him… and I will not smite him the second time (לֹא אֶשְׁנֶה)."
- **Prov 17:9** — "he that repeateth (שֹׁנֶה) a matter separateth very friends."

### 4. Disguising / Altering Appearance
- **1 Sam 21:13** — "he changed (וַיְשַׁנּוֹ, Piel) his behaviour before them" — David feigning madness before Achish.
- **1 Ki 14:2** — "disguise thyself (הִשְׁתַּנִּית, Hithpael) that thou be not known" — Jeroboam's wife.
- **Jer 52:33** — "[Jehoiachin] changed (שִׁנָּה) his prison garments."

### 5. Being Diverse / Differentiated from Others
- **Est 1:7** — "the vessels being diverse (שׁוֹנִים) one from another."
- **Est 3:8** — Haman: "their laws are diverse (שֹׁנוֹת) from all people."

### 6. Perverting — Moral Alteration
- **Prov 31:5** — "lest they drink, and forget the law, and pervert (יְשַׁנֶּה) the judgment of any of the afflicted."
- **Est 2:9** — "he speedily… preferred (וַיְשַׁנֶּהָ, Piel) her and her maidens" — altered/advanced her status.

### 7. Doing Violence by Repetition
- **2 Sam 20:10** — "Joab struck him not again (לֹא שָׁנָה), for he died" — a second blow that was unneeded.
- **Neh 13:21** — "if ye do so again (תִּשְׁנוּ), I will lay hands on you."
- **Prov 26:11** — "as a dog returneth (שָׁב, different root) to his vomit, so a fool returneth (שׁוֹנֶה) to his folly" — repeating folly.
