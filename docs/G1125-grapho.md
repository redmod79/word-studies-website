## Semantic Categories

### 1. Authoritative Appeal to Scripture (γέγραπται)
The dominant use: quoting OT text with the perfect passive "it is (and stands) written." The perfect tense stresses the permanent, binding force of the written word.
- **Mat 4:4** — "It is written, Man shall not live by bread alone, but by every word of God"
- **Mat 4:7** — "It is written again, Thou shalt not tempt the Lord thy God"
- **Rom 1:17** — "as it is written, The just shall live by faith"
- **1Co 1:19** — "For it is written, I will destroy the wisdom of the wise"

### 2. Apostolic Correspondence
The present/aorist active: Paul, John, and Peter writing letters to specific communities, explaining purpose, content, and tone.
- **1Jo 1:4** — "these things write we unto you, that your joy may be full"
- **1Jo 2:1** — "My little children, these things write I unto you, that ye sin not"
- **2Co 2:4** — "out of much affliction and anguish of heart I wrote unto you with many tears"
- **2Pe 3:1** — "This second epistle, beloved, I now write unto you; in both which I stir up your pure minds"

### 3. Command to Record Revelation ("Write!")
The imperative γράψον, overwhelmingly in Revelation. The exalted Christ and heavenly voices command inscription of visions and beatitudes.
- **Rev 1:11** — "What thou seest, write in a book, and send it unto the seven churches"
- **Rev 1:19** — "Write the things which thou hast seen, and the things which are, and the things which shall be hereafter"
- **Rev 2:1** — "Unto the angel of the church of Ephesus write; These things saith he that holdeth the seven stars"
- **Rev 14:13** — "Write, Blessed are the dead which die in the Lord from henceforth"

### 4. The Prophets Wrote / Moses Wrote
Historical reference to OT authors as writers — the scriptures attributed to their literary activity.
- **Jhn 1:45** — "We have found him, of whom Moses in the law, and the prophets, did write"
- **Jhn 5:46** — "had ye believed Moses, ye would have believed me: for he wrote of me"
- **Luk 24:44** — "all things must be fulfilled, which were written in the law of Moses, and in the prophets, and in the psalms"
- **Acts 24:14** — "believing all things which are written in the law and in the prophets"

### 5. Written Things That Must Be Fulfilled
γεγραμμένα as a category of predictive writing requiring eschatological completion.
- **Luk 18:31** — "all things that are written by the prophets concerning the Son of man shall be accomplished"
- **Luk 21:22** — "these be the days of vengeance, that all things which are written may be fulfilled"
- **Acts 13:29** — "when they had fulfilled all that was written of him, they took him down from the tree"
- **1Co 10:11** — "they are written for our admonition, upon whom the ends of the world are come"

### 6. Physical and Legal Inscription
Concrete writing on objects — title-boards, writing tablets, ground, bills of divorce, personal notes.
- **Mat 27:37** — "set up over his head his accusation written, THIS IS JESUS THE KING OF THE JEWS"
- **Jhn 19:19–22** — "Pilate wrote a title… What I have written I have written"
- **Luk 1:63** — "he asked for a writing table, and wrote, saying, His name is John"
- **Luk 16:6–7** — "Take thy bill… write fifty" (a commercial document)

### 7. Names Written in the Book of Life
Eschatological registration: γράφω as the divine act of recording or not recording names in the heavenly registry.
- **Rev 13:8** — "all that dwell upon the earth shall worship him, whose names are not written in the book of life of the Lamb"
- **Rev 20:15** — "whosoever was not found written in the book of life was cast into the lake of fire"
- **Rev 21:27** — "they which are written in the Lamb's book of life"
- **Luk 10:20** — "rejoice, because your names are written in heaven"
