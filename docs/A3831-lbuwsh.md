## Semantic Categories

### 1. Physical Garments Worn by Humans
- **Dan 3:21** — "Then these men were bound in their coats, their hosen, and their hats, and their [other] **garments** (lebusheyahon), and were cast into the midst of the burning fiery furnace."

Here lebush appears as the plural suffix form, listing the general clothing worn by Shadrach, Meshach, and Abednego when thrown into the furnace. It is one item in a list of four garment types (sarbal "coats/mantles," patiysh "hosen," karbela "hats/caps," and lebush "other garments"), indicating ordinary clothing as distinct from more specific attire.

### 2. Divine Garment — Theophanic Appearance
- **Dan 7:9** — "I beheld till the thrones were cast down, and the Ancient of days did sit, whose **garment** (lebusheh) was white as snow, and the hair of his head like the pure wool."

Here lebush appears as a 3ms suffix form describing the clothing of the Ancient of Days in Daniel's vision. The garment is qualified by chivvar (H2358, Aramaic "white") and compared to snow (telag, H8517). This is the only Aramaic theophanic clothing description in the OT.
