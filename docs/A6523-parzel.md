## Semantic Categories

### 1. The Iron Legs — Fourth Kingdom's Crushing Strength
The dominant use: iron symbolizes the fourth world empire (legs of the statue in Nebuchadnezzar's dream), characterized by its crushing, subduing power.
- **Dan 2:33** — "His legs of iron, his feet part of iron and part of clay."
- **Dan 2:40** — "the fourth kingdom shall be strong as iron: forasmuch as iron breaketh in pieces and subdueth all things: and as iron that breaketh all these, shall it break in pieces and bruise."

### 2. Iron and Clay — Divided Vulnerability
Iron mixed with potter's clay represents a kingdom that is partly strong and partly brittle — unable to adhere into a unified whole.
- **Dan 2:41** — "thou sawest the feet and toes, part of potters' clay, and part of iron, the kingdom shall be divided"
- **Dan 2:42** — "as the toes of the feet were part of iron, and part of clay, so the kingdom shall be partly strong, and partly broken"
- **Dan 2:43** — "they shall mingle themselves with the seed of men: but they shall not cleave one to another, even as iron is not mixed with clay"

### 3. Destroyed Together with All Metals by the Stone
All materials of the image — including iron — are shattered simultaneously by the stone cut without hands.
- **Dan 2:35** — "the iron, the clay, the brass, the silver, and the gold, broken to pieces together, and became like the chaff"
- **Dan 2:45** — "it brake in pieces the iron, the brass, the clay, the silver, and the gold"

### 4. Iron Band on the Tree Stump
A distinct symbolic use: a band of iron and brass restrains the stump of the tree in Nebuchadnezzar's personal humbling vision.
- **Dan 4:15** — "leave the stump of his roots in the earth, even with a band of iron and brass, in the tender grass of the field"
- **Dan 4:23** — same binding imagery, until seven times pass over him

### 5. Metals of Pagan Worship and Temple Vessels
Iron appears in a list of materials from which idols are made, or vessels used for profane worship by Belshazzar.
- **Dan 5:4** — "praised the gods of gold, and of silver, of brass, of iron, of wood, and of stone"
- **Dan 5:23** — "vessels of his house before thee... praised the gods of silver, and gold, of brass, iron, wood, and stone"

### 6. The Iron-Toothed Fourth Beast
In Daniel's vision of the four beasts, the fourth beast has iron teeth — a parallel to the statue's iron legs, reinforcing the fourth-kingdom symbol.
- **Dan 7:7** — "exceedingly strong; and it had great iron teeth: it devoured and brake in pieces, and stamped the residue with the feet of it"
- **Dan 7:19** — "exceeding dreadful, whose teeth were of iron, and his nails of brass"
