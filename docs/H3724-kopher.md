## Lexicon Definition

Properly, a cover, i.e. (literally) a village (as covered in); (specifically) bitumen (as used for coating), and the henna plant (as used for dyeing); figuratively, a redemption-price: — bribe, camphire, pitch, ransom, satisfaction, sum of money, village.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Ransom Price for Life / Soul
The dominant usage — a price paid to redeem a life from death or liability.
- **Exo 30:12** — "then shall they give every man a ransom for his soul unto the LORD, when thou numberest them; that there be no plague among them"
- **Exo 21:30** — "If there be laid on him a sum of money, then he shall give for the ransom of his life whatsoever is laid upon him"
- **Job 33:24** — "Deliver him from going down to the pit: I have found a ransom"
- **Pro 13:8** — "The ransom of a man's life are his riches: but the poor heareth not rebuke"

### 2. Ransom Prohibited / Insufficient
Contexts where kopher is explicitly refused or declared inadequate.
- **Num 35:31** — "ye shall take no satisfaction for the life of a murderer, which is guilty of death: but he shall be surely put to death"
- **Num 35:32** — "ye shall take no satisfaction for him that is fled to the city of his refuge"
- **Psa 49:7** — "None of them can by any means redeem his brother, nor give to God a ransom for him"
- **Pro 6:35** — "He will not regard any ransom; neither will he rest content, though thou givest many gifts"

### 3. Substitutionary Ransom
A person or nation given in place of another.
- **Isa 43:3** — "I gave Egypt for thy ransom, Ethiopia and Seba for thee"
- **Pro 21:18** — "The wicked shall be a ransom for the righteous, and the transgressor for the upright"
- **Job 36:18** — "then a great ransom cannot deliver thee"

### 4. Bribe (Corrupt Use of Ransom Concept)
The ransom-price concept applied illegitimately — a payment to pervert justice.
- **1 Sam 12:3** — "of whose hand have I received any bribe to blind mine eyes therewith?"
- **Amo 5:12** — "they afflict the just, they take a bribe, and they turn aside the poor in the gate"

### 5. Covering Material (Pitch/Bitumen)
The literal "covering" sense — a physical coating substance.
- **Gen 6:14** — "shalt pitch it within and without with pitch"

### 6. Henna Plant (Camphire)
Botanical usage — the henna plant, whose flowers provide a covering dye.
- **Song 1:14** — "My beloved is unto me as a cluster of camphire in the vineyards of Engedi"
- **Song 4:13** — "Thy plants are an orchard of pomegranates, with pleasant fruits; camphire, with spikenard"
