## Semantic Categories

### 1. Judicial / Legal Witness
Two or three witnesses required to establish a legal case — the OT evidentiary law (Deut 17:6; 19:15) applied in NT practice.

- **Mat 18:16** — "take with thee one or two more, that in the mouth of two or three witnesses every word may be established"
- **Mat 26:65** — "What further need have we of witnesses? behold, now ye have heard his blasphemy"
- **2 Cor 13:1** — "In the mouth of two or three witnesses shall every word be established"
- **Heb 10:28** — "He that despised Moses' law died without mercy under two or three witnesses"

### 2. Eyewitness of Christ's Ministry and Resurrection
Apostolic testimony based on direct historical experience — the qualifying criterion for the Twelve (Acts 1:22).

- **Acts 1:22** — "must one be ordained to be a witness with us of his resurrection"
- **Acts 2:32** — "This Jesus hath God raised up, whereof we all are witnesses"
- **Acts 3:15** — "whom God hath raised from the dead; whereof we are witnesses"
- **Acts 10:39** — "we are witnesses of all things which he did both in the land of the Jews, and in Jerusalem"

### 3. Apostolic Commission to Bear Witness
Being appointed as μάρτυς is a divine commission, not merely a personal attestation.

- **Acts 1:8** — "ye shall be witnesses unto me both in Jerusalem, and in all Judaea, and in Samaria, and unto the uttermost part of the earth"
- **Acts 22:15** — "thou shalt be his witness unto all men of what thou hast seen and heard"
- **Acts 26:16** — "to make thee a minister and a witness both of these things which thou hast seen"
- **1 Pet 5:1** — "a witness of the sufferings of Christ, and also a partaker of the glory that shall be revealed"

### 4. God / Christ as Witness
Used for divine attestation — calling God or Christ as the ultimate truthful witness.

- **Rom 1:9** — "God is my witness, whom I serve with my spirit in the gospel of his Son"
- **1 Thess 2:5** — "God is witness: nor a cloke of covetousness"
- **Rev 1:5** — "from Jesus Christ, who is the faithful witness, and the first begotten of the dead"
- **Rev 3:14** — "These things saith the Amen, the faithful and true witness, the beginning of the creation of God"

### 5. Martyr — Witness Who Dies for Testimony
The semantic extension where bearing witness leads to death — the technical "martyr" sense.

- **Acts 22:20** — "when the blood of thy martyr Stephen was shed, I also was standing by"
- **Rev 2:13** — "thou holdest fast my name, and hast not denied my faith, even in those days wherein Antipas was my faithful martyr, who was slain"
- **Rev 17:6** — "the woman drunken with the blood of the saints, and with the blood of the martyrs of Jesus"

### 6. Cloud of Witnesses — Exemplars from History
Heb 12:1 applies the word to OT saints whose faithful lives stand as an encouraging gallery of testimony.

- **Heb 12:1** — "Wherefore seeing we also are compassed about with so great a cloud of witnesses, let us lay aside every weight"
