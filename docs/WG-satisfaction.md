# WG: Satisfaction / Fullness

## Overview
The Hebrew satisfaction concept centers on the root שׂבע and its cognates, supplemented by related verbs for filling (male) and eating (akal). These words work together in a recurring biblical pattern: eat → be satisfied → bless God (or forget God).

## Roles in the Concept

- **Prerequisite:** akal (אָכַל) — the eating that precedes satisfaction
- **Core verb:** saba (שָׂבַע) — the experience of being satisfied/sated
- **State adjective:** sabea (שָׂבֵעַ) — the resulting condition ("full of days," "sated")
- **Abstract noun:** soba (שֹׂבַע) — satisfaction as a concept ("bread to the full")
- **Spatial filling:** male (מָלֵא) — filling a vessel/space (overlaps but distinct from appetite-satisfaction)
- **Liquid satiation:** ravah (רָוָה) — drenching, watering, saturating (the liquid-domain parallel to saba)
