## Lexicon Definition

A fulness, i.e., a large number, throng, populace. From πλήθω (plēthō, "to fill"). Neuter collective noun denoting a mass or body of persons (or things) viewed as a unit. KJV renderings: multitude, bundle, company.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Corporate Body of Believers
The gathered community of faith viewed as a single unit. Often takes collective action (believing, agreeing, choosing).

- **Acts 4:32** — "the multitude of them that believed were of one heart and of one soul"
- **Acts 6:2** — "the twelve called the multitude of the disciples unto them"
- **Acts 6:5** — "the saying pleased the whole multitude: and they chose Stephen"
- **Acts 15:12** — "all the multitude kept silence, and gave audience to Barnabas and Paul"

### 2. Crowd Following Jesus or the Apostles
A large group gathered around a central figure, typically in response to teaching or miracles.

- **Mark 3:7** — "a great multitude from Galilee followed him"
- **Luke 6:17** — "the company of his disciples, and a great multitude of people"
- **Luke 19:37** — "the whole multitude of the disciples began to rejoice and praise God"
- **Acts 5:16** — "there came also a multitude out of the cities round about unto Jerusalem"

### 3. Civic or General Populace
An assembled group of people in a public setting, not necessarily believers.

- **Acts 14:4** — "the multitude of the city was divided"
- **Acts 19:9** — "spake evil of that way before the multitude"
- **Acts 21:36** — "the multitude of the people followed after, crying, Away with him"
- **Acts 25:24** — "all the multitude of the Jews have dealt with me"

### 4. Large Quantity of Things (Non-Human)
Applied to animals, objects, or abstract quantities rather than people.

- **Luke 5:6** — "they inclosed a great multitude of fishes"
- **John 21:6** — "they were not able to draw it for the multitude of fishes"
- **Acts 28:3** — "Paul had gathered a bundle of sticks" (unique physical sense)
- **James 5:20** — "shall hide a multitude of sins"
- **1 Peter 4:8** — "charity shall cover the multitude of sins"

### 5. Heavenly / Liturgical Assembly
A gathered group in worship or supernatural context.

- **Luke 1:10** — "the whole multitude of the people were praying without at the time of incense"
- **Luke 2:13** — "a multitude of the heavenly host praising God"

### 6. Converts / New Believers as a Group
People who come to faith described as a collective body.

- **Acts 5:14** — "believers were the more added to the Lord, multitudes both of men and women"
- **Acts 14:1** — "a great multitude both of the Jews and also of the Greeks believed"
- **Acts 17:4** — "of the devout Greeks a great multitude, and of the chief women not a few"
