## Lexicon Definition

From ὑπό and ἀκούω; to hear under (as a subordinate), i.e. to listen attentively; by implication, to heed or conform to a command or authority:—hearken, be obedient to, obey.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Cosmic/Natural Obedience to Christ's Authority
- **Matt 8:27** — "even the winds and the sea obey him!"
- **Mark 1:27** — "even the unclean spirits, and they do obey him"
- **Mark 4:41** — "even the wind and the sea obey him"
- **Luke 8:25** — "he commandeth even the winds and water, and they obey him"
- **Luke 17:6** — "ye might say unto this sycamine tree, Be thou plucked up... and it should obey you"

The elements and unclean spirits respond to Jesus' word — obedience here means yielding to an authority that is inherent, not negotiated.

### 2. Obedience to the Gospel / Faith
- **Acts 6:7** — "a great company of the priests were obedient to the faith"
- **Rom 10:16** — "they have not all obeyed the gospel"
- **2 Thess 1:8** — "vengeance on them... that obey not the gospel of our Lord Jesus Christ"
- **Heb 5:9** — "the author of eternal salvation unto all them that obey him"

Faith and obedience converge: to "obey the gospel" is to respond to God's word with the full-person hearing that the etymology demands. This is the "obedience of faith" (ὑπακοὴ πίστεως) of Rom 1:5 and 16:26, using the cognate noun G5218.

### 3. Obedience to Sin vs. Righteousness (Pauline Ethical Framework)
- **Rom 6:12** — "Let not sin therefore reign in your mortal body, that ye should obey it"
- **Rom 6:16** — "to whom ye yield yourselves servants to obey, his servants ye are... whether of sin unto death, or of obedience unto righteousness"
- **Rom 6:17** — "ye have obeyed from the heart that form of doctrine which was delivered you"

Paul frames the moral life as a choice of masters: every person obeys something. The verb's subordination nuance — "hearing under" — makes it the perfect word for servanthood.

### 4. Household Obedience (Haustafel)
- **Eph 6:1** — "Children, obey your parents in the Lord: for this is right"
- **Eph 6:5** — "Servants, be obedient to them that are your masters according to the flesh"
- **Col 3:20** — "Children, obey your parents in all things"
- **Col 3:22** — "Servants, obey in all things your masters according to the flesh"

The household codes use ὑπακούω specifically for children and servants — persons structurally "under" authority, matching the ὑπό prefix exactly.

### 5. Personal Obedience of Faith
- **Heb 11:8** — "By faith Abraham, when he was called to go out... obeyed; and he went out, not knowing whither he went"
- **Phil 2:12** — "as ye have always obeyed, not as in my presence only, but now much more in my absence, work out your own salvation"
- **1 Pet 3:6** — "Even as Sara obeyed Abraham, calling him lord"

Abraham's obedience in Heb 11:8 is the paradigm: faith and obedience are not separate acts but a single response — he heard under God's authority and moved.

### 6. Disobedience to Apostolic Word
- **2 Thess 3:14** — "if any man obey not our word by this epistle, note that man"

The negative use — refusing to heed apostolic instruction — carries the implication that apostolic teaching has the same authority-structure as the gospel itself.

### 7. Physical Hearing / Answering a Door
- **Acts 12:13** — "a damsel came to hearken, named Rhoda"

The sole non-metaphorical occurrence. The literal meaning surfaces: "to hear in response" — Rhoda came to listen at the gate and answer.
