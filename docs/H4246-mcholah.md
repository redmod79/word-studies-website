## Semantic Categories

### 1. Victory Celebration — Women Dancing After Battle
- **Exo 15:20** — "Miriam the prophetess, the sister of Aaron, took a timbrel in her hand; and all the women went out after her with timbrels and with dances"
- **1 Sam 18:6** — "the women came out of all cities of Israel, singing and dancing, to meet king Saul, with tabrets, with joy, and with instruments of musick"
- **1 Sam 21:11** — "did they not sing one to another of him in dances, saying, Saul hath slain his thousands, and David his ten thousands?"
- **1 Sam 29:5** — "Is not this David, of whom they sang one to another in dances, saying, Saul slew his thousands, and David his ten thousands?"

### 2. Religious or Communal Festival Dancing
- **Exo 32:19** — "as soon as he came nigh unto the camp, that he saw the calf, and the dancing: and Moses' anger waxed hot"
- **Jdg 11:34** — "his daughter came out to meet him with timbrels and with dances: and she was his only child"
- **Jdg 21:21** — "if the daughters of Shiloh come out to dance in dances, then come ye out of the vineyards"

### 3. Poetic Simile — Dance as Image of Beauty/Movement
- **Song 6:13** — "What will ye see in the Shulamite? As it were the company of two armies" (lit. "as the dance of Mahanaim")
