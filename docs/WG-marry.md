# WG: Marry

## Overview
The NT vocabulary of marriage and divorce clusters around a small set of words that together define the institution, its dissolution, and its eschatological significance. γαμέω (the act of marrying) is the verbal core; the group also includes the noun for the institution (γάμος), the relational partner (γυνή), the dissolution (ἀπολύω), the consequent moral violation (μοιχάω), and the unmarried state (ἄγαμος).

## Roles in the Concept

- **Action:** γαμέω (G1060) — the act of entering marriage
- **Institution:** γάμος (G1062) — the marriage relationship or wedding event itself
- **Partner:** γυνή (G1135) — the wife / woman as relational anchor
- **Negation:** ἄγαμος (G22) — the unmarried state (alpha-privative)
- **Dissolution:** ἀπολύω (G630) — divorce, the legal severance
- **Violation:** μοιχάω (G3429) — adultery, the moral consequence of wrongful remarriage

## Eschatological Dimension
The group has a vertical axis: in the resurrection (Matt 22:30; Mark 12:25; Luke 20:34–35), γαμέω and γαμίζω (give in marriage) are both absent — marriage is a present-age institution. γάμος reappears only in the eschatological register of Rev 19:7–9 as the "marriage of the Lamb," where it is thoroughly redefined.
