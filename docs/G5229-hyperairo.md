## Lexicon Definition

**Strong's / Thayer:** ὑπεραίρω — middle voice from ὑπέρ (G5228, "over, above") and αἴρω (G142, "to lift, raise, take up"); "to raise oneself over," i.e. (figuratively) **to become haughty**; — *"exalt self, be exalted above measure."*

**Semantic range:**
1. *Literal (not attested in NT):* to lift up over, raise above (active voice, classical usage).
2. *Middle, reflexive:* to lift oneself up, raise oneself over (others or a standard) — 2 Cor 12:7 renders this passively in KJV ("be exalted above measure"), but the Greek form ὑπεραίρωμαι is middle-sense: "that I might not exalt myself."
3. *Middle, with ἐπί + accusative:* to exalt oneself *against / over* (someone or something) — hostile self-elevation above a rival or authority (2 Thess 2:4).

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Self-Exaltation as Internal Pride (Danger to the Believer)
Paul's personal experience: the temptation for a recipient of great revelation to become inwardly proud. The "thorn" is given as preventive against this very verb.
- **2 Corinthians 12:7** — "And lest I should be *exalted above measure* through the abundance of the revelations, there was given to me a thorn in the flesh, the messenger of Satan to buffet me, lest I should be *exalted above measure*." *(Greek: ἵνα μὴ ὑπεραίρωμαι... ἵνα μὴ ὑπεραίρωμαι — present middle subjunctive, bracketing the verse by inclusio.)*

### 2. Self-Exaltation as Rebellious Usurpation (Hostility Against God)
A participial description of a figure who actively lifts himself above every rival deity or object of worship. The preposition ἐπί + accusative marks the object over which the subject exalts himself.
- **2 Thessalonians 2:4** — "Who opposeth and *exalteth himself* above all that is called God, or that is worshipped; so that he as God sitteth in the temple of God, shewing himself that he is God." *(Greek: ὁ ἀντικείμενος καὶ ὑπεραιρόμενος ἐπὶ πάντα λεγόμενον θεὸν ἢ σέβασμα — present middle participle.)*

**Key lexical kin for 2 Thess 2:4:** though ὑπεραίρω itself is not used in Dan 11:36, Theodotion renders the Hebrew ἁ וְיִתְרוֹמֵם וְיִתְגַּדֵּל עַל־כָּל־אֵל with the synonymous pair **ὑψωθήσεται καὶ μεγαλυνθήσεται ἐπὶ πάντα θεόν** — "he shall be exalted and magnified above every god." Paul's phrase ὑπεραιρόμενος ἐπὶ πάντα λεγόμενον θεόν in 2 Thess 2:4 reproduces (a) the verbal idea of exalting oneself, (b) the preposition ἐπί + accusative, and (c) the object πάντα θεόν. The underlying Hebrew verbs are **H7311 רוּם (rûm)** "be high, lifted up" and its hithpael "exalt oneself," and **H1431 גָּדַל (gādal)** "be great, magnify oneself." These are the standard OT verbs of arrogant self-exaltation against God (cf. Isa 10:15; Isa 14:13–14; Ezek 28:2; Dan 8:11, 25; 11:36–37).
