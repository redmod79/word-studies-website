## Semantic Categories

### 1. The Artisan: Goldsmith / Founder (literal metalworker)
The Qal participle used as a professional title — one who smelts and shapes metal.

- **Judg 17:4** — "restored the money... gave them to the founder (tsaraph), who made thereof a graven image"
- **Neh 3:8** — "Uzziel the son of Harhaiah, of the goldsmiths (tsaraph)"
- **Isa 40:19** — "the goldsmith (tsaraph) spreadeth it over with gold"
- **Jer 10:14** — "every founder (tsaraph) is confounded by the graven image"

### 2. Metallurgical Smelting / Refining
The literal process: applying intense heat to separate pure metal from dross or to shape raw ore.

- **Pro 25:4** — "Take away the dross from the silver, and there shall come forth a vessel for the finer (tsaraph)"
- **Isa 1:25** — "I will purely purge away thy dross, and take away all thy tin"
- **Jer 6:29** — "the bellows are burned... the founder (tsaraph) melteth in vain: for the wicked are not plucked away"
- **Zech 13:9** — "I will bring the third part through the fire, and will refine (tsaraph) them as silver is refined (tsaraph)"

### 3. God Testing / Refining His People
The metaphorical application: God as the divine refiner subjecting individuals or the nation to trial in order to prove or purify them.

- **Judg 7:4** — "the LORD said... I will try (tsaraph) them for thee there: and it shall be, that of whom I say... This shall go with thee"
- **Isa 48:10** — "I have refined thee (tsaraph), but not with silver; I have chosen thee in the furnace of affliction"
- **Psa 17:3** — "thou hast tried (tsaraph) me, and shalt find nothing; I am purposed that my mouth shall not transgress"
- **Psa 66:10** — "thou, O God, hast proved us: thou hast tried (tsaraph) us, as silver is tried (tsaraph)"
- **Mal 3:2–3** — "he is like a refiner's (tsaraph) fire... he shall sit as a refiner (tsaraph) and purifier of silver"

### 4. God's Word as Refined / Pure
The passive participle applied to divine speech: God's words have already passed the smelter's test and are free of impurity.

- **2 Sam 22:31 / Psa 18:30** — "the word of the LORD is tried (tsaraph): he is a buckler to all those that trust in him"
- **Psa 12:6** — "the words of the LORD are pure words: as silver tried (tsaraph) in a furnace of earth, purified seven times"
- **Psa 119:140** — "Thy word is very pure (tsaraph): therefore thy servant loveth it"
- **Pro 30:5** — "Every word of God is pure (tsaraph): he is a shield unto them that put their trust in him"

### 5. Eschatological Refining
End-time purification of a remnant through trial — fire as judgment that separates the faithful from the wicked.

- **Dan 11:35** — "some of them of understanding shall fall, to try (tsaraph) them, and to purge, and to make them white, even to the time of the end"
- **Dan 12:10** — "many shall be purified, and made white, and tried (tsaraph Niphal); but the wicked shall do wickedly"
- **Mal 3:3** — "he shall purify the sons of Levi, and purge them as gold and silver"
