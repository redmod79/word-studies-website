# WG: Testimony / Witness

## Overview
The testimony/witness concept in the Greek NT involves 6 words from the μαρτυρ- root working together: a noun for the person (μάρτυς), a verb for the act (μαρτυρέω), two nouns for the content (μαρτυρία and μαρτύριον), and two negatives for false witness (ψευδομαρτυρέω and ψευδομαρτυρία). The shared Hebrew source is H5707 עֵד (ʻêd, "witness").

## Relationships

### Shared Root
All six words derive from the μαρτυρ- stem (the μάρτυς/witness root). ψευδο- compounds add the "false" negation prefix. The Hebrew semantic source is H5707 עֵד (ʻêd), which carries into G3144 μάρτυς most directly (co-occ=38, PMI=30.47) and into G3140 μαρτυρέω (co-occ=5). G3142 μαρτύριον also maps strongly to H5715 עֵדוּת (ʻêdûwth, the covenant-document testimony, co-occ=53, PMI=6.50) — the OT cultic sense of testimony as artifact/document is carried specifically by this pairing.

### Key Distinctions Within the Group
- **μάρτυς (G3144)** — the witness as *person*; later the technical term for Christian martyr
- **μαρτυρέω (G3140)** — the *verbal act* of bearing witness
- **μαρτυρία (G3141)** — the *process or occasion* of giving testimony
- **μαρτύριον (G3142)** — the *content* or tangible evidence; also used for the "tabernacle of testimony" (OT)
- **ψευδομαρτυρέω / ψευδομαρτυρία** — false/perjured testimony (appears primarily in Synoptic trial scenes and quotations of the Decalogue)

## Roles in the Concept

- **Person:** μάρτυς (μάρτυς) — who bears the testimony
- **Action:** μαρτυρέω — the act of testifying
- **Process/occasion:** μαρτυρία — the testimony as event or transaction
- **Evidence/content:** μαρτύριον — the testimony as thing or proof
- **Negation:** ψευδομαρτυρέω / ψευδομαρτυρία — false witness (Decalogue prohibition)

## Key Passages Where Multiple Group Words Appear Together

- **Mark 14:55–56** — μαρτυρία + ψευδομαρτυρέω: trial of Jesus; false testimony sought; council cannot agree
- **John 1:7** — μαρτυρία + μαρτυρέω: "came for a witness, to bear witness of the Light"
- **John 5:31–32** — μαρτυρία + μαρτυρέω: "if I bear witness… there is another that beareth witness"
- **Rev 12:11** — μαρτυρία: "word of their testimony; loved not their lives unto the death" (martyr frame)
