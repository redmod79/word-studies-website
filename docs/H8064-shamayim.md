## Lexicon Definition

Dual of an unused singular שָׁמֶה (shâmeh); from an unused root meaning *to be lofty*; the sky (as aloft; the dual perhaps alluding to the visible arch in which the clouds move, as well as to the higher ether where the celestial bodies revolve):—air, astrologer, heaven(-s). The dual form persists even when context is singular ("the sky"), a grammatical fossil characteristic of paired / expansive entities in Hebrew (cf. מַיִם *mayim* "waters," פָּנִים *pânîm* "face").

## Semantic Categories

Derived from scanning all 395 verses. The three usages flagged by the user (atmospheric sky, celestial realm, divine dwelling) are all richly attested, plus several extensions.

### 1. Atmospheric Sky / Weather Dome
The visible dome from which rain, dew, hail, thunder, and wind come; the space birds traverse.
- **Gen 1:8** — "God called the firmament Heaven." (rāqîaʿ identified *as* shâmayim)
- **Gen 7:11** — "the windows of heaven were opened" (flood rain)
- **Deut 11:11** — "drinketh water of the rain of heaven"
- **Gen 1:26 / 2:19** — "fowl of the air (shâmayim)" — standard idiom for birds
- **Hag 1:10** — "heaven over you is stayed from dew"
- **1 Kgs 18:45** — "heaven was black with clouds and wind"

### 2. Celestial Realm (Sun, Moon, Stars, "Host of Heaven")
The higher region of sun, moon, stars, and constellations; seat of the *ts'bāʾ hashshâmayim* ("host of heaven").
- **Gen 1:14–17** — luminaries set "in the firmament of the heaven"
- **Gen 15:5 / 22:17** — "stars of heaven" as innumerable seed-promise
- **Deut 4:19** — "the sun, and the moon, and the stars, even all the host of heaven"
- **Judg 5:20** — "They fought from heaven; the stars in their courses fought against Sisera"
- **Isa 13:10** — "stars of heaven and the constellations thereof shall not give their light"
- **Dan 8:10** — "it waxed great, even to the host of heaven; and it cast down some of the host and of the stars"
- **Jer 8:2** — "the sun, and the moon, and all the host of heaven, whom they have loved... worshipped" (apostate astral cult)
- **2 Kgs 17:16; 21:3, 5; Zeph 1:5** — condemnation of "worship the host of heaven"

### 3. Divine Dwelling / Throne Realm

## Grammatical Notes

- **Dual form:** שָׁמַיִם ends in the dual suffix *-ayim*. No singular *shâmeh* is attested in biblical Hebrew. The dual is morphologically frozen — *shâmayim* always agrees with plural/dual verbs and adjectives regardless of whether context is "sky" (singular idea) or "heavens" (plural idea). Ancient grammarians linked the dual to a two-layered cosmology (lower cloud-sky + upper stellar ether), but in practice the form functions as a plurale tantum.
- **Always determinate in theological phrases:** "*the* heavens" (*ha-shâmayim*) is the default; anarthrous forms cluster in poetic and construct phrases ("stars of heaven").
- **Construct chains:** *tsᵉbāʾ hashshâmayim* ("host of heaven"), *rūaḥ hashshâmayim* ("wind/spirit of heaven"), *ʿôph hashshâmayim* ("fowl of heaven"), *ʾĕlōhê hashshâmayim* ("God of heaven") — highly conventionalized.
- **"Four winds of the heavens"** (*ʾarbaʿ rūḥôt hashshâmayim*, Dan 8:8; Zech 2:6; 6:5; Jer 49:36) — idiom for the four cardinal directions / totality of the earth's compass, not literally winds in the atmosphere.
