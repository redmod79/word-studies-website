# Niphal Imperfect + לֹא: The Formula of Divine Denial of "Repenting" (Hebrew)

## Definition
A recurring formulaic construction in which YHWH (or a speaker appealing to YHWH's character) denies the action of נחם ("repent, be sorry, change one's mind") by negating a prefix-conjugation form of the verb with לֹא. The shape is:

> **לֹא** + *prefix-conjugation of* **נחם** *(Niphal, or functionally-equivalent Hithpael)*

Because נחם in the Niphal/Hithpael is a tolerative/middle verb ("allow oneself to be moved to regret / reverse course"), the denial `לֹא + yiqtol` is not a single future refusal but a categorical, characterizing negation: God does not (and will not) submit himself to that kind of reversal.

1. **Categorical/gnomic denial** — negation of a characteristic action, a statement of divine nature (Num 23:19; 1 Sam 15:29).
2. **Oath-bound irrevocability** — denial anchoring a sworn decree as unalterable (Ps 110:4).
3. **Judicial finality** — denial sealing a pronounced judgment; YHWH will not relent (Jer 4:28; Ezek 24:14; Zech 8:14, perfect variant).

## Form Recognition
The construction pairs three morpho-syntactic features:

- **Negative particle** לֹא — objective, unconditional negation (GKC §152).
- **Prefix conjugation (Impf. / yiqtol) of נחם** — non-perfective aspect; with לֹא expresses a general, non-time-bound denial rather than a specific past event.
- **Stem = Niphal** (or **Hithpael** in Num 23:19; Deut 32:36; Ps 135:14) — both stems carry the tolerative/middle sense "let oneself be moved to regret/relent."
- **Parser codes:** `sp=verb vs=nif vt=impf lex=נחם` (Niphal); `sp=verb vs=hit vt=impf lex=נחם` (Hithpael).

A closely related variant substitutes a 1cs Niphal **perfect** (`נִחַמְתִּי`) with לֹא when YHWH speaks in the first person of an already-uttered decree (Jer 4:28; Zech 8:14). The construction is formulaic in either prefix or suffix form — the *negation of נחם* is what defines the formula.

## Functions with Examples

### 1. Categorical / Gnomic Denial — "God is not the sort who…"
The לֹא + yiqtol shape expresses an atemporal truth about God's nature. Balaam's oracle and Samuel's verdict on Saul both generalize from divine essence (`לֹא אִישׁ אֵל` / `לֹא אָדָם הוּא`): because God is not man, the repenting proper to man is categorically denied him.

| Reference | Hebrew | Parsing | Notes |
|-----------|--------|---------|-------|
| Num 23:19 | וּבֶן־אָדָם וְיִתְנֶחָם | Hithpael.Impf.3ms | Paired with לֹא… וִיכַזֵּב; interrog. ה marks rhetorical denial |
| 1 Sam 15:29 | וְלֹא יִנָּחֵם | Niphal.Impf.3ms | Grounded by כִּי־clause: *not a man, that he should relent* |

Waltke-O'Connor (§23.4f) classes Niphal נחם among tolerative uses: "the subject allows an implicit or explicit agent to effect upon him the action." The negation therefore denies that YHWH will *permit* himself to be so moved.

### 2. Oath-Bound Irrevocability — sealing a sworn decree
When paired with שׁבע ("swear"), the formula seals an oath as unrescindable. The Niphal perfect נִשְׁבַּע sets the oath; the Niphal imperfect יִנָּחֵם with לֹא declares it will never be rolled back.

| Reference | Hebrew | Parsing | Notes |
|-----------|--------|---------|-------|
| Ps 110:4 | נִשְׁבַּע יְהוָה וְלֹא יִנָּחֵם | Niph.Perf. + Niph.Impf.3ms | Oath + denial of reversal; priesthood לְעוֹלָם |

### 3. Judicial Finality — judgment decreed, not recalled
In prophetic judgment speeches YHWH speaks in the 1cs, binding the announced verdict. Jer 4:28 and Ezek 24:14 stack negated 1cs verbs of reversal (שׁוב, פרע, חוס, נחם); Zech 8:14 retrospects the already-executed wrath of the exile as something YHWH "did not relent from."

## Why the Niphal/Hithpael Stem Matters
Neither stem depicts a Qal-style active "turn from a plan." Both are **tolerative/middle**: the subject *permits himself* to undergo an internal reversal. Denying these stems of YHWH denies not merely an external act but the *possibility of his being moved* to that reversal — a point Waltke-O'Connor (§23.4f) makes the defining feature of tolerative Niphals ("half-willing" allowance). לֹא therefore targets the susceptibility itself, not an episodic event.

The Qal of נחם does not occur; compassion/grief/relent meaning is carried by Niphal, Piel ("comfort"), Pual, and Hithpael. When the Bible negates *God's* נחם, it always uses the Niphal (or its Hithpael equivalent in Num 23:19; cf. Deut 32:36; Ps 135:14) — the very stems whose positive sense elsewhere says YHWH *did* relent (Exod 32:14; Jon 3:10; Amos 7:3, 6). The formula thus presupposes the Niphal's ordinary compatibility with YHWH as subject, and denies it precisely in contexts where a different theological stake is being made (covenant oath, final judgment, divine constancy).

## Aspect Note: Why לֹא + Yiqtol ≠ "will not" in a flat future sense
GKC §107 and Waltke-O'Connor §31.3 note that the non-perfective (imperfect) with לֹא expresses **objective, unconditional negation** and can denote habitual or characteristic non-occurrence. `לֹא יִנָּחֵם` is therefore better read as "he does not (is not the one to) relent" rather than a bare future "he will not relent at some moment." This matches the gnomic/characterizing force observed in Num 23:19 and 1 Sam 15:29, and the irrevocability sense in Ps 110:4.

## Contrast with Positive Niphal נחם of YHWH

| Shape | Function | Example |
|------|----------|---------|
| **Niphal wayq. of נחם (affirmative)** | Narrative: YHWH did relent | וַיִּנָּחֶם יְהוָה — Exod 32:14; Jon 3:10 |
| **Niphal perf. of נחם (affirmative, 1cs)** | Stated reversal | נִחַמְתִּי — Jer 18:8, 10; 26:3; 42:10 |
| **לֹא + Niphal impf. of נחם** | Categorical / oath / judicial denial | Num 23:19; 1 Sam 15:29; Ps 110:4; Ezek 24:14 |
| **לֹא + Niphal perf. 1cs of נחם** | Retrospective/performative denial | Jer 4:28; Zech 8:14 |

The same stem carries both poles. The distinctive theological-grammatical work of *this* formula is the **combination** of לֹא with the non-perfective (or 1cs perfect) of נחם in the mouth of (or about) YHWH — a register reserved for the unalterable.
