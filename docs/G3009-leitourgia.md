## Lexicon Definition

Public function (as priest ["liturgy"]) or benefaction. From G3008 leitourgeo. Denotes an official public service — originally a civic duty performed by wealthy citizens for the state (Greek cultural background), applied in biblical usage to priestly/Levitical service and to Christian ministry acts.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Priestly/Levitical Temple Service
- **Luke 1:23** — "as soon as the days of his ministration were accomplished, he departed to his own house"
- **Heb 9:21** — "he sprinkled with blood both the tabernacle, and all the vessels of the ministry"

Formal cultic service rendered by priests. Zacharias completing his priestly course (Luke) and the Mosaic tabernacle apparatus (Hebrews) both use leitourgia for the officially prescribed worship duties.

### 2. Christ's Superior Heavenly Ministry
- **Heb 8:6** — "now hath he obtained a more excellent ministry, by how much also he is the mediator of a better covenant, which was established upon better promises"

Applied to Christ's high-priestly ministry — a "more excellent" leitourgia contrasted with the earthly Levitical service.

### 3. Sacrificial Service of Faith
- **Phil 2:17** — "if I be offered upon the sacrifice and service of your faith, I joy, and rejoice with you all"
- **Phil 2:30** — "for the work of Christ he was nigh unto death, not regarding his life, to supply your lack of service toward me"

Paul uses leitourgia for acts of sacrificial devotion — both his own poured-out ministry and Epaphroditus's near-fatal service. The sacrificial overtone from the cultic register carries forward.

### 4. Charitable Service to the Saints
- **2 Cor 9:12** — "the administration of this service not only supplieth the want of the saints, but is abundant also by many thanksgivings unto God"

The financial collection for Jerusalem believers is called a leitourgia — material aid framed as a public/priestly act of worship.
