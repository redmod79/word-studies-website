## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same across testaments:**
- καιρός carries ʻêth's qualitative force: time as opportunity, appointment, and occasion — not mere duration
- The eschatological trajectory of ʻêth in Daniel ("time of the end") flows directly into NT kairos language (Mark 1:15; Rev 1:3)
- The "time of trouble" pattern (ʻêth + trouble in Jeremiah/Daniel) echoes in NT eschatological discourse (Mark 13; Rev)
- Wisdom about discerning the right moment (Eccl 3; Esth 4:14) continues in NT exhortations (Eph 5:16 "redeeming the time")

**What shifts:**
- In the OT, ʻêth frequently marks *past* historical moments ("at that time"). In the NT, καιρός predominantly looks *forward* to the climactic eschatological moment
- The Ecclesiastes pattern of ʻêth as cyclical seasons (birth/death, plant/harvest) largely recedes; NT kairos becomes more sharply eschatological and redemptive-historical
- The NT adds a Christological focus: the "fullness of time" (Gal 4:4; Eph 1:10) is the arrival of the person of Christ — something ʻêth only anticipates structurally
