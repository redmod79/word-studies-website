## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same across testaments:**
- The core metaphor of erasure — removing something written or recorded — persists from OT through NT. Both testaments use machah/exaleipho for blotting out sins (Psa 51 → Acts 3:19), blotting out names from God's book (Exo 32:33 → Rev 3:5), and wiping away tears (Isa 25:8 → Rev 21:4).
- God remains the primary agent of both erasure and comfort in both testaments.

**What shifts:**
- The NT concentrates the word more narrowly. Where machah covers 7 semantic categories across 32 OT verses, exaleipho appears only 5 times in the NT, focusing exclusively on sin-erasure (Acts, Colossians) and eschatological comfort (Revelation).
- The physical root sense of machah ("rub, smooth, make fat" — Isa 25:6 Pual) disappears entirely in the NT; exaleipho retains only the figurative "erase/obliterate" meaning.
- Colossians 2:14 introduces a new application: blotting out a legal document (cheirographon), extending the erasure metaphor to written ordinances nailed to the cross — an image without direct OT precedent, though it builds on the book/record tradition of Exo 32 and Psa 69.
