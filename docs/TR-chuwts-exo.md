## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same across testaments:**
- The core spatial-boundary concept transfers directly: a defined interior (camp, city, community) versus the space outside it
- The cultic "without the camp" formula passes intact from Leviticus through LXX into Hebrews 13
- The inside/outside binary as a marker of belonging versus exclusion

**What shifts:**
- NT spiritualizes the boundary: "those without" (1 Cor 5:12, Col 4:5, 1 Thess 4:12) means non-believers, not spatial outsiders
- Hebrews 13:13 inverts the valence: "Let us go forth therefore unto him without the camp" — what was the place of disposal/shame becomes the place of encounter with Christ
- Revelation's eschatological "without" (Rev 22:15) transforms the spatial concept into permanent soteriological exclusion
