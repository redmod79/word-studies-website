## Lexicon Definition

(Aramaic) corresponding to H8138 שָׁנָה: to change, alter, be different. BDB outline: **Pe.** (Peal) — intransitive: *change, be changed, be different from* (מִן); of garment by fire, brightness of face, purpose. **Pa.** (Pael) — transitive: *change* (heart), *frustrate* word/edict. **Ithpa.** (Ithpaal) — passive/reflexive: *be changed*; of face, time/conditions. **Haph.** (Haphel) — causative: *change, alter* times, laws, decrees; frustrate edict.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Changed Physical Appearance / Countenance
The most frequent intransitive use: a face, complexion, or form that *visibly shifts* under emotional or supernatural pressure.
- **Dan 3:19** — "the form of his visage was changed against Shadrach, Meshach, and Abednego" (Ithpa — passive)
- **Dan 5:6** — "the king's countenance was changed, and his thoughts troubled him" (Pe — intransitive)
- **Dan 5:9** — "king Belshazzar greatly troubled, and his countenance was changed in him" (Pe)
- **Dan 7:28** — "my cogitations much troubled me, and my countenance changed in me" (Ithpa)

### 2. Inviolable Laws and Decrees
A major use in the narrative: the Medo-Persian law *cannot* be changed — a plot device of Daniel 6, and a legal formula in Ezra.
- **Ezr 6:11** — "whosoever shall alter this word, let timber be pulled down from his house" (Haph)
- **Dan 6:8** — "that it be not changed, according to the law of the Medes and Persians" (Haph)
- **Dan 6:15** — "the law of the Medes and Persians is, That no decree nor statute which the king establisheth may be changed" (Haph)
- **Dan 6:17** — "the king sealed it… that the purpose might not be changed concerning Daniel" (Pe)

### 3. Divine Change of Times, Seasons, and Laws
The Haphel (causative) applied to cosmic/historical epochs — God *causes* change; the little horn *attempts* it.
- **Dan 2:21** — "he changeth the times and the seasons: he removeth kings, and setteth up kings" (Haph — God as subject)
- **Dan 7:25** — "he shall speak great words against the most High… and think to change times and laws" (Haph — the little horn as subject)

### 4. Being Different / Diverse in Nature
The Pe and Pa passive participle describe the fourth beast as categorically *unlike* all preceding kingdoms.
- **Dan 7:3** — "four great beasts came up from the sea, diverse one from another" (Pe)
- **Dan 7:7** — "a fourth beast, dreadful and terrible… it was diverse from all the beasts that were before it" (Pa passive ptc)
- **Dan 7:19** — "the fourth beast, which was diverse from all the others" (Pe)
- **Dan 7:23** — "the fourth kingdom upon earth, which shall be diverse from all kingdoms" (Pe)

### 5. Changed Heart and Frustrated Purpose
The Pael (transitive) and Ithpaal used for deliberate inner or diplomatic transformation.
- **Dan 4:16** — "let his heart be changed from man's, and let a beast's heart be given unto him" (Pa)
- **Dan 3:28** — "God… who hath sent his angel, and delivered his servants… and have changed the king's word" (Pa — i.e., frustrated/overridden it)
- **Dan 2:9** — "ye have prepared lying and corrupt words… if the conditions be changed" (Ithpa)
