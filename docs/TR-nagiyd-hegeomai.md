| nâgîyd (H5057) | ἄρχων (G758) | 10x — PMI 3.40 | Secondary; "ruler/prince" |

**Semantic shift in translation:** The LXX uses the participial form ἡγούμενος ("the one leading"), which conveys ongoing leadership role rather than a fixed status title. This is consistent with the underlying Hebrew — nâgîyd is not a throne title but a relational designation: one who stands at the front before/for others. The cognitive sense of ἡγέομαι ("to consider, think") is secondary in LXX; the ruling sense dominates.

## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same:**
- The ἡγούμενος consistently governs "the people of God" — in OT it is Israel, in NT it is the church (Heb 13) or all peoples (Matt 2:6)
- The divine appointment structure is preserved: the nâgîyd was chosen and anointed by God; the NT ἡγούμενος speaks "the word of God" and watches for souls

**What shifts:**
- In NT, ἡγέομαι more commonly means "to consider/reckon" (Phil 2:3,6; 3:7–8; 2Pet 1:13) — the leadership sense is concentrated in Hebrews and the Matthean birth narrative
- The nâgîyd formula in OT is individual ("thee I have made nâgîyd"); the NT ἡγούμενος is plural — the single divinely-anointed leader has become a plurality of appointed church overseers
- Dan 9:25's "Messiah the nâgîyd" is the bridge point: in NT, Jesus fulfills the messianic nâgîyd title while the participial ἡγούμενοι distributes delegated leadership to church leaders
