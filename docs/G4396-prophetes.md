# G4396 — προφήτης (prophētēs)

- **Root:** Compound of πρό (pro, "before/forth") + φημί (phēmi, "to speak") — one who speaks forth

## Lexicon Definition

From a compound of πρό (G4253) and φημί (G5346); a foreteller ("prophet"); by analogy an inspired speaker; by extension a poet.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. OT Prophets as Scripture Authors / Spokesmen
The dominant usage — referring to named or unnamed OT prophets whose words are cited as fulfilled.
- **Mat 1:22** — "that it might be fulfilled which was spoken of the Lord by the prophet"
- **Mat 2:17** — "Then was fulfilled that which was spoken by Jeremy the prophet"
- **Luk 3:4** — "As it is written in the book of the words of Esaias the prophet"
- **Acts 8:28** — "sitting in his chariot read Esaias the prophet"

### 2. The Law and the Prophets — Canonical Authority
Prophets as a division of Scripture, paired with Moses/the Law.
- **Mat 5:17** — "Think not that I am come to destroy the law, or the prophets"
- **Mat 22:40** — "On these two commandments hang all the law and the prophets"
- **Luk 16:29** — "They have Moses and the prophets; let them hear them"
- **Acts 13:15** — "after the reading of the law and the prophets"

### 3. Jesus Identified as a Prophet / "The Prophet"
The people and disciples recognizing or debating Jesus's prophetic identity.
- **Mat 21:11** — "This is Jesus the prophet of Nazareth of Galilee"
- **Jhn 6:14** — "This is of a truth that prophet that should come into the world"
- **Jhn 7:40** — "Of a truth this is the Prophet"
- **Jhn 4:19** — "Sir, I perceive that thou art a prophet"

### 4. The Prophet Like Moses — Eschatological Figure
The specific "prophet" of Deuteronomy 18:15, applied to Christ.
- **Acts 3:22** — "A prophet shall the Lord your God raise up unto you of your brethren, like unto me"
- **Acts 3:23** — "every soul, which will not hear that prophet, shall be destroyed from among the people"
- **Acts 7:37** — "A prophet shall the Lord your God raise up unto you of your brethren, like unto me"

### 5. Prophets Persecuted and Killed
A recurring motif: Israel's pattern of rejecting and killing prophets.
- **Mat 23:31** — "ye are the children of them which killed the prophets"
- **Mat 23:37** — "O Jerusalem, Jerusalem, thou that killest the prophets"
- **Acts 7:52** — "Which of the prophets have not your fathers persecuted?"
- **1Th 2:15** — "Who both killed the Lord Jesus, and their own prophets"

### 6. NT Church Office — Prophets as Spiritual Gift Holders
Prophets as a distinct role within the early Christian community.
- **1Co 12:28** — "God hath set some in the church, first apostles, secondarily prophets"
- **1Co 14:29** — "Let the prophets speak two or three, and let the other judge"
- **1Co 14:32** — "the spirits of the prophets are subject to the prophets"
- **Eph 4:11** — "he gave some, apostles; and some, prophets; and some, evangelists"

### 7. Prophets in Revelation — Eschatological Witness
Prophets as end-time figures and recipients of divine vindication.
- **Rev 11:10** — "these two prophets tormented them that dwelt on the earth"
- **Rev 18:20** — "Rejoice over her, thou heaven, and ye holy apostles and prophets"
- **Rev 22:6** — "the Lord God of the holy prophets sent his angel"
