## Lexicon Definition

From Strong's: "Of uncertain affinity; properly, beautiful, but chiefly (figuratively) good (that is, valuable or virtuous, for appearance or use, and thus distinguished from G18, which is properly intrinsic)." The core idea is that which is beautiful, fit, or excellent in outward form and function — qualitatively attractive, fine, noble, or admirable. Kalos emphasizes the observable/appreciable excellence of a thing (its fitness, fairness, or fineness as perceived by others) rather than purely ethical goodness.

The Pastoral Epistles (1-2 Tim, Titus) are dramatically saturated with kalos — "good works" is a signature phrase. Heavy in Synoptic parables about trees/fruit/seed/soil where observable quality matters. Absent from Revelation and most of the General Epistles.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Fine / Excellent in Quality (tangible, observable excellence)
- **John 2:10** — "Every man at the beginning doth set forth good (kalos) wine; and when men have well drunk, then that which is worse: [but] thou hast kept the good (kalos) wine until now." Standard for wine appreciated by a sommelier-like steward.
- **Luke 21:5** — "the temple, how it was adorned with goodly (kalois) stones and gifts" — aesthetic/material excellence.
- **Matt 13:45** — "seeking goodly (kalous) pearls" — pearls judged fine by a merchant's eye.
- **1 Tim 4:4** — "every creature of God is good (kalon), and nothing to be refused" — the created order as well-made, fit for use.

### 2. Sound / Productive / Fit for Its Purpose
- **Matt 3:10; 7:17-19; 12:33; Luke 3:9; 6:43** — "good tree... good fruit" — a tree that is what a tree ought to be, producing what fruit ought to be.
- **Matt 13:8, 23; Mark 4:8, 20; Luke 8:15** — "good ground / good soil" — soil fit for its purpose of bearing grain.
- **Matt 13:24, 37, 38** — "good seed" — viable, pure seed of the intended kind.
- **Mark 9:50; Luke 14:34** — "Salt is good (kalon)" — salt functioning as salt should.

### 3. Morally Admirable / Noble (visible to others)
- **Matt 5:16** — "that they may see your good (kala) works, and glorify your Father" — works whose excellence is publicly observable.
- **1 Pet 2:12** — "having your conversation honest (kalēn) among the Gentiles... by your good (kalōn) works, which they shall behold, glorify God."
- **Rom 12:17; 2 Cor 8:21** — "provide things honest (kala) in the sight of all men" — conduct visibly above reproach.
- **Heb 13:18** — "a good (kalēn) conscience" — a conscience one can rightly display.

### 4. Beneficial / Advantageous / Expedient
- **Matt 17:4; Mark 9:5; Luke 9:33** — "it is good (kalon) for us to be here" — this is a fine situation.
- **1 Cor 7:1, 8, 26** — "it is good for a man not to touch a woman... good for them if they abide" — advantageous, prudent.
- **Rom 14:21** — "it is good (kalon) neither to eat flesh, nor to drink wine... whereby thy brother stumbleth."
- **Matt 18:8-9; Mark 9:42-47** — "it is better (kalon... mallon) to enter into life maimed" — comparative: preferable course.

### 5. Genuine / Authentic / True to Type

## Notes on Ancient Wine Usage (John 2:10)

Kalos in John 2:10 describes wine as judged by a trained household steward (architriklinos) at a wedding banquet. The criteria built into the word are observable excellence — what a connoisseur recognizes as fine-quality wine. The word itself does not specify *which* quality (sweetness vs. strength vs. freshness vs. aging) — it is the generic "excellent, fine" register. For specifics on what ancient wine-stewards prized, see the WG-wine.md word-group study and the G3631-oinos.md and TR-yayin-oinos.md studies, which document the range of oinos (fermented, unfermented, aged, new, mixed). The contribution kalos makes is simply that the wine Jesus produced met or exceeded the steward's standard of excellence; the steward's remark compares it favorably to the earlier wine of the feast.

Kalos is used elsewhere for observable-quality items: "goodly pearls" (Matt 13:45), "goodly stones" of the temple (Luke 21:5), "good ground" (Matt 13:8), "good seed" (Matt 13:24), "good fruit" (Matt 7:17). The common thread is recognizable excellence of the right kind — a thing that is what it ought to be, at a superior grade.
