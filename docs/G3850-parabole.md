## Lexicon Definition

From παραβάλλω; a similitude ("parable"), i.e. (symbolically) a fictitious narrative (of common life conveying a moral), apothegm or adage. By extension, a comparison, figure, or type — something set alongside another for illustration or analogy.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Extended Narrative Comparison (Jesus' Parables)
The dominant Synoptic sense — an illustrative story drawn from ordinary life to teach kingdom truth.
- **Matt 13:3** — "he spake many things unto them in parables... Behold, a sower went forth to sow"
- **Matt 13:24** — "Another parable put he forth... The kingdom of heaven is likened unto a man which sowed good seed"
- **Luke 15:3** — "he spake this parable unto them" (introducing the lost sheep)
- **Mark 4:2** — "he taught them many things by parables"

### 2. Short Saying / Proverbial Comparison
A brief analogical saying rather than a developed narrative.
- **Mark 3:23** — "said unto them in parables, How can Satan cast out Satan?"
- **Luke 4:23** — "Ye will surely say unto me this proverb, Physician, heal thyself"
- **Luke 6:39** — "a parable... Can the blind lead the blind?"
- **Mark 4:30** — "with what comparison shall we compare it?" (parabolē rendered "comparison")

### 3. Object Lesson from Nature
Observation of the natural world as a prophetic signal.
- **Matt 24:32 / Mark 13:28** — "learn a parable of the fig tree; When his branch is yet tender..."
- **Luke 21:29** — "Behold the fig tree, and all the trees"

### 4. Veiled / Hidden Speech
Deliberate concealment that separates insiders from outsiders.
- **Matt 13:10-13** — "Why speakest thou unto them in parables?... because they seeing see not"
- **Mark 4:11** — "unto you it is given to know the mystery of the kingdom of God: but unto them that are without, all these things are done in parables"
- **Mark 4:34** — "without a parable spake he not unto them"
- **Matt 13:35** — "I will open my mouth in parables; I will utter things which have been kept secret"

### 5. Typological Figure / Symbolic Correspondence (Hebrews)
A historical reality that functions as a figure pointing beyond itself. This use aligns with Louw-Nida subdomain 58.010 (Archetype/Antitype).
- **Heb 9:9** — "Which was a figure (parabolē) for the time then present, in which were offered both gifts and sacrifices" — the earthly sanctuary as figure of the present/coming reality
- **Heb 11:19** — "from whence also he received him in a figure (parabolē)" — Isaac's deliverance as figure of resurrection
