# Vision-Introduction Formulas (Greek)

## Definition

A cluster of recurring Greek syntactic formulas used — chiefly in Revelation, but also in Luke-Acts and the Synoptics — to open a visionary unit, frame it in the seer's experience, and mark its content as received revelation rather than ordinary narrative. Four canonical frames:

1. **καὶ εἶδον** (*kai eidon*) "and I saw" — `V-2AAI-1S` of ὁράω (G3708). ~44× in Revelation. Functions as the Greek equivalent of Hebrew Qal-wayyiqtol *wā-ʾerʾeh*.
2. **ἐγενόμην ἐν πνεύματι** (*egenomēn en pneumati*) "I came to be / was in (the) Spirit" — `V-2ADI-1S` of γίνομαι (G1096) + `PREP` ἐν + `N-DSN` πνεῦμα (G4151). State-entry formula marking the seer's reception of the vision.
3. **ἐν πνεύματι** (*en pneumati*) — the bare locative phrase re-used to mark vision-internal transitions (Rev 17:3; 21:10).
4. **καὶ ἰδού** (*kai idou*) "and behold" — `CONJ` + `V-2AMM-2S` of ὁράω (G3708). Presentative particle inside the vision, equivalent to Hebrew *wə-hinnēh*.

The frames nest: Rev 1:10 opens with ἐγενόμην ἐν πνεύματι (state-entry), then 1:12 καὶ … εἶδον (seeing-mainline); Rev 4:1 μετὰ ταῦτα εἶδον → 4:2 εὐθέως ἐγενόμην ἐν πνεύματι → καὶ ἰδοὺ θρόνος (presentative).

## Form Recognition

### Frame 1 — καὶ εἶδον (seer-mainline)

- `καὶ` (G2532) CONJ + `εἶδον` V-2AAI-1S of ὁράω (G3708) — second-aorist active indicative, 1st singular.
- Aspectually perfective: each εἶδον frames a bounded visionary scene as a whole, resets the narrative deictic center, and does **not** assert chronological succession with adjacent εἶδον frames (see companion entry [apocalyptic-narrative-prolepsis](apocalyptic-narrative-prolepsis.md)).
- Distribution: Rev 5:1, 2, 6, 11; 6:1, 2, 5, 8, 9, 12; 7:1, 2; 8:2, 13; 9:1, 17; 10:1, 5; 13:1, 2, 11; 14:1, 6, 14; 15:1, 2, 5; 16:13; 17:3, 6; 18:1; 19:11, 17, 19; 20:1, 4, 11, 12; 21:1, 2, 22. Also Dan 7:2, 4, 6, 7, 9, 11, 13, 21 LXX-Theodotion καὶ ἐθεώρουν / καὶ ἰδού.
- Variant: **μετὰ ταῦτα εἶδον** "after these things I saw" — Rev 4:1; 7:1, 9; 15:5; 18:1. Marks narrative succession in the seer's experience, not historical sequence of the referents.

### Frame 2 — ἐγενόμην ἐν πνεύματι (state-entry)

- `ἐγενόμην` V-2ADI-1S of γίνομαι (G1096) — second-aorist middle (deponent) indicative, 1st singular. "I came to be / became / found myself."
- `ἐν` (G1722) + `πνεύματι` N-DSN (G4151) — locative dative of sphere.
- Four occurrences, structurally identical, bracketing the four major vision-cycles of Revelation:

| Reference | Greek | Parsing | Function |
|-----------|-------|---------|----------|
| Rev 1:10 | ἐγενόμην ἐν Πνεύματι ἐν τῇ κυριακῇ ἡμέρᾳ | V-2ADI-1S + PREP + N-DSN + PREP + T-DSF + A-DSF + N-DSF | Opens the whole book; Patmos / Lord's-day locus |
| Rev 4:2 | εὐθέως ἐγενόμην ἐν Πνεύματι | ADV + V-2ADI-1S + PREP + N-DSN | Opens throne-vision (chs. 4–16) |
| Rev 17:3 | ἀπήνεγκέν με εἰς ἔρημον ἐν Πνεύματι | V-AAI-3S + P-1AS + PREP + A-ASF + PREP + N-DSN | Opens harlot-vision (chs. 17–18) |
| Rev 21:10 | ἀπήνεγκέν με ἐν Πνεύματι ἐπὶ ὄρος μέγα καὶ ὑψηλόν | V-AAI-3S + P-1AS + PREP + N-DSN + PREP + N-ASN + A-ASN + CONJ + A-ASN | Opens new-Jerusalem vision (chs. 21–22) |

The first two use active-intransitive γίνομαι; the last two transpose the subject to an angelic guide (ἀπήνεγκέν 3ms of ἀποφέρω G667) with the seer as accusative object (με), while still tagging the state with ἐν Πνεύματι.

- **Parser signature:** `V-2ADI-1S γίνομαι` + `PREP ἐν` + `N-DSN πνεῦμα` (or same dative phrase attached to ἀποφέρω).
- **Morphological note:** γίνομαι is a lexical middle (deponent) — the middle voice encodes subject-affecting change-of-state ("I came to be"), not passivity. Rendering "I was in the Spirit" (KJV) obscures the ingressive aspect; "I came to be / found myself in (the) Spirit" is aspectually more accurate (Wallace 559, 563; BDAG γίνομαι §5).

### Frame 3 — καὶ ἰδού (vision-internal presentative)

- `καὶ` (G2532) + `ἰδού` (G3708) — morphologically V-2AMM-2S of ὁράω ("behold!"), frozen as a discourse particle.
- Does not inflect for person; functions as a presentative introducing each new element within a vision.
- Parallel to Hebrew *wə-hinnēh* (הִנֵּה) — both the LXX and NT Revelation translate *wə-hinnēh* with καὶ ἰδού consistently.
- Distribution in Revelation: 4:1, 2; 5:6; 6:2, 5, 8; 7:9; 14:1, 14; 15:5 v.l.; 19:11. Typically immediately follows εἶδον or ἐγενόμην ἐν πνεύματι and introduces the object of sight.

### Frame 4 — ancillary openers

- **ἐδείχθη / ὤφθη** (V-API-3S of δείκνυμι / ὁράω) — "was shown / appeared." Rev 12:1 σημεῖον … ὤφθη introduces the woman-sign as already-appeared entity. LXX renders Hebrew Niphal *nirʾāh* (Dan 8:1) with ὤφθη.
- **ἐν ἐκστάσει** (N-DSF ἔκστασις G1611) "in (a) trance" — Acts 10:10; 11:5; 22:17. Luke's equivalent of Rev's ἐν πνεύματι, drawn from LXX usage of תַּרְדֵּמָה.
- **τὸ ὅραμα** (N-ASN G3705) "the vision" — content-noun; Acts 10:17; 11:5; 16:9, 10; 18:9; Matt 17:9.
- **λέγων / λέγοντος** + content — speech within vision. (See companion entry [participle-legon-discourse](participle-legon-discourse.md).)

Note the twinned ἐν-datives (sphere "in Spirit" + time "on the Lord's-day"). The state-entry then pivots to auditory mode (ἤκουσα aor. act.) before visual mode (καὶ ἐπιστρέψας εἶδον, 1:12). This mirrors Ezek 1:1 where *nip̄təḥû haššāmayim* precedes *wāʾerʾeh*.

### 2. εὐθέως ingressive (Rev 4:2)

| εὐθέως | εὐθέως | ADV | immediately |
| ἐγενόμην | γίνομαι | V-2ADI-1S | I came-to-be |

The adverb εὐθέως reinforces the ingressive force of γίνομαι aorist-middle — not "I was" (durative) but "I came into (the) Spirit-state." καὶ ἰδού then presentatively unveils the throne. This is the classic Hebrew-style *wayhî* → *wə-hinnēh* sequence rendered into Greek.

### 3. Angelic-carry transpositions (Rev 17:3; 21:10)

Both use the same frame: `ἀπήνεγκέν με` (V-AAI-3S ἀποφέρω + P-1AS) + `ἐν Πνεύματι` + directional phrase (εἰς ἔρημον / ἐπὶ ὄρος μέγα καὶ ὑψηλόν).

The grammatical subject shifts from the seer (ἐγενόμην, 1:10; 4:2) to the angel (ἀπήνεγκέν, 17:3; 21:10), but ἐν Πνεύματι remains as the locative tag for the seer's state. The seer is being *moved-while-in-Spirit* — a Greek formalization of Ezek 8:3 / 11:1 / 11:24 where the Spirit lifts and carries the prophet (wayyiqtol Qal of נשא + רוח-agent). Contrast Rev 21:10 with Ezek 40:2: *bəmarʾôt ʾĕlōhîm hĕbîʾanî … ʿal-har gābōah məʾōd* "in visions of God he brought me … to a very high mountain" — Greek ἀπήνεγκέν με ἐν Πνεύματι ἐπὶ ὄρος μέγα καὶ ὑψηλόν is a near-verbatim rendering.

### 4. καὶ εἶδον as mainline (Rev 13:1; 14:1; 20:11)

| Rev 14:1 | καὶ εἶδον, καὶ ἰδοὺ τὸ Ἀρνίον ἑστὸς | CONJ + V-2AAI-1S + CONJ+(V-2AMM-2S-frozen) + T-NSN+N-NSN + V-2RAP-ASN |
| Rev 20:11 | καὶ εἶδον θρόνον μέγαν λευκόν | CONJ + V-2AAI-1S + N-ASM + A-ASM + A-ASM |

The εἶδον-opener takes a direct accusative object (or accusative + participle) that specifies the content of the seeing. The participle's tense/voice carries the aspect of the seen-object (e.g., Rev 14:1 ἑστός V-2RAP-ASN perfect-active-ptcp = "standing-in-settled-state").

### 5. ἐν ἐκστάσει (Lukan variant)

Acts 10:10, 11:5, 22:17 use `γενέσθαι ἐπ' αὐτὸν ἔκστασιν` or `ἐν ἐκστάσει ἰδεῖν ὅραμα`. The sphere-dative construction matches Revelation's ἐν πνεύματι, but Luke prefers ἔκστασις (a "displacement/trance") over πνεῦμα, probably because his context (Peter on rooftop, Paul in temple) is not overtly pneumatic-eschatological.

## Semitic-to-Greek Translation Equivalences

| Hebrew / Aramaic | Greek | Notes |
|------------------|-------|-------|
| *wā-ʾerʾeh* (Qal wayq 1cs ראה) | καὶ εἶδον (V-2AAI-1S ὁράω) | Aspectual match: Hebrew wayyiqtol = Greek aorist indicative |
| *rāʾîtî* (Qal qatal 1cs) | εἶδον / ἐθεώρουν (Theodotion prefers θεωρέω for Aramaic חזה) | qatal retrospective = aorist |
| *ḥāzēh hăwêt* (Peal ptcp + perf, Aram.) | ἐθεώρουν (impf of θεωρέω, LXX-Th) | periphrastic durative ↔ Greek imperfective |
| *wə-hinnēh* | καὶ ἰδού | Systematic LXX/NT equivalence |
| *nirʾāh* (Niphal perf 3ms) | ὤφθη (V-API-3S ὁράω) | Niphal passive ↔ aorist passive |
| *hirʾanî* (Hiphil perf 3ms + 1cs suf.) | ἔδειξέν μοι (V-AAI-3S δείκνυμι + P-1DS) | causative Hiphil ↔ active δείκνυμι + dat. |
| *wayhî bišnat* | usually no direct rendering; LXX often καὶ ἐγένετο ἐν τῷ ἔτει | Greek drops bare *bišnat* sometimes |
| *bərûaḥ* (Ezek 11:24; 37:1) | ἐν πνεύματι | Direct locative-sphere match |

Rev's ἐγενόμην ἐν πνεύματι can be read as the Greek fusion of Hebrew *wayhî* (became / was) + *bərûaḥ* (in-spirit), producing a single state-entry formula with no exact OT parent but a transparent Semitic substrate. Compare Ezek 37:1 LXX καὶ ἐγένετο ἐπ' ἐμὲ χεὶρ Κυρίου καὶ ἐξήγαγέν με ἐν πνεύματι Κυρίου καὶ ἔθηκέν με ἐν μέσῳ τοῦ πεδίου — the closest single-verse prototype for Rev 17:3 / 21:10.

## Interpretive Neutrality

**This entry is context-neutral.** The formulas establish a block of text as genre-marked vision-report; they do not settle:

- whether the seer's "being in Spirit" denotes ecstatic displacement, heightened awareness, Sabbath-day worship-state, or pneumatic revelation-channel;
- whether καὶ εἶδον frames are sequential in their referents or recapitulative (see [apocalyptic-narrative-prolepsis](apocalyptic-narrative-prolepsis.md));
- whether the four ἐγενόμην-ἐν-πνεύματι breaks mark the four major divisions of Revelation (as many commentators hold: 1:10; 4:2; 17:3; 21:10) or merely episodic transitions.

These debates are legitimate but belong to passage-analysis and commentary, not to the grammar entry.
