## Lexicon Definition

From G1491 (εἶδος, eidos = form/appearance); an image (i.e. for worship); by implication, a heathen god.

The word derives from the Greek concept of *visible form* — an eidōlon is literally a "shaped thing," a representation given a concrete form, then applied specifically to images made for worship and, by extension, to the false deities they represent.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. The Physical Idol — Man-Made Image

The eidōlon is a concrete fabricated object, typically cast or carved. NT writers describe its inert nature: silent, motionless, unable to perceive.

- **Acts 7:41** — "they made a calf in those days, and offered sacrifice unto the idol, and rejoiced in the works of their hands" — the golden calf called eidōlon; note "works of their hands"
- **Rev 9:20** — "idols of gold, and silver, and brass, and stone, and of wood: which neither can see, nor hear, nor walk" — catalogue of materials with explicit negation of all animate capacities
- **1 Cor 12:2** — "ye were Gentiles, carried away unto these dumb idols, even as ye were led" — called *dumb* (voiceless), led by them rather than speaking to them

### 2. Idols in Cultic Practice — Sacrifice, Pollution, Conscience

Paul addresses practical questions about food offered to idols (εἰδωλόθυτον, G1494). He insists an idol is *nothing in the world* — yet warns that not everyone has this knowledge.

- **1 Cor 8:4** — "the eating of those things that are offered in sacrifice unto idols, we know that an idol is nothing in the world"
- **1 Cor 8:7** — "some with conscience of the idol unto this hour eat it as a thing offered unto an idol" — "conscience of the idol" = residual sense of the idol's reality
- **1 Cor 10:19** — "that the idol is any thing, or that which is offered in sacrifice to idols is any thing?" — rhetorical: the idol has no ontological standing
- **Acts 15:20** — "abstain from pollutions of idols" — Jerusalem council ruling; the idol produces moral/ritual defilement even if it is nothing

### 3. Idols as Rivals to the Living God

The sharpest NT usage sets eidōla in direct contrast to the θεὸς ζῶν (living God), framing idolatry as a defection from the one true God. The command to flee idols echoes OT prophetic calls.

- **1 Thess 1:9** — "ye turned to God from idols to serve the living and true God" — the definitive conversion pattern: away from eidōla, toward the living (ζάω) God
- **2 Cor 6:16** — "what agreement hath the temple of God with idols? for ye are the temple of the living God" — juxtaposition: eidōla vs. the dwelling place of God
- **Rom 2:22** — "thou that abhorrest idols, dost thou commit sacrilege?" — Jews who claim to abhor idols yet rob temples condemned for inconsistency
- **1 John 5:21** — "Little children, keep yourselves from idols. Amen." — closing word of the epistle; absolute warning, no qualification
