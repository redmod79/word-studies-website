## Overview

## Roles in the Concept

- **Act:** proskynéō (G4352) / shachah (H7812) — the physical act of prostrating or bowing
- **Service:** latreúō (G3000) — the ongoing devoted work of worship (priestly service, cultic ministry)
- **Stance:** sébomai (G4576) — inner reverence/piety; fear-worship; the posture of a devout person
- **Agent:** proskynētēs (G4353) — the worshipper (who performs proskyneo)
- **Aramaic cognate:** segid (H5457) — the same prostration act in Aramaic (Daniel's golden image scene)

## Key Distinction: proskynéō vs. latreúō

Mat 4:10 deliberately uses both in the same breath: "Thou shalt worship (proskynéō) the Lord thy God, and him only shalt thou serve (latreúō)." This is the LXX rendering of Deut 6:13. The two words are not synonyms:
- **proskynéō** = the outward act of bowing/falling before; the gesture of submission
- **latreúō** = the sustained commitment of service; the work of devotion over time

## Key Passages Where Multiple Group Words Appear

- **Mat 4:9–10** — proskyneo + latreuo together: Satan's temptation vs. Deut 6:13
- **Rev 13:4** — proskyneo the dragon AND proskyneo the beast: double false-worship indictment
- **Rev 19:10 / 22:9** — angel refuses proskyneo; redirects to God: "worship God"
- **John 4:20–24** — proskyneo ×5 in a single discourse: the word's fullest NT theological statement
