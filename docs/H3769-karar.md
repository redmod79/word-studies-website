## Semantic Categories

### 1. Ecstatic Worship Dance Before God
- **2 Sam 6:14** — "And David danced [מְכַרְכֵּר] before the LORD with all [his] might; and David [was] girded with a linen ephod."
- **2 Sam 6:16** — "And as the ark of the LORD came into the city of David, Michal Saul's daughter looked through a window, and saw king David leaping and dancing [מְכַרְכֵּר] before the LORD; and she despised him in her heart."

Both occurrences describe the same event: David's uninhibited whirling before the ark during its processional entry into Jerusalem. The word carries connotations of vigorous circular motion, performed as an act of worship "before the LORD" (לִפְנֵי יְהוָה). In 6:16 it is paired with פָּזַז (pazaz, H6339, "leaping"), intensifying the picture of full-bodied physical exuberance.
