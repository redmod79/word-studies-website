## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same across testaments:**
- The full semantic range transfers: scroll, legal document, letter, divine register, authoritative religious text
- The "book of the law" formula (sepher hattowrah → to biblion tou nomou) carries intact from Deuteronomy through the NT
- The "book of life" / divine register concept bridges from Exo 32:32 and Psa 69:28 directly into Rev 3:5, 20:12

**What shifts:**
- The OT formulaic "written in the book of the chronicles of the kings" (41x in 2 Kings alone) has no NT counterpart — this genre of royal annals citation ends with the Hebrew monarchy
- The letter/correspondence sense (24x in OT) narrows in the NT, where ἐπιστολή (epistole) takes over as the standard word for letters/epistles
- In Revelation, biblion acquires an intensified apocalyptic-eschatological weight: the sealed seven-sealed scroll (Rev 5–6) and the book of life (Rev 20:12) concentrate the "divine register" and "sealed prophetic scroll" categories from the OT into a climactic NT framework
