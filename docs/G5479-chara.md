## Lexicon Definition

From χαίρω (G5463); cheerfulness, i.e. calm delight — translated: gladness, greatly, joy, joyful(-ness), joyous. The nominal counterpart to the common verb of rejoicing; denotes the inner emotional state of gladness/delight itself rather than the act of rejoicing.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Joy at Christ's Appearing / Salvation Events
Messianic and gospel-event joy tied to Christ's coming, resurrection, or news of salvation.
- **Mat 2:10** — "they rejoiced with exceeding great joy" (at the star)
- **Luk 2:10** — "good tidings of great joy, which shall be to all people"
- **Mat 28:8** — "departed quickly from the sepulchre with fear and great joy"
- **Luk 24:52** — "returned to Jerusalem with great joy" (after ascension)

### 2. Joy of the Kingdom / Joy in the Holy Ghost
Joy as a constitutive element of the Kingdom of God, a Spirit-produced state rather than a circumstantial emotion.
- **Rom 14:17** — "the kingdom of God is... righteousness, and peace, and joy in the Holy Ghost"
- **Gal 5:22** — "the fruit of the Spirit is love, joy, peace..."
- **Act 13:52** — "the disciples were filled with joy, and with the Holy Ghost"
- **1Th 1:6** — "received the word... with joy of the Holy Ghost"

### 3. Full / Fulfilled Joy (plērōthē)
Joy brought to completion, especially as result of Christ's word, prayer, or fellowship.
- **Jhn 15:11** — "that my joy might remain in you, and that your joy might be full"
- **Jhn 16:24** — "ask, and ye shall receive, that your joy may be full"
- **Jhn 17:13** — "that they might have my joy fulfilled in themselves"
- **1Jn 1:4** — "these things write we unto you, that your joy may be full"

### 4. Joy Amid / Through Suffering
Joy coexisting with or produced by affliction, trial, and chastening — a distinctively Christian paradox.
- **Jas 1:2** — "count it all joy when ye fall into divers temptations"
- **Heb 12:2** — "who for the joy that was set before him endured the cross"
- **2Co 8:2** — "in a great trial of affliction the abundance of their joy... abounded"
- **1Pe 1:8** — "ye rejoice with joy unspeakable and full of glory"

### 5. Joy Over Repentance / Salvation of Others
Heavenly and pastoral joy at conversion, perseverance, or the faith of others.
- **Luk 15:7** — "joy shall be in heaven over one sinner that repenteth"
- **Luk 15:10** — "joy in the presence of the angels of God over one sinner"
- **1Th 2:19-20** — "what is our hope, or joy, or crown of rejoicing?... ye are our glory and joy"
- **3Jn 1:4** — "I have no greater joy than to hear that my children walk in truth"

### 6. Joy as Disposition in Ministry / Fellowship
Joy as the affective climate of apostolic labor and Christian assembly.
- **2Co 1:24** — "helpers of your joy"
- **Phl 2:2** — "fulfil ye my joy, that ye be likeminded"
- **Phl 4:1** — "my brethren dearly beloved and longed for, my joy and crown"
- **Heb 13:17** — "that they may do it with joy, and not with grief"

### 7. Superficial / Uprooted Joy
Emotional response to the word that lacks depth and perishes under pressure — the only clearly negative use.
- **Mat 13:20** / **Mar 4:16** / **Luk 8:13** — "heareth the word, and anon with joy receiveth it... yet hath he not root"
