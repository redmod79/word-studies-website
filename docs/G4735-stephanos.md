## Semantic Categories

### 1. Crown of Thorns (Mock Royal Crown)
The Passion narrative uses stephanos for the soldiers' mockery, where a thorny wreath substitutes for royal honor.

- **Mat 27:29** — "when they had platted a crown of thorns, they put it upon his head, and a reed in his right hand"
- **Mark 15:17** — "they clothed him with purple, and platted a crown of thorns, and put it about his head"
- **John 19:2** — "the soldiers platted a crown of thorns, and put it on his head, and they put on him a purple robe"
- **John 19:5** — "Then came Jesus forth, wearing the crown of thorns, and the purple robe"

### 2. Athletic Victor's Wreath (Perishable Prize)
Greek athletic games awarded a chaplet of laurel, olive, or pine. Paul uses this image to contrast temporal and eternal reward.

- **1 Cor 9:25** — "Now they do it to obtain a corruptible crown; but we an incorruptible"

### 3. Eschatological Crown of Reward
The dominant epistolary use. Stephanos names the eschatological reward promised to the faithful — never earned by merit in context, always received from Christ.

- **2 Tim 4:8** — "there is laid up for me a crown of righteousness, which the Lord, the righteous judge, shall give me at that day"
- **James 1:12** — "he shall receive the crown of life, which the Lord hath promised to them that love him"
- **1 Pet 5:4** — "when the chief Shepherd shall appear, ye shall receive a crown of glory that fadeth not away"
- **Rev 2:10** — "be thou faithful unto death, and I will give thee a crown of life"
- **Rev 3:11** — "hold that fast which thou hast, that no man take thy crown"

### 4. Persons as Crown (Apostolic Joy)
Paul uses stephanos metaphorically for beloved communities — the persons themselves are his crown of honor, his public validation.

- **1 Thess 2:19** — "what is our hope, or joy, or crown of rejoicing? Are not even ye in the presence of our Lord Jesus Christ at his coming?"
- **Phil 4:1** — "my brethren dearly beloved and longed for, my joy and crown, so stand fast in the Lord"

### 5. Heavenly Crowned Beings (Apocalyptic Vision)
Revelation deploys stephanos across multiple symbolic figures — elders, the conquering rider, locusts, the cosmic woman, and the Son of Man figure.

- **Rev 4:4** — "four and twenty elders sitting, clothed in white raiment; and they had on their heads crowns of gold"
- **Rev 4:10** — "the four and twenty elders fall down... and cast their crowns before the throne"
- **Rev 6:2** — "he that sat on him had a bow; and a crown was given unto him: and he went forth conquering"
- **Rev 12:1** — "a woman clothed with the sun... and upon her head a crown of twelve stars"
- **Rev 14:14** — "upon the cloud one sat like unto the Son of man, having on his head a golden crown"
