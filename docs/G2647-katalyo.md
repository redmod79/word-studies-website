## Lexicon Definition

From the compound κατά + λύω: to loosen down (disintegrate). The tool definition: "to loosen down (disintegrate), i.e. (by [implication]...)" — the DB entry is truncated. Verb form analysis and verse contexts confirm two primary senses: (1) to demolish / dissolve / bring to nothing; (2) to halt/lodge for the night (as in "to unhitch" — unyoking animals at a stopping place).

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Demolish / Throw Down Physical Structures
The dominant NT usage — buildings, particularly the Temple.

- **Matt 24:2** — "there shall not be left here one stone upon another, that shall not be thrown down"
- **Mark 13:2** — "there shall not be left one stone upon another, that shall not be thrown down"
- **Luke 21:6** — "the days will come, in the which there shall not be left one stone upon another, that shall not be thrown down"

### 2. Destroy the Temple (Accusation Against Jesus)
A distinct sub-cluster: the charge at Jesus's trial and at the cross, citing his statement about rebuilding in three days.

- **Matt 26:61** — "This fellow said, I am able to destroy the temple of God, and to build it in three days"
- **Matt 27:40** — "Thou that destroyest the temple, and buildest it in three days, save thyself"
- **Mark 14:58** — "I will destroy this temple that is made with hands, and within three days I will build another made without hands"
- **Mark 15:29** — "Ah, thou that destroyest the temple, and buildest it in three days"
- **Acts 6:14** — "Jesus of Nazareth shall destroy this place, and shall change the customs"

### 3. Abolish / Nullify (Law, Covenant, God's Work)
Metaphorical destruction of institutions, principles, or God's purposes.

- **Matt 5:17** — "Think not that I am come to destroy the law, or the prophets: I am not come to destroy, but to fulfil"
- **Rom 14:20** — "For meat destroy not the work of God"
- **Gal 2:18** — "if I build again the things which I destroyed, I make myself a transgressor"

### 4. Overthrow / Come to Nothing (Human Plans)
Gamaliel's argument about the fate of movements not from God.

- **Acts 5:38** — "if this counsel or this work be of men, it will come to nought"
- **Acts 5:39** — "if it be of God, ye cannot overthrow it"

### 5. Lodge / Stay as a Guest
The hospitality sense — stopping for the night, "unhitching" at a resting place.

- **Luke 9:12** — "Send the multitude away, that they may go into the towns and country round about, and lodge, and get victuals"
- **Luke 19:7** — "That he was gone to be guest with a man that is a sinner" (Zacchaeus)

### 6. Dissolve (Body / Earthly Dwelling)
Extended metaphor: the mortal body as a tent being struck/dissolved.

- **2 Cor 5:1** — "if our earthly house of this tabernacle were dissolved, we have a building of God"
