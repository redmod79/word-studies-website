## Lexicon Definition

From G601 (apokalypto); disclosure: — appearing, coming, lighten, manifestation, be revealed, revelation.

The noun denotes the act or result of uncovering — removing what conceals so that something hidden becomes visible or known. Etymologically: apo (away from) + kalyptō (to cover, veil).

## Hebrew Source

No direct Hebrew-to-Greek LXX mapping found for G602 as a noun. However, the verb form G601 (apokalyptō) translates H1540 (galah, "to uncover, remove, go into exile") in the LXX. The noun apokalypsis itself is a distinctly NT formation with no regular OT Hebrew equivalent, though the concept of divine disclosure corresponds to H2377 (chazon, "vision") and H1540 (galah, "to uncover").
