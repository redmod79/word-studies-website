## Lexicon Definition

From H6186 (ʻarak, "to arrange"); a pile, equipment, estimate, arrangement, valuation. Used for (1) things "set in order" (showbread, tabernacle furniture), (2) the fixed monetary valuation of persons, animals, houses, and fields dedicated to YHWH by vow, (3) a taxation/levy imposed by a ruler, (4) a stated price or proportion, and (5) rank/equal (one "of one's arrangement"). Translated in KJV as *estimation, valuation, price, taxation, order, proportion, suit, equal*.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Priestly Valuation / Monetary Estimate for a Vowed Person
The signature use: the priest assigns a fixed silver equivalent to a person devoted by vow, so the vow can be discharged without physical dedication. This is the "redemption tariff."

- **Lev 27:2** — "When a man shall make a singular vow, the persons [shall be] for the LORD by thy estimation (ʻerek)."
- **Lev 27:3** — "thy estimation shall be of the male from twenty years old even unto sixty years old... fifty shekels of silver."
- **Lev 27:4** — "And if it [be] a female, then thy estimation shall be thirty shekels."
- **Lev 27:8** — "But if he be poorer than thy estimation, then he shall present himself before the priest, and the priest shall value him; according to his ability that vowed shall the priest value him."

### 2. Valuation of Vowed Property (animals, houses, fields)
The same mechanism extends to non-human dedications, with an added 20% surcharge if the owner redeems them back.

- **Lev 27:13** — "But if he will at all redeem it, then he shall add a fifth [part] thereof unto thy estimation."
- **Lev 27:16** — "thy estimation shall be according to the seed thereof: an homer of barley seed [shall be valued] at fifty shekels of silver."
- **Lev 27:19** — "if he that sanctified the field will in any wise redeem it, then he shall add the fifth [part]... unto thy estimation."
- **Lev 27:27** — "if [it be] of an unclean beast, then he shall redeem [it] according to thine estimation."

### 3. Fixed Silver Reparation in the Trespass Offering
The guilty party brings a ram "with thy estimation" — a silver penalty calibrated by the priest for profaning holy things.

- **Lev 5:15** — "he shall bring for his trespass unto the LORD a ram without blemish... with thy estimation by shekels of silver, after the shekel of the sanctuary."
- **Lev 5:18** — "he shall bring a ram without blemish out of the flock, with thy estimation, for a trespass offering."
- **Lev 6:6** — "he shall bring his trespass offering... a ram without blemish... with thy estimation."

### 4. Redemption-Price Standard (firstborn, sanctuary shekel)
A fixed benchmark of five sanctuary shekels buys back a firstborn son; the sanctuary shekel of 20 gerahs is the universal metric.

- **Num 18:16** — "those that are to be redeemed from a month old shalt thou redeem, according to thine estimation, for the money of five shekels."
- **Lev 27:25** — "all thy estimations shall be according to the shekel of the sanctuary: twenty gerahs shall be the shekel."

### 5. Civil Taxation / Assessed Levy
Outside the sanctuary, ʻerek denotes an assessed amount — a tax, levy, or stipulated wage.

- **2 Kgs 12:4** — "all the money that every man is set at (ʻerek)" — temple revenue.
- **2 Kgs 23:35** — "he exacted the silver and the gold... of every one according to his taxation (ʻerek)" — Jehoiakim's tribute levy.
- **Jdg 17:10** — "I will give thee ten [shekels] of silver by the year, and a suit (ʻerek) of apparel" — a stipulated yearly allowance.

### 6. "Things Set in Order" (Arrangement, Row, Array)
The concrete sense closest to the root verb H6186: objects placed in sequence, specifically the showbread and tabernacle furniture.

- **Exo 40:4** — "set in order (ʻerek) the things that are to be set in order upon it."
- **Exo 40:23** — "he set the bread in order (ʻerek) upon it before the LORD."

### 7. Comparison / Equal / Worth
A figurative outgrowth: something of comparable rank, worth, or proportion.

- **Psa 55:13** — "a man mine equal (ʻerek), my guide, and mine acquaintance."
- **Job 28:13** — "Man knoweth not the price (ʻerek) thereof."
- **Job 41:12** — "nor his comely proportion (ʻerek)."

## Semantic-Domain Note: Redemption of Vowed Persons

Lev 27:1–8 establishes the canonical Torah mechanism for discharging a vow that has devoted a *person* to YHWH:

1. **The vow is valid and binding** (Lev 27:2). A *neder* that names a person is real — the person is "for the LORD."
2. **But the person is not to be literally offered.** Instead the priest assigns a silver valuation (*ʻerek*) calibrated by age and sex (27:3–7): 50 shekels (adult male, 20–60), 30 (adult female), 20/10 (male/female 5–20), 5/3 (male/female month–5), 15/10 (60+).
3. **Poverty clause** (27:8): "if he be poorer than thy estimation, then he shall present himself before the priest, and the priest shall value him; according to his ability that vowed shall the priest value him." The tariff is scaled to what the vower can actually pay — no one is excluded from the redemption off-ramp by poverty.
4. **Only objects placed under *cherem* are non-redeemable** (Lev 27:28–29). A standard person-vow (neder) is always an ʻerek case, never a cherem case, unless explicitly devoted under the stricter ban formula.
5. **The category explicitly contemplates the scenario of Jdg 11** — a hastily-sworn person-vow. Torah's answer is: pay the priestly valuation. The silver discharges the vow; the person goes home.

The consistent collocations — *kohen* (priest), *keseph* (silver), *sheqel haqodesh* (sanctuary shekel), *gaʼal* (redeem), *qadash* (sanctify) — mark ʻerek as a *sanctuary-mediated, priest-administered, silver-denominated, always-redeemable* valuation. It is the Torah's built-in legal mechanism for converting a person-vow into a monetary offering.
