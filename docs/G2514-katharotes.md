## Lexicon Definition

From G2513 (katharos); cleanness (ceremonially): — purifying.

The -ότης (-otēs) suffix forms an abstract quality noun, denoting the state or condition of being clean. This is the abstract quality counterpart to the adjective καθαρός (clean/pure) and the process noun καθαρισμός (the act of cleansing).

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Ceremonial/Ritual Cleanness
- **Heb 9:13** — "For if the blood of bulls and of goats, and the ashes of an heifer sprinkling the unclean, sanctifieth to the purifying of the flesh"

The sole occurrence situates katharotēs within the OT sacrificial system: the red heifer ashes (Num 19) produce ceremonial cleanness of the flesh. The author of Hebrews uses this as the lesser term in an a fortiori argument (9:13-14) — if animal blood achieves fleshly katharotēs, how much more does Christ's blood purge the conscience.
