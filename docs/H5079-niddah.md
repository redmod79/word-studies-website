## Semantic Categories

### 1. Menstrual Impurity (Ritual)
The primary usage — a woman's bodily discharge rendering her ritually unclean for a set period.
- **Lev 12:2** — "according to the days of the separation (niddah) for her infirmity shall she be unclean"
- **Lev 15:19** — "she shall be put apart (niddah) seven days"
- **Lev 15:20** — "every thing that she lieth upon in her separation (niddah) shall be unclean"
- **Lev 15:25** — "out of the time of her separation (niddah)... all the days of the issue of her uncleanness shall be as the days of her separation (niddah)"

### 2. Sexual Prohibition Linked to Menstruation
Niddah as the basis for prohibiting sexual contact during menstrual impurity.
- **Lev 15:24** — "if any man lie with her at all, and her flowers (niddah) be upon him, he shall be unclean seven days"
- **Lev 18:19** — "thou shalt not approach unto a woman... as long as she is put apart (niddah) for her uncleanness"
- **Eze 18:6** — "neither hath come near to a menstruous (niddah) woman" (mark of the righteous man)
- **Eze 22:10** — "in thee have they humbled her that was set apart (niddah) for pollution"

### 3. Purification Water (Red Heifer Rite)
Extended to the water mixed with ashes of the red heifer — "water of niddah" — used to cleanse corpse contamination. The impurity concept shifts from bodily to contact-with-death.
- **Num 19:9** — "it shall be kept... for a water of separation (niddah): it is a purification for sin"
- **Num 19:13** — "the water of separation (niddah) was not sprinkled upon him, he shall be unclean"
- **Num 19:20** — "the water of separation (niddah) hath not been sprinkled upon him; he is unclean"
- **Num 31:23** — "it shall be purified with the water of separation (niddah)"

### 4. Moral/National Defilement (Metaphorical)
Prophetic and post-exilic writers use niddah as a metaphor for the moral filth of idolatry, disobedience, and national sin.
- **2Ch 29:5** — "carry forth the filthiness (niddah) out of the holy place" (Hezekiah's temple reform)
- **Ezr 9:11** — "an unclean land with the filthiness (niddah) of the people of the lands"
- **Eze 36:17** — "their way was before me as the uncleanness of a removed woman (niddah)"
- **Zec 13:1** — "a fountain opened... for sin and for uncleanness (niddah)"

### 5. Metaphor for Abhorrence / Rejection
Used to express God's revulsion or the state of being cast off.
- **Lam 1:17** — "Jerusalem is as a menstruous woman (niddah) among them"
- **Eze 7:19** — "their gold shall be removed (niddah)" — wealth becomes loathsome
- **Eze 7:20** — "therefore have I set it far (niddah) from them"
