## Semantic Categories

### 1. Numerical "Once" (Single Time vs. Multiple)
Bare counting — one time as opposed to twice or thrice. No overtone of finality.
- **2 Cor 11:25** — "Thrice was I beaten with rods, once was I stoned, thrice I suffered shipwreck"
- **Phil 4:16** — "For even in Thessalonica ye sent once and again unto my necessity"
- **1 Thess 2:18** — "we would have come unto you, even I Paul, once and again; but Satan hindered us"

### 2. Unrepeatable Sacrifice / Offering
Christ's offering presented as a single, sufficient act requiring no repetition — contrasted with the repeated Levitical offerings.
- **Heb 9:26** — "but now once in the end of the world hath he appeared to put away sin by the sacrifice of himself"
- **Heb 9:28** — "So Christ was once offered to bear the sins of many"
- **1 Pet 3:18** — "For Christ also hath once suffered for sins, the just for the unjust"
- **Heb 9:7** — "But into the second [went] the high priest alone once every year" (Levitical contrast — annual repetition)

### 3. Unrepeatable Human Experience
A single event in the nature of things — death happens once per person.
- **Heb 9:27** — "And as it is appointed unto men once to die, but after this the judgment"

### 4. Past Completed State
A former condition or event viewed as settled — "at one time" (temporal past).
- **Heb 6:4** — "those who were once enlightened, and have tasted of the heavenly gift"
- **Heb 10:2** — "the worshippers once purged should have had no more conscience of sins"
- **1 Pet 3:20** — "when once the longsuffering of God waited in the days of Noah"
- **Jude 1:3** — "the faith which was once delivered unto the saints"
- **Jude 1:5** — "though ye once knew this, how that the Lord, having saved the people out of the land of Egypt"

### 5. Eschatological Finality
A future decisive act that will end the present order — unrepeatable cosmic shaking.
- **Heb 12:26** — "Yet once more I shake not the earth only, but also heaven"
- **Heb 12:27** — "And this word, Yet once more, signifieth the removing of those things that are shaken"

### Note on hapax vs. ephapax in Hebrews
Both words appear in Hebrews. The author uses hapax (G530) eight times and ephapax (G2178) three times (7:27, 9:12, 10:10). Ephapax always modifies the sacrificial act directly. Hapax has broader range — it modifies sacrifice (9:26, 28), human death (9:27), past experience (6:4, 10:2), the Levitical annual entrance (9:7), and the eschatological shaking (12:26–27). Grammatically, both are adverbs modifying the verb of their clause. Neither word inherently specifies the scope of what it modifies beyond its immediate clause.
