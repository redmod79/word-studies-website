## Lexicon Definition

From H2763 (charam). Physical (as shutting in) a net — either literally (a fishing net) or figuratively. Usually a doomed object: that which has been placed under the ban, set apart as devoted exclusively to the LORD and therefore withdrawn from common use, whether for sacred dedication or for irrevocable destruction. Abstractly: extermination. KJV renders: (ac-)curse(-d, -d thing), dedicated thing, things which should have been utterly destroyed, (appointed to) utter destruction, devoted (thing), net.

The word carries two distinct but linked semantic streams: (1) sacred/destructive ban — object or person irrevocably given to YHWH, often by destruction; (2) net/snare — that which "shuts in" or encloses prey. Both streams share the root idea of enclosure/seclusion from normal use.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. The Sacred Ban: Persons/Objects Devoted to YHWH by Destruction
The herem as irrevocable consecration-by-destruction in holy war.
- **Deut 7:26** — "Neither shalt thou bring an abomination into thine house, lest thou be a cursed thing like it"
- **Josh 6:17** — "And the city shall be accursed, even it, and all that are therein, to the LORD"
- **Josh 6:18** — "keep yourselves from the accursed thing, lest ye make yourselves accursed"
- **1 Sam 15:21** — "sheep and oxen, the chief of the things which should have been utterly destroyed"

### 2. Dedicated / Devoted Property Given to the Sanctuary
Non-destructive sense: property irrevocably transferred to YHWH's priests.
- **Lev 27:21** — "a field devoted; the possession thereof shall be the priest's"
- **Lev 27:28** — "no devoted thing, that a man shall devote unto the LORD... shall be sold or redeemed"
- **Num 18:14** — "Every thing devoted in Israel shall be thine" (spoken to Aaron)
- **Ezek 44:29** — "every dedicated thing in Israel shall be theirs"

### 3. Breach of the Ban — Corporate Guilt
Narratives where taking from the herem incurs corporate wrath.
- **Josh 7:1** — "the children of Israel committed a trespass in the accursed thing: for Achan... took"
- **Josh 7:11–13** — Israel "hath taken of the accursed thing... neither will I be with you any more, except ye destroy the accursed from among you"
- **Josh 22:20** — "Did not Achan the son of Zerah commit a trespass in the accursed thing, and wrath fell on all the congregation"
- **1 Chr 2:7** — "Achar, the troubler of Israel, who transgressed in the thing accursed"

### 4. Judicial Curse / Utter Destruction (Prophetic)
The herem applied to peoples and nations as divine judgment.
- **1 Kgs 20:42** — "a man whom I appointed to utter destruction"
- **Isa 34:5** — "upon the people of my curse, to judgment"
- **Isa 43:28** — "given Jacob to the curse, and Israel to reproaches"
- **Mal 4:6** — "lest I come and smite the earth with a curse"

### 5. Eschatological Removal of the Ban
Reversal of herem status as a sign of restored peace.
- **Zech 14:11** — "there shall be no more utter destruction; but Jerusalem shall be safely inhabited"

### 6. Fishing Net / Dragnet (Homonym or Extended Sense)
The root idea "that which encloses"; used literally of nets and figuratively of snares.
- **Ezek 26:5** — "a place for the spreading of nets in the midst of the sea" (of Tyre)
- **Ezek 32:3** — "I will therefore spread out my net over thee" (Pharaoh)
- **Ezek 47:10** — "a place to spread forth nets" (En-gedi to En-eglaim)
- **Hab 1:15–17** — "they catch them in their net... therefore they sacrifice unto their net"
- **Eccl 7:26** — "the woman, whose heart is snares and nets"
- **Mic 7:2** — "they all lie in wait for blood; they hunt every man his brother with a net"

Lexicographers debate whether the "net" uses represent a true homonym (II חרם) or a figurative extension of the root sense of "enclosure/seclusion." Either way, the KJV concordance treats them under one Strong's number.
