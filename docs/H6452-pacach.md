## Lexicon Definition

A primitive root; to hop, i.e. (figuratively) skip over (or spare); by implication, to hesitate; also (literally) to limp, to dance: — halt, become lame, leap, pass over.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Divine Protective Passing-Over (Sparing from Judgment)
The core Passover usage — God "hopping over" the blood-marked Israelite houses during the tenth plague.
- **Exo 12:13** — "when I see the blood, I will pass over you, and the plague shall not be upon you to destroy you, when I smite the land of Egypt"
- **Exo 12:23** — "the LORD will pass over the door, and will not suffer the destroyer to come in unto your houses to smite you"
- **Exo 12:27** — "It is the sacrifice of the LORD'S passover, who passed over the houses of the children of Israel in Egypt, when he smote the Egyptians, and delivered our houses"

### 2. Hovering / Shielding (Protective Action)
Extended metaphor of divine protection, combining the Passover "sparing" sense with imagery of a bird defending its nest.
- **Isa 31:5** — "As birds flying, so will the LORD of hosts defend Jerusalem; defending also he will deliver it; and passing over he will preserve it"

### 3. Limping / Hesitating (Indecision)
The physical sense of uneven, hobbling movement applied metaphorically to spiritual vacillation between YHWH and Baal.
- **1Ki 18:21** — "How long halt ye between two opinions? if the LORD be God, follow him: but if Baal, then follow him. And the people answered him not a word"

### 4. Becoming Lame (Physical Injury)
Niphal reflexive — the physical sense of the root, denoting permanent crippling.
- **2Sa 4:4** — "his nurse took him up, and fled: and it came to pass, as she made haste to flee, that he fell, and became lame. And his name was Mephibosheth"

### 5. Frenzied Leaping / Ritual Dance
Piel intensive — energetic, repeated hopping or leaping in the context of pagan ritual.
- **1Ki 18:26** — "they took the bullock which was given them, and they dressed it, and called on the name of Baal from morning even until noon, saying, O Baal, hear us. But there was no voice, nor any that answered. And they leaped upon the altar which was made"

### 6. Physical Lameness (Adjective / Participial)
- **2Sa 5:6** — "Except thou take away the blind and the lame, thou shalt not come in hither" (tagged H6452 in concordances; morphologically the derived adjective H6455)
