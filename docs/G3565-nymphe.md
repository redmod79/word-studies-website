## Lexicon Definition

From a primary but obsolete verb νύπτω (nýptō, "to veil as a bride"); a young married woman (as veiled), by implication a bride; also the younger wife in the household of her husband's parents, i.e., a daughter-in-law. The dual sense — "bride" and "daughter-in-law" — is built into the word itself and reflects the ancient household where the bride moved into her husband's father's house after marriage.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Daughter-in-Law (Household Relation)
The married woman in relation to her husband's parents. Used in synoptic sayings about household division.
- **Mat 10:35** — "the daughter in law against her mother in law"
- **Luk 12:53** — "the mother in law against her daughter in law, and the daughter in law against her mother in law"

### 2. Bride (Literal Wedding Context)
The veiled young wife in immediate wedding setting; paired with νυμφίος (bridegroom).
- **Jhn 3:29** — "He that hath the bride is the bridegroom"
- **Rev 18:23** — "the voice of the bridegroom and of the bride shall be heard no more at all in thee" (echoing Jer 7:34 / 16:9 / 25:10)

### 3. Bride (Eschatological / Symbolic)
The people of God presented as bride to the Lamb. Exclusive to Revelation.
- **Rev 21:2** — "new Jerusalem... prepared as a bride adorned for her husband"
- **Rev 21:9** — "I will shew thee the bride, the Lamb's wife"
- **Rev 22:17** — "the Spirit and the bride say, Come"
