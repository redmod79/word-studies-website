## Lexicon Definition

**Thayer's Greek Lexicon:**
λικμάω, λικμῷ: future λικμήσω; from λικμός, a winnowing-van:
1. **To winnow** — to cleanse away the chaff from grain by winnowing (Homer, Xenophon, Plutarch; also LXX)
2. **To scatter** — a sense unknown to secular authors; opposed to συνάγω (Jer 31:10; Isa 17:13; Amos 9:9)
3. **To crush to pieces, grind to powder** — τινά (Matt 21:44; Luke 20:18; cf. Dan 2:44 Theod.; Wis 11:19)

*Note: Sense 3 is unique to biblical Greek — classical authors do not use λικμάω for crushing. The LXX uses it for scattering nations (sense 2); the NT shifts to physical destruction (sense 3), echoing the stone of Dan 2:44.*

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Eschatological Judgment by the Rejected Stone
The word's only NT use is in Jesus' quotation/application of Ps 118:22 — the stone rejected by the builders becomes the cornerstone, and then an instrument of crushing judgment against those who oppose it.

- **Mat 21:44** — "And whosoever shall fall on this stone shall be broken: but on whomsoever it shall fall, it will grind him to powder."
- **Luke 20:18** — "Whosoever shall fall upon that stone shall be broken; but on whomsoever it shall fall, it will grind him to powder."

*The imagery draws on Dan 2:44 (the stone that smote the image becoming a great mountain, filling the earth). λικμάω captures the total reduction — not merely breaking but pulverizing.*
