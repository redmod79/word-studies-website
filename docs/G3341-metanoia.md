## Lexicon Definition

*From Strong's:* From G3340 (metanoeō); (subjectively) compunction for guilt, including reformation; by implication, reversal of another's decision. The noun form of metanoeō — capturing the state or disposition of changed thinking, not merely the act.

*Etymology:* A compound noun from μετά (after, implying change) and νοέω (to exercise the mind). As the noun form of the verb metanoeō, it denotes the settled condition or result of having changed one's mind — the posture of repentance rather than the moment of repenting. The word is fundamentally cognitive-volitional, not merely emotional.
