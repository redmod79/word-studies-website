## Lexicon Definition

A primitive root; to know (properly, to ascertain by seeing); used in a great variety of senses — figuratively, literally, euphemistically, and inferentially — including observation, care, recognition; and causatively, instruction, designation, punishment, etc. KJV glosses include: acknowledge, acquaintance, advise, answer, appoint, assuredly, be aware, (un-)awares, can(-not), certainly, comprehend, consider, cunning, declare, be diligent, (can/cause to) discern, discover, endued with, familiar friend, famous, feel, be (ig-)norant, instruct, kinsfolk, kinsman, (cause to let/make) know, knowledge, (be/make) known, be learned, lie by man, mark, perceive, privy to, prognosticator, regard, have respect, skilful, shew, skill, be sure, of a surety, teach, tell, understand, wist, wit, wot.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Cognitive Knowing — factual awareness, information
- **Gen 3:5** — "God doth know that in the day ye eat thereof…"
- **Gen 4:9** — "I know not: Am I my brother's keeper?"
- **Job 19:25** — "For I know that my redeemer liveth"
- **Ecc 9:5** — "the living know that they shall die: but the dead know not any thing"

### 2. Experiential / Relational Knowing — knowing persons, not just facts
- **Gen 18:19** — "For I know him, that he will command his children…" (God "knows" Abraham = chosen covenant partner)
- **Exo 33:17** — "I know thee by name"
- **Amos 3:2** — "You only have I known of all the families of the earth" (election sense)
- **Jer 1:5** — "Before I formed thee in the belly I knew thee"

### 3. Covenantal Recognition Formula — "they shall know that I am the LORD"
Ezekiel's signature phrase; also prominent in Exodus plague narratives and later prophets.
- **Exo 7:5** — "And the Egyptians shall know that I am the LORD"
- **Eze 6:7** — "and ye shall know that I am the LORD" (one of ~65 in Ezekiel)
- **Joe 3:17** — "So shall ye know that I am the LORD your God dwelling in Zion"
- **Zec 2:11** — "thou shalt know that the LORD of hosts hath sent me"

### 4. Sexual Knowing (Euphemism) — consummation of union
- **Gen 4:1** — "And Adam knew Eve his wife; and she conceived"
- **Gen 4:17, 25** — "Cain knew his wife"; "Adam knew his wife again"
- **Gen 19:5, 8** — Sodom: "bring them out unto us, that we may know them"; Lot: "[daughters] which have not known man"
- **Gen 24:16; 38:26** — "neither had any man known her"; "[Judah] knew her again no more"
- **Num 31:17-18, 35** — "every woman that hath known man by lying with him"; "that had not known a man"
- **Jdg 11:39; 19:25; 21:11-12** — Jephthah's daughter "knew no man"; Gibeah outrage; wives for Benjamin
- **1Ki 1:4** — "the king knew her not"

Total sexual-euphemism uses ~15 verses. The euphemism is always Qal, typically perfect, with a personal object; context (marriage, virginity, rape, offspring) disambiguates from cognitive senses.

### 5. Causative Revelation — "make known, declare, teach" (Hiphil)
- **Psa 25:4** — "Shew me thy ways, O LORD; teach me thy paths"
- **Psa 103:7** — "He made known his ways unto Moses"
- **Pro 1:23** — "I will make known my words unto you"
- **Dan 2:28** — "But there is a God in heaven that revealeth secrets, and maketh known…"

### 6. Skill / Expertise — practical competence
- **Gen 25:27** — "Esau was a cunning (yodea) hunter" (lit. "one knowing of hunting")
- **1Sa 16:18** — "cunning in playing" (of David)
- **2Ch 2:7, 12-14** — "a man cunning to work in gold"
- **Isa 29:11-12** — "one that is learned"

### 7. Perception / Discernment — noticing, considering, marking
- **Gen 19:33, 35** — Lot "perceived not" his daughters' actions
- **1Sa 3:7** — "Samuel did not yet know the LORD"
- **Psa 139:23** — "Search me, O God, and know my heart"
- **Pro 27:23** — "Be thou diligent to know the state of thy flocks"
