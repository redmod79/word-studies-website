## Key Grammatical Features

### 1. The four-verb sequence of the little horn's activity (v. 25)

The little horn's career against the Most High is narrated in **four yiqtol (imperfect) clauses** in strict sequence, three of them headed by a fronted non-subject element (object or prepositional complement) — the Aramaic parallel to Hebrew's **X-yiqtol** foregrounding construction.

1. **יְמַלִּל** (Pael impf. 3ms of מלל "to speak") — with fronted object מִלִּין ("words") and complement לְצַד עִלָּאָה ("against the Most High"). The fronting gives rhetorical weight to the object of his blasphemy: *words* are what he hurls, and *against the Most High* is where he aims them.
2. **יְבַלֵּא** (Pael impf. 3ms of בְּלָא "to wear out, exhaust") — with fronted complement לְקַדִּישֵׁי עֶלְיוֹנִין ("[against] the saints of the Most High"). See §2 below on the Pael of בלה/בלא.
3. **יִסְבַּר** (Peal impf. 3ms of סבר "to think, suppose, intend") + **InfC לְהַשְׁנָיָה** (Haphel infinitive construct of שׁנה "to change"). The lexeme סבר does *not* mean "succeed" — it means "suppose, plan, intend." The horn's intention, not its achievement, is what the verb reports.
4. **יִתְיַהֲבוּן** (Ithpeel/Hithpeel impf. 3mp of יהב "to give") — a passive (see §3 below) with durative prepositional phrase עַד־עִדָּן וְעִדָּנִין וּפְלַג עִדָּן.

### 2. Pael יְבַלֵּא — "wear out" (intensive-iterative)

- **Stem:** Pael (the D-stem in Aramaic, cognate to Hebrew Piel). In Biblical Aramaic the Pael typically carries an **intensive / iterative / factitive** force over against the simple Peal (G-stem).
- **Root:** בְּלָא / בלה "to wear out, decay, become old" (Peal is intransitive: "to wear away"). The Pael is transitive-factitive: "*cause to wear away* / wear (someone) down."
- **Sense:** Not a single-blow destruction but a **grinding, attritional** action — wearing the saints down across an extended span. This lexical-aspectual choice matches the prepositional complement that follows in clause 5: the "time and times and half a time" is not the span of a discrete act but the duration of a sustained oppression.
- **Parallel to the Greek:** LXX-Theodotion renders יְבַלֵּא with κατατρίψει (fut. act. 3s of κατατρίβω, "wear down by rubbing") — an exact lexical match for the intensive-attritional sense of the Pael.

### 3. Ithpeel יִתְיַהֲבוּן — the divine passive

- **Stem:** Ithpeel (the tG-stem in Aramaic, cognate to Hebrew Niphal/Hithpael). The prefix ית־ marks the passive/reflexive of the Peal. The Aramaic passive system is stem-based (Ithpeel ↔ Peal; Ithpaal ↔ Pael; Hophal ↔ Haphel), not a separate voice morpheme as in Greek.
- **Form:** יִתְיַהֲבוּן — ית־ passive prefix + יהב root + imperfect 3mp ending. The impf. 3mp is formally the impersonal / indefinite-subject construction the Aramaic translator reaches for when **the unnamed agent is God**: "they-shall-be-given (into his hand)" = "*it/he* shall be given by an unnamed agent." This is the classic **divine passive** (*passivum divinum*).
- **What is given:** Antecedent is ambiguous by design — either the *saints* (from v. 25 clause 2, יְבַלֵּא־object), or the *times and law* (clause 4 object), or *both*. The grammar does not resolve it; the effect is that whatever belongs to the Most High's sphere is conceded into the horn's hand for a fixed term.
- **Agent implied, not expressed:** The passive is not a statement that the horn *seizes*, but that *God permits*. This is essential to the theology of the vision: even the horn's period of ascendancy is handed to him by divine decree, bounded by a divine term.
- **Greek parallel:** Theodotion renders יִתְיַהֲבוּן with δοθήσεται (future passive 3s of δίδωμι) — "it shall be given." The same Greek verb, now as aorist passive ἐδόθη, is used six times in Revelation 13 (vv. 5 [twice], 7 [twice], 14, 15) for the beast's authority, mouth, war against the saints, and time-limit of 42 months. Rev 13 is **drawing directly on the Danielic template**: the same divine-passive diction, the same horn-against-saints action, the same bounded time.

### 4. The duration formula: עַד־עִדָּן וְעִדָּנִין וּפְלַג עִדָּן

- **Structure:** עַד ("until") + three asyndetically-sequenced time-units: (a) עִדָּן (ms sg. absolute, "a time"), (b) וְעִדָּנִין (mp absolute, "and times"), (c) וּפְלַג עִדָּן (construct chain: "and half of a time").
- **The middle term is plural, not dual.** Biblical Aramaic has a dual (for natural pairs — eyes, hands, etc.) but עִדָּנִין is the standard masculine *plural* ending, not the dual ־ַיִן. Reading the middle term as "two times" (to force 1 + 2 + ½ = 3½) is a Septuagint-Theodotion-KJV *interpretive* equation, not a grammatical entailment. The plural is formally indefinite — "a time, and times, and half a time" is what the Aramaic says; the numeric equivalence "3½ times" is an inference matched by Dan 12:7 and Rev 12:14 parallels.
- **Scope:** The prepositional phrase modifies **יִתְיַהֲבוּן** (clause 5 predicate), not the Pael יְבַלֵּא of clause 2 directly. Syntactically the duration is the duration of *the giving*, i.e., the term of the horn's permitted custody of what-is-given. But because clause 5 is the narrative *result* of clauses 1–4 (their object is what is given), the duration functionally bounds the whole sequence.
- **Parallel to Theodotion:** ἕως καιροῦ καὶ καιρῶν καὶ ἡμίσους καιροῦ — three genitive time-units after ἕως, with ἡμίσους ("half") genitive-modifying καιροῦ, exactly mirroring the Aramaic construct פְלַג עִדָּן.

### 5. Sequence reversal in v. 26: the judgment *follows* the time-times-half-time

This is the load-bearing grammatical observation of the passage.

- **Clause 1 of v. 26 is WXYq** (waw + subject דִינָא + yiqtol יִתִּב) — a *subject-fronted* yiqtol. In Aramaic narrative-prophetic discourse this clause type is **disjunctive / contrastive / scene-shifting**. The parser assigns it Type WXYq (capital X = subject in pre-verbal position), distinct from the V-initial WYq0 clauses of v. 25 clauses 3 and 5.
- **The fronted subject דִינָא** ("the judgment") is the new topic. The contrastive force is: *while* (v. 25) the saints are being worn out and handed over for the time-times-half-time, *but then* (v. 26) the judgment sits.
- **Temporal ordering is therefore explicit in the syntax:**
  1. wearing-out **begins** (v. 25 cl. 2, Pael impf.)
  2. saints/times/law **given into his hand** for time-times-half-time (v. 25 cl. 5, Ithpeel impf. + עַד)
  3. **THEN** (v. 26 cl. 1, WXYq disjunctive) the judgment **sits** (יִתִּב, Peal impf. 3ms of יתב "to sit")
  4. **THEN** (v. 26 cl. 2, WxY0 coordinate-sequential) they **take away** his dominion (יְהַעְדּוֹן, Haphel impf. 3mp of עדה "to pass over/cause to pass")
  5. purpose/result: to consume and destroy **unto the end** (v. 26 clauses 3–4, two parallel InfC of purpose + עַד־סוֹפָא)
- **Critical implication:** The Aramaic **does not** allow the judgment to precede the time-times-half-time. The disjunctive WXYq marks the judgment as the *next* scene after the horn's bounded term. The saints are *already* in the horn's hand when the judgment sits.
- **This is the template for Rev 13:5–10 and the Rev 12/13/14 sequence generally.** Rev 13:5 ἐδόθη αὐτῷ… ποιῆσαι μῆνας τεσσεράκοντα [καὶ] δύο is the precise Greek echo of Dan 7:25's יִתְיַהֲבוּן… עַד־עִדָּן וְעִדָּנִין וּפְלַג עִדָּן; the judgment-sits of Rev 14:7ff. corresponds to Dan 7:26's וְדִינָא יִתִּב.

### 6. The Haphel infinitives of destruction (v. 26)

- **לְהַשְׁמָדָה** (Haphel InfCst of שׁמד "to exterminate, annihilate") and **וּלְהוֹבָדָה** (Haphel InfCst of אבד "to perish," causative "to destroy") are two parallel **purpose-infinitives** governed by the finite verb יְהַעְדּוֹן of clause 2 ("they shall take away … in order to consume and destroy").
- **Haphel** is the Aramaic causative stem (cognate to Hebrew Hiphil). Both infinitives name *causative destruction* — not a passive letting-go but an active, decreed elimination.
- **עַד־סוֹפָא** ("unto the end") fixes the terminus: the destruction is complete, not partial. The emphatic-state suffix ־ָא on סוֹפָא is the Aramaic definite article.
- **Narrative contrast with v. 25's bounded term:** The horn's possession is bounded by עַד + time-units (a *duration*); the horn's destruction is bounded by עַד + סוֹפָא (an *endpoint*). The first is a limit; the second is a completion.
