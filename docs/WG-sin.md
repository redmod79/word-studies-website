# WG: Sin (Hebrew Sin-Terminology Cluster)

## Overview
The Hebrew Bible uses a cluster of overlapping but distinct terms to describe wrongdoing, each emphasizing a different facet of sin: missing the mark (châṭâʼ), crookedness/guilt-consequence (ʻâvôn), willful rebellion (peshaʻ), incurred liability (ʼâsham), and defiant resistance (mârâh). These words frequently appear together in confessional and covenant contexts, particularly in the triad pesha + avon + chattaah (Exo 34:7; Lev 16:21; Dan 9:24).

## Roles in the Concept

- **Missing / going astray:** châṭâʼ (H2398) — the broadest term; includes inadvertent sin
- **Crookedness / consequence:** ʻâvôn (H5771) — emphasizes moral distortion and its entailed guilt
- **Willful revolt:** peshaʻ (H6588) — deliberate rebellion against known authority
- **Incurred liability:** ʼâsham (H816) — the guilt-state requiring restitution
- **Defiant resistance:** mârâh (H4784) — bitter refusal to obey, provoking authority
- **The offering that addresses sin:** chaṭṭâʼâh (H2403) — both the offense and its ritual remedy
