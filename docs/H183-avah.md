## Lexicon Definition

A primitive root; to wish for — covet, (greatly) desire, be desirous, long, lust (after).

The verb denotes a strong inward inclination of will or appetite toward an object. It is morally neutral in itself: it can describe holy longing (Isa 26:9; Ps 132:13–14), legitimate craving (Deut 12:20; 14:26), or illicit lust (Num 11:4; Prov 21:10). The ethical valence is supplied by the object desired, not by the verb itself.

## Verb Stem Distribution

The verb occurs almost exclusively in two stems, each with a distinct nuance:

| Stem | Count | Typical Meaning |
|------|-------|-----------------|
| Piel | ~10 | to desire, long for (transitive; takes a direct object or cognate accusative) |
| Hithpael | ~15 | to crave, lust after (reflexive/intensive; the subject works up desire within himself) |

**Piel** is used when the focus is on the desired object: "thy soul desireth" (Deut 14:26, 1Ki 11:37), "the LORD hath desired Zion" (Ps 132:13–14), "with my soul have I desired thee" (Isa 26:9).

**Hithpael** conveys the reflexive working-up of appetite or craving, often with a negative moral tone: "fell a lusting" (Num 11:4), "neither shalt thou desire thy neighbour's wife" (Deut 5:21), "the soul of the sluggard desireth" (Prov 13:4), "woe unto you that desire the day of the LORD" (Amos 5:18).

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Permissive / Legitimate Appetite (esp. food)
- **Deut 12:20** — "thou shalt say, I will eat flesh, because thy soul longeth to eat flesh; thou mayest eat flesh, whatsoever thy soul lusteth after"
- **Deut 14:26** — "thou shalt bestow that money for whatsoever thy soul lusteth after, for oxen, or for sheep, or for wine, or for strong drink, or for whatsoever thy soul desireth"
- **1 Sam 2:16** — "give me flesh... whatsoever thy soul desireth"

In these texts the verb (Piel) designates ordinary, sanctioned craving — a desire God explicitly permits to be satisfied.

### 2. Royal / Political Desire
- **2 Sam 3:21** — "thou mayest reign over all that thine heart desireth"
- **1 Ki 11:37** — "thou shalt reign according to all that thy soul desireth, and shalt be king over Israel"

The verb describes the scope of legitimate ambition granted to a king.

### 3. Longing for Water / Physical Thirst
- **2 Sam 23:15 = 1 Chr 11:17** — "And David longed, and said, Oh that one would give me drink of the water of the well of Bethlehem"

Hithpael — intense, felt craving that breaks into speech.

### 4. Holy Longing for God / for Zion
- **Isa 26:9** — "With my soul have I desired thee in the night; yea, with my spirit within me will I seek thee early"
- **Ps 132:13** — "For the LORD hath chosen Zion; he hath desired it for his habitation"
- **Ps 132:14** — "This is my rest for ever: here will I dwell; for I have desired it"

The same verb that describes craving flesh in Numbers describes Yahweh's election-love for Zion and the saint's night-longing for God.

### 5. Illicit Lust / Covetousness
- **Num 11:4** — "the mixt multitude... fell a lusting"
- **Num 11:34** — "Kibrothhattaavah: because there they buried the people that lusted"
- **Deut 5:21** — "Neither shalt thou desire thy neighbour's wife, neither shalt thou covet thy neighbour's house"
- **Ps 106:14** — "But lusted exceedingly in the wilderness, and tempted God in the desert"

Hithpael stem, always with an illegitimate or out-of-bounds object. The wilderness rebellion is the paradigm.

### 6. Wicked / Foolish Desire in Wisdom Literature
- **Prov 13:4** — "The soul of the sluggard desireth, and hath nothing"
- **Prov 21:10** — "The soul of the wicked desireth evil"
- **Prov 21:26** — "He coveteth greedily all the day long"
- **Prov 23:3, 6** — "Be not desirous of his dainties... neither desire thou his dainty meats"
- **Prov 24:1** — "Be not thou envious against evil men, neither desire to be with them"
- **Job 23:13** — "what his soul desireth, even that he doeth" (of God's sovereign will)

### 7. Prophetic Complaint / Woe
- **Jer 17:16** — "neither have I desired the woeful day"
- **Amos 5:18** — "Woe unto you that desire the day of the LORD!"
- **Mic 7:1** — "my soul desired the firstripe fruit"
- **Ps 45:11 (MT 45:12)** — "So shall the king greatly desire thy beauty"

In the Decalogue, the parallel prohibition uses châmad ("thou shalt not covet," Exod 20:17 // Deut 5:21a), while Deut 5:21b uses ʼâvâh ("neither shalt thou desire thy neighbour's wife"). The pairing treats the two verbs as near-synonyms for the tenth commandment.

The LXX renders ʼâvâh almost uniformly with ἐπιθυμέω/ἐπιθυμία, the same verb/noun used in the NT Decalogue citation (Rom 7:7; 13:9) and in James 1:14–15 for the pattern of desire → sin → death. This establishes a direct Hebrew-to-Greek bridge from Num 11:4 (lusting in the wilderness) through 1 Cor 10:6 ("we should not lust after evil things, as they also lusted"), where Paul uses the cognate ἐπιθυμητής for the wilderness generation.
