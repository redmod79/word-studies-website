## Lexicon Definition

> Masculine plural of the same as [H1330]; (collectively and abstractly) virginity; by implication and concretely, the tokens of it: — maid, virginity.

The plural form is morphologically intensive/abstract (like *nᵉʻûrîm* "youth" from *naʻar*): it names the **state** of virginity, and by metonymy the **physical evidences** of that state (the bloodied garment of Deut 22:15-17).

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Abstract state of virginity (a woman's unmarried, sexually inexperienced condition)
- **Lev 21:13** — "And he shall take a wife in her virginity." (priestly marriage qualification)
- **Ezek 23:3** — "there were their breasts pressed, and there they bruised the teats of their virginity."
- **Ezek 23:8** — "in her youth they lay with her, and they bruised the breasts of her virginity."

### 2. Tokens / physical evidence of virginity (bloodied cloth after consummation)
- **Deut 22:15** — "the father... and her mother, take and bring forth the tokens of the damsel's virginity unto the elders."
- **Deut 22:17** — "These are the tokens of my daughter's virginity. And they shall spread the cloth before the elders of the city."
- **Deut 22:20** — "But if this thing be true, and the tokens of virginity be not found for the damsel."

### 3. "A maid" — the person characterized by this state (metonymy)
- **Deut 22:14** — "I took this woman, and when I came to her, I found her not a maid."
- **Deut 22:17** — "I found not thy daughter a maid."

### 4. Personal / lamented virginity (the state mourned when life is cut short before marriage)
- **Judges 11:37** — "let me alone two months, that I may go up and down upon the mountains, and bewail my virginity."
- **Judges 11:38** — "and bewailed her virginity upon the mountains."
