# WG: Profanation / Holy-Common Boundary

## Overview

The Hebrew vocabulary of profanation operates on the fundamental distinction between qodesh (holy/set apart) and chol (common/profane). Profanation is the act of moving something from the sacred sphere into the common sphere — treating what God has sanctified as though it were ordinary. This word group maps the verb that performs that crossing (chalal), the adjective that names the resulting state (chol), the closely related defilement vocabulary (tame, chaneph), and the antonym that defines the boundary (qodesh).

## Roles in the Concept

- **The boundary:** qodesh (קֹדֶשׁ) H6944 — defines what is sacred, set apart, withdrawn from common use
- **The state of the common:** chol (חֹל) H2455 — the non-sacred sphere; not inherently negative, but the opposite of qodesh
- **The transgression:** chalal (חָלַל) H2490 — the act of moving something from qodesh to chol; treating the sacred as common
- **The overlap (ritual):** tame (טָמֵא) H2930 — ritual impurity that can also defile sacred things, but operates by a different mechanism (contact/contagion vs. unauthorized use)
- **The overlap (moral):** chaneph (חָנֵף) H2610 — moral pollution, godlessness; affects the land and persons, not specifically cultic objects

## Key Passages Where Multiple Group Words Appear Together

- **Lev 10:10** — "And that ye may put difference between holy (qodesh) and unholy (chol), and between unclean (tame) and clean" — the foundational priestly instruction establishing the two axes: holy/common and clean/unclean
- **Lev 20:3** — "because he hath given of his seed unto Molech, to defile (tame) my sanctuary (miqdash), and to profane (chalal) my holy (qodesh) name" — both defilement types in one verse
- **Eze 22:26** — "Her priests have violated my law, and have profaned (chalal) mine holy things (qodesh): they have put no difference between the holy (qodesh) and profane (chol), neither have they shewed difference between the unclean (tame) and the clean" — the most comprehensive single verse, naming chalal, qodesh, and chol together with tame
- **Eze 44:23** — "And they shall teach my people the difference between the holy (qodesh) and profane (chol), and cause them to discern between the unclean (tame) and the clean" — the eschatological restoration of the Lev 10:10 distinction
