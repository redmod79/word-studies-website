## Semantic Categories

### 1. Disciplinary Destruction of the Flesh
Punitive but redemptive — destruction of the sinful flesh as a corrective measure aimed at spiritual salvation.
- **1Co 5:5** — "To deliver such an one unto Satan for the destruction (ὄλεθρος) of the flesh, that the spirit may be saved in the day of the Lord Jesus."

### 2. Sudden Eschatological Destruction
Unexpected ruin overtaking the unprepared at the Day of the Lord — likened to labor pains: sudden, inescapable.
- **1Th 5:3** — "For when they shall say, Peace and safety; then sudden destruction (ὄλεθρος) cometh upon them, as travail upon a woman with child; and they shall not escape."

### 3. Everlasting Destruction from God's Presence
Final and eternal exclusion from the Lord — the ultimate consequence of rejecting the gospel.
- **2Th 1:9** — "Who shall be punished with everlasting destruction (ὄλεθρος) from the presence of the Lord, and from the glory of his power."

### 4. Destruction Paired with Perdition
Catastrophic ruin linked to covetousness and sinful desire — ὄλεθρος and ἀπώλεια (perdition) appear together as twin consequences.
- **1Ti 6:9** — "But they that will be rich fall into temptation and a snare, and into many foolish and hurtful lusts, which drown men in destruction (ὄλεθρος) and perdition (ἀπώλεια)."
