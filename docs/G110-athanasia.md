## Semantic Categories

### 1. Eschatological Transformation at the Resurrection
- **1 Cor 15:53** — "For this corruptible must put on incorruption, and this mortal [must] put on immortality."
- **1 Cor 15:54** — "So when this corruptible shall have put on incorruption, and this mortal shall have put on immortality, then shall be brought to pass the saying that is written, Death is swallowed up in victory."

Both occurrences use the verb ἐνδύω (endyo, "put on" as a garment). Immortality is something mortal beings do not presently possess but will receive — depicted as clothing the mortal body at the resurrection.

### 2. Exclusive Divine Attribute
- **1 Tim 6:16** — "Who only hath immortality, dwelling in the light which no man can approach unto; whom no man hath seen, nor can see: to whom [be] honour and power everlasting. Amen."

Here athanasia is predicated of God alone (μόνος, "only"). It is presented as an inherent attribute of deity, distinguished from all created beings.
