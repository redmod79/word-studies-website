## Lexicon Definition

From G4931 (συντελέω); entire completion, i.e. consummation (of a dispensation). Denotes a joint or full end, a bringing to completion together — not merely cessation but a culminating endpoint that completes a purpose.

**Outline of Biblical Usage:**
1. Completion, consummation, end — specifically of an age or dispensation
2. Used in the LXX of Daniel (9:27; 12:4, 13) for the consummation of prophetic periods

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Eschatological Consummation — The Harvest / Judgment Parables
The three occurrences in Matthew 13 all frame the end of the age as a harvest-event involving judgment, separation of righteous and wicked, and angelic agency.
- **Matthew 13:39** — "The enemy that sowed them is the devil; the harvest is the end of the world; and the reapers are the angels."
- **Matthew 13:40** — "As therefore the tares are gathered and burned in the fire; so shall it be in the end of this world."
- **Matthew 13:49** — "So shall it be at the end of the world: the angels shall come forth, and sever the wicked from among the just."

### 2. The Disciples' Inquiry — The Signs of the End
The Olivet Discourse opens with the disciples asking about the timing and signs of the συντέλεια.
- **Matthew 24:3** — "Tell us, when shall these things be? and what shall be the sign of thy coming, and of the end of the world?"

### 3. Christ's Abiding Presence to the End
Jesus' final promise in Matthew links his ongoing presence to the duration reaching the consummation.
- **Matthew 28:20** — "Teaching them to observe all things whatsoever I have commanded you: and, lo, I am with you alway, even unto the end of the world. Amen."

### 4. The Climax of Redemptive History — Christ's Once-for-All Sacrifice
Hebrews uses συντέλεια to locate Christ's incarnation and sacrifice at the decisive turning-point of history, contrasting it with what would have been required if his sacrifice were not final.
- **Hebrews 9:26** — "For then must he often have suffered since the foundation of the world: but now once in the end of the world hath he appeared to put away sin by the sacrifice of himself."
