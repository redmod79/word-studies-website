## Lexicon Definition

A primary verb meaning to nourish, feed, or bring up. The semantic range spans:
1. Physical sustenance — providing food to sustain life
2. Rearing/nurturing — bringing up a child to maturity
3. Ongoing provision — sustained care over time

The root idea involves active, ongoing provision for life and growth, distinct from a single meal (which would use ἐσθίω/esthio) or pastoral shepherding (ποιμαίνω/poimaino).

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Divine Provision for Creation
God as the one who feeds/sustains living creatures.
- **Mat 6:26** — "Behold the fowls of the air: for they sow not, neither do they reap, nor gather into barns; yet your heavenly Father **feedeth** them."
- **Luk 12:24** — "Consider the ravens: for they neither sow nor reap; which neither have storehouse nor barn; and God **feedeth** them."

### 2. Human Acts of Feeding/Charity
Providing food as an act of mercy or care.
- **Mat 25:37** — "Then shall the righteous answer him, saying, Lord, when saw we thee an hungred, and **fed** thee? or thirsty, and gave thee drink?"

### 3. Rearing/Bringing Up
Nurturing a child to maturity (extended period, not a single meal).
- **Luk 4:16** — "And he came to Nazareth, where he had been **brought up**: and, as his custom was, he went into the synagogue on the sabbath day."

### 4. Economic/Political Sustenance
Ongoing provision of resources between parties.
- **Act 12:20** — "And Herod was highly displeased with them of Tyre and Sidon: but they came with one accord to him... because their country **was nourished** by the king's country."

### 5. Self-Indulgent Nourishing
Luxurious feeding of oneself (negative connotation).
- **Jas 5:5** — "Ye have lived in pleasure on the earth, and been wanton; **ye have nourished** your hearts, as in a day of slaughter."

### 6. Divine Protection in the Wilderness
God sustaining His people through extended periods of trial.
- **Rev 12:6** — "And the woman fled into the wilderness, where she hath a place prepared of God, that **they should feed** her there a thousand two hundred and threescore days."
- **Rev 12:14** — "And to the woman were given two wings of a great eagle, that she might fly into the wilderness, into her place, where **she is nourished** for a time, and times, and half a time, from the face of the serpent."

## Theological Note on Revelation 12:6, 14

The two occurrences in Revelation 12 use τρέφω to describe identical events with different time expressions:
- **Rev 12:6:** "1260 days" (ἡμέρας χιλίας διακοσίας ἑξήκοντα)
- **Rev 12:14:** "time, times, and half a time" (καιρὸν καὶ καιροὺς καὶ ἥμισυ καιροῦ)

This parallel confirms the prophetic equivalence: 1260 days = time + times + half a time = 3.5 prophetic years. The verb τρέφω emphasizes sustained divine care throughout this entire period, not merely protection but active provision — recalling Israel's wilderness experience where God fed (τρέφω in LXX Exod 16:32; Deut 8:3, 16) the nation with manna for 40 years.
