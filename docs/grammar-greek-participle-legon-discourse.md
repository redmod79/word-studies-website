# Present Participle λέγων as Discourse Connector (Greek)

## Definition

The present active participle of λέγω (G3004) — λέγων (masc. sg.), λέγουσα (fem. sg.), λέγοντες (masc. pl.), λέγοντος (gen. sg.) — is the most common speech-introducing participle in the NT. It connects a verb of communication, motion, or perception to the direct or indirect speech that follows. The speech content introduced by λέγων is grammatically a **content clause** specifying *what* is said.

1. **Recitative (content-specifying)** — introduces direct speech that gives the content of the main verb's action
2. **Attendant circumstance** — accompanies a main verb, describing how or in what manner the action occurred
3. **Prophetic/formulaic citation** — genitive absolute form (λέγοντος) introducing scriptural quotations

## Form Recognition

- **Morph code pattern:** `V-PAP-NSM` (verb, present, active, participle, nominative singular masculine)
- **Key inflected forms:** λέγων (NSM), λέγουσα (NSF), λέγοντες (NPM), λέγοντος (GSM)
- **Lemma:** λέγω (G3004)
- **Parser search:** `mood=participle tense=present` with lemma λέγω
- **Syntactic marker:** almost always immediately precedes direct or indirect speech

## Functions with Examples

### 1. Recitative / Content-Specifying Participle

The dominant function. λέγων follows a verb of speaking, commanding, proclaiming, or crying out, and introduces the actual words spoken. The content after λέγων gives *what* was said — it is not a separate action but the substance of the main verb's activity.

| Reference | Greek Construction | Morph Code | KJV |
|-----------|-------------------|------------|-----|
| Matt 3:1-2 | κηρύσσων ... λέγων Μετανοεῖτε | V-PAP-NSM | "preaching ... and saying, Repent ye" |
| Mark 1:15 | λέγων ὅτι Πεπλήρωται ὁ καιρός ... μετανοεῖτε | V-PAP-NSM | "saying, The time is fulfilled ... repent ye" |
| Matt 2:13 | φαίνεται ... λέγων Ἐγερθεὶς παράλαβε | V-PAP-NSM | "appeareth ... saying, Arise, and take" |
| Acts 2:40 | παρεκάλει αὐτοὺς λέγων Σώθητε | V-PAP-NSM | "exhort, saying, Save yourselves" |
| Luke 4:35 | ἐπετίμησεν αὐτῷ ... λέγων Φιμώθητι | V-PAP-NSM | "rebuked him, saying, Hold thy peace" |
| Rev 18:2 | ἔκραξεν ἐν ἰσχυρᾷ φωνῇ λέγων Ἔπεσεν | V-PAP-NSM | "cried mightily ... saying, Babylon ... is fallen" |

**Diagnostic:** When λέγων follows a verb of communication (κηρύσσω, παρακαλέω, ἐπιτιμάω, κράζω, etc.), the speech that follows is the *content* of that communication. The participle functions like a colon or quotation mark in English.

**Note on ὅτι recitativum:** In Mark 1:15, λέγων is followed by ὅτι before the quoted speech. This "recitative ὅτι" is redundant with λέγων — both signal that content follows. The presence of ὅτι makes the content-clause function explicit, but λέγων serves the same function even without ὅτι.

### 2. Attendant Circumstance / Manner

Less common for λέγων specifically, but present participles in general can describe the manner or accompanying circumstances of the main action. With λέγω, this occurs when the saying accompanies but does not define the main action.

| Reference | Greek Construction | Morph Code | KJV |
|-----------|-------------------|------------|-----|
| Matt 8:2 | προσεκύνει αὐτῷ λέγων Κύριε | V-PAP-NSM | "worshipped him, saying, Lord..." |
| Matt 5:2 | ἐδίδασκεν αὐτοὺς λέγων | V-PAP-NSM | "taught them, saying" |

**Diagnostic:** When the main verb already describes an action (worshipping, teaching), λέγων adds the accompanying speech. But even here the speech content is typically what fills out the main verb's meaning — the leper's worship consists in his request, the teaching consists in what follows.

### 3. Prophetic Citation Formula (Genitive Absolute)

The genitive form λέγοντος introduces OT quotations through the formula "spoken through the prophet, saying."

| Reference | Greek Construction | Morph Code | KJV |
|-----------|-------------------|------------|-----|
| Matt 1:22 | διὰ τοῦ προφήτου λέγοντος | V-PAP-GSM | "by the prophet, saying" |
| Matt 2:15 | διὰ τοῦ προφήτου λέγοντος | V-PAP-GSM | "by the prophet, saying" |

### 4. Feminine/Neuter Agreement with Non-Personal Subjects

When the subject is grammatically feminine (φωνή, "voice") or neuter, the participle agrees: λέγουσα (NSF).

| Reference | Greek Construction | Morph Code | KJV |
|-----------|-------------------|------------|-----|
| Matt 3:17 | φωνὴ ἐκ τῶν οὐρανῶν λέγουσα Οὗτός ἐστιν | V-PAP-NSF | "a voice from heaven, saying, This is" |
| Rev 4:1 | φωνὴ ... λαλούσης μετ' ἐμοῦ, λέγων Ἀνάβα | V-PAP-NSM | "voice ... which said, Come up hither" |

**Note:** Rev 4:1 is grammatically notable: φωνή is feminine, but λέγων appears as masculine (constructio ad sensum — the speaker behind the voice is conceived as masculine).

## Structural Pattern in Revelation 14:6-9

All three angels' messages share the same grammatical construction:

| Angel | Main Verb | Participle | Content Type |
|-------|-----------|------------|-------------|
| First (14:7) | ἔχοντα εὐαγγέλιον ... εὐαγγελίσαι (14:6) | λέγων ἐν φωνῇ μεγάλῃ | Imperatives: Φοβήθητε, δότε, προσκυνήσατε |
| Second (14:8) | ἠκολούθησεν | λέγων | Declarations: Ἔπεσεν ἔπεσεν Βαβυλών |
| Third (14:9) | ἠκολούθησεν ... λέγων ἐν φωνῇ μεγάλῃ | λέγων | Conditional: Εἴ τις προσκυνεῖ τὸ θηρίον |

In Rev 14:6-7, the chain is: angel → ἔχοντα εὐαγγέλιον αἰώνιον (having an everlasting gospel) → εὐαγγελίσαι (to proclaim as good news) → λέγων (saying). The participle λέγων specifies the content of the εὐαγγελίσαι proclamation. The parallel with Matt 3:1-2 (κηρύσσων ... λέγων, "preaching ... saying") and Mark 1:14-15 (κηρύσσων τὸ εὐαγγέλιον ... λέγων, "preaching the gospel ... saying") is structurally exact: the participle introduces the substance of the proclamation.

## Contrast with Related Forms

| Form | Function | Example |
|------|----------|---------|
| λέγων (present participle) | Introduces speech content as ongoing/connected to main verb | Matt 3:2 |
| εἰπών (aorist participle) | Introduces speech as prior/completed action | Less common as speech-introducer |
| λέγει (present indicative) | Independent speech act, historical present | Matt 4:6 |
| εἶπεν (aorist indicative) | Reports speech as completed past event | Matt 2:5 |
| ὅτι (recitative conjunction) | Marks beginning of quoted speech (sometimes after λέγων) | Mark 1:15 |

The present tense of λέγων is significant: the speaking is portrayed as simultaneous with or unfolding from the main verb. It is not a completed speech act (which would use εἰπών) but an ongoing articulation of the main verb's content.
