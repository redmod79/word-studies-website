## Semantic Categories

### 1. Corporate Purging — Removing Moral Corruption from the Community
- **1 Cor 5:7** — "Purge out therefore the old leaven, that ye may be a new lump, as ye are unleavened. For even Christ our passover is sacrificed for us."

The church at Corinth is commanded to expel corrupting influence (the "old leaven" — a member practicing sexual immorality, per 5:1). The ek- prefix reinforces thoroughness: not merely cleanse but cleanse out entirely. The Passover metaphor ties the purging to Israel's removal of all leaven before the feast (Exod 12:15).

### 2. Individual Self-Purging — Separating from Dishonorable Associations
- **2 Tim 2:21** — "If a man therefore purge himself from these, he shall be a vessel unto honour, sanctified, and meet for the master's use, and prepared unto every good work."

Here the subject purges himself. The "these" refers to the dishonorable vessels / false teachers of 2:17–20. The result is fitness for the Master's use — a vessel set apart (sanctified) for honorable service.
