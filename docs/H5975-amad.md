## Lexicon Definition

A primitive root: to stand, in various relations (literal and figurative, intransitive and transitive) — rendered: abide (behind), appoint, arise, cease, confirm, continue, dwell, be employed, endure, establish, leave, make, ordain, be (over), place, (be) present (self), raise up, remain, repair, serve, set (forth, over, -tle, up), (make to, make to be at a, with-) stand (by, fast, firm, still, up), (be at a) stay (up), tarry.

The verb has a wide semantic range but a common core: assuming or maintaining an upright, established, fixed position. That position may be physical (standing on one's feet), relational (standing before someone in service or in hostility), durational (enduring, continuing, remaining), or institutional (being set up, appointed, installed). In the Hiphil the verb is causative — "cause to stand," hence "appoint, station, establish." The root is closely related semantically to H6965 קוּם (qûm, to rise up), H5324 נָצַב (nâtsab, to station), and H3320 יָצַב (yâtsab, to take one's stand) — these four verbs form the core "stand / take a stand" field in Biblical Hebrew.

## Semantic Categories

Derived from scanning the 497 unique KJV verses across all 36 books.

### 1. Physical Standing (Posture / Location)

The verb's base sense — assuming or maintaining an upright bodily posture, usually in a specific location.

- **Gen 18:8** — "he stood by them under the tree" (Abraham standing to serve)
- **Gen 24:30** — "he stood by the camels at the well" (Laban)
- **Ex 33:10** — "the cloudy pillar stood at the tabernacle door"
- **Judg 4:20** — "Stand in the door of the tent"
- **Neh 8:5** — "when he opened [the book], all the people stood up"
- **Dan 8:3** — "there stood before the river a ram which had two horns"

### 2. Standing Before Someone (Service, Presence, Attendance)

The verb in collocation with לִפְנֵי *liphnê* "before the face of" — to stand in the presence of a superior, typically denoting service, ministry, or audience.

- **Gen 18:22** — "Abraham stood yet before the LORD"
- **Deut 1:38** — "Joshua… which standeth before thee, he shall go in thither"
- **1 Sam 16:22** — "Let David, I pray thee, stand before me" (as Saul's attendant)
- **1 Kgs 17:1** — "the LORD God of Israel liveth, before whom I stand" (Elijah)
- **1 Kgs 22:21** — "there came forth a spirit, and stood before the LORD"
- **Jer 15:1** — "Though Moses and Samuel stood before me"
- **Zec 3:1** — "Joshua the high priest standing before the angel of the LORD"

### 3. Priestly / Levitical Cultic Standing (Service at the Sanctuary)

A technical use: ministers standing in the sanctuary to officiate. The participle "them that stand" functions as a standing title for the serving order.

- **Num 3:6** — "Bring the tribe of Levi near, and present them before Aaron the priest"
- **Deut 10:8** — "to stand before the LORD to minister unto him, and to bless in his name"
- **Deut 18:5, 7** — "to stand to minister in the name of the LORD"
- **1 Chr 6:31–33** — Levites whom David "set over the service of song… they waited with their children"
- **2 Chr 29:11** — "the LORD hath chosen you to stand before him, to serve him"
- **Eze 44:11** — "they shall stand before them to minister unto them"

### 4. Standing Before YHWH / Before the Angelic Court

A specialized extension of category 2 — standing in the heavenly throne-council, usually of angelic beings, and in Daniel of the visionary encountering the angelic presence.

- **1 Kgs 22:19** — "all the host of heaven standing by him on his right hand"
- **Isa 6:2** — "Above it stood the seraphims"
- **Zec 1:8, 10, 11** — "a man… he stood among the myrtle trees… the angel of the LORD that stood among the myrtle trees"
- **Zec 4:14** — "These are the two anointed ones, that stand by the Lord of the whole earth"
- **Dan 8:15** — "there stood before me as the appearance of a man" (Gabriel)
- **Dan 10:11** — "stand upright, for unto thee am I now sent"
- **Dan 12:1** — "Michael… the great prince which standeth for the children of thy people"
- **Dan 12:5** — "behold, there stood other two, the one on this side of the bank of the river"

### 5. Standing Up of Kings / Kingdoms (Political Accession)

A distinct political idiom centered in Daniel: a kingdom or ruler "stands up" at the moment of rising to power or taking the throne. Used repeatedly of successive world empires and of the "king of fierce countenance."

- **Dan 8:22** — "four stood up for it, four kingdoms shall stand up out of the nation"
- **Dan 8:23** — "a king of fierce countenance… shall stand up"
- **Dan 11:2** — "there shall stand up yet three kings in Persia"
- **Dan 11:3** — "a mighty king shall stand up"
- **Dan 11:4** — "when he shall stand up, his kingdom shall be broken"
- **Dan 11:7, 20, 21** — successive rulers "stand up in his estate"
- **Dan 11:14** — "there shall many stand up against the king of the south"
- **Dan 12:1** — "at that time shall Michael stand up"

### 6. Enduring / Remaining / Continuing (Durative)

When the object of "standing" is time or permanence, the verb shades into "remain, endure, continue." Common in wisdom and prophetic contexts about what survives or lasts.

- **Ps 19:9** — "the fear of the LORD is clean, enduring for ever"
- **Ps 33:11** — "the counsel of the LORD standeth for ever"
- **Ps 102:26** — "They shall perish, but thou shalt endure"
- **Ps 111:3** — "his righteousness endureth for ever"
- **Ecc 1:4** — "the earth abideth for ever"
- **Prov 12:7** — "the house of the righteous shall stand"
- **Isa 40:8** — [cognate sense] "the word of our God shall stand for ever" (here H6965, but parallel idiom)

### 7. Taking a Stand / Withstanding (Resistance)

Either one's own stand in battle/judgment, or an opponent's inability to stand. Often with נֶגֶד or לִפְנֵי.

- **Josh 10:8** — "there shall not a man of them stand before thee"
- **Nah 1:6** — "Who can stand before his indignation?"
- **Mal 3:2** — "who shall stand when he appeareth?"
- **Dan 8:4** — "no beasts might stand before him" (ram)
- **Dan 8:7** — "there was no power in the ram to stand before him" (goat)
- **Dan 10:13** — "the prince of the kingdom of Persia withstood me one and twenty days"
- **Dan 11:16** — "none shall stand before him"

### 8. Hiphil "Cause to Stand" — Appoint, Station, Set Up, Establish

The causative stem carries the institutional senses: installing a person in office, stationing a guard, erecting an object, confirming a covenant, setting one on one's feet.

- **Gen 47:7** — "Joseph brought in Jacob his father, and set him before Pharaoh"
- **Lev 14:11** — "the priest… shall present… the man… before the LORD"
- **Num 27:22** — Moses "set [Joshua] before Eleazar the priest"
- **1 Kgs 12:32** — Jeroboam "ordained a feast… and he placed in Bethel the priests"
- **Neh 6:7** — "appointed prophets to preach"
- **Ps 33:9** — "he commanded, and it stood fast" (Hiphil — "he caused it to stand")
- **Dan 8:18** — "he touched me, and set me upright" (way-yaʿămîdēnî)
- **Dan 11:11, 13** — king of the north "shall set forth a great multitude"
- **Dan 11:31** — "arms shall stand on his part" (Qal here, but paired with Hiphil of *naph'l* "take away the daily")

- The top three collocations (ʻal, pânîym, YHWH) together establish the verb's core usage domain: **standing in a position before someone**. - The *râʼâh* collocation (47x) generates the visionary formula "I saw, and behold, [X] stood" — foundational for Daniel 7–12 and Zechariah's night visions.
