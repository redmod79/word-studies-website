# WG: Appearing / Manifestation of Christ

## Overview
The NT uses a cluster of words to express the appearing, manifestation, and visible disclosure of Christ — both his first coming and his expected return. The concept blends visual-disclosure language (the word becomes visible/bright) with royal-advent language (the king arrives in public splendor).

## Roles in the Concept

- **ἐπιφάνεια (G2015):** The noun of visible appearance — emphasizes the *luminous, visual* quality of the divine appearing; used exclusively of Christ's advents in the Pastoral Epistles
- **ἐπιφαίνω (G2014):** The verb form — to shine upon, to become visible; used of God's grace and salvation appearing (Tit 2:11, 3:4), and of physical light (Acts 27:20, Luke 1:79)
- **παρουσία (G3952):** Official royal arrival/presence — the *technical* Second Coming term; focuses on the fact of his being-present rather than the visual splendor; co-occurs with ἐπιφάνεια in 2Th 2:8
- **φανερόω (G5319):** To render visible/manifest — broader than ἐπιφάνεια; applies to the incarnation ("manifested in the flesh"), to final disclosure, and to making hidden things apparent

## Semantic Distinctions

| Word | Core Nuance | Realm |
|------|------------|-------|
| ἐπιφάνεια | Radiant, visible appearing | Visual/luminous; royal splendor |
| παρουσία | Presence / official arrival | Royal advent; being-here |
| φανερόω | Make visible, disclose | Broad manifestation; revelation of hidden things |
| ἐπιφαίνω | Shine upon, appear | Divine light dawning on people |

**Key distinction:** In 2Th 2:8, Paul uses both ἐπιφάνεια and παρουσία for the same event ("the brightness [ἐπιφάνεια] of his coming [παρουσία]"). This shows they are complementary, not synonymous: παρουσία is the arrival-event; ἐπιφάνεια is the blinding splendor of it.

## Key Passages Where Multiple Group Words Appear Together
