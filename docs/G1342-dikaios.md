## Semantic Categories

### 1. Righteous Character of God and Christ
God and Christ are described as dikaios — their nature is the standard of all righteousness.
- **John 17:25** — "O righteous Father, the world hath not known thee"
- **1 John 1:9** — "he is faithful and just to forgive us our sins, and to cleanse us from all unrighteousness"
- **Rev 16:5** — "Thou art righteous, O Lord, which art, and wast"
- **Rev 19:2** — "true and righteous are his judgments: for he hath judged the great whore"

### 2. Christ as "The Just One" (Messianic Title)
A specific titular use — Jesus identified by name as the Just One, contrasted with a murderer or sinners.
- **Acts 3:14** — "ye denied the Holy One and the Just, and desired a murderer"
- **Acts 7:52** — "they have slain them which shewed before of the coming of the Just One"
- **Acts 22:14** — "that thou shouldest...see that Just One"
- **1 Pet 3:18** — "Christ also hath once suffered for sins, the just for the unjust"

### 3. The Righteous Person — Godly Character and Conduct
Applied to individuals recognized as righteous before God, often paired with devotion and obedience.
- **Mat 1:19** — Joseph "being a just [man]"
- **Luke 1:6** — Zacharias and Elizabeth "both righteous before God, walking in all the commandments"
- **Luke 2:25** — Simeon "the same man was just and devout"
- **Mark 6:20** — Herod knew John "was a just man and an holy"

### 4. Righteous vs. Sinners — The Division of Humanity
Dikaios used contrastively against sinners (hamartōlós), the wicked, or the unjust.
- **Mat 9:13** — "I am not come to call the righteous, but sinners to repentance"
- **Mat 13:49** — "the angels shall come forth, and sever the wicked from among the just"
- **Rom 3:10** — "There is none righteous, no, not one"
- **Luke 15:7** — "ninety and nine just persons, which need no repentance"

### 5. "The Just Shall Live by Faith" — OT Quotation (Hab 2:4)
A repeated NT quotation from Habakkuk; the strongest direct chain from Hebrew tsaddiq through LXX dikaios into NT theology.
- **Rom 1:17** — "as it is written, The just shall live by faith"
- **Gal 3:11** — "the just shall live by faith"
- **Heb 10:38** — "the just shall live by faith: but if any man draw back..."

### 6. Just / Right Actions and Judgments
Dikaios applied to actions, decisions, or ordinances that conform to what is right.
- **John 5:30** — "my judgment is just; because I seek not mine own will"
- **John 7:24** — "judge not according to the appearance, but judge righteous judgment"
- **Col 4:1** — "Masters, give unto your servants that which is just and equal"
- **2 Thess 1:6** — "it is a righteous thing with God to recompense tribulation"
