# WG: Acceptance / Divine Pleasure

## Overview
The acceptance concept in Hebrew Scripture centres on God evaluating and receiving (or rejecting) offerings, persons, and conduct. It involves the verb of evaluation (râtsâh), its nominal result (râtsôwn), a near-synonym for delight (châphêts), and the primary offering types that serve as objects of acceptance.

## Roles in the Concept

- **Evaluator:** râtsâh (רָצָה H7521) — the act of evaluating and finding acceptable or unacceptable
- **State:** râtsôwn (רָצוֹן H7522) — the resulting condition of favour, acceptance, or goodwill
- **Synonym:** châphêts (חָפֵץ H2654) — parallel evaluative verb, emphasizing inclination/desire
- **Object (grain):** minchâh (מִנְחָה H4503) — the grain/meal offering presented for acceptance
- **Object (burnt):** ʻôlâh (עֹלָה H5930) — the whole burnt offering presented for acceptance
- **Confused:** nâsâʼ (נָשָׂא H5375) — "accept" in the sense of "lift up/bear" (overlaps in KJV translation but different mechanism: bearing vs. evaluating)
