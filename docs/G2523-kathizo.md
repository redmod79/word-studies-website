## Lexicon Definition

From Strong's: "another (active) form for καθέζομαι; to seat down, i.e. set (figuratively, appoint); intransitively, to sit (down); by implication, to dwell, to pass time; specially, to take up a formal position (as judge or teacher), to settle down firmly or permanently." Functions both transitively ("cause to sit, seat, enthrone") and intransitively ("sit down, take one's seat").

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Enthronement of Christ at God's Right Hand
A specialized post-resurrection usage locating the exalted Christ at the Father's right hand, typically drawing on Ps 110:1.
- **Mar 16:19** — "he was received up into heaven, and sat (ekathisen) on the right hand of God"
- **Eph 1:20** — "raised him from the dead, and set him (kathisas) at his own right hand in the heavenly places" (transitive — God seats Christ)
- **Heb 1:3** — "sat down (ekathisen) on the right hand of the Majesty on high"
- **Heb 8:1** — "an high priest, who is set (ekathisen) on the right hand of the throne of the Majesty"
- **Heb 10:12** — "sat down (ekathisen) on the right hand of God"
- **Heb 12:2** — "is set down (kekathiken) at the right hand of the throne of God"

### 2. Thrones Promised to the Faithful / Saints
Christ extends the enthronement motif to his followers, who are promised seated authority.
- **Mat 19:28** — "ye also shall sit (kathisesthe) upon twelve thrones, judging the twelve tribes of Israel"
- **Luk 22:30** — "sit (kathesthe) on thrones judging the twelve tribes of Israel"
- **Rev 3:21** — "will I grant to sit (kathisai) with me in my throne, even as I also overcame, and am set down (ekathisa) with my Father in his throne"
- **Rev 20:4** — "I saw thrones, and they sat upon them (ekathisan), and judgment was given unto them"

### 3. Usurped / Contested Enthronement
Negative instances of seating oneself to claim authority.
- **2Th 2:4** — "so that he as God sitteth (kathisai, aor. inf.) in the temple of God, shewing himself that he is God"
- **Act 12:21** — "Herod, arrayed in royal apparel, sat (kathisas) upon his throne, and made an oration"
- **Mat 20:21, 23 / Mar 10:37, 40** — James and John request to "sit" (kathisōsin / kathisai) at Christ's right and left in his kingdom

### 4. Judicial Seating (Moses' Seat / Bēma)
The verb registers formal judicial authority, often paired with βῆμα ("judgment seat") or "seat of Moses."
- **Mat 23:2** — "The scribes and the Pharisees sit (ekathisan) in Moses' seat"
- **Jhn 19:13** — "[Pilate] sat down (ekathisen) in the judgment seat (bēma) in a place called the Pavement"
- **Act 25:6** — "sitting (kathisas) on the judgment seat (bēma) commanded Paul to be brought"
- **Act 25:17** — "I sat (kathisas) on the judgment seat, and commanded the man to be brought forth"
- **1Co 6:4** — "set (kathizete) them to judge who are least esteemed in the church" (causative — "appoint as judges")

### 5. Seating to Teach (Rabbinic Posture)
A teacher sat to deliver authoritative instruction; katheizō carries this formal register.
- **Mat 5:1** — "he went up into a mountain: and when he was set (kathisantos), his disciples came"
- **Luk 4:20** — "he closed the book… and sat down (ekathisen). And the eyes of all them… were fastened on him"
- **Luk 5:3** — "he sat down (kathisas), and taught the people out of the ship"
- **Jhn 8:2** — "he sat down (kathisas), and taught them" (temple)
- **Mar 9:35** — "he sat down (kathisas), and called the twelve, and saith unto them…"

### 6. General Physical Sitting / Taking a Seat
Ordinary narrative usage with no special authority coloring.
- **Mat 13:48** — "drew to shore, and sat down (kathisantes), and gathered the good into vessels"
- **Mat 26:36 / Mar 14:32** — "Sit ye (kathisate) here, while I go and pray"
- **Mar 11:7 / Jhn 12:14** — Jesus "sat upon" (ekathisen) the colt at the Triumphal Entry
- **Mar 12:41** — "Jesus sat (kathisas) over against the treasury"
- **Act 2:3** — "cloven tongues like as of fire… it sat (ekathisen) upon each of them"
- **Act 8:31** — "come up and sit (kathisai) with him"
- **Act 13:14** — "went into the synagogue… and sat down (ekathisan)"
- **1Co 10:7** — "The people sat down (ekathisen) to eat and drink, and rose up to play"

### 7. Settling / Dwelling / Continuing (Durative)
The "settle down, remain" sense — time spent in a place.
- **Luk 24:49** — "tarry ye (kathisate) in the city of Jerusalem, until ye be endued with power"
- **Act 18:11** — "he continued (ekathisen) there a year and six months, teaching the word of God"

### 8. Prudential Sitting to Calculate
Lukan parabolic usage — sitting as deliberation before undertaking a project.
- **Luk 14:28** — "intending to build a tower, sitteth not down (kathisas) first, and counteth the cost"
- **Luk 14:31** — "king, going to make war… sitteth not down (kathisas) first, and consulteth"
- **Luk 16:6** — "Take thy bill, and sit down (kathisas) quickly, and write fifty"
