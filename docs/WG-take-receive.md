## Relationships

### Word Family (lambano compounds)
lambano (G2983) is the root; the compounds add directional prefixes:
- **para-** (alongside) → paralambano: receive by tradition, take with
- **kata-** (down/intensely) → katalambano: seize eagerly, overtake
- **epi-** (upon) → epilambanomai: take hold of, lay hold
- **ana-** (up) → analambano (G353, not in group): take up
- **apo-** (from/back) → apolambano (G618, not in group): receive back, receive in full

## Roles in the Concept

- **General take/receive:** lambano (G2983) — the unmarked default, covering the full semantic range
- **Give (counterpart):** didomi (G1325) — the other half of the exchange; 26x co-occurrence with lambano makes this the most significant pairing
- **Welcome/accept:** dechomai (G1209) — emphasizes favorable disposition of the receiver
- **Receive by tradition:** paralambano (G3880) — receive what is handed down or alongside
- **Seize/comprehend:** katalambano (G2638) — aggressive taking or mental grasping
- **Lay hold of:** epilambanomai (G1949) — purposeful grasping, often for rescue or arrest

## Key Passages Where Multiple Group Words Appear Together

- **Mat 10:8, 41** — lambano + didomi: "freely ye have received, freely give"; lambano + dechomai: "he that receiveth... shall receive"
- **1Cor 9:24-25** — lambano + katalambano: "one receiveth the prize... so run that ye may obtain"
- **1Cor 11:23** — lambano + paralambano: "I have received of the Lord that which also I delivered unto you"
- **1Th 2:13** — dechomai + paralambano: "when ye received [paralambano] the word... ye received [dechomai] it not as the word of men"
