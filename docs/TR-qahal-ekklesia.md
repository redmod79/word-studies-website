**Semantic note:** The LXX translators divided between two Greek words for qahal. *Ekklesia* emphasized the "called out" / constituted assembly (civic, covenant, formal); *synagoge* (lit. "bring together") emphasized gathering. The NT consistently assigned *ekklesia* to the Christian assembly and largely reserved *synagoge* for Jewish gatherings — a meaningful choice that signals continuity with Israel's covenant qahal while marking the new covenant community.

## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same:**
- Core meaning: the constituted, gathered assembly of God's covenant people
- Formal/representative character: not an informal crowd but a called/summoned body
- Leadership structure: elders, appointed officers (qahal had its princes; ekklesia has its elders/apostles)
- Worship in the assembly (Ps 22:22 → Heb 2:12 shows singing praise in the qahal/ekklesia)

**What shifts:**
- Constituency: qahal = national Israel; ekklesia = multi-ethnic body in Christ (Gal 3:28)
- Summons: qahal = trumpet/herald call to gather; ekklesia = the called-out ones by the gospel
- Scope: ekklesia develops a universal/cosmic dimension (Eph 1:22, Col 1:18) that qahal does not reach
