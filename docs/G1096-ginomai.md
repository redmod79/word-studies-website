## Lexicon Definition

A prolongation and middle voice form of a primary verb; to cause to be ("gen"-erate). By extension: to become, come into being, happen, come to pass, arise, be made, be done. One of the most versatile verbs in the NT, functioning as a copula of change or transition.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Narrative Transition / "It Came to Pass" (ἐγένετο)
The most distinctive use — a Semitic-style narrative marker inherited from LXX וַיְהִי (wayehi).
- **Mat 7:28** — "And it came to pass (egeneto), when Jesus had ended these sayings, the people were astonished"
- **Luk 2:1** — "And it came to pass (egeneto) in those days, that there went out a decree from Caesar Augustus"
- **Act 5:5** — "And Ananias hearing these words fell down, and gave up the ghost: and great fear came (egeneto) on all"

### 2. Happening / Occurring — Events Taking Place
- **Mat 8:26** — "Then he arose, and rebuked the winds and the sea; and there was (egeneto) a great calm"

- **Rev 16:18** — "And there were voices, and thunders, and lightnings; and there was (egeneto) a great earthquake"

### 4. Being Made / Coming into Existence

### 5. Emphatic Denial — μὴ γένοιτο (mē genoito, "God forbid")
The aorist middle optative used as the strongest possible negation in NT Greek.

- 14 of 15 occurrences are in Paul (12 in Romans alone); 1 in Luke.
