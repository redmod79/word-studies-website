## Lexicon Definition

From H6586; a revolt (national, moral or religious): — rebellion, sin, transgression, trespass.

The root פָּשַׁע (H6586) carries the sense of breaking away from just authority — it denotes active defection, not merely moral failure. The noun pesha designates the act or condition of such revolt.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Formulaic Prophetic Indictment of Nations
The Amos oracle refrain applies pesha to national crimes, using a fixed "three transgressions / four" escalation formula.
- **Amos 1:3** — "For three transgressions of Damascus, and for four, I will not turn away the punishment thereof"
- **Amos 1:6** — "For three transgressions of Gaza, and for four..." (repeated for Tyrus, Edom, Ammon, Moab, Judah, Israel)
- **Amos 5:12** — "For I know your manifold transgressions and your mighty sins"
- **Micah 1:5** — "For the transgression of Jacob is all this, and for the sins of the house of Israel"

### 2. Covenant Violation Requiring Divine Response
Pesha paired with YHWH's name and the divine response of judgment or forgiveness.
- **Exod 23:21** — "he will not pardon your transgressions: for my name is in him"
- **Exod 34:7** — "forgiving iniquity and transgression and sin, and that will by no means clear the guilty" (the classic triad)
- **Ezek 18:30–31** — "Repent... Cast away from you all your transgressions, whereby ye have transgressed; and make you a new heart"
- **Dan 9:24** — "Seventy weeks are determined... to finish the transgression, and to make an end of sins"

### 3. Personal Confession Before God
The Psalms use pesha primarily in penitential contexts — the speaker acknowledging personal revolt.
- **Ps 32:1** — "Blessed is he whose transgression is forgiven, whose sin is covered"
- **Ps 51:1, 3** — "blot out my transgressions... For I acknowledge my transgressions: and my sin is ever before me"
- **Ps 103:12** — "As far as the east is from the west, so far hath he removed our transgressions from us"
- **Job 13:23** — "How many are mine iniquities and sins? make me to know my transgression and my sin"

### 4. Transgression as Social and Moral Disorder (Wisdom)
Proverbs extends pesha from the covenant sphere to general moral life — covering or ignoring sin vs. confronting it.
- **Prov 10:12** — "love covereth all sins (pesha)"
- **Prov 17:9** — "He that covereth a transgression seeketh love; but he that repeateth a matter separateth very friends"
- **Prov 28:2** — "For the transgression of a land many are the princes thereof"
- **Prov 29:16** — "When the wicked are multiplied, transgression increaseth"

### 5. Bearing and Blotting Out Transgression
Isaiah clusters pesha with God's sovereign act of removal — bearing, blotting, and atoning.
- **Isa 43:25** — "I, even I, am he that blotteth out thy transgressions for mine own sake"
- **Isa 53:5** — "he was wounded for our transgressions, he was bruised for our iniquities"
- **Isa 53:8** — "for the transgression of my people was he stricken"
- **Micah 7:18** — "Who is a God like unto thee, that pardoneth iniquity, and passeth by the transgression of the remnant of his heritage?"
