# WG: Hebrew Wrath / Indignation

## Overview
The Hebrew vocabulary for divine anger involves a cluster of seven words spanning 517 distinct verses. Each word captures a distinct shade of wrath: the flaring of nostrils (aph), the burning heat (charon, chemah), the outburst (qetseph), the passionate overflow (ebrah), and the deepest settled fury (zaʻam, zaʻaph). They regularly co-occur in the prophets as stacked intensifiers, accumulating force by layering synonyms. All seven are primarily rendered by just two Greek words in the LXX (ὀργή and θυμός), collapsing Hebrew's rich spectrum into two broad categories.

The LXX uses orgē and thymos to cover all seven Hebrew words. When orgē and thymos appear paired in the LXX (as in Psa 78:49 and Nah 1:6), they echo the Hebrew practice of stacking multiple wrath terms.

## Roles in the Concept

- **Breadth:** aph (אַף) — the dominant, broadest Hebrew word for anger; 269 occurrences; used of God and humans
- **Heat:** chemah (חֵמָה) — burning, boiling fury, venom; 117 occurrences; the most visceral term
- **Intensity:** charon (חָרוֹן) — fierce burning heat; almost always modifies aph ("charon aph" = fierce anger)
- **Explosion:** qetseph (קֶצֶף) — sudden outburst, like a splintering; 29 occurrences; divine anger that erupts suddenly
- **Overflow:** ebrah (עֶבְרָה) — surging passion, the overflow of emotion; 34 occurrences
- **Settled Fury:** zaʻam (זַעַם) — the deepest divine displeasure; 22 occurrences; especially of God's long-building fury against sin; used for bounded eschatological periods
- **Raging:** zaʻaph (זַעַף) — intense rage, often with human subjects; 6 occurrences; closest near-synonym to zaʻam

## Semantic Notes

The Hebrew authors intentionally stack these words to create rhetorical intensity. No single English word captures all the nuances, so the KJV alternates "anger," "wrath," "fury," "indignation," and "rage" across translations. The key distinctions:

- **aph / chemah / ebrah** — occur with both divine and human subjects
- **zaʻam / charon** — almost exclusively divine; zaʻam especially marks God's righteous displeasure against sin and nations
- **qetseph** — sudden divine outburst at a particular offense (Israel's rebellion, idolatry)
- **zaʻaph** — the smallest of the group; occasionally human; closest to zaʻam phonetically
