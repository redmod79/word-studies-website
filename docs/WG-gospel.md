# WG: Gospel

## Overview
The gospel concept cluster centers on the proclamation of good news in the NT. The core noun (euangelion) denotes the message content; the cognate verb (euangelizo) denotes the act of announcing it; the agent noun (euangelistes) denotes the person who delivers it. Two closely associated verbs describe the mode (kerysso — to herald publicly) and the expected response (pisteuo — to believe).

- **kerysso** (G2784), **kerygma** (G2782), and **pisteuo** (G4100) are not etymologically related to the euangel- family but form a functional chain: herald → proclamation → believe

## Roles in the Concept

- **Content:** euangelion (G2098) — the good news message itself
- **Proclamation verb:** euangelizo (G2097) — to announce the good news
- **Proclamation content (herald):** kerygma (G2782) — the heralded message, what is proclaimed
- **Delivery mode:** kerysso (G2784) — to publicly herald, preach
- **Response:** pisteuo (G4100) — to believe, trust, have faith
- **Agent:** euangelistes (G2099) — the one who proclaims
- **Anticipation:** proeuangelizomai (G4283) — the gospel preached in advance (Gal 3:8 — the promise to Abraham)
