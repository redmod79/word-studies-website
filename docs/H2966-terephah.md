## Semantic Categories

### 1. Animal Torn by Beasts (dietary/purity prohibition)
The primary usage — flesh of an animal killed by predators, prohibited as food.
- **Exo 22:31** — "ye shall be holy men unto me: neither shall ye eat any flesh that is torn of beasts in the field; ye shall cast it to the dogs"
- **Lev 22:8** — "That which dieth of itself, or is torn with beasts, he shall not eat to defile himself therewith"
- **Ezek 44:31** — "The priests shall not eat of any thing that is dead of itself, or torn"

### 2. Nebelah-Terephah Pair (two categories of improperly-killed meat)
Consistently paired with nebelah (H5038) in dietary/purity legislation.
- **Lev 7:24** — "the fat of the beast that dieth of itself [nebelah], and the fat of that which is torn with beasts [terephah]"
- **Lev 17:15** — "every soul that eateth that which died of itself [nebelah], or that which was torn with beasts [terephah]"
- **Ezek 4:14** — "my soul hath not been polluted: for from my youth up... have I not eaten of that which dieth of itself [nebelah], or is torn in pieces [terephah]"

### 3. Pastoral/Economic Context (animal husbandry)
Loss of animals to predators as an economic matter.
- **Gen 31:39** — "That which was torn of beasts I brought not unto thee; I bare the loss of it" (Jacob to Laban)
- **Exo 22:13** — "If it be torn in pieces, then let him bring it for witness, and he shall not make good that which was torn"

### 4. Lion's Prey (prophetic metaphor)
- **Nah 2:12** — "The lion did tear in pieces enough for his whelps... and filled his holes with prey [terephah], and his dens with ravin [terephah]"
