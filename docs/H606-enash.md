## Lexicon Definition

(Aramaic) or אֱנַשׁ ʼĕnash; (Aramaic), corresponding to H582 (אֱנוֹשׁ); a man: — man, whosoever.

The word is the Aramaic counterpart of Hebrew *enosh*, built on the same Semitic root conveying humanity in its frailty/mortality. In the Aramaic sections of Daniel and Ezra it functions as the ordinary word for "man/human being," often in generic, distributive, or collective senses ("men in general," "mankind," "anyone").

Because *enash* is Aramaic, it only appears in the Aramaic sections of the OT: Ezra 4:8–6:18; 7:12–26 and Daniel 2:4b–7:28. Within Daniel it is concentrated in the Aramaic court narratives and the night-vision of ch. 7. Absent from ch. 8 onward (Daniel switches back to Hebrew, where H582 *enosh* appears in 8:17).

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Humanity in General / Mankind (collective)
Used for the human race as a class, often contrasted with beasts or with divine realities.

- **Dan 2:38** — "wheresoever the children of men (enash) dwell, the beasts of the field..."
- **Dan 4:17** — "the most High ruleth in the kingdom of men (enash)"
- **Dan 4:25** — "till thou know that the most High ruleth in the kingdom of men (enash)"
- **Dan 2:43** — "they shall mingle themselves with the seed of men (enash)"

### 2. Humans Distinguished From / Contrasted With Beasts
A recurring Danielic motif — human estate vs. bestial estate (Nebuchadnezzar's humiliation; the beasts of ch. 7).

- **Dan 4:16** — "Let his heart be changed from man's (enash), and let a beast's heart be given unto him"
- **Dan 4:33** — "he was driven from men (enash) ... till his hairs were grown like eagles' feathers"
- **Dan 5:21** — "he was driven from the sons of men (enash); and his heart was made like the beasts"
- **Dan 7:4** — "made stand upon the feet as a man (enash), and a man's heart was given to it"

### 3. An Individual / "A Man" (a particular person or any single person)
Generic singular — "a man," "any man," sometimes approaching indefinite pronoun.

- **Dan 2:10** — "There is not a man (enash) upon the earth that can shew the king's matter"
- **Dan 5:5** — "came forth fingers of a man's (enash) hand, and wrote"
- **Dan 3:10** — "every man (enash) that shall hear the sound of the cornet..."

### 4. Distributive / Indefinite ("whosoever," "any")
Legal/decree language: "any person whoever."

- **Ezr 6:11** — "whosoever (enash) shall alter this word, let timber be pulled down from his house"
- **Dan 6:7** — "whosoever shall ask a petition of any God or man (enash) for thirty days"
- **Dan 6:12** — "any God or man (enash) within thirty days"

### 5. "Son of Man" — Human-like Figure in Vision
The decisive phrase *kebar enash* ("like a son of man") appears in Dan 7:13, and the parallel description in 7:4 (beast "made stand upon the feet as a man") and 7:8 ("eyes like the eyes of man") use the same root. In 7:13 *enash* denotes the apparent form of the heavenly figure brought before the Ancient of Days.

- **Dan 7:13** — "[one] like the Son of man (kebar enash) came with the clouds of heaven, and came to the Ancient of days"
- **Dan 7:8** — "in this horn [were] eyes like the eyes of man (enash), and a mouth speaking great things"
- **Dan 7:4** — "made stand upon the feet as a man (enash)"

### 6. Residents / Subjects (used as plural collective in administrative contexts)
- **Ezr 4:11** — "Thy servants the men (enash) on this side the river"
