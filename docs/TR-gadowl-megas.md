## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same across testaments:**
- The core semantic range transfers intact: physical size, importance/rank, intensity of emotion, loud sound
- The "great + voice" collocation (qol gadol → phōnē megalē) is preserved verbatim
- "Great day of the LORD" (yom gadol) becomes "that great day" in NT eschatology
- The small-great merism (qatan/gadol → mikros/megas) survives unchanged

**What shifts:**
- NT concentrates megas heavily in apocalyptic contexts (75 of 200 verses in Revelation), giving it a stronger eschatological flavor than OT gadol, which distributes more evenly across narrative, poetry, and prophecy
- In Revelation, "great" often becomes a fixed epithet: "that great city," "the great dragon," "the great whore" — a formulaic apocalyptic use less prominent in OT
