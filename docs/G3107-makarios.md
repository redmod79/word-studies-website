## Semantic Categories

### 1. Beatitude Pronouncements — Spiritual Reversal
Formulaic declarations that those in disadvantaged or humble conditions are blessed, because God will reverse their situation.
- **Matt 5:3** — "Blessed are the poor in spirit: for theirs is the kingdom of heaven."
- **Matt 5:4** — "Blessed are they that mourn: for they shall be comforted."
- **Luke 6:20** — "Blessed be ye poor: for yours is the kingdom of God."
- **Luke 6:21** — "Blessed are ye that hunger now: for ye shall be filled."

### 2. Blessedness in Persecution and Suffering
Those enduring persecution, reproach, or trial for the sake of Christ or righteousness are pronounced blessed.
- **Matt 5:10** — "Blessed are they which are persecuted for righteousness' sake."
- **Matt 5:11** — "Blessed are ye, when men shall revile you, and persecute you."
- **1 Pet 3:14** — "But and if ye suffer for righteousness' sake, happy are ye."
- **1 Pet 4:14** — "If ye be reproached for the name of Christ, happy are ye."

### 3. Blessedness of Faithful Servants — Watchfulness and Obedience
Those found faithfully serving, watching, or obeying when the Lord comes.
- **Matt 24:46** — "Blessed is that servant, whom his lord when he cometh shall find so doing."
- **Luke 12:37** — "Blessed are those servants, whom the lord when he cometh shall find watching."
- **Rev 16:15** — "Blessed is he that watcheth, and keepeth his garments."
- **Rev 22:14** — "Blessed are they that do his commandments."

### 4. Blessedness of Spiritual Perception — Seeing, Hearing, Believing
Those who perceive what God is doing, or who believe without physical evidence.
- **Matt 13:16** — "But blessed are your eyes, for they see: and your ears, for they hear."
- **Matt 16:17** — "Blessed art thou, Simon Barjona: for flesh and blood hath not revealed it."
- **John 20:29** — "Blessed are they that have not seen, and yet have believed."
- **Luke 1:45** — "Blessed is she that believed."

### 5. Blessedness of Forgiveness — Sins Covered
Psalm 32 quotation applying the OT beatitude formula to forgiveness.
- **Rom 4:7** — "Blessed are they whose iniquities are forgiven, and whose sins are covered."
- **Rom 4:8** — "Blessed is the man to whom the Lord will not impute sin."

### 6. Divine Attribute — "The Blessed God"
Applied to God himself as an intrinsic quality, not a pronounced state.
- **1 Tim 1:11** — "the glorious gospel of the blessed God"
- **1 Tim 6:15** — "the blessed and only Potentate, the King of kings"

### 7. Revelation's Seven Beatitudes
A distinct literary cycle of seven pronouncements across the book.
- **Rev 1:3** — "Blessed is he that readeth, and they that hear the words of this prophecy."
- **Rev 14:13** — "Blessed are the dead which die in the Lord from henceforth."
- **Rev 19:9** — "Blessed are they which are called unto the marriage supper of the Lamb."
- **Rev 20:6** — "Blessed and holy is he that hath part in the first resurrection."
