# Grammar Analysis: Daniel 8:11-12 — Voice Alternation and Agency Encoding

Seven finite verbs span two verses and **four distinct Hebrew stems** — Hiphil active, Hophal passive, Niphal passive, and Qal base — arranged in a deliberate active-passive-active chiastic pattern that controls who is grammatically credited (or denied credit) for each action in the horn's assault on the heavenly sanctuary system. The verb chain is: v.11 **higdil** (Hiphil) + **huram** (Hophal) + **hushlak** (Hophal); v.12 **tinnaten** (Niphal) + **tashlekh** (Hiphil) + **asah** (Qal) + **hitslichah** (Hiphil). This entry isolates the voice-alternation grammar as the organizing principle. For full parsing, construct-chain analysis, and clause-structure detail, see [dan-8-10-12](dan-8-10-12.md) and [dan-8-11](dan-8-11.md).

## Voice-Alternation Architecture

The seven verbs divide into three zones by voice:

```
ZONE A (v.11a)    ACTIVE     higdil        horn self-magnifies
ZONE B (v.11b-12a) PASSIVE    huram         tamid "was taken away"
                   PASSIVE    hushlak       sanctuary-place "was cast down"
                   PASSIVE    tinnaten      host "was given over"
ZONE C (v.12b-d)  ACTIVE     tashlekh      horn casts down truth
                   ACTIVE     asah          horn "does/acts"
                   ACTIVE     hitslichah    horn "prospers"
```

This produces an **A-B-A chiasm on voice**: ACTIVE (1 verb) -- PASSIVE (3 verbs) -- ACTIVE (3 verbs). The passive zone occupies the center of the passage and covers precisely the actions that touch the heavenly sanctuary system (*tamid*, *mekon miqdasho*, *tsaba*). The active zones cover the horn's self-exaltation and its assault on abstract-corporate targets (truth, generic doing/prospering).

### Zone A: Hiphil Active -- Self-Magnification (v.11a)

**Verb 1: הִגְדִּיל** -- Hiphil.Perf.3ms of גדל (H1431)

The Hiphil of גדל without an expressed object is the standard Hebrew register for **reflexive-intensive self-exaltation**: "he magnified [himself]" (KJV supplies the reflexive in brackets). The Hiphil stem introduces an external causer -- but when no object is present, the subject becomes both causer and patient of the "making great." This is the internal-causative / resultative function of the Hiphil (GKC section 53d; Waltke-O'Connor IBHS section 27.2e; Joüon-Muraoka section 54d). The prepositional complement *ʿad sar-hats-tsaba* "unto the Prince of the host" specifies the vertical target of the magnification.

**Agency:** The horn is the unambiguous grammatical agent. The 3ms person (anomalous in a f.s. verb chain) has been explained as *constructio ad sensum* with the masculine historical referent behind the f.s. *qeren* symbol, or as agreement with the fronted masculine *sar*.

### Zone B: Passive Cluster -- Agent-Suppressed Sanctuary Actions (v.11b-12a)

**Verb 2: הוּרַם** -- Hophal.Perf.3ms of רום (H7311)

The Hophal is the **passive of the Hiphil** -- where the Hiphil says "he raised/removed," the Hophal says "it was raised/removed" with the causer grammatically suppressed. The morphological marker is the *u*-class preformative (הוּ-) and short-*a* theme vowel, replacing the Hiphil's long-*i* theme. The consonantal text (הרים) is identical for both Hophal *huram* and Hiphil *herim*; the voice distinction depends entirely on the Masoretic pointing. MT preserves the Hophal (harder reading); a minority tradition repoints as Hiphil, making the horn the overt agent. BHS/BHQ follow MT Hophal.

**Subject:** הַתָּמִיד "the continual [offering]" -- the *tamid* receives the action of being taken away. **Agent:** unnamed. The fronted מִמֶּנּוּ "from him / by him" is ambiguous between ablative-of-separation and agent-marker-with-passive (GKC section 121f), leaving the question of who removes the *tamid* grammatically open.

**Verb 3: הֻשְׁלַךְ** -- Hophal.Perf.3ms of שׁלך (H7993)

Same stem, same function as verb 2. The Hiphil of שׁלך means "cast/throw"; the Hophal means "was cast down" without naming the thrower. **Subject:** מְכוֹן מִקְדָּשׁוֹ "the place of his sanctuary." **Agent:** unnamed. The two consecutive Hophals form a **passive pair** that refuses to identify the agent of the sanctuary-related violence, even though the author demonstrably knows how to use the active Hiphil of the same root (verb 5 below uses Hiphil of שׁלך).

**Verb 4: תִּנָּתֵן** -- Niphal.Impf.3fs of נתן (H5414)

The Niphal is the **simple passive of the Qal** -- "was given" / "was handed over." Unlike the Hophal (passive of Hiphil), the Niphal does not encode a two-level causation structure (causer + instrument + patient); it simply says the subject received the action. But in contexts where no agent is expressed, the Niphal regularly carries **divine-passive** force -- the unnamed giver is understood to be God. This is the Hebrew structural ancestor of the Greek *edothe* pattern in Revelation (Rev 6:2, 4, 8; 13:5, 7).

**Subject:** צָבָא "host / army" -- fronted into topic position (WXYq clause type). The 3fs verb-form *tinnaten* aligns with the continuous feminine subject-chain of the horn (vv.10, 12b-d all 3fs), creating an ambiguity: is the *tsaba* the subject being given over, or is the horn (f.s.) the subject that receives an army? Both readings are grammatically available. **Agent:** unnamed.

**Stem distinction within the passive zone:** The Hophal pair (verbs 2-3) and the Niphal (verb 4) are **not interchangeable** passives. The Hophal is the passive of a **causative** action -- there is a two-level frame (ultimate causer + proximate action + patient), and the grammar suppresses the causer level. The Niphal is the passive of a **basic** action -- a single-level frame where the patient simply receives the action. The stem shift from Hophal to Niphal at the verse boundary signals a different kind of passivization:

| Passive stem | Root active stem | What is suppressed | Semantic implication |
|-------------|------------------|--------------------|---------------------|
| **Hophal** (verbs 2-3) | Hiphil (causative) | The **causer** who made the removal/casting happen | A causative agent exists but is unnamed -- room for divine-permissive reading |
| **Niphal** (verb 4) | Qal (basic) | The **doer** of the giving | Basic passive -- focus on the receiving, not on who gave |

### Zone C: Active Recovery -- Horn as Named Agent (v.12b-d)

**Verb 5: תַשְׁלֵךְ** -- Hiphil.Impf.3fs of שׁלך (H7993)

The same root as verb 3 (*shalak* "throw/cast"), now in the **active Hiphil** instead of the passive Hophal. The stem-switch on a single root across four clauses is the sharpest index of deliberate voice alternation in the passage. The object is אֱמֶת "truth / faithfulness" and the locative is אַרְצָה "to the ground." The horn is the named grammatical agent (3fs subject continuing the f.s. horn-chain).

**Verb 6: עָשְׂתָה** -- Qal.Perf.3fs of עשׂה (H6213)

The only **Qal** (base stem) in the chain. No causation, no passivization -- plain basic action: "she acted / she did." The absence of a stated object gives this verb an **absolute** or **intransitive** force: generic habitual action without a specified target. This is the ground-level voice -- the horn simply acts. The Qal is neutral ground between the Hiphil causative and the passive stems; its position between the Hiphil *tashlekh* and the Hiphil *hitslichah* creates a momentary voice-neutral pivot.

**Verb 7: הִצְלִיחָה** -- Hiphil.Perf.3fs of צלח (H6743)

The Hiphil of צלח carries **internal-causative** force: "she caused [things] to prosper" or "she prospered" (the Hiphil is sometimes intransitive-resultative when no object is expressed). The horn is the agent of its own prospering. The weqatal pair *asah + hitslichah* echoes the Danielic success-formula reversed at Dan 8:24 (*we-hitsliah we-asah* in inverted order).

## The shalak Voice-Chiasm

The root שׁלך (H7993, "throw/cast") appears three times in vv.10-12, creating a voice-chiasm across the entire unit:

| Verse | Form | Stem | Voice | Object cast down |
|-------|------|------|-------|-----------------|
| 8:10 | וַתַּפֵּל | נפל Hiphil.Wayq.3fs | **Active** | host + stars (*artsah*) |
| 8:11 | הֻשְׁלַךְ | שׁלך **Hophal**.Perf.3ms | **Passive** | sanctuary-place |
| 8:12 | תַשְׁלֵךְ | שׁלך **Hiphil**.Impf.3fs | **Active** | truth (*artsah*) |

The two active-voice throwing verbs (v.10 *tappel*, v.12 *tashlekh*) bracket the single passive-voice throwing verb (v.11 *hushlak*). The locative אַרְצָה "to the ground" appears in both active clauses but is absent from the passive -- as if the grammar withholds the full specification of the casting-down when it withholds the agent. The voice-chiasm on the casting-down motif is:

```
ACTIVE casting (host)   —   PASSIVE casting (sanctuary)   —   ACTIVE casting (truth)
    named agent                  agent suppressed                  named agent
    artsah                       no artsah                         artsah
```

## Stem-by-Stem Agency Map

| Stem | Count | Verbs | Agency encoding |
|------|-------|-------|-----------------|
| **Hiphil** (causative active) | 3 | *higdil*, *tashlekh*, *hitslihah* | Horn is **overt causer** -- responsible for magnifying self, casting truth, prospering |
| **Hophal** (causative passive) | 2 | *huram*, *hushlak* | Action has a **causer who is unnamed** -- the *tamid* is removed and the sanctuary-place cast down by someone the grammar will not identify |
| **Niphal** (simple passive) | 1 | *tinnaten* | The host is **given over** -- basic passive with divine-passive register available |
| **Qal** (basic active) | 1 | *asah* | Horn **simply acts** -- neutral voice baseline, no causative force |

The stem distribution is not random. All three actions touching the **heavenly sanctuary apparatus** (*tamid*, *mekon miqdash*, *tsaba* against the *tamid*) are passive-voice, agent-suppressed. All three actions touching **self-exaltation, truth, and general doing/prospering** are active-voice with the horn as named agent. The grammar draws a line between what the horn demonstrably does (magnify itself, cast down truth, act and prosper) and what happens to the sanctuary system (removal, casting down, handing over), keeping the second category in the passive voice.

## Tense Alternation within the Voice Pattern

The voice alternation interlocks with a tense alternation:

| Verb | Stem | Tense | Domain |
|------|------|-------|--------|
| *higdil* | Hiphil | **Perfect** | N (narration) |
| *huram* | Hophal | **Perfect** | N (narration) |
| *hushlak* | Hophal | **Perfect** | N (narration) |
| *tinnaten* | Niphal | **Imperfect** | D (discursive) |
| *tashlekh* | Hiphil | **Imperfect** | D (discursive) |
| *asah* | Qal | **Perfect** | D (discursive) |
| *hitslihah* | Hiphil | **Perfect** | D (discursive) |

The verse boundary (v.11 / v.12) coincides with a **domain shift** from N (narrative) to D (discursive/prospective). Within the narrative domain (v.11), all three verbs are perfect with fronted focus-constituents (WxQ0, WxQX, WQtX clause types). Within the discursive domain (v.12), the first two verbs are imperfect (WXYq, WYqX) before the final two settle into weqatal (WQt0, WQt0). The imperfect forms in v.12a-b carry iterative/habitual/prospective aspect -- the giving-over and casting-down of truth are presented as **ongoing or characteristic** processes, not completed events. The closing weqatal pair summarizes the outcome.

## What the Voice Alternation Encodes

The grammar makes three distinctions through voice selection:

1. **What the horn does to itself** (Hiphil reflexive-internal causative): self-magnification (*higdil*). The horn is both agent and patient. Voice: active.

2. **What happens to the sanctuary system** (Hophal/Niphal passive): the *tamid* is removed (*huram*), the sanctuary-place is cast down (*hushlak*), a host is given over against the *tamid* (*tinnaten*). The horn is **not** the grammatical agent of any of these actions. The Hophal encodes a two-level causation with the causer suppressed; the Niphal encodes a basic passive with the doer suppressed. Whether the unnamed agent is God (divine-passive reading), the horn (implicitly), or some unspecified power, the grammar deliberately refuses to assign the credit.

3. **What the horn does to abstract-corporate targets** (Hiphil/Qal active): truth is cast down (*tashlekh*), the horn acts (*asah*), the horn prospers (*hitslihah*). Voice: active, horn as named agent.

The contrast between categories 2 and 3 is the central grammatical finding. The author demonstrably knows how to write the horn as an active-voice Hiphil agent (verbs 1, 5, 7) and demonstrably chooses not to do so for the sanctuary-related actions (verbs 2, 3, 4). The voice-choice is deliberate, not accidental -- confirmed by the same-root voice-switch on שׁלך between verbs 3 (Hophal) and 5 (Hiphil).
