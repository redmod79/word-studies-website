## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same across testaments:**
- The core association with idolatry and desecration of sacred space persists: Dan 11:31 (polluting the sanctuary) → Matt 24:15 (standing in the holy place)
- The pairing with desolation (שָׁמֵם shamem → ἐρήμωσις erēmōsis) is preserved intact across the bridge

**What shifts:**
- In the OT, shiqquwts names specific pagan deities (Milcom, Chemosh, Molech) and their physical images; in the NT, bdelygma is never attached to named deities but functions as eschatological sign-language (Matt 24, Mark 13) or moral/spiritual metaphor (Luke 16:15, Rev 17:4)
- Revelation expands bdelygma beyond cult objects to characterize an entire system ("Babylon") and its moral corruption — a broader application than the concrete idol-referent of shiqquwts
