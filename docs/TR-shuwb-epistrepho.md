## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same across testaments:**
- The volitional, directional sense: both shub and epistréphō describe a deliberate change of course toward God
- The call-and-response pattern: God calls, the person/people turns — Jer 3:12/Acts 3:19
- The pairing with repentance vocabulary: shub + nacham (OT) parallels epistréphō + metanoeō (NT)
- Physical and spiritual senses coexist in both testaments

**What shifts:**
- OT shub is overwhelmingly corporate/national ("Return, O Israel"); NT epistréphō is more often individual conversion
- The restitution/compensation sense of shub (Hiphil) does not carry through epistréphō — the LXX routes that meaning through apodidōmi (G591) and apokathistēmi (G600) instead
- NT epistréphō acquires a more specifically conversion-from-paganism sense absent from the OT (1 Thess 1:9 "turned from idols")
- The adverbial "again" function of shub (87 OT verses) is handled by palin (G3825) in the NT, not by epistréphō
