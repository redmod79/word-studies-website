## Lexicon Definition

Feminine noun; compound of νέος (new) and μήν (month/moon). The new moon; the first day of the lunar month, observed as a festival in Jewish religious practice.

**Note on form:** The textual tradition preserves two orthographic variants — νουμηνία (contracted Attic form) and νεομηνία (uncontracted Ionic/Koine form). The Greek NT text at Col 2:16 reads νεομηνίας (genitive singular feminine). Both forms refer to the same word.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Calendrical Observance in a Festival Triad
- **Col 2:16** — "Let no man therefore judge you in meat, or in drink, or in respect of an holyday (heorte), or of the new moon (noumenia), or of the sabbath days (sabbaton)"

The sole NT use places noumenia as the middle term in a three-part sequence of Jewish sacred times: annual festivals (heorte) → monthly new moons (noumenia) → weekly sabbaths (sabbaton). This descending pattern (yearly → monthly → weekly) mirrors OT calendar formulae (cf. 1 Chr 23:31, 2 Chr 2:4, 2 Chr 31:3, Neh 10:33, Isa 1:13, Ezek 45:17, Hos 2:11).
