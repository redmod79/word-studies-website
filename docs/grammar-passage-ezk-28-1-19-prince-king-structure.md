## Key Grammatical Features

### 1. Two Full Word-of-YHWH Formulas

Ezek 28:1 and 28:11 are identical at the parsed level: `וַיְהִי` Qal wayyiqtol 3ms + `דְבַר יְהוָה` construct chain + `אֵלַי` + `לֵאמֹר`. The repeated formula marks a new discourse block; v.11 is not merely a continuation heading.

### 2. `nagid` and `melek` Are Adjacent but Not Identical Headings

Ezek 28:2 uses `לִנְגִיד צֹר` ("to prince/chief of Tyre"), a construct chain with `nagid` as head. Ezek 28:12 uses `עַל־מֶלֶךְ צֹור` ("over king of Tyre"), again a construct chain but with `melek`. The grammar does not collapse the two titles; it places two titled address-headings in adjacent oracle units.

### 3. The Second Unit Is Genre-Marked as Lament

Ezek 28:12 begins with `שָׂא קִינָה`, Qal imperative 2ms of נשׂא plus the direct object `קִינָה`. This converts the second oracle from direct indictment into a qinah/lament performance. See [qinah-lamentation-foreign-oracles](../hebrew/qinah-lamentation-foreign-oracles.md).

### 4. Distinct Judgment Modes

In 28:7-10 the prince-oracle uses hostile human agents: "strangers" and "terrible of the nations" draw swords, profane splendor, and bring him down. In 28:16-18 the lament unit shifts into a dense first-person divine judgment chain: `אֶחַלֶּלְךָ`, `אַבֶּדְךָ`, `הִשְׁלַכְתִּיךָ`, `אֹוצִא`, `אֲכָלַתְךָ`, `אֶתֶּנְךָ`. See [ezk-28-16-18-divine-judgment-verb-chain](ezk-28-16-18-divine-judgment-verb-chain.md).
