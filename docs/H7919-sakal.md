## Lexicon Definition

A primitive root; to be (causatively, make or act) circumspect and hence, intelligent:—consider,
expert, instruct, prosper, (deal) prudent(-ly), (give) skill(-ful), have good success, teach,
(have, make to) understand(-ing), wisdom, (be, behave self, consider, make) wise(-ly), guide
wittingly.

The Hiphil dominates overwhelmingly. In Gen 3:6 (InfCon) and throughout Psalms, Proverbs, and
Daniel, the Hiphil encodes both the internal state (being circumspect, having insight) and its
outcomes (succeeding, prospering). The Piel appears in Gen 48:14 ("guiding his hands wittingly").
A Qal base is implied by the root but is extremely rare or absent in actual usage — the language
uses Hiphil even where an intransitive "be wise" sense is intended.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Prudent Behavior — Wise Conduct in Practice
The Hiphil describes an outwardly observable way of acting: circumspect, skillful, discerning.

- **1Sa 18:14** — "And David behaved himself wisely in all his ways; and the LORD was with him."
- **1Sa 18:30** — "David behaved himself more wisely than all the servants of Saul; so that his name was much set by."
- **Amos 5:13** — "Therefore the prudent shall keep silence in that time; for it is an evil time."
- **Pro 16:20** — "He that handleth a matter wisely shall find good: and whoso trusteth in the LORD, happy is he."

### 2. Comprehension — Understanding Divine Reality
Grasping what is true, especially what God has done or is doing.

- **Psa 14:2** — "The LORD looked down from heaven upon the children of men, to see if there were any that did understand, and seek God."
- **Isa 44:18** — "They have not known nor understood: for he hath shut their eyes, that they cannot see."
- **Dan 12:10** — "None of the wicked shall understand; but the wise shall understand."
- **Deu 32:29** — "O that they were wise, that they understood this, that they would consider their latter end!"

### 3. Instruction — Imparting Wisdom to Others
The causative force of Hiphil makes śākal the verb for teaching, giving insight.

- **Psa 32:8** — "I will instruct thee and teach thee in the way which thou shalt go."
- **Dan 11:33** — "And they that understand among the people shall instruct many."
- **Neh 9:20** — "Thou gavest also thy good spirit to instruct them."
- **2Ch 30:22** — "Hezekiah spake comfortably unto all the Levites that taught the good knowledge of the LORD."

### 4. Success and Prosperity — Wisdom Bearing Fruit
In legal and historical narratives, śākal describes the outcome of wise, obedient living.

- **Josh 1:8** — "Thou shalt make thy way prosperous, and then thou shalt have good success."
- **2Ki 18:7** — "The LORD was with him; and he prospered whithersoever he went forth."
- **Jer 23:5** — "A King shall reign and prosper, and shall execute judgment and justice in the earth."
- **Deu 29:9** — "Keep therefore the words of this covenant, and do them, that ye may prosper in all that ye do."

### 5. Divine Insight — Revelation-Grade Understanding
A subset of occurrences describes understanding granted supernaturally by God.

- **Dan 9:22** — "I am now come forth to give thee skill and understanding."
- **1Ch 28:19** — "All this, said David, the LORD made me understand in writing by his hand upon me."
- **Dan 1:17** — "God gave them knowledge and skill in all learning and wisdom."
- **Psa 119:99** — "I have more understanding than all my teachers: for thy testimonies are my meditation."

### 6. The Wise Ones (maśkîlîm) — Those Who Lead Others to Righteousness
Daniel's end-time usage elevates śākal to describe an eschatological category of people.

- **Dan 12:3** — "They that be wise (maśkîlîm) shall shine as the brightness of the firmament; and they that turn many to righteousness as the stars for ever and ever."
- **Dan 11:33** — "They that understand among the people shall instruct many: yet they shall fall by the sword, and by flame."
- **Dan 11:35** — "Some of them of understanding shall fall, to try them, and to purge, and to make them white, even to the time of the end."
