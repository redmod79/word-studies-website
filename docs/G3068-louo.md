## Semantic Categories

### 1. Spiritual/Ceremonial Whole-Person Cleansing
- **John 13:10** — "He that is washed [leloumenos, perf pass] needeth not save to wash [nipto] his feet, but is clean every whit." The louo-bathed person is already wholly clean; only the feet require nipto.
- **Hebrews 10:22** — "having our hearts sprinkled from an evil conscience, and our bodies washed [lelousmenos, perf pass] with pure water." Paired with sprinkling of the conscience; draws on Levitical full-body bathing.
- **Revelation 1:5** — "Unto him that loved us, and washed [louo] us from our sins in his own blood." Complete cleansing from sin through blood (KJV reading).

### 2. Physical Washing of the Whole Body
- **Acts 9:37** — "whom when they had washed [lousantes, aor act], they laid her in an upper chamber." Preparation of Dorcas's corpse — full-body washing as burial custom.
- **Acts 16:33** — "and washed [elousen, aor act] their stripes; and was baptized, he and all his, straightway." The Philippian jailer washes Paul and Silas's wounds — physical care of their bodies.

### 3. Animal Self-Bathing (Proverbial)
- **2 Peter 2:22** — "the sow that was washed [lousamene, aor mid] to her wallowing in the mire." The sow bathes its whole body, then returns to mud. Middle voice = self-bathing. Illustrates reversion to former unclean state.
