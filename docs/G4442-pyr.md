## Semantic Categories

### 1. Eschatological Punishment (Lake of Fire / Gehenna)
Eternal fire as the final destiny of the wicked — the unquenchable fire, the lake burning with fire.

- **Mat 25:41** — "Depart from me, ye cursed, into everlasting fire, prepared for the devil and his angels"
- **Mar 9:44** — "Where their worm dieth not, and the fire is not quenched" (also vv. 46, 48)
- **Rev 20:14-15** — "And death and hell were cast into the lake of fire… whosoever was not found written in the book of life was cast into the lake of fire"
- **Jude 1:7** — "suffering the vengeance of eternal fire" (Sodom as type)

### 2. Judgment Parables / Destruction of the Unfruitful
Fire as the instrument of divine sentence — cut down and cast into the fire; tares gathered and burned.

- **Mat 3:10, 12** — "every tree which bringeth not forth good fruit is hewn down, and cast into the fire… the chaff he will burn with fire unquenchable"
- **Mat 7:19** — "Every tree that bringeth not forth good fruit is hewn down, and cast into the fire"
- **Mat 13:40, 42, 50** — "gathered and burned in the fire… cast them into a furnace of fire"
- **2Th 1:8** — "In flaming fire taking vengeance on them that know not God"

### 3. Divine Theophany / God's Presence
Fire as visible manifestation of God or the exalted Christ — consuming, radiant, holy.

- **Heb 12:29** — "our God is a consuming fire"
- **Acts 2:3** — "there appeared unto them cloven tongues like as of fire" (Pentecost)
- **Rev 1:14; 2:18** — "his eyes were as a flame of fire" (the risen Christ)
- **Acts 7:30** — "an angel of the Lord in a flame of fire in a bush" (burning bush, Stephen's speech)
- **Rev 4:5** — "seven lamps of fire burning before the throne"

### 4. Cosmic / Apocalyptic Plagues
Fire as a force in Revelation's cosmic judgment scenes — cast upon the earth, mingled with blood.

- **Rev 8:5, 7-8** — fire from the altar cast to the earth; hail and fire mingled with blood
- **Rev 9:17-18** — "breastplates of fire… fire and smoke and brimstone… the third part of men killed, by the fire"
- **Rev 13:13** — the false prophet "maketh fire come down from heaven"
- **2Pe 3:7** — "the earth… reserved unto fire against the day of judgment"
- **Luk 17:29** — "rained fire and brimstone from heaven… destroyed them all" (Sodom as anti-type)

### 5. Testing / Refining / Purifying
Fire as a metaphor for trial, purification, or proving — the testing of faith like gold; works revealed.

- **1Pe 1:7** — "the trial of your faith… though it be tried with fire, [is] more precious than gold"
- **1Co 3:13, 15** — "it shall be revealed by fire… but he himself shall be saved; yet so as by fire"
- **Rev 3:18** — "I counsel thee to buy of me gold tried in the fire, that thou mayest be rich"
- **Heb 11:34** — "quenched the violence of fire" (heroes of faith)

### 6. Figurative / Metaphorical
Fire as an image of the tongue's destructive power, of hostile division, or of moral obligation.

- **Jas 3:5-6** — "how great a matter a little fire kindleth… the tongue is a fire, a world of iniquity"
- **Luk 12:49** — "I am come to send fire on the earth; and what will I, if it be already kindled?"
- **Rom 12:20** — "in so doing thou shalt heap coals of fire on his head"
- **Jude 1:23** — "others save with fear, pulling them out of the fire"
