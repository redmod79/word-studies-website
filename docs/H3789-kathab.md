## Lexicon Definition

A primitive root; to grave, by implication, to write (describe, inscribe, prescribe, subscribe): — describe, record, prescribe, subscribe, write(-ing, -ten).

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Divine Writing — God as Author
- **Exo 24:12** — "I will give thee tables of stone, and a law, and commandments which I have written"
- **Exo 31:18** — "two tables of testimony, tables of stone, written with the finger of God"
- **Exo 34:1** — "I will write upon these tables the words that were in the first tables"
- **Deu 10:4** — "And he wrote on the tables, according to the first writing, the ten commandments"

God writes directly only twice in Scripture (the Decalogue tablets). This category establishes writing as a divine act before it becomes a human one.

### 2. Prophetic/Divine Command to Write
- **Exo 17:14** — "Write this for a memorial in a book" (first occurrence)
- **Isa 8:1** — "Take thee a great roll, and write in it with a man's pen"
- **Isa 30:8** — "Now go, write it before them in a table, and note it in a book"
- **Jer 30:2** — "Write thee all the words that I have spoken unto thee in a book"
- **Jer 36:2** — "Take thee a roll of a book, and write therein all the words"
- **Hab 2:2** — "Write the vision, and make it plain upon tables"

God commands writing to preserve prophetic testimony for future generations.

### 3. Authoritative Record — "As It Is Written"
- **Jos 1:8** — "thou shalt meditate therein... all that is written therein"

- **2Ki 14:6** — "according unto that which is written in the book of the law of Moses"
- **2Ki 23:21** — "Keep the passover unto the LORD your God, as it is written"
- **Neh 8:14** — "they found written in the law which the LORD had commanded by Moses"
- **Dan 9:13** — "As it is written in the law of Moses, all this evil is come upon us"

The passive participle *kâtûb* ("written") functions as a technical term for textual authority — what is "written" carries binding force.

### 4. Royal/Historical Chronicle Formula
- **1Ki 11:41** — "are they not written in the book of the acts of Solomon?"

This stereotyped formula accounts for ~40 occurrences in Kings and Chronicles alone, appealing to external written records as historical verification.
