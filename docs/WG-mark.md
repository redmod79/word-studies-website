## Roles in the Concept

- **The mark (cháragma G5480):** The engraved badge — received voluntarily (under compulsion) as proof of submission
- **The forehead (métōpon G3359):** Primary visible location — public display of allegiance
- **The right hand (cheír G5495):** Secondary location — the working hand, enabling commerce
- **The name (ónoma G3686):** Equivalent to or paired with the mark — identity of the beast inscribed
- **The number (arithmós G706):** The computational identity of the beast (666) — third element alongside mark and name
- **The seal (sphragís G4973):** God's counter-mark on his servants — ownership and protection (Rev 7:2-3; 9:4)

### The Three-Part Identity Marker (Rev 13:17; 15:2)
Rev 13:17 identifies three related but distinct elements: **the mark** (cháragma), **the name** (ónoma) of the beast, and **the number** (arithmós) of his name. These appear to be alternative expressions of the same allegiance — or a three-part progression — rather than strictly three different things. Rev 15:2 confirms that overcomers have victory over all three.

## Key Passages

- **Rev 13:16-17** — Introduction: the mark imposed for commerce
- **Rev 14:9-11** — First warning: receiving the mark = facing eternal wrath
- **Rev 15:2** — Victory: overcomers are those who conquered the mark
- **Rev 16:2** — Judgment: the first bowl falls on those who bear the mark
- **Rev 19:20** — Judgment: those deceived into receiving the mark cast into the lake of fire
- **Rev 20:4** — Reward: those who refused the mark reign with Christ
