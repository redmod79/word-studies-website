## Lexicon Definition

Or (fully) geyr (gare); from H1481; properly, a guest; by implication, a foreigner: — alien, sojourner, stranger.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Equal Standing Under Law — "One Law" Formula
The ger stands under the same torah as the native-born Israelite, with equal cultic and judicial obligations.
- **Exo 12:49** — "One law shall be to him that is homeborn, and unto the stranger that sojourneth among you."
- **Lev 24:22** — "Ye shall have one manner of law, as well for the stranger, as for one of your own country."
- **Num 15:15–16** — "One ordinance shall be both for you of the congregation, and also for the stranger... One law and one manner shall be for you, and for the stranger."
- **Num 15:29** — "Ye shall have one law for him that sinneth through ignorance, both for him that is born among the children of Israel, and for the stranger."

### 2. Sabbath, Feast, and Worship Inclusion
The ger is explicitly included in Sabbath rest, festival observance, and sacrificial worship.
- **Exo 20:10** — "the seventh day is the sabbath of the LORD thy God: in it thou shalt not do any work... nor thy stranger that is within thy gates"
- **Deut 5:14** — "thy manservant and thy maidservant may rest as well as thou... and thy stranger"
- **Exo 23:12** — "the son of thy handmaid, and the stranger, may be refreshed"
- **Deut 16:11, 14** — the ger included in Feast of Weeks and Feast of Tabernacles rejoicing
- **Exo 12:48** — "when a stranger shall sojourn with thee, and will keep the passover to the LORD, let all his males be circumcised"
- **Num 9:14** — "if a stranger shall sojourn among you, and will keep the passover unto the LORD"

### 3. Protection From Oppression — Ethical Mandate
Commands against mistreating the ger, grounded in Israel's own experience as gerim in Egypt.
- **Exo 22:21** — "Thou shalt neither vex a stranger, nor oppress him: for ye were strangers in the land of Egypt."
- **Exo 23:9** — "thou shalt not oppress a stranger: for ye know the heart of a stranger, seeing ye were strangers in the land of Egypt."
- **Lev 19:33–34** — "if a stranger sojourn with thee... thou shalt love him as thyself; for ye were strangers in the land of Egypt."
- **Deut 10:18–19** — "He doth execute the judgment of the fatherless and widow, and loveth the stranger... Love ye therefore the stranger."
- **Deu 27:19** — "Cursed be he that perverteth the judgment of the stranger, fatherless, and widow."

### 4. Social Welfare Triad — Ger, Yathom, Almanah
The ger regularly appears in a fixed triad with the fatherless and the widow as a protected class requiring provision.
- **Deut 14:29** — "the stranger, and the fatherless, and the widow... shall come, and shall eat and be satisfied"
- **Deut 24:19–21** — gleaning laws: "it shall be for the stranger, for the fatherless, and for the widow" (3x)
- **Deut 26:12–13** — third-year tithe distributed to "the Levite, the stranger, the fatherless, and the widow"
- **Jer 7:6** — "if ye oppress not the stranger, the fatherless, and the widow"
- **Zec 7:10** — "oppress not the widow, nor the fatherless, the stranger, nor the poor"
- **Mal 3:5** — God will be "a swift witness against... those that oppress... the stranger"

### 5. Israel as Ger Before God
Israel itself is described as ger in relation to YHWH and his land, establishing theological humility.
- **Gen 15:13** — "thy seed shall be a stranger in a land that is not theirs"
- **Exo 2:22** — Moses names his son Gershom: "I have been a stranger in a strange land"
- **Lev 25:23** — "the land is mine; for ye are strangers and sojourners with me"
- **1 Chr 29:15** — "we are strangers before thee, and sojourners, as were all our fathers"
- **Psa 119:19** — "I am a stranger in the earth"

### 6. Cultic Obligations Binding on the Ger
Specific ritual prohibitions and requirements apply equally to the ger.
- **Lev 17:8, 10** — blood-prohibition and burnt-offering regulations apply to "the strangers which sojourn among you"
- **Lev 17:12–13** — "No soul of you shall eat blood, neither shall any stranger... which hunteth"
- **Lev 16:29** — Day of Atonement affliction applies to native and ger alike
- **Num 15:30** — the presumptuous sinner, "whether he be born in the land, or a stranger," is cut off
- **Lev 20:2** — child-sacrifice prohibition applies to "the strangers that sojourn in Israel"
- **Lev 24:16** — blasphemy penalty: "as well the stranger, as he that is born in the land"

### 7. Eschatological Inheritance
Ezekiel's future-temple vision extends land inheritance to the ger.
- **Eze 47:22** — "ye shall divide it by lot for an inheritance unto you, and to the strangers that sojourn among you... they shall have inheritance with you among the tribes of Israel"
- **Eze 47:23** — "in what tribe the stranger sojourneth, there shall ye give him his inheritance"
