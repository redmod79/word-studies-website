# WG: Heir / Inheritance

## Overview
The inheritance concept in the NT centers on the kleros word family — five cognates spanning the person who inherits, the act of inheriting, the thing inherited, the allotment process, and the shared-heir compound. The cluster intersects heavily with ἐπαγγελία (promise), since Pauline and Hebrews theology anchors heirship in divine promise rather than legal claim.

## Roles in the Concept

- **Person:** kleronomos (G2818) — the heir, the one who stands to receive
- **Action:** kleronomeo (G2816) — to inherit, to come into possession
- **Object:** kleronomia (G2817) — the inheritance itself, the thing received
- **Process:** kleroo (G2820) — the allotment, assignment by lot (Eph 1:11)
- **Shared status:** synkleronomos (G4789) — co-heir, fellow participant in inheritance
- **Basis:** epangelia (G1860) — the promise that grounds heirship (vs. law)
