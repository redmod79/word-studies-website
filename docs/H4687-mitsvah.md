## Lexicon Definition

A command, whether human or divine (collectively, the Law):—(which was) commanded(-ment), law, ordinance, precept.

The word denotes a specific enjoined directive — a concrete charge issued by an authority. In most OT usage the authority is God (commandments of the LORD), though it extends to kings (royal decrees) and fathers (patriarchal charges).

## Semantic Categories

### 1. The Mosaic Law-Code (Commandments Given at Sinai)
The normative use: mitsvah as the specific legal directives of the covenant law.

- **Lev 27:34** — "These [are] the commandments, which the LORD commanded Moses for the children of Israel in mount Sinai."
- **Deut 6:1** — "Now these [are] the commandments, the statutes, and the judgments, which the LORD your God commanded to teach you"
- **Num 15:39** — "ye may look upon it, and remember all the commandments of the LORD, and do them"
- **Exo 24:12** — "I will give thee tables of stone, and a law, and commandments which I have written"

### 2. Covenant Faithfulness Formula (Keeping/Doing Commandments)
The most frequent construction: shâmar (keep) or asah (do) + mitsvah. This is the standard expression for covenant loyalty in Deuteronomy and the Deuteronomistic History.

- **Deut 7:9** — "the faithful God, which keepeth covenant and mercy with them that love him and keep his commandments"
- **Deut 28:1** — "if thou shalt hearken diligently unto the voice of the LORD thy God, to observe and to do all his commandments"
- **1 Ki 2:3** — "keep... his commandments, and his judgments, and his testimonies, as it is written in the law of Moses"
- **Psa 119:47** — "And I will delight myself in thy commandments, which I have loved"

### 3. Covenant Consequences (Blessing, Curse, and Life)
Keeping or breaking commandments carries covenantal stakes — life/death, blessing/curse.

- **Deut 11:27-28** — "A blessing, if ye obey the commandments of the LORD your God... And a curse, if ye will not obey"
- **Deut 6:25** — "it shall be our righteousness, if we observe to do all these commandments before the LORD our God"
- **Prov 4:4** — "keep my commandments, and live"
- **Prov 19:16** — "He that keepeth the commandment keepeth his own soul; [but] he that despiseth his ways shall die"

### 4. Penitential Confession (Breaking/Forsaking Commandments)
Post-exilic prayer and confession uses mitsvah to name what was abandoned.

- **Ezra 9:10** — "what shall we say after this? for we have forsaken thy commandments"
- **Neh 1:7** — "we have not kept the commandments, nor the statutes, nor the judgments"
- **2 Ki 17:16** — "they left all the commandments of the LORD their God, and made them molten images"
- **Dan 9:5** — "We have sinned... and have rebelled, even by departing from thy precepts and from thy judgments"

### 5. Royal and Administrative Decree (Human Command)
The word extends to human authority — king's orders, a father's charge, official directives.

- **2 Ki 18:36** — "the people held their peace... for the king's commandment was, saying, Answer him not"
- **2 Chr 24:21** — "they... stoned him with stones at the commandment of the king"
- **Jer 35:16** — "the sons of Jonadab the son of Rechab have performed the commandment of their father"
- **Job 23:12** — "Neither have I gone back from the commandment of his lips; I have esteemed the words of his mouth more than my necessary [food]"

### 6. Wisdom Tradition (Commandments as Light and Life)
Proverbs and Qohelet treat keeping commandments as the path to flourishing — framed as parental instruction rather than Sinai law, but using the same vocabulary.

- **Prov 6:23** — "For the commandment [is] a lamp; and the law [is] light"
- **Prov 3:1** — "My son, forget not my law; but let thine heart keep my commandments"
- **Psa 119:98** — "Thou through thy commandments hast made me wiser than mine enemies"
- **Ecc 12:13** — "Fear God, and keep his commandments: for this [is] the whole [duty] of man"
