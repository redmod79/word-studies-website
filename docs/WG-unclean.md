# WG: Unclean / Defile / Purify

## Overview
The unclean/defile concept in the NT involves a cluster of words spanning the impurity pole (akathartos, akatharsia, koinos, koinoō, bdelygma) and the purity pole (katharos, katharizō). These words map the full semantic field from defilement through purification, with roots in the Levitical purity system (H2931 tame' / H2891 taher) that the LXX translates using this group.

## Roles in the Concept

- **Impure state (demonic/ritual):** akathartos (G169) — unclean spirits, unclean animals, unclean persons
- **Impure quality (moral):** akatharsia (G167) — uncleanness as vice-list item (Gal 5:19, Eph 5:3)
- **Profane state (common):** koinos (G2839) — what is not holy; less severe than akathartos
- **Act of defiling:** koinoō (G2840) — to render profane or unclean (Mark 7:15, Acts 21:28)
- **Detestable object:** bdelygma (G946) — the abomination that produces defilement
- **Pure state:** katharos (G2513) — clean, pure — the goal of purification
- **Act of purifying:** katharizō (G2511) — to cleanse — the reversal of defilement

## Key Passages Where Multiple Group Words Appear Together

- **Acts 10:14–15** — akathartos + koinos (Peter refuses unclean food) → koinoō + katharizō (God reverses: "what God hath cleansed, call not common")
- **Acts 10:28** — akathartos + koinos (applied to persons, not just food: "not call any man common or unclean")
- **Rev 21:27** — koinoō + bdelygma ("nothing that defileth, neither whatsoever worketh abomination" excluded from New Jerusalem)
- **Mat 23:25–26** — katharizō + katharos (Jesus' woe: "cleanse first that which is within... that the outside may be clean also")
