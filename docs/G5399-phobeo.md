## Lexicon Definition

To frighten, i.e. (passively) to be alarmed; by analogy, to be in awe of, revere. From φόβος (phobos, G5401). Used of both terror before danger and reverent awe before God or authority.

Nearly all forms are middle/passive voice — the active ("to frighten someone") does not occur in the NT. The word is consistently deponent, meaning "to be afraid" or "to fear/revere."

**Present vs. Aorist imperative pattern:** "Fear not" commands split between present imperative (μὴ φοβοῦ — "stop fearing," ongoing) and aorist imperative (φοβήθητε — "do not begin to fear," punctiliar). The aorist imperative in Rev 14:7 (Φοβήθητε τὸν θεόν) is a decisive call to action.
