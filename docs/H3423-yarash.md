## Lexicon Definition

A primitive root; **to occupy (by driving out previous tenants, and possessing in their place)**; by implication, to seize, to rob, to inherit; also to expel, to impoverish, to ruin — KJV: cast out, consume, destroy, disinherit, dispossess, drive(-ing) out, enjoy, expel, without fail, (give to, leave for) inherit(-ance, -or), magistrate, be (make) poor, come to poverty, (give to, make to) possess, get (have) in (take) possession, seize upon, succeed, utterly.

The word fuses two sides of a single action: the incoming party takes possession, the previous tenant is expelled. Qal stem carries the possessing sense ("take/hold as one's own, inherit"); Hiphil stem carries the causative/dispossessing sense ("drive out, cause to be dispossessed").

The Qal/Hiphil split is semantically load-bearing: the same verb describes Israel *taking* the land (Qal) and Yahweh *driving out* the nations (Hiphil). The Niphal exclusively describes economic ruin (Prov 20:13; 23:21; 30:9; Gen 45:11).

## Semantic Categories

### 2. Yahweh driving out the nations (Hiphil — divine dispossession)
