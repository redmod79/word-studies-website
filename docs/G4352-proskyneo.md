## Lexicon Definition

Properly, to kiss the hand to (toward) one, in token of reverence. Among the Orientals, especially the Persians, to fall upon the knees and touch the ground with the forehead as an expression of profound reverence ("to make a salam"). In the NT, by kneeling or prostration to do homage (to one) or make obeisance, whether in order to express respect or to make supplication.

Two main uses:
- **a.** Homage shown to men of superior rank
- **b.** Homage rendered to God, the ascended Christ, heavenly beings, and demons

Sept. very often for הִשְׁתַּחֲוָה (to prostrate oneself, H7812 שָׁחָה shachah).

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Prostration Before Jesus — Healing / Petition
Individuals fall before Jesus while making a verbal request; the act signals recognition of his authority and desperate need.

- **Mat 8:2** — "there came a leper and worshipped him, saying, Lord, if thou wilt, thou canst make me clean"
- **Mat 9:18** — "there came a certain ruler, and worshipped him, saying, My daughter is even now dead"
- **Mat 15:25** — "Then came she and worshipped him, saying, Lord, help me"
- **Mark 5:6** — "But when he saw Jesus afar off, he ran and worshipped him"

### 2. Recognition of Divine Sonship
Worship that confesses who Jesus is — the magi, disciples after miracles, the healed blind man.

- **Mat 2:2, 2:11** — magi come "to worship him" (the newborn King of the Jews); "fell down, and worshipped him"
- **Mat 14:33** — after walking on water: "they worshipped him, saying, Of a truth thou art the Son of God"
- **John 9:38** — healed blind man: "Lord, I believe. And he worshipped him"
- **Mat 28:9, 28:17** — post-resurrection: "they came and held him by the feet, and worshipped him"

### 3. The True Worship Discourse (John 4)
Jesus redefines worship as spiritual and universal rather than geographic — the decisive NT statement on the nature of worship.

- **John 4:20** — "Our fathers worshipped in this mountain; and ye say that in Jerusalem is the place where men ought to worship"
- **John 4:23** — "the true worshippers shall worship the Father in spirit and in truth"
- **John 4:24** — "God is a Spirit: and they that worship him must worship him in spirit and in truth"

### 4. The Exclusive Worship Command
Satan's temptation and Jesus' definitive response quoting Deut 6:13 — proskynéō reserved for God alone.

- **Mat 4:9** — Satan: "All these things will I give thee, if thou wilt fall down and worship me"
- **Mat 4:10** — "Thou shalt worship the Lord thy God, and him only shalt thou serve"
- **Luke 4:7** — "If thou therefore wilt worship me, all shall be thine"

### 5. Heavenly Throne Room Worship (Revelation)
Recurring pattern: elders, angels, and creatures fall before the throne and worship God or the Lamb.

- **Rev 4:10** — "The four and twenty elders fall down before him that sat on the throne, and worship him that liveth for ever"
- **Rev 7:11** — "all the angels... fell before the throne on their faces, and worshipped God"
- **Rev 11:16** — "the four and twenty elders... fell upon their faces, and worshipped God"
- **Rev 19:4** — "the four beasts and four and twenty elders fell down and worshipped God that sat on the throne"

### 6. Eschatological False Worship
Revelation's counterpoint: the earth worships the beast, the dragon, and idols; angels explicitly forbid worshipping them.

- **Rev 13:4** — "they worshipped the dragon... they worshipped the beast, saying, Who is like unto the beast?"
- **Rev 13:8** — "all that dwell upon the earth shall worship him" (the beast)
- **Rev 14:9, 14:11** — "If any man worship the beast and his image... they have no rest day nor night"
- **Rev 9:20** — "repented not... that they should not worship devils, and idols"
- **Rev 19:10, 22:9** — angel refuses John's worship: "See thou do it not... worship God"
