## Lexicon Definition

A primitive root (possibly identical with H6030 through the idea of looking down or browbeating); to depress literally or figuratively, transitive or intransitive — abase self, afflict(-ion, self), chasten self, deal hardly with, defile, exercise, force, gentleness, humble (self), hurt, ravish, sing (by mistake for H6030), submit self, weaken, in any wise.

The Niphal/Pual are the corresponding passive forms ("was afflicted" — Isa 53:7, Psa 119:67).

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Cultic Self-Denial ("Afflict Your Souls")
The Day of Atonement formula — self-denial through fasting.
- **Lev 16:29** — "ye shall afflict your souls, by a statute for ever"
- **Lev 16:31** — "ye shall afflict your souls, by a statute for ever"
- **Lev 23:27** — "a day of atonement... and ye shall afflict your souls"
- **Lev 23:29** — "whatsoever soul it be that shall not be afflicted in that same day, he shall be cut off"
- **Num 29:7** — "ye shall afflict your souls: ye shall not do any work"
- **Isa 58:3,5** — "wherefore have we afflicted our soul, and thou takest no knowledge?"
- **Ezra 8:21** — "that we might afflict ourselves before our God"

### 2. Oppression of People / National Affliction
External oppression by enemies or taskmasters.
- **Gen 15:13** — "they shall afflict them four hundred years" (Egypt)
- **Exo 1:11-12** — "they did set over them taskmasters to afflict them... the more they afflicted them, the more they multiplied"
- **Deut 26:6** — "the Egyptians evil entreated us, and afflicted us"
- **2Ki 17:20** — "the LORD rejected all the seed of Israel, and afflicted them"
- **Psa 94:5** — "they break in pieces thy people, O LORD, and afflict thine heritage"

### 3. Divine Discipline / Humbling
God deliberately humbling or testing through hardship.
- **Deut 8:2** — "the LORD thy God led thee... to humble thee, and to prove thee"
- **Deut 8:3** — "he humbled thee, and suffered thee to hunger, and fed thee with manna"
- **Deut 8:16** — "that he might humble thee, and that he might prove thee, to do thee good at thy latter end"
- **Psa 119:67** — "before I was afflicted I went astray: but now have I kept thy word"
- **Psa 119:71** — "it is good for me that I have been afflicted; that I might learn thy statutes"
- **Psa 119:75** — "thou in faithfulness hast afflicted me"

### 4. Sexual Violation
Forcible humiliation — used for rape and sexual coercion.
- **Gen 34:2** — "he took her, and lay with her, and defiled her"
- **2Sa 13:12,14** — "do not force me... being stronger than she, forced her"
- **Judg 19:24** — "humble ye them, and do with them what seemeth good"
- **Lam 5:11** — "they ravished the women in Zion"
- **Eze 22:10-11** — "have they humbled her that was set apart... hath lewdly defiled his daughter in law"

### 5. Prophetic Suffering / The Afflicted Servant
Affliction as unjust suffering borne by the righteous.
- **Isa 53:4** — "we did esteem him stricken, smitten of God, and afflicted"
- **Isa 53:7** — "he was oppressed, and he was afflicted, yet he opened not his mouth"
- **Psa 88:7** — "thy wrath lieth hard upon me, and thou hast afflicted me with all thy waves"
- **Psa 102:23** — "he weakened my strength in the way; he shortened my days"

### 6. Voluntary Self-Humbling
Reflexive — deliberate humbling before God or authority.
- **Gen 16:9** — "return to thy mistress, and submit thyself under her hands"
- **Dan 10:12** — "thou didst set thine heart to understand, and to chasten thyself before thy God"
- **1Ki 2:26** — "because thou hast been afflicted in all wherein my father was afflicted"
- **Psa 35:13** — "I humbled my soul with fasting"

### 7. Ecclesiastes "Exercise" Sense
A distinct Qal usage: God giving humans occupation/travail to be exercised with.
- **Ecc 1:13** — "this sore travail hath God given to the sons of men to be exercised therewith"
- **Ecc 3:10** — "the travail, which God hath given to the sons of men to be exercised in it"
