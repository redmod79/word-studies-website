## Semantic Categories

### 1. Legal / Official Status (of institutions)
The adjective describes a gathering or body as properly constituted under civil law — officially sanctioned, not a riot or mob.
- **Acts 19:39** — "But if ye inquire any thing concerning other matters, it shall be determined in a **lawful** assembly." (ἐν τῇ ἐννόμῳ ἐκκλησίᾳ — the regular civic assembly of Ephesus, contrasted with the tumultuous mob in the theater)

### 2. Being Subject / Bound to a Law (of persons)
The adjective describes a person as standing within the sphere of a law's authority — obligated to and governed by it.
- **1 Cor 9:21** — "To them that are without law, as without law, (being not without law to God, but **under the law** to Christ,)" (ἔννομος Χριστοῦ — Paul, though free from Mosaic law, is subject to Christ's law; ἔννομος paired with its antonym ἄνομος in the same verse)
