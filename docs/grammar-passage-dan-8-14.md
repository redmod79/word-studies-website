## Key Grammatical Features

### 1. וְנִצְדַּק — Niphal Perfect 3ms of צדק
- **Stem:** Niphal (passive) — the subject receives the action
- **Tense:** Perfect with waw (וְ) in prophetic context = future certainty (prophetic perfect)
- **Significance:** The sanctuary is the RECIPIENT of vindication — passive voice. This is the ONLY Niphal occurrence of צדק in the entire OT.
- **KJV translates as "cleansed"** but the Hebrew root צדק means "to be righteous/just/vindicated" — not ritual cleansing. The ritual cleansing word would be טָהֵר (taher, H2891).
- **Word study:** [H6663-tsadaq.md](../../word-studies/H6663-tsadaq.md)

### 2. קֹדֶשׁ — Noun.ms.Abs (subject of passive verb)
- **As subject of Niphal verb:** The holy place is what receives vindication
- **Without article:** Lacks the definite article (הַ) yet functions as definite from context — the sanctuary already under discussion in Dan 8:11-13

### 3. עֶרֶב בֹּקֶר — Asyndetic noun pair
- **Asyndeton** (no conjunction between "evening" and "morning") suggests a compound time unit, not two separate things
- The pair functions as a single measurement: "evening-morning" = one unit

### 4. Clause 2 is Elliptical
- No explicit verb — the entire time phrase "unto evening-morning 2,300" serves as the temporal framework
- Clause 3 (WQtX) provides the main predication: what happens AFTER the 2,300
