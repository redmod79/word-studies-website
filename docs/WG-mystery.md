# WG: Mystery / Divine Secret

## Overview
The NT concept of divine mystery involves a cluster of five words forming a disclosure arc: the secret is *hidden* (apokryptō, kryptos), then *revealed* (apokalyptō) and *made known* (gnōrizō), with the disclosed secret itself called a *mystērion*. All five appear in the Pauline epistles; the arc is rooted in the Aramaic tradition of Daniel 2.

## Relationships

### Conceptual Arc
The five words form a narrative sequence:
1. **apokryptō** — the secret was concealed (Col 1:26 "hath been hid from ages")
2. **kryptos** — its state: hidden (1 Cor 2:7 "hidden wisdom")
3. **mystērion** — the secret itself (the thing hidden)
4. **apokalyptō** — the act of unveiling (Eph 3:5 "is now revealed")
5. **gnōrizō** — the communication of what was unveiled (Eph 1:9 "Having made known")

Daniel 2 is the conceptual source for the entire NT disclosure pattern: the king has a secret (râz), God reveals it (gᵉlâh), and Daniel makes it known. Paul uses the same structure for the gospel.

## Roles in the Concept

- **The secret:** mystērion (μυστήριον) — what God has kept hidden
- **Prior state:** kryptos (κρυπτός) — the condition of hiddenness
- **The concealing act:** apokryptō (ἀποκρύπτω) — how it was kept hidden
- **The disclosure act:** apokalyptō (ἀποκαλύπτω) — the unveiling moment
- **The communicating act:** gnōrizō (γνωρίζω) — making the disclosed secret known to others

## Key Passages Where Multiple Group Words Appear

- **Col 1:26** — *mystērion* + *apokryptō*: "the mystery which hath been hid (apokekrymmenon) from ages... but now is made manifest"
- **Eph 3:3–5** — *mystērion* + *apokalyptō* + *gnōrizō*: "by revelation he made known (apokalypsei) unto me the mystery... which in other ages was not made known (egnōristhē), as it is now revealed (apokalypsthē)"
- **1 Cor 2:7** — *mystērion* + *kryptos* + *apokryptō*: "wisdom of God in a mystery, even the hidden (apokekrymmenēn) wisdom"
- **Mat 11:25** — *apokalyptō* + *apokryptō*: "thou hast hid (apekrypas) these things from the wise... and hast revealed (apekalypsas) them unto babes"
