## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same across testaments:**
- The literal-to-figurative spectrum: both labash and endyo range from physical garments to abstract qualities
- The causative dimension: God as the one who clothes (Hiphil in OT, divine passive in NT)
- The identity-transformation meaning: being clothed changes who the person is or how they appear (priestly investiture → baptismal identity)

**What shifts:**
- OT emphasizes priestly and royal investiture as the primary figurative domain; NT extends this to all believers ("put on Christ," "put on the new man")
- The OT Spirit-clothing idiom (Judg 6:34 — the Spirit "clothed" Gideon) finds its NT counterpart in "being clothed with power from on high" (Luke 24:49, using a different Greek word but the same concept)
- The eschatological "putting on" of incorruption (1 Cor 15:53-54) has no direct Hebrew precedent with labash, though Isa 51:9 and 52:1 command Zion to "put on" strength and beautiful garments
