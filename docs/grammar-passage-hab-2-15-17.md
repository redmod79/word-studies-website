## Key Grammatical Features

### 1. הֹוי — Woe-oracle interjection opening the oracle

- **Form:** Interjection (הוֹי), regularly used to introduce prophetic woe-oracles; followed by participial phrases identifying the one addressed.
- **Significance:** This is the fourth of five הוֹי-oracles in Hab 2:6-20 (vv. 6, 9, 12, 15, 19). The interjection plus substantival participles (מַשְׁקֵה, מְסַפֵּחַ) functions to indict the addressee by labeling his characteristic behavior — "Woe to [the one] giving-drink … [the one] pouring-out." Participles here are not merely descriptive but charge-stating: they fix the accused by his habitual act.
- **Grammar reference:** see `concepts/mashal-speech-act.md` for related prophetic speech-act analysis.

### 2. מַשְׁקֵה and מְסַפֵּחַ — Substantival participles in the indictment

- **Form:** מַשְׁקֵה is a Hiphil masculine singular participle in the **construct state** from שׁקה ("cause to drink"); מְסַפֵּחַ is a Piel masculine singular participle, absolute, from ספח ("attach/pour out").
- **Significance:** After הוֹי, Hebrew prophetic speech regularly attaches a string of participles that function as substantives — naming the one addressed by his action. The Hiphil of שׁקה is a causative ("make drink" > "give drink"), and the **construct state** on the participle binds it directly to its object רֵעֵהוּ ("his neighbor") without an intervening article — literally "the one-causing-to-drink-of his neighbor." The Piel of ספח intensifies the act of pouring/attaching the vessel.
- **Grammar reference:** [`hebrew/stem-hiphil.md`](../hebrew/stem-hiphil.md), [`hebrew/construct-state.md`](../hebrew/construct-state.md), [`hebrew/construct-chain-suffix.md`](../hebrew/construct-chain-suffix.md)

### 3. שַׁכֵּר — Piel infinitive absolute in imperatival/continuative force

- **Form:** Piel **infinitive absolute** of שׁכר ("be drunk" / Piel "make drunk"), in sequence with preceding participles through וְאַף ("and even").
- **Significance:** The infinitive absolute here carries over the force of the preceding participles — "and (he is) even making-drunk." This is a classic use of the infinitive absolute as a **continuation** of a finite or participial verb in the same clause-sequence, allowing the indictment to intensify without repeating the participial morphology. The Piel of שׁכר shifts the Qal stative "be drunk" into a causative-factitive "make drunk" — the subject is actively inducing drunkenness in the neighbor.
- **Grammar reference:** [`hebrew/infinitive-absolute-intensification.md`](../hebrew/infinitive-absolute-intensification.md)
- **Word study:** [`word-studies/H7937-shakar.md`](../../word-studies/H7937-shakar.md)

### 4. לְמַעַן הַבִּיט — Purpose clause with Hiphil infinitive construct

- **Form:** לְמַעַן ("in order that") + Hiphil **infinitive construct** of נבט ("look at, gaze upon"). The Hiphil of נבט is the standard stem for intentional looking/regarding.
- **Significance:** לְמַעַן + infinitive construct marks the **purpose** of the entire preceding indictment: the neighbor is made drunk *so that* the addressee can gaze on his nakedness. This nails the moral character of the crime — it is not an incidental result but the deliberate goal. The infinitive construct form is typical after לְמַעַן for expressing purpose, where a finite verb would be awkward.
- **Grammar reference:** [`hebrew/stem-hiphil.md`](../hebrew/stem-hiphil.md), [`hebrew/infinitive-construct-natan-predicative.md`](../hebrew/infinitive-construct-natan-predicative.md) (analogous infinitive-construct syntax)

### 5. שְׁתֵה … וְהֵעָרֵל — Qal and Niphal imperatives in sarcastic reversal (v. 16)

- **Form:** שְׁתֵה is a Qal masculine singular imperative of שׁתה ("drink!"). וְהֵעָרֵל is a Niphal masculine singular imperative — either from ערל ("be uncircumcised" > figuratively "expose yourself") or, with one consonantal reordering read by LXX/Vg as וְהֵרָעֵל ("and stagger"). The MT parsing as Niphal imperative of ערל is what the parser recognizes.
- **Significance:** Two imperatives addressed to the oppressor form a **sarcastic invitation** — the one who forced his neighbor to drink is now commanded to drink himself. The Niphal of ערל is significant: Niphal carries reflexive/passive force, so the command reads "make-yourself-exposed / let-yourself-be-treated-as-uncircumcised." The pairing enacts the talionic principle — the same shame inflicted is now commanded upon the perpetrator. These are *prophetic imperatives*: the command functions as a declaration of coming judgment rather than a literal instruction.
- **Grammar reference:** [`hebrew/imperative-mood.md`](../hebrew/imperative-mood.md), [`hebrew/stem-niphal.md`](../hebrew/stem-niphal.md)
- **Word study:** [`word-studies/H8354-shathah.md`](../../word-studies/H8354-shathah.md)

### 6. כֹּוס יְמִין יְהוָה — Triple construct chain (v. 16)

- **Form:** Three-member construct chain: כֹּוס (fs construct "cup of") → יְמִין (fs construct "right-hand of") → יְהוָה (absolute "YHWH"). Parser confirms: "cup — CONSTRUCT / right-hand — CONSTRUCT / YHWH — ABSOLUTE."
- **Significance:** The subject of תִּסֹּוב ("shall come around") is this extended construct chain. In a chain of three, only the final member takes the absolute state; the whole chain functions as a single noun-phrase: "the-cup-of-the-right-hand-of-YHWH." The right hand is the agent of action; the cup is what that hand administers. This is the standard prophetic "cup of wrath" motif (cf. Jer 25:15-29; Isa 51:17, 22; Ps 75:8), now grammatically anchored to YHWH as the source of the reversal.
- **Grammar reference:** [`hebrew/construct-state.md`](../hebrew/construct-state.md), [`hebrew/construct-chain-suffix.md`](../hebrew/construct-chain-suffix.md)
- **Word study:** [`word-studies/H3563-kowc.md`](../../word-studies/H3563-kowc.md)

### 7. תִּסֹּוב … יְכַסֶּךָּ … יְחִיתַן — Imperfects of coming judgment (vv. 16-17)

- **Form:** Three Qal/Piel/Hiphil imperfects:
  - תִּסֹּוב (Qal impf 3fs, סבב "turn, come around")
  - יְכַסֶּךָּ (Piel impf 3ms + 2ms suffix, כסה "cover" — intensified/factitive: "completely cover")
  - יְחִיתַן (Hiphil impf 3ms + 3fp suffix, חתת "terrify" — causative: "make them terrified")
- **Significance:** All three imperfects denote **future, certain divine action**. The Piel of כסה (יְכַסֶּךָּ) carries factitive force — not just "cover" but "overwhelm, completely cover," with the 2ms pronominal suffix making the addressee the direct object. The Hiphil of חתת is a causative "cause-to-be-terrified," with 3fp suffix pointing back to בְּהֵמֹות ("beasts"). These imperfects drive the *lex talionis* reversal of v. 16 into concrete historical devastation.
- **Grammar reference:** [`hebrew/qal-imperfect.md`](../hebrew/qal-imperfect.md), [`hebrew/hiphil-imperfect.md`](../hebrew/hiphil-imperfect.md), [`hebrew/stem-hiphil.md`](../hebrew/stem-hiphil.md)

### 8. חֲמַס לְבָנֹון / שֹׁד בְּהֵמֹות / דְּמֵי אָדָם / חֲמַס אֶרֶץ — Stacked construct chains of indictment (v. 17)

- **Form:** Four construct-absolute pairs in rapid sequence, identified by the parser:
  - חֲמַס לְבָנֹון — "violence of Lebanon"
  - שֹׁד בְּהֵמֹות — "devastation of beasts"
  - דְּמֵי אָדָם — "bloods of humanity"
  - חֲמַס אֶרֶץ — "violence of land/earth"
- **Significance:** The syntactic ambiguity of the construct is theologically pointed. חֲמַס לְבָנֹון can be either a **subjective genitive** ("violence which Lebanon committed") or **objective genitive** ("violence done against Lebanon"). The context of v. 17 — cover, terrify, blood-guilt — strongly favors the **objective** reading: the violence done *to* Lebanon and its beasts returns upon the oppressor to cover him. This is the syntactic hinge of the entire judgment: the genitive relation itself carries the talionic logic.
- **Grammar reference:** [`hebrew/construct-state.md`](../hebrew/construct-state.md), [`hebrew/syntax-compound-object-waw.md`](../hebrew/syntax-compound-object-waw.md)

## Clause Structure

### v. 15 — Participial indictment chain (all Q-domain, i.e. direct speech)

```
Clause 1: [Intj] הוֹי                               — "Woe!"
Clause 2: [PreC] מַשְׁקֵה + [Objc] רֵעֵהוּ         — "the one giving-drink to his neighbor"
Clause 3: [PreC] מְסַפֵּחַ + [Objc] חֲמָתְךָ       — "pouring out your wrath/bottle"
Clause 4: [Conj] וְ + [Modi] אַף + [Pred] שַׁכֵּר  — "and even making-drunk" (InfAbs)
Clause 5: [Pred] לְמַעַן הַבִּיט + [Cmpl] עַל מְעוֹרֵיהֶם — "in order to look upon their nakedness" (InfC purpose)
```

The interjection + string of participles + infinitive absolute + לְמַעַן-infinitive construct is a tightly constructed **indictment formula** — one continuous charge with no finite main verb in the Hebrew until v. 16. The participles and infinitives function nominally to name the accused by his deeds.

### v. 16 — Perfect + imperatives + imperfect verdict

```
Clause 1 (ZQt0): [Pred] שָׂבַעְתָּ + [Cmpl] קָלוֹן + [Adju] מִכָּבוֹד  — "You are sated with shame rather than glory" (Qal perfect — stative completed)
Clause 2 (ZImX): [Pred] שְׁתֵה + [Subj] גַם אַתָּה                       — "Drink, you too!" (Qal imperative)
Clause 3 (WIm0): [Conj] וְ + [Pred] הֵעָרֵל                               — "and expose yourself" (Niphal imperative)
Clause 4 (ZYqX): [Pred] תִּסֹּוב + [Cmpl] עָלֶיךָ + [Subj] כֹּוס יְמִין יְהוָה  — "It shall turn upon you — the cup of YHWH's right hand" (Qal impf)
Clause 5 (Ellp): [Conj] וְ + [Subj] קִיקָלוֹן + [Cmpl] עַל כְּבוֹדֶךָ     — "and shame-vomit upon your glory" (elliptical — supplies תִּסֹּב)
```

The movement is striking: **perfect** (the shame you sought has already filled you) → **imperatives** (taunt: drink and expose yourself) → **imperfect** (the cup now turns, future certain). Clause 5 is marked "Ellp" (elliptical) — the verb תִּסֹּב from clause 4 is implied, with קִיקָלוֹן as the new subject.

### v. 17 — Causal clause (כִּי) with double imperfect

```
Clause 1 (XYqt): [Conj] כִּי + [Subj] חֲמַס לְבָנוֹן + [PreO] יְכַסֶּךָּ       — "For violence-of-Lebanon shall cover you"
Clause 2 (WXYq): [Conj] וְ + [Subj] שֹׁד בְּהֵמוֹת + [PreO] יְחִיתַן + [Adju] מִדְּמֵי אָדָם וַחֲמַס אֶרֶץ קִרְיָה וְכָל — "and destruction of beasts shall terrify them, because of bloods of men and violence of land/city/all"
Clause 3 (Ptcp): [PreC] יֹשְׁבֵי + [Cmpl] בָהּ                                  — "dwellers in it" (participial qualifier of קִרְיָה)
```

The כִּי opens a **causal/explanatory clause** grounding vv. 15-16: the reversal comes *because* the violence done to Lebanon and its beasts, and the shed human blood, demands recompense. The WX-YQ clause type (Waw-X-Yiqtol) is a foregrounded parallel to the first clause, maintaining focus on the subject (שֹׁד בְּהֵמוֹת) rather than continuing the verbal chain. The 3fs suffix on בָהּ refers back to קִרְיָה (feminine singular).

## Exegetical Summary of Grammatical Signals

1. **vv. 15-16a** unfold in the shape of a classic הוֹי-oracle: interjection + participles + infinitive absolute + purpose clause — no finite main verb. This piles the indictment without breath.
2. **v. 16** pivots on three verbal moods: perfect (the shame has happened), imperatives (mocking taunt), imperfect (divine reversal coming).
3. **The triple construct כֹּוס יְמִין יְהוָה** (cup-of-right-hand-of-YHWH) grammatically anchors the reversal to YHWH as direct agent; it is His right hand that administers the cup He has been drinking from.
4. **The 2ms pronominal suffixes** (חֲמָתְךָ, עָלֶיךָ, כְּבוֹדֶךָ, יְכַסֶּךָּ) keep the address pointed and personal throughout.
5. **v. 17** grounds the whole in חֲמַס ("violence") — twice as construct head — tying the Chaldean's violence to Lebanon and to the blood of humanity, with the objective-genitive reading ("violence done against") carrying the moral weight of the imperfects יְכַסֶּךָּ and יְחִיתַן.
