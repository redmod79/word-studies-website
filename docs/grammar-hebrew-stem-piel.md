# Piel Stem (Hebrew)

## Definition
The Piel is the D-stem (*duplication* stem) of the Hebrew verbal system, characterized by a doubled (geminated) middle root consonant. Its core semantic contribution is **bringing about a state** — the subject causes the object to enter the condition described by the root. This contrasts with the Hiphil, which causes an *action* or *event*.

1. **Factitive** — causes an adjectival/stative condition: "make X [adjective]"
2. **Resultative** — brings about a completed result upon the object
3. **Declarative-Estimative** — declares/considers the object to be in a state
4. **Denominative** — derives verbal action from a noun

## Form Recognition
- **Doubled middle consonant** (dagesh forte in R2) across all conjugations
- **Vowel pattern:** *qiṭṭēl* (perfect), *yəqaṭṭēl* (imperfect), *qaṭṭēl* (imperative/infinitive), *məqaṭṭēl* (participle)
- **Hireq** under first root letter in perfect; **sheva** under preformative in imperfect
- **Parser code:** `vs=piel`

## Functions with Examples

### 1. Factitive (Causing a State)
The Piel causes the object to enter a state expressed by the adjectival/stative root meaning. Qal קָדַשׁ = "be holy" (intransitive stative); Piel קִדַּשׁ = "make holy, consecrate" (transitive factitive). Qal חָלַל = "be profaned"; Piel חִלֵּל = "profane, desecrate."

| Reference | Hebrew | Parsing | KJV Translation |
|-----------|--------|---------|-----------------|
| Gen 2:3 | יְקַדֵּשׁ | Piel.Wayq.3ms | "sanctified it" |
| Exo 13:2 | קַדֶּשׁ | Piel.Impv.2ms | "Sanctify unto me all the firstborn" |
| Exo 20:11 | יְקַדְּשֵׁהוּ | Piel.Wayq.3ms+3ms | "hallowed it" |
| Lev 18:21 | תְחַלֵּל | Piel.Impf.2ms | "neither shalt thou profane the name" |
| Lev 19:8 | חִלֵּל | Piel.Perf.3ms | "he hath profaned the hallowed thing" |
| Eze 22:26 | יְחַלְּלוּ | Piel.Wayq.3mp | "have profaned mine holy things" |
| Isa 43:28 | (חלל Piel) | Piel.Wayq.1s | "I have profaned the princes of the sanctuary" |

The factitive is the most characteristic Piel function. It causes a state *without regard to the process* (Waltke-O'Connor, IBHS p.400). The Piel of קדשׁ makes something holy; the Piel of חלל does the opposite — it removes holiness, rendering something common/profane.

### 2. Resultative (Bringing About a Completed Result)
With roots that are already fientive (active) in the Qal, the Piel intensifies toward a thorough or completed result on the object.

| Reference | Hebrew | Parsing | KJV Translation |
|-----------|--------|---------|-----------------|
| Exo 9:25 | שִׁבֵּר | Piel.Perf.3ms | "brake every tree of the field" |
| Exo 23:24 | תְּשַׁבֵּר | Piel.Impf.2ms | "thou shalt … quite break down their images" |
| Exo 32:19 | יְשַׁבֵּר | Piel.Wayq.3ms | "brake them beneath the mount" (tablets) |
| Gen 6:18 | שַׁחֵת | Piel.InfCon | "to destroy all flesh" |
| Exo 21:34 | יְשַׁלֵּם | Piel.Impf.3ms | "shall make it good" (complete restitution) |

Qal שָׁבַר = "break"; Piel שִׁבֵּר = "shatter" (thorough, completed breaking). Qal שָׁחַת = "spoil/ruin"; Piel = "destroy" (bring to full destruction). Qal שָׁלֵם = "be complete/at peace"; Piel = "make complete/restore/repay."

### 3. Declarative-Estimative (Declaring a State)
The subject pronounces or considers the object to be in the state described by the root. The subject does not cause the physical condition but formally recognizes or declares it.

| Reference | Hebrew | Parsing | KJV Translation |
|-----------|--------|---------|-----------------|
| Lev 13:6 | טִהֲרוֹ | Piel.Perf.3ms+3ms | "the priest shall pronounce him clean" |
| Lev 13:13 | טִהַר | Piel.Perf.3ms | "he shall pronounce him clean" |
| Lev 14:7 | טִהֲרוֹ | Piel.Perf.3ms+3ms | "shall pronounce him clean" |
| Lev 13:34 | טִהַר | Piel.Perf.3ms | "the priest shall pronounce him clean" |

Qal טָהֵר = "be clean" (stative); Piel = "declare clean, pronounce clean." The priest examines the skin condition and then *declares* the person clean — he does not *make* the person physically clean (the healing has already occurred). KJV "pronounce clean" accurately reflects the declarative force.

### 4. Denominative (Verbal Action from a Noun)
The Piel is the most common stem for forming denominative verbs — verbs derived from nouns rather than from verbal roots.

| Reference | Hebrew | Parsing | KJV Translation |
|-----------|--------|---------|-----------------|
| Exo 28:41 | (כהן Piel) | Piel.Perf.3p | "minister unto me in the priest's office" |
| Exo 29:1 | כַהֵן | Piel.InfCon | "to minister … in the priest's office" |
| Gen 12:2 | אֲגַדְּלָה | Piel.Impf.1s | "make thy name great" |
| Gen 8:15 | יְדַבֵּר | Piel.Wayq.3ms | "God spake" |

כֹּהֵן (noun) = "priest" → Piel כִּהֵן = "act as priest, serve in priestly office." The noun is primary; the verb describes performing the role denoted by the noun. דִּבֵּר "speak" occurs almost exclusively in the Piel; some grammarians treat it as denominative from דָּבָר "word" (Waltke-O'Connor, IBHS p.411). גִּדֵּל "make great" from גָּדוֹל "great" overlaps with the factitive category.

## Contrast with Related Forms

| Stem | Core Function | Example Root קדשׁ |
|------|---------------|-------------------|
| **Qal** | Base stative/fientive | קָדַשׁ "be holy" (intransitive) |
| **Piel** | Factitive — cause a state | קִדַּשׁ "make holy, consecrate" (transitive) |
| **Hiphil** | Causative — cause an event/action | הִקְדִּישׁ "cause to be holy, dedicate" |
| **Pual** | Passive of Piel | קֻדַּשׁ "be consecrated" (Exo 29:33) |
| **Hithpael** | Reflexive of Piel | הִתְקַדֵּשׁ "sanctify oneself" (Exo 19:22) |

**Piel vs. Hiphil:** The key distinction (Waltke-O'Connor, IBHS pp.400, 416): the Piel causes a *state* — "John made the cabbage cooked" (factitive); the Hiphil causes an *event* — "John caused the cabbage to cook" (causative). With the Piel, the object is passively transposed into a new condition. With the Hiphil, the object actively participates in a caused process.

**Piel vs. Pual:** Active/passive pair. Piel קִדַּשׁ "he consecrated" → Pual קֻדַּשׁ "it was consecrated."
