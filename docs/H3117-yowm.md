## Semantic Categories

### 1. Literal Calendar Day (24-hour or daylight period)
The most basic usage: the day as a unit of time, whether the light period alone or the full
evening-to-morning cycle (Gen 1 pattern).

- **Gen 1:5** — "And God called the light Day, and the darkness he called Night. And the evening and the morning were the first day."
- **Gen 1:8** — "And the evening and the morning were the second day."
- **Lev 7:15** — "The flesh of the sacrifice of his peace offerings for thanksgiving shall be eaten the same day that it is offered."
- **Num 3:13** — "On the day that I smote all the firstborn in the land of Egypt I hallowed unto me all the firstborn in Israel."

### 2. Historical Period / Era ("In the Days of X")
The plural *yamim* (days) frames biographical and regnal periods. Often identifies when a prophet
ministered or when an event took place.

- **Amos 1:1** — "The words of Amos…which he saw concerning Israel in the days of Uzziah king of Judah."
- **Mic 1:1** — "The word of the LORD…in the days of Jotham, Ahaz, and Hezekiah, kings of Judah."
- **Judg 2:7** — "The people served the LORD all the days of Joshua, and all the days of the elders that outlived Joshua."
- **2 Sam 2:11** — "The time that David was king in Hebron over the house of Judah was seven years and six months."

### 3. Appointed / Holy Day (Ritual and Covenantal Days)
Days designated for worship, sacrifice, ordination, or divine encounter—where the specific day
carries covenantal significance.

- **Lev 6:20** — "This is the offering of Aaron and of his sons, which they shall offer unto the LORD in the day when he is anointed."
- **Num 3:1** — "These also are the generations of Aaron and Moses in the day that the LORD spake with Moses in mount Sinai."
- **1 Sam 1:3** — "This man went up out of his city yearly to worship and to sacrifice unto the LORD of hosts in Shiloh."
- **Lev 8:35** — "Therefore shall ye abide at the door of the tabernacle…day and night seven days."

### 4. The Day of the LORD (Eschatological/Theophanic)
A concentrated prophetic use—"that day" or "the day of the LORD"—denoting divine judgment,
deliverance, or theophanic intervention. This accounts for the extreme density of *yom* in
Obadiah (28.6/100), Zephaniah (26.4/100), Joel, Amos, and Malachi.

- **Amos 2:16** — "He that is courageous among the mighty shall flee away naked in that day, saith the LORD."
- **Obad 1:11** — "In the day that thou stoodest on the other side, in the day that the strangers carried away captive his forces."
- **Zeph 1:7** — "Hold thy peace at the presence of the Lord GOD: for the day of the LORD is at hand."
- **Mal 3:2** — "But who may abide the day of his coming? and who shall stand when he appeareth?"

### 5. Lifetime / Biographical Span
The plural *yamim* = the total span of a person's life; "length of days" = long life; "all the days of" = throughout one's lifetime.

- **Josh 1:5** — "There shall not any man be able to stand before thee all the days of thy life."
- **Prov 3:2** — "For length of days, and long life, and peace, shall they add to thee."
- **Prov 3:16** — "Length of days is in her right hand; and in her left hand riches and honour."
- **Eccl 2:23** — "For all his days are sorrows, and his travail grief; yea, his heart taketh not rest in the night."

### 6. Adverbial and Idiomatic Expressions
*Yom* in construct phrases that function adverbially: "this day" (today), "all the day" (continuously),
"day and night" (without ceasing), "for ever" (extended beyond days). These are largely frozen idioms.

- **Deut 1:10** — "The LORD your God hath multiplied you, and, behold, ye are this day as the stars of heaven for multitude."
- **Num 6:4** — "All the days of his separation shall he eat nothing that is made of the vine tree."
- **Josh 1:5** — "All the days of thy life" (yom = throughout a defined duration)
- **Ps 42:3** — "My tears have been my meat day and night" (yom + layil as a merism for continuous time)
