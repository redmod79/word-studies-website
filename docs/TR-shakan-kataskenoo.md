**Structure of G2681:** κατά (down, intensifying) + σκηνή (skēnē, tent). Literally: to camp/encamp down. The LXX translators' choice to render shākan with a verb built from σκηνή (tent) is a deliberate theological decision — it preserves the tent/tabernacle imagery inherent in the Hebrew root (whose derived noun is מִשְׁכָּן, the Tabernacle). The same semantic content crosses the language boundary intact.

**Semantic continuity:** The tabernacle-presence nuance of shākan is preserved through the LXX rendering. Where shākan emphasizes God choosing to encamp among people, κατασκηνόω in the LXX maintains this sense of purposeful, condescending divine settlement.

## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same:**
- The tent/tabernacle nuance is fully preserved: both shākan and κατασκηνόω are built from "tent" roots (mishkan / skēnē)
- The sense of purposeful settlement — not wandering, but choosing to rest in a place — carries over
- The eschatological direction: "I will dwell among you" in the OT becomes "the tabernacle of God is with men" (Rev 21:3, using cognate σκηνόω G4637)

**What shifts:**
- In the NT, κατασκηνόω appears only 4x and shifts from primarily divine dwelling (God's choice) to: (1) birds nesting (parabolic), and (2) the flesh resting in hope (resurrection). The heavy OT concentration on God's indwelling presence disperses into more varied usages.
- The NT theological weight of this concept transfers largely to cognate G4637 σκηνόω (skēnoō) — the simpler form — which carries the great incarnational statement (John 1:14: "the Word...dwelt [eskenōsen] among us") and the eschatological climax (Rev 21:3: "he will dwell [skēnōsei] with them").

**The trajectory:**
OT: שָׁכַן → tabernacle presence (God choosing to dwell among Israel in the wilderness sanctuary)
LXX: κατασκηνόω → translation preserving the tent imagery
NT (G2681): birds nesting (parable); flesh resting (resurrection quotation)
NT (cognate G4637): Incarnation (John 1:14) → eschatological fulfillment (Rev 21:3)
