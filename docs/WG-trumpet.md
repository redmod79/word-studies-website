# WG: Trumpet

## Overview
The Greek trumpet word family in the NT — the instrument (σάλπιγξ), the act of sounding it (σαλπίζω), and the person who sounds it (σαλπιστής) — forms a tight lexical group derived from a single root. All three appear primarily in eschatological and apocalyptic contexts, with strong OT roots in two distinct Hebrew instruments: the *shofar* (ram's horn) and the *chatsotsrah* (silver priestly trumpet).

## Roles in the Concept

- **Instrument:** G4536 σάλπιγξ — the physical trumpet (or the sound it produces)
- **Action:** G4537 σαλπίζω — the act of blowing / sounding the trumpet
- **Agent:** G4538 σαλπιστής — the person who performs the action

## Key Passages Where Multiple Group Words Appear Together

- **1 Cor 15:52** — "at the last trump [σάλπιγξ G4536]: for the trumpet shall sound [σαλπίσει G4537], and the dead shall be raised incorruptible"
- **Rev 8:6** — "the seven angels which had the seven trumpets [σαλπίγγων G4536] prepared themselves to sound [σαλπίσωσιν G4537]"

## Dominant Context: The Seven Trumpets of Revelation 8–11

G4537 appears 10/12 times in Revelation. Each of the seven angels sounds (σαλπίζω) in sequence, releasing cosmic judgments. The pattern is formulaic:
> "The [ordinal] angel sounded [ἐσάλπισεν], and [judgment followed]..."

This is the NT's primary deployment of the word group — not musical performance, but divine signaling of eschatological events.
