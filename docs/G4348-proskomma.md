## Lexicon Definition

Literally, a "stub" — an obstruction that causes the foot to strike and trip. Figuratively: an obstacle that causes moral or spiritual failure; an occasion of apostasy or sin. Unlike σκάνδαλον (the trap-bait), πρόσκομμα pictures the stationary obstacle one's foot encounters while walking.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. The Stumbling-Stone of Israel's Unbelief (Rom 9 / 1 Pet 2)
Paul and Peter both cite Isa 8:14 using πρόσκομμα + σκάνδαλον / πέτρα to describe Christ as the stone over which unbelieving Israel trips.

- **Rom 9:32** — "they stumbled at that stumblingstone (λίθῳ τοῦ προσκόμματος)"
- **Rom 9:33** — "Behold, I lay in Sion a stumblingstone (λίθον προσκόμματος) and rock of offence"
- **1 Pet 2:8** — "a stone of stumbling (λίθος προσκόμματος), and a rock of offence"

### 2. Meat/Liberty as Stumbling-Block to the Weak Brother
The Pauline ethical use: the strong believer's exercise of freedom can become a πρόσκομμα to the conscience-weak brother.

- **Rom 14:13** — "that no man put a stumblingblock (πρόσκομμα) or an occasion to fall in his brother's way"
- **Rom 14:20** — "it is evil for that man who eateth with offence (διὰ προσκόμματος)"
- **1 Cor 8:9** — "lest by any means this liberty of yours become a stumblingblock (πρόσκομμα) to them that are weak"

## Pauline Context — Rom 14:13, 20; 1 Cor 8:9 (paired with G4625 and G4624)

Three of the six NT uses cluster in Paul's weak-brother ethics. Rom 14:13 sets πρόσκομμα beside σκάνδαλον as a doublet; Rom 14:20 attaches "eating with πρόσκομμα" to the warning "destroy not the work of God"; 1 Cor 8:9 brands liberty itself as potentially a πρόσκομμα to the weak. In this context, πρόσκομμα names the *effect* a believer's action has on another — a stationary obstacle set in a brother's path.
