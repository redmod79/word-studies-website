## Lexicon Definition

From κατά (G2596) and ἀλλάσσω (G236); to change mutually, i.e. (figuratively) to compound a difference, to reconcile:

1. **To change, exchange** — as of coins (classical Greek usage)
2. **To reconcile** — to restore to favor, bring back into right relationship; in the NT, always of restoring the relationship between God and man or between estranged persons

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Divine Reconciliation of Enemies
God reconciles those who were in a state of enmity — the initiative belongs to God, not the sinner.

- **Rom 5:10** — "For if, when we were enemies, we were reconciled to God by the death of his Son, much more, being reconciled, we shall be saved by his life."
- **2 Cor 5:18** — "And all things are of God, who hath reconciled us to himself by Jesus Christ, and hath given to us the ministry of reconciliation."

### 2. God's Cosmic Reconciling Activity
God is the agent reconciling the entire world, with non-imputation of trespasses as a defining feature.

- **2 Cor 5:19** — "To wit, that God was in Christ, reconciling the world unto himself, not imputing their trespasses unto them; and hath committed unto us the word of reconciliation."

### 3. The Appeal to Be Reconciled
The imperative form — calling people to accept the reconciliation God has made available.

- **2 Cor 5:20** — "Now then we are ambassadors for Christ, as though God did beseech you by us: we pray you in Christ's stead, be ye reconciled to God."

### 4. Human Reconciliation (Marital)
The only non-divine usage — an estranged wife restoring her relationship with her husband.

- **1 Cor 7:11** — "But and if she depart, let her remain unmarried, or be reconciled to her husband: and let not the husband put away his wife."
