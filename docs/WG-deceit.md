## Relationships

### Shared Roots
- **mirmah (H4820)**, **remiyyah (H7423)**, **tormah (H8649)** — all from the root רמה (ramah, "to deceive/deal treacherously"). Three cognate nouns spanning active fraud, negligent treachery, and rare poetic synonym.
- **sheqer (H8267)** — from שׁקר (shaqar, "to deal falsely")
- **kazab (H3577)** — from כָּזַב (kazab, "to lie, be a liar")
- **kachash (H3585)** — from כָּחַשׁ (kachash, "to be untrue, deny, disown")
- **tok (H8496)** — from same base as תָּוֶךְ (tavek, "midst"), in the sense of injury/oppression

## Roles in the Concept

- **Broadest term:** sheqer (שֶׁקֶר) — the most frequent and generic word for falsehood/lying (109v)
- **Calculated fraud:** mirmah (מִרְמָה) — deliberate deception for advantage; dominant in commercial and political contexts (38v)
- **Fabricated lie:** kazab (כָּזָב) — a conscious, factual lie; invented falsehood (29v)
- **Negligent treachery:** remiyyah (רְמִיָּה) — deception through slackness, laxness disguising infidelity (15v)
- **Denial/disowning:** kachash (כַּחַשׁ) — lying by withholding truth or feigning ignorance (6v)
- **Rare cognates:** tormah (תׇּרְמָה, 6v) near-synonym of mirmah; tok (תֹּךְ, 3v) adds the nuance of oppressive injury through fraud
