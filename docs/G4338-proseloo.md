## Semantic Categories

### 1. Permanent Cancellation of a Legal Document by Nailing

The word's sole use describes the act of affixing a debt-certificate to the cross, rendering it permanently void. Three verbs operate on the same object (the χειρόγραφον, "handwriting of ordinances"): ἐξαλείψας ("blotting out"), ἦρκεν ἐκ τοῦ μέσου ("taken out of the way"), and προσηλώσας ("nailing"). The nailing is the climactic, irrevocable action.

- **Col 2:14** — "Blotting out the handwriting of ordinances that was against us, which was contrary to us, and took it out of the way, nailing it to his cross"
