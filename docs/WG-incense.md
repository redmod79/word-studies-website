# WG: Incense / Fumigation

## Concept
The semantic cluster of Hebrew and Greek words related to **aromatic incense** — both the compound itself and the act of burning it. Central to tabernacle/temple worship as a symbol of prayer ascending before God; condemned when offered to idols or unauthorized persons.

### Word Relationships

**Root cluster (all from H6999 קָטַר):** H7004, H6988, H7002 are all nominal derivatives of the root verb qâṭar (to fumigate/burn). The verb appears most often; qeṭoreth (H7004) is the primary substantive.

**Frankincense distinction:** H3828 לְבוֹנָה (lᵉbôwnâh) is a specific aromatic resin (from its whiteness), one of the components in the sacred blend, sometimes listed alongside qeṭoreth.

**LXX/NT bridge:** G2368 θυμίαμα translates H7004 in 52 LXX co-occurrences and appears 6 times in NT (Luke 1:10–11; Rev 5:8; 8:3–4). See [TR-qtoreth-thymiama.md](TR-qtoreth-thymiama.md).

## Shared Context

These words cluster in Exodus 30 (incense instructions), Leviticus 16 (Day of Atonement), Numbers 7 (census offerings), and 2 Chronicles (temple worship). The prophetic books (Isaiah, Ezekiel, Jeremiah) use qâṭar (H6999) overwhelmingly in condemnation of idolatrous incense.
