## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same:**
- The physical act (prostration, bowing) remains the primary image throughout OT, LXX, and NT
- The exclusive worship command (only to God/YHWH) carries directly from Deuteronomy into Jesus' response to Satan and Revelation's anti-beast warnings
- Paired with speech ("fell down and said...") in both OT narratives and NT accounts

**What shifts:**
- In OT, shachah freely describes prostration before kings, commanders, and superiors (social use); in NT, proskynéō is used for humans only in narrative-context petitioning of Jesus — and Revelation 19:10/22:9 explicitly corrects even prostration before angels
- Jesus becomes the new object of worship alongside the Father — without replacement of the OT monotheistic framework; the NT never calls this idolatry
- John 4's "spirit and truth" discourse detaches worship from sacred geography entirely
- Revelation introduces a stark eschatological binary: worship God (throne) vs. worship the beast (mark) — the shachah/proskynéō vocabulary frames the ultimate conflict
