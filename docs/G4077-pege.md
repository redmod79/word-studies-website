## Semantic Categories

### 1. Physical Water Source (Well/Spring)
- **John 4:6** — "Now Jacob's well (πηγή) was there. Jesus therefore, being wearied with his journey, sat thus on the well"
- **John 4:14** — "the water that I shall give him shall be in him a well (πηγή) of water springing up into everlasting life"

The Samaritan well scene uses πηγή for the physical well, then transitions it into metaphor. John 4:6 uses πηγή twice (nominative + dative), treating the well as a natural spring-fed source.

### 2. Bodily Flow (Physiological Source)
- **Mark 5:29** — "And straightway the fountain (πηγή) of her blood was dried up"

The only medical/physiological use. Refers to the source of the woman's hemorrhage — the originating flow rather than the blood itself.

### 3. Metaphor for Speech/Character (Ethical Source)
- **James 3:11** — "Doth a fountain (πηγή) send forth at the same place sweet water and bitter?"
- **James 3:12** — "so can no fountain (πηγή) both yield salt water and fresh"

James uses πηγή as a figure for the human tongue/heart as the source from which speech flows. The argument: a single source cannot produce contradictory outputs.

### 4. Dry/Empty Source (False Teachers)
- **2 Peter 2:17** — "These are wells (πηγαί) without water, clouds that are carried with a tempest"

Plural πηγαί applied to false teachers — sources that promise refreshment but deliver nothing. The emphasis falls on the expected function (water supply) being absent.

### 5. Cosmic/Eschatological Water Sources
- **Revelation 8:10** — "it fell upon the third part of the rivers, and upon the fountains of waters"
- **Revelation 14:7** — "worship him that made heaven, and earth, and the sea, and the fountains of waters"
- **Revelation 16:4** — "the third angel poured out his vial upon the rivers and fountains of waters"

In the Revelation judgment sequences, πηγαί τῶν ὑδάτων ("fountains of waters") appears as a standard phrase alongside rivers — the freshwater supply infrastructure of the earth subject to divine judgment.

### 6. Eschatological Life-Giving Source
- **Revelation 7:17** — "the Lamb...shall lead them unto living fountains (πηγὰς ζωσάς) of waters"
- **Revelation 21:6** — "I will give unto him that is athirst of the fountain (πηγή) of the water of life freely"

The redemptive counterpart to the judgment uses. God/the Lamb as the πηγή of living water echoes John 4:14 and the OT spring-of-life motif.
