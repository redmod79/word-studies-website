Ezekiel is the sole user of this noun. The word appears exclusively in the two great allegory chapters: Ezekiel 16 (Jerusalem as unfaithful wife, 11 occurrences) and Ezekiel 23 (Oholah/Oholibah, 8 occurrences). No other biblical author uses taznuwth — the root verb zanah (H2181) and the cognate nouns zanuwn (H2183) and zenuwth (H2184) carry the same semantic range elsewhere (Hosea, Jeremiah, Numbers).

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Covenant Infidelity as Marital Unfaithfulness
The dominant usage: Jerusalem/Judah's idolatry depicted as a wife's harlotry against her husband (YHWH).
- **Eze 16:15** — "playedst the harlot because of thy renown, and pouredst out thy fornications on every one that passed by"
- **Eze 16:22** — "in all thine abominations and thy whoredoms thou hast not remembered the days of thy youth"
- **Eze 16:36** — "thy nakedness discovered through thy whoredoms with thy lovers, and with all the idols of thy abominations"
- **Eze 23:35** — "Because thou hast forgotten me, and cast me behind thy back, therefore bear thou also thy lewdness and thy whoredoms"

### 2. Escalation and Multiplication of Idolatry
Taznuwth frequently appears with verbs of increase, emphasizing that the infidelity was not a single act but a compounding pattern.
- **Eze 16:25** — "hast opened thy feet to every one that passed by, and multiplied thy whoredoms"
- **Eze 16:26** — "hast increased thy whoredoms, to provoke me to anger"
- **Eze 16:29** — "thou hast moreover multiplied thy fornication in the land of Canaan unto Chaldea"
- **Eze 23:14** — "she increased her whoredoms"
- **Eze 23:19** — "she multiplied her whoredoms, in calling to remembrance the days of her youth"

### 3. Alliance with Foreign Nations as Whoredom
Political alliances with pagan empires are cast as acts of harlotry — Egypt, Assyria, Babylon.
- **Eze 16:26** — "committed fornication with the Egyptians thy neighbours"
- **Eze 23:7** — "she committed her whoredoms with them, with all them that were the chosen men of Assyria"
- **Eze 23:8** — "Neither left she her whoredoms brought from Egypt"
- **Eze 23:17** — "the Babylonians came to her into the bed of love, and they defiled her with their whoredom"

### 4. Exposure and Judgment
The discovery and punishment of whoredom — YHWH exposing what was done in secret, leading to shaming and retribution.
- **Eze 23:18** — "she discovered her whoredoms, and discovered her nakedness: then my mind was alienated from her"
- **Eze 23:29** — "shall leave thee naked and bare: and the nakedness of thy whoredoms shall be discovered"
- **Eze 16:33** — "thou givest thy gifts to all thy lovers, and hirest them, that they may come unto thee on every side for thy whoredom"

### 5. Child Sacrifice as Extension of Whoredom
The most extreme expression — sacrificing children to idols is framed as part of the same pattern of harlotry.
- **Eze 16:20** — "thou hast sacrificed unto them to be devoured. Is this of thy whoredoms a small matter"
