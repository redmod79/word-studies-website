## Lexicon Definition

A throwing beyond others, i.e. (figuratively) supereminence; adverbially (with G2596 or G2596 and G2532) pre-eminently. Used exclusively in adverbial phrases: *kath' huperbolen* ("beyond measure," "exceedingly"), *kath' huperbolen eis huperbolen* ("far more exceedingly," lit. "excess unto excess").

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Surpassing degree of divine realities
- **2 Cor 4:17** — "For our light affliction, which is but for a moment, worketh for us a far more exceeding [and] eternal weight of glory" — *kath' huperbolen eis huperbolen* (double use: excess unto excess), applied to the weight of glory
- **2 Cor 4:7** — "that the excellency of the power may be of God, and not of us" — *huperbole* as nominative subject: the surpassing quality belongs to God's power
- **1 Cor 12:31** — "and yet shew I unto you a more excellent way" — *kath' huperbolen hodon*: introducing the love chapter (1 Cor 13) as a way that surpasses spiritual gifts

### 2. Extreme degree of human experience (suffering/trial)
- **2 Cor 1:8** — "we were pressed out of measure, above strength, insomuch that we despaired even of life" — *kath' huperbolen*: trial in Asia exceeded Paul's capacity
- **2 Cor 12:7** — "lest I should be exalted above measure through the abundance of the revelations" — *huperbole* used twice in this verse for the excess of revelations (dative *huperbolē*) that prompted the thorn in the flesh

### 3. Extreme degree of sin
- **Rom 7:13** — "that sin by the commandment might become exceeding sinful" — *kath' huperbolen hamartolos*: sin's true nature exposed through the law as surpassingly sinful

### 4. Extreme degree of human zeal
- **Gal 1:13** — "beyond measure I persecuted the church of God, and wasted it" — *kath' huperbolen*: Paul's pre-conversion persecution described as exceeding all bounds

## Morphological Notes

All 8 tokens appear in only three forms:
- **Accusative singular** (ὑπερβολήν) — 6 tokens: used in the prepositional phrase *kath' huperbolen* ("according to excess" = exceedingly/beyond measure)
- **Nominative singular** (ὑπερβολή) — 1 token: 2 Cor 4:7 as grammatical subject
- **Dative singular** (ὑπερβολῇ) — 1 token: 2 Cor 12:7 ("by the excess of the revelations")

The accusative *kath' huperbolen* construction dominates (6 of 8 tokens), functioning as a fixed adverbial phrase meaning "to an extraordinary degree."
