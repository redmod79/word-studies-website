# WG: Becoming / Coming-into-Being

## Overview
The concept of becoming and coming-into-existence in Greek is anchored by γίνομαι (ginomai) and its cognate family, contrasted with the static being of εἰμί. This group covers origin, birth, generation, and active making — the full spectrum of how things come to be in the NT.

## Roles in the Concept

- **Core Process:** ginomai (γίνομαι) — the broadest verb for coming-into-being, happening, or changing state
- **Biological Origin:** gennaō (γεννάω) — begetting and birth
- **Origin Event:** genesis (γένεσις) — the birth itself, the beginning
- **Resulting Kind:** genos (γένος) — the race, stock, or kind that results from generation
- **Collective Span:** genea (γενεά) — a generation, the people who share an origin
- **Active Agency:** poieō (ποιέω) — making, doing — the agent's side of creation
- **Static Contrast:** ēn (ἦν) — simple being, the backdrop against which becoming stands out

## Key Passages Where Multiple Group Words Appear Together

- **John 1:1-3** — ēn (1:1, "the Word was") + ginomai (1:3, "all things were made by him") — the prologue's famous contrast between eternal being and created becoming
- **John 8:58** — ginomai ("before Abraham was born") vs. eimi ("I am") — the sharpest theological contrast in the group
- **Gal 4:4** — ginomai twice: "made (genomenon) of a woman, made (genomenon) under the law" — the incarnation as divine becoming
