## Lexicon Definition

A primitive root; properly, to dash in pieces, i.e. kill (a human being), especially to murder:—put to death, kill, (man-) slay(-er), murder(-er).

## Semantic Categories

### 1. The Sixth Commandment — Absolute Prohibition
- **Exo 20:13** — "Thou shalt not kill."
- **Deut 5:17** — "Thou shalt not kill."
- **Jer 7:9** — "Will ye steal, murder, and commit adultery..." (rebuke for breaking the Decalogue)
- **Hos 4:2** — "By swearing, and lying, and killing, and stealing..." (covenant indictment)

### 2. The "Slayer" — Accidental / Unintentional Homicide
Overwhelmingly the participle רֹצֵחַ designates a person who killed without malice and flees to a city of refuge.
- **Num 35:11** — "that the slayer may flee thither, which killeth any person at unawares"
- **Deut 4:42** — "the slayer might flee thither, which should kill his neighbour unawares"
- **Deut 19:4** — "Whoso killeth his neighbour ignorantly, whom he hated not in times past"
- **Josh 20:3** — "the slayer that killeth any person unawares and unwittingly"

### 3. The Murderer — Intentional, Premeditated Killing
Same verb, but distinguished by context, motive (enmity, lying in wait), and instrument.
- **Num 35:16** — "he is a murderer: the murderer shall surely be put to death"
- **Num 35:19** — "The revenger of blood himself shall slay the murderer"
- **Num 35:31** — "Moreover ye shall take no satisfaction for the life of a murderer"
- **1 Kgs 21:19** — "Hast thou killed, and also taken possession?" (Elijah to Ahab over Naboth)

### 4. Judicial Execution of the Murderer
The same root is used for the lawful putting-to-death of the convicted murderer — showing the word itself is morally neutral and determined by context.
- **Num 35:27** — "and slay the slayer; he shall not be guilty of blood"
- **Num 35:30** — "the murderer shall be put to death by the mouth of witnesses"

### 5. Prophetic Indictment of Violent Society
The Piel stem intensifies the sense to iterative, calculated killing.
- **Isa 1:21** — "it was full of judgment; righteousness lodged in it; but now murderers"
- **Hos 6:9** — "the company of priests murder in the way by consent"
- **Psa 94:6** — "They slay the widow and the stranger, and murder the fatherless"
- **Psa 62:3** — "ye shall be slain all of you" (threat against adversaries)

### 6. Being Slain / Passive Victimhood
Niphal stem — the one killed rather than the killer.
- **Jdg 20:4** — "the Levite, the husband of the woman that was slain"
- **Pro 22:13** — "The slothful man saith... I shall be slain in the streets"
