# WG: Graven Image — OT Idol Vocabulary

## Overview
Five Hebrew words form the core vocabulary for prohibited images and idols in the OT. Together they cover the physical fabrication methods (carving, casting), the theological judgment on such objects (worthless, vain), and the associated visual concept (likeness, similitude) that the second commandment prohibits.

## Relationships

### Shared Roots
- pecel (H6459) and peciyl (H6456) — both from root פסל (pacal, H6458, "to carve/hew")
- massekah (H4541) — from נסך (nacak, H5258, "to pour out"); the casting/pouring method
- eliyl (H457) — from אל (al, H408, "not/nothing"); etymologically = "good for nothing"
- temunah (H8544) — from מין (miyn, H4327, "to portion out/form"); a shaped likeness

## Roles in the Concept

- **Prohibited object (carved):** pecel (H6459) — the specific word in the 2nd commandment; a wood or stone idol shaped by chiseling
- **Prohibited object (cast):** massekah (H4541) — the molten/poured metal image; paired with pecel to cover both fabrication methods
- **Near-synonym (carved):** peciyl (H6456) — same root as pecel, overlapping usage; predominates in Deuteronomy 7, Micah, Hosea
- **Theological verdict:** eliyl (H457) — the idol judged as worthless/vain; emphasizes its ontological emptiness
- **Visual form prohibited:** temunah (H8544) — the "likeness" or "similitude"; names not the object but the visual resemblance it attempts to capture

## Key Passages Where Multiple Group Words Appear

- **Exo 20:4** — pecel + temunah: "Thou shalt not make unto thee any graven image, or any likeness of any thing"
- **Deu 27:15** — pecel + massekah: "Cursed be the man that maketh any graven or molten image"
- **Lev 26:1** — eliyl + pecel: "Ye shall make you no idols nor graven image"
- **Hab 2:18** — pecel + massekah + eliyl: "What profiteth the graven image... the molten image, and a teacher of lies"
- **Jdg 18:17-18** — pecel + massekah + peciyl: the Danites take Micah's carved image, ephod, teraphim, and molten image
