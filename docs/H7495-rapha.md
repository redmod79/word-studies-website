## Lexicon Definition

Or רָפָה (râphâh); a primitive root; properly, to mend (by stitching), i.e. (figuratively) to cure: — cure, (cause to) heal, physician, repair, thoroughly, make whole.

**Qal** dominates (58%) and covers the full semantic range: God as active healer (Gen 20:17, 2Ki 20:5), participle *ropheʼ* as "physician" (Gen 50:2, Jer 8:22), and general healing action. **Niphal** (25%) is the passive "be healed" — used for physical skin conditions (Lev 13–14), incurable wounds (Deut 28:27, 35), and the prophetic declaration of Isa 53:5. **Piel** (12%) carries intensive force: Elijah repairing the altar (1Ki 18:30), Elisha healing the waters (2Ki 2:21), and false prophets superficially healing the people's hurt (Jer 6:14, 8:11). **Hithpael** (5%) appears only for kings going to be healed of battle wounds (2Ki 8:29, 9:15; 2Ch 22:6).
