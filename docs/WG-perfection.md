# WG: Perfection / Completion (τελ- Family)

## Overview

The Greek NT vocabulary of perfection and completion clusters around the root τελ- (tel-), derived from τέλος (telos, "end/goal"). The group expresses: moral-spiritual maturation, task accomplishment, priestly consecration, and the designed end-state of persons or processes. Distinguished from the πλήρ- (plēr-) "fulfillment/filling" family by its orientation toward a purposive goal rather than quantitative fullness.

## Roles in the Concept

- **Process verb:** τελειόω (G5048) — bringing persons or love to designed completeness; passive-dominant (57% passive forms)
- **State adjective:** τέλειος (G5046) — the resulting condition: mature, complete, lacking nothing
- **Quality noun:** τελειότης (G5047) — abstract state of perfection
- **Act noun:** τελείωσις (G5050) — the event of being perfected / fulfilled
- **Completion verb:** τελέω (G5055) — finishing a task, paying what is owed, accomplishing
- **Restoration verb:** καταρτίζω (G2675) — mending what is broken, fitting together what is disjointed
- **Accomplishment verb:** ἐπιτελέω (G2005) — carrying a begun work through to its end
