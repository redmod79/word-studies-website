## Lexicon Definition

A runner ahead, i.e. scout (figuratively, a precursor). From πρό (pro, "before") and the alternate of δραμεῖν (dramein, aorist of τρέχω, "to run"). Literally "one who runs before" — a forerunner or advance scout sent ahead of a main body.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Priestly Forerunner — One Who Enters Ahead on Behalf of Others
- **Heb 6:20** — "Whither the forerunner is for us entered, [even] Jesus, made an high priest for ever after the order of Melchisedec."

This is the sole NT occurrence. The word is used substantivally (with the article ὁ implied by context) to describe Jesus as one who has entered the inner sanctuary ahead of believers, securing their future access. The construction ὑπὲρ ἡμῶν ("for us" / "on our behalf") emphasizes the representative, vicarious nature of the forerunner's action. The forerunner enters not for his own sake but to open the way for those who follow.

The immediate context (Heb 6:19-20) places prodromos in a sanctuary-access framework: hope as an anchor enters "within the veil" (εἰσερχομένην εἰς τὸ ἐσώτερον τοῦ καταπετάσματος), and the forerunner has entered there on behalf of believers.
