## Lexicon Definition

From G40 (hagios); sacredness, i.e. properly the quality of holiness. Unknown to secular Greek authors (Thayer). Two senses: (1) majesty, the incomparable divine holiness — God's own holy nature; (2) moral purity — the quality of holiness as a personal attribute in believers.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Divine Nature / Majestic Holiness — Christ's Holy Essence
- **Rom 1:4** — "And declared [to be] the Son of God with power, according to the spirit of holiness (πνεῦμα ἁγιωσύνης), by the resurrection from the dead"
  - Genitive singular: ἁγιωσύνης modifying πνεῦμα
  - Contrasts with "according to the flesh" (κατὰ σάρκα) in v. 3 — flesh/holiness antithesis frames Christ's two natures
  - "Spirit of holiness" is a Hebraistic construction (cf. LXX Psa 50:13 πνεῦμα τὸ ἅγιόν) describing Christ's inherent holy quality, not merely the Holy Spirit as a person
  - The resurrection vindicated and declared (ὁρίζω, aorist passive participle) what the spirit of holiness already was

### 2. Moral Purity — Believers' Pursuit of Holiness
- **2 Cor 7:1** — "Having therefore these promises, dearly beloved, let us cleanse ourselves from all filthiness (μολυσμός) of the flesh and spirit, perfecting holiness (ἐπιτελοῦντες ἁγιωσύνην) in the fear of God"
  - Accusative singular: direct object of ἐπιτελέω (present active participle — ongoing completion)
  - Context: follows the separation call of 2 Cor 6:14–18 and the promise of divine sonship (6:18)
  - Two-fold cleansing: filthiness of flesh (σάρξ) AND spirit (πνεῦμα) — holiness is comprehensive
  - "Perfecting" (ἐπιτελέω) implies holiness is a quality being brought to completion, not instantaneously achieved

### 3. Eschatological Holiness — Blamelessness at Christ's Return
- **1 Thess 3:13** — "To the end he may stablish your hearts unblameable (ἄμεμπτος) in holiness (ἐν ἁγιωσύνῃ) before God, even our Father, at the coming (παρουσία) of our Lord Jesus Christ with all his saints (ἅγιοι)"
  - Dative singular with ἐν: sphere or manner — hearts established "in the sphere of holiness"
  - Eschatological setting: "at the coming (parousia) of our Lord Jesus"
  - "Before God" (ἔμπροσθεν τοῦ Θεοῦ) — holiness that withstands divine inspection
  - Notable collocation: ἁγιωσύνη (the quality) appears alongside ἅγιοι (the people) in the same verse
