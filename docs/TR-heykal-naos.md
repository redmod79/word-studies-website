## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same across testaments:**
- Both heykal and naos designate the dwelling place of deity (not the surrounding courts/precinct)
- Both carry the sense of sacred interiority — where God's presence resides
- The heavenly temple concept spans both testaments (Psalm 11:4; Isaiah 6:1 → Revelation 11:19; 15:5)

**What shifts:**
- NT extends naos metaphorically to believers' bodies and the church (1 Cor 3:16; 6:19; Eph 2:21) — a usage not found for heykal in the OT
- The "no temple" vision (Rev 21:22) represents a theological terminus: God and the Lamb replace the naos entirely
- Heykal could mean "palace" (secular royal building); NT naos never has this secular sense
