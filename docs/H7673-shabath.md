## Semantic Categories

### 1. God's Primal Rest at Creation
The Qal verb describes God ceasing from creative work on the seventh day — the archetype of all Sabbath rest.

- **Gen 2:2** — "and he rested on the seventh day from all his work which he had made"
- **Gen 2:3** — "because that in it he had rested from all his work which God created and made"
- **Exo 31:17** — "for in six days the LORD made heaven and earth, and on the seventh day he rested, and was refreshed"

### 2. Sabbath Keeping — Sacred Weekly and Annual Cessation
The Qal or reflexive use commands Israel to cease labor on the seventh day and sacred occasions.

- **Exo 16:30** — "So the people rested on the seventh day"
- **Exo 23:12** — "on the seventh day thou shalt rest: that thine ox and thine ass may rest"
- **Exo 34:21** — "on the seventh day thou shalt rest: in earing time and in harvest thou shalt rest"
- **Lev 23:32** — "ye shall afflict your souls: ... from even unto even, shall ye celebrate your sabbath"

### 3. Land Sabbath — The Earth's Rest and Its Consequences
Leviticus legislates a Sabbath for the land; Chronicles records its fulfillment in the Babylonian exile.

- **Lev 25:2** — "then shall the land keep a sabbath unto the LORD"
- **Lev 26:34–35** — "Then shall the land enjoy her sabbaths ... as long as it lieth desolate it shall rest; because it did not rest in your sabbaths"
- **2 Chr 36:21** — "until the land had enjoyed her sabbaths: for as long as she lay desolate she kept sabbath"

### 4. General Cessation of Activity
Qal uses where an activity, phenomenon, or circumstance simply stops — not specifically a Sabbath or judgment context.

- **Gen 8:22** — "While the earth remaineth, seedtime and harvest … shall not cease"
- **Josh 5:12** — "the manna ceased on the morrow after they had eaten of the old corn of the land"
- **Job 32:1** — "So these three men ceased to answer Job, because he was righteous in his own eyes"
- **Prov 22:10** — "Cast out the scorner, and contention shall go out; yea, strife and reproach shall cease"

### 5. Prophetic Judgment: God Causes to Cease (Hiphil)
The dominant use in the prophets — God issues oracles in which he announces that he will cause specific things (joy, worship, wickedness, nations) to cease as an act of judgment.

- **Eze 26:13** — "I will cause the noise of thy songs to cease; and the sound of thy harps shall be no more heard"
- **Jer 7:34** — "Then will I cause to cease from the cities of Judah … the voice of mirth, and the voice of gladness"
- **Hos 2:11** — "I will also cause all her mirth to cease, her feast days, her new moons, and her sabbaths"
- **Isa 13:11** — "I will cause the arrogancy of the proud to cease"

### 6. Daniel's Prophecy — Cessation of Sacrifice (Hiphil)
Two occurrences in Daniel use the Hiphil in eschatological/prophetic passages.

- **Dan 9:27** — "he shall cause the sacrifice and the oblation to cease"
- **Dan 11:18** — "a prince for his own behalf shall cause his reproach to cease"
