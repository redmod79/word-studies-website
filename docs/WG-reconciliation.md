## Roles in the Concept

- **Standard action:** katallassō (G2644) — the reconciling act; God active, humans passive (Rom 5:10; 2 Cor 5:18-20); also human-to-human (1 Cor 7:11)
- **Received state:** katallagē (G2643) — the reconciliation as gift/status (Rom 5:11; 11:15; 2 Cor 5:18-19)
- **Cosmic scope:** apokatallassō (G604) — intensified reconciliation encompassing "all things" and "principalities" (Eph 2:16; Col 1:20, 22)
- **Human application:** diallassō (G1259) — brother-to-brother reconciliation before worship (Matt 5:24)
- **Peacemaking:** eirēnopoieō (G1517) — making peace through the blood of the cross (Col 1:20)
- **Mediation:** mesitēs (G3316) — the go-between who enables reconciliation (1 Tim 2:5; Heb 8:6; 9:15; 12:24)

## Key Passages Where Multiple Group Words Appear Together

- **2 Cor 5:18-19** — katallassō (G2644) + katallagē (G2643): "who hath reconciled us to himself... the ministry of reconciliation... reconciling the world... the word of reconciliation"
- **Col 1:20-22** — apokatallassō (G604) + eirēnopoieō (G1517): "having made peace through the blood of his cross, by him to reconcile all things"
