## Lexicon Definition

From σῆμα (sēma), a mark; to indicate, signify. The core sense is communication through signs rather than direct plain statement — pointing to something by means of an indicator or token.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Indicating the Manner of Death (Johannine Usage)
- **John 12:33** — "This he said, signifying what death he should die." (Jesus speaking of being "lifted up")
- **John 18:32** — "That the saying of Jesus might be fulfilled, which he spake, signifying what death he should die."
- **John 21:19** — "This spake he, signifying by what death he should glorify God."

All three Johannine uses follow the identical pattern: Jesus speaks indirectly about the future, and the narrator explains that his words were "signifying" (σημαίνων) the manner of death — either his own or Peter's. The word captures how Jesus communicated prophetic truth through veiled or symbolic language rather than explicit statement.

### 2. Prophetic Revelation through the Spirit
- **Acts 11:28** — "And there stood up one of them named Agabus, and signified by the spirit that there should be great dearth throughout all the world."

Here the word describes a prophet (Agabus) communicating future events through the Holy Spirit. The medium is prophetic sign-action or symbolic declaration, not mere verbal prediction.

### 3. Formal Communication / Making Known
- **Acts 25:27** — "For it seemeth to me unreasonable to send a prisoner, and not withal to signify the crimes laid against him."

Festus uses the word in a legal/administrative sense — to formally indicate or specify the charges. This is the most "ordinary" use, closest to the basic meaning of making something known through designation.

### 4. Divine Apocalyptic Communication
- **Rev 1:1** — "The Revelation of Jesus Christ, which God gave unto him, to shew unto his servants things which must shortly come to pass; and he sent and signified it by his angel unto his servant John."

This programmatic use in Revelation's opening verse is theologically dense. The chain of communication runs: God → Jesus Christ → angel → John → the servants. The verb ἐσήμανεν (aorist active) describes how Christ communicated the revelation — through signs (σημεῖα). This establishes that Revelation's content is sign-communicated, mediated through symbolic imagery rather than literal plain-language description.
