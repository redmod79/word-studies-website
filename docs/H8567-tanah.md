## Semantic Categories

The word's two attestations divide cleanly into two tonal poles, unified by a common action: *publicly and repeatedly recounting an event as ritual memorial*.

### 1. Ritual Recital of Victory / Righteous Acts
- **Jdg 5:11** — "there shall they **rehearse** the righteous acts (צִדְקוֹת) of the LORD, even the righteous acts toward the inhabitants of his villages in Israel"
  - Deborah's song; recitation occurs at the watering places, by delivered people, celebrating YHWH's saving deeds.

### 2. Ritual Lamentation / Commemoration of Tragedy
- **Jdg 11:40** — "the daughters of Israel went yearly to **lament** the daughter of Jephthah the Gileadite four days in a year"
  - Annual four-day memorial for Jephthah's daughter; a fixed, recurring, communal act of recounting.

Both uses share: (1) a plural subject (communal), (2) a recurring/durative aspect (Piel), (3) vocal recitation, (4) a remembered event as object. The semantic core is "to recount as memorial" — praise when the memory is glorious, lament when it is tragic.
