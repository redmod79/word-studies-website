## Relationships

### Word Families
- **pharma- family:** pharmakeia (G5331), pharmakeus (G5332), pharmakos (G5333) — all from pharmakon (drug/potion). Concentrated in Galatians 5 and Revelation 9, 18, 21, 22.
- **mag- family:** mageia (G3095), mageuō (G3096), magos (G3097) — from foreign (Persian) origin. Concentrated in Matthew 2 and Acts 8, 13.
- **Standalone:** baskainō (G940) — "evil eye" bewitchment; only in Gal 3:1.

### Co-occurrence (appear in same verses)

No pairwise co-occurrences exist. Each word appears in entirely different verses. The group coheres through semantic domain (Louw-Nida 53.10 — Magic), not textual co-occurrence.

## Roles in the Concept

- **The Drug-Based Practice:** pharmakeia (G5331) — sorcery through substances/potions; paired with idolatry in vice lists; instrument of Babylon's end-time deception
- **The Drug-Based Practitioners:** pharmakeus (G5332) + pharmakos (G5333) — those who practice pharmakeia; excluded from the holy city (Rev 21:8; 22:15)
- **The Magic Art:** mageia (G3095) — general magic practice (Acts 8:11, Simon Magus)
- **The Magic Action:** mageuō (G3096) — to practice magic (Acts 8:9, Simon Magus)
- **The Magician:** magos (G3097) — dual usage: positive (wise men, Matt 2) and negative (sorcerer, Acts 13)
- **Bewitching by Fascination:** baskainō (G940) — to bewitch through the evil eye or false persuasion (Gal 3:1)

## Key Passages

- **Gal 5:19–21** — pharmakeia in the "works of the flesh" list, paired with idolatry
- **Rev 9:21** — pharmakeia among sins the impenitent refuse to abandon
- **Rev 18:23** — pharmakeia as Babylon's instrument for deceiving all nations
- **Rev 21:8; 22:15** — pharmakeus/pharmakos excluded from the holy city
- **Acts 8:9–11** — mageuō/mageia: Simon Magus bewitching Samaria
- **Acts 13:6–8** — magos: Bar-Jesus the sorcerer opposing Paul
- **Matt 2:1–16** — magos: wise men visiting the infant Jesus (positive usage)
- **Gal 3:1** — baskainō: "who hath bewitched you?"
