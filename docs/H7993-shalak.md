## Lexicon Definition

> A primitive root; to throw out, down or away (literally or figuratively): — adventure, cast (away, down, forth, off, out), hurl, pluck, throw.

## Verb Stem Distribution

| Stem | Count | Voice | Typical Meaning |
|------|-------|-------|-----------------|
| Hiphil | 112 | causative active | to throw, cast, hurl, fling (actor actively casts object) |
| Hophal | 13 | causative passive | to be cast, be thrown, be flung (object passively cast down) |

The verb occurs exclusively in causative stems — never in Qal. This is morphologically significant: *shâlak* is inherently a causative action. The Hiphil names the agent who does the casting; the Hophal names the object that undergoes being cast, leaving the caster implicit or suppressed. The Hophal participle *mušlāḵ* ("cast out/down") and Hophal perfect *hošlaḵ* ("was cast") are the key passive forms.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Physical Casting / Discarding (Everyday Action)
The literal sense: a person throws an object away, out, or into a location.
- **Gen 21:15** — "she **cast** the child under one of the shrubs" (Hagar)
- **Exo 4:3** — "**Cast** it on the ground. And he cast it on the ground, and it became a serpent"
- **Exo 32:24** — "I **cast** it into the fire, and there came out this calf"
- **Eccl 3:5-6** — "a time to **cast away** stones... a time to **cast away**" (proverbial)

### 2. Casting Down From Height / Violent Overthrow
Object thrown from an elevated position to a lower one. Often involves bodies, corpses, or the demeaning exposure of the dead.
- **2 Ki 9:25-26** — "**cast him** in the portion of the field of Naboth" (Jehu on Joram's corpse)
- **2 Ki 9:33** — (context for Jezebel thrown from window, though shalak not in 9:33 itself; cf. the broader Jehu cycle 2 Ki 9:25-26)
- **Jdg 9:53** — "a certain woman **cast** a piece of a millstone upon Abimelech's head"
- **2 Sa 20:21-22** — "his head shall be **thrown** to thee over the wall"
- **Job 18:7** — "his own counsel shall **cast him down**"
- **Eze 19:12** — "she was **cast down** to the ground" (vine parable — Hophal perfect 3fs)

### 3. Casting of Corpses (Dishonorable Burial / Exposure)
A distinct sub-pattern: the dead are "cast" rather than buried — sign of judgment or shame.
- **Jos 8:29** — "**cast** it [the king of Ai's corpse] at the entering of the gate"
- **Jos 10:27** — "**cast them** into the cave"
- **1 Ki 13:24-28** — "his carcase was **cast** in the way" (Hophal participle 3x)
- **Jer 14:16** — "the people... shall be **cast out** in the streets" (Hophal participle mp)
- **Jer 22:19** — "with the burial of an ass... **cast forth** beyond the gates"
- **Jer 36:30** — "his dead body shall be **cast out**" (Hophal participle 3fs)

### 4. Divine Hurling / YHWH as Subject
God throws, flings, or hurls in judgment — often with decisive, overwhelming force.
- **Exo 15:25** — "the LORD shewed him a tree, which when he had **cast** into the waters"
- **Jos 10:11** — "the LORD **cast down** great stones from heaven" (the Beth-horon hailstones)
- **Jon 2:3** — "thou hadst **cast me** into the deep" (Jonah speaking; divine hurling in 1:15 through sailors; here the cast is attributed to God)
- **Neh 9:11** — "thou **threwest** [them] into the deeps" (Egyptians in Red Sea)
- **Psa 147:17** — "He **casteth forth** his ice like morsels"
- **Nah 3:6** — "I will **cast** abominable filth upon thee"
- **Mic 7:19** — "thou wilt **cast** all their sins into the depths of the sea"

### 5. Covenant Rejection / Casting Out of the Land or Presence
Metaphorical casting — God removes a people or person from covenant standing, from His presence, or from the land.
- **Deu 29:28** — "**cast them** into another land"
- **2 Ki 13:23** — "neither **cast** he them from his presence as yet"
- **2 Ki 17:20** — God "**cast them** out of his sight"
- **2 Ki 24:20** — "he had **cast them** out from his presence"
- **Jer 7:15** — "I will **cast you** out of my sight"
- **Jer 52:3** — "till he had **cast them** out from his presence"
- **Psa 51:11** — "**Cast me** not away from thy presence"
- **Psa 71:9** — "**Cast me** not off in the time of old age"

### 6. Discarding as Religious / Moral Act
Throwing something away as an act of renunciation, repentance, or purification.
- **Eze 18:31** — "**Cast away** from you all your transgressions"
- **Eze 20:7-8** — "**Cast** ye away every man the abominations of his eyes"
- **Isa 2:20** — "a man shall **cast** his idols of silver... to the moles and to the bats"
- **Nah 9:26** — "**cast thy law** behind their backs" (negative: repudiation of Torah)
- **2 Ch 33:15** — Manasseh "**cast** them out of the city" (idols)

### 7. Apocalyptic / Exalted-Being Cast From Heaven
A distinct theological pattern: a figure of cosmic or royal pretension is hurled down from an exalted position — the heaven-to-earth / height-to-ground vector applied to rebellious grandeur.
- **Isa 14:19** — "thou art **cast out** of thy grave like an abominable branch" (Hophal perfect 2ms; the taunt against the king of Babylon, whose fall is described earlier in 14:12 with *nāp̄al*)
- **Eze 28:17** — "I will **cast thee** to the ground, I will lay thee before kings" (Hiphil perfect 1s + 2ms suffix; spoken to the prince of Tyre)
- **Dan 8:11** — "the place of his sanctuary **was cast down**" (Hophal perfect 3ms: *hušlaḵ*)
- **Dan 8:12** — "it **cast down** the truth to the ground" (Hiphil imperfect 3fs)
- **Lam 2:1** — "the Lord... **cast down** from heaven unto the earth the beauty of Israel" (Hiphil perfect 3ms)

Note the directional vector: *from* a height (heaven, grave, throne, sanctuary, the beauty of Israel) *to* the ground/earth. The Hophal passive is concentrated in this category — the exalted object does not cast itself; it is cast by an unnamed (or suppressed) agent.

The dominant bridge is *rhíptō*. The LXX translators consistently pair *shâlak* with *rhíptō* when the action involves forceful hurling. Notably, the NT's apocalyptic casting language — especially **Rev 12:9-10** ("the dragon was **cast out**" — *eblēthē*, aorist passive of *bállō*) — continues the Hophal pattern: the exalted rebellious being is cast down by an unnamed or divine agent. Rev 12 uses *bállō* rather than *rhíptō*, but the passive voice preserves the Hophal's grammatical signature.
