# WG: Dragon / Serpent / Adversary

## Overview
The dragon imagery in Revelation draws on a cluster of terms — Greek and Hebrew — that together map the adversary figure across both Testaments. The central text (Rev 12:9) explicitly equates four of these terms in a single verse: δράκων (dragon), ὄφις (serpent), διάβολος (devil), and Σατανᾶς (Satan). The Hebrew background is תַּנִּין (tannîyn), the great sea serpent of the OT.

## Relationships

### Explicit Identifications in Scripture
Rev 12:9 and 20:2 provide the defining equation:
> "the great **dragon** (G1404)... that old **serpent** (G3789), called the **Devil** (G1228), and **Satan**"

This is the only NT passage where four adversary terms appear together, making it the interpretive key for the whole cluster.

H8577 tannîyn → δράκων is the dominant LXX rendering, establishing the continuity from OT serpent/monster imagery into Revelation's dragon symbolism.

## Roles in the Concept

- **δράκων (G1404)** — the master figure; the dragon who acts, persecutes, delegates, deceives; exclusively in Revelation
- **ὄφις (G3789)** — the historical serpent from Genesis onward; connects the Revelation dragon back to Eden
- **διάβολος (G1228)** — the functional title of the adversary (accuser/slanderer); used across the whole NT
- **θηρίον (G2342)** — the agent empowered by the dragon; the beast who acts in the earthly sphere (see [WG-beast.md](WG-beast.md))
- **תַּנִּין (H8577)** — the OT lexical root; the great serpent figure of creation narratives and prophetic oracles
