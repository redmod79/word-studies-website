## Semantic Categories

### 1. Exceptional Size or Impressiveness — Applied to Beasts and Physical Objects
The word describes prophetic beasts of extraordinary magnitude and fearsome physical attributes (teeth, appearance). The reduplication conveys that these are not merely large but surpassingly, overwhelmingly great.

- **Dan 7:3** — "And four great beasts came up from the sea, diverse one from another."
- **Dan 7:7** — "a fourth beast, dreadful and terrible, and strong exceedingly; and it had great iron teeth"
- **Dan 7:17** — "These great beasts, which are four, are four kings, which shall arise out of the earth."

### 2. Superlative Speech — Arrogant or Domineering Words
The horn's speech is described as rabrab — not merely "great" words but overwhelmingly presumptuous, domineering utterances. This category carries the "domineering (in character)" sense from the lexicon.

- **Dan 7:8** — "in this horn were eyes like the eyes of man, and a mouth speaking great things"
- **Dan 7:11** — "I beheld then because of the voice of the great words which the horn spake"
- **Dan 7:20** — "that horn that had eyes, and a mouth that spake very great things, whose look was more stout than his fellows"

### 3. Exceptional Quality — Applied to Gifts and Signs
Describes gifts or divine signs that are extraordinary in magnitude or impressiveness.

- **Dan 2:48** — "the king made Daniel a great man, and gave him many great gifts"
- **Dan 4:3** — "How great are his signs! and how mighty are his wonders!"
