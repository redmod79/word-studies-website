# WG: Laying On of Hands (Sacrificial Identification and Transfer)

## Overview
The laying-on-of-hands concept in Hebrew Scripture involves a fixed vocabulary cluster appearing together in sacrificial and commissioning contexts. The core gesture (samak) connects with the body parts involved (yad, rosh), the subsequent ritual actions (shachat), and the offering types it applies to (chattaah, olah). A near-synonym (tamak) covers the sustaining sense that overlaps with samak's secondary meaning.

## Roles in the Concept

- **Action:** samak (סָמַךְ) — the deliberate pressing/laying of hands
- **Instrument:** yad (יָד) — the hand(s) performing the act
- **Target:** rosh (רֹאשׁ) — the head of the animal/person receiving the gesture
- **Sequel:** shachat (שָׁחַט) — the slaughter that immediately follows hand-laying
- **Context (sin):** chattaah (חַטָּאָה) — the sin offering requiring hand-laying
- **Context (whole):** olah (עֹלָה) — the burnt offering requiring hand-laying
- **Near-synonym:** tamak (תָּמַךְ) — grasping/sustaining; overlaps with samak's secondary "uphold" sense but lacks the ritual hand-laying usage
