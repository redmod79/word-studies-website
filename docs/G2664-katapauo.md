## Lexicon Definition

Strong's: "from κατά and παύω; to settle down, i.e. (literally) to colonize, or (figuratively) to (cause to) desist." The compound combines the perfective/intensive force of κατά with παύω ("to stop, cause to cease"). Two distinct senses emerge:
- **Transitive / causative** — "to cause to cease, to restrain, to bring (someone) to rest" (Acts 14:18; Heb 4:8).
- **Intransitive** — "to rest, to cease (from)" (Heb 4:4, 4:10).

The cognate noun κατάπαυσις (G2663) is the nominal form of this verb's intransitive sense ("a resting, a rest, place of rest").

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Causative — to restrain, hold back, prevent action
The transitive sense: causing others to cease an intended act.
- **Acts 14:18** — "with these sayings scarce restrained they the people, that they had not done sacrifice unto them"

### 2. Causative — to give rest, bring (someone) into rest
The transitive sense applied to bringing a group into a state of rest (the question being whether Joshua accomplished this).
- **Hebrews 4:8** — "For if Jesus [Joshua] had given them rest, then would he not afterward have spoken of another day"

### 3. Intransitive — to rest, to cease from work
The intransitive sense: the subject itself ceases activity. Drawn from the Genesis 2:2 quotation.
- **Hebrews 4:4** — "And God did rest the seventh day from all his works"
- **Hebrews 4:10** — "he that is entered into his rest, he also hath ceased from his own works, as God did from his"
