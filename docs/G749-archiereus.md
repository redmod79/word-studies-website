## Semantic Categories

### 1. The Ruling Priestly Aristocracy ("chief priests")
The plural ἀρχιερεῖς refers to the priestly ruling class — members of the Sanhedrin from high-priestly families who collectively held political and religious authority.
- **Mat 2:4** — "he had gathered all the chief priests and scribes...he demanded of them where Christ should be born"
- **Mat 26:59** — "the chief priests, and elders, and all the council, sought false witness against Jesus"
- **Mar 15:11** — "the chief priests moved the people, that he should rather release Barabbas"
- **Jhn 11:47** — "the chief priests and the Pharisees a council, and said, What do we?"

### 2. The Individual High Priest in the Passion Narratives
The singular identifies the specific officeholder (Caiaphas, Annas) presiding over Jesus' trial.
- **Mat 26:57** — "they that had laid hold on Jesus led him away to Caiaphas the high priest"
- **Mat 26:63** — "the high priest answered and said unto him, I adjure thee by the living God"
- **Mar 14:63** — "the high priest rent his clothes, and saith, What need we any further witnesses?"
- **Jhn 18:19** — "The high priest then asked Jesus of his disciples, and of his doctrine"

### 3. The High Priest as Persecutor of the Early Church
In Acts, the high priest authorizes persecution of the apostles and of Paul before his conversion.
- **Act 5:17** — "the high priest rose up, and all they that were with him...were filled with indignation"
- **Act 5:27** — "the high priest asked them, saying, Did not we straitly command you?"
- **Act 9:1** — "Saul...went unto the high priest, and desired of him letters to Damascus"
- **Act 23:2** — "the high priest Ananias commanded them that stood by him to smite him on the mouth"

### 4. Christ as the True/Eternal High Priest (Hebrews)
Hebrews applies archiereus to Jesus as the definitive high priest — superior to the Levitical priesthood, after the order of Melchizedek.
- **Heb 2:17** — "in all things it behoved him to be made like unto his brethren, that he might be a merciful and faithful high priest"
- **Heb 4:14** — "we have a great high priest, that is passed into the heavens, Jesus the Son of God"
- **Heb 7:26** — "such an high priest became us, who is holy, harmless, undefiled, separate from sinners"
- **Heb 9:11** — "Christ being come an high priest of good things to come, by a greater and more perfect tabernacle"

### 5. The OT High Priest and the Day of Atonement Ritual
Hebrews refers to the earthly high priest's annual entry into the Most Holy Place as the type that Christ fulfills.
- **Heb 5:1** — "every high priest taken from among men is ordained for men in things pertaining to God, that he may offer both gifts and sacrifices for sins"
- **Heb 9:7** — "into the second went the high priest alone once every year, not without blood"
- **Heb 9:25** — "the high priest entereth into the holy place every year with blood of others"
- **Heb 13:11** — "the bodies of those beasts, whose blood is brought into the sanctuary by the high priest for sin, are burned without the camp"

### 6. The High Priest in OT Historical References
Occasional references to historical high priests outside the passion and Hebrews contexts.
- **Mar 2:26** — "he went into the house of God in the days of Abiathar the high priest"
- **Luk 3:2** — "Annas and Caiaphas being the high priests"
- **Jhn 11:49** — "Caiaphas, being the high priest that same year"
- **Jhn 11:51** — "being high priest that year, he prophesied that Jesus should die for that nation"
