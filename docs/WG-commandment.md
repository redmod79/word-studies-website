# WG: Commandment

## Overview
The commandment concept in the NT involves a cluster of Greek words for authoritative directives, along with the key verbs that describe the human response to them (keeping, doing, loving). The core word ἐντολή (entolē) dominates usage, while cognates and near-synonyms distinguish different nuances of command.

## Roles in the Concept

- **The System:** νόμος (nomos) — the law as a whole
- **The Specific Directive:** ἐντολή (entolē) — a particular commandment within the law, or Christ's personal command
- **The Act of Commanding:** ἐντέλλομαι (entellomai) — verb form; God/Christ enjoins
- **The Human Counterfeit:** ἔνταλμα (entalma) — human traditions masquerading as commandments
- **The Authoritative Decree:** ἐπιταγή (epitagē) — emphasizes the weight of authority behind the command
- **The Response of Obedience:** τηρέω (tēreō) — to keep, guard, observe
- **The Motive and Content:** ἀγαπάω (agapaō) — love both motivates and summarizes the commandment(s)

## Key Passages Where Multiple Group Words Appear Together

- **Matt 22:36-40** — entolē + nomos: "Which is the great commandment in the law?"
- **John 14:15** — entolē + tēreō + agapaō: "If ye love me, keep my commandments"
- **John 15:10** — entolē + tēreō + agapaō: "If ye keep my commandments, ye shall abide in my love"
- **Rom 7:8-12** — entolē + nomos: Sin exploits the commandment within the law
- **1 John 5:2-3** — entolē + tēreō + agapaō: "This is the love of God, that we keep his commandments"
- **Matt 15:3-9** — entolē + entalma: God's commandment vs. human precepts
