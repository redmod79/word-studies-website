## Lexicon Definition

The right side or (feminine) the right hand, as that which usually takes hold; used of the favored or stronger side.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Divine Session — Christ at God's Right Hand
The dominant usage (approx. 20 verses). Drawn from Psalm 110:1 quotations and allusions.
- **Mat 22:44** — "The LORD said unto my Lord, Sit thou on my right hand"
- **Acts 2:33** — "being by the right hand of God exalted"
- **Heb 1:3** — "sat down on the right hand of the Majesty on high"
- **1 Pet 3:22** — "who is gone into heaven, and is on the right hand of God"

### 2. Authority and Power
The right hand as instrument of sovereign action.
- **Acts 5:31** — "Him hath God exalted with his right hand to be a Prince and a Saviour"
- **Rev 1:16** — "he had in his right hand seven stars"
- **Rev 5:1** — "in the right hand of him that sat on the throne a book written within"
- **Rev 2:1** — "he that holdeth the seven stars in his right hand"

### 3. Judgment Positioning — Favored vs. Unfavored
The right side as the position of favor in judgment scenes.
- **Mat 25:33** — "he shall set the sheep on his right hand, but the goats on the left"
- **Mat 25:34** — "Then shall the King say unto them on his right hand, Come, ye blessed"
- **Mat 27:38** — "two thieves crucified with him, one on the right hand, and another on the left"

### 4. Physical Body Part
Literal reference to the right eye, hand, ear, cheek, or foot.
- **Mat 5:29** — "if thy right eye offend thee, pluck it out"
- **Mat 5:30** — "if thy right hand offend thee, cut it off"
- **Luk 22:50** — "smote the servant of the high priest, and cut off his right ear"
- **Rev 13:16** — "to receive a mark in their right hand"

### 5. Fellowship / Partnership
The right hand extended as a gesture of acceptance or alliance.
- **Gal 2:9** — "they gave to me and Barnabas the right hands of fellowship"
- **Acts 3:7** — "he took him by the right hand, and lifted him up"

### 6. Spatial Orientation
Simple directional usage without authority connotation.
- **Mar 16:5** — "they saw a young man sitting on the right side"
- **Luk 1:11** — "an angel of the Lord standing on the right side of the altar"
- **Jhn 21:6** — "Cast the net on the right side of the ship"
- **2 Cor 6:7** — "the armour of righteousness on the right hand and on the left"
