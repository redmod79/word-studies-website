## Semantic Categories

### 1. Being Consumed / Exhausted (Resources, People)
The most frequent sense — supplies running out, people perishing, strength spent.
- **Gen 47:15** — "And when money *failed* in the land of Egypt"
- **Num 14:35** — "in this wilderness they shall be *consumed*, and there they shall die"
- **Deut 2:14–16** — "until all the generation of the men of war were *consumed* and dead"
- **Jer 44:27** — "all the men of Judah... shall be *consumed* by the sword and by the famine"
- **Lam 3:22** — "It is of the LORD's mercies that we are not *consumed*"
- **Psalm 104:35** — "Let the sinners be *consumed* out of the earth"

### 2. Completion / Finishing of an Action or Process
Marks the endpoint of a task, event, or period — often in temporal clauses with עַד (ʻad "until").
- **Deut 31:24** — "when Moses had made an *end* of writing the words of this law"
- **Jos 4:10** — "until every thing was *finished* that the LORD commanded Joshua"
- **Jos 10:20** — "when Joshua and the children of Israel had made an *end* of slaying them"
- **1 Ki 6:22** — "until he had *finished* all the house"
- **1 Ki 7:22** — "so was the work of the pillars *finished*"
- **Job 31:40** — "The words of Job are *ended*"

### 3. Crossing / Passing Over Completely
A distinctive Joshua cluster — all Israel "clean passed over" (tamam + ʻabar).
- **Jos 3:16** — "the people passed over right against Jericho" (waters cut off until people *completely* crossed)
- **Jos 3:17** — "until all the people were *clean* passed over Jordan"
- **Jos 4:1** — "when all the people were *clean passed over* Jordan"
- **Jos 4:11** — "when all the people were *clean passed over*"

### 4. Coming to Fullness / Completion of a Period
Temporal and prophetic milestones.
- **Gen 47:18** — "When that year was *ended*"
- **Jer 1:3** — "unto the *end* of the eleventh year of Zedekiah"
- **Dan 8:23** — "when the transgressors are *come to the full*"
- **Dan 9:24** — "to *finish* the transgression, and to make an *end* of sins"

### 5. Moral Integrity / Uprightness
The Hithpael reflexive: "show oneself complete/blameless."
- **2 Sam 22:26** — "with the upright man thou wilt *shew thyself upright*"
- **Psalm 18:25** — "with an upright man thou wilt *shew thyself upright*"

### 6. Judgment Consumption by God

- **Eze 22:15** — "I will scatter thee... and will *consume* thy filthiness"
- **Jer 14:15** — "by sword and famine shall those prophets be *consumed*"
- **Jer 24:10** — "I will send the sword... till they be *consumed* from off the land"
- **Psalm 73:19** — "they are utterly *consumed* with terrors"

- **H5486 סוּף (suph)** — to come to an end, cease. Emphasizes terminal cessation.
- **H6663 צָדַק (tsadaq)** — be righteous, be just. Overlaps in the moral-integrity semantic space (tamam Hithpael "show oneself upright").

### Word Family / Cognates
- **H8535 תָּם (tam)** — complete, whole, perfect, plain (adjective). Describes a person or thing as whole/blameless. Gen 25:27 Jacob is *ish tam* "plain man."
