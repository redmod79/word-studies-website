## Semantic Categories

### 1. Eternal Duration — "for ever (and ever)"
Used adverbially or adjectivally of God, His name, His kingdom, and His living nature as endless.

- **Dan 2:20** — "Blessed be the name of God for ever and ever: for wisdom and might are his"
- **Dan 7:18** — "the saints of the most High shall take the kingdom, and possess the kingdom for ever, even for ever and ever"
- **Dan 2:44** — "a kingdom, which shall never be destroyed"
- **Dan 6:26** — "he is the living God, and stedfast for ever, and his kingdom...shall not be destroyed"

### 2. Everlasting Kingdom / Dominion
Collocates with מַלְכוּ (kingdom, 7x) and שׁלְטָן (dominion, 5x) to assert the indestructibility of God's reign.

- **Dan 4:3** — "his kingdom is an everlasting kingdom, and his dominion is from generation to generation"
- **Dan 4:34** — "his dominion is an everlasting dominion, and his kingdom is from generation to generation"
- **Dan 7:14** — "his dominion is an everlasting dominion, which shall not pass away, and his kingdom that which shall not be destroyed"
- **Dan 7:27** — "his kingdom is an everlasting kingdom, and all dominions shall serve and obey him"

### 3. Court Formula — "O king, live for ever"
A frozen diplomatic greeting (לְעָלְמִין חֱיִי) addressed to Babylonian and Persian kings; the word anchors a formulaic loyalty expression.

- **Dan 2:4** — "Then spake the Chaldeans to the king in Syriack, O king, live for ever"
- **Dan 3:9** — "They spake and said to the king Nebuchadnezzar, O king, live for ever"
- **Dan 6:6** — "assembled together to the king, and said thus unto him, King Darius, live for ever"
- **Dan 6:21** — "Then said Daniel unto the king, O king, live for ever"

### 4. Ancient / Past Time ("old")
The backward-facing use, denoting remote antiquity (the same semantic pole as H5769 בִּימֵי עוֹלָם "days of old").

- **Ezr 4:15** — "find in the book of the records...that this city of old time hath made insurrection against kings"
- **Ezr 4:19** — "it is found that this city of old time hath made insurrection"
