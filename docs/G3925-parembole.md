## Lexicon Definition

The overwhelming LXX equivalent is H4264 machaneh. The LXX consistently renders machaneh as parembole throughout the Pentateuch and historical books (Exod 14:19–20, 29:14, 32:17; Lev 4:12; Num 5:3; Deut 23:14–15; Josh 6:11; Judg 7:8).

## Key LXX Background

The phrase ἔξω τῆς παρεμβολῆς ("outside the camp") in Hebrews 13:11, 13 directly echoes the Pentateuchal formula מִחוּץ לַמַּחֲנֶה (Lev 4:12, 16:27, Num 19:3). In the OT, items burned "outside the camp" included sin-offering remains (Lev 4:12, 21), the Day of Atonement offerings (Lev 16:27), the red heifer (Num 19:3), and persons with skin diseases or discharges (Lev 13:46, Num 5:3). The Hebrews author applies this typology to Jesus' suffering "outside the gate" (Heb 13:12).
