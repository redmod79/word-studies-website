# WG: Tabernacle / Tent Dwelling

## Overview
The σκηνή word family covers the concept of tent-dwelling and the portable sanctuary. In the NT, this cluster carries the theology of God's temporary, mobile dwelling — from the wilderness tabernacle through Christ's incarnation ("tabernacled among us," John 1:14) to the eschatological promise ("the tabernacle of God is with men," Rev 21:3). Distinguished from the ναός (temple/permanent sanctuary) word group by its emphasis on portability, temporariness, and incarnational presence.

## Relationships

### Semantic Distinction: skēnē vs. naos
- **σκηνή (skēnē)** — tent, portable tabernacle; emphasizes temporariness and divine condescension (God dwelling *with* people in their wanderings)
- **ναός (naos)** — inner temple sanctuary; emphasizes permanence and holy separation (the dwelling *of* deity)
- In Rev 15:5 both words appear together: "the temple (naos) of the tabernacle (skēnē) of the testimony" — the permanent and portable concepts merged in the heavenly reality

### Word Family Distinctions
- **σκηνή** — the tent structure; used for the Mosaic tabernacle, literal tents, heavenly tabernacle
- **σκηνόω** — to pitch a tent, dwell in a tent; John 1:14 "the Word tabernacled among us"
- **σκήνωμα** — the dwelling itself or the state of dwelling; used for Peter's body (2 Pet 1:13-14) and God's dwelling (Acts 7:46)
- **σκῆνος** — tent as metaphor for the body; exclusively figurative in NT (2 Cor 5:1, 4)
- **σκιά** — shadow; appears alongside σκηνή in Hebrews (8:5; 10:1) to describe the earthly tabernacle as a shadow-copy of the heavenly

## Roles in the Concept

- **Structure (portable):** σκηνή (skēnē) — the physical/spiritual tent-sanctuary
- **Action (dwelling):** σκηνόω (skēnoō) — the act of tabernacling/encamping
- **State/Place (result):** σκήνωμα (skēnōma) — the dwelling-place or state of encampment
- **Metaphor (body):** σκῆνος (skēnos) — the body as a temporary tent
- **Relation (copy-to-original):** σκιά (skia) — the earthly tabernacle as shadow of the heavenly
- **Overlap (permanent structure):** ναός (naos) — the temple as permanent counterpart

## Key Passages Where Multiple Group Words Appear Together

- **Rev 21:3** — "the tabernacle (σκηνή) of God is with men, and he will dwell (σκηνόω) with them" — 2 group words; the eschatological climax of the tabernacle theme
- **Rev 13:6** — "to blaspheme his name, and his tabernacle (σκηνή), and them that dwell (σκηνόω) in heaven" — 2 group words
- **Rev 15:5** — "the temple (ναός) of the tabernacle (σκηνή) of the testimony in heaven" — 2 group words; unique fusion of temple + tabernacle
- **Heb 8:2, 5** — "the true tabernacle (σκηνή)...shadow (σκιά) of heavenly things" — 2 group words; type-antitype framework
- **2 Cor 5:1-4** — "our earthly house of this tabernacle (σκῆνος)...clothed upon...tabernacle (σκῆνος)" — body-as-tent metaphor
- **John 1:14** — "the Word was made flesh, and dwelt (σκηνόω) among us" — incarnation as tabernacling
