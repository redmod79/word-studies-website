## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same across testaments:**
- God as the primary source of blessing, humans as recipients and mediators
- The bidirectional nature: humans bless God (praise) and God blesses humans (bestows favor)
- The *baruk* / εὐλογητός formula for praising God persists (Luke 1:68, Eph 1:3, 1 Pet 1:3)
- The Abrahamic promise framework is explicitly quoted and applied (Acts 3:25, Gal 3:8)
- The blessing-curse antithesis continues (Rom 12:14, James 3:9-10)

**What shifts:**
- The NT universalizes the Abrahamic blessing through Christ — "all nations" fulfilled through the gospel rather than through physical descent
- The imperative to bless enemies (Matt 5:44, Rom 12:14, 1 Cor 4:12) extends the blessing ethic beyond covenant community
- εὐλογητός (G2128) becomes exclusively reserved for God in the NT (never applied to humans), while εὐλογημένος (perfect participle) is used for blessed humans — a distinction not present in Hebrew
- The meal-blessing usage (Mark 6:41, 14:22 — eucharistic blessing) develops a liturgical specificity beyond the OT priestly blessing
