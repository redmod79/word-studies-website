## Lexicon Definition

From H7429 (râmas). "Abasement (the act or the thing)" — tread (down), treading, (to be) trodden (down) under foot (Strong's). The noun denotes either the activity of trampling or, more frequently, the *state/result* of being trampled: the object, land, or people given over to trampling. The BHSA/DSS gloss is "trampled land," capturing the typical usage as a designation of what has become subjugated ground.

The *miqtal* pattern (prefixed *m-* + three-radical root) on an action verb like *râmas* characteristically yields (a) an abstract action-noun, (b) a place-noun (where the action happens), or (c) a passive result-noun (what has been acted upon). *Mirmâs* covers all three senses depending on context, with the passive-result sense dominant in prophetic usage.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Judgment on Covenant Land / Vineyard Given Over to Trampling
- **Isa 5:5** — "I will take away the hedge thereof, and it shall be eaten up; [and] break down the wall thereof, and it shall be trodden down (*mirmâs*)" (the Song of the Vineyard — covenant Israel as vineyard abandoned to trampling)
- **Isa 7:25** — "it shall be for the sending forth of oxen, and for the treading (*mirmas*) of lesser cattle" (hills of Judah reduced to pasture during Assyrian crisis)

The first two Isaiah uses set the fundamental frame: covenant land, once under God's cultivated protection, is handed over as trampled ground.

### 2. Imperial Conquest / Spoil-and-Trampling
- **Isa 10:6** — "I will send him [Assyria] against an hypocritical nation… to take the spoil, and to take the prey, and to tread them down (*lě-mirmas*) like the mire of the streets" (Assyria as YHWH's rod; the nation reduced to *mirmas* as "mire in the streets")
- **Mic 7:10** — "now shall she be trodden down (*lě-mirmas*) as the mire of the streets" (the tables turned — Israel's enemy in turn becomes *mirmas*)

*Mirmâs* + "mire of the streets" (*ṭîṭ ḥûṣôt*) is a fixed prophetic simile for total humiliation. It recurs in Isa 10:6 and Mic 7:10 with near-identical wording.

### 3. Covenant-Curse / Overflowing Scourge
- **Isa 28:18** — "your covenant with death shall be disannulled… when the overflowing scourge shall pass through, then ye shall be trodden down (*lě-mirmâs*) by it" (the false covenant unable to shield; the agents of trampling are the very powers the leaders thought they had bought off)

### 4. Abuse by Corrupt Shepherds (Inner-Covenant Oppression)
- **Eze 34:19** — "[as for] my flock, they eat that which ye have trodden with your feet (*mirmas raglêkem*); and they drink that which ye have fouled" (the bad shepherds' trampled residue — internal oppression, not external invasion; shepherds abuse what belongs to the flock)

### 5. Apocalyptic Sanctuary Trampling (Dan 8)
- **Dan 8:13** — "How long [shall be] the vision [concerning] the daily [sacrifice], and the transgression of desolation, to give both the sanctuary and the host to be trodden under foot (*mirmâs*)?" (the little-horn vision; sanctuary and people given over as *mirmâs* — the climax of prophetic *mirmâs* language, transferring the field from vineyard/nation to sanctuary/host)

In Dan 8:13 the noun stands *predicatively with a verb of giving*: "to give (*la-tēt*) the holy place and the host for/as *mirmâs*." This is the same construction as Isa 10:6 (*lě-mirmas ḥûṣôt*) and Mic 7:10 — "to be/become a trampled thing."

## LXX Mapping

Verse-level alignment data (PMI-weighted) shows no single dominant Greek equivalent — the LXX translators rendered *mirmâs* variously, often by restructuring the clause rather than using a one-to-one noun. Observed renderings in verse-level alignment (strongs_translations.db):

| LXX Strategy | Example | Notes |
|--------------|---------|-------|
| καταπατέω (G2662) as verb | Isa 10:6 LXX — *katapatein tas poleis* "to trample the cities" | Converts the noun phrase "to be mirmas" into an infinitive verb; G2662 is the dominant LXX rendering for the **verb** *râmas* (H7429, PMI 8.03) |
| συμπατέω / καταπατέω with *eis* | Isa 5:5; Isa 28:18 (*estai eis katapatēma* / periphrases) | Periphrastic — "shall be *for* trampling" |
| Clause-level restructuring | Dan 8:13 (Old Greek: *hē hamartia erēmōseōs… hē dotheisa kai to hagion kai hē dunamis*) | OG smooths away the noun; Theodotion preserves it closer to Hebrew |
| Mic 7:10 — *eis katapatēma / hōs pēlos* | "as mire" | OG reproduces the simile; noun-form of katapateo |

The Greek root **καταπατέω (G2662)** and its cognate noun **καταπάτημα (katapatēma)** carry the *mirmâs* semantic load into the NT, where G2662 appears in theologically weighted sanctuary/people contexts:

- **Matt 5:13** — salt "trodden under foot of men" (*katapateisthai*)
- **Matt 7:6** — pearls "trampled under feet"
- **Heb 10:29** — apostate who "hath trodden under foot the Son of God"
- **Luke 21:24** — "Jerusalem shall be **trodden down** (*patoumenē*, from G3961 *pateō*) of the Gentiles, until the times of the Gentiles be fulfilled"
- **Rev 11:2** — "the court… is given unto the Gentiles: and the holy city shall they **tread under foot** (*patēsousin*, G3961) forty [and] two months"

Luke 21:24 and Rev 11:2 use G3961 *pateō* rather than G2662 — a related verb in the same domain; the sanctuary/holy-city-trampling image carries through regardless of which compound is used. Both compounds share the prefix-less root *pat-* with G2662.
