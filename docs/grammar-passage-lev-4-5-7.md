## Key Grammatical Features

### 1. Weqatal Chain in 3rd-Person Priestly Prescription (WQtX Clause Type)

- **Forms:** וְלָקַח (v.5), וְהֵבִיא (v.5), וְטָבַל (v.6), וְהִזָּה (v.6), וְנָתַן (v.7) — all waw + Qal/Hiphil Perfect 3ms
- **Clause analysis:** ETCBC classifies these as **WQtX** (waw-qatal-X) — the standard weqatal clause type in legal prescription
- **Significance:** Leviticus 4 uses 3rd-person weqatal chains ("and the priest shall...") rather than the 2nd-person form characteristic of Deuteronomic law ("and thou shalt..."). The 3ms subject הַכֹּהֵן recurs explicitly in each clause, reinforcing that these are priestly operating instructions, not covenant obligations addressed to Israel. Each weqatal advances the ritual sequence one step: the conjunction וְ carries imperatival force forward from the protasis in Lev 4:3 (the conditional כִּי-clause establishing the sin offering requirement).
- **Grammar reference:** [weqatal-legal-imperatival](../hebrew/weqatal-legal-imperatival.md) — though that entry focuses on the 2ms Deuteronomic type, the same underlying form (waw + perfect carrying forward obligatory force) operates here in 3ms priestly register.

### 2. הִזָּה (hizzah) — Hiphil Perfect 3ms of נזה (H5137): Causative Sprinkling

- **Form:** Hiphil stem — the causative of a root whose Qal means "to spatter/spurt forth." The Hiphil makes the priest the active causer: he *causes the blood to sprinkle*.
- **Significance:** Of 24 total occurrences of נזה, 20 are Hiphil — this is almost exclusively a priestly ritual word. The Hiphil form appears in every sanctuary blood-application context (Lev 4:6,17; 5:9; 8:11,30; 14:7,16,27,51; 16:14,15,19; Num 8:7; 19:4,18,19). The 4 Qal occurrences describe involuntary spattering (Lev 6:20; 2 Ki 9:33; Isa 63:3) — blood spraying from violence rather than applied by priestly hand. This stem distinction marks the boundary between controlled ritual action and uncontrolled bloodshed.
- **Grammar reference:** [stem-hiphil](../hebrew/stem-hiphil.md)
- **Word study:** [H5137-nazah](../../word-studies/H5137-nazah.md)

### 3. The Verb Tense Shift in v.7b: Weqatal to Yiqtol (WxY0 Clause)

- **Forms:** v.7a uses weqatal: וְנָתַן (Qal.Perf.3ms, "and he shall put"). v.7b shifts to yiqtol with fronted object: וְאֵת כָּל־דַּם הַפָּר **יִשְׁפֹּךְ** (Qal.Impf.3ms, "and all the blood of the bull he shall pour").
- **Clause analysis:** ETCBC classifies v.7b as **WxY0** — a waw-X-yiqtol clause, where the object (אֵת כָּל־דַּם הַפָּר) is fronted before the verb. This is not the same pattern as the preceding WQtX weqatal clauses.
- **Significance:** The fronted object (כָּל "all") + imperfect (yiqtol) marks a shift in emphasis. The preceding weqatal chain describes partial applications — some blood on the finger, some sprinkled, some on the horns. The fronted כָּל signals a contrast: *all* the remaining blood now goes to one location. The imperfect verb form in this context carries the same prescriptive force as the weqatals but the syntax foregrounds the totality of the blood being disposed. The shift from "some of the blood" (מִן הַדָּם) to "all the blood" (כָּל דַּם) is grammatically marked by the clause-type change.

### 4. The Four-Verb Blood-Path Sequence: טבל → נזה → נתן → שׁפך

The passage prescribes four distinct blood-handling verbs in strict sequence. Each verb denotes a physically different action and targets a different location in the sanctuary:

| Step | Hebrew | Lemma (Strong's) | Stem | Action | Target Location |
|------|--------|-------------------|------|--------|-----------------|
| 1 | וְטָבַל | טבל (H2881) | Qal | dip finger in blood | the blood basin (source) |
| 2 | וְהִזָּה | נזה (H5137) | Hiphil | sprinkle seven times | before YHWH, before the veil of the sanctuary |
| 3 | וְנָתַן | נתן (H5414) | Qal | put/apply blood | upon the horns of the incense altar |
| 4 | יִשְׁפֹּךְ | שׁפך (H8210) | Qal | pour out all remaining | at the base of the burnt-offering altar |

**Verb semantics and physical distinction:**

- **טבל** (tabal, H2881, Qal "dip") — 16 OT occurrences, all meaning physical immersion of an object into a liquid. The priest submerges his finger into the collected blood. This is the point of contact — the priest's body touches the sin-bearing blood. The Qal stem indicates a simple, direct physical act.

- **נזה** (nazah, H5137, Hiphil "sprinkle") — Causative: the priest causes the blood to leave his finger in controlled spatters. The modifier שֶׁבַע פְּעָמִים ("seven times") and the locative phrases לִפְנֵי יְהוָה ("before YHWH") and אֶת־פְּנֵי פָּרֹכֶת הַקֹּדֶשׁ ("before the veil of the sanctuary") place this action inside the Holy Place, directed toward the veil separating it from the Most Holy Place. The blood does not pass through the veil — it is sprinkled *before* it (לִפְנֵי), marking the boundary of God's localized presence.

- **נתן** (natan, H5414, Qal "put/give") — The broadest Hebrew transfer verb, here narrowed to physical placement: blood applied to the horns (קַרְנֹות) of the incense altar. The construct chain קַרְנֹות מִזְבַּח קְטֹרֶת הַסַּמִּים ("horns of the altar of the incense of spices") is a four-level chain — horns-of / altar-of / incense-of / the-spices — locating this action precisely at the altar that stands inside the Holy Place before the veil (Exo 30:6).

- **שׁפך** (shaphak, H8210, Qal "pour out") — The verb of bulk disposal: all remaining blood is poured at the *base* (יְסוֹד) of the burnt-offering altar, which stands in the courtyard at the *entrance* (פֶּתַח) of the tent of appointment. This is the only action in the sequence that takes place outside the Holy Place.

### 5. Spatial Grammar: The Blood Path as Inward-then-Outward Movement

The prepositions and locative phrases map the blood's physical trajectory through the sanctuary:

| Verb | Preposition | Spatial Target | Location in Sanctuary |
|------|-------------|----------------|-----------------------|
| לָקַח | מִן (from) | דַּם הַפָּר (blood of the bull) | Courtyard — at the place of slaughter |
| הֵבִיא | אֶל (to/into) | אֹהֶל מֹועֵד (tent of appointment) | Crossing from courtyard into the Holy Place |
| טָבַל | בְּ (in) | הַדָּם (the blood) | Inside — priest contacts the blood |
| הִזָּה | לִפְנֵי (before) | יְהוָה / פָּרֹכֶת הַקֹּדֶשׁ (YHWH / veil of the sanctuary) | Holy Place — directed toward the veil |
| נָתַן | עַל (upon) | קַרְנֹות מִזְבַּח (horns of the altar) | Holy Place — incense altar |
| יִשְׁפֹּךְ | אֶל (to) | יְסוֹד מִזְבַּח הָעֹלָה (base of burnt-offering altar) | Courtyard — back outside at the entrance |

The movement is: courtyard (take/bring) → into the Holy Place (dip/sprinkle/apply) → back to the courtyard (pour). The verbs הֵבִיא (Hiphil of בוא, "cause to come in") explicitly marks the inward crossing. The final verb שׁפך at the courtyard altar marks the return outward. The blood path thus penetrates the sanctuary to its deepest accessible point (the veil boundary) and then withdraws.

## Construct Chains

### Verse 5
1. **דַּם הַפָּר** — "blood of the young bull" (2-level: blood-of → the-bull)
2. **אֹהֶל מֹועֵד** — "tent of appointment" (2-level: tent-of → appointment)

### Verse 6
1. **פְנֵי יְהוָה** — "face of YHWH" = "before YHWH" (idiomatic spatial; 2-level)
2. **פְּנֵי פָּרֹכֶת הַקֹּדֶשׁ** — "face of the curtain of holiness" = "before the veil of the sanctuary" (3-level chain: face-of → curtain-of → the-holiness). The double use of פְנֵי (construct of פָּנֶה "face") — once with YHWH, once with the veil — equates the two locatively: the space before the veil is the space before YHWH.

### Verse 7
1. **קַרְנֹות מִזְבַּח קְטֹרֶת הַסַּמִּים** — "horns of the altar of the incense of the spices" (4-level chain: horns-of → altar-of → incense-of → the-spices). The longest construct chain in these verses, precisely identifying the incense altar by its function and material.
2. **כָּל דַּם הַפָּר** — "all blood of the young bull" (3-level: all-of → blood-of → the-bull). The quantifier כָּל in construct governs the entire following phrase.
3. **יְסוֹד מִזְבַּח הָעֹלָה** — "base of the altar of the burnt offering" (3-level: base-of → altar-of → the-burnt-offering).
4. **פֶּתַח אֹהֶל מֹועֵד** — "opening of the tent of appointment" (3-level: opening-of → tent-of → appointment).
