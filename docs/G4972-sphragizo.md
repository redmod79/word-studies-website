## Lexicon Definition

To stamp with a signet or private mark for security or preservation; to close up, make secure; to set a seal upon, mark with a seal as a means of identification, confirmation, or authentication.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Physical Sealing — Security and Closure
- **Matthew 27:66** — "So they went, and made the sepulchre sure, sealing the stone, and setting a watch"
- **Revelation 20:3** — "cast him into the bottomless pit, and shut him up, and set a seal upon him"

### 2. Divine Authentication — Confirming Truth
- **John 3:33** — "He that hath received his testimony hath set to his seal that God is true"
- **John 6:27** — "for him hath God the Father sealed" (authenticating Christ's mission)

### 3. Spiritual Sealing — Ownership and Protection by the Spirit
- **2 Corinthians 1:22** — "Who hath also sealed us, and given the earnest of the Spirit in our hearts"
- **Ephesians 1:13** — "after that ye believed, ye were sealed with that holy Spirit of promise"
- **Ephesians 4:30** — "grieve not the holy Spirit of God, whereby ye are sealed unto the day of redemption"

### 4. Eschatological Sealing — Marking God's People
- **Revelation 7:3** — "till we have sealed the servants of our God in their foreheads"
- **Revelation 7:4** — "I heard the number of them which were sealed: an hundred and forty and four thousand"

### 5. Concealing / Closing Up Revelation
- **Revelation 10:4** — "Seal up those things which the seven thunders uttered, and write them not"
- **Revelation 22:10** — "Seal not the sayings of the prophecy of this book" (contrast — do NOT seal)
- OT background: **Daniel 9:24** — "to seal up the vision and prophecy" (LXX uses σφραγίζω)

### 6. Securing a Delivery
- **Romans 15:28** — "When therefore I have performed this, and have sealed to them this fruit"
- **2 Corinthians 11:10** — "no man shall stop me of this boasting" (metaphorical: seal off/block)
