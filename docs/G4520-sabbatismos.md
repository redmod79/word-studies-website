## Semantic Categories

### 1. Sabbath-Rest as a Distinct State of Repose

The word is a verbal noun from σαββατίζω (to observe/keep the Sabbath), not merely a synonym for cessation (κατάπαυσις). The -ισμός suffix marks it as the act or state of sabbath-keeping.

- **Heb 4:9** — "There remaineth therefore a rest [σαββατισμός] to the people of God." — The single NT usage, deliberately set apart from the nine uses of κατάπαυσις (G2663) in the same chapter (vv. 1, 3, 5, 10-11).

### 2. Contextual Setting: The Heb 4 Rest Argument

σαββατισμός appears in a chain of reasoning (Heb 4:1-11) that moves through multiple "rests":
- Creation rest — God rested on the seventh day (v. 4; Gen 2:2)
- Promised Land rest — offered to the wilderness generation, forfeited through unbelief (vv. 6-8)
- Davidic day rest — Ps 95:11 still open (v. 7)
- Present entering rest — for those who believe (v. 3)
- The remaining rest — v. 9 uses σαββατισμός here specifically

The shift to σαββατισμός at v. 9 (rather than continuing with κατάπαυσις) signals the author's intent to invoke the Sabbath archetype — rest patterned on God's seventh-day rest (v. 4, 10).
