## Semantic Categories

### 1. Mixing Flour with Oil (Grain-Offering Formula)
The dominant usage. The Qal passive participle *belulah/beluloth* appears as a technical term in the *minchah* (grain-offering) prescriptions.
- **Exo 29:2** — "cakes unleavened tempered with oil, and wafers unleavened anointed with oil"
- **Lev 2:4–5** — "cakes of fine flour... mingled with oil"
- **Num 7:13** — "both of them full of fine flour mingled with oil for a meat offering"
- **Num 28:5** — "a tenth part of an ephah of flour for a meat offering, mingled with the fourth part of an hin of beaten oil"

### 2. Confounding Language / Divine Judgment
The etymological basis for the name Babel (H894). God disrupts human unity by mixing/confounding their speech.
- **Gen 11:7** — "let us go down, and there confound their language, that they may not understand one another's speech"
- **Gen 11:9** — "Therefore is the name of it called Babel; because the LORD did there confound the language of all the earth"

### 3. Illicit Mixing Among Peoples
Reflexive (Hithpael): voluntary cultural/religious assimilation condemned as covenant unfaithfulness.
- **Hos 7:8** — "Ephraim, he hath mixed himself among the people; Ephraim is a cake not turned"

### 4. Withering / Fading
Extended metaphor of dissolution. The Hiphil carries a causative sense of being made to wither.
- **Isa 64:6** — "we all do fade as a leaf; and our iniquities, like the wind, have taken us away"

### 5. Anointing / Moistening
The base physical sense of overflowing with oil, used of personal anointing and animal care.
- **Ps 92:10** — "I shall be anointed with fresh oil"
- **Judg 19:21** — "gave provender unto the asses" (mixing fodder)
