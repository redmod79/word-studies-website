## Lexicon Definition

From Strong's: "from ἀ- (as a negative particle) and νόμος; lawless, i.e. (negatively) not subject to (the Jewish) law; (by implication, a Gentile), or (positively) wicked."

The word denotes one who stands outside or against νόμος (law). The adjective can be used substantively with the article (ὁ ἄνομος, "the lawless one") to designate a specific referent. Semantic range: (a) without-the-Law in the sense of Gentile / non-Jew (neutral), (b) lawless / wicked as moral designation (negative), (c) substantival "the lawless one" (definite singular referent).

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Articular Substantive — "The Lawless One" (definite singular referent)
- **2 Thess 2:8** — "And then shall that Wicked (ὁ ἄνομος) be revealed, whom the Lord shall consume with the spirit of his mouth, and shall destroy with the brightness of his coming" — the only NT occurrence of ἄνομος with the definite article functioning as a title for a specific end-time figure.

### 2. Plural — "Transgressors / Lawless Men" (Isa 53:12 citation)
- **Mark 15:28** — "And the scripture was fulfilled, which saith, And he was numbered with the transgressors (μετὰ ἀνόμων)" — quotation of Isa 53:12 LXX applied to Christ's crucifixion.
- **Luke 22:37** — "that this that is written must yet be accomplished in me, And he was reckoned among the transgressors (μετὰ ἀνόμων)" — same Isaiah quotation, spoken by Jesus.

### 3. Adverbial / Agentive — "By Lawless Hands"
- **Acts 2:23** — "ye have taken, and by wicked hands (διὰ χειρὸς ἀνόμων) have crucified and slain" — Peter's Pentecost sermon; ἀνόμων here modifies "hands," describing the agents of the crucifixion as lawless. Genitive plural.

### 4. Gentile / Outside-the-Mosaic-Law (neutral socio-religious)
- **1 Cor 9:21** — "To them that are without law (τοῖς ἀνόμοις), as without law (ὡς ἄνομος), (being not without law to God (μὴ ὢν ἄνομος θεοῦ), but under the law to Christ,) that I might gain them that are without law (τοὺς ἀνόμους)" — four occurrences in one verse; Paul's missional self-description regarding Gentiles outside the Torah-covenant. Neutral covenantal sense rather than moral condemnation.

### 5. Moral Designation — "Lawless" in Vice Lists
- **1 Tim 1:9** — "the law is not made for a righteous man, but for the lawless (ἀνόμοις) and disobedient, for the ungodly and for sinners, for unholy and profane…" — heading position in a vice list; paired with ἀνυπότακτος ("disobedient"), ἀσεβής ("ungodly"), ἁμαρτωλός ("sinner").

### 6. Adjectival — "Unlawful Deeds"
- **2 Pet 2:8** — "that righteous man dwelling among them, in seeing and hearing, vexed his righteous soul from day to day with their unlawful deeds (ἀνόμοις ἔργοις)" — describing the deeds of Sodom's inhabitants; adjective modifying ἔργοις.

## Grammatical Notes

- **Articular vs. anarthrous:** The articular form ὁ ἄνομος (with definite article) occurs only at **2 Thess 2:8**, where it functions as a definite singular substantival title. The other NT occurrences are all anarthrous — either plural substantives (Mark 15:28; Luke 22:37; Acts 2:23; 1 Cor 9:21 three of four instances; 1 Tim 1:9) or attributive adjectives (2 Pet 2:8, modifying ἔργοις).
- **1 Cor 9:21** contains the densest concentration: four occurrences in one verse, including one predicate usage (ὡς ἄνομος, "as without law") and one with a genitive complement (ἄνομος θεοῦ, "lawless toward God").
- **Case distribution across 10 tokens:** nominative (2 Thess 2:8; 1 Cor 9:21 predicate); dative plural (Mark 15:28, Luke 22:37, 1 Cor 9:21 initial, 1 Tim 1:9, 2 Pet 2:8); accusative plural (1 Cor 9:21 final); genitive plural (Acts 2:23 — χειρὸς ἀνόμων); genitive singular with complement (1 Cor 9:21 — ἄνομος θεοῦ).
