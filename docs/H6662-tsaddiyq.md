## Lexicon Definition

From H6663 (tsadaq); **just** — just, lawful, righteous (man).

Denotes a person (or God) who conforms to a standard of right conduct, justice, or covenant faithfulness. Used both as a substantive adjective ("the righteous one/ones") and attributively ("a righteous man").

## Semantic Categories

### 1. The Righteous vs. The Wicked (Antithetical Contrast)
The dominant usage — tsaddiyq appears in direct antithetical parallelism with רָשָׁע (rasha, H7563) in 81 verses (41% of all occurrences). The righteous and wicked are contrasted in character, speech, fate, and God's response. This pairing drives most of Proverbs.

- **Pro 10:6** — "Blessings are upon the head of the just: but violence covereth the mouth of the wicked."
- **Pro 10:25** — "As the whirlwind passeth, so is the wicked no more: but the righteous is an everlasting foundation."
- **Pro 28:1** — "The wicked flee when no man pursueth: but the righteous are bold as a lion."
- **Mal 3:18** — "Then shall ye return, and discern between the righteous and the wicked, between him that serveth God and him that serveth him not."

### 2. God as Righteous (Divine Attribute)
Applied to YHWH to describe His moral character — especially in covenant contexts, confessions of sin, and responses to judgment. God's righteousness is confessed by those acknowledging their own guilt.

- **Exo 9:27** — "The LORD is righteous, and I and my people are wicked."
- **Lam 1:18** — "The LORD is righteous; for I have rebelled against his commandment."
- **Jer 12:1** — "Righteous art thou, O LORD, when I plead with thee."
- **Dan 9:14** — "The LORD our God is righteous in all his works which he doeth."

### 3. Judicial/Legal Standing (The One in the Right)
In legal settings tsaddiyq denotes the party legally innocent or acquitted, as opposed to the guilty/condemned. Also applied to righteous laws and just rulers.

- **Exo 23:7** — "The innocent and righteous slay thou not: for I will not justify the wicked."
- **Deu 25:1** — "Then shall they justify the righteous, and condemn the wicked."
- **2Sa 23:3** — "He that ruleth over men must be just, ruling in the fear of God."
- **Deu 4:8** — "What nation is there so great, that hath statutes and judgments so righteous as all this law."

### 4. Righteous Conduct / Ethical Character
The tsaddiyq is one who acts with integrity, avoids lying, cares for the poor, and lives uprightly. Behavior defines the category — righteousness is expressed in daily ethical life.

- **Eze 18:5** — "But if a man be just, and do that which is lawful and right..."
- **Pro 13:5** — "A righteous man hateth lying: but a wicked man is loathsome."
- **Pro 20:7** — "The just man walketh in his integrity: his children are blessed after him."
- **Pro 29:7** — "The righteous considereth the cause of the poor: but the wicked regardeth not to know it."

### 5. Destiny and Future Hope of the Righteous
A cluster of uses — especially in Psalms and Proverbs — about what awaits the tsaddiyq: divine protection, inheritance, fruitfulness, and ultimate vindication.

- **Pro 10:30** — "The righteous shall never be removed: but the wicked shall not inhabit the earth."
- **Pro 14:32** — "But the righteous hath hope in his death."
- **Pro 11:28** — "But the righteous shall flourish as a branch."
- **Isa 3:10** — "Say ye to the righteous, that it shall be well with him: for they shall eat the fruit of their doings."

### 6. The Just Who Lives by Faith (Prophetic)
Prophetic usage connects tsaddiyq with faithful trust in YHWH amid injustice and adversity — notably in Habakkuk 2:4, which becomes a key NT quotation.

- **Hab 2:4** — "But the just shall live by his faith."
- **Hab 1:4** — "The wicked doth compass about the righteous; therefore wrong judgment proceedeth."
- **Isa 53:11** — "By his knowledge shall my righteous servant justify many."
- **Jer 23:5** — "I will raise unto David a righteous Branch, and a King shall reign and prosper."
