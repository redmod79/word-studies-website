## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same:**
- The full semantic range of yôwm (literal day, historical period, biographical span, eschatological day) carries directly into NT ἡμέρα
- The "Day of the LORD" formula is the most theologically significant transfer: OT *yôm YHWH* → NT *hēméra tou kyriou* (1 Cor 5:5; 1 Thess 5:2; 2 Pet 3:10)
- The "in the days of X" historical framing works identically in NT narrative (Matt 2:1; Luke 1:5)

**What shifts:**
- The eschatological "Day of the LORD" narrows in the NT to specifically the return/judgment day of Jesus Christ — the referent changes while the formula and vocabulary remain identical
- σήμερον (G4594) takes over the adverbial "today" function that H3117 carried idiomatically (e.g., Heb 3:7–15 quotes Ps 95:7 *sēmeron* = "today")
