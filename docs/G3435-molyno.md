## Semantic Categories

### 1. Conscience Defilement
- **1 Cor 8:7** — "their conscience being weak is defiled" — present passive: conscience is being soiled by the ongoing practice of eating idol-food without knowledge; the defilement attaches to the internal moral faculty

### 2. Garment Defilement (Moral Purity Imagery)
- **Rev 3:4** — "which have not defiled their garments" — aorist active: believers in Sardis who did not soil their garments; garments symbolize moral conduct; the faithful "few" kept theirs unsoiled
- **Rev 14:4** — "which were not defiled with women" — aorist passive: the 144,000 described as not having been defiled; paired with "they are virgins"; the defilement is spiritual unfaithfulness expressed through the figure of sexual impurity

### 3. LXX Usage (Figurative Soiling)
The LXX uses μολύνω to translate H1351 גָּאַל (ga'al, "to soil, pollute, stain") — a verb for visible contamination of garments or hands (Isa 59:3, Lam 4:14, Zeph 3:1). This OT background anchors the NT figurative use: what was literal soiling of garments in the prophets becomes moral soiling in the apostolic writings.
