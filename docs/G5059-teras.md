## Lexicon Definition

> τέρας, -ας; of uncertain affinity; a prodigy or omen: — wonder.

A marvel or portent that astonishes the beholder. In Classical Greek it denoted an ominous sign from the gods, a prodigy in nature, or a monstrous/miraculous event. In the NT it always appears in the plural and is invariably paired with σημεῖα (signs).

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Apostolic / Divine Authentication (Acts dominant)
Wonders worked through the apostles or early church as validation of the gospel message, typically paired with God's agency, the Spirit, or Jesus' name.
- **Acts 2:43** — "many wonders and signs were done by the apostles"
- **Acts 4:30** — "that signs and wonders may be done by the name of thy holy child Jesus"
- **Acts 5:12** — "by the hands of the apostles were many signs and wonders wrought"
- **Acts 14:3** — "granted signs and wonders to be done by their hands"

### 2. Christ's Own Ministry Attested
The formula applied to Jesus' earthly ministry as God's accreditation of him.
- **Acts 2:22** — "Jesus of Nazareth, a man approved of God among you by miracles and wonders and signs"
- **Heb 2:4** — "God also bearing them witness, both with signs and wonders, and with divers miracles"

### 3. Mosaic / Exodus Echo
Wonders recalling the Exodus typology — deliverance from Egypt as the prototype of all later signs-and-wonders language (reflecting LXX usage of מוֹפֵת/מוֹפְתִים).
- **Acts 7:36** — Stephen: Moses "shewed wonders and signs in the land of Egypt, and in the Red sea"
- **Acts 2:19** — Joel quoted: "I will shew wonders in heaven above, and signs in the earth beneath"

### 4. Eschatological Counterfeit (false signs-and-wonders)
Wonders worked by deceivers — the same vocabulary as authentic divine authentication, deliberately mimicked.
- **Mat 24:24** — "false Christs, and false prophets… shall shew great signs and wonders"
- **Mar 13:22** — parallel: "false Christs and false prophets shall rise, and shall shew signs and wonders"
- **2Th 2:9** — the lawless one's coming "is after the working of Satan with all power and signs and lying wonders" (τέρασιν ψεύδους — "wonders of falsehood")

### 5. Sight-Based Faith Rebuked
A single occurrence where demanding to see wonders is cast as deficient faith.
- **Jhn 4:48** — "Except ye see signs and wonders, ye will not believe"

### 6. Paul's Apostolic Credentials
Paul's own defense of his ministry — "signs and wonders" as the mark of true apostleship, worked through the Spirit.
- **Rom 15:19** — "Through mighty signs and wonders, by the power of the Spirit of God"
- **2Co 12:12** — "the signs of an apostle were wrought among you… in signs, and wonders, and mighty deeds"
