## Semantic Categories

### 1. Physical Sealing for Security
The sole occurrence describes a physical act of securing the lions' den with a stone and signet impressions, rendering the enclosure tamper-proof so that "the purpose might not be changed concerning Daniel."
- **Dan 6:17** — "and the king sealed it (חַתְמַהּ) with his own signet, and with the signet of his lords; that the purpose might not be changed concerning Daniel"

The act involves: (1) a stone placed over the mouth of the den, (2) the king's personal signet ring impressed in wax or clay on the stone, (3) the lords' signets added alongside as witnesses. The double-seal guaranteed that neither the king nor the lords could unilaterally remove the stone — a safeguard initiated by the plotters against Daniel.
