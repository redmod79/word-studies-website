## Lexicon Definition

From G5278 (ὑπομένω); cheerful (or hopeful) endurance, constancy. KJV translations: enduring, patience, patient continuance, patient waiting.

The word pictures bearing a weight *from below* — the posture of one who stays under a burden rather than fleeing it. This is active, steadfast endurance rather than passive resignation.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Endurance through Tribulation
Patience produced *by* and exercised *in* suffering; a response to external pressure.

- **Rom 5:3–4** — "tribulation worketh patience; and patience, experience; and experience, hope"
- **2Co 6:4** — "in much patience, in afflictions, in necessities, in distresses"
- **Jas 1:3** — "the trying of your faith worketh patience"
- **Rev 13:10** — "here is the patience and the faith of the saints" — amid captivity and sword

### 2. Patient Persistence toward God's Will
Steadfast continuation in obedience; endurance oriented toward a promised outcome.

- **Luk 8:15** — "keep [the word], and bring forth fruit with patience"
- **Rom 2:7** — "patient continuance in well doing" to seek glory and immortality
- **Heb 10:36** — "ye have need of patience, that... ye might receive the promise"
- **Heb 12:1** — "run with patience the race that is set before us"

### 3. Eschatological Waiting
Patience directed toward Christ's return or a future divine act; hope-shaped endurance.

- **Rom 8:25** — "if we hope for that we see not, then do we with patience wait for it"
- **1Th 1:3** — "patience of hope in our Lord Jesus Christ"
- **2Th 3:5** — "the Lord direct your hearts... into the patient waiting for Christ"
- **Rev 3:10** — "thou hast kept the word of my patience"

### 4. Virtue in Character Lists
Hupomone as one among a cluster of ethical qualities; patience as character trait alongside faith, love, godliness.

- **1Ti 6:11** — "follow after righteousness, godliness, faith, love, patience, meekness"
- **Tit 2:2** — "sober, grave, temperate, sound in faith, in charity, in patience"
- **2Pe 1:6** — "to temperance patience; and to patience godliness"
- **Rev 2:19** — "I know thy works, and charity, and service, and faith, and thy patience"

### 5. Communal and Apostolic Testimony
Patience as a publicly visible mark — of apostolic ministry, of a congregation, of the saints as a community.

- **2Co 12:12** — "the signs of an apostle were wrought among you in all patience"
- **2Th 1:4** — "we glory in you... for your patience and faith in all your persecutions"
- **Rev 1:9** — "companion in tribulation, and in the kingdom and patience of Jesus Christ"
- **Rev 14:12** — "here is the patience of the saints: here are they that keep the commandments of God"
