# G1459 — ἐγκαταλείπω (enkataleipō)

- **Original:** ἐγκαταλείπω

- **Part of Speech:** Verb
- **Root:** Compound — ἐν (en, G1722 "in") + κατά (kata, G2596 "down") + λείπω (leipō, G3007 "to leave"). Intensified form: "to leave down in [a place]."

## Lexicon Definition

To leave behind in some place; (in a good sense) to leave surviving, reserve; (in a bad sense) to desert, abandon, forsake. Compound of ἐν (in) and καταλείπω (to leave behind).

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Cry of Dereliction — God Forsaking (Ps 22:1 Quotation)
- **Matt 27:46** — "My God, my God, why hast thou forsaken me?" (Aorist active indicative, 2nd person)
- **Mark 15:34** — "My God, my God, why hast thou forsaken me?" (Aorist active indicative, 2nd person)

Both are direct quotation of Ps 22:1 (LXX Ps 21:2). The compound prefix ἐγκατα- intensifies the sense: not merely "left" but "left down inside" a situation of distress.

### 2. Divine Promise Not to Forsake
- **Acts 2:27** — "Thou wilt not leave my soul in hell" (Future active indicative — Peter quoting Ps 16:10 LXX)
- **Heb 13:5** — "I will never leave thee, nor forsake thee" (Aorist active subjunctive — quoting Deut 31:6/Josh 1:5)

These are divine assurances of non-abandonment. The double negative in Heb 13:5 (οὐ μή + subjunctive) is the strongest possible Greek negation.

### 3. Human Abandonment / Desertion
- **2 Tim 4:10** — "Demas hath forsaken me, having loved this present world" (Aorist active indicative)
- **2 Tim 4:16** — "All men forsook me... I pray God that it may not be laid to their charge" (Aorist active indicative)

Paul's personal experience of being left behind by associates and defenders at his trial.

### 4. Apostolic Endurance — Not Forsaken
- **2 Cor 4:9** — "Persecuted, but not forsaken; cast down, but not destroyed" (Present passive participle)

Part of a paradox series. The passive voice indicates being the object of others' potential abandonment, yet negated — God does not forsake.

### 5. Abandoning the Assembly
- **Heb 10:25** — "Not forsaking the assembling of ourselves together" (Present active participle)

The only occurrence where the subject voluntarily leaves a practice or community, rather than being abandoned by someone.

### 6. Remnant Preservation
- **Rom 9:29** — "Except the Lord of Sabaoth had left us a seed" (Aorist active indicative — Paul quoting Isa 1:9 LXX)

Here the sense is "leave behind" in a positive/neutral way — a remnant preserved from total destruction.
