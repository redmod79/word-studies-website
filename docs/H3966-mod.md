## Lexicon Definition

From the same root as (unused); properly, *vehemence*, i.e. (with or without preposition)
*vehemently*; by implication wholly, speedily, etc. — often with other words as an intensive
or superlative; especially when repeated: diligently, especially, exceeding(-ly), far, fast,
good, great(-ly), louder and louder, might(-ily, -y), (so) much, quickly, (so) sore, utterly,
very (much, sore), well.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Divine Evaluation / Quality Intensifier
Meod modifies divine assessment or describes exceptional goodness/beauty/value.
- **Gen 1:31** — "God saw every thing that he had made, and, behold, it was *very* good"
- **Gen 24:16** — "And the damsel was *very* fair to look upon"
- **1 Sam 25:15** — "the men were *very* good unto us, and we were not hurt"
- **Jer 24:2–3** — "the good figs, *very* good; and the evil, *very* evil"

### 2. Emotional Intensity (Fear, Anger, Distress)
The most common narrative function: amplifying emotional states of biblical characters.
- **Gen 4:5** — "Cain was *very* wroth, and his countenance fell"

- **2 Sam 13:15** — "Amnon hated her *exceedingly*; so that the hatred... was greater than the love"

### 3. Physical Abundance / Material Greatness
Intensifies descriptions of wealth, size, multitude, or military strength.
- **Gen 13:2** — "Abram was *very* rich in cattle, in silver, and in gold"
- **1 Ki 10:2** — "she came to Jerusalem with a *very* great train... and *very* much gold"
- **2 Chr 32:27** — "Hezekiah had *exceeding* much riches and honour"
- **Ezek 37:2** — "behold, there were *very* many in the open valley"

### 4. Covenant Command: "With All Thy Might"
Used as a substantive (noun) in the Shema context — the only place meod functions as
a capacity or faculty rather than a pure intensifier.
- **Deut 6:5** — "thou shalt love the LORD thy God with all thine heart, and with all thy soul, and with all thy *might* (meod)"
- **2 Ki 23:25** — "like unto him was there no king... that turned to the LORD with all his heart... and all his *might*"

### 5. Doubled for Superlative (meod meod)
When meod appears twice in sequence it marks an extreme superlative — "exceedingly greatly."
- **Gen 17:2** — "I will multiply thee *exceedingly*" (meod meod)

- **Gen 27:33** — "Isaac trembled *very exceedingly*" (meod meod)

### 6. Eschatological / Prophetic Intensification
