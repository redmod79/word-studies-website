# WG: Priests / Priestly Office (Greek NT)

## Overview

The NT priestly concept clusters around six Greek words: the general term for priest (ἱερεύς), the ruling high priest (ἀρχιερεύς), the action of priestly service (ἱερατεύω), the priestly body as a collective (ἱεράτευμα), the act of offering (προσφέρω), and the priestly order/arrangement (τάξις). These words intersect most densely in Hebrews and Revelation.

## Roles in the Concept

- **Person (general):** ἱερεύς (G2409) — any priest, Levitical or Melchizedekian
- **Person (chief):** ἀρχιερεύς (G749) — the ruling high priest; also used collectively for "chief priests" in the Synoptics
- **Action:** ἱερατεύω (G2407) — the performance of priestly duties in rotation (Luke 1:8)
- **Collective body:** ἱεράτευμα (G2406) — all priests as a corporate entity; applied to believers in 1 Pet 2:5, 9
- **Work performed:** προσφέρω (G4374) — to bring/offer; the definitive priestly act
- **Structural order:** τάξις (G5010) — the organized arrangement; Hebrews uses it to define the two priesthood types: "order of Aaron" (implied) vs. "order of Melchisedec"

## Key Passages Where Multiple Group Words Appear Together

- **Heb 5:6** — ἱερεύς + τάξις — "Thou art a priest for ever after the order of Melchisedec"
- **Heb 5:1** — ἀρχιερεύς + προσφέρω — "every high priest...is ordained for men...to offer both gifts and sacrifices"
- **Heb 7:11** — ἱερεύς + τάξις + (implied) ἀρχιερεύς — "perfection were by the Levitical priesthood...another priest should rise after the order of Melchisedec"
- **Heb 10:11** — ἱερεύς + προσφέρω — "every priest standeth daily ministering and offering...the same sacrifices"
- **Luke 1:8** — ἱερατεύω + τάξις — "while he executed the priest's office...in the order of his course" (Zechariah)
- **Matt 8:4** — ἱερεύς + προσφέρω — "shew thyself to the priest, and offer the gift that Moses commanded"
