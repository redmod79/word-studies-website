## Lexicon Definition

A primitive root; to ascend, intransitively (be high) or actively (mount); used in a great variety of senses, primary and secondary, literal and figurative — arise (up), (cause to) ascend up, at once, break (the day) (up), bring (up), (cause to) burn, carry up, cast up, shew, climb (up), (cause to, make to) come (up), cut off, dawn, depart, exalt, excel, fall, fetch up, get up, (make to) go (away, up), grow (over), increase, lay, leap, levy, lift (self) up, light, mention, mount up, offer, make to pay, perfect, prefer, put (on), raise, recover, restore, (make to) rise (up), scale, set (up), shoot forth (up), (begin to) spring (up), stir up, take away (up), work.

The core idea is motion from lower to higher — spatial, ritual, social, or figurative. The causative stems extend this into "bring up / offer up," the most common cultic sense.

**Pattern:** Highest density in the historical narratives (Judges, 1 Samuel, Joshua, 2 Kings) where military/pilgrimage movement dominates, and in the shorter prophets (Jonah, Joel, Amos, Nahum, Obadiah) where judgments "come up" against nations. Surprisingly low density in Leviticus despite the cultic "offer up" usage — because Leviticus prefers the nominal עֹלָה (H5930) with other verbs (קָרַב) for presenting the sacrifice.

## Semantic Categories
