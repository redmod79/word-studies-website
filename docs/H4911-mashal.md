## Lexicon Definition

Denominative from H4912; to liken, i.e. (transitively) to use figurative language (an allegory, adage, song or the like); intransitively, to resemble: — be(-come) like, compare, use (as a) proverb, speak (in proverbs), utter.

The verb operates on two axes that mirror its parent noun: an **active** axis ("to utter a mashal" — to speak in figurative/proverbial form) and a **stative** axis ("to be like, resemble, become similar"). English glosses split along this line: "speak in proverbs / use a proverb / utter a parable" vs. "become like / be like / compare."

## Semantic Categories

### 1. To Speak / Utter a Mashal (active speech-act)
The verb takes a noun-cognate object (mashal/chidah); the speaker produces a figurative or oracular utterance.
- **Num 21:27** — "they that speak in proverbs (ha-moshelim) say, Come into Heshbon..."
- **Eze 17:2** — "put forth a riddle, and speak a parable (u-meshol mashal) unto the house of Israel"
- **Eze 24:3** — "And utter a parable (u-meshol... mashal) unto the rebellious house"
- **Eze 20:49** — "they say of me, Doth he not speak parables? (ha-lo memashel meshalim hu)"

### 2. To Use / Cite a Current Proverb (factitive)
Concentrated in Ezekiel's polemic: the people "use" a circulating proverb that expresses defective theology, and YHWH announces He will cause the proverb to cease.
- **Eze 12:23** — "they shall no more use it as a proverb (velo yimshelu oto... mashal) in Israel"
- **Eze 16:44** — "every one that useth proverbs (ha-moshel) shall use this proverb (yimshol) against thee"
- **Eze 18:2** — "What mean ye, that ye use this proverb (moshlim et ha-mashal ha-zeh) concerning the land of Israel"
- **Eze 18:3** — "ye shall not have occasion any more to use this proverb (limshol ha-mashal ha-zeh) in Israel"

### 3. To Become Like / Resemble (Niphal stative — descent motif)
Always with a ל or כ comparison-particle; consistently deployed in laments where the speaker fears being assimilated to the dead or to perishing beasts.
- **Psa 28:1** — "lest... I become like (ve-nimshalti im) them that go down into the pit"
- **Psa 49:12** — "man being in honour abideth not: he is like (nimshal) the beasts that perish"
- **Psa 49:20** — "Man that is in honour, and understandeth not, is like (nimshal) the beasts that perish"
- **Psa 143:7** — "hide not thy face from me, lest I be like (ve-nimshalti im) them that go down into the pit"

### 4. To Be Compared / Set Alongside (Hiphil comparison)
Idol-polemic idiom: asking with whom YHWH can be compared.
- **Isa 46:5** — "To whom will ye liken me, and make me equal, and compare me (ve-tamshiluni), that we may be like?"

### 5. To Have Become Like (taunt-context Niphal)
One occurrence within a mocking taunt-song — the fallen tyrant is "become like" the dead already in Sheol.
- **Isa 14:10** — "Art thou also become weak as we? art thou become like unto us? (elenu nimshalta)"

### 6. Elevated / Grief-Stricken Self-Comparison
- **Job 30:19** — "He hath cast me into the mire, and I am become like (va-etmashel — Hithpael) dust and ashes"

The verb bifurcates in the LXX along the same Hebrew-stem lines: **Qal/Piel "speak/use a mashal" → παραβολή + verb**; **Niphal "become like" → ὁμοιόω**. The ὁμοιόω bridge is the most theologically load-bearing, since it carries the "likeness" semantic into Jesus' parable-formula ("the kingdom of heaven is like unto...") and into Heb 2:17 ("made like unto his brethren").
