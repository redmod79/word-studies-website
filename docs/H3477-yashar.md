## Semantic Categories

### 1. Royal/Covenantal Evaluation — "doing right in the sight of the LORD"
The dominant formulaic usage. Kings are evaluated by whether they did "that which was right" (hayyashar) in the eyes of YHWH.
- **1Ki 15:5** — "David did that which was right in the eyes of the LORD, and turned not aside from any thing that he commanded him"
- **2Ki 22:2** — "And he did that which was right in the sight of the LORD, and walked in all the way of David his father"
- **2Ch 29:2** — "And he did that which was right in the sight of the LORD, according to all that David his father had done"
- **Deu 6:18** — "And thou shalt do that which is right and good in the sight of the LORD"

### 2. Moral Character — The Upright Person
Describes a person of integrity; used as a substantive ("the upright") to denote a character type, often in contrast to the wicked.
- **Job 1:1** — "that man was perfect and upright, and one that feared God, and eschewed evil"
- **Pro 2:21** — "For the upright shall dwell in the land, and the perfect shall remain in it"
- **Pro 11:3** — "The integrity of the upright shall guide them: but the perverseness of transgressors shall destroy them"
- **Mic 7:2** — "The good man is perished out of the earth: and there is none upright among men"

### 3. Upright in Heart — Internal Disposition
Specifically combined with "heart" (leb) to denote inner moral alignment, not just external behavior.
- **Psa 7:10** — "My defence is of God, which saveth the upright in heart"
- **Psa 11:2** — "that they may privily shoot at the upright in heart"
- **Psa 32:11** — "Be glad in the LORD, and rejoice, ye righteous: and shout for joy, all ye that are upright in heart"
- **Psa 125:4** — "Do good, O LORD, unto those that be good, and to them that are upright in their hearts"

### 4. Attribute of God / Divine Standards
Applied to God himself, his statutes, his word, and his ways.
- **Deu 32:4** — "a God of truth and without iniquity, just and right is he"
- **Psa 19:8** — "The statutes of the LORD are right, rejoicing the heart"
- **Psa 25:8** — "Good and upright is the LORD: therefore will he teach sinners in the way"
- **Psa 92:15** — "To shew that the LORD is upright: he is my rock, and there is no unrighteousness in him"

### 5. Right in One's Own Eyes — Autonomous Judgment
Used critically for self-directed moral reasoning without divine reference.
- **Jdg 17:6** — "every man did that which was right in his own eyes"
- **Jdg 21:25** — "every man did that which was right in his own eyes"
- **Pro 12:15** — "The way of a fool is right in his own eyes"
- **Pro 21:2** — "Every way of a man is right in his own eyes: but the LORD pondereth the hearts"

### 6. Literal Physical Straightness
Applied to physical objects or paths — the original spatial sense.
- **Eze 1:7** — "And their feet were straight feet"
- **Eze 1:23** — "under the firmament were their wings straight"
- **Jer 31:9** — "I will cause them to walk by the rivers of waters in a straight way"
- **Psa 107:7** — "And he led them forth by the right way, that they might go to a city of habitation"

### 7. The Book of Jasher
Proper noun usage — the "Book of the Upright" (Sepher Hayyashar), a lost ancient text cited in the OT.
- **Jos 10:13** — "Is not this written in the book of Jasher?"
- **2Sa 1:18** — "behold, it is written in the book of Jasher"
