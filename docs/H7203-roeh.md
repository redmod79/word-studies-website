## Lexicon Definition

Active participle of H7200 (ra'ah); a seer (as often rendered); but also (abstractly) a vision. KJV renderings: *seer, vision*.

H7203 is the lexicalized active participle of H7200 — literally "one who sees." Strong's assigns it a separate number because it functions as a substantive noun (a professional title) rather than a verbal participle. Note that many additional occurrences of the identical form הָרֹאֶה ("the seer") in Chronicles and Samuel are parsed under the root verb H7200 rather than this noun entry; the morphological form is the same, but KJV concordance mapping varies.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Professional Title — "The Seer" (Samuel)
The dominant use: a recognized office-holder who receives and communicates divine revelation, equivalent to but older than the title "prophet" (nabi').
- **1 Sam 9:9** — "(Beforetime in Israel, when a man went to inquire of God, thus he spake, Come, and let us go to the seer: for he that is now called a Prophet was beforetime called a Seer.)"
- **1 Sam 9:11** — "as they went up the hill to the city, they found young maidens going out to draw water, and said unto them, Is the seer here?"
- **1 Sam 9:18** — "Then Saul drew near to Samuel in the gate, and said, Tell me, I pray thee, where the seer's house is."
- **1 Sam 9:19** — "And Samuel answered Saul, and said, I am the seer: go up before me unto the high place"

### 2. Abstract Sense — "Vision" (Prophetic Capacity)
A single occurrence uses ro'eh abstractly for the faculty or act of visionary perception, in the context of prophets who have lost it.
- **Isa 28:7** — "the priest and the prophet have erred through strong drink... they err in vision (ro'eh), they stumble in judgment"
