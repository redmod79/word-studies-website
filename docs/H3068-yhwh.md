# H3068 — יְהֹוָה (YHWH / Yᵉhôvâh)

## Core Data
- **Strong's:** H3068
- **Original:** יְהֹוָה (the Tetragrammaton — four-letter divine name YHWH / יהוה)
- **Transliteration:** Yᵉhôvâh (conventional "Jehovah"; reconstructed "Yahweh")
- **Pronunciation:** yeh-ho-vaw' (vowel-pointed form; original pronunciation uncertain)
- **Part of Speech:** Proper noun with reference to deity (n-pr)
- **Root:** Related to הָיָה (hāyâh, H1961) "to be, exist, become" — the verb behind the "I AM" self-designation of Exo 3:14
- **Occurrences:** 6,534 tokens in 5,520 unique OT verses (most frequent proper noun in the Hebrew Bible)
- **First Occurrence:** Gen 2:4 — "These [are] the generations of the heavens and of the earth when they were created, in the day that the LORD (Yᵉhôvâh) God made the earth and the heavens"
- **Hapax Legomenon:** No — appears in 36 of 39 OT books

## Lexicon Definition

From the verb "to be" (cf. H1961); (the) self-Existent or Eternal; **Jehovah**, the personal, covenantal, national name of the God of Israel. Rendered in most English versions as **LORD** (small caps) to signal the distinction from אֲדֹנָי (Adonai, H136, "Lord/lord"). Compare H3050 (יָהּ, Yāh — short form) and H3069 (יְהֹוִה, Yᵉhôvih — form vocalized when juxtaposed with Adonai).

## Morphology & Root

- Consonants: **י־ה־ו־ה** (y-h-w-h), the Tetragrammaton ("four-lettered name").
- Widely analyzed as a form of the verb **הָיָה** (H1961, *hāyâh*, "to be / to become / to exist"), or its older byform **הוה** (*hwh*).
- Exo 3:14 gives the self-exegesis: **אֶהְיֶה אֲשֶׁר אֶהְיֶה** (*ʾehyeh ʾăsher ʾehyeh*), "I AM THAT I AM" — Qal 1cs imperfect of H1961. The name YHWH in Exo 3:15 immediately follows, functioning as the third-person counterpart ("He is / He causes to be").
- Causative/Hiphil reading ("He who causes to be") has been proposed but is contested; most grammarians prefer the simple Qal sense ("He is / He exists / He will be").
- Masoretic vowel points (שְׁוָא, חֹלֶם, קָמֶץ) were borrowed from אֲדֹנָי (or אֱלֹהִים when adjacent to Adonai) to cue the *Qere perpetuum* (spoken substitute). The hybrid "Jehovah" arose from reading those borrowed vowels on the original consonants.

## Semantic Categories

The name is a proper noun, so "semantic categories" here mean *functional contexts of use* rather than lexical senses.

### 1. Self-Revelation / Disclosure of the Name
The name is treated as the personal, covenantal self-identification of God.
- **Exo 3:14–15** — "I AM THAT I AM... The LORD (YHWH) God of your fathers... this [is] my name for ever, and this [is] my memorial unto all generations."
- **Exo 6:2–3** — "I [am] the LORD: And I appeared unto Abraham, unto Isaac, and unto Jacob, by [the name of] God Almighty (El Shaddai), but by my name JEHOVAH was I not known to them."
- **Exo 34:5–7** — "the LORD descended in the cloud... and proclaimed the name of the LORD... The LORD, The LORD God, merciful and gracious, longsuffering, and abundant in goodness and truth."
- **Isa 42:8** — "I [am] the LORD: that [is] my name: and my glory will I not give to another, neither my praise to graven images."

### 2. Covenant Confession (Shema)
The name anchors Israel's monotheistic confession.
- **Deu 6:4** — "Hear, O Israel: The LORD (YHWH) our God [is] one LORD (YHWH)."
- **Deu 6:5** — "thou shalt love the LORD thy God with all thine heart..."
- **1 Kgs 18:39** — "The LORD, he [is] the God; the LORD, he [is] the God."

### 3. Prophetic Messenger Formula
- **Jer 1:4** — "Then the word of the LORD came unto me, saying..."
- **Eze 1:3** — "The word of the LORD came expressly unto Ezekiel the priest..."
- **Amo 1:3** — "Thus saith the LORD; For three transgressions of Damascus..."

### 4. Cultic / Liturgical Address
- **Lev 1:1–2** — "And the LORD called unto Moses... If any man of you bring an offering unto the LORD..."
- **Psa 100:1** — "Make a joyful noise unto the LORD, all ye lands" (vocative and liturgical).
- **1 Sa 1:3** — "to worship and to sacrifice unto the LORD of hosts in Shiloh."

### 5. Covenant Titles and Epithets
- **YHWH Ṣᵉbāʾôt** ("LORD of hosts") — ~285x (1 Sa 1:3; Isa 1:9; Hag 1:2; Mal 1:4).

- **YHWH Ṣidqēnû** ("The LORD our righteousness"), **YHWH Shammah** ("The LORD is there"), **YHWH Rōʿî** ("The LORD is my shepherd"), etc.

## LXX Mapping

The Septuagint almost universally renders YHWH with **κύριος** (kyrios, "Lord") — the single most consequential translation decision in biblical history, carrying the divine name through to the Greek NT.

| ἔναντι (enanti) | — | 162 | 1.75 | "before" — renders *lipnê YHWH* ("before the LORD") phrases. |
| παντοκράτωρ (pantokratōr) | G3841 | 121 | 1.83 | "Almighty" — frequent rendering of *YHWH Ṣᵉbāʾôt* ("LORD of hosts"). |
| θεός (theos) | G2316 | 1,391 | 1.12 | "God" — often picks up the Elohim that co-occurs with YHWH; occasionally substitutes for YHWH itself. |
| σαβαώθ (sabaōth) | G4519 | 53 | 1.88 | Transliteration of *Ṣᵉbāʾôt* ("hosts/armies"); retained in "LORD of Sabaoth" (Rom 9:29; Jam 5:4). |

**Note on Jewish pronunciation practice:** By the Second Temple period, reverence for the Name led to oral substitution of **אֲדֹנָי** (Adonai) when reading YHWH aloud, or **הַשֵּׁם** (*Ha-Shem*, "the Name") in non-liturgical contexts. The LXX's choice of κύριος institutionalized this substitution in Greek. Masoretic pointing (shewa / holem / qamets borrowed from Adonai or Elohim) is a written cue for this *Qere perpetuum*, not a guide to original pronunciation — the hybrid "Jehovah" arose when Christian readers took those vowels as native to YHWH. Modern reconstructions ("Yahweh") rely on early patristic transliterations (Ἰαβέ, Ἰαουέ) and comparative morphology.
