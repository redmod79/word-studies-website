## Semantic Categories

### 1. Fulfillment of OT Prophetic Scripture
The noun applied retrospectively to Isaiah and other OT prophets — *the* prophecy of a named author, understood as a body of divinely authorized prediction.

- **Mat 13:14** — "And in them is fulfilled the prophecy of Esaias, which saith, By hearing ye shall hear, and shall not understand"

### 2. Spiritual Gift among Believers (1 Corinthians)
Paul's primary use: prophecy as a functional gift of edification distributed in the congregation alongside tongues, knowledge, and discernment. The focus is on the gift's operation and relative priority.

- **Rom 12:6** — "whether prophecy, [let us prophesy] according to the proportion of faith"
- **1 Cor 12:10** — "to another prophecy; to another discerning of spirits; to another divers kinds of tongues"
- **1 Cor 13:2** — "And though I have [the gift of] prophecy, and understand all mysteries...but have not charity"
- **1 Cor 14:22** — "prophesying [is a sign] not for them that believe not, but for them which believe"
- **1 Thess 5:20** — "Despise not prophesyings"

### 3. Prophetic Words Guiding Ministry
Specific utterances delivered over a person as directional or confirming revelation — the word or words themselves, not merely the capacity.

- **1 Tim 1:18** — "according to the prophecies which went before on thee, that thou by them mightest war a good warfare"
- **1 Tim 4:14** — "the gift that is in thee, which was given thee by prophecy, with the laying on of the hands"

### 4. The Foundational Nature of Written Prophecy (2 Peter)
Prophecy in its literary-canonical sense — OT scripture as the product of Spirit-moved human authors. Two programmatic statements on hermeneutics and inspiration.

- **2 Pet 1:20** — "no prophecy of the scripture is of any private interpretation"
- **2 Pet 1:21** — "the prophecy came not in old time by the will of man: but holy men of God spake [as they were] moved by the Holy Ghost"

### 5. The Book of Revelation as "This Prophecy"
Revelation self-designates as a prophecy (1:3) and enforces this identity with four closing uses. The phrase "the prophecy of this book" frames the entire document as a unified predictive work requiring faithful keeping.

- **Rev 1:3** — "Blessed [is] he that readeth, and they that hear the words of this prophecy"
- **Rev 11:6** — "power to shut heaven, that it rain not in the days of their prophecy"
- **Rev 22:7** — "blessed [is] he that keepeth the sayings of the prophecy of this book"
- **Rev 22:18** — "I testify unto every man that heareth the words of the prophecy of this book"
