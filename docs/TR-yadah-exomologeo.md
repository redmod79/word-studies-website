**Semantic preservation in translation:** The LXX exomologeo preserves yadah's dual semantic range. The Greek compound (ek + homologeo = "out + confess/agree") carries both senses: public confession of sin and public acknowledgment/thanksgiving to God. The stem-based distinction (Hiphil = praise, Hithpael = confess) is collapsed into one Greek verb, but context disambiguates.

## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same across testaments:**
- Both senses (confession of sin and public thanksgiving/acknowledgment) carry through unchanged from Hebrew to Greek
- The relational directedness persists — always confession/praise directed toward God or before witnesses
- The formulaic pairing with prayer (palal / proseuchomai) carries from Dan 9:4,20 to James 5:16

**What shifts:**
- In the OT, the praise/thanksgiving sense (Hiphil) vastly dominates (~90%); in the NT, the confession sense dominates (~64%)
- The NT develops a confessional-christological usage absent in the OT: "confess that Jesus Christ is Lord" (Phil 2:11), "confess his name" (Rev 3:5)
- The Hebrew stem distinction (Hiphil vs. Hithpael) that formally separates praise from confession is lost in Greek, where context alone disambiguates
