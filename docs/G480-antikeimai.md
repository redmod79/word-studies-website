## Lexicon Definition

Strong's: "from ἀντί and κεῖμαι; to lie opposite, i.e. be adverse (figuratively, repugnant) to: — adversary, be contrary, oppose."

The verb is a compound stative: it denotes not primarily an act of aggression but a *position* — to lie, stand, or be set over against someone or something. Because it is deponent (middle/passive in form, active in sense), the participle ὁ ἀντικείμενος can be used substantivally: "the one (who is) set in opposition," "the opponent," "the adversary."

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Personal Human Opponents (plural, concrete)
The substantival plural participle names a group standing visibly against God's messenger/work.
- **Luke 13:17** — "all his adversaries (πάντες οἱ ἀντικείμενοι αὐτῷ) were ashamed" — Jesus' synagogue-ruler critics after the sabbath healing.
- **Luke 21:15** — "all your adversaries (πάντες οἱ ἀντικείμενοι ὑμῖν) shall not be able to gainsay nor resist" — persecutors of disciples.
- **1 Cor 16:9** — "a great door… and many adversaries (ἀντικείμενοι πολλοί)" — opposition coupled with open mission.
- **Phil 1:28** — "in nothing terrified by your adversaries (ὑπὸ τῶν ἀντικειμένων)" — civic/pagan pressure on the Philippian church.

### 2. Abstract / Behavioral Opposition (finite verb, impersonal subject)
The finite indicative describes a *thing* or *state* that stands in mutual incompatibility with another.
- **Gal 5:17** — "the flesh lusteth against the Spirit… these are contrary the one to the other (ταῦτα… ἀλλήλοις ἀντίκειται)."
- **1 Tim 1:10** — "if there be any other thing that is contrary to sound doctrine (τῇ ὑγιαινούσῃ διδασκαλίᾳ ἀντίκειται)."

### 3. Singular Personal Adversary — Definite / Titular Usage
When articulated and singular, ὁ ἀντικείμενος functions as a quasi-title for a singular opposing figure.
- **1 Tim 5:14** — "give none occasion to the adversary (τῷ ἀντικειμένῳ) to speak reproachfully." Dative singular with the article; in the Pastorals' vocabulary this is taken by most lexica (BDAG, Thayer) and commentators as a near-title for Satan (cf. 1 Tim 3:6–7; 5:15, where Σατανᾶς occurs in the immediate context of 5:14).
- **2 Thess 2:4** — "ὁ ἀντικείμενος καὶ ὑπεραιρόμενος ἐπὶ πάντα λεγόμενον θεόν…" — "the one opposing and exalting himself over all that is called God." Nominative singular participle, articulated and paired with a second articulated participle (ὑπεραιρόμενος, G5229). Grammatically this is a standard substantival participle construction identifying a definite figure characterized by ongoing opposition.

(Note that 2 Thess 2:4 and 1 Tim 5:14 are the only two singular, articulated forms of ἀντικείμενος in the NT; the identification of the referent in 2 Thess 2:4 is an interpretive question for the study using this data, not a lexical one.)

### Hebrew / LXX Connection
- **שָׂטָן (satan) H7854** — "adversary, opponent; the Adversary." The Hebrew title which the NT transliterates as Σατανᾶς. In the LXX, שָׂטָן is most often rendered by διάβολος (Job 1–2; Zech 3), but the semantic field of ἀντίκειμαι overlaps with שָׂטָן's root idea of "one who stands against." The KJV shares "adversaries" as a gloss between G480 and H7854 (see Easily Confused above), reflecting real overlap in referent-possibility in the NT.
