**Semantic continuity in translation:** The qowl → phone bridge is exceptionally stable. φωνή captures all the major senses of qowl: articulate speech/voice, inarticulate sound, thunder, and instrumental sound. The Hebrew "obey the voice" formula (shama + bqowl) sometimes triggers LXX translators to use the hearing verb (akouo/eisakouo) instead of phone, but this is a collocational shift, not a semantic one.

## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same across testaments:**
- The core semantic range is fully preserved: articulate divine voice, human voice in prayer/lament, trumpet/thunder sound
- The covenant-obedience formula "hear/obey the voice" continues from Deuteronomy through Hebrews
- The prophetic-herald "voice crying in the wilderness" passes directly from Isaiah to the Gospels
- Theophanic voice-as-power imagery (Psa 29 → Rev 1:10, 1:15)

**What shifts:**
- NT phone acquires christological specificity: the voice at baptism (Mat 3:17), transfiguration (Mat 17:5), and Revelation's "voice as of a trumpet" (Rev 1:10) are identifiable as Christ's or the Father's voice, extending the OT theophanic pattern
- Eschatological intensification: the "voice of the archangel" and "last trump" (1 Th 4:16, 1 Cor 15:52) build on the OT trumpet-voice pattern of Josh 6 and Exo 19
- The Johannine distinctives: "My sheep hear my voice" (Jhn 10:27) applies the covenant-obedience formula to the Good Shepherd
