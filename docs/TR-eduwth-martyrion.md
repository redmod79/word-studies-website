**The key LXX compound phrases preserved into NT:**
- **σκηνὴ τοῦ μαρτυρίου** ("tabernacle of the testimony") — translates מִשְׁכַּן הָעֵדֻת (Num 9:15 etc.)
- **κιβωτὸς τοῦ μαρτυρίου** ("ark of the testimony") — translates אֲרוֹן הָעֵדוּת (Exo 25:22 etc.)

**Semantic continuity in translation:** μαρτύριον captures ʻêdûwth precisely — it denotes the testimony as *thing* or *evidence* (not the person, not the act), which matches the Hebrew cultic use where the documents and the chest housing them are "the testimony." The LXX makes no semantic shift; the word pair is structurally stable.

## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same:**
- μαρτύριον throughout LXX and NT retains the sense of formal, institutionalized witness — a fixed, public, authoritative testimony rather than casual speech
- The cultic frame (tabernacle, ark) is explicitly recalled in Revelation, linking the heavenly sanctuary directly to the Mosaic prototype

**What shifts:**
- In the OT, ʻêdûwth is almost exclusively tied to the physical sanctuary artifacts (ark, tables, tabernacle); in the NT, μαρτύριον generalizes to *the testimony of Christ* and apostolic proclamation while retaining the formal/covenant character
- The OT testimony is a static written document (the law on stone); the NT testimony is a dynamic proclamation (resurrection witness), though both function as authoritative divine covenant communication
