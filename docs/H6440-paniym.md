## Semantic Categories

### 1. Surface / Face of the Earth and Waters
The concrete spatial sense — face as an exposed surface oriented outward.

- **Gen 1:2** — "darkness was upon the face of the deep … Spirit of God moved upon the face of the waters"
- **Gen 1:29** — "every herb bearing seed, which is upon the face of all the earth"
- **Gen 4:14** — "thou hast driven me out from the face of the earth; and from thy face shall I be hid"

### 2. The Physical Face of a Person or Being
The literal face — human or angelic — as the visible front of a person.

- **Gen 32:30** — "I have seen God face to face, and my life is preserved"
- **Ezek 1:10** — "the face of a man … face of a lion … face of an ox … face of an eagle"
- **1 Sam 16:7** — "man looketh on the outward appearance [face]… the LORD looketh on the heart"
- **2 Sam 14:24** — "Let him not see my face … Absalom returned … and saw not the king's face"

### 3. The Divine Face / Presence of God
God's *pânîym* is his relational, accessible presence — both sought and feared. To be hidden from God's face is exile; to see it is fullness of life.

- **Exo 33:14** — "My presence [pânîym] shall go with thee, and I will give thee rest"
- **Exo 33:20** — "Thou canst not see my face: for there shall no man see me, and live"
- **Ps 27:8** — "Thy face, LORD, will I seek"
- **Ps 51:11** — "Cast me not away from thy presence; and take not thy holy spirit from me"
- **Ps 16:11** — "In thy presence is fulness of joy; at thy right hand there are pleasures for evermore"
- **Isa 63:9** — "the angel of his presence saved them"

### 4. Spatial Preposition: Before / In Front of
The most frequent use — *liphnê* (לִפְנֵי, "before") marks spatial, temporal, and relational positioning. This is the prepositional idiom "to the face of = in front of."

- **Num 3:4** — "Nadab and Abihu died before the LORD"
- **Deu 1:8** — "I have set the land before you: go in and possess"
- **Jos 1:5** — "there shall not any man be able to stand before thee all the days of thy life"
- **1 Sam 1:12** — "as she continued praying before the LORD, Eli marked her mouth"

### 5. Countenance / Expression / Honor
The face as revealing emotional or social status — honoring, shining, lifting up.

- **Num 6:25** — "The LORD make his face shine upon thee, and be gracious unto thee"
- **Ps 21:6** — "thou hast made him exceeding glad with thy countenance"
- **Lev 19:32** — "honour the face of the old man, and fear thy God"
- **1 Sam 16:7** — "Look not on his countenance, or on the height of his stature"

### 6. Person / Respect of Persons
*Pânîym* in plural with "to lift up the face" (נָשָׂא פָּנִים) or "to regard the face" = to show partiality, to favor or honor a specific individual.

- **Deu 1:17** — "Ye shall not respect persons [pânîym] in judgment … for the judgment is God's"
- **Lev 19:15** — "thou shalt not respect the person of the poor, nor honour the person of the mighty"

### 7. Bread of the Face / Presence (Shewbread)
The twelve loaves placed "before [the face of] God" on the temple table — *lechem pânîym*.

- **Exo 25:30** — "thou shalt set upon the table shewbread before me alway"
