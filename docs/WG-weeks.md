## Roles in the Concept

- **Foundation — sheba (H7651):** The number seven that structures all else. The week has seven days; the Feast of Weeks comes after seven weeks; the Sabbath is the seventh day; Daniel's prophecy spans seventy sevens.
- **Anchor — shabbath (H7676):** The weekly reset point. The Sabbath is the structural unit that makes counting possible; Lev 23:15 counts from the Sabbath to the next feast.
- **Festival — chag (H2282):** The festive occasion. Chag marks the celebratory character of appointed times; appears most often in "Feast of Weeks," "Feast of Unleavened Bread," and "Feast of Tabernacles."
- **Period — shabuwa (H7620):** The counted interval. Shabuwa names the seven-day unit itself; it is both the ordinary week (Genesis, Leviticus) and the prophetic heptad (Daniel 9).

## Key Passages Where Multiple Members Appear Together

- **Lev 23:15** — "And ye shall count unto you from the morrow after the sabbath (H7676)... seven (H7651) sabbaths shall be complete" — the counting formula that leads to the Feast of Weeks
- **Deut 16:9–10** — "Seven (H7651) weeks (H7620) shalt thou number... thou shalt keep the feast (H2282) of weeks" — all three count-words together
- **2Chr 8:13** — "on the sabbaths (H7676), and on the new moons, and on the solemn feasts (H2282), three times in a year... the feast of weeks (H7620)" — the complete liturgical calendar
- **Dan 9:24–27** — "Seventy weeks (H7620) are determined" — shabuwa extended from 7-day periods to 7-year periods (weeks of years), the most expansive prophetic use
