## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same across testaments:**
- Knowledge as a divine gift (Exo 31:3; Col 2:3)
- Knowledge paired with wisdom as complementary virtues (Pro 2:6; Col 2:3)
- Rejection of knowledge as culpable (Hos 4:6; Rom 1:28)
- Knowledge of God as the highest form (Pro 2:5; 2Co 10:5)

**What shifts:**
- OT da'ath has a strong experiential/perceptual dimension (LXX splits this to aisthesis); NT gnosis leans more intellectual
- NT gnosis acquires a cautionary edge — "knowledge puffeth up" (1Co 8:1), "oppositions of science falsely so called" (1Ti 6:20) — absent from OT da'ath, which is almost universally positive when directed toward God
- NT adds epignosis (full/thorough knowledge) as an intensified form, used especially by Paul for knowledge of God and Christ
