## Lexicon Definition

Not present in Strong's lexicon DB. The word νίκη is the classical Greek feminine noun meaning "victory, conquest." It is the base from which the verb νικάω (nikaō, G3528, "to conquer/overcome") and the neuter noun νῖκος (nikos, G3534, "victory/conquest") are derived. Thayer notes that νῖκος is "a later form equivalent to νίκη." In the NT, the classical form νίκη appears only in 1 John 5:4, while the later Koine form νῖκος is used in Matthew 12:20 and 1 Corinthians 15:54-57.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Victory Through Faith Over the World-System
The single occurrence defines victory (nikē) not as military conquest or personal achievement but as the outcome of being born of God, equated with faith itself.
- **1 John 5:4** — "For whatsoever is born of God overcometh the world: and this is the victory (nikē) that overcometh the world, even our faith."

The verse uses both the noun nikē and the cognate verb nikaō (twice). The construction "this is the victory that overcometh" (αὕτη ἐστὶν ἡ νίκη ἡ νικήσασα) identifies the victory itself as the active agent — faith is not merely the instrument of victory but is the victory. The aorist participle νικήσασα treats the overcoming as an accomplished fact.

Context: 1 John 5:1-5 connects belief in Jesus as Christ (v.1) → love and commandment-keeping (v.2-3) → overcoming the world through faith (v.4-5). The "world" (kosmos, G2889) here refers to the hostile system opposed to God, consistent with John's usage in 1 John 2:15-17.
