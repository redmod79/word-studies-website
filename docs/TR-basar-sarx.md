**Semantic shift in translation:** LXX translators divided bâsâr's range: σάρξ received the personal/relational uses ("all flesh," "one flesh," "flesh of my flesh") while κρέας handled purely food/sacrifice contexts. This means NT σάρξ inherits bâsâr's existential range; κρέας (G2907) handles only butcher's meat in the NT and never carries the ethical weight sarx does.

## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same across testaments:**
- The "all flesh" (kol bâsâr / pasa sarx) idiom for total humanity carries through intact; OT prophetic quotations are rendered directly into NT Greek with no sense shift
- Physical body / literal flesh meaning persists in both testaments
- "One flesh" (Gen 2:24) is directly quoted in NT (Matt 19:5; Mark 10:8; Eph 5:31) with full semantic equivalence

**What shifts:**
- Paul develops sarx as a theological ethical category for the sinful nature opposing the Spirit — this has no direct OT bâsâr parallel; bâsâr can express human weakness and mortality but not the moral "old nature" concept Paul builds in Romans 7–8 and Galatians 5
- The "flesh vs. Spirit" antithesis (Gal 5:17; Rom 8:4–9) is a distinctly NT pneumatological development on an OT terminological foundation
- LXX effects a conceptual split: σάρξ inherits bâsâr's personal/relational range; κρέας absorbs the food/sacrifice range
