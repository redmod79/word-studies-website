## Lexicon Definition

From H8262 שָׁקַץ (shaqats). Filth, i.e. (figuratively and specifically) an idolatrous object — "abominable(-tion)." The noun denotes the detestable thing itself (the creature or object classified as loathsome), as distinct from the abstract moral category (H8441 to'ebah) or the idolatrous cult object (H8251 shiqquwts). Sheqets is the concrete classification label applied to creatures in the dietary/purity laws.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Aquatic Creatures Lacking Fins and Scales
- **Lev 11:10** — "all that have not fins and scales in the seas, and in the rivers... they shall be an abomination (sheqets) unto you"
- **Lev 11:11** — "They shall be even an abomination (sheqets) unto you; ye shall not eat of their flesh, but ye shall have their carcases in abomination (sheqets)"
- **Lev 11:12** — "Whatsoever hath no fins nor scales in the waters, that shall be an abomination (sheqets) unto you"

Three consecutive verses establish the classification criterion for aquatic creatures: fins and scales required. Those lacking them receive the sheqets label.

### 2. Prohibited Birds
- **Lev 11:13** — "these are they which ye shall have in abomination (sheqets) among the fowls; they shall not be eaten, they are an abomination (sheqets): the eagle, and the ossifrage, and the ospray"

Certain bird species are classified as sheqets. No anatomical criterion is stated — the text provides a species list (Lev 11:13-19) introduced by this classification label.

### 3. Winged Swarming Creatures
- **Lev 11:20** — "All fowls that creep, going upon all four, shall be an abomination (sheqets) unto you"
- **Lev 11:23** — "But all other flying creeping things, which have four feet, shall be an abomination (sheqets) unto you"

Winged insects that walk on all fours are classified sheqets. The text carves out an exception (Lev 11:21-22) for those with jointed legs for leaping (locust, beetle, grasshopper) — these are not sheqets.

### 4. Land-Swarming Creatures
- **Lev 11:41** — "every creeping thing that creepeth upon the earth shall be an abomination (sheqets); it shall not be eaten"
- **Lev 11:42** — "Whatsoever goeth upon the belly, and whatsoever goeth upon all four, or whatsoever hath more feet among all creeping things that creep upon the earth, them ye shall not eat; for they are an abomination (sheqets)"

Ground-swarming creatures (belly-crawlers, multi-legged things) receive the sheqets classification. This section pairs sheqets with H8318 sherets ("swarming thing") — the two words co-occur in 5 of 11 verses (45%).

### 5. Contact Defilement (Purity-Law Context)
- **Lev 7:21** — "the soul that shall touch any unclean thing... or any abominable (sheqets) unclean thing, and eat of the flesh of the sacrifice of peace offerings... shall be cut off"

The earliest occurrence uses sheqets as an adjective-like modifier ("abominable unclean thing") within a purity ruling about contact defilement and sacrificial eligibility. A person touching a sheqets creature and then eating the peace offering is "cut off."

### 6. Prophetic Recall of Dietary Classification
- **Isa 66:17** — "eating swine's flesh, and the abomination (sheqets), and the mouse, shall be consumed together, saith the LORD"
- **Eze 8:10** — "every form of creeping things, and abominable (sheqets) beasts, and all the idols of the house of Israel, pourtrayed upon the wall"

Both prophetic uses recall the Levitical creature categories. Isaiah 66:17 places sheqets between swine and mouse — dietary violations at the eschatological judgment. Ezekiel 8:10 describes temple wall images of sheqets beasts alongside idols, merging creature-classification language with idolatry exposure.

## Related Words
