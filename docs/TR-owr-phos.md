## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same across testaments:**
- Light as a divine attribute (Psa 27:1 → 1Jo 1:5)
- Light vs. darkness as moral categories (Isa 5:20 → Jhn 3:19-21)
- Light for the nations/Gentiles (Isa 49:6 → Act 13:47)
- Light as God's first creative act (Gen 1:3 → 2Co 4:6, Jhn 1:4-5)

**What develops:**
- The NT concentrates the "God is light" metaphor christologically — Jesus claims to be the light (Jhn 8:12, 9:5), not merely a reflection of it
- Believers become "children of light" (Jhn 12:36, 1Th 5:5, Eph 5:8) — a social identity marker absent from OT usage of ʼôwr
- The eschatological replacement of sun-light with divine light (Rev 21:24, 22:5) echoes but intensifies Isa 60:19-20
