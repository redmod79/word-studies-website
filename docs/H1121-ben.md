## Semantic Categories

### 1. Biological Son / Literal Offspring
The primary sense — a male child in direct filial relationship to his father.

- **Gen 21:3** — "Abraham called the name of his son that was born unto him, whom Sarah bare to him, Isaac."
- **Prov 17:6** — "Children's children are the crown of old men; and the glory of children are their fathers."
- **Mal 1:6** — "A son honoureth his father, and a servant his master: if then I be a father, where is mine honour?"
- **Mal 3:17** — "I will spare them, as a man spareth his own son that serveth him."

### 2. Patronymic — Genealogical Identifier
"X son of Y" is the standard Hebrew personal-identification formula. The vast majority of
occurrences in narrative books use *ben* this way.

- **Josh 1:1** — "Joshua the son of Nun" (patronymic identification)
- **Zech 1:1** — "Zechariah, the son of Berechiah, the son of Iddo" (genealogical chain)
- **1 Chron 1:5** — "The sons of Japheth; Gomer, and Magog..." (genealogical table)

### 3. Sons/Children of Israel — National and Ethnic Identity
The construct *bene X* forms collective ethnic, tribal, or priestly designations. "Bene
yisrael" is the single most frequent multi-word phrase using *ben* in the OT.

- **Exod 1:7** — "the children of Israel were fruitful, and increased abundantly..."
- **Lev 1:5** — "the priests, Aaron's sons, shall bring the blood..."
- **Deut 14:1** — "Ye are the children of the LORD your God: ye shall not cut yourselves..."
- **Isa 1:2** — "I have nourished and brought up children, and they have rebelled against me."

### 4. Age Expression — "X Years Old"
*Ben* + number + *shanah* ("year") forms the Hebrew idiom for age. Accounts for 122 KJV
"old" renderings. Nearly every narrative age reference uses this construction.

- **Num 1:3** — "From twenty years old (lit. *ben esrim shanah*) and upward, all that are able to go forth to war"
- **Gen 5:32** — "Noah was five hundred years old (*ben-chameish meot shanah*)"
- The Levitical service ages (30 years old, 50 years old) in Numbers all use this idiom.

### 5. Class Membership / Character Designation
*Ben* + a noun denotes membership in a category or a characteristic quality — an idiom for
profession, spiritual type, or nature. Collocation H376 אִישׁ (ish) 324x confirms "sons of men"
pattern.

- **1 Kgs 20:35** — "a certain man of the sons of the prophets" (guild membership)
- **Job 1:6** — "the sons of God came to present themselves before the LORD" (celestial class)
- **Psalm 8:4** — "the son of man (*ben-adam*), that thou visitest him" — a human being
- **Ezek 2:1** — "Son of man, stand upon thy feet" — prophetic address: Ezekiel as human representative before God (90+ uses)

### 6. Young Animal / Animal Offspring
*Ben* applied to animals denotes young or newborn offspring.

- **Lev 1:5** — "young bullock" (*ben-bakar*, lit. "son of the herd")
- **Num 6:12** — "a lamb of the first year" (*ben-shanah*, lit. "son of a year")
- Also: lion whelps (*ben-ari*), donkey colt (*ben-atonah*, Zech 9:9), nestlings

### 7. Divine Sonship — Covenantal and Messianic
God designates Israel, the Davidic king, and the promised Messiah with the language of sonship.
This is the theological apex of *ben*'s semantic range.

- **Exod 4:22** — "Thus saith the LORD, Israel is my son, even my firstborn"
- **2 Sam 7:14** — "I will be his father, and he shall be my son" (Davidic covenant)
- **Psalm 2:7** — "the LORD hath said unto me, Thou art my Son; this day have I begotten thee"
- **Isa 9:6** — "unto us a son is given: and the government shall be upon his shoulder"
