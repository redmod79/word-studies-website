## Semantic Categories

### 1. Priestly/Ritual Purification — Body Washing
The dominant category. Washing the body (or "flesh," basar) in water as part of ritual purification, especially for priests before service and for anyone transitioning from unclean to clean status.
- **Exo 29:4** — "and shalt wash them with water" (Aaron and sons at tabernacle door)
- **Exo 40:12** — "and wash them with water" (priestly consecration)
- **Lev 16:4** — "shall be girded with a linen girdle, and with the linen mitre shall he be attired: these are holy garments; therefore shall he wash his flesh in water" (Day of Atonement preparation)
- **Lev 16:24** — "And he shall wash his flesh with water in the holy place" (after atonement rites)

### 2. Impurity Removal — Contact Defilement
Persons who become ritually unclean through contact must wash their body in water and remain unclean until evening. Appears as a formulaic pattern in Leviticus 15.
- **Lev 15:5** — "shall wash his clothes, and bathe himself in water, and be unclean until the even"
- **Lev 15:11** — "he shall wash his clothes, and bathe himself in water, and be unclean until the even"
- **Lev 15:27** — "shall wash his clothes, and bathe himself in water, and be unclean until the even"
- **Num 19:7** — "the priest shall wash his clothes, and he shall bathe his flesh in water"

### 3. Sacrificial Preparation — Washing Inwards and Legs
Washing the entrails and legs of sacrificial animals before burning on the altar — a ritual cleansing of the offering itself.
- **Exo 29:17** — "wash the inwards of him, and his legs"
- **Lev 1:9** — "his inwards and his legs shall he wash in water"
- **Lev 1:13** — "he shall wash the inwards and the legs with water"
- **Lev 8:21** — "he washed the inwards and the legs in water"

### 4. Hospitality — Foot Washing
Washing feet upon arrival as a gesture of hospitality in patriarchal and later narrative contexts.
- **Gen 18:4** — "wash your feet, and rest yourselves under the tree" (Abraham to the three visitors)
- **Gen 24:32** — "gave them water, and they washed their feet" (Laban's hospitality)
- **Gen 43:24** — "they washed their feet" (Joseph's steward to the brothers)
- **Jdg 19:21** — "they washed their feet, and did eat and drink"

### 5. Personal Hygiene and Self-Preparation
Non-ritual washing for cleanliness, grooming, or preparing oneself for an occasion.
- **Ruth 3:3** — "Wash thyself therefore, and anoint thee, and put thy raiment upon thee"
- **2 Sam 12:20** — "David arose from the earth, and washed, and anointed himself"
- **2 Sam 11:2** — "a woman washing herself" (Bathsheba bathing)
- **Song 5:3** — "I have washed my feet; how shall I defile them?"

### 6. Healing Washing
Washing in water as a means of divine healing or restoration.
- **2 Ki 5:10** — "Go and wash in Jordan seven times, and thy flesh shall come again to thee"
- **2 Ki 5:12** — "may I not wash in them, and be clean?"
- **2 Ki 5:13** — "wash, and be clean"

### 7. Metaphorical/Figurative Washing
Moral or spiritual cleansing, or victory imagery.
- **Isa 1:16** — "Wash you, make you clean; put away the evil of your doings"
- **Isa 4:4** — "the Lord shall have washed away the filth of the daughters of Zion"
- **Psalm 26:6** — "I will wash mine hands in innocency: so will I compass thine altar"
- **Psalm 73:13** — "Verily I have cleansed my heart in vain, and washed my hands in innocency"
- **Psalm 58:10** — "he shall wash his feet in the blood of the wicked" (victory metaphor)
