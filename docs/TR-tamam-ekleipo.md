## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same across testaments:**
- The core sense of "failing/ceasing" transfers directly — resources fail (Gen 47:15 → Luk 16:9), years do not fail (Psa 102:27 → Heb 1:12)
- The word carries an existential weight in both testaments: what is consumed ceases to exist

**What shifts:**
- OT tamam has a rich dual polarity (positive completion + negative consumption + moral integrity) that collapses in the NT to primarily negative "fail/cease" via ekleipo
- The positive "completion" sense of tamam is carried into the NT not by ekleipo but by synteleo (G4931) and teleo (G5055) — the semantic load distributes across multiple Greek verbs
- The moral-integrity sense (Hithpael "show oneself upright") has no NT trace through ekleipo at all
