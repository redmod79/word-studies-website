# WG: Soul / Life

## Overview
The soul/life concept in the NT involves three Greek words for "life" — ψυχή (psychē), ζωή (zōē), and βίος (bios) — each with distinct semantic ranges. Psychē is the self/person/life-force; zōē is the vital principle and eternal life; bios is livelihood and manner of living. Two additional words round out the concept cluster: καρδία (kardia) overlaps with psyche in describing the inner person, and ἀπόλλυμι (apollymi) is the primary verb of destruction/loss paired with psyche.

## Roles in the Concept

- **The Self:** psychē (ψυχή) — the whole living person, the "who" that lives and feels
- **The Principle:** zōē (ζωή) — the quality or force of being alive, especially eternal life
- **The Livelihood:** bios (βίος) — the means and manner of living (possessions, daily life)
- **The Interior:** kardia (καρδία) — the inner seat of thought and will (overlaps with psyche's inner-self sense)
- **The Threat:** apollymi (ἀπόλλυμι) — the verb of losing, destroying, or perishing
- **The Adjective:** psychikos (ψυχικός) — "natural/soulish" as opposed to pneumatikos "spiritual"

## Key Passages Where Multiple Group Words Appear Together
