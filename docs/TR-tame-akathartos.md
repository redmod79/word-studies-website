## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same across testaments:**
- The exact word ἀκάθαρτος carries over from LXX into NT intact — no Greek coinage needed
- The contact-transmission principle remains: "touch not the unclean thing" (2 Cor 6:17, echoing Isa 52:11)
- The binary clean/unclean structure is maintained, with the NT opposing ἀκάθαρτος against καθαρός exactly as the OT opposes ṭâmêʼ against ṭâhôwr

**What shifts:**
- The primary referent shifts: OT applies ṭâmêʼ overwhelmingly to animals, diseased persons, and dead bodies; NT applies ἀκάθαρτος primarily to **spirits** (25 of 29 NT occurrences involve "unclean spirits")
- The Levitical dietary and physical categories fade in NT usage; the moral/spiritual categories (latent in Isa 6:5, Isa 64:6) become dominant
- Acts 10–11 (Peter's vision) explicitly transitions the word's application: Gentiles once called "unclean" (κοινός/ἀκάθαρτος) are declared clean by God — a conscious reversal of the OT boundary
