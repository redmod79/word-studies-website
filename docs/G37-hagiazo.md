## Lexicon Definition

From ἅγιος (G40); to make holy, i.e. (ceremonially) purify or consecrate; (mentally) to venerate.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Reverence for God's Name
- **Matt 6:9** — "Our Father which art in heaven, Hallowed be thy name."
- **Luke 11:2** — "When ye pray, say, Our Father which art in heaven, Hallowed be thy name."

Aorist passive imperative: a petition that God's name be treated as holy, acknowledged as sacred.

### 2. Christ's Sanctifying Work on Behalf of Believers
- **Heb 2:11** — "For both he that sanctifieth and they who are sanctified are all of one"
- **Heb 10:10** — "By the which will we are sanctified through the offering of the body of Jesus Christ once for all"
- **Heb 10:14** — "For by one offering he hath perfected for ever them that are sanctified"
- **Heb 13:12** — "Wherefore Jesus also, that he might sanctify the people with his own blood, suffered without the gate"

The largest cluster. Christ is the active agent; believers are passive recipients. Hebrews uses both perfect passive (completed act) and present passive (ongoing process).

### 3. Sanctification Through Truth and the Word
- **John 17:17** — "Sanctify them through thy truth: thy word is truth."
- **John 17:19** — "And for their sakes I sanctify myself, that they also might be sanctified through the truth."
- **Eph 5:26** — "That he might sanctify and cleanse it with the washing of water by the word"
- **1 Tim 4:5** — "For it is sanctified by the word of God and prayer."

The instrumentality of truth, word, and prayer in the sanctifying process.

### 4. Positional Sanctification / Status of Believers
- **1 Cor 1:2** — "to them that are sanctified in Christ Jesus, called to be saints"
- **1 Cor 6:11** — "but ye are washed, but ye are sanctified, but ye are justified in the name of the Lord Jesus"
- **Acts 20:32** — "the word of his grace, which is able to build you up, and to give you an inheritance among all them which are sanctified"
- **Acts 26:18** — "that they may receive forgiveness of sins, and inheritance among them which are sanctified by faith"
- **Jude 1:1** — "to them that are sanctified by God the Father, and preserved in Jesus Christ, and called"

Perfect passive participle — a completed state describing believers' standing.

### 5. The Father's Sanctifying Authority
- **John 10:36** — "Say ye of him, whom the Father hath sanctified, and sent into the world"
- **1 Thess 5:23** — "And the very God of peace sanctify you wholly"

The Father as the agent who sets apart — both the Son for his mission and believers for holiness.

### 6. Ritual / Relational Consecration
- **Matt 23:17** — "the temple that sanctifieth the gold"
- **Matt 23:19** — "the altar that sanctifieth the gift"
- **1 Cor 7:14** — "the unbelieving husband is sanctified by the wife"
- **Heb 9:13** — "the blood of bulls and of goats... sanctifieth to the purifying of the flesh"

Contact-holiness: the greater consecrates the lesser through association or ritual.

### 7. Human Response / Volitional Sanctification
- **1 Pet 3:15** — "But sanctify the Lord God in your hearts"
- **2 Tim 2:21** — "a vessel unto honour, sanctified, and meet for the master's use"
- **Rev 22:11** — "he that is righteous, let him be righteous still: and he that is holy, let him be holy still"
- **Rom 15:16** — "the offering up of the Gentiles might be acceptable, being sanctified by the Holy Ghost"

Human agency in sanctification — setting God apart in the heart, purging oneself, pursuing holiness.
