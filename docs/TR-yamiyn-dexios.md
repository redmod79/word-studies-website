Psalm 110:1 is the most-quoted OT verse in the NT. The yamiyn → dexios bridge carries this single verse into at least six direct NT quotations and numerous allusions. The "right hand of God" becomes the NT's primary spatial metaphor for Christ's exalted position.

## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same across testaments:**
- The right hand as the position of honor and authority
- The right hand of God as the instrument of saving power
- The right-hand seat as the place of highest favor
- Physical/literal right hand in narrative contexts

**What shifts:**
- The OT uses yamiyn for geographic direction (south) — this sense does not carry into Greek dexios in the NT
- The OT sacrificial/priestly usage (right shoulder, right thumb) does not appear in the NT
- The NT concentrates heavily on the Christological sense: "seated at the right hand of God" dominates NT dexios usage (~20 of 53 occurrences), transforming an OT royal-enthronement image into the primary description of Christ's post-ascension status
