## Semantic Categories

### 1. Legal Sentence Executed by Judicial Authority
Judgment as a verdict decreed and enforced — by royal command, divine justice, or both operating together.

- **Ezra 7:26** — "And whosoever will not do the law of thy God, and the law of the king, let judgment be executed speedily upon him, whether [it be] unto death, or to banishment, or to confiscation of goods, or to imprisonment."
- **Daniel 4:37** — "Now I Nebuchadnezzar praise and extol and honour the King of heaven, all whose works [are] truth, and his ways judgment: and those that walk in pride he is able to abase."

In Ezra 7:26 the judgment is the punitive sentence prescribed by law. In Dan 4:37 it is God's intrinsic character — his every act is perfectly just.

### 2. The Heavenly Court in Session (Daniel 7 Courtroom Scene)
The three occurrences in Daniel 7 form a single eschatological courtroom drama. The word marks the moment the tribunal is convened, the verdict rendered, and the sentence carried out.

- **Daniel 7:10** — "...the judgment was set, and the books were opened."
- **Daniel 7:22** — "Until the Ancient of days came, and judgment was given to the saints of the most High; and the time came that the saints possessed the kingdom."
- **Daniel 7:26** — "But the judgment shall sit, and they shall take away his dominion, to consume and to destroy [it] unto the end."

The court "sitting" (H3488 יְתִב, yethib) and the "books" being opened are the formal marks of a judicial proceeding. Judgment is first *set* (7:10), then *given* to the saints (7:22), then *acts* against the beast's dominion (7:26) — three stages of a single verdict.
