## Lexicon Definition

*(BDB/Strong's):* "A lethargy or (by implication) trance: — deep sleep." Built on the root H7290 רָדַם (primitive root: "to stun, i.e. stupefy with sleep or death"). The noun denotes a divinely induced unconscious state distinct from ordinary sleep (שֵׁנָה / יָשֵׁן) — a stupor in which the subject is unaware of his surroundings yet can be the passive recipient of revelation or divine action.

## Semantic Categories

Derived from ALL VERSE TEXTS. Seven occurrences fall into three clearly distinct usage patterns.

### 1. Divinely Imposed Sleep for Creative/Covenantal Purpose
God himself is the agent; the tardēmāh is the means by which He acts decisively on a passive human subject.
- **Gen 2:21** — "the LORD God caused a deep sleep (tardēmāh) to fall upon Adam" (context: creation of woman from rib)
- **Gen 15:12** — "a deep sleep (tardēmāh) fell upon Abram; and, lo, an horror of great darkness fell upon him" (context: cutting of the Abrahamic covenant)
- **1 Sam 26:12** — "they were all asleep; because a deep sleep (tardēmāh) from the LORD was fallen upon them" (context: YHWH-sent stupor on Saul's entire camp)

### 2. Night-Vision / Revelatory Backdrop
The tardēmāh is the receptive human state during which God communicates via vision or dream. The noun names the condition; the revelation is the content.
- **Job 4:13** — "In thoughts from the visions of the night, when deep sleep (tardēmāh) falleth on men" (Eliphaz's spirit-encounter)
- **Job 33:15** — "In a dream, in a vision of the night, when deep sleep (tardēmāh) falleth upon men, in slumberings upon the bed" (Elihu on how God speaks)

### 3. Judicial / Negative Stupor
A stupefying torpor characterized as loss, loss of agency, or divine withdrawal-of-sight.
- **Prov 19:15** — "Slothfulness casteth into a deep sleep (tardēmāh)" (moral torpor producing ruin)
- **Isa 29:10** — "the LORD hath poured out upon you the spirit of deep sleep (רוּחַ תַּרְדֵּמָה / ruaḥ tardēmāh), and hath closed your eyes" (judicial blinding of prophets and seers)

The same noun can thus name a *receptive* state (categories 1–2) or a *punitive* state (category 3) — what distinguishes them is whether YHWH sends tardēmāh *for* revelation/action *to* the subject or *against* perception *in* the subject.
