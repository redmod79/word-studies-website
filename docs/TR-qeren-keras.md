## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same:**
- The core image: a horn is projecting strength, whether literal (animals, altar corners) or metaphorical (power, dominion)
- The political symbolism: horns = rulers/kingdoms, already established in Daniel and carried unchanged into Revelation
- The altar-horn usage: both OT tabernacle texts and Rev 9:13 use κέρας for the projecting corners

**What shifts:**
- In the OT, "horn of salvation" describes God's past acts of deliverance for Israel; in Luk 1:69 it becomes a forward-looking title for the Messiah
- Revelation concentrates all the figurative uses onto the beast vision, making κέρας primarily an eschatological symbol for NT readers where the OT used it more broadly across personal, liturgical, and prophetic contexts
