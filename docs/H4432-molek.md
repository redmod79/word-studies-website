## Lexicon Definition

From Strong's / BDB: "Molek (i.e. king), the chief deity of the Ammonites." The form is derived from the root מלך ("to reign, be king") but is distinguished from the common noun מֶלֶךְ (H4428) by its vowel pattern. Ammonite patron deity associated with child sacrifice by fire; cognate with Canaanite/Phoenician *Milcom* (H4445) and the West Semitic *mlk*-offering terminology. See also: Gesenius, "the powerful one / the king-god."

**Patterns:** Densest in Leviticus (the Holiness Code, chs. 18 & 20), where Molech-worship is a capital offense prohibited alongside sexual and ritual defilement. Historical narrative records Solomon's compromise (1 Kgs 11:7) and Josiah's reform (2 Kgs 23:10). Prophetic books indict the cult as active in Judah (Jer 32:35) and trace it back into the wilderness (Amos 5:26). No occurrences in Torah narrative, wisdom literature, or Psalms — the term is concentrated in legal prohibition and historical/prophetic judgment contexts.

## Semantic Categories

All nine occurrences refer to the same deity, so semantic range is narrow; categories reflect the **contexts of reference**, not distinct senses.

### 1. Capital Prohibition in the Holiness Code
Molech-worship is legislated as a capital offense of seed-offering, punished by stoning and *kareth* (being cut off).
- **Lev 18:21** — "thou shalt not let any of thy seed pass through [the fire] to Molech, neither shalt thou profane the name of thy God"
- **Lev 20:2** — "Whosoever... that giveth [any] of his seed unto Molech; he shall surely be put to death"
- **Lev 20:3** — "he hath given of his seed unto Molech, to defile my sanctuary, and to profane my holy name"
- **Lev 20:5** — "to commit whoredom with Molech"

### 2. Royal Apostasy — Solomon's High Place
A single historical notice where Molech-worship enters Israelite practice through Solomon's political marriages.
- **1 Kgs 11:7** — "Then did Solomon build an high place for Chemosh, the abomination of Moab... and for Molech, the abomination of the children of Ammon"

### 3. The Topheth / Valley of Hinnom Cult
Jerusalem-local cult site where child-sacrifice by fire is practiced under later monarchy.
- **2 Kgs 23:10** — "he defiled Topheth, which [is] in the valley of the children of Hinnom, that no man might make his son or his daughter to pass through the fire to Molech"
- **Jer 32:35** — "they built the high places of Baal, which [are] in the valley of the son of Hinnom, to cause their sons and their daughters to pass through [the fire] unto Molech; which I commanded them not, neither came it into my mind"

### 4. Wilderness Retrojection / Astral Syncretism
Amos charges that Molech-worship was carried through the wilderness alongside astral/Mesopotamian images.
- **Amos 5:26** — "But ye have borne the tabernacle of your Moloch and Chiun your images, the star of your god" (cited in Acts 7:43)
