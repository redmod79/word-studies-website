## Lexicon Definition

A primitive root; properly, to miss; hence (figuratively and generally) to sin; by inference, to forfeit, lack, expiate, repent, (causatively) lead astray, condemn: — bear the blame, cleanse, commit (sin), by fault, harm he hath done, loss, miss, (make) offend(-er), offer for sin, purge, purify (self), make reconciliation, (cause, make) sin(-ful, -ness), trespass.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. To Sin — Moral Transgression Against God
The dominant usage. The verb denotes violation of God's commands or covenant relationship.
- **Gen 39:9** — "how then can I do this great wickedness, and sin (châṭâʼ) against God?"
- **Exo 20:20** — "that his fear may be before your faces, that ye sin (châṭâʼ) not"
- **1 Ki 8:46** — "If they sin (châṭâʼ) against thee, (for there is no man that sinneth not)"
- **Ecc 7:20** — "there is not a just man upon earth, that doeth good, and sinneth (châṭâʼ) not"

### 2. Confession of Sin — "I/We Have Sinned"
A recurring penitential formula, especially in Qal perfect first-person forms.
- **Exo 9:27** — "Pharaoh said... I have sinned (châṭâʼ) this time: the LORD is righteous"
- **Num 21:7** — "the people came to Moses, and said, We have sinned (châṭâʼ)"
- **2 Sam 12:13** — "David said unto Nathan, I have sinned (châṭâʼ) against the LORD"
- **Dan 9:5** — "We have sinned (châṭâʼ), and have committed iniquity, and have done wickedly"

### 3. To Cause to Sin — Hiphil Causative
Almost exclusively used of Jeroboam and the kings of the northern kingdom.
- **1 Ki 14:16** — "Jeroboam, who did sin, and who made Israel to sin (hechĕṭîʼ)"
- **1 Ki 15:30** — "the sins of Jeroboam... which he made Israel sin (hechĕṭîʼ)"
- **2 Ki 3:3** — "the sins of Jeroboam... which made Israel to sin (hechĕṭîʼ)"
- **2 Ki 21:11** — "Manasseh... hath made Judah also to sin (hechĕṭîʼ) with his idols"

### 4. To Purify / Cleanse — Piel Intensive
The Piel stem reverses the Qal meaning: instead of "to sin," it means "to un-sin," i.e., to remove sin, purify, or perform the purification ritual. This is the key semantic bridge to H2403 chattaʼah as "purification offering."
- **Exo 29:36** — "thou shalt cleanse (chiṭṭêʼtâ, Piel) the altar, when thou hast made an atonement for it"
- **Lev 8:15** — "Moses took the blood... and purified (yᵉchaṭṭêʼ, Piel) the altar"
- **Psa 51:7** — "Purge me (tᵉchaṭṭᵉʼênî, Piel) with hyssop, and I shall be clean"
- **Eze 43:20** — "thus shalt thou cleanse (chiṭṭêʼtâ, Piel) and purge it"

### 5. To Purify Oneself — Hithpael Reflexive
Used exclusively in Numbers for ritual purification from corpse contamination.
- **Num 19:12** — "He shall purify himself (yitchaṭṭâʼ, Hithpael) with it on the third day"
- **Num 19:20** — "the man that shall be unclean, and shall not purify himself (yitchaṭṭâʼ)"
- **Num 31:19** — "purify (titchaṭṭᵉʼû, Hithpael) both yourselves and your captives"

### 6. To Miss the Mark — Literal / Physical Sense
The root meaning preserved in a single occurrence.
- **Judg 20:16** — "every one could sling stones at an hair breadth, and not miss (yachăṭîʼ, Hiphil)"

### 7. To Bear Loss / Forfeit
A secondary Qal sense: to incur penalty or loss through failure.
- **Gen 31:39** — "I bare the loss of it (ʼăchaṭṭennâh); of my hand didst thou require it"
- **Gen 43:9** — "if I bring him not unto thee... then I shall bear the blame (châṭâʼtî)"
