## Lexicon Definition

An encampment (of travellers or troops); hence, an army, whether literal (of soldiers) or figurative (of dancers, angels, cattle, locusts, stars; or even the sacred courts): — army, band, battle, camp, company, drove, host, tents.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Israel's Sacred Encampment (Wilderness Camp)
The dominant usage — the organized camp of Israel in the wilderness, with the tabernacle at its center and YHWH dwelling in its midst.
- **Num 2:17** — "Then the tabernacle of the congregation shall set forward with the camp of the Levites in the midst of the camp"
- **Num 5:3** — "without the camp shall ye put them; that they defile not their camps, in the midst whereof I dwell"
- **Deut 23:14** — "For the LORD thy God walketh in the midst of thy camp, to deliver thee"
- **Exo 19:16–17** — "the people that was in the camp trembled... Moses brought forth the people out of the camp to meet with God"

### 2. Ritual Removal — "Without the Camp"
A technical ritual formula: unclean persons and sacrificial remains are sent or carried "outside the camp" to preserve the camp's holiness.
- **Lev 4:12** — "Even the whole bullock shall he carry forth without the camp unto a clean place"
- **Lev 4:21** — "he shall carry forth the bullock without the camp, and burn him"
- **Lev 13:46** — "he shall dwell alone; without the camp shall his habitation be"
- **Lev 16:27** — "the bullock for the sin offering... shall one carry forth without the camp; and they shall burn in the fire"
- **Num 5:2** — "Command the children of Israel, that they put out of the camp every leper"
- **Num 19:3** — "he may bring her forth without the camp, and one shall slay her"
- **Num 19:9** — "lay them up without the camp in a clean place"

### 3. Military Host / Army in the Field
The camp of an army deployed for battle, whether Israelite or enemy.
- **Jdg 7:15** — "he worshipped, and returned into the host of Israel, and said, Arise; for the LORD hath delivered into your hand the host of Midian"
- **1 Sam 17:4** — "there went out a champion out of the camp of the Philistines, named Goliath"
- **2 Ki 7:5–7** — "when they were come to the uttermost part of the camp of the Syrians... the Lord had made the host of the Syrians to hear a noise"
- **2 Ki 19:35** — "the angel of the LORD went out, and smote in the camp of the Assyrians"

### 4. Patriarchal / Civilian Traveling Company
A group of people or animals traveling together, before the term took on its dominant military/cultic meaning.
- **Gen 32:7–8** — "he divided the people... and the flocks... into two bands [machanoth]"
- **Gen 32:10** — "I am not worthy of the least of all the mercies... for with my staff I passed over this Jordan; and now I am become two bands"
- **Gen 33:8** — "What meanest thou by all this drove (machaneh) which I met?"
- **Gen 50:9** — "it was a very great company"

### 5. Divine / Angelic Host
The camp or army of God, angelic beings encountered by Jacob.
- **Gen 32:2** — "And when Jacob saw them, he said, This is God's host (machaneh): and he called the name of that place Mahanaim"
- **Joel 2:11** — "the LORD shall utter his voice before his army: for his camp is very great"
- **Psa 27:3** — "Though an host should encamp against me, my heart shall not fear"

### 6. Figurative / Poetic Uses
- **Song 6:13** — "What will ye see in the Shulamite? As it were the company of two armies" (dance imagery)
- **Ezek 1:24** — "the noise of their wings, like the noise of great waters, as the voice of the Almighty... as the noise of an host"
