## Lexicon Definition

A primitive root; to give, used with greatest latitude of application — put, make, add, apply, appoint, ascribe, assign, avenge, bestow, bring forth, cast, cause, charge, commit, consider, count, cry, deliver (up), direct, distribute, do, fasten, frame, get, give (forth, over, up), grant, hang up, have, lay, lend, let (out), lift up, make, occupy, offer, ordain, pay, perform, place, pour, pull, put, recompense, render, requite, restore, send (out), set (forth), shew, shoot forth, sing, strike, submit, suffer, surely, take, thrust, trade, turn, utter, weep, willingly, withdraw, yield. The verb is fundamentally relational — a transfer from an agent to a recipient — but the range of transferred objects (physical things, persons, places, commands, speech, states) is vast.

## Semantic Categories

Seven recurring usage patterns emerge from the verse data. Most uses combine more than one; categories are functional, not exclusive.

### 1. Material transfer — giving an object or possession
- **Gen 3:6** — "she gave (tittēn) also unto her husband with her"
- **Gen 14:20** — "and he gave (yitten) him tithes of all"
- **Gen 24:53** — "gave also to her brother and to her mother precious things"
- **Esth 8:1** — "the king give (natan) the house of Haman"

### 2. Land-grant / covenant inheritance — YHWH giving the land
- **Gen 12:7** — "Unto thy seed will I give (etten) this land"
- **Gen 15:18** — "Unto thy seed have I given (natatti) this land"
- **Deut 1:8** — "the land which the LORD sware... to give (latet) unto them"
- **Josh 1:3** — "Every place that the sole of your foot shall tread upon, that have I given (netattiw) unto you"

This is the signature use in Deuteronomy and Joshua — natan is the formulaic verb of YHWH's patriarchal promise.

### 3. Delivering enemies / persons into another's hand (Niphal-heavy)
- **Jdg 1:2** — "the LORD said, Judah shall go up: behold, I have delivered (natatti) the land into his hand"
- **Jdg 1:4** — "the LORD delivered (wayyitten) the Canaanites and the Perizzites into their hand"
- **Dan 1:2** — "the Lord gave (wayyitten) Jehoiakim king of Judah into his hand"
- **Jer 20:4** — "I will give (etten) all Judah into the hand of the king of Babylon"

Typical pattern: natan + `beyad` ("into the hand of"). Can be favorable (victory for Israel) or punitive (Israel given over to exile).

### 4. Dedication / vow — giving persons or things over to YHWH
- **1 Sam 1:11** — "then I will give him (unetattiw) unto the LORD all the days of his life" — Hannah's vow formula, dedicating Samuel for lifelong cultic service; note the duration clause `kol yemei chayyav`
- **Num 3:9** — "And thou shalt give (wenatattah) the Levites unto Aaron and to his sons: they are wholly given (netunim) unto him"
- **Num 8:16** — "For they are wholly given (netunim netunim) unto me... instead of... the firstborn"
- **Num 8:19** — "And I have given (wa'ettenah) the Levites as a gift (netunim) to Aaron"
- **Num 18:6** — "behold, I have taken your brethren the Levites... a gift (matanah) for the LORD, to do the service of the tabernacle"

The Levite-transfer passages repeat natan in a piled, figura-etymologica construction (`netunim netunim`, "given, given") to mark irrevocable dedication. The vow pattern in 1 Sam 1:11 uses exactly this language (`unetattiw laYHWH`) — Hannah is not offering a sacrifice, she is transferring custody of a person to the sanctuary for lifelong service. The construction parallels the Levitical model: a living human `natan`-ed to YHWH is assigned to service, not slaughtered.

Note: Exo 13:12's firstborn-dedication formula does NOT use natan — it uses H5674 (`wehaʻavartaʼ`, "thou shalt set apart / cause to pass over"). The firstborn-given motif surfaces with natan only indirectly, e.g. when the Levites are described as substituting for the firstborn (Num 3:12; 8:16).

### 5. Placement / installation — setting, putting, appointing
- **Gen 1:17** — "God set (wayyitten) them in the firmament"
- **Gen 9:13** — "I do set (natatti) my bow in the cloud"
- **Gen 17:5** — "for a father of many nations have I made thee" (lit. "given thee")
- **Deut 1:15** — "made (wa'etten) them heads over you" (= appointed)
- **Jer 1:5** — "I ordained (netattika) thee a prophet unto the nations"

Often overlapping with "make" (H7760 sum / H6213 ʻasah). When natan is used, the nuance is installation-by-transfer: the agent grants a position to the recipient.

### 6. Speech / voice — giving/uttering a sound
- **Psa 46:6** — "he uttered (natan) his voice, the earth melted"
- **Jer 4:16** — "give out (yittenu) their voice against the cities of Judah"
- **Joel 2:11** — "the LORD shall utter (natan) his voice before his army"
- **Prov 1:20** — "Wisdom... uttereth (titten) her voice in the streets"

Collocation: natan + qol ("voice"). This is the standard Hebrew idiom for vocalizing (thundering, shouting, lamenting).

### 7. Legal / commanded obligation — giving commandments, laws, decrees
- **Exod 31:18** — "he gave (wayyitten) unto Moses... two tables of testimony"
- **Deut 4:8** — "this law, which I set (noten) before you this day"
- **Ezek 11:19** — "I will give (wenatatti) them one heart"
- **Jer 31:33** — "I will put (etten) my law in their inward parts"

The covenant stipulation formula: YHWH `natan`-s his statutes to Israel. The interiorizing new-covenant promise also uses natan (heart, law written within).

| Translation | Competing Strong's | Key Difference |

| "give" / "gave" | H3051 yahab, H7760 sum, H3052 yehab (Aramaic) | natan = default general giver; yahab = near-synonym with imperative bias; sum = place/put; yehab = Aramaic equivalent |
| "put" / "set" | H7760 sum, H7896 shith, H3240 yanach | sum/shith = positional placement; natan only when "put" involves transfer or assignment |
| "deliver" | H5337 natsal (hiphil "rescue"), H4560 masar ("hand over") | natsal = rescue FROM; natan + beyad = hand over TO; masar is more formal/rare |
| "offer" (a sacrifice) | H7126 qarab (hiphil), H5927 ʻalah (hiphil) | qarab/ʻalah are the cultic offering verbs; natan is transfer of custody, not altar-presentation. See the Jephthah lexical contrast below. |
| "make (into)" | H6213 ʻasah, H7760 sum | natan = install/appoint by giving; ʻasah = fashion/produce; sum = set up |

**Lexical contrast with sacrificial presentation:** When a person or animal is `natan`-ed to YHWH, the verb marks transfer of custody — typically for ongoing service (Levites, Samuel). When a sacrifice is made, Hebrew reaches for `heʻalah` (H5927 hiphil, "cause to ascend, offer up as burnt offering") or `hiqrib` (H7126 hiphil, "bring near, present"). Jdg 11:31 (Jephthah's vow) uses `wehayah laYHWH` ("shall be to YHWH") + `wehaʻalitihu ʻolah` ("and I will offer it up a burnt offering") — it does NOT use natan. The switch from the Hannah/Levite natan-dedication pattern to the ʻolah-sacrificial pattern is the pivotal lexical datum whenever Jephthah's vow is analyzed.
