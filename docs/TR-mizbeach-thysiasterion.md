**Semantic shift in translation:** Minimal. Both words derive from their respective "sacrifice" roots (z-b-ch / thy-) and both mean "a place of sacrifice." The LXX translators reserved θυσιαστήριον exclusively for the altar of the true God (and legitimate worship contexts) while using βωμός for pagan altars — a distinction not present in the Hebrew, where mizbeach covers both.

## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same across testaments:**
- The altar remains the designated location where sacrifice meets God
- Blood, fire, and priestly mediation remain associated
- The altar sanctifies what is placed upon it (Matt 23:19 echoes Exo 29:37)

**What shifts:**
- The NT introduces a heavenly altar (Rev 6:9, 8:3, 9:13) not paralleled in OT usage of mizbeach
- Heb 13:10 speaks of "an altar" that Christians possess, applying the concept beyond the physical structure
- The NT altar of incense gains prominence in Revelation's heavenly scenes
