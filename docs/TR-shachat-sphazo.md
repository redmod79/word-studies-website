## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same:**
- The sacrificial-slaughter register: σφάζω carries shachat's cultic meaning directly — the Passover lamb and burnt offering vocabulary becomes the NT's primary language for Christ's death
- The violence register: both shachat and sphazō cover massacre/murder alongside sacrifice; 1 Ki 18:40 parallels 1 John 3:12 structurally
- The blood connection: both words co-occur with blood (H1818 / G129) in their respective corpora — slaughter and blood are inseparable in both testaments

**What shifts:**
- In the OT, shachat describes physical acts on animals (ritual) and enemies (war/massacre); in the NT, σφάζω shifts almost entirely to Christ (the Lamb) and martyrs — the sacrificial register is absorbed into Christology
- The Perfect Passive Participle (ἐσφαγμένον) dominates in Revelation (6 of 8 Revelation uses) — "the Lamb in the state of having been slaughtered"; the OT expressed the act, the NT expresses the permanent condition
- The beast's mortal wound (Rev 13:3 — ἐσφαγμένην εἰς θάνατον, "wounded unto death") inverts the Lamb imagery: a counterfeit slaughter that mimics but cannot redeem
