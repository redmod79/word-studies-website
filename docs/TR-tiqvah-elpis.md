**Semantic shift in translation:** The LXX gathers several distinct Hebrew concepts under elpis: not only tiqvah (expectation/cord) but also machaseh (shelter), mibtach (security), and betach (safety/trust). The Greek noun elpis thus absorbs a broader semantic field than any single Hebrew source. The literal "cord" sense of tiqvah (Jos 2:18, "line of scarlet thread") does not transfer — elpis carries only the metaphorical "expectation" meaning into the NT.

## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same across testaments:**
- The core meaning of confident expectation directed toward God's promises remains stable from tiqvah through elpis. Both Jeremiah 29:11 (tiqvah) and Titus 1:2 (elpis) ground hope in God's declared intention.
- Both OT and NT use hope in contrast with the hopeless condition of the wicked or those outside covenant (Pro 10:28 / Eph 2:12).

**What shifts:**
- The NT narrows the object of hope: while OT tiqvah can attach to general outcomes (Jos 2:18 scarlet cord, Ezek 19:5 dynastic expectation), NT elpis overwhelmingly points to eschatological fulfillment — resurrection, eternal life, Christ's return.
- The LXX broadened elpis to absorb Hebrew trust/refuge vocabulary (machaseh, mibtach, betach). The NT inherits this broadened range, which is why elpis can mean both "expectation of future events" and "present confidence/anchor" (Heb 6:18-19).
