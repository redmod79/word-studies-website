# Grammar Analysis: Daniel 8:26

**Context-neutral passage analysis.** This entry describes the Hebrew morphology and clause architecture of Daniel 8:26 — Gabriel's authentication-and-sealing formula concluding the 8:1–25 vision cycle. It documents what the grammar constrains and what it leaves open. Interpretive schools (Antiochus / Rome / papal / futurist) are catalogued, not adjudicated.

Two grammatical features drive the exegesis:

1. **מַרְאֵה הָעֶרֶב וְהַבֹּקֶר** — "the vision of the evening and the morning": the evening-morning pair (already encountered asyndetically and numeric in 8:14 עֶרֶב בֹּקֶר) is re-cited here *with both articles and the copulative waw*. The construction functions as **a single titular unit** — the oracle is named after the 2,300 evening-mornings it announced.
2. **סְתֹם הֶחָזוֹן כִּי לְיָמִים רַבִּים** — "seal-up the vision, for [it is] unto many days": Qal imperative of סתם + causal כִּי + prepositional phrase of duration (**no verb** in the כִּי-clause — verbless temporal predication). The imperative addressed to Daniel authorizes deferral of disclosure; the lexical choice of **יָמִים רַבִּים** (rather than יָמִים רַחוֹקִים "far-off days," עֵת קֵץ "time of end," or אַחֲרִית הַיָּמִים "latter days") introduces a distinctive temporal register that re-surfaces in Dan 10:14 and frames the sealing/unsealing theme completed in Dan 12:4, 9.

## Clause Structure (ETCBC-style)

| # | Type | Domain | Function | Text |
|---|------|--------|----------|------|
| 1 | **NmCl** (verbless / nominal) | Q (quotation — Gabriel speaking) | Topic-comment authentication | וּמַרְאֵה הָעֶרֶב וְהַבֹּקֶר … אֱמֶת הוּא — "And the vision of the evening and the morning … truth [is] it" |
| 2 | AjCl (asyndetic relative) | Q | Attributive relative modifying מַרְאֵה | אֲשֶׁר נֶאֱמַר — "which has been told" |
| 3 | **Impv** | Q | Direct command to Daniel | וְאַתָּה סְתֹם הֶחָזוֹן — "and (as for) you — seal-up the vision" |
| 4 | **NmCl** (verbless, prep-predicate) | Q | Causal ground for clause 3 | כִּי לְיָמִים רַבִּים — "for [it is] for many days" |

**Architectural summary.** Verse 26 is built of two verbless clauses bracketing a single imperative. The two NmCls perform two distinct verbless functions:

- Clause 1 is a **predicate-noun nominal clause** with the independent pronoun הוּא functioning as copula: "[the] vision … truth [is] it." The word order places the subject (the long appositional noun phrase) first and the predicate (אֱמֶת) with copular pronoun at the end — a known BH pattern for emphatic identification (Joüon §154k; GKC §141f-h).
- Clause 4 is a **prepositional-predicate nominal clause**: the predicate is not a noun but the prepositional phrase לְיָמִים רַבִּים with tacit subject (recoverable from חָזוֹן in clause 3). English supplies "it is/shall be"; Hebrew merely juxtaposes the preposition + noun phrase after the causal כִּי. The construction parallels Dan 10:14 כִּי־עוֹד חָזוֹן לַיָּמִים ("for yet the vision [is] for the days").

See [syntax-waw-conjunction](../hebrew/syntax-waw-conjunction.md) on the topic-shifting waw in וְאַתָּה (clause 3) and [stem-niphal](../hebrew/stem-niphal.md) for the passive נֶאֱמַר (clause 2).

## Construct Chain

| Construct | Absolute | Gloss |
|-----------|----------|-------|
| מַרְאֵה | הָעֶרֶב וְהַבֹּקֶר | "sight/vision of the evening and the morning" — construct + coordinated double-absolute, both absolutes definite (articled) |

**The double-absolute chain.** Hebrew construct chains normally take a single absolute. When two coordinated nouns function as the genitive, both may carry the article if they are definite, and the construct noun does NOT take the article (the construct noun inherits definiteness from the absolute). This is the classical rule: a construct noun never takes the definite article itself; its definiteness is determined by the absolute. Here מַרְאֵה is implicitly definite because both absolutes are articled (הָעֶרֶב, הַבֹּקֶר). Translations render "*the* vision of *the* evening and *the* morning."

The articles on עֶרֶב and בֹּקֶר in v. 26 contrast pointedly with the **articleless** (and **asyndetic**) pair in 8:14 עֶרֶב בֹּקֶר. The 8:14 pair was numeric ("2,300 evening-mornings" as a time-count); the 8:26 pair is **anaphoric** — it refers back to *that* vision Daniel has just seen, with the articles flagging the already-introduced referent.

## Key Grammatical Features

### 1. מַרְאֵה הָעֶרֶב וְהַבֹּקֶר — The evening-morning pair as a single anaphoric unit

**Morphology.**
- מַרְאֵה — construct form of the noun מַרְאֶה (lemma מראה, H4758) "sight, appearance, vision." The construct form drops the final seghol and repoints the vowel.
- הָעֶרֶב — article + Noun.ms.Abs (lemma ערב, H6153) "evening, sunset."
- וְהַבֹּקֶר — waw + article + Noun.ms.Abs (lemma בקר, H1242) "morning, daybreak."

**Why "single unit" rather than "two visions."** Three grammatical observations converge to mark the evening-morning pair as one referential unit, not two separate visions:

1. **Single construct head.** מַרְאֵה is one noun in the construct state, not two. If Gabriel intended "the vision of the evening and [another] vision of the morning," we would expect either (a) a repeated construct (מַרְאֵה הָעֶרֶב וּמַרְאֵה הַבֹּקֶר, "the vision of the evening and the vision of the morning") or (b) a coordinated construct chain with dual heads. Instead the construction is one head governing two coordinated absolutes — a single complex absolute.
2. **Anaphoric articles.** הָעֶרֶב and הַבֹּקֶר each carry the definite article, pointing back to a specific, already-introduced referent — the 2,300 evening-morning count of 8:14 — not to a generic morning and a generic evening.
3. **Singular concord on the predicate.** The predicate clause אֱמֶת הוּא uses the **singular** pronoun הוּא (3ms) matching מַרְאֵה (3ms), not a plural matching the two absolutes. If "evening" and "morning" were independent referents, a plural predicate pronoun (הֵם) or a plural copula would be expected. The singular copula agrees with the single governing noun מַרְאֵה.

These three features — one construct head, anaphoric articles, singular predicate concord — together mark the evening-morning pair as a single compound referent, consistent with the way the reading of 8:14 as "2,300 evening-mornings" (one 24-hour liturgical day unit, analogous to Gen 1:5's וַיְהִי־עֶרֶב וַיְהִי־בֹקֶר "there was evening and there was morning, [one] day") treats the pair as unitary.

**Contrast with 8:14.** See the parsing table in [dan-8-14](dan-8-14.md). In 8:14 the nouns are:
- **asyndetic** (no waw joining them),
- **articleless** (both in absolute state without ה),
- governed by עַד (preposition of extent).

In 8:26 the nouns are:
- **syndetic** (waw וְ between them),
- **articled** (both with ה),
- governed by מַרְאֵה (construct-head noun).

The 8:14 pair is the *numeric* evening-morning unit being counted (2,300 of them). The 8:26 pair is the *name* of the vision in retrospect — "the vision-of-the-evening-and-the-morning," a substantivized backward reference to what Daniel saw in 8:1–14. The shift from asyndetic indefinite to syndetic definite is precisely the expected grammatical move from "2,300 time-units" (numeric) to "that oracle you just heard" (anaphoric title).

**Lexical distinction — מַרְאֶה (H4758) vs. חָזוֹן (H2377).** Verse 26 uses **both** nouns, and their distribution is exegetically significant:
- Clause 1 (authentication): the **מַרְאֵה** of the evening and the morning — using מַרְאֶה to label the specific component seen visually (the 2,300-days oracle).
- Clause 3 (sealing): seal-up **הֶחָזוֹן** — using חָזוֹן to label the whole vision-prophecy complex.

Daniel uses both words throughout chs. 8–9 with discernible (if not rigid) distribution: חָזוֹן tends to label the overall vision-prophecy (8:1, 2, 13, 15, 17, 26b; 9:21, 24), while מַרְאֶה tends to label specific visible tableaux or elements within it (8:16, 26a, 27; 10:1, 7, 8, 16, 18). The switch **within the same verse** (מַרְאֵה … חָזוֹן) is therefore deliberate: Gabriel authenticates *the specific evening-morning oracle* (מַרְאֵה) and commands Daniel to seal *the entire vision-prophecy* (חָזוֹן). This two-term distribution bears on the Daniel 9:23–24 re-opening: Gabriel there tells Daniel בִּין בַּדָּבָר וְהָבֵן בַּמַּרְאֶה ("understand the word and discern the מַרְאֶה") — narrowing attention back to the specific visible element that was labeled מַרְאֶה in 8:26 (and that remained uninterpreted — see 8:27 "but none understood [it]").

For the full semantic mapping see [H4758-mareh](../../word-studies/H4758-mareh.md), [TR-mareh-opsis](../../word-studies/TR-mareh-opsis.md), [H2377-chazon](../../word-studies/H2377-chazon.md), and [TR-chazon-horasis](../../word-studies/TR-chazon-horasis.md).

### 2. אֲשֶׁר נֶאֱמַר אֱמֶת הוּא — "Which has been told [is] truth [itself]"

**Morphology.**
- אֲשֶׁר — standard BH relative particle (lemma אשׁר).
- נֶאֱמַר — **Niphal perfect 3ms** (`Verb.Niphal.Perf.3ms`) of אמר "say, speak." The Niphal of אמר carries passive sense ("be said, be told, be spoken"), with the agent left unspecified. See [stem-niphal](../hebrew/stem-niphal.md) for the Niphal's passive function.
- אֱמֶת — Noun.fs.Abs (lemma אמת, H571) "truth, firmness, faithfulness, reliability" — from the root אמן ("be firm, faithful") via metathesis/assimilation.
- הוּא — independent 3ms pronoun functioning here as the copula ("it [is]"). In predicate-noun nominal clauses the pronoun closes the clause and signals identification.

**The Niphal as divine passive.** נֶאֱמַר is agentless: the verse does not state *who* told it. Contextually this is Gabriel speaking, but the very form (Niphal passive) generalizes the statement — the oracle has been told *by the prophetic chain itself*, authoritatively and ex cathedra. This is the agent-less or "divine passive" pattern: the grammar suppresses the agent to emphasize the authoritative character of the utterance (parallel function in Greek ἐδόθη — see [divine-passive](../greek/divine-passive.md) and [aorist-passive-indicative](../greek/aorist-passive-indicative.md)). Compare the Niphal בִּרְאוֹתֹו ("when he saw") mode — passive participles in Daniel's vision accounts frequently hide the human/angelic agent behind the verbal form.

**The predicate אֱמֶת הוּא.** The noun אֱמֶת is used predicatively — "*truth* [is] it." In Hebrew this is stronger than adjectival אֲמִתִּי ("true"). To predicate a noun of something is to identify it *with* the quality as a substance, not merely characterize it *by* the quality. Parallels:
- Ps 119:142 צִדְקָתְךָ צֶדֶק לְעוֹלָם וְתוֹרָתְךָ אֱמֶת ("Your righteousness [is] righteousness for ever, and Your Torah [is] truth").
- Ps 119:160 רֹאשׁ־דְּבָרְךָ אֱמֶת ("the head/essence of Your word [is] truth").
- Dan 10:1 וֶאֱמֶת הַדָּבָר וְצָבָא גָדוֹל ("and [the] word [was] truth — and a great warfare").
- Dan 11:2 וְעַתָּה אֱמֶת אַגִּיד לָךְ ("and now I will tell you [the] truth" — accusative of manner).

The Danielic pattern uses אֱמֶת as a technical authentication-marker for prophetic oracles. Its placement here closes the vision-block of 8:1–25 with a formula of eschatological reliability: the oracle Daniel has seen is not obscure speculation — it *is* truth.

**Force of the construction.** The three-part predicate — relative clause + predicate-noun + copular pronoun — forms a tightly structured authentication formula:

> [subject: the evening-morning vision] + [relative: which has been told] + [predicate: truth] + [copula: it (is)]

The pronoun הוּא does double duty: (a) grammatical copula closing the clause, (b) independent reaffirmation resumptive of the lengthy subject phrase. The effect is emphatic: the reliability of the oracle is pressed in proportion to the unusual length of the interval to its fulfillment (see §4 below).

### 3. וְאַתָּה סְתֹם הֶחָזוֹן — "And you, seal-up the vision"

**Morphology.**
- וְאַתָּה — waw + independent 2ms pronoun. The pronoun is **not required** grammatically; imperatives carry their own subject-morphology (סְתֹם is itself 2ms). The explicit אַתָּה is therefore a **pragmatic marker** — "now, *as for you*" or "*you*, specifically" — a topic-shift/focus construction (Joüon §146a-b; Waltke-O'Connor §16.3.2e). The pronoun shifts attention from the authentication-clause (about the vision) to the command-clause (directed at Daniel).
- סְתֹם — **Qal imperative 2ms** (`Verb.Qal.Impv.2ms`) of סתם (H5640) "shut up, stop up, close, keep secret, seal." The imperative is unexpanded (no lengthened form), carrying direct-command force addressed personally.
- הֶחָזוֹן — Art + Noun.ms.Abs (lemma חזון, H2377) "vision." The article is anaphoric ("*the* vision [just seen]").

**Semantic range of סתם.** The verb is rarer than its near-synonym חתם ("seal with a seal," H2856). BDB distinguishes:
- סתם (H5640) — "stop up, shut up, keep close, keep secret" — with the connotation of concealment or physical closure (wells stopped up, words kept secret, a disease that stops up).
- חתם (H2856) — "affix a seal, seal a document" — with the connotation of authentication via an imprinted mark.

In Dan 12:4 the two verbs appear in collocation: סְתֹם הַדְּבָרִים וַחֲתֹם הַסֵּפֶר — "shut up the words and seal the book." Here in 8:26 only סתם is used. In Dan 12:9 סְתֻמִים וַחֲתֻמִים הַדְּבָרִים uses the two verbs as coordinated Qal passive participles describing the state of the words. Thus the three "sealing" occurrences (Dan 8:26; 12:4; 12:9) progress:

- **8:26** סְתֹם (Qal Impv.) — command to shut up the vision; only H5640.
- **12:4** סְתֹם … חֲתֹם (paired Qal Impvs.) — command expanded to include authenticating-seal + physical concealment; both verbs.
- **12:9** סְתֻמִים וַחֲתֻמִים (paired Qal pass. ptcps.) — the state reported back, after Daniel asks when; both verbs.

The asymmetry matters. Dan 8:26 commands *concealment* (סתם alone) but not *sealing-with-authentication* (חתם). This fits the exegetical picture: the 2,300-day oracle of Dan 8:14 is *concealed* (not yet interpretable) until later — and in fact Dan 9:21–27, which is explicitly Gabriel returning to pick up where he left off (see 9:21 הָאִישׁ גַּבְרִיאֵל אֲשֶׁר רָאִיתִי בֶחָזוֹן בַּתְּחִלָּה "the man Gabriel, whom I had seen in the vision at the beginning"), supplies the key that unseals at least the temporal portion. The full eschatological unsealing is reserved for Dan 12:4, 9 and — in the NT inclusio — Rev 5:1–9 / 22:10 (the book that *only* the Lamb can open; the book that at the end *is not to be sealed* ἐσφραγισμένον).

For the full verbal field and collocations see [H2856-chatham](../../word-studies/H2856-chatham.md).

**Why Daniel is the addressee.** The imperative is singular, addressed to Daniel personally (וְאַתָּה, 2ms). Daniel is not only recipient of the oracle but appointed custodian of its concealment. This *prophetic-guardian* function appears also in Isa 8:16 צוֹר תְּעוּדָה חֲתוֹם תּוֹרָה בְּלִמֻּדָי ("bind up the testimony, seal the torah among my disciples"), and in Rev 10:4 σφράγισον ἃ ἐλάλησαν αἱ ἑπτὰ βρονταί, καὶ μὴ αὐτὰ γράψῃς ("seal up what the seven thunders said, and do not write them"). The grammar establishes a pattern: prophetic material is sealed *by the human mediator at divine/angelic command*, not self-sealed or auto-withheld.

### 4. כִּי לְיָמִים רַבִּים — "For [it is] for many days"

**Morphology.**
- כִּי — subordinating conjunction; causal ("because, for") or explicative ("that, namely"). Here causal, giving the ground for the imperative: "seal it, *because* it [is] for many days."
- לְ — preposition "to, for, belonging to, reaching-to." With a temporal noun it marks duration or terminus: "for a [specified] time," "unto," "until."
- יָמִים — Noun.mp.Abs (lemma יום, H3117) "days." See word study [H3117-yowm](../../word-studies/H3117-yowm.md).
- רַבִּים — Adj.mp.Abs (lemma רב, H7227) "many, great, much, numerous." Here masculine plural agreeing with יָמִים.

**The verbless causal clause.** The כִּי-clause has **no verb**. Hebrew supplies the subject (it = the vision) and copula (is/shall be) from context. This is the standard **prepositional-predicate nominal clause** (Joüon §154h; GKC §141). The structure כִּי + לְ + noun is BH idiom for "because [it is] for [that duration]":
- Jer 29:10 כִּי־כֹה אָמַר יְהוָה כִּי לְפִי מְלֹאת לְבָבֶל שִׁבְעִים שָׁנָה — "for thus says YHWH: when the seventy years of Babylon are completed …" (also prep + temporal noun).
- Dan 10:14 וּבָאתִי לַהֲבִינְךָ אֵת אֲשֶׁר־יִקְרָה לְעַמְּךָ בְּאַחֲרִית הַיָּמִים כִּי־עוֹד חָזוֹן לַיָּמִים — "I have come to make you understand what shall befall your people in the latter days; for [there is] yet a vision for the days" (same causal-כִּי + לְ + יָמִים, same deferral-to-future function).
- Hab 2:3 כִּי עוֹד חָזוֹן לַמּוֹעֵד וְיָפֵחַ לַקֵּץ — "for yet the vision [is] for the appointed time, and it pants toward the end."

The three parallels (Jer 29, Dan 10, Hab 2) establish that כִּי + prepositional temporal predicate is a fixed causal idiom for "because the matter is set for such-and-such a time-horizon." The Hebrew does not need to supply a verb; the preposition does the temporal work.

**The lexical choice: יָמִים רַבִּים vs. other "long-time" idioms.** Biblical Hebrew has several ways to describe a long temporal horizon, and the choice matters:

| Idiom | Gloss | Daniel occurrences | Force |
|-------|-------|--------------------|-------|
| יָמִים רַבִּים | "many days" | 8:26; 10:14 (as לַיָּמִים); Gen 21:34; Josh 24:7; 1 Ki 2:38; Ezek 12:27; Hos 3:4 | A quantitative long duration, often used when the writer cannot or will not specify the exact number; commonly with a *delay/postponement* connotation (cf. Ezek 12:27 "the vision that he sees [is] for many days [to come]") |
| אַחֲרִית הַיָּמִים | "latter days, end of days" | 2:28; 10:14 | Canonical "latter-day" formula; eschatological terminus |
| עֵת קֵץ | "time of end" | 8:17, 19; 11:35, 40; 12:4, 9 | Demarcated end-time; technical Danielic terminology for the eschatological period |
| יוֹם יְהוָה | "day of YHWH" | not in Daniel | Prophetic terminus-ad-quem (cf. Amos, Joel, Zeph) |
| מוֹעֵד | "appointed time" | 8:19; 11:27, 29, 35; 12:7 | A fixed appointed moment, determinative |
| קֵץ | "end, limit" | 8:17, 19; 9:26; 11:6, 13, 27, 35, 40, 45; 12:4, 6, 9, 13 | A terminating boundary |

Gabriel's choice of יָמִים רַבִּים (rather than the stronger עֵת קֵץ or אַחֲרִית הַיָּמִים) is conspicuous because the chapter has already *used* עֵת קֵץ twice: 8:17 כִּי לְעֶת־קֵץ הֶחָזוֹן ("for the vision [is] for the time of end") and 8:19 כִּי לְמוֹעֵד קֵץ ("for [it is] for the appointed-time of end"). Verses 17 and 19 employ precisely the structure כִּי + לְ + temporal noun that re-appears at v. 26 — but with different temporal nouns:

- v. 17 כִּי לְעֵת־קֵץ הֶחָזוֹן — "for the vision [is] for [the] time of end"
- v. 19 כִּי לְמוֹעֵד קֵץ — "for [it is] for [the] appointed-time of end"
- **v. 26 כִּי לְיָמִים רַבִּים** — "for [it is] for many days"

The threefold repetition of the כִּי + לְ + temporal-noun formula frames the whole angelic address (17, 19, 26). Verses 17 and 19 locate the vision on the eschatological endpoint; v. 26 locates it on a long intermediate duration. Reading these three כִּי-clauses together:

- v. 17 — the vision concerns (is oriented toward) the time of end.
- v. 19 — the vision concerns (is oriented toward) the appointed-time of end.
- v. 26 — the vision is for (extends over, covers, is deferred to) many days.

Verses 17 and 19 use terminal/punctiliar language (עֵת קֵץ, מוֹעֵד קֵץ — *the end point*), while v. 26 uses durational/extensive language (יָמִים רַבִּים — *the long span*). The two temporal specifications are compatible: the oracle *reaches* the time of end (v. 17, 19) *by way of* a long duration (v. 26). Thus the oracle is (i) headed for the קֵץ (its destination), and (ii) stretched over יָמִים רַבִּים (its duration). Grammatically the prepositions differ only in emphasis: לְ with עֵת־קֵץ = "for/unto the time of end" (terminus); לְ with יָמִים רַבִּים = "for/over many days" (duration).

**The intra-Danielic echo.** The identical לַיָּמִים construction returns at Dan 10:14 in a near-identical formula:

> וּבָאתִי לַהֲבִינְךָ אֵת אֲשֶׁר־יִקְרָה לְעַמְּךָ בְּאַחֲרִית הַיָּמִים **כִּי־עוֹד חָזוֹן לַיָּמִים**
>
> "And I am come to make you understand what shall befall your people in the latter days; **for yet [there is] a vision for the days**."

Dan 10:14 explicitly refers back to 8:26: Gabriel/the-angelic-messenger (10:11–14) uses the same grammatical frame (כִּי + [verbless] + לְ + יָמִים) to reopen a discussion about prophecy-deferred-to-a-long-duration. The verbless כִּי + לְ + יָמִים idiom is effectively Daniel's technical language for temporally-protracted prophetic horizon. See the passage entry for Dan 10:14 (not yet in library — candidate for future study) and the canonical echo in Hab 2:3.

**What the grammar constrains.** The construction is emphatic that the vision *is not for immediate fulfillment* — יָמִים רַבִּים makes it a multiplicity of days, not a few. The grammar does not quantify further: it does not say "a thousand days" or "the age to come" or "until Antiochus." What it DOES say is that the temporal horizon of the oracle is (a) numerous (רַבִּים), (b) day-shaped (יָמִים), and (c) extends to the oracle's intended audience (לְ = for/unto). Any reading that requires fulfillment *within Daniel's lifetime* has to account for the angelic rationale: "seal it, because it is for many days" — the sealing presupposes a significant temporal gap between revelation and fulfillment.

**Inter-textual resonance with NT "bounded seal" grammar.** The sealing-and-unsealing sequence Daniel→Revelation is grammatically paralleled:

- Dan 8:26 סְתֹם הֶחָזוֹן כִּי לְיָמִים רַבִּים — Qal Impv. + verbless causal + prep. "for many days."
- Dan 12:4 סְתֹם הַדְּבָרִים וַחֲתֹם הַסֵּפֶר עַד־עֵת קֵץ — Qal Impv. pair + עַד ("until") + עֵת קֵץ ("time of end").
- Dan 12:9 כִּי־סְתֻמִים וַחֲתֻמִים הַדְּבָרִים עַד־עֵת קֵץ — Qal pass. ptcp. pair + causal כִּי + עַד + עֵת קֵץ.
- **Rev 10:4** σφράγισον ἃ ἐλάλησαν αἱ ἑπτὰ βρονταί καὶ μὴ αὐτὰ γράψῃς — Aor. Impv. (G4972 σφραγίζω) + prohibitive Aor. Subj., paralleling the concealment function.
- **Rev 22:10** μὴ σφραγίσῃς τοὺς λόγους τῆς προφητείας τοῦ βιβλίου τούτου, ὁ καιρὸς γὰρ ἐγγύς ἐστιν — prohibitive Aor. Subj. + γάρ-clause with ἐγγύς ("near"). The Apocalypse REVERSES Daniel's command: where Daniel is told "seal, for [it is] for many days," John is told "do not seal, for the time is near." The two commands are grammatical antitheses framing the biblical-prophetic corpus from Daniel to John. See [aorist-passive-indicative](../greek/aorist-passive-indicative.md) on the σφραγίζω forms.

## Intra-Danielic Placement

Verse 26 is the **pivot** between the vision-and-interpretation block (8:1–25) and the epilogue (8:27).

| Pericope | Content | Verbal spine |
|----------|---------|--------------|
| 8:1–2 | Setting of the vision | Qal perf. נִרְאָה + wayyiqtol narration |
| 8:3–8 | Ram-and-goat vision | Wayyiqtol vision-report (ויַּרְא … וַיַּעֲלֶה …) |
| 8:9–12 | Little-horn expansion | Qal perf. + weqatal + Hiphil impf. (yiqtol description) |
| 8:13–14 | 2,300 evening-mornings oracle | Qal impf./Niphal-perf. resolution (וְנִצְדַּק) — see [dan-8-14](dan-8-14.md) |
| 8:15–17 | Gabriel commissioned | Wayyiqtol commissioning; first כִּי לְעֵת־קֵץ (v. 17) |
| 8:18–19 | Daniel fortified | Wayyiqtol + second כִּי לְמוֹעֵד קֵץ (v. 19) |
| 8:20–25 | Interpretation of vision | Pronominal topic-shifts + predictive weqatal/x-yiqtol — see [dan-8-23-25](dan-8-23-25.md) |
| **8:26** | **Authentication + sealing command** | **Nominal clause + Impv. + nominal clause; third כִּי לְיָמִים רַבִּים** |
| 8:27 | Daniel's reaction | Wayyiqtol narration resumes — וָאֶהְיֶה נִהְיֵיתִי וְנֶחֱלֵיתִי, וָאָקוּם |

Verse 26 therefore closes the oracular block (17–25) with the same grammatical formula that opened it (v. 17 כִּי לְעֵת־קֵץ). The threefold inclusio (v. 17, 19, 26) brackets the angelic address in its own envelope: the oracle is temporally characterized at its opening, its middle, and its close.

Verse 27 returns to Daniel's narrative perspective, and crucially the final clause of 8:27 — וְאֵין מֵבִין ("and there was none understanding [it]") — **grammatically confirms the sealing**: even Daniel, the recipient, did not grasp the מַרְאֶה. The imperative to seal (v. 26) and the stative "none understood" (v. 27) bracket each other: Gabriel commanded sealing, and the narrative immediately reports the vision *is* sealed to understanding. This grammatical pairing prepares for Dan 9:22–23, where Gabriel returns (זָה וָאָבִין — "I have come to give you understanding") to supply what v. 26 had concealed.

## Interpretive Options for "Many Days" (כִּי לְיָמִים רַבִּים)

**The grammar permits multiple chronological schemes.** The following positions are all represented in historical commentary. This entry catalogs them without adjudicating.

### A. Antiochus IV Epiphanes (Maccabean-era terminus)

**Grammatical fit.**
- יָמִים רַבִּים = approximately the 370 years from Daniel's vision (ca. 551 BC) to the 2,300 evening-mornings spanning 171–165 BC Antiochene persecution. "Many days" is read quantitative-but-bounded.
- The sealing is pedagogical-practical: Daniel cannot interpret the oracle because its historical referents (Persian and Greek kingdoms, Seleucid persecution) are far future from his vantage.
- Unsealing occurs gradually through Dan 9 (seventy-weeks supplement), culminating at the Maccabean revolt.

**Grammatical strain.**
- The angelic address is twice located on עֵת קֵץ (vv. 17, 19) — "time of end." Reading the קֵץ as Antiochus' death (164 BC) requires identifying "the end" with a local terminus, not the eschaton.
- יָמִים רַבִּים is a relatively soft quantifier; a Maccabean fulfillment is 3–4 centuries out, which is considerably longer than the typical use of the idiom (often weeks/months/years in narrative — 1 Ki 2:38; Neh 1:4 — though it can stretch to the "many days" of exile in Hos 3:4 and the "many days" of Ezek 12:27, which *are* multi-century eschatological horizons).

### B. Rome (pagan + papal continuum, historicist reading)

**Grammatical fit.**
- יָמִים רַבִּים = the multi-millennial span from Daniel (6th c. BC) to the Second Advent. "Many days" is read as an indeterminate long horizon matching the 2,300-day=2,300-year application (year-day principle per Num 14:34; Ezek 4:6).
- עֵת קֵץ (vv. 17, 19) is the eschatological end-time, not a Maccabean local terminus.
- The sealing is progressive-unsealing: partial disclosure at Dan 9 (terminus a quo for the 2300), further disclosure at Dan 10–12, full disclosure at the "time of end" (Dan 12:4, 9).

**Grammatical fit with NT.**
- Rev 22:10 μὴ σφραγίσῃς … ὁ καιρὸς γὰρ ἐγγύς ἐστιν — unsealing in John's era (AD 95), consistent with the progressive unsealing of the Danielic oracle as its fulfillment approaches. The grammatical inversion (Daniel: "seal, for it is for many days" → John: "do not seal, for the time is near") fits a continuous-history reading.

**Grammatical strain.**
- The year-day principle is not explicit in Daniel 8:26 itself; it is an inter-textual application from Num 14:34 and Ezek 4:6. The grammar of 8:26 neither requires nor forbids it.

### C. Futurist / Dispensational (eschaton-only terminus)

**Grammatical fit.**
- יָמִים רַבִּים = the entire church age (from Daniel to the eschatological tribulation). The sealing is sustained until a future Antichrist figure inaugurates the final "time of end."
- עֵת קֵץ (vv. 17, 19) = the future tribulation period; 8:26 is sealed *throughout* until that yet-to-come eschaton.

**Grammatical strain.**
- Reading יָמִים רַבִּים as "from Daniel to still-future X" without intermediate fulfillment leaves the concealment command in an unusually extended state; most BH uses of יָמִים רַבִּים presuppose eventual resolution *within the horizon of the text's audience*.

### D. Preterist (AD-70 terminus)

**Grammatical fit.**
- יָמִים רַבִּים = the ~640 years from Daniel (ca. 551 BC) to the destruction of Jerusalem (AD 70); the "time of end" is the end of the Jewish-temple age.
- The sealing is lifted by the NT (Matt 24; Luke 21) as the events are enacted.

**Grammatical strain.**
- The שַׂר־שָׂרִים ("Prince of princes") and the king's terminal destruction "without hand" (8:25) are difficult to read as AD-70 destruction of Jerusalem rather than destruction of the persecutor.

---

### What the grammar does and does not decide

**What the grammar decides.**
- The evening-morning vision (מַרְאֵה) is authenticated as truth (clause 1).
- Its interpretive disclosure is *deferred* (clause 3: command to seal).
- The deferral is *long* (clause 4: יָמִים רַבִּים) — and specifically *day-shaped* duration, not simply "a long time" unspecified.
- The oracle is oriented toward the eschatological end-point (vv. 17, 19 read together with v. 26).
- The addressee of the sealing command is Daniel *personally* (2ms imperative + pragmatic אַתָּה).

**What the grammar does not decide.**
- *How many* days "many" is (no numeric quantifier).
- Whether "days" is meant literally or symbolically (year-day debate is not settled lexically within v. 26).
- Whether the "time of end" (vv. 17, 19) and the "many days" (v. 26) name the same moment, or respectively the *terminus* and *span* of the same prophetic horizon.
- Which historical moment(s) supply the resolution (Antiochene / pagan Rome / papal / AD-70 / future eschaton).

These are theological, chronological, and typological questions; the grammar of 8:26 narrows the options but does not close them.
