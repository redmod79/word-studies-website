## Semantic Categories

### 1. Physical Blows / Stripes (Human Violence or Judicial Punishment)
The most concrete usage — literal wounds or lashes inflicted on a person.

- **Luke 10:30** — "fell among thieves, which stripped him of his raiment, and wounded him [πληγὰς]"
- **Acts 16:23** — "when they had laid many stripes [πληγὰς] upon them, they cast them into prison"
- **Acts 16:33** — "washed their stripes [πληγῶν]" — jailer's response after the earthquake
- **Luke 12:48** — "things worthy of stripes [πληγῶν], shall be beaten with few stripes"

### 2. Apostolic Suffering (Paul's Catalog of Hardships)
Paul uses the word to inventory his physical sufferings as a credential of genuine apostolic ministry.

- **2 Cor 6:5** — "In stripes [πληγαῖς], in imprisonments, in tumults, in labours, in watchings, in fastings"
- **2 Cor 11:23** — "in stripes [πληγαῖς] above measure, in prisons more frequent, in deaths oft"

### 3. The Beast's Deadly Wound
A singular πληγή (θανάτου = "of death") describes a mortal wound the beast receives and from which it recovers — a defining mark of the beast narrative in Rev 13.

- **Rev 13:3** — "I saw one of his heads as it were wounded to death [πληγὴν τοῦ θανάτου]; and his deadly wound was healed"
- **Rev 13:12** — "causeth the earth...to worship the first beast, whose deadly wound was healed [πληγὴ τοῦ θανάτου]"
- **Rev 13:14** — "the beast, which had the wound [πληγὴν] by a sword, and did live"

### 4. The Seven Last Plagues (Eschatological Judgment Series)
The dominant usage: seven angels pour out the seven last plagues as the complete and final expression of God's wrath.

- **Rev 15:1** — "seven angels having the seven last plagues [πληγὰς]; for in them is filled up the wrath of God"
- **Rev 15:6** — "the seven angels came out of the temple, having the seven plagues [πληγὰς]"
- **Rev 15:8** — "no man was able to enter into the temple, till the seven plagues [πληγαὶ] of the seven angels were fulfilled"
- **Rev 16:9** — "blasphemed the name of God, which hath power over these plagues [πληγὰς]"
- **Rev 21:9** — "one of the seven angels which had the seven vials full of the seven last plagues [πληγῶν]"

### 5. Plagues as Divine Retribution / Warning
Broader uses: plagues as consequence of sin, instrument of prophecy, or threatened consequence for corrupting Scripture.

- **Rev 9:18** — "By these three plagues [πληγῶν] was the third part of men killed, by the fire, and by the smoke, and by the brimstone"
- **Rev 9:20** — "the rest of the men which were not killed by these plagues [πληγαῖς] yet repented not"
- **Rev 11:6** — "have power over waters to turn them to blood, and to smite the earth with all plagues [πληγῇ]"
- **Rev 18:4** — "that ye receive not of her plagues [πληγῶν]" — call to come out of Babylon
- **Rev 18:8** — "Therefore shall her plagues [πληγαὶ] come in one day, death, and mourning, and famine"
- **Rev 22:18** — "God shall add unto him the plagues [πληγὰς] that are written in this book"
