## Relationships

### Shared Roots
- ἐντυγχάνω (G1793), ὑπερεντυγχάνω (G5241), ἔντευξις (G1783) — all from root τυγχάνω (G5177) "to hit upon" + ἐν "in"
- ὑπερεντυγχάνω adds ὑπέρ "on behalf of" as intensifying prefix
- μεσίτης (G3316) shares the mediatorial function but derives from μέσος (G3319) "middle"

### Co-occurrence (appear in same verses)

No pairwise co-occurrence. Each word occupies distinct verses:

- G5241 appears only in Rom 8:26
- G1783 appears in 1 Tim 2:1; 4:5

However, Rom 8:26–27 and 8:34 place G5241 and G1793 in adjacent verses within the same argument (the Spirit intercedes [G5241] → he who searches hearts knows the Spirit's mind because he intercedes [G1793]).

## Roles in the Concept

- **Action:** ἐντυγχάνω (G1793) — the interceding itself; appealing to an authority on someone's behalf
- **Intensified action:** ὑπερεντυγχάνω (G5241) — intercession with intensity, used of the Spirit's groaning beyond words
- **Event/practice:** ἔντευξις (G1783) — intercession as a noun; prescribed practice in congregational prayer
- **Agent:** μεσίτης (G3316) — the mediator who stands between parties; Christ as mediator of a better covenant

## Key Passages Where Multiple Group Words Appear in Close Context

- **Rom 8:26–34** — G5241 (v. 26: Spirit intercedes with groanings) + G1793 (v. 27: Spirit intercedes according to God's will; v. 34: Christ intercedes at God's right hand). The fullest NT treatment of intercession, with both Spirit and Christ as intercessors.
- **1 Tim 2:1–5** — G1783 (v. 1: intercessions prescribed for all people) + G3316 (v. 5: one mediator between God and men). Connects the practice of intercession to Christ's unique mediatorial role.
