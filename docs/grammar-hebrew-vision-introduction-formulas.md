# Vision-Introduction Formulas (Hebrew / Aramaic)

## Definition

A cluster of recurring syntactic formulas used by the Hebrew prophets and apocalypticists to open a visionary unit, frame it temporally, and mark its content as received-seeing rather than authored-speech. The two dominant building blocks are:

1. **Dating frame** — *wayhî bišnat X lə-Y* (*וַיְהִי בִּשְׁנַת X לְ־Y*) "and it was in year X of Y," with bare *bišnat X* (no *wayhî*) as an equally canonical variant (Dan 8:1; 9:1; 10:1; Isa 6:1).
2. **Seeing frame** — *wā-ʾerʾeh* / *wāʾereh* (*וָאֶרְאֶה*, Qal wayyiqtol 1cs of ראה) "and I saw," with *rāʾîtî* (*רָאִיתִי*, Qal qatal 1cs) as the perfect-form alternant (Zech 1:8); causative *hirʾanî* (*הִרְאַנִי*, Hiphil perfect 3ms + 1cs suffix) "he caused me to see" (Amos 7:1, 4, 7; 8:1; Jer 24:1; Zech 1:20; 3:1) shifts the agency to the revealer.

The two frames are stackable: Dan 8:1–2 combines *bišnat šālôš …* (date) + *ḥāzôn nirʾāh ʾēlay* (vision received, Niphal) + *wāʾereh baḥāzôn* (seeing, wayyiqtol). Ezek 1:1 fuses them in one verse: *wayhî bišlōšîm šānâ … nip̄təḥû haššāmayim wāʾerʾeh marʾôt ʾĕlōhîm*.

## Form Recognition

### Frame 1 — Temporal anchoring

- **Full form:** *וַיְהִי בִּשְׁנַת* X *לְ־*Y (Ezek 1:1; 8:1; 26:1; 29:1; 31:1; 32:1; 2 Kgs 25:1; Jer 32:1)
  - `wayhî` = היה Qal wayyiqtol 3ms — narrative-launcher, untranslated or "and it came to pass"
  - `bišnat` = ב (Prep) + שׁנה Noun.fs.Cst — construct "in (the) year of"
  - numeral (cardinal, absolute)
  - `lə-` + regnal name or event (dative of reference, "belonging-to / of")
- **Reduced form:** *בִּשְׁנַת* X *לְ־*Y (Dan 7:1; 8:1; 9:1; 10:1; Isa 6:1) — opening *wayhî* elided, clause fronted by the prepositional dating phrase directly. Daniel favors this.
- **Parser signatures:**
  - `וַיְהִי` → `Verb.Qal.Wayq.3ms` of lemma היה
  - `בִּשְׁנַת` → `Prep + Noun.fs.Cst` of lemma שׁנה
  - `לְ + PropN.ms.Abs` → regnal-year dative

| *hirʾanî* / *hirʾākā* | Hiphil perfect 3ms (ראה) + pron. suffix | divine-revealer-subject | Amos 7:1, 4, 7; 8:1; Jer 24:1; Zech 1:20; 3:1 |
| *hinnēh* | Interjection (הנה) | vision-presentative | regularly follows any of the above — Zech 1:8; Amos 7:1; Dan 7:2; Ezek 1:4 |

### Frame 3 — Aramaic counterparts (Dan 2, 4, 7)

- *ḥāzēh hăwêt* (*חָזֵה הֲוֵית*) "I was seeing" — Peal active participle + Peal perfect of הוה (periphrastic imperfective for durative seeing). Dan 7:2, 4, 6, 7, 9, 11 (×2), 13, 21.
- *bēʾdayin* (*בֵּאדַיִן*) "then / thereupon" (Dan 7:1, 11, 19) — narrative connector after vision-receipt.
- *ʾareu* / *wārəʾēh* + *ʾălû* (Aramaic cognate of *hinnēh*) — presentative after seeing verb.

The Hiphil encodes the *causer of the seeing* as grammatical subject. Amos's fivefold sequence (7:1, 4, 7; 8:1; 9:1 with variation) is the most concentrated cluster; Zechariah shares the pattern (1:20; 3:1). Daniel notably does **not** use the Hiphil frame for his main visions — Daniel's seeing is Qal (wayyiqtol *wāʾereh*, qatal *ḥāzēh hăwêt* in the Aramaic chapters), with explanatory agency handled by separate discourse (Gabriel's *hābēn*, Dan 8:16; 9:23).

## Function Summary — Why These Formulas Matter for Genre

A block of text that combines two or more of the following features is almost certainly a formal vision-unit, not ordinary historical narrative:

1. Fronted dating phrase (*bišnat* / *wayhî bišnat*) naming a regnal year.
2. First-person seer identification (*ʾănî dāniyyēʾl*; *wa-ʾănî hāyîtî*).
3. A "seeing" verb (Qal wayyiqtol, Qal qatal, Niphal, or Hiphil of ראה; Aramaic Peal of חזה) as the organizing mainline.
4. *wə-hinnēh* / Aramaic *wa-ʾălû* presentatives punctuating the constituents of the vision.
5. Repetition of the seeing verb across the pericope (Dan 8:2 has *wā-ʾereh … wā-ʾereh* in successive clauses; Dan 7 strings *ḥāzēh hăwêt* 7× through the chapter).

## Contrast with Related Forms

| Form | Function | Distinguishing signal |
|------|----------|----------------------|
| *wayhî bišnat* vision-opener | genre marker for formal vision/prophetic unit | *bišnat* + regnal-year dative *lə-* + subsequent seeing verb |

## Relation to Greek (Revelation / Acts / Synoptic Transfiguration)

The Greek NT visionary formulas are **self-conscious translations and extensions of the Hebrew patterns above.** See companion entry [greek/vision-introduction-formulas](../greek/vision-introduction-formulas.md) for the corresponding Greek frames:

- καὶ εἶδον (V-2AAI-1S ὁράω) ≈ *wā-ʾerʾeh*
- ἐγενόμην ἐν πνεύματι (V-2ADI-1S γίνομαι + PREP + N-DSN) ≈ *wayhî*-launcher fused with a state-of-spirit locative
- ἐν πνεύματι / ἐν ἐκστάσει as the Greek-idiom equivalent of the Hebrew-idiom "vision" locative
- καὶ ἰδού (particle of ὁράω) ≈ *wə-hinnēh*

## Interpretive Neutrality

**This entry is context-neutral.** The formulas identify a unit of text as genre-marked vision-report; they do not by themselves determine the vision's referent (historical, typological, eschatological, symbolic, etc.). All interpretive schools of Daniel (Antiochene preterist, historicist year-day, futurist dispensational, symbolic-idealist) and of Ezekiel / Zechariah / Revelation accept the formulas as vision-frames — they differ on what is seen, not on the grammar of the seeing.
