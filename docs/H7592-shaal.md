## Lexicon Definition

A primitive root; **to inquire**; by implication, **to request**; by extension, **to demand**. Rendered in KJV: ask (counsel, on), beg, borrow, lay to charge, consult, demand, desire, earnestly, enquire, greet, obtain leave, lend, pray, request, require, salute, straitly, surely, wish.

The semantic range runs on a gradient from neutral information-gathering ("inquire") through interpersonal requesting ("beg, petition, pray") to transactional transfer ("borrow, lend") and authoritative demand ("require, demand"). The same verb covers both oracular consultation of YHWH and a child's question to a parent.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Ordinary interrogative — asking for information
- **Gen 24:47** — "And I asked her, and said, Whose daughter art thou?"
- **Gen 32:29** — "Jacob asked him, and said, Tell me, I pray thee, thy name"
- **Gen 37:15** — "the man asked him, saying, What seekest thou?"
- **Neh 1:2** — "I asked them concerning the Jews that had escaped"

### 2. Catechetical — child asking a parent about covenant meaning
- **Exo 13:14** — "when thy son asketh thee in time to come, saying, What is this?"
- **Deut 6:20** — "when thy son asketh thee in time to come, saying, What mean the testimonies..."
- **Josh 4:6, 21** — "when your children ask their fathers in time to come, saying, What mean these stones?"

### 3. Oracular consultation of YHWH (often with Urim, ark, or prophet)
- **Num 27:21** — "he shall stand before Eleazar the priest, who shall ask counsel for him after the judgment of Urim"
- **Judg 1:1** — "the children of Israel asked the LORD, saying, Who shall go up for us?"
- **1 Sam 22:10** — "he inquired of the LORD for him"
- **1 Sam 28:6** — "when Saul inquired of the LORD, the LORD answered him not"
- **2 Sam 2:1; 5:19, 23** — David "inquired of the LORD" before each campaign

### 4. Illegitimate/idolatrous consultation
- **Deut 18:11** — "a consulter (shôʼêl) with familiar spirits" (participial form)
- **1 Chr 10:13** — Saul "asked counsel of one that had a familiar spirit, to inquire of it"
- **Hos 4:12** — "My people ask counsel at their stocks"
- **Eze 21:21** — Nebuchadnezzar "consulted with images" using divination

### 5. Petition / request granted by a superior
- **1 Sam 1:17, 20, 27** — Hannah's petition for a son ("the LORD hath given me my petition which I asked")
- **1 Kgs 3:5, 10, 11** — "Ask what I shall give thee" (Solomon's request for wisdom)
- **2 Kgs 2:9–10** — "Ask what I shall do for thee" (Elijah to Elisha); "Thou hast asked a hard thing"
- **Psa 2:8** — "Ask of me, and I shall give thee the heathen for thine inheritance" (messianic)

### 6. Accommodated / self-willed request — granted but not approved
- **1 Sam 8:10** — "Samuel told all the words of the LORD unto the people that **asked** of him a king" (cf. 12:13, 17, 19)
- **Deut 14:26** — "whatsoever thy soul **asketh** of thee" (paired with תָּאָה, the permissive-appetite frame)
- **Psa 78:18** — "they tempted God in their heart by asking meat for their lust"
- **Psa 106:15** (cf. 78:29) — context of desire-driven asking that God grants to judgment

### 7. Commercial / transactional — borrow, lend, demand back
- **Exo 3:22; 11:2; 12:35–36** — Israel "borrowed" jewels of the Egyptians (Hiphil "lent")
- **Exo 22:14** — "if a man borrow ought of his neighbour"
- **2 Kgs 4:3** — Elisha: "Go, borrow thee vessels abroad"
- **1 Sam 1:28** — "I have lent him to the LORD" (Hiphil of shaʼal — causative: grant-what-was-asked)

### 8. Greeting formulae — "ask after [one's] welfare"
- **Gen 43:27** — "he asked them of their welfare"
- **Exo 18:7** — "they asked each other of their welfare"
- **Judg 18:15; 1 Sam 10:4; 17:22; 25:5** — idiomatic salutation
- **Psa 122:6** — "Pray for (ask the peace of) Jerusalem"

### 9. Divine demand / required response
- **Deut 10:12** — "what doth the LORD thy God require (shâʼal) of thee, but to fear the LORD thy God..."
- **Mic 6:8** context overlap (though Mic 6:8 uses darash) — compare the "require" semantic
- **Job 38:3; 40:7; 42:4** — "I will demand of thee, and answer thou me" (YHWH's courtroom interrogation)
