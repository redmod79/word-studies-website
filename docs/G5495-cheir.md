## Lexicon Definition

χείρ: "the hand" — the physical hand, whether of humans or, figuratively, of God. Used for the anatomical body part, the agent of healing touch, the instrument of arrest, the medium of labor, and the locus of divine power. Covers open, extended, and grasping action; KJV never distinguishes singular/plural contextually since both translate simply as "hand/hands."

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Physical Hand / Anatomical Body Part

The neutral, literal use — describing the human hand as a body part.

- **Mar 3:1** — "there was a man there which had a withered hand"
- **Luk 24:39–40** — "Behold my hands and my feet, that it is I myself: handle me, and see"
- **Jhn 20:25** — "Except I shall see in his hands the print of the nails"
- **1Co 12:15, 21** — "if the foot shall say, Because I am not the hand, I am not of the body… the eye cannot say unto the hand, I have no need of thee"

### 2. Healing Touch and Laying On of Hands

The hand as instrument of miraculous healing, blessing, or sacramental impartation.

- **Mat 8:3** — "Jesus put forth his hand, and touched him, saying, I will; be thou clean"
- **Mar 6:5** — "he laid his hands upon a few sick folk, and healed them"
- **Act 8:17–19** — "laid they their hands on them, and they received the Holy Ghost"
- **1Ti 4:14** — "given thee by prophecy, with the laying on of the hands of the presbytery"
- **Heb 6:2** — "doctrine of… laying on of hands" — listed as elementary doctrinal teaching

### 3. Arrest and Hostile Seizure ("Lay Hands On")

The hand as instrument of violent or authoritative seizure — arrest, attack, persecution.

- **Mat 26:50** — "laid hands on Jesus, and took him"
- **Jhn 7:30** — "no man laid hands on him, because his hour was not yet come"
- **Luk 21:12** — "they shall lay their hands on you, and persecute you"
- **Act 4:3** — "they laid hands on them, and put them in hold unto the next day"

### 4. Divine Power and Sovereign Agency ("The Hand of God")

The hand as metonym for divine action, judgment, protection, or appointment.

- **Act 11:21** — "the hand of the Lord was with them: and a great number believed"
- **1Pe 5:6** — "Humble yourselves therefore under the mighty hand of God, that he may exalt you"
- **Heb 10:31** — "a fearful thing to fall into the hands of the living God"
- **Act 4:28, 30** — "whatsoever thy hand and thy counsel determined before to be done… By stretching forth thine hand to heal"

### 5. Into the Hands Of — Transfer of Custody and Authority

The hand as receptacle of delegated power; surrender, betrayal, or deliverance into another's control.

- **Mat 17:22** — "The Son of man shall be betrayed into the hands of men"
- **Luk 23:46** — "Father, into thy hands I commend my spirit"
- **Jhn 3:35** — "The Father loveth the Son, and hath given all things into his hand"
- **Jhn 10:28–29** — "no man shall pluck them out of my hand… no man is able to pluck them out of my Father's hand"

### 6. Manual Labor, Craftsmanship, and Personal Autograph

The hand as organ of work — physical labor, creation, and the personal handwritten signature in Paul's letters.

- **1Co 4:12** — "working with our own hands: being reviled, we bless"
- **Gal 6:11** — "Ye see how large a letter I have written unto you with mine own hand"
- **Act 20:34** — "these hands have ministered unto my necessities, and to them that were with me"
- **Heb 1:10** — "Thou, Lord… the heavens are the works of thy hands"
