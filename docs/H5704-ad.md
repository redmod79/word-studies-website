## Semantic Categories

### 1. Temporal Terminus — "until" an Event or Time
The most frequent use: ʻad marks the endpoint of a duration, the point at which a condition ceases or changes.
- **Gen 3:19** — "till thou return unto the ground" (death as temporal limit)
- **Gen 8:7** — "until the waters were dried up from off the earth"
- **Gen 49:10** — "until Shiloh come; and unto him shall the gathering of the people be"
- **Exo 12:6** — "ye shall keep it up until the fourteenth day"
- **Lev 11:24** — "shall be unclean until the even" (recurring ~30× in Lev 11–15, 22; Num 19)
- **Dan 8:14** — "Unto two thousand and three hundred days; then shall the sanctuary be cleansed"
- **Dan 12:9** — "the words are closed up and sealed till the time of the end"

### 2. Spatial Extent — "unto" a Place or Boundary
ʻad marks the spatial endpoint of motion or extent, often in geographic boundary descriptions.
- **Gen 10:19** — "the border of the Canaanites was from Sidon... unto Gaza... unto Sodom"
- **Gen 13:3** — "from the south even to Bethel"
- **Jos 1:4** — "from the wilderness and this Lebanon even unto the great river"
- **1 Sam 3:20** — "all Israel from Dan even to Beersheba"
- **2 Sam 24:2** — "Go now through all the tribes of Israel, from Dan even to Beersheba"
- **Neh 3:13** — "they built it, and set up the doors thereof... unto the dung gate"

### 3. Degree / Extent — "even to" the Point of
ʻad can mark the extent or degree to which something reaches, intensifying a description.
- **Gen 14:23** — "from a thread even to a shoelatchet" (intensifying: not even the smallest thing)
- **Gen 19:4** — "both old and young, all the people from every quarter" (totality)
- **Gen 26:13** — "grew until he became very great"
- **Jonah 2:5** — "the waters compassed me about, even to the soul"
- **Jonah 4:9** — "I do well to be angry, even unto death"
- **Hab 3:13** — "thou woundedst the head out of the house of the wicked, by discovering the foundation unto the neck"

### 4. Duration — "while" / "as long as"
ʻad marks ongoing duration, the period during which something continues.
- **Exo 33:22** — "while my glory passeth by, I will put thee in a clift of the rock"
- **Neh 4:21** — "from the rising of the morning till the stars appeared"
- **1 Sam 20:41** — "David... fell on his face to the ground, and bowed himself three times"
- **Job 27:5** — "till I die I will not remove mine integrity from me"

### 5. "For Ever" — Perpetuity Formulae
Combined with ʻowlam (H5769) or H5703, ʻad forms the standard perpetuity idioms.

- **2 Sam 7:13** — "I will stablish the throne of his kingdom for ever"
