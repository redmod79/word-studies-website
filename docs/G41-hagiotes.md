## Lexicon Definition

Sanctity (properly, the state); rendered "holiness." From ἅγιος (G40). The -ότης (-otes) suffix denotes an abstract quality or state, making hagiotes the word for the inherent condition of being holy — holiness as a fixed attribute rather than a process or quality manifested in action.

## Textual Note: 2 Corinthians 1:12

The Nestle 1904 critical text reads ἐν **ἁγιότητι** καὶ εἰλικρινίᾳ τοῦ Θεοῦ ("in holiness and sincerity of God") at 2 Cor 1:12. The Textus Receptus reads ἁπλότητι ("simplicity"). ## Semantic Categories

### 1. God's Own Inherent Holiness — Shared with Believers through Discipline
- **Heb 12:10** — "but he for [our] profit, that [we] might be partakers (μεταλαβεῖν, metalabein) of his holiness (ἁγιότητος αὐτοῦ)"

The sole KJV occurrence presents hagiotes as God's personal, intrinsic holiness — not the process of sanctification (hagiasmos, G38) or a quality displayed in conduct (hagiosyne, G42), but the settled divine state itself. The genitive αὐτοῦ ("his") marks it as belonging to God, and the infinitive μεταλαβεῖν ("to partake/share in") indicates believers receive a share of it through the discipline described in Heb 12:5-11.

## Grammatical Context (Heb 12:10)

The Greek construction εἰς τὸ μεταλαβεῖν τῆς ἁγιότητος αὐτοῦ uses:
- **εἰς** + articular infinitive (τὸ μεταλαβεῖν) — purpose clause: "in order to partake"
- **μεταλαμβάνω** (G3335) — aorist active infinitive: "to receive a share of, partake"
- **τῆς ἁγιότητος** — genitive of the thing shared: "of his holiness"
- **αὐτοῦ** — possessive genitive: "his" (referring to God)

The verse contrasts human fathers (who discipline κατὰ τὸ δοκοῦν αὐτοῖς, "according to what seemed good to them") with God (who disciplines ἐπὶ τὸ συμφέρον, "for our benefit"), with the stated purpose being participation in God's own holiness.
