## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same across testaments:**
- The verb consistently describes total destruction by fire — complete consumption, not partial scorching
- The "outside the camp" burning of sin offerings carries directly from Lev 4/16 into Heb 13:11
- Judgment-by-fire imagery (cities, nations) spans from Joshua/Jeremiah into Revelation

**What shifts:**
- OT uses are predominantly literal (physical burning of objects, animals, cities); NT adds eschatological/metaphorical layers (tares at harvest, works tested by fire, end-time destruction)
- The ritual-disposal sense (Leviticus) narrows in the NT to a single explicit reference (Heb 13:11), but that reference is theologically pivotal — connecting Jesus' suffering "outside the gate" to the OT saraph pattern
