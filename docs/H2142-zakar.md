## Lexicon Definition

A primitive root; also as denominative from [H2145], properly to mark (so as to be recognized), i.e. to remember; by implication, to mention; also to be male:— burn [incense], earnestly, be male, (make) mention (of), be mindful, recount, record(-er), remember, make to be remembered, bring (call, come, keep, put) to (in) remembrance, still, think on, well.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. God Remembering His Covenant / His People
The dominant theological use: God's zakar toward his people is an act of loyal covenant fidelity, not mere mental recall.

- **Gen 8:1** — "And God remembered Noah... and God made a wind to pass over the earth"
- **Gen 9:15** — "And I will remember my covenant... and the waters shall no more become a flood"
- **Lev 26:42** — "Then will I remember my covenant with Jacob... and I will remember the land"
- **Exo 2:24** — "And God heard their groaning, and God remembered his covenant with Abraham"

### 2. Israel Commanded to Remember (Imperative)
Zakar as a covenant obligation — remembering Exodus, Sabbath, and Torah as loyalty markers.

- **Exo 20:8** — "Remember the sabbath day, to keep it holy"
- **Deu 5:15** — "And remember that thou wast a servant in the land of Egypt... therefore the LORD thy God commanded thee to keep the sabbath day"
- **Num 15:39** — "...that ye may look upon it, and remember all the commandments of the LORD, and do them"
- **Mal 4:4** — "Remember ye the law of Moses my servant, which I commanded unto him in Horeb"

### 3. Prayer: Appealing to God to Remember
Supplicants invoke God's zakar as an appeal to covenantal faithfulness — "remember me" as a cry for deliverance.

- **Jdg 16:28** — "O Lord GOD, remember me, I pray thee, and strengthen me... only this once"
- **1Sa 1:11** — "O LORD of hosts... remember me, and not forget thine handmaid" (Hannah's vow)
- **Neh 13:14** — "Remember me, O my God, concerning this, and wipe not out my good deeds"
- **Hab 3:2** — "in wrath remember mercy"

### 4. Forgetting as Covenant Failure (zakar negated)
The antonym of zakar (H7911 shâkach, "forget") defines unfaithfulness. Not-remembering = spiritual apostasy.

- **Jdg 8:34** — "And the children of Israel remembered not the LORD their God, who had delivered them"
- **Isa 43:25** — "I, even I, am he that blotteth out thy transgressions... and will not remember thy sins"
- **Jer 31:34** — "I will forgive their iniquity, and I will remember their sin no more"
- **Eze 36:31** — "Then shall ye remember your own evil ways, and your doings that were not good"

### 5. Proclamation / Making Mention (Hiphil)
Hiphil zakar = "cause to be remembered" = public declaration, praise, invocation.

- **Isa 12:4** — "make mention that his name is exalted"
- **Isa 62:6** — "ye that make mention of the LORD, keep not silence"
- **1Chr 16:4** — "he appointed certain of the Levites... to record, and to thank and praise the LORD"
- **Jer 2:2** — "I remember thee, the kindness of thy youth" (God's remembrance as recital/proclamation)

### 6. The "Recorder" — Official Royal Title (Hiphil Participle)
Hiphil participle מַזְכִּיר (mazkîr) = "one who causes to remember" = court recorder/herald/annalist.

- **2Sa 8:16** — "Jehoshaphat the son of Ahilud was recorder"
- **1Ki 4:3** — "Jehoshaphat the son of Ahilud, the recorder"
- **2Ki 18:18** — "Joah the son of Asaph the recorder"
