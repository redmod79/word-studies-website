## Lexicon Definition

A primitive root; to wind tightly (as a rope), i.e. to bind; specifically, by a pledge; figuratively, to pervert, destroy; also to writhe in pain (especially of parturition): — at all, band, bring forth, (deal) corrupt(-ly), destroy, offend, lay to (take a) pledge, spoil, travail, very, withhold.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Taking a Pledge / Binding by Surety
The primary, concrete sense — seizing a garment or property as collateral for a debt.
- **Exo 22:26** — "If thou at all take thy neighbour's raiment to pledge, thou shalt deliver it unto him by that the sun goeth down"
- **Deu 24:6** — "No man shall take the nether or the upper millstone to pledge: for he taketh a man's life to pledge"
- **Deu 24:17** — "nor take a widow's raiment to pledge"
- **Job 22:6** — "For thou hast taken a pledge from thy brother for nought, and stripped the naked of their clothing"
- **Job 24:3** — "They drive away the ass of the fatherless, they take the widow's ox for a pledge"
- **Job 24:9** — "They pluck the fatherless from the breast, and take a pledge of the poor"
- **Pro 20:16** — "Take his garment that is surety for a stranger: and take a pledge of him for a strange woman"

### 2. Destruction / Corruption
Figurative extension from binding/twisting to perverting or ruining.

- **Isa 32:7** — "he deviseth wicked devices to destroy the poor with lying words"
- **Mic 2:10** — "it is polluted, it shall destroy you, even with a sore destruction"
- **Pro 13:13** — "Whoso despiseth the word shall be destroyed" (Niphal)

### 3. Moral Corruption / Dealing Corruptly
Acting perversely or faithlessly, especially against God.
- **Neh 1:7** — "We have dealt very corruptly against thee, and have not kept the commandments"

- **Ecc 5:6** — "why should God... destroy the work of thine hands?" (Piel of corruption/ruin)
- **Isa 10:27** — "the yoke shall be destroyed" (Pual — broken/corrupted)

### 4. Travail / Bringing Forth in Pain
Writhing in labour pains — the physical twisting/winding of parturition.

- **Sng 8:5** — "there thy mother brought thee forth: there she brought thee forth that bare thee"

### 5. Spoiling / Offending
Broader harm or violation.
- **Sng 2:15** — "Take us the foxes, the little foxes, that spoil the vines"

## Note on Isaiah 53:5

The KJV "he was wounded for our transgressions" in Isa 53:5 uses H2490 חָלַל (chalal, "to pierce, profane") in the Pual stem (מְחֹלָל, "pierced/wounded"), not H2254 chabal. The related noun חֲבוּרָה (chaburah, H2250, "bruise/stripe") appearing later in the same verse ("with his stripes we are healed") derives from a different root (H2266 chabar "to join/bind"). Despite phonetic similarity, chabal, chalal, and chabar are distinct roots with distinct semantic ranges.
