# WG: Beast

## Overview
The English word "beast" in the KJV translates several distinct Greek and Hebrew words, each with different connotations. ## Roles in the Concept

- **Wild/Predatory:** θηρίον (G2342) — dangerous, untamed animal; in Revelation, the evil beast power
- **Living/Holy:** ζῷον (G2226) — living being; in Revelation, the four living creatures around God's throne
- **Domestic/Tame:** κτῆνος (G2934) — beast of burden, domesticated animal
- **Combat:** θηριομαχέω (G2341) — fighting wild beasts (metaphorical in 1 Cor 15:32)
- **OT Generic:** בְּהֵמָה (H929) — broad OT term for domestic animals, cattle
- **OT Living:** חַי (H2416) — "living thing," used broadly for any animal
- **OT Apocalyptic:** חֵיוָא (H2423) — Aramaic "beast," Daniel's four kingdoms as beasts

## Key Distinctions

### θηρίον vs ζῷον in Revelation
This is the most significant distinction obscured by the KJV:
- **ζῷον (zōon)** — Rev 4:6-9: four holy living creatures around the throne, full of eyes, constantly worshipping God
- **θηρίον (thērion)** — Rev 13:1-2: the beast from the sea, receiving power from the dragon, demanding false worship

These two are polar opposites in Revelation's symbolic world: ζῷον represents true worship of God; θηρίον represents false worship imposed by evil. The KJV renders both as "beast."

### Daniel → Revelation Connection
The Aramaic חֵיוָא (H2423) in Daniel 7 (four beasts from the sea representing four kingdoms) provides the direct background for θηρίον in Revelation 13 (beast from the sea). The LXX translates חֵיוָא with θηρίον, establishing the terminological bridge.
