## Lexicon Definition

A primitive root; properly, to flow as water (i.e. to rain); transitively, to lay or throw (especially an arrow, i.e. to shoot); figuratively, to point out (as if by aiming the finger), to teach. KJV renderings: archer, cast, direct, inform, instruct, lay, shew, shoot, teach(-er, -ing), through.

Alternate form: יָרָא (yara') in 2 Chronicles 26:15.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Divine and Authoritative Instruction (Hiphil — "teach, direct")
The dominant usage. God, priests, or wisdom figures direct others in the right path.
- **Exo 4:12** — "Now therefore go, and I will be with thy mouth, and teach thee what thou shalt say"
- **Psa 25:8** — "Good and upright is the LORD: therefore will he teach sinners in the way"
- **Psa 25:12** — "What man is he that feareth the LORD? him shall he teach in the way that he shall choose"
- **Psa 86:11** — "Teach me thy way, O LORD; I will walk in thy truth"

### 2. Priestly Torah-Instruction
The designated priestly function of rendering authoritative rulings and teaching the law.
- **Lev 10:11** — "And that ye may teach the children of Israel all the statutes"
- **Deu 33:10** — "They shall teach Jacob thy judgments, and Israel thy law"
- **2Ki 17:27–28** — "let him teach them the manner of the God of the land... and taught them how they should fear the LORD"
- **Eze 44:23** — "And they shall teach my people the difference between the holy and profane"

### 3. Shooting / Archery (Qal and Hiphil)
The physical sense of casting or propelling an arrow.
- **1Sa 20:20** — "I will shoot three arrows on the side thereof, as though I shot at a mark"
- **2Sa 11:24** — "And the shooters shot from off the wall upon thy servants"
- **2Ch 35:23** — "And the archers shot at king Josiah"
- **Psa 64:4** — "That they may shoot in secret at the perfect: suddenly do they shoot at him, and fear not"

### 4. Casting / Throwing / Laying (Qal)
The basic physical sense of hurling or placing something.
- **Gen 31:51** — "this pillar, which I have cast betwixt me and thee"
- **Exo 15:4** — "Pharaoh's chariots and his host hath he cast into the sea"
- **Exo 15:25** — "the LORD shewed him a tree, which when he had cast into the waters"
- **Job 38:6** — "or who laid the corner stone thereof"

### 5. Wisdom Instruction (Hiphil — proverbial/dialogic)
Teaching within the wisdom tradition, from parent to child or sage to learner.
- **Pro 4:4** — "He taught me also, and said unto me, Let thine heart retain my words"
- **Pro 4:11** — "I have taught thee in the way of wisdom; I have led thee in right paths"
- **Job 6:24** — "Teach me, and I will hold my tongue: and cause me to understand wherein I have erred"
- **Job 36:22** — "Behold, God exalteth by his power: who teacheth like him?"

### 6. Rain / Watering (Qal/Hiphil — literal "flow as water")
The original concrete sense preserved in a few passages.
- **Hos 6:3** — "he shall come unto us as the rain, as the latter and former rain unto the earth"
- **Hos 10:12** — "it is time to seek the LORD, till he come and rain righteousness upon you"
- **Pro 11:25** — "he that watereth shall be watered also himself"

### 7. Prophetic Direction / Pointing Out
Directing or indicating (the "pointing the finger" sense).
- **Gen 46:28** — "he sent Judah before him unto Joseph, to direct his face unto Goshen"
- **Exo 15:25** — "the LORD shewed him a tree" (God pointing out/indicating)
- **Isa 28:26** — "For his God doth instruct him to discretion, and doth teach him"
