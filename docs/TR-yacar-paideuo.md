## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same across testaments:**
- The dual semantic range (corrective discipline + formative instruction) persists from yasar through paideuo with remarkable consistency
- The father-son metaphor for divine discipline: Deut 8:5 "as a man chasteneth his son" maps directly to Heb 12:6-10
- The restorative intent of discipline — not punitive destruction but corrective formation — is present in both Testaments
- The love-motivation: discipline flows from relational concern (Prov 3:12 → Heb 12:6, Rev 3:19)

**What shifts:**
- OT yasar includes contexts of national/covenant judgment (Lev 26:18 "punish you seven times") that are absent from NT paideuo, which is exclusively addressed to individuals and the believing community
- NT paideuo develops an explicitly educational sense (Acts 7:22 Moses "learned"; Acts 22:3 Paul "taught") that, while present in yasar (Isa 28:26 God "instructs"), becomes more prominent
- The Pastoral Epistles extend paideuo into a gracious-instruction-by-grace sense (Tit 2:12 "teaching us") with no direct punishment element — a semantic expansion beyond OT yasar
