## Relationships

### Shared Root
All members except proskyneo share the root pipto (πίπτω). Each compound adds a preposition that specializes the direction:
- **ek-** (out of) → ekpipto: fall away from
- **em-** (into) → empipto: fall into
- **epi-** (upon) → epipipto: fall upon
- **kata-** (down) → katapipto: fall down
- **pros-** (toward) → prospipto: fall before

## Roles in the Concept

- **Core action:** pipto (G4098) — the fundamental verb of falling
- **Worship partner:** proskyneo (G4352) — the act that follows prostration; pipto provides the physical posture, proskyneo the spiritual meaning
- **Directional variants:**
  - epipipto (G1968) — aggressive/overwhelming: Spirit falling upon (Acts 10:44), embracing (Acts 20:10)
  - ekpipto (G1601) — separation/loss: falling from grace (Gal 5:4), driven off course (Acts 27:26)
  - prospipto (G4363) — approach/supplication: falling before someone to beg (Mar 5:33; 7:25)
  - empipto (G1706) — entrapment: falling into pit (Mat 12:11), into hands of God (Heb 10:31)
  - katapipto (G2667) — collapse: physical falling down (Acts 26:14; 28:6)

## Key Passages Where Multiple Group Words Appear Together

- **Mat 2:11** — pipto + proskyneo: "fell down, and worshipped him" (first NT occurrence of the pair)
