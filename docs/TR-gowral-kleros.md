**Semantic widening in LXX:** In Hebrew, gowral focuses on the lot itself (the pebble/stone used for casting) and secondarily the outcome (what was allotted). The LXX equivalent kleros absorbed both gowral ("lot-casting") and nachalah ("inherited land"), making kleros the comprehensive Greek term for anything received by divine allotment — lots, inheritance, and portion.

## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same across testaments:**
- The literal sense of casting lots persists unchanged — the Gospel crucifixion accounts use kleros exactly as the OT uses gowral (physical lot-casting to divide property)
- The concept that God stands behind the outcome of the lot (Pro 16:33) carries into Acts 1:26 (apostolic lot for Matthias)

**What shifts:**
- In the NT, the kleros word-family shifts overwhelmingly toward the figurative "inheritance/heritage" sense: spiritual inheritance in Christ (Col 1:12, Eph 1:11, 1Pe 5:3), not physical land allotment
- The concrete act of lot-casting nearly disappears after Acts 1 — the NT church replaces lot-casting with Spirit-led decision (Acts 13:2, 15:28), while retaining the vocabulary of "allotted portion" for spiritual realities
