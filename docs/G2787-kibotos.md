The single Greek term κιβωτός bridges two distinct Hebrew words. H727 (aron) is overwhelmingly used for the Ark of the Covenant throughout the OT; H8392 (tevah) is used for Noah's ark and the basket of Moses (Exod 2:3). The LXX translators chose the same Greek word for both, creating a semantic unity that the NT inherits and exploits typologically.
