The Magnificat (Lk 1:46–55) is the densest NT concentration of gadal/megaluno theology: Mary's soul "magnifies" (megalunei) the Lord in direct echo of the Psalms' call to magnify YHWH.

## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same:**
- The doxological use (magnifying God/YHWH) carries straight through from Psalms into Luke-Acts
- The verb is always transitive in the "making great" sense — there is always an agent and an object
- The honorific/promotional sense (raising status before others) persists in Acts 5:13, 19:17

**What shifts:**
- The physical growth and child-rearing senses of gadal (Qal: "the child grew") do not appear in NT megaluno — those are rendered by auxano and trephoo in the NT
- The self-magnification / arrogant pride sense (Dan 8:4; Jer 48:26) is absent in NT megaluno; the NT authors reserve megaluno entirely for legitimate magnification of God or persons of God
- Daniel's "magnify himself" language (Dan 8:4, 11, 25; 11:36) feeds into NT antichrist typology but through different Greek vocabulary (huperairetai, G5229, in 2 Thess 2:4)
