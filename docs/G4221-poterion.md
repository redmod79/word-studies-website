## Lexicon Definition

A drinking-vessel; by extension the contents of such a vessel. Neuter of a derivative of the alternate of πίνω (pínō, "to drink"). In biblical usage the literal vessel frequently becomes a metonym for what it holds, and then a figure for allotted experience — whether blessing or judgment.

**Patterns:** Exclusively a Gospels + 1 Corinthians + Revelation word. Absent from Paul's non-Corinthian epistles, Acts, General Epistles, and Hebrews. The concentration is telling — every use sits in one of three settings: (1) Jesus teaching/Passion (Gospels), (2) the Lord's Supper (1 Cor), or (3) apocalyptic judgment (Revelation). 1 Corinthians has the highest density because ch. 10–11 is the NT's sustained treatise on the eucharistic cup.
