## Semantic Categories

### 1. The Heavenly / Resurrection Body
Paul uses oikētḗrion metaphorically for the glorified resurrection body — a "house from heaven" that believers long to be clothed with, contrasting the mortal body as a dissolving earthly tent (2 Cor 5:1).

- **2 Cor 5:2** — "For in this we groan, earnestly desiring to be clothed upon with our house (oikētḗrion) which is from heaven"

### 2. Assigned Spiritual Domain / Proper Station
The fallen angels abandoned their own oikētḗrion — the proper sphere or station assigned to them — a use pointing not to a physical building but to a divinely appointed realm.

- **Jude 1:6** — "And the angels which kept not their first estate, but left their own habitation (oikētḗrion), he hath reserved in everlasting chains under darkness unto the judgment of the great day"
