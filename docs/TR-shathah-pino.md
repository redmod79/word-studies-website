```
שָׁתָה (shâthâh)    →  πίνω (pínō)          →  πίνω (pínō)
H8354, 193x OT         ~179x as translation     66x in NT
"drink, imbibe"        of shâthâh               "drink, imbibe"
```

The shâthâh → pínō bridge is one of the cleanest one-to-one lexical correspondences between the Hebrew Bible and the LXX/NT. Both words are the "default verb of imbibing" in their respective languages — neither carries any inherent restriction to wine, nor causative force (causation is H8248 shaqah in Hebrew, G4222 potizō in Greek).

## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same:**
- The verb itself is semantically unmarked in both testaments — "imbibe any liquid."
- The "eat and drink" pair (akal+shathah → esthio+pino) persists as the biblical idiom for ordinary sustenance, fellowship, and covenant meals.
- The "cup of wrath" metaphor transfers intact from Hebrew prophets into Revelation.
- Causative ("give to drink") stays a separate verb: shaqah → potizō.

**What shifts:**
- The NT adds a **sacramental overlay** (pínō + potērion at the Last Supper / 1 Cor 11) that the OT itself does not carry for shathah.
- The OT has 16 Qal imperatives of shathah distributed across narrative, prophetic judgment, wisdom, and festal settings. The NT concentrates its imperatives of pínō in the Last Supper ("Drink ye all of it") and Johannine discourse ("If any man thirst, let him come unto me, and drink," Jn 7:37).
- **No Torah commanded drinking of yayin or shekar by laypersons.** All four Torah Qal imperatives of shathah are in Gen 24 (Rebekah/servant, water only). Torah wine/shekar references with shathah are either prohibitive (Lev 10:9; Num 6:3; Deu 29:6) or permissive (Num 6:20; Deu 14:26 uses akal for the feast verb). This pattern carries into the LXX unchanged.
