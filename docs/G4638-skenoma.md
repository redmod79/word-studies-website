## Lexicon Definition

An encampment; i.e. (figuratively) the Temple (as God's residence), or the human body (as a temporary abode of the soul).

## Semantic Categories

### 1. God's Dwelling Place (Temple/Tabernacle)
- **Acts 7:46** — "Who found favour before God, and desired to find a tabernacle (skēnōma) for the God of Jacob."
  Stephen's speech recounts David's desire to build a permanent dwelling for God, echoing Ps 132:5. The -μα suffix emphasizes the completed structure — the result of encamping.

### 2. The Human Body as Temporary Dwelling
- **2 Pet 1:13** — "Yea, I think it meet, as long as I am in this tabernacle (skēnōma), to stir you up by putting you in remembrance"
- **2 Pet 1:14** — "Knowing that shortly I must put off this my tabernacle (skēnōma), even as our Lord Jesus Christ hath shewed me."
  Peter uses skēnōma for the mortal body — a tent-dwelling that is "put off" (ἀπόθεσις G595) at death. The demonstrative "this" (τοῦτο) underscores the body's present, temporary character.
