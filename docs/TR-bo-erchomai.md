## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same across testaments:**
- **Messianic "Coming One" title.** The participle "the coming one" (habbāʾ / ὁ ἐρχόμενος) becomes a fixed messianic designation by the intertestamental period and enters the NT untranslated in sense.
- **Eschatological day-language.** "The day of the LORD cometh" (Joel, Mal, Zech) carries over into "he cometh with clouds" (Rev) and "the day of the Lord cometh as a thief" (1 Thess 5:2, 2 Pet 3:10).
- **Angelic/divine approach formula.** bôʾ for an angel arriving (Dan 8:17, 9:22, 10:12) matches érchomai for Gabriel to Mary (Luke 1:28) and the angel to the empty tomb (Matt 28:2).
- **Prophetic fulfillment "come to pass."** Hebrew bôʾ for a word "coming to pass" survives via γίνομαι primarily, but érchomai also carries it (e.g., "until all be fulfilled" idioms).

**What shifts:**
- **The Hiphil causative "bring" mostly splits off.** In the NT, "bring" is carried by ἄγω, φέρω, and compounds — érchomai itself is almost exclusively intransitive. The Hebrew one-root coverage of come/bring becomes two lexical fields in Greek.
- **The sexual euphemism disappears.** NT Greek uses different idioms; érchomai does not inherit bôʾ's "go in unto" sense.
- **"Enter" narrows into εἰσέρχομαι.** What Hebrew did with bôʾ + preposition, NT Greek specializes with the εἰσ- compound — entering the kingdom, entering rest, entering the holy place (Heb 9).
- **Christological intensification.** bôʾ was used of anyone arriving; ὁ ἐρχόμενος becomes a near-title. The Hebrew verb's semantic breadth gets concentrated around one figure in NT usage.
