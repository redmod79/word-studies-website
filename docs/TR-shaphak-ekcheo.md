## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same across testaments:**
- **Three theological domains remain intact:** (1) blood shed sacrificially or violently, (2) Spirit poured out as divine gift, (3) wrath poured out in judgment.
- **Joel 2:28 → Acts 2:17** preserves the exact verb chain, making Pentecost a lexical fulfillment of the OT promise.
- **Altar imagery** — blood poured at the base/horns — carries into Rev 16 bowl imagery, where phialē (bowl) is the same vessel used in OT altar service (mizraq).

**What shifts:**
- **Object of outpouring expands in the NT.** Hebrew shaphak most often takes blood or wrath; in the NT, ekcheō adds "love" (Rom 5:5) and "the gift of the Holy Ghost" (Acts 10:45) as objects. The covenant blood of Christ (Mat 26:28) is a new theological category — voluntary self-outpouring.
- **Agency inverts in apocalyptic usage.** In the OT, God pours out wrath directly or via prophets; in Revelation 16 the act is ritualized — angels pour out vials from the temple, modeled on priestly libation.
- **Concentration by genre.** In the OT, shaphak is spread across Torah, Prophets, and Writings. In the NT, ekcheō concentrates in Revelation (bowls) and Acts (Spirit) — the two apocalyptic-eschatological loci of the NT.
