## Semantic Categories

### 1. Corporate Intercession for Others (1Ti 2:1)
- **1Ti 2:1** — "I exhort therefore, that, first of all, supplications, prayers, intercessions, [and] giving of thanks, be made for all men"

Listed alongside δέησις (deesis, "supplications"), προσευχή (proseuche, "prayers"), and εὐχαριστία (eucharistia, "giving of thanks") in a four-fold taxonomy of public prayer. The plural form (ἐντεύξεις, accusative plural) and the context "for all men" indicate communal petitionary prayer directed toward others' benefit.

### 2. Consecrating Prayer (1Ti 4:5)
- **1Ti 4:5** — "For it is sanctified by the word of God and prayer"

Here in singular genitive (ἐντεύξεως), the word denotes prayer that consecrates food — a petitionary approach to God that renders created things holy for use. Paired with "the word of God" (λόγος θεοῦ) as dual means of sanctification.
