## Morphological Note

The sole NT form is **ὀρθοτομοῦντα** — present active participle, accusative singular masculine [V-PAP-ASM]. The participial form indicates an ongoing, characteristic activity rather than a one-time act: Timothy is to be continually "rightly dividing" the word of truth.

## Etymology and Literal Sense

The compound consists of two elements:

1. **ὀρθός (orthos, G3717)** — "straight, upright, right" (NT: Acts 14:10 "stand upright," Heb 12:13 "straight paths"; 2 occurrences)
2. **τέμνω (temnō)** — "to cut" (base of τομώτερος G5114, "sharper," Heb 4:12 — "sharper than any two-edged sword")

The literal meaning is "to cut straight." The image may derive from:
- Road-building (cutting a straight path through terrain)
- Stone-masonry or woodworking (making a precise, straight cut)
- Plowing (cutting a straight furrow)

The related adverb ὀρθῶς (orthōs, G3723, 4 occurrences) means "rightly, correctly" — used in Luke 7:43 "Thou hast rightly judged," Luke 10:28 "Thou hast answered right," Luke 20:21 "thou teachest rightly."

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Correct Handling of the Word of Truth
- **2 Tim 2:15** — "Study to shew thyself approved unto God, a workman that needeth not to be ashamed, rightly dividing the word of truth."

The sole NT usage places orthotomeo within a workman metaphor: the teacher of Scripture is a craftsman whose material is "the word of truth" and whose task is to handle it with precision — cutting straight, not crooked.

### 2. LXX Background: Making Straight a Path
The word appears in the LXX at Proverbs 3:6 and 11:5, where it renders the Hebrew verb **ישׁר (yashar, H3474)** in the Piel stem ("to make straight, to direct"):

- **Prov 3:6** (LXX orthotomeo → Heb. yashar Piel) — "In all thy ways acknowledge him, and he shall **direct** thy paths." (KJV)
- **Prov 11:5** (LXX orthotomeo → Heb. yashar Piel) — "The righteousness of the perfect shall **direct** his way." (KJV)

In both LXX passages, the underlying concept is making a path straight — God straightening one's course (Prov 3:6) or righteousness straightening one's way (Prov 11:5). This "path-straightening" imagery is the semantic bridge to the NT use: handling truth correctly is making a straight path through it rather than deviating.
