The Psa 110:1 chain is the most significant: the Hebrew shiyth ("I will set") becomes tithemi in the LXX, and this exact phrasing is preserved in all six NT quotations. This makes shiyth → tithemi one of the most frequently activated OT→NT word bridges.

## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same across testaments:**
- The core sense of deliberate, authoritative placement persists: God places, sets, appoints
- The "make X into Y" (transformative) sense carries through (Psa 110:1 → NT quotations)
- Both verbs function as general-purpose placement words with wide semantic range

**What shifts:**
- NT tithemi absorbs the much larger H7760 suwm tradition (224 co-occ vs 35 for shiyth), so tithemi in the NT carries freight from both Hebrew placement verbs
- NT adds a distinctive "lay down one's life" (John 10:11, 15, 17; 1 John 3:16) idiom not present in the OT shiyth usage
- The OT "set the heart" (attention) idiom does not carry over — NT uses different constructions for attention
