## Semantic Categories

### 1. Eschatological/Messianic Rule with Authority
The future-tense uses in Revelation, always paired with "rod of iron" (rhabdos siderous), depicting sovereign dominion over nations.
- **Mat 2:6** — "out of thee shall come a Governor, that shall rule (poimanei) my people Israel"
- **Rev 2:27** — "he shall rule (poimanei) them with a rod of iron; as the vessels of a potter shall they be broken to shivers"
- **Rev 12:5** — "a man child, who was to rule (poimainein) all nations with a rod of iron"
- **Rev 19:15** — "he shall rule (poimanei) them with a rod of iron: and he treadeth the winepress of the fierceness and wrath of Almighty God"

### 2. Pastoral Care of the Church
Imperative/infinitive commands to church leaders — tend, protect, nourish the congregation.
- **Jhn 21:16** — "Feed (poimaine) my sheep" — Jesus's post-resurrection charge to Peter
- **Acts 20:28** — "to feed (poimainein) the church of God, which he hath purchased with his own blood"
- **1Pet 5:2** — "Feed (poimanate) the flock of God which is among you, taking the oversight thereof"

### 3. The Lamb as Eschatological Shepherd
A reversal: the Lamb who was slain now shepherds the redeemed.
- **Rev 7:17** — "the Lamb which is in the midst of the throne shall feed (poimanei) them, and shall lead them unto living fountains of waters"

### 4. Literal Shepherding / Self-Serving Feeding
The word used of actual livestock care, or pejoratively of selfish leaders.
- **Luk 17:7** — "having a servant plowing or feeding cattle (poimainonta)"
- **1Cor 9:7** — "who feedeth (poimainei) a flock, and eateth not of the milk of the flock?"
- **Jude 1:12** — "feeding (poimainontes) themselves without fear: clouds they are without water"
