## Lexicon Definition

From נָדַר (H5087); a promise (to God); also (concretely) a thing promised — vow(-ed).

Neder is a concrete noun denoting either the verbal act of vowing to God or, metonymically, the thing (offering, animal, person, or consecrated property) promised. It is built on the cognate verb H5087 (nadar) and frequently appears with it in the figura etymologica "to vow a vow" (נָדַר נֶדֶר).

## Semantic Categories

### 1. The Conditional "If–Then" Vow to God
A promise offered in distress or petition, obligating the vower to a specific return if God acts.
- **Gen 28:20** — "And Jacob vowed a vow, saying, If God will be with me..."
- **Num 21:2** — "And Israel vowed a vow unto the LORD... If thou wilt indeed deliver this people into my hand..."
- **Judg 11:30** — "And Jephthah vowed a vow unto the LORD..."
- **1 Sam 1:11** — "And she [Hannah] vowed a vow, and said, O LORD of hosts, if thou wilt indeed look..."

### 2. The Vow as Sacrificial Offering
Neder designates a class of voluntary offering alongside burnt, peace, and freewill offerings.
- **Lev 7:16** — "if the sacrifice of his offering be a vow, or a voluntary offering"
- **Lev 22:21** — "a sacrifice of peace offerings unto the LORD to accomplish his vow"
- **Num 15:3, 8** — "in performing a vow, or in a freewill offering"
- **Deut 12:6, 11, 17, 26** — vows brought to the central sanctuary

### 3. The Nazirite Vow (Personal Consecration)
A self-dedicatory separation to the LORD.
- **Num 6:2** — "When either man or woman shall separate themselves... to vow a vow of a Nazarite"
- **Num 6:5** — "All the days of the vow of his separation"
- **Num 6:21** — "This is the law of the Nazarite who hath vowed"

### 4. The Woman's Vow Under Authority (Num 30)
Extensive legal treatment of who may ratify or annul a woman's vow — father or husband.
- **Num 30:3** — "If a woman also vow a vow unto the LORD, and bind herself by a bond"
- **Num 30:5** — "if her father disallow her... not any of her vows... shall stand"
- **Num 30:9** — "But every vow of a widow, and of her that is divorced... shall stand"
- **Num 30:13** — "Every vow, and every binding oath to afflict the soul"

### 5. Paying / Fulfilling Vows (Liturgical Idiom)
Vows are "paid" (שָׁלַם, H7999) to the LORD, usually in the sanctuary.
- **Deut 23:21** — "thou shalt not slack to pay it"
- **2 Sam 15:7** — "let me go and pay my vow which I have vowed"
- **Ps 50:14** — "pay thy vows unto the most High"
- **Ps 116:14, 18** — "I will pay my vows unto the LORD"
- **Ecc 5:4** — "When thou vowest a vow unto God, defer not to pay it"

### 6. The Danger of Rash Vowing
Wisdom and prophetic warnings about careless or defiling vows.
- **Pro 20:25** — "It is a snare to the man who devoureth that which is holy, and after vows to make inquiry"
- **Ecc 5:4** — "he hath no pleasure in fools: pay that which thou hast vowed"
- **Deut 23:18** — "Thou shalt not bring the hire of a whore... for any vow"
- **Jer 44:25** — vows to the queen of heaven condemned

### 7. Gentile / Non-Israelite Vows
Neder used for sincere vows by outsiders.
- **Jon 1:16** — the sailors "offered a sacrifice unto the LORD, and made vows"
- **Isa 19:21** — "the Egyptians... shall vow a vow unto the LORD, and perform it"
