## Lexicon Definition

To subdue (literally or figuratively); to conquer, overcome, prevail, gain the victory. From the root νίκη (nikē, "victory"). Used of military conquest, moral triumph, legal vindication, and eschatological victory.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Eschatological Victory — Believers Overcoming (Promises to the Churches)
The dominant usage: promises to faithful believers who "overcome" in the letters to the seven churches and the final vision.
- **Rev 2:7** — "To him that overcometh will I give to eat of the tree of life"
- **Rev 2:11** — "He that overcometh shall not be hurt of the second death"
- **Rev 2:26** — "And he that overcometh, and keepeth my works unto the end, to him will I give power over the nations"
- **Rev 3:21** — "To him that overcometh will I grant to sit with me in my throne"
- **Rev 21:7** — "He that overcometh shall inherit all things; and I will be his God, and he shall be my son"
- **1 John 5:4** — "whatsoever is born of God overcometh the world: and this is the victory that overcometh the world, even our faith"

### 2. Cosmic Victory — Christ Overcoming
Christ's own victory as the basis for believers' victory.
- **John 16:33** — "be of good cheer; I have overcome the world"
- **Rev 5:5** — "the Lion of the tribe of Juda, the Root of David, hath prevailed to open the book"
- **Rev 17:14** — "the Lamb shall overcome them: for he is Lord of lords, and King of kings"
- **Rev 3:21** — "even as I also overcame, and am set down with my Father in his throne"

### 3. Moral Victory — Overcoming Evil
- **Rom 12:21** — "Be not overcome of evil, but overcome evil with good" (both active and passive in one verse)
- **1 John 2:13** — "ye have overcome the wicked one"
- **1 John 4:4** — "Ye are of God, little children, and have overcome them"

### 4. Military/Hostile Conquest — Adversary Overcoming
Used of enemies overcoming God's people or of physical military victory.
- **Luke 11:22** — "when a stronger than he shall come upon him, and overcome him, he taketh from him all his armour"
- **Rev 11:7** — "the beast that ascendeth out of the bottomless pit shall make war against them, and shall overcome them, and kill them"
- **Rev 13:7** — "it was given unto him to make war with the saints, and to overcome them"
- **Rev 6:2** — "he went forth conquering, and to conquer"

### 5. Legal/Forensic Vindication
- **Rom 3:4** — "That thou mightest be justified in thy sayings, and mightest overcome when thou art judged" (quoting Psalm 51:4 LXX, where Hebrew has זָכָה *zakah*, "be clear/pure")
