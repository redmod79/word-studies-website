## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same across testaments:**
- Core referent: the cessation of biological life — maveth and thanatos share this in all contexts
- Death as cosmic power: the OT already personifies maveth as a force (Hos 13:14; Jer 9:21 "death come up into our windows") — NT continues this in Rev 6:8 and 1 Cor 15:26
- Death as consequence: Deut 21:22 (sin worthy of death) → Rom 6:23 (wages of sin is death) — the moral logic is identical
- LXX continuity: NT writers quoting death-related OT texts use thanatos as the transparent equivalent of maveth with no semantic adjustment needed

**What shifts:**
- **Christological focus:** The NT concentrates maveth/thanatos into a single decisive event — Christ's death — making θάνατος the noun around which atonement theology organizes. The OT scatters maveth across mortality in general.
- **Resurrection reversal:** The OT knows victory over death as future hope (Isa 25:8; Hos 13:14). The NT announces it as accomplished fact (1 Cor 15:20; 2 Tim 1:10 — Christ "hath abolished death").
- **Second death (eschatological):** Rev 20:6, 14; 21:8 add a new category absent from OT usage — a final post-resurrection death, "the lake of fire," that has no direct maveth parallel.
- **Spiritual death:** Rom 8:6 ("to be carnally minded is death") and 1 John 3:14 ("passed from death unto life") extend thanatos into a metaphysical present state — a development beyond OT maveth's primarily physical register.
