# WG: Refining / Testing

## Overview
The fire-refining and proving cluster in Hebrew Scripture describes the process of testing through heat and examination. The dominant image is the metalworker who applies fire to ore, burning away dross to recover pure metal. This image is applied metaphorically both to God testing His people and to the reliability of God's own word. The cluster overlaps with but is distinct from ritual purity vocabulary (taher/tame): these words focus on *proof through trial* rather than *cleanness by washing*.

## Roles in the Concept

- **Primary agent:** tsaraph (H6884) — the smelting/refining act itself (fire applied to metal or person)
- **Secondary agent:** bachan (H974) — examination and proof (weights, character, hearts)
- **Result:** barar (H1305) — the clarified, purified, chosen outcome of the process
- **Instrument:** mitzreph (H4715) — the physical crucible in which refining happens

## Key Passages Where Multiple Group Words Appear Together

- **Pro 17:3** — "The fining pot (mitzreph H4715) is for silver, and the furnace for gold: but the LORD trieth (bachan H974) the hearts" — instrument + testing verb in one proverb
- **Dan 11:35** — "to try them (tsaraph H6884), and to purge (barar H1305), and to make them white" — two group words as parallel verbs describing end-time refinement
- **Psa 66:10** — "thou hast tried (bachan H974) us… thou hast tried (tsaraph H6884) us, as silver is tried (tsaraph)" — both verbs in the same verse, used together deliberately
