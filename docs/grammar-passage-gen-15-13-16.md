## Clause Structure

### v.13 — six clauses (speech-report + content)

| # | Type | Domain | Text | Function |
|---|------|--------|------|----------|
| 1 | **Way0** | N (narr.) | וַיֹּ֣אמֶר לְאַבְרָ֗ם | wayyiqtol speech-frame |
| 2 | **xYq0** | Q (quot.) | יָדֹ֨עַ תֵּדַ֜ע | InfAbs + Impf. emphasis |
| 3 | **xYqX** | Q | כִּי גֵ֣ר יִהְיֶ֣ה זַרְעֲךָ֗ בְּאֶ֨רֶץ֙ | content-clause under *kî* |
| 4 | **NmCl** | Q | לֹ֣א לָהֶ֔ם | verbless rel.-gapped NmCl ("[which is] not theirs") |
| 5 | **WQt0** | Q | וַעֲבָד֖וּם | **weqatal** continuation |
| 6 | **WQt0** | Q | וְעִנּ֣וּ אֹתָ֑ם אַרְבַּ֥ע מֵאֹ֖ות שָׁנָֽה | weqatal + acc.-of-duration |

Key shift: the main predicate יִהְיֶ֣ה is **Qal imperfect** (*yiqtol* = non-perfective future), but the two follow-up verbs עֲבָד֖וּם and עִנּ֣וּ are morphologically **perfect** (*qatal*) — functioning as *weqatal* (waw-consecutive perfect) to extend the future/modal force of the preceding yiqtol (GKC §112; IBHS §32.1–2; Joüon §119). The *morphology* is perfect; the *syntax* is futural.

### v.16 — two clauses

| # | Type | Domain | Text | Function |
|---|------|--------|------|----------|
| 1 | **WXYq** | Q | וְדֹ֥ור רְבִיעִ֖י יָשׁ֣וּבוּ הֵ֑נָּה | waw + fronted Subj + yiqtol: return-prediction |
| 2 | **AjCl** | Q | כִּ֧י לֹא שָׁלֵ֛ם עֲוֹ֥ן הָאֱמֹרִ֖י עַד־הֵֽנָּה | *kî*-adjunct verbless clause |

Clause 2 is **verbless** (*Adjective Clause*, BHSA "AjCl"): *šālēm* is an **adjective** (Adj.ms.Abs), not a verb. The Amorite-iniquity clause has **no finite verb at all** — no perfect, no imperfect. Tense is unmarked; context supplies the temporal value "is not yet full [at the moment Abram is addressed]."

## Construct Chains

Source: `hebrew_parser.py --construct "Gen 15:13"` / `…15:16"`.

- **v.13** אַרְבַּ֥ע מֵאֹ֖ות שָׁנָֽה ("four hundred years") — three-member numerical apposition with אַרְבַּע (Cst "four") governing מֵאֹות (Abs "hundred") and שָׁנָה (Abs "year"). The first term is construct; the two absolutes stand in numerical-apposition rather than a cascading possessive chain (Gesenius §134 on cardinal numerals; Waltke-O'Connor §15.2.5 on apposition with cardinals).
- **v.16** עֲוֹ֥ן הָאֱמֹרִ֖י ("iniquity of the Amorite") — two-member chain: **עֲוֹן** (Noun.ms.**Cst**) + **הָאֱמֹרִי** (Adj.ms.Abs, definite with article). The nomen rectum is definite ⇒ the whole chain is definite: "**the** iniquity **of** the Amorite(s)". The Amorite is presented as a **corporate singular** (gentilic singular + article), matching the "generation" singular of the preceding clause.

## Key Grammatical Features

### 1. *yāḏōaʿ tēḏaʿ* (v.13) — Qal **Infinitive Absolute + Imperfect** emphatic

- **Form:** Qal InfAbs יָדֹ֨עַ (unvaryingly anarthrous, preverbal, no person/number marking, fixed stem-vowel pattern *qātôl*) + Qal **yiqtol 2ms** תֵּדַ֜ע of the same root ידע (H3045).
- **Function:** The preverbal **InfAbs + finite verb** construction intensifies the verb's modality (GKC §113n; Joüon §123d–l; IBHS §35.3.1f). With a future-modal *yiqtol* this yields a **solemn assurance** — KJV "know of a surety," RSV "know of a certainty," "surely know." The construction is not a poetic flourish but a formal oath-register rhetorical device.
- **Significance:** The InfAbs+Impf opens the divine speech and covers *everything that follows in vv. 13–16* with an oath-strength guarantee. It parallels the same idiom at Gen 2:16–17 מֹות תָּמוּת, 1 Sam 14:44, 1 Kgs 2:42 — locus where YHWH or a king pledges that the next clause will happen.
- **Grammar reference:** [hebrew/infinitive-absolute-intensification](../hebrew/infinitive-absolute-intensification.md); imperfect aspect [hebrew/qal-imperfect](../hebrew/qal-imperfect.md).
- **Word study:** [H3045-yada](../../word-studies/H3045-yada.md).

### 2. *yihyeh* + *wa-ʿăbādûm* + *wə-ʿinnû* (v.13) — **yiqtol → weqatal** chain (futural)

- **Form:** Main predicate יִהְיֶ֣ה (Qal.Impf.3ms of היה) followed by two *waw-consecutive perfects*:
  - עֲבָד֖וּם = Qal.Perf.3mp + 3mp obj. suffix ("and they shall serve them")
  - עִנּ֣וּ = Piel.Perf.3mp ("and they shall afflict")
- **Function:** Despite the *qatal* morphology, these are **weqatal** forms whose syntactic role is to continue the future/modal force of the preceding yiqtol (GKC §112; Joüon §119). The Piel of ענה (H6031, "to afflict, humble, oppress") adds **factitive-intensifying** force ("bring-about-a-state of lowness") over against Qal ענה "be-lowly." Subject-number shifts from sg. זַרְעֲךָ ("thy seed" — collective singular with 3ms verb *yihyeh*) to pl. ("they shall serve … afflict") by ad-sensum agreement.
- **Significance:** The three-verb chain **stranger → serve → afflict** gives the tripartite shape of the sojourn-oppression prophecy. Piel ענה reuses the same verb that marks Egypt's oppression narratively at Exo 1:11–12, linking prediction to fulfilment lexically. See also Num 14:34 where Qal yiqtol *tiśʾû* and weqatal *wîdaʿtem* form the same future-chain syntax.
- **Grammar reference:** [hebrew/syntax-waw-conjunction](../hebrew/syntax-waw-conjunction.md), [hebrew/qal-imperfect](../hebrew/qal-imperfect.md), [hebrew/weqatal-apodosis-vow](../hebrew/weqatal-apodosis-vow.md) (weqatal-after-yiqtol pattern).
- **Word study:** [H1961-hayah](../../word-studies/H1961-hayah.md).

### 3. *ʾarbaʿ mēʾôt šānāh* (v.13) — **accusative of duration** (400 years)

- **Form:** Cardinal construct **אַרְבַּע** (Noun.s.Cst "four") + absolute plural **מֵאֹות** ("hundreds") + absolute singular **שָׁנָה** ("year"). The counted noun is in the *singular* after "hundred(s)," following the normal Hebrew pattern for numerals ≥ 200 (GKC §134g; Joüon §142n). The full phrase stands in adverbial **accusative of extent/duration** position — no preposition, no *lamed*.
- **Function:** Accusative of duration answers "for how long?" and externalises a bounded span over the entire weqatal chain (serve + afflict). It is a **single time-envelope** over the whole prediction, not merely over the last verb.
- **Significance — the 400/430-year count:** The 400-year figure here is repeated with the same syntax at Acts 7:6 (ἔτη τετρακόσια, Stephen quoting this verse in LXX-form); Exo 12:40 gives the kindred-but-distinct figure **430 years** for the actual sojourn (מוֹשַׁב "dwelling"). Gen 15:13's 400 is a **round prophetic number** applied to the entire seed-affliction; Exo 12:40's 430 is a measured chronological span. Both are accusatives of duration. Which events are counted (Abram's call? Isaac's birth? Joseph's descent? the beginning of affliction?) is **not specified by the grammar** — the syntax fixes the duration but not its *termini*.
- **Grammar reference:** accusative-of-duration entry not yet canonicalised (gap — candidate for `hebrew/accusative-of-duration.md`; Greek parallel exists at [greek/aorist-infinitive-accusative-of-duration](../greek/aorist-infinitive-accusative-of-duration.md)).
- **Word study:** [H3117-yowm](../../word-studies/H3117-yowm.md), [H8141-shaneh](../../word-studies/H8141-shaneh.md).

### 4. *dān ʾānōkî* (v.14) — **Qal participle + fronted pronoun** (emphatic divine adjudication)

- **Form:** Qal Ptcp.ms.Abs of דין (H1777 "judge") + 1cs pronoun **אָנֹכִי** (the "heavy" form of *ʾănî*). Participle precedes the pronoun; the pronoun follows in topic-focus position.
- **Function:** *ʾānōkî* (vs. shorter *ʾănî*) typically introduces solemn divine self-identification (Exo 20:2 אָנֹכִי יהוה אֱלֹהֶיךָ; Gen 28:13). The Ptcp+Pron sequence here is essentially a **verbless predicate-nominal** construction ("judging-one [am] I") with futural/imminent force — the Ptcp of *dîn* + subject pronoun forming the equivalent of a performative oath: "I **will** judge."
- **Significance:** The grammar lifts the judgment-of-the-nation above the surrounding imperfect-weqatal mainline. v. 14 is framed as **YHWH's personal, emphatic self-bound act** ("**I Myself** will be the judge"), distinct from the impersonal "they shall serve / afflict" of v. 13. The ptcp+pronoun idiom gives *imminent-future state* — not merely prediction, but self-commitment.
- **Grammar reference:** none yet canonicalised for Ptcp + *ʾānōkî*; partial overlap with [hebrew/symbol-decoding-predicate-nominal](../hebrew/symbol-decoding-predicate-nominal.md) verbless-clause grammar.

### 5. *tiqqābēr* (v.15) — **Niphal imperfect** 2ms (passive future)

- **Form:** Niphal.Impf.2ms תִּקָּבֵ֖ר of קבר (H6912 "bury"). Niphal morphology: prefix ת + doubled/assimilated nun + theme vowel *ē* (*tiqqābēr* ← *tinqābēr*). Niphal of קבר is lexicalised **passive** ("be buried").
- **Function:** Straight passive — "you shall be buried." Paired with active Qal.Impf.2ms תָּבֹא ("you shall come/go [to your fathers]") in the same verse, producing an active→passive pair describing the two moments of Abram's death-and-burial.
- **Significance:** The **Qal/Niphal active-passive pair** within one verse is a standard Hebrew way of marking a biographical closure as a decreed-but-peaceful event. The accompanying adjunct בְּשָׁלוֹם ("in peace") is anchored by the passive verb — Abram's personal end is grammatically *quiet* even as the grammar around it (vv. 13, 16) is violent.
- **Grammar reference:** [hebrew/stem-niphal](../hebrew/stem-niphal.md), [hebrew/niphal-imperfect-divine-denial](../hebrew/niphal-imperfect-divine-denial.md) (Niphal-imperfect morphology exemplified).

### 6. *dôr rəbîʿî* (v.16) — "**fourth generation**" — WXYq return-prediction

- **Form:** **וְדֹור רְבִיעִי** — Conj + Noun.ms.**Abs** דור (H1755) + ordinal Adj.ms.**Abs** רְבִיעִי. Clause type **WXYq** (waw + fronted non-verbal X + yiqtol): the subject is fronted for topical contrast with the preceding 400-year span.
- **Function of *dôr*:** In Hebrew, דור (H1755) is a **flexible time-span term**, not a fixed calendar unit. It can denote:
  - a *lifespan* ("a generation" as the life-arc of an individual, Job 42:16 "four generations" tracked as Job's remaining life);
  - an *age-cohort* (Ps 78:6; Deut 32:5, 20);
  - an *epoch / era* (Isa 51:8 *dôr dôrîm*; Ps 33:11 *ledōr wādōr*);
  - a *genealogical link* between ancestor and descendant (Gen 7:1 "in this generation").
  - Lexical range from lifespan-of-Job (~140 years) down to shorter filial spacing — Gen 15:16's *dôr* must be calibrated against the 400-year frame of v. 13. Four × 100 ≈ 400, which fits the *dôr*-as-century reading; four × ~80–100 ≈ 320–400 also fits. Genealogies in Exo 6:16–20 (Levi → Kohath → Amram → Moses = four generations) provide one canonical calibration; Num 32:13 (Israel wanders till "all that generation… was consumed") provides another.
- **Significance:** The grammar gives only (a) the cardinal position ("the **fourth**" in a series) and (b) a return-destination הֵנָּה ("hither" — to Canaan, the land of the present speech-act). **It does not fix the length of the *dôr*.** That is carried by the 400-year accusative of v. 13, by the Exo 6 / Num 14 genealogical data, and by Exo 12:40. The two numbers (400 years / 4 generations) are co-predicated of the same event; the syntax of Gen 15 does not tell the reader how to reconcile them — any reconciliation is hermeneutical, not grammatical.
- **Grammar reference:** [hebrew/syntax-wx-qatal-circumstantial](../hebrew/syntax-wx-qatal-circumstantial.md) (WX-yiqtol is the futural analogue of WX-qatal circumstantial); fronted-subject topicalization.
- **Word studies:** no existing entry for **H1755 דור** (*dôr*) — **gap**, high-priority candidate for `word-studies/H1755-dowr.md`; related **H8141 שׁנה** covered at [H8141-shaneh](../../word-studies/H8141-shaneh.md); [H3318-yatsa](../../word-studies/H3318-yatsa.md) (v.14 *yēṣəʾû*); [H1961-hayah](../../word-studies/H1961-hayah.md) (v.13 *yihyeh*).

### 7. *lōʾ šālēm ʿăwōn hā-ʾĕmōrî* (v.16) — **verbless AjCl with stative adjective** *šālēm*

- **Form:** The clause has **no verb**. ETCBC/BHSA tags it **AjCl** (Adjective Clause), a subtype of NmCl where the predicate is an adjective.
  - Conj **כִּי** ("for")
  - Negation **לֹא** ("not")
  - PreC **שָׁלֵם** — Adj.ms.Abs of שׁלם (H8003, "complete, whole, finished, full")
  - Subj **עֲוֹן הָאֱמֹרִי** — definite construct chain ("the iniquity of the Amorite")
  - Time adjunct **עַד־הֵנָּה** ("up to here" = "up to this point / as yet")
- **Function of *šālēm*:** This is the **adjective** *šālēm* (H8003, "complete, full"), **not** the Piel/Qal verb *šillēm / šālam* (H7999, "to be at peace, be completed, repay"). The distinction matters:
  - H8003 **שָׁלֵם** (adj.) = "whole, intact, complete, at-full-measure" (Gen 15:16 *lōʾ šālēm*; Deut 25:15 אֶבֶן שְׁלֵמָה "a full/perfect stone-weight"; Deut 27:6 אֲבָנִים שְׁלֵמוֹת "whole stones"; 1 Kgs 6:7 אֶבֶן־שְׁלֵמָה "stone made ready"; Prov 11:1 אֶבֶן שְׁלֵמָה "a just weight"; the noun שָׁלוֹם *šālôm* H7965 is the closest semantic neighbour but a distinct lexeme).
  - H7999 **שׁלם** (verb) Piel = "to repay, requite, fulfil"; Qal = "to be complete, at peace." Same consonantal root; BHSA treats the forms distinctly by part-of-speech.
- **Significance:** Because the clause is **verbless**, there is **no grammatical tense marker** on the central claim. The temporal value "[iniquity] is **not yet** complete" comes entirely from the phrase *ʿad-hēnnāh* ("up to this point / as yet / hitherto") — the Hebrew equivalent of English "not yet." The grammar offers a **state-description** (the Amorite iniquity is currently under full-measure), not a dynamic prediction. This is an **aspect-neutral stative** predication, which is why English translators must add the implicit *is*/*is yet* ("iniquity is not *yet* full"). What the clause licenses:
  - A *measured* or *filled-up* conception of guilt (iniquity as a vessel with a set capacity, paralleling Dan 8:23 עַד־הָתֵם הַפֹּשְׁעִים "when transgressors have come to full-measure"; Matt 23:32 πληρώσατε τὸ μέτρον τῶν πατέρων ὑμῶν "fill up the measure of your fathers"; 1 Thess 2:16 εἰς τὸ ἀναπληρῶσαι αὐτῶν τὰς ἁμαρτίας πάντοτε).
  - A *delay-reason* for the 400-year span: the imperfect verb יָשׁוּבוּ ("they shall return") of the preceding clause has as its grammatical cause (כִּי) not Israel's unreadiness but the **Amorite's unripeness for judgment**.
- **Construct chain:** *ʿăwōn hā-ʾĕmōrî* definite by anchor-definiteness of the gentilic-singular-with-article הָאֱמֹרִי. "Amorite" is grammatically a **collective singular** (Adj.ms.Abs functioning substantively, same as הַכְּנַעֲנִי, הַחִתִּי in the same contexts) representing the whole population as a single corporate entity (cross-reference [hebrew/corporate-solidarity](../hebrew/corporate-solidarity.md)).
- **Grammar reference:** adjective clause / verbless NmCl — see [hebrew/symbol-decoding-predicate-nominal](../hebrew/symbol-decoding-predicate-nominal.md) for the verbless-clause family; tense-neutral stative grammar has no canonical entry yet.
- **Word studies:** [H5771-avon](../../word-studies/H5771-avon.md) (*ʿāwōn*, "iniquity"); **no existing entry for H8003 שָׁלֵם** — **gap**, high-priority candidate for `word-studies/H8003-shalem.md` (especially given its KJV translation here as "full" and its distinctness from H7999 שׁלם and H7965 שׁלום); no entry for **H567 אֱמֹרִי** (Amorite) — candidate word-study.

Pattern: the passage has **no finite qatal** (perfect) that is not a *weqatal*. Every independent verb is either (a) *wayyiqtol* in the narrative frame (v. 13a וַיֹּאמֶר), (b) *yiqtol* (imperfect, future/modal), (c) participle *dān* (imminent-state), or (d) absent altogether (v. 16 verbless). The absence of true perfects is itself diagnostic: this is **pure prophecy**, discourse in the futural/modal register, anchored only to the moment of the wayyiqtol speech-frame. The one exception — the verbless *lōʾ šālēm* clause — is **stative**, describing a presently-holding condition that functions as the *reason* for the 400-year delay.

## The Delay-of-Judgment Syntax (Summary)

Two clauses bear the theological weight of vv. 13–16, and both have unusual grammar:

1. **v. 13b–c:** *yiqtol + weqatal + weqatal + accusative-of-duration* — a single syntactic unit encompassing "sojourn + servitude + affliction" under one 400-year time-envelope. Morphology is "perfect" but function is future; duration is accusative but covers the whole chain. No preposition on *ʾarbaʿ mēʾôt šānāh* — the phrase simply **sits in the acc. slot** adverbially.
2. **v. 16b:** a **verbless adjective-clause** negated with *lōʾ* and bounded by *ʿad-hēnnāh*, giving the reason (*kî*) for the delay of return. No verb = no tense = state-description: *šālēm* is a **measure predicate** ("complete, full-up, at-capacity"), applied to *ʿăwōn hā-ʾĕmōrî*. The delay is grammatically *not* about Israel (who are simply subjects of the future *yāšûbû*); it is about the Amorite's unfilled iniquity-measure.

Reading grammatically, v. 16 says: "And a fourth *dôr* they shall return hither — because the Amorite's iniquity [is] not-at-full-measure as-yet." The predicate *šālēm* is what licenses the theological category of **"full measure of iniquity"** (cf. Dan 8:23; 2 Macc 6:14; Matt 23:32; 1 Thess 2:16) — but the application of that category to any specific historical moment (the Conquest, Jerusalem AD 70, final judgment) is extra-grammatical. The grammar provides the *shape* of the delay-of-judgment reasoning; it does not close the hermeneutical question of *when* any given iniquity-measure reaches שָׁלֵם.
