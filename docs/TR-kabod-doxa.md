## The Bridge

**LXX co-occurrences:** 170 (PMI 6.32, score 32.49) — the highest-scored Hebrew source for δόξα by a wide margin.

The Septuagint translators chose δόξα to render כָּבוֹד with exceptional consistency, and in so doing transformed the Greek word. Classical δόξα meant *opinion* or *reputation* — what others think of you. But kabod carries the weight of *inherent, manifested reality* — what a person or deity actually is, expressed visibly. The LXX translation elevated δόξα from subjective perception to objective radiance, so that by the NT era, δόξα had absorbed kabod's full freight of divine Shekinah, covenant honor, and eschatological expectation.

## Hebrew Anchor: כָּבוֹד (H3519)

**Occurrences:** 189 unique verses across 27 OT books
**Primary translations (KJV):** glory (22 vv), the glory (40 vv), honour (10 vv), glorious (5 vv)
**Root:** כָּבַד (kabed, H3513) — to be heavy, to be weighty

The concrete root (heaviness, weight) generates the abstract meaning: what has *weight* commands *honor*. In divine contexts, kabod becomes the Shekinah — the tangible, luminous presence of God that fills the tabernacle and temple.

### Key NT Passages Echoing OT Kabod

**Prologue (John 1:14):**

*— σκηνόω (dwelt/tabernacled) deliberately echoes the tabernacle-filling of Exo 40:34. The Shekinah kabod now embodied.*

**Transfiguration (Luke 9:32):**
"when they were awake, they saw his glory, and the two men that stood with him"
*— Moses and Elijah appear; the visible radiance of Sinai/tabernacle now rests on Christ.*

**Isaiah quotation (John 12:41):**
"These things said Esaias, when he saw his glory, and spake of him."
*— The kabod Isaiah saw in Isa 6 is identified as the glory of the pre-incarnate Christ.*

**New Temple (Rev 21:23):**
"And the city had no need of the sun, neither of the moon, to shine in it: for the glory (doxa) of God did lighten it, and the Lamb is the light thereof."
*— The eschatological fulfillment of the Shekinah: no constructed sanctuary needed; God's doxa is the light directly.*

**Prophetic fulfillment (Rom 8:18):**
"For I reckon that the sufferings of this present time are not worthy to be compared with the glory (doxa) which shall be revealed in us."
*— Isa 40:5's promise of universal kabod revelation applied to the resurrection state of believers.*

## The Semantic Shift

| Aspect | OT כָּבוֹד (kabod) | NT δόξα (doxa) |
|--------|-----------------|--------------|
| **Physical basis** | Pillar of cloud/fire, filling the sanctuary | Light, brilliance of transformation |
| **Location** | Tabernacle, Temple, Sinai | Christ, believers, new creation |
| **Grammar** | Often "the glory of the LORD" (divine title) | Often "glory of God" + doxologies |
| **Temporal frame** | Present Shekinah, covenant reminder | Past (pre-existent), present (incarnate), future (eschatological) |
| **Human share** | Israel receives reflected glory (Num 14:21) | Believers transformed from glory to glory (2 Cor 3:18) |
