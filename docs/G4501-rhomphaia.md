## Semantic Categories

### 1. Prophetic Soul-Piercing Grief
The only non-Revelation use — Simeon's prophecy that grief will penetrate Mary like a sword blade, exposing the inner conflict Christ provokes.

- **Luke 2:35** — "(Yea, a sword shall pierce through thy own soul also,) that the thoughts of many hearts may be revealed."

### 2. Sword Proceeding from Christ's Mouth
The dominant Revelation image: rhomphaia (not machaira) as the specific sword that issues from Christ's mouth — his spoken word as an instrument of sovereign judgment. Emphasized as sharp and two-edged.

- **Rev 1:16** — "out of his mouth went a sharp twoedged sword: and his countenance was as the sun shineth in his strength."
- **Rev 2:12** — "These things saith he which hath the sharp sword with two edges."
- **Rev 2:16** — "I will come unto thee quickly, and will fight against them with the sword of my mouth."
- **Rev 19:15** — "out of his mouth goeth a sharp sword, that with it he should smite the nations."

### 3. Eschatological Weapon of Judgment
Used in apocalyptic scenes where rhomphaia is the instrument of death — either listed among the horsemen's weapons (Rev 6) or as the means by which the enemies of Christ are slain (Rev 19).

- **Rev 6:8** — "power was given unto them over the fourth part of the earth, to kill with sword (rhomphaia), and with hunger, and with death, and with the beasts of the earth."
- **Rev 19:21** — "the remnant were slain with the sword of him that sat upon the horse, which sword proceeded out of his mouth."
