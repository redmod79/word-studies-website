## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same across testaments:**
- Sophia/chokmah is consistently presented as originating from God — both OT and NT treat wisdom as a divine gift (Exo 28:3 "filled with the spirit of wisdom"; Jas 1:5 "ask of God"; 1 Cor 12:8 "by the Spirit")
- The practical/ethical dimension persists — OT wisdom guides conduct (Deu 4:6), NT wisdom guides conduct (Col 4:5 "walk in wisdom"; Jas 3:13 "shew out of a good conversation")
- Personification of wisdom appears in both — OT Wisdom literature (Pro 8:22-31) and NT (Luk 11:49 "the wisdom of God said"; 1 Cor 1:30 Christ "made unto us wisdom")

**What shifts:**
- The NT introduces a sharp worldly-vs-divine polarity absent from most OT usage. Paul's sophia-of-this-world vs. sophia-of-God contrast (1 Cor 1-3) has no close OT parallel — OT chokmah is generally treated as unitary (the fear of the LORD is its beginning)
- Christ becomes identified with wisdom itself (1 Cor 1:24, 30) — the OT personification of Wisdom in Proverbs 8 remains a figure; Paul collapses the figure into a named person
- The "mystery" dimension emerges: sophia as "hidden" wisdom "ordained before the world" (1 Cor 2:7) develops beyond OT categories, where chokmah is generally accessible to the seeker
