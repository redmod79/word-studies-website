## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same across testaments:**
- The core meaning "to circumcise" passes directly from Hebrew to Greek with no semantic distortion
- The physical-to-spiritual metaphor trajectory already present in the OT (Deut 10:16, 30:6, Jer 4:4) is maintained and developed in the NT
- The association with covenant identity persists — circumcision marks covenant membership in both testaments

**What shifts:**
- In the OT, physical circumcision is universally commanded for covenant participation (Gen 17:14, Exo 12:48). In the NT, physical circumcision becomes optional or even counterproductive for Gentile believers (Gal 5:2-3, 1 Cor 7:18-19)
- The OT's heart-circumcision promise (Deut 30:6) is eschatological — something God **will** do. The NT treats it as fulfilled in Christ (Col 2:11) — something God **has** done
- The Deut 10:16 imperative ("circumcise your heart yourselves") becomes Stephen's indictment of those who failed to do so (Acts 7:51)
