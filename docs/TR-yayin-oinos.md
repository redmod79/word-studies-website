## Continuity and Shifts

**What stays the same across testaments:**
- Wine as a literal agricultural product and daily beverage
- Warnings against excess/drunkenness (Lev 10:9 → Eph 5:18; Prov 20:1 → 1Ti 3:8)
- Abstinence for those set apart (Num 6:3 Nazirite → Luk 1:15 John the Baptist)
- Babylon's wine as a metaphor for deceptive, intoxicating false teaching that corrupts nations (Jer 51:7 → Rev 14:8, 17:2, 18:3)

**What shifts:**
- The OT uses yayin across wisdom, worship, blessing, and judgment contexts in roughly equal measure. The NT concentrates oinos heavily in two clusters: the Gospels (parabolic/narrative) and Revelation (apocalyptic judgment). The middle NT (epistles) uses it sparingly, only for conduct instructions.
- Revelation develops the "Babylon's wine" image far beyond its Jeremiah seed, creating a double-cup motif: Babylon's cup of fornication-wine (Rev 14:8, 17:2, 18:3) answered by God's cup of wrath-wine (Rev 14:10, 16:19, 19:15).
