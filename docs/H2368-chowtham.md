## Lexicon Definition

A signature-ring; seal, signet. From חָתַם (chatham, H2856). Also written חֹתָם. The physical instrument used for making seal impressions — the signet ring or seal stone.

## Semantic Categories

### 1. Standard of Engraving Craftsmanship
- **Exo 28:11** — "With the work of an engraver in stone, like the engravings of a signet, shalt thou engrave the two stones with the names of the children of Israel"
- **Exo 28:21** — "the stones shall be with the names of the children of Israel... like the engravings of a signet, every one with his name"
- **Exo 28:36** — "thou shalt make a plate of pure gold, and grave upon it, like the engravings of a signet, HOLINESS TO THE LORD"
- **Exo 39:6** — "graven, as signets are graven, with the names of the children of Israel"
- **Exo 39:14** — "according to the names of the children of Israel... like the engravings of a signet"
- **Exo 39:30** — "wrote upon it a writing, like to the engravings of a signet, HOLINESS TO THE LORD"

The phrase *pittuchey chowtham* ("engravings of a signet") occurs 6 times, always in the priestly garment context. The signet sets the standard for precision engraving — the finest and most exact work known to Israelite craftsmen.

### 2. Personal Pledge / Identity Token
- **Gen 38:18** — "she said, Thy signet, and thy bracelets, and thy staff that is in thine hand"

Judah's chowtham functions as a personal identifier so distinctive that it serves as irrefutable proof of identity (Gen 38:25). Surrendering one's signet ring was surrendering one's personal authority.

### 3. Instrument of Royal Authority
- **1 Ki 21:8** — "she wrote letters in Ahab's name, and sealed them with his seal"

Jezebel uses Ahab's chowtham to authenticate forged letters with royal authority, showing that the signet ring carried the legal weight of the person it represented.

### 4. Metaphor for Intimacy and Permanence
- **Song 8:6** — "Set me as a seal upon thine heart, as a seal upon thine arm: for love is strong as death"
- **Jer 22:24** — "though Coniah the son of Jehoiakim king of Judah were the signet upon my right hand, yet would I pluck thee thence"
- **Hag 2:23** — "I will take thee, O Zerubbabel, my servant... and will make thee as a signet: for I have chosen thee, saith the LORD of hosts"

The signet worn on the person (heart, arm, right hand) represents closeness and value. Jer 22:24 reverses the image — God removes Coniah like tearing off a signet ring. Hag 2:23 restores the image for Zerubbabel, reversing the Coniah judgment.

### 5. Natural Imagery
- **Job 38:14** — "It is turned as clay to the seal; and they stand as a garment"
- **Job 41:15** — "His scales are his pride, shut up together as with a close seal"

Job uses chowtham for comparisons from nature: the earth taking form like clay under a seal stamp (dawn light), and Leviathan's impenetrable scales fitted together like a sealed surface.
